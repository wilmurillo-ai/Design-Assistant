## Reddit

### r/ProductManagement

**Title:** I built a "thinking partner" skill to help PMs articulate ideas that feel right but hard to explain

**Body:**

I've been a PM for years, and I noticed that the hardest part isn't finding ideas — it's articulating them clearly enough to:

- Get engineers excited to build it
- Get investors to understand the opportunity
- Get yourself to stop second-guessing

Most AI tools immediately judge your idea ("here's the SWOT analysis") or generate stuff you didn't ask for ("here's a full PRD").

So I built something different: a skill that walks you through 5 conversations to help you articulate what you already intuitively know.

**The 5 conversations:**

1. **The Origin Story** — "What did you observe that others missed?"
2. **The Opportunity Space** — "How big could this be?"
3. **The First 100 Users** — "Who shows up on Day 1?"
4. **The Movie Scene** — "Walk me through a user's Friday night"
5. **The Landscape** — "Who else has tried, and what can we learn?"

The output is a **Clarity Map** — not a verdict on whether your idea is good or bad, but a clear picture of what you understand and where to dig deeper.

**Real example:**

PM says: "I want to build a nightclub discovery mini-program."

After 15 minutes of conversation, we arrived at: "Your product's job is to make the user look like the friend who always knows where to go."

That insight was hiding in plain sight — the PM just needed help pulling it out.

It's open source and free: https://github.com/viliawang-pm/product-sense-coach

Would love feedback from other PMs. Does this match your experience? What conversations would you add?

Hi HN,

I'm a PM who's been struggling with the gap between product intuition and articulation. Most AI tools either judge your idea immediately or generate content you didn't ask for.

So I built a "thinking partner" skill that walks you through 5 conversations:

1. The Origin Story — "What did you observe that others missed?"

It's inspired by Zhang Xiaolong's (WeChat creator) philosophy: "Product managers should understand human nature, create simple rules, and let users evolve within them."

Would love feedback on the conversation design. What questions would you add for the HN audience's typical projects?
