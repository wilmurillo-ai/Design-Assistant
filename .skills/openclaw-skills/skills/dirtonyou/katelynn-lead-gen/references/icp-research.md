# ICP Research Tactics

This reference covers how to find and qualify leads efficiently using publicly available sources.

---

## Search Strategies by ICP Type

### B2B / SaaS Companies
- **Crunchbase**: Filter by industry, HQ, funding stage, employee count
  - `site:crunchbase.com <industry> <location>`
- **ProductHunt**: Find recent product launches — founders are often reachable
  - `site:producthunt.com <category>`
- **LinkedIn via Google**: `site:linkedin.com/company <ICP keywords>`
- **G2 / Capterra**: Companies listing competitor products are active buyers
- **GitHub**: If targeting dev-heavy companies, org pages reveal tech stack and team size

### Professional Services / Agencies
- **Clutch.co**: Filterable agency directory with client reviews
- **UpCity / Agency Spotter**: Local/niche agencies
- **LinkedIn**: `site:linkedin.com/in "<Title>" "<Location>"` (e.g., `"Head of Growth" "Austin"`)

### E-commerce / DTC Brands
- **Shopify store search**: `site:myshopify.com <niche>`
- **Similarweb / SemRush**: Find traffic-rich stores in a category
- **Jungle Scout / Helium 10** (if Amazon): Find growing Amazon sellers

### Local Businesses
- **Google Maps**: Search category + city, read reviews for owner name
- **Yelp / TripAdvisor**: Good for hospitality, restaurants, services
- **Chamber of Commerce directories**: Often list owner names and contact info

## Signals That Increase Lead Quality

The best outreach hooks come from **recency** and **specificity**. Look for:

| Signal | Where to Find It | Why It Works |
|--------|-----------------|--------------|
| Recent funding round | Crunchbase, TechCrunch, press release | Company is actively growing — spending budget |
| New hire in a key role | LinkedIn "joined X weeks ago" | New leaders want to make an impact quickly |
| Job posting for a role your product/service replaces | LinkedIn Jobs, Indeed | They feel the pain you solve |
| Blog post, podcast appearance, or conference talk | Google, Spotify, YouTube | Shows what they care about — reference it |
| Product launch | ProductHunt, Twitter/X | Momentum moment — receptive to tools that amplify it |
| Award or recognition | Trade publications, "best of" lists | Positive signal — they're open to a congratulations opener |
| Recent pivot or rebrand | Press, LinkedIn company page | Major change = new needs |
| Customer complaint trend | G2, Trustpilot | If their customers complain about X and you solve X, gold |

## Finding Contact Information

**Always try these in order before giving up:**

1. **Company website**: /about, /team, /contact pages
2. **LinkedIn profile**: Often lists role, sometimes email format
3. **Email pattern inference**: If you know `john.smith@company.com` works for one person,
   try `firstname.lastname@`, `firstname@`, `f.lastname@` for others
4. **Hunter.io / Apollo.io**: Free tiers allow limited lookups (user must have accounts)
5. **Twitter/X bio**: Many founders and executives list email or Calendly links
6. **GitHub profile**: Developer founders often have contact info there

**Never fabricate or guess email addresses.** If you can't find contact info, record
"LinkedIn outreach recommended" in the leads table.

## Qualifying a Lead Before Writing the Message

Ask these questions mentally before drafting:

1. Does this company/person actually match the ICP criteria?
2. Can I find one genuine, specific thing about them to reference?
3. Is there a reason they'd care about the value prop *right now* (timing signal)?
4. Is the contact person the right decision-maker for this offer?

If the answer to #1 or #4 is "no", flag the lead as low-confidence rather than spending time on the message.

---

## Batch Research Efficiency Tips

- Open 5-10 tabs at once and scan each quickly for the hook before deep-diving
- Write the hook in the leads table immediately — memory is lossy
- If a source requires login (LinkedIn), note what you found via Google snippet instead
- Aim to spend ~3-5 minutes per lead on research; more than 10 means the source is too hard
