## Kumamoto / Japan Semiconductor Hub Ecosystem

### JASM (TSMC Japan) — Fab 1 + Fab 2
- Location: Kikuyo, Kumamoto Prefecture
- Partners: TSMC (majority), Sony Semiconductor, Denso, Toyota
- Fab 1: 12/16nm, 22/28nm, 40nm — operational
- Fab 2: upgraded from 6/7nm to **3nm** (confirmed Feb 2026); investment ~$17B; construction started Oct 2025, paused Dec 2025; target late 2027 (at risk of delay due to node change). Pivot driven by weak auto demand + AI surge
- Water: Aso volcanic caldera groundwater — key site selection factor

### Rapidus (Hokkaido) — for comparison
- Location: Chitose, Hokkaido (IIM-1 facility)
- Technology: 2nm GAA (IBM partnership); 1.4nm planned ~2029 with IBM
- Pilot line operational since April 2025; first 2nm transistor prototype demonstrated July 2025 (12 days vs expected 6 weeks)
- ASML NXE:3800E EUV installed Dec 2024; 200+ tools installed by June 2025
- Mass production target: 2H FY2027 (25K wafers/month)
- Risk: Very high (no prior leading-edge manufacturing experience; 5K→25K wafer ramp in one year is aggressive)
- Government subsidy: cumulative ~2.9T yen (~$19B) through METI
- Private investment: 167.6B yen from 32 companies (SoftBank, Sony, Toyota, NTT, Kioxia, etc.)
- 60+ companies in discussions, no public customer commitments yet; IPO target FY2031
