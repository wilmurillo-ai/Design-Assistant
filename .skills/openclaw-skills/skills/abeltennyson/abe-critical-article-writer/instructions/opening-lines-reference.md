# Opening Lines Reference Library

## Rhetorical Question Openers

"What am I missing here?"
"How does anyone think this is sustainable?"
"Since when did we stop questioning the numbers?"
"Has anyone actually looked at the underlying assumptions?"
"What would happen if [X] stopped working tomorrow?"

## Direct Observation Openers

"Well, who could've seen this coming…"
"I'm starting to think [X] looks more and more like a Ponzi scheme"
"Just noticed something interesting about how [topic] is evolving"
"I've been watching this unfold, and something doesn't add up"
"The more I look at [topic], the less sense it makes"

## Comparative Openers

"Here's the difference between what [Company A] claims and what [Company B] is actually doing"
"We've seen this pattern before, and here's how it ended last time"
"Compare [Product A]'s financial model to [Product B]'s—notice anything?"

## Statistical/Data Openers

"The numbers on this are genuinely wild"
"Looking at the data from [source], three things jump out immediately"
"I pulled together data from [X sources], and here's what it reveals"

## Experience-Based Openers

"After [X years] building [Y], I've started to notice [pattern]"
"I've been using [product] for [timeframe], and something shifted recently"
"Testing [solution] across [contexts], I'm seeing [consistent finding]"

## Vulnerability Openers

"I'm terrible at finance, but something about these projections doesn't make sense"
"I could be completely wrong about this, but here's what concerns me"
"Full transparency: I don't understand how this is supposed to work, and I'm not sure anyone does"

## Pattern Recognition Openers

"Watching [Company A], [Company B], and [Company C] all move in [direction] this quarter"
"Three separate incidents this week all point to the same uncomfortable conclusion"
"This is the 5th time I've seen [pattern] across [industry], and each time it ends the same way"

## Challenge Openers

"Stop what you're doing and read this research on [topic]"
"If you believe [common claim], you need to see this data"
"Your understanding of [topic] is probably incomplete—here's why"

---

## Closing Observation Options

"That's when you know something is fundamentally wrong with the model."
"...which raises the question nobody wants to ask."
"Makes you wonder if anyone in the room is actually doing the math."
"And if [X] isn't sustainable, everything built on top of it starts looking different."
"Which means what we're really watching is [reframing]."
"So here's what I want to know: [provocative question]"

**Use these as inspiration, but adapt to your specific content and maintain authenticity.**
