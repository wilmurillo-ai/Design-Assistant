# VeritaSource - Source Quality Reference List

> ⚠️ **Important Disclaimer**: This is a **reference document**, not a definitive blacklist. Source quality is context-dependent, and ratings reflect community consensus rather than absolute truth.
>
> **Purpose**: Provide guidance for evaluating source credibility. Users retain full control over which sources to include or exclude.
>
**Basis**: Ratings compiled from Wikipedia [Perennial Sources List](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources/Perennial_sources), [维基百科可靠来源](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:%E5%8F%AF%E9%9D%A0%E6%9D%A5%E6%BA%90/%E5%B8%B8%E8%A7%81%E6%9C%89%E4%BA%89%E8%AE%AE%E6%9D%A5%E6%BA%90%E5%88%97%E8%A1%A8), NewsGuard, Media Bias/Fact Check, and other third-party assessments.
>
> **Limitations**: 
> - Western-centric bias in evaluation frameworks
> - Temporal limitations (ratings change over time)
> - Context matters (a source rated low for politics may be fine for other topics)
>
> **User Control**: You can always request inclusion of any source, regardless of its rating here.
> 
Last Updated: March 2026 (Wikipedia sources verified)

## I. Chinese Unreliable Sources

### 1. Content Farms

Content farms are websites that rapidly produce large amounts of online articles to attract traffic for commercial benefits like advertising revenue. Main characteristics include: heavy plagiarism, secondary reprints, garbage content, sensational headlines, and anonymous authors.

| Website/Platform Type | Reason for Unreliability | Content Type | Alternative |
|--------------|-----------|---------|---------|
| **各类"小X百科网"** (Various "XX Encyclopedia" sites) | 采集站，爬虫抓取原创内容后拼凑或机翻；大量AI生成内容 (Scraper sites, machine translation/paraphrasing; heavy AI-generated content) | 百科知识、教程类 (Encyclopedia, tutorials) | Wikipedia、知乎、专业书籍 |
| **程序员XXX网** (Programmer XXX sites) | 爬取技术博客内容，机器翻译StackOverflow/GitHub内容 (Crawling technical blogs, machine-translating StackOverflow/GitHub content) | 技术教程、代码片段 (Technical tutorials) | GitHub、StackOverflow、官方文档 |
| **兰州养生网** (Lanzhou Health Network) | 臭名昭著的内容农场，曾创建数千个分身域名 (Notorious content farm, created thousands of clone domains) | 健康养生、生活百科 (Health, wellness) | 权威医疗机构网站、政府健康平台 |
| **kknews.cc** | 机器翻译采集站，内容质量极低 (Machine translation scraper, extremely low content quality) | 新闻聚合 (News aggregation) | 正规新闻网站 |
| **热备资讯类网站** (Hot Backup News sites) | 采集拼接内容，排版混乱 (Scraped and stitched content, messy layout) | 各类资讯 (Various news) | 原始出处网站 |
| **代码日志类采集站** (Code Log scraper sites) | 盗用技术文章，移除原作者信息 (Stealing technical articles, removing original author info) | 技术博客 (Technical blogs) | 博客园 (CNBlogs), 稀土掘金 (Juejin), CSDN优质作者 |

### 5. AI-Generated Fake News Websites

According to NewsGuard's 2023 report, **277 unreliable AI-generated news websites** (UAINs) have been identified, including Chinese-language sites:

| Characteristic | Description |
|-----|------|
| Unsupervised | Little or no human oversight |
| AI-generated content | Mainly or entirely written by bots |
| Multi-language coverage | 13 languages including Chinese |
| Ad revenue | Purely for profiting from Google Ads |
| Mixed quality | True and false information indistinguishable, no credibility at all |

**Identification methods**:
- Lack of About Us, contact information, etc.
- Missing or suspicious author information
- Illogical content structure、语法错误
- Abnormally high update frequency (hundreds per day)

### 4. Content Farms and MFA (Made for Advertising)

**特征**：低质量内容、过度广告、标题党、AI生成文章、流量套利

| Type | Description | Examples/Characteristics |
|------|------|----------|
| **AI生成新闻网站** (UAINs) | 完全/主要由AI撰写的内容，通用名称如"iBusiness Day"、"Ireland Top News"、"Daily Time Update" | NewsGuard已识别1,000+此类网站，每天产生1,200+篇文章 |
| **标题党内容农场** | 耸人听闻的标题、实质内容极少 | ViralNova、Upworthy（历史上） |
| **内容聚合器** | 收集标题而不增加价值，重度变现 | 各种低质量新闻聚合器 |
| **SEO优化薄内容** | 针对长尾关键词但深度极浅 | 各种自动化内容网络 |
| **程序化广告农场** | 数千个域名的网络，含自动化内容 | 各种MFA网络 |

**关键统计数据**：
- MFA网站占广告展示的**21%**（ANA研究）
- 估计每年在MFA网站上浪费**130亿美元**
- NewsGuard发现**140+主要品牌**在AI生成的不可靠网站上投放广告

## III. Red Flags for Identifying Unreliable Sources

Based on recommendations from multiple fact-checking organizations, please note the following warning signals:

### 🔴 Domain Red Flags
- URLs ending in `.com.co` (masquerading as real news)
- Strange domain extensions (e.g., `.lo`, etc.)
- Unusual domain names
- Domains containing random character combinations

### 🔴 Content Red Flags
- **ALL CAPS headlines**
- Sensational language ("shocking", "outrageous", "explosive")
- No author attribution
- No "About Us" or contact information
- Many spelling/grammar errors
- Labeled as "sponsored content"
- Clickbait: "You won't believe what happened next"

### 🔴 Engagement Strategy Red Flags
- Designed to make you **very angry**
- Encourages **doxing** individuals
- Only appears on social media, not traditional media

### 🔴 Design Red Flags
- Unprofessional website design
- Excessive ads/pop-ups
- Auto-playing videos
- Clickbait slideshows

### 🔴 Content Characteristics Red Flags
- Lack of cited sources or suspicious sources
- Pages filled with ads
- Messy layout, many irrelevant links
- 内容逻辑混乱
- Abnormally high update frequency (hundreds per day)

## Usage Recommendations

1. **Consider context**: A source rated "unreliable" for political news may be acceptable for entertainment or opinion pieces
2. **Cross-validate**: Important information should be verified across multiple sources with different perspectives
3. **Use your judgment**: These ratings are guidelines, not rules
4. **Request specific sources**: If you need information from a particular source, explicitly request it regardless of its rating
5. **Develop media literacy**: Learn to recognize common patterns of clickbait and misinformation

## Important Notes on Source Evaluation

### Context Dependency

Source reliability is **highly context-dependent**:

| Source Type | Political News | Technology | Entertainment | Opinion |
|-------------|----------------|------------|---------------|---------|
| Tabloid newspaper | Unreliable | May be OK | Often suitable | N/A |
| Self-media platform | Generally unreliable | Mixed | Suitable | Can be valuable |
| Government media | Fact-check political claims | Usually reliable | N/A | Represents official view |
| Academic journal | N/A | Reliable | N/A | Peer-reviewed |

### When to Disregard These Ratings

You may choose to use sources rated here as "unreliable" when:
- You need diverse perspectives on controversial topics
- You're researching media bias itself
- The source has specific expertise in a niche area
- You're looking for opinion pieces or commentary
- Primary sources are unavailable and this is the best available

### Bias Acknowledgment

This list may reflect:
- **Western-centric bias**: Non-Western media may be underweighted
- **Mainstream preference**: Independent/alternative sources may be underrepresented
- **Institutional bias**: Established institutions rated higher than challengers

Always supplement these ratings with your own knowledge and judgment.
