## Regulatory Framework

| Authority | Full Name | Scope |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| **IA** | Insurance Authority | Primary regulator; sole regulator of all insurers and insurance intermediaries since 2019 |
| **SFC** | Securities and Futures Commission | Co-regulates ILAS (Investment-Linked Assurance Schemes); issues codes for linked products |
| **HKMA** | Hong Kong Monetary Authority | Co-regulates bancassurance (bank-sold insurance) |
| **LegCo** | Legislative Council | Legislative oversight of Insurance Ordinance amendments |

**Governing Legislation:**

| Instrument | Description | Effective |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Insurance Ordinance (Cap. 41) | Primary law governing all insurance and reinsurance | In force |

| Insurance (Valuation and Capital) Rules (Cap. 41R) | Pillar 1 RBC quantitative requirements | 1 Jul 2024 |
| Insurance (Submission of Statements, Reports and Information) Rules (Cap. 41S) | Pillar 3 reporting requirements | 1 Jul 2024 |
| Companies Ordinance (Cap. 622) | Governs company incorporation; amended 2025 for re-domiciliation | May 2025 |
| Securities and Futures Ordinance (Cap. 571) | Governs ILP / ILAS; Professional Investor definition | In force |

## Key IA Requirements

### 1. Market Entry & Licensing

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Authorisation | Required under Insurance Ordinance (Cap. 41) before carrying on insurance business in or from Hong Kong |
| Composite Insurer | Permitted; minimum capital HK$20 million (vs HK$10 million for mono-line) |
| Foreign Branch | Permitted; must be incorporated in jurisdiction with comprehensive company and insurance law; subject to effective home supervision |
| Re-Domiciliation | New mechanism effective May 2025; particularly relevant for Bermuda-incorporated insurers writing majority of business in Hong Kong |
| Intermediary Limit | Insurance agent may be appointed to a maximum of **4 authorised insurers**, of which **no more than 2** carry on long-term business |

### 2. Risk-Based Capital (RBC) — Three-Pillar Framework 🆕

**Effective 1 July 2024** — Hong Kong's most significant regulatory change in decades.

**Pillar 1 — Quantitative Capital Requirements:**

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Prescribed Capital Amount (PCA) | Insurers must hold capital sufficient to absorb a **1-in-200-year adverse event** (99.5% confidence over 1 year) |
| Risk Modules | Market Risk, Life Insurance Risk, General Insurance Risk, Counterparty Default Risk, Operational Risk |
| Minimum Capital Amount | 50% of PCA (unless IA varies on case-by-case basis) |
| Hard Floor | Capital base must be at least HK$20 million |
| Asset-Liability Matching | RBC approach rewards good ALM; insurers with solid risk management carry lower capital requirements |
| HKFRS 17 Alignment | Insurance liabilities valued per Part 4 of Insurance (Valuation and Capital) Rules; compatible with IFRS 17 principles |

**Pillar 2 — Enterprise Risk Management (ERM):**

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| ERM Framework | Qualitative requirements for risk governance and management |
| ORSA | Own Risk and Solvency Assessment; **already in force** (early implementation ahead of Pillar 3) |
| Risk Appetite | Documented risk appetite statement required |

**Pillar 3 — Reporting and Disclosure:**

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Regulatory Reporting | Commenced 1 Jul 2024 |
| Public Disclosure | Draft rules consulted March 2025; **targeted for implementation in 2026** |
| D-SII (Systemically Important Insurers) | IA framework launched Oct 2025; two-stage assessment: quantitative indicators (size 40%, substitutability 25%, interconnectedness 25%, liquidity 10%) |

> **Config Gap:** Pillar 3 public disclosure rules targeted for 2026 — InsureMO reporting module may need additional disclosure output fields. Monitor LegCo negative vetting process.

### 3. Participating Business (GL34 + Practice Note 2025) 🆕

**Practice Note on Remuneration Structures — Effective 1 January 2026:**

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Scope | All participating long-term insurance policies with regular premium payment terms |
| Commission Spreading | Commissions must be spread across the policy term; concentrated upfront commissions restricted |
| Non-Financial Metrics | Insurer must use objective non-financial performance metrics in designing commission arrangements |
| Overriding Commissions | Spreading requirement does NOT apply to overriding commissions paid to agent managers |
| Record Keeping | Documentation justifying commission design must be retained for **7 years** |
| Demonstration of Compliance | Insurers must be prepared to demonstrate compliance on IA request |
| Regulatory Purpose | Under GL34: ensure participating business managed sustainably; fair treatment of policyholders throughout policy duration |
| IA Further Review | IA indicated it will further review insurers' approach to **design and sale** of participating policies (ongoing in 2026) |

> **Config Gap:** Commission system must support spreading schedule tied to policy term; non-financial metric flag for performance-based components; 7-year record retention.

### 4. ILAS / ILP (Investment-Linked Assurance Schemes)

**Co-regulated by IA (GL15, GL26) and SFC:**

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Green Light Process | New ILAS products submitted to IA for approval via green light process |
| Expedited Approval | Protection-linked plans (specified minimum mortality + simple features) eligible for expedited approval |
| Fund Switching | Unrestricted switching (no annual cap; unlike MAS 12/year limit in SG) |
| FNA + RPQ | Financial Needs Analysis and Risk Profile Questionnaire required for standard ILAS |
| Disclosure | Insurance and investment components separated in product illustration |
| Bid/Ask Spread | Disclosed per SFC requirements |

**Indexed Universal Life (IUL) — New Regime 2025 🆕:**

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Classification | Class C (linked long-term) = ILAS; subject to GL15 and GL26 |
| Professional Investors (PI) Only | IUL may only be sold to PIs as defined in SFO (Cap. 571) |
| Relaxed Requirements for PIs | FNA and RPQ NOT required; but suitability assessment required |
| PI Verification | Insurer must have controls to confirm PI status before introducing product; re-verify on change of policy owner |
| Green Light | NOT required for ILAS (including IUL); but product documents required for remuneration purposes |
| GL16 Application | Certain GL16 provisions (long-term non-Class C) apply to IUL given its universal life features |
| Further Guidance | IA indicated additional guidance forthcoming for IUL sold to PIs |

### 5. Distribution Channels

| Channel | Status | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Tied Agency | ✅ | Max 4 insurers (max 2 long-term); IA licensed |
| Independent Broker | ✅ | IA licensed; activity-based regulation |
| Bancassurance | ✅ | IA + HKMA dual oversight |
| Direct | ✅ | Permitted for some products |
| Digital / Online | ✅ | No active marketing from outside HK; conduct standards apply |
| Referral Schemes | ⚠️ | Under IA regulatory review and focus in 2025–2026 |
| Active Marketing from Outside HK | ❌ | Prohibited under Insurance Ordinance (restriction since 2019 regime change) |

### 6. Long-Term Insurance — Underwriting and Product

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Cooling-Off Period | 21 days for long-term policies |
| Non-Disclosure | Common law duty of utmost good faith; material non-disclosure may void policy |
| Policy Illustration | Projection scenarios required; GL16 guidance |
| Compulsory Insurance | Motor third-party liability; Employee compensation |

### 7. Policyholder Protection Fund (PPF) — Pending 🆕

| Status | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Under Consideration | HK Government considering legislation for PPF |
| Structure | Two separate schemes: one for life, one for general insurance |
| Trigger | Available to pay claims in event of insurer insolvency |
| Timeline | Legislation being developed; no confirmed effective date as of Q1 2026 |

> **Config Gap (future):** PPF levy collection and remittance may require InsureMO billing module enhancement when enacted.

### 8. Greater Bay Area (GBA) Cross-Border Insurance

| Item | Status |
|------|--------|
| GBA Cross-Border Scheme | Under active discussion; possible mutual recognition of prudential supervision between HK and Mainland China |
| Cross-Border Motor Insurance | Products recently established under IA / GBA framework; connect (Insurance Connect) scheme referenced |
| Offshore Reinsurance | Growing trend of HK life insurers reinsuring legacy business blocks to offshore reinsurers (Bermuda/HK-based) |
| Re-Domiciliation | May 2025 mechanism enables Bermuda insurers to re-domicile to HK; several large groups actively using mechanism |

### 9. General Insurance — Capital and Solvency

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Assets in HK | General insurers (excl. professional reinsurers and captives) must maintain HK assets ≥ 80% of net liabilities + solvency margin |
| Solvency Formula | 20% of relevant premium income up to HK$200M + 10% above HK$200M; or 20% of outstanding claims up to HK$200M + 10% above; subject to HK$10M minimum (HK$20M for statutory classes) |
| RBC (from Jul 2024) | RBC PCA replaces old solvency margin as primary measure |

### 10. Insurance-Linked Securities (ILS)

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Regime | Established 2021; specific ILS rules in Insurance Ordinance |
| Vehicle | Special Purpose Reinsurance Vehicle (SPRV) authorised by IA for "special purpose business" |
| SGX Listing | 28 catastrophe bonds issued as of Nov 2024, 8 listed on SGX |
| Treatment | ILS protection treated as insurance for HK law purposes; subject to specific rules |

## INVARIANT Declarations

```
INVARIANT 1: Insurance agent may be appointed to maximum 4 authorised insurers;
             no more than 2 of which may carry on long-term business
  Source: Insurance Ordinance (Cap. 41)
  Effect: Agent appointment workflow must enforce 4-insurer / 2-long-term limits

INVARIANT 2: Active marketing of insurance services to HK public from outside HK is prohibited
  Source: Insurance Ordinance (as amended 2019)
  Effect: Digital and cross-border channels must include HK-residency / location check

INVARIANT 3: IUL (Indexed Universal Life) products may only be sold to Professional Investors
  Source: IA/SFC Joint Circular 2025
  Effect: IUL product must enforce PI verification at policy application and on ownership change

INVARIANT 4: Commission arrangements for par policies must comply with spreading requirements
             and retain documentation for 7 years
  Source: IA Practice Note (effective 1 Jan 2026)
  Effect: Commission system must apply spreading schedule; doc management must retain commission
          design records for 7 years minimum

INVARIANT 5: Capital base must not fall below the higher of PCA, Minimum Capital Amount, or HK$20M
  Source: Insurance (Valuation and Capital) Rules (Cap. 41R)
  Effect: Capital adequacy monitoring must use RBC PCA as primary threshold (not old solvency margin)

INVARIANT 6: ORSA must be maintained; results must be fed back to risk management framework
  Source: RBC Pillar 2; ORSA already in force (early implementation)
  Effect: Risk management module must include ORSA documentation and periodic refresh cycle
```
