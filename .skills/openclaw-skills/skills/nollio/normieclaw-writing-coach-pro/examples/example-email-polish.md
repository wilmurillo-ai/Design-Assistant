# Example: Email Polish

## Context
A project manager needs to send a status update to their VP. The draft is wordy, wishy-washy, and buries the lead.

## Before

> Subject: Update on Q2 project stuff
>
> Hi Jennifer,
>
> I wanted to reach out and give you a quick update on where things stand with the Q2 website redesign project. We have been making progress on various aspects of the project and I think we are in a pretty good place overall, although there are definitely some areas where we could potentially be doing better.
>
> The design team has been working really hard on the new homepage mockups and they were completed last Friday. I think they look very nice. The development team has started implementing the frontend components and they are estimated to be done by the end of next week hopefully.
>
> One thing I should mention is that we have been experiencing some delays with the content migration due to the fact that the legacy CMS has a lot of formatting issues that need to be cleaned up manually. This is taking longer than we originally anticipated. We're currently exploring some options to try to speed this up.
>
> In terms of the budget, we are currently tracking at about $12,000 against our $15000 budget which I think is reasonable at this point in time.
>
> Please let me know if you have any questions or if there's anything else you need from me.
>
> Best,
> Marcus

## Agent Review (Quick Review Mode)

Found 6 issues:

1. **"wanted to reach out and give you"** → "Here's" — remove throat-clearing. Get to the point.
2. **"due to the fact that"** → "because" — wordy phrase
3. **"at this point in time"** → "currently" or cut entirely — filler
4. **"very nice"** → cut or replace with specific feedback — "very" weakens the sentence
5. **"$15000"** → "$15,000" — inconsistent number formatting (you wrote "$12,000" with a comma earlier)
6. **"they are estimated to be done by the end of next week hopefully"** → "they should finish by end of next week" — passive construction + hedging

## After (Rewrite Mode)

> Subject: Q2 Website Redesign — Status Update
>
> Hi Jennifer,
>
> Here's where we stand on the Q2 website redesign.
>
> **On track:** Homepage mockups are done (completed Friday). The dev team is building frontend components now and should finish by end of next week.
>
> **Risk:** Content migration is behind schedule. The legacy CMS has formatting issues that require manual cleanup — more than we scoped. We're evaluating tools to speed this up and I'll have a revised timeline by Thursday.
>
> **Budget:** Tracking at $12,000 against our $15,000 budget. On target.
>
> Happy to jump on a call if you want to discuss the migration delay.
>
> Marcus

## What Changed

- **Cut word count by 52%** (186 → 89 words). Same information, half the reading time.
- **Led with structure**: bolded section headers make it scannable
- **Eliminated hedging**: "I think we are in a pretty good place overall, although there are definitely some areas where we could potentially be doing better" → cut entirely (says nothing)
- **Named the risk clearly**: instead of burying delays in paragraph 3, flagged it explicitly
- **Added a commitment**: "I'll have a revised timeline by Thursday" gives the VP something concrete
- **Fixed number formatting**: consistent comma usage in dollar amounts
- **Removed filler phrases**: "I wanted to reach out", "in terms of", "at this point in time"
