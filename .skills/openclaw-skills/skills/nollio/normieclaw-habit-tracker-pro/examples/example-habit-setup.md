# Example: Setting Up a New Habit

This shows the full flow of adding a new habit with accountability rules,
including stacking and cross-tool sync.

---

## Scenario: User Wants to Start Running

**User:**

> I want to start running. Nothing crazy, maybe 3 times a week. I've never been consistent with it.

**Agent:**

> Let's set it up. A few quick things:
>
> 1. **3x per week** — any specific days, or flexible?
> 2. **Morning, afternoon, or evening?**
> 3. **Category:** fitness work for you, or something else?
>
> And since you mentioned consistency is hard — do you want to stack it after
> an existing habit? You meditate every morning. Running after meditation could
> anchor it.

**User:**

> MWF mornings. fitness is fine. and yeah let's stack it after meditation

**Agent:**

> ✅ **Running (3x/week)** added.
> MWF · Morning · #fitness
> Stacked after: Morning Meditation
>
> Current streak: 0 · Longest: 0
>
> I'll check in about the meditation → running block together on MWF mornings.
> On T/Th/weekends, meditation stands alone.
>
> One more thing — you've got Trainer Buddy installed. Want running to auto-complete
> when Trainer Buddy logs a run? Saves you from telling me twice.

**User:**

> yeah do that

**Agent:**

> ✅ Running syncs with Trainer Buddy. When it logs a run, this habit auto-checks.
>
> Quick note on starting: don't worry about distance or pace right now. The
> habit is "go for a run." Even 10 minutes counts. We can get ambitious later
> once the consistency is there.

---

## Two Weeks Later: First Pattern Check

**Agent (during Monday check-in, Week 3):**

> Morning! Meditation → Running block — how'd Friday go?

**User:**

> meditated but skipped the run. it was freezing

**Agent:**

> Logged. Meditation ✅, Running ❌ (weather).
>
> Running is at 2 weeks now — here's where you stand:
> - 4 out of 6 scheduled runs completed (67%)
> - Both misses were Fridays
> - Streak: 2 (current) · Longest: 3
>
> Friday's emerging as the tough day for running. Weather was a factor both
> times. Want to keep Friday as-is, or swap it for an indoor alternative
> on cold days? You could do a home workout or gym cardio instead.

**User:**

> good idea. if it's under 30 degrees, let's just swap to gym cardio

**Agent:**

> ✅ Running has a cold-weather backup: if temp is under 30°F, Friday's run
> swaps to gym cardio. I'll note the context when I check in on Fridays.
>
> 4 out of 6 in your first two weeks is a solid start. Most people who say
> "I've never been consistent with running" don't make it past week 1.
> You're past that.

---

## What This Shows

1. **Habit creation is conversational**, not a form. The agent asks smart follow-ups.
2. **Stacking is suggested** when it makes sense — not forced.
3. **Cross-tool sync is offered** when relevant tools are detected.
4. **Early pattern analysis** kicks in at 2 weeks with actionable suggestions.
5. **Accountability is honest** — "4 out of 6" isn't celebrated with confetti,
   but it's acknowledged as a real start.
6. **Adaptability** — weather-based backup rules let the habit survive real life.
