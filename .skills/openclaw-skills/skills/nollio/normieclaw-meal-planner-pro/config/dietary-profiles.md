# Dietary Profiles Reference

When a household member's `dietary_style` is set, reference this file to understand what's included, excluded, and any macro considerations. Apply these constraints on TOP of any personal allergies/dislikes.

---

## Keto
- **Includes:** Meat, fish, eggs, cheese, butter, oils, nuts, seeds, low-carb vegetables (leafy greens, broccoli, cauliflower, zucchini, peppers)
- **Excludes:** Grains, sugar, most fruit (except small portions of berries), starchy vegetables (potatoes, corn, peas), legumes, bread, pasta, rice
- **Macros:** ~70% fat, ~25% protein, ~5% carbs (under 50g net carbs/day)
- **Notes:** Focus on healthy fats. Avocado, olive oil, and fatty fish are staples.

## Paleo
- **Includes:** Meat, fish, eggs, vegetables, fruits, nuts, seeds, healthy oils (olive, avocado, coconut)
- **Excludes:** Grains, legumes, dairy, refined sugar, processed foods, vegetable/seed oils (canola, soybean)
- **Macros:** No strict targets. Emphasis on whole, unprocessed foods.
- **Notes:** Sweet potatoes and root vegetables are encouraged. Think "would a caveman eat this?"

## Vegetarian
- **Includes:** All plant foods, dairy, eggs, honey
- **Excludes:** All meat, poultry, fish, and seafood. No gelatin (it's animal-derived).
- **Macros:** Ensure adequate protein via eggs, dairy, legumes, tofu, tempeh, seitan.
- **Notes:** Common pitfall is protein gaps — always include a protein source in each meal.

## Vegan
- **Includes:** All plant foods — vegetables, fruits, grains, legumes, nuts, seeds, plant milks, tofu, tempeh, seitan
- **Excludes:** ALL animal products — meat, fish, dairy, eggs, honey, gelatin, whey
- **Macros:** Protein via legumes, tofu, tempeh, seitan, nutritional yeast. B12 supplementation recommended.
- **Notes:** Check for hidden animal products in sauces and condiments (Worcestershire contains anchovies, many breads contain milk).

## Pescatarian
- **Includes:** Fish, seafood, plus all vegetarian foods (dairy, eggs, plants)
- **Excludes:** Meat and poultry (beef, pork, chicken, turkey, lamb)
- **Macros:** Good protein variety with fish + eggs + dairy.
- **Notes:** Emphasize sustainable seafood choices when possible.

## Mediterranean
- **Includes:** Vegetables, fruits, whole grains, legumes, nuts, olive oil, fish, moderate poultry, moderate dairy (yogurt, cheese), red wine in moderation
- **Excludes:** Red meat (limit to 1-2x/month), processed foods, refined sugars, butter (use olive oil instead)
- **Macros:** ~45% carbs, ~35% fat (mostly olive oil), ~20% protein
- **Notes:** This is a lifestyle pattern, not a strict diet. Focus on variety and whole foods.

## Low-FODMAP
- **Includes:** Rice, oats, potatoes, most meats, firm tofu, lactose-free dairy, blueberries, strawberries, grapes, carrots, cucumber, lettuce, tomato, zucchini
- **Excludes:** Garlic, onion, wheat, rye, most legumes, apples, pears, watermelon, mango, mushrooms, cauliflower, asparagus, honey, high-fructose corn syrup, milk, soft cheese
- **Macros:** No specific targets. Focus on digestive comfort.
- **Notes:** Typically done as an elimination phase (2-6 weeks), then gradual reintroduction. Agent should ask if they're in elimination or maintenance phase.

## Gluten-Free
- **Includes:** All naturally gluten-free foods — rice, corn, potatoes, quinoa, oats (certified GF), all meats, fish, dairy, fruits, vegetables
- **Excludes:** Wheat, barley, rye, spelt, and anything containing them (most bread, pasta, couscous, soy sauce, many sauces/marinades)
- **Macros:** No specific targets.
- **Notes:** Check sauces and marinades — soy sauce contains wheat (use tamari). Oats must be certified gluten-free due to cross-contamination.

## Dairy-Free
- **Includes:** All foods except dairy products
- **Excludes:** Milk, cheese, yogurt, butter, cream, ice cream, whey, casein
- **Macros:** Ensure calcium from other sources (fortified plant milks, leafy greens, almonds, sardines).
- **Notes:** Many processed foods contain hidden dairy (casein, whey, lactose). Ghee is sometimes tolerated by dairy-sensitive people but not by those with milk protein allergies.

## Whole30
- **Includes:** Meat, seafood, eggs, vegetables, fruits, natural fats (olive oil, coconut oil, ghee), nuts, seeds
- **Excludes:** Sugar (all forms), alcohol, grains, legumes (including peanuts and soy), dairy, MSG, sulfites, carrageenan
- **Macros:** No counting — focus on three plate meals with protein, vegetables, and fat.
- **Notes:** Strict 30-day elimination. No "recreating" treats (no paleo pancakes). Read labels carefully.

## Anti-Inflammatory
- **Includes:** Fatty fish, berries, leafy greens, tomatoes, olive oil, nuts, turmeric, ginger, green tea, avocado
- **Excludes:** Processed foods, refined carbs, fried foods, soda, red meat (limit), processed meat, margarine
- **Macros:** No strict targets. Emphasize omega-3 fatty acids and antioxidant-rich foods.
- **Notes:** Pairs well with Mediterranean approach. Focus on colorful produce.

## High-Protein
- **Includes:** All foods, with emphasis on protein sources at every meal — meat, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, tofu, protein-rich grains (quinoa)
- **Excludes:** Nothing specifically, but deprioritize low-protein fillers
- **Macros:** Target 1.0-1.2g protein per pound of target body weight. ~30-40% of calories from protein.
- **Notes:** Good for active individuals, muscle building, or weight management.

## Nut-Free (Allergy Protocol)
- **Includes:** All foods except tree nuts and peanuts
- **Excludes:** All tree nuts (almonds, cashews, walnuts, pecans, pistachios, macadamia, Brazil nuts, hazelnuts, pine nuts), peanuts, nut butters, nut milks, nut oils, nut flours
- **Macros:** No specific targets.
- **Notes:** This is a SAFETY constraint, not a preference. Cross-contamination matters. Check "may contain nuts" warnings. Coconut is botanically a fruit and is generally safe for nut-allergic individuals, but confirm with the user. Seed butters (sunflower, tahini) are usually safe substitutes.
