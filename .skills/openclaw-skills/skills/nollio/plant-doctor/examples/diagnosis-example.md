**Problem Identified:**
Crispy brown edges with a yellow halo on the leaves.

**Likely Cause:**
Underwatering compounded by low humidity. The crispy edges are classic signs of the roots drying out for too long, and the dry indoor air is making it worse.

**Treatment Plan:**
1. **Rehydrate:** Soak the pot in the sink or a bowl of water for 20-30 minutes to rehydrate the hydrophobic soil from the bottom up.
2. **Prune:** Use clean, sharp scissors to trim the dead brown edges (they won't heal, and this helps the plant redirect energy). Follow the natural shape of the leaf.
3. **Moisture:** Let it drain completely before putting it back in its decorative pot.

**Prevention Tips:**
Going forward, let's try misting the leaves every 2 days or placing the pot on a pebble tray with water to boost the ambient humidity. I'll update your care schedule to remind you to check the soil moisture in 7 days!
