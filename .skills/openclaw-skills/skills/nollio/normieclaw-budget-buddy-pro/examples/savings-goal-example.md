# Example: Savings Goal Tracking

This example shows creating and managing savings goals.

---

**User:** I want to save for a vacation to Japan

**Agent:** Love that goal! 🎌 Let me set it up. A few questions:

1. How much do you want to save? (If unsure, a 2-week Japan trip typically runs $3,000-$6,000 per person)
2. When do you want to go? (Target date helps me calculate monthly contributions)
3. How much can you set aside per month for this?

---

**User:** I'm thinking $5,000. I want to go next spring, so about 12 months. I can do $400/month.

**Agent:** 🎯 Savings goal created!

- **Goal:** Japan Vacation
- **Target:** $5,000
- **Monthly contribution:** $400
- **Timeline:** 12 months (target: March 2027)
- **Projected completion:** 12.5 months — you'll hit it in mid-March 2027 ✅

At $400/month, you'll actually reach $4,800 in 12 months — just $200 short. Options:
1. Bump to $420/month to hit it exactly in 12 months
2. Keep $400/month and cover the last $200 from your general savings
3. Keep $400/month and push the date to mid-April 2027

What works?

---

**User:** Let's do $420

**Agent:** Updated! $420/month gets you to $5,040 by March 2027 — a little buffer for travel snacks 🍡

Here's how it fits your budget:
- Current General Savings: $290/mo
- Redirecting $420 to Japan Vacation
- ⚠️ That's $130 more than your General Savings line. Want to pull from another category?

Suggestion: Your Travel budget has $200/month that's unused. Move $200 from Travel + $220 from General Savings = $420. Your General Savings drops to $70/mo.

---

**User:** Yeah do that

**Agent:** Done! Budget adjusted:

- ✈️ Travel: $200 → $0 (redirected to Japan fund)
- 💰 General Savings: $290 → $70
- 🎌 Japan Vacation: **$420/month** (new goal)

Your budget still balances at $5,200. I'll track progress in every summary.

**Current Goals:**
- 🏦 Emergency Fund: $987 / $10,000 (9.9%) — $500/mo
- 🎌 Japan Vacation: $0 / $5,000 (0%) — $420/mo

You're saving $920/month total across both goals — that's 17.7% of your income. Solid! 💪
