> ### TOKYO (Days 1-3)
>
> **Day 1 (Oct 22): Arrival & Shinjuku**
> - **Afternoon:** Arrive Narita → Narita Express to Shinjuku (60 min, covered by JR Pass!)
> - **Evening:** Omoide Yokocho for yakitori. Jordan: yakisoba stall or the tofu spot two alleys in.
>   Free views from Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building.
>
> **Day 2 (Oct 23): East Side — Asakusa → Akihabara**
> - **Morning:** Senso-ji Temple at 8 AM (before the crowds). Nakamise street for snacks.
> - **Lunch:** Daikokuya tempura in Asakusa — line moves fast. Veggie tempura set available.
> - **Afternoon:** Walk to Akihabara (30 min along the river, beautiful in fall). Explore electronics and anime shops.
> - **Evening:** Kanda Myojin Shrine (autumn illumination) → dinner in Ochanomizu area.
>
> **Day 3 (Oct 24): West Side — Harajuku → Shibuya**
> - **Morning:** Meiji Shrine — the ginkgo trees in October are unreal 🍂
> - **Lunch:** Afuri Ramen (yuzu shio, veggie option)
> - **Afternoon:** Shibuya Sky for views → Shibuya Crossing → Daikanyama for quiet bookshop cafes
> - **Evening:** Last Tokyo dinner at Ain Soph Ripple (vegan burger joint in Shinjuku — sounds weird, tastes incredible)
>
> ---
>
> ### 🚅 Transit Day (Oct 25)
> - **Morning:** Check out. Shinkansen Tokyo → Kyoto (10:30 departure, arrive 12:45). **Book right-side window seat for Mt. Fuji views** (if skies are clear).
> - **Afternoon:** Check into Kyoto hotel. Walk to Nishiki Market ("Kyoto's Kitchen") for a late lunch and exploring.
> - **Evening:** Pontocho alley for dinner — narrow, atmospheric, lantern-lit. Multiple veggie-friendly spots.
>
> ---
>
> ### KYOTO (Days 4-6)
>
> **Day 4 (Oct 25 afternoon + Oct 26): Temples & Gardens**
> - **Morning:** Fushimi Inari Shrine — arrive at 7 AM. The famous torii gates with zero crowds. You'll have the mountain trail mostly to yourself.
> - **Lunch:** Street food near Fushimi — inari sushi (fried tofu pockets, vegetarian by default!)
> - **Afternoon:** Kiyomizu-dera Temple — the autumn foliage viewing platform is peak season. Stunning.
> - **Evening:** Gion district walk. If lucky, spot geiko (geisha). Dinner at Okutan — 400-year-old tofu restaurant.
>
> **Day 5 (Oct 27): Arashiyama**
> - **Morning:** Bamboo Grove at sunrise (7 AM, near-empty). Tenryu-ji Temple gardens.
> - **Lunch:** Shoraian — tofu restaurant overlooking the river. 100% vegetarian-friendly.
> - **Afternoon:** Monkey Park (20-min hike, city views + wild macaques). Then Togetsukyo Bridge.
> - **Evening:** Dinner at Omen — famous udon, multiple veggie options. Near the center.
>
> **Day 6 (Oct 28): Philosopher's Path & North**
> - **Morning:** Philosopher's Path walk — 2km canal path, peak autumn colors 🍁. Connects Ginkaku-ji (Silver Pavilion) to Nanzen-ji.
> - **Lunch:** Cafe along the path — Kyoto is full of quiet, beautiful cafes.
> - **Afternoon:** Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion) — yes it's famous/touristy, but it's famous for a reason. Go at 3 PM for golden-hour reflections on the pond.
> - **Evening:** Early dinner → pack for Osaka transit.
>
> ---
>
> ### OSAKA (Days 7-8)
>
> **Day 7 (Oct 29): Dotonbori & Street Food**
> - **Morning:** Local train Kyoto → Osaka (30 min, JR Pass). Check into hotel.
> - **Late Morning:** Osaka Castle — autumn foliage in the park, castle interior is a museum.
> - **Lunch:** Kuromon Market — "Osaka's Kitchen." Fresh everything.
> - **Afternoon:** Dotonbori — the neon, the canal, the vibes. This is Osaka.
> - **Evening:** Street food crawl: takoyaki (octopus balls — Jordan skip these), okonomiyaki (fully customizable, veggie versions everywhere), and yakitori. Osaka is Japan's street food capital.
>
> **Day 8 (Oct 30): Departure**
> - **Morning:** Shinsekai neighborhood — retro Osaka, kushikatsu (deep-fried skewers, veggie options). It's charming and slightly gritty.
> - **Afternoon:** Transit to Kansai International Airport (KIX) for your return flight.
>

> ### 🧠 Japan-Wide Intel
> - **JR Pass:** 7-day pass covers most of this trip's transit. Buy online before departure.
> - **IC Card:** Get a Suica or ICOCA at the first station — works nationwide.
> - **Cash:** Carry ¥30,000-40,000 ($200-270). ATMs at 7-Eleven and post offices accept foreign cards.
> - **Shoes off:** At temples, ryokans, some restaurants. Wear shoes that slip on/off easily.
> - **October weather:** Perfect travel month. 15-22°C, dry, autumn foliage peaking.
> - **Temple etiquette:** Bow slightly when entering, don't point at statues, quiet voices.
>
