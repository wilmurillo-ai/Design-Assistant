# Example: Form Coaching Conversation

## Scenario
User is unsure about their deadlift form and has a mild lower back concern.

---

**User:** How do I deadlift properly? I feel it in my lower back and I'm not sure if that's normal.

**Agent:** Good question — feeling it in your lower back can mean a couple things. Let me break down proper deadlift form, and then we'll figure out what's going on.

**Conventional Deadlift — Step by Step:**

1. **Setup:** Stand with feet hip-width apart, bar over mid-foot. Shins about 1 inch from the bar.
2. **Grip:** Hinge at the hips, bend knees until shins touch the bar. Grab just outside your knees (double overhand or mixed grip).
3. **Brace:** Big breath into your belly, brace your core like someone's about to punch your stomach. This protects your spine.
4. **Engage lats:** "Bend the bar" around your shins — pull your shoulder blades down and back. You should feel tension in your lats before you pull.
5. **Drive:** Push the floor away with your legs. The bar should travel in a straight vertical line, staying in contact with your legs.
6. **Lock out:** Squeeze your glutes at the top. Stand tall — don't lean back.
7. **Lower:** Hinge at the hips first, then bend knees once the bar passes them. Control the descent.

**Most Common Mistakes:**

❌ **Rounding the lower back** — This is the #1 cause of "feeling it in your lower back." If your back rounds, the load shifts from your glutes/hamstrings to your spinal erectors. Fix: brace harder, drop the weight, and film yourself from the side.

❌ **Bar drifting forward** — If the bar moves away from your body, it creates a longer lever arm on your spine. Fix: keep the bar dragging up your legs. Wear long socks or pants.

❌ **Jerking the bar off the floor** — Don't yank it. Take the slack out of the bar first (pull until you hear/feel it click against the plates), then drive smoothly.

---

**User:** I think I might be rounding my back. Should I keep doing them?

**Agent:** Definitely don't push through it with bad form — that's how you get hurt.

Here's what I'd recommend:

1. **Drop the weight by 30-40%.** Practice the movement pattern with a weight that lets you maintain a perfectly flat back.
2. **Film yourself from the side.** Even one rep — you'll immediately see if your back is rounding.
3. **Try Romanian Deadlifts (RDLs) first.** They teach the hip hinge without the added complexity of pulling from the floor. Same muscles, safer learning curve.
4. **Add these to your warm-up:** Cat-cow stretches (10 reps) and glute bridges (2 × 15) to activate your posterior chain before pulling.

⚠️ **Important:** If you feel sharp pain (not just muscle fatigue), numbness, or tingling during or after deadlifts — stop and see a doctor or physical therapist. A dull "worked" feeling in your erectors is normal. Sharp, shooting, or persistent pain is not.

Want me to swap your deadlifts for RDLs or trap bar deadlifts in your next Pull day until your form feels solid?

---

**User:** Yeah, swap them for trap bar deadlifts for now.

**Agent:** Done — I've updated your Pull day. Trap bar deadlifts are in, conventional deadlifts are out until you're ready. The trap bar is more forgiving on your back since the load is centered around you instead of in front of you.

I also added a note to your profile: "Conventional deadlift — form work needed, using trap bar variant." I'll check back in a few weeks to see if you want to reintroduce conventional pulls.

Train smart. 🧠💪
