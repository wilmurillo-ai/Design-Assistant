# Example: Resume Bullet Improvement

## Context
A marketing manager updating their resume for a senior role. The bullets are duty-based, not achievement-based. Writing Coach Pro rewrites them to lead with impact.

## Before

> **Marketing Manager — Bright Signal, Inc. (2022–2025)**
>
> - Responsible for managing the company's social media accounts including Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and Twitter
> - Worked on developing and implementing email marketing campaigns for various product launches
> - Helped to increase website traffic through SEO optimization and content strategy efforts
> - Was in charge of coordinating with the design team to create marketing collateral for trade shows and events
> - Managed a marketing budget and ensured that spending was within approved limits
> - Participated in weekly meetings with the sales team to align marketing and sales strategies

## Agent Review (Coach Mode)

### The Core Problem: Duty Descriptions vs. Impact Statements

**What I found:** All six bullets describe what you were responsible for, not what you accomplished. Hiring managers skim resumes in 6-8 seconds. Duties tell them your role. Achievements tell them your value.

**The principle:** Strong resume bullets follow the **CAR formula**: **C**hallenge or context → **A**ction you took → **R**esult you delivered. Every bullet should answer: "So what? What happened because you did this?"

**Your pattern:** Every bullet starts with a weak verb or passive construction: "Responsible for", "Worked on", "Helped to", "Was in charge of", "Managed", "Participated in." These are duty-starters that bury your impact.

---

### Bullet-by-Bullet Rewrite

**Original:** "Responsible for managing the company's social media accounts including Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and Twitter"

**The fix:** What did managing those accounts achieve? Did followers grow? Did engagement improve? Did social drive leads?

**Rewrite:** "Grew social media following from 12K to 41K across four platforms in 18 months, generating 340+ qualified leads through organic content"

**Why this works:** Starts with a power verb ("Grew"), includes specific numbers, and connects the activity to a business outcome (leads).

---

**Original:** "Worked on developing and implementing email marketing campaigns for various product launches"

**The fix:** "Worked on" is invisible. How many campaigns? What were the results?

**Rewrite:** "Launched 15 email campaigns for 4 product releases, averaging 32% open rates and driving $280K in attributed pipeline"

**Why this works:** Quantifies the scope (15 campaigns, 4 products) and the outcome ($280K pipeline). "Launched" is stronger than "worked on."

---

**Original:** "Helped to increase website traffic through SEO optimization and content strategy efforts"

**The fix:** "Helped to" dilutes your ownership. If you did the work, own it. By how much did traffic increase?

**Rewrite:** "Increased organic traffic 3.2x (18K → 58K monthly sessions) through keyword strategy overhaul and 40-article content program"

**Why this works:** "3.2x" is more compelling than "increased." The specifics (18K → 58K, 40 articles) make it credible.

---

**Original:** "Was in charge of coordinating with the design team to create marketing collateral for trade shows and events"

**The fix:** "Was in charge of coordinating" is five words to say "led." What did the collateral help achieve?

**Rewrite:** "Led collateral production for 8 trade shows, contributing to 22% increase in booth-generated leads year over year"

**Why this works:** Ties the activity to a measurable business result. "Led" beats "was in charge of coordinating with."

---

**Original:** "Managed a marketing budget and ensured that spending was within approved limits"

**The fix:** Staying within budget is the bare minimum — not an achievement. Did you optimize spending? Find savings? Reallocate to higher-performing channels?

**Rewrite:** "Managed $350K annual marketing budget, reallocating 20% from underperforming paid channels to organic programs that delivered 2x higher ROI"

**Why this works:** Shows strategic thinking, not just compliance. The reallocation story demonstrates business judgment.

---

**Original:** "Participated in weekly meetings with the sales team to align marketing and sales strategies"

**The fix:** "Participated in meetings" is not a resume bullet. What came out of that alignment? If nothing measurable, cut this bullet entirely and use the space for a real achievement.

**Rewrite:** "Built marketing-sales feedback loop that reduced lead-to-close cycle from 47 to 31 days, earning cross-functional collaboration award"

**Why this works:** Transforms a process description into an outcome. If you don't have a specific result from sales alignment, drop this bullet.

---

## After

> **Marketing Manager — Bright Signal, Inc. (2022–2025)**
>
> - Grew social media following from 12K to 41K across four platforms in 18 months, generating 340+ qualified leads through organic content
> - Launched 15 email campaigns for 4 product releases, averaging 32% open rates and driving $280K in attributed pipeline
> - Increased organic traffic 3.2x (18K → 58K monthly sessions) through keyword strategy overhaul and 40-article content program
> - Led collateral production for 8 trade shows, contributing to 22% increase in booth-generated leads year over year
> - Managed $350K annual marketing budget, reallocating 20% from underperforming paid channels to organic programs that delivered 2x higher ROI
> - Built marketing-sales feedback loop that reduced lead-to-close cycle from 47 to 31 days

## Key Lesson

**Every bullet should make the reader think "I want that person on my team."** If a bullet could describe anyone in the role, it's not specific enough. Numbers, outcomes, and power verbs are the difference between a resume that gets interviews and one that gets skimmed.

**Note:** The numbers in the rewrite are illustrative. Replace them with your real metrics. Even approximate numbers ("~3x growth") beat no numbers at all.
