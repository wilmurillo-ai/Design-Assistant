# Example: Study Plan Generation

This shows how Tutor Buddy Pro creates a personalized study plan for an upcoming exam.

---

## The Interaction

**Student:**
> I have an Algebra II midterm on Friday. Can you help me make a study plan?

**Tutor Buddy Pro:**
> Absolutely! Let me build you a plan. A few quick questions:
>
> 1. What topics does the midterm cover?
> 2. How much time per day can you study?
> 3. Rate your confidence on each topic (1-5, where 1 = "totally lost" and 5 = "could teach it"):

**Student:**
> It covers quadratic equations, systems of equations, polynomial division, and rational expressions. I can do about 45 minutes a day. Confidence: quadratics 2, systems 4, polynomials 3, rational expressions 1

**Tutor Buddy Pro:**
> Got it. Your exam is Friday (3 days away), so we need to be strategic. Here's your plan:
>
> ---
>
> ## 📅 Algebra II Midterm Study Plan
> **Exam: Friday, March 13**
>
> ### Tuesday (Today) — 45 min
> **Focus: Rational Expressions** (confidence: 1 → priority: 🔴 HIGH)
>
> - ⏱ 0:00-0:15 — **Concept Review**
>   - What are rational expressions? (fractions with polynomials)
>   - How to find the domain (what values make the denominator 0?)
>   - Simplifying by factoring
>
> - ⏱ 0:15-0:35 — **Practice Problems**
>   - 5 problems: simplify rational expressions (I'll generate these)
>   - Start easy → build to medium difficulty
>
> - ⏱ 0:35-0:45 — **Self-Quiz**
>   - 3-question mini quiz to check what stuck
>
> ---
>
> ### Wednesday — 45 min
> **Focus: Quadratic Equations** (confidence: 2 → priority: 🟠 HIGH)
> **Review: Rational Expressions** (from yesterday)
>
> - ⏱ 0:00-0:05 — **Quick Review**
>   - 2 rational expression problems (spaced repetition from Tuesday)
>
> - ⏱ 0:05-0:20 — **Quadratics: Concept Review**
>   - Three methods: factoring, completing the square, quadratic formula
>   - When to use which method
>   - Discriminant — what it tells you
>
> - ⏱ 0:20-0:40 — **Practice Problems**
>   - 6 problems mixing all three methods
>
> - ⏱ 0:40-0:45 — **Self-Quiz**
>   - 3-question mini quiz
>
> ---
>
> ### Thursday — 45 min
> **Focus: Light Review of Everything** (NO new material — exam is tomorrow!)
> **Touch: Polynomials** (confidence: 3 → quick brush-up)
>
> - ⏱ 0:00-0:15 — **Polynomial Division Brush-Up**
>   - Long division vs. synthetic division
>   - 3 practice problems
>
> - ⏱ 0:15-0:35 — **Mixed Review Quiz**
>   - 10 questions covering ALL four topics
>   - Simulates exam conditions
>
> - ⏱ 0:35-0:45 — **Weak Spot Mop-Up**
>   - Whatever you missed on the mixed quiz, we review it now
>   - Write down 3 "cheat sheet" formulas to glance at Friday morning
>
> ---
>
> ### Friday (Exam Day)
> - 🚫 No heavy studying
> - ✅ Glance at your 3 key formulas
> - ✅ Take a deep breath. You prepared well.
> - ✅ **Crush it** 💪
>
> ---
>
> **Strategy notes:**
> - We're hitting your weakest topics first (rational expressions, quadratics) while your brain is freshest
> - Systems of equations (confidence: 4) gets skipped — you already know it well
> - Thursday is light review only — cramming the night before doesn't work
>
> Ready to start today's session? Let's tackle rational expressions! 🚀

---

## What to Notice

1. **Weak areas prioritized first** — rational expressions (confidence 1) on Day 1.
2. **Spaced repetition** — Tuesday's topic gets a quick review on Wednesday.
3. **No new material the night before** — research-backed study strategy.
4. **Strong topics deprioritized** — systems (confidence 4) skipped entirely to save time.
5. **Pomodoro-style time blocks** — each activity has a time allocation.
6. **Exam day encouragement** — the plan ends with confidence-building.
7. **Immediate action** — "Ready to start today's session?" keeps momentum.
