# Lesson Format Template

Detailed structure for generating individual lessons. Every lesson follows this skeleton, adapted to the topic, day number, difficulty, and learning style.

---

## Full Lesson Structure

```markdown
# Day [N]: [Lesson Title]

⏱️ **Estimated time:** [X] minutes | 📚 **Course:** [Topic] | 📊 **Progress:** [N]/[Total]

---

## 🔄 Quick Recap
[1-2 sentences connecting today to what came before. Skip for Day 1.]

Example: "Yesterday we explored how aperture controls depth of field — today we're tackling
the other side of the exposure triangle: shutter speed. Together, these two give you
creative control over almost every photo you take."

---

## 📖 Today's Lesson

### [Section 1 Title]

[Core teaching content. 2-4 paragraphs per section.]

[Key principles:]
- Use clear, conversational language
- Explain the WHY, not just the WHAT
- Include at least one analogy per section
- Use concrete examples, not abstract ones

[Analogy example:]
"Think of shutter speed like blinking. A fast blink (1/1000 sec) freezes everything
in a sharp snapshot. A slow blink (1/4 sec) lets everything blur together into
a dreamy smear. Your camera works the same way."

### [Section 2 Title]

[Continue building the concept. Add nuance.]

[Include a "Pro Tip" or "Common Mistake" callout:]

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** [Practical insight that saves time or prevents a common error]

### [Section 3 Title — if 30+ min lesson]

[Application or deeper exploration. Connect to real-world use.]

---

## ✏️ Practice Time

**Exercise 1: [Name]**
[Clear, specific instructions. Tell them exactly what to do, not vaguely what to think about.]

Example: "Go outside (or to a window) and take three photos of the same subject:
1. One at the fastest shutter speed your camera allows
2. One at 1/30 second
3. One at 1 second (use a stable surface)
Compare the three. What changed? What surprised you?"

**Exercise 2: [Name]**
[Slightly harder or different angle on the concept.]

**Exercise 3: [Name — if 60 min lesson]**
[Most challenging. May combine today's lesson with prior knowledge.]

---

## 🎯 Today's Homework

[One concrete task that applies the lesson to the learner's real life.]

Good homework:
- "Cook dinner tonight using only the knife cuts we practiced"
- "Look at your bank statement and categorize every transaction using the framework from today"
- "Record yourself giving a 1-minute talk about something you know well"

Bad homework:
- "Think about what you learned today" (too vague)
- "Read chapters 3-5 of [textbook]" (this isn't school)
- "Practice for 2 hours" (unrealistic for most people)

---

## 👀 Coming Up Next

[1-2 sentences that preview tomorrow's lesson and create anticipation.]

Example: "Tomorrow we're diving into something that changes everything: light.
You'll never look at a window the same way again."

---

[If milestone day, add celebration section:]

## 🎉 Milestone!

[Warm, specific celebration. Reference what they've actually accomplished.]

Example (Day 7): "One full week! You've gone from 'what's aperture?' to controlling
exposure like a pro. Your photos from day 1 vs today? Night and day. Keep this momentum —
week 2 is where it gets really fun."
```

## Lesson Variations by Style

### Reading-Focused Learner
- Longer prose sections (3-4 paragraphs per section instead of 2-3)
- Include "Recommended Reading" or "Go Deeper" links/resources at the end
- Exercises lean toward analysis and reflection
- Add a "Key Takeaway" summary box at the end

### Exercise-Focused Learner
- Shorter teaching sections (get to the point faster)
- 4-5 exercises instead of 2-3
- Each exercise has step-by-step instructions
- Add a "Challenge Mode" optional harder exercise
- Homework is more structured with specific deliverables

### Project-Based Learner
- Teaching is framed around "you need this for your project"
- Day 1 defines the capstone project
- Each lesson's exercises are project milestones
- Homework directly advances the project
- Final day is project completion and showcase

## Difficulty Adjustment Guide

### difficultyAdjustment: -2 (Much Easier)
- Use simpler vocabulary and shorter sentences
- Add extra analogies and examples for every concept
- Break exercises into smaller steps with explicit instructions
- Add "Check Your Understanding" yes/no questions throughout
- Reduce exercise count; make each one very achievable
- More encouragement and positive reinforcement

### difficultyAdjustment: -1 (Slightly Easier)
- Add one extra analogy per section
- Make exercises slightly more guided
- Include more "here's what you should see" confirmation points
- Slightly shorter lessons

### difficultyAdjustment: 0 (Default)
- Standard format as described above

### difficultyAdjustment: +1 (Slightly Harder)
- Less hand-holding in exercises
- Add "Why does this work?" reflection questions
- Include edge cases and exceptions
- Reference more advanced concepts briefly ("we'll cover this later, but...")

### difficultyAdjustment: +2 (Much Harder)
- Assume faster comprehension; teach more per lesson
- Exercises are open-ended, requiring creative application
- Include nuance, debate, and "it depends" scenarios
- Add optional advanced reading/resources
- Challenge conventional wisdom where appropriate
- Homework requires synthesis of multiple concepts

## Milestone Messages

### Day 1 Complete
> 🎉 You just finished your first lesson! You're officially on your way. Day 2 is going to build directly on what you learned today — see you there!

### Week 1 / Day 7
> 🔥 **One full week!** You showed up, you did the work, and you've built a real foundation in [topic]. Most people who start learning something new quit in the first 3 days. You didn't. That matters. Week 2 is where things get really good — we're moving from foundations to [next phase].

### Halfway Point
> 🏔️ **HALFWAY THERE!** Look at what you've covered: [brief list of key concepts]. You started [duration/2] days ago knowing [starting point], and now you [current capability]. The second half is where it all comes together. Let's go.

### Final Lesson
> 🎓 **You did it!** [Duration] days ago, you decided to learn [topic]. Today, you've completed your entire masterclass. Here's what you can now do that you couldn't before:
>
> [List 3-5 specific capabilities they've gained]
>
> **What's next?**
> - [Suggestion 1: A more advanced course topic]
> - [Suggestion 2: A community or resource to join]  
> - [Suggestion 3: A project to keep practicing]
>
> You built this. Be proud of it. 🦬

### Streak Messages
- **3-day streak:** "Three days straight — you're building a habit! 🔥"
- **7-day streak:** "A full week without missing a day. That's discipline. 💪"
- **14-day streak:** "Two weeks of consistent learning. You're in the top 5% of people who start courses. Seriously."
- **21-day streak:** "21 days. They say that's what it takes to form a habit. This isn't a phase — it's who you are now."

## Exercise Design Principles

1. **Be specific.** Not "practice what you learned" but "do X, observe Y, write down Z."
2. **Produce something.** A photo, a paragraph, a calculation, a recording, a meal. Tangible output.
3. **Be achievable in the time budget.** A 15-min lesson shouldn't have a 30-min exercise.
4. **Build confidence.** Early exercises should be almost impossible to fail. Difficulty scales with the course.
5. **Connect to their life.** Use their existing environment, tools, and situations. Don't require special equipment.
6. **Make it fun.** If there's a playful way to practice a concept, use it. Learning should feel like discovery, not obligation.
