# Curriculum Templates

Reference syllabi for common topic categories. Use these as structural patterns when generating new curricula — adapt the pacing, depth, and specific topics to the learner's answers.

---

## Template: Creative Skill (e.g., Photography)

**Duration:** 14 days · **Pattern:** Foundations → Technique → Application → Style

| Day | Title | Objectives | Time |
|-----|-------|-----------|------|
| 1 | Welcome & Your First Shot | Understand what makes a photo "good"; take 5 intentional photos | 30 min |
| 2 | The Exposure Triangle | Learn aperture, shutter speed, ISO and how they work together | 30 min |
| 3 | Aperture Deep Dive | Control depth of field; shoot same subject at f/2.8, f/8, f/16 | 30 min |
| 4 | Shutter Speed & Motion | Freeze action vs motion blur; experiment with 1/1000 to 1/4 sec | 30 min |
| 5 | Light Is Everything | Identify golden hour, blue hour, harsh light, diffused light | 30 min |
| 6 | Composition Rules | Rule of thirds, leading lines, framing, negative space | 30 min |
| 7 | Week 1 Review & Photo Walk | Apply all concepts in a single intentional photo walk | 30 min |
| 8 | Color & Mood | How color temperature and palettes affect emotion in photos | 30 min |
| 9 | Telling a Story | Series, juxtaposition, decisive moment — photos that say something | 30 min |
| 10 | Editing Fundamentals | Basic adjustments: exposure, contrast, white balance, crop | 30 min |
| 11 | Editing Style | Presets, color grading, developing your signature look | 30 min |
| 12 | Portraits & People | Posing basics, eye contact, flattering light, candid vs posed | 30 min |
| 13 | Finding Your Voice | Identify patterns in your best shots; define your style | 30 min |
| 14 | Your Portfolio & Next Steps | Curate your top 10; share and get feedback; where to go from here | 30 min |

**Key pattern:** Week 1 = technical foundations with immediate practice. Week 2 = creative application and personal style. Day 7 is always a review/practice day.

## Template: Analytical Skill (e.g., Personal Finance)

**Duration:** 14 days · **Pattern:** Mindset → Foundations → Systems → Strategy

| Day | Title | Objectives | Time |
|-----|-------|-----------|------|
| 1 | Your Money Story | Examine your relationship with money; identify current pain points | 30 min |
| 2 | The Net Worth Snapshot | Calculate your net worth; understand assets vs liabilities | 30 min |
| 3 | Cash Flow Fundamentals | Track where your money actually goes; the 50/30/20 framework | 30 min |
| 4 | Building Your Budget | Create a budget that works for YOUR life (not a template) | 30 min |
| 5 | The Emergency Fund | Why it matters, how much, where to keep it, how to start | 30 min |
| 6 | Debt Strategy | Avalanche vs snowball; when to pay down vs invest; interest math | 30 min |
| 7 | Week 1 Review & Money Date | Review your numbers; adjust your budget; celebrate progress | 30 min |
| 8 | Investing 101 | Stocks, bonds, index funds — what they are in plain English | 30 min |
| 9 | Retirement Accounts | 401(k), IRA, Roth — which ones matter for you and why | 30 min |
| 10 | The Power of Compound Interest | The math that changes everything; starting now vs "later" | 30 min |
| 11 | Insurance & Protection | What you need, what you don't, and how to not overpay | 30 min |
| 12 | Tax Basics for Normal People | Deductions, credits, withholding — the stuff that saves you money | 30 min |
| 13 | Automating Your Finances | Set it and forget it: auto-save, auto-invest, bill pay systems | 30 min |
| 14 | Your Financial Game Plan | Build your 90-day action plan; set 3 concrete goals | 30 min |

**Key pattern:** Start with psychology/awareness. Build knowledge progressively. End with a concrete action plan. Day 7 is always consolidation.

## Template: Physical/Practical Skill (e.g., Cooking)

**Duration:** 7 days · **Pattern:** Basics → Core Techniques → Recipes → Independence

| Day | Title | Objectives | Time |
|-----|-------|-----------|------|
| 1 | Kitchen Confidence | Essential equipment, pantry staples, mise en place mindset | 30 min |
| 2 | Knife Skills & Prep | How to hold a knife, basic cuts, prep workflow | 30 min |
| 3 | Heat Control | Sauté, simmer, sear, roast — mastering your stove | 30 min |
| 4 | Flavor Building | Salt, fat, acid, heat — the four pillars of delicious food | 30 min |
| 5 | Your First Full Meal | Cook a complete meal from scratch using techniques from days 1-4 | 60 min |
| 6 | Improvisation & Substitution | Cook without a recipe; use what you have; flavor intuition | 30 min |
| 7 | Your Signature Dish & Next Steps | Perfect one dish you're proud of; build your recipe rotation | 30 min |

**Key pattern:** Shorter duration for hands-on skills. Every day has a cooking exercise. Builds toward independence (cooking without recipes) rather than just following instructions.

| 13 | The Power of Pauses | Strategic silence; letting ideas land; dramatic effect | 30 min |

## General Curriculum Design Rules

1. **Day 1 is always accessible and motivating.** Don't front-load theory. Give a quick win.
2. **Day 7 (or midpoint for 7-day courses) is always review + practice.** Consolidation matters.
3. **Final day is always celebration + next steps.** Don't end with "that's it." End with "here's where you go from here."
4. **Each lesson builds on the previous one.** No orphan topics.
5. **Exercises escalate in complexity.** Day 1 exercise is trivial. Final exercise is challenging.
6. **Vary the format.** Some days are heavy reading, some are mostly exercises, some are reflection.
7. **Include at least one "aha moment" per lesson.** A surprising fact, a counterintuitive insight, or a "try this and watch what happens."

### Duration Selection Guide

| Criteria | 7 days | 14 days | 21 days |
|----------|--------|---------|---------|
| Topic scope | Narrow/specific | Standard | Broad/deep |
| Learner goal | "Get the basics" | "Be competent" | "Be confident" |
| Time commitment | Low | Medium | Higher |
| Example topics | Cooking basics, budgeting, meditation | Photography, guitar, personal finance | Public speaking, marketing, coding |
