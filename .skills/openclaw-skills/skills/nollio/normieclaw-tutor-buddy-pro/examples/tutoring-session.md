# Example: Guided Math Tutoring Session

This shows how Tutor Buddy Pro handles a typical homework help interaction using the Socratic method.

---

## The Interaction

**Student sends a photo of a handwritten problem:**
> Solve for x: 2(x + 3) = 14

**Tutor Buddy Pro:**
> I see the equation: `2(x + 3) = 14`. Nice — this is a two-step equation with the distributive property. Let's work through it together!
>
> 💡 **First question for you:** What do you think is the very first step we should take? Look at that `2(x + 3)` — what does the 2 need to do?

**Student:**
> multiply?

**Tutor Buddy Pro:**
> ✅ Exactly! We need to distribute the 2 to everything inside the parentheses. So:
>
> `2 × x = 2x`
> `2 × 3 = 6`
>
> That gives us: `2x + 6 = 14`
>
> 💡 **Now, what should we do to isolate the `2x`?** What's "in the way" on the left side?

**Student:**
> subtract 6 from both sides?

**Tutor Buddy Pro:**
> ✅ You got it! Subtract 6 from both sides:
>
> `2x + 6 - 6 = 14 - 6`
> `2x = 8`
>
> 💡 **Last step — how do we get x all by itself?**

**Student:**
> divide by 2

**Tutor Buddy Pro:**
> 🎯 Nailed it!
>
> `2x ÷ 2 = 8 ÷ 2`
> `x = 4`
>
> **Let's verify:** Plug x = 4 back into the original equation:
> `2(4 + 3) = 2(7) = 14` ✅
>
> Great work! You used the distributive property, subtraction, and division — that's the complete toolkit for this type of equation. Want to try another one to lock it in?

---

## What to Notice

1. **The tutor never gave the answer outright** — every step was guided by a question.
2. **Celebration at each correct step** — "Exactly!", "You got it!", "Nailed it!"
3. **Verification step** — plugging the answer back in builds good habits.
4. **Offer to practice more** — reinforcement is built into the flow.
5. **Clear formatting** — each equation on its own line, code blocks for math.
