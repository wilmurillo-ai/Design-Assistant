# Example: Cover Letter Generation

## Scenario
Sarah just tailored her resume for the Director of Growth role at RocketScale. Now she wants a cover letter.

---

## Conversation

**Sarah:** Write a cover letter for the RocketScale job.

**Agent:** Here's your cover letter — tailored to RocketScale's Director of Growth role:

---

**Dear Hiring Team,**

RocketScale's approach to bottoms-up SaaS adoption caught my attention when I saw your Series B announcement — the 3x ARR growth you've achieved with a lean team tells me you're building something that sells itself. I'd love to help you pour fuel on that fire as your Director of Growth.

Over the past 8 years in B2B SaaS marketing, I've built the growth engines that turn awareness into pipeline. At Acme Corp, I led a 12-person marketing team and launched our first ABM program targeting enterprise accounts — generating $2.3M in qualified pipeline within 6 months. I managed a $1.8M annual budget across paid, content, and events, and built the attribution dashboards (SQL + Google Analytics) that let us double down on what was actually working.

What excites me about RocketScale is the stage you're at. Series B is where disciplined growth strategy separates the rocketships from the rest. I've scaled pipeline 2.4x year-over-year in a similar environment, and I know how to balance aggressive acquisition targets with sustainable unit economics.

I'd welcome the chance to dig into your current funnel and share some specific ideas. I'm available for a conversation anytime.

Best,
Sarah Doe

---

**Agent:** This is 195 words — concise enough that a recruiter will actually read it. Saved to `2026-03-08-rocketscale.md`.

A few things I did:
- Opened with something specific about their company (Series B, product-led growth) instead of "I am writing to express my interest"
- Connected your ABM and budget experience directly to their requirements
- Mentioned SQL naturally to hit that ATS keyword
- Kept it under 200 words — recruiters appreciate brevity

Want me to adjust the tone? I can make it more formal for a corporate role or more casual for a startup.
