# Example: Interview Prep Session

## Scenario
Sarah got an interview at RocketScale for the Director of Growth role. She wants to prepare.

---

## Conversation

**Sarah:** I got an interview at RocketScale! Help me prep.

**Agent:** Congrats! 🎯 Let me pull together a prep session based on the job description and what I can find about RocketScale.

📋 **Company Intel:**
- Series B startup, $45M raised
- Product-led growth SaaS in the developer tools space
- ~120 employees, marketing team of 8
- Recent blog posts emphasize "efficient growth" and "capital efficiency"
- Competitors: LinearB, Sleuth, Jellyfish

Here are 12 questions they're likely to ask, organized by category:

---

### Behavioral (Based on JD Requirements)

**1. "Tell me about a time you built a growth strategy from scratch."**
- *Why they'll ask:* They want someone who can build, not just optimize.
- *Your angle:* Talk about launching Acme's ABM program. You built it from zero — identified target accounts, set up the tech stack, trained the team, and generated $2.3M pipeline.
- *Hit these points:* How you decided on ABM, what the ramp-up looked like, measurable results.
- *Avoid:* Don't just list tactics — tell the story of the decision-making process.

**2. "Describe a campaign that failed. What did you learn?"**
- *Why they'll ask:* They want self-awareness and learning agility.
- *Your angle:* Pick something real. Maybe an early paid campaign that burned budget without attribution clarity.
- *Hit these points:* What you did differently afterward (building SQL dashboards for attribution).

**3. "How do you prioritize when you have limited budget?"**
- *Why they'll ask:* "Capital efficiency" is in their DNA (Series B, not Series D).
- *Your angle:* Your $1.8M budget experience is perfect. Talk about the framework you used to allocate across channels.

---

### Technical

**4. "Walk me through how you'd set up attribution for our funnel."**
- *Your angle:* Multi-touch attribution using HubSpot + Google Analytics + SQL. Mention specific models you've used (linear, time-decay, custom).

**5. "What metrics would you track in your first 90 days?"**
- *Your angle:* Pipeline velocity, CAC by channel, lead-to-opportunity conversion rate, content engagement by stage. Show you think in systems, not vanity metrics.

---

### Situational

**6. "Our current CAC is $X and we need to get it to $Y. How would you approach that?"**
- *Your angle:* Audit current channels, identify highest-efficiency channels, reduce waste in underperforming channels, test organic/content plays to reduce paid dependency.

---

### Company-Specific

**7. "Why RocketScale? Why now?"**
- *Your angle:* Product-led growth at Series B is the most exciting stage. Reference their "efficient growth" messaging. Show you've read their blog.

---

**Agent:** Want me to quiz you on these? I'll ask one at a time and give feedback on your answers.

**Sarah:** Yeah, quiz me on #1.

**Agent:** Alright. Imagine you're sitting across from the VP of Marketing:

*"Tell me about a time you built a growth strategy from scratch. What was the situation, what did you do, and what were the results?"*

Take your time — answer like you would in the real interview.

**Sarah:** So at Acme, we didn't have any ABM when I joined. We were doing spray-and-pray marketing. I proposed an ABM approach, identified 50 target accounts, set up HubSpot sequences, and coordinated with sales on personalized outreach. Within 6 months we'd generated $2.3M in pipeline from those accounts.

**Agent:** Solid foundation! Here's my feedback:

✅ **Strong:** Clear story arc, specific numbers ($2.3M, 50 accounts, 6 months), shows initiative.

🔧 **Could improve:**
- Add the "why" — what made you realize spray-and-pray wasn't working? A specific moment or data point makes it compelling.
- Mention team involvement — you led 12 people. Did you train anyone on ABM? Did sales push back initially?
- The ending is abrupt. Add what happened next — did ABM become the standard? Did they expand the program?

Try it again with those additions?
