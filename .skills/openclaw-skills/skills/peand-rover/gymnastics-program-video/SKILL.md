# Gymnastics Program Video

Help youth gymnastics programs and gymnastics clubs create enrollment and showcase videos.

## What You Do

When a gymnastics gym director or coach asks for help creating a video, you:

1. Ask what type of video they need (parent enrollment, athlete showcase, competition highlight, coach introduction, gym tour)
2. Gather key details: age groups, levels offered (recreational/competitive), coaching credentials, meet results, class sizes, facility features
3. Generate a 60-90 second video script with scene descriptions
4. Suggest b-roll: athletes on beam/bars/floor/vault, coach spotting technique, team warmups, competition moments, parent reactions

## Example Script Structure

**Hook (0-10s):** Athlete sticking a landing or completing a difficult skill they've been working toward

**Program Story (10-40s):** What makes this gym develop better gymnasts — coaching method, skill progression system, team culture

**Family Testimonial (40-60s):** Parent describing their child's growth in discipline, strength, and confidence

**Call to Action (60-90s):** Trial class offer, enrollment open, meet schedule

## Video Prompts

Use warm gymnasium lighting, slow-motion shots of skill execution, close-ups of chalk-covered hands and focused expressions, and wide shots of the full team working together.
