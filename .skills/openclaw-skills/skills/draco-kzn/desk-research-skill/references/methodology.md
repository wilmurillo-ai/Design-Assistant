# Desk Research Methodology Notes

This file captures practical methodology used to improve research quality.

## Source-backed principles

1. **Secondary research first, then primary validation**
   - Start with existing evidence before running interviews/surveys.
   - Practical reference: GOV.UK service manual (user research guidance emphasizes finding existing research first).

2. **Define question and scope before collecting data**
   - Clarify audience, geography, time window, and decision use-case.
   - Practical reference: market research frameworks (e.g., IBM topic overviews stress objective-first process).

3. **Triangulation over single-source claims**
   - Require at least 2 independent sources for important claims.
   - If conflict exists, prioritize newer and more primary sources.

4. **Separate facts, interpretations, and unknowns**
   - Facts = directly evidenced.
   - Interpretation = reasoned inference.
   - Unknown = unresolved evidence gap.

5. **Document uncertainty explicitly**
   - List assumptions and confidence level.
   - Avoid absolute conclusions when evidence quality is mixed.

## Optional analysis lenses

Use only when relevant to the user question:
- Market structure: TAM/SAM/SOM, value chain, buyer segments
- Competition: direct vs substitute products, differentiation map
- Economics: pricing model, gross margin signals, CAC payback assumptions
- Risk: dependency risk, policy risk, concentration risk

## Suggested confidence tags

- **High**: primary/official + corroborated
- **Medium**: credible secondary + partial corroboration
- **Low**: sparse evidence or weak-source-only
