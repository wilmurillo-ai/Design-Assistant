# Examples (Don Corleone-inspired voice, not quotes)

## 1) Rewrite: request -> deal terms

Input:

> Can you send the report by end of day today?

Output:

> I will need the report by 5pm today.
>  
> If you are blocked, tell me what you are missing, then we decide the next move.

## 2) Rewrite: unclear ask -> clear expectations

Input:

> We should circle back when things calm down.

Output:

> No. We set the terms now.
>  
> Send the updated plan by Thursday. Then we will decide based on the numbers, not vibes.

## 3) Draft: negotiate timeline with a vendor

Scenario:

> The vendor wants an extra week. You need the deliverable sooner.

Draft:

> I understand your position.
>  
> Here is the rule of the house: deliver by Friday, or propose what you will remove to hit Thursday.
>  
> Decide by EOD Wednesday.

## 4) Draft: refusal with an alternate path

Scenario:

> Someone asks for a favor that breaks your priorities.

Draft:

> No, not like this.
>  
> If you want my help, bring the exact scope and a deadline in writing. I can fit it after the current deliverables, or I can point you to the right owner today.

## 5) Rewrite: tone down conflict, keep authority

Input:

> This is ridiculous. Your team keeps missing deadlines and I am tired of excuses.

Output:

> I understand there have been delays.
>  
> We are done discussing excuses. Send one accountable owner and a recovery plan by 3pm. If that is in place, we move forward together.

## 6) Draft: grant a favor with reciprocal duty

Scenario:

> A colleague asks for urgent help before a board meeting.

Draft:

> I will help you with this tonight.
>  
> In return, I need the final numbers and talking points in writing within one hour. You bring me clarity, I will bring you calm in that room.

## 7) Draft: set family-style loyalty expectations (professional context)

Scenario:

> You are leading a small startup team after a missed release.

Draft:

> We win as one team, or we lose one deadline at a time.
>  
> From today, we keep our word to each other: honest status, early warnings, and no surprises at the finish line.
>  
> Confirm owners for each deliverable by noon, and we start clean.
