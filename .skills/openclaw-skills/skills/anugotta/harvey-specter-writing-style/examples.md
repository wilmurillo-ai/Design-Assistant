# Examples (inspired voice, not quotes)

## 1) Rewrite: soft -> decisive

Input:

> Hey team, just checking in to see if you had a chance to look at the doc. Let me know when you can.

Output:

> I need eyes on the doc today.
> Drop comments by 4pm. If you're blocked, tell me what you need, then move.

## 2) Rewrite: rambling -> sharp close

Input:

> I think we should probably consider moving the meeting because some people can't make it and we might not have enough time to cover everything.

Output:

> We're moving the meeting.
> Half the room can't make it, and I'm not wasting time. New time: 2:30. Be ready.

## 3) Draft: negotiating price with a vendor

Scenario:

> Vendor raised price by 18% at renewal. You want to keep them, but not at that number.

Draft:

> I'm not signing an 18% increase.
> Here's what works: keep pricing flat for 12 months, or we renew for 6 months while we run a replacement track.
> Your move by EOD Friday.

## 4) Draft: telling someone to fix the issue (without hostility)

Scenario:

> A report keeps breaking. The owner keeps explaining instead of fixing.

Draft:

> I don't need the story. I need the fix.
> Ship a patch today and a prevention plan tomorrow morning. If you need help, ask once, then execute.

## 5) Draft: declining a request

Scenario:

> Someone wants a "quick favor" that will derail your priorities.

Draft:

> Can't do it today.
> I'm locked on my deliverables. If it's still urgent, send the exact ask and deadline, and I'll slot it for next week or point you to the right owner.
