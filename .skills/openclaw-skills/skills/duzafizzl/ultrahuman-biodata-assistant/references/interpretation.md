# Interpreting Ultrahuman metrics (heuristics)

Use these as **orientation only**. They are not medical advice. Always phrase answers descriptively (e.g. "your metrics suggest …", "often associated with …").

## Recovery and readiness

- **Recovery Score (0–10):** Higher is generally better. Scores around 7+ often indicate good recovery; below 5 may suggest prioritizing rest.
- **Recovery Index:** Values above ~70 are often in a "good" range; below ~50 may warrant a lighter day. Exact bands depend on the user’s baseline.
- **HRV:** Higher HRV (relative to the user’s own baseline) often indicates better recovery. Avoid comparing raw numbers across different people or devices.

## Sleep

- **Sleep Score (0–10):** 7+ is often "good"; below 5 may indicate poor sleep quality or short duration.
- **Sleep Efficiency:** Above ~85% is often considered good; below ~75% may indicate more wake time or fragmentation.
- **Total Sleep:** 7–9 hours is commonly cited as a target range for adults; individual needs vary.

## Metabolic / glucose (if CGM linked)

- **Time in Target:** Higher percentage (e.g. >70%) often indicates more time in the desired glucose range.
- **Glucose Variability:** Lower variability (e.g. <20–25%) is often associated with more stable metabolism; high variability may warrant attention to diet/timing (descriptive only).
- **Average Glucose:** Context-dependent; target ranges are set by the user or their care team. Do not give medical targets.

## Wording

- Prefer: "Your recovery score is 7, which many people associate with good readiness."
- Avoid: "You are fully recovered" or "You should not train."
- If values are borderline or missing, say so and suggest checking again or confirming with the app.
