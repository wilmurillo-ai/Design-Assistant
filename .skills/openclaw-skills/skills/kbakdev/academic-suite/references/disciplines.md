# Discipline-Specific Conventions

## General Requirements (Polish doctoral dissertations)

Polish doctoral dissertations (rozprawa doktorska) typically require:
- Clear research problem, questions, and hypotheses
- Literature review demonstrating knowledge of the field
- Described and justified methodology
- Original contribution to the discipline (wkład do nauki)
- Conclusions addressing the research questions
- Proper citation style consistent throughout

The dissertation must demonstrate the candidate's ability to conduct independent research.

## Nauki o zarządzaniu i jakości (Management & Quality Sciences)

### Key frameworks
- KNOiZ PAN subdisciplines classification (2.0)
- Prakseology: sprawność as umbrella for skuteczność, ekonomiczność, korzystność
- Research methods: case studies, surveys, mixed methods common
- Grounding in both theory and practice expected

### Common expectations
- Clear positioning within subdisciplines
- Critical discussion with theory (not textbook description)
- Empirical component with justified sample selection
- Practical implications alongside theoretical contribution
- Glossary of domain-specific terms

### Frequent supervisor concerns
- Too descriptive, not enough critical analysis
- Weak connection between literature review and research design
- Definitions not precise enough for the discipline
- Missing or unclear operationalization of variables
- Conclusions not fully supported by presented evidence

## Computer Science / IT

### Common expectations
- Technical precision in definitions
- Clear system/architecture descriptions
- Reproducible methodology
- Benchmarks and quantitative evaluation
- Comparison with existing solutions/approaches

## Social Sciences (general)

### Common expectations
- Ethical considerations for human subjects research
- Triangulation of methods when possible
- Reflexivity about researcher's position
- Clear operationalization of constructs
- Validity and reliability discussion

## Economics & Finance

### Common expectations
- Quantitative rigor (statistical methods, models)
- Data sources clearly described
- Time periods and geographical scope defined
- Policy implications discussed
- Limitations of economic models acknowledged
