# Review Standards by Work Type

## PhD Dissertation (rozprawa doktorska)

**Expected standard:** Independent, original research contributing to the discipline

- Originality: Must present novel contribution (new model, framework, empirical findings, methodology)
- Literature: Comprehensive, 100+ sources typical, critical engagement required
- Methodology: Must be rigorous, justified, and described for replication
- Length: Typically 150-300 pages (field dependent)
- Key question: "Does this demonstrate the candidate's ability to conduct independent scientific research?"

**Common reviewer red flags:**
- Descriptive literature review without critical analysis
- Weak link between theory chapters and research chapter
- Hypothesis not clearly falsifiable
- Conclusions that go beyond what the data supports
- "Wkład do nauki" is vague or overstated

## Master's Thesis (praca magisterska)

**Expected standard:** Demonstrates ability to apply scientific methods under supervision

- Originality: Some original element expected but can be smaller scope
- Literature: 50-80 sources typical
- Methodology: Must be appropriate and described, can be simpler
- Length: Typically 60-120 pages
- Key question: "Does this show the student understands and can apply research methods?"

## Journal Article (artykuł naukowy)

**Expected standard:** Publishable contribution to the field

- Originality: Clear, concise, novel contribution
- Literature: Focused and current, quality over quantity
- Methodology: Rigorous, reproducible, appropriate
- Length: Typically 5,000-10,000 words
- Key question: "Does this advance knowledge in the field?"

**Journal-specific considerations:**
- Fit with journal scope
- Adherence to journal formatting guidelines
- Contribution relative to journal prestige level

## Conference Paper

**Expected standard:** Promising work worth discussing in academic community

- Originality: Can be work-in-progress or preliminary results
- Literature: Focused background, less exhaustive than journal
- Methodology: Can be exploratory
- Length: Typically 4,000-8,000 words
- Key question: "Is this interesting and methodologically sound enough for academic discussion?"

## Scoring Guide (all types)

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 6 | Excellent — exemplary, no significant issues |
| 5 | Very good — minor issues only |
| 4 | Good — some weaknesses but fundamentally sound |
| 3 | Adequate — notable weaknesses requiring attention |
| 2 | Weak — significant issues undermining the work |
| 1 | Poor — fundamental problems |
| 0 | Unacceptable — does not meet minimum standards |
