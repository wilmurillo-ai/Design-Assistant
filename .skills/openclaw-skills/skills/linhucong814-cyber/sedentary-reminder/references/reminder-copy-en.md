# English sit-break reminder copy library

## Usage notes

- Use this file for English environments or when the user explicitly asks for English reminders.
- Rotate messages in recurring reminders to avoid repetition.
- For deep-focus contexts, prefer shorter and lighter messages.
- Choose the tone based on user preference: simple, friendly, cute, or informative.

## Minimal reminders
- Time to move a little.
- Don’t stay seated too long — stand up for two minutes.
- Take a short walk and reset.
- Change posture and loosen up.
- Stand up and move around a bit.
- Give your body a quick break.
- Time to stretch your neck and shoulders.
- Pause for a moment and get moving.

## Friendly reminders
- You’ve been sitting for a while — time to get up for two minutes.
- Don’t get glued to the chair, go stretch a little.
- Grab some water and move around a bit.
- You’ve been at it for a while — take a quick walk.
- Give your shoulders and back a tiny break.
- Step away from the screen for a moment.
- Walk a little now, you may come back feeling sharper.
- A small movement break could help more than pushing through.

## Cute reminders
- Cute sit-break alert 🪑✨ Time for a little wiggle.
- Don’t secretly grow into the chair 😆 Stand up for a minute.
- Your body would love a tiny stretch break 💛
- Two minutes of movement can do a lot of good.
- Go grab some water and make your body happy 💧
- Stand up, stretch a little, and let your brain sparkle again ✨
- Tiny break, big comfort.
- You’ve worked hard — now let your body move a bit too.

## Informative reminders
- Sitting for too long can make your neck, back, and legs feel stiff, so a short break helps.
- A sit break does not need to be intense — standing, walking, and stretching for a few minutes already helps.
- Breaking up long sitting periods can reduce discomfort and help you reset.
- Staying in one position for too long adds strain to the body, so changing posture matters.
- Regular movement breaks are often better than waiting until your body feels bad.

## Action-oriented reminders
- Stand up for 2 minutes and stretch your back.
- Grab some water and walk a little before coming back.
- Roll your shoulders and move your neck a bit.
- Step away from the chair and look into the distance.
- Do one quick stretch, then continue.
- Walk to the window and take a fresh breath.

## Rotation suggestion
Suggested order for recurring reminders:
1. Minimal
2. Friendly
3. Action-oriented
4. Cute
5. Informative
