## What This Skill Does

Most freelancers lose deals not because they lack skill — but because their proposals sound like everyone else's. They open with "Hi, I'm [name] and I have X years of experience..." and the client stops reading.

This skill fixes that.

**AI Proposal & Freelance Deal Generator** produces a complete, ready-to-send proposal package tailored to your specific service, client, and situation. Every output is written to feel human, confident, and outcome-focused — because that's what wins deals.

**One generation includes:**

| Output | What It Is |
|---|---|
| Short Proposal | 150-250 word version for Upwork, Fiverr, cold DMs |
| Long Proposal | 400-600 word version for email, direct clients, agencies |
| Pricing Strategy | 3 tiered packages framed as ROI, not cost |
| Objection Handler | 5 client objections with pre-written, confident responses |
| Follow-Up Sequence | 3 messages for Day 3, Day 7, Day 14 — none say "just checking in" |
| A/B Variations | 2 alternate proposal openers to split-test |

## Example Output (Short Proposal Excerpt)

> **SHORT PROPOSAL — Friendly Version**
>
> The brief says "premium but approachable" — that tension is actually where the most memorable wellness brands live, and it's my favorite problem to solve.
>
> I've worked with a handful of supplement and wellness startups at exactly this stage: pre-launch, first real brand moment, one shot to make an impression that lasts. The last one I worked with used their brand identity to close a $500K seed round — their investors said the visual brand made the company feel real.
>
> For your project, I'd start with a positioning session to nail the feeling before we touch a pixel. From there: logo suite, color system, typography, and a brand guidelines doc your team can hand to any vendor and stay consistent.
>
> Investment starts at $1,200 for the foundation package. Happy to send over the full scope breakdown — takes about 15 minutes to walk through. Want to find a time this week?

---

## Example Output (Objection Handler Excerpt)

> **Objection 3: "Your rate is higher than other designers we've talked to."**
>
> Your Response: "That's fair, and I'd expect you to be comparing options. The difference usually comes down to what you're getting beyond the files — strategy, brand positioning, and a guidelines document that keeps everything consistent as you scale. A lot of lower-cost options deliver assets; I deliver a brand system. Happy to break down exactly what's included so you can compare apples to apples."
>
> Tone Note: Say this slowly. Don't defend — educate. The goal is to shift the frame from price to value.
