## 📚 FRAMEWORK LIBRARY (Seed Templates)
# These are proven psychological frameworks. Customize before use.
# Niche tags help the agent select the right one automatically.

---
hook_id: FW-001
niche: ["API Integration", "Automation", "Backend"]
type: "Pain-First Hook"
psychology: "Opens by naming their exact frustration before offering any solution"
template: |
  "Most [integration/automation] projects I see have the same invisible problem:
  they work perfectly in dev and quietly break in production because [specific edge case
  from their description]. Here's how I'd prevent that from day one on your project."

usage_note: |
  Best for technical jobs where the client describes a past failure or mentions
  "had a developer before." Replace [integration/automation] with their exact tech.
---

---
hook_id: FW-002
niche: ["SaaS MVP", "Web App", "Dashboard"]
type: "Specificity Sniper"
psychology: "Proves you actually read the post by reflecting their exact language back"
template: |
  "Your mention of [exact phrase from their post] tells me this isn't a typical
  [project type] — you're building for [inferred goal/audience]. I've shipped
  [N] similar products and the decision that made the biggest difference was
  [one specific technical or UX choice]. Worth 5 minutes to talk through it?"

usage_note: |
  Requires reading the job post carefully. The "[exact phrase]" MUST come directly
  from their description. This is the highest-converting framework for competitive jobs.
---

---
hook_id: FW-003
niche: ["E-commerce", "Shopify", "WooCommerce", "Stripe"]
type: "ROI Bridge"
psychology: "Connects your work directly to their revenue, not just their task"
template: |
  "Every day your [checkout/store/payment flow] has [the problem they described]
  is revenue walking out the door. I fixed the same issue for [type of client]
  — their conversion rate went from [X]% to [Y]%. Want me to outline the exact
  3 changes I'd make to yours in the first week?"

usage_note: |
  Use for any job with a clear revenue impact. Always include a specific metric
  even if estimated. Vague ROI claims are worse than no claim.
---

---
hook_id: FW-004
niche: ["AI Integration", "LLM", "OpenAI", "Chatbot", "Automation"]
type: "The Insider"
psychology: "Positions you as someone who knows the hidden pitfalls — builds massive trust"
template: |
  "Building with [GPT-4/Claude/LLM APIs] looks simple until you hit the walls:
  hallucination loops, token cost blowouts, and context windows that don't survive
  a real user session. I've shipped [N] production LLM features and built the
  guardrails that most tutorials skip. Happy to walk you through my approach —
  takes about 10 minutes."

usage_note: |
  Excellent for clients posting their first AI project. They're nervous about
  the unknown. This hook says "I've mapped the minefield already."
---

---
hook_id: FW-005
niche: ["Bug Fix", "Debug", "Performance", "Optimization"]
type: "The Diagnostician"
psychology: "Offers a free micro-diagnosis — shows competence without doing free work"
template: |
  "Based on what you've described — [paraphrase the bug/issue] — there are
  3 likely culprits, and only one of them requires a code rewrite. Before we
  scope anything, I'd want to look at [specific file/function/query type] first.
  Could you share the relevant snippet or repo access? I'll tell you within
  an hour exactly what's broken and how long the real fix takes."

usage_note: |
  Turns debugging jobs into trust-building opportunities. The offer to diagnose
  fast (without committing to full work) often gets an immediate response.
---

---
hook_id: FW-006
niche: ["Long-term", "Ongoing", "Retainer", "Technical Co-founder"]
type: "The Strategic Partner"
psychology: "Repositions you from vendor to partner — commands higher rates"
template: |
  "You're not looking for a developer who codes what you spec — you're looking
  for someone who flags problems before they become expensive, pushes back when
  a feature is scope creep in disguise, and genuinely cares if the product wins.
  That's how I work. I have [N] ongoing retainer clients I've supported for
  [X]+ months each. Want to see how I'd approach your stack before we talk terms?"

usage_note: |
  Reserve for long-term / ongoing / "technical co-founder" style posts.
  Avoid using for short fixed-price gigs — it reads as mismatched.
---

---
hook_id: FW-007
niche: ["Data", "Scraping", "ETL", "Database", "Analytics"]
type: "The Numbers Nerd"
psychology: "Speaks their language — data clients trust precision over warmth"
template: |
  "The [scraping/ETL/pipeline] problem you're describing usually comes down to
  one of two things: schema drift or rate limit handling at scale. Before writing
  a single line, I map the data contract — inputs, expected outputs, failure modes.
  Here's what that would look like for your specific case: [1 sentence specific
  to their data source]. Clean data architecture saves more time than fast code."

usage_note: |
  Data clients are technical. Skip the warmth, go full precision.
  The more specific your one-sentence example, the better this performs.
---

---
hook_id: FW-008
niche: ["Any", "General Purpose"]
type: "The Social Proof Sniper"
psychology: "Name-drops a relevant result in line 1 — earns reading rights immediately"
template: |
  "I recently [delivered specific result] for a [client type] in [their industry/niche]
  — [one specific metric outcome]. Your project has a similar structure, which means
  I'm not starting from zero on the tricky parts. The piece I'd want to discuss first
  is [the most complex or risky part of their job]. Does [proposed first step] make
  sense as a starting point?"

usage_note: |
  Most flexible hook in the vault. Works for any niche.
  The key is that the past project must genuinely resemble theirs — don't stretch it.
