# 🤝 AI Sales Closing & Offer Generator

> Turn stalled deals into signed contracts. Generate closing messages,
> objection handling responses, pricing strategy, irresistible offer
> designs, and upsell scripts — all written to sound like a seasoned
> human closer, not a chatbot.

---

## What This Skill Does

This skill is your AI sales closer. Paste in your product, the customer's
message, and your price — and get a complete playbook to move the deal forward:

- **Closing Message** — a short, ready-to-send message that moves the deal forward right now
- **Objection Handling** — 3 word-for-word response variants tailored to the customer's specific pushback
- **Pricing Strategy** — how to present, frame, and defend your price without caving to pressure
- **Offer Design** — core offer + bonus stack + risk reversal + urgency, written as a full offer summary
- **Upsell / Cross-sell Script** — how to expand the relationship after the initial close

## How to Use

### Step 1 — Fill in the required fields

| Field | What to Enter |
|---|---|
| Product / Service | What you sell and what outcome it delivers |
| Customer Type | Who the buyer is — role, company, context |
| Customer's Message | Paste their exact words — the objection or last message |
| Price | Your price or pricing tiers |
| Goal | What you want to happen next |
| Tone | How you want to come across (4 presets) |

### Step 2 — Add optional context for sharper output

| Field | Why It Helps |
|---|---|
| Deal Context | Adds timeline and history so responses feel more relevant |
| Customer Priority | Tailors the value argument to what they actually care about |
| Competitor | Handles the comparison directly instead of generically |

### Step 3 — Copy, adapt, send

Each section is copy-paste ready. Paste the closing message directly
into email, LinkedIn, or WhatsApp. Use the objection variants as-is
or blend them. Paste the offer summary into your proposal.

### Tone Options

| Tone | Best For |
|---|---|
| Confident & Direct | Decisive buyers, short sales cycles, transactional deals |
| Warm & Consultative | Relationship-driven sales, first-time buyers, high-trust products |
| Logical & ROI-Led | Procurement teams, analytical buyers, enterprise deals |
| Urgency & Scarcity | Fence-sitters, end of quarter, limited availability situations |

## Example

### Input

```
Product: AI writing tool, annual subscription, $1,200/year
Customer Type: Marketing manager at a 50-person SaaS company
Customer Message: "It looks interesting but $1,200 is a lot. We're using
  a free tool right now. Can you do anything on price?"
Price: $1,200/year ($100/month billed annually)
Goal: Close today
Tone: Warm & Consultative
Deal Context: Demo went well 5 days ago. First follow-up. No prior objections.
Customer Priority: Saving time on content production
```

---

### Output — Closing Message

Hey [Name] — totally fair, and I appreciate you being direct.

Here's the honest math: if this saves your team 5 hours a month on
content (most users save 8–10), that's 60 hours a year. At $30/hour,
that's $1,800 in time back — for a $1,200 investment.

I can lock in this year's rate and add a free onboarding call so your
team actually uses it from day one. Want me to send the start link now?

*Why this works: validates their concern, reframes price as ROI, adds
a low-cost bonus, and ends with a micro-commitment instead of an open question.*

---

### Output — Objection Handling (Price Objection)

**Variant 1 — Reframe**
> "The free tool handles the basics — totally get it. Where teams like
> yours hit a wall is [specific gap this product solves]. That's the
> $1,200 question: is 60 hours of your team's time worth more than that?"

**Variant 2 — Proof**
> "One of our customers in a similar role said the same thing. Three
> months in, she'd used it to produce 4x more content without adding
> headcount. Happy to share her story if it would help."

**Variant 3 — Advance**
> "Completely makes sense. Budget decisions need to feel right. Can I ask
> — is it the price itself, or are you not yet sure it will save enough
> time to justify it?"

**The Question to Ask if still stuck:**
> "If the price were off the table, would this be a yes?"

---

### Output — Offer Design (Full Offer Summary)

You get [AI Writing Tool] — the tool that [marketing teams at companies
like yours] use to cut content production time by 40% without hiring.

Here's what is included when you start today:
- Full annual access to all features
- Free 1:1 onboarding call to set up your workflows (valued at $200)
- Content template library — 50+ proven frameworks ready to use

If it does not save you time in the first 30 days, email us and we will
refund in full. No questions.

This pricing locks in until [Friday / end of month]. After that, it moves
to $1,500/year for new customers.

---

### Output — Upsell Script

**Trigger moment:** 30 days after they start, when they have seen their
first results.

> "Hey [Name] — saw you have been using [Tool] regularly, great to see.
> Quick question: are you on the individual plan or do you have other
> people on the team who could use this? We have a team plan that adds
> 3 seats for $1,800/year — works out to $600/year per person vs $1,200.
> Worth a look?"

## Tips for Higher Close Rates

1. **Paste their exact words.** The more specific the objection input,
   the more targeted the response. "It's too expensive" and "I need to
   run it by my boss" are very different objections.
2. **Use Variant 3 (Advance) first.** It keeps the conversation moving
   without arguing — 80% of the time that surfaces the real blocker.
3. **The offer summary is your proposal.** You can paste it directly into
   a one-page PDF or email and it covers all the key buying criteria.
4. **Never apologize for your price.** Confidence signals value.
   Hesitation signals the price is negotiable.
5. **Upsell at the moment of first success**, not at the moment of sign-up.
   Timing makes the difference between pushy and helpful.

## Limitations

- Generates copy and strategy only — does not send messages automatically.
- Results improve significantly when you paste the customer's actual words.
- Always review and personalize before sending — adapt [Name] and any
  bracketed details to the specific customer.
- Closing scripts are starting points, not rigid scripts. The best closers
  adapt in real time.
