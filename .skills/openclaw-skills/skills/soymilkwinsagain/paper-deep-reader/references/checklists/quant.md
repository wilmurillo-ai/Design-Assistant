# Checklist: Quantitative Finance

Run this after the general checklist.

## Financial setup

- [ ] I stated whether the paper is about pricing, hedging, forecasting, execution, or allocation.
- [ ] I described the state variables, stochastic dynamics, and information structure.
- [ ] I recorded the key market assumptions, including no-arbitrage, utility, or constraint assumptions when relevant.

## Evaluation quality

- [ ] I checked whether results are out-of-sample rather than only in-sample.
- [ ] I recorded the main risk-adjusted metrics.
- [ ] I noted turnover, transaction costs, slippage, leverage, capacity, or liquidity assumptions when relevant.
- [ ] I checked whether the backtest horizon and protocol are credible.

## Skepticism

- [ ] I considered look-ahead bias, leakage, and survivorship bias.
- [ ] I checked whether the claimed edge survives realistic frictions.
- [ ] I distinguished theoretical pricing results from implementable trading claims.
- [ ] I identified at least one likely regime-dependence or practical limitation.
