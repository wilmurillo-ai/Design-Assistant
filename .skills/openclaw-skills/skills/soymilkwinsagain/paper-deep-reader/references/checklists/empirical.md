# Checklist: Empirical / Economics / Social Science

Run this after the general checklist.

## Identification and estimand

- [ ] I stated the estimand or target quantity clearly.
- [ ] I reconstructed the identification strategy in words, not just notation.
- [ ] I listed the assumptions required for the core claim.
- [ ] I separated causal, predictive, and descriptive claims.

## Data and measurement

- [ ] I described the data source, sample, and measurement choices.
- [ ] I noted at least one way the result could depend on sample construction or variable definition.
- [ ] I identified whether institutional context is crucial to interpretation.

## Evidence quality

- [ ] I checked whether the baseline specification is sensible.
- [ ] I recorded the most informative robustness, placebo, falsification, or sensitivity checks.
- [ ] I noted important threats to validity that remain unresolved.
- [ ] I commented on effect size or substantive significance, not only statistical significance.

## Skepticism

- [ ] I did not present regression output as self-justifying evidence.
- [ ] I made clear where the paper's interpretation outruns its identification strategy.
