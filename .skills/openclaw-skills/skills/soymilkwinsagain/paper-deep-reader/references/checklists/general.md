# General Checklist

Run this checklist before finalizing any note.

## Reconstruction

- [ ] I stated the paper's actual research question.
- [ ] I recorded the problem setting or regime clearly.
- [ ] I identified the paper type and domain correctly enough to choose the right adapter.
- [ ] I stated the main contribution precisely rather than using praise words.
- [ ] I preserved the minimum mathematical or technical spine needed to understand the paper.

## Evidence

- [ ] I tied the main claims to concrete evidence.
- [ ] I distinguished authors' claims from my own evaluation.
- [ ] I checked whether the strongest support lives in the appendix or supplement.
- [ ] I identified at least one missing check, weak comparison, or caveat if one exists.

## Judgment

- [ ] I explained why the paper's mechanism should work, not just what result was reported.
- [ ] I recorded at least one limitation, failure mode, or regime boundary.
- [ ] I stated what is genuinely reusable from the paper.
- [ ] I avoided overstating certainty when the paper itself is unclear or underspecified.

## Durability

- [ ] The note could still help me six months later.
- [ ] The note is readable without reopening the paper immediately.
- [ ] The note contains enough formal detail that I could re-explain the paper to someone else.
