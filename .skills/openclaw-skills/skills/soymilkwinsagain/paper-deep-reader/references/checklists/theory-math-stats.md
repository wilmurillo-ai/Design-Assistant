# Checklist: Theory / Mathematics / Statistics

Run this after the general checklist.

## Formal core

- [ ] I preserved the main theorem, proposition, bound, or estimator claim in a form close to the paper.
- [ ] I listed the assumptions that matter most.
- [ ] I distinguished structural assumptions from technical convenience assumptions.
- [ ] I explained what quantity is being bounded, estimated, or characterized.

## Proof understanding

- [ ] I extracted the proof strategy rather than copying proof lines.
- [ ] I identified the lemma, reduction, invariant, or decomposition doing the heavy lifting.
- [ ] I noted whether the argument is constructive, asymptotic, or existence-based.

## Comparative judgment

- [ ] I checked whether the theorem is actually stronger than prior work or simply differently framed.
- [ ] I noted at least one special case or reduction to a known result when relevant.
- [ ] I discussed whether the result is mostly of theoretical, methodological, or practical value.

## Skepticism

- [ ] I recorded at least one place where the assumptions seem narrow, opaque, or difficult to verify in practice.
- [ ] I did not oversell asymptotic guarantees as finite-sample performance.
