## The Big Picture: Why Some Restaurant Videos Go Viral

Every viral restaurant video does at least one of these:
1. **Surprises** the viewer (size, price, quality, process)
2. **Makes them hungry** (sensory: sound, visual, texture)
3. **Makes them feel something** (story, nostalgia, pride)
4. **Triggers FOMO** ("I need to go there")
5. **Invites participation** (comments, tags, debates)

---

## Formula 1: The Big Reveal 🎬

**Structure:**
```
[Visual setup] → [Moment of anticipation] → [Payoff reveal]
```

**Hook types that work:**
- "Watch till the end..." (creates completion bait)
- Starting mid-action (no intro, no logo, no music fade-in)
- Text on screen in first 0.5 seconds

**Example:**
> *Visual: Close-up of a ball of dough. Chef starts stretching. Music kicks in. Text: "Why does our pizza taste different?" — 30 seconds of prep — final reveal: wood-fired pizza coming out of oven. Text overlay: "The answer is in the dough." CTA: "Try it yourself. Link in bio."*

**Results pattern:** 200K-2M views, high save rate (people save to show friends)

**Best for:** Pizza, patisserie, sushi, burgers

---

## Formula 2: The ASMR Process Video 🎧

**Structure:**
```
[No text needed] → [Pure sensory footage] → [Subtle end card]
```

**Key rules:**
- NO music (ambient kitchen sound only)
- Film at 4K, stabilized
- Get very close to the action
- Watch time > 80% = TikTok/IG will push this hard

**Best shots for ASMR:**
| Action | Sound That Hooks |
|---|---|
| Smash burger on griddle | Aggressive sizzle |
| Cheese pull | Stretching sound + visual |
| Knife on cutting board | Crisp tapping |
| Bread crust break | Sharp crack |
| Pouring sauce | Liquid glug |
| Pizza dough spin | Whoosh + thud |
| Ice cream scoop | Scrape + plop |
| Wok on fire | Roar + sizzle |

**Example:**
> *No hook text. Just a chef's hands breaking a golden croissant in half. The layers visible. The sound: crack, then silence. 12 seconds. End card: [restaurant name] [city]. No CTA.*

**Results pattern:** Very high completion rate, strong algorithmic push, 50K-500K views

## Formula 3: The Emotional Story 💔

**Structure:**
```
[Relatable problem or setup] → [Personal journey] → [Resolution + place in today's world]
```

**Story angles that work for restaurants:**
1. **Origin story**: "I started this with €3,000 and a dream"
2. **Family recipe**: "This recipe hasn't changed in 40 years"
3. **Near-failure moment**: "We almost closed in 2020"
4. **Staff story**: "He started as a dishwasher, now he runs the kitchen"
5. **Why they do it**: "I don't cook for the money. I cook because..."

**Example:**
> *Chef talking directly to camera while chopping: "My dad came to Belgium from Morocco in 1985. He had one recipe he brought with him. I've been making it every day for 20 years. It's on our menu. It will always be on our menu." Cut to dish being served. Text: "Try the recipe that crossed continents." 45 seconds.*

**Results pattern:** High shares, high saves, comments full of personal stories (algorithm loves this)

**Best for:** Family restaurants, ethnic cuisine, long-established restaurants

## Formula 4: The Transformation (Raw → Ready) ⏱️

**Structure:**
```
[Unrecognizable raw state] → [Dramatic transformation] → [Beautiful finished product]
```

**Speed:** Real-time for short processes, 3-5x speedup for longer ones

**What makes it work:**
- The gap between raw and final should be surprising
- Sound design matters (raw = quiet, final = satisfying)
- End on a close-up of the finished product with steam/drip/shine

**Example:**
> *Time 0:00 — A gray, unassuming raw chicken breast lands on the grill. Time 0:08 — It sears, crust forms. Time 0:18 — Plating: sauce, garnish, roasted vegetables. Time 0:25 — Close-up: fork cuts through, steam rises. Text: "From cold kitchen to your table in 18 minutes." 25 seconds.*

**Results pattern:** Very high watch time (completion bait), strong rewatch value

## Formula 5: The Social Proof Cascade 🌟

**Structure:**
```
[Show 1 real review] → [Customer reaction clip] → [The dish they reviewed] → [CTA]
```

**The key:** Real reviews feel more powerful than anything you say about yourself.

**3-step execution:**
1. Screenshot a 5-star Google review (with first name visible)
2. Find a short clip of a customer reacting to that dish
3. Show the dish being made or plated
4. End with: "Want to be our next happy customer?"

**Example:**
> *Text overlay: "We got this review last week 👀" → Screenshot of 5-star review: "Best sushi in Ghent, honest prices, chef is a genius" → Cut to chef plating the exact dish mentioned → Slow zoom on finished plate → CTA: "Book your table. Link in bio."*

**Results pattern:** High trust conversion — people who see this actually book

## Formula 6: The "You Won't Believe" Portion Reveal 🤯

**Structure:**
```
[Set expectation low] → [Reveal high] → [Reaction or scale reference]
```

**Execution:**
- Hold up the dish next to your face/hand for scale
- Or use another familiar object (phone, cup, ruler as joke)
- Real customer reaction works even better than chef reaction

**Example:**
> *Chef holds up a massive kebab durum next to his face. His eyes go wide. Text: "We might have overdone it this time 😅" — pan to the cross-section showing the filling. CTA: "Order yours before we come to our senses 😂"*

**Results pattern:** Very high comment rate ("I need this"), strong shares (people tag friends)

## Formula 7: The Weekly/Daily Routine Documentary 📅

**Structure:**
```
[Before opening] → [Key ritual or process] → [Why it matters] → [The result]
```

**Best timings to film:**
- 06:00-08:00: Delivery arrival, fresh ingredients, baker starts
- 09:00-11:00: Kitchen prep, sauces made, dough rising
- 11:30-13:00: Pre-service rush, staff meals, plating practice
- 17:00-19:00: Service begins, full house

**Example:**
> *Every Monday we do this. 6am. Market run. Chef picks vegetables personally. Text: "Why does our soup taste like your grandma's? Because we start here." 30 second montage of market → kitchen → soup simmering. End: chef tasting it, nodding. "Ready." Text: "Soup of the week: [name]."*

**Results pattern:** Strong brand identity building, loyal community, high return viewers

## Format-Specific Best Practices

### TikTok Slideshow (Photo Format)
When you don't have video footage, slideshows still perform well.

**Optimal slide structure:**
1. Slide 1: Hook text on dark/blurred background ("Wait till you see slide 5 👀")
2. Slides 2-4: Build-up (raw, prep, in-progress)
3. Slide 5: The big reveal (finished dish, beautiful plating)
4. Slide 6: Social proof (quote from a review)
5. Slide 7: CTA ("Try it. Link in bio.")

**Music choice:** Pick from "Viral Food" playlist in TikTok Creative Center

### Instagram Reels vs. TikTok
| Element | TikTok | Instagram Reels |
|---|---|---|
| Ideal length | 7-30 seconds | 15-60 seconds |
| Caption | Question to drive comments | Hashtag-heavy, storytelling |
| Music | Trending audio (TikTok Creative Center) | Popular Reels audio |
| First frame | Text hook or movement | Aesthetic or branded |
| CTA | In video + caption | In video + Story link sticker |
| Hashtags | 3-5 (TikTok de-emphasizes) | 15-20 (still matters) |

## The Viral Snowball Effect: Case Study Structure

**Phase 1 — Discovery (0 to 1K followers)**
- Post 5x/week
- Every video is an experiment
- Analyze: what format, what time, what sound
- Best video tells you your "winning formula"

**Phase 2 — Momentum (1K to 10K followers)**
- 3-4x/week with higher consistency
- Replicate the best-performing format 3x
- Start mixing: 2 proven formats + 1 new experiment/week
- Google Reviews start snowballing from TikTok traffic

**Phase 3 — Authority (10K to 100K followers)**
- 3x/week: 1 hero video (big effort) + 2 supporting (easy format)
- Collab with local food influencers
- Start posting customer testimonials/reactions
- Website traffic spikes on video posting days — optimize booking flow

**Phase 4 — Compounding (100K+)**
- You are the marketing. The content IS the business.
- Each video is a sales funnel: hook → trust → booking
- Expand: YouTube Shorts, Facebook Reels, Google Posts

## Audio Strategy

**Where to find trending audio:**
1. TikTok Creative Center → Trending sounds (filter by country + category: food)
2. Watch top 10 food videos in Belgium this week — note the audio they use
3. Save 5-10 trending sounds per week to your TikTok profile

**When to use trending audio:**
- When the video concept matches the audio vibe
- Never force it — bad audio-video match hurts performance
- Videos with original sound (ASMR, voiceover) can outperform trends

**Rule of thumb:**
> If the video is about feeling/emotion → use trending music
> If the video is about process/technique → use original sound
