# Webinar Builder — Intake Questions (Phase 0)

Complete all 9 sections before building anything.
The more specific the answers, the more specific the output.
Generic answers produce generic webinars.

---

## Section A — About You

- **Your Name / Brand:**
- **Your Niche or Industry:** *(e.g. AI automation, real estate investing, fitness coaching, SaaS)*
- **Your Audience:** *(Be specific — age range, income, job, situation, what they want)*
- **Your Credibility / Origin Story:** *(What have you built? What's your personal story? Include numbers, results, pivotal experiences — especially struggle and transformation)*
- **Your Platform / Community:** *(Where does your audience live? X/Twitter, Instagram, LinkedIn, email list, Discord, Skool, Circle — approximate size)*

---

## Section B — Your Main Offer

> The webinar exists to sell ONE thing. Be clear before building.

- **Offer Name:**
- **Offer Type:** *(membership / course / coaching / group program / software / done-for-you service)*
- **Offer Price:** *(e.g. $197 one-time / $47/month / $997)*
- **What Does Your Customer GET?** *(List everything — features AND outcomes. Be specific.)*
- **What Problem Does It Solve?** *(The exact pain before buying. What keeps them up at night?)*
- **What Result Does It Promise?** *(What does life look like 30–90 days after buying? Specific outcomes, numbers, transformations.)*
- **Normal / Retail Price:** *(What will it cost after this webinar?)*
- **Webinar-Only Price or Bonus:** *(What makes the webinar offer special? Discount? Exclusive bonus? One-time pricing?)*

---

## Section C — Upsells and Order Bumps

- **Order Bump:** *(e.g. $7 replay bundle — what is it, what's included, what's the price?)*
- **Upsell 2 (if any):** *(VIP day, done-for-you upgrade, mastermind add-on)*

---

## Section D — The Big Idea and Webinar Angle

> The one insight that if believed makes the offer inevitable.
> Find this before naming the webinar.

- **Your Big Idea / Reframe:** *(The one perspective shift your webinar delivers. E.g. "Stop chasing clients — start building systems that attract them.")*
- **Proposed Webinar Title:** *(Use formulas from positioning-framework.md or leave blank for suggestions)*
- **Webinar Promise:** *(What will they have or know by the end?)*
- **3–5 Things You'll Cover:** *(Key sections — written as curiosity-driven outcomes, not topic headers)*

---

## Section E — Traffic and Promotion

- **Paid Ad Budget:** *(e.g. $0 organic only / $200 / $500 / $1,000+ — which platform?)*
- **Organic Amplifiers:** *(Team, community, or affiliates who can post — how many people, how often?)*
- **Email List Size:** *(Approximate — e.g. 500 / 2,000 / 10,000+. If none, write "None.")*
- **Other Traffic Sources:** *(Podcast, YouTube, partner promotions, Discord, etc.)*

---

## Section F — Platform and Tech Stack

- **Webinar Platform:** *(Zoom / StreamYard / EverWebinar / WebinarJam / YouTube Live / "Not sure")*
- **Email / CRM Platform:** *(GoHighLevel / ActiveCampaign / ConvertKit / Mailchimp)*
- **Payment Processor:** *(Stripe / PayPal / ThriveCart / Kajabi)*
- **Landing Page Builder:** *(GoHighLevel / ClickFunnels / Kajabi / Lovable / Framer / "Not sure")*
- **Slide Deck Tool:** *(Manus / Google Slides / PowerPoint / Canva / Keynote)*

---

## Section G — Audience Psychology

> This section produces the best copy. Be raw and specific.
> The more you sound like your audience, the more they feel understood.

- **Top 3 Objections:** *(Why do they NOT buy? E.g. "I don't have time," "I'm not technical enough," "I've tried things like this before")*
- **What Do They WANT Most?** *(Not what they need — what they WANT. Their dream outcome in their own words.)*
- **What Are They AFRAID Of?** *(Worst-case scenario they're trying to avoid — embarrassment, loss, being left behind)*
- **What Have They Already Tried?** *(What solutions failed? Why did they fail?)*
- **Words and Phrases They Use:** *(How do they describe their problem in their own voice? Copy exact language from DMs, posts, comments if you have it.)*

---

## Section H — Tone and Copywriting Style

- **Preferred Writing Tone:** *(Direct and no-BS / Inspirational / Story-driven / Faith-based / Conversational / Professional)*
- **Copywriters / Voices to Model:** *(Russell Brunson / Dan Kennedy / Alex Hormozi / Gary Halbert / Jason Fladlien / Daniel Throssell / "No preference")*
- **Things to AVOID:** *(Hype/clickbait / excessive emojis / corporate language / religious references / profanity / specific words)*
- **Email Sign-Off Name:** *(How do you sign your emails?)*

---

## Section I — Goals and Success Metrics

- **Registration Goal:** *(e.g. 200 / 500 / 1,000)*
- **Revenue Goal:** *(e.g. $2,000 / $5,000 / $10,000 from this webinar)*
- **Conversion Rate Goal:** *(e.g. 10% of live attendees buy)*
- **Webinar Date / Timeline:** *(e.g. "2 weeks from now" / "Evergreen automated" / specific date)*
- **Anything Else the AI Should Know:** *(Constraints, past attempts, things you definitely want included or excluded)*

---

## What to Do With These Answers

1. Paste all completed answers into the AI with the instruction: *"Build my complete webinar strategy using these intake answers."*
2. The AI will build all phases in order: positioning → framework → offer → emails → traffic → slides → checklist.
3. If any critical field is missing (offer price, audience, big idea), the AI will ask before proceeding — not guess.
4. If non-critical information is missing, the AI will make a smart assumption marked with `[ASSUMPTION: ...]` so you can correct it.
