# Email Sequences — Complete Framework

---

## Copywriting Voices by Sequence

| Sequence | Voice | Why |
|----------|-------|-----|
| A1 Confirmation | Brunson | Create identity, tribe, future pacing |
| A2 Day Before | Brunson / Throssell | Origin story, open loop, theatrical curiosity |
| A3 One Hour | Kennedy | Command, authority, no fluff |
| A4 Ten Minutes | Kennedy | Ultra-brief, maximum urgency |
| B1–B4 Replay Buyers | Kennedy | They already bought. Hit harder. Close harder. |
| C1–C4 Non-Buyers | Brunson then Kennedy | Start warm, end hard |

---

## Sequence A — Pre-Webinar (All Registrants)

### A1 — Immediate Confirmation

**Subject formula options:**
- *"Don't ignore this. (Your seat details inside.)"*
- *"You're in. Here's what happens next."*
- *"You just made a decision most people won't."*

**Preview text:** Creates open loop or confirms identity

**Must include:**
- Webinar date, time, timezone (clearly formatted)
- Webinar link placeholder (send actual link in A3)
- "Add to Calendar" CTA
- 3 prep instructions framed as actions, not information
- Identity statement: *"You just made a decision most people won't..."*
- Tribe creation: *"While everyone else is [doing wrong thing], you..."*
- P.S. that qualifies the reader — filters out browsers, elevates serious buyers

**Kennedy element:** Command language. *"Add it to your calendar RIGHT NOW. Not later. Now."*

**Brunson element:** Future pacing — *"This is 90 minutes that could change your next 90 days."*

---

### A2 — Day Before

**Subject formula options:**
- *"I was homeless at 16. Here's what that taught me about [topic]."*
- *"Knowledge isn't the problem (here's what is)"*
- *"The thing nobody tells you about [making money with / succeeding at / X]"*
- *"I probably shouldn't tell you this. (But I'm going to anyway.)"* ← Throssell

**Must include:**
- Full origin story arc: struggle → wall → epiphany → result → what you're teaching now
- Curiosity loop about one specific thing they'll learn that can only be closed by attending live
- Preview of the content without giving it away
- The stakes: what happens if they miss it

**Throssell technique (anti-sell opening):**
*"I want to tell you something I don't usually say publicly. Most of the people coming to my training tomorrow? They're going to leave exactly the same as when they arrived..."*
This reverse psychology forces serious buyers to prove they're different by showing up and implementing.

**Open loop P.S.:** Reference one specific thing from the first 10 minutes that they can only learn by being there. *"Pay attention when we get to the [X] section. That's the moment most AI gurus will never tell you about."*

---

### A3 — One Hour Before

**Subject formula options:**
- *"We go live in 60 minutes. Here's your link."*
- *"Starting in 1 hour. Read this first."*
- *"Don't join without reading this."*

**Structure:** Ultra-short. Command-style. No story.

1. State the time remaining
2. Give the link (this is the first time they see the actual link)
3. 3 commands: Close X. Do Y. Bring Z.
4. One line about the first 10 minutes: *"Don't miss the first 10 minutes. That's where [specific thing] happens."*
5. Sign off. Done.

**Kennedy rule:** At 60 minutes out, brevity = urgency. Every extra word dilutes the urgency signal.

---

### A4 — Ten Minutes Before

**Subject formula options:**
- *"10 minutes. Join now."*
- *"We start in 10 minutes."*
- *"[LIVE] Starting in 10 minutes"*

**Structure:** 3–5 lines maximum. Nothing else.

*"We start in 10 minutes.*
*JOIN HERE NOW: [LINK]*
*The first 10 minutes are the most important.*
*Don't miss them.*
*— [Name]"*

**Do NOT add more.** Resist every urge to add context, story, or bullet points. Shorter = more urgent.

## Sequence B — Post-Webinar Replay Purchasers

These contacts already spent money. They are the warmest segment. Hit harder, sooner. Price anchor early. 

### B1 — Immediately After Webinar

**Subject:** *"Here's everything I promised + the offer I'll never make again."*

**Must include:**
- Deliver all replay links and bonus files (they paid for these)
- Transition immediately to the main offer
- Price anchoring: state normal price vs. webinar price and the math
- 5-day deadline stated clearly
- Hard deadline language: *"After that, this page redirects to $[normal price]. No exceptions. No 'just this once.'"*
- P.S. directing them to the most valuable specific replay moment

**Kennedy element:** *"I'm not in the business of training people to wait."*

---

### B2 — Day 2

**Subject:** *"Which of the [8] models is yours? (I'm asking for real.)"*

**Must include:**
- Genuine engagement question about which model/framework resonated
- Invite reply — this drives deliverability AND creates conversation-to-conversion opportunities
- Soft push on offer: *"3 days left on the [lifetime/special] offer"*
- Personalization promise: *"Inside [community/program] we build your plan around YOUR model — not a generic template"*

**Brunson element:** Personalization — makes every buyer feel the offer was built specifically for them.

---

### B3 — Day 4 (48-Hour Warning)

**Subject:** *"48 hours. Then it's $[normal price]. Here's the math."*

**Must include:**
- The math table (Kennedy's math close):
  - Monthly price × 12 months = Year 1 cost
  - Monthly price × 36 months = 3-year cost
  - Webinar price = one time
- Competitor/isolation frame: *"Every week you're not inside a community of builders is a week you're building alone. Alone is slower. Alone is more expensive."*
- Hard no-extension policy: *"I won't extend it. I won't reopen it. I won't 'make an exception.' I respect you too much to train you to wait."*
- Empathy P.S.: *"If $[price] is genuinely a financial stretch right now, reply and tell me. I'd rather have an honest conversation than watch you miss this. But if money isn't the real issue — be honest with yourself about what is."*

---

### B4 — Day 5 Final Close (Send TWICE: 9am + 7pm)

**Subject:** *"This is the last email. (Closes tonight at midnight.)"*

**Must include:**
- Acknowledge this is the final email in the sequence
- Origin question close (Brunson): *"When you registered for this webinar — what were you hoping would change?"*
- The gap frame (Kennedy): *"The gap between where you are and where you want to be is still there. The question is whether you're going to close it — or keep looking at it."*
- Identity statement: *"People who don't act on moments like this don't act on the next one either."* (Reframes inaction as a character trait)
- Midnight hard close: specific time, not "tonight"
- Grace exit (Brunson): *"Whether you join or not, I hope today was worth your time."* (Preserves relationship for future offers)

**Evening send (7pm) subject variant:** *"Tonight: [Offer] closes at midnight. Last email."*

## Sequence C — Post-Webinar Non-Buyers

Start with value. Move to urgency. Close hard. These contacts are cooler — build warmth before pressure.

### C1 — Immediately After Webinar

**Subject:** *"You were there. Here's what to do next."*

**Must include:**
- Acknowledge they attended
- Pattern interrupt on inaction: *"Three months later, nothing changed. And they're looking for the next training."*
- Two-choice close (Fladlien): Present $7 replay AND $[main offer] as two paths — both are "yes," never "no"
- Command close: *"Pick one."* (Two words. Massive compliance pull.)
- P.S. reply bridge: *"If you're not sure which is right for you, reply and tell me where you're at."*

---

### C2 — Day 2

**Subject:** *"Which of the [8] models is yours?"*

**Must include:**
- Self-selection mechanism: List all models with who each is "best for" — lets them pre-sell themselves by choosing their identity match
- Reply mechanic: *"Hit reply. Tell me which one."*
- Personalization promise: *"We build your plan around YOUR model on Day 1"*
- Buried deadline: *"3 days left" — mentioned once, not hammered*

---

### C3 — Day 4 (48-Hour Warning)

**Subject:** *"48 hours left (then it's $[normal price]). Here's what that actually means."*

**Must include:**
- Math drip: break down monthly cost over 6mo/1yr/2yr/3yr
- Contrast cut: *"Or: $[offer price]. One time. Today."*
- Isolation frame: *"Every week you're not inside a community of builders is a week you're building alone."*
- Mechanism scarcity in P.S.: *"[Code/system] is already in place. I can't override it even if I wanted to."* (Makes deadline feel structural, not arbitrary)

---

### C4 — Day 5 Final Close (Send TWICE: 9am + 7pm)

**Subject:** *"Last email. [Offer] closes tonight."*

**Must include:**
- Ultra-brief opening: *"This is the last one."*
- Origin question (Brunson): *"Why did you register for this webinar?"*
- Gap frame: *"Something made you stop and sign up. That thing hasn't gone away."*
- Lean forward close (Brunson): *"If something in you is leaning forward right now — listen to that part."* (Converts hesitators who've already decided but need permission)
- Permission exit: *"No hard feelings."* (Preserves relationship)

**Evening send subject variant:** *"Midnight tonight. Last call."*

## Subject Line Principles

**Pattern interrupt beats benefit statement.**
- Not: *"Join my webinar to learn AI income"*
- Yes: *"Don't ignore this."*

**Open loop demands resolution.**
The reader should be unable to answer the subject line without opening the email.

**Specificity beats vagueness.**
- Not: *"Important update about the training"*
- Yes: *"We go live in 60 minutes. Here's your link."*

**Urgency is time-stamped.**
- Not: *"This closes soon"*
- Yes: *"48 hours. Then it's $47/month. Forever."*

**Kennedy for deadlines. Brunson for stories. Throssell for pattern interrupts.**
Match the voice to the job of the email.
