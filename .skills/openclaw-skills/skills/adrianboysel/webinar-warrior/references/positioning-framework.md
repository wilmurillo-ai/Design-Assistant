# Positioning Framework

---

## Webinar Title Formulas

Choose one. Fill in the blanks. The title becomes the hook for the registration page, the first slide, and all ad copy.

**Formula 1 — The Reframe (highest conversion)**
`"Stop [Old Behavior] — Start [New Behavior]"`
Example: *"Stop Using AI Like a Tool — Start Using It Like Infrastructure"*
Use when: Your audience is doing something that seems right but is fundamentally wrong.

**Formula 2 — The How-To Without**
`"How to [Desired Outcome] Without [Most Common Objection]"`
Example: *"How to Build AI Income Without Technical Skills or a Big Audience"*
Use when: Your audience wants the outcome but believes they can't have it because of a specific constraint.

**Formula 3 — The Secret System**
`"The [Audience] [System/Method/Framework] for [Outcome]: What [Authority Figure/Elite Group] Know That You Don't"`
Example: *"The Operator Framework for AI Income: What the Top 1% of AI Earners Know That Everyone Else Doesn't"*
Use when: Your audience is motivated by being in the know and gaining access to insider information.

**Formula 4 — The Contrarian Truth**
`"Why [Common Belief] Is Wrong — And What Actually [Produces Result]"`
Example: *"Why AI Knowledge Won't Make You Money — And What Actually Will"*
Use when: Your audience has a belief that's holding them back. This title shatters the belief from the first touchpoint.

**Formula 5 — The Specific Result**
`"How to [Specific Measurable Outcome] in [Timeframe] [With/Without Constraint]"`
Example: *"How to Build a $5K/Month AI Income System in 90 Days Without Quitting Your Job"*
Use when: Your audience responds to specific numbers and timeframes. Best for skeptical audiences.

## The Hook Test

Before finalizing any title, apply this test:

1. Can the audience answer it in their head without attending? (If YES → rewrite. Unanswerable = register.)
2. Does knowing the answer change how they see their situation? (If NO → rewrite. Must create a shift.)
3. Does the answer point directly to your offer? (If NO → rewrite. Hook must serve the close.)
4. Would you click it scrolling through your feed at 11pm? (If NO → rewrite.)

## The Primary Promise Structure

**Template:**
*"By the end of this webinar, you will have [specific outcome] — plus [secondary benefit that addresses a hidden desire]."*

**Rules:**
- Lead with HAVE, not KNOW or UNDERSTAND. People buy transformation, not information.
- Be specific. "A framework" beats "strategies." "Your 90-day plan" beats "clarity."
- Address a hidden desire in the secondary benefit — something they want but didn't say out loud.

**Example (AI income):**
*"By the end of this training, you will have the exact framework for turning AI knowledge into automated income — plus a custom plan for YOUR next 90 days, built during this session."*

## What We'll Cover (The 5 Points)

Write these as curiosity-driven outcomes — not topic headers.

**Wrong (topic headers):**
- Introduction to AI income models
- How to use the Operator Framework
- Traffic strategies for webinars

**Right (curiosity-driven outcomes):**
- Why most AI users — even the smartest ones — never turn knowledge into income (and the one shift that changes everything)
- The 4-step system operators use to build income that runs without them
- Which of the 8 AI income models fits YOUR skills and leverage — decided today, not someday

**The test:** After reading each point, does the audience think "I need to know this" — or "that sounds useful"? Write for the first reaction. The second doesn't convert.

## The Outcome Statement

One line. Lives at the bottom of the registration page. Inoculates against the fear of "another useless training."

**Template:**
*"You'll leave with [X], [Y], and [Z] — not another list of [the thing they're tired of]."*

**Example:**
*"You'll leave with clarity, a framework, and a custom action plan — not another list of AI tools."*

## The Registration Page Formula (Brunson)

The ONLY copy on the registration page:

1. **The hook** (headline) — one bold curiosity-driven claim
2. **The 3 secrets** — what they'll discover (curiosity-driven, not topic headers)
3. **The form** — name and email, nothing else

No paragraphs. No bios. No features list. No testimonials.
Curiosity creates registrations. Information kills them.

## Positioning Yourself (Fladlien's Test)

After your positioning section, the audience should conclude:
*"Either I do this through them, or I don't do it at all."*

Two push-backs to overcome:
1. *"I don't necessarily believe you know what you're talking about"*
2. *"There are 15 other people I could go to instead — I'll take the free stuff and go to them later"*

**How to overcome both:**

Push-back 1 (credibility) → Lived results, not credentials.
Credentials are claimed. Results are real. "$4,000 in one day building two websites" beats "certified AI specialist."

Push-back 2 (alternatives) → The unusual combination.
What combination of experiences, failures, and insights makes YOUR perspective unique? Not just what you know — what you've lived through that they haven't.

**The juxtaposition:**
*"Most people teaching [topic] are doing it from theory. I'm teaching it from what I built yesterday. Here's exactly what happened..."*

## Webinar Positioning vs. Brand Positioning

The webinar title and hook live separately from your brand name and positioning.
The webinar is an event with its own identity.

**Brand:** Skill Stacker — AI income and community platform
**Webinar:** "AI Income System Training — Stop Using AI Like a Tool, Start Using It Like Infrastructure"

The webinar hook is sharper, more specific, and more controversial than the brand.
That's correct. The hook's job is to create a registration — not represent the full brand.
