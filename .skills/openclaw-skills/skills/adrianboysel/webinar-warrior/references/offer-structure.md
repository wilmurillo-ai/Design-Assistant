# Offer Structure Framework

---

## The 3-Tier Architecture

Every webinar funnel has three offer tiers.
Each tier serves a different buyer readiness level.

---

## Tier 1 — Free Registration

**Job:** Get them in the room. Lower the barrier to zero.

**What to include (frame as outcomes, not features):**
- The live training itself (90 minutes)
- The framework they'll walk away with
- The models / systems / breakdown they'll see
- The live Q&A
- The roadmap or action plan built during the session

**What NOT to include:**
- Replays (that's Tier 2)
- Slide decks (that's Tier 2)
- Anything they can access without showing up live

**Registration page rule:** The only copy on the page should be the hook (one thing), the three secrets (three things), and the registration form. Curiosity-based. Never explain too much — that kills registrations.

---

## Tier 2 — Order Bump ($7–$47)

**When:** Immediately on the confirmation page after registration. One time. Never available again at this price.

**Job:** Cover ad costs, warm up buyers before the webinar, increase overall revenue floor.

**What to include:**
- Full webinar replay (lifetime access)
- Slide deck PDF
- 90-Day Roadmap Template (fillable)
- Main topic cheat sheet or model guide
- Optional: 1–2 bonus templates or scripts

**Headline formula:** *"Want the Replay + Bonus Resources?"*

**Copy structure:**
1. Acknowledge they might miss it / want to rewatch
2. List what's included
3. State the price (one time, only here)
4. Scarcity: "One-time offer. Only available on this page."
5. CTA button

**Conversion target:** 18–22% of registrants purchase the order bump.

**Revenue impact at scale:**
- 500 registrants × 20% × $7 = $700 before webinar starts
- 1,000 registrants × 20% × $7 = $1,400 before webinar starts

---

## Tier 3 — Main Offer (Webinar-Only Pricing)

**Job:** The primary revenue event. Built around the Big Domino. Only available during/after the webinar.

**Pricing strategy:**
- Webinar-only price must feel like a crime compared to normal price
- Minimum 50% discount from normal retail
- Show the math explicitly: *"Normal price: $47/month × 12 = $564/year. Today: $197 once. Never again."*

**Scarcity requirements:**
- Must be genuinely unavailable after the window closes
- Redirect the page after close — don't just say it expires
- No exceptions, no "just this once" — your credibility depends on it

## The Bonus Strategy (Fladlien Method)

### Core Principle
Spend far more time on bonuses than on what they pay for.
Show as little as possible of the core offer. Show as much as possible of what they get free.

### Each Bonus Must Do ONE of Three Things

**Type 1 — Objection Killer**
Directly addresses the top reason someone would not buy.

Examples:
- Objection: "I don't have time" → Bonus: Quick-start shortcut that gets results in 30 minutes/day
- Objection: "I'm not technical" → Bonus: Done-for-you templates that require zero setup
- Objection: "I can't find clients" → Bonus: Client acquisition framework (only if you can deliver this)

**Type 2 — Dramatic Demonstration**
Value so clear and immediate that people would buy the whole package just to get this one thing.

Examples:
- A custom GPT pre-built for their exact use case
- A done-for-you framework they'd normally spend weeks creating
- Access to a tool or community that has a high standalone value

**Type 3 — Proof Element**
A result so specific it validates the entire offer. Turn your best case study into a bonus.

Examples:
- "How I built two websites in one day for $2,000 each — the exact workflow"
- "How [client name] went from zero to [result] in [timeframe] — step by step"

### Bonus Presentation Order
1. Second-best bonus (open strong)
2. Mid-tier bonuses (build momentum)
3. Tease best bonus throughout: *"I've saved the best one for last..."*
4. Best bonus (close with the wow)

### CTA After Every Bonus
*"Just for this one bonus alone — doesn't it already make sense to go to [link] right now and get started?"*
Then introduce the next bonus.

You are pitching every time you add a bonus. 7 bonuses = 7 pitches. This is why Fladlien outperforms presenters who pitch once at the end.

## The Stack (Brunson Method)

### The Principle
People only remember the last thing you showed them. If you show all your value items and then say "it's $997" — they associate the price with the last thing (usually a minor bonus), not the full value.

The Stack forces them to associate the price with everything.

### How to Execute

After every new element revealed, restate the full accumulated value:

*"So that means today you're going to get:*
*→ [Item 1] — value $X*
*→ Plus [Item 2] — value $X*
*→ Total value so far: $[running total]"*

Keep restacking until you've shown everything. Then:

*"Everything you've seen today — [full list] — total value of $[anchor price]. I'm not going to charge you $[anchor]. I'm not even going to charge you $[midpoint]. Today, because you're here live, your investment is only $[offer price]."*

### Restacking Rule
Brunson removed the Stack from one presentation and made zero sales. Put it back and made 17 sales. Never present without the Stack.

## If-All Statements (Brunson)

Used to get three yeses at full anchor value before the price reveal.

*"Now if all this package did was [single specific outcome] — would it be worth $[anchor price]?"*
*(pause — let them say yes)*

*"And if all this did was [different specific outcome] — would it be worth it?"*
*(pause)*

*"And one more — if all this did was [third outcome] — would it be worth $[anchor price]?"*
*(pause)*

*"Well good — because that's exactly what's going to happen. And you don't have to pay $[anchor]. Today you can get all of this for just $[offer price]."*

After they've said yes three times that it's worth $[anchor], they cannot logically say no to a fraction of that price.

## Risk Reversal

### Placement
- Mention guarantee during pain section (*"And there's zero risk because..."*)
- Reference after bonuses (*"Plus you're protected by..."*)
- Dedicated slide before final CTA (*"Here's how I take all the risk..."*)

### Types of Guarantee

**Standard money-back:** "If you're not satisfied, I'll refund you — no questions asked."
(Works but creates passive buyers who never implement)

**Action-based (better):** "Complete [specific actions] within [timeframe]. If you don't see [specific result], I'll refund every dollar."
(Better because it requires engagement — engaged buyers get results — results reduce refund requests)

**Confidence guarantee:** "I'm so confident this will work that I'll work with you until it does."
(Highest perceived value — only use if you can actually fulfill it)

### The Risk Reversal Frame
*"I'm taking all the risk. Not you. The worst case scenario is you go through everything, implement nothing, and ask for your money back. But here's what I know: the people who show up and do the work always get results. So the only way you lose is if you don't show up."*

## Countdown Clock Close (Brunson)

### Setup
After the pitch, set a visible countdown clock for 30 minutes.
Tell the audience: *"I'm going to be here answering your questions for the next 30 minutes. And when that clock hits zero, this offer disappears."*

### The 30-Minute Q&A Window
- Pre-load the 20 questions you always get
- Answer 1–2 live questions from chat per slide
- Keep energy HIGH throughout — never let it dip
- Restate the CTA every 5–7 minutes
- Remind them of the deadline every time you restate the CTA

### Why This Works
75% of webinar sales come in during this window — not during the pitch.
The combination of urgency (clock) + permission (Q&A format) + energy (you're still present) creates the conditions for fence-sitters to act.

Never end the webinar when the content ends. End it when the clock ends.
