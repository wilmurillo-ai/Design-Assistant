# Slide Deck Framework
## The Complete 47-Slide Webinar Structure

---

## Core Principle

The script comes before the slides.
Write what you're going to say first.
Then build slides that support what's already written.
Slides are props. You are the presentation.

---

## The 90-Minute Budget

| Section | Time | Slide Count | Notes |
|---------|------|-------------|-------|
| Opening + Pain + Hook + Tease | 10 min | 6–7 | High energy. Fastest pacing. |
| Credibility + Position + Paradigm | 5 min | 3 | Slow down. Let the story land. |
| The 3 Secrets / Mechanisms | 10 min | 3–4 | One slide per secret. Commitment row on each. |
| Core Teaching (Framework) | 12 min | 5–6 | Step-by-step. Case study. |
| Models / Proof / Examples | 18 min | 9–10 | Most content. Real numbers. |
| Choose Your Path + Roadmap | 8 min | 3–4 | Personal. Make them decide. |
| Bridge + Client Problem | 3 min | 2 | Plant the seed before the pitch. |
| Offer + Bonuses + Stack + Close | 15 min | 7–9 | Fladlien bonuses + Brunson stack. |
| Q&A + Countdown Close | 9 min | 2–3 | Pre-loaded questions. Clock running. |
| **TOTAL** | **90 min** | **~47 slides** | |

**Hard ceiling: 50 slides.** Every slide over 50 either gets rushed or cut live. Both hurt conversion.

## Slide-by-Slide Blueprint

### SECTION 1: OPENING (Slides 1–7) — 10 minutes

**Slide 1 — Title (display during join window)**
- Webinar name (large, bold)
- Presenter name + brand
- Date and time
- Have this up while people join. Loop energy for 5–10 minutes before starting.

**Slide 2 — Who This Is For**
- 4 bullet rows: "You're in the right place if..."
- Each row: one specific identity statement
- Bottom: "This is [X] minutes of execution. Not inspiration."
- Job: qualify the room. Filter browsers. Elevate committed attendees.

**Slide 3 — Pain Part 1: The Nightmare**
- Headline: "You've done everything right. So why isn't it working?"
- 5 "you've done this" statements (courses, tools, accounts, tutorials, knowledge)
- Bottom black bar: "And you're in the exact same position you were 6 months ago."
- Job: create immediate recognition. They feel seen before you've sold anything.

**Slide 4 — Pain Part 2: The Real Problem**
- Headline: "This is not a motivation problem."
- Two-column split: what they have (knowledge) vs. what they need (infrastructure)
- Job: name the enemy. Give the pain a face. Set up the paradigm.

**Slide 5 — The Paradigm / Big Domino**
- Large centered statement — THE one belief that changes everything
- Minimal design. Maximum white space.
- Job: the "oh this changes everything" moment. Land it. Pause after this slide.

**Slide 6 — The Shift**
- Two columns: Old Way (gray/muted) vs. New Way (bold/black)
- 5–8 direct comparison rows
- Job: make the paradigm concrete through contrast.

**Slide 7 — The Tease (Open Loops)**
- Headline: "Here's what's coming in the next 90 minutes."
- 5–6 curiosity statements (not topic headers — open loops)
- Each starts with "Why..." or "The exact..." or "What actually..."
- Job: keep them for the full 90 minutes. Every loop must close before the webinar ends.

---

### SECTION 2: CREDIBILITY + POSITION (Slides 8–10) — 5 minutes

**Slide 8 — Origin Story**
- Timeline: 5 nodes left to right
- Node 1: The backstory (struggle, starting point)
- Node 2: The grind (what you built despite it)
- Node 3: The wall (what kept failing)
- Node 4: The epiphany (the moment everything changed)
- Node 5: The result (where you are now — specific numbers)
- Job: establish credibility through lived experience, not credentials.

**Slide 9 — Position (Why You)**
- Two columns: What others have vs. What this training has
- Left: vague credentials, theory, generic frameworks
- Right: active results, specific numbers, lived experience
- Bottom bar: "You're not learning from someone who studied this. You're learning from someone doing this right now."
- Job: make you the only logical guide for this specific journey.

**Slide 10 — The Promise**
- What they'll walk away with (5 specific outcomes — not topics)
- Each framed as an accomplishment they'll have, not content they'll see
- Job: give them the ruler by which to measure success. Set the bar. Own it.

---

### SECTION 3: THE 3 SECRETS (Slides 11–13) — 10 minutes

**One slide per secret. Same master layout for all three.**

**Master Secret Slide Layout:**
- Left column (black fill): Secret number + Secret name (large)
- Right column:
  - THE FALSE BELIEF (boxed, italic, what they currently believe)
  - THE TRUTH (black bar, bold statement that shatters the belief)
  - THE PROOF (boxed, specific example, case study, or result)
- Bottom: COMMITMENT ROW — black bar left (commitment question) + white bar right ("Drop your answer in the chat / YES or NO")

**Secret 1: The Vehicle**
False belief about the METHOD. (They think it's too hard / too technical / not for them.)

**Secret 2: The Internal Belief**
False belief about THEMSELVES. (They think they don't have the right skills / experience / background.)

**Secret 3: The External Belief**
False belief about OUTSIDE FORCES. (They think the market is saturated / finding clients is impossible / timing is wrong.)

**After all 3 secrets:** Transition slide — "Let me show you the framework." (minimal, clean, builds anticipation)

---

### SECTION 4: CORE TEACHING — FRAMEWORK (Slides 14–19) — 12 minutes

**Slide 14 — Framework Overview**
- Visual: 4 boxes in horizontal row with arrows between
- Each box: number + step name + one-line description
- Job: show the map before walking the territory.

**Slides 15–18 — Framework Steps (one per slide)**

**Master Step Slide Layout:**
- Left column (full height, black fill): large step number + step name
- Right column:
  - Headline: definition of this step (action-oriented)
  - 3 content boxes stacked: what this means / common mistake / how to do it
  - Bottom: COMMITMENT ROW (same format as Secret slides)

**Slide 19 — Framework in Action (Case Study)**
- Featured case study with real numbers
- Show the framework applied to a real example
- "This is not a theory slide" — make it undeniably real
- Earnings shown prominently: actual dollar amount, actual time, actual tools

---

### SECTION 5: MODELS / PROOF / EXAMPLES (Slides 20–29) — 18 minutes

**Slide 20 — Models Overview**
- Numbered list of all models (e.g., all 8)
- Clean, scannable
- Bottom: "One of these is yours. Circle the one that resonates as we go through each."

**Slides 21–28 — One Model Per Slide**

**Master Model Slide Layout:**
- Top row: model number (black box) + model name + "Best For" badge
- Left column: What it is / Revenue range / Entry point
- Right column: Real example box (specific result, specific person, specific timeline)
- Tool pills: specific tools for this model
- Bottom: key insight or belief-shifting statement

**Slide 29 — Summary Table**
- All models in one table: # / Model / Best For / Entry Point / Revenue Range
- Bottom: "Which one made you lean forward? That's your starting point."

---

### SECTION 6: CHOOSE YOUR PATH + ROADMAP (Slides 30–33) — 8 minutes

**Slide 30 — The 3 Questions**
- 3 self-assessment cards: Skills / Time / Leverage
- Each: bold question + subtext that helps them answer honestly
- Job: make them do the thinking. Don't answer for them — yet.

**Slide 31 — The Path Guide**
- 5 rows: "If [situation] → Start with [model]"
- Arrow format, clean, decisive
- Bottom bar: "There is no wrong answer. There is only the model that fits where you are right now."

**Slide 32 — 90-Day Roadmap**
- 3 columns: Month 1 / Month 2 / Month 3
- Each: labeled phase + 4 bullet actions
- Bottom bar: Goal statement with specific number (e.g., "$5,000/month by Day 90")

**Slide 33 — The Bridge (Plant the Seed)**
- Large centered statement: "Here's where most people get stuck."
- Named the specific problem (client-getting / lead generation / whatever applies)
- Bottom: "This is the one thing I can't fully solve in 90 minutes. It requires a system — and a community."
- Job: create the need for the offer without pitching yet.

---

### SECTION 7: TRANSITION TO OFFER (Slides 34–35) — 3 minutes

**Slide 34 — The Two Choices Bridge (Fladlien)**
- Two cards side by side:
  - LEFT (white): "Go it alone" — you have everything to start, that's a valid path
  - RIGHT (black): "Build with us" — everything above plus the community, system, support
- Bottom bar: "I chose Option 2 for you. Let me show you what's waiting inside."

**Slide 35 — Transition**
- Minimal slide: "Here's how to go deeper."
- Subtext: "Everything I've shown you today is the what and the how. [Program] is where you do it."

---

### SECTION 8: OFFER + BONUSES + STACK (Slides 36–44) — 15 minutes

**Slide 36 — Program Intro**
- Program name, one-line positioning statement
- NOT this / NOT this / THIS IS IT — three contrast boxes
- Bottom bar: what the community/program actually is in one sentence

**Slides 37–40 — Bonus Slides (4 consolidated slides)**

Each bonus slide follows this layout:
- Slide label: "BONUS 01 OF 04" etc.
- Headline: bonus name
- Badge: KILLS OBJECTION / PROOF ELEMENT / DRAMATIC VALUE
- Two-column card (each card = one bonus item within this slide)
- Each card: top black bar (bonus name) + description + value badge
- Bottom CTA bar: "Both/all of these are yours. Go to [LINK] right now."

Save best bonus for last on a solo slide.

**Slide 41 — The Stack**
- Full offer restacked with running total
- Every item listed with individual value
- Accumulated total value at bottom
- Job: associate the price with everything, not just the last item shown

**Slide 42 — If-All Statements**
- Three questions, three checkboxes
- *"If all this did was [X] — would it be worth $[anchor]? ✓"*
- *"If all this did was [Y] — would it be worth it? ✓"*
- *"If all this did was [Z] — would it be worth it? ✓"*
- Bottom: "Then you'll love what I'm about to tell you."

**Slide 43 — Pricing**
- Two cards: monthly/normal price vs. webinar-only price
- Monthly card: gray/muted — full cost breakdown over 1, 2, 3 years
- Webinar card: black — one-time price, savings calculation
- Bottom: "This disappears when you close this page. After today: [normal price] or nothing."

**Slide 44 — CTA**
- "Are you serious about [outcome]? Or are you still just exploring?"
- Large black CTA bar with offer link
- 3 reassurance pills: what makes this safe / what makes it special / when it expires

---

### SECTION 9: RISK + Q&A + CLOSE (Slides 45–47) — 9 minutes

**Slide 45 — Risk Reversal / Guarantee**
- "I'm taking all the risk. Not you."
- Large centered guarantee statement in bordered box
- Specific guarantee terms
- Bottom: "The only way you lose is if you don't show up."

**Slide 46 — Q&A**
- "Questions?" — large, centered
- Subtext: "Drop them in the chat."
- Pre-load 20 questions you always get
- Keep this slide up for the full 30-minute countdown window

**Slide 47 — Close**
- Three action boxes: "You joined" / "You're still deciding" / "Start here regardless"
- Final statement from presenter
- Sign-off

## Design System (Manus / Google Slides)

**Colors:** Black and white only. One accent color maximum if brand requires.
**Corners:** Sharp only. No rounded corners anywhere.
**Effects:** No drop shadows, gradients, or blur.
**Header:** Webinar name (left) + Presenter/Brand (right). Identical position every slide.
**Footer:** Website URL (left) + Slide number (right). Identical position every slide.
**Content area:** Between header rule and footer rule. Never cross these lines.
**Elements:** All individually moveable. No grouped elements unless specified.
**Text:** All live editable text. Never embed text in images.
**Export:** Must be fully editable in Google Slides (.pptx format).

**Per-slide rule:** One job. One message. One moment.
If a slide is trying to do two things, split it into two slides.
If a slide has nothing to say, delete it.
