# TCS Expense Categories — Eligibility & Filing Rules

## Travel Expenses – Conveyance
**What's eligible:** Uber, Ola, Lyft, taxis, auto-rickshaws, airport
transfers, local cabs booked for business travel.

**Form fields:** S/WON, From Location, To Location, Bill Date, Bill No,
Currency, Bill Amount, Remarks

**Tips:**
- For Uber: use the Trip ID as Bill No
- From/To should be specific: "Home – Troy MI" or "DTW Airport"
- Mark as Business trip type in the Uber app before travel

---

## Ticket Expenses – Air Tickets
**What's eligible:** Airfare (economy class only unless policy allows
otherwise), excess baggage fees, airport taxes paid separately.

**Form fields:** Bill Date, Bill No (ticket/PNR number), Currency,
Bill Amount, Remarks

**Tips:**
- Excess baggage is a SEPARATE line item from the airfare ticket
- Keep Delta/airline receipt PDF separately — do not merge with food bills
- Note flight number and route in Remarks

---

## Hotel Accommodation
**What's eligible:** Hotel room charges only. The folio/checkout invoice.

**What's NOT eligible here:** Room service / in-room dining (→ Meal),
minibar (usually not reimbursable), personal laundry.

**Form fields:** Bill Date, From Date, To Date, Bill No (folio/conf no),
Currency, Bill Amount, Remarks

---

## Travel Expenses – Meal
**What's eligible:** All food and non-alcoholic beverages during travel.
Restaurant bills, hotel restaurant bills, room dining, airport food,
café receipts, food delivery (Zomato/Swiggy).

**What to watch:** Alcohol may not be reimbursable depending on project
policy. Check with your manager.

**Form fields:** Bill Date, From Date, To Date, Name of Hotel/Restaurant,
Bill No, Currency, Bill Amount, Remarks

**Tips:**
- For Zomato/Swiggy: combine restaurant bill + platform fee + delivery into
  one entry. Use the Order ID as Bill No.
- For multi-bill days: you can file one entry per restaurant OR one combined
  entry per day — check your project's preference
- Sweets/mithai purchased as gifts: flag these — some projects don't allow
  gifting claims

---

## Client Entertainment – Travel
**What's eligible:** Meals or entertainment where a client was present.
Requires client name(s) and purpose in Remarks.

**Form fields:** Same as Meal, plus client details in Remarks

---

## Communication Expenses
**What's eligible:** International SIM cards, roaming charges, travel
Wi-Fi dongles, data packs purchased for business travel.

---

## Conference and Training Courses
**What's eligible:** Conference registration fees, training course fees,
workshop materials purchased at the event.

---

## General Rules

1. **Bill No is mandatory** for most categories — use "attached" if the
   receipt has no number, but try to extract one
2. **Original currency** should be entered as shown on the bill; the system
   converts automatically
3. **Client Recoverable checkbox**: tick this if the project is
   client-billable and your manager confirms
4. **Remarks field**: always fill — include what was purchased and who
   was present (for group meals)
5. **Employee Contribution**: enter 0 unless you personally paid for
   something that doesn't qualify (e.g., alcohol portion of a bill)
