# Creem Preflight Checklist (audit-ready)

> Tailor wording to match your actual product. Do not promise anything you can’t operationally support.

## 1) Legal pages exist and are linked publicly
- [ ] Privacy Policy is published and linked in footer
- [ ] Terms of Service is published and linked in footer
- [ ] Refund Policy is published and linked in footer
- [ ] Content Policy / Acceptable Use is published and linked in footer
- [ ] Contact / Support page exists with support email and response expectations

## 2) Contact + identity are consistent everywhere
- [ ] Seller/legal entity name is consistent across pages
- [ ] Domain shown matches the actual deployed domain
- [ ] Support email is consistent (no multiple emails unless intentionally routed)
- [ ] Contact address is present where required (policy pages or contact page)

## 3) Refund & cancellation are explicit (most common blocker)
- [ ] Refund eligibility is clearly stated (what qualifies / what does not)
- [ ] Refund request process is stated (email, required info)
- [ ] Refund timeline is stated (e.g., review within X days; processing times)
- [ ] Cancellation policy is stated (how to cancel; when it takes effect)
- [ ] Auto-renew disclosure exists (subscription renews unless canceled)

## 4) Pricing & product description are clear
- [ ] Pricing page shows what the customer receives (limits/credits/seats)
- [ ] Pricing shows billing period (monthly/annual) and currency
- [ ] If credits are used: explain what a credit is and when it is consumed
- [ ] No misleading “unlimited” claims without constraints/abuse guardrails

## 5) Checkout flow disclosures
- [ ] Before payment, user can access Terms/Privacy/Refund links
- [ ] Subscription checkout includes auto-renew and cancellation disclosure
- [ ] Post-purchase: user knows how to access billing portal/support

## 6) Content policy covers prohibited use
- [ ] Prohibits illegal content, harassment/hate, explicit sexual content involving minors
- [ ] Prohibits IP infringement, impersonation, fraud
- [ ] Prohibits attempts to bypass safety/security controls
- [ ] Explains enforcement (suspension/termination, reporting channel)

## 7) Privacy policy covers the basics
- [ ] What data you collect + purpose
- [ ] Who you share with (processors: hosting, analytics, email, payment via Creem)
- [ ] Retention + deletion requests
- [ ] User rights (depending on regions)

## 8) Operational readiness (don’t publish what you can’t run)
- [ ] Support process exists (SLA expectations)
- [ ] Refund requests can be handled within promised time
- [ ] Abuse reports have an internal handling path

## 9) Common “rejection risk” checks
- [ ] Refund policy doesn’t conflict with pricing page / FAQ
- [ ] Terms don’t claim you are the Merchant of Record if Creem is MoR
- [ ] Contact info is not hidden behind login
