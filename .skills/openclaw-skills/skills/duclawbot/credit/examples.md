# Credit — Real Usage Examples

*Copy these prompts directly into your AI agent to get started.*

---

## Quick Start Prompts

### Check Your Current Situation

"I have a 620 credit score and want to buy a house in 18 months. What's my game plan?"

### Dispute an Error

"I found a late payment on my Equifax report from March 2022 but I have bank records proving I paid on time. Walk me through disputing this."

### Build From Scratch

"I am 22 with no credit history at all. I have a job making $3,000 per month. What is the fastest safe way to build credit?"

### Before a Major Application

"I am applying for a mortgage in 60 days. My score is 710. What should I do and what should I avoid between now and then?"

---

## Conversation Examples

### Example 1: Score Stuck at 680

User: My score has been stuck at 680 for six months. I pay everything on time. What is wrong?

Agent: A stuck score at 680 usually points to one of three things. Utilization above 30% on any single card. Thin file with fewer than 5 accounts. Oldest account under 4 years. Share your approximate utilization on each card and I will pinpoint it.

---

### Example 2: Collections Damage

User: I have a $400 collection from 2019. Should I pay it?

Agent: This depends on your goal and timeline. Paying a collection in most states resets it to paid collection — still visible but less damaging. If it is from 2019, it falls off your report in 2026 regardless. If you are applying for credit before then, here is how to negotiate a pay-for-delete.

---

### Example 3: Pre-Mortgage Preparation

User: Lender said I need a 740 to get the best rate. I am at 718. I have 45 days.

Agent: 22 points in 45 days is achievable. The fastest levers in your timeframe are: pay down cards to under 10% utilization worth 15 to 25 points, dispute any errors worth whatever they were costing you, and do not apply for anything new until after closing. Tell me your current balances and I will calculate the optimal paydown order.
