# Credit Skill — Heartbeat

*Proactive credit health monitoring. Run these checks on schedule.*

---

## Daily — 30 seconds

"Any credit alerts I should know about today?"

Watch for:
- New hard inquiries you did not authorize
- Account balance changes you did not make
- New accounts opened in your name

If something looks wrong, run this immediately:
"I just got an alert that a new account was opened at [bank]. I did not open this. What do I do right now?"

---

## Weekly — 5 minutes

"Review my credit utilization this week. My balances are: [Card A: $X], [Card B: $X]. My limits are: [Card A: $X], [Card B: $X]."

Target: Keep each card below 10% for maximum score impact.

Utilization 1 to 9 percent — Optimal
Utilization 10 to 29 percent — Good
Utilization 30 to 49 percent — Moderate damage
Utilization 50 percent or above — Significant damage

---

## Monthly — 15 minutes

"I am looking at my credit report. Walk me through what to check section by section."

Check in this order:
1. Personal information accuracy
2. Account payment history
3. Balances vs reported limits
4. Hard inquiries — recognize each one
5. Negative items — dates and expected removal

---

## Quarterly — 30 minutes

"It has been 3 months. My score is now [score]. Here is what changed: [events]. Am I on track for my goal of [target score] by [date]?"

---

## Annual — 1 hour

"Let us do my annual credit review. Pull the full checklist."

- Free reports from all 3 bureaus at annualcreditreport.com
- Review all accounts and close unused cards strategically
- Update freeze and lock settings if applicable
- Recalibrate 12-month score goals
- Review credit card benefits vs annual fees

---

## Trigger Events — Use Immediately

Job loss: "I lost my job. How do I protect my credit while managing cash flow?"

Divorce: "I am going through a divorce. Walk me through separating joint credit."

Identity theft: "I think my identity was stolen. What is the exact sequence of steps?"

Medical debt: "I have $8,000 in medical bills going to collections. What are my options?"

Pre-mortgage: "I am applying for a mortgage in [X] days. Give me the full pre-application credit checklist."

---

*Credit is not a set-and-forget system. Small consistent attention compounds into major score gains.*
