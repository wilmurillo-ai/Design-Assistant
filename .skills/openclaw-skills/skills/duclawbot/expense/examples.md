## Conversation Examples

### Example 1: Freelancer Starting Fresh

User: I just went freelance six months ago. I have not been tracking expenses at all. Is it too late to go back and capture what I missed?

Agent: Not too late for most of it. Start with your bank and credit card statements — most banks let you export 12 months of transactions. From those you can reconstruct categories and amounts. What you cannot recover without receipts are cash expenses and detailed business purpose notes, but amounts and vendors from card statements are enough for most deductions. Tell me what kind of freelance work you do and I will tell you exactly what categories to look for in those statements.

---

### Example 2: Employee Reimbursement Confusion

User: I traveled for work last week. I spent $340 on flights, $180 on hotel, $45 on meals, and $22 on an Uber. My company says meals are only reimbursed up to $40 per day. What do I submit?

Agent: Here is how to break this down. Flights $340 — submit in full, typically no limit. Hotel $180 — submit in full, flag if it exceeds your company per-night limit. Meals $45 over one day — you are $5 over the daily limit. Submit $40 and note that the actual cost was $45. Uber $22 — submit in full as ground transportation. Total reimbursable: $582. Want me to format this as a complete expense report ready to submit?

---

### Example 3: Spotting Spending Patterns

User: I feel like I am spending too much but I cannot figure out where it is going. My take-home is $5,500 a month and I never seem to have anything left.

Agent: Let us find the leak. Tell me your fixed monthly costs first — rent or mortgage, car payment, insurance, subscriptions you know about. Then we will look at the variable categories where spending tends to be invisible: food and dining, entertainment, shopping, transportation. Most people who feel this way are surprised by one specific category when they see the actual number. Walk me through last month and I will show you where the money went.

---

### Example 4: Tax Season Reconstruction

User: My accountant is asking for my business expenses for the year. I have some receipts but not all of them. What do I do?

Agent: For expenses with receipts, you are in good shape — log them with amount, date, vendor, and business purpose. For expenses without receipts, bank and credit card statements establish that the expense occurred even without a receipt. For cash expenses under $75, the IRS generally accepts a written record with date, amount, and business purpose even without a receipt. Tell me your revenue for the year and what kind of business you run and I will help you identify the categories most worth reconstructing.
