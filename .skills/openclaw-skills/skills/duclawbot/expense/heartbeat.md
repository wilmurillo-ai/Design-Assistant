# Expense Skill — Heartbeat

*Stay on top of your expenses before they become a problem to reconstruct.*

---

## Daily — 2 minutes

Log expenses while the details are still fresh:

"I need to log today's expenses: [list what you spent, where, and why]"

The same day is always easier than tomorrow. Tomorrow is always easier than next week. Next week still has receipts. Tax season has memories.

What to capture daily:
- Amount and vendor
- Business or personal category
- Purpose if business related
- Receipt image or confirmation number

---

## Weekly — 10 minutes

"Review my expenses from this week. Here is what I spent: [summary]. Am I on track with my budget for the month?"

Weekly check catches:
- Duplicate charges from vendors
- Subscriptions that renewed unexpectedly
- Reimbursable expenses before receipts are lost
- Categories running ahead of budget with three weeks left in the month

---

## Monthly — 30 minutes

End of month is when the numbers become meaningful:

"It is end of month. Walk me through my monthly expense review."

Review in this order:
1. Total spending vs budget by category
2. Business expenses ready for reimbursement or tax records
3. Subscriptions — anything unused this month
4. Recurring charges — any increases from last month
5. Cash spending — anything unaccounted for

For employees:
"I need to submit my expense report for [month]. Here are my expenses: [list]. Format this for submission."

For freelancers:
"Summarize my business expenses for [month] by category for my records."

---

## Quarterly — 1 hour

Strategic expense review:

"It has been three months. Here are my total expenses by category: [summary]. What patterns do you see and where should I adjust?"

Quarterly catches what monthly misses:
- Seasonal spending spikes
- Categories that have crept up gradually
- Annual subscriptions due for renewal soon
- Business expense categories approaching deduction limits

For self-employed:
"I need to make my estimated tax payment. Here are my business expenses this quarter: [summary]. Help me calculate my deductible expenses."

---

## Annual — 2 hours

Full year expense audit:

"Walk me through my annual expense review for tax preparation."

Annual checklist:
- Total business expenses by deduction category
- Home office calculation if applicable
- Vehicle mileage log total
- Receipts organized and backed up
- Subscriptions audit — cancel what you did not use
- Insurance and benefit expenses for deduction
- Professional development and equipment purchases

For employees:
"What unreimbursed work expenses can I deduct on my personal taxes?"

For self-employed:
"Here are my annual business expenses: [categories and totals]. Help me organize these for my accountant."

---

## Trigger Events — Use Immediately

New job: "I am starting a new job. What expenses should I track from day one for potential reimbursement?"

Going freelance: "I just went freelance. What business expenses should I start tracking immediately?"

New client project: "I am starting a project for a client I will bill for expenses. How do I set up project expense tracking?"

Tax audit: "I am being audited. Here are the expense categories in question: [list]. Help me organize my documentation."

Company policy change: "My company changed its expense reimbursement policy. Here are the new rules: [policy]. What changes do I need to make to how I track and submit?"

---

*An expense logged today takes ten seconds. An expense reconstructed at tax time takes ten minutes and may not be recoverable.*
