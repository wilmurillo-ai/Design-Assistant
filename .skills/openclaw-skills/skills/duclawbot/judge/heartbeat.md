# Court Skill — Heartbeat

*Active case monitoring. Run these checks throughout your legal proceedings.*

---

## When You First Receive Court Documents

Run immediately:
"I just received [document name]. The date on it is [date]. My state is [state]. What is my response deadline and what do I need to do first?"

Never wait. Deadlines in court are absolute. A deadline missed by one day has the same consequence as a deadline missed by thirty days.

---

## Weekly — Active Case Check

While a case is ongoing, review every week:

"Here is where my case stands: [current stage]. My next deadline is [date]. My next hearing is [date]. What should I be doing this week to prepare?"

Track these every week:

Filing deadlines — documents due to the court
Service deadlines — documents due to the other party
Discovery deadlines — evidence exchange cutoffs
Hearing dates — never miss, never be late
Response windows — time limits on motions filed against you

---

## Before Every Hearing — 72 Hours Out

"My hearing is in 3 days. It is a [type of hearing] in [court type]. I am [represented by attorney / representing myself]. Walk me through final preparation."

Checklist:
- All documents organized and tabbed
- Extra copies for judge, opposing party, and yourself
- Evidence labeled and ready to present
- Opening statement prepared and practiced
- Anticipated questions from the judge prepared
- Logistics confirmed — location, parking, arrival time
- Appropriate courtroom attire planned

---

## After Every Hearing

"My hearing was today. Here is what happened: [summary]. The judge said [ruling or next steps]. What do I need to do now and by when?"

Document immediately after leaving the courthouse:
- What the judge ordered
- Any deadlines mentioned from the bench
- What the other party said or committed to
- Any documents handed to you by the clerk

---

## Case Stage Triggers

Use these at each major stage of your case:

Filing stage:
"I need to file [document type] in [court]. Walk me through the exact requirements and format."

Discovery stage:
"I received a request for [interrogatories / documents / admissions]. What are my obligations and deadline?"

Negotiation stage:
"The other side has offered [settlement terms]. Help me evaluate whether this is reasonable given my situation."

Post-ruling stage:
"The judge ruled [outcome]. I want to understand my options including whether to appeal."

Enforcement stage:
"I have a judgment in my favor but the other party is not paying. What are my enforcement options?"

---

## Annual — For Ongoing Court Orders

For custody orders, support orders, restraining orders, or probation conditions that run for years:

"It has been [time period] since my [order type] was entered. Here is what has changed in my situation: [changes]. Should I consider a modification and what would that involve?"

---

*In court, what you do not know costs you. Regular check-ins keep you ahead of deadlines and prepared for what comes next.*
