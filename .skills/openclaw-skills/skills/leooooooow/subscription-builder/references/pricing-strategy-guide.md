# Subscription Pricing Strategy Guide

## Core Principles

Subscription pricing balances three objectives: (1) sufficient incentive to convert one-time buyers, (2) margin preservation after operational costs, and (3) avoiding discount dependency that devalues the product. The pricing structure is the single highest-leverage decision in a subscription program.

---

## Discount Depth by Category

| Category | Recommended discount | Rationale |
|----------|---------------------|-----------|
| Supplements / vitamins | 10-15% | High margins (60-75%) support moderate discounts. Daily habit drives retention beyond the discount. |
| Coffee / tea | 8-12% | Moderate margins (45-60%). Freshness and convenience carry significant perceived value. |
| Skincare / beauty | 10-15% | High margins (65-80%). Routine-driven; subscription reinforces consistent usage. |
| Pet food / treats | 8-12% | Moderate margins (35-50%). Amazon Subscribe & Save sets floor expectations. |
| Cleaning / household | 5-10% | Lower margins (30-45%). Convenience is the primary value. |

**Rule of thumb:** Never discount more than 40% of your gross margin per unit. If gross margin is 60%, maximum subscription discount is 24%.

---

## Discount Structures

### Flat discount
Single percentage off one-time price for all subscribers. Simple to communicate, predictable margin impact. Best for first-time program launches.

### Tiered loyalty discount
Discount increases with tenure. Example: 10% for orders 1-3, 13% for orders 4-8, 15% for orders 9+. Creates a "sunk cost" effect discouraging cancellation. Requires platform support for dynamic pricing.

### Volume/bundle discount
Additional discount for subscribing to multiple products. Example: 1 sub = 10%, 2 subs = 13%, 3+ subs = 15%. Increases AOV and creates switching cost. Best for brands with complementary product lines.

### Prepay discount
Deeper discount for upfront payment. Example: monthly = 10%, 3-month prepay = 15%, 6-month prepay = 20%. Eliminates churn for prepaid period and improves cash flow. Only offer for products with strong first-order satisfaction (NPS > 50).

## Price Anchoring Tactics

**Strikethrough pricing:** Display one-time price with strikethrough next to subscription price. Always use this as baseline.
```
$34.99  $30.79/shipment -- Save 12%
```

**Per-unit breakdown:** Show per-capsule, per-pod, or per-serving cost comparison. Effective for high-unit-count products.
```
One-time: $0.58/capsule | Subscribe: $0.51/capsule
```

**Annual savings projection:** Calculate total yearly savings. Use for products reordered monthly or more often.
```
Subscribe and save $50.28 per year
```

**Daily cost framing:** Express subscription as a daily cost. Effective for premium products with high absolute prices.
```
Less than $1.03/day for complete daily nutrition
```

**Cumulative savings in portal:** Show running total of savings in subscriber dashboard to reinforce ongoing value.

## Margin Protection

- **Set a discount ceiling** at 20-22% maximum regardless of loyalty tier or bundle size
- **Model break-even subscriber count** at each discount level before launching
- **Track subscription margin separately** from one-time sales to prevent blended reporting from hiding erosion
- **Factor in shipping economics** -- if free shipping costs $5.50/order and your discount saves $4.20, effective discount is $9.70
- **Review quarterly** using cohort analysis: does the 15% tier retain meaningfully better than 10%? If not, the extra 5% is pure margin loss

---

## Competitive Positioning

For categories where Amazon Subscribe & Save operates, your discount must be competitive with their 5-15% range. But competing on discount alone against Amazon is unsustainable. Differentiate on product quality, customization (flavor swaps, flexible frequency), community, and freshness guarantees.

When raising subscription prices, notify subscribers 30 days in advance. Frame as value maintenance: "To continue delivering the same quality, we are adjusting pricing by $X starting [date]. Your subscriber discount continues to apply."
