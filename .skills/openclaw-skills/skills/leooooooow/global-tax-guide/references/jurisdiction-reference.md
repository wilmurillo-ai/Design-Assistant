# Jurisdiction Reference — Ecommerce Tax Thresholds

Current registration thresholds and key rules for major ecommerce markets. Verify before use — thresholds change; this reference reflects rules as of late 2024.

---

## European Union

### VAT Overview
- **Scheme**: EU VAT (standard), EU OSS (One Stop Shop for cross-border B2C), IOSS (Import OSS for goods under €150)
- **Standard rates**: Germany 19%, France 20%, Netherlands 21%, Spain 21%, Italy 22%, Poland 23%
- **Cross-border threshold**: €10,000 combined annual revenue across all EU B2C sales triggers OSS obligation (replaced per-country thresholds in July 2021)

### EU OSS
- Single registration in one EU member state covers all B2C EU sales
- Quarterly filing
- No per-country registration required once OSS is active
- Registration: Via tax authority of chosen member state (e.g., Germany's BZSt, Ireland Revenue)

### IOSS (Import OSS)
- Applies to imports of goods valued under €150 sold to EU consumers
- Covers customs duty simplification alongside VAT collection
- Required for DTC sellers shipping from outside EU to EU consumers on low-value orders

### Product-Specific EU Rate Variations
| Category | Standard Rate | Reduced Rate Available |
|---|---|---|
| Food and beverages | Standard | Yes — many basic foodstuffs at 0–6% in various countries |
| Children's clothing | Standard (except some countries) | Yes — 0% in Ireland, reduced in some others |
| Books (physical) | Standard | Yes — reduced rate in most EU countries; check by country |
| Medical devices | Standard | Yes — reduced in many EU countries |
| Digital educational content | Standard | Yes — some EU countries apply reduced rates |
| Cosmetics | Standard | Generally no — standard rate applies |

## United Kingdom

### VAT Overview
- **Rate**: 20% standard, 5% reduced, 0% zero-rated
- **Registration threshold**: £85,000 annual UK taxable turnover (non-established sellers must register when they make any UK supplies)
- **Filing**: Quarterly via HMRC Making Tax Digital (MTD) system
- **Registration**: HMRC VAT registration portal

### UK-Specific Post-Brexit Rules
- UK is separate from EU VAT system since January 2021
- UK IOSS equivalent: UK customs uses its own import VAT system (seller registers for UK VAT to account for import VAT on consignments under £135)
- Northern Ireland: Different rules — EU VAT rules apply to goods (not services) due to Windsor Framework

### Zero-Rated UK Categories
- Most food (not hot food, confectionery, alcohol, or crisps)
- Children's clothing and footwear
- Books, newspapers, magazines
- Prescription drugs and many medical supplies
- Domestic energy

## United States

### Sales Tax Overview
- No federal sales tax — each state has its own rules
- Post-Wayfair (2018) economic nexus applies in all 46 taxing states
- **Most common threshold**: $100,000 annual revenue OR 200 transactions in the state
- Marketplace facilitator laws apply in all 46 taxing states — platform-facilitated sales are covered

### State-by-State Threshold Reference (Major States)
| State | Revenue Threshold | Transaction Threshold | MF Law |
|---|---|---|---|
| California | $500,000 | No separate count threshold | Yes |
| Texas | $500,000 | No | Yes |
| New York | $500,000 | 100 transactions | Yes |
| Florida | $100,000 | 200 transactions | Yes |
| Illinois | $100,000 | 200 transactions | Yes |
| Pennsylvania | $100,000 | No | Yes |
| Ohio | $100,000 | 200 transactions | Yes |
| Washington | $100,000 | No | Yes |

**Note**: California's $500,000 threshold is higher than most states. Sellers below $500k in California have no economic nexus there via website sales.

### No Sales Tax States
Delaware, Montana, New Hampshire, Oregon, Alaska (state level — some Alaska municipalities have local taxes).

### Registration Resources
- Sales tax registration: via each state's Department of Revenue website
- Automation: TaxJar, Avalara, or Vertex for multi-state compliance

## Australia

### GST Overview
- **Rate**: 10% flat across most goods and services
- **Threshold**: AUD $75,000 annual revenue from Australian customers (both physical and digital goods)
- **Filing**: Quarterly or monthly BAS (Business Activity Statement) via ATO
- **Registration**: Australian Business Register (ABR) for ABN + ATO for GST registration
- **Marketplace facilitator**: Major platforms (Amazon AU, eBay AU) act as MF; DTC sellers register directly

### Digital Products
- Foreign businesses supplying digital products to Australian consumers must register once they meet the $75,000 threshold
- This includes apps, streaming services, software, and digital downloads

## Singapore

### GST Overview
- **Rate**: 9% (increased from 8% in January 2024)
- **Global turnover threshold for mandatory registration**: SGD $1,000,000 annual global turnover
- **Overseas Vendor Registration (OVR)**: Applies to overseas sellers of digital services to Singapore consumers — SGD $100,000 local Singapore revenue threshold
- **Filing**: Quarterly via IRAS myTax Portal
- **Registration**: IRAS GST registration

### OVR for Digital Services
Foreign businesses supplying digital services (including apps, software, streaming) to Singapore non-GST registered customers must register under OVR once they exceed the SGD $100,000 Singapore revenue threshold.

## New Zealand

### GST Overview
- **Rate**: 15% flat
- **Threshold**: NZD $60,000 annual revenue from New Zealand customers
- **Filing**: Bi-monthly or 6-monthly returns via Inland Revenue
- **Registration**: Inland Revenue NZ GST registration

---

## Canada

### GST/HST Overview
- **Federal GST rate**: 5%
- **HST** (Harmonized Sales Tax, combines federal + provincial): Applies in Ontario (13%), New Brunswick (15%), Newfoundland (15%), Nova Scotia (15%), PEI (15%)
- **Registration threshold**: CAD $30,000 annual worldwide revenue
- **Marketplace facilitator**: Major platforms act as MF in Canada
- **Filing**: Quarterly (most small/medium businesses) via CRA

---

## Japan

### Consumption Tax
- **Rate**: 10% (standard), 8% (reduced rate on food and non-alcoholic beverages)
- **Registration threshold**: JPY 10,000,000 annual taxable sales (approximately USD $65,000) — foreign businesses may have lower effective thresholds for digital services
- **Filing**: Annual or quarterly via NTA (National Tax Agency)
