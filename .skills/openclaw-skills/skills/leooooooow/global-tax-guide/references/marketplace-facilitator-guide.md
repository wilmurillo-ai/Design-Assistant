# Marketplace Facilitator Guide — Platform-by-Platform Reference

This reference documents how major ecommerce platforms handle tax collection as marketplace facilitators. Use it to determine what tax obligations fall on the seller vs. what the platform handles automatically.

**Important**: Platform policies change without announcement. Always verify current MF status directly from the platform's official policy page and record the date of verification.

---

## What Is a Marketplace Facilitator?

A marketplace facilitator (MF) is a platform that:
1. Lists or advertises products on behalf of third-party sellers
2. Collects payment from the buyer
3. Collects, remits, and files the applicable tax (VAT, GST, sales tax) on behalf of the seller

**When the platform is the MF**: The seller does not need to collect or remit tax for those sales. The platform handles it. The seller's obligation is zero for MF-covered transactions.

**When the platform is NOT the MF** (e.g., your own Shopify store): The seller is responsible for collecting and remitting tax based on their own nexus and registration obligations.

## Amazon

### US Sales Tax
- Amazon is registered as a marketplace facilitator in all 46 US taxing states
- Amazon collects and remits sales tax on all FBA and marketplace-fulfilled orders
- Seller responsibility: Zero for marketplace sales. Sellers need their own registration only for direct/off-Amazon sales channels
- Source: Amazon Seller Central > Tax Policies > Marketplace Tax Collection

### EU VAT
- Amazon acts as deemed supplier for non-EU sellers (and EU sellers above certain thresholds) for EU B2C sales through Amazon's EU marketplaces
- Amazon collects and remits VAT on these transactions
- Seller still needs valid VAT numbers in countries where they store inventory (FBA warehouses create tax nexus)
- **Critical exception**: If you store inventory in Amazon warehouses in Germany, France, Italy, Spain, Poland, or Czech Republic, you may need local VAT registration in those countries regardless of MF status for the sales themselves
- Source: Amazon Seller Central > Tax Policies > VAT Services

### UK VAT
- Amazon acts as marketplace facilitator for UK B2C sales on amazon.co.uk
- Amazon collects and remits UK VAT for eligible transactions
- Seller still needs UK VAT registration if storing inventory in UK warehouses
- Source: Amazon Seller Central > UK Tax Policies

### Australia GST
- Amazon AU acts as marketplace facilitator for goods sold by overseas sellers to Australian consumers
- Amazon collects and remits 10% GST
- Source: Amazon AU Seller Central

## TikTok Shop

### US Sales Tax
- TikTok Shop acts as marketplace facilitator for US sales in all applicable states
- TikTok Shop collects and remits sales tax on TikTok Shop-facilitated orders
- Source: TikTok Shop Seller Center > Tax Settings

### UK VAT
- TikTok Shop UK acts as marketplace facilitator for UK VAT purposes
- TikTok Shop collects and remits 20% UK VAT on UK sales through the platform
- Source: TikTok Shop UK Seller Center > VAT Policy

### EU VAT
- TikTok Shop EU marketplaces (where available) collect and remit VAT as marketplace facilitator
- Check country-specific coverage as TikTok Shop EU expansion is ongoing
- Source: TikTok Shop EU Seller Center

### Southeast Asia (ID, MY, TH, SG, VN, PH)
- TikTok Shop handles local marketplace tax collection in applicable SEA markets
- Local rules vary; confirm per-country coverage via TikTok Shop Seller Center in each market

## Shopee

### Southeast Asia
- Shopee acts as marketplace facilitator for VAT/GST collection in Indonesia (PPN 11%), Thailand (VAT 7%), and where local regulations apply
- Singapore: Shopee collects GST on applicable transactions
- Vietnam: Shopee registered with Vietnamese tax authorities and handles applicable VAT
- Philippines: Shopee registered for local VAT and handles collection on marketplace sales
- Source: Shopee Seller Education Hub > Taxes

### Regional Variation
Shopee's MF status differs significantly by country and changes as regulations evolve. Always verify current status in your target market's Seller Education Hub before assuming coverage.

## Lazada

- Seller obligations for marketplace-facilitated sales are covered by Lazada in applicable markets
- Source: Lazada Seller Center > Tax Policies

- eBay collects and remits sales tax on all eBay-facilitated US sales
- Source: eBay Tax Policy > Managed Payments

### EU/UK VAT
- eBay collects and remits VAT as marketplace facilitator for cross-border B2C sales where EU/UK law designates eBay as responsible
- Source: eBay VAT Policy

## Etsy

- Etsy collects and remits sales tax as marketplace facilitator in all applicable US states
- Source: Etsy Legal > Tax Policy

### EU/UK VAT
- Etsy collects and remits VAT for cross-border EU and UK B2C sales
- Source: Etsy VAT Policy

## Shopify (Your Own Store)

**Shopify is NOT a marketplace facilitator.** Shopify provides a platform for you to run your own store, but the seller (you) is responsible for all tax collection and remittance obligations on your own Shopify store sales.

Shopify's built-in tax tools (Shopify Tax, or integrations with TaxJar and Avalara) can automate calculation and help you track nexus, but the legal obligation to collect and remit remains with the seller.

---

## WooCommerce / Own Website

Same as Shopify — no marketplace facilitator status. The seller is fully responsible for all tax obligations on sales through their own website, regardless of the payment processor used.

## How to Verify MF Status

1. Go to the platform's Seller Center or Help Center
2. Search for "marketplace facilitator tax" or "VAT policy" + the specific country
3. Look for explicit language stating the platform "collects and remits" tax on your behalf
4. Record the URL and the date you verified — policies change and documentation matters
5. If the policy is unclear, contact the platform's seller support in writing and save the response
