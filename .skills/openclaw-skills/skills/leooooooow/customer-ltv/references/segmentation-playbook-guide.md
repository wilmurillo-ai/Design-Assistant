# LTV Segmentation Playbook Guide

This guide provides segment-specific tactics, email cadences, offer strategies, and RFM overlay methodologies for each LTV cohort. Use this as your operational reference for building segment-differentiated campaigns.

---

## Champions (Top 1% LTV)

Champions are your most valuable customers, representing the highest lifetime value and profitability. These customers have proven strong repeat behavior, high AOV, and genuine affinity for your brand.

### Customer Profile
- **Segment Size:** 1% of active customer base
- **Avg LTV:** 3–5x category median (e.g., $3,500–$7,000 for luxury fashion, $1,200–$2,500 for vitamins)
- **Repeat Rate:** 50%+ annually; 3+ purchases in last 12 months
- **Recency:** 0–60 days since last purchase (active)
- **AOV:** 2–4x category average

### Core Strategy

Champions require WHITE-GLOVE, EXCLUSIVE, non-discounted treatment. Your goal is brand loyalty, co-creation, and VIP experiences—not transaction volume. Treat them as partners, not customers.

**Retention Focus:** Prevent churn at all costs. Loss of one champion customer equals loss of $3,500–$7,000+ in expected revenue. Invest 5–10x more in retention vs. acquisition.

**Upsell/Expansion:** Premium offerings, limited editions, exclusive previews, subscription/membership acceleration, and co-creation opportunities.

### Messaging Framework

| Channel | Tone | Content Focus | Frequency | Success Metric |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Email | Concierge, insider, exclusive | Early access, VIP events, thought leadership | 2–3x/week | RPE [$$]; Open rate >45% |
| SMS | Personal, direct, time-sensitive | Win-back alerts, exclusive drops (24-hour early), concierge note | 1x/week | Response rate >15% |
| Phone/WhatsApp | High-touch, personalized | Check-ins, preference discovery, custom recommendations | 1–2x/quarter | Engagement score; satisfaction |
| Direct Mail | Luxury, premium | Physical catalogs, personalized gift, handwritten note | 1x/quarter | Retention rate; NPS |

### Email Cadence & Templates

**Weekly Send Schedule:**
- **Monday 10am ET:** "Founder's Letter" or CEO insights — exclusive to Champions (thought leadership, not sales)
- **Wednesday 2pm ET:** "Early Access" — new product drops 48 hours before public (scarcity, exclusivity)
- **Friday 6pm ET:** "Curated for You" — personalized selections based on purchase history (high-touch, low-pressure)
- **Ad-hoc (1–2x/month):** VIP events, exclusive workshops, referral incentives, co-creation invitations

### Offer Strategy

| Offer Type | Discount Depth | Frequency | Incentive Mix |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Standard Promotion | 0% (exclusive access, premium benefit instead) | N/A | Early access, limited edition, exclusive color/size, priority shipping |
| Win-Back (45+ days) | 20–30% off premium items OR exclusive experience | 1–2x/year | High-value benefit: personal styling call, concierge service, VIP event invitation |
| Subscription/Membership | 10–15% off annual plan | 1–2x/year | White-glove service, priority support, exclusive content, community |
| Referral | $[amount] credit per referral | Ongoing | Position as "exclusive insider program"—champions refer other champions |

**Never:** Use generic "20% off everything" broadcasts. Use discounts sparingly and only for win-back scenarios.

### Upsell Sequences

**Trigger 1: Recent High-Value Purchase**
Day 1: Send "Bundle Completion" email with complementary premium products
- Example: Customer bought skincare. Email suggests premium beauty tools or treatment add-ons.
- Offer: None (full price); position as "perfect match for [PRODUCT]"

Day 7: Follow-up SMS with personal note from [Founder/CEO]
- Example: "Hi [NAME], saw your order—wanted to reach out personally about [NEXT CATEGORY]"
- Offer: 15% off if they respond positively

**Trigger 2: Anniversary or Birthday**
Day 0: Email with exclusive gift or discount + personalization angle
- Example: "It's been [X years] since we met. Thank you. Here's something special."
- Offer: Special gift with purchase, exclusive color variant, or anniversary discount

Day 14: SMS check-in
- Tone: Caring, not transactional

**Trigger 3: Product Affinity Pattern**
If customer has purchased from Category A only (e.g., skincare), and Category B is highly relevant (beauty tools):
- Email: "Given your love of [CATEGORY A], you might be interested in [CATEGORY B]. See how [FOUNDER/BRAND] uses both together: [VIDEO]"
- Offer: None initially; second email 10 days later with 15% off if no click

### Suppression Rules
- **NEVER suppress Champions from any channel** based on inactivity
- Suppress only if hard bounce, spam complaint, or explicit unsubscribe
- Monitor churn risk score weekly; flag any champion with score >70 for immediate win-back

## VIP (Top 10% LTV)

VIPs are your second-tier high-value customers. They demonstrate strong repeat behavior, premium purchase patterns, and engagement. While not champions, they're highly profitable and retention-focused.

### Customer Profile
- **Segment Size:** Next 9% of customers (cumulative top 10%)
- **Avg LTV:** 1.5–3x category median
- **Repeat Rate:** 35%+ annually; 2–3 purchases in last 12 months
- **Recency:** 0–90 days since last purchase (active or warm)
- **AOV:** 1.5–2.5x category average

### Core Strategy

VIPs get ESCALATION FLOWS, EXCLUSIVE ACCESS, and SUBSCRIPTION/MEMBERSHIP CONVERSION focus. They're on the cusp of champion status, and your goal is to move them there.

**Retention Focus:** Maintain engagement; prevent downgrade to High-Value. Loss of one VIP = $2,000–$5,000 revenue impact.

**Upsell/Expansion:** Subscription conversion (with loyalty discounts), tiered product bundles, category expansion, exclusive community access.

### Messaging Framework

| Channel | Tone | Content Focus | Frequency | Success Metric |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Email | Exclusive, prestigious, escalation | VIP deals, exclusive collections, tiered loyalty | 2–3x/week | RPE [$$]; Open rate >35% |
| SMS | Direct, exclusive, timely | New launches, exclusive discounts, VIP events | 2x/week | Click rate >8% |
| Push Notification | Promotional, time-sensitive | Flash sales, exclusive drops | 1–2x/week | Click rate >5% |
| In-App/Web | Exclusive messaging | Tier status, exclusive products, loyalty progress | N/A | Engagement rate |

### Email Cadence & Templates

**Weekly Send Schedule:**
- **Monday 10am ET:** VIP exclusive collection or product launch (non-member content)
- **Wednesday 2pm ET:** "VIP Only" deal (15–20% off selected premium items or new arrivals)
- **Friday 6pm ET:** Community/lifestyle content or subscriber incentive message (education + soft sell)
- **Ad-hoc (2–3x/month):** VIP events, subscription conversion incentive, loyalty tier status updates

## High-Value (Top 20% LTV)

High-Value customers are your consistent purchasers with solid repeat behavior. They're profitable and represent potential for upward mobility to VIP/Champion status.

### Customer Profile
- **Segment Size:** Next 10% of customers (cumulative top 20%)
- **Avg LTV:** 1–1.5x category median
- **Repeat Rate:** 25%+ annually; 1–2 purchases in last 12 months
- **Recency:** 0–90 days since last purchase (active to warm)
- **AOV:** 1–1.5x category average

### Core Strategy

High-Value customers get FREQUENCY ACCELERATION, BUNDLE OFFERS, and LOYALTY ENROLLMENT focus. Your goal is to increase purchase frequency and bundle size, moving them toward VIP status.

**Retention Focus:** Prevent downgrade to Mid-Tier. Identify frequency decline early (2–3 month gap instead of 1-month pattern).

**Upsell/Expansion:** Bundle discounts ("Buy 2 get 15% off"), loyalty program enrollment with bonus points, category expansion, subscription trials.

### Messaging Framework

| Channel | Tone | Content Focus | Frequency | Success Metric |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Email | Friendly, value-focused, promotional | Weekly deals, bundles, loyalty benefits | 2–3x/week | RPE [$$]; Click rate >8% |
| SMS | Direct, promotional, time-bound | Flash sales, bundle offers, loyalty updates | 1x/week | Conversion rate >2% |
| Push | Promotional, timely | Product launches, exclusive deals | 1–2x/week | Click rate >3% |

### Email Cadence & Templates

**Weekly Send Schedule:**
- **Tuesday 10am ET:** Weekly deal or bundle offer (rotating categories)
- **Thursday 3pm ET:** Featured product or bundle (promote category expansion)
- **Saturday 9am ET:** Weekend flash sale or loyalty bonus points offer
- **Ad-hoc:** Loyalty tier status updates, birthday/anniversary offers, category-specific promotions

| Win-Back (90+ days) | 25% off + free shipping | 1–2x/year | Personal tone; reference past purchases |

**Trigger 1: Frequency Stalling (Customer's typical gap = 30 days, now at 45 days)**

- Retain on email at standard frequency (2–3x/week) if active or within 90 days

## Mid-Tier (Mid 60% LTV)

Mid-Tier customers represent your core volume base. While individually lower LTV, in aggregate they're a significant revenue source. Focus on frequency acceleration and preventing downgrade to Low-Value.

### Customer Profile
- **Segment Size:** Middle 60% of customers
- **Avg LTV:** 0.5–1x category median
- **Repeat Rate:** 15–30% annually; 1 purchase in last 12 months average
- **Recency:** 0–120 days since last purchase (mostly active)
- **AOV:** 0.8–1.2x category average

### Core Strategy

Mid-Tier customers get SECOND-PURCHASE CONVERSION and CROSS-SELL focus. Your goal is to move them to High-Value by increasing frequency and introducing complementary categories.

**Retention Focus:** Prevent downgrade to Low-Value through consistent offers and engagement.

**Acquisition Focus:** For one-time customers in this cohort, drive second purchase through incentives and social proof.

## Low-Value (Bottom 20% LTV)

Low-Value customers represent marginal economics. Individually, they don't justify high-touch retention. Strategy: Suppress aggressively to protect margin, or include only in minimal-cost campaigns (organic/owned channels).

### Customer Profile
- **Segment Size:** Bottom 20% of customers
- **Avg LTV:** <0.5x category median
- **Repeat Rate:** <15% annually; likely 1-time or rare repeat
- **Recency:** Variable (many cold)
- **AOV:** 0.5–0.9x category average

### Core Strategy

Low-Value customers get SUPPRESSED or MINIMAL-TOUCH treatment. Do NOT allocate marketing spend (email, SMS, paid social) to this segment except in specific scenarios.

**Margin Focus:** Reduce communication cost by suppressing from paid channels. Better to avoid cost than chase unprofitable revenue.

**Exception:** Annual re-engagement (monce per year) or organic/owned channels (social, search) only.

### Messaging Framework

| Channel | Strategy | Content | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Email | Suppress after 90+ days inactive | N/A | None |
| SMS | Suppress entirely | N/A | None |
| Paid Social | Exclude from retargeting | N/A | None |
| Organic/Owned | Include only if free | Annual clearance or post-acquisition window | 0-1x/year |

### Offer Strategy

| Scenario | Discount Depth | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Annual Re-engagement (if testing) | 30–40% off | "We're back with new stuff—20% off everything" |
| Organic Promotion (free channel) | 25–30% off | Include in seasonal/holiday broadcast if organic |
| Suppress (Recommended) | N/A | Do not mail; let them self-serve |

### Suppression Rules (CRITICAL)

| Condition | Action |
|---|---|
| Low-Value + 90+ days inactive | Move to "Suppressed—Low-Value" list; exclude from all sends |
| Low-Value + Email bounce | Remove immediately |
| Low-Value + Spam complaint | Remove immediately |
| Annual re-engagement (optional) | Send single email 1x/year if testing; suppress if no response |
| Organic/Owned channels |  OK to include (no cost) |
| Paid channels (email, SMS, social) | Never; suppress entirely |

### Success Metrics (Primarily Cost Control, Not Revenue)

| Metric | Target | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Email volume (% of total sends) | <10% | Email Manager |
| SMS sends to segment | 0 | Email Manager |
| Paid social spend on segment | $0 | Paid Social Manager |
| List decay (removal rate) | >20% annually | Data Quality |

## Lapsed Low-Value (180+ Days Inactive, Previously Bottom 20%)

These are customers who were already low-value and are now dormant. Margin math does NOT support win-back; suppress entirely or test single low-cost re-engagement.

### Customer Profile
- **Previous LTV:** <bottom 20% threshold
- **Inactivity:** 180+ days since last purchase
- **Strategy:** Suppress or ignore

### Strategy

**Option 1: Suppress (Recommended)**
- Create "Suppressed—Lapsed Low-Value" list
- Exclude from all channels (email, SMS, push, paid social)
- Maintain in database for historical reference
- Annual exception: Once per year, include in single holiday/clearance broadcast

**Option 2: Single Touch (If Testing)**
- Day 1 (Email only): "Come back with us—20% off everything"
  - Soft, non-aggressive tone
  - Offer: 20–25% off
  - CTA: "Explore"
- Post-Email (Day 14): If no response, suppress permanently

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Target | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Email volume (% of total base) | <5% | Email Manager |
| Campaign cost | Minimal (organic only) | Marketing Ops |
| Conversion rate | <1% (not expected to convert) | Analytics |

## RFM Overlay Methodology

Apply RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) scoring on top of LTV segmentation to identify at-risk or high-opportunity customers within each tier.

### RFM Score Calculation

**Recency (0–100 points):**
- 0–30 days since last purchase: 100 points (Active)
- 31–60 days: 75 points (Warm)
- 61–90 days: 50 points (Cool)
- 91–180 days: 25 points (Cold)
- 180+ days: 0 points (Lapsed)

**Frequency (0–100 points):**
- 5+ purchases in 12 months: 100 points
- 3–4 purchases: 75 points
- 2 purchases: 50 points
- 1 purchase: 25 points
- 0 purchases: 0 points

**Monetary (0–100 points):**
- Total 12-month spend >[90th percentile]: 100 points
- [70th–90th percentile]: 75 points
- [50th–70th percentile]: 50 points
- [30th–50th percentile]: 25 points
- <[30th percentile]: 0 points

**Composite Churn Risk Score:**
```
Risk Score = 100 - ((Recency × 0.5) + (Frequency × 0.3) + (Monetary × 0.2))
```

**Interpretation:**
- 0–30: Very Low Risk (protect, upsell)
- 31–50: Low Risk (maintain, monitor frequency)
- 51–70: Moderate Risk (escalate engagement, win-back early)
- 71–85: High Risk *aggressive win-back, premium offer)
- 86–100: Critical Risk (phone outreach if high LTV, else suppress)

### Action Triggers by Segment & RFM Score

| Segment | Risk 0–30 | Risk 31–50 | Risk 51–70 | Risk 71–85 | Risk 86–100 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Champions** | Upsell, VIP events | Maintain frequency | Email + SMS check-in | Phone outreach + premium offer | CEO/founder personal call |
| **VIP** | Upsell, exclusive access | Maintain frequency | Email + SMS engagement | Email + SMS + phone offer | Aggressive win-back, high incentive |
| **High-Value** | Bundle upsell, loyalty | Monitor frequency | Email + SMS re-engagement | Email + SMS with 25% off | Single email 25% off; suppress if no response |
| **Mid-Tier** | Cross-sell, frequency | Monitor; light email | Email re-engagement | Email with 30% off | Suppress; annual re-engagement only |
| **Low-Value** | Skip | Skip | Skip | Suppress | Suppress entirely |
