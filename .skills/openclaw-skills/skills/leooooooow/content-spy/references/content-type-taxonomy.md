# Content Type Taxonomy — Ecommerce Social Content

Use this standard taxonomy when tracking competitor content to ensure consistency across analyses. Each post should be classified into one primary type and may have one secondary tag.

---

## Primary Content Types

### 1. Product Demonstration
**Definition:** Content that shows the product being used, applied, or performing its function. Focus is on the product's behavior or results.
**Subtypes:**
- Unboxing/first impression
- Application demo (how to use)
- Before/after result demonstration
- Feature showcase
- Comparison (vs. another product)

**TikTok signals:** Usually medium view count; reliable engagement from buyers already aware of category. Rarely goes viral organically.

---

### 2. Educational / Informational
**Definition:** Content that teaches the viewer something useful, with the product as context (not the focus). Value is in the information, not the product itself.
**Subtypes:**
- Ingredient explanation
- How-to tutorial (using product as part of broader routine/process)
- FAQ answer
- Category education ("what is X?")
- Myth debunking

**TikTok signals:** Highest save rate of any content type. Often generates follows. Good for top-of-funnel awareness and building authority in category.

---

### 3. Social Proof / Testimonial
**Definition:** Content featuring customer experiences, reviews, or results. Brand may appear minimally; customer is the focus.
**Subtypes:**
- Customer video testimonial
- Review reading/response
- UGC reposts (with permission)
- Transformation/results story
- Star rating showcase

**TikTok signals:** Very high engagement, especially for before/after. Comment rate is high. Often generates highest conversion rates of any content type when well-executed.

---

### 4. Promotional / Commercial
**Definition:** Content with a primary intent to drive immediate purchase. Includes discount announcements, limited-time offers, and direct product pushes with price.
**Subtypes:**
- Sale announcement
- Limited time offer
- Bundle deal
- Flash sale countdown
- New product launch

**TikTok signals:** Lower organic reach in algorithm; often boosted with paid spend. High click-through when the offer is strong. Overuse trains audience to expect discounts.

---

### 5. Trend Participation
**Definition:** Content built around a current TikTok audio, meme format, or viral template, with the product incorporated. The trend is the format; the product is the content.
**Subtypes:**
- Trending audio + product
- POV format
- "Tell me you're a [X] without telling me" format
- Challenge participation
- Stitch/duet with viral content

**TikTok signals:** Highly unpredictable. Can achieve 10–100× normal reach when trend timing is right. Short shelf life — trends saturate within 1–2 weeks.

---

### 6. Behind the Scenes / Brand Story
**Definition:** Content that shows the human side of the brand — founders, team, production, warehouse, office. Builds trust and differentiation through authenticity.
**Subtypes:**
- Day in the life (founder/team)
- Production/sourcing process
- Brand origin story
- Values/mission content
- Team introduction

**TikTok signals:** Polarizing — performs very well for brands with a strong founder story or unusual origin; performs poorly for generic brand content. Typically lower commercial intent.

---

### 7. Creator / Influencer Content
**Definition:** Content produced by external creators or influencers featuring the product. May be seeded, gifted, or paid. Creator's own audience and style is the primary context.
**Subtypes:**
- Creator product review
- Creator routine/integration
- Gifted content (unsponsored look)
- Paid partnership content
- Ambassador long-form integration

**TikTok signals:** Performance varies widely by creator fit. Best performing when creator's authentic style matches brand values. "Feels like ad" is the primary risk.

---

### 8. Interactive / Community
**Definition:** Content designed to generate comments, responses, or participation. May use polls, questions, debates, or invitations to share.
**Subtypes:**
- Question to audience ("Which do you prefer?")
- Debate/this-or-that
- Request for advice/opinions
- Comment response video (stitching own comments)
- "Tag someone who needs this"

**TikTok signals:** Generates high comment volume which boosts algorithm distribution. Best for engagement rate; not always the strongest for purchase conversion.

---

## Secondary Tags

Apply one secondary tag per post if applicable:

| Tag | Definition |
|---|---|
| `#seasonal` | Content tied to a specific season, holiday, or cultural moment |
| `#paid-promo` | Marked as paid partnership or boosted with paid spend |
| `#ugc-style` | Brand-created content designed to look like organic UGC |
| `#series` | Part of a multi-part series (episode 1, 2, 3...) |
| `#response` | Response to a comment, question, or viral moment |
| `#collab` | Co-created content with another brand or creator |
| `#product-launch` | Tied to a new product introduction |
| `#evergreen` | Content with long relevance window (not tied to a moment or trend) |

## Engagement Rate Benchmarks (TikTok)

Use to calibrate "high / medium / low" engagement assessments:

| Follower Range | Strong Engagement Rate | Average | Weak |
|---|---|---|---|
| < 10k | > 10% | 5–10% | < 5% |
| 10k–100k | > 5% | 2–5% | < 2% |
| 100k–500k | > 3% | 1–3% | < 1% |
| 500k+ | > 1.5% | 0.5–1.5% | < 0.5% |

*Engagement rate = (likes + comments + shares) / views × 100*

Note — View count is not the same as follower count. A video can have 500k views from a 20k follower account if it gets algorithmic distribution.
