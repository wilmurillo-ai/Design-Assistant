# Objection Handlers for E-Commerce Demo Scripts

When prospects watch your demo, objections form in their minds. The best demos address these hesitations *before* they become deal-killers. This guide provides natural, conversational responses to 25+ common objections—insert them into your demo script where they logically fit rather than as a separate FAQ section.

---

## Price & Cost Objections

### Objection: "That's expensive / More than I want to spend"

**When to address:** Right after showing the core value proposition
**Script approach:** Lead with total cost of ownership, then ROI
**Sample response:**
```
"I hear you on budget. Here's how I think about it: 
This costs $X per month. But let's talk about what you're currently spending.

You're paying for [manual labor / spreadsheets / support tickets / lost sales]. 
That's costing you $Y annually.

Our customers see ROI in 3-4 months through reduced overhead and recovered sales. 
After that, it's pure savings. So the question isn't the cost—it's whether you can 
afford to wait another year doing this the hard way."
```

### Objection: "We need to check with our budget owner"

**When to address:** During closing conversation
**Script approach:** Make it easy to say yes to the conversation
**Sample response:**
```
"That makes total sense. Here's what I'd suggest: 
Let me send you a one-page ROI breakdown that shows exactly how we pay for ourselves.

When you share it with your budget owner, they'll have concrete numbers. 
Then we can all hop on a quick call—it's usually 20 minutes—and answer any questions. 

Does next Thursday work for you?"
```

### Objection: "We found a cheaper competitor"

**When to address:** When directly asked, not preemptively
**Script approach:** Shift from price to total value
**Sample response:**
```
"Great that you're comparing options. Price is one factor, but it's usually not the one 
that makes the difference in the first year. 

Let me ask you: Is their implementation as fast? Do they have the same integrations 
you need? What's their support like? Can they scale with you?

Most 'cheaper' options end up costing more when you factor in implementation, 
training, and what you lose by not having [key feature you have that they don't].

How about we set up a side-by-side comparison? I'll show you exactly where we win 
and where you'd need to make trade-offs with the other option."
```

## Product Capability Objections

### Objection: "Does it work with [specific platform/tool they use]?"

**When to address:** During the feature demo that touches on integrations
**Script approach:** Confident, specific integration list
**Sample response:**
```
"Yes, absolutely. We integrate with [list 5-7 specific tools they likely use].

[SHOW: Integration logos or settings screen]

If you use something we don't natively integrate with, we have a full API and 
Zapier connection, so we can connect to almost anything.

What systems do you currently use? I can tell you exactly how the integration works."
```
