# TikTok Video Analyzer

Analyze a TikTok video at a lightweight, practical level using the information available: title, screenshots, visible structure, metrics, comments, and optional captions or transcript.

Use this skill when a user wants a fast, useful breakdown of why a TikTok video may be working, what can be learned from it, and which parts are worth adapting for ecommerce, creator content, or short-form strategy.

## Problem it solves
People often see a strong TikTok video but cannot explain why it works. They can describe the vibe, but not the structure, hooks, visual pulls, or commerce lessons. This skill provides a lightweight analysis without pretending to do full video-model understanding when only partial inputs are available.

## What this skill is for
This is a lightweight analyzer, not a full video intelligence engine.
It works best when the user can provide some combination of:
- Video title
- Screenshots / key frames
- View, like, comment, and engagement data
- Comments
- Visible hooks or on-screen text
- Optional captions / transcript

It is especially useful for:
- “Why did this video work?”
- “What can I copy from this structure?”
- “What parts are worth applying to ecommerce or TikTok Shop?”
- “How do I turn this video into a social post or content brief?”

## Use when
- A user provides a TikTok link plus screenshots/data
- A team wants a quick teardown of a winning or losing video
- The goal is learning, adaptation, repurposing, or content strategy
- The user wants to extract content lessons from a video case

## Do not use when
- The user expects full frame-by-frame, full-transcript, or deep cinematic analysis without supporting inputs
- The task requires real-time scraping or guaranteed live data retrieval
- The user needs complete video-level analysis across pacing, timing, audio, and every scene in depth

## Inputs to ask for
- Video title or description
- Key screenshots / frames
- Metrics: views, likes, engagement, duration, account size
- Comments or sentiment samples
- Optional transcript / captions
- The user’s goal: learn why it worked, adapt it, repurpose it, critique it, etc.

## Workflow
1. Clarify what evidence is available and what is missing
2. Identify the likely attention drivers: visual novelty, hook, pace, structure, proof, curiosity, emotional pull
3. Separate strong signals from weaker guesses
4. Explain why the video may be performing and what should not be over-attributed
5. Translate insights into practical lessons for content strategy, ecommerce, or creator execution
6. Optionally convert the analysis into post ideas, talking points, or content briefs

## Output requirements
Return:
1. Quick verdict on why the video may be working
2. Top performance drivers
3. Elements worth copying
4. Elements not worth blindly copying
5. Ecommerce / TikTok Shop relevance, if applicable
6. Optional repurposing ideas: post, thread, creator brief, hook list, etc.
7. Confidence notes when analysis is based on partial evidence

## Quality standards
- Be honest about evidence quality and uncertainty
- Do not pretend to have full video understanding when inputs are partial
- Focus on practical takeaways, not vague admiration
- Make the output useful for creators, operators, and ecommerce teams
- Distinguish between likely drivers and speculation

## Example prompts
- Why did this TikTok likely perform so well?
- Here are screenshots and stats — tell me what this video is doing right
- Analyze this TikTok lightly, then tell me what an ecommerce brand should learn from it
