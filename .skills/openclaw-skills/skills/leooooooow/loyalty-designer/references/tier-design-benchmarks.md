# Loyalty Tier Design Benchmarks

Industry benchmarks and design standards for tiered loyalty programs across ecommerce categories.

---

## Tier Naming Conventions by Brand Positioning

| Brand Positioning | Tier 1 (Entry) | Tier 2 (Mid) | Tier 3 (Top) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mass market / accessible | Member | Silver | Gold |
| Premium consumer | Classic | Premium | Elite |
| Luxury | Member | Preferred | Platinum |
| Community / lifestyle | Explorer | Adventurer | Pioneer |
| Beauty / wellness | Glow | Radiance | Luminous |

Rule: Tier names should escalate in perceived value and fit the brand's vocabulary. Never use "Bronze/Silver/Gold" for a luxury brand — it cheapens the program.

---

## Tier Distribution Benchmarks

Healthy tier distribution across enrolled members:

| Tier | Target % of Enrolled Members | If Too High | If Too Low |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 (entry) | 55-75% | Expected | Re-check enrollment friction |
| Tier 2 (mid) | 20-35% | Lower threshold | Raise threshold or improve comms |
| Tier 3 (top) | 5-15% | Lower threshold significantly | Acceptable — aspirational |

**Warning signal:** If more than 40% of members reach Tier 2 automatically on first purchase, the tier is too easy and loses aspirational value.

## Tier Threshold Benchmarks by Category

| Category | Avg AOV | Typical Tier 2 Threshold | Typical Tier 3 Threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| Beauty / skincare | $45-75 | $150-250/year | $400-600/year |
| Apparel / fashion | $65-120 | $250-400/year | $600-1,000/year |
| Pet supplies | $55-90 | $200-300/year | $500-800/year |
| Home goods | $80-150 | $300-500/year | $800-1,500/year |
| Food / supplements | $40-70 | $150-200/year | $300-500/year |
| Electronics accessories | $35-90 | $150-300/year | $400-700/year |

Note: Thresholds should be set based on YOUR data, not these benchmarks. These are starting reference points. If you have purchase frequency data, use: Tier 2 = median annual spend of top 30% of buyers.

## Points Multiplier Benchmarks

| Tier | Common Points Multiplier | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | 1× | Standard earn rate |
| Tier 2 | 1.5× or 2× | Most common mid-tier multiplier |
| Tier 3 | 2× or 3× | High-tier multiplier; 3× requires strong margin |

Points multipliers are the most common benefit because they're self-limiting (points still need to be redeemed). A 2× multiplier costs ~2× the baseline earn rate for that tier's purchase volume.

## Tier Benefit Benchmarks

### Tier 2 Common Benefits
- 1.5-2× points multiplier
- Free shipping on orders over reduced threshold (e.g., $35 instead of $50)
- Birthday gift or reward (small product or discount)
- Early sale access (24-48 hours before general public)
- Priority customer service email/chat

### Tier 3 Common Benefits
- 2-3× points multiplier
- Free shipping always (no minimum)
- Annual free product gift
- Dedicated support line
- Pre-launch / exclusive product access
- Invitations to brand events (in-person or virtual)
- Personalized recommendations from brand team

### Non-Discount Benefits Performance
Research consistently shows that customers value experience and access benefits almost as highly as discount benefits, but at much lower cost to the brand:

| Benefit | Perceived Customer Value (1-10) | Typical Merchant Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Free shipping (always) | 8.5 | Low-Medium (variable by order) |
| Early access to sale | 7.5 | Near zero |
| Birthday gift | 7.0 | Low (COGS only) |
| Dedicated support | 7.0 | Low-Medium (staff time) |
| 10% discount | 8.0 | High (direct margin) |
| Pre-launch access | 7.5 | Near zero |
| Personal stylist / advisor | 8.0 | Medium-High (staff time) |

Design for at least 2 non-discount benefits per tier to reduce margin dependency.

## Tier Downgrade Policy Benchmarks

Most programs use rolling 12-month qualifying periods:

- Qualifying period: Rolling 12 months from date of tier achievement
- Downgrade notice: 30-60 days before period end
- Grace period: Some programs offer 30-90 days to re-qualify
- Soft landing: Common to downgrade one level at a time (Gold → Silver, not Gold → Member in one step)

Customer communication at downgrade is critical. The message should:
1. Acknowledge the customer's history
2. Show what they need to do to re-achieve tier (specific spend amount)
3. Highlight what Silver/entry tier benefits they'll still have
4. Avoid making the customer feel punished
