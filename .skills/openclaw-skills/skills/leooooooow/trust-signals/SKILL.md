# Trust Signals

Audit product pages for missing or weak trust signals including reviews, guarantees, certifications, and social proof that affect purchase confidence. This skill helps ecommerce operators find the specific credibility gaps that make first-time visitors bounce or abandon carts even when the product itself is competitive on price and features.

## Use when

- You notice a Shopify or DTC product page is getting traffic and add-to-cart events but a disproportionately low checkout completion rate and you suspect buyer hesitation rather than pricing or product-market fit
- A new product launch is underperforming older hero SKUs in the same catalog and the main difference appears to be the absence of accumulated reviews, press mentions, or category badges on the newer listing
- You are preparing a paid traffic campaign (Meta, TikTok, Google) and want to pre-audit the landing page for trust signal coverage before pushing cold traffic that has no existing brand familiarity
- You are expanding into a new geographic market or marketplace and need to re-evaluate which trust signals translate (star ratings, return policies, payment logos) versus which are country-specific (local trust seals, certifications, regulator badges)

## What this skill does

This skill performs a structured audit of a product page or product detail page against a checklist of trust signal categories — review volume and recency, rating distribution, third-party certifications, return and shipping guarantees, payment method badges, security indicators, press and media mentions, user-generated photo or video content, expert endorsements, and comparison claims with substantiation. It identifies which signals are present but weak (e.g., five reviews from 2022 on a premium-priced item), which are missing entirely, and which are present but poorly placed relative to the decision moments on the page. It outputs a prioritized remediation plan ranked by the probable conversion lift and the effort required to implement each change.

## Inputs required

- **Product page URL or content** (required): The live URL, a screenshot set covering above-the-fold through checkout CTA, or a pasted dump of the page copy and structure including review section, policy links, and badges.
- **Product context** (required): Price point, category, average competitor price in the same category, target customer (first-time buyer versus repeat, impulse versus considered purchase), and the traffic source the page typically serves.
- **Current performance** (optional): Conversion rate, bounce rate, time on page, and scroll depth metrics if available. Including these lets the audit prioritize signals likely to move the specific weakness the page has.
- **Brand assets available** (optional): Existing press mentions, certifications held, review platform data exports, UGC library, or partnerships that could be surfaced but currently are not visible on the page.

## Output format

The output has four sections. Section one, Trust Signal Inventory, lists every category of trust signal with a present/weak/missing rating and a one-line evidence note. Section two, Critical Gaps, highlights the three to five missing or weak signals most likely to be costing conversions for this specific price point, category, and traffic source, with the reasoning for each. Section three, Remediation Plan, gives a prioritized action list with expected impact (high/medium/low), implementation effort (quick fix versus project), and any third-party tools or assets needed. Section four, Placement Map, recommends where on the page each signal should appear — hero zone, above the add-to-cart button, near price, near shipping copy, or in the FAQ — matched to the moment of hesitation it addresses.

## Scope

- Designed for: ecommerce operators, DTC brand teams, conversion rate optimization specialists, Shopify and TikTok Shop sellers
- Platform context: Shopify, WooCommerce, TikTok Shop, Amazon brand stores, custom ecommerce sites, platform-agnostic
- Language: English

## Limitations

- Works from the page content and context you provide; does not crawl live pages, fetch real-time review data, or query analytics platforms directly
- Cannot validate the legal or regulatory accuracy of specific trust claims (medical, financial, safety certifications) — those require appropriate professional review before publication
- Conversion impact estimates are directional based on category benchmarks and common patterns; actual lift must be validated through A/B testing on the specific audience and traffic mix
