# Trend Type Glossary — TikTok and Short-Form Video Formats

Reference guide for common TikTok trend format types, their mechanics, and ecommerce adaptation notes.

---

## Audio Trends

### Trending Audio — Viral Song
**What it is:** A commercial or independent song that gains sudden velocity on TikTok due to one creator's viral use of it. Other creators adopt the same audio to participate in the trend.
**How it works:** The trend is usually tied to a specific visual behavior that emerged with the original viral use — creators mimic both the audio and the visual format.
**Ecommerce adaptation:** Use the audio to frame a product reveal, transformation, or day-in-the-life moment. The key is matching the audio's emotional tone with the visual message.
**Shelf life:** 5–14 days from first major viral post.
**Discovery signals:** Appears multiple times in 15-minute FYP session; rising rapidly in TikTok Creative Center trending sounds.

---

### Trending Audio — Sound Bite
**What it is:** A spoken audio clip (often from a movie, TV show, interview, or another creator) that becomes a meme format. Creators lip-sync or act out the audio in their own context.
**How it works:** The audio has a memorable line or setup/punchline structure. Creators substitute their own visual context for the "punchline" moment.
**Ecommerce adaptation:** Substitute the product as the "character" in the audio's narrative. Best when the audio's content aligns with the product's benefit (e.g., an audio about "finally finding the right thing" works for product discovery content).
**Shelf life:** 3–10 days; sound bite trends saturate faster than song trends.
**Risk:** Sound bite trends sometimes originate from copyrighted or sensitive source material — check origin before using.

## Format Trends

### POV Format
**What it is:** "POV — you [scenario]" — content from the viewer's perspective, placing them in a specific relatable or aspirational situation.
**Structure:** Text overlay "POV — you [did something]..." + video showing the downstream consequence or experience.
**Ecommerce adaptation:** "POV — you finally found a [product category] that actually works" → show the product experience. Strong for before/after or lifestyle upgrade content.
**Best for:** Products with clear lifestyle upgrade value. Works across most categories.

---

### Get Ready With Me (GRWM)
**What it is:** Creator prepares for something (morning routine, going out, workout) while talking to camera or with music and voiceover. Products appear as part of the natural routine.
**Structure:** 30–90 seconds of routine steps; products placed in context of each step.
**Ecommerce adaptation:** Position your product as the most important step in the routine. Creator-style narration, not promotional script. The ritual frames the product.
**Best for:** Skincare, makeup, supplements, wellness products, clothing/fashion, fitness accessories.

---

### Transformation / Before-After
**What it is:** Content that shows a before state and an after state, with the product or behavior change as the explanation for the transformation. Uses a visual transition at the midpoint.
**Structure:** 3–5 second "before" → transition effect → 3–5 second "after"
**Ecommerce adaptation:** Before = problem or without-product state; After = result or with-product state. The transition moment is the product reveal.
**Best for:** Products with visible results — skincare, fitness, home organization, before/after cleaning, weight loss, hair care.

---

### This-or-That / Either-Or
**What it is:** Creator shows two options and makes a choice, inviting audience to pick their preference in comments.
**Structure:** "Option A vs. Option B" with quick visual comparisons; creator states preference.
**Ecommerce adaptation:** "Which [product variant/color/bundle] are you?" or "This [product] or that [product]?" Drives comment engagement, which boosts algorithmic distribution.
**Best for:** Brands with multiple product variants, colors, or bundles. High comment rate = good for reach; lower conversion intent.

---

### Day In My Life
**What it is:** Creator shows segments of their day with quick cuts and text overlays. Products appear naturally integrated into daily routines.
**Structure:** 30–90 seconds, 6–12 scene cuts, text overlays labeling each moment.
**Ecommerce adaptation:** Feature product in 2–3 scenes where it would naturally appear. Avoid making the entire video about the product — the lifestyle is the content; the product is the integration point.
**Best for:** Lifestyle products, supplements, skincare, clothing, tech accessories, home goods.

---

### Stitch / Response Format
**What it is:** Creator responds to or builds on another creator's video, adding their own perspective. The original video plays for 2–5 seconds, then the creator appears to respond.
**Structure:** "Original video clip" → creator response "Well actually..." or "Adding to this..."
**Ecommerce adaptation:** Find viral content in your category making a claim your product challenges or supports. Stitch with a product-backed response. Works well for "I tried [competitor approach] and..." content.
**Best for:** Brands with a clear point of view or contrarian angle vs. conventional category wisdom.

---

### "Tell Me Without Telling Me" Format
**What it is:** "Tell me you're a [type of person] without telling me you're a [type of person]" — creator demonstrates the trait through behavior rather than stating it.
**Structure:** Text overlay sets up "Tell me you're a [X person] without telling me" → video shows the behavior that signals it.
**Ecommerce adaptation:** "Tell me you care about your skin without telling me you care about your skin" → show product use or ritual. Positions the product as a signal of the buyer's identity/values.
**Best for:** Products tied to identity or lifestyle aspirations. Works across beauty, fitness, wellness, premium/quality positioning.

---

### Educational Hook ("Did You Know?")
**What it is:** Content that opens with a surprising fact, then delivers educational context. Keeps viewers watching because they don't want to miss the explanation.
**Structure:** "Did you know..." or "Nobody talks about this..." → 15–30 second explanation → product as the solution or application.
**Ecommerce adaptation:** Open with a surprising fact about the product's category, ingredient, or use case. Deliver genuine value. Product appears as the natural application of the insight.
**Best for:** Skincare/beauty (ingredients), supplements (nutrition science), electronics (tech specs), sustainability-focused brands.

---

### Duet
**What it is:** Creator appears side-by-side with another creator's video, reacting or responding in real time.
**Structure:** Original video plays on one side; creator appears on other side responding.
**Ecommerce adaptation:** Duet with a satisfied customer's organic review, or with a viral video about a problem your product solves.
**Best for:** Brands with strong user-generated content or customer testimonials to amplify.

---

## Seasonal / Cultural Trend Formats

### "Things I'm Adding to My [Season/Year] Routine"
**Period:** Peaks at seasonal transitions (Jan, April, September)
**Ecommerce adaptation:** Position product as an essential seasonal routine addition.

### "What's in My Bag / What I Carry"
**Period:** Perennial but peaks back-to-school (August) and holiday gifting
**Ecommerce adaptation:** Feature compact products naturally.

### "The [Year] Version of Me Doesn't..."
**Period:** January and post-summer
**Ecommerce adaptation:** Position product as part of a personal upgrade or new chapter.

## Format Lifespan Guide

| Format Type | Average Lifespan | Saturation Speed | Evergreen Potential |
|---|---|---|---|
| Trending audio + visual | 5–10 days | Fast | Low |
| Sound bite meme | 3–7 days | Very fast | Very low |
| POV format | 2–4 weeks | Slow | High |
| GRWM | 6+ months | Very slow | Very high |
| Before/after transformation | 12+ months | Very slow | Very high |
| This-or-that | 3+ months | Slow | High |
| Educational hook | 12+ months | Very slow | Very high |
| Day in my life | 6+ months | Slow | High |

Use short-lifespan audio trends for reach spikes; use long-lifespan format trends as the backbone of your evergreen content strategy.
