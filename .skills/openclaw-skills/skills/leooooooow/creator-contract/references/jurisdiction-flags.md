# Jurisdiction Flags

What changes by region. The contract draft can flag these but cannot localize automatically — surface the items below in a "Localize before signing" callout.

## United States

- **FTC Endorsement Guides (16 CFR Part 255)**: clear and conspicuous disclosure required. #ad in the first 3 caption lines is the safer minimum; #sponsored or #partner is acceptable; "thanks for the gift" alone is not.
- **California AB 5 / Borello test**: Creator may be classified as employed not contractor in some setups. Single-deal collaborations are typically safe; ongoing exclusive arrangements may not be.
- **Tax**: 1099-NEC if payments exceed $600/year. W-9 collection at signing.

## United Kingdom

- **ASA / CAP**: paid-for content must be disclosed via Instagram's "Paid partnership" tool or #ad at start of caption. ASA is aggressive on enforcement.
- **HMRC**: VAT may apply if Creator is VAT-registered.
- **IR35**: relevant for ongoing engagements; less so for single-deal collaborations.

## European Union

- **AGCM (Italy), DGCCRF (France), Bundeskartellamt (Germany)**: vary in detail but converge on requirement for clear paid disclosure.
- **VAT**: B2B reverse charge if Creator is VAT-registered in another EU state.
- **GDPR**: data shared in the contract (real names, addresses) is personal data; minimize sharing.

## Brazil

- **CONAR**: self-regulatory; clear disclosure required (#publi or #publicidade).
- **Tax**: withholding may apply for foreign brands paying Brazilian creators.

## Southeast Asia (TikTok-heavy)

- **Singapore**: ASAS guidelines, similar to ASA.
- **Indonesia (Shopee, TikTok Shop)**: BPOM rules for cosmetics, supplements; halal certification claims must be backed.
- **Philippines / Vietnam**: nascent regulation; brand reputational risk drives stricter self-policing.

## Australia

- **AANA / ACCC**: paid disclosure required; "ad" or "advertisement" is preferred over emoji-only disclosures.
- **Tax**: ABN required for the Creator, withholding if no ABN.

---

## Disclosure Phrasing — Acceptable vs Risky

| Phrasing | Verdict |
|----------|---------|
| "#ad" in first 3 lines + spoken disclosure | Strong everywhere |
| "Paid partnership with @brand" via platform tool | Strong (Instagram, TikTok) |
| "#sponsored" | Acceptable in most regions |
| "#thanks @brand for the gift" only | Risky — implies no compensation expectation |
| Buried disclosure (line 8 of caption) | Risky — fails the "clear and conspicuous" test |
| Emoji-only (e.g., 💸) | Risky / not accepted in UK, AU, EU |

## Withholding Tax Reminder

When a US brand pays a non-US creator, request W-8BEN to claim treaty benefits and reduce withholding (typically 30% default, often 0–10% under treaty).
