# Usage Rights Glossary

Plain-English definitions for the rights vocabulary that shows up in every creator contract. Use these to write the Plain-English summary lines in the contract draft.

## Organic Rights

The Brand can post the Creator's content on Brand-owned channels (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, website, email) as a regular non-promoted post. Standard for almost every deal. Almost always granted in perpetuity for Brand-owned channels with Creator credit.

## Paid Rights

The Brand can run the content as a paid ad. Where the ad runs and for how long is the negotiation. Three sub-types:

- **Brand-handle paid**: ad runs from Brand's own ad account / handle
- **Whitelisted paid**: ad runs from Creator's handle (looks like organic from the audience's view) via Spark Ads (TikTok), Branded Content tool (Instagram), or YouTube partnership ads
- **Programmatic / off-platform paid**: ad runs on Google, Meta Audience Network, programmatic display

Rule of thumb: paid rights cost 30–100% on top of the equivalent organic-only fee, depending on duration.

## Whitelisting

Specific to paid rights. The Creator gives the Brand limited access to their account so the Brand can run paid ads from the Creator's handle. Requires:

- Spark Ads access code (TikTok) or partnership invite (Instagram Branded Content)
- Duration of access (must match paid-rights window)
- Reporting access for the Brand to monitor performance

Whitelisting amplifies paid-rights value because audience trust is higher when the ad appears to come from a real creator.

## Repurposing Rights

Use of the content outside its original platform: website hero, email creative, retail signage, packaging, podcast clip, etc. Brand should always ask for repurposing rights for owned channels; pricier creators may carve out specific use cases.

## Perpetual Rights

No end date. The Brand can use the content forever. Standard for organic on Brand-owned channels; expensive (and resisted) for paid use. If the Brand wants perpetual paid, it should pay an uplift (often 50–100% of the flat fee).

## Exclusivity vs Usage Rights

Easy to confuse:

- **Usage rights** = what the Brand can do with this specific content
- **Exclusivity** = what the Creator cannot do with their channel during a window (post for competitors)

A deal can be exclusive without granting paid rights, or vice versa.

## Term-of-Use Vocabulary Cheat Sheet

| Phrase | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| "Worldwide, perpetual, royalty-free" | Brand can use the content anywhere, forever, with no further payment |
| "Non-exclusive" | Brand has rights, but Creator can also use the content |
| "Sublicensable" | Brand can grant the rights to a third party (e.g., agency, retail partner) |
| "First-look window" | Creator must offer Brand the next campaign before competitors for [N] days |
| "Right of first refusal" | Brand can match a competing offer to retain the Creator |
| "Buyout" | One-time payment for full rights, usually perpetual and unlimited |
