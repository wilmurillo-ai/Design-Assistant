# TikTok Growth

Help ecommerce teams, TikTok Shop operators, and creators decide what to post on TikTok to improve the odds of generating traction, clicks, and sales.

Use this skill when a user wants practical TikTok content guidance tied to commerce outcomes rather than vague “growth hacks.” This skill is best for product-led short-form planning, creator content direction, TikTok Shop content systems, and weekly content planning for brands or affiliates.

## Problem it solves
Many teams know they “should post more on TikTok” but do not know what kinds of videos are actually worth making. They jump between trends, hooks, and formats without a coherent content system. This skill turns product, audience, and account context into content pillars, video angle ideas, and testing priorities.

## What this skill is for
This is not a generic TikTok growth encyclopedia.
It is a TikTok Shop and ecommerce-oriented content strategy skill that helps answer questions like:
- What should we post this week?
- Which content angles are more likely to drive sales?
- What kinds of videos should this product or creator test first?
- How should we balance reach content vs. conversion content?

## Use when
- A brand or creator asks what to post on TikTok
- A TikTok Shop team needs content direction tied to sales outcomes
- The user wants a weekly posting plan, testing matrix, or content pillars
- A product has potential but the team lacks clear content angles
- A creator account feels random and needs structure

## Do not use when
- The task is real-time trend scraping or news monitoring
- The user wants automated TikTok posting or browser automation
- The user needs deep analytics from a specific video rather than content planning
- The user needs fully written scripts only, without strategy context

## Inputs to ask for
- Product, category, and price point
- Target audience and buyer awareness level
- TikTok Shop status or creator/account type
- Current content situation: what has worked, failed, or felt repetitive
- Main goal: reach, engagement, clicks, conversions, creator recruiting, etc.
- Offer, differentiators, and objections
- Available assets: demos, testimonials, creators, reviews, product visuals

## Workflow
1. Clarify the product, buyer, account type, and business goal
2. Identify the most likely purchase triggers, objections, and curiosity hooks
3. Build 3-5 content pillars tied to actual commerce outcomes
4. Suggest video types and recurring formats for each pillar
5. Separate top-of-funnel reach ideas from conversion-oriented ideas
6. Rank testing priorities so the user knows what to make first
7. Convert strategy into a short weekly or batch content plan

## Output requirements
Return:
1. 3-5 content pillars
2. Best content angles by pillar
3. Suggested TikTok video types or series formats
4. Hook directions to test
5. Reach vs. conversion balance recommendation
6. Weekly post plan or priority matrix
7. Optional watchouts: what not to overdo or test too early

## Quality standards
- Advice must feel specific to TikTok Shop / ecommerce, not generic social media advice
- Recommendations should connect content to likely business outcomes
- Output should be practical enough for a creator or operator to use immediately
- Avoid fake certainty, generic trend talk, or overpromising “viral” results
- Keep language concrete, creator-native, and commercially useful

## Example prompts
- What should we post on TikTok Shop for this product?
- Give me a TikTok content plan that is more likely to drive sales
- Our TikTok content feels random. Build a better content system
