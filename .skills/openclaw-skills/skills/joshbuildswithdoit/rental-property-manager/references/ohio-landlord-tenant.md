# Ohio Landlord-Tenant Law Quick Reference

## Key Statutes
- **Ohio Revised Code Chapter 5321** — Landlord-Tenant Act
- **ORC 5321.01-19** — Definitions, obligations, remedies

## Notice Requirements
- **Month-to-month termination:** 30 days written notice
- **Non-payment of rent:** 3-day notice to vacate (ORC 1923.04)
- **Lease violation:** 30-day notice to cure or vacate
- **Entry by landlord:** 24 hours reasonable notice (except emergency)

## Security Deposits
- No statutory cap on amount (but market standard is 1 month)
- Must return within 30 days of move-out
- Itemized deduction list required if withholding any portion
- Interest not required unless local ordinance (Cleveland requires interest on deposits held 6+ months)

## Eviction Process (Ohio)
1. Serve 3-day notice (non-payment) or 30-day notice (other)
2. File complaint in municipal court
3. Court hearing (typically 7-14 days after filing)
4. If judgment for landlord: writ of restitution issued
5. Set-out by bailiff (typically 5-10 days after writ)
- **Total timeline:** 3-6 weeks minimum

## Habitability Standards
- Working plumbing, heating, electrical
- Structural integrity
- Pest control
- Smoke detectors on every level
- CO detectors near sleeping areas (as of 2016)
- Working locks on exterior doors

## Fair Housing (Federal + Ohio)
Protected classes: race, color, religion, sex, national origin, familial status, disability
- Ohio adds: military status, ancestry
- Cleveland adds: sexual orientation, gender identity, source of income

## Tax Considerations
- Schedule E reporting for rental income
- Deductible: mortgage interest, property taxes, insurance, repairs, depreciation, management fees
- Passive activity loss rules apply (max $25K deduction if AGI < $100K, phases out by $150K)
- 1031 exchange available for property-to-property trades
