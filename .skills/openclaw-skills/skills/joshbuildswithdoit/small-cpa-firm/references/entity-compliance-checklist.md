# Entity Compliance Checklist — Annual Requirements by Type

## LLC (Operating / Single-Member)
- [ ] Annual meeting minutes (recommended even if single-member — maintains corporate veil)
- [ ] Operating agreement reviewed and current
- [ ] Registered agent active and current
- [ ] State annual report filed (if required by state — Ohio eliminated for LLCs in 2024)
- [ ] BOI report filed with FinCEN (initial + updates within 30 days of changes)
- [ ] EIN letter on file
- [ ] Bank account reconciled and separate from personal
- [ ] Tax return filed (Schedule C for SMLLC, 1065 for multi-member)

## LLC (S-Corp Election — Form 2553)
All LLC items above, PLUS:
- [ ] Reasonable compensation paid to owner-employees (W-2 issued)
- [ ] Payroll taxes filed and deposited on schedule
- [ ] Form 1120-S filed by March 15 (or extension)
- [ ] K-1s issued to shareholders by March 15
- [ ] Shareholder basis worksheet updated
- [ ] Health insurance premiums reported on W-2 (if applicable)
- [ ] Retirement plan contributions within limits (employee + employer)

## S-Corporation (Standalone)
All S-Corp election items above, PLUS:
- [ ] Board of directors meeting minutes (at least annual)
- [ ] Shareholder meeting minutes (at least annual)
- [ ] Stock ledger current
- [ ] Bylaws reviewed
- [ ] State biennial report filed (Ohio corporations)
- [ ] Franchise tax paid (if applicable by state)

## C-Corporation
All S-Corp standalone items above, PLUS:
- [ ] Form 1120 filed by April 15 (calendar year) or 15th of 4th month after FYE
- [ ] Estimated tax payments (quarterly if >$500 expected tax)
- [ ] Accumulated earnings review ($250K threshold for service corps, $150K for personal holding)
- [ ] Transfer pricing documentation (if related-party transactions)

## Partnership (1065)
- [ ] Partnership agreement reviewed and current
- [ ] Capital account reconciliation
- [ ] Form 1065 filed by March 15 (or extension)
- [ ] K-1s issued to all partners by March 15
- [ ] Partner basis worksheets updated
- [ ] Guaranteed payment documentation
- [ ] Self-employment tax calculations for general partners
- [ ] BOI report filed

## Trust (1041)
- [ ] Trust document reviewed for required distributions
- [ ] Trustee meeting documentation (for complex trusts)
- [ ] Form 1041 filed by April 15 (or extension to September 30)
- [ ] K-1s issued to beneficiaries
- [ ] DNI (Distributable Net Income) calculated
- [ ] Asset titling verification (all assets properly titled to trust)
- [ ] Successor trustee designation current
- [ ] State trust tax filing (if required)

## 501(c)(3) Nonprofit
- [ ] Form 990 / 990-EZ / 990-N filed by May 15 (or extension to November 15)
- [ ] Board meeting minutes (minimum quarterly recommended)
- [ ] Conflict of interest policy reviewed and signed annually
- [ ] Compensation reasonableness review (Form 990 Part VII)
- [ ] Public support test calculation (1/3 support test)
- [ ] Donation acknowledgment letters sent (for gifts >$250)
- [ ] State charitable registration renewed (varies by state)
- [ ] Unrelated Business Income (UBI) reviewed — Form 990-T if applicable
- [ ] Document retention policy current
- [ ] Whistleblower policy on file

## KKOS Trifecta Structure (Operating + Holding + Management)

### Operating LLC
- [ ] All standard LLC items
- [ ] Management fee paid to Management LLC (documented, at arm's length)
- [ ] Lease payments to Holding LLC (if applicable, at fair market rate)

### Holding LLC
- [ ] All standard LLC items
- [ ] Asset titling verified (real estate, equipment, IP held here)
- [ ] Lease agreements with Operating LLC current and at market rate
- [ ] Insurance on held assets current
- [ ] No direct operations conducted (holding only)

### Management LLC (S-Corp elected)
- [ ] All S-Corp items
- [ ] Management agreements with Operating LLCs documented
- [ ] Management fees at arm's length (benchmarked to market rates)
- [ ] Owner W-2 reasonable compensation reviewed
- [ ] Retirement plan contributions maximized
- [ ] Health insurance properly structured (S-Corp rules)

### Annual Trifecta Review
- [ ] All inter-entity transactions documented at arm's length
- [ ] No commingling of funds across entities
- [ ] Each entity has separate bank accounts, books, and records
- [ ] Minutes for each entity reference the others appropriately
- [ ] Asset protection review — are all valuable assets in Holding LLC?
- [ ] Income optimization — is Management LLC capturing enough margin for retirement/benefit planning?
