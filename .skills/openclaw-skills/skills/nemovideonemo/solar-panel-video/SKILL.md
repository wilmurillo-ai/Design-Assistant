# Solar Panel Video — AI Video Production for Solar Installation & Renewable Energy Companies

The solar industry is experiencing unprecedented growth driven by falling panel costs, generous federal tax credits (30% ITC through 2032), rising electricity prices, and increasing consumer awareness of climate change. Yet most solar installation companies — especially local and regional installers — rely on outdated marketing: door-to-door sales, static flyers, and generic stock photo websites. Video marketing is the single most effective tool for solar lead generation, but professional video production has been prohibitively expensive for the average solar contractor. Solar Panel Video changes this entirely by enabling AI-powered video creation tailored specifically to the solar industry's unique needs, terminology, compliance requirements, and customer journey.

## 1. Industry Context

### Market Size & Growth
- The U.S. residential solar market exceeded **$30 billion** in 2025, with over 700,000 residential installations completed annually (SEIA/Wood Mackenzie).
- The commercial & industrial (C&I) solar segment adds another **$12 billion**, with warehouse rooftops, retail centers, and office buildings driving demand.
- Community solar is the fastest-growing segment, projected to reach **$10 billion** by 2028 as states expand virtual net metering policies.
- Battery energy storage systems (BESS) attached to solar installations grew **90% year-over-year** in 2025, driven by Tesla Powerwall, Enphase IQ Battery, and Franklin WH.
- Global solar market is projected to reach **$370 billion by 2030** (Grand View Research).

### Why Video Matters for Solar
1. **High-ticket purchase**: A residential solar system costs $15,000-$35,000 before incentives. Buyers need extensive education and trust-building before committing. Video delivers both.
2. **Complex product**: Most homeowners don't understand net metering, inverters, panel degradation, or financing structures. Animated explainer videos simplify these concepts dramatically.
3. **Visual product**: Solar installations are inherently visual — panels on roofs, drone shots of arrays, before/after bill comparisons. This is a product made for video.
4. **Local competition**: In most metro areas, 20-50+ solar companies compete for the same homeowners. Video differentiates — companies with video content generate **3x more leads** than those without (Wyzowl 2025).
5. **Long sales cycle**: The average solar purchase takes 3-6 months from first interest to signed contract. Video nurture sequences keep prospects engaged throughout.
6. **Trust deficit**: The solar industry has suffered from aggressive door-to-door sales tactics. Professional video content builds credibility and counters negative perceptions.
7. **Platform algorithm advantage**: YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram all prioritize video content. Solar companies with active video channels dominate local search results.

### Key Industry Trends Driving Video Demand
- **Virtual sales consultations** became standard post-COVID and require high-quality video proposals
- **Satellite-based roof assessment** tools (Aurora Solar, OpenSolar) generate data that can be visualized in AI videos
- **State-level incentive changes** create urgent need for frequently updated explainer content
- **Solar + storage + EV** bundles require more complex educational content
- **Workforce shortage** means companies need training videos for new installers

## 4. Script & Content Guidelines

### Solar-Specific Writing Rules

**DO:**
- Lead with savings, not environmentalism (for residential) — homeowners buy solar to save money first, save the planet second
- Use specific dollar amounts: "The average Phoenix homeowner saves $1,847 per year" not "save thousands"
- Mention the 30% Federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) — this is the #1 motivator for 2024-2032
- Include local utility rates: "With APS rates increasing 5.8% this year..."
- Show the math: system cost → tax credit → financing payment → monthly savings → payback period
- Use "energy independence" and "protection from rate increases" as emotional hooks
- Include a clear CTA: "Get your free solar assessment" / "See how much you'd save"

**DON'T:**
- Never guarantee specific savings without disclaimers — savings vary by roof, shade, usage
- Don't bash utilities directly (can backfire and create regulatory issues)
- Don't use overly technical jargon without explanation (kWh, DC/AC ratio, azimuth)
- Don't promise specific payback periods without qualifying statements
- Don't show competitor logos or make comparison claims without documentation
- Avoid "too good to be true" claims like "free solar panels" or "zero cost solar"

### Script Structure (60-90 sec Explainer Template)
1. **Hook (0-5 sec):** Start with the pain point — "Tired of $300+ electricity bills?"
2. **Problem (5-15 sec):** Utility rates increasing, unreliable grid, missed tax credit deadline approaching
3. **Solution (15-35 sec):** How solar works in simple terms — panels → inverter → your home → grid credits
4. **Proof (35-50 sec):** Customer story, savings data, number of installations completed
5. **Incentives (50-65 sec):** Federal ITC, state credits, utility rebates — make it specific and urgent
6. **CTA (65-75 sec):** Clear next step — "Get your free roof assessment in 2 minutes"
7. **Closer (75-90 sec):** Company branding, trust signals (licenses, reviews, BBB rating)

### Tone by Audience
- **Homeowners:** Warm, reassuring, jargon-free, focus on savings and simplicity
- **Commercial clients:** Professional, ROI-focused, data-driven, mention depreciation benefits (MACRS)
- **Community solar subscribers:** Inclusive, accessible, "no roof required" messaging
- **Real estate agents:** Property value increase angle ($4/watt installed adds to home value per Zillow)

## 6. Compliance & Safety

### Federal & State Regulations
- **FTC Truth in Advertising:** All savings claims must be substantiated and qualified. "Results may vary based on roof orientation, shading, electricity usage, and utility rates."
- **State contractor licensing:** Video must not imply services in states where the company is not licensed. Check CSLB (California), DPOR (Virginia), etc.
- **ITC claims:** Always specify "consult your tax advisor" when discussing the 30% federal tax credit. The credit applies to the tax liability, not as a direct rebate.
- **Financing disclosures:** If mentioning loan terms, must include APR, term length, and "subject to credit approval" per TILA (Truth in Lending Act).
- **Net metering disclaimers:** NEM policies vary by state and utility. NEM 3.0 in California significantly changed the value proposition — always specify the applicable policy.

### Industry-Specific Compliance
- **Solar panel warranties:** Don't make warranty claims that exceed manufacturer specifications (typically 25-year performance warranty, 12-25 year product warranty)
- **Energy production estimates:** Must be based on credible modeling (PVWatts, Aurora Solar, Helioscope). Don't exaggerate production.
- **Battery backup claims:** Clarify that battery backup provides limited power during outages — not whole-home backup unless specifically sized for it.
- **Grid independence claims:** Very few residential systems are truly "off-grid." Don't imply grid independence unless the system is designed for it.
- **HOA and permitting:** Don't guarantee HOA approval. Reference the Solar Rights Act in applicable states (California, Arizona, etc.) but note that architectural guidelines may apply.

### Safety Content Requirements
- **Installation safety:** All installation footage should show proper PPE (hard hats, harnesses, safety glasses) and OSHA-compliant practices.
- **Electrical safety:** Never show DIY solar installation or encourage homeowners to work on electrical systems.
- **Roof safety:** Emphasize professional installation. Falls are the #1 cause of injury in solar installation.
- **Fire safety:** Address the rapid shutdown requirement (NEC 2017/2020) and how modern systems comply.
- **Environmental claims:** "Carbon neutral" or "zero carbon" claims must be accurate and substantiated per FTC Green Guides.

### Data Privacy
- **Customer testimonials:** Obtain written release forms before using customer names, addresses, or footage in videos.
- **Satellite imagery:** If using satellite images of specific homes, ensure compliance with local privacy laws.
- **Lead capture:** Video landing pages must include privacy policy links and comply with CCPA/state privacy laws.
- **Energy usage data:** If referencing customer utility data in personalized videos, handle in compliance with utility data privacy rules.

### Platform-Specific Ad Policies
- **Facebook/Meta:** Solar ads may require special category targeting in some regions. Avoid "before/after" framing that implies guaranteed results.
- **Google Ads:** Solar ads must comply with Google's financial services policies if promoting financing. Landing pages must include company contact info and privacy policy.
- **YouTube:** Ensure all claims are substantiated. YouTube may restrict ads with misleading environmental claims.
- **TikTok:** Follow community guidelines on commercial content. Disclose sponsored/partnership content appropriately.
