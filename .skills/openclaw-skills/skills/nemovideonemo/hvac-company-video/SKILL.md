# HVAC Company Video — AI Video Production for Heating & Cooling Lead Generation

Create a continuous stream of marketing videos for HVAC businesses. Generate seasonal campaign ads, emergency service creatives, equipment showcases, energy efficiency explainers, and local search video assets — built for the HVAC company that needs to fill shoulder-season gaps, not produce a documentary.

## 1. Industry Context

HVAC is one of the most seasonally volatile home service businesses. A company can be overwhelmed with emergency calls in July and nearly idle in October. This feast-or-famine cycle makes consistent marketing both critical and uniquely difficult — the time you most need to advertise (slow season) is when cash flow is tightest, and the time you can most afford it (peak season) is when you are too busy to think about marketing.

Video solves two problems simultaneously. First, it dramatically outperforms static content in local search and social media — Google Business Profile listings with video receive significantly more engagement, Facebook video ads consistently deliver lower cost-per-lead than image ads for home services, and Nextdoor posts with video generate more comments and recommendations. Second, video is the most effective format for building the trust that HVAC sales demand: replacing a central air system costs $8,000-$25,000, and homeowners making that decision need to trust the company before they will even schedule an estimate.

The production challenge is acute for HVAC companies. Their most knowledgeable and camera-credible people — senior technicians and the owner — are also the highest-revenue generators. Pulling a lead tech off a $450 diagnostic call for a half-day video shoot is a direct revenue hit of $1,500 or more in lost billable time. The result: most HVAC companies either have no video or have one corporate overview from four years ago that mentions equipment they no longer install.

Modern HVAC marketing demands 10-20 fresh video creatives per month: seasonal offers rotate quarterly, ad platforms punish stale creative with rising costs, and each service category (AC repair, furnace installation, duct cleaning, heat pump, indoor air quality) needs its own targeted content. At traditional production rates of $800-$2,000 per video, the content budget would dwarf the ad spend. AI video production makes the math work: the same company can produce 15-20 videos per month at a fraction of traditional costs, test multiple angles, and replace underperforming creatives weekly.

## 2. Video Categories and Specifications

### 2.1 Emergency Service Response Ads
- **Purpose**: Google Ads search campaigns, Local Services Ads, Google Business Profile
- **Duration**: 10-20 seconds
- **Structure**: Open with the emergency ("AC stopped working in 108-degree heat?"), show rapid professional response, close with phone number and response time guarantee
- **Key rule**: Emergency content must convey speed and availability above all else. "Same-day service" or "2-hour response window" — specific commitments beat vague promises
- **Tone**: Calm, competent, urgent without being alarmist. "We're already on the way."
- **Seasonal split**: AC emergency ads run April-September, heating emergency ads run October-March, rotate at the equinoxes
- **Technical**: 16:9 for Google Search/Display, 9:16 vertical for mobile-first and social placements

### 2.2 Seasonal Campaign Videos
- **Purpose**: Paid social media, email campaigns, website seasonal landing pages
- **Duration**: 15-45 seconds
- **Seasonal calendar**:
  - **Q1 (Jan-Mar)**: Heating system health checks, "is your furnace ready for the last cold snap?", early-bird AC tune-up pre-season booking discounts, heat pump efficiency education
  - **Q2 (Apr-Jun)**: AC tune-up season launch, "beat the summer rush — schedule now", allergy season indoor air quality promotions, smart thermostat upgrade offers
  - **Q3 (Jul-Sep)**: Peak cooling — emergency repair visibility, "your AC is working hardest right now", early furnace inspection booking, back-to-school IAQ for families
  - **Q4 (Oct-Dec)**: Heating season prep, furnace and boiler tune-ups, holiday hosting comfort ("20 guests for Thanksgiving — is your system ready?"), end-of-year equipment replacement tax incentive education
- **Structure**: Season-specific urgency hook, clear offer with price or discount, limited-time framing, single CTA
- **Compliance**: Include "prices vary by system type and location" disclaimer when showing prices

### 2.3 Equipment Installation Showcases
- **Purpose**: Website service pages, YouTube channel, showroom displays, sales consultation support
- **Duration**: 60-120 seconds
- **Structure**: Identify the homeowner problem (old system, high bills, uneven temperatures), introduce the solution (specific equipment category — not brand names unless authorized dealer), walk through the installation process timeline, show the result (comfort, efficiency, quiet operation), close with financing options and scheduling CTA
- **Topics**: Central AC replacement, furnace installation, heat pump conversion, ductless mini-split systems, whole-home air purification, smart thermostat integration, zoning system installation, duct replacement and sealing
- **Key rule**: Focus on outcomes the homeowner cares about — comfort, monthly cost reduction, noise levels, warranty protection — not technical specifications. Save SEER ratings and BTU calculations for the estimate appointment.

### 2.4 Energy Efficiency Education Content
- **Purpose**: Organic social media, YouTube SEO, email nurture sequences, community workshops
- **Duration**: 60-180 seconds
- **Topics**: Understanding SEER and HSPF ratings in plain language, how to read your utility bill and spot HVAC waste, rebate and tax credit programs (federal 25C/25D credits, utility rebates, state programs), heat pump vs furnace comparison for different climates, smart thermostat programming tips that actually save money, when to repair vs replace (the 50% rule, age thresholds, refrigerant phase-outs), duct leakage and its impact on bills, insulation and HVAC efficiency connection
- **Tone**: Teacher, not salesperson. Build authority by educating first. The CTA is "schedule a free energy assessment" — a consultation, not a sale.
- **SEO strategy**: Title each video for the question homeowners actually search: "Is a heat pump worth it in [State]?" / "How much does a new AC cost in 2026?"

### 2.5 Maintenance Agreement Enrollment Videos
- **Purpose**: Post-service email sequence, website membership page, annual renewal campaigns
- **Duration**: 30-60 seconds
- **Structure**: Frame the problem (unexpected breakdowns, peak-season pricing, voided warranties), present the maintenance agreement as the solution (what is included: bi-annual tune-ups, priority scheduling, discount on repairs, extended equipment life), show the cost comparison (one emergency call vs annual agreement cost), close with enrollment CTA
- **Key insight**: Maintenance agreements are the HVAC industry's most important recurring revenue stream. A company with 500 maintenance agreements has predictable revenue through every shoulder season. These videos are business-critical, not optional.
- **Tone**: Straightforward value proposition. No hard sell — the math sells itself.

### 2.6 Social Media Organic Content
- **Purpose**: Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube Shorts — organic reach and brand building
- **Duration**: 15-60 seconds
- **Content types**:
  - Day-in-the-life: "5 AM emergency call, 110 degrees in the attic — here is what HVAC techs actually do"
  - Satisfying transformations: Filthy filter vs new filter, rusty heat exchanger close-up, ductwork before-and-after
  - Myth busters: "Closing vents in unused rooms does NOT save energy — here is why it actually hurts your system"
  - Tool and tech showcases: Thermal camera diagnostics, refrigerant recovery, combustion analysis
  - "What is this?" series: Show an unusual part or failure and let the audience guess
- **Tone**: Authentic, slightly raw. Overly produced content underperforms on social — it registers as advertising and gets scrolled past.
- **Format**: 9:16 vertical, captions burned in (85% of social video is watched on mute), hook in first 2 seconds

### 2.7 Indoor Air Quality Awareness Videos
- **Purpose**: Dedicated IAQ service pages, allergy season campaigns, post-COVID health-conscious audience targeting
- **Duration**: 30-90 seconds
- **Topics**: What is actually in your home's air (dust mites, pet dander, VOCs, mold spores), how your HVAC system affects air quality, MERV ratings explained simply, UV purification and bipolar ionization options, humidity control and its impact on health and home, whole-home air purifier vs portable unit comparison, duct cleaning — when it matters and when it is a waste
- **Positioning**: IAQ is HVAC's fastest-growing service category. These videos create demand for a service most homeowners do not know they need.

### 2.8 Recruitment and Culture Videos
- **Purpose**: Indeed, ZipRecruiter, trade school partnerships, company career page
- **Duration**: 60-120 seconds
- **Structure**: Show the work environment (clean, organized trucks and warehouse, modern diagnostic equipment), highlight benefits (pay range, insurance, retirement, tool allowance, training and certification support, company vehicle), feature team members, end with application CTA
- **Context**: The HVAC industry faces a critical labor shortage — tens of thousands of unfilled technician positions nationally, with demand growing as systems become more complex (heat pumps, smart controls, refrigerant transitions). Companies that recruit with video attract significantly more qualified applicants.

## 3. Production Workflow

### Step 1: Company Intake
Collect from the HVAC company:
- Company name, year established, owner name, number of technicians and trucks
- Service area with specific cities, neighborhoods, and zip codes
- Complete service list: residential AC, residential heating, commercial HVAC, duct work, IAQ, new construction
- Equipment brands carried (for authorized dealer content) and brands serviced
- Key differentiators: response time guarantees, financing options, warranty programs, maintenance agreement details
- Licensing and certifications: state contractor license, EPA 608 certification, NATE certification, manufacturer certifications
- Brand assets: logo (vector preferred), brand colors (hex codes), truck photos, team photos, showroom photos
- Google Business Profile URL, review count and rating
- Current marketing spend breakdown (search, social, direct mail, referral)
- Active promotions, seasonal offers, financing programs, referral programs
- Service area climate profile: heating degree days, cooling degree days, primary fuel source (gas, electric, oil, propane)

### Step 2: Content Calendar Creation
Build a 30-day video calendar:
- Week 1: 2 emergency/seasonal ads + 1 geo-targeted local search ad + 1 social organic clip
- Week 2: 2 service-specific landing page videos + 1 energy efficiency education piece + 1 social organic clip
- Week 3: 1 maintenance agreement promo + 1 IAQ awareness video + 2 social organic clips
- Week 4: 1 community/culture video + 1 equipment showcase + 2 social organic clips + 1 seasonal ad refresh
- Total: 14-18 videos per month, rotating creative to prevent ad fatigue and match seasonal demand shifts

### Step 3: Script and Visual Development
- Write scripts in the company owner's or lead tech's voice — match their communication style
- Include specific local references: neighborhoods, landmarks, climate-specific pain points ("Phoenix summers," "Minnesota winters")
- Every script must pass the "would an actual HVAC tech say this?" test — remove generic marketing language
- Visual direction must match the company's real presentation: show realistic service scenarios, not stock footage aesthetics
- Climate-match all content: do not show snow in a Florida HVAC ad, do not show desert landscapes for a Michigan company

### Step 4: Production and Review
- Generate initial video draft
- Quality checklist: brand consistency, phone number accuracy, license number displayed where required, offer terms correct, seasonal relevance verified
- Client review with one round of revisions included
- Final export in all required formats: 16:9 (YouTube, Google), 9:16 (TikTok, Reels, Stories), 1:1 (Facebook feed), 4:5 (Instagram feed)

### Step 5: Distribution and Optimization
- Name files with platform and purpose: `[company]-[service]-[platform]-[date].[ext]`
- Include recommended post copy for each platform
- Suggest targeting parameters: radius from service area centroid, homeowner status, home value range, age demographics, interest targeting (home improvement, energy efficiency)
- Schedule creative refresh at 14-day intervals for paid placements
- Track which creatives are deployed and flag when rotation is due

## 4. Platform-Specific Guidelines

### Google Business Profile and Local Services Ads
- Upload as "Video" post type on GBP, keep under 30 seconds for auto-play
- First frame must be visually compelling — this becomes the thumbnail in search results
- Include company name, phone number, and license number in the video itself (Google strips descriptions on some surfaces)
- Post at least 1 new video per week for freshness signals
- LSA video should emphasize trust factors: years in business, number of reviews, Google Guaranteed badge if applicable

### Facebook and Instagram Ads
- Test 3 creative variants per ad set: different hooks with the same offer
- 9:16 for Stories/Reels placements, 4:5 for feed, 1:1 for right column
- First 3 seconds are everything — lead with movement, a text hook, or a surprising visual (the filthy filter, the frozen coil)
- Always include captions: most Facebook video is watched without sound
- Rotate creative every 10-14 days — HVAC ad fatigue is faster than average because the audience is geographically concentrated
- Homeowner targeting: layer homeowner status + home value + zip code for precision

### Nextdoor
- Reference the specific neighborhood by name — Nextdoor users are hyperlocal
- Tone must be neighborly: "Hey [Neighborhood], a quick heads-up about your AC before this weekend's heat wave..."
- Avoid hard selling — Nextdoor communities self-police aggressive marketing aggressively
- Best performing HVAC content on Nextdoor: seasonal prep tips, energy-saving advice, storm damage readiness

### YouTube
- Pre-roll ads: 15 seconds, skippable after 5 — strongest hook and branding in the first 5 seconds
- SEO content: 3-8 minute how-to and education videos targeting "[City] + [HVAC question]" searches
- Shorts: Repurpose social content, 9:16, under 60 seconds
- Thumbnail best practices: show a real person, include large readable text, use contrasting colors

### TikTok and Instagram Reels
- Raw and real outperforms polished and corporate every time
- Best HVAC content: attic crawl reveals, "guess how old this furnace is," satisfying coil cleaning, thermal camera footage, tool demos
- Post 3-5x per week consistently; algorithm rewards frequency and consistency
- Trending audio can boost reach only if the fit is natural — forced trends look desperate and undermine credibility

## 5. Compliance and Legal

- Display state HVAC contractor license number in all advertising where required by state law
- EPA Section 608 certification must be current for any content involving refrigerant handling
- "Licensed, bonded, and insured" claims must be verifiable and current
- Pricing in videos must include "starting at" or "prices vary by system" qualifiers unless it is a fixed-price service
- Energy savings claims ("save up to 40% on energy bills") must be supportable — reference ENERGY STAR or manufacturer data, not invented numbers
- Equipment efficiency claims must reference current rating standards (SEER2, HSPF2 as of 2023 federal standard change)
- Financing offers must comply with Truth in Lending Act (TILA): if showing monthly payment, must disclose APR, term, and total cost or reference "see dealer for details"
- Rebate and tax credit claims must be current — federal and state programs change annually; verify before publishing
- Customer testimonials require written release; before-and-after property images require homeowner consent
- Do not guarantee specific energy savings outcomes for individual homes — too many variables (insulation, windows, occupancy, thermostat behavior)

## 6. Metrics and Optimization

Track these KPIs to measure HVAC video marketing ROI:
- **Cost per lead by video**: Compare cost per phone call or form submission across creatives and seasons
- **View-through rate**: Percentage watching >75% of the video (benchmark: 20-30% for 15s HVAC ads)
- **Click-through rate**: Benchmark 1.2-2.5% for HVAC video ads on Facebook (varies by season — peak season CTR is higher)
- **Google Business Profile views**: Week-over-week video view count after posting
- **Phone call volume**: Track with unique phone numbers per campaign (CallRail, CallTrackingMetrics, or ServiceTitan built-in tracking)
- **Maintenance agreement signups**: Track which video drove the enrollment (UTM parameters or unique landing pages)
- **Seasonal lead cost comparison**: Compare cost per lead in peak season vs shoulder season — video should narrow this gap
- **Creative fatigue indicator**: CTR drop of >20% from peak signals time to rotate

Refresh cycle: Replace underperforming creatives every 14 days. Archive top performers and seasonal content for re-use next year with updated offers and dates.
