## Deep Analysis Example

📊 NVDA antitrust probe — deep analysis

### What's really going on

This is the EU's third major tech antitrust action this year (after Apple App Store and Google Cloud). Brussels is establishing precedent for regulating AI infrastructure monopolies. NVDA has 80%+ market share in AI training chips — that alone puts a target on its back.

The timing matters: EU elections are next year, and tech regulation is a popular stance. This probe is partly political. But political motivation doesn't make the fines smaller.

### Historical precedent

Google's EU antitrust case (2017): stock dropped 2% on announcement, recovered within 3 weeks, total fine was $2.7B (1.5% of revenue). Stock was up 35% within 12 months of the ruling.

Qualcomm EU antitrust (2018): $1.2B fine, stock barely reacted. The market had already priced it in.

Key pattern: EU fines are a cost of doing business for mega-cap tech. The stock impact is front-loaded on the announcement. Unless there's a structural remedy (forced to license IP, split the business), the long-term impact is minimal.
