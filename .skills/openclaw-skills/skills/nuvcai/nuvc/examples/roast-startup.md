# Example: Roast a Startup

## Prompt
> "Roast my idea — it's like Notion but for pets"

## What happens
The agent runs:
```bash
node nuvc-api.mjs roast "A productivity app like Notion but designed specifically for pet owners. Track vet appointments, feeding schedules, medication reminders, grooming logs, and share pet profiles with your vet and pet sitter."
```

## Sample output

```
## 🔥 NUVC Startup Roast

### THE ROAST
Ah yes, because what the world desperately needed was a second brain for people who can't remember when to feed their dog. You've taken a $10B productivity tool and shrunk its TAM to "people who love spreadsheets AND animals." That's a very specific Venn diagram.

### THE REAL TALK
The core problem is real — pet owners do forget vet appointments and medication schedules. But you're competing with: (1) the Notes app, (2) Google Calendar reminders, and (3) the 47 existing pet management apps on the App Store. "Notion but for X" is the 2024 version of "Uber but for X" — it sounds like a product thesis, not a business.

Your biggest issue: pet owners won't pay $10/month for this when a free reminder app does 80% of the job. The vet-sharing and pet-sitter collaboration features are interesting — that's where the real value might hide — but you buried the lede by leading with "Notion for pets."

### THE SILVER LINING
The veterinary record sharing angle is genuinely underserved. If you repositioned as "the pet health record that travels with your pet" — connecting owners, vets, sitters, and boarding facilities — you'd have a B2B2C play that could actually work. Think medical records, not productivity.

### VERDICT
**Maybe** — but only if you kill the "Notion for pets" framing and pivot to pet health records. The infrastructure play (connecting all the humans around a pet) is more fundable than "pretty pet scheduler."
```
