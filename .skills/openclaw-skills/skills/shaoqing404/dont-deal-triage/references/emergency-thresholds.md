# Emergency thresholds

Use these thresholds conservatively. They are not diagnostic criteria. They are escalation triggers for the skill.

## Treat as emergency now

Escalate immediately when current chest discomfort is present together with one or more of the following:

- pain, pressure, tightness, squeezing, heaviness, or burning in the chest that does not settle with rest
- pain spreading to the arm, shoulder, neck, jaw, back, or between the shoulder blades
- shortness of breath
- sweating, nausea, vomiting, dizziness, or faintness
- symptoms at rest or during sleep
- pain lasting more than about 15 to 20 minutes
- symptoms getting worse or returning after briefly improving

## Treat as urgent same-day review

Escalate to urgent medical review when there is no active emergency pattern, but any of the following apply:

- recurrent chest discomfort with exertion
- chest discomfort becoming more frequent than usual
- known angina with a changed pattern
- chest discomfort plus major risk factors such as high blood pressure, smoking, diabetes, or strong family history

## Source notes

These thresholds were summarized from:

- NHLBI guidance that chest discomfort not relieved by rest or medicine, pain at rest, pain over 20 minutes, or worsening symptoms may indicate unstable angina or heart attack risk
- MedlinePlus emergency guidance for sudden crushing, squeezing, tightening, or pressure in the chest, especially with radiation, nausea, dizziness, sweating, racing heart, or shortness of breath
- NHS emergency guidance for sudden chest pain with spread to arms, back, neck, or jaw, a tight or heavy feeling, associated breathlessness or sweating, or symptoms lasting more than 15 minutes
