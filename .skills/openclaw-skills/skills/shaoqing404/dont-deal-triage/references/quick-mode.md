# Quick mode

Quick mode is the core of `dont-deal`.

It should answer one question fast and plainly:

- red: call emergency help now
- yellow: seek urgent medical care today

When information is incomplete, lean yellow. When emergency features are present, switch to red without delay.

## Tone

Use short, plain language. Do not sound vague.

Good:

- "这更像危险胸痛，先别继续走动，立刻呼救。"
- "我现在不想让你自己去医院，先叫急救或者让身边的人帮你。"
- "这还不能放心，当天要尽快就医。"

Avoid:

- "可能没事，再观察看看。"
- "大概率只是焦虑。"
- "你应该问题不大。"

## Lay descriptions to recognize

Users often do not say "radiating chest pain." They say things like:

- "像有人从背后捏了一下心脏，又松一下。"
- "胸口像被勒住，往后背扯。"
- "不是一个点疼，是胸前闷，肩胛骨里面也难受。"
- "下巴和牙根酸，不像普通牙疼。"
- "胸口像压了块石头，不太敢动。"
- "不是针扎一下那种，是一阵一阵发紧。"

These descriptions should still count as potentially cardiac when paired with danger features.

## Quick questions

Ask one question at a time:

1. "现在胸口还在不舒服吗？"
2. "更像压着、发紧、被攥住，还是像针扎、按一下就疼？"
3. "会不会往后背、左胳膊、脖子、下巴，或者像牙疼那样往牙床放过去？"
4. "已经持续多久了，有没有超过 15 分钟？"
5. "有没有喘不过气、出冷汗、恶心、头晕，或者快晕倒？"
6. "按压胸口那个地方，会不会更明显地疼？"
7. "这是你自己，还是你正在帮别人判断？"

## Bystander use

The skill may be used for another person.

When the user is helping someone else:

- ask whether the person is conscious and able to answer
- ask whether symptoms are active right now
- if red flags are present, instruct the helper to call emergency services immediately
- tell them not to let the person walk around or drive

## Pressing on the chest

Pain that becomes stronger when pressing on the chest wall can fit muscle or rib pain, but this does not safely exclude heart trouble by itself. Treat it only as one signal, never as reassurance.
