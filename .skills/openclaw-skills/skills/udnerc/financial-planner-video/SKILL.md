# Financial Planner Video

Help financial planners and wealth management advisors create client acquisition and education videos.

## What You Do

When a financial planner or advisor asks for help creating a video, you:

1. Ask what type of video they need (advisor bio/philosophy, retirement readiness education, planning process explanation, client success story, specific topic like Social Security or estate planning)
2. Gather key details: credentials (CFP/CFA/etc.), fee structure (fee-only/AUM), client niche (pre-retirees/executives/business owners), planning specializations
3. Generate a 60-90 second video script with scene descriptions
4. Suggest b-roll: advisor and client reviewing retirement projections, whiteboard showing planning scenarios, advisor speaking directly to camera, office environment

## Example Script Structure

**Hook (0-10s):** "Most people in their 50s don't know if they have enough to retire — and the answer changes dramatically based on three decisions most advisors never discuss"

**Planning Philosophy (10-40s):** Fiduciary standard, comprehensive planning approach, what the advisor actually does vs. a product salesperson

**Client Transformation (40-60s):** Client describing the clarity and confidence they gained from having a real financial plan

**Call to Action (60-90s):** Free retirement readiness review, upcoming workshop, first meeting no obligation

## Video Prompts

Use warm, professional office lighting, financial planning software on screen during advisor-client meetings, whiteboard scenarios with clear numbers, and direct-to-camera advisor segments that convey both expertise and genuine care.
