# Car Dealership Video — AI Video Marketing for Auto Dealers & Car Lots

Car buying has moved online — not all the way, but far enough that the dealership with no video presence is invisible to the majority of buyers during the phase that matters most: research. The average car buyer spends 14.5 hours researching before visiting a dealership. They watch walkaround videos, compare financing options, look up the sales team, and read reviews. The dealership that shows up in video during those 14.5 hours — with real inventory, real staff, real customer stories — has already won half the sale before the buyer walks through the door. Car Dealership Video gives every dealership the ability to show up where buyers are looking, with the content they're looking for.

## 1. Industry Context

### Market Size & Landscape
- The U.S. automotive retail industry generates approximately **$1.3 trillion** in annual revenue (NADA 2025).
- Approximately **18,000** franchised new car dealerships operate nationwide, supplemented by **130,000+** independent used car dealers.
- Average franchised dealership annual revenue: **$50-$80 million** (new + used + F&I + service + parts).
- New vehicle average transaction price (2025): **$48,300**; used vehicle average: **$28,100** — making automotive one of the highest-ticket retail categories in any market.
- **F&I (finance and insurance)** profit per vehicle retailed averages **$1,800-$2,400** — finance department video content that demystifies the F&I process and reduces buyer anxiety directly impacts per-deal profit.
- **Fixed operations** (service and parts) now represent **46%** of franchised dealer gross profit — service department video marketing is as important as new car sales content.
- **Digital retail** has permanently shifted: **92% of car buyers** begin their search online; **72%** watch video during research; buyers who watch dealership video are **2.3x more likely** to visit that dealership (Google Automotive Research).
- **EV transition** is creating a new marketing need: dealers selling electric vehicles face consumer education challenges (range anxiety, charging infrastructure, incentive complexity) that video is uniquely positioned to address.
- **Used car market** has become highly competitive post-2020 — independent dealers with video presence outperform those without in online classified conversion (Cars.com, AutoTrader, Facebook Marketplace).
- **Buy-here-pay-here** dealers serve the credit-challenged segment (approximately **12 million** car buyers annually) — a segment with specific trust barriers that transparent video marketing can significantly address.

### Why Video is Critical for Automotive Retail
1. **The 14.5-hour research window**: The average car buyer spends the majority of their purchase journey online before visiting any dealer. Walkaround video, financing explainers, and dealership trust content that appears during this window creates preference before the first phone call.
2. **Inventory differentiation**: Every dealer listing on Cars.com shows the same static photos. The dealer who adds a 90-second walkaround video to their listing stands out immediately — automotive data consistently shows that listings with video receive **5-10x more views** than photo-only listings.
3. **Service retention**: The dealer who produces consistent service tip content, maintenance reminder videos, and technician introduction content builds the service relationship that drives repeat service visits and, ultimately, the next vehicle purchase.
4. **Trust in a low-trust industry**: Automotive retail consistently ranks among the lowest-trust purchase categories. Transparency video — showing the inspection process, explaining fees, introducing the finance manager by name — meaningfully shifts buyer perception toward trust.
5. **EV education opportunity**: The dealer who produces clear, honest electric vehicle content (real-world range, home charging setup, available incentives, total cost of ownership) captures the significant number of EV-curious buyers who are intimidated by the transition.
6. **Social media inventory reach**: A $45,000 F-150 walkaround video posted to Facebook Marketplace, Instagram, and TikTok reaches buyers who would never have found the dealer through traditional advertising — organic vehicle content routinely achieves 10,000-100,000+ views without paid promotion.

### Customer Decision Journey
- **Trigger event**: Current vehicle approaching end of useful life, lease expiration, lifestyle change, rate/incentive opportunity, accident replacement
- **Research phase**: Google search → manufacturer website → Cars.com/AutoTrader → YouTube walkarounds → dealership website/video → Google reviews → visit decision
- **Timeline**: Average purchase research period: **89 days** for new vehicles; **60 days** for used vehicles
- **Decision factors**: (1) Vehicle availability and match to needs, (2) Price and perceived value, (3) Dealership trust and transparency, (4) Financing accessibility, (5) Service reputation, (6) Convenience and experience expectations
- **Where video intervenes**: Walkaround video during the inventory research phase, trust video during the dealership selection phase, and financing explainer video during the F&I anxiety phase are the three highest-impact video touchpoints in automotive retail.

## 4. Script & Content Guidelines

### Automotive-Specific Writing Rules

**DO:**
- Lead walkaround videos with the buyer's decision criteria: "If you're looking for a truck that can tow 11,000 pounds and still get 20 MPG on the highway, let me show you why this F-250 Power Stroke is the one." Start with the use case, not the specs.
- Use specific numbers everywhere: miles, towing capacity, fuel economy, warranty months, financing APR, CARFAX score, service records. Buyers have researched; they know what good numbers look like.
- For financing content, address the anxiety directly: "A lot of people think they can't get financed. Here's the reality: we've approved buyers with 520 credit scores and discharged bankruptcies. We work with 14 lenders. Come talk to us before you assume the answer is no."
- Show the service department as a profit center and trust builder: introduce the service manager by name, show the lift bays and diagnostic equipment, mention OEM-certified training. Service trust is the foundation of vehicle repurchase loyalty.
- For EV content, lead with practical answers to the questions buyers actually have: "How far does it really go on a full charge in winter?" / "How much does it actually cost to charge versus gas?" Answer honestly, including the limitations.
- Customer delivery videos should capture the genuine moment: the first time a buyer sits in their new car, the key handoff, the sales associate going through the features. Authentic joy converts better than scripted celebration.

**DON'T:**
- Don't quote specific prices or APRs in video content that won't be updated regularly — compliance violations and consumer protection issues arise from outdated price/rate representations.
- Don't make claims about a vehicle's condition or history that aren't verified — a "clean CARFAX" claim requires you to have the actual CARFAX report in hand.
- Don't film service work on vehicles without customer permission — customers may not want their specific vehicle identified in marketing content.
- Don't use high-pressure sales language in video — the modern car buyer is specifically trying to avoid high-pressure environments, and video that communicates pressure repels the online-research buyer who represents the majority of today's leads.
- Don't neglect the trade-in content — fear about trade-in value is one of the primary barriers to purchase initiation. Clear, honest trade-in process explanation (with a value estimate invite) is a significant lead generation tool.

### Top Performing Content Formats

**The inventory walkaround**: 90-120 seconds per unit. Exterior walk (front, sides, rear, wheels), interior (dashboard, seat material, technology features), under hood (engine overview, condition), trunk/cargo. Close with the key differentiator for this specific vehicle and a direct call to action ("Call us at [number] or click the link to schedule a test drive — this one won't last"). Unit-specific walkaround video on Cars.com listings generates 5-10x more inquiries per listing than photo-only.

**The "we approved [X]" financing story**: 60-second anonymized finance story — buyer came in expecting to be declined, we found a lender, they drove home in a [vehicle]. This content specifically converts the credit-challenged buyer who has been turned away elsewhere and is the primary acquisition tool for BHPH and second-chance financing dealers.

**The customer delivery moment**: 30-45 seconds of genuine customer reaction — the handshake, the key presentation, the first-time-in-the-driver-seat moment. Posted to Instagram and TikTok with the customer's permission, this content generates high organic engagement and serves as social proof that the dealership produces happy customers, not just transactions.

**The service tip series**: 60-second educational clips about vehicle maintenance — "when to rotate your tires," "what that warning light actually means," "how to check your brake wear." Posted weekly on YouTube and Facebook, this content builds ongoing audience engagement from existing customers and positions the dealer's service department as the trusted source for automotive maintenance knowledge.

## 6. Compliance & Safety

### Advertising Compliance
- **FTC Used Car Rule**: Dealers must accurately represent vehicle condition, history, and warranty status. Video content showing or describing vehicles must not misrepresent known defects or material conditions.
- **Truth in Lending Act (TILA/Regulation Z)**: Financing terms in video must comply with TILA trigger term requirements — if APR is mentioned, other required disclosures must accompany it. Consult compliance counsel before including specific financing terms in video.
- **State dealer advertising regulations**: Most states have specific dealer advertising regulations governing price representations, savings claims, and financing offers. State-level compliance review is required for promotional video content.

### Manufacturer Compliance
- Franchised dealers must comply with OEM advertising co-op requirements and brand standards. Video content using manufacturer logos, model names, or trademarked imagery must meet OEM co-op guidelines to qualify for reimbursement.
- Some OEMs prohibit dealers from using specific competitive comparisons in advertising.

### Consumer Protection
- Price claims must reflect the actual selling price (not MSRP minus "up to $X off" unless the actual selling price is the advertised price).
- "As low as" financing rates must be achievable by a meaningful percentage of actual buyers, not only top-tier credit applicants.
- Before/after price comparisons must use accurate comparable vehicles and documented previous prices.

### Vehicle History and Condition
- CARFAX and AutoCheck references must reflect actual current reports.
- "Certified" claims require actual OEM or independent certification with documented inspection.
- Mileage representations must be accurate at time of filming — video describing a vehicle's mileage must be updated or replaced if the vehicle remains unsold.
