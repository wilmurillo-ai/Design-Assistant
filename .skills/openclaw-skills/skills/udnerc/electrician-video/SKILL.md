# Electrician Video — AI Video Production for Electrical Contractors

Create trust-building video content for electrical contracting businesses. Generate panel upgrade showcases, EV charger promos, storm response ads, and safety education content — designed for the homeowner who cannot see the danger behind their walls until you show them.

## 1. Industry Context

Electrical contracting is a $200B+ US industry undergoing a generational demand surge. Three forces are driving unprecedented workload: aging housing stock (42 million US homes built before 1980 with potentially outdated electrical systems), the EV transition (requiring 240V charger installations at millions of homes), and the smart home boom (demanding dedicated circuits and upgraded panels). Every one of these trends creates homeowners who need an electrician but have never hired one before.

This creates a unique marketing challenge. Unlike plumbing — where a burst pipe is obvious — electrical problems are invisible. A homeowner cannot see that their 60-amp Federal Pacific panel is a fire hazard. They do not know their aluminum wiring connections are slowly oxidizing. They will not realize their home cannot support an EV charger until the installation fails inspection. Video is the single most effective medium for making invisible electrical dangers visible and converting unaware homeowners into booked estimates.

The trust barrier is exceptionally high in electrical work. Homeowners know that bad electrical work can burn their house down, and they have no way to evaluate quality after the drywall goes back up. They are choosing based entirely on pre-purchase trust signals: reviews, professionalism, and — increasingly — video content that demonstrates competence, explains the process, and shows the finished result. An electrician with three well-produced videos on their Google Business Profile will consistently outperform a competitor with five-star reviews but no visual content.

## 2. Video Categories and Specifications

### 2.1 Electrical Panel Upgrade Showcase
- **Purpose**: Google search, website service page, Facebook ads targeting homeowners in pre-1990 neighborhoods
- **Duration**: 45-90 seconds
- **Structure**: Open with the risk ("This panel was installed in 1978 — here's why that matters"), show the old panel with specific issues identified (scorch marks, double-tapped breakers, obsolete brand), transition to the replacement process (time-lapse or key moments), close with the new panel — clean, labeled, properly grounded — and a scheduling CTA
- **Key messaging**: Safety first, not upselling. "We're not trying to sell you something you don't need. We're showing you what's behind your panel door so you can make an informed decision."
- **Visual style**: Well-lit, close-up shots of panel internals. Label danger points with text overlays. Show the electrician's hands — gloved, deliberate, professional.
- **Compliance**: Never show live work on energized panels in marketing content. Include "work performed by licensed electrician" disclaimer.

### 2.2 EV Charger Installation Marketing
- **Purpose**: Targeting new EV owners and EV dealership partnerships
- **Duration**: 30-60 seconds
- **Structure**: Congratulate the new EV owner, explain the home charging advantage over public charging (convenience, cost per mile), briefly show installation process (panel capacity check, dedicated circuit run, charger mount, final test), close with pricing framework ("Level 2 installations start at $X — schedule your free assessment")
- **Audience insight**: Most EV buyers research home charging before or immediately after purchasing the vehicle. The decision window is 2-4 weeks around purchase date.
- **Distribution**: Partner with local EV dealerships to include in new owner welcome packets. Run Facebook ads targeting "interested in electric vehicles" + homeowner + service area zip codes.
- **Tone**: Tech-forward, enthusiastic. The customer just made a purchase they are excited about — match their energy.

### 2.3 Storm Damage Emergency Response
- **Purpose**: Pre-storm preparation ads and post-storm rapid deployment
- **Duration**: 15-30 seconds
- **Structure**: "After every major storm, we deploy [X] trucks within 24 hours to restore power safely. Do not touch downed lines. Do not reset tripped breakers repeatedly. Call [Company] — we'll assess the damage and get your home safe."
- **Timing**: Pre-produce and hold ready. Deploy on social media within 2 hours of a significant weather event in the service area.
- **Tone**: Authoritative and calming. This is emergency communication, not marketing — but it builds enormous goodwill and brand recall.
- **Safety messaging**: Always include "if you see a downed power line, stay 35 feet away and call 911" — this is both responsible and demonstrates expertise.

### 2.4 Whole-Home Generator Marketing
- **Purpose**: Targeting homeowners after power outages, medical equipment users, home office workers
- **Duration**: 60-120 seconds
- **Structure**: Open with the problem (extended power outage scenario — food spoiling, sump pump failing, medical equipment offline, remote work impossible), introduce the solution (automatic standby generator), explain how it works in simple terms (detects outage, starts automatically in seconds, runs on natural gas or propane), show installation overview, close with free assessment CTA
- **Seasonal timing**: Run campaigns October-November (winter storm prep) and May-June (hurricane/severe storm season prep). Post-outage: deploy immediately after any extended outage in the service area.
- **Pricing approach**: Generators are a significant investment ($5,000-$15,000 installed). Focus on value (protected home, no spoiled food, no hotel stays, medical safety) rather than leading with price.

### 2.5 Safety Education Series
- **Purpose**: YouTube SEO, social media organic, email content, community goodwill
- **Duration**: 60-180 seconds
- **Topics**:
  - "5 signs your home's electrical system needs attention" (flickering lights, warm outlets, frequent breaker trips, burning smell, two-prong outlets throughout)
  - "Why your 1970s home might need rewiring" (aluminum wiring risks, ungrounded outlets, insufficient capacity for modern loads)
  - "Extension cord safety — the mistakes almost everyone makes"
  - "What to do (and absolutely not do) during a power outage"
  - "GFCI outlets explained — why they trip and when to worry"
  - "Is your home ready for an electric vehicle?"
  - "Surge protection: power strips vs. whole-home systems"
- **Tone**: Patient teacher, not alarmist salesman. The goal is to educate genuinely — the sales follow naturally when the viewer realizes they need professional help.
- **Structure**: Identify the problem, explain why it matters, show what to look for, recommend when to call a professional. Never recommend DIY electrical work.

### 2.6 Smart Home and Modern Living Upgrades
- **Purpose**: Targeting home renovators, new construction buyers, tech enthusiasts
- **Duration**: 45-90 seconds
- **Topics**: Recessed lighting conversion, under-cabinet lighting, home office dedicated circuits, USB outlet upgrades, smart switch and dimmer installations, whole-home audio pre-wire, home theater wiring, outdoor kitchen electrical, pool and hot tub wiring, workshop sub-panel installations
- **Tone**: Lifestyle-focused. Show the finished result and how it improves daily life, not the technical process.
- **Visual style**: Clean, well-lit spaces. Before-and-after is powerful here — dark room with single overhead fixture vs. layered lighting design.

### 2.7 Commercial Electrical Services
- **Purpose**: Targeting property managers, business owners, general contractors
- **Duration**: 60-120 seconds
- **Topics**: Tenant improvement build-outs, parking lot lighting upgrades (LED conversion), emergency exit and safety lighting compliance, electrical preventive maintenance programs, high-voltage industrial installations, data center power infrastructure
- **Tone**: Professional, specification-focused. Commercial clients make decisions based on capability, compliance, and reliability — not emotional appeal.
- **Structure**: Capabilities overview, relevant certifications and licensing, project portfolio highlights with scope and timeline, client references (with permission), single CTA to schedule a site assessment.

## 3. Production Workflow

### Step 1: Business Intake
Collect from the electrical contractor:
- Company name, license numbers (state and local), insurance coverage limits
- Service area with specific cities and zip codes
- Residential vs. commercial split (percentage of business)
- Core services: panel upgrades, rewiring, EV chargers, generators, smart home, solar, commercial, industrial
- Key differentiators: years in business, number of licensed electricians on staff, warranty programs, response time, financing options
- Brand assets: logo, truck photos, job site photos (completed work), team photos
- Safety record: OSHA compliance, safety training certifications, zero-incident streaks
- Current digital presence: Google Business Profile URL, review count/rating, website
- Customer base demographics: primarily older homes, new construction, commercial properties
- Existing partnerships: builders, real estate agents, EV dealerships, solar companies

### Step 2: Content Strategy
Map video production to the business's revenue priorities:
- Highest-margin service → most video content (typically panel upgrades and generator installs)
- Growing demand service → educational content to build category awareness (typically EV chargers)
- Emergency services → pre-produced rapid-deployment content
- Recruitment needs → culture and career path videos if hiring
- Target: 10-12 videos per month across categories

### Step 3: Script Development
- Every script reviewed for technical accuracy (do not describe electrical processes incorrectly — it destroys credibility with both electricians and informed homeowners)
- Safety disclaimers on every video showing any electrical work: "All work performed by licensed electricians. Do not attempt electrical work without proper training and licensing."
- Local code references where applicable (NEC 2023 plus local amendments)
- Pricing ranges reviewed and approved by the business owner before inclusion

### Step 4: Production and Quality Control
- Technical accuracy review: verify all electrical terminology, code references, and process descriptions
- Safety compliance check: no depiction of unsafe practices, proper PPE shown, disclaimer present
- Brand consistency: logo placement, colors, phone number, license number
- Export in all required formats with platform-specific optimization

## 4. Platform-Specific Guidelines

### Google Business Profile and Local Services Ads
- Electrical is a Google Guaranteed category — video content increases trust signal alongside the green checkmark
- Post 1-2 new videos per week to maintain profile freshness
- Thumbnail must show professional appearance: uniform, clean job site, modern equipment
- Include license number in video when required by state advertising law

### Facebook and Instagram
- Homeowner targeting: interests in home improvement, home ownership status, age 30-65, specific zip codes
- Lead generation forms outperform traffic campaigns for electrical services — attach video to lead form ads
- Test emotional vs. rational appeals: "Protect your family from hidden electrical dangers" vs. "Save 20% on your panel upgrade this month"
- Retarget website visitors with video testimonials within 7 days of visit

### YouTube
- How-to content drives organic search traffic year-round: "How to tell if your electrical panel needs replacing"
- YouTube is the second-largest search engine — optimize titles for "[City] electrician" queries
- Longer educational content (3-7 minutes) builds authority and ranks for informational searches
- Shorts: Repurpose satisfying before-and-after panel upgrades and neat wiring jobs

### Nextdoor
- Post after completing a job in a specific neighborhood: "Just finished a panel upgrade on Oak Street — happy to answer any electrical questions for the neighborhood"
- Safety-focused content performs exceptionally well on Nextdoor: "Quick safety note after last night's storm..."
- Respond to "electrician recommendation" threads with video portfolio links

## 5. Compliance and Legal

- State electrical license number must appear in all advertising (requirement varies by state — verify local law)
- Never depict or instruct viewers to perform any electrical work themselves — liability risk is extreme
- "Licensed, bonded, and insured" claims must be current and verifiable
- Energy savings claims must include "results may vary" qualification
- Generator sizing recommendations in video must include "site assessment required for accurate sizing"
- EV charger compatibility claims must note "electrical assessment required — panel capacity varies by home"
- All safety statistics cited must have a verifiable source
- Videos showing work in customer homes require property release

## 6. Metrics and Optimization

Track these KPIs for electrical service video marketing:
- **Cost per booked estimate**: The ultimate metric — what does it cost in ad spend to get a homeowner to schedule an assessment?
- **Video-to-call rate**: Percentage of video viewers who call within 24 hours (tracked via unique phone numbers)
- **Service page bounce rate**: Compare bounce rate on service pages with vs. without embedded video
- **Google Business Profile engagement**: Views, calls, and direction requests attributed to video posts
- **Lead quality score**: Do video-generated leads convert to jobs at a higher rate than non-video leads? (typically yes — video pre-qualifies)
- **Creative rotation**: Swap underperforming ads every 14 days. Top performers can run 30-45 days before fatigue in electrical (longer than general home services due to higher consideration period)
