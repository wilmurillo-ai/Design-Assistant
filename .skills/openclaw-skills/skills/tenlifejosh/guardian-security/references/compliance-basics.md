# Compliance Basics — Reference Guide

Basic legal compliance for digital products, email marketing, and the minimum
requirements that keep the company on the right side of the law.

---

## 1. CAN-SPAM COMPLIANCE (Email Marketing)

### Required Elements in Every Commercial Email
```
MANDATORY:
✅ Accurate "From" name and email address
   Not: "A friend" or misleading sender name
   Yes: "Joshua Noreen" or "Ten Life Creatives"

✅ Non-deceptive subject line
   Can't imply it's a personal email if it's commercial
   Can't say "RE: Your order" if there's no prior order

✅ Physical mailing address
   Must be a valid physical address (can be a P.O. Box)
   Required in every commercial email footer
   Example: "Ten Life Creatives | Parker, CO 80134"

✅ Unsubscribe mechanism
   Clear, easy way to opt out
   Must honor unsubscribes within 10 business days
   Can't require more than email address to unsubscribe

✅ Identify as advertisement (commercial emails)
   Not required to say "THIS IS AN AD" but must not be disguised

PENALTIES: Up to $50,120 per email for violations
```

### Email Compliance Checklist
```
Before sending any commercial email campaign:
- [ ] From name is accurate (not deceptive)
- [ ] Subject line is honest
- [ ] Physical address in footer
- [ ] Unsubscribe link present and functional
- [ ] Reply-to address is real and monitored
- [ ] List is permission-based (people opted in)
- [ ] Suppression list applied (recent unsubscribes removed)
```

## 2. FTC REQUIREMENTS (Digital Products & Marketing)

### Disclosure Requirements
```
TESTIMONIALS & ENDORSEMENTS:
  If a testimonial claims specific results, it must be typical OR disclosed
  
  WRONG: "[Customer]: This made me $5,000 in my first month!" (with no disclosure)
  RIGHT: "[Customer]: This made me $5,000 — results vary based on effort and market.
          Most users see [typical outcome]."
  
  WRONG: Using AI-generated testimonials or fake reviews
  RIGHT: Only real testimonials from real customers
  
INCOME CLAIMS:
  Any specific income number requires:
    - Evidence that it's achievable
    - Disclosure that typical results differ
    - "Results not typical" type language
  
  "Make $10,000/month!" without evidence = FTC violation
  "Joshua earned $X using this system; your results will vary based on 
   your effort, market, and execution" = compliant

MATERIAL CONNECTIONS:
  If someone promotes our product and received payment/free product:
  They must disclose this (#ad, #sponsored, or similar)
```

### Product Claims Compliance
```
DIGITAL PRODUCT CLAIMS:
  ✅ Specific features we can demonstrate
  ✅ Page counts, word counts, exercise counts
  ✅ "Based on my personal experience" (opinion)
  
  ❌ "Guaranteed to [specific outcome]" (unless you can actually guarantee it)
  ❌ "Will definitely [result]" (unless proven)
  ❌ "The only [product] you'll ever need" (could be misrepresented)
  ❌ Clinical/medical claims for wellness products
  
SAFE HARBOR LANGUAGE:
  "This worked for my family — your results may vary"
  "Designed to help you [do X]" (not "will definitely do X")
  "Based on [X experience/research]" with actual basis
```

## 3. COPYRIGHT COMPLIANCE

### Content We Can and Cannot Use
```
WE CAN USE (without concern):
  - Our own original content, photos, writing
  - Content with explicit free commercial license (Unsplash, Pexels, Pixabay)
  - Content under Creative Commons with appropriate license
  - Facts and ideas (not protected)
  - US government publications (public domain)

WE CANNOT USE WITHOUT PERMISSION:
  - Stock photos downloaded from Google Images
  - Photos from other websites
  - Other people's written content (significant portions)
  - Music, video, audio from third parties
  - Logos and trademarks of other companies
  - Screenshots of other people's products (for commercial use)

FAIR USE (limited):
  - Short quotes with attribution for commentary/education
  - Not a get-out-of-copyright-free card
  - Best practice: avoid relying on fair use for commercial content
```

## 4. REFUND POLICY COMPLIANCE

### Digital Product Refunds
```
LEGAL REQUIREMENTS:
  - US law doesn't require refunds for digital products that have been downloaded
  - But: Deceptive or misleading product descriptions = refund may be required
  - Best practice: Have a clear, reasonable refund policy stated on sales page
  
OUR POLICY:
  "If you're not satisfied, contact us within 30 days of purchase for a refund.
  We want you to have a product that actually helps you."
  
WHAT THIS PROTECTS:
  - Customer trust (reasonable policy = more sales)
  - Chargeback protection (clear policy reduces disputes)
  - Platform relationships (Gumroad favors sellers with reasonable policies)
```
