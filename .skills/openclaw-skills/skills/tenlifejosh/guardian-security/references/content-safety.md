# Content Safety — Reference Guide

Reviewing public content for legal, ethical, and reputational risk. The Guardian
perspective on what can cause harm before it's published.

---

## 1. CONTENT SAFETY OVERVIEW

Content safety review complements, but is separate from, quality QA (Sentinel's job).
Sentinel asks: Is this good quality? 
Guardian asks: Could this cause legal, ethical, or reputational harm?

---

## 2. CONTENT SAFETY REVIEW CHECKLIST

### Legal Risk Review
```
FALSE ADVERTISING:
- [ ] No unqualified "guaranteed" results claims
- [ ] No specific income/results without disclaimer and proof
- [ ] No claims about government approval that don't exist
- [ ] No medical/health claims that could be construed as medical advice
- [ ] No testimonials that imply typical results without disclosure

COPYRIGHT & TRADEMARK:
- [ ] No unlicensed use of copyrighted images
- [ ] No reproduction of substantial portions of copyrighted text
- [ ] No use of third-party brand names/logos in misleading way
- [ ] No content that could be confused with another brand

DEFAMATION:
- [ ] No false statements of fact about named individuals or companies
- [ ] Opinions clearly framed as opinions
- [ ] Any competitor comparisons factually based and verifiable

PRIVACY:
- [ ] No customer data published without consent
- [ ] No private information about identifiable individuals
- [ ] Screenshots don't contain identifiable private info
```

### Ethical Risk Review
```
MANIPULATION:
- [ ] No dark patterns (fake countdown timers, false scarcity)
- [ ] No artificially manufactured urgency
- [ ] No misleading before/after comparisons
- [ ] No fake "social proof" (real testimonials only)

HARM POTENTIAL:
- [ ] Faith content not spiritually manipulative or coercive
- [ ] Financial content doesn't encourage harmful financial behavior
- [ ] No content that could harm vulnerable populations
- [ ] Mental health adjacent content is responsible and properly caveated
```

### Reputational Risk Review
```
THE SCREENSHOT TEST:
  Could this be screenshot out of context and harm our reputation?
  
THE JOURNALIST TEST:
  If a reporter was looking for a negative story, could they use this?
  
THE FIVE-YEAR TEST:
  Would we be comfortable with this associated with our name in 5 years?
```

## 3. FAITH CONTENT SAFETY

### Special Considerations for Faith Products
```
Prayful, prayer journals, scripture products require extra care:

ACCEPTABLE:
  Content that invites, not demands
  Acknowledges doubt alongside faith
  Respects different faith expressions within Christianity
  Honest about what prayer is and isn't

RISK AREAS (handle carefully):
  Claims of specific spiritual outcomes ("this will improve your prayer life guaranteed")
  Content that could be read as exclusionary of certain Christians
  Health and wellness claims tied to faith practice
  Content about mental health adjacent to spiritual practice

ESCALATE TO HUTCH IF:
  Content makes specific claims about God's will or promises
  Content could be read as health advice
  Content touches on theologically contested areas
```

```

LEGAL FINDINGS: [Any legal concerns found]
ETHICAL FINDINGS: [Any ethical concerns found]
REPUTATIONAL FINDINGS: [Any reputational risks]

REQUIRED CHANGES: [Specific changes needed before publishing]
ESCALATION NEEDED: YES / NO
```
