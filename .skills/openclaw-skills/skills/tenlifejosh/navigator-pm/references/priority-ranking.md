# Priority Ranking — Reference Guide

Scoring frameworks for ranking competing initiatives, the ICE/RICE method applied
to Ten Life Creatives, and the decision protocols that resolve priority conflicts.

---

## 1. THE PRIORITY RANKING IMPERATIVE

"Everything is a priority" = nothing is a priority.
When a founder has limited time, every hour spent on Priority B is an hour not spent on Priority A.
Priority ranking forces honesty about what actually matters most.

---

## 2. THE TLC PRIORITY SCORECARD

### Weighted Scoring for Ten Life Creatives
Given zero runway and need for fast revenue, weight accordingly:

```
CATEGORY: Revenue Speed (40 points max)
  How quickly does this generate revenue?
  40 pts: Revenue within 1 week
  30 pts: Revenue within 1 month
  20 pts: Revenue within 3 months
  10 pts: Revenue within 6 months
  5 pts: Revenue within 12 months
  0 pts: No direct revenue path

CATEGORY: Effort Required (30 points max)
  How much time/effort to execute?
  30 pts: < 4 hours total
  25 pts: < 1 day
  20 pts: < 1 week
  10 pts: 1-2 weeks
  5 pts: 2-4 weeks
  0 pts: > 1 month

CATEGORY: Strategic Value (20 points max)
  Does this build toward long-term goals?
  20 pts: Core to primary mission, builds lasting asset
  15 pts: Directly advances stated annual goal
  10 pts: Supports annual goal indirectly
  5 pts: Useful but not connected to annual goals
  0 pts: Tangential or off-strategy

CATEGORY: Confidence (10 points max)
  How confident are we it will work?
  10 pts: Proven approach with our audience
  7 pts: Strong evidence it will work
  5 pts: Reasonable evidence
  2 pts: Mostly assumption
  0 pts: Speculative

TLC SCORE = Revenue Speed + Effort + Strategic Value + Confidence
Maximum: 100 points
Prioritize: Highest score first
```

## 3. PRIORITY RANKING PROTOCOL

### When Two Initiatives Compete for the Same Time/Attention

```
STEP 1: Score both using TLC Priority Scorecard
STEP 2: If scores within 10 points: ask the tiebreaker question
  "If we could only do ONE of these forever, which do we pick?"
  That instinct reveals the true priority.
STEP 3: The lower-scoring initiative goes on the backlog with a "re-evaluate in [date]" note
STEP 4: Document the decision (Hutch Commandment #9: version control for decisions too)
```

## 4. THE WEEKLY PRIORITY DECISION

### Setting Priorities Each Monday
```
INPUTS:
  1. Quarterly OKRs (what must happen by quarter end)
  2. Last week's status (what's behind? what's ahead?)
  3. Upcoming deadlines or opportunities
  4. Current energy and resource availability

PROCESS:
  List all candidate priorities for the week
  Score each quickly (5-min per initiative)
  Select top 3 (maximum)
  For each: name the specific action + what success looks like

RULE: Three priorities maximum
  If you have 5 "top priorities" → you have 0 top priorities
  Forced ranking builds execution discipline
```

## 5. THE STOP LIST

### What We Are NOT Doing (As Important As What We Are)

Every quarter, maintain a "Stop List":
```
NOT DOING THIS QUARTER:
  - [Initiative] — Why: [Specific reason, e.g., "Revenue timing: 6+ months away"]
  - [Platform expansion] — Why: "Current products not fully optimized on existing platforms"
  - [Feature addition] — Why: "Core product needs testing before expanding"

The Stop List:
  - Prevents bandwidth from leaking to interesting-but-not-priority work
  - Forces honest accounting of what was deprioritized and why
  - Provides a record to review: "We deprioritized X in Q1 — should we revisit now?"
```

## 6. DEALING WITH NEW OPPORTUNITIES

### The 48-Hour New Idea Rule
When an exciting new opportunity appears:
```
Hour 0-48: Resist the urge to act.
  Record it: "New opportunity: [description]"
  Score it quickly with TLC scorecard
  
After 48 hours:
  Does it score higher than our current top priority? 
    YES: Bring to Hutch with recommendation to swap priorities
    NO: Add to backlog. It wasn't as urgent as it seemed.
    
The 48-hour rule filters out excitement from importance.
Most "urgent" new opportunities look less urgent after 48 hours.
The real ones still look urgent.
```
