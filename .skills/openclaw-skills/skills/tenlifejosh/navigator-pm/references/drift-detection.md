# Drift Detection — Reference Guide

How to identify when the company is drifting from its goals, the early warning
signals that predict strategic failures, and the interventions that course-correct.

---

## 1. WHAT IS STRATEGIC DRIFT?

Strategic drift is the gradual, often invisible movement away from stated goals.
It rarely happens in one dramatic decision. It happens in a hundred small ones:
- "This opportunity will only take a few days"
- "Let me explore this while I have momentum"
- "We can revisit the original plan next week"

Three months later: no progress on the main goal, multiple half-finished side projects,
and a founder who can't remember what the strategy was.

---

## 2. DRIFT EARLY WARNING SIGNALS

### Red Flags (Catch Before They Compound)
```
ACTIVITY SIGNALS:
  ⚠️ Weekly priorities change every week with no completion
  ⚠️ Projects started > projects completed (ratio > 2:1)
  ⚠️ Multiple new initiatives started without completing existing ones
  ⚠️ "Research" lasting more than 1 week without leading to action
  ⚠️ Meetings or planning sessions that produce no actionable output

GOAL ALIGNMENT SIGNALS:
  ⚠️ Can't state the #1 priority without thinking
  ⚠️ Week passes without any progress on quarterly OKR
  ⚠️ Two weeks pass without progress on quarterly OKR
  🚨 Three weeks pass = DRIFT CONFIRMED

FOCUS SIGNALS:
  ⚠️ New platform being researched while existing platforms underoptimized
  ⚠️ New product started before existing products are fully launched
  ⚠️ "Shiny object" discussions occupying more than 20% of planning time
```

### The Drift Audit (Monthly)
```
QUESTION 1: What was our stated #1 priority 30 days ago?
[Name the specific thing]

QUESTION 2: How much progress did we make on it in the last 30 days?
[Quantify if possible: 0%, 25%, 50%, 75%, 100%]

QUESTION 3: If < 50% complete: What caused the gap?
  A) Legitimate pivot: We got new information that changed the priority
  B) Drift: We got distracted by other things

If B → drift detected → intervention needed

QUESTION 4: What did we spend time on that wasn't the stated priority?
[List the activities]

QUESTION 5: Was that time better than spending it on the priority?
  If YES: Update the priority (the drift revealed a better path)
  If NO: Document the pattern. Set up better focus enforcement.
```

## 3. TYPES OF DRIFT

### Type 1: Shiny Object Drift
New opportunity appears → immediate exploration → original priority forgotten
**Warning sign**: New projects starting before old ones finish
**Fix**: 48-hour new idea rule (no action for 48 hours on any new idea)

### Type 2: Exhaustion Drift  
Core priority is hard → easier tasks get done instead → core priority stalls
**Warning sign**: Lots of small tasks completed, no big wins
**Fix**: Start each day with the hard thing. Not email. Not admin. The hard thing.

### Type 3: Research Drift
"I need to understand this better" → perpetual research → no execution
**Warning sign**: Research lasting > 1 week on a decision that shouldn't require that
**Fix**: Set a research deadline. "Decision by Friday, regardless of what we find."

### Type 4: Perfectionism Drift
Product/strategy must be perfect before launch → never launches → no feedback
**Warning sign**: Same project at "80% complete" for 3+ weeks
**Fix**: Ship the 80% version. Get feedback. Improve. Ship again.

## 4. DRIFT INTERVENTION PROTOCOL

```
LEVEL 1 — Minor drift detected (1-2 weeks off target):
  Action: Note in Monday memo
  Recommit to original priority
  Specific plan for this week to catch up

LEVEL 2 — Moderate drift detected (2-4 weeks off target):
  Action: Escalate in memo with specific ask
  Present Hutch with: Current status vs. target, why drift happened,
  recommendation: continue original goal OR formally pivot
  Get explicit decision, not implicit drift

LEVEL 3 — Severe drift detected (4+ weeks off target OR multiple simultaneous):
  Action: Strategic reset
  Full inventory of active projects
  Kill or park anything not in top 3
  Explicit recommitment or formal pivot
  May require 30/60/90 replanning
```

---

## 5. THE DRIFT PREVENTION SYSTEM

### Weekly Rituals That Prevent Drift
```
1. Monday memo always includes status vs. quarterly OKR (not just last week's activities)
2. "What are we NOT doing this week?" stated explicitly each Monday
3. New ideas logged in the backlog, NOT acted on immediately
4. Monthly drift audit (10 minutes, answers the 5 questions above)
5. Quarterly check: Are our quarterly OKRs still the right OKRs?
```
