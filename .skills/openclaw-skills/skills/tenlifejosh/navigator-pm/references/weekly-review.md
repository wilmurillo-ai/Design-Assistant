# Weekly Review — Reference Guide

The Monday memo format, on-track/off-track analysis, and the weekly planning ritual
that keeps a company executing instead of reacting.

---

## 1. WHY WEEKLY REVIEW MATTERS

The weekly review is the single highest-leverage strategic habit. An hour on Monday:
- Prevents a week spent on wrong priorities
- Catches drift before it compounds
- Forces clarity on what actually matters
- Creates accountability to stated goals

Without it: six months pass and the company is still doing what it was doing on day one,
just more tired and less sure why.

### Standard Monday Memo
```markdown

**For:** Hutch / Joshua

### Wins
- [Specific win #1 — quantified where possible]
- [Specific win #2]

## STATUS: Are We On Track?
```

## 3. THE ON-TRACK / OFF-TRACK ASSESSMENT

### How to Evaluate Each Initiative
```
CRITERIA FOR "ON TRACK":
  - Progressing at or above the expected pace toward the quarterly OKR
  - No material blockers that will prevent hitting the quarterly target
  - Last week's priority happened (or had a good reason not to)

CRITERIA FOR "AT RISK":
  - Progressing but below expected pace (could catch up, but needs focus)
  - Known blocker that's not yet resolved
  - Two consecutive weeks without progress on stated priority

CRITERIA FOR "OFF TRACK":
  - Three or more consecutive weeks without progress
  - Material setback that changes the quarterly projection
  - Fundamental assumption proven wrong

ACTION REQUIRED BY STATUS:
  🟢 ON TRACK: Continue. Acknowledge the win.
  🟡 AT RISK: Identify specific blocker. Set specific fix this week. Escalate if needed.
  🔴 OFF TRACK: Stop the current approach. Diagnose root cause. Reset or pivot.
```

## 4. THE THREE-QUESTION WEEKLY REVIEW

Minimum viable weekly review — 15 minutes:

```
QUESTION 1: Did the most important thing from last week happen?
  YES → What made it possible? (Replicate that)
  NO → Why not? (Address the root cause, not the symptom)

QUESTION 2: What are the top 3 priorities for this week?
  These must be specific enough to know if they happened by Friday.
  NOT: "Work on GND"
  YES: "Send 10 cold emails to Colorado Springs plumbers by Wednesday"

QUESTION 3: What's blocking us?
  Identify the single biggest thing slowing progress.
  Not a list — the SINGLE biggest thing.
  Name it. What will resolve it?
```

## 5. WEEKLY RHYTHM IMPLEMENTATION

### The Weekly Cadence
```
MONDAY (9 AM):
  Navigator produces Monday memo
  Hutch reviews and confirms or adjusts priorities
  Agents get their priorities for the week

WEDNESDAY (mid-week check):
  Quick pulse check: Are top priorities on track?
  Any blockers emerging that weren't on Monday's radar?
  Adjust if needed

FRIDAY (EOD):
  Quick review: What happened vs. what we said?
  Notable wins to capture
  Input for next Monday's memo

This rhythm creates a 3-day execution cycle between check-ins.
Not so tight it micromanages. Not so loose the week disappears.
```

## 6. MEMO ANTI-PATTERNS

### What Makes Monday Memos Fail

**The Status Update Trap**:
Memo describes what happened without evaluating whether it was the right thing.
Fix: Explicitly evaluate status against target, not just report activity.

**The Happy Talk Problem**:
Only reporting wins, hedging the misses.
Fix: Misses are required. State them specifically. No shame — just honesty.

**The Wish List Priority**:
"Priorities" that aren't actually achievable in a week.
Fix: Each priority must be achievable by Friday. If not, break it down.

**The Missing Decision**:
A decision that should come from Joshua buried in the memo without being called out.
Fix: "Decisions Needed" section is mandatory. Put real decisions there.

**The Vague Blocker**:
"We've been busy" as a blocker explanation.
Fix: Name the specific thing that needs to happen. Assign it.
