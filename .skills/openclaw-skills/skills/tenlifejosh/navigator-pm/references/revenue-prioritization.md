# Revenue Prioritization — Reference Guide

Always filtering strategy through revenue impact and timing. The framework for
making sure we're working toward money when money is what we need.

---

## 1. THE REVENUE FIRST MANDATE

At zero runway: every strategic decision must pass the revenue test first.
Not: "Is this strategically important?"
First: "Does this generate revenue, and when?"

This isn't pessimism. It's the constraint that prevents working on the right thing
at the wrong time. A product that won't generate revenue for 12 months is irrelevant
when you need revenue in 30 days.

---

## 2. REVENUE TIMING CATEGORIES

### Category Framework
```
IMMEDIATE REVENUE (< 30 days):
  Examples: Following up with warm leads, launching already-built product,
  updating a listing that's converting poorly, direct sales outreach
  Priority: HIGHEST
  
NEAR-TERM REVENUE (30-90 days):
  Examples: New product development, new platform setup, email list building
  Priority: HIGH
  
MEDIUM-TERM REVENUE (90-180 days):
  Examples: SEO content, long-form audience building, partnership development
  Priority: MEDIUM (can pursue in parallel with immediate/near-term)
  
LONG-TERM REVENUE (180+ days):
  Examples: Major platform builds, course creation, app development
  Priority: LOW (deprioritize at zero runway)
```

## 4. REVENUE QUALITY METRICS

### Not All Revenue Is Equal
```
REVENUE QUALITY SCORE:
  High quality: Recurring (MRR) or repeat purchase potential
  Medium quality: One-time but high margin
  Low quality: One-time, low margin, high service burden

GND ($99/month): HIGH — recurring, grows with each client
Digital products: MEDIUM-HIGH — one-time but scalable, passive
Custom services: LOW — one-time, time-intensive, hard to scale

IMPLICATION: GND MRR is the highest quality revenue to build.
Each new GND client adds $99/month forever (or until they cancel).
```
