# Headline Formulas — Reference Guide

Proven headline structures for every context: product titles, email subjects, social hooks,
article titles, and CTAs. The toolkit for writing first lines that pull people in.

---

## 1. THE HEADLINE HIERARCHY

Every piece of content has a headline hierarchy:
1. Main headline (most important — decides if they read at all)
2. Subheadline (explains the headline, pulls them further)
3. Section headers (guides readers who skim)
4. First sentences (hooks within the body)

Master each level. They multiply your conversion.

---

## 2. UNIVERSAL HEADLINE FORMULAS

### The Specificity Formula
Replace every generic word with a specific one:
```
GENERIC → SPECIFIC:
"Improve your productivity" → "Reclaim 2 hours every morning"
"Get organized" → "End the Sunday chaos in one hour"
"Make more money" → "Add $500/month with one change"
"Better family relationships" → "Cut bedtime arguments to zero"
```

### The How-To Formula
```
"How to [Achieve Outcome] [Qualifier]"
"How to [Achieve Outcome] Without [Common Objection]"
"How to [Achieve Outcome] in [Specific Timeframe]"
"How to [Achieve Outcome] Even If [Limiting Belief]"

EXAMPLES:
"How to Organize Your Family's Week in One Hour"
"How to Write Cold Emails That Get Replies (Without Being Pushy)"
"How to Build a Website in 30 Days Without Knowing Code"
"How to Start a Budget Even If You've Failed Before"
```

### The Number Formula
```
"[Number] [Things/Ways/Tips/Reasons] to [Outcome]"
"The [Number] [Nouns] Every [Person] Needs"
"[Number] [Things] I Learned From [Experience]"
"[Number] [Timeframe] Rules for [Topic]"

EXAMPLES:
"7 Family Organization Habits That Take 5 Minutes Each"
"The 3 Questions Every Cold Email Should Answer"
"5 Things I Learned After 3 Years of Running a Service Business"
"The 90-Day Rule for Building a Product Business"
```

### The Problem-Solution Formula
```
"[Problem Statement]: [Solution Teaser]"
"[Specific Pain]? [Solution]."
"Still [Problem]? [Here's What Works]"

EXAMPLES:
"Sunday Night Dread? This Fixes It."
"Still Losing Track of Family Schedules? One System Changes Everything."
"Can't Find a Family Planner That Sticks? You're Using the Wrong Kind."
```

### The Contrast Formula
```
"[Bad thing] → [Good thing]"
"Stop [Bad Behavior]. Start [Good Behavior]."
"Not [Wrong Approach]. [Right Approach]."
"Less [What They're Doing]. More [What They Should Do]."

EXAMPLES:
"Chaotic Sundays → Planned Mondays"
"Stop Tracking Expenses. Start Predicting Them."
"Not Another App. A System That Lives on Paper."
```

### The Secret/Hidden Knowledge Formula
```
"The [Secret/Hidden/Overlooked] [Thing] About [Topic]"
"What Nobody Tells You About [Topic]"
"The [Topic] Advice That Changed [My/Our] [Life/Business]"
"The Real Reason [Problem Exists]"

EXAMPLES:
"The Hidden Reason Family Planners Don't Work"
"What Nobody Tells You About Running a Service Business"
"The Cold Email Strategy That Gets 40% Reply Rates"
```

## 3. CONTEXT-SPECIFIC HEADLINE FORMULAS

### Product Title Headlines
```
USE WHEN: Naming a product or writing its primary headline

FORMULA: [Who It's For] + [What They Get] + [Key Differentiator]
"The Family Planner for Parents Who've Given Up on Planners"
"A Budget Tracker That Actually Shows Where the Money Goes"
"Cold Email Templates for Service Businesses"

OR: [Outcome] + [Simple Descriptor]
"Family Organization Made Simple"
"Monthly Budget Control in One Notebook"
"The Complete First Job Guide"
```

### Email Subject Lines
```
USE WHEN: Writing subject lines for any email type

CURIOSITY:
"The thing about [topic]..."
"Quick question about your [specific thing]"
"What I found when I looked at your [thing]"

DIRECT VALUE:
"[Specific outcome] in [specific timeframe]"
"The only [thing] you need for [purpose]"

PERSONAL:
"[First name], quick question"
"RE: [relevant context]"
"Your [specific thing]"

STORY-BASED:
"What happened when I tried [thing]"
"The [timeframe] that changed [something]"
"I [did/tried something] — here's what happened"
```

### Social Media Hooks
```
USE WHEN: Opening lines for tweets, LinkedIn posts, IG captions

INSIGHT:
"Most [common belief] is wrong. Here's what works:"
"The best [X] I ever got:"
"Counterintuitive: [surprising take]"

STORY:
"[Timeframe] ago I [situation]."
"[Specific experience] taught me [lesson]."
"The [thing] that almost broke me:"

LIST:
"[Number] things I wish I knew before [situation]:"
"The only [number] [things] that matter in [topic]:"

QUESTION:
"What's the one thing that [outcome] for you?"
"How do you [handle challenge]?"
```

### Article/Blog Post Titles
```
USE WHEN: Writing content that needs to rank and get shared

SEO-FIRST:
"[Primary Keyword]: The Complete Guide [Year]"
"How to [Primary Keyword] [Timeframe]"
"Best [Keyword] for [Audience] in [Year]"

ENGAGEMENT-FIRST:
"The [Counterintuitive Thing] About [Topic]"
"Why [Common Belief] Is Wrong"
"[Number] [Topic] Lessons From [Timeframe] of [Experience]"

BOTH:
"How to [Primary Keyword] Without [Common Problem]: [Specific Result]"
"[Number] [Topic] Strategies That Actually Work (Not Just Theory)"
```

## 4. THE HEADLINE TESTING FRAMEWORK

### Testing Headlines Before Publishing
```
THE 5-SECOND TEST:
  Read it to a stranger. What does it make them want?
  If they don't immediately want to read more → revise

THE SPECIFICITY CHECK:
  Can this headline apply to someone else's product?
  If yes → too generic, add specificity

THE PROMISE CHECK:
  Does the headline make a specific promise?
  Can the content actually deliver on that promise?

THE EMOTION CHECK:
  Does it trigger curiosity, desire, fear of loss, or relief?
  If none of those → it's probably too neutral
```

### Headline Score (Rate Before Publishing)
```
Rate each headline 1-5 on:
  Curiosity gap (makes you want to read more): /5
  Specificity (not generic): /5
  Promised benefit (clear value): /5
  Authenticity (sounds real, not manufactured): /5
  
Total: /20
Publish if: ≥ 16/20
Revise if: 12-15/20
Discard if: < 12/20
```
