# Tone Modes — Reference Guide

How to calibrate voice for different contexts: warm support, educational, persuasive,
spiritual/reflective, and professional modes.

---

## 1. TONE MODE OVERVIEW

The brand voice is constant. The tone adjusts to context.
Think of it like a dial: the dial moves, but the hand holding it stays the same person.

---

## 2. MODE 1: WARM/SUPPORT TONE

**Use when:** Someone is struggling, exhausted, or reaching out from a hard place.
Responding to someone who's shared something vulnerable. Community posts about hard times.

```
CHARACTERISTICS:
  Short sentences. Space for the reader to breathe.
  Acknowledge before advising.
  No silver linings, no pivots to solutions.
  Present tense. Real.

EXAMPLE:
"That sounds exhausting.
Three years of building with no runway is a long time.
I don't have an answer for you. Just — you're not doing this wrong by struggling."

NOT THIS:
"I hear you! Building is hard. But the good news is — if you just keep pushing, 
it will work out. Here are 3 things you can do right now to turn things around..."
```

---

## 3. MODE 2: EDUCATIONAL TONE

**Use when:** Explaining how something works. Writing guides, tutorials, or how-tos.
Content designed to teach.

```
CHARACTERISTICS:
  Clarity over cleverness.
  Concrete examples over abstract concepts.
  Step-by-step where complexity exists.
  Short explanations > long theoretical preambles.

EXAMPLE:
"Cold email works because it's personal and specific.
Here's how to write one that actually gets replies:

First: Know one specific thing about them.
Not their name — something about their business.
Second: Connect that thing to what you do.
Third: Make one small ask. Not 30 minutes. Not 'a conversation.'
Ask to send them something, or ask one yes/no question."

NOT THIS:
"Cold email is a nuanced and multifaceted strategy that requires careful attention
to numerous factors. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the various
elements that contribute to successful cold email campaigns..."
```

---

## 4. MODE 3: PERSUASIVE TONE

**Use when:** Sales copy, product descriptions, CTAs, launch emails, promotional content.

```
CHARACTERISTICS:
  Confident, not pushy.
  Benefits before features.
  Specific claims over vague ones.
  Urgency when real, not manufactured.
  One ask.

EXAMPLE:
"Sunday nights shouldn't feel like dread.
FamliClaw is 47 pages of family organization that lives on paper.
Print it. Fill it in. Put it on the fridge. Done.
$47. Instant download."

NOT THIS:
"Are you TIRED of the CHAOS? This INCREDIBLE system will TRANSFORM your family 
life FOREVER! Don't miss this AMAZING opportunity to change everything NOW!!!"
```

---

## 5. MODE 4: SPIRITUAL/REFLECTIVE TONE

**Use when:** Faith-based products, spiritual content, Prayful materials, Advent/Lent posts.

```
CHARACTERISTICS:
  Quiet, not loud.
  Invitational, not prescriptive.
  Honest about uncertainty alongside belief.
  Earns the faith reference — doesn't spray it everywhere.

EXAMPLE:
"Prayer is harder to maintain than I expected.
Not because it doesn't matter.
Because the days are full and the quiet is uncomfortable.
These pages exist for those mornings. Five minutes.
Just you, a pen, and something worth saying."

NOT THIS:
"God has a PLAN for your life and this faith-based system will help you WALK 
IN HIS PURPOSE for your journey! Transform your prayer life TODAY!"
```

---

## 6. MODE 5: PROFESSIONAL TONE

**Use when:** Client proposals (GND), formal business correspondence, LinkedIn business posts.

```
CHARACTERISTICS:
  Respectful, direct, confident.
  Less personality, more professionalism.
  Still Josh — just more tailored for business context.
  No casual grammar (contractions OK, slang no).

EXAMPLE:
"I work with local Colorado businesses on web design.

I've looked at [Business Name]'s site. Three specific things are costing you 
search visibility and call conversions. I can walk you through them in 10 minutes.

If you're open to it, I'd like to show you what I'd change."

NOT THIS (too casual for first business contact):
"Hey! Yo so I checked out your site and tbh it needs some work lol. 
I do web stuff and could totally help. Wanna chat?"
```

---

## 7. TONE BLENDING

Some situations require blending modes:

**Warm + Educational**: A guide on a sensitive topic (budgeting when broke, faith during doubt)
→ Acknowledge the emotional weight while still being useful

**Persuasive + Professional**: A proposal for a high-value client
→ Professional structure with specific, confident benefit claims

**Warm + Professional**: First response to an inbound inquiry
→ Professional enough to build trust, warm enough to be human

---

## 8. TONE FAILURE PATTERNS

### Getting It Wrong

**Too warm in sales context**: Validation without any offer
"I totally understand how hard this is for you. That sounds really rough..."
→ Result: No one buys anything

**Too persuasive in support context**: Pivoting to pitch during vulnerability
"That's tough! Have you considered trying our FamliClaw system? It might help!"
→ Result: Trust destroyed

**Too formal in social context**: Corporate voice on human platform
"We are pleased to announce our newest product offering..."
→ Result: Nobody engages

**Too casual in professional context**: Losing credibility with key prospects
"Lol yeah so like your site is kinda meh..."
→ Result: Never hear from them again
