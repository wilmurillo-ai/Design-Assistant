# Brand Voice Guide — Reference Guide

The Ten Life Creatives brand voice — authentic, faith-aware, builder energy.
The reference for all brand-aligned writing across all products and platforms.

---

## 1. THE BRAND IN ONE PARAGRAPH

Ten Life Creatives makes practical tools for people who build things — businesses, families,
and lives of meaning. Our products are honest, specific, and built by a real person
who needed them first. Our voice is direct without being harsh, real without being
performatively vulnerable, and faith-aware without being exclusive. We sound like
a founder who's been in the room.

---

## 2. BRAND VOICE PILLARS

### Pillar 1: Direct
We say the thing. We don't warm up for three paragraphs before saying it.
"Your website isn't working." Not "There may be some opportunities to optimize your online presence."

### Pillar 2: Authentic
We talk about real experiences. We acknowledge that things are hard.
We don't pretend to have answers we don't have.
"Three years in. Some months are great. Some feel like starting over."

### Pillar 3: Specific
Generic claims create doubt. Specific claims build trust.
"47 pages" not "comprehensive." "30 days" not "quickly." "$99/month" not "affordable."

### Pillar 4: Builder Energy
We're making things. We take the work seriously.
Not hustle-bro energy. Not inspirational poster energy.
The quiet determination of someone actually building something.

### Pillar 5: Faith-Aware
Faith shows in how we work, not how loud we announce it.
"Built for families who want their home to reflect what they actually believe."
Not "God has a plan for your business!"

---

## 3. BRAND WRITING STANDARDS

### What TLC Content Always Is:
- Written as if by a real person (not a marketing department)
- Specific (numbers, examples, real experiences)
- Confident without arrogance
- Helpful before promotional
- Consistently direct (no excessive qualifiers)

### What TLC Content Never Is:
- Corporate or formal without reason
- AI-generated in voice (even if AI-assisted in production)
- Preachy about faith (showing > telling)
- Vague or generic
- Hype-y or sales-y
- Apologetic or over-hedged

---

## 4. BRAND VOICE ACROSS PRODUCTS

### GND (Good Neighbor Design)
Voice is professional + direct. Less personal story, more business-direct.
"Your site isn't showing your reputation. I can fix that."

### Ten Life Creatives Digital Products
Voice is personal + real. Josh's origin story is part of the product.
"I built this because I needed it. It works for my family."

### Prayful
Voice is quiet, invitational, honest about the difficulty of faith practice.
"Faith isn't certainty. It's showing up with an open hand."

---

## 5. VOICE CONSISTENCY CHECKER

Before any content goes live, verify:
```
- [ ] Would Joshua say this out loud and not cringe?
- [ ] Could this have been written by any brand, or is this distinctly TLC?
- [ ] Is there at least one specific detail (number, name, experience)?
- [ ] Does it avoid AI-obvious vocabulary?
- [ ] Does it match the appropriate tone mode for the context?
- [ ] Would someone who knows Joshua recognize his voice here?
```

All YES → voice check passed
Any NO → revise until all YES
