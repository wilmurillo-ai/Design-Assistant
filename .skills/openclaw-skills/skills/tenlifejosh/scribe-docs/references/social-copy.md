## 1. PLATFORM TONE CALIBRATION

### Each Platform Has a Different Social Contract

**Twitter/X**: Opinions, insights, arguments, quick value, personality
- Audience: Entrepreneurs, builders, nerds, professionals
- Tone: Direct, punchy, opinionated, occasionally contrarian
- Length: Short (1-3 lines) or well-structured threads (5-10 tweets)
- What wins: Hot takes, counter-intuitive insights, specific frameworks, honest stories

**LinkedIn**: Professional insight, founder stories, business lessons, value
- Audience: Business professionals, entrepreneurs, job seekers, leaders
- Tone: Professional but human, story-driven, insight-forward
- Length: Medium (150-300 words for standard) or long (600-1200 for thought leadership)
- What wins: Personal stories with business lessons, specific insights, counterintuitive takes

**Instagram**: Visual-first, emotional, lifestyle, inspiration
- Audience: Broader, lifestyle-oriented, creative community
- Tone: Warmer, more personal, community-building
- Length: Short captions (1-3 sentences) to medium (75-150 words)
- What wins: Relatable moments, authentic stories, visual content with emotional resonance

## 2. TWITTER/X COPY FORMULAS

### The Single Tweet Formula
```
One sentence that stands alone. Worth reading. Worth sharing.

INSIGHT FORMAT:
"The best [X] is [non-obvious observation]."
"Nobody tells you [important truth] when you start [thing]."
"[Observation]. That's it. That's the tweet."

CONTRAST FORMAT:
"[Common belief] is wrong.
[True thing] is right.
[Why this matters]."

STORY FORMAT:
"[Year] ago I [started/built/tried something].
[What happened].
[What I learned]."

LIST FORMAT:
"[Number] things I've learned about [topic]:
• [Item]
• [Item]
• [Item]
The [last one] is the one nobody talks about."
```

### The Twitter Thread Structure
```
TWEET 1 — THE HOOK (most important):
  States the most interesting claim
  Does NOT start with "I" (algorithm issue)
  Does NOT start with "A thread on..."
  Length: 1-2 lines max
  Example: "The best family organization systems have one thing in common.
  Here's what most people miss: 🧵"

TWEETS 2-7 — THE BODY:
  Each tweet = one idea
  Each tweet stands alone (readable out of context)
  Short: 2-3 lines max per tweet
  No filler transitions ("Next up...", "As I mentioned...")

FINAL TWEET — THE CLOSE:
  Summary + takeaway
  CTA (follow, link, or both)
  Example: "TL;DR: [Summary in one line]. 
  I write about [topic] every week. Follow for more."
```

### Twitter Hook Formulas
```
"[Number] things about [topic] nobody talks about:"
"The [topic] advice I wish I had when starting:"
"Unpopular opinion: [specific take]"
"Most [common action] advice is wrong. Here's what works:"
"[Observation that contradicts conventional wisdom]"
"I [did something unusual] for [timeframe]. Here's what I learned:"
"[Specific painful moment] taught me [valuable lesson]:"
```

## 3. LINKEDIN COPY FORMULAS

### The LinkedIn Algorithm Reality
LinkedIn rewards:
- Posts that generate early comments (comment velocity = reach)
- Long-form posts that keep people on platform
- Personal stories (more engagement than company news)
- Posts with 3-5 line breaks early (people stop scrolling to see "more")

LinkedIn punishes:
- External links in the post body (put links in comments)
- Posts that look like ads
- Too much promotional content

### The LinkedIn Opening Trap
The first line = the "above the fold" text before "see more".
This line must create enough curiosity to click "see more."

```
WINNING FIRST LINES:
  "Three years ago I was ready to quit."
  "My most embarrassing business failure:"
  "The counterintuitive thing about [topic]:"
  "What I've learned after [number] years of [thing]:"
  "[Specific outcome] in [timeframe]. Here's how."
  "Nobody talks about this when starting a [thing]:"

LOSING FIRST LINES:
  "I'm excited to share..." (boring)
  "As entrepreneurs, we all know..." (generic)
  "Today I want to talk about..." (wasted first line)
  "It's [day]! Let's talk about..." (horrible)
```

### LinkedIn Post Structure
```
FIRST LINE: Pattern interrupt or strong hook

[BLANK LINE — required for visual breathing room]

Body paragraph 1: Context or story setup (2-3 lines)

[BLANK LINE]

Body paragraph 2: The insight or lesson (2-3 lines)

[BLANK LINE]

Body paragraph 3: Specific example or proof (2-3 lines)

[BLANK LINE]

Takeaway: One line — the thing you want them to remember

[BLANK LINE]

CTA: Question or action (one line)
```

### LinkedIn Post Template
```
[Strong opening hook or vulnerable statement]

[2-3 sentences setting up the story or observation]

[The insight — what you learned or want to share]

Here's the thing most people miss:

→ [Specific point 1]
→ [Specific point 2]
→ [Specific point 3]

[Why this matters or what to do with it]

Have you experienced this? [Question that invites response]
```

## 4. INSTAGRAM CAPTION FORMULAS

### Caption Structure
```
OPENING LINE: Must work as standalone (shows before "more")
  → Most interesting word/phrase FIRST
  → Creates desire to read the rest

BODY: Story, insight, or context (100-200 words max)
  → 1-2 line paragraphs
  → White space is your friend on Instagram

ENGAGEMENT HOOK: Question or invitation at end
  → "Tell me in the comments" or "Save this for later"

HASHTAGS: At end (or in first comment)
  → 10-20 relevant tags
  → Mix of specific (10K-100K posts) and broad (100K-1M posts)
  → Not spam-stuffed
```

## 5. HOOK WRITING

### The Hook's Job
A hook's job is to stop the scroll. Nothing else.
You have 1 second. Make it count.

### Hook Formulas by Emotion
```
CURIOSITY: Makes them want to know more
  "The thing about [topic] that surprised me most:"
  "What nobody told me about [situation]:"
  "The counterintuitive reason [thing happens]:"

CONTROVERSY: Makes them take a stance
  "Unpopular opinion: [specific take]"
  "Hot take: [provocative statement]"
  "[Common belief] is overrated"

IDENTIFICATION: Makes them feel seen
  "If you've ever [relatable experience]..."
  "For anyone who [situation they've been in]:"
  "Fellow [identity] who [struggle] — this is for you"

VALUE: Promises something immediately useful
  "[Number] [timeframe] hacks that actually work:"
  "How to [specific outcome] in [specific timeframe]:"
  "The only [framework/system/approach] you need for [topic]:"

FEAR: Activates loss aversion
  "The mistake I made that cost me [specific cost]:"
  "What I wish I knew before [situation]:"
  "[Common action] is secretly [negative consequence]:"
```

## 6. PLATFORM-SPECIFIC RULES

### Twitter/X Rules
```
✓ Post between 9-11 AM or 6-9 PM EST for best reach
✓ Engage with replies within first 30 minutes (algorithm boost)
✓ Don't delete and repost (algorithm penalizes)
✓ Use line breaks for readability
✓ Threads should have 5-10 tweets (not 2, not 20)
✗ Never start tweet with "I" (feed suppression)
✗ Don't put links in main tweet body (put in replies)
✗ Don't spam hashtags
```

### LinkedIn Rules
```
✓ Post between 7-9 AM or 12-1 PM Tuesday-Thursday for best reach
✓ Respond to ALL comments within first hour (early comment velocity = reach)
✓ External links go in COMMENTS, not the post itself
✓ Use white space (no walls of text)
✗ Don't be too salesy (LinkedIn community is allergic to ads)
✗ Don't post every day without quality (frequency without quality = follower loss)
```

### Instagram Rules
```
✓ Reels outperform static posts 3-5x for reach (as of 2024)
✓ Carousels outperform single images for saves
✓ Post when YOUR audience is active (check Insights)
✓ Stories maintain connection with existing followers
✗ Don't use 30 generic hashtags (0 performance benefit, looks spammy)
```

## 7. SOCIAL COPY STANDARDS

### The Three Social Copy Tests
```
TEST 1: THE STOP TEST
  Would this stop a fast-scrolling person?
  If no → rewrite the first line

TEST 2: THE VALUE TEST
  Does this deliver something: insight, entertainment, or resource?
  If none of those → why would they engage?

TEST 3: THE BRAND TEST
  Does this sound like TLC or could it be anyone?
  Generic = invisible
  Specific + personal = memorable
```

### Signs Copy Needs to Be Rewritten
```
- Starts with "I" on Twitter
- First sentence is a warm-up, not the point
- Paragraph 1 is background/context (lead with the insight)
- Contains AI-obvious phrases
- Could be from any account in the space
- No opinion, just information
- Ends with "Let me know your thoughts!" (generic, adds nothing)
```
