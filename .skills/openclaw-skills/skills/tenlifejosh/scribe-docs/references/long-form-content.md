# Long-Form Content — Reference Guide

Articles, blog posts, guides, and educational content. The structure and standards
for long-form writing that earns attention and builds authority.

---

## 1. WHEN LONG-FORM IS WORTH IT

Long-form content (800+ words) earns its existence when:
- The topic requires depth (short treatment would be incomplete)
- It's designed to rank for a specific search term
- It's building authority on a topic
- It's a pillar piece that supports a product or service

Don't write long just to be comprehensive. Write long because the value requires it.

---

## 2. THE LONG-FORM STRUCTURE

### The ABCD Framework
```
A — ATTENTION: The first 200 words
  Hook the reader with their problem or the most surprising thing you'll say
  Make them commit to reading further
  Never "In this article, we will discuss..."

B — BUILD: The middle 60-70%
  Deliver the promised value
  Each section = one complete idea
  Subsections break it up visually
  Examples for every concept

C — CLOSE: The last 10-15%
  Synthesize the key takeaways
  Action-oriented: what should they do with this?
  Optional: what's next (related content, product, CTA)

D — DETAILS: Supporting elements
  Headers (H2, H3) for scannability
  Short paragraphs (3-5 lines max)
  Bold key sentences for scanners
  Lists where appropriate (not everywhere)
```

---

## 3. ARTICLE STRUCTURE TEMPLATE

```markdown
# [Headline — specific, benefit-oriented]

[Opening hook — 2-3 sentences that validate the problem or make a surprising claim]

[Bridge — 1-2 sentences that connect the hook to what this article covers]

---

## [Section 1 — First major point/concept]

[2-3 sentences introducing the concept]

[The meat: explanation, examples, specifics — 150-250 words]

**Key takeaway from this section**: [Bold the main point so scanners see it]

---

## [Section 2 — Second major point/concept]

[Follow same pattern]

---

## [Section 3+]

[Continue as needed — 3-6 sections typical for 800-2500 word articles]

---

## The Takeaway

[Synthesis of the key points — 2-3 sentences]

[What to do with this — actionable close]

[Optional CTA — link to product, email signup, or related content]
```

## 4. SEO-FIRST LONG-FORM CONTENT

### Writing for Search Intent
```
BEFORE WRITING: Answer these questions
  1. What keyword is this ranking for?
  2. What does someone searching this keyword want to find?
     → Information? How-to? Product? Comparison?
  3. What would FULLY satisfy that search intent?

KEYWORD PLACEMENT:
  Primary keyword: Title, first 100 words, H2 headers (where natural)
  Secondary keywords: Body text (where natural)
  Rule: Write for humans first. Google follows engagement signals.

WORD COUNT TARGETS:
  Informational "how-to": 1000-2000 words
  Comprehensive guides: 2000-3500 words
  Quick tips/lists: 800-1200 words
  Opinion/perspective pieces: 600-1000 words
```

## 5. THE OPENING 200 WORDS

This section determines whether anyone reads the rest. Get it right.

```
OPENING PATTERNS THAT WORK:

VALIDATE THE PROBLEM:
"Family organization advice always misses the same thing.
All of it — apps, planners, systems — assumes you'll maintain it.
Nobody maintains it.
Here's what actually works."

CHALLENGING ASSUMPTION:
"The 30-minute Sunday planning session doesn't work.
Not because you won't do it. Because 30 minutes isn't enough to feel in control,
and too much to do when you're tired.
Here's how to fix that."

THE CONFESSION:
"I've built five organization systems for my family.
Four failed within 6 weeks.
The one that stuck taught me something counterintuitive about how systems work."
```

## 6. LONG-FORM CONTENT STANDARDS

```
STRUCTURE:
  - Headers every 200-300 words
  - No paragraph over 100 words
  - Lists where there are 3+ parallel items
  - Bold for emphasis (not decoration)

VOICE:
  - Same Josh voice — just more of it
  - Opinions present throughout
  - Examples specific (not "for example, imagine a business...")
  - Direct address: "you" not "one" or "readers"

QUALITY BAR:
  - Would this answer the search question completely?
  - Would someone share this with a friend who has this problem?
  - Is every section earning its word count?
```
