## How the TikTok Algorithm Works (2026)

### The Test-and-Expand Loop

TikTok follows a specific distribution pattern:

1. **Micro-audience test**: Video shown to a tiny group matched by sound, hashtags, and inferred interests
2. **Follower test (NEW 2026)**: Video shows primarily to existing followers during the first few days
3. **Early-hour signals**: The system watches completion rate, rewatch/looping, replies, shares, "Not Interested" flags, and saves
4. **Decisioning**: Strong positive signals trigger expansion into more cohorts
5. **Broader distribution**: If the video continues performing, it reaches the wider For You Page

### 2026 Algorithm Changes — Critical Updates

- **Follower engagement is now central**: Must perform well with followers BEFORE going wider. Major shift from earlier years.
- **70%+ completion rate needed for viral distribution** (up from 50% in 2024)
- **Rewatch rate is the #1 signal**: 15-20%+ rewatch rate = strong positive. One viewer watching 3x > three viewers watching once.
- **Original content heavily favored**: Low-effort reposts, watermarked content, and mass-produced videos are down-ranked
- **Search is a direct ranking metric**: TikTok is becoming a search engine

### Priority Signals (Ranked)
1. **Rewatch/loop rate** (highest weight)
2. **Completion/hold rate**
3. **Shares and meaningful comments**
4. **Saves**
5. **Follows from the video**
6. **Metadata** (captions, on-screen text, spoken words) — affects search ranking

### What TikTok Does NOT Consider
- Follower count (a new creator can go viral)
- Previous video performance (each video is judged independently)
- Whether you have a Pro account or not

## 70%+ Completion Rate Requirement

This is the gating signal for broader distribution. To achieve it:

- **Hook in the first 3 seconds**: Must stop the scroll immediately
- **No padding**: Every second must earn its place
- **Promise + delivery**: Hook makes a promise; content delivers on it
- **Loop point**: End mirrors start so viewers who rewatch don't feel a break
- **Captions**: Improve watch time for viewers watching on mute
- **Build tension**: Don't give everything away immediately — keep viewers watching to see the payoff

---

## Rewatch Rate as #1 Signal

Rewatch rate above 15-20% = strong positive signal. Strategies to drive it:

- **Loop structure**: End the video in a way that flows seamlessly back into the beginning
- **Information density**: Pack so much value that viewers need to rewatch to absorb it all
- **"Did they say what I think they said?"**: Bold claims or surprising statements make people rewatch
- **Visual interest**: Something happening on screen that rewards close attention
- **Lists and steps**: Viewers rewatch to copy down the information

---

## Content Structure: Hook → Build → Payoff

Every TikTok video should follow this structure:

- **Hook (0-3 seconds)**: Pattern interrupt + promise of value
  - Text overlay, bold statement, mid-sentence start, direct eye contact
  - Must stop the scroll — if the hook fails, nothing else matters
- **Build (3 seconds to payoff)**: Escalating interest, tension, or value
  - Each second should deliver something new
  - Build anticipation for the payoff
- **Payoff (final moments)**: Deliver on the hook's promise
  - Never leave viewers hanging — deliver what was promised
- **Loop point**: End mirrors start for rewatch signal

## Video Length Strategy (2026)

- **60-180 seconds** are outperforming 15-second clips
- A 15-second video watched fully > 60-second video watched halfway
- Story-based content performs exceptionally in longer formats
- Don't artificially cut good content short
- Don't pad content to reach a length target
- **Edutainment sweet spot**: Under 90 seconds with high search value

## Audio Strategy

- Use trending audio when it fits naturally
- Original voiceovers get algorithmic preference in 2026
- Create unique sounds — these can become trending themselves
- "Rising + high fit" sounds outperform broad viral audio (land in right interest cluster)
- Even when using trending audio, add your own twist
- TikTok transcribes and analyzes all audio — spoken keywords matter

## TikTok SEO (Emerging in 2026)

TikTok is becoming a search engine. Optimize for it:

- Write descriptive, keyword-rich captions that summarize the video
- Use on-screen text overlays with keywords
- Spoken content should include target keywords (TikTok transcribes audio)
- Structure "how-to" content clearly for TikTok Search results
- Think in terms of queries: "How to [X]", "Best [Y] for [Z]"
- Include relevant hashtags that match search intent (3-5 is sufficient)

## Hook Formulas

- "I tested [X] for 30 days — here's what happened"

## Warm-Up Protocol

### Before Your First Post
1. Watch content in your niche for 30 min (trains the algorithm to understand your interest cluster)
2. Follow 20-30 relevant creators in your niche
3. Like and comment on 10-15 videos

### First Week
1. Post your first 3 videos before engaging heavily (content-first platform)
2. Use trending sounds from Day 1
3. Keep videos under 60 seconds to start
4. Focus on one content pillar for clarity

### Weeks 2-4
1. Increase to 3-5 posts/week
2. Use all content formats (Duets, Stitches, Responses)
3. Respond to 100% of comments
4. Begin going live (strengthens follower engagement signals)

### Long-Term Expectation
- Don't expect results until video 10-20 — the algorithm is testing you
- Each video is judged independently — one bad video doesn't tank the account
- Consistency matters more than any single viral hit

---

## Growth Tactics

### Consistency Over Virality
- Post 3-5 times per week minimum
- Batch film: Record 10-30 videos per content pillar per week
- The algorithm rewards consistency more than ever in 2026

### Engagement Rules
- Respond to 100% of comments within 24 hours
- Duet top comments and questions
- Create response videos to popular comments
- Engage with creators in complementary niches
- Use the Q&A feature to turn audience questions into content

### Follower Nurturing (Critical for 2026)
Since the algorithm now tests with followers first:
- Create content that specifically serves your existing audience
- Use polls and Q&As to understand what followers want
- Go live regularly to strengthen follower engagement signals
- Don't chase trends irrelevant to your followers' interests

## What to Avoid

- **Never repost content with watermarks from other platforms** — TikTok down-ranks it
- **Don't delete videos frequently** — can negatively impact account standing
- **Avoid generic trend-jumping without unique value** — you need your own spin
- **Don't mix unrelated topics in one video** — one clear topic per video
- **Never use bots or artificial engagement** — account at risk
- **Don't film vertically then crop** — native vertical (9:16) only
- **Don't ignore your existing followers** — their engagement unlocks wider distribution in 2026

## Profile Optimization

A strong profile is the bridge between a viral video and a new follower. Most profile visits happen within 24 hours of a video performing well — optimize before posting.

| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|--------------|
| **Username** | Simple, memorable, matches other platforms if possible |
| **Display name** | Include a keyword describing what you do (e.g., "Jane • Web Design Tips") |
| **Profile photo** | Real face photo — people follow people, not logos |
| **Bio** | Max 80 chars. What you do + who it's for + reason to follow. One line, no fluff. |
| **Link in bio** | Single destination — landing page, Linktree, or email capture |
| **Pinned videos** | Pin your 3 best-performing or most representative videos |
| **Category** | Set your account category accurately — helps TikTok place you in the right interest cluster |

**Bio formula**: "[What you create] for [who] → [outcome/CTA]"
Example: "Web design tips for small biz owners → Free audit in bio"

## How to Post (Publishing Workflow)

### Option 1: TikTok Content Posting API (Business Accounts)
TikTok provides a Content Posting API for approved business accounts.

```bash
# Requires: TikTok Developer Account, app with video.publish scope
# Access token obtained via OAuth 2.0

# Step 1: Initialize a video upload
POST https://open.tiktokapis.com/v2/post/publish/video/init/
Content-Type: application/json
Authorization: Bearer {access_token}

{
  "post_info": {
    "title": "Your caption text here",
    "privacy_level": "PUBLIC_TO_EVERYONE",
    "disable_duet": false,
    "disable_comment": false,
    "disable_stitch": false,
    "video_cover_timestamp_ms": 1000
  },
  "source_info": {
    "source": "PULL_FROM_URL",
    "video_url": "https://yourhostedvideo.com/video.mp4",
    "chunk_size": 10000000,
    "total_chunk_count": 1
  }
}
```

### Option 2: Scheduling Tools (Buffer, Later, Hootsuite, Publer)
If a scheduling integration is configured, check `skills/tiktok/config.json` for the tool and credentials. Use the tool's API to schedule the post with video file, caption, and hashtags.

### Option 3: Generate-Only Mode (Default)
If no publishing integration is configured:
- Output the full video script (hook, body, payoff)
- Output the caption (copy-ready, under 2,200 chars but ideally keyword-rich and concise)
- Output the hashtag block (3-5 relevant hashtags)
- Flag the best trending sound to use (if applicable)
- Specify ideal posting time based on the account's audience activity

**Always ask**: "Should I post this directly, or generate the content for you to post?"

## TikTok Analytics: Where to Find Each Metric

Navigate to **TikTok Studio** (studio.tiktok.com) or the in-app Creator Tools to find:

| Metric | Where to Find It |
|--------|-----------------|
| Completion rate | Video Analytics → "Watched full video %" |
| Rewatch rate | Video Analytics → "Average watched time" vs video length |
| Traffic source breakdown | Video Analytics → "Traffic source types" (FYP, Search, Profile, Sounds, Hashtags) |
| Follower engagement | Creator Tools → Follower Activity tab |
| Best posting times | Creator Tools → Follower Activity → by hour/day |
| Top-performing content | Profile → Sort by "Most Played" |

**Key rule**: Check analytics 24-48 hours after posting, not immediately. TikTok distributes over time and initial signals can be misleading.
