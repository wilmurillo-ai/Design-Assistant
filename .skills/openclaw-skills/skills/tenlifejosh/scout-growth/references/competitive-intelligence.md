## 1. COMPETITIVE RESEARCH PROTOCOL

### The Three Circles of Competition
```
CIRCLE 1 — Direct Competitors:
  Solve the same problem, for the same audience, at the same price point
  → Highest threat, requires most analysis

CIRCLE 2 — Indirect Competitors:
  Solve the same problem differently (different format, medium, approach)
  → Medium threat, important for positioning

CIRCLE 3 — Alternative Solutions:
  How the audience currently solves the problem WITHOUT a product like yours
  → Often the real competition (DIY, spreadsheets, YouTube videos, consulting)
  → Understand this to craft positioning against "doing nothing"
```

### Research Sources for Competitive Analysis
```
FOR DIGITAL PRODUCTS (Gumroad, KDP, Etsy):
  Gumroad Discover → Category search → Sort by "Popular"
  Amazon → Search product topic → Sort by "Best Sellers"
  Etsy → Search relevant terms → Sort by "Best Sellers"
  Google → "[Topic] guide/workbook/system" + Shopping tab

FOR BRANDS / SERVICES:
  Their website (pricing, positioning, target audience)
  Their social media (what content, what engagement)
  Their reviews (G2, Trustpilot, Capterra, Google Reviews)
  LinkedIn (team size, growth, recent activity)
  Wayback Machine (how have they changed?)

FOR CONTENT CREATORS:
  YouTube (subscriber count, view counts, top videos)
  Podcast charts (Apple/Spotify ranking in category)
  Twitter/X (follower count, engagement rate)
  Newsletter (estimate subscriber count from web presence)
```

### Review Mining (The Goldmine)
```
Amazon/Gumroad/Product Hunt negative reviews reveal:
  - What the product promised but didn't deliver
  - What buyers wish it included
  - What format frustrated them
  - What would have made them give 5 stars instead of 2

PROCESS:
1. Find competitor's product
2. Sort reviews by LOWEST rating (1-2 stars)
3. Read 20-30 negative reviews
4. Extract patterns: what do 3+ reviews say?
5. These patterns = your product opportunities

Example insights from review mining:
  "Too theoretical, not enough practical examples" → Build a practical version
  "Nothing Colorado-specific" → Build a local version
  "Too long, I never finished it" → Build a concise version
  "Doesn't work for parents" → Build a parent-specific version
```

## 3. POSITIONING MAP ANALYSIS

### Two-Axis Positioning Map
```
Choose two dimensions that matter to buyers:
  Axis 1 examples: Price (low → high) | Depth (shallow → deep) | Tone (casual → professional)
  Axis 2 examples: Audience (broad → niche) | Format (digital → physical) | Speed (slow → fast)

Example: Family organization digital products
  Axis 1: Price (low → high)
  Axis 2: Specificity (generic → family-specific)

          Generic
             |
Low price ---|--- High price
             |
         Family-specific

Plot each competitor:
  Competitor A: Low price, Generic (bottom-left)
  Competitor B: High price, Generic (top-left)  
  Competitor C: Medium price, Somewhat specific (center)
  
  Open space: Family-specific + mid-price → OUR OPPORTUNITY
```

### Positioning Gap Analysis
```
After mapping competitors:
1. Where are they clustered? (Crowded = hard to differentiate)
2. Where is there white space? (Open = potential opportunity)
3. Does the white space have buyers? (Only matters if people want what's there)
4. Can we credibly occupy that space? (Fit with our brand and capabilities)

White space without buyers = market gap, not opportunity
White space with buyers + no competitors = opportunity signal
```

## 4. DIFFERENTIATION ANALYSIS

### Differentiation Dimensions
```
Where can we be different?:

AUDIENCE:
  More specific audience (families with autistic kids vs. all families)
  Underserved demographic (single moms, first-generation immigrants)
  Specific life stage (new parents, empty nesters, recent graduates)

PROBLEM:
  More specific problem (morning routine vs. general organization)
  Deeper problem (root cause vs. symptom)
  Adjacent problem (no one else has addressed)

FORMAT:
  More accessible (simpler, shorter, cheaper)
  More comprehensive (deeper, more complete)
  Different medium (audio instead of PDF, physical instead of digital)

TONE/VOICE:
  More authentic (real experience, not corporate advice)
  More direct (no fluff, faster results)
  Faith-aligned (for faith-conscious buyers)

PROOF:
  More specific testimonials
  Before/after data
  Creator's own transformation story

EXPERIENCE:
  Better onboarding
  Community included
  Implementation support
  Updates/improvements over time
```

## 5. PRICING INTELLIGENCE

### Competitive Pricing Research
```
DATA COLLECTION:
  1. List all direct competitors
  2. Record their prices (current)
  3. Record what's included at each price
  4. Note any pricing structure (one-time, subscription, tiered)
  5. Look for bundle or promo pricing

ANALYSIS:
  Price range in market: $[low] – $[high]
  Most common price point: $[X] (mode)
  Average price: $[X]
  Where are best sellers priced? $[X]

PRICE-VALUE MAPPING:
  Draw table: Price | Included content | Stars/reviews
  Look for: Where does value spike? Where is it flat?
  Find: The price point that offers most value per dollar

RECOMMENDATION FRAMEWORK:
  Price BELOW average: Fast buyer acquisition, more competition
  Price AT average: Must compete on content quality and marketing
  Price ABOVE average: Need clear quality signal (design, depth, reputation)
  Price 2x average: Need strong differentiation + proof of results
```

## 6. COMPETITOR CONTENT ANALYSIS

### What Competitor Content Reveals
```
WHAT TO ANALYZE:
  Top social posts (highest engagement) → what resonates with their audience
  Top blog posts (most links/shares) → what topics drive interest
  Best-reviewed content → what buyers love most about them
  Email subject lines → what messaging converts

HOW TO FIND IT:
  Twitter/X: Sort their tweets by "Top" in advanced search
  YouTube: Sort videos by "Most popular"
  Blog: Check SimilarWeb for top pages, or look at comment counts
  Email: Subscribe to their list, analyze for 2-4 weeks

WHAT TO EXTRACT:
  Topics their audience engages with most
  Angles they've missed or underserved
  Questions their audience asks in comments
  Gaps in their content (topics they never cover)
```

## 7. COMPETITIVE SWOT

### Relative SWOT (Us vs. Competitor)
```
## Competitive SWOT: [Our Product] vs. [Competitor]

STRENGTHS (where we're stronger):
  - [Specific advantage we have that they lack]
  - [Feature/quality/price/audience they can't match]

WEAKNESSES (where they're stronger):
  - [Where they have an advantage we currently lack]
  - [What we'd need to neutralize their advantage]

OPPORTUNITIES (what we can exploit):
  - [Gap in their offering we can fill]
  - [Audience they're ignoring]
  - [Their weakness is our strength]

THREATS (what we need to defend against):
  - [If they improve X, it neutralizes our advantage]
  - [If they copy our approach]
  - [External change that helps them more than us]
```

## 8. BATTLECARD FORMAT

### Competitive Battlecard for Sales/Marketing
```
## Competitive Battlecard: [Competitor Name]

WHEN THEY COME UP IN SALES CONVERSATIONS:
  Common objection: "I've seen [Competitor] and it seems similar"
  
OUR RESPONSE:
  Acknowledge: "[Competitor] is solid for [specific audience/use case]"
  Differentiate: "The difference is [specific, honest differentiator]"
  Prove: "Our customers who tried them said [specific feedback]"
  
WHERE WE WIN:
  ✓ [Specific situation where we're clearly better]
  ✓ [Specific audience we serve better]
  ✓ [Feature/quality/price advantage]

WHERE THEY WIN:
  ✓ [Be honest — where are they legitimately stronger?]
  (Knowing this prevents "selling to the wrong person")

IDEAL BUYER FOR US (vs. them):
  US: Buyers who want [specific characteristic]
  THEM: Better fit for buyers who want [different characteristic]
```

## 9. MONITORING COMPETITORS OVER TIME

### Competitive Monitoring Protocol
```
WEEKLY (quick scan, 15 minutes):
  [ ] Check competitor's social media: new posts or campaigns?
  [ ] Check their website: new products, price changes, new copy?
  [ ] Any new reviews posted? (Positive or negative)

MONTHLY (standard review, 1 hour):
  [ ] Update competitor profiles with any changes
  [ ] New competitors entered the space?
  [ ] Any pricing changes across the market?
  [ ] Any content or format innovations worth noting?

QUARTERLY (strategic review, 2 hours):
  [ ] Full competitive landscape refresh
  [ ] Update positioning map
  [ ] Reassess our differentiation — is it still holding?
  [ ] Recommend any product or positioning adjustments based on competitive moves

ALERT TRIGGERS (act immediately):
  - Competitor cuts price by 20%+ (pricing threat)
  - Competitor launches product directly competing with our upcoming launch
  - Competitor gets major press coverage (market validation OR they're pulling ahead)
  - New entrant with significant resources appears in our space
```
