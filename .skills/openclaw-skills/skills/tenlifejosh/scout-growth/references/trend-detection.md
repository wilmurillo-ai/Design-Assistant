# Trend Detection — Reference Guide

Emerging trends, early signals, platform trends, and category growth signals.
Identifying what's rising before it peaks.

---

## 1. TREND ANATOMY

### The Trend Lifecycle
```
STAGE 1 — Emergence (0-6 months):
  Signals: Small Reddit threads, first YouTube videos, under 1K searches/month
  Opportunity: Very high potential, very high risk (market might not develop)
  Action: Watch, gather more signals before investing

STAGE 2 — Early Growth (6-18 months):
  Signals: Growing Google Trends, influencers discussing, first products appearing
  Opportunity: High potential, moderate risk
  Action: Build product NOW, establish early

STAGE 3 — Mainstream Growth (18-36 months):
  Signals: Major media coverage, many products, high search volume
  Opportunity: Proven market, moderate competition
  Action: Compete with differentiation

STAGE 4 — Peak (36+ months):
  Signals: Mainstream awareness, very high competition, commoditizing prices
  Opportunity: Low unless strong differentiation
  Action: Niche down or exit

STAGE 5 — Decline:
  Signals: Falling search trends, fewer new products, negative sentiment
  Action: Wind down, harvest remaining value
```

## 2. TREND DETECTION SOURCES

### Primary Trend Signals
```
GOOGLE TRENDS:
  Key insight: Direction matters more than absolute volume
  Upward slope over 2+ years = growing trend
  Spike and return to baseline = event, not trend
  Steady climb = genuine trend to act on

TWITTER/X TRENDING:
  Real-time signal for immediate events
  Good for: Cultural moments, breaking news
  Not good for: Long-term business trends

AMAZON "New Releases" best sellers:
  What new products are selling = what audiences want NOW
  Check weekly for category you're in

TIKTOK "For You" page in your niche:
  What content is going viral = what topics have mass appeal
  High-view videos on niche topics = potential product demand

REDDIT "Rising" in relevant subreddits:
  New discussions getting traction = emerging community interest
  Check weekly
```

### Secondary Trend Confirmation
```
Trend is confirmed when it appears in 3+ independent sources
Single-source trends = noise, not signal

CONFIRMATION CHECKLIST:
  [ ] Google Trends shows upward slope
  [ ] Multiple Reddit discussions in last 90 days
  [ ] Products appearing on Gumroad/Amazon in this space
  [ ] Influencers discussing it (YouTube, Twitter)
  [ ] Questions appearing in communities

3+ boxes checked = TREND CONFIRMED
1-2 boxes = EMERGING SIGNAL (worth watching)
0 boxes = NOISE (don't act)
```

## 3. GOOGLE TRENDS ANALYSIS PROTOCOL

### Step-by-Step Analysis
```
1. Go to trends.google.com
2. Search the core topic term
3. Set time range to "5 years" (not default 12 months)
4. Note: Is it going up, flat, or down overall?
5. Set geography to "United States" for our market
6. Check "Related topics" and "Related queries"
   → "Breakout" topics = very fast growing, possible early trend
7. Compare to 2-3 related terms using "Compare"
   → Shows relative interest and which term is winning

INTERPRETING SEASONALITY:
  Seasonal peaks are not trends
  Family planning peaks in August (back to school) and January (new year)
  Faith products peak in November-December and Lent season
  "Back to school" is not a productivity trend — it's seasonal
```

## 5. TREND CALENDAR FOR TLC PRODUCTS

### Known Seasonal Patterns
```
FAITH PRODUCTS:
  Lent (Feb/March): Prayer, reflection, scripture products peak
  Advent (December): Devotional, Christmas-focused products
  Easter: Faith renewal, fresh start themes
  New Year: Spiritual goals, prayer journaling

FAMILY PRODUCTS:
  August: Back to school, family organization
  September: Fall routine setting
  January: New year planning, fresh start

CAREER PRODUCTS:
  January: New year career goals
  May/June: Graduation season (First Job Playbook)
  September: Fall job search season

FINANCIAL PRODUCTS:
  January: New year budgeting
  April: Tax season aftermath
  November: Holiday budget planning
```
