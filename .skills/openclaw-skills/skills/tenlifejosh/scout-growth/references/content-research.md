# Content Research — Reference Guide

Identifying performing content, trending topics, keyword gaps, and content opportunity mapping.
Research that drives content strategy and informs what to create next.

---

## 1. CONTENT OPPORTUNITY RESEARCH

### Finding Content That Performs
```
WHAT TO LOOK FOR:
  High engagement + low competition = content opportunity
  High search volume + few quality results = SEO opportunity
  Trending topic + no definitive guide = authority opportunity

SOURCES:
  BuzzSumo: Most shared content on any topic
  AnswerThePublic: Questions people ask about any topic
  Reddit "Top posts all time": What resonates with communities
  YouTube: Videos with high views + poor engagement = underserved topic
  Pinterest: Trending in visual/planning categories
```

### The Content Gap Analysis
```
STEP 1: Map what competitors cover
  List top 5 competitors/creators in your space
  List their most popular content
  
STEP 2: Find what nobody covers well
  Topics with lots of questions but no great answers
  Topics where top results are outdated
  Topics where results are too complex for the target audience
  
STEP 3: Score gaps
  High value gap: High search volume + poor existing content
  Medium value gap: Moderate volume + OK content we could do better
  Low value gap: Low volume but passionate audience
  
STEP 4: Prioritize
  High value + fits our brand → Create
  Medium value + unique angle → Consider
  Low value → Skip unless strategic fit
```

## 2. KEYWORD RESEARCH FOR CONTENT

### Long-Tail Keyword Strategy
```
SEED KEYWORD: "family organization"

LONG-TAIL VARIATIONS:
  "family organization system for working moms"
  "family organization chart printable"
  "how to organize family schedule with multiple kids"
  "family organization binder free printable"
  
WHY LONG-TAIL:
  Less competition
  More specific intent (easier to convert)
  Collectively add up to more volume than head terms
  Tell you exactly what to write about
  
FINDING LONG-TAIL:
  Google autocomplete: Type seed keyword, browse suggestions
  "People also ask" box in Google results
  AnswerThePublic.com
  Ubersuggest free tier
  Reddit: What questions get asked about this topic?
```

## 3. CONTENT PERFORMANCE BENCHMARKS

### Platform Content Benchmarks
```
TWITTER/X:
  Engagement rate <1%: Below average
  1-3%: Average
  3-5%: Good
  5%+: Excellent

YOUTUBE:
  View-to-subscriber ratio >10%: Viral
  3-10%: Good performance
  1-3%: Average
  <1%: Below average

BLOG:
  Bounce rate <60%: Good engagement
  Time on page >2 min: Reading the content
  Return visitor rate >20%: Building audience
```

## 5. TOOL DISCOVERY

### Finding New Tools for TLC
```
SOURCES FOR TOOL DISCOVERY:
  Product Hunt: New tools launched daily (producthunt.com)
  Hacker News "Ask HN": What tools do people recommend?
  Reddit r/webdev, r/SaaS, r/nocode: Community recommendations
  Twitter: "just launched" in any relevant topic area

EVALUATION CRITERIA FOR NEW TOOLS:
  Does it solve a real problem we have?
  What's the cost? (Zero runway = free tiers only)
  How much time to implement?
  What's the alternative we're currently using?
  
TOOL CATEGORIES TO MONITOR:
  Automation: n8n, Make.com, Zapier alternatives
  AI writing: New models/tools that could improve our output
  PDF generation: Better/faster tools than current stack
  Analytics: Better reporting for our products/content
  Distribution: New platforms or channels for digital products
```
