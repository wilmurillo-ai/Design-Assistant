## 2. VOICE & TONE STANDARDS

### The Brand Voice Spectrum
```
                    WHERE WE LIVE
Corporate ←————————————————|——→ Casual/Slangy
Cold ←—————————————————————|——→ Overly warm
Hype/Sales-y ←—————————————|——→ Underconfident
AI-Generated ←—————————————|——→ Authentically Human
Generic ←——————————————————|——→ Specific/Real
```

### Voice Principles with Examples

**1. Direct, Not Corporate**
```
❌ "We are pleased to present our comprehensive digital solution..."
✅ "This is a 47-page system for organizing your family's life."

❌ "Our product leverages cutting-edge methodology..."
✅ "It works because it's actually simple — not clever."
```

**2. Specific, Not Vague**
```
❌ "Help your family stay organized"
✅ "Know who's driving the kids to practice, when the mortgage is due, and what's for dinner — without a 20-minute family meeting every Sunday"

❌ "Perfect for entrepreneurs"
✅ "Built for founders who are also parents, spouses, and people with actual lives"
```

**3. Confident, Not Arrogant**
```
❌ "The world's best family planning system"
✅ "A system that works — tested by families who were drowning in chaos"

❌ "After using this, you'll never need anything else"
✅ "One system, not five apps you forget to check"
```

**4. Real, Not Performed**
```
❌ "We're passionate about empowering families to thrive in today's fast-paced world"
✅ "Joshua built this because he and his wife were losing track of everything. It helped them."

❌ "At Ten Life Creatives, we believe in your potential"
✅ "We make tools that work. You make them useful."
```

**5. Faith-Aware, Not Preachy**
```
❌ "God has a plan for your business and we're here to help you walk in it"
✅ "Built for people who believe the work matters"

❌ "Faith-based productivity that honors the Lord"
✅ "For families who want their home to reflect their values"

The rule: Faith shows in how we work, not in how loudly we announce it.
```

### Tone Modes by Context

| Situation | Tone | Example phrase |
|---|---|---|
| Product marketing | Direct + aspirational | "One system. Zero chaos." |
| Failure/hard topic | Human + grounded | "Three years is a long time to grind." |
| Tutorial/how-to | Clear + encouraging | "Start here. Do this. That's it." |
| Social media | Casual + real | "Nobody tells you this, but..." |
| Email (existing customers) | Warm + direct | "Quick note —" |
| Cold outreach | Confident + specific | "Saw your site. Have an idea." |

### What's Always Off-Brand
```
NEVER:
- Corporate buzzwords: leverage, synergy, ecosystem, scalable, robust
- AI tells: "delve into", "it is worth noting", "furthermore", "holistic"
- Empty openers: "I hope this finds you well", "In today's world..."
- Fake urgency: "Act now before it's too late!" (unless genuinely limited)
- Generic inspirational: "Dream big. Work hard."
- Over-formality: "Please do not hesitate to contact us"
- Underconfident hedging: "This may potentially possibly help you..."
```

## 3. VISUAL IDENTITY STANDARDS

### Color Usage
```
PRIMARY COLORS:
  Deep Navy: #1E2A3A (headlines, primary backgrounds)
  Clean White: #FFFFFF (text backgrounds, space)
  Warm Off-White: #F8F5F0 (secondary backgrounds)

ACCENT COLORS:
  Trust Blue: #2563EB (CTAs, links, accent elements)
  Warm Amber: #D97706 (highlights, secondary accents)
  Success Green: #059669 (confirmation, success states)

FAITH PRODUCT PALETTE (Prayful, scripture products):
  Soft Gold: #B8860B
  Deep Burgundy: #722F37
  Parchment: #F5E6C8

RULES:
  - Never more than 3 colors in one design
  - Navy + white + one accent = standard combination
  - Never use colors not in the brand palette without founder sign-off
  - Faith products can use faith palette; other products use primary palette
```

### Typography
```
HEADINGS:
  Primary: Merriweather Bold (serif, authoritative)
  Alternative (web): Georgia, serif
  
BODY TEXT:
  Primary: Open Sans Regular (clean, readable)
  Alternative (web): Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif

SIZES (standard):
  H1 (main title): 28-32pt
  H2 (section head): 20-24pt
  H3 (subhead): 16-18pt
  Body: 11-12pt
  Caption: 9-10pt
  
RULES:
  - Maximum 2 font families per design
  - Bold used for emphasis, not decoration
  - Never use decorative/script fonts for body text
  - All text must pass WCAG AA contrast requirements (4.5:1)
```

### Logo Usage
```
CLEAR SPACE: Minimum 1x logo height on all sides
MINIMUM SIZE: 100px wide (digital) | 1 inch wide (print)

APPROVED BACKGROUNDS:
  White (standard)
  Navy (inverted — use white logo)
  Light grey (#F5F5F5)

NEVER:
  - Stretch or distort
  - Apply shadow or glow effects
  - Use on busy photographic backgrounds
  - Change colors
  - Use low-resolution version
```

## 4. CROSS-PRODUCT CONSISTENCY

### The Product Family Test
All Ten Life Creatives products should feel like they come from the same company.
If a customer bought FamliClaw and then discovered Legacy Letters, they should immediately recognize the family resemblance.

### Consistency Checkpoints Across Products

```
VISUAL CONSISTENCY:
- [ ] Same color palette (or product-specific sub-palette that coordinates)
- [ ] Same typography family
- [ ] Similar cover design aesthetic (not identical, but recognizable)
- [ ] Similar interior layout approach
- [ ] Logo treatment consistent

VOICE CONSISTENCY:
- [ ] Same authorial voice (Joshua's voice or TLC voice)
- [ ] Same level of directness
- [ ] Same level of formality
- [ ] Same approach to faith references (subtle, not preachy)

QUALITY CONSISTENCY:
- [ ] Page quality consistent across products at same price tier
- [ ] Content depth consistent with price point
- [ ] Completeness consistent (no obviously rushed product next to a polished one)
```

## 5. BRAND CONSISTENCY AUDIT CHECKLIST

### Per-Asset Consistency Check
```
VOICE:
- [ ] Opening line sounds like us (not corporate, not generic)
- [ ] No forbidden phrases or words
- [ ] Confidence level is right (not arrogant, not hedging)
- [ ] Faith references (if any) are authentic and understated

VISUAL (for assets with visual elements):
- [ ] Colors are from brand palette
- [ ] Typography is from brand fonts
- [ ] Logo (if present) uses approved version and placement
- [ ] Images align with brand aesthetic (real, not stock-photo-generic)

MESSAGING:
- [ ] Core value proposition clear and consistent with other products
- [ ] Target audience description consistent
- [ ] Problem/solution framing consistent with brand positioning
- [ ] Price positioning consistent with quality tier

CROSS-REFERENCE:
- [ ] Consistent with what we've said about this product before
- [ ] No contradictions with other live products/content
- [ ] Consistent company description (if described)
```

## 6. COMMON INCONSISTENCY PATTERNS

### Brand Drift Warning Signs
These signal brand drift — catch before they accumulate:

**Voice Drift:**
- New team member or AI-generated content sounds different
- One-off post that's more formal/casual than usual
- Faith references suddenly appearing/disappearing inconsistently

**Visual Drift:**
- A new product with a completely different color scheme
- Logo used at tiny size or on wrong background
- Typography mixed without consistency

**Messaging Drift:**
- Describing target audience differently in different places
- Price positioning inconsistent (premium in some places, discount in others)
- Value proposition shifting between platforms

### When to Flag vs When to Block
```
BLOCK (asset cannot ship):
  - Wrong logo used
  - Colors that are clearly off-brand (nothing close to palette)
  - Voice so off-brand it would confuse customers about whether this is from us

FLAG (note for future, can ship):
  - One phrase that's slightly off-tone
  - Minor color variation (20% off exact brand color)
  - Formality level slightly different from ideal
```

## 7. BRAND EVOLUTION PROTOCOL

### When Brand Can Evolve
Brand standards are not permanent. They evolve when:
- Founder explicitly decides to update brand
- Company expands into new category requiring new identity
- Rebrand decision made (major decision, requires founder)

### How Updates Propagate
1. Founder approves brand change
2. Update brand guide (this document + COMPANY-INTEGRATION.md)
3. Update live assets in priority order (highest visibility first)
4. Document in changelog
5. Notify all agents of the change

### What Stays Constant
Even during brand evolution, these don't change without exceptional reason:
- Authentic, direct voice (core personality)
- Faith-aware but not preachy approach
- Quality-first commitment
- Respect for the buyer
