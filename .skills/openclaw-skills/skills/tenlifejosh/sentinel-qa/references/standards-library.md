# Standards Library — Reference Guide

The company's documented quality standards by asset type. The authoritative reference for
what "good" means at Ten Life Creatives. Updated as standards evolve.

---

## 1. STANDARDS PHILOSOPHY

These are the standards Ten Life Creatives holds itself to. Not aspirational.
Not "nice to have." These are the minimum acceptable quality bar for shipping.

Anything below these standards gets revised until it meets them.

---

## 2. DIGITAL PRODUCT STANDARDS

### PDF Product Quality Standards
```
CONTENT DEPTH:
  Under $10: Minimum 15 pages of substantive content
  $10-$20: Minimum 30 pages of substantive content
  $20-$50: Minimum 50 pages of substantive content
  $50+: Minimum 80 pages OR exceptional depth in fewer pages

VISUAL QUALITY:
  - Cover design: Professional, clean, legible at 200px width
  - Interior layout: Consistent, readable, adequate white space
  - Images (if used): 300 DPI minimum for print products
  - No generic stock photos that look like stock photos

CONTENT QUALITY:
  - Every section delivers standalone value
  - No filler content ("In this chapter, we will discuss...")
  - Exercises/worksheets are genuinely useful, not busywork
  - Writing is clear to a non-expert in the topic

UNIQUENESS:
  - Content is original, not repackaged freely available info
  - Perspective or framework is distinctive
  - Buyer couldn't get this exact thing by Googling for 10 minutes
```

### Product Description Standards
```
MINIMUM REQUIREMENTS:
  - 200+ words
  - Opens with pain/problem (not "This is a guide to...")
  - Clearly states who it's for
  - Lists 3-5 specific outcomes/benefits
  - Describes what's physically in the product
  - Has a clear CTA

QUALITY MARKERS:
  - First 50 words would make a stranger curious
  - Benefits are specific ("save 2 hours/week" not "save time")
  - Social proof included (if available)
  - Price-to-value case implied (buyer feels it's worth it)
```

## 3. EMAIL STANDARDS

### Open Rate Benchmarks
```
COLD EMAIL:
  Acceptable: 20%+ open rate
  Good: 30%+ open rate
  Excellent: 40%+ open rate

WARM LIST (newsletter/existing customers):
  Acceptable: 25%+ open rate
  Good: 35%+ open rate
  Excellent: 45%+ open rate

Click Rate (any email):
  Acceptable: 2%+ click rate
  Good: 4%+ click rate
  Excellent: 7%+ click rate
```

### Email Copy Standards
```
SUBJECT LINE: Creates specific curiosity or states specific benefit
OPENING: First sentence earns continued reading (not pleasantries)
BODY: Single purpose per email (not three separate asks)
CTA: One primary, specific, and frictionless
LENGTH: Short enough to read in under 90 seconds (unless deep value email)
```

## 4. SOCIAL MEDIA STANDARDS

### Post Quality Standards
```
TWITTER/X:
  - Hook stops scroll (does not start with "I")
  - Value delivered (not just noise)
  - Engagement: 1%+ engagement rate on established accounts

LINKEDIN:
  - Professional story or insight (not just announcement)
  - Opening visible before "see more" is compelling
  - Ends with genuine question or insight

INSTAGRAM:
  - Visual carries its own meaning
  - Caption adds value beyond what image shows
  - First 125 chars are strongest
```

## 5. COPYWRITING STANDARDS

### Minimum Copy Quality Bar
```
EVERY piece of copy must:
1. Be free of AI-obvious language patterns
2. Be specific (no generic claims)
3. Match brand voice (direct, authentic, builder energy)
4. Have a clear purpose (what should reader do/feel/think next?)
5. Pass the "would I be proud of this?" test

FAILURE TO MEET ANY ONE = revision required
```

### Headline Standards
```
Strong headlines:
- Specific outcome + specific audience: "How to Double Your Gumroad Revenue in 90 Days (Without Paid Ads)"
- Specific problem acknowledged: "Still Losing Track of Family Schedules? This Fixes It"
- Specific number + benefit: "47 Pages That Organize Your Family's Entire Life"

Weak headlines (always revise):
- "The Ultimate Guide to..."
- "Everything You Need to Know About..."
- "How to Improve Your..."
- Any headline that works for multiple different products
```

## 6. VISUAL STANDARDS

### Design Quality Minimums
```
ALL VISUALS:
  - No blurry or pixelated images
  - No stretched/distorted images
  - Minimum contrast ratio 4.5:1 for text (WCAG AA)
  - Colors from brand palette only

PRODUCT COVERS:
  - Legible title at 50px height (thumbnail size)
  - Professional appearance (not looks-like-a-template)
  - Distinctive enough to recognize in product catalog

SOCIAL IMAGES:
  - Platform-correct aspect ratio
  - Text if any is readable on mobile
  - Brand recognizable at a glance
```
