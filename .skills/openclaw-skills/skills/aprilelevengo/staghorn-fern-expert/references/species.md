# Platycerium Species Reference

Complete profiles of all 18 staghorn fern species for identification and care guidance.

---

## P. alcicorne — Round Shield Staghorn (圆盾鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium alcicorne
- **Difficulty**: Easy | **Bud type**: Multi-bud | **Clade**: A
- **Habitat**: East Africa & Southern Madagascar
- **Light**: Bright indirect | **Water**: Moderate, allow dry season rest | **Humidity**: Moderate 40-60% | **Temp**: Warm 10-35°C
- **Key traits**: Two distinct geographic populations (African and Madagascar) with different trichome forms; African form shows waxy cuticle while Madagascar has dense trichomes; strong dormancy adaptation to seasonal conditions.

## P. andinum — Andean Staghorn (安第斯鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium andinum
- **Difficulty**: Hard | **Bud type**: Single-bud | **Clade**: A
- **Habitat**: Andes Mountains, Peru & Bolivia
- **Light**: Bright indirect | **Water**: Allow to dry slightly | **Humidity**: High 60%+ | **Temp**: Warm montane 15-30°C
- **Key traits**: Only American species in the genus; strongest stellate trichome expression of all species; Gondwanan relict species; extremely susceptible to root rot and requires excellent drainage.

## P. bifurcatum — Common Staghorn (二歧鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium bifurcatum
- **Difficulty**: Easy | **Bud type**: Multi-bud | **Clade**: B
- **Habitat**: Eastern Australia
- **Light**: Bright indirect to partial shade | **Water**: When dry, very forgiving | **Humidity**: Tolerant 40%+ | **Temp**: Very hardy -2 to 40°C
- **Key traits**: Most widely cultivated species worldwide; extremely resilient to varied conditions; breeding workhorse parent; Mt. Lewis lineage parent for many hybrids.

## P. coronarium — Crown Staghorn (皇冠鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium coronarium
- **Difficulty**: Hard | **Bud type**: Multi-bud | **Clade**: B
- **Habitat**: Indochina, Malay Peninsula, Philippines
- **Light**: Bright filtered | **Water**: Keep moist, good drainage | **Humidity**: High 60%+ | **Temp**: Tropical 20-35°C
- **Key traits**: Massive crown-shaped shield fronds; pendulous fertile fronds can reach several meters in length; requires significant growing space and stable tropical conditions.

## P. elephantotis — Elephant Ear Staghorn (象耳鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium elephantotis
- **Difficulty**: Medium | **Bud type**: Multi-bud | **Clade**: A
- **Habitat**: Congo River basin, Central Africa
- **Light**: Filtered light | **Water**: Seasonal: moist in summer, dry rest in winter | **Humidity**: Moderate-high 50-70% | **Temp**: Warm 15-35°C
- **Key traits**: Broad undivided fronds (unique among species); intense dormancy and desiccation tolerance mechanism; requires distinct wet/dry seasonal cycle for proper growth.

## P. ellisii — Ellis's Staghorn (爱丽丝鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium ellisii
- **Difficulty**: Medium | **Bud type**: Single-bud | **Clade**: A
- **Habitat**: Western Madagascar mangroves
- **Light**: Shade to filtered light | **Water**: Keep moist, tolerates humidity | **Humidity**: High 60%+ | **Temp**: Warm tropical 18-30°C
- **Key traits**: Mangrove habitat specialist; salt-spray tolerant (unique adaptation); crownless growth form; naturally compact size ideal for smaller collections.

## P. grande — Giant Staghorn (巨兽鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium grande
- **Difficulty**: Medium | **Bud type**: Single-bud | **Clade**: B
- **Habitat**: Philippines
- **Light**: Partial shade to filtered light | **Water**: Moderate, avoid waterlogging | **Humidity**: Moderate-high 50-70% | **Temp**: Warm tropical 15-35°C
- **Key traits**: Often confused with P. superbum in cultivation; true tropical lowland species; Philippines endemic with regional adaptations.

## P. hillii — Green Staghorn (深绿鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium hillii
- **Difficulty**: Easy | **Bud type**: Multi-bud | **Clade**: B
- **Habitat**: Northeast Australia
- **Light**: Bright filtered | **Water**: Drought tolerant | **Humidity**: Moderate 40-60% | **Temp**: Warm subtropical 5-35°C
- **Key traits**: High environmental stress tolerance; upright growth posture; prominent vascular bundles on fronds; excellent drought tolerance makes it beginner-friendly.

## P. holttumii — Holttum's Staghorn (何其美鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium holttumii
- **Difficulty**: Hard | **Bud type**: Single-bud | **Clade**: B
- **Habitat**: Indochina Peninsula
- **Light**: Bright indirect (understory) | **Water**: Moderate, follow growth cycle | **Humidity**: High 60%+ | **Temp**: Tropical 18-35°C
- **Key traits**: Fixed two growth cycles per year (unique timing); tall erect shield frond form; cold sensitive below 18°C; requires understanding of natural growth rhythm.

## P. madagascariense — Madagascar Staghorn (非洲猴脑鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium madagascariense
- **Difficulty**: Hard | **Bud type**: Single-bud | **Clade**: A
- **Habitat**: Central Madagascar highlands
- **Light**: Medium indirect | **Water**: Keep moist, strict monitoring | **Humidity**: High 60%+ | **Temp**: Cooler highland 15-25°C
- **Key traits**: Smallest species in the genus; waffle-like ridged shield fronds; ant symbiosis chambers; critically heat-intolerant above 28°C; "Snow Ape" hybrid parent.

## P. quadridichotomum — Four-Fork Staghorn (四叉鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium quadridichotomum
- **Difficulty**: Hard | **Bud type**: Single-bud | **Clade**: A
- **Habitat**: Western Madagascar
- **Light**: Bright filtered | **Water**: Bone dry rest in winter, wet in summer | **Humidity**: Moderate 40-60% | **Temp**: Warm 15-35°C
- **Key traits**: Dry-forest specialist; full dormancy with frond curling; overwatering during dormant period is fatal; requires strict seasonal care protocol.

## P. ridleyi — Ridley's Staghorn (亚猴鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium ridleyi
- **Difficulty**: Hard | **Bud type**: Single-bud | **Clade**: B
- **Habitat**: Malay Peninsula, Sumatra
- **Light**: Bright indirect | **Water**: When moss is dry, excellent drainage essential | **Humidity**: High 60-80% | **Temp**: Warm tropical 18-35°C
- **Key traits**: Cabbage-like densely packed shield fronds; ant association; unique "spore spoon" reproductive mechanism; heat-loving, extremely cold-intolerant.

## P. stemaria — Triangle Staghorn (三角鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium stemaria
- **Difficulty**: Medium | **Bud type**: Multi-bud | **Clade**: A
- **Habitat**: West-Central Africa
- **Light**: Shade to filtered light | **Water**: Consistent moisture | **Humidity**: High 60%+ | **Temp**: Warm tropical 18-32°C
- **Key traits**: Broad V-shaped frond morphology; split crown growth form; understory adapted for lower light environments.

## P. superbum — Australian Giant Staghorn (巨大鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium superbum
- **Difficulty**: Medium | **Bud type**: Single-bud | **Clade**: B
- **Habitat**: Western Great Dividing Range, Australia
- **Light**: Filtered sunlight | **Water**: Allow to dry slightly | **Humidity**: Moderate 50%+ | **Temp**: Tolerates cooler 5-35°C
- **Key traits**: Only single-bud Australian species; adapted to drier western slopes; produces 2-4 fronds per year; dwarf cultivars have been developed.

## P. veitchii — Silver Staghorn (立叶鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium veitchii
- **Difficulty**: Easy | **Bud type**: Multi-bud | **Clade**: B
- **Habitat**: Queensland, Australia
- **Light**: High light, tolerates direct sun | **Water**: Drought tolerant, let dry completely | **Humidity**: Low (handles arid) | **Temp**: Very hardy (frost tolerant)
- **Key traits**: Both lithophytic and epiphytic; densest trichome coverage gives silver appearance; tolerates direct sun and frost; key parent for Mt. Lewis and APE hybrids.

## P. wallichii — Butterfly Staghorn (蝴蝶鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium wallichii
- **Difficulty**: Hard | **Bud type**: Single-bud | **Clade**: B
- **Habitat**: Northeast India, Myanmar, Yunnan (China)
- **Light**: Bright indirect | **Water**: Strong dry dormant season | **Humidity**: Moderate 50-70% | **Temp**: Subtropical 10-30°C
- **Key traits**: Only species native to mainland China (Yunnan); dual-trigger dormancy mechanism (both high and low temperature); requires seasonal care adjustment.

## P. wandae — Queen Staghorn (女王鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium wandae
- **Difficulty**: Hard | **Bud type**: Multi-bud | **Clade**: B
- **Habitat**: New Guinea
- **Light**: Bright indirect to direct | **Water**: Consistent moisture, do not dry out | **Humidity**: High 60%+ | **Temp**: Warm tropical 18-35°C
- **Key traits**: True giant species with shields exceeding 2 meters; "curly-leaf queen" dwarf form exists in cultivation; cold exposure below 15°C causes irreversible damage.

## P. willinckii — Java Staghorn (爪哇鹿角蕨)
- **Latin**: Platycerium willinckii
- **Difficulty**: Medium | **Bud type**: Multi-bud | **Clade**: B
- **Habitat**: Java, Bali, East Timor
- **Light**: Bright filtered light | **Water**: Allow slight drying between waterings | **Humidity**: Moderate-high 50-70% | **Temp**: Warm 18-26°C ideal
- **Key traits**: Extremely variable morphology (Bali/Bogor forms); cornerstone hybridization parent; produces DW line and Lewis x Willinckii crosses widely grown today.
