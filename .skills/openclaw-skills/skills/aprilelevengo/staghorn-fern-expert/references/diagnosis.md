# Problem Diagnosis Guide

## Brown Frond Tips
Ranked by likelihood:
1. Low humidity (most common) — tips dry out when humidity < 40%. Fix: mist daily, add humidifier, group plants
2. Underwatering — weight test: if mount feels very light, soak 15-30 min. Fix: adjust watering frequency
3. Sunburn — direct afternoon sun scorches tips. Fix: move to bright indirect light
4. Fertilizer burn — salt buildup on tips. Fix: flush with plain water, reduce fertilizer concentration
5. Cold damage — frost or cold draft exposure. Fix: move away from windows in winter

## Brown Shield Fronds
- THIS IS NORMAL — old shields turn brown as new ones grow over them
- Do NOT remove brown shields — they protect roots and store nutrients
- Only worry if shields are BLACK and MUSHY (see Rot section)

## Yellowing Fronds
1. Overwatering — soggy substrate, no dry period. Fix: let dry between waterings
2. Insufficient light — fronds stretch and pale. Fix: move to brighter location
3. Nutrient deficiency — if fertilizer hasn't been applied in months. Fix: balanced 20-20-20 at half strength

## Dying Plant (Emergency)
Eight causes ranked by likelihood:
1. Overwatering / root rot (#1 killer)
2. Insufficient light
3. Low humidity
4. Cold damage
5. Pest infestation
6. Fertilizer burn
7. Physical damage to crown
8. Disease (fungal/bacterial)

**Crown check (critical):**
- Crown firm + green = alive, recoverable
- Crown soft + black = dead, not recoverable

**Emergency triage for rot:**
1. Remove from mount/pot immediately
2. Cut away ALL black/mushy tissue with sterile blade
3. Dust cut surfaces with cinnamon (natural antifungal)
4. Air dry 24-48 hours in shade
5. Remount on fresh, dry sphagnum
6. No water for 3-5 days after remounting

## Pest Identification
| Pest | Signs | Treatment |
|------|-------|-----------|
| Scale | Brown bumps on fronds, sticky residue | Isopropyl alcohol on cotton swab, manual removal |
| Mealybugs | White cottony masses in frond crevices | Isopropyl alcohol spray, repeat weekly |
| Spider mites | Fine webbing, stippled fronds | Increase humidity, spray water forcefully |
| Fungus gnats | Small flies around base, larvae in moss | Let moss dry more, use yellow sticky traps |
| Thrips | Silver streaks on fronds | Insecticidal soap, isolate plant |

**IMPORTANT: NEVER use neem oil on staghorn ferns — it clogs trichomes and can kill the plant.**
- Safe alternative: DIY insecticidal soap (1 tsp castile soap per liter water)
- For heavy infestations: systemic Imidacloprid

## Dormancy vs Problem
Some species naturally go dormant — this is NOT a problem:
- P. elephantotis: winter dry rest, fronds may brown/curl
- P. quadridichotomum: full dormancy, fronds curl completely. FATAL to water during dormancy
- P. wallichii: dual-trigger dormancy (heat and cold)
- P. veitchii: tolerates drought conditions naturally
