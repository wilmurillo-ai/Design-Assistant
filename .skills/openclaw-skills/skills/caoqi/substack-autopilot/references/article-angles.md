# Article Angle Frameworks

Each angle provides a proven structure for a specific type of Substack article. Adapt content to your brand's niche.

---

## `platform-critique`
**Purpose:** Build authority by exposing systemic problems creators face on major platforms.

**Structure:**
1. **Hook** — A surprising stat or uncomfortable truth
2. **The pattern** — What actually happens (use creator stories/data)
3. **Why platforms do this** — Economic incentives, not malice
4. **What creators can do** — Practical steps (include your platform as one option)
5. **CTA** — Low-friction next step

**Tone:** Analytical, empathetic. Not angry — just clear-eyed.

**Example hook:** "YouTube removed 10 million videos last quarter. Most creators never found out why."

---

## `rewards` / `monetization`
**Purpose:** Explain your platform's value proposition through the creator's financial lens.

**Structure:**
1. **The problem** — How current monetization fails most creators
2. **How our system works** — Clear, jargon-free explanation
3. **Comparison** — Specific contrast with incumbent (YouTube Partner Program requirements, etc.)
4. **How to maximize** — Actionable tips
5. **CTA** — Sign up / start earning

**Tone:** Practical, optimistic. Speak to creators who haven't made money yet.

---

## `anti-censorship`
**Purpose:** Resonate with creators who've been suppressed, shadowbanned, or removed.

**Structure:**
1. **The data** — Removal stats, transparency report numbers
2. **The pattern** — What creators actually experience
3. **Why it happens** — Ad revenue model → brand safety → creator suppression
4. **What creators can do** — Diversify, own your audience, cross-post
5. **CTA** — Your platform as part of the solution

**Tone:** Validating. Creators already know this — your job is to name it clearly and offer a path forward.

---

## `privacy`
**Purpose:** Educate creators on data collection and position your platform as the privacy-respecting alternative.

**Structure:**
1. **What platforms actually collect** — Specific, factual
2. **What it means for your audience** — Viewer data, not just creator data
3. **Why this matters more now** — Regulation, trust, long-term relationships
4. **Your platform's approach** — Specific privacy features
5. **CTA**

**Tone:** Informative, not alarmist. Lead with facts.

---

## `tutorial`
**Purpose:** Drive signups by showing exactly how to get value from your platform.

**Structure:**
1. **What you'll achieve** — Specific outcome (e.g., "rank in the top 50 leaderboard")
2. **Week 1: Setup** — Numbered steps
3. **Week 2: Growth** — Numbered steps
4. **Week 3+: Optimization** — Numbered steps
5. **The compound effect** — Why starting now matters
6. **CTA**

**Tone:** Coach-like. Step-by-step. Assume zero prior knowledge.

---

## `creator-economy`
**Purpose:** Big-picture analysis of why the creator economy is broken and how it should be fixed.

**Structure:**
1. **The headline stat** — Creator economy size vs. creator share
2. **How the value chain works (against creators)** — Follow the money
3. **Why this hasn't changed** — Failed workarounds
4. **What a fixed version looks like** — Criteria for a better model
5. **Your platform's approach** — How you're addressing the problem
6. **CTA**

**Tone:** Analytical, forward-looking. Position your brand as part of the solution, not a utopian promise.
