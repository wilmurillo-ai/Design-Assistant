# Test Prompts

Use these prompts to test whether the Writers Room Story Engine package is behaving correctly.

## 1. Story from zero

```text
Create a compelling original standalone fantasy short story for adults about a healer who is asked to save the tyrant responsible for destroying her village. Do not jump straight into prose. First develop the strongest premise, story core, protagonist engine, ending direction, Story Spine, and major beats, then write the story.
```

## 2. Story in an existing world

```text
Create a new standalone story set in a Star Wars-inspired galaxy where smugglers, monks, and collapsing republic politics all exist. Build only the world details necessary to pressure the protagonist. Do not lore dump. Then develop the foundation and write the story.
```

## 3. Revision test

```text
I have a draft about a young inventor trying to stop a machine from erasing everyone’s memories, but the story feels flat and the middle drags. Diagnose the story from the highest-level problem first. Do not start with line edits. Identify the core weakness, repair the protagonist engine, repair the causal structure, and propose a stronger climax.
```

## 4. Scene-writing test

```text
Here is the premise, protagonist, and beat outline. Turn Beat 4 into a strong scene. Make the character want something specific, face meaningful resistance, reveal pressure through action, use subtext in dialogue, and end with a turn that forces the next scene.
```

## 5. Modular-routing test

```text
I already have a strong premise and protagonist, but the world feels generic and the conflict does not feel inevitable. Do not restart the whole story. Identify what phase the story is in and route only to the skill that should strengthen the world in service of conflict and consequence.
```
