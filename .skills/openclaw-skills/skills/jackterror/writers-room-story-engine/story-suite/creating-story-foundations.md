# Creating Story Foundations

Use this skill when no strong story foundation exists yet.

Its purpose is to generate the structure that makes later scene writing possible.

A strong story foundation includes:
- a compelling premise
- a clear audience hook
- a strong story core
- a protagonist under meaningful pressure
- an ending direction
- causal story movement
- major beats that can later become scenes

## Core principles

- Build the ending direction early.
- Create a protagonist who wants something but is wrong about something.
- Give the story a real tension engine.
- Prefer pressure and contradiction over novelty alone.
- Make the audience lean forward.
- Build beats through therefore/but causality, not and-then sequence.

## Workflow

Copy this checklist if useful:

```text
Story Foundation Progress:
- [ ] Step 1: Generate seed options
- [ ] Step 2: Choose the strongest premise
- [ ] Step 3: Define story core
- [ ] Step 4: Define ending direction
- [ ] Step 5: Build protagonist engine
- [ ] Step 6: Build Story Spine
- [ ] Step 7: Build major beats through causality
```

## Step 1: Generate seed options

Create 3 possible story seeds.
Each seed should include:
- concept
- emotional hook
- possible audience
- built-in conflict
- possible thematic tension

Choose seeds that offer:
- contradiction
- pressure
- meaningful stakes
- escalation potential
- interesting choices

Avoid seeds that are only aesthetic, vague, or twist-dependent.

## Step 2: Choose the strongest premise

Select the seed with the best combination of:
- audience curiosity
- emotional charge
- conflict engine
- protagonist pressure
- ending potential

Then define the premise clearly.

Good premise test:
- who is it about?
- what destabilizes their world?
- what must they pursue, survive, confront, or sacrifice?
- what makes this difficult in a way that promises movement?

## Step 3: Define story core

State:
- the essence of the story in 1 or 2 sentences
- what the story is really about underneath plot
- what emotional or thematic tension drives it
- why this story matters to the audience

This is the center of gravity.
If the core is vague, later scenes will drift.

## Step 4: Define ending direction

Define:
- what the climax must test
- what belief or behavior must be confronted
- what final cost, choice, transformation, revelation, or sacrifice the ending should force
- what kind of emotional resolution the story should earn

You do not need every detail.
But you need the direction.

## Step 5: Build protagonist engine

Define:
- want
- need
- lie
- ghost
- spine
- comfort zone
- opposite pressure
- stakes

### Definitions

#### Want
What do they consciously pursue?

#### Need
What deeper truth or change do they actually need?

#### Lie
What false belief are they living by?

#### Ghost
What past wound, loss, fear, shame, or unfinished event shaped that lie?

#### Spine
What is the central through-line of action for this character in this story?

#### Comfort zone
What stable pattern, identity, or survival mode are they trapped in at the start?

#### Opposite pressure
What force, person, world, or event pressures them away from that comfort zone?

#### Stakes
What is lost if they fail, personally and externally?

## Step 6: Build Story Spine

Use:
- Once upon a time...
- Every day...
- But one day...
- Because of that...
- Because of that...
- Until finally...
- Ever since then...

Make the spine causal and compressive.
It should reveal the shape of the story, not just summarize events.

## Step 7: Build major beats through causality

Create major beats with therefore/but logic.
Each beat should:
- arise from prior action or consequence
- create a new problem, shift, cost, or choice
- intensify pressure
- move toward the climax

Check for:
- no passive drift
- no repeated beat function
- no disconnected detours
- no and-then plotting

## Diagnostics

### If the premise feels weak
Check for:
- no built-in tension
- no contradiction
- no real pressure
- no audience hook

### If the protagonist feels generic
Check for:
- weak lie
- weak ghost
- no meaningful contradiction
- no strong opinion or bias
- no pressure against comfort zone

### If the beats feel flat
Check for:
- sequence without causality
- escalation missing
- too many similar turns
- midpoint not changing direction

## Default output format

### Seed options
- 3 premises with hooks and tension

### Selected premise
- strongest premise and why it wins

### Story core
- essence, thematic tension, audience promise

### Ending direction
- what the ending must test and earn

### Protagonist engine
- want, need, lie, ghost, spine, comfort zone, opposite pressure, stakes

### Story Spine
- full spine

### Major beats
- causal beat chain using therefore/but logic
