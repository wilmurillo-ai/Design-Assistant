# Message rules

## Tone

Keep messages calm, practical, and brief.
Do not sound excited just because aurora activity increased.
Do not bury the action in a technical explanation.

## Good communication outcome

The user should be able to answer these questions immediately:
- Is tonight worth attention?
- Is Dereham enough, or is the coast materially better?
- What is the best window?
- Is this mainly a camera chance or a decent by-eye chance?
- Should I act now or wait for the next update?

## Alert suppression

Do not send repeated updates for small changes.
Suppress a new message unless one of the material-change thresholds was hit.

## Confidence labels

Use:
- low: stale, conflicting, or weak signals
- medium: decent agreement but still weather-sensitive
- high: strong agreement with favorable sky conditions

## Chance language

Map the percentages to plain English as follows:
- 0% to 19%: not worth planning around
- 20% to 34%: worth watching, still marginal
- 35% to 54%: a real candidate night, coast may be justified
- 55% and up: unusually strong candidate for Norfolk if sky allows

## By-eye guidance

Prefer these phrases:
- camera-only likely
- possible by eye
- good by-eye chance

Avoid stronger claims unless the signal is exceptional and sky conditions support it.
