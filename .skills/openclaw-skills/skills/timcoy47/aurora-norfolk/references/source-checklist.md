# Source checklist

Collect enough information to make a practical viewing judgment.

## Space weather

Check:
- short-lead aurora guidance
- broader geomagnetic outlook for the next 3 days
- whether current conditions are strengthening, stable, or fading

Look for:
- whether the auroral oval is far enough south to matter for Norfolk
- whether the timing overlaps with darkness in the UK
- whether the setup supports only camera detection or a reasonable by-eye chance

## Local sky conditions

Check for both Dereham and the chosen coast point:
- cloud cover in the likely viewing window
- visibility or haze proxy if available
- rain, mist, or low cloud that would ruin the attempt
- whether the coast appears materially clearer than inland

## Sanity checks

Cross-check with a UK-facing aurora alert source when available.

If the UK-facing signal looks weak and the local sky is poor, bias toward stand_down or watch.
