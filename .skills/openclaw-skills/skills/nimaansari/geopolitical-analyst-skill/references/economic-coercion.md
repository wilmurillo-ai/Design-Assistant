# Economic Coercion & Weaponized Interdependence

## Purpose

Modern conflicts often use economic weapons instead of military ones:
- Currency manipulation
- Trade wars
- Financial pressure
- Supply chain disruption
- Debt weaponization

Understanding economic coercion means predicting:
- When will economic pressure translate to political concession?
- What's the breaking point?
- Can target resist?
- How does it escalate to war?

---

## Economic Coercion Methods

### Method 1: Currency Manipulation
**Mechanism:** Artificially weaken/strengthen currency to create economic pain

**Example:**
- Rapid depreciation = inflation, purchasing power loss
- Artificial appreciation = exports uncompetitive

**Effectiveness:** MEDIUM (difficult to sustain; market forces push back)
**Timeline to effect:** Weeks to months
**Political pressure it creates:** MEDIUM (affects population purchasing power)

### Method 2: Trade War / Tariffs
**Mechanism:** Impose tariffs to block imports, force negotiations

**Example:** US imposes 25% tariffs on Chinese goods

**Effectiveness:** HIGH (hurts exporters immediately)
**Timeline to effect:** Days (exporters feel pain)
**Political pressure:** HIGH (business lobbies hard for negotiation)

### Method 3: Financial Sanctions
**Mechanism:** Freeze assets, block financial transactions, seize foreign reserves

**Example:** Freeze oligarch accounts; block central bank reserves

**Effectiveness:** VERY HIGH (immediate pain)
**Timeline to effect:** Hours (money stops flowing)
**Political pressure:** VERY HIGH (financial elites demand resolution)

### Method 4: Supply Chain Weaponization
**Mechanism:** Control critical input (semiconductors, energy, food)

**Example:** Restrict chip sales; cut energy supply; block food imports

**Effectiveness:** VERY HIGH (target can't substitute)
**Timeline to effect:** Days to weeks (depends on supply)
**Political pressure:** VERY HIGH (populations demand resolution)

### Method 5: Debt Weaponization
**Mechanism:** Hold target's debt; threaten default, inflation

**Example:** If target holds sender's debt, can dump it; threatens sender's credit

**Effectiveness:** MEDIUM-HIGH (works if debt exposure is high)
**Timeline to effect:** Days (markets react immediately)
**Political pressure:** HIGH (affects interest rates, investment)

### Method 6: Remittance Disruption
**Mechanism:** Block money transfers to target country

**Example:** Freeze transfers from diaspora; blocks income to families

**Effectiveness:** MEDIUM (affects families but not government)
**Timeline to effect:** Days (families immediately affected)
**Political pressure:** MEDIUM (domestic pressure from families)

## Escalation Ladder: Economic to Military

Economic coercion often escalates to military conflict:

```
Economic pressure (Stage 1)
    ↓
Target adapts (Stage 2)
    ↓
Sender escalates (Stage 3)
    ↓
Target retaliates economically (Stage 4)
    ↓
Coalition breaks / coercion fails (Stage 5)
    ↓
Sender considers military option (Stage 6)
    ↓
Military conflict begins (Stage 7)
```

## Breaking Point Assessment

When does economic coercion force capitulation?

**Factors:**
- **Severity:** How much economic pain?
- **Duration:** How long can target endure?
- **Alternative:** Can target find alternative sources?
- **Political elasticity:** How much can population endure before demanding change?
- **Leader's resolve:** Will leader submit or fight?
