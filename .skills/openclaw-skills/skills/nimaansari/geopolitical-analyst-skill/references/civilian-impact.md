# Civilian Impact Assessment & Humanitarian Consequences

## Purpose

Predict humanitarian consequences of conflict:
- Casualty estimates
- Displacement scale
- Food security impact
- Healthcare system collapse
- Atrocity risk

---

## Casualty Estimation

**Methodology:**
- Combat deaths (military casualties)
- Civilian deaths from direct violence
- Excess deaths from disease, malnutrition
- Psychological trauma, refugees

**Estimation model:**
- Conflict duration estimate × average daily casualty rate from similar conflicts
- Adjust for population size, infrastructure, healthcare

**Example:**
- Similar conflicts in this terrain = 50-100 civilian deaths per day
- Conflict lasts 6 months = 9,000-18,000 civilian deaths
- Plus military casualties (50-100 per day) = 18,000-36,000 total

---

## Displacement Estimation

**Factors:**
- Pre-war population
- Conflict intensity and spread
- Nearby safe havens
- Road network (can people flee?)
- Government restrictions

**Rule of thumb:**
- Minor conflict: 5-10% displaced
- Moderate conflict: 10-30% displaced  
- Severe conflict: 30-50% displaced
- Total war: 50%+ displaced

---

## Food Security Impact

**Mechanisms:**
- Planting disrupted → next harvest fails
- Supply routes cut → starvation
- Infrastructure destroyed → distribution fails
- Economic collapse → can't buy food

**Timeline:** Crisis within weeks if agriculture interrupted; months if trade disrupted

---

## Healthcare Collapse

**Signal:** If hospitals destroyed or medical supplies cut, disease outbreaks follow

**Timeline:** Weeks to months for epidemic

---
