# Historical Analogs & Comparative Case Analysis

## Purpose

"Those who don't learn from history are condemned to repeat it."

Historical analogs help predict outcomes by comparing:
- Current situation to past similar situations
- What happened then?
- What factors predicted outcome?
- Are those factors present now?

---

## Finding Good Analogs

**Good analog has:**
- Similar actors (or actors with similar interests)
- Similar power balance
- Similar geographic/strategic context
- Similar conflict type
- Similar historical moment

**Bad analog:**
- Superficially similar but fundamentally different
- Different actors with different motivations
- Different technological capability
- Different historical context

## Common Historical Patterns

### Pattern 1: Alliance Cohesion
**Pattern:** Alliances hold during existential threat; dissolve after threat ends

**Examples:** NATO held during Cold War; nearly dissolved after 1991

**Current application:** If threat level changes, predict alliance stress

### Pattern 2: Economic Nationalism
**Pattern:** Economic crisis → nationalism → protectionism → trade war

**Examples:** 1930s Great Depression → WWII; 2008 crisis → Brexit, Trump

**Current application:** If economic stress high, expect nationalist movements

### Pattern 3: Military Escalation Spirals
**Pattern:** Once military incidents start, de-escalation becomes nearly impossible

**Examples:** WWI (arms race → tensions → war), Korean War (border skirmish → full war)

**Current application:** If incidents occurring, predict escalation

### Pattern 4: Leadership Change Shifts Policy
**Pattern:** New leader often reverses predecessor's alliances/policies

**Examples:** Egypt (Nasser → Sadat reversed Soviet alliance), many countries

**Current application:** Upcoming leadership change = predict foreign policy shift
