# Scenario Construction Reference

## Purpose

The scenario framework (used in FULL assessments) forces the analyst to think beyond the most likely outcome and identify tail risks before they materialize.

---

## Three-Scenario Rule

Always generate exactly three scenarios for any FULL assessment:

| Scenario | Probability Band | Construction Rule |
|----------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Base Case** | Most likely | Extrapolate current trajectory. What happens if nothing changes dramatically? |
| **Alternative** | Plausible | One key variable changes. What if the main actor makes a different choice? |
| **Tail Risk** | Low probability, high impact | What is the worst credible outcome? Not fantasy — must have a plausible trigger |

---

## Scenario Structure

Each scenario must include:

```
Scenario name: [Short descriptive label]
Probability: HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW
Trigger condition: [The specific event or decision that activates this scenario]
Timeline: [When this could materialize: days / weeks / months]
Key indicator: [The single early-warning signal to watch]
Implication: [Who is affected, how, and at what scale]
```

---

## Probability Calibration

Do not use arbitrary probability assignments. Calibrate based on:

| Factor | Pushes probability UP | Pushes probability DOWN |
|--------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Historical precedent | Strong precedent in same region | No prior occurrence |
| Actor incentives | Actor gains significantly | Actor gains little or loses |
| Capability | Actor has the means | Actor lacks the means |
| External pressure | Reinforcing pressures | Countervailing pressures |
| GDELT trend | Trend confirms direction | Trend contradicts direction |
| Time pressure | Deadline or trigger event approaching | No forcing function |

## Common Scenario Archetypes for Geopolitical Events

### Conflict Escalation Scenarios
- **Horizontal escalation:** Conflict spreads geographically to neighboring territory
- **Vertical escalation:** Conflict intensifies (conventional → unconventional, tactical → strategic)
- **Third-party intervention:** External actor enters the conflict directly
- **Proxy activation:** Regional proxy force opens new front

### Conflict Resolution Scenarios
- **Negotiated ceasefire:** Mediated pause in fighting
- **Military decision:** One side achieves decisive advantage
- **Stalemate freeze:** No winner, conflict freezes along current lines (Korea/Ukraine model)
- **Collapse:** One party's political/military structure collapses

### Political Crisis Scenarios
- **Managed transition:** Orderly transfer of power
- **Contested succession:** Multiple factions claim legitimacy
- **Military intervention:** Armed forces seize power or impose solution
- **Popular uprising:** Mass mobilization forces political change

## Indicator Tracking

For each scenario, specify 1–2 measurable indicators to monitor:

**Military indicators:**
- Troop mobilization / repositioning
- Air defense activation
- Naval movements
- Cross-border fire incidents (frequency, type, origin)

**Political indicators:**
- Leadership rhetoric changes (ultimatum language)
- Diplomatic personnel movements (withdrawals, expulsions)
- Emergency legislative sessions
- Coalition formation / defection

**Economic indicators:**
- Currency movement
- Capital flight signals
- Energy price correlation
- New sanctions designations

**Media/Information indicators:**
- GDELT tone trend shift > 2 points in 48h
- State media narrative shift
- Social media mobilization spikes
- Foreign correspondent departures

## Tail Risk Identification Checklist

Before finalizing scenarios, check:

- [ ] Is there a low-probability path to regional war?
- [ ] Could a third party be drawn in unexpectedly?
- [ ] Is there a WMD/CBRN dimension, however unlikely?
- [ ] Could domestic political collapse follow from this event?
- [ ] Are there economic contagion risks (energy, food, financial)?
- [ ] Is there a humanitarian catastrophe scenario?

If yes to any: include it as the Tail Risk scenario, clearly labeled with its trigger condition.
