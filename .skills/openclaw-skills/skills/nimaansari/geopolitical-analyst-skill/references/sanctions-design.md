# Sanctions Design & Effectiveness Framework

## Purpose

Sanctions are the primary coercive tool for democracies. But most sanctions fail.

This module explains:
- What makes sanctions work?
- What makes them fail?
- How to design sanctions that actually achieve objectives?
- How to measure effectiveness?

---

## When Sanctions Work

### Condition 1: Clear Objective
**Requirement:** Sanction sender has specific, achievable goal

**Good objective:** "Stop development of nuclear weapons"
**Bad objective:** "Change government behavior" (too vague)

**Why it matters:** Target must know what it takes to lift sanctions. If goal is vague, target can't comply even if willing.

### Condition 2: Achievable Objective
**Requirement:** Goal is within target's power to achieve

**Achievable:** "Release prisoners" (government can order release)
**Not achievable:** "Improve human rights" (requires cultural change, takes decades)

**Why it matters:** If goal is impossible, sanctions just punish without changing behavior.

### Condition 3: Credible Threat of Escalation
**Requirement:** If target doesn't comply, sanctions will intensify

**Credible:** "First round: financial sector. Second round: energy. Third round: broad trade war"
**Not credible:** "This is the final sanction" (with no escalation path)

**Why it matters:** Target must believe sanctions will get worse. Otherwise, target waits out current pain.

### Condition 4: Coalition Holding
**Requirement:** All major trading partners enforce sanctions together

**Effective coalition:** US + EU + Japan + allies (covers 80%+ of trade)
**Broken coalition:** US sanctions; China + Russia trade with target

**Why it matters:** If target can trade elsewhere, sanctions are painless.

## Why Sanctions Fail

### Failure Mode 1: No Clear Objective
**Symptom:** Sanction sender says "to punish" or "to pressure" without specific goal
**Result:** Target doesn't know what to do to lift sanctions; complies only if cost is unbearable
**Historical example:** Most unilateral sanctions fail because of vague objectives

### Failure Mode 2: Goal is Impossible
**Symptom:** Objective requires cultural change, regime change, or fundamental shift
**Result:** Target can never comply; sanctions just become ongoing punishment
**Historical example:** US sanctions on Cuba (60+ years) unchanged behavior; sanctions just punished

### Failure Mode 3: Coalition Breaks
**Symptom:** Some countries enforce; others trade with target
**Result:** Target gets circumvention routes; sanctions become costly to sender, painless to target
**Historical example:** Iran sanctions (US enforced; Europe, China traded anyway) partially ineffective

### Failure Mode 4: Target Has Alternative
**Symptom:** Target can source goods elsewhere or has natural resources to sell
**Result:** Target resists pressure by finding alternative suppliers
**Historical example:** Russia post-2022 (found alternative suppliers; some sectors unaffected)

### Failure Mode 5: Domestic Opposition in Sender Country
**Symptom:** Sanctions hurt sender's business; business lobbies for removal
**Result:** Sender politics shift; sanctions lifted before goal achieved
**Historical example:** US agricultural sanctions on China (farmers lobby → partial lifting)

### Failure Mode 6: Sanctions Backfire (Rally Around Flag Effect)
**Symptom:** Population in target country rallies behind government against foreign pressure
**Result:** Sanctions strengthen target government; opposition weakens
**Historical example:** Zimbabwe (sanctions strengthened Mugabe domestically)

## Effective Sanctions Design

### Principle 1: Targeted Sanctions, Not Broad
**Design:** Target specific sectors/individuals, not whole economy

**Effective:** Sanction oil sector (hurts revenue), sanction key officials (personal cost)
**Ineffective:** Sanction everything (creates nationalist backlash; target prepares for war)

### Principle 2: Cost Escalation Path
**Design:** Make clear path for intensification if target doesn't comply

**Example:**
- Round 1: Targeted financial sanctions
- Round 2: Energy sector (if not complying)
- Round 3: Trade war (if still not complying)

**Why it works:** Target knows cost will increase unless it complies.

### Principle 3: Off-Ramp for Compliance
**Design:** Make clear what compliance looks like and how sanctions will be lifted

**Bad design:** Sanctions stay indefinitely even if target complies
**Good design:** Specific conditions for lifting; transparent timeline

**Why it works:** Target believes sanctions are temporary if it complies.

### Principle 4: Coalition Maintenance
**Design:** Constant diplomatic effort to keep coalition united

**Mechanism:** Regular meetings, shared enforcement, side payments to reluctant members
**Cost:** Significant diplomatic capital
**Result:** Coalition holds; target has no escape route

## Sanctions Effectiveness Measurement

### Metric 1: Compliance
**Question:** Did target change behavior as requested?
- YES: Sanctions succeeded
- PARTIAL: Sanctions worked but target negotiated limit
- NO: Sanctions failed

### Metric 2: Target Cost vs Sender Cost
**Question:** Did target suffer more than sender?
- Balanced: Both pay roughly equal cost
- Target high: Sanctions working effectively
- Sender high: Coalition breaking; sanctions backfiring

### Metric 3: Duration to Compliance
**Question:** How long until target complies?
- Fast (months): Effective sanctions
- Slow (years): Partially effective
- Never: Sanctions failed

### Metric 4: Secondary Effects
**Question:** Did sanctions cause unintended consequences?
- Humanitarian crisis: Sanctions may backfire
- Regime strengthened: Sanctions backfired
- Coalition breaking: Sanctions becoming ineffective

## Sanctions Design Template

```markdown
## Sanctions Assessment: [Sanction regime]

### Objective
**Stated objective:** [What is sender trying to achieve?]
**Clarity:** CLEAR / VAGUE
**Achievability:** ACHIEVABLE / DIFFICULT / IMPOSSIBLE

### Sanctions Mechanism
**Type:** [Targeted / Broad / Financial / Trade / Energy / Other]
**Intensity:** [Light / Moderate / Severe]
**Escalation path:** [What's next if target doesn't comply?]

### Coalition
**Enforcers:** [Which countries?]
**Coverage:** [% of target's trade?]
**Holding:** STRONG / CRACKING / BREAKING
**Escape routes:** [Where can target trade instead?]

### Target Cost
**Economic impact:** [Estimate %GDP loss]
**Domestic pressure:** [Increasing / Stable / Decreasing]
**Compliance incentive:** [Strong / Moderate / Weak]

### Compliance Status
**Target response:** [Complying / Negotiating / Resisting / Escalating]
**Likelihood of compliance:** [High / Medium / Low]
**Timeline to compliance:** [Months / Years / Never]

### Failure Risk
**Coalition breaking risk:** [High / Medium / Low]
**Backfire risk (rally-around-flag):** [High / Medium / Low]
**Secondary humanitarian consequences:** [High / Medium / Low]

### Probability of Success
**Overall effectiveness probability:** [%]
**Timeline to success:** [If it happens, when?]
```
