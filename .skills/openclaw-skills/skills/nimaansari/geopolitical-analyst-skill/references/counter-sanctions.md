# Counter-Sanctions & Retaliation Strategy

## Purpose

When sanctioned, targets retaliate with their own sanctions. This creates sanctions spiral.

Understanding counter-sanctions means predicting:
- How will target retaliate?
- What leverage does target have?
- Will retaliation break the sender's coalition?
- Can sanctions spiral be managed?

---

## Counter-Sanction Leverage

### Target's Available Leverage Points

**1. Energy Supply**
- Oil/gas exports if target is major exporter
- Example: Russia cuts gas to Europe; OPEC cuts oil

**2. Critical Materials**
- Rare earth elements, minerals, agricultural products
- Example: China threatens rare earths restrictions

**3. Debt/Financial**
- If target holds sender's debt, can dump it
- Example: Russia dumps US Treasuries

**4. Trade Dependency**
- If sender depends on target's goods, retaliate by cutting them
- Example: China restricts rare earths, agricultural exports

**5. Currency/Financial System**
- If target controls alternative payment system, use it
- Example: Russia-China reduce SWIFT dependence

---

## Counter-Sanctions Options

### Option 1: Mirror Sanctions
**What:** Copy sender's sanctions; sanction sender's equivalent sectors
- Example: US sanctions Russia oil → Russia sanctions US grain
- Effectiveness: LOW-MEDIUM (depends on symmetry of damage)

### Option 2: Asymmetric Retaliation
**What:** Target sender's vulnerabilities differently than sender targets target
- Example: US sanctions Russia; Russia hacks US infrastructure
- Effectiveness: HIGH (can cause disproportionate damage)

### Option 3: Coalition Fracture
**What:** Offer inducements to coalition members to defect
- Example: Russia offers cheap energy to countries that lift sanctions
- Effectiveness: MEDIUM (depends on coalition cohesion)

### Option 4: Domestic Support
**What:** Support opposition/dissidents in sender country
- Example: Russia-backed media in US, disinformation campaigns
- Effectiveness: MEDIUM-HIGH (political pressure on sender)

---

## Sanctions Spiral Dynamics

Once counter-sanctions begin, spiral often escalates:

```
Sender imposes sanctions
    ↓
Target retaliates with counter-sanctions
    ↓
Sender escalates sanctions
    ↓
Target escalates counter-sanctions
    ↓
... spiral continues until:
- One side backs down
- Both exhaust options
- War begins
```

---

## De-escalation from Sanctions Spiral

**Options:**
1. **Negotiated Lift** — Both sides agree to remove simultaneously
2. **Phased De-escalation** — Gradual mutual sanctions removal
3. **Third-Party Mediation** — Mediator negotiates lift
4. **Face-Saving Mechanism** — Find way for both sides to claim victory and lift

**Probability of success:** LOW-MEDIUM (requires trust and good faith)
