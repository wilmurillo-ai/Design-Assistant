# Technology Dependencies & Geopolitical Leverage

## Purpose

Technology is a primary domain of great-power competition. Control of critical technologies = geopolitical leverage.

Understanding tech dependencies means predicting:
- Who controls critical technologies?
- Who depends on whom?
- How vulnerable is each actor to tech disruption?
- What would happen if supply cut?

---

## Critical Technologies

### Category 1: Semiconductors
**Who produces:** Taiwan (TSMC majority), South Korea (Samsung), US (Intel), China (limited)
**Who depends:** Everyone (all modern electronics need chips)
**Leverage:** TSMC disruption = global economy disruption
**Current risk:** Taiwan independence crisis = chip supply crisis

### Category 2: Rare Earth Elements
**Who produces:** China (70% global supply), US (10%), others (20%)
**Who depends:** US, Europe, Japan (military, clean energy)
**Leverage:** China can restrict supplies to coerce
**Current risk:** US-China tech competition = supply restrictions

### Category 3: AI Capabilities
**Who leads:** US (OpenAI, Google), China (Alibaba, Baidu)
**Who depends:** Everyone (AI becoming essential)
**Leverage:** Control of AI = control of future tech
**Current risk:** US-China AI race = weaponized AI

### Category 4: 5G/6G Networks
**Who produces:** China (Huawei), US (Cisco), Europe (Nokia, Ericsson)
**Who depends:** All countries need telecom infrastructure
**Leverage:** Network control = surveillance/sabotage capability
**Current risk:** Western exclusion of Huawei = alternative networks emerging

### Category 5: Cloud/Data Infrastructure
**Who dominates:** US (Amazon AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud)
**Who depends:** Global companies, governments
**Leverage:** US dominates data storage = potential surveillance/access
**Current risk:** Countries want data sovereignty = decentralization of cloud

## Vulnerability Assessment

For each critical technology:

| Tech | Who Controls | Who Depends | Vulnerability | Leverage |
|------|---|---|---|---|
| Semiconductors | Taiwan | Global | CRITICAL | VERY HIGH |
| Rare earths | China | US/allies | HIGH | HIGH |
| AI | US/China | Global | HIGH | HIGH |
| 5G | China/West | Global | MEDIUM | MEDIUM |
| Cloud | US | Global | MEDIUM | MEDIUM |

## Tech Dependency Assessment Template

```markdown
## Technology Dependency Analysis: [Country]

### Critical Technology Dependencies
**Technology 1:** [Which technology?]
- Current supplier: [Country]
- Alternative suppliers: [Countries or none]
- Substitutability: [Can be replaced / Difficult / Impossible]
- Time to substitute: [Months / Years / Decades]
- Vulnerability: [HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW]

### Technology Control
**Technology 1:** [Which technology?]
- Does [country] control production: [Yes / Partial / No]
- Market share: [%]
- Leverage if restricted: [HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW]

### Supply Chain Risk
**Bottleneck technologies:** [Which techs are bottlenecks?]
**Single-source risk:** [Are any technologies sourced from single country?]
**Redundancy:** [Can supply chains be redundant?]

### Geopolitical Implications
**If supply disrupted, impact on [country]:** [Economic / Military / Societal]
**Probability of disruption:** [High / Medium / Low]
**Timeline to alternatives:** [How long to develop indigenous tech?]
```
