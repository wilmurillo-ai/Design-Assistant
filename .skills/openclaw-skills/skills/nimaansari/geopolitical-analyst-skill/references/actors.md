# Actor Classification Reference

## Taxonomy

| Class | Code | Definition | Sub-types |
|-------|------|-----------|----------|
| State | `STATE` | Official government institutions and their armed forces | Government, Military, Intelligence, Police, Paramilitary (state-directed) |
| Non-State Armed | `NSA` | Armed groups operating outside state control | Insurgent, Militia, PMC, Warlord faction, Resistance movement |
| Proxy | `PROXY` | Actors operating on behalf of an external state power | State-backed militia, Foreign expeditionary force, Covert operative network |
| International/Regional Body | `IO` | Multilateral organizations | UN agencies, Regional bodies (EU/AU/AL/NATO/SCO/etc.), Treaty organizations |
| Civil Society | `CIVIL` | Non-armed non-state actors | NGOs, Protest movements, Media organizations, Religious institutions |
| Economic Actor | `ECON` | Actors whose primary role is economic | Corporations, Sanctioned entities, Central banks, Trade blocs |

**Terminology rule:** Terms such as "terrorist", "extremist", "freedom fighter", "rogue state", or "liberation movement" are politically contested and carry different meanings across legal and political systems. Do not apply them as analytical labels. Instead:
- Use the actor's organizational name
- Note their classification by specific authorities when relevant: *"designated as a terrorist organization by [US/EU/UN — specify which]"*
- Note their self-description when relevant: *"describes itself as a resistance movement"*
- Use behavioral descriptors: *"uses suicide bombing tactics"*, *"targets civilian infrastructure"*, *"engages in political assassination"*

The same actor may be simultaneously designated by one authority and recognized as a legitimate political actor by another. Both facts are analytically relevant and should be stated.

## Capability Scale

Rate relative to other actors in the specific event — not globally:

| Rating | Definition |
|--------|-----------|
| HIGH | Can independently shape the outcome of this event |
| MEDIUM | Can significantly influence but not determine outcome |
| LOW | Limited ability to affect outcome without external support |
| UNKNOWN | Insufficient data to assess |

---

## Interest Typology

When identifying actor interests, use these categories:

| Type | Examples |
|------|---------|
| **Territorial** | Land control, buffer zones, border changes |
| **Political** | Regime survival, electoral advantage, legitimacy |
| **Economic** | Resource access, trade routes, sanctions relief |
| **Security** | Threat reduction, arms access, alliance building |
| **Ideological** | Religious/ethnic expansion, ideology export |
| **Reputational** | International standing, credibility signaling |
