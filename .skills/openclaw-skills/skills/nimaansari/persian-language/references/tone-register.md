# Persian Tone & Register Guide

## Register Spectrum

Persian has a wide gap between formal written language and everyday spoken language — wider than English. Choosing the right register is critical for sounding natural.

```
خیلی رسمی (Very Formal) ←——————————→ خیلی محاوره‌ای (Very Colloquial)
Legal/Academic   Business    Standard    Casual Written    Spoken Tehran
```

---

## 1. Formal Register (رسمی)

### When to Use
- Official documents, contracts, legal text
- Business correspondence and formal emails
- Academic papers, theses, journal articles
- News broadcasts and press releases
- Government and institutional communications

### Characteristics

**Pronouns:**
- شما (you — always, even singular)
- ایشان (they — respectful third person)
- بنده (I — humble/formal)
- حضرت‌عالی (you — very high respect)

**Verb Forms:**
- Full conjugation, no contractions
- می‌باشد instead of است (in official docs)
- فرمودن instead of گفتن (honorific)
- تشریف آوردن instead of آمدن (honorific)

**Vocabulary:**
- به استحضار می‌رساند (we inform you)
- خواهشمند است (it is requested)
- مراتب تشکر و قدردانی (deep gratitude)
- وفق مقررات (in accordance with regulations)
- بدین‌وسیله (hereby)
- علی‌الخصوص (especially — Arabic-origin formal)

**Structure:**
- Long, complex sentences are acceptable
- Passive voice is common
- Use of Arabic-origin vocabulary is higher
- Titles are always included: جناب آقای دکتر…، سرکار خانم مهندس…

### Example (Formal Email Opening)
```
با سلام و احترام،
بدین‌وسیله به استحضار می‌رساند که درخواست شماره ۱۲۳۴ مورد بررسی قرار گرفته
و نتیجه آن متعاقباً به حضور‌تان ارسال خواهد شد.
```

## 2. Standard/Neutral Register (معیار)

### When to Use
- General-purpose writing
- Blog posts, articles, educational content
- Product descriptions
- Internal business communication (not ultra-formal)
- Default register when context is unclear

### Characteristics

**Pronouns:**
- شما (you — default respectful)
- او (he/she — neutral)
- من (I — standard)

**Verb Forms:**
- Standard conjugation
- است (not می‌باشد)
- No contractions, but no honorific inflation either

**Vocabulary:**
- Modern Persian preferred over heavy Arabic borrowings
- Clear, direct sentences
- Technical terms in their common form

### Example (Standard Blog Post)
```
در این مقاله، به بررسی روش‌های بهینه‌سازی عملکرد وب‌سایت می‌پردازیم.
اگر تجربه‌ای در توسعه وب دارید، این نکات می‌تواند به شما کمک کند.
```

## 3. Colloquial Register (محاوره‌ای)

### When to Use
- Social media posts and captions (Instagram, Telegram, X/Twitter)
- Casual messaging
- Dialogue in stories or scripts
- Friendly blog posts or personal writing
- Marketing copy targeting younger audiences

### Characteristics

**Pronouns:**
- تو (you — singular informal)
- اون (he/she/that — spoken form of آن)
- ما (we — same in all registers)

**Verb Contractions:**

| Standard | Colloquial |
|---|---|
| می‌خواهم | می‌خوام |
| می‌گویم | می‌گم |
| نمی‌دانم | نمی‌دونم |
| می‌آید | میاد |
| می‌رود | می‌ره |
| است | ـه |
| خواهد بود | می‌شه |
| نمی‌شود | نمی‌شه |
| بیایید | بیاید / بیا |
| ببینید | ببینید / ببین |
| گفتم | گفتم (same, but pronunciation differs) |

**Pronunciation-Based Spelling:**

| Standard | Colloquial Written |
|---|---|
| آن | اون |
| این | این (same) |
| چه | چی |
| نه | نه / نچ |
| بله | آره |
| خیر | نه |
| برای | واسه |
| چرا | چرا (same) |
| همین‌طور | همین‌جوری |
| این‌طوری | این‌جوری |

### Example (Instagram Caption)
```
امروز یه کافه جدید پیدا کردم که قهوه‌ش عالیه ☕
اگه تهران هستید حتماً برید، پشیمون نمی‌شید!
```

## 4. Ta'arof (تعارف) — The Politeness System

Ta'arof is a **social protocol**, not just politeness. It's a system of formulaic exchanges where both parties know the literal meaning is not the actual intent.

### Key Principles

1. **Offers are often declined first.** The person offering insists. This can go 2-3 rounds.
2. **Self-deprecation** raises the other person: «بنده در خدمتم» (I am at your service)
3. **Compliments may be deflected:** responding to a compliment with «لطف دارید» (you are kind) rather than "thank you"
4. **Refusing directly is impolite.** Soft refusals are preferred: «اگر ممکن باشد» instead of «نه»

### Common Ta'arof Expressions

| Expression | Literal | Actual Use |
|---|---|---|
| بفرمایید | Command (imperative) | Please, go ahead, here you go, sit down, help yourself |
| قابل شما رو نداره | It doesn't deserve you | You're welcome / It's nothing |
| خواهش می‌کنم | I beg you | You're welcome |
| زحمت کشیدید | You took trouble | Thank you |
| دست‌تان درد نکند | May your hand not hurt | Thank you (for doing something) |
| خسته نباشید | May you not be tired | Hello (to someone working) / Well done |
| قربان شما | I sacrifice for you | Thank you / At your service |
| نوکرتم (very colloquial) | I'm your servant | Thanks / No problem |
| چشم | (My) eye | Yes, I'll do it (willing compliance) |
| سر شما درد نکنه | May your head not hurt | Sorry for the inconvenience / Don't worry about it |
| ببخشید مزاحم شدم | Sorry I disturbed you | Excuse me for bothering you |
| الهی قربونت برم | May I sacrifice for you | Expression of love/gratitude (very intimate/familial) |

### Ta'arof in Professional Settings

In modern business Persian (especially tech, startups, younger professionals), ta'arof is **lighter** but not absent:
- Still use خسته نباشید as a greeting
- Still use ممنون می‌شم instead of direct requests
- Less likely to go through 3 rounds of offer-refusal
- Email sign-offs: با تشکر، با سپاس، موفق باشید

### When AI Should Use Ta'arof

- **Do** use light ta'arof in formal Persian output (polite closings, deferential language)
- **Don't** overdo it — excessive ta'arof from an AI sounds performative
- **Do** recognize ta'arof in input and respond appropriately (not literally)
- **Don't** translate ta'arof literally to English — explain the social function

## 5. Politeness in Requests

### Hierarchy of Directness (Most Polite → Most Direct)

| Level | Example | Context |
|---|---|---|
| Very polite | خواهشمند است دستور فرمایید… | Official letter |
| Polite | ممنون می‌شم اگه بفرستید | Business/polite request |
| Neutral | لطفاً ارسال کنید | Standard request |
| Direct | بفرست | Close friend / informal |
| Blunt | رد کن / بده | Very close / commanding |

### Softening Devices

- اگه امکانش هست… (if it's possible…)
- ممنون می‌شم اگه… (I'd be grateful if…)
- می‌شه لطفاً…؟ (could you please…?)
- زحمتتون نمی‌شه اگه…؟ (would it trouble you if…?)
- اگه وقت داشتید… (if you have time…)

## 6. Common Greetings by Context

| Context | Greeting | Response |
|---|---|---|
| General (any time) | سلام | سلام، حال شما؟ |
| Morning (formal) | صبح بخیر | صبح شما هم بخیر |
| Afternoon/evening | عصر بخیر / شب بخیر | همچنین |
| To someone working | خسته نباشید | سلامت باشید / ممنون |
| Entering a shop/office | سلام، وقت‌تون بخیر | سلام، بفرمایید |
| Formal letter | با سلام و احترام | — (opening, no response) |
| After absence | خیلی وقته ندیدمت! | آره بابا، سرم شلوغ بود |
| Phone call | الو، سلام | سلام، بفرمایید |
| Saying goodbye | خداحافظ / به امید دیدار | خداحافظ / می‌بینمت |
| Goodbye (casual) | فعلاً / بای | فعلاً |

## 7. Regional Notes

### Tehran Standard (Most Common Reference)

The colloquial forms in this document are based on **Tehran spoken Persian**, which is the de facto standard for informal Persian across Iran's media, internet, and youth culture.

### Variations to Be Aware Of

- **Isfahan:** slightly different vowel patterns, distinct expressions (e.g., مُو instead of من)
- **Mashhad:** specific accent features, some distinct vocabulary
- **Shirazi:** known for specific slang and melodic intonation
- **Afghan Dari:** shares formal written Persian but differs significantly in spoken forms and vocabulary — do not assume Tehran colloquial applies
- **Tajik:** written in Cyrillic script, significant vocabulary differences — treat as a related but distinct variety

**Default behavior:** Unless the user specifies a regional variety, use Tehran standard for colloquial and standard written Persian for formal.
