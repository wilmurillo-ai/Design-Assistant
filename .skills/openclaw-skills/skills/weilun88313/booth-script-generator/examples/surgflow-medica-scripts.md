# Example: SurgFlow at MEDICA 2026

## Input

> We sell surgical workflow coordination software (SurgFlow) to operations directors and CMOs at 200+ bed hospitals in DACH. Main differentiator: we're the only solution that integrates live OR scheduling with post-op handoff — competitors do one or the other. Show: MEDICA 2026. Typical deal: €150K, 9-month cycle. Need scripts for cold walk-ups and for competitor customers (main competitor: OR-Sync).

## Path A — Cold Walk-Up

### Opening Lines (3 variants)

1. "Are you in operations or clinical management by any chance? We've been having a surprisingly consistent conversation with directors here about OR handoff delays — curious if that's a pain point at your hospital too."

2. "Quick question — how many ORs does your facility run? We're working with hospitals on reducing the gap between scheduling and post-op coordination, and the range we see is pretty wide."

3. "I saw you slow down near our display — are you dealing with coordination across multiple OR teams, or more of a scheduling side of things?"

### 30-Second Pitch

SurgFlow connects live OR scheduling with post-op handoff in a single platform. Most hospitals run these on separate systems — or spreadsheets — which creates delays and errors at shift change. We work with hospitals across DACH, typically cutting OR coordination overhead by 30–40%. Takes about 20 minutes to show you how it works — want to book a slot here at the show?

*(71 words)*

### 2-Minute Pitch

Right now, most hospital OR teams use two separate tools — one for scheduling the next day's cases, another for tracking post-op handoffs. Or worse, one of those "tools" is a whiteboard. When those systems don't talk to each other, you get delays: nurses waiting for handoff notes, surgeons bumping cases because the schedule hasn't updated, coordination errors that nobody catches until something goes wrong.

SurgFlow connects both sides in real time. The scheduling team sees live OR status. Post-op notes flow automatically into the handoff queue. Everything is timestamped and auditable.

We typically implement in 6–8 weeks with zero downtime — we migrate from whatever legacy system you're running, including OR-Sync and SAP. Our DACH customers at Frankfurt University Hospital and Charité saw a 37% reduction in coordination-related OR delays in the first quarter.

Who we work best with: hospitals running 10+ ORs with a dedicated operations director or COO who owns the scheduling process. Does that sound like your setup?

### Qualification Questions (6)

1. "How does your team currently handle the handoff between OR scheduling and post-op coordination — is that in one system or separate?"
2. "When a case runs long or gets bumped, how does that ripple through the rest of the day's schedule? Who has to manually update things?"
3. "Is OR operations something you own directly, or do you collaborate with a clinical lead on that?"
4. "Have you looked at coordination platforms before, or is this more of a 'we're realizing it's a problem' stage?"
5. "What would drive a decision to actually change the system — is there a specific incident, a regulatory audit, a capacity expansion coming up?"
6. "If this were something you wanted to evaluate, who else would need to be in the room — IT, CFO, clinical leadership?"

### Closing + CTA

**Hot** (2+ qualification signals confirmed): "It sounds like this is directly in your lane. I have a 20-minute live demo slot tomorrow at 2pm — can I book that for you right now? I'll send a calendar invite to your work email."

**Warm** (some interest, unclear authority or timeline): "Let me send you our DACH case study and a short overview — what email is best? We'll follow up the week after the show with a proper intro call."

**Cold** (student, wrong role, just browsing): "Good to meet you. We'll be following up with everyone we spoke to here — is [email on badge] the best way to reach you, or is there a colleague who handles this more directly?"

## Path C — Competitor's Customer (OR-Sync)

### Opening Lines (3 variants)

1. "Are you an OR-Sync shop currently? We talk to a lot of teams using them — curious what your experience has been."

2. "I noticed you're from [Hospital] — we've been speaking with a few facilities that are re-evaluating their OR coordination setup. Is that something on your radar at all?"

3. "What are you using currently for OR scheduling? I ask because there's a pretty common pattern we hear from hospitals on certain platforms."

### 30-Second Pitch

We often talk to teams who've been on OR-Sync for a few years and find it handles the scheduling side well, but the post-op handoff piece is still manual or bolted on separately. SurgFlow was built to connect both ends natively. If that handoff gap is something you're dealing with, it'd take 20 minutes to show you what it looks like when that's solved.

*(71 words)*

### 2-Minute Pitch

OR-Sync is a solid scheduling tool — we hear that consistently. Where hospitals usually hit the wall is the moment a case closes and the post-op team takes over. OR-Sync doesn't own that piece, so hospitals end up running a separate system, a shared drive, or a whiteboard alongside it. That's where coordination errors live.

SurgFlow was built to own both sides. We also migrate from OR-Sync without downtime — most of our DACH customers came from OR-Sync and were live on SurgFlow in 6–8 weeks.

I'm not here to tell you to rip and replace if what you have is working. But if the handoff piece is a gap — and if you've got more than 10 ORs, it usually is — it's worth 20 minutes to see what a unified workflow looks like.

### Qualification Questions (5)

1. "How long have you been on OR-Sync, and what does your post-op handoff workflow look like alongside it?"
2. "Is the handoff gap something your operations team has flagged as a priority, or is it more background noise?"
3. "Has there been any conversation about evaluating alternatives, or is the contract fairly locked in?"
4. "What would have to be true for a platform change to make sense — is it contract timing, a specific feature, or something else?"
5. "Who would drive that evaluation if it happened — you, or someone else in the org?"

### Closing + CTA

**Hot**: "If the contract window is opening up or this is active, let's get 30 minutes with your operations director and our implementation lead before the show ends. I can pull that together."

**Warm**: "Let me send you a side-by-side that shows specifically where we extend what OR-Sync does — it's two pages, very factual. And we can talk after the show when the dust settles."

**Cold**: "Appreciate your time. If the situation changes or the contract comes up for renewal, SurgFlow is worth a look. I'll make sure you're on our list."
