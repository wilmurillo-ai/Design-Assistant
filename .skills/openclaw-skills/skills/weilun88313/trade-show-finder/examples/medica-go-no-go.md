# Example: MEDICA Go / No-Go Decision

**Prompt:** "Should we exhibit at MEDICA 2026? We sell surgical workflow software to 200+ bed hospitals in DACH."

---

## Executive Recommendation

**Exhibit at MEDICA 2026.** It is a strong fit for a DACH-focused hospital workflow vendor because it combines hospital buyer visibility, category credibility, and enough scale to justify a pre-booked meeting strategy.

## ICP / Goal Snapshot

- Company / offer: Surgical workflow software
- ICP: 200+ bed hospitals in DACH
- Buyers: Hospital operations, OR leadership, procurement, digital transformation
- Goal: Pipeline generation
- Region: DACH / Europe
- Motion: Exhibit

| [MEDICA](https://www.medica-tradefair.com/) | Nov 16-19, 2026 | Dusseldorf, Germany | 88 | Exhibit | High hospital visibility, strong DACH relevance, and enough scale for new-pipeline generation |

## Show Fit Score

- ICP / buyer fit: 31/35
- Commercial objective fit: 23/25

**Total: 88/100 · Priority 1**

## Execution Readiness

`Not assessed` — the prompt does not include budget band, booth size, or team size. The strategic fit is strong, but operational feasibility still needs budget validation.

## Top Recommendation(s)

**MEDICA** is worth exhibiting at if the team can support it with pre-booked meetings and a focused hospital-buyer message. The show is broad, but the hospital operations and digital workflow angle is commercially relevant enough to justify booth presence.

## Why Not / Tradeoffs

- MEDICA is broad and expensive; without a disciplined meeting plan, the booth can drift into low-quality traffic
- It is not automatically the best place for deep technical validation; if the team's immediate goal were only product validation with smaller specialist audiences, a narrower event could sometimes be more efficient
