# Booth Giveaway Planner — OpenClaw Skill

> Choose giveaway items that actually support booth traffic, lead quality, and brand recall.

**Best for**: teams deciding what to give broadly, what to gate, and what swag is not worth ordering.

## What This Skill Does

Give the agent your industry, ICP, product, and budget, and it outputs:

- 5–8 giveaway ideas, each with a genuine brand connection (not just "logo on item")
- Type classification: Branded Utility, Conversation Starter, or Qualifier
- Estimated unit cost and minimum order quantity
- Which visitor type each item is best for (all visitors / warm leads / decision makers)
- Budget allocation suggestion and distribution strategy
- Lead-time warning if the show is under 4 weeks away

**Pre-Show · Planning**

## When to Use

**4–8 weeks before the show**, when you're finalizing booth investments and need to decide what to order and how much to spend on giveaways.

Not for post-show or on-site use — giveaway ordering requires lead time.

## Quick Examples

```
We sell industrial IoT sensors to plant managers. Exhibiting at Hannover Messe, booth ~20sqm, expect 500 visitors. Budget is $6/item for general giveaways, up to $30 for qualified leads. What should we give away?
```

```
SaaS company targeting HR directors at HR Tech Conference. Budget $10/item. Our product automates onboarding workflows. Need 5-6 ideas.
```

```
Medical device company at MEDICA, booth 5C42. ICP: procurement managers and clinical directors at 200+ bed hospitals. Budget $8/item, total $2,000. What giveaways make sense?
```

## How It Works

The skill guides the agent through:

1. **Context gathering** — industry, ICP, product story, budget
2. **Strategy classification** — Branded Utility vs. Conversation Starter vs. Qualifier tier
3. **Idea generation** — 5–8 options with brand connection, cost, and visitor targeting
4. **Planning notes** — budget split, distribution strategy, lead-time check
