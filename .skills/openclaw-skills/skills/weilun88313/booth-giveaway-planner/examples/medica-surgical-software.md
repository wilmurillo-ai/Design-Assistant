# Example: Surgical Workflow Software at MEDICA

## Input

> We sell surgical workflow coordination software (SurgFlow) to operations directors and CMOs at 200+ bed hospitals in the DACH region. Exhibiting at MEDICA 2026, booth 5C42, ~20 sqm. Expecting 400-600 visitors over 4 days. Budget: $8/item general, up to $40 for decision makers. Total budget ~$3,500. Primary goal: schedule product demos.

---

## Output

### Giveaway Strategy

**Budget split recommendation:**
- $2,200 on a volume utility item (~275 units @ $8) for all engaged visitors
- $900 on a qualifier premium item (~22 units @ $40) for booked demos and C-level contacts
- $400 contingency / extras

**Goal**: Use the general item to start conversations; use the premium item to reward the 20-25 decision makers who commit to a follow-up demo.

---

### Ideas

---

### 1. OR-Ready Pocket Reference Card Holder (Rigid, Branded)
**Type**: Branded Utility
**Brand Connection**: Surgeons and OR coordinators carry reference cards, protocols, and scheduling printouts constantly. A slim, durable card holder keeps critical documents organized — exactly the problem SurgFlow solves for the whole team, in physical form.
**Unit Cost (est.)**: $5–$8 (MOQ: ~100 units)
**Best For**: All engaged visitors
**Customization Note**: Laser-engrave or emboss logo + tagline on metal/leather finish. Avoid cheap plastic — it reads as low-quality for a clinical audience.

---

### 2. Surgical Coordination Desk Pad (A3, 50 sheets)
**Type**: Branded Utility
**Brand Connection**: Operations directors and clinical leads use paper planning pads for shift handoffs and scheduling. A premium desk pad keeps SurgFlow's name on their desk every working day.
**Unit Cost (est.)**: $6–$9 (MOQ: ~150 units)
**Best For**: Operations directors, nursing managers
**Customization Note**: Print a subtle "Daily Surgical Workflow" template on each sheet — branded but functional, not a sales brochure.

---

### 3. "Zero Coordination Errors" Enamel Pin (Limited Edition)
**Type**: Conversation Starter
**Brand Connection**: SurgFlow's core promise is eliminating OR coordination errors. A bold enamel pin — sharp, wearable, conversation-worthy — creates badge-level recall. "What does that mean?" opens the demo pitch naturally.
**Unit Cost (est.)**: $3–$5 (MOQ: ~200 units)
**Best For**: All visitors (especially those who stop to ask about it)
**Customization Note**: Small, quality enamel work. Include a short tagline on the backing card that ties it to a stat: "Hospitals using SurgFlow cut OR delays by 34%."

---

### 4. Premium Leather A5 Notebook (Qualifier Tier)
**Type**: Qualifier
**Brand Connection**: CMOs and operations directors use notebooks in every executive meeting. A quality leather notebook with a discreet SurgFlow imprint is something they'll keep and use — keeping the brand present in boardroom conversations.
**Unit Cost (est.)**: $30–$40 (MOQ: ~25 units)
**Best For**: Decision makers (CMO, COO, VP Operations) who book a live demo during the show
**Customization Note**: Debossed logo only — no print-on-demand sticker approach. Quality matters for this tier. Include a handwritten notecard inside: "Thanks for your time at MEDICA."

---

### 5. USB-C Charging Cable Set (Branded Carry Pouch)
**Type**: Branded Utility
**Brand Connection**: Show floors drain phone batteries. A 3-in-1 cable (USB-C / Lightning / Micro-USB) in a SurgFlow-branded pouch is immediately useful and travels home with the visitor.
**Unit Cost (est.)**: $7–$9 (MOQ: ~100 units)
**Best For**: All visitors
**Customization Note**: Pouch quality matters more than cable — use a zipper fabric pouch, not a plastic bag. Add a short URL on the pouch to the demo booking page.

---

### 6. MEDICA 2026 Survival Kit (Pocket-Size)
**Type**: Conversation Starter
**Brand Connection**: A small kit (blister plasters, hand sanitizer, energy gum, earplugs) in a branded tin is playful and practical — "We know MEDICA is exhausting, SurgFlow helps with the other kind of coordination fatigue." Creates a warm, human opening.
**Unit Cost (est.)**: $6–$8 (MOQ: ~100 units)
**Best For**: All visitors, especially on Day 3–4 when fatigue shows
**Customization Note**: Lid text: "MEDICA Survival Kit — from SurgFlow." Insert a small card with demo booking QR code inside.

---

### Distribution Strategy

- **General items** (ideas 1, 2, 3, 5, 6): Give freely to any visitor who engages in a conversation. Do NOT require a badge scan first — offer the item, then offer to scan.
- **Qualifier item** (idea 4): Reserve for visitors who book a live demo at the booth or who are confirmed C-level contacts. Staff should carry 5 units each, not leave them on a table.

### Lead-Time Check

MEDICA 2026 is in November. Assuming current date is March–April, all items above have sufficient lead time (custom production typically 3–5 weeks). If ordering after September 1, flag ideas 1 and 4 as potentially requiring rush fees.
