## Strategic Recommendations

### Messaging

- Lead with operational workflow outcomes, not hardware transformation
- Emphasize fast deployment and low disruption versus enterprise-scale infrastructure narratives
- Avoid claiming "full OR transformation" if the product is strongest in coordination and scheduling

### Booth / Staff Briefing

- Expect enterprise-hospital comparison questions from visitors who have seen robotics-heavy competitors
- Prepare one concise answer for "How are you different from the bigger OR platform vendors?"
- Train staff to convert hardware-centric conversations into workflow efficiency and adoption discussions

### White Space

- Mid-market and regional hospital groups appear under-served in the visible messaging set
- There is room for a "faster time-to-value, lower change-management burden" angle

## Knowledge Gaps to Verify On-Site

- Whether any primary threat is launching a new software module or only promoting existing hardware narratives
- Whether workflow claims are tied to named proof points or remain generic show-floor copy
- Which booths are attracting hospital operations roles versus surgeons or device specialists
