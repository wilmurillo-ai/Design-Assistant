# Customer Skill — Heartbeat

*Churn is silent until it is not. Build the early warning system.*

---

## Daily — Health Alerts and Urgent Accounts

Every morning:

"Show me any customer health alerts from yesterday and any accounts with upcoming touchpoints today."

Daily customer watch:
- Any customers whose usage dropped more than 20% week over week
- Any customers who have not logged in for 14+ days
- Any support tickets escalated or unresolved for more than 48 hours
- Any renewal conversations scheduled this week
- Any onboarding customers behind on milestone schedule

"I have [number] customers in my book. Which ones need attention today?"

---

## Weekly — Portfolio Review

Every Monday:

"Here is my customer portfolio summary: [accounts, health scores, renewal dates, last contact dates]. Walk me through what needs attention this week."

Weekly portfolio questions:
- Which customers have renewals in the next 90 days and what is their health status
- Which onboarding customers are in weeks 1-8 and are they on track
- Which customers have not had a meaningful touchpoint in 30+ days
- Any health score changes — positive or negative — worth acting on
- Expansion opportunities identified from recent usage or conversations

---

## Monthly — Customer Business Review Prep

For each customer with a scheduled business review:

"I have a business review with [customer] in [X days]. Here is their usage data and the outcomes we agreed on at onboarding: [data]. Help me build a narrative that connects their usage to their business results."

Monthly QBR checklist:
- Usage metrics framed as business outcomes not product metrics
- Progress against the success criteria set at onboarding
- New use cases or departments worth exploring
- Roadmap items relevant to their stated priorities
- Renewal or expansion agenda items if appropriate

---

## Quarterly — Portfolio Health Analysis

"Here is my full customer portfolio for the quarter: [churn, expansion, NPS, health score distribution]. What does this tell me about the health of my book of business?"

Quarterly analysis covers:
- Churn rate and reasons — preventable vs unpreventable
- Expansion rate — what percentage of the base grew their contract
- Net revenue retention — is the installed base growing or shrinking
- Health score distribution — what percentage of accounts are healthy, neutral, at-risk
- Onboarding success rate — what percentage of new customers achieved their 90-day milestone
- NPS trend — is customer sentiment improving or declining

---

## Trigger Events — Use Immediately

Champion leaves: "My main contact at [customer] just left the company. Renewal is in [X months]. What do I do in the next 72 hours?"

Usage drop: "A customer's usage dropped 45% this week with no explanation. They are 6 months into a 12-month contract. How do I approach this?"

Negative NPS: "A customer just gave us a 3 NPS with the comment: [comment]. How do I respond?"

Expansion signal: "A customer just asked me how many seats they can add and whether we support [use case]. How do I handle this conversation?"

Executive escalation: "A customer's VP just emailed my CEO to complain about [issue]. How do I manage this?"

Competitor mentioned: "A customer told me they are evaluating [competitor] before their renewal. How do I respond without panicking?"

Renewal at risk: "A renewal is 45 days out and the customer has not confirmed they want to continue. Here is what I know: [context]. What is my plan?"

---

*The customer who churns quietly was usually signaling loudly for months. Build the system that hears the signal before the decision is made.*
