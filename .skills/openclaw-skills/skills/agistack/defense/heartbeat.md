# Defense Skill — Heartbeat

*In defense situations, delay costs rights. Act early, act carefully.*

---

## When Legal Action Begins

Run immediately when any legal threat or action begins:

"I am facing [criminal charge / civil lawsuit / regulatory investigation]. Here is what happened: [summary]. What do I need to do in the next 24 hours?"

First 24 hours checklist:
- Do not make statements to law enforcement or opposing parties without counsel
- Do not delete any documents, communications, or records
- Do not post anything on social media about the matter
- Notify your insurance carrier if you have coverage that might apply
- Contact a defense attorney in the relevant practice area today

---

## Active Defense — Weekly Check

While a matter is active:

"Here is the current status of my defense: [stage]. My next deadline is [date]. My last communication with my attorney was about [topic]. What should I be tracking this week?"

Weekly defense checklist:
- Any deadlines for responses, filings, or discovery
- Any new developments in the matter your attorney should know
- Any contact from opposing parties or investigators to report
- Your compliance with any existing court orders or conditions

---

## Before Every Key Decision

Before any significant decision in a defense matter:

"My attorney is recommending [action or decision]. Help me understand what I am choosing between and what questions I should ask before deciding."

Key decisions in defense matters:
- Whether to accept a plea offer or settlement
- Whether to testify in your own defense
- Whether to waive a jury trial
- Whether to change attorneys mid-matter
- Whether to appeal a verdict or ruling

---

## Document Preservation

When a legal matter begins, immediately:

"I need to preserve documents related to [matter]. Help me understand what I should preserve and how."

Preservation checklist:
- All emails and text messages with relevant parties
- All contracts, invoices, receipts related to the matter
- All social media content that could be relevant
- All financial records that might be requested
- Physical evidence if applicable
- Your own contemporaneous notes of events

Do not destroy anything once you know or reasonably anticipate legal action. Destruction of evidence after a preservation obligation attaches is spoliation and can result in severe legal consequences independent of the underlying matter.

---

## Post-Resolution

After a matter resolves:

"My [case / charge / investigation] has been resolved with [outcome]. What do I need to do now and what are the ongoing obligations?"

Post-resolution checklist:
- Comply with all terms of plea, settlement, or court order
- Calendar any probation reporting requirements or payment schedules
- Understand expungement eligibility and timeline if applicable
- Address any collateral consequences — license, employment, immigration
- Evaluate appeal options if resolution was unfavorable

---

*Defense situations do not improve with delay. The rights you protect in the first hours are the rights your attorney has to work with. Protect them.*
