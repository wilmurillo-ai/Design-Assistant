# Funnel Skill — Heartbeat

*What gets measured gets managed. What gets managed gets better.*

---

## Daily — Core Metrics Pulse

"Here are my funnel metrics for today: signups [number], activations [number], conversions [number]. Anything I should pay attention to?"

Daily watch:
- Signup volume vs 7-day average — sudden drops indicate traffic or landing page issues
- Activation rate — is yesterday's cohort activating at normal rate
- Any spike in support tickets or churn signals from recent cohorts
- Paid channel spend vs conversion — is CAC holding

---

## Weekly — Funnel Health Review

"Here are my weekly funnel numbers: [visitors, signups, activations, trials, conversions, churn]. Walk me through what the data is telling me."

Weekly review covers:
- Visitor-to-signup conversion rate trend
- Signup-to-activation rate by acquisition channel
- Trial-to-paid conversion rate by cohort week
- Churn rate for the week — any spike worth investigating
- Experiment results from tests running this week

"Which stage of my funnel moved the most this week and what might explain it?"

---

## Monthly — Deep Funnel Analysis

"Run my monthly funnel deep dive. Here is my full data: [metrics by stage]."

Monthly analysis:
- Full funnel conversion rates vs prior month and vs 3-month trend
- CAC by channel — which channels are becoming more or less efficient
- LTV:CAC ratio — is the unit economics improving or degrading
- Cohort retention curves — are newer cohorts retaining better than older ones
- Revenue per visitor — the single number that captures full-funnel efficiency
- Experiment log — what was tested, what was learned, what changed

---

## Quarterly — Strategy and Prioritization

"It is end of quarter. Here is my funnel performance summary: [data]. Help me identify the single highest-leverage optimization for next quarter."

Quarterly prioritization framework:
- Which funnel stage has the largest absolute drop-off
- Which stage has the largest gap vs industry benchmark
- Which stage has the highest-confidence intervention available
- What experiments are planned and what is the expected impact

"Given everything I know about my funnel, where should my team spend its optimization energy next quarter?"

---

## Experiment Tracking — Every Test

Before launching any experiment:
"I want to test [hypothesis] by changing [element] for [audience segment]. My current conversion rate is [baseline]. Help me design this test correctly."

Experiment design checklist:
- Hypothesis stated clearly — what change, what expected outcome, why
- Sample size calculated — how long does the test need to run
- Single variable isolated — only one thing changing at a time
- Success metric defined in advance — what number determines winner
- Guardrail metrics identified — what cannot get worse

After each experiment:
"My test ran for [duration]. Here are the results: [data]. Help me interpret what this means and what to do next."

---

## Trigger Events — Use Immediately

Sudden signup drop: "My signups dropped 40% overnight. Here is what I know: [data]. Help me diagnose what happened."

Conversion rate cliff: "My trial-to-paid conversion dropped from 18% to 9% over the last two weeks. What should I investigate?"

Churn spike: "My monthly churn jumped from 3% to 7% this month. How do I figure out why?"

New channel launch: "I am about to launch [paid channel / content program / partnership]. Help me set up measurement before I spend anything."

Pricing change: "I am changing my pricing next month. Help me think through the funnel implications and what to monitor after launch."

---

*The funnel that improves consistently beats the funnel that is occasionally brilliant. Build the measurement habit and the improvement becomes systematic.*
