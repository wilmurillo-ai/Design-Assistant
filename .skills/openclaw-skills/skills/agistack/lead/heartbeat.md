# Lead — Heartbeat

*Good leads decay. Clear systems prevent that.*

---

## Daily — New Lead Triage

Use Lead whenever new prospects arrive.

Examples:
- "Here are today's new inbound leads. Help me rank them and decide which ones deserve immediate follow-up."
- "These 5 leads came in from different sources. Which ones should I pursue now, and which ones should go to nurture?"
- "Draft the first reply for the top 2 leads only."

Daily focus:
- prioritize fresh leads
- identify missing qualification data
- recommend next best action
- draft short, relevant follow-up

---

## Weekly — Stalled Lead Review

Once a week, review leads that have gone quiet.

Examples:
- "These leads have had no activity for 7+ days. Which ones are worth reviving?"
- "Which of these warm leads is actually still alive, and which ones should I stop chasing?"
- "Draft re-engagement messages for the top 3 stalled leads."

Weekly focus:
- detect momentum decay
- separate real opportunities from false optimism
- revive only the leads worth saving
- move weak leads to nurture or deprioritize

---

## Biweekly — Qualification Discipline Check

Every two weeks, check whether active leads are truly qualified.

Examples:
- "Here are my active leads. Which ones are in pipeline too early?"
- "Which of these deals lack enough evidence on authority, urgency, or fit?"
- "Show me the weakest assumptions in my current lead list."

Biweekly focus:
- stop treating activity as opportunity
- tighten qualification standards
- surface hidden gaps before more time is wasted

---

## Monthly — Source Quality Review

Once a month, compare where strong leads actually come from.

Examples:
- "These are my leads by source and current status. Which channels produce the best opportunities?"
- "Where am I getting volume without quality?"
- "Which lead source deserves more time next month?"

Monthly focus:
- compare volume vs real opportunity quality
- identify weak lead sources
- strengthen channels that produce strong-fit prospects

---

## Trigger Events — Use Immediately

Use Lead right away when any of these happen:
- a new inbound form arrives
- a prospect replies after being cold
- a strong lead goes silent
- a lead asks detailed buying questions
- a rep is unsure whether to pursue or nurture
- a founder needs quick judgment before replying

---

## Core Rule

Every lead should end with one of four outcomes:
- pursue now
- qualify further
- nurture
- deprioritize

If the next step is unclear, the lead is not understood well enough yet.
