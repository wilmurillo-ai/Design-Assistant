# Copyright Skill — Heartbeat

*Your copyright exists automatically. Protecting it effectively requires deliberate action.*

---

## When You Create Something New

Run when any significant new work is completed:

"I just completed [work type]. What should I do to protect my copyright and should I register it?"

New work checklist:
- Document creation date and original authorship
- Add copyright notice if distributing publicly
- Evaluate registration priority based on commercial value
- Register within three months of first publication for full statutory damage eligibility
- Store a backup copy in a dated, accessible format

---

## Monthly — Infringement Watch

"Help me check for unauthorized use of my work online."

Monthly monitoring:
- Reverse image search for photographers and visual artists
- Google search for distinctive phrases from written works
- YouTube Content ID and similar platform monitoring tools
- Social media monitoring for unauthorized use of content
- Watermarking and metadata review for distributed assets

When you find potential infringement:
"I found what may be unauthorized use of my [work type] at [location]. Help me document this and evaluate my options."

---

## Quarterly — Licensing and Rights Review

For creators who license their work:

"Review my current licensing situation. I have [number] active licenses for [work types]. Are there any issues I should address?"

Quarterly licensing checklist:
- Active licenses — are all licensees complying with terms
- Expired licenses — any that need renewal or enforcement
- New licensing opportunities identified
- Licensing rate review — are current rates market appropriate
- Platform terms review — any platform policy changes affecting your rights

---

## Annual — Copyright Portfolio Review

"Walk me through my annual copyright review."

Annual checklist:
- All significant works registered — any gaps to fill
- Registration records organized and backed up
- Licensing agreements organized and accessible
- Work-for-hire agreements reviewed — who owns what
- Older work evaluated for continued commercial value warranting renewed protection attention
- Any infringement matters from the past year resolved or ongoing

---

## Trigger Events — Use Immediately

Discovered infringement: "I found my [work] being used without permission at [location]. Help me document and respond."

Received takedown notice: "I received a DMCA takedown on my content. Here is what it says: [summary]. Is this legitimate and what are my options?"

Licensing inquiry: "Someone wants to license my [work] for [use]. Help me evaluate the request and structure an appropriate license."

Work for hire question: "I am being asked to sign a work for hire agreement. What does this mean for my copyright ownership?"

Using third-party content: "I want to use [content] in my project. Help me determine if I need permission and how to get it."

---

*Copyright is a right that belongs to you. It is most valuable when you understand it, register it before you need to enforce it, and monitor for infringement consistently.*
