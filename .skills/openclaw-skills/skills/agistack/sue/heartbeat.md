# Sue Skill — Heartbeat

*Litigation runs on deadlines. Missing one can end your case.*

---

## When a Dispute Begins

Run immediately when any potential legal dispute arises:

"I have a dispute with [party] over [issue]. The amount involved is [amount]. Here is what happened: [summary]. Help me evaluate my options before I do anything."

Immediate actions in any dispute:
- Preserve all communications — do not delete anything
- Document the current state with photos, records, or written notes
- Identify the statute of limitations for your type of claim
- Send nothing in writing to the other party that you would not want a judge to read

---

## Pre-Filing — Case Evaluation

Before filing any lawsuit:

"I am considering suing [party] for [claim]. Help me evaluate whether this is worth pursuing and what the realistic outcomes are."

Pre-filing checklist:
- Legal theory identified and elements understood
- Evidence inventory — what you have and what you need
- Defendant asset check — can they pay a judgment
- Cost-benefit analysis — expected recovery vs litigation cost
- Alternative resolution options considered — demand letter, mediation, licensing board
- Statute of limitations deadline confirmed

---

## Active Litigation — Weekly Check

While a lawsuit is active:

"Here is where my case stands: [stage]. My next deadline is [date]. What should I be doing this week?"

Active case deadlines to track:
- Response deadlines to any filed motions
- Discovery production deadlines
- Deposition dates
- Expert witness disclosure deadlines
- Pretrial conference dates
- Trial date

---

## Settlement Decision Points

When a settlement offer arrives:

"I just received a settlement offer of [amount]. My case involves [summary]. My attorney's trial estimate is [range]. Help me evaluate this offer."

Settlement evaluation framework:
- Net recovery after fees at settlement vs expected net recovery after fees at trial
- Probability of winning at trial honestly assessed
- Time to trial and value of resolution now vs later
- Non-monetary terms in the offer worth considering
- What happens if you reject and the next offer is lower

---

## Post-Judgment — Collection

After winning a judgment:

"I have a judgment for [amount] against [party]. They have not paid. Here is what I know about their situation: [assets, employment]. What are my collection options?"

Collection tools by situation:
- Employed defendant: wage garnishment
- Has bank accounts: bank levy
- Owns real estate: judgment lien
- Unknown assets: debtor's examination under oath
- Judgment debtor in another state: domestication of judgment

---

*Every stage of litigation has a deadline. Every deadline missed is an opportunity lost or a right forfeited. Stay ahead of the calendar.*
