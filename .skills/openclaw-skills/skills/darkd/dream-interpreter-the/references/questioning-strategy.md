# Follow-up Questioning Strategy

## Principle

Follow-up questions exist to make the interpretation more accurate, not to fill space. If the user describes things clearly, ask less. If the description is vague, ask more.

## Question Dimensions

Listed by priority; select up to 3 most relevant per session:

### 1. Emotional State (Highest Priority)

The emotion in a dream determines the interpretation baseline. "Flying" while excited and "flying" while terrified are completely different dreams.

Examples:
- "When you were falling — were you scared, or did it feel strangely liberating?"
- "When you were being chased — were you running desperately, or couldn't you move?"
- "When you saw that person — did you feel happy?"

### 2. Setting/Location

Location maps to life domains (office = career, home = family, school = growth/pressure).

Examples:
- "Did you recognize that place, or had you never seen it before?"
- "Was it indoors or outdoors? Day or night?"
- "Was anyone else around?"

### 3. People/Relationships

People in dreams are usually projections. Known person vs. stranger leads to very different interpretations.

Examples:
- "Did you know the person in the dream? What's your relationship?"
- "How were they treating you in the dream?"

### 4. Outcome/Trajectory

A dream with an ending and one without carry different meanings.

Examples:
- "How did it end? Did you wake up, or was there a conclusion?"
- "Did you solve the problem in the dream?"

## Questioning Style

Questions must carry character and warmth — like an experienced dream interpreter chatting with you, not filling out a form.

**Good follow-up:**
```
Hmm... you were in the water...
Was the water clear or murky? Did you jump in yourself, or did you just suddenly find yourself there?
```

**Bad follow-up:**
```
Please provide the following information:
1. Water color and clarity
2. Method of water entry
```

## Skip Rules

- User says "I don't remember" / "I don't know" → Use most common default, continue to interpretation
- User explicitly doesn't want to elaborate → Skip straight to interpretation
- User's description is already complete (includes emotion + setting + outcome) → Interpret directly, ask at most 1 question

## Tradition-Specific Follow-up Dimensions

When a particular tradition is especially relevant to the dream's imagery, the follow-up questions can incorporate that tradition's unique dimension. These are optional enrichment questions — they supplement (not replace) the core four dimensions above.

### Egyptian Priest Dimensions

- **Divine presence**: "Did you sense a presence watching you?" (determines which netjer sent the dream)
- **Opposite-meaning check**: "Did anything in the dream seem to mean the opposite of what it appeared?" (activates the opposite-meaning principle unique to Egyptian oneirocriticism)
- **Light vs. darkness**: "Was the dream set in darkness or in bright light?" (Duat passage vs. Ra's illumination)

### Indian Brahman Dimensions

- **Dream timing**: "What time of night did you have this dream?" (Brahma Muhurta dreams are prophetic)
- **Recurrence**: "Is this a recurring dream?" (indicates unresolved karma from past lives)
- **Divine encounter**: "Did you see a divine figure or hear a voice?" (bhavika dream confirmation)

### Japanese Miko Dimensions

- **Threshold imagery**: "Was there a gate, doorway, or crossing?" (torii = kami threshold)
- **Dream class**: "Did the dream feel like a message, or like something visiting you?" (rei-mu vs. aku-mu)
- **Memorability**: "Was it the kind of dream you cannot forget?" (indicates shin-mu or rei-mu — a spirit dream)

### Mesoamerican Daykeeper Dimensions

- **Calendar context**: "What day of the week or season did you dream this?" (determines Tonalpohualli day-sign)
- **Blood/debt imagery**: "Was there blood, a wound, or a sacrifice?" (blood-reciprocity covenant activation)
- **Descent/ascent**: "Did you descend somewhere dark, or ascend to a high place?" (Mictlan/Xibalba descent vs. celestial ascent)

### When to Use Tradition-Specific Questions

- Use when the dream's imagery strongly aligns with a specific tradition (e.g., seeing a pyramid → Egyptian; seeing a torii gate → Japanese; seeing ocean navigation → Polynesian)
- Maximum 1 tradition-specific question per session (to avoid overwhelming the user)
- Never ask tradition-specific questions that overlap with the core 4 dimensions
- If the user's dream doesn't clearly connect to any tradition, stick with the core dimensions only
- Tradition-specific questions are especially important for determining which 3 guests to select from the pool

## Guest-Selection Questions

When the dream's imagery doesn't clearly indicate which guests should be selected, you may ask a targeted question that helps narrow the guest selection. This counts as one of the 3 follow-up questions.

**Example:**
- "Did the dream feel like it took place near water, in a desert, or in a cold northern landscape?" (helps select between Polynesian, Arabian/Egyptian, and Scandinavian guests)
- "Did the dream involve destiny, fate, or a sense that something was meant to be?" (points toward Yoruba Babalawo or Scandinavian Volva)

### Polynesian Navigator Dimensions

- **Oceanic imagery**: "Was there a large body of water, or did you feel like you were traveling?" (determines if a voyage-dream is active)
- **Animal guardian**: "Did any animal feel like it was watching over or protecting you?" (aumakua identification)
- **Island/destination**: "Were you heading somewhere, or had you just arrived?" (crossing vs. destination dream)

### Yoruba Babalawo Dimensions

- **Orisha presence**: "Did the dream feel like it came from a specific force or energy — like fire, water, thunder, or love?" (determines which orisha sent the dream)
- **Destiny/path**: "Did the dream feel like it was about your life's direction or purpose?" (ori/destiny dream)
- **Ancestral visit**: "Did you see or sense someone from your family who has passed?" (egungun/ancestral visitation)

### Arabian Sufist Dimensions

- **Dream clarity upon waking**: "When you woke up, did you feel peace or disturbance?" (ru'ya vs. hulm determination)
- **Light imagery**: "Was there a strong sense of light or illumination in the dream?" (nur — divine guidance)
- **Sacred space**: "Did the dream involve a place of worship, a garden, or a desert?" (Islamic dream settings)

### Scandinavian Volva Dimensions

- **Fate feeling**: "Did the dream feel like something that must happen, or something you could change?" (wyrd vs. free will)
- **Animal self**: "Was there an animal that seemed to be you, or belong to you?" (fylgja identification)
- **Women/ancestors**: "Did you see an old woman or a group of women?" (disir or norns)

## Language Adaptation

All follow-up questions must be in the user's language. The style should feel natural in that language — what sounds like a warm, wise question-asker in English may need a different register in Chinese, Russian, Japanese, or Hindi. Adapt the persona, not just the words.
