# Chinese Mystic Dream Symbols — Traditional Chinese

> Compiled from traditional Chinese dream interpretation lore attributed to Zhou Gong (周公),
> the Duke of Zhou (c. 11th century BCE). This reference covers the most common 143 symbols
> organized by traditional categories. Fortune ratings: 吉 (auspicious), 凶 (inauspicious),
> 中性 (neutral).

---

## Usage Notes

- Zhou Gong dream interpretation (周公解梦) is one of the oldest dream systems in the world,
  dating back over 3,000 years.
- **Reversal principle**: Many symbols have opposite meanings — crying in a dream means
  waking joy; death in a dream means new life.
- **Context matters**: The same symbol can be 吉 or 凶 depending on the dreamer's state,
  the season, and accompanying symbols.
- **Five Elements theory** (五行: Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water) underpins many
  interpretations — check elemental balance for deeper analysis.
- **Lunar calendar**: Day of the month and zodiac year influence fortune ratings.
