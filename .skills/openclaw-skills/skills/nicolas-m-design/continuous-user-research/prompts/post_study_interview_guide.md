# Optional Post-Study Interview Guide (20-30 minutes)

Use this guide only when diary entries leave key uncertainty, contradictions, or unexplained behavior shifts.

## Interview goals
- Confirm or challenge top weekly themes.
- Fill evidence gaps that could change product decisions.
- Understand change-over-time drivers not captured in short entries.

## Suggested agenda
1. Warm-up (2-3 min)
2. Timeline recap from participant's own entries (5-7 min)
3. Theme validation and contradiction probing (8-10 min)
4. Counterfactual and improvement prompts (5-7 min)
5. Close and member-check summary (2-3 min)

## Script
### 1) Warm-up
- "Thanks for joining. We want to better understand your experience over the last [X] days."
- "We'll reference some of your diary entries and ask for context where needed."

### 2) Timeline recap
- "Looking back at the week, what moments were most important in your journey?"
- "Which day or moment changed your behavior the most?"

### 3) Validate key themes
For each candidate theme:
- "In your entries, we saw [neutral summary]. Does that reflect your experience?"
- "What did we miss or misunderstand in that interpretation?"

### 4) Probe surprising behaviors
- "We noticed [observed shift]. What influenced that change?"
- "What was happening around you at that time (time pressure, device, people, environment)?"

### 5) Counterfactual and product opportunity
- "What small product change would have improved that moment for you?"
- "If we changed [specific element], how likely would that affect your next decision?"

### 6) Close
- "Is there anything important we did not ask that would help us make a better decision?"
- "Thank you. We'll combine this with your diary entries in a redacted summary."

## Interview guardrails
- Use neutral wording.
- Do not ask participants to confirm a pre-selected solution.
- Distinguish observed behavior from retrospective interpretation.
- Mark confidence lower when interview statements conflict with diary records.
