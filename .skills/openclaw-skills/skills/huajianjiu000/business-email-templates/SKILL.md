# Business Email Templates

## What This Skill Does

Every business email is an opportunity to make an impression. I provide polished, effective email templates for situations you face daily—so you can communicate professionally in seconds.

## Features

- **Ready to Send**: Just fill in the blanks
- **Industry-Neutral**: Works for any business
- **Tone Options**: Formal, friendly, persuasive
- **Subject Lines**: Included for each template
- **Follow-up Ready**: Sequential emails included

## Email Templates

### Cold Outreach Email
```
Subject: Quick question about [relevant topic]

Hi [Name],

I noticed [specific observation about their company/post/work].

I'm [your name] with [your company], and I help 
[businesses/professionals like them] to [specific outcome].

Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call to explore 
if this could be valuable for you?

Best,
[Your name]
[Title] | [Company]
[LinkedIn/Website]
```

### Follow-up After No Response
```
Subject: Re: [Original subject] - One more thing

Hi [Name],

I wanted to follow up on my previous email. I know 
you're busy, so I'll keep this short.

[One key benefit or new information].

If now isn't a good time, no worries—just let me know 
when would work better.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Meeting Request
```
Subject: Meeting request: [Topic]

Hi [Name],

I'd love to connect to discuss [topic]. 

Based on your role with [company], I think there could 
be value in [specific reason for meeting].

Are you available for a [30-minute] call [day options]?

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Project Update
```
Subject: Project [Name] - Week [X] Update

Hi [Team/Manager],

Here's the update for week [X]:

✓ Completed:
- [Task 1]
- [Task 2]

→ In Progress:
- [Task] - [Status] - ETA [Date]

⚠️ Blockers:
- [Issue] → [Solution/Request]

Next week:
- [Planned task 1]
- [Planned task 2]

Let me know if you need anything else.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Rescheduling
```
Subject: Quick request - Can we reschedule?

Hi [Name],

Something came up and I need to reschedule our 
[meeting type] scheduled for [original date/time].

I have availability on:
- [Option 1]
- [Option 2]

Would either of these work for you?

Apologies for any inconvenience.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Declining a Request
```
Subject: Re: [Original subject]

Hi [Name],

Thank you for thinking of me for [opportunity/project].

Unfortunately, I won't be able to [specific commitment] 
at this time due to [current priorities/bandwidth].

[If applicable: I'd love to be considered for future 
opportunities when my schedule opens up.]

I appreciate your understanding.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Introduction Email
```
Subject: Introduction: [Name A] & [Name B]

Hi [Name A], [Name B],

I wanted to introduce you two since [reason].

[Name A] - [Brief intro with context for why meeting you]

[Name B] - [Brief intro with context for why meeting you]

I believe you both could benefit from connecting 
regarding [shared interest].

I'll let you two take it from here!

Best,
[Your name]
```

## Email Best Practices

- **Subject line**: 50 characters or less
- **First sentence**: Hook them immediately
- **Call to action**: Clear next step
- **Signature**: Consistent and professional
- **Proofread**: Check before sending
