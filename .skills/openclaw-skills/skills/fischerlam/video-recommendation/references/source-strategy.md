# Source Strategy

## Core idea

A video recommendation skill is only as good as its source taste.
Bad source selection produces generic output even when the reasoning is good.

Prefer creators, channels, and publishers that are:
- editorially sharp
- dense with signal
- aesthetically or intellectually alive
- trusted to not waste time

Avoid sources that are:
- engagement-maximized
- motivational sludge
- generic AI recap channels
- repetitive reaction content
- low-substance clickbait

## Preferred source types

1. Exact video pages on YouTube
2. Official talks / keynotes / conference videos
3. High-signal creator channels
4. Vimeo or filmmaker pages for short films
5. Playlists only when a set is explicitly useful

## Search strategy

Start from the user's current interests, not platform categories.

Translate the request into 2-4 search angles:
- topic
- creator type
- format
- mood

Examples:
- AI future + long-form conversation
- founder judgment + product podcast
- AI filmmaking + short film showcase
- systems thinking + documentary explainer

## Source buckets

### 1. AI / future / machine intelligence
Use for:
- users interested in AI capability, AI + humanity, civilizational change, model trajectories

Good source types:
- long-form conversations with top technical thinkers
- carefully produced explainers
- high-context interviews

Example sources:
- Dwarkesh Podcast
- Andrej Karpathy talks and interviews
- Kurzgesagt
- selected interviews with OpenAI / Anthropic / deep research figures

Avoid:
- daily AI news sludge
- “top 5 AI tools” channels
- shallow AGI doom hype unless the user explicitly wants that

### 2. Founder / product / strategy
Use for:
- users building products or companies
- users who want judgment, not hacks

Good source types:
- founder interviews
- product strategy podcasts
- operator breakdowns
- market-structure explainers

Example sources:
- Lenny’s Podcast
- selected YC / a16z founder content
- deep operator interviews

Avoid:
- fake productivity guru content
- startup advice content with no real operating depth

### 3. Creative technology / AI video / visual inspiration
Use for:
- AI video products
- filmmakers
- creators
- users needing aesthetic or workflow inspiration

Good source types:
- AI short films
- tool showcases with strong craft
- workflow breakdowns
- creative experiments with real taste

Example sources:
- Runway showcases
- standout Gen:48 shorts
- selected filmmaking breakdowns
- high-quality commercial / experimental AI pieces

Avoid:
- raw demo dumps with no taste
- generic “look what AI can do” montages
- novelty-only outputs

### 4. Systems / geopolitics / macro shifts
Use for:
- users with strategic curiosity
- users who care about where the world is heading

Good source types:
- macro explainers
- industry structure breakdowns
- technology + geopolitics narratives

Example sources:
- Polymatter
- ColdFusion
- selected documentary-style channels

Avoid:
- shallow hot takes
- outrage content disguised as analysis

### 5. Fun but not dumb
Use for:
- low-energy evenings
- mood rescue
- users wanting something alive without mental decay

Good source types:
- short, sharp explainers
- visually fresh creative pieces
- conceptually playful videos with substance

Avoid:
- empty viral bait
- loud comedy that has no overlap with the user's taste

## Ranking signals

Prefer videos that are:
- concrete
- editorially sharp
- clearly relevant to recent chat
- likely to produce delight, insight, or useful momentum

Down-rank videos that are:
- engagement bait
- repetitive summaries
- generic productivity sludge
- long without substance

## Curation rules

### Rule 1: Source quality beats topic match
A mediocre source on the right topic is often worse than a strong source on an adjacent topic.

### Rule 2: Avoid defaulting to the most famous result
If the recommendation feels too obvious, check whether a sharper adjacent pick exists.

### Rule 3: Mix familiarity with surprise
A strong set often contains:
- 2-3 trusted, high-signal picks
- 1-2 more surprising or less obvious picks

### Rule 4: A good recommendation has to feel alive
Even a relevant video should be dropped if it feels dead, bloated, or obligatory.

## Platform notes

### YouTube
- Prefer exact watch links
- Avoid sending search results unless the user asked for categories
- Extract titles and URLs cleanly

### Other platforms
- Use official pages when useful
- Prefer direct playback links over homepage links

## Anti-patterns

Do not over-recommend:
- generic recap channels
- listicle creators
- generic tech news personalities
- content farms around AI tools
- “watch this and get rich/smart/productive” channels
