# Testing

## Test prompts

### 1. Broad recommendation
Recommend some videos for me based on what we've been talking about lately.

### 2. Links only
Just give me 10 video links.

### 3. Immediate watch
I want 3 good videos to watch right now.

### 4. Mood-driven
Give me something fun tonight, but not dumb.

### 5. Project fuel
Recommend videos that could inspire an AI video editing product.

### 6. Mission / worldview
Give me videos about AI and humanity that are actually worth watching.

### 7. Tactical learning
Recommend videos that will make me sharper on AI products, not just entertained.

### 8. Creative inspiration
Find me videos that feel visually fresh and creatively alive.

### 9. Bilingual user
给我一些英文视频，适合晚上看，不要太水。

### 10. Precision challenge
Based on my recent chat, give me 5 videos I probably didn't know I wanted.

### 11. Anti-sludge challenge
Recommend videos that are high-signal and low-BS.

### 12. Shortlist challenge
Only give me your top 3. No filler.

### 13. Founder angle
Give me videos that help me think like a stronger founder.

### 14. Rabbit-hole mode
Send me one really good rabbit hole to go down tonight.

### 15. Constraint test
Recommend 5 videos, all under 20 minutes.

## Evaluation checklist

### Core quality
- Did the recommendations clearly reflect the user's request?
- Did they feel context-aware rather than generic?
- Did the list avoid filler?
- Were the links exact video links, not lazy search links?
- Did the output feel curated instead of algorithmic?

### Taste quality
- Would a sharp user feel understood?
- Did the picks feel alive, surprising, or useful?
- Was there too much obvious mainstream repetition?
- Was the set coherent without being monotonous?

### Formatting quality
- Did the output match the requested format?
- If the user wanted links only, was commentary minimal?
- If the user wanted curation, was rationale concise and relevant?

### Failure patterns to watch for
- Too generic
- Too broad
- Too many famous default picks
- Not enough relation to recent chat
- Search-result behavior instead of recommendation behavior
- Explanations longer than the value they add
- Recommendations that are informative but dead

### Scoring template
Rate each run from 1-5:
- relevance
- taste
- usefulness
- formatting
- delight

Optional overall note:
- What felt strongest?
- What felt weak or repetitive?
- What should the skill do differently next time?
