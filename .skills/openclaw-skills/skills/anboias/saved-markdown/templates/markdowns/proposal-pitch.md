# Proposal / Pitch Template

## When to Use This Template

- User asks for a proposal, pitch, business case, service quote, or project plan
- Keywords: proposal, pitch, business case, quote, estimate, project plan, SOW, engagement letter
- Output is meant to persuade or secure a deal/project/budget
- User is presenting a solution to a client's problem

## Styling Guidelines

- **Start with the client's problem, not your solution** — The first section should be about them
- **Bold totals and key numbers** — Investment totals, timeline dates, ROI figures should pop
- **Deliverables as checkboxes** — ✅ prefix makes deliverables feel concrete and committed
- **Timeline as a table** — Phase, Duration, Deliverables columns. No ambiguity about what happens when.
- **Clear pricing** — Line items, not a single lump sum. Transparency builds trust.
- **Next Steps are actions** — Each step should be something specific the client can do, with a verb leading.

## Professional Tips

1. **Start with the client's problem** — Never open with "About Us". Open with their pain point to show you understand it.
2. **Concrete dates, not "TBD"** — "Kickoff: April 1, Delivery: June 15" beats "8-12 weeks from contract signing"
3. **Deliverables are verifiable** — "Responsive web application deployed to production" beats "Website development"
4. **Show ROI when possible** — If you can estimate cost savings, revenue increase, or time saved, include it. Even rough numbers help.
5. **One clear CTA** — End with exactly one next step: book a call, sign the letter, reply to this email
6. **Price with line items** — Breaking down the cost shows transparency and lets the client understand where money goes
7. **Valid-until date** — Proposals without expiry dates get deprioritized indefinitely. Create urgency.
8. **Keep it under 3 pages equivalent** — Nobody reads 20-page proposals. Be concise.

**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Valid until:** 2026-04-15

FreshBite's current website was built in 2021 and hasn't been updated since. You need a modern, fast, mobile-first site that drives online orders and reflects the brand's growth to 6 locations.

A complete redesign focused on mobile ordering, location discovery, and brand consistency across all 6 FreshBite locations.
