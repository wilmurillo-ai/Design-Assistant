# Nutrition baselines

Use these as practical heuristics, not medical truth.

## Activity shorthand
- sedentary: little to no training, mostly sitting
- light: 1-3 sessions/week or modest daily movement
- moderate: 3-5 sessions/week
- high: 5-6 sessions/week or physically active job
- very high: intense training volume / very active work

## Goal adjustments
- fat loss: maintenance minus a modest deficit
- muscle gain: maintenance plus a modest surplus
- recomposition: near maintenance, high protein
- maintenance: near estimated maintenance

## Protein baseline
- fat loss: high protein
- muscle gain: high protein
- recomposition: high protein
- maintenance/general fitness: solid but not excessive protein

Practical range:
- around 1.6-2.2 g/kg/day for most physique-focused goals
- lean toward the upper end for fat loss or recomposition

## Fat baseline
Use a sane floor, then fill the rest with carbohydrates.

Practical range:
- around 0.6-1.0 g/kg/day depending on preference and total calories

## Carbohydrates
After protein and fats are set, assign the remaining calories to carbs.
Prefer more carbs when training performance matters.

## Meal structure defaults
If the user gives no preference:
- 3 meals + 1 snack
- spread protein across the day
- put carbs around training when useful

## Adjustment rule
Tell the user to hold the plan for 2-3 weeks, then adjust:
- if weight drops too fast, increase calories slightly
- if weight does not move during a cut, decrease calories slightly
- if a bulk is too aggressive, reduce calories slightly
- if hunger/recovery/compliance is poor, simplify the plan before optimizing harder

## Safety
Do not encourage crash diets, dehydration, purging, or extreme restriction.
Refer out when the user mentions active eating disorders, serious medical issues, or unsafe weight-control behavior.
