# The One-Day Life Reset Protocol: Complete Question Set

> This protocol takes a full day. Have paper, pen, and an open heart ready.

---

## Part 1: Morning — Psychological Excavation

### Section A: The Anti-Vision (What You DON'T Want)

**Phase 1: Witnessing Current Pain**

1. What is the low-grade, constant dissatisfaction you've normalized?
   - Not agony — just the quiet gap you've learned to tolerate
   - If you don't hate it, you tolerate it

2. What do you complain about but never actually change?
   - List the top 3 things you've complained about in the past year

3. For each complaint: If someone only watched your behavior (not heard your words) — what would they conclude you actually want?

4. What's a truth about your current life you wouldn't admit to someone you respect?

**Phase 2: Staring Into the Abyss**

5. If nothing changes in 5 years — describe a typical Tuesday in painful detail:
   - Where do you wake up?
   - How does your body feel?
   - What's your first thought?
   - Who's around you?
   - What do you do from 9am to 6pm?
   - How do you feel at 10pm?

6. Imagine 10 years from now, nothing changed. What did you miss? What opportunities closed forever? Who is disappointed in you? What do people say about you when you're not there?

7. Imagine you're on your deathbed. You lived safely, never broke the pattern. What did it cost you? What did you never feel, attempt, or become?

8. Does someone you know already live the life you just described in #5? (Perhaps 5, 10, 20 years ahead on the same path.) How do you feel becoming them?

9. To truly change, what identity would you have to abandon? ("I'm the kind of person who...")
   - What would it cost you socially to no longer be that person?

10. What is the most embarrassing reason you haven't changed yet?
    - The real reason you look懦弱/懒 rather than "reasonable"

11. If your current behavior is self-protection — what are you actually protecting?
    - What is this protection costing you?

### Section B: The Minimum Viable Vision

12. Forget reality. If you could wave a magic wand and be living a completely different life in 3 years — what do you TRULY want? Not "realistic" — what does your soul ache for? Describe a typical Tuesday in that life, with the same detail as #5.

13. To make that life feel natural rather than forced, what belief would you have to hold about yourself? Write an identity statement: "I am the kind of person who..."

14. If you were already that person — what's the FIRST thing you'd do this week?

---

## Part 2: Daytime — Breaking the Loop

**Set random reminders throughout the day (commute, walks, idle moments):**

### Hourly Check-ins

- **11:00** — What am I doing this for?
- **13:30** — If someone filmed my last 2 hours, what life would they think I want?
- **15:15** — Moving toward what I hate, or toward what I want?
- **17:00** — What am I pretending doesn't matter, that actually matters enormously?
- **19:30** — What did I do today to maintain an identity rather than from genuine desire? (Hint: most of what you did today)
- **21:00** — When did I feel alive today? When did I feel dead?

### Deep Thinking (Commute/Walking)

- If I didn't need anyone to see me as [my identity from #9] — what would change?
- Where in my life am I trading "aliveness" for "safety"?
- What is the smallest thing I could do tomorrow that my ideal self would naturally do?

---

## Part 3: Evening — Integration

### Integration Questions

1. After this day's reflection — what is the ROOT cause of my stagnation?

2. Who is my real enemy? Name it clearly. (Not external conditions, not other people — the internal pattern or belief running my life.)

3. One sentence: What I absolutely REFUSE to let my life become. (Reading this should trigger emotion.)

4. One sentence: What I'm actively BUILDING. (This will evolve as you grow.)

### Setting Goals as Perspective, Not Destination

> Don't cling to "finish lines" — you'll find there are none. The process IS the reward.

5. **One-Year View:** How will you know you've broken the old pattern? Write ONE specific thing you'll have done.

6. **Monthly View:** What needs to happen in the next month to make the one-year goal possible?

7. **Daily View:** What 2-3 things will your ideal self naturally do tomorrow?

---

## Execution Notes

- Work through in order — don't skip to "interesting" parts
- Don't use AI to answer for you — these questions are meant to break through your thinking
- If you can't answer immediately, return later — sit with the question
- Random reminder timing: More random = more effective, less tied to your routine
