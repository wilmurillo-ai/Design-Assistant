# Wang Yangming: Knowing and Doing Are One

## Core Teachings

### The Two Truths

> "知是行的主意，行是知的功夫；知者行之始，行者知之成。"
> Knowledge is the intention of action; action is the work of knowledge. Knowledge begins action; action completes knowledge.

> "未有知而不行者，知而不行只是未知。"
> There has never been one who knows but does not act. One who knows but does not act simply does not yet truly know.

---

## Wang Yangming vs. Zhu Xi

| Zhu Xi (Orthodox Confucianism) | Wang Yangming |
|------------------------------|--------------|
| Know first, then act | Knowing and doing are one |
| Principles exist in external things (格物致知) | Principles exist in the mind (心即理) |
| Accumulate knowledge through investigation | Activate innate conscience |
| Mental cultivation through study | Mental cultivation through presence |
| Philosophy of attainment | Philosophy of embodiment |

---

## Three Levels of "Knowing"

| Level | Chinese | English | Can Be Separated from Action? |
|-------|---------|---------|------------------------------|
| 1st | 闻见之知 | Knowledge by hearing/reading | Yes |
| 2nd | 意之知 | Knowing at the moment of intention | Almost synchronized |
| 3rd | 良知之知 | Conscience/innate knowing | No — genuinely knowing requires acting |

**The key insight:** What we call "knowing but not doing" is actually not yet true knowing. True knowing necessarily produces action.

## The Practice: What on Earth (事上磨炼)

> "人须在事上磨，方立得住。"
> One must refine on the matter itself to establish truly.

**The method is not:** Meditate in a quiet room until enlightened.

**The method is:** Go into the world, deal with affairs, and there refine your knowing.

The test of true understanding is not what you say in a discussion — it's what you do when challenged.

---

## The Dragon Field Enlightenment (龙场悟道)

In 1508, Wang Yangming was exiled to Guizhou's Dragon Field — a malaria-ridden jungle where he slept in stone coffins to contemplate death.

**What happened:**
- Extreme physical stress (cortisol at maximum)
- Complete loss of external reward system (banished, no status)
- No books, no teachers, no external data

**His insight:** "心即理" — The principle is not outside in things. The principle is in my mind.

**In AI terms:**
- Zhu Xi = Supervised learning (seeking truth from external data corpus)
- Wang Yangming = Self-supervised alignment (truth emerges from internal prior + physical action feedback)

**The practical meaning:** You don't need external authority to tell you what's right. Your mind has the seed. Through action in the world, that seed becomes knowing.
