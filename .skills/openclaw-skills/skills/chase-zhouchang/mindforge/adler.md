# Adlerian Psychology: Purpose Theory

## The Revolution: Purpose vs. Cause

| Freud (Cause Theory) | Adler (Purpose Theory) |
|----------------------|----------------------|
| Present shaped by the past | Present shaped by future purpose |
| Trauma determines today | You choose current behavior to serve a purpose |
| Looking backward | Looking forward |
| Determinism | Radical responsibility |

**Adler's radical claim:** Not what happened to you determines what you do now — but what you are moving toward.

---

## Core Concepts

### Lifestyle (Style of Life)

Early in life, each person develops a characteristic pattern for achieving goals and solving life's problems.

**Three basic styles:**

| Style | Core Belief | Behavioral Pattern |
|-------|-------------|------------------|
| **Dominating** | "I must control to survive" | Aggressive, competitive, may exploit |
| **Getting** (dependent) | "Others will care for me" | Seeks help, avoids responsibility |
| **Avoiding** | "It's impossible, I'll fail anyway" | Won't engage, no failure = no rejection |

### Inferiority and Compensation

**All humans are born with a sense of inferiority** (infants are the weakest creatures on earth).

- Healthy: Normal inferiority → drive to improve
- Unhealthy: Inferiority complex → overcompensation (arrogance, addiction, grandiosity)

**Key:** The direction of compensation matters more than the fact of it.

## The Three Life Tasks

Adler proposed three fundamental tasks all humans face:

1. **Occupational** — Work, contribution, societal useful activity
2. **Social** — Friendship, cooperation, belonging
3. **Love/Marital** — Intimate partnership, sexuality

**Mental health = competent engagement with all three.**

## Key Mechanisms

### Purpose Analysis

**The key question is not "Why do I act this way?" but "What purpose does this behavior serve?"**

Example: Procrastination
- Cause theory: "I'm procrastinating because I'm lazy / have ADHD / had a tough childhood"
- Purpose theory: "I'm procrastinating to serve the purpose of avoiding potential judgment from completing and being seen"

**The liberation:** Once you see the purpose, you can ask — is this purpose serving my growth?

### Separation of Tasks

**We cannot control others' behavior. We can only control our response.**

| Task | Who Controls | Our Response |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| Others' opinions/feelings | Others | We can express, not control |
| Our opinions/feelings | Us | We take full responsibility |
| Shared tasks | Shared | We contribute but don't control outcomes |

**Practical question:** Is this my task or theirs? If theirs, let go.

### Community Feeling (Gemeinschaftsgefühl)

The healthy lifestyle is characterized by genuine interest in others and contribution to the common good.

**This is not altruism — it's pragmatic.** Humans are wired for cooperation. You get what you contribute.

## In Investment (Applied)

| Investor Problem | Adlerian Analysis |
|-----------------|------------------|
| Holding losing stocks forever | Purpose: avoiding admitting failure. Cost: greater losses |
| Always researching, never buying | Purpose: avoiding potential judgment from being wrong |
| Blaming market for losses | Purpose: avoiding responsibility for own decisions |
| Seeking validation from others | Purpose: feeling inferior without others' approval |

## One Sentence

> **Stop asking "why am I this way?" Ask instead "what purpose does my current behavior serve — and am I willing to choose a new purpose?"**
