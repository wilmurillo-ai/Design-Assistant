# Three Schools, One Truth

## The Common Foundation

| School | Core Contribution | Key Term |
|--------|-------------------|---------|
| Wang Yangming | Knowing and doing are one | 致良知 (follow innate knowing) |
| Adler | Purpose theory | We are shaped by future goals, not past causes |
| Action Framework | Behavioral science | B=MAT, tiny habits |

**Their shared insight:** All three point to the same truth:

> **Your present action is always your choice — even when it feels like it isn't.**

---

## Wang Yangming: Unity of Knowing and Doing

| Confucian Orthodoxy (Zhu Xi) | Wang Yangming |
|------------------------------|--------------|
| Know first, then act | Knowing and doing are one |
| Know: external principles (向外求) | Know: innate conscience (良知) |
|格物致知: accumulate knowledge | 致良知: activate innate knowing |
| Know first, then act | Act to complete knowing |

### Three Levels of "Knowing" in Wang Yangming

| Level | Type | Can Be Separated from Action? |
|-------|------|------------------------------|
| 1st | Intellectual knowledge | Yes |
| 2nd | Intention (心动的一刻已有知) | Almost synchronized |
| 3rd | Conscience/良知 | No — if you truly know, you must act |

**Key insight:** "知而不行只是未知" — If you know but don't act, you don't truly know.

---

## Adler: Purpose Theory vs. Cause Theory

| Freud (Cause Theory) | Adler (Purpose Theory) |
|----------------------|----------------------|
| Past causes present | Future purpose drives present |
| You're shaped by trauma | You choose your current purpose |
| Fix the past to fix today | Change your purpose to change today |
| Determinism | Radical responsibility |

**The revolution:** What you do NOW is determined by what purpose you serve NOW — not by what happened THEN.

### The Key Question

**Not "why am I this way?" but "what purpose does my current behavior serve?"**

- Procrastinating? → Purpose: avoiding judgment from finishing and sharing
- Staying in dead-end job? → Purpose: safety + not looking like a failure to others

**The liberation:** Once you see the purpose, you can choose a new one.

---

## Action Framework: B=MAT

```
Behavior = Motivation + Ability + Trigger
```

| Element | Question | Common Mistake |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| Motivation | How much do I want this? | Vague or conflicting goals |
| Ability | How easy is it? | Target too hard, not too small |
| Trigger | What's the cue? | No environmental design |

**The key insight:** Lowering Ability (making it tiny) works better than raising Motivation.

## The Three-in-One Practice

### When "knowing but not doing":

**Step 1 — Awareness (Which school):**
- "What's my unconscious purpose?" (Adler)
- "What's my excuse?" (Brain: old program)
- "What's my conscience saying?" (Yangming)

**Step 2 — Choice:**
- "Is this MY choice or theirs?" (Adler: separation of tasks)
- "Am I willing to own it?" (Yangming: all from self)
- "What am I waiting for?" (Action: minimum action)

**Step 3 — Action:**
- Make it tiny (Fogg)
- Design the trigger (environment)
- Execute now (don't wait for "readiness")

---

## One Sentence

> **All three traditions converge: You are not determined by your past. You are defined by what you choose to do RIGHT NOW — and only action writes that choice into your brain.**
