# Source quality rubric

Use this rubric to weight evidence quality and resolve conflicts.

## Tiers (best to weakest)
1. Primary evidence
- Original datasets, official filings, court records, standards documents, direct transcripts.

2. Authoritative institutions
- National statistics offices, central banks, WHO/CDC-like agencies, regulator publications.

3. Peer-reviewed research and systematic reviews
- Prefer recent meta-analyses/reviews over single underpowered studies.

4. Reputable specialist journalism
- Strong editorial standards, transparent corrections, links to primary material.

5. General media / secondary summaries
- Useful for discovery; verify against higher tiers.

6. Social posts, anonymous blogs, repost aggregators
- Treat as leads only.

## Conflict resolution
- Prefer newest high-quality source when methods are comparable.
- Prefer direct measurement over commentary.
- Prefer methodology transparency over brand reputation alone.
- If disagreement persists, output `MIXED` or `UNVERIFIABLE` with explicit uncertainty.

## Freshness guidance
- Fast-changing topics (AI models, security incidents, markets): stale after ~30 days unless historical claim.
- Policy/legal: check effective date and jurisdiction.
- Science/medicine: check publication year, sample size, and replication status.

## Minimum citation hygiene
- Include title, publisher/author, date, and URL.
- Never cite a source you did not inspect.
- If access-limited, state limitation explicitly.
