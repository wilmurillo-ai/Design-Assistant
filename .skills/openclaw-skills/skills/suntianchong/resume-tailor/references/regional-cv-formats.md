# Regional CV/Resume Format Guide

## United States & Canada

- **Length**: 1 page (entry/mid-level), 2 pages (senior+). Never 3+ pages.
- **No photo**: including a photo can lead to discrimination concerns and may get resume discarded
- **No DOB, marital status, nationality**
- **Objective/Summary**: optional but helpful; 2-4 sentences
- **References**: "Available upon request" — don't list references on resume
- **Format**: Reverse-chronological. Action verb bullet points. Metrics.

## United Kingdom

- **Title**: "CV" not "Resume"
- **Length**: 2 pages standard (1 page for graduate/entry-level)
- **No photo** (same as US)
- **Personal statement**: 3-5 lines at the top, tailored to role
- **References**: Often listed at end (2 references with contact info), or "References available on request"
- **Date format**: DD/MM/YYYY

## Germany & DACH Region

- **Photo required**: Professional headshot in top-right corner (critical — missing photo is a red flag)
- **"Lebenslauf"** is the term for CV
- **Personal details**: DOB, nationality, marital status are standard and expected
- **Length**: 2 pages
- **Handwritten signature**: Some traditional companies still expect this on the last page
- **Application photo**: Should be a professional studio headshot, not a selfie
- **Cover letter ("Anschreiben")**: Almost always required and taken very seriously

## France

- **Photo**: Optional but common
- **Personal details**: Often included (age, nationality)
- **Length**: 1 page preferred
- **Language**: Must be in French unless applying to international company
- **"Lettre de motivation"**: Cover letter is expected and important

## China

- **Photo required**: Professional photo
- **Personal details**: DOB, gender, nationality, political affiliation (for government/SOE roles), marital status
- **Length**: 1-2 pages
- **Format**: Often includes GPA prominently; Chinese universities name recognition matters
- **Language**: Mandarin Chinese unless applying to foreign company

## Japan

- **"Rirekisho"**: Traditional Japanese resume form — often a standardized template
- **Photo required**: Taken within 3 months, professional attire
- **Handwritten** vs digital: Some traditional companies still prefer handwritten rirekisho
- **Personal details**: DOB, age, gender, address, emergency contact
- **Cover letter ("Shokumukeirekisho")**: Separate document from resume, details work history

## India

- **Length**: 2-3 pages accepted
- **Photo**: Common but not universal
- **Personal details**: Often includes DOB, gender, marital status
- **Father's name**: Sometimes included for government roles
- **Languages known**: List languages spoken
- **Declaration**: Many Indian CVs end with a "Declaration" section certifying accuracy

## Australia & New Zealand

- **Similar to UK style**
- **Length**: 2-3 pages acceptable
- **No photo** typically
- **Referees**: 2 referees with contact info at end, or "Available upon request"
- **Personal statement**: Not always expected

## European Union (General)

- **Europass format**: A standardized EU CV template — widely accepted, sometimes preferred for academic or government roles. Available at europass.eu.
- **Photo**: Varies by country (required in Germany, optional in Nordic countries)
- **GDPR note**: Some countries (Netherlands, Sweden) expect a GDPR consent clause if submitting electronically

## Key Cross-Cultural Tips

1. **Always research the specific company's hiring culture** — multinationals often follow US/UK norms regardless of country
2. **Language**: Always translate/write in the country's primary language unless you know English is acceptable
3. **Academic credentials**: Explain foreign credentials if applying internationally (e.g., "GPA 3.8/4.0 (top 10% of class)")
4. **Dates**: Use the local date format to avoid confusion
