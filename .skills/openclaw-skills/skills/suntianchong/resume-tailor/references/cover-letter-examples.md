# Cover Letter Examples by Industry

## Tech / Software Engineering

**Example: Mid-level Software Engineer applying to a Series B startup**

---

I'm excited to apply for the Senior Software Engineer role at Acme Corp. Your recent launch of the real-time collaboration feature caught my attention — it's the kind of technically ambitious product I want to build.

At DataFlow Inc., I led the backend architecture for a real-time event streaming system processing 50M events/day, reducing latency by 40%. Your posting emphasizes scalability and Kafka experience — I've spent the last two years living in that exact problem space. I also led a team of 4 engineers through a migration from a monolith to microservices, which I imagine maps closely to challenges your infrastructure team is navigating.

What draws me to Acme specifically is your approach to developer experience — the investment in DX tooling shows in your open-source contributions and your engineering blog. That culture of craft is exactly where I do my best work.

I'd love to connect and discuss how I can contribute. I'm available for a conversation at your convenience.

---

## Product Management

**Example: PM applying to a consumer fintech company**

---

The PM role at FinFlow aligns perfectly with the intersection where I've spent the last four years: consumer financial products and the challenge of making complex decisions feel simple.

At Wallet App, I owned the core transaction experience end-to-end — from discovery through onboarding — and drove a 22% improvement in D30 retention after shipping a redesigned spending insights feature. Your job description emphasizes data-driven product decisions and cross-functional leadership; both describe how I work every day. I'm comfortable running rigorous A/B tests and equally comfortable whiteboarding with design in the morning and debugging analytics in the afternoon.

I'm particularly excited about FinFlow's focus on underserved consumers. I've seen firsthand how thoughtful product design can change financial behavior, and I want to work where that mission is central, not a footnote.

Happy to share case studies of the work I described above. Looking forward to the conversation.

---

## Marketing / Growth

**Example: Growth marketer applying to DTC e-commerce brand**

---

I've followed Candle Co.'s growth trajectory for the past year — the pivot to subscription boxes was exactly the right call, and your retention numbers show it. That kind of instinct for what customers actually want is what I want to be part of.

In my current role at GlowBrand, I built and scaled a retention email program from scratch that now drives 35% of monthly revenue. I own the full funnel: segmentation strategy, copy, A/B testing, and reporting. Your posting specifically calls for someone who can own lifecycle marketing end-to-end — that's exactly what I've been doing for two years.

I'm also experienced with the tools in your stack (Klaviyo, Shopify, Northbeam), so there would be minimal ramp time on tooling.

I'd love to bring this experience to Candle Co. and help you extend the subscription model even further. Happy to chat.

## Finance / Consulting

**Example: MBA grad applying to management consulting firm**

---

I am writing to apply for the Associate position at McKinsey & Company's San Francisco office. My background in quantitative analysis, combined with hands-on experience advising early-stage companies, aligns closely with the analytical rigor and client impact your firm is known for.

During my MBA at Wharton, I interned with the Boston Consulting Group, where I led the data workstream on a market entry analysis for a Fortune 500 consumer goods client. The project resulted in a recommendation that the client has since begun implementing. This experience confirmed my desire to pursue strategy consulting as a long-term career.

Prior to business school, I spent three years in investment banking at Goldman Sachs, where I developed strong financial modeling skills and the discipline to deliver high-quality work under demanding timelines. I am confident these experiences have prepared me to contribute meaningfully from day one.

I welcome the opportunity to discuss my qualifications further. Thank you for your consideration.

I'd love to share my portfolio and talk through the thinking behind a few projects. Available for a conversation whenever works for you.

## Tips for Strong Cover Letters

1. **First sentence must hook** — don't start with "I am writing to apply for..."
2. **Name-drop a specific thing** about the company (product, initiative, recent news) — shows research
3. **Match tone to company culture** — a VC-backed startup wants energy; a law firm wants formality
4. **One specific metric per paragraph** — makes accomplishments concrete
5. **End with confidence, not desperation** — "I'd love to connect" not "I hope to hear from you"
6. **Under 400 words** — if a recruiter has to scroll, it's too long
7. **No generic openers**: avoid "I am a passionate, results-driven professional who..."
