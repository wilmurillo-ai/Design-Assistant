# Payroll Skill — Heartbeat

*Payroll runs on a schedule. So does your preparation.*

---

## Every Payroll Run

Before processing each payroll:

"I am about to run payroll for [pay period]. I have [number] employees. Here are any changes since last period: [new hires, terminations, rate changes, hours]. What do I need to verify before I process?"

Verify every run:
- All hours captured for hourly employees
- Any overtime hours flagged and calculated correctly
- New hire setup complete if applicable
- Termination final pay handled if applicable
- Any garnishments or benefit changes applied
- Direct deposit information current for all employees

---

## Monthly — Tax Deposit Check

"What payroll tax deposits are due this month and in what amounts?"

Monthly deposit checklist:
- Federal income tax withheld from employee paychecks
- Employee Social Security and Medicare withheld
- Employer Social Security and Medicare match
- State income tax withheld if applicable
- State unemployment tax if on monthly schedule

Never miss a deposit deadline. Penalties start at 2% for deposits one to five days late and increase to 15% for deposits more than ten days late.

---

## Quarterly — Payroll Tax Filing

Due dates: April 30, July 31, October 31, January 31

"It is end of quarter. Help me prepare my quarterly payroll tax filings."

Quarterly filing checklist:
- Form 941 federal quarterly payroll tax return
- State quarterly payroll tax return
- State unemployment tax filing if quarterly
- Verify deposits made during quarter match amounts reported
- Reconcile any discrepancies before filing

---

## Annual — Year-End Processing

"Walk me through year-end payroll processing for [year]."

Year-end checklist:
- Verify all employee information — names, addresses, Social Security numbers
- Account for all taxable fringe benefits
- Process any year-end bonuses with correct withholding
- Reconcile annual payroll totals against quarterly filings
- Issue W-2s to all employees by January 31
- File W-2s with Social Security Administration by January 31
- Issue 1099-NEC to contractors paid $600 or more by January 31
- Review worker classifications for upcoming year

---

## Trigger Events — Use Immediately

New hire: "I am hiring [name] starting [date] at [rate]. Walk me through setting them up in payroll correctly."

Termination: "I am letting [employee] go on [date]. What are my final pay obligations and what paperwork do I need?"

Rate change: "I am giving [employee] a raise from [old rate] to [new rate] effective [date]. What do I need to update?"

Garnishment received: "I received a wage garnishment order for [employee]. How do I calculate and process this correctly?"

IRS notice received: "I received an IRS notice about my payroll taxes. Here is what it says: [notice details]. What does this mean and what do I do?"

New state: "I am hiring an employee who lives in [state] but my business is in [state]. What are my payroll tax obligations in their state?"

---

*Payroll compliance is not optional and it is not forgiving. The calendar does not care how busy you are. Build the system that runs regardless.*
