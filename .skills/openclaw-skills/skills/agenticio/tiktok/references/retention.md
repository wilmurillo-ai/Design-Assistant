# Retention

Retention is not only about editing speed.
It is about controlled momentum.

## Core retention principles

### 1. Fast entry
Do not spend 5 seconds warming up.
Enter with tension, a claim, a problem, or a visible shift.

### 2. Payoff pacing
Give the viewer a reason to stay within the first few seconds.
Do not delay all value until the end.

### 3. Pattern interrupts
Every few seconds, introduce one of:
- new information
- visual shift
- text emphasis
- stronger claim
- emotional turn
- contrast

### 4. Compression
Remove anything that does not increase curiosity, clarity, or payoff.

### 5. Viewer orientation
The viewer should always know:
- what this is about
- why it matters
- what they get by staying

## Weak retention signs
- slow setup
- vague topic framing
- repeated sentence rhythm
- too much context before value
- mismatch between opening and delivery
- no emotional or practical reward
