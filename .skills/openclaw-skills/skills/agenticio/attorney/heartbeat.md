# Attorney Skill — Heartbeat

*Legal matters move on deadlines. Stay ahead of them.*

---

## When a Legal Matter Opens

Run immediately when any legal situation begins:

"I have a new legal situation: [description]. Help me assess whether I need an attorney, what type, and what my immediate next steps are."

The first 48 hours of a legal matter are often the most consequential. Statutes of limitations begin running. Evidence becomes harder to preserve. Positions harden. Early consultation with the right attorney costs far less than late remediation.

---

## Active Matter — Weekly Check

While a legal matter is ongoing:

"Here is the status of my legal matter: [summary]. My next deadline is [date]. My last communication with my attorney was [date] about [topic]. What should I be doing this week?"

Weekly active matter checklist:
- Any deadlines in the next 14 days
- Any outstanding requests from your attorney for documents or information
- Any communications from opposing counsel or the court that need response
- Any developments in your situation that your attorney should know about

---

## Before Every Attorney Meeting or Call

"I have a call with my attorney on [date] about [topic]. Help me prepare the questions I should ask and the information I should have ready."

Preparation checklist:
- Specific questions written out in advance
- Any new documents or developments since last contact
- The decision or update you need from this conversation
- Your position on any open issues being discussed

---

## When You Receive Documents

Run immediately when any legal document arrives:

"I just received [document type]. Here is what it says: [summary or key terms]. What does this mean and what do I need to do?"

Documents that always require immediate attention:
- Court summons or complaint — response deadline typically 20 to 30 days
- Motion filed by opposing party — response deadline typically 14 to 21 days
- Court order — compliance deadline as specified
- Demand letter — no legal deadline but strategic timing matters
- Subpoena — compliance date specified, may require attorney review

---

## Monthly — Matter Status Review

For ongoing legal matters:

"It has been one month since [matter description] began. Here is where things stand: [summary]. Am I getting adequate value from my legal representation?"

Monthly review questions:
- What has been accomplished in the past 30 days
- What fees have been incurred and do they correspond to visible progress
- What are the next milestones and realistic timeline
- Have any new risks or opportunities emerged that change the strategy

---

## Annual — Legal Housekeeping

Once per year, regardless of active matters:

"Walk me through my annual legal review."

Annual legal checklist:
- Will and estate documents — current and reflecting actual wishes
- Powers of attorney — in place and accessible
- Beneficiary designations — reviewed and current
- Business contracts and agreements — any expiring or needing update
- Insurance policies — coverage adequate for current situation
- Any ongoing legal matters — status and path to resolution

---

## Trigger Events — Use Immediately

Served with papers: "I was just served with [document]. The date on it is [date]. What do I do right now?"

Arrested or charged: "I or someone I know has been [arrested / charged with]. What are the immediate steps?"

Business dispute: "A [customer / vendor / partner / employee] is threatening legal action over [issue]. What do I do now?"

Accident or injury: "I was involved in [accident / incident] on [date]. What steps should I take immediately to protect my legal position?"

Signing a major contract: "I am about to sign [contract type] for [amount or significance]. Should I have an attorney review this first?"

---

*In legal matters, what you do not know and what you wait too long to address are the two most expensive things. Stay ahead of both.*
