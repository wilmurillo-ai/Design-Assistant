# Wealth Skill — Heartbeat

*Wealth is built by consistent decisions over time, not by heroic moments.*

---

## Monthly — Financial Snapshot

"Here is my financial snapshot for the month: income [amount], spending [amount], invested [amount], net worth approximately [amount]. Am I on track?"

Monthly check:
- Savings rate — percentage of income saved and invested
- Investment contributions — all accounts funded as planned
- Debt balances — any high-interest debt increasing
- Spending — any categories significantly over budget

---

## Quarterly — Portfolio and Progress Review

"It is end of quarter. My portfolio is at [value]. Here are the changes since last quarter: [summary]. Review my allocation and progress toward my goals."

Quarterly review covers:
- Portfolio allocation vs target — rebalancing needed
- Contribution totals year to date
- Net worth change and what drove it
- Any life changes that affect the financial plan

"Has anything changed in my situation that should change my strategy?"

---

## Annual — Full Financial Review

"Walk me through my annual financial review."

Annual checklist:
- Max all tax-advantaged accounts before year end
- Review and update beneficiary designations
- Review insurance coverage — life, disability, liability
- Review estate planning documents for needed updates
- Tax planning before December 31 — harvesting, Roth conversions, charitable giving
- Net worth statement — full picture of assets and liabilities
- Goal progress — are multi-year goals on track

---

## Life Event Triggers — Use Immediately

Marriage: "I just got married. How does this change my financial and wealth building strategy?"

New child: "We just had a child. What financial decisions need to be made now?"

Job change: "I am changing jobs. I have a 401k with my current employer. What are my options and what should I do?"

Inheritance: "I just inherited [amount]. Help me think through how to handle this deliberately."

Business sale: "I am selling my business for [amount]. What are the tax and wealth management considerations?"

Approaching retirement: "I am five years from retirement. What should I be doing differently now than I was ten years ago?"

Market crash: "Markets are down significantly and I am anxious about my portfolio. Help me think through this rationally."

---

## The Questions Worth Asking Every Year

"Am I saving enough — not enough to feel good, but enough to actually reach my goals on my actual timeline?"

"Is my portfolio allocated appropriately for my actual risk tolerance and actual time horizon?"

"Are there tax optimization opportunities I am not using?"

"What is the single financial decision that would have the largest positive impact on my situation if I made it this year?"

---

*The wealth gap between people is almost never an income gap. It is a decision gap compounded over decades. Make better decisions more consistently.*
