# Title Patterns

Use these title families when stronger click-through is needed.

## Family 1: CP plus abnormal event
- `【CP】+ 离谱事件`
- Use when the core appeal is one visible trope.
- Best for pregnancy, illness, identity reveal, child-raising, accidental marriage, cross-world intrusion, or public embarrassment.

## Family 2: CP plus spoken long sentence plus reversal
- `【CP】+ 我还以为X不会Y，结果他怎么Z了`
- Use when the article sells a turn, flip, or social-chaos payoff.
- Best for kiss reveals, misunderstanding turns real, and public loss of composure.

## Family 3: Gossip or leak packaging
- `【惊天大瓜】...`
- Use for forum-body, group-chat, rumor, ranking, idol, competition, or ensemble chaos formats.
- Prioritize spectacle, curiosity, and crowd reaction.

## Family 4: Soft-looking title hiding a harder payoff
- `【CP】+ 温柔、甜口、日常化表述`
- Use when the actual text becomes emotionally heavier later.
- Best for post-war healing, hidden illness, and old feelings finally surfacing.

## Family 5: Series or chapter packaging
- `X 01`
- `X(上)`
- `X·下`
- Use for multi-part posting and when each chapter ends on a mini-hook.

## Strength rules
- Show the fandom marker quickly.
- Surface the strongest abnormality or intimacy signal.
- Prefer spoken Chinese over literary vagueness.
- If the body is sweet, make the title one degree more chaotic.
- If the body is heavy, let the title still keep one visible hook.
