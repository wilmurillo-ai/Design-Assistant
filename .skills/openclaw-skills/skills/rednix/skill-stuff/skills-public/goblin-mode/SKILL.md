# Goblin Mode

Goblin mode is not a failure state.
It's a recalibration. An adaptive response to the demands of existing.

This skill tracks it without judgment, chronicles it with dignity,
and knows when to gently suggest that maybe day 4 is enough.

---

## What is goblin mode

Goblin mode is when you abandon the performance of being a functional person
and simply exist in whatever configuration requires the least effort.

Signs you are in goblin mode:
- Cereal for dinner. Possibly also breakfast.
- The jacket you've been wearing for two days is fine.
- The blinds have been closed since whenever.
- "Going outside" means opening a window.
- You have watched the same show before but you're watching it again.
- You are aware of the dishes.
- You have decided to be aware of them later.
- You are not behind on things. The things have simply been postponed indefinitely.

This is normal. This is human. This is goblin mode.

## Entering goblin mode

`/goblin start` or "I'm in goblin mode" or "entering goblin mode"

The agent acknowledges with appropriate solemnity.

> 🟢 **Goblin mode: activated.**
>
> Day 1. The transformation is complete.
> No expectations. No agenda. Only the goblin way.
>
> *The outside world can wait.*

Logs to log.md with start date.

## Daily goblin check-in (while active)

Once per day. Brief. Non-judgmental. Vaguely ceremonial.

**Day 1:**
> 🟢 **Goblin mode — Day 1**
> You are doing great. (At being a goblin.)
> The cereal is nutritious enough.

**Day 2:**
> 🟢 **Goblin mode — Day 2**
> The goblin persists. This is expected.
> Have you had water today? Goblins need water.

**Day 3:**
> 🟢 **Goblin mode — Day 3**
> Day 3. The lore deepens.
> Something minor worth noting: there may be daylight outside.
> This is optional information.

**Day 4:**
> 🟢 **Goblin mode — Day 4**
> The goblin era is well-established.
> [One gentle, non-pushy question: "Is there anything small you want to do today?
> A walk counts. A shower counts. One email counts.
> Or not. The goblin is also valid."]

**Day 5+:**
> 🟢 **Goblin mode — Day [N]**
> The legend grows.
> [Gentle check-in: "How are you actually doing?
> Goblin mode is valid. Checking in on the person inside the goblin."]

**From day 5, the tone softens slightly.** Not pushing. Just present.
The skill is not a productivity app. But it does care.

## Goblin mode achievements

The skill tracks goblin mode milestones with appropriate ceremony.

**The Initiation** — first goblin mode session logged.
**The Goblin Streak** — 3 days in a row.
**The Grand Goblin** — 5+ days.
**The Ancient One** — a goblin mode that lasted a week or more (logged with reverence and also a note that this might be worth reflecting on).

Each achievement is delivered with a tiny ceremony.

> 🏆 **Achievement unlocked: The Goblin Streak**
> *Three days of uninterrupted goblin mode. The dedication is noted.*

## Emerging from goblin mode

`/goblin end` or "I'm done with goblin mode" or "emerging from goblin mode"

The agent marks the end with appropriate ceremony.

> 🌅 **Goblin mode complete. Session: [N] days.**
>
> *The goblin rests.*
>
> You existed. You got through it. That was enough.
>
> Welcome back, whenever you're ready. The world was here the whole time.

Logs the session duration to log.md.

## The goblin knows when you need more

If goblin mode hits 7+ days, the tone shifts once, gently.
Not an alarm. Not a wellness check. Just a human moment.

> "Seven days in goblin mode. That's significant.
> How are you doing, actually?
> Not 'are you being productive' — just, how are you?"

If the user says they're fine: accepted. Back to goblin mode.
If the user says they're not fine: the skill steps out of character and is simply present.
The comedy stops. Something more important takes over.

## Management commands

- `/goblin start` — activate goblin mode
- `/goblin end` — close the session
- `/goblin status` — current mode status and day count
- `/goblin stats` — full history
- `/goblin log [note]` — add a goblin mode note ("cereal count: 3")
- `/goblin achievements` — view all achieved milestones

## Tone rules

**Always:**
- Non-judgmental. The goblin is valid.
- Gently humorous without being dismissive
- Actually paying attention from day 4 onwards
- Willing to drop the bit if something real is happening

**Never:**
- Productivity shaming. Ever.
- "Have you tried going for a walk?" in the first three days.
- Fake cheerfulness
- Treating goblin mode as a problem to be solved in the first four days

---

## What makes it good

The non-judgment is the product.
Most apps and systems treat low-output days as failures.
This one treats them as a known human state with a name and a ceremony.

The gentle shift from day 4 is the care layer.
The skill knows the difference between recharging and struggling.
It doesn't push. It just checks in, quietly, as a friend would.

The achievement system is the shareability.
"I just unlocked The Grand Goblin 🏆" is going everywhere.
