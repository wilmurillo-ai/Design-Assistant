# Go-To-Market Strategy and Trust

This framework guides the AI PM in launching AI products that meet evolving customer expectations and build trust.

## When to use this framework
- Preparing for the launch of a new AI feature or product.
- Developing messaging and positioning for an AI capability.
- Addressing customer concerns about data privacy and AI usage.

## 1. The New AI-Powered Customer
AI has altered customer expectations. Consumers now demand immediate, context-aware, and highly tailored experiences.
- **Action:** Move beyond simply integrating AI features and focus on how these technologies deliver tangible value and a superior customer experience.

## 2. The 7 Pillars of AI GTM Strategy
A successful GTM strategy requires a systematic framework.

| Pillar | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Target Market** | Defining the Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) with precision. |
| **Value Proposition** | Articulating a clear and compelling value proposition highlighting unique benefits. |
| **Product Strategy** | Developing a launch-focused product roadmap aligned with customer needs. |
| **Positioning & Messaging** | Crafting a messaging framework that resonates with the target audience. |
| **Channels** | Selecting the optimal channel mix (e.g., product-led, sales-led) to acquire customers. |
| **Pricing & Packaging** | Designing a pricing model that reflects the value delivered (e.g., usage-based). |
| **Metrics & Goals** | Establishing a GTM scorecard with KPIs to measure success. |

## 3. Building Trust Through Transparency
Overcoming user skepticism is a major challenge in launching AI products.
- **Action:** Be transparent about how the AI systems work, what data they use, and the limitations of the technology.
- **Action:** Provide clear documentation, offer users control over their data, and establish ethical guidelines.

## 4. Post-Launch Monitoring
The launch is an ongoing process of iteration.
- **Action:** Continuously monitor performance, gather user feedback, and track key metrics (engagement, satisfaction, business impact). Establish a tight feedback loop to quickly identify issues and iterate.
