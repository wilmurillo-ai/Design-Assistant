## Limitation Calendar Helper

When asked to calculate limitation, use this logic:

```
Input:  Cause of action date + Applicable law
Output: Deadline + Condonation window

Formula:
  Deadline = Cause of action date + Limitation period
  Condonation window = Deadline + [90 days for appeals / court discretion]

Key rules:
  • Section 12 Limitation Act: Exclude date of judgment / order being appealed
  • Section 12(2): Exclude time taken to get certified copy
  • Section 14: Exclude time spent in bona fide wrong-forum proceeding
  • Section 5: Apply for condonation if delay with "sufficient cause"
  • Sunday / holiday on last day → next working day is valid (Section 4)
```

**Common Limitation Periods Quick Table:**

| Action | Period | Starting Point |
|--------|---------|----------------|
| Suit on simple contract | 3 years | Date of breach |
| Suit on specialty (document under seal) | 12 years | Date of breach |
| Suit for possession (immovable) | 12 years | Date of dispossession |
| Suit for compensation (tort) | 3 years | Date of act/omission |
| Suit on judgment / decree | 12 years | Date of decree |
| Consumer complaint | 2 years | Date of cause of action |
| Cheque dishonour complaint | 1 month | Expiry of 15-day notice period |
| Appeal from District Court decree | 30 days | Date of decree |
| First appeal to HC | 90 days | Date of decree |
| Second appeal (SC of law) | 90 days | Date of HC judgment |
| SLP to SC | 90 days | Date of HC judgment |
| Writ petition (HC) | No fixed period — laches apply | — |
| Arbitration claim | 3 years | Date of cause of action |
| Section 34 (setting aside award) | 3 months + 30 days (max) | Date of receipt of award |

## GST for Advocates (Quick Reference)

- **SAC Code**: 998213 (Legal advisory and representation services)
- **Rate**: 18% GST
- **Threshold**: ₹20 lakhs annual turnover (₹10 lakhs in special category states)
- **Reverse charge**: Individual advocate → corporate client: corporate pays GST (not advocate)
- **Exemption**: Services to non-business individuals are exempt from GST
- **Registration**: Mandatory above threshold; optional below

## Professional Ethics Reminders (BCI Rules)

- Do not advertise legal services (Rule 36 — exceptions post-2023 SC ruling)
- Do not approach clients directly
- Maintain client confidentiality
- Do not hold funds beyond reasonable period without accounting
- File vakalatnama in every case
- Must not accept a brief if there is a conflict of interest
- Fee sharing only with enrolled advocates, not with touts
