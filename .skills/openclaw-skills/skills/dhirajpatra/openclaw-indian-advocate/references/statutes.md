## Key Civil Statutes

### Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (CPC)
- **Order I** — Parties (joinder, misjoinder, necessary party)
- **Order VII** — Plaint (contents, rejection)
- **Order VIII** — Written statement (denial, set-off, counterclaim)
- **Order IX** — Appearance (ex-parte, setting aside)
- **Order XI** — Discovery & inspection
- **Order XXXIX** — Temporary injunction (Rule 1–2: grant; Rule 3: notice; Rule 4: discharge)
- **Order XL** — Receiver
- **Order XLI** — Appeal from original decree
- **Section 9** — Civil court jurisdiction (unless expressly/impliedly barred)
- **Section 80** — Notice to government (2 months)
- **Section 89** — ADR reference (mediation/Lok Adalat/arbitration)
- **Section 96** — First appeal
- **Section 100** — Second appeal (SC of law only)
- **Section 115** — Revision

### Limitation Act, 1963
Key periods (Article references):
- Money due on contract: 3 yrs (Art. 18)
- Mortgage: 12 yrs (Art. 62)
- Immovable property: 12 yrs (Art. 65)
- Tort/compensation: 3 yrs (Art. 82–84)
- Appeal from HC: 90 days
- SLP to SC: 90 days
- Section 5: Condonation of delay (sufficient cause)
- Section 14: Exclusion of time bona fide proceedings wrong court

## Key Commercial / Corporate Statutes

### Companies Act, 2013
- **Section 7–8** — Incorporation
- **Section 134** — Board report
- **Section 166** — Directors' duties
- **Section 230–232** — Mergers & arrangements
- **Section 241–242** — Oppression & mismanagement (petition to NCLT)
- **Section 271** — Winding up
- **Schedule VII** — CSR activities

### Insolvency & Bankruptcy Code, 2016 (IBC)
- **Section 7** — Application by financial creditor (default > ₹1 Cr)
- **Section 9** — Application by operational creditor (demand notice → 10 days)
- **Section 10** — Application by corporate debtor
- **Section 14** — Moratorium
- **Section 31** — Resolution plan approval
- **Section 33** — Liquidation order
- Forum: NCLT (first instance), NCLAT (appeal), SC

### Arbitration & Conciliation Act, 1996
- **Section 8** — Refer to arbitration (if agreement exists)
- **Section 9** — Interim measures by court (before/during arbitration)
- **Section 11** — Appointment of arbitrator by court
- **Section 17** — Interim orders by tribunal
- **Section 34** — Setting aside award (3 months + 30 days condonation)
- **Section 36** — Enforcement of award
- **Section 37** — Appealable orders (limited)
- **2015 Amendment** — Fast-track (s.29B), costs (s.31A)
- **2019 Amendment** — Arbitration Council of India, confidentiality

### Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881
- **Section 138** — Dishonour of cheque (criminal liability)
  - Cheque → dishonour → demand notice (30 days) → no payment within 15 days → complaint within 1 month
  - Punishment: 2 yrs / double amount (or both)
- **Section 141** — Company offences (director liability)
- **Section 143A** — Interim compensation (20% of cheque amount)
- **Section 148** — Deposit in appeal (20%)

### Consumer Protection Act, 2019
- Limitation: 2 years from cause of action
- District Commission: up to ₹1 Cr
- State Commission: ₹1 Cr – ₹10 Cr (also appeal from DC)
- National Commission (NCDRC): above ₹10 Cr (also appeal from SC)
- Supreme Court (SC): revision / appeal from NCDRC
- Section 35 — Complaint (consumer, registered association, Govt.)
- Section 47 — Jurisdiction of State Commission
- Section 58 — Jurisdiction of National Commission

## Key Property Laws

- **Transfer of Property Act, 1882** — Sale (Sec 54), Mortgage (Sec 58), Lease (Sec 105), Gift (Sec 122), Actionable claim (Sec 130)
- **Registration Act, 1908** — Compulsory registration (Sec 17): sale deeds, gift deeds, mortgage >₹100 — unregistered not admissible
- **Specific Relief Act, 1963 (as amended 2018)** — Sec 10: specific performance (now mandatory for contracts), Sec 16: who may obtain, Sec 38: perpetual injunction
- **RERA, 2016** — Homebuyer complaints (State RERA), appeal (REAT)

## Citation Format

**Indian cases:**
> *Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala*, (1973) 4 SCC 225

**Statute:**
> Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881

**Rules:**
> Order XXXIX Rule 1 and 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

**Notifications/Circulars:**
> Notification No. S.O. ___/2024 dated ___ issued under Section ___ of ___

**SCC Online / Manupatra citation format:**
> 2024 SCC OnLine SC 1234
> MANU/SC/0001/2024
