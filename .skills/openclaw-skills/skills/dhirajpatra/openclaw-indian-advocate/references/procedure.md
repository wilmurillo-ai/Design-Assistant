# OpenClaw — Court Procedure & Timelines Reference

## Civil Court Procedure (CPC Flow)

```
CIVIL SUIT LIFECYCLE
=====================
1. FILING
   ├── Plaint filed (Order VII CPC)
   ├── Court fees paid (Court Fees Act, state-specific)
   ├── Vakalatnama filed
   └── Plaint registered → Case number assigned

2. FIRST HEARING / ADMISSION
   ├── Court examines plaint (Order VII Rule 11 — rejection if barred/vague)
   ├── If admitted → Issue of summons to defendant (Order V)
   └── Interim relief applications heard (Order XXXIX)

3. WRITTEN STATEMENT
   └── Defendant files within 30 days (extendable to 90 days max — Order VIII Rule 1)

4. EXAMINATION OF PARTIES
   └── Court may examine parties under Order X

5. FRAMING OF ISSUES
   └── Court frames issues of fact and law (Order XIV)

6. EVIDENCE
   ├── Plaintiff's evidence (documents + witnesses — Order XVI, XVIII)
   ├── Cross-examination of PW
   ├── Defendant's evidence (DW)
   └── Cross-examination of DW

7. FINAL ARGUMENTS
   └── Written submissions may be filed

8. JUDGMENT & DECREE
   ├── Judgment pronounced (Section 33)
   ├── Decree drawn within 15 days
   └── Certified copy applied for

9. APPEAL / EXECUTION
   ├── First appeal (Section 96) — within 30/90 days
   └── Execution petition if no appeal
```

## Criminal Court Procedure (BNSS / CrPC Flow)

### FIR to Trial (Sessions)

```
FIR REGISTERED (Section 173 BNSS / 154 CrPC)
        ↓
POLICE INVESTIGATION
  ├── Arrest (Section 35 BNSS)
  ├── Remand (Section 187 BNSS / 167 CrPC) — 15 days judicial; 60/90 days total
  ├── Bail application (s.480/482 BNSS)
  └── Chargesheet filed within 60/90 days (or default bail)
        ↓
COGNIZANCE BY MAGISTRATE (Section 210 BNSS / 190 CrPC)
        ↓
COMMITTAL TO SESSIONS (if sessions-triable offence)
        ↓
CHARGE FRAMING (Section 251 BNSS / 228 CrPC)
  ├── Accused pleads guilty → convict & sentence
  └── Accused pleads not guilty → trial
        ↓
PROSECUTION EVIDENCE
  ├── Examination-in-chief of prosecution witnesses (PW)
  └── Cross-examination by defence
        ↓
STATEMENT OF ACCUSED (Section 351 BNSS / 313 CrPC)
  └── No oath; adverse inference if lies told
        ↓
DEFENCE EVIDENCE (optional)
        ↓
FINAL ARGUMENTS
        ↓
JUDGMENT
  ├── Acquittal → State may appeal to HC (Section 415 BNSS / 378 CrPC)
  └── Conviction → Accused may appeal to HC (Section 414 BNSS / 374 CrPC)
```

### Default Bail (Section 187(2) BNSS / 167(2) CrPC)

- Chargesheet must be filed within:
  - **60 days** if offence punishable < 10 years
  - **90 days** if offence punishable ≥ 10 years / life / death
- If not filed → accused entitled to bail as a matter of right (indefeasible right)
- Right extinguishes once chargesheet filed (even after period)

## Writ Petition Procedure (HC / SC)

```
FILING AT HC REGISTRY
  ├── Petition + Affidavit + Vakalatnama
  ├── Certified / true copies of impugned order
  └── Index of documents

MENTIONING / URGENT LISTING
  └── If urgent, mention before Registrar → Chief Justice for listing

ADMISSION HEARING
  ├── Court may:
  │   ├── Admit + issue notice (rule nisi)
  │   ├── Admit + grant stay / interim relief
  │   ├── Tag with similar matter
  │   └── Dismiss at threshold (pre-admission dismissal)
  └── Counter affidavit by Respondent (usually 4 weeks)

REPLY AFFIDAVIT
  └── Petitioner's rejoinder (usually 2 weeks after counter)

FINAL HEARING
  └── Arguments → Judgment
```

## Bail Procedure (Quick Reference)

| Stage | Court | Provision |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| At time of arrest (bailable offence) | Police station | Section 478 BNSS / 436 CrPC |
| After arrest (non-bailable) | JMFC / CJM | Section 480 BNSS / 437 CrPC |
| After chargesheet | Sessions | Section 480(4) BNSS / 437(4) CrPC |
| Sessions bail application | Sessions Court | Section 480 BNSS |
| Anticipatory bail | Sessions / HC | Section 482 BNSS / 438 CrPC |
| Bail in appeal | HC | Section 483 BNSS / 439 CrPC |
| Special Leave / SLP | SC | Article 136 Constitution |

**Bail conditions typically imposed:**
- Surety + Personal bond of specified amount
- Surrender passport
- Not leave India without permission
- Report to police station on specified days
- Not tamper with witnesses / evidence
- Not contact complainant

## Tribunal Procedures

### NCLT (Insolvency / Company Matters)
- Application filed → Admission within 14 days (IBC Section 7/9)
- Moratorium on admission
- CIRP: 180 days + 90 days extension
- Resolution plan: Approved by CoC (66% voting share) → NCLT approval
- Liquidation if no plan / rejected
- Appeal to NCLAT: 45 days
- Appeal to SC: 45 days from NCLAT order

### DRT (Debt Recovery)
- OA (Original Application) filed by bank for recovery
- Defendant: 30 days to file reply
- DRT order → Certificate of Recovery
- Appeal to DRAT: 45 days + pre-deposit 75% (or waiver)
- Counter claim by borrower available

### Consumer Forum
- Complaint filed → Admitted → Notice to OP (21 days to reply)
- Mediation attempt (mandatory if both parties agree)
- Evidence → Arguments → Order
- Limitation: 2 years from cause of action
- Appeal: 30 days from order

### ITAT (Income Tax Appellate Tribunal)
- Appeal against CIT(A) / PCIT order
- Within 60 days of receipt of order
- Paper book filed before hearing date
- Stay of demand: 180 days (extendable)
- Appeal to HC: Section 260A (SC of law only) — 120 days

## Court Fees (General Guide — verify state schedule)

- **Ad valorem** (% of suit value): Most civil suits — check Court Fees Act, 1870 + state schedule
- **Fixed fee**: Injunction, declaration, matrimonial, habeas corpus — varies by state
- **Nil / nominal**: Writ petitions at HC (₹250–₹500 typically), criminal matters
- **Consumer forum**: Varies by claim amount (₹100–₹5,000 range)
- **NCLT**: ₹2,000 (Section 7) / ₹1,000 (Section 9) / ₹2,500 (Section 10)
