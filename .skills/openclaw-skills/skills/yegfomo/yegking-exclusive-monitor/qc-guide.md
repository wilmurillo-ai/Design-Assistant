## Step-by-Step QC Checklist

### 1. 📦 Box
- Check brand logo placement and font style
- Verify the size label matches the shoe size
- Look for clean print quality — no smudging or misalignment
- Box should feel sturdy (not flimsy cardboard)

### 2. 👟 Overall Shape & Silhouette
- Compare the overall shape against the retail reference
- Pay attention to the toe box height and width
- Check the ankle collar shape and height
- Ensure symmetry between left and right shoe

### 3. 🪡 Stitching Quality
- Look for even, consistent stitch count per inch
- No loose threads, skipped stitches, or uneven tension
- On Jordan 1s: check the wing logo stitching and ankle area
- On Yeezys: check the knit pattern uniformity

### 4. 🎨 Materials & Colorway
- Check leather texture (should be smooth, not plasticky)
- Verify colorway accuracy under natural lighting
- On Dunk/AF1: check swoosh placement and color accuracy
- Look for even dye distribution — no fading patches

### 5. 👁️ Logo & Branding Details
- Nike/Adidas logo should be perfectly centered
- Font weight and spacing should match retail
- Check hang tags, insole branding, and tongue label
- Verify the heel tab logo stitching (on Jordan 1, Travis Scott, etc.)

### 6. 🔩 Sole & Outsole
- Check sole color — should match retail (not yellowed or off-tone)
- Verify tread pattern sharpness — no blurry or fused details
- Boost material (on Yeezys) should look pebbly and dense
- Air unit (on Jordan/Nike) should be clearly visible and correct shape

### 7. 👁️ Lace Holes (Eyelets)
- Should be cleanly punched — no rough edges
- Metal eyelets should be evenly spaced and aligned
- Check that the laces are the correct width and material

### 8. 🛏️ Insole
- Pull out the insole and check both sides
- Branding should be legible and correctly placed
- Insole thickness should feel substantial, not paper-thin
- Check for correct arch support shape

### 9. 🦶 Heel Area
- Heel counter should be stiff and hold its shape
- Check heel tab: height, angle, and stitching
- Verify the heel logo/label placement

## Common Red Flags ⚠️

| Issue | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Glue marks or excess adhesive | Poor quality control at factory |
| Uneven sole thickness | Off-balance midsole press |
| Mismatched left/right stitching | Assembly error |
| Wrong font on labels | Wrong template used |
| Yellowed boost/sole | Old stock or poor storage |

## Batch-Specific QC Notes

- **LJR Batch**: Known for best stitching on Jordans — focus QC on toe box shape and sole color
- **PK Batch**: Best for Yeezy/Adidas — focus QC on boost texture and knit pattern accuracy
- **OG Batch**: Highest-end production — check for minimal glue and perfect box printing
