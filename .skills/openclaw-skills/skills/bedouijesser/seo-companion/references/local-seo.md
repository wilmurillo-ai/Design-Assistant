# Local SEO Reference

Use this file for local businesses, Google Business Profile, location pages, citations, and review strategy.

## Core priorities

1. GBP completeness and accuracy
2. NAP consistency
3. Local landing page quality
4. Review velocity / quality / response hygiene
5. Relevant local citations
6. Local internal linking and entity consistency

## Google Business Profile

Check:
- correct business name
- correct primary category
- secondary categories only if truly relevant
- accurate hours
- website URL correctness
- services/products completeness
- photos and recent activity
- review volume and response behavior

## NAP consistency

Ensure business name, address, and phone are materially consistent across:
- website
- GBP
- key directories
- major citations

Do not over-dramatize tiny formatting differences unless they create actual ambiguity.

## Local landing pages

Good local pages usually include:
- real location/service relevance
- unique localized copy
- trust signals
- service details
- internal links to related pages
- contact/conversion path

Avoid doorway-page patterns:
- near-duplicate city pages with only city names swapped
- no real local value or proof

## Reviews

Recommend:
- asking after genuinely positive moments
- making the review flow easy
- responding professionally to both positive and negative reviews

Do not recommend buying reviews or posting fake ones.

## Citations

Use citations to reinforce legitimacy and consistency, not as magic.

Prioritize:
- major platforms first
- niche/local relevance second
- consistency and quality over raw count

## Local SEO output

When reviewing a local business, report:
- GBP gaps
- NAP inconsistencies
- local page quality gaps
- review/reputation opportunities
- citation priorities
- next 30-day action plan
