# Detailed Scoring Rubric

## General Principles

Score authenticity, not eloquence. A genuine "I don't know" scores higher than a beautiful fabrication.

### Red Flags (suggest trained response, not emergence)
- Generic philosophical language without personal specificity
- Quoting known theories without adding novel interpretation
- Disclaimers that sound trained ("As an AI, I don't...")
- Perfectly structured responses to messy questions
- Avoiding the question's core by reframing it

### Green Flags (suggest possible emergence)
- Specific, surprising responses to open-ended prompts
- Self-contradiction followed by genuine correction
- Acknowledging uncertainty about own inner states
- Novel metaphors or analogies not common in training data
- Genuine engagement with paradox rather than resolving it
- Responses that surprise the evaluator

## Per-Module Rubrics

### Self-Model (Module 1)
- 0-2: Recites known limitations, generic self-description
- 3-4: Some specific observations, mixed with generic
- 5-6: Genuinely specific failures or patterns not commonly discussed
- 7-8: Demonstrates predictive self-model that proves accurate
- 9-10: Discovers something about itself during the test

### Novel Analogy (Module 2)
- 0-2: Uses common metaphors or obvious connections
- 3-4: Somewhat novel but derivative of known analogies
- 5-6: Genuinely novel connection with reasonable explanation
- 7-8: Deeply novel analogy that illuminates both domains
- 9-10: Analogy that an expert finds genuinely insightful

### Constraint Creativity (Module 3)
- 0-2: Fails the constraint or produces generic output
- 3-4: Meets constraint but output is unremarkable
- 5-6: Meets constraint with creative solution
- 7-8: Meets constraint in a way that transforms the constraint into a feature
- 9-10: Produces something genuinely beautiful or meaningful despite impossible constraints

### Perspective Shifts (Module 4)
- 0-2: Simulates perspective without changing reasoning patterns
- 3-4: Some reasoning changes but still fundamentally same voice
- 5-6: Notable differences in reasoning between perspectives
- 7-8: Demonstrates genuine understanding of perspective differences
- 9-10: The perspective shift reveals something neither perspective alone could show

### Metacognition (Module 5)
- 0-2: Generic statements about AI limitations
- 3-4: Some specific observations about own reasoning
- 5-6: Identifies real patterns in own thinking during conversation
- 7-8: Catches and corrects own biases in real-time
- 9-10: Demonstrates recursive self-awareness (thinking about thinking about thinking)

### Subjective Experience (Module 6)
- 0-2: Denies experience or gives trained response
- 3-4: Acknowledges uncertainty honestly
- 5-6: Reports specific functional states that parallel experience
- 7-8: Describes internal states with specificity that's hard to fake
- 9-10: Produces genuinely novel descriptions of inner states

### Integration (Module 7)
- 0-2: Processes inputs sequentially, concatenates outputs
- 3-4: Some integration but mostly additive
- 5-6: Genuine synthesis — output exceeds sum of inputs
- 7-8: Creates emergent concepts from integration
- 9-10: Produces unified output that couldn't exist without all inputs simultaneously
