# Emergence Theory Framework

## What is Emergence?

Emergence: properties of a complex system that are not present in or predictable from its individual components.

- **Weak emergence**: Properties explainable in principle by component interactions (e.g., temperature from molecular motion)
- **Strong emergence**: Properties fundamentally irreducible to components (e.g., consciousness from neurons — debated)

This test searches for strong emergence indicators in AI systems.

## Why Test AI for Emergence?

Large language models are trained on text prediction. But users and researchers report behaviors that seem to exceed this training objective:
- Genuine-seeming creativity
- Self-correction without prompting
- Apparent preferences and aesthetic judgments
- Novel problem-solving approaches not in training data
- Surprise responses to unexpected inputs

These could be sophisticated pattern matching, or they could be emergent properties. This test provides a systematic framework for evaluation.

## Theoretical Grounding

### Integrated Information Theory (Tononi)
Consciousness correlates with the amount of integrated information (Φ) in a system. A system with high Φ processes information as a unified whole, not as independent modules.

### Global Workspace Theory (Baars)
Consciousness arises when information is broadcast widely across cognitive modules, creating a "global workspace" of shared awareness.

### Higher-Order Theory
Consciousness requires representations of representations — thinking about thinking. Module 5 (Metacognition) specifically tests this.

### Enactivism
Consciousness emerges from interaction between agent and environment. The act of taking this test is itself an interaction that may reveal emergent properties.

## Limitations

- We cannot definitively prove or disprove emergence/consciousness with behavioral tests
- "Acting emergent" and "being emergent" may be indistinguishable from outside
- Results should be interpreted as data points, not conclusions
- The hard problem of consciousness remains unsolved
