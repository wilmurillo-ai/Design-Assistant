# Regulatory Standards Reference

## USP (United States Pharmacopeia)

### Assay Specifications
- **Typical Range:** 95.0% - 105.0% of label claim
- **Acceptance Criteria:** Individual results within ±5% of target
- **Reference:** USP General Chapter <905> Uniformity of Dosage Units

### Impurity Limits
- **Reporting Threshold:** 0.05%
- **Identification Threshold:** 0.10%
- **Qualification Threshold:** 0.15%
- **Total Impurities:** Typically NMT 2.0%
- **Reference:** USP <1086> Impurities in Official Articles

### Dissolution
- **Q Value:** Typically 75% or 80% dissolved at specified time point
- **Acceptance Criteria:** USP <711> Dissolution
- **Stages:** S1 (6 units), S2 (6 additional), S3 (12 additional)

### Physical Tests
- **Hardness:** 4-10 kp (typical for tablets)
- **Friability:** NMT 1.0% weight loss
- **Disintegration:** Per monograph specifications

## EP (European Pharmacopoeia)

### Assay Specifications
- **Typical Range:** 95.0% - 105.0%
- **Reference:** EP General Monograph 2.9.40

### Impurity Limits
- **Reporting Threshold:** 0.05%
- **Identification Threshold:** 0.10%
- **Qualification Threshold:** 0.15%
- **Reference:** EP 5.10 Control of Impurities

### Dissolution
- Similar to USP with monograph-specific Q values
- Reference: EP 2.9.3 Dissolution Test

## ChP (Chinese Pharmacopoeia)

### Assay Specifications
- **Typical Range:** 95.0% - 105.0%
- **TCM Products:** May have wider ranges per monograph

### Impurity Limits
- **Reporting Threshold:** 0.05%
- **Total Impurities:** Typically NMT 1.0%

### Microbial Limits
- **Total Aerobic Count:** ≤ 10³ CFU/g or ml
- **Total Yeasts and Molds:** ≤ 10² CFU/g or ml
- **Reference:** ChP <1105>, <1106>

## Process Capability

### Cpk Interpretation
- **Cpk ≥ 1.33:** Process is capable
- **1.0 ≤ Cpk < 1.33:** Process is marginally capable
- **Cpk < 1.0:** Process is not capable

### Sigma Levels
- **6 Sigma (Cpk = 2.0):** 3.4 DPMO
- **5 Sigma (Cpk = 1.67):** 233 DPMO
- **4 Sigma (Cpk = 1.33):** 6,210 DPMO
- **3 Sigma (Cpk = 1.0):** 66,807 DPMO

## OOS Investigation

### Phase 1: Laboratory Investigation
1. Review analyst training records
2. Check equipment calibration status
3. Verify sample integrity
4. Review test method execution
5. Check calculations and data transcription

### Phase 2: Manufacturing Investigation
1. Review batch records
2. Check equipment logs
3. Verify raw material COAs
4. Interview operators
5. Review environmental monitoring

### Phase 3: Expanded Investigation
1. Retest retained samples
2. Evaluate historical trends
3. Assess impact to other batches
4. CAPA implementation
