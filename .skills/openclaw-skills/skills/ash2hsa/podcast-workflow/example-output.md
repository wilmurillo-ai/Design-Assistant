1/ Most startup founders hire wrong in the same way. Here's the framework Sarah Chen used to scale 3 companies past Series B 🧵

2/ The first mistake: filtering for "culture fit." Your early team should challenge your defaults, not confirm them. You want culture *add*, not culture *match*.

3/ The interview question that actually predicts startup performance: "Tell me about a time you changed your mind about something important at work." The quality of their answer reveals intellectual honesty.

4/ On equity: founders consistently overpay cash and underpay equity. It filters for the wrong people. The candidates who negotiate hardest on salary are usually not the ones who think long-term.

5/ When to hire specialists vs. generalists: if a function needs deep expertise to *start*, hire a specialist. If it needs to *figure itself out*, hire a generalist who can grow into it.

6/ The reference check most founders skip: ask about failures, not strengths. "What's something [candidate] struggled with?" gets you more signal in 2 minutes than a 45-minute interview.
