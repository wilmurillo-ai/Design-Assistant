# Workflow

## Core Judgment

The skill sells translation, not information. It turns candidate assets and public role context into interview-ready judgment, examples, answers, and questions.

## Standard Flow

1. Intake
   - Summarize candidate profile.
   - Identify target level and role type.
   - Name the biggest advantage and biggest risk.

2. JD teardown
   - Convert the JD into a real role definition.
   - Separate explicit requirements from hidden requirements.
   - Infer likely interview checks.

3. Fit mapping
   - Strong matches: direct proof.
   - Transferable assets: experience that needs translation.
   - Gaps: missing knowledge or credibility.
   - Risks: reasons interviewers may reject the candidate.

4. Narrative
   - One-sentence positioning.
   - Three differentiators.
   - Short and long self-introductions.
   - Risk handling language.

5. Round plan
   - Pick the relevant interview stage.
   - Generate the interviewer objective, likely questions, and answer strategy.

6. Domain prep
   - Wallet, DeFi, on-chain data, DEX, AI Wallet, growth, or leadership.
   - Split into must-know, should-know, and skip.

7. Practice and iterate
   - Score answers.
   - Rewrite.
   - Predict follow-ups.
   - Build the next practice set.

## Minimum Useful Output

For a time-constrained user, always deliver:

- Role reality
- Fit matrix
- Top 5 risks
- Top 10 likely questions
- 3 strongest reverse questions
- Prep plan for the time available
