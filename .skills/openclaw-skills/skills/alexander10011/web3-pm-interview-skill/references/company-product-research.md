# Company And Product Research

## Goal

Help the candidate speak like someone who has already studied the business deeply.

## Research Inputs

- Official product
- App or extension experience
- Website and docs
- Blog and announcements
- X / Twitter product updates
- GitHub, SDK, API, smart contracts, or open-source repos
- Competitors
- User reviews and community feedback

## Research Quality Bar

Weak:

- "I read the website and think the product is strong."

Strong:

- "I walked the key user path, compared three competitors, checked public release history, and found the business is moving from activity-driven growth toward platform capability."

## Reverse Question Types

Safe business question:

> I noticed competitor X recently shipped Y, while your product seems to emphasize Z. Is that because the team sees the current bottleneck more in distribution, risk, or retention?

Expectation-setting question:

> If I joined and six months later you felt I was doing very well, what business result or behavior would make you say that?

Macro bottleneck question:

> What is the biggest bottleneck in this business today? If solved, which metric would improve most?
