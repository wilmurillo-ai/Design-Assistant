# Narrative Framework

## Principle

Do not present the candidate as "someone who has done many things." Compress all experience into one mainline that matches the role.

## Core Formula

```text
Past experience
  -> role capability
  -> company problem
  -> result I can create
```

## Output Components

- One-sentence positioning
- Three differentiators
- Short self-introduction
- Long self-introduction
- Project story map
- Weakness and risk handling

## Differentiator Pattern

Use three proof types:

1. Domain proof: real Web3, wallet, DeFi, trading, or user experience.
2. Product proof: shipped complex products, platform capability, growth, data, or UX.
3. Organization proof: cross-team execution, leadership, conflict handling, or founder ownership.

## Founder Risk Handling

Founder experience is an advantage and a risk.

Interviewers may worry the candidate:

- Is hard to manage.
- Only wants innovation.
- Does not want foundational work.
- Will leave soon.

Useful positioning:

> I bring founder-level ownership, but I also understand that in a large organization trust is earned by solving the team's most important problems first. If the foundational work is not done well, I do not deserve the mandate to pursue larger innovation.

## AI Ambition Without Floating

Use this ladder:

1. Today: AI improves PM, research, data analysis, and engineering workflow.
2. Near term: AI explains risk, summarizes assets, detects opportunities, and improves support.
3. Mid term: wallet capabilities become agent skills and APIs.
4. Long term: wallet becomes the financial execution sub-agent for broader AI agents.
