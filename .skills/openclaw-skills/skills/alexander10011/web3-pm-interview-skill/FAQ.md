# FAQ

## Who is this for?

This is for Web3 product manager candidates, especially those targeting wallet, exchange, DeFi, DEX, on-chain data, growth, AI Wallet, Agentic Wallet, senior PM, product lead, or product director roles.

It is most useful for candidates with some relevant experience who need better interview positioning, domain prep, mock review, or case structure.

## Is this only for Web3-native candidates?

No.

The examples include:

- Web2 fintech PM -> Web3 wallet PM
- Web3 Growth / BD -> Exchange Growth PM
- DeFi researcher -> Product PM
- Senior wallet / DeFi / on-chain data PM
- Product director candidates

## Is this a Web3 beginner course?

No.

This is not a beginner course. It is an interview preparation system. It assumes the candidate is preparing for a real role and needs to turn background, JD, domain knowledge, and company research into interview-ready output.

## How do I use it with ChatGPT?

1. Create a Custom GPT.
2. Paste `SKILL.md` into the instructions.
3. Upload `references/`, `templates/`, and `examples/` as knowledge.
4. Start with one of the prompts in `README.md`.

## How do I use it with Claude?

1. Create a Claude Project.
2. Paste `SKILL.md` into project instructions.
3. Upload `references/`, `templates/`, and `examples/` as project knowledge.
4. Ask for a JD diagnosis or interview battle plan.

## How do I use it with Codex or a local agent?

Use the repository as a skill folder or point the agent to `SKILL.md`. The agent should load references only when relevant.

## What should I send first?

Minimum:

- Resume or 5-bullet background
- Target JD
- Company and role
- Interview stage
- Time left before interview

If you only have one thing ready, send the JD first.

## Can this guarantee an offer?

No.

It improves preparation quality, clarity, and interview readiness. It cannot guarantee an offer.

## Does this include real interview questions?

It includes anonymized and generalized question patterns. It does not publish private interview recordings, private recruiter messages, named interviewer analysis, or confidential company information.

## Can I contribute my own interview experience?

Yes, but only if it is anonymized.

Do not include:

- Real interviewer names
- Raw transcripts
- Compensation details
- Private messages
- Internal company information
- Non-public metrics

See `CONTRIBUTING.md`.

## What is the privacy standard?

Preserve the reusable method. Remove private specifics.

Use roles like:

- Hiring manager
- Wallet lead
- Cross-functional interviewer
- Bar raiser
- HRBP
