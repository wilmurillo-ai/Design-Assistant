# Anonymous Case: Wallet / AI Wallet Product Director

## Candidate Type

Senior product candidate or founder targeting a wallet product director role. The role requires business judgment, organization fit, management maturity, and AI Wallet perspective.

## Target Role

Product Director for wallet, AI Wallet, or Agentic Wallet direction.

## Role Reality

The interview is not only checking whether the candidate understands wallet products. It checks:

- Can the candidate judge the business?
- Can they identify the main user path and business bottleneck?
- Can they manage risk?
- Can they align cross-functional teams?
- Can they build trust with senior leaders?
- Can they form a realistic 30/60/90 plan?

## Interview Loop

| Round | Objective |
|---|---|
| HR screen | Basic background, management experience, compensation, motivation |
| Direct leader | First impression, business fit, product judgment, team fit |
| Cross-functional leader | Product depth, management scenarios, collaboration |
| Senior leader | Macro thinking, risk awareness, culture, pressure test |
| HR culture / management | Management style, conflict, performance, prioritization |

## Preparation Checklist

For the first business round:

1. Analyze the JD deeply.
2. Prepare short and long self-introductions in Chinese and English.
3. Prepare core project Q&A.
4. Learn role-specific domain knowledge.
5. Study the target product deeply.
6. Study competitor moves.
7. Prepare your own plan: what to do, how, and why.
8. Prepare strong reverse questions.

## Questions To Expect

### Direct Leader

- Introduce yourself.
- Why did you make key decisions in past projects?
- What was your biggest business challenge?
- How do you view competitor moves?
- Why this company and this business?
- If you owned this area, what would you do?
- What feature would you build and why?

### Cross-functional Leader

- What business would you build after joining the wallet team?
- How would you 10x wallet users?
- Why did a competitor launch and then remove a feature?
- How would you handle a public challenge from a peer team?
- What if your leader supports a direction you believe is wrong?
- How can AI combine with the current business?
- How is product work different at much larger scale?

### Senior Leader

- Why did you choose one strategy instead of another?
- Did your past approach create risk?
- How should AI and Web3 combine?
- What would you do first after joining?
- How do you manage risk in a financial product?

### HR / Management

- How do you manage team performance?
- How do you handle priority conflicts?
- How do you handle disagreement with peer teams?
- What offers do you have?
- What is your compensation expectation?

## Pressure Interview Strategy

When challenged repeatedly:

1. Do not panic.
2. Ask for a moment to think.
3. Slow down.
4. Answer with structure.
5. Name tradeoffs.
6. Admit uncertainty when needed.
7. Return to business logic.

## Strong Reverse Questions

Business bottleneck:

> What is the biggest bottleneck in this business today? If solved, which metric would improve most?

Expectation setting:

> If I joined and six months later you felt I was doing very well, what business result or behavior would make you say that?

Cross-functional expectation:

> Given my area would connect with your team, what kind of users, capabilities, or operating rhythm would you hope I bring?

## Research Method

High-quality research should combine:

- Product experience
- Product map
- Competitor analysis
- Public update history
- GitHub or technical architecture if available
- AI-assisted synthesis

Do not just ask an LLM a broad question. The quality of input determines the quality of analysis.

## Director-Level Mainline

> I can enter a complex wallet business, quickly identify the main user path and business bottleneck, align stakeholders, control risk, and turn strategy into execution.

## 30/60/90 Emphasis

30 days:

- Validate product scope, metrics, roadmap, stakeholders, and hidden risks.

60 days:

- Align on one or two priority initiatives and start execution.

90 days:

- Deliver visible result, establish operating rhythm, and propose next-stage roadmap.
