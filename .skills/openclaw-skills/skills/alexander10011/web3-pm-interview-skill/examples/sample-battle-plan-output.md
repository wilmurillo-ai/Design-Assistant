# Sample Battle Plan Output

## Scenario

Candidate:

- Senior Product Manager from Web2 fintech
- 7 years of experience
- Strong transaction, risk, onboarding, and data product background
- Limited hands-on DeFi experience

Target:

- Wallet Senior PM at a major Web3 wallet / exchange
- Hiring manager round in 7 days

## Conclusion

Fit is medium-high, but only if the candidate translates fintech transaction experience into wallet transaction path ownership and quickly closes the wallet / DeFi basics gap.

## Core Reasons

1. The candidate has strong financial product instincts: onboarding, KYC, transaction success, risk, and support reduction.
2. The target wallet role likely values similar capabilities, but expressed through signing, swap, bridge, asset display, and transaction safety.
3. The main risk is credibility: the interviewer may doubt whether the candidate has real wallet and DeFi user feel.

## Role Reality

This is not simply a "wallet feature PM" role.

It is likely hiring for:

- Wallet activation and transaction conversion
- Safe swap / bridge / asset flows
- Risk explanation and failed transaction reduction
- Cross-functional execution with engineering, data, risk, and growth
- Ability to connect CEX, DEX, and self-custody UX

## Fit Matrix

| Role Need | Candidate Proof | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Transaction flow ownership | Cross-border payment product | Needs wallet-specific language |
| Risk and compliance awareness | KYC and risk review | Needs self-custody and signing risk |
| Data-driven product work | Transaction dashboard | Needs on-chain metrics |
| API / partner integration | Payment partner APIs | Needs protocol / wallet integration examples |
| Web3 user feel | Basic wallet usage | Needs deeper DeFi practice |

## Interview Mainline

One sentence:

> I am a transaction and risk product PM from fintech, and I can bring that discipline into wallet products by improving activation, transaction success, and user trust across swap, bridge, and asset flows.

Three selling points:

1. I understand financial transaction UX and failure recovery.
2. I can use data to improve funnel, success rate, and support load.
3. I know how to work with engineering and partners on API-heavy product delivery.

## Biggest Risks And De-risking

Risk 1: Limited Web3 depth.

Answer:

> My Web3 depth is still growing, but I am not coming in as a tourist. I have been using wallets, swap, bridge, and DeFi flows directly, and I am deliberately translating my fintech transaction experience into wallet-specific problems like signing, approval, gas, slippage, failed transactions, and risk explanation.

Risk 2: Too Web2.

Answer:

> The mental model is different, especially because users hold keys and transactions are irreversible. But the product discipline still transfers: identify the user intent, reduce failure points, explain risk clearly, and measure conversion and error rates.

## Likely Hiring Manager Questions

1. Why move from fintech to Web3 wallet?
2. What is different between Web2 payments and wallet transactions?
3. How would you improve first transaction conversion?
4. How would you reduce failed bridge or swap transactions?
5. How do you balance security and conversion?
6. What wallet products do you use and what would you improve?
7. How would you define success for this role in 90 days?
8. What DeFi concepts do you understand?
9. How would you work with engineering on transaction simulation?
10. What questions do you have for me?

## Must-know Domain Prep

P0:

- Seed phrase, private key, signing
- Approve, allowance, revoke
- Gas, nonce, slippage
- Swap and bridge user path
- Transaction simulation
- Wrong-chain and failed transaction scenarios

P1:

- AMM basics
- Lending basics
- TVL, volume, active wallets, swap conversion
- Account abstraction basics

Skip for this round:

- Deep LRT mechanics
- Complex MEV details
- Advanced on-chain indexing

## Reverse Questions

1. What is the biggest drop-off point in the current wallet transaction path: funding, signing, swap, bridge, or post-transaction trust?
2. If I joined and six months later you felt I was doing very well, what metric or behavior would make you say that?
3. How does the team balance security warnings with conversion, especially for new wallet users?

## 7-day Prep Plan

Day 1:

- Read the JD and rewrite it into role reality.
- Prepare one-sentence positioning.

Day 2:

- Use target wallet product.
- Complete swap, bridge, approve, revoke, and asset review flows.

Day 3:

- Study wallet basics: signing, gas, nonce, approval, slippage.

Day 4:

- Study DeFi basics: AMM, lending, APY, TVL.

Day 5:

- Prepare 3 project stories mapped to wallet role needs.

Day 6:

- Mock hiring manager interview.

Day 7:

- Tighten self-introduction, reverse questions, and risk handling.
