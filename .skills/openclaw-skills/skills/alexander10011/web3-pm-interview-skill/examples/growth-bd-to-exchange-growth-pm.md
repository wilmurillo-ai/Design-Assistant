# Anonymous Case: Web3 Growth / BD -> Exchange Growth PM

## Candidate Type

Web3 growth, BD, operations, or community candidate with campaign, partnership, KOL, user acquisition, and ecosystem experience. Wants to move into product or growth PM.

## Target Role

Exchange or wallet Growth PM covering user acquisition, activation, campaign design, trading conversion, and retention.

## Role Reality

This is not a pure marketing or BD role.

The real hiring model is:

> A growth product owner who can turn distribution, incentives, campaigns, and partnerships into measurable activation, trading, retention, and revenue loops.

## Core Translation

| Candidate Asset | Product Translation |
|---|---|
| KOL and community campaigns | Acquisition channels and funnel inputs |
| Partnership BD | Supply-side growth and ecosystem leverage |
| Airdrop / task campaigns | Incentive design and activation mechanics |
| Trading competitions | Volume, frequency, and retention loops |
| Community feedback | User research and product signal |

## Biggest Risks

1. Interviewer may see the candidate as an operator, not a PM.
2. Candidate may over-focus on campaigns and under-focus on retention.
3. Candidate may not connect growth to revenue or unit economics.
4. Candidate may lack product prioritization and roadmap thinking.

## Winning Mainline

> I understand Web3 distribution and user psychology from the front line, and I want to productize that experience into repeatable growth loops: acquisition, activation, first transaction, repeat transaction, and retention.

## De-risking Language

For "you are more BD/ops than PM":

> That is a fair concern. My edge is that I have seen where users actually come from and why campaigns fail or work. To become effective as a PM, I would translate that into funnel metrics, productized incentives, experiment design, and repeatable growth loops rather than one-off campaigns.

For "growth is just subsidy":

> Subsidy can buy short-term activity, but it is not a model. I would evaluate whether incentives create durable behavior: funded wallets, first transaction, repeat trading, retained assets, or referred users. If the loop stops when subsidy stops, it is weak.

## High-Probability Questions

1. How would you 10x wallet users?
2. What is the difference between campaign growth and product growth?
3. How do you judge whether an incentive campaign worked?
4. What is your north star metric for exchange or wallet growth?
5. How would you reduce users who only come for airdrops?
6. How would you design a referral or task campaign?
7. How do you work with product, data, BD, and marketing?
8. How do you prioritize growth experiments?
9. How do you evaluate KOL ROI?
10. How do you convert new users into traders?

## Metrics To Own

- New registered users
- Funded wallets
- First transaction
- First swap / trade
- Activation rate
- Cost per activated user
- 7-day and 30-day retention
- Trading frequency
- Net revenue per user
- Campaign payback period

## Product Ideas That Sound Like PM Work

1. First transaction onboarding flow
2. Incentive center with lifecycle segmentation
3. Trading mission system tied to real user stages
4. Referral loop with anti-fraud controls
5. Smart campaign attribution dashboard
6. Dormant user reactivation based on asset and behavior signals

## Reverse Questions

1. Which growth problem matters most now: acquisition, activation, retention, or trading frequency?
2. How does the team distinguish real users from incentive-only users?
3. What is one growth loop that has worked repeatedly, not just once?

## 7-day Prep Plan

Day 1:

- Convert campaign examples into funnel metrics.

Day 2:

- Study target product onboarding and first transaction path.

Day 3:

- Build a growth loop map.

Day 4:

- Prepare 3 experiments with hypothesis, metric, and risk.

Day 5:

- Study anti-fraud and subsidy efficiency.

Day 6:

- Mock product sense and growth strategy questions.

Day 7:

- Finalize 90-day growth plan.
