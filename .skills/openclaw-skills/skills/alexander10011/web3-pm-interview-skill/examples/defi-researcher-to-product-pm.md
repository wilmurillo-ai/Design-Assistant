# Anonymous Case: DeFi Researcher -> Product PM

## Candidate Type

DeFi researcher, analyst, investment professional, or protocol researcher with strong market and protocol understanding but limited direct PM experience.

## Target Role

DeFi PM, protocol integration PM, on-chain data PM, yield product PM, or wallet DeFi PM.

## Role Reality

This is not a research publishing role.

The real hiring model is:

> A product owner who can translate protocol understanding into user-facing workflows, data definitions, risk controls, integration priorities, and measurable business outcomes.

## Core Translation

| Research Asset | Product Translation |
|---|---|
| Protocol mechanism analysis | Integration requirements and UX explanation |
| Yield research | Product selection, risk labels, and ranking |
| Token / market analysis | User demand and asset discovery |
| Dune / dashboard work | Metrics, monitoring, and data definitions |
| Risk analysis | Warnings, eligibility, limits, and education |

## Biggest Risks

1. Interviewer may think the candidate only writes reports.
2. Candidate may speak in protocol language, not user language.
3. Candidate may lack execution, roadmap, and stakeholder management proof.
4. Candidate may over-optimize for correctness and under-emphasize shipping.

## Winning Mainline

> My edge is deep DeFi understanding, but the value I bring as a PM is translating that into product decisions: which protocols to support, how to model positions, how to explain yield and risk, and how to measure whether users actually benefit.

## De-risking Language

For "you are more researcher than PM":

> Research is only useful if it changes product decisions. I would use protocol research to decide what to integrate, what data fields are needed, how to explain risk, what to monitor, and how to measure impact on TVL, conversion, retention, and support tickets.

For "you may be too theoretical":

> I agree the risk exists. That is why I frame every recommendation with an MVP, required data, engineering dependency, metric, and fallback plan.

## High-Probability Questions

1. How do you decide whether a DeFi protocol is worth integrating?
2. How would you design a DeFi position page?
3. How do you explain yield to retail users without misleading them?
4. What is the difference between APR and APY?
5. How would you handle protocol risk scoring?
6. How do you calculate PnL for LP positions?
7. How do you compare LST, LRT, lending, and vault products?
8. How do you define wallet DeFi north star metrics?
9. How would you work with engineers and data teams?
10. What would you ship in the first 60 days?

## Product Artifacts To Prepare

1. Protocol integration scoring matrix
2. DeFi position data model
3. Yield and risk explanation framework
4. 30/60/90 plan for wallet DeFi
5. One product review of a wallet DeFi page

## Protocol Integration Scorecard

| Dimension | Question |
|---|---|
| User demand | Do target users already hold or use this protocol? |
| TVL / volume | Is there enough liquidity and activity? |
| Risk | Audit, exploit history, admin key, oracle risk |
| Data | Are position, yield, and risk fields accessible? |
| UX | Can users understand and act safely? |
| Strategic value | Does it support chain, asset, or ecosystem strategy? |
| Maintenance | How expensive is ongoing support? |

## Reverse Questions

1. Is the current DeFi priority more about protocol coverage, data accuracy, conversion, or risk control?
2. How does the team decide whether to show a yield product, integrate actions, or only provide read-only display?
3. What data quality issue creates the most user trust problem today?

## 7-day Prep Plan

Day 1:

- Pick 3 target protocols and build an integration scorecard.

Day 2:

- Review target wallet DeFi pages and map user flows.

Day 3:

- Prepare yield, risk, and position data definitions.

Day 4:

- Convert 2 research reports into product recommendations.

Day 5:

- Prepare engineering collaboration examples.

Day 6:

- Mock protocol integration and data accuracy questions.

Day 7:

- Finalize 30/60/90 and reverse questions.
