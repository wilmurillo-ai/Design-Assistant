# Anonymous Case: Wallet / DeFi / On-chain Data Senior PM

## Candidate Type

Founder and senior product candidate with Web3 product experience, real on-chain usage, technical background, and platform product experience.

## Target Role

Senior PM for wallet DeFi and on-chain data.

## Role Reality

The written role may look like a DeFi product role, but the real hiring model is closer to:

> Wallet DeFi data platform PM responsible for protocol integration, on-chain data parsing, yield definitions, risk monitoring, and front-end business consumption.

## Interview Loop

| Round | Objective |
|---|---|
| HR screen | Basic fit, motivation, compensation, timing |
| Hiring manager | Direct usefulness, role fit, communication |
| Cross-functional | Product fundamentals, AI usage, business collaboration |
| Business owner | Strategy, DeFi depth, AI view, org fit |
| Bar raiser / tech leader | Senior bar, hands-on ability, technical collaboration, culture |
| HRBP | Offer risk, stability, level and compensation calibration |

## Most Effective Preparation

### Translate JD Into Real Role

Do not stop at "DeFi PM." Identify the data and platform layer:

- Protocol integration
- Multi-chain expansion
- Position data modeling
- APR / APY / PnL definitions
- Data quality and SLA
- Security scoring and monitoring
- Wallet front-end consumption

### Translate Product Surface Into Business Model

Study the product UI and infer:

- Asset-based yield recommendation
- Protocol and chain aggregation
- Campaign bonus and base yield packaging
- Security score front-end display
- Deposit, redeem, gas, and balance flow

### Translate Public Updates Into Product Evolution

Use product announcements and public updates to infer current priorities:

- Alpha / airdrop / TGE distribution
- Meme and trading retention
- Security center and address management
- DeFi / Earn for fund retention
- AI skills as next interaction layer

### Translate Interviewer Role Into Strategy

Replace real names with roles:

| Role | Likely Focus | Best Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| Direct manager | Can you execute this job? | Emphasize role fit and delivery |
| Cross-functional lead | Do you have product and business fundamentals? | Discuss data, transaction, risk, user |
| Business owner | Can you enter core business scope? | Combine platform thinking and execution |
| Tech leader | Are you hands-on and credible with engineers? | Discuss real metrics, costs, tech collaboration |
| HRBP | Are you an offer risk? | Reduce stability and motivation concerns |

## High-Probability Questions

- Why this company rather than another wallet company?
- How do you understand an on-chain transaction as a PM?
- What protocols do you use and why?
- How can AI improve wallet or DeFi products?
- What is the difference between MCP and skills?
- If DeFi cannot rely only on campaigns, how should it grow?
- What is the north star metric for wallet DeFi?
- If TVL grows but transaction count falls, how do you diagnose it?
- As a founder, can you accept doing foundational work first?
- How do you work with technical teams?

## Winning Mainline

> I can turn complex on-chain capabilities into productized wallet experiences, and I can connect those capabilities to real business outcomes.

Proof:

1. Platform product experience proves complex system capability.
2. Real Web3 usage proves user feel.
3. Founder or business experience proves ownership and commercial judgment.
4. AI experience proves forward-looking product thinking.

## Risk Handling

Founder risk:

> I bring founder ownership, but in a large organization I know trust must be earned by solving the team's most important problems first. Foundational work is not beneath me; it is the basis for being trusted with larger innovation.

AI risk:

> I would not start with "AI helps users make money." I would start with explanation, risk, workflow efficiency, and constrained execution. Ambition needs safety boundaries.
