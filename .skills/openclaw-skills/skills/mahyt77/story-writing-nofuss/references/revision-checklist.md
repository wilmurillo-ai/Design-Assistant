# Revision Checklist

Use this checklist for systematic revision passes. Do each pass separately—don't try to fix everything at once.

---

## Pass 1: Structural (The Big Picture)

**Read through the entire manuscript without line-editing.**

### Plot & Structure
- [ ] Does every chapter advance the plot?
- [ ] Are there any plot holes or logic gaps?
- [ ] Does the pacing feel right? (Slow sections, rushed sections?)
- [ ] Is the climax earned? (Setup throughout the story?)
- [ ] Does the ending satisfy? (Not necessarily happy, but inevitable?)

### Character Arcs
- [ ] Does the protagonist change from beginning to end?
- [ ] Is their want vs. need clear?
- [ ] Do secondary characters have their own arcs?
- [ ] Are motivations clear and consistent?

### Theme
- [ ] What is the story *really* about?
- [ ] Is the theme explored through action, not just stated?
- [ ] Are there thematic echoes/reinforcements?

### World/Setting
- [ ] Is the world consistent? (Rules, magic, technology, etc.)
- [ ] Does setting serve the story? (Not just backdrop?)
- [ ] Are sensory details present but not overwhelming?

**Action items from Pass 1:**
- List chapters to reorder, cut, or expand
- Note character inconsistencies to fix
- Identify scenes that need complete rewrites

---

## Pass 2: Scene-Level

**Examine each scene individually.**

### Every Scene Must:
- [ ] Have a clear purpose (plot advancement, character development, or both)
- [ ] Begin with a hook (action, question, tension)
- [ ] End with change (something is different than it started)
- [ ] Create or resolve tension

### Scene Checklist
- [ ] **Goal** - What does the POV character want in this scene?
- [ ] **Conflict** - What stands in their way?
- [ ] **Disaster** - What goes wrong or raises stakes?
- [ ] **Setting** - Where are we? Is it clear?
- [ ] **POV** - Whose head are we in? Is it consistent?

### Show vs. Tell
- [ ] Are emotions shown through action/dialogue/body language?
- [ ] Is backstory woven in naturally, not dumped?
- [ ] Are important moments dramatized (not summarized)?

**Red flags:**
- "He felt..." / "She was..." (filter words)
- Long exposition paragraphs
- Characters explaining things they already know

---

## Pass 3: Line-Level (Prose)

**Now polish the actual sentences.**

### Tightening
- [ ] Cut unnecessary words (very, really, just, that, began to, started to)
- [ ] Replace weak verbs + adverbs with strong verbs
- [ ] Eliminate redundancies
- [ ] Vary sentence length and structure

### Voice & Style
- [ ] Is the narrative voice consistent?
- [ ] Does dialogue sound natural? (Read aloud!)
- [ ] Are there any clichés to replace?

### Common Issues to Hunt
- [ ] **Filter words:** saw, heard, felt, noticed, realized, wondered
- [ ] **Crutch words:** just, really, very, somehow, suddenly
- [ ] **Adverbs:** Replace with stronger verbs where possible
- [ ] **Passive voice:** Use active unless passive serves a purpose
- [ ] **Dialogue tags:** Use "said" mostly, action beats for variety

### Sensory Details
- [ ] Are all five senses used? (Not just visual?)
- [ ] Are details specific and concrete?
- [ ] Is description integrated with action?

---

## Pass 4: Polish

**Final sweep before external readers.**

### Mechanics
- [ ] Grammar and punctuation
- [ ] Spelling (use spellcheck, but don't trust it blindly)
- [ ] Consistency (names, places, timeline, details)

### Formatting
- [ ] Chapter headings consistent?
- [ ] Scene breaks clear? (***, #, or blank lines)
- [ ] Paragraph breaks logical?

### Front Matter & Back Matter
- [ ] Title page
- [ ] Table of contents (if needed)
- [ ] Acknowledgments
- [ ] About the author

### Fresh Eyes
- [ ] Let it rest for at least a week
- [ ] Read aloud (catches awkward phrasing)
- [ ] Change font/size (makes it feel new)

---

## Revision Workflow Tips

1. **Don't combine passes.** You'll miss things.
2. **Track changes.** Use comments or a revision journal.
3. **Know when to stop.** Perfection is the enemy of done.
4. **Get beta readers** after Pass 4, not before.
5. **Be willing to cut.** Kill your darlings if they don't serve the story.

---

## Red Flags by Pass

| Pass | Warning Signs |
|------|---------------|
| 1 | "This chapter is boring" → Cut or combine |
| 2 | "I don't know what this scene is for" → It has no purpose |
| 3 | "This sentence is clunky" → Simplify |
| 4 | "I've read this three times" → It's done, move on |

---

**Remember:** Revision is where good writing happens. First drafts are supposed to be messy.
