# Fiction Genres Encyclopedia

Comprehensive list of fiction genres and subgenres for story planning. Use this reference when selecting genre in the discovery process.

---

## Six Major Fiction Categories

All fiction falls under these six broad categories:

1. **Literary Fiction** - Character-driven, artistic merit
2. **Action/Adventure** - Plot-driven, excitement
3. **Speculative Fiction** - Imagined worlds/realities
4. **Horror** - Fear, dread, supernatural
5. **Crime** - Crimes and their investigation
6. **Romance** - Love relationships

---

## Literary Fiction

- **Young Adult (YA)** - Ages 13-18

- **Chick Lit** - Women's popular fiction

### By Style/Approach
- **Classic/Literary Fiction** - Artistic merit, character-driven, social commentary
- **Coming-of-Age (Bildungsroman)** - Growth from youth to adulthood
- **Encyclopedic Novel** - Comprehensive, ambitious scope
- **Epic** - Heroic adventures, long format
- **Fabulation** - Magical realism-adjacent, non-traditional
- **Metafiction** - Self-referential, draws attention to itself
- **Nonsense** - Absurdist, playful

- **Philosophical Fiction** - Explores philosophical ideas
- **Pop Culture Fiction** - Filled with media references
- **Postmodern** - Experimental, fragmented
- **Realist Fiction** - Truthful to real world
- **Satire** - Ridicules vices/follies for improvement

  - Juvenalian (harsh, bitter)

- **Social/Political Fiction** - Social commentary
- **Theatre-Fiction** - Set in theatre world

### By Setting/Time

  - Nautical Fiction

## Genre Combinations

Most modern fiction blends genres:

**Popular Blends:**
- **Romantasy** - Romance + Fantasy
- **Sci-Fi Horror** - Science Fiction + Horror
- **Historical Mystery** - Historical + Mystery
- **Paranormal Romance** - Romance + Supernatural
- **Urban Fantasy Mystery** - Urban Fantasy + Mystery
- **Romantic Thriller** - Romance + Thriller
- **Science Fantasy** - Sci-Fi + Fantasy
- **Horror Comedy** - Horror + Comedy

**How to Combine:**
1. Choose primary genre (drives plot structure)
2. Choose secondary genre (adds flavor/elements)
3. Ensure conventions of both are respected
4. Know your target audience expectations

## Choosing Your Genre

### Consider:
1. **What do you like to read?** (Write what you enjoy)
2. **What story are you telling?** (Let story dictate genre)
3. **Who's your audience?** (Genre signals to readers)
4. **What are your goals?** (Commercial vs. literary)
5. **What conventions will you follow/subvert?**

### Market Reality:
- **Romance** - Largest market, voracious readers
- **Mystery/Thriller** - Huge, loyal audience
- **Fantasy/Sci-Fi** - Dedicated fanbase
- **Literary Fiction** - Prestigious, smaller market
- **Horror** - Niche but passionate
- **Young Adult** - Cross-over appeal

### Don't Worry Too Much:
- Genres are marketing tools, not cages
- Your story can blend multiple genres
- Focus on telling a good story first
- Genre can be adjusted later for marketing

## Genre Conventions

Each genre has reader expectations:

### Romance Must Have:
- Central love story
- Emotionally satisfying ending (HEA or HFN)

### Mystery Must Have:
- Crime to solve
- Clues for reader
- Resolution revealing culprit

### Horror Must Have:
- Fear/dread as primary emotion
- Threat (supernatural or human)
- Confrontation with fear

### Fantasy Must Have:
- Impossible elements (magic, creatures, worlds)
- Internal consistency
- World-building

### Science Fiction Must Have:
- Speculative technology/science
- Exploration of "what if"
- Logical extrapolation
