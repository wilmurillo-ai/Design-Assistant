# Query & Submission Guide

For traditional publishing: query letters, synopses, and tracking submissions.

---

## Query Letter

**Length:** 250-400 words (one page max)  
**Format:** Professional business letter

### Structure

**1. Hook (1-2 sentences)**
Grab attention with your story's unique premise.

> *Example:* "When a grieving widow discovers her husband's secret second family, she must decide whether to expose his betrayal or protect the children who never knew their father existed."

**2. Book Details (1 sentence)**
Title, word count, genre, comp titles.

> *Example:* "THE OTHER WIFE is a women's fiction novel complete at 92,000 words, perfect for fans of Liane Moriarty and Celeste Ng."

**3. Story Summary (2-3 paragraphs)**
- Protagonist and their situation
- Inciting incident
- Main conflict and stakes
- Hint at the choice they'll face
- **Do NOT** reveal the ending

**4. Bio (1-2 sentences)**
Relevant credentials only.

> *Example:* "I hold an MFA in Creative Writing from [University]. My short fiction has appeared in [Literary Journal]. This is my first novel."

**5. Closing (1 sentence)**
Professional sign-off.

> *Example:* "Thank you for your time and consideration. I look forward to hearing from you."

## Synopsis

### One-Page Synopsis (for queries)
**Length:** 400-500 words

**Include:**
- Main character and their goal
- Central conflict
- Major plot turns
- **The ending** (yes, spoil it)
- Character arc resolution

**Format:**
- Third person, present tense
- Focus on main plot (skip subplots)
- Show emotional stakes

### Five-Page Synopsis (when requested)
**Length:** 1,200-1,500 words

**Include everything from one-page, plus:**
- Key subplot developments
- Secondary character arcs
- More detail on major turning points
- Theme exploration

## Finding Agents

### Research Tools
- **QueryTracker.net** - Agent database with response times
- **ManuscriptWishList.com** - Agents post what they want
- **PublishersMarketplace.com** - Deal announcements ($25/month)
- **Literary Rambles** - Agent spotlights and interviews

### Red Flags
- Reading fees (legitimate agents don't charge)
- No recent sales in your genre
- Vague or nonexistent client list
- Poor communication during query process

### Querying Strategy
1. **Batch your queries** (5-10 agents at a time)
2. **Start with dream agents** (if they're accepting queries)
3. **Track responses** (see spreadsheet below)
4. **Wait 2-4 weeks** between batches
5. **Adjust based on feedback** (many rejections = query or pages need work)

## Common Submission Guidelines

### What Agents Typically Request

**Query Only:**
- Just the query letter
- Wait for response before sending more

**Query + First Chapter:**
- Query letter
- First chapter (or first 10 pages) pasted below

**Query + Synopsis:**
- Query letter
- One-page synopsis
- Sometimes first chapter

**Full Submission:**
- Query letter
- Synopsis (1 or 5 pages)
- First 3 chapters or 50 pages
- Sometimes full manuscript (if complete)

### Formatting

- **Font:** Times New Roman, 12pt
- **Spacing:** Double-spaced
- **Margins:** 1 inch
- **Contact info:** Name, email, phone on every page
- **Page numbers:** Last name / Title / # (e.g., Smith / THE WIFE / 1)

## Timeline Expectations

| Stage | Typical Time |
|-------|--------------|
| Agent response to query | 2-8 weeks |
| Agent reads requested pages | 2-6 weeks |
| Agent offers representation | Varies |
| Manuscript revisions (if offered) | 1-3 months |
| Submission to editors | 1-3 months |
| Editor response | 2-6 months |
| Publication (if sold) | 18-24 months |

**Total time from query to book:** 2-4 years

## Alternatives to Traditional Publishing

### Small Press
- Often accept unagented submissions
- Lower advances, but more attention
- Check: small press sales, distribution, rights terms

### Self-Publishing
- Full creative control
- Higher royalties per book
- You handle: editing, cover, formatting, marketing
- Platforms: Amazon KDP, IngramSpark, Draft2Digital

### Hybrid
- Some traditional, some self-published
- Build audience with self-pub, aim for trad for bigger projects

## Rejection Is Normal

**Famous rejection counts:**
- *Harry Potter:* 12 rejections
- *Carrie:* 30 rejections (Stephen King threw draft in trash)
- *Dune:* 17 rejections over 6 years
- *Chicken Soup for the Soul:* 144 rejections

**Remember:**
- Rejection ≠ your work is bad
- It means "not right for me at this time"
- One yes is all that matters
- Keep writing, keep querying
