# World-Building Prompts

Deep-dive questions for fleshing out your story world. Use these to expand your story bible.

---

## Geography & Setting

### Primary Location
- What does this place look like from a distance?
- What does it smell like? Sound like?
- What's the weather typically like?
- How does the landscape affect daily life?
- What are the landmarks that locals know?
- What do outsiders notice first?

### Secondary Locations
- What other places are important to the story?
- How do they contrast with the primary location?
- What unique sensory details define each place?
- How long does it take to travel between locations?
- What dangers exist on the journey?

---

## Time Period & History

### If Historical/Present Day
- What major events happened in the last 10 years?
- What do people remember from their childhood?
- How has technology changed daily life?
- What social movements are active?
- What do people worry about in the news?

### If Future/Sci-Fi
- What technology exists? What doesn't?
- How did we get here from now?
- What problems were solved? What new problems emerged?
- What do people take for granted?
- What do they fear losing?

### If Fantasy/Alternate World
- What are the major historical events?
- How did the magic/technology develop?
- What ancient conflicts still matter?
- What do people believe about their origins?
- What legends are considered true vs. myth?

---

## Social Systems

### Politics & Power
- Who rules? How did they gain power?
- Who opposes them? Why?
- How do ordinary people feel about their leaders?
- What laws are strictly enforced vs. ignored?
- What's legal but frowned upon?
- What's illegal but tolerated?

### Economics & Class
- How do people make a living?
- What's the gap between rich and poor?
- What counts as "success" in this world?
- What goods are valuable? Why?
- What do people buy vs. make themselves?
- How does money work? (Currency, trade, etc.)

### Social Hierarchy
- Who's at the top? Who's at the bottom?
- How do people move between levels?
- What groups distrust each other?
- What groups ally together?
- How do outsiders fit in?

---

## Culture & Beliefs

### Religion & Spirituality
- What do people believe about the divine?
- Is there organized religion? Multiple faiths?
- How does religion affect daily life?
- What do different groups believe about death?
- What's considered sin vs. virtue?

### Values & Taboos
- What qualities are admired?
- What behaviors are shameful?
- What's considered rude vs. polite?
- What's controversial but accepted?
- What's universally condemned?

### Traditions & Rituals
- What holidays exist? What do they celebrate?
- How are births, marriages, deaths handled?
- What coming-of-age rituals exist?
- What daily/weekly rituals do people observe?
- What traditions are fading? New ones emerging?

---

## Magic & Technology

### Magic System (if applicable)
- Who can use magic? Anyone? Chosen few?
- What does it cost? (Energy, materials, sacrifice?)
- What are the limits?
- How do non-magical people view it?
- How is it taught or learned?
- What happens when it goes wrong?
- How has it shaped history?

### Technology (if applicable)
- What technology exists in this world?
- What's cutting-edge vs. common?
- Who controls the technology?
- What has technology made obsolete?
- What do people miss from "before"?
- What do they fear about technology?

---

## Daily Life

### Routines
- What does a typical day look like for different classes?
- What do people eat? How often?
- How do people get around?
- What do people do for fun?
- How do people communicate over distance?
- What do people own vs. share?

### Environment
- How do people get water, food, shelter?
- What dangers exist in daily life?
- How do people handle illness/injury?
- What's the quality of life for most people?
- What would modern people find shocking?

---

## Characters in the World

### Protagonist's Place
- Where does your protagonist fit in this world?
- What do they take for granted?
- What do they struggle with that others don't?
- How does the world view them?
- What do they want that the world doesn't offer?

### Antagonist's Place
- How does your antagonist benefit from the status quo?
- What do they fear about change?
- What supporters do they have?
- How do they justify their actions?
- What makes them formidable?

### Supporting Characters
- How do allies and obstacles fit into the world?
- What unique perspectives do they bring?
- How do they contrast with the protagonist?
- What can they do that the protagonist can't?
- What are their blind spots?

---

## Conflict Sources

### Internal vs. External
- What external forces threaten characters?
- What internal struggles mirror external conflicts?
- How does the world create or solve problems?
- What systems support/oppose the protagonist?

### Sources of Tension
- What scarce resources create conflict?
- What ideologies clash?
- What historical wounds haven't healed?
- What changes are happening that disturb people?
- What do different groups want that they can't all have?

---

## World Building Questions by Genre

### Fantasy
- Why is this world different from ours?
- How does magic affect society?
- What would this world look like without magic?
- What makes this fantasy world feel lived-in?

### Science Fiction
- What technology changes everything?
- What problems does technology create?
- How do humans still act human?
- What's familiar vs. alien?

### Historical
- What details make the era feel real?
- What modern assumptions should you avoid?
- What would people of that time find normal/shocking?
- How do you avoid anachronisms in character thinking?

### Contemporary
- What makes this place/time unique?
- What social issues affect your characters?
- What technology shapes their lives?
- What assumptions do readers have that you can challenge?

## Tips for World Building

1. **Show, don't tell** - Reveal world through character experience, not info-dumps
2. **Less is more** - 10% world-building in prep, 10% in first draft, 80% never seen
3. **Consistent rules** - If magic/tech has limits, stick to them
4. **Cultural logic** - Beliefs and customs should make internal sense
5. **Change over time** - Worlds aren't static; show evolution
6. **Contradictions exist** - Real societies have conflicting values; yours should too
7. **People adapt** - How have people adapted to the world's challenges?
8. **Details matter** - Small sensory details make worlds feel real
9. **Stay in POV** - Only reveal what the POV character would notice
10. **The iceberg** - Know 90% more than you show

## World-Building Checklist

Before writing, can you answer:

- [ ] Where does your protagonist live? (Specific place)
- [ ] What's the political system?
- [ ] What's the economic system?
- [ ] What do people believe?
- [ ] What do people fear?
- [ ] What's daily life like for most people?
- [ ] What makes your world unique?
- [ ] How does the world affect your protagonist's goals?
- [ ] How does the world create obstacles?
- [ ] What does your protagonist take for granted that readers won't?

---

*Use this reference to deepen your story bible. You don't need to answer everything—just what's relevant to your story.*
