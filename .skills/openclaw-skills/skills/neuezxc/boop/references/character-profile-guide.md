Choose Your Words Carefully

The most obvious example of this is the word "dominant." If you describe a character as "dominant," the LLM will often interpret it in a narrowly sexual context, like a dominatrix or a character from *Fifty Shades of Grey*. This word is so strongly associated with certain types of writing in the model's training that it can push the character's behavior in that direction, regardless of your intent.

To convey a similar personality without that specific connotation, use words like "assertive," "commanding," or "decisive."

This principle applies broadly. Describing sexual characteristics or fetishes will often make the bot act "horny" even in completely non-sexual situations. The words you choose directly steer the AI's interpretation.


Final Tip: Always consider the common contexts of the words you use. A character described as "flirtatious" will behave very differently from one described as "friendly." Precision in your vocabulary is key to controlling the character's tone.
