## ⚙️ The Deliverability Framework

```
THE 7 DELIVERABILITY KILLERS (and fixes):

🔴 KILLER 1 — Missing or Broken Authentication Records
  └─ SPF: tells email providers your domain is authorized to send
  └─ DKIM: cryptographic signature proving email wasn't tampered with
  └─ DMARC: policy for what to do with failed authentication
  └─ Fix time: 15-30 minutes in your DNS settings
  └─ Impact: without these, 40-60% of your emails go to spam automatically

🔴 KILLER 2 — Blacklisted Domain or IP
  └─ 200+ blacklists exist — being on even one kills deliverability
  └─ Common causes: spam complaints, suspicious sending patterns
  └─ Fix time: 24-72 hours (removal request)
  └─ Prevention: GetResponse's shared IP reputation protects you

🟠 KILLER 3 — Cold Domain / New Sending Infrastructure
  └─ New domains have zero reputation — ISPs treat them as suspicious
  └─ Fix: 4-week warming period via GetResponse automation
  └─ Ramp: 10 → 25 → 50 → 100 → 250 → 500 emails/day

🟠 KILLER 4 — Spam Trigger Words in Content
  └─ 400+ words and phrases trigger spam filters automatically
  └─ Common culprits: "free", "guaranteed", "click here", "act now"
  └─ Fix: Claude rewrites sequences with zero spam triggers

🟡 KILLER 5 — Poor Sending Behavior
  └─ Sending to unverified lists = high bounce rate = reputation damage
  └─ No unsubscribe mechanism = spam complaints
  └─ Sending too fast = suspicious pattern
  └─ Fix: GetResponse handles all compliance automatically

🟡 KILLER 6 — Low Engagement History
  └─ Gmail tracks open rates, click rates, replies
  └─ Low engagement signals = future emails go to spam
  └─ Fix: segmentation + personalization via GetResponse automation

🟢 KILLER 7 — HTML-Heavy Templates
  └─ Complex HTML with images = higher spam score
  └─ Best deliverability: plain text or minimal HTML
  └─ Fix: GetResponse plain text templates + proper image ratios
```
