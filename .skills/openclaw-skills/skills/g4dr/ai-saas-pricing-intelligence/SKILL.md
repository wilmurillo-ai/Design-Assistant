## ⚙️ The Pricing Intelligence Framework

```
WILLINGNESS-TO-PAY SIGNAL SOURCES:

💬 G2 REVIEWS — Most Valuable Source
  └─ "Worth every penny at this price" → currently underpriced
  └─ "Great tool but expensive" → price sensitivity exists, value must be communicated better
  └─ "Switched because of pricing" → price is a churn driver, fix packaging not price
  └─ "Cheaper than [competitor] and does more" → positioning opportunity

📱 REDDIT SIGNALS
  └─ "Would pay $X for something that does Y" → direct WTP data
  └─ "Too expensive for solo founders" → missing a lower tier
  └─ "Enterprise plan is overkill, need something in between" → missing mid tier
  └─ "Cancelled because price doubled at renewal" → expansion pricing issue

🐦 TWITTER/X SIGNALS
  └─ "Why is [competitor] so expensive?" → market price sensitivity
  └─ "Just cancelled X, switched to Y" → price-driven churn patterns
  └─ Pricing announcement reactions → what price points trigger backlash

🚀 PRODUCT HUNT SIGNALS
  └─ Launch comments about pricing → first impressions at scale
  └─ "Would pay X but not Y" → specific price point thresholds
```

## ⚙️ Full Workflow

```
INPUT: Your product + competitors + current pricing
        ↓
STEP 1 — Competitor Pricing Page Scrape
  └─ Every competitor: all tiers, prices, features, limits
  └─ Extract: price points, tier names, key differentiators per tier
  └─ Detect: annual vs monthly discount, free tier strategy
  └─ Map: feature distribution across tiers
        ↓
STEP 2 — Willingness-to-Pay Mining
  └─ G2: reviews mentioning price, value, expensive, worth it
  └─ Reddit: WTP statements, pricing complaints, cancellation reasons
  └─ Twitter: pricing reactions, competitor price comparisons
  └─ Product Hunt: launch pricing feedback for competitors
        ↓
STEP 3 — Pricing Gap Detection
  └─ Where is the market underserved?
  └─ Missing solo/starter tier? (below $20/month)
  └─ Missing mid-market tier? ($100-300/month gap?)
  └─ Missing enterprise floor? ($500+/month with custom above)
  └─ Feature packaging gap: competitors bundle wrong things together?
        ↓
STEP 4 — Optimal Pricing Model
  └─ Price anchor: what's the highest tier that sets the frame?
  └─ Value metric: seats / usage / revenue / contacts — which aligns with customer value?
  └─ Tier structure: 3 tiers vs 4 vs usage-based vs hybrid
  └─ Annual discount: 20% standard, 40%+ for cash-strapped segments
        ↓
STEP 5 — Packaging Strategy
  └─ Which features go in which tier? (not by cost, by value)
  └─ What's the "magic feature" that upgrades starter → growth?
  └─ What's the "team feature" that upgrades growth → scale?
  └─ Freemium: yes/no/free trial — based on competitor data
        ↓
STEP 6 — Claude AI Writes Complete Pricing Page
  └─ Page headline that frames value before price
  └─ Tier names that signal identity not just features
  └─ Feature lists with benefit framing not technical framing
  └─ FAQ section addressing top 5 pricing objections
  └─ Social proof placement for conversion optimization
        ↓
STEP 7 — InVideo AI Produces Pricing Video
  └─ 60-second "here's why we price this way" video
  └─ Transparency builds trust, reduces friction
  └─ Perfect for pricing page, onboarding, and pricing update announcements
        ↓
OUTPUT: Competitor map + WTP analysis + optimal pricing model + packaging strategy + pricing page copy + video
```
