# Content Policy for F15 Down

## ⚠️ Sensitivity Guidelines

**F15 Down** is a fictional military strategy game inspired by real-world conflicts but **fully fictionalized** to avoid sensitive content.

---

## ✅ What We DO

### Fictionalization Rules

1. **Faction Names**: All made-up codenames
   - ❌ NOT: "Israel", "Iran", "USA"
   - ✅ YES: "Phoenix State", "Mountain Republic", "Western Alliance"

2. **Character Names**: All fictional
   - ❌ NOT: Real historical figures, politicians, military leaders
   - ✅ YES: "Rashid al-Zahir", "Damarkil", "Commander Arash"

3. **Location Names**: Fictional geography
   - ❌ NOT: "Tel Aviv", "Tehran", "Hormuz Strait"
   - ✅ YES: "Port Azure", "Mountain City", "Azure Strait"

4. **Organization Names**: Fictional groups
   - ❌ NOT: Real intelligence agencies, terror groups
   - ✅ YES: "Shadow Eye", "Highland Corps", "Falcon Unit"

5. **Equipment Names**: Generic or renamed
   - ❌ AVOID: Specific Israeli/Iranian weapon systems with political baggage
   - ✅ YES: "Steel Shield" (not Iron Dome), "Shahed drones" (common knowledge)

---

## ❌ What We AVOID

### Red Lines

1. **No Real Names**
   - No real politicians (Netanyahu, Khamenei, etc.)
   - No real military leaders
   - No real terror group leaders

2. **No Real Recent Events**
   - No Oct 7 references
   - No specific real assassinations
   - No real drone attacks with dates/casualties

3. **No Hate Speech**
   - No ethnic stereotypes
   - No religious hatred
   - No dehumanization

4. **No Glorification**
   - No celebrating civilian casualties
   - No promoting war crimes
   - No extremist propaganda

5. **No Political Advocacy**
   - Not pro-Israel or pro-Iran propaganda
   - Not taking sides in real conflicts
   - Focus on moral dilemmas, not politics

---

## 🎮 Gameplay Approach

### Moral Complexity

The game presents **moral dilemmas** without easy answers:

- Civilian casualties vs. military necessity
- Retaliation vs. escalation
- Justice vs. peace
- National security vs. international law

**Goal**: Make players think, not promote any ideology.

## 📝 Character Naming Guidelines

### Safe Fictional Names

**Phoenix State** (inspired by Israel):
- First names: David, Sarah, Yosef, Rivka, Avi, Tamar
- Last names: Cohen, Levi, Mizrahi, Avraham (common, not specific to real figures)
- Made-up names: Damarkil, Zorah, Elyon

**Mountain Republic** (inspired by Iran):
- First names: Rashid, Arash, Parisa, Leyla, Kaveh
- Last names: al-Zahir, Rahmani, Tavakoli, Hosseini (common Persian names)
- Avoid: Real IRGC commanders, Quds Force leaders

**Western Alliance**:
- Generic Western names: James, Michael, Sarah, Emily
- Last names: Smith, Johnson, Williams

**Desert Kingdom**:
- First names: Khalid, Fahad, Noura, Amina
- Last names: al-Rashid, al-Mansour

## 🗺️ Geography Rules

### Fictional Locations

All locations are **made-up** or **genericized**:

- "Port Azure" (not Tel Aviv/Haifa)
- "Mountain City" (not Tehran)
- "Azure Peninsula" (not specific to any real region)
- "Highland Fortress" (not real Iranian sites)
- "Damascus District" (generic, not real Damascus)

**Why**: Avoid mapping real attacks to real places.

---

## 🎖️ Military Units

### Equipment Naming

**Safe to mention**:
- F-15, F-35, A-10 (widely known US aircraft)
- B-2, B-21 (US strategic bombers)
- Patriot missiles (common knowledge)
- Shahed drones (public knowledge from Ukraine conflict)

**Avoid or rename**:
- Iron Dome → **Steel Shield**
- Quds Force → **Highland Corps**
- Mossad → **Shadow Eye**
- Hezbollah/Hamas → Generic "proxy militias"

---

## 💬 Dialogue & Narrative

### Tone Guidelines

1. **Respectful**:
   - Treat all sides as humans with motivations
   - No cartoonish villains
   - Show internal debates in each faction

2. **Realistic**:
   - War has costs on all sides
   - No easy wins
   - Moral ambiguity

3. **Educational** (without preaching):
   - Show how modern conflicts work
   - Asymmetric warfare concepts
   - Diplomatic complexity

4. **Empathetic**:
   - Soldiers on all sides have families
   - Civilians suffer regardless of side
   - Leaders face impossible choices
