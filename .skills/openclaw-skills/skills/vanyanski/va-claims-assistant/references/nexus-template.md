# Nexus Letter Template & Guidance

## What Is a Nexus Letter?
A medical opinion written by a licensed provider connecting a current diagnosis to military service. Required when a condition is NOT presumptive under PACT Act or other presumptive rules.

## Standard Used by VA
The VA uses "at least as likely as not" (≥50% probability) as the threshold for service connection. The letter must use this exact language.

## Template

---

**[Provider Letterhead / Date]**

To: Department of Veterans Affairs  
Re: [Veteran Name], [Last 4 SSN], Nexus Opinion for [Condition]

I am [Name, MD/DO/PA/NP], licensed in [State], license number [#]. I have reviewed [Veteran Name]'s service treatment records, post-service medical records, and conducted a clinical evaluation on [Date].

**Current Diagnosis:** [Diagnosis, ICD-10 code]

**Opinion:** It is my medical opinion that [Veteran Name]'s [condition] is **at least as likely as not** (50% or greater probability) caused by / a result of / aggravated beyond its natural progression by [his/her/their] military service.

**Rationale:**
1. [Veteran] served as [MOS/Rate] from [dates], during which [describe relevant exposure/event/duty].
2. Service records document [relevant in-service finding/complaint/incident on DATE].
3. Medical literature supports a nexus between [exposure/event] and [condition]. [Cite 1-2 studies if possible.]
4. [Veteran]'s current presentation is consistent with this etiology.

**Conclusion:** Based on my clinical expertise, review of records, and the medical literature, I conclude that [condition] is at least as likely as not related to [Veteran]'s military service.

[Signature, credentials, date, NPI number]

---

## Tips for Strong Nexus Letters
- Avoid: "may be," "could be," "possibly" — these are too weak
- Use: "at least as likely as not," "more likely than not," "highly likely"
- Include provider's NPI and license number (VA may verify)
- Attach relevant medical literature citations
- Private nexus letters (not from VA doctors) generally carry more weight
- DBQ (Disability Benefits Questionnaire) can serve as nexus letter if completed by private doctor

## Finding Providers for Nexus Letters
- Telehealth nexus services (multiple online providers, $150-500)
- VA-accredited claims agents can refer to providers
- Primary care physician (if relationship established)
