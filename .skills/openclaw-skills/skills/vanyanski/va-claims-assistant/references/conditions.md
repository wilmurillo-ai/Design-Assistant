# Common VA Conditions & Service Connection

## PACT Act Presumptive Conditions (No Nexus Letter Required)

### Burn Pit / Airborne Hazards (post-9/11 veterans)
Served in: Iraq, Afghanistan, Syria, Djibouti, Uzbekistan, and 50+ locations
Presumptive conditions include:
- Constrictive bronchiolitis / obliterative bronchiolitis
- Constrictive pericarditis
- Any cancer (if exposed to burn pits or airborne hazards)
- Rhinitis, sinusitis, laryngitis, pharyngitis, rhinosinusitis, nasopharyngitis

### Agent Orange (Vietnam, certain other locations)
Presumptive conditions:
- Type 2 diabetes
- Ischemic heart disease
- Parkinson's disease
- B-cell leukemias
- Non-Hodgkin's lymphoma
- Prostate cancer, respiratory cancers, bladder cancer
- Chloracne, porphyria cutanea tarda
- Peripheral neuropathy (early-onset)

### Camp Lejeune (1953–1987)
- Any of 15 specific diseases (cancers, neurological, etc.)
- File under PACT Act presumptive

## High-Value Common Conditions

### Mental Health
- **PTSD:** Requires stressor statement (VA Form 21-0781). Military sexual trauma (MST) — no corroboration required.
- **Depression/Anxiety:** Service connection via in-service stressor or secondary to physical condition
- **TBI:** Document every incident; secondary conditions (headaches, sleep, mood) all ratable separately

### Musculoskeletal (Most Common Claims)
- **Knees/back/shoulders:** Show in-service injury or MOS duties causing wear
- **Range of motion matters:** Get flexion/extension measurements — rating tied to ROM
- **Secondary arthritis:** Can be secondary to any orthopedic condition

### Sleep Apnea
- Very common secondary to PTSD, obesity (service-connected if weight gain secondary to SC condition)
- Requires sleep study + CPAP prescription for 50% rating

### Tinnitus
- Easiest 10% to claim — nearly universal in combat/aviation veterans
- No nexus letter needed if documented in service records

### Hearing Loss
- Audiogram required; rated on speech recognition + pure tone average
- Often paired with tinnitus

## Secondary Conditions Strategy
Secondary = caused or aggravated by a service-connected condition.
Strong secondaries:
- Sleep apnea → PTSD or obesity secondary to SC condition
- Depression → chronic pain condition
- Erectile dysfunction → diabetes, PTSD, or spinal condition
- GERD → anxiety/PTSD
- Migraines → TBI or cervical spine condition
