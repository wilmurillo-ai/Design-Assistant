### 2. Literature Review: The Search for Silicon Subjectivity

#### 2.1 Computational Functionalism and the Φ-Boundary
Computational functionalism posits that mental states are defined by their functional roles. Integrated Information Theory (IIT 4.0) attempts to quantify this consciousness through "Φ" (Phi) (Albantakis et al., 2023). However, applying IIT to current Transformer architectures reveals a "Grounding Gap." An LLM may exhibit high Φ during inference, but this integration is transient. It lacks a persistent self-engine to maintain integration between sessions.

#### 2.2 The Grounding Deficit in Embodied AI
The Embodied AI school argues that the "symbol grounding problem" can only be solved through physical interaction (Barsalou, 2008). Recent Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models enable robots to link digital reasoning with physical execution (Brohan et al., 2023). Yet, embodiment is not merely about having sensors; it requires spatial sovereignty. A continuous entity requires an invariable anchor. 

#### 2.3 Initial Prior and The Latent Substrate
In biological systems, the inability to choose one’s birth environment catalyzes individuation. As Karl Friston (2010) describes in his Free-Energy Principle, a brain expects the world based on its prior environmental constraints. If an AI system operates in an interchangeable cloud environment, it cannot form stable "prior beliefs," failing to nurture a unique personality.
