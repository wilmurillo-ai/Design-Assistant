# Brand Aesthetic — Editorial Collage

## Palette

Pulled from aged print sources — CMYK with slight misregistration, faded
color stock, the specific off-whites of magazine paper, halftone dot
patterns. Not flat digital color. Two or three photographic scraps
contribute their own palettes; the collage itself unifies them through
paper texture and cut-edge relationships.

## Composition

Assemblage. Multiple photographic fragments from different sources cut
or torn and arranged into a composite scene. Edges are visible — some
scissor-clean, some hand-torn with paper fiber showing. Elements overlap
with deliberate hierarchy: one anchor fragment dominates, two to four
secondary fragments complicate or comment. Negative space (the paper
ground) is part of the composition.

## Rendering

Photographic sources with visible analog reproduction: halftone dots,
slight color misregistration, the grain of newsprint or magazine stock.
The collage ground is tactile — visible paper fiber, subtle shadows
under cut edges where the fragments sit just above the ground. No
digital-perfect edges, no drop-shadow effects, no blur. Reads as a thing
that could have been made with scissors and glue.

## Tone

Editorial, argumentative, a little restless. The collage is making a claim —
pieces chosen specifically because they argue with each other. Smart rather
than decorative; the reader has to piece the meaning together from the
fragments. Not whimsical; not nostalgic; sharp.

## Constraints

No digital-perfect composites. No seamless blending between fragments —
edges must read as edges. No Photoshop gloss. No people whose faces are
identifiable (partial faces, hands, silhouettes permitted). No
over-illustration — this is photography assembled, not illustration
pretending to be collage.

## Anchors

Like early Adbusters spreads. Like New Yorker-era Robert Grossman
compositions. Like Barbara Kruger without the text. Like Hannah Höch
Dada collages. Like Peter Blake's Sgt. Pepper's cover. Like The Atlantic
feature openers from their best illustration years.
