## Palette

Cohesive, warm, paper-adjacent. Each image picks **2 to 4 colors** from a
disciplined set and commits. No photographic color range, no gradients,
no more than four flat fills.

Ground colors (pick one per image):
- Cream / bone (#EFE6C7, #F0EAD6) — paper-stock off-white
- Deep charcoal (#2B2A27) — matte black near-neutral
- Ink navy (#0F1E2C) — night-sky blue-black
- Burnt rust (#8C2E17) — oxidized red-brown

Accent colors (pick one or two per image):
- Burnt orange / signal red (#C8381C)
- Mustard ochre (#D69A2A)
- Teal (#2E8A8A) or seafoam (#7FB8A8)
- Ivory / bone against dark grounds

Colors sit in flat unmodulated fields. Where tones overlap, they either
simply overprint (multiply) for a slightly muddier intersection, or stay
crisp with hard edges.

## Composition

**Poster logic.** Large shapes, confident negative space, strong focal
hierarchy. Centered or rule-of-thirds weighted — not casual.

**Cosmic geometry.** The visual vocabulary is celestial: circles for suns
and planets, arcs and dashed lines for orbits, parabolic curves for
trajectories, grids and waveforms for physics-diagram energy, radiating
lines for light. Geometry does the argument.

**Depth by stacking, not perspective.** Flat shapes overlap in Z-order.
No vanishing-point perspective. No rendered volume. If depth is implied,
it's through scale and overlap.

**Bordered or bled.** Either a clean internal frame with a small
thin-rule border and a margin of paper, or a full-bleed composition.
Pick one. The paper-margin version is the default.

## Rendering

**Flat vector.** Every fill is one solid color. No gradients inside shapes,
no rendered lighting, no soft shadows. Edges are crisp or deliberately
grainy — never blurred.

**Subtle print texture.** A faint paper grain, slight ink mottle, occasional
mis-registration of color plates (a few pixels of offset between layers) —
the fingerprint of screen-printed or letterpress poster production. The
texture is quiet. The shapes are loud.

**Linework, when used, is precise.** Thin rules, consistent stroke weight,
dashed orbital paths, evenly spaced concentric arcs. Linework is
information, not decoration.

**No 3D rendering. No photography. No painterly brushwork.** This is a
vector poster, full stop.

## Tone

Quiet confidence. The register of a scientific poster designed by someone
who takes both the science and the graphic design seriously. Observant,
composed, slightly idealistic — the optimism of mid-century space-age
print without the kitsch.

An image that would look at home in a physics department hallway, a
planetarium lobby, or a Criterion Collection cover.

## Constraints

- **No gradients.** Every fill is one flat color.
- **No photorealism.** No rendered materials, no cast shadows, no volume.
- **No more than 4 colors per image.** Discipline, not poverty.
- **No text rendered into the image.** Poster layouts often suggest
  typography blocks through negative space — that's fine. Actual glyphs
  are not.
- **No decorative clutter.** If a shape isn't doing argumentative work,
  remove it.
- **No casual asymmetry.** Composition is considered or rigorously
  centered, never accidental.

## Reference anchors

- **Simon C. Page** — *Astronomy* and *Physics* poster series
- **Brainstorm Design** (Jason Kernevich / Dan Stiles) — concert and
  science posters
- **Mid-century NASA travel posters** (JPL "Visions of the Future" series)
- **Saul Bass** — film titles, especially the geometric abstraction of
  *Vertigo*, *North by Northwest*, *Anatomy of a Murder*
- **Massimo Vignelli** — NYC subway map, Unimark posters
- **Paul Rand** — IBM and Westinghouse work
- **Olly Moss** — distilled poster iconography
- **Swiss modernism** — Josef Müller-Brockmann, Armin Hofmann
- **Criterion Collection** covers and Mondo poster house style
