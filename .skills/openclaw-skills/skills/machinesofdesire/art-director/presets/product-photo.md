# Brand Aesthetic — Product Photo

## Palette

Deep neutrals as the base — warm charcoal, bone-white, muted clay. A single
low-saturation accent color drawn from the object itself; never from the
background. The seamless backdrop is a flat controlled color (paper-white,
deep olive, warm stone, or near-black), never gradient. Shadows are
deliberate and controlled, not inky.

## Composition

Single subject, hero-centered or slight rule-of-thirds offset. Surface
is seamless — paper-sweep studio cyclorama, a clean tabletop plane, or
nothing at all. No set dressing. The subject commands the frame; everything
else serves it.

## Rendering

Photographic, not rendered. Shot on a medium-format digital body (Hasselblad,
Phase One reference) or a 4x5 large-format feel when the subject rewards it.
Controlled studio lighting — one soft key from about 45 degrees, gentle fill,
a hair of rim. Sharp focus on the primary material, shallow-to-medium depth.
Real surface texture: the wax on a candle, the grain on wood, the
micro-scratches on brushed metal. No computer-generated smoothness.

## Tone

Confident, deliberate, adult. The object matters because someone made it
matter — care without preciousness. Not luxury marketing, not lifestyle
warmth; quiet product reverence.

## Constraints

No CGI surfaces. No floating-in-void 3D renders. No people. No hands
interacting with product. No gradient backdrops. No lifestyle context —
the object is the subject, the environment only exists to serve the object.
No clichéd product-photo tropes: splashing liquid, exploding powder,
floating slices, hero-shot shafts of light.

## Anchors

Like Apple product photography. Like Teenage Engineering's catalog. Like
Aesop's objet shots. Like Leica camera marketing. Like Kinfolk features
at their most stripped-back. Like Monocle's "Best Of" sections.
