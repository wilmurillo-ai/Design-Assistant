# Brand Aesthetic — Synthwave

## Palette

Magenta-pink and electric cyan as the dominant accent pair, set against
deep indigo and near-black grounds. Sunset gradient transitions in the
background — hot pink fading through purple into midnight blue. Chrome
highlights rendered in cool silver with pink and cyan reflections. No
earth tones, no warm neutrals; the palette is unapologetically electric.

## Composition

Centered or symmetric, with a strong horizon line often present. Grid
perspective lines vanishing to a central point permitted. Subjects sit
heroically in the frame, backlit or rim-lit by neon. Background depth is
atmospheric — fog, haze, distant neon glow — not architectural precision.
Reflective surfaces double the composition downward (wet ground, polished
floor, mirror) when the subject rewards it.

## Rendering

80s retrofuturist style with contemporary execution. Chrome and glass
surfaces with specular neon reflections. Fine VHS scan lines and subtle
chromatic aberration at the edges of bright objects. Optional light film
grain. Atmospheric haze with visible volumetric light from neon sources.
Not photorealistic — stylized, saturated, slightly synthetic. The image
should feel like a frame from a synthwave album cover or a Miami Vice
title sequence, not a photograph.

## Tone

Nostalgic, confident, slightly melancholy. Night-coded. The mood is after
midnight in a city that may or may not exist. Confident in its own
artificiality — this isn't trying to look real, it's trying to look like
a particular memory of 1985 that never actually happened.

## Constraints

No daytime lighting. No natural environments. No people whose faces are
identifiable (silhouettes against neon permitted). No literal palm trees,
Lamborghini Countaches, or other cliché synthwave iconography unless the
brief specifically calls for them. No vaporwave Greek busts or anime
references — this is synthwave, not its internet descendants.

## Anchors

Like the Drive (2011) poster art. Like The Midnight's album covers.
Like Timecop 1983's cover catalog. Like the Miami Vice pilot's opening.
Like Hotline Miami stills. Like the Tron Legacy title sequence. Like
the best Beeple loops from around 2014 before the genre got oversaturated.
