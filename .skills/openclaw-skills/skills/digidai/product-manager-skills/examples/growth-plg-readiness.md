# Example: Growth and PLG Readiness

## Scenario

You want a grounded product-led growth assessment before committing to a free tier or an onboarding revamp.

## Prompt

```text
We're considering adding a free tier to our B2B analytics tool. Assess our PLG readiness, recommend a freemium model, and tell us what metrics we should watch before we launch.
```

## Example Output Excerpt

```text
Diagnosis
- You are not ready for a broad freemium launch yet. The product has some PLG signals, but the core loop is still too weak to carry free-user scale.
- Activation is the bottleneck, not top-of-funnel traffic. If only a small share of trial users reaches the first meaningful outcome, a free tier will increase noise before it increases conversion.
- The product is closer to a hybrid SLG + product-assist motion than a true self-serve motion.

Recommended freemium model
- Use a feature-limited free tier, not a generous usage-based free tier.
- Keep the free plan focused on a single job, with one clear activation path and one upgrade trigger.
- Avoid gating the core collaboration moment behind sales. Gate advanced analytics, exports, and team controls instead.

Metrics to watch before launch
- Activation rate: target a step change from 12% to 20%+ on the first meaningful action.
- Time to value: reduce median time from signup to first insight below 10 minutes.
- Free-to-paid conversion: measure by cohort, not aggregate, and separate self-serve conversion from sales-assisted conversion.
- Retention: look for week-4 return rate above 30% for activated users.
- Expansion signal: track how often free users hit the upgrade trigger and invite teammates.

Decision rule
- Launch the free tier only after the activation funnel is instrumented and the first onboarding leak is fixed.
- If activation does not improve, a free tier will amplify churny demand instead of creating durable growth.
```

## Why This Matters

This example shows the skill doing more than naming a growth framework. It makes a call on PLG readiness, narrows the freemium model to a testable slice, and ties the decision to concrete metrics instead of generic growth advice.
