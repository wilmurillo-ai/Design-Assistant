## 1. Welcome Email Sequence (5 Emails)

### Email 1: Welcome & Quick Win (Send: Immediately)

**Subject:** Welcome! Here's your first quick win
**Preview text:** You made a great decision. Let me show you why.

**Body Framework:**
1. Welcome + acknowledge their decision (1-2 sentences)
2. Set expectations -- what they'll get (bullet list, 3-4 items)
3. Deliver immediate value -- one actionable tip they can use right now
4. Single CTA: Complete your profile / Take the first step

**Principle:** Deliver value immediately. Don't just say "welcome" -- give them something useful.

---

### Email 2: The Problem We Solve (Send: Day 2)

**Subject:** The real reason [problem] happens
**Preview text:** It's not what you think...

**Body Framework:**
1. Describe the problem they're facing (empathy)
2. Explain why common solutions don't work (agitate)
3. Introduce your approach as the fix (solve)
4. Social proof: One quick result or testimonial
5. CTA: Try [specific feature] or Read [relevant content]

**Principle:** Show you understand their pain better than anyone else.

---

### Email 3: Social Proof & Results (Send: Day 4)

**Subject:** How [customer] got [specific result]
**Preview text:** And you can too -- here's exactly how.

**Body Framework:**
1. Quick case study: situation -> action -> result
2. What made the difference (1-2 key insights)
3. How they can get the same result
4. CTA: Start [specific action] now

**Principle:** Prove it works with real results. Numbers > claims.

---

### Email 4: Overcome the #1 Objection (Send: Day 6)

**Subject:** "I don't have time for [thing]" -- Sound familiar?
**Preview text:** Here's the 10-minute version that works.

**Body Framework:**
1. Acknowledge the most common objection
2. Reframe it -- show why it's easier than they think
3. Give a simplified version / quick-start approach
4. CTA: Try the 10-minute version

**Principle:** Address the biggest barrier head-on.

---

### Email 5: The Ask (Send: Day 8)

**Subject:** Quick question about your experience
**Preview text:** I'd love your honest feedback.

**Body Framework:**
1. Check in -- how's their experience so far?
2. Ask a specific question (reply-based engagement)
3. Offer help or resource based on where they are
4. Soft CTA: upgrade, book a call, or explore next steps

**Principle:** A reply is worth 10x a click. Create two-way conversation.

### Welcome Series Metrics
- Open rate target: 40-60%
- Click rate target: 8-15%
- Reply rate target: 3-5% (especially Email 5)
- Unsubscribe rate: < 0.5% per email

## 2. Nurture Email Sequence (6 Emails)

**Strategy:** Value -> Trust -> Offer (70/20/10 rule: 70% pure value, 20% soft positioning, 10% direct offer)

### Email 1: The Big Insight (Day 1)

**Subject:** The [industry] mistake that costs you [money/time]
**Preview text:** 90% of [audience] make this mistake. Here's the fix.

**Framework:**
1. Hook with surprising stat or counterintuitive insight
2. Explain the mistake most people make
3. Give the fix (actionable, not vague)
4. No selling -- pure value
5. PS: "More insights like this coming this week"

---

### Email 2: The Framework (Day 3)

**Subject:** My [number]-step framework for [desired result]
**Preview text:** I've used this with [X] clients. It works every time.

**Framework:**
1. Share a repeatable framework (3-5 steps)
2. Make it immediately implementable
3. Include one real example of it working
4. No selling -- establish expertise
5. CTA: "Try step 1 today and reply with your results"

---

### Email 3: The Story (Day 5)

**Subject:** How I went from [before state] to [after state]
**Preview text:** It wasn't what I expected...

**Framework:**
1. Personal or client story (beginning -> struggle -> breakthrough -> result)
2. The one thing that made the difference
3. Lesson the reader can apply
4. Subtle mention of product/service as part of the story
5. No hard sell -- let the story speak

---

### Email 4: The Objection Crusher (Day 8)

**Subject:** "But I've already tried [common solution]..."
**Preview text:** Here's why that didn't work -- and what will.

**Framework:**
1. Address the #1 reason leads don't buy
2. Explain why their past approach didn't work
3. Show your different approach
4. Social proof: quick testimonial or result
5. Soft CTA: "If this resonates, here's what I'd suggest"

---

### Email 5: The Value Stack (Day 10)

**Subject:** Everything you get when you [join/subscribe/buy]
**Preview text:** I laid it all out -- and it's more than you think.

**Framework:**
1. Recap all value provided in previous emails
2. Transition: "Imagine having this systematized"
3. Present product/service as the complete solution
4. Clear value stack (what they get + value of each component)
5. Direct CTA to buy/join

---

### Email 6: The Deadline / Last Chance (Day 12)

**Subject:** Last chance: [offer expires / spots filling up]
**Preview text:** After today, this goes away.

**Framework:**
1. Brief reminder of the offer
2. Quick recap of one powerful testimonial
3. Address one final objection
4. Clear urgency (real deadline, limited spots, price increase)
5. Strong CTA
6. PS: "If now isn't the right time, no worries. I'll keep sending you free value."

### Nurture Sequence Metrics
- Completion rate: 60%+ should receive all 6 emails
- Conversion rate: 2-8% of recipients
- Best performing: Usually Email 5 or 6

## 3. Launch Email Sequence (8 Emails)

### PRE-LAUNCH PHASE

**Email 1: The Teaser (Day -7)**
- Subject: Something big is coming...
- Preview: I've been working on this for months. You'll be the first to know.
- Build anticipation without revealing. Hint at problem solved. Make them feel like insiders.
- CTA: "Reply if you want early access"

**Email 2: The Problem (Day -5)**
- Subject: Why [common approach] is failing you
- Preview: There's a better way. I'll show you in 5 days.
- Deep dive into the problem. Show why current solutions fall short. Tease the answer.
- CTA: Save the date / Mark your calendar

**Email 3: The Sneak Peek (Day -2)**
- Subject: First look: [Product Name]
- Preview: Here's what it does and why it matters.
- Reveal product name and function. 2-3 key features with outcomes. Beta user results.
- CTA: "Join the waitlist for launch day access"

### LAUNCH PHASE

**Email 4: Launch Day! (Day 0 -- Morning)**
- Subject: It's live: [Product Name] is here
- Preview: The door is open. Here's everything you need to know.
- Announce launch. Clear value prop. Full feature breakdown. Launch pricing.
- CTA: Get [Product] Now
- PS: "Launch pricing ends [date]"

**Email 5: Social Proof (Day 1)**
- Subject: "[specific result quote]" -- [Customer Name]
- Preview: People are already seeing results. Here's proof.
- Lead with best testimonial. 2-3 more quick wins. Address "is this for me?" Reiterate offer.
- CTA: Join them

**Email 6: FAQ / Objection Handling (Day 3)**
- Subject: Your top 5 questions about [Product], answered
- Preview: I've been getting these questions all week. Here are honest answers.
- Answer top 5 real questions. Be honest. Include guarantee/risk reversal.
- CTA: "Still have questions? Reply to this email"

### CLOSING PHASE

**Email 7: The Deadline Warning (Day 5)**
- Subject: 48 hours left for [launch pricing / bonus / early access]
- Preview: After Friday, the price goes up. Just being transparent.
- Remind of deadline. Recap best customer result. Value stack. Address "I'll do it later."
- CTA: Get in before the deadline

**Email 8: Final Hours (Day 7)**
- Subject: Last call -- [offer] expires tonight
- Preview: This is genuinely the last email about this.
- Brief, respectful urgency. One-sentence value reminder. One-sentence result. Clear deadline.
- CTA: Final chance
- PS: "If this isn't for you right now, no hard feelings. Back with free content next week."

### Launch Metrics

| Metric | Good | Great | Exceptional |
|---|---|---|---|
| Open rate (launch day) | 30% | 45% | 60%+ |
| Click rate (launch day) | 5% | 10% | 15%+ |
| Conversion (full sequence) | 2% | 5% | 10%+ |
| Revenue per subscriber | $1-3 | $3-8 | $8+ |

## 4. Client Proposal Template (3-Tier Pricing)

### Cover Page
```
[Your Company Logo]
Marketing Strategy Proposal
Prepared for: [Client Name]
Prepared by: [Your Name / Agency]
Date: [Date]
Valid until: [Date + 30 days]
CONFIDENTIAL
```

### Executive Summary Template
```
[Client Name] is at an inflection point. With [current situation],
there's a significant opportunity to [desired outcome].

Based on our analysis of [what you reviewed], we've identified [X]
key areas where strategic improvements could drive [specific result].

This proposal outlines a [timeframe] engagement focused on [primary
service areas], designed to [primary outcome].

We recommend beginning with [first phase] to establish baselines
and quick wins, then scaling efforts based on performance data.
```

### 3-Tier Pricing Model

| Component | Growth | Accelerate | Dominate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Strategy & Planning | Quarterly review | Monthly strategy | Weekly strategy |
| Content Creation | 4 pieces/month | 8 pieces/month | 16 pieces/month |
| Social Media | 3 platforms | 5 platforms | All platforms |
| Paid Ads Management | Up to $5K spend | Up to $15K spend | Up to $50K spend |
| SEO | Basic on-page | Full SEO program | Full SEO + link building |
| Email Marketing | -- | Monthly newsletter | Full automation |
| Reporting | Monthly report | Bi-weekly report | Weekly dashboard |
| Meetings | Monthly call | Bi-weekly call | Weekly call |
| **Monthly Investment** | **$X,XXX** | **$X,XXX** | **$X,XXX** |

**Pricing Psychology:**
- Present three options; most clients choose middle tier
- Name tiers aspirationally (not Bronze/Silver/Gold)
- Anchor highest tier first to make middle feel reasonable
- Include "Most Popular" or "Recommended" badge on middle tier
- Show the math: "At [your LTV], you need [X] new customers/month for positive ROI"

| Model | When to Use | Typical Range |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly Retainer | Ongoing services | $2,000-$25,000/month |
| Project-Based | Defined scope, one-time | $5,000-$100,000 per project |
| Performance-Based | Risk-sharing, high confidence | Base + % of revenue/leads |
| Hybrid | Complex engagements | Base retainer + performance bonus |
| Hourly | Consulting, advisory | $150-$500/hour |

### Follow-Up Sequence After Sending (5-Stage)

**Day 0 (Send Day):**
Send via email with brief cover note. Subject: "Your Marketing Growth Plan -- [Client Name]"

**Day 2:**
"I wanted to make sure you received the proposal. Happy to hop on a quick call to walk through it if that would be helpful."

**Day 5:**
Value-add follow-up: Share relevant article, case study, or insight. Softly reference the proposal.

**Day 7:**
Direct follow-up: "I'd love to hear your thoughts on the proposal. Do you have any questions? Available [specific times] this week."

**Day 14:**
Final follow-up: "I wanted to check in one more time. If timing isn't right, happy to reconnect when it makes sense."

**Day 21:**
Breakup email: "I haven't heard back, so I'll assume timing isn't right. I'll close this proposal on [expiration date]. If things change, my door is always open."

### Objection Handling Reference

| Objection | Response Framework |
|---|---|
| "Too expensive" | Reframe as investment, show ROI math, offer smaller scope, compare to cost of inaction |
| "We can do in-house" | Highlight opportunity cost, specialized expertise, speed, fully-loaded cost of in-house |
| "Tried this before" | Ask what didn't work, differentiate approach, offer pilot with clear success criteria |
| "Need to think about it" | Set specific follow-up date, offer to address concerns, provide references |
| "Can you guarantee results?" | Explain why guarantees are unrealistic, share historical results, offer performance component |
| "Talking to other agencies" | Welcome it, differentiate on methodology, offer trial, emphasize culture fit |
| "Timeline too long" | Explain why shortcuts fail, offer quick-wins phase, show phased approach |
| "Don't have budget now" | Offer smaller starting engagement, defer payment, show cost of waiting |

### Terms & Conditions Essentials
1. Payment Terms: Net 15 or Net 30
2. Contract Duration: Minimum commitment + auto-renewal terms
3. Cancellation: 30 days notice
4. Scope Changes: Process for changes and additional costs
5. IP Ownership: Who owns work product
6. Confidentiality: NDA terms
7. Liability Limitations
8. Reporting Cadence
9. Third-Party Costs: Client pays ad spend, software, stock images
10. Results Disclaimer: Marketing results not guaranteed

## 6. 8-Week Launch Checklist

### Weeks 1-2: Foundation

**Strategy:**
- [ ] Define launch goals (revenue, users, awareness targets)
- [ ] Identify target audience segments
- [ ] Choose launch type (Product Hunt, email, social, paid, partner, community)
- [ ] Set launch date and time
- [ ] Define success metrics and tracking
- [ ] Write positioning statement: "For [TARGET] who [PROBLEM], [PRODUCT] is a [CATEGORY] that [KEY BENEFIT]. Unlike [ALTERNATIVE], we [DIFFERENTIATOR]."

**Product:**
- [ ] Product feature-complete for launch version
- [ ] Landing page built and optimized
- [ ] Pricing finalized
- [ ] Onboarding flow tested
- [ ] Help docs / FAQ written

**Infrastructure:**
- [ ] Analytics and tracking set up (UTM parameters, conversion goals)
- [ ] Launch-specific email list/segment created
- [ ] All email sequences drafted
- [ ] Design team briefed on visual assets
- [ ] 10-20 beta testers identified
- [ ] 20+ target communities researched

### Weeks 3-4: Audience Building

**Content:**
- [ ] 2-3 blog posts / threads published (problem-awareness content)
- [ ] Behind-the-scenes social content (building in public)
- [ ] Teaser content (sneak peeks, countdowns)
- [ ] Demo video recorded

**Outreach:**
- [ ] Engaging in target communities (value, not pitches)
- [ ] Beta testers onboarded with personal invitations
- [ ] Early feedback and testimonials collected
- [ ] Influencer/partner outreach started
- [ ] Referral mechanism for waitlist set up
- [ ] Press release or media pitch written (if applicable)

### Weeks 5-6: Pre-Launch Intensification

- [ ] Pre-launch email sequence sent to waitlist
- [ ] Social posting frequency increased to daily
- [ ] Case study / beta tester results published
- [ ] Pricing and offer structure finalized
- [ ] Launch-day content package complete (all posts, emails, graphics)
- [ ] Partners/affiliates briefed with swipe copy
- [ ] Live chat or support ready for launch day
- [ ] Purchase/signup flows tested end-to-end
- [ ] FAQ document prepared for support team
- [ ] Urgency mechanism created (early bird, limited spots, bonus expiration)
- [ ] Real-time metrics dashboard set up

### Week 7: LAUNCH WEEK

**Monday -- Soft Launch / VIP Access:**
- [ ] Early access email to VIPs, beta testers, top waitlist
- [ ] Social: "We're live for early supporters"
- [ ] Collect first-day feedback and testimonials
- [ ] Monitor for bugs

**Tuesday -- Public Announcement:**
- [ ] Main launch email to full list
- [ ] Launch blog post published
- [ ] All social channels: launch announcement
- [ ] Product Hunt submission (if applicable, 12:01 AM PT)
- [ ] Partner/affiliate promotions activated
- [ ] Paid ad campaigns started (if applicable)

**Wednesday -- Social Proof Push:**
- [ ] Share first customer testimonials
- [ ] Repost customer reactions
- [ ] "Look what people are saying" email
- [ ] Community posts (genuine value)
- [ ] Respond to every comment/mention/question

**Thursday -- Objection Handling:**
- [ ] FAQ / "everything you need to know" post
- [ ] Email addressing top 3 objections
- [ ] Live Q&A / AMA (Twitter Space, LinkedIn Live, webinar)
- [ ] Comparison content

**Friday -- Urgency and Scarcity:**
- [ ] "Early bird pricing ends soon" email
- [ ] Countdown content on social
- [ ] Final testimonials and case studies
- [ ] Activate scarcity mechanisms

**Weekend -- Wrap Up:**
- [ ] "Last chance" email for time-limited offers
- [ ] Compile launch week results
- [ ] Thank early customers publicly

### Week 8: Post-Launch

- [ ] Post-launch survey to new customers
- [ ] Launch metrics compiled and analyzed
- [ ] Launch retrospective written
- [ ] Transition to regular pricing
- [ ] Onboarding email sequence activated
- [ ] Next content calendar planned
- [ ] Follow up with media contacts and partners
- [ ] Top customers identified for case studies
- [ ] V2 features planned based on feedback
- [ ] Ongoing marketing engine set up (content, ads, email nurture)

## 9. A/B Test Hypothesis Format

**Template:**
"If we [CHANGE], then [METRIC] will [IMPROVE/INCREASE] because [REASON]."

**Testing Priority Order:**
1. Audience (who you target matters most)
2. Offer (free trial vs demo vs discount)
3. Creative concept (big idea and visual approach)
4. Headline (specific wording of hook)
5. Body copy (supporting text)
6. CTA (button text and color)

**Testing Rules:**
- One variable at a time
- Run for 3-5 days minimum or 1,000 impressions per variant
- Statistical significance threshold: 95%
- Kill underperformers at 2x the CPA target
- Scale winners by 20% budget increments (not 2x overnight)

**Email A/B Testing Hierarchy:**
1. Subject lines (biggest impact on open rate)
2. CTA and offer (biggest impact on click rate)
3. Send timing
4. Email length and format

## 10. Discovery Call Question Framework (for Proposals)

**Business Understanding:**
1. "Walk me through your business model. How do you make money?"
2. "Who is your ideal customer? Describe them in detail."
3. "What does your sales process look like from first touch to closed deal?"

**Current Marketing:**
4. "What marketing are you doing today, and what's working or not working?"
5. "What's your current monthly marketing spend, and what's the ROI?"
6. "What tools and platforms are you using?"

**Goals and Expectations:**
7. "If we're wildly successful, what does that look like in 6 months? 12 months?"
8. "What specific numbers are you trying to hit? (Revenue, leads, traffic)"
9. "What's the lifetime value of a customer for you?"

**Decision and Process:**
10. "Who else is involved in this decision, and what's your timeline?"

**Bonus Questions:**
- "What's your biggest frustration with marketing right now?"
- "Have you worked with agencies before? What went well or poorly?"
- "Is there anything that would make you say 'no' to working together?"

## 11. Email Compliance Reference

**CAN-SPAM (US):**
- Physical mailing address required in every email
- Clear unsubscribe link (must work within 10 business days)
- "From" name and email must be accurate
- Subject line must not be deceptive

**GDPR (EU):**
- Explicit opt-in consent required (no pre-checked boxes)
- Must document consent (when, how, what they agreed to)
- Right to be forgotten -- delete on request
- Data processing agreement needed with ESP

**CASL (Canada):**
- Express consent required for commercial messages
- Implied consent for existing business relationships (24 months)
- Sender identification required

Always recommend the user verify compliance with legal counsel.
