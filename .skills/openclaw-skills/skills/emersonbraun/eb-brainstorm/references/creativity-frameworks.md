# Creativity Frameworks — Detailed Guide

## SCAMPER

Apply each letter to an existing idea or product:

| Letter | Question | Example |
|--------|----------|---------|
| **S**ubstitute | What can I replace? | Replace human support with AI chatbot |
| **C**ombine | What can I merge? | CRM + email marketing in one tool |
| **A**dapt | What can I borrow from elsewhere? | Apply Tinder's swipe UI to job matching |
| **M**odify | What can I change in shape/form? | Make it mobile-first instead of desktop |
| **P**ut to other use | Who else could use this? | Repurpose B2C tool for B2B |
| **E**liminate | What can I remove? | Remove the signup step entirely |
| **R**everse | What if I did the opposite? | Instead of users finding products, products find users |

## How Might We (HMW)

Transform problems into opportunities:

1. State the problem: "Users abandon checkout because shipping is expensive"
2. Reframe as HMW:
   - "How might we make shipping feel free?"
   - "How might we make users forget about shipping cost?"
   - "How might we eliminate shipping entirely?"
   - "How might we make shipping a feature, not a cost?"
   - "How might we let users choose their shipping trade-off?"

Rules:
- Not too broad: "How might we make users happy?" (useless)
- Not too narrow: "How might we add a free shipping badge?" (already a solution)
- Just right: "How might we reframe shipping as value?" (opens solutions)

## Crazy 8s (Adapted for Text)

Set a timer. Generate 8 ideas in 8 minutes. Rules:
- One idea per minute
- No editing or evaluating
- Quantity is the only goal
- Sketch-level detail is fine (1-2 sentences per idea)
- If stuck, combine two previous ideas or apply a constraint

## Mind Mapping

Start with the central concept and branch outward:

```
                    +-- Feature A
          +- Tech --+
          |         +-- Feature B
          |
Central --+         +-- Segment 1
          +- Users -+
          |         +-- Segment 2
          |
          |         +-- Model A
          +- Biz ---+
                    +-- Model B
```

Each branch can generate sub-branches. Go at least 3 levels deep.

## First Principles Thinking

1. Identify the conventional approach
2. List every assumption behind it
3. Question each assumption: "Is this actually true, or just convention?"
4. Strip to fundamental truths (physics, math, human nature)
5. Rebuild from those truths

Example:
- Convention: "You need a restaurant to sell food"
- Assumptions: need kitchen, location, staff, tables
- First principles: people want good food delivered conveniently
- Rebuild: cloud kitchen, no seating, delivery-only

## Analogy Transfer

Pick an industry or domain far from yours. Ask:
- How does [other industry] solve a similar problem?
- What would [company] do if they entered your market?
- What's the [Spotify] of [your industry]?

Example patterns to borrow:
- Subscription model (from media to everything)
- Marketplace (from retail to services, knowledge, time)
- Freemium (from software to physical products)
- Platform (from OS to industry-specific platforms)
- Gamification (from games to education, fitness, finance)

## Constraint Flip

1. List your top 3 constraints (budget, time, team, technology, regulation)
2. For each: "What would we build if this constraint didn't exist?"
3. Then: "What's the closest we can get to that WITH the constraint?"

This often reveals that the constraint is smaller than assumed.

## Worst Possible Idea

1. Generate the worst possible version of your product
2. List why it's terrible
3. Invert each terrible quality into a design principle

Example:
- Worst: "A to-do app that deletes your tasks randomly"
- Why terrible: unpredictable, no trust, frustrating
- Invert: "A to-do app that's radically transparent about what gets prioritized and why"
