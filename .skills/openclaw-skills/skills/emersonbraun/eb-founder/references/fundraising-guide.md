## Do I Need Investment?

Before seeking investment, answer these questions:

**Signs that you do NOT need investment:**
- The product can be built for <$5K and 3 months of work
- The business model generates revenue from day 1
- You can bootstrap with paying customers
- There is no need for rapid scale (winner-takes-all)

**Signs that you DO need investment:**
- Winner-takes-most market requiring speed
- High development cost (hardware, regulation, certification)
- Long sales cycle (enterprise) requires runway
- Network effects requiring fast critical mass

**Alternatives to VC:**
- Bootstrap (customers fund growth)
- Revenue-based financing (recurring revenue as collateral)
- Government grants (FAPESP, FINEP, BNDES, Sebrae)
- Crowdfunding (Kickstarter, Catarse)
- Indie hacking (profitable from day 1)

## Round Types

| Round | Typical Size | Dilution | What It Proves |
|-------|-------------|----------|----------------|
| **Pre-seed** | $50K-500K | 10-15% | Idea + team + early signals |
| **Seed** | $500K-3M | 15-25% | Early PMF + early metrics |
| **Series A** | $3M-15M | 20-30% | Proven PMF + replicable growth |
| **Series B** | $15M-50M | 15-25% | Go-to-market scale |
| **Series C+** | $50M+ | 10-20% | Market dominance + internationalization |

### Common Instruments
- **SAFE** (Simple Agreement for Future Equity): YC standard, no interest, converts in the next round. Simpler, faster.
- **Convertible Note**: Debt that converts to equity. Has interest + cap + discount.
- **Equity Round**: Preferred shares with full terms (liquidation preference, anti-dilution, board seats).

### Key Terms
- **Pre-money valuation**: Company value BEFORE investment
- **Post-money valuation**: Pre-money + investment
- **Dilution**: % the founder gives up. Ex: If pre-money valuation is $4M and you raise $1M, post-money = $5M, dilution = 20%
- **Vesting**: Founder shares are "earned" over time (typical: 4 years with 1-year cliff)
- **Cap table**: Table showing each shareholder's percentage
- **Liquidation preference**: Investor receives X before founders in case of a sale

## Pitch Deck

### Classic Structure (10-12 slides)

1. **Title**: Name + 1-sentence tagline
2. **Problem**: What is the pain? Quantify it (time lost, money wasted)
3. **Solution**: How do you solve it? Demo or screenshot
4. **Market Size**: TAM → SAM → SOM with sources
5. **Business Model**: How do you make money? Unit economics
6. **Traction**: Real metrics (users, revenue, growth rate)
7. **Competitors**: Positioning matrix (never say "we have no competitors")
8. **Differentiator**: What makes it defensible? Moat?
9. **Team**: Why will YOU win?
10. **Financials**: 3-year projections (be realistic, not a fantasy hockey stick)
11. **Ask**: How much do you want to raise? For what? Milestones with that money
12. **Contact**: Email, LinkedIn, website

### Golden Rules
- **20 slides MAX**. Ideal: 10-12
- **20 minutes** of presentation (5-min pitch + 15 min Q&A)
- **30pt** minimum font size (Guy Kawasaki's rule)
- **1 idea per slide**
- **Numbers > words**: "40% of users return on D7" > "high retention"
- **Don't lie**: Investors find out. Credibility is unrecoverable
- **Tell a story**: Problem → Hero (you) → Journey → Victory

## Valuation

### Pre-seed / Seed (little revenue)
Most commonly used methods:
- **Comparables**: What valuation did similar companies at the same stage raise at?
- **Berkus Method**: Assign value to 5 factors (idea, prototype, team, relationships, sales). Each worth $0-500K
- **Rule of thumb**: Pre-seed: $3-8M. Seed: $5-20M. Varies GREATLY by sector and team.

### Series A+ (with revenue)
- **ARR Multiple**: Typical B2B SaaS: 10-20x ARR at seed/A. Top tier: 30-50x
- **GMV Multiple**: Marketplace: 0.5-2x annualized GMV
- **Simplified DCF**: 5-year projection + terminal value. Less used at early stage

### Negotiation Tips
- Never state your valuation first if you can avoid it
- Have a BATNA (Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement) — other term sheets
- 15-25% dilution per round is healthy. More than 30% is a warning sign
- Pro-rata rights for existing investors is standard
