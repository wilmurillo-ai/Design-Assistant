# Platform Guardrails

Use this reference when deciding whether to engage on Reddit or X.

## Core Rule

Prefer helpful participation over promotion. If the product mention is not clearly useful in context, skip it.

## Reddit Guidance

Reddit is community-first and moderation-heavy.

Default behavior:

- answer the question before mentioning a product
- keep tone plain and specific
- assume direct self-promo is risky unless the thread explicitly asks for recommendations
- avoid dropping links in the first sentence
- watch for subreddit-specific anti-promo norms

High-risk situations:

- obvious promo-only comments
- copy-pasted replies across threads
- recommending a product in a thread that is not asking for solutions
- ignoring subreddit rules
- pretending to be an independent user while promoting your own product

## X Guidance

X is faster, shorter, and more tolerant of direct conversation, but automation and irrelevant replies are still risky.

Default behavior:

- match the pace and brevity of the thread
- avoid repetitive CTA language
- do not hijack unrelated conversations
- keep replies relevant to the original post

High-risk situations:

- high-volume unsolicited replies
- duplicate or near-duplicate content
- irrelevant replies used only to drive clicks
- automated engagement patterns that look synthetic

## Skip Conditions

Recommend skipping when:

- the fit is weak
- the thread is emotionally charged and your product mention adds no value
- rules or norms appear anti-promo
- the same account has already been contacted recently
- you would need to misrepresent identity or experience

## Safer Defaults

When unsure:

- use `Help only`
- omit the link
- suggest a general workflow instead of pitching
- tell the user to wait for a better-fit thread
