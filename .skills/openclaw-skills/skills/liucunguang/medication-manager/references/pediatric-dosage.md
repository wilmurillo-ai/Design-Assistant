# Pediatric Dosage Reference

Common pediatric dosages for children. **Always verify weight and age before dosing.**

> **Disclaimer**: This is a quick-reference guide. Always confirm with current medical guidelines and the specific drug's package insert.

## Fever / Pain

| Drug | Dose | Max Daily | Interval | Notes |
|------|------|-----------|----------|-------|
| Ibuprofen (布洛芬) | 5-10 mg/kg | 40 mg/kg | 6-8h | ≥6 months |
| Paracetamol (对乙酰氨基酚) | 10-15 mg/kg | 60 mg/kg | 4-6h | ≥2 months |

**Example calculations:**
- 10kg child: Ibuprofen 50-100mg/dose; Paracetamol 100-150mg/dose
- 15kg child: Ibuprofen 75-150mg/dose; Paracetamol 150-225mg/dose
- 20kg child: Ibuprofen 100-200mg/dose; Paracetamol 200-300mg/dose

## Antibiotics (common)

| Drug | Dose | Frequency | Duration | Notes |
|------|------|-----------|----------|-------|
| Amoxicillin (阿莫西林) | 25-50 mg/kg/day | Divided 2-3× | 7-10d | Contraindicated in penicillin allergy |
| Azithromycin (阿奇霉素) | 10 mg/kg/day | 1×/day | 3d (or 5d taper) | After meals |
| Cefaclor (头孢克洛) | 20-40 mg/kg/day | Divided 3× | 7-10d | Contraindicated in cephalosporin allergy |
| Cefdinir (头孢地尼) | 7-14 mg/kg/day | Divided 1-2× | 5-10d | Contraindicated in cephalosporin allergy |

### Oseltamivir (奥司他韦) Weight-Based Dosing

| Weight | Dose | Frequency | Duration |
|--------|------|-----------|----------|
| ≤15kg | 30mg | 2×/day | 5d |
| 15-23kg | 45mg | 2×/day | 5d |
| 23-40kg | 60mg | 2×/day | 5d |
| >40kg | 75mg | 2×/day | 5d |

> Best started within 48 hours of symptom onset.

## Contraindicated in Children

| Drug | Reason | Age Limit |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Levofloxacin/Moxifloxacin (左氧氟沙星/莫西沙星) | Cartilage development | <18 years |
| Aspirin (for fever) | Reye's syndrome risk | <12 years |
| Norfloxacin (诺氟沙星) | Cartilage development | <18 years |
| Compound Licorice tablets (复方甘草片) | Contains opium/camphor | <6 years |
| Codeine (可待因) | Respiratory depression | <12 years |
| Promethazine (异丙嗪) | Respiratory depression | <2 years |

## Important Notes

1. **Dose by weight, not age** — weight is more accurate for pediatric dosing
2. **Fever meds**: alternating ibuprofen and paracetamol is not recommended as routine practice
3. **Antibiotics**: always complete the full course; do not stop early
4. **Inhaled meds**: rinse mouth after use (older children) or wipe face (infants)
5. **Always confirm allergies** before prescribing, especially penicillin and cephalosporins
6. **Avoid unnecessary medications** in children — supportive care is often sufficient
