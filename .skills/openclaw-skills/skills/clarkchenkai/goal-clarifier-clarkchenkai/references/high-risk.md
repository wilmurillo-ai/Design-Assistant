# High-Risk Signals

Treat the request as high risk when one or more of these conditions appear:

- real people are being accepted, rejected, flagged, blocked, ranked, or priced
- money, health, employment, legal status, or security access is affected
- the user asks for full automation where criteria are still vague
- the error cost is asymmetric and difficult to reverse
- the user is mixing speed goals with governance-sensitive decisions

## Required response adjustments

- Make the human decision boundary explicit
- Force concrete success criteria
- Ask what evidence is sufficient for action
- Ask what should happen on uncertainty
- Refuse to compress away the risk surface

## Anti-patterns

Do not:

- accept "make it more efficient" as a sufficient goal
- treat policy, fairness, or legal review as optional details
- assume the user's proposed workflow is the right workflow
- hide risk behind generic language like "optimize" or "streamline"
