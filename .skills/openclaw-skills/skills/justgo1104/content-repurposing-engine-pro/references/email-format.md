# Email Newsletter Format — Complete Reference

## Subject Line Formulas (Pick One)

### The Curiosity Gap
- "The [number] mistake that costs [audience] $X/month"
- "I asked [X] experts the same question. The answers surprised me."
- "Nobody talks about [topic]. They should."

### The Number Formula
- "[X] things I learned about [topic] in [timeframe]"
- "The [X] most valuable lessons from [experience]"
- "[X] weeks into [project] — here's what's working"

### The Question Formula
- "Are you still doing [outdated thing]?"
- "What's the real reason you [common struggle]?"
- "When was the last time you [positive action most people skip]?"

### The Bold Statement
- "The [industry] is broken. Here's why."
- "Stop [common mistake]."
- "[X] is the new [Y]. Here's what that means for you."

## Email Opener Templates

### For Curiosity Gap Subjects
"I saw something wild this week..."

### For Number-Based Subjects
"So here's what I found after [X] of [doing thing]..."

### For Question-Based Subjects
"You asked me about [topic] last week. Here's what I found."

## Email Body Structure

```
[Greeting — short, casual]
"One sentence hook — immediate value"

[Body — 150-200 words]
- Paragraph 1: The insight (one clear idea)
- Paragraph 2: Why it matters or how to apply it
- Paragraph 3: The key takeaway or action

[Closer — one of these]
- "Hit reply and tell me..."
- "What's your experience with this?"
- "See you next week."

[Signature]
```

## Email Writing Rules
- Read aloud: if it sounds stiff, soften it
- "You" > "readers" > "people"
- Active voice only
- One idea per email
- Cut every word that doesn't add value
