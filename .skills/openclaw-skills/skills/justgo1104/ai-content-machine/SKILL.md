# AI Content Machine

Transform one piece of long-form content into perfectly-formatted posts for every platform — in under 60 seconds.

## The Content Repurposing System

### Step 1 — Identify Your Source Content

Best sources for repurposing:
- **Blog post** (800-2000 words) → 5-7 platform outputs
- **Newsletter** (500-800 words) → 3-4 platform outputs
- **YouTube/Podcast transcript** → 5-8 platform outputs
- **Client case study** → 4-6 platform outputs

**Golden rule:** Every piece of long-form content you create should be designed to become 5-10 pieces of short-form content. Plan the "spin-off" content before you write the original.

---

### Step 2 — Choose Your Platform Stack

**Pick 3 maximum** (spreading to all 6 at once dilutes quality):
- **LinkedIn** — Best for: B2B, professional services, thought leadership
- **Twitter/X** — Best for: Hot takes, news commentary, industry opinions
- **Email Newsletter** — Best for: Nurturing existing audience, deep value
- **Instagram** — Best for: Visual creators, lifestyle, behind-the-scenes
- **Product Hunt** — Best for: Product launches, tools, SaaS
- **Blog/DEV.to** — Best for: SEO, long-form discovery

**Recommendation for most people:** LinkedIn + Twitter + Email (covers B2B and personal brand)

---

### Step 3 — The Platform-Optimized Formats

#### LinkedIn Post Format
```
[HOOK — Bold statement or question in first line]
[Context — 2-3 sentences expanding the hook]
[Body — Core insight, 2-4 short paragraphs]
[CTA — Question that drives comments]
[#Tag1 #Tag2 #Tag3 — 2-3 relevant hashtags]
```
Length: 150-250 words | Tone: Professional but personal

#### Twitter/X Thread Format
```
(1/N) [HOOK — The entire premise in one tweet]
(2/N) [Context — Why this matters]
(3/N) [Point 1 — With a concrete example]
(4/N) [Point 2 — With a counter-intuitive insight]
(5/N) [CLOSER — What to do with this]
```
Length: Under 260 characters per tweet | Tone: Direct, provocative

#### Email Newsletter Format
```
Subject: [Punchy — questions, numbers, or curiosity]
Opener: [First sentence must stop the scroll]
Body: [Core value from the content, conversational]
Closer: [Natural transition or "reply to this email" CTA]
```
Length: 150-250 words | Tone: Warm, one-to-one

#### Instagram Caption Format
```
[First line — Most compelling/relevant single line]
[2-3 sentence story that connects]
[CTA — "Save this post", question, or "Link in bio"]
[5-7 #hashtags #spaced #out]
```
Length: 150-200 characters | Tone: Conversational, visual storyteller

### Step 4 — AI Generation Prompts (Use These)

**LinkedIn Post Prompt:**
```
Write a LinkedIn post based on this content.

Rules:
- First line must stop the scroll — bold claim, surprising stat, or provocative question
- 3-4 short paragraphs, each max 2-3 sentences
- Conversational but authoritative tone
- End with a question that drives comments
- Add 2-3 relevant hashtags at the bottom
- Total: 150-250 words

Content to transform:
[Paste content here]
```

**Twitter Thread Prompt:**
```
Turn this content into a Twitter/X thread with 4-5 tweets.

Rules:
- Tweet 1: HOOK that stops the scroll (bold claim, question, or surprising number)
- Tweets 2-4: Core content in a logical progression
- Tweet 5: CLOSING — key takeaway or "do this differently" CTA
- Each tweet under 250 characters
- Number each tweet: (1/5), (2/5), etc.
- No emojis in hook (they dilute impact)

Content:
[Paste content here]
```

**Email Newsletter Prompt:**
```
Write an email newsletter section based on this content.

Rules:
- Subject line: Maximum 50 characters, curiosity-driven
- First sentence: Punchy, immediate hook
- Body: 150-200 words, warm and conversational tone
- Write as if talking to ONE reader, not an audience
- End with a natural soft CTA (not a sales pitch)
- No formatting needed — plain text email

Content:
[Paste content here]
```

### Step 5 — Build a Content Repurposing Workflow

**Weekly batch process (90 minutes):**
1. Monday: Write or record one piece of long-form content (article/newsletter/video)
2. Tuesday-Thursday: Run through AI Content Machine prompts for your 3 platforms
3. Friday: Edit AI outputs (remove generic parts, add personal touch)
4. Schedule: Buffer, Later, or Hootsuite for the week

**Content calendar:**
- 1 long-form piece/week (the anchor)
- 3 LinkedIn posts from it (Mon, Wed, Fri)
- 1 Twitter thread from it (Tue)
- 1 email newsletter segment (Thu)
- 1 Instagram caption (Sat)

**Total: 6 pieces from 1 source = 52x content multiplier annually**
