# Regional Press Release Conventions

Read this file when writing a press release targeting a specific region or market. Each section covers the dominant style guide, dateline format, tone, regulatory landscape, distribution norms, best send times, and common mistakes for that region.

---

## Western Europe

Covers France, Germany/DACH, UK, Ireland, Benelux, Iberian Peninsula, Italy, and Switzerland.

### Dominant Style Guides

- **France**: AFP (Agence France-Presse) style. Strict informational tone. No promotional language.
- **Germany/DACH**: dpa (Deutsche Presse-Agentur) style. Formality is paramount. Marketing language like "weltführend" (world-leading) is prohibited.
- **UK/Ireland**: PA Media style (formerly Press Association). British English. Oxford comma IS used (unlike AP).
- **Spain**: EFE (Agencia EFE) style. Formal, institutional tone.
- **Italy**: ANSA (Agenzia Nazionale Stampa Associata) style. Formal, relationship-driven media culture.
- **Benelux**: ANP (Netherlands), Belga (Belgium). Often bilingual (NL/FR in Belgium).

### Key Conventions

- **French releases**: Use Unicode guillemets for quotes — `«\u00A0Like this\u00A0»` with non-breaking spaces inside (not `<<` `>>` or English `""`). Third person, present tense. Answer QQOQCP (Qui, Quoi, Ou, Quand, Comment, Pourquoi). "POUR DIFFUSION IMMEDIATE" for release designation.
- **German releases**: An image is mandatory. 1.5 line spacing. ~15 words per sentence. Every claim must be verifiable. Switzerland uses "Medienmitteilung," Austria uses "Pressemeldung."
- **UK releases**: End with a "Notes to Editors" section (more formalized than US boilerplate). Single quotes common. Honorifics used (Sir, Dame, Professor).
- **Spanish releases**: "Nota de prensa" is the standard term. Formal register. Include a "Nota para editores" section similar to UK.
- **Italian releases**: "Comunicato stampa" is the standard term. Relationship with journalists matters more than in Anglo-Saxon markets.

### Dateline Formats

| Country | Format | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| France | `CITY, le [day] [month] [year] --` | `PARIS, le 26 mars 2026 --` |
| Germany | `CITY, [Day]. [Month] [Year] --` | `BERLIN, 26. März 2026 --` |
| UK | `CITY, [Day] [Month] [Year] --` | `LONDON, 26 March 2026 --` |
| Spain | `CITY, [day] de [month] de [year] --` | `MADRID, 26 de marzo de 2026 --` |
| Italy | `CITY, [day] [month] [year] --` | `MILANO, 26 marzo 2026 --` |

### Regulatory Landscape

- **France (AMF)**: Listed companies on Euronext Paris must comply with EU Market Abuse Regulation (MAR). Immediate disclosure of inside information. File with AMF before or simultaneously with media.
- **Germany (BaFin)**: EU MAR applies across DACH. DPRG professional standards. DRPR published October 2024 AI guidelines requiring mandatory labeling of unchecked AI-generated content.
- **UK (FCA)**: MAR and FCA Disclosure Guidance. Use RNS (Regulatory News Service) for regulatory announcements.
- **Spain (CNMV)**: EU MAR applies. Releases for listed companies must go through CNMV's information channel.
- **Italy (CONSOB)**: EU MAR applies. SDIR (Sistema di Diffusione delle Informazioni Regolamentate) for listed companies.

### Distribution Norms

- **France**: AFP, PR Newswire France, Business Wire France, Cision France, Datapresse. Never send as attachment.
- **Germany**: news aktuell (dpa subsidiary) dominant, operates "ots" network. PresseBox for B2B/tech.
- **UK**: PA Media/Mediapoint, PR Newswire UK, Business Wire UK. CIPR and PRCA are professional bodies.
- **Spain**: Agencia EFE, Europa Press. Comunicae and Cision Spain for digital distribution.
- **Italy**: ANSA, Adnkronos. Direct journalist relationships often more effective than wire services.

### Best Send Times

| Market | Optimal window | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| France | Tue-Thu, 10h-12h CET | August (vacances), Friday PM, lunch 12h-14h, Monday AM (backlog) |
| Germany/DACH | Tue-Thu, 10:00-12:00 CET | Bruckentage (bridge days around holidays), Karneval week in Rhineland |
| UK | Tue-Thu, 10 AM-2 PM GMT/BST | Bank holidays, Friday PM |
| Spain | Tue-Thu, 10:00-13:00 CET | Siesta hours (14-16h), August |
| Italy | Tue-Wed, 10:00-12:00 CET | August entirely (especially Ferragosto week), Friday PM |
| Benelux | Tue-Thu, 09:30-12:00 CET | School holidays (vary by region), Carnival in southern Netherlands/Belgium |

### Common Mistakes

- Using "nous" or promotional language in French releases (instant credibility killer)
- Translating word-for-word instead of culturally adapting (French text expands ~20-25% vs English)
- Using ASCII `<<` `>>` or English `""` instead of Unicode guillemets `«\u00A0\u00A0»` with non-breaking spaces in French releases
- Forgetting mandatory images for German media
- Overselling in UK releases (understatement works better — UK journalists are "very discerning, tough, and even sarcastic")
- Ignoring August in France, Spain, and Italy — media is effectively on holiday

## Eastern Europe

Covers Poland, Czech Republic, Hungary, Romania, Bulgaria, Baltics (Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania), and Western Balkans.

### Key Conventions

- **Style guides**: National wire agencies set the tone — PAP (Poland), CTK (Czech Republic), MTI (Hungary), Agerpres (Romania), BTA (Bulgaria), BNS/ELTA (Baltics)
- **Language**: Local language strongly preferred for domestic media. English acceptable for international-facing releases.
- **Tone**: Generally formal. Post-Soviet markets especially value institutional credibility and official sources.
- **Structure**: Standard inverted pyramid. EU-aligned markets follow conventions similar to Western Europe.

### Dateline Formats

Follow the local convention of the primary wire agency. Generally: `CITY, [Day] [Month] [Year] --` (e.g., `WARSAW, 26 March 2026 --` for English-language releases).

### Regulatory Landscape

- EU member states follow EU MAR for listed companies
- National regulators: KNF (Poland), CNB (Czech Republic), MNB (Hungary)
- Non-EU markets (Western Balkans) have their own securities commissions

### Distribution Norms

- National wire agencies are primary channels
- Cision and Meltwater have coverage in major markets
- Direct journalist relationships are particularly important — media landscapes are smaller and more personal
- PR Newswire has limited coverage; Business Wire better for financial releases

### Best Send Times

| Market | Optimal window | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Poland/Czech/Hungary | Tue-Thu, 09:00-12:00 CET | National holidays, Easter/Christmas periods |
| Romania/Bulgaria | Tue-Thu, 09:00-11:00 EET | August, Orthodox Easter week |
| Baltics | Tue-Thu, 09:00-11:00 EET | Midsummer (June), Christmas-New Year |

## Nordics

Covers Sweden, Denmark, Norway, Finland, and Iceland.

### Key Conventions

- **Style guides**: TT (Sweden), NTB (Norway), Ritzau (Denmark), STT (Finland)
- **Language**: English widely understood and accepted, but local language strongly preferred for domestic media
- **Tone**: Informal and egalitarian compared to most of Europe. Avoid hierarchy signaling. The Jante Law cultural undertone means self-promotion is viewed negatively — let facts speak.
- **Structure**: Standard inverted pyramid. Lean writing valued — Nordics appreciate brevity even more than Anglo-Saxon markets.

### Dateline Formats

`CITY, [Day] [Month] [Year] --` (e.g., `STOCKHOLM, 26 March 2026 --`)

### Regulatory Landscape

- **Sweden (Finansinspektionen)**, **Norway (Finanstilsynet)**, **Denmark (Finanstilsynet)**, **Finland (Finanssivalvonta)**: EU MAR applies (Norway via EEA agreement)
- Nordic markets have strict transparency requirements — listed companies must disclose promptly via Nasdaq Nordic or Oslo Børs

### Distribution Norms

- National wire agencies (TT, NTB, Ritzau, STT) are primary
- Mynewsdesk (Swedish platform) widely used across all Nordics
- Cision has strong Nordic coverage
- Direct pitching is effective — media communities are small and accessible

### Best Send Times

| Market | Optimal window | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| All Nordics | Tue-Thu, 09:00-11:00 CET | Midsummer week (late June), Christmas-New Year, July (summer holidays) |
| Norway specifically | Same | Also avoid Easter week (major holiday) |

## North America

Covers the United States and Canada.

### Dominant Style Guides

- **US**: AP Stylebook (Associated Press). Governs virtually all US press releases.
- **Canada**: CP Style Guide (Canadian Press). Similar to AP but with Canadian English conventions.

### Key AP/CP Style Rules

- **Headlines**: Sentence case (not Title Case). Capitalize only first word and proper nouns.
- **Oxford comma**: NOT used in AP Style ("red, white and blue"). CP Style also omits it.
- **Numbers**: Spell out one through nine, numerals for 10+. Always numerals for ages, percentages, money.
- **Dates**: Month abbreviations with dates (Jan. 2, not January 2). No "th" or "rd."
- **Time**: Use a.m. and p.m. (lowercase, with periods), not AM/PM.
- **Percentages**: "percent" not "%" in AP. CP accepts either.
- **Attribution**: "said" only. Full name + title first reference, last name thereafter.
- **State abbreviations (US)**: AP abbreviations (Calif., not CA). 8 states never abbreviated: Alaska, Hawaii, Idaho, Iowa, Maine, Ohio, Texas, Utah.
- **30 major US cities stand alone** without state: Atlanta, Baltimore, Boston, Chicago, Cincinnati, Cleveland, Dallas, Denver, Detroit, Honolulu, Houston, Indianapolis, Las Vegas, Los Angeles, Miami, Milwaukee, Minneapolis, Nashville, New Orleans, New York, Oklahoma City, Philadelphia, Phoenix, Pittsburgh, St. Louis, Salt Lake City, San Antonio, San Diego, San Francisco, Seattle.
- **Canadian cities**: Toronto, Montreal, Vancouver, Ottawa, Calgary stand alone. Others require province.

### Dateline Formats

| Country | Format | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| US | `CITY, State (Month Day, Year) --` | `NASHVILLE, Tenn. (March 26, 2026) --` |
| Canada | `CITY, Province (Month Day, Year) --` | `TORONTO (March 26, 2026) --` |

### Canada-Specific Conventions

- **Bilingual requirements**: Federal government and regulated industries often require FR/EN releases simultaneously
- **SEDAR+**: Listed companies file on SEDAR+ (replacing old SEDAR/EDGAR equivalent)
- **TSX continuous disclosure**: Toronto Stock Exchange has immediate disclosure obligations for material events
- **Media landscape**: Globe and Mail, CBC, La Presse (FR), Le Devoir (FR). Regional media very strong (e.g., Winnipeg Free Press, Calgary Herald)

### Regulatory Requirements (US)

- **SEC Regulation FD** (2000): Simultaneous public disclosure required when material nonpublic info is shared
- **Form 8-K**: Earnings releases furnished under Item 2.02 within 4 business days
- **Regulation G**: Non-GAAP measures require reconciliation to nearest GAAP measure
- **PSLRA Safe Harbor**: Forward-looking statements require specific cautionary language
- **FTC Endorsement Guides** (revised 2023): Clear disclosure of material connections. Acceptable: #ad, #sponsored. Not acceptable: #ambassador, #collab

### Distribution Norms

- **US**: PR Newswire ($195 membership + $325+/release), Business Wire ($400+), GlobeNewswire ($195+/release)
- **Canada**: Canada Newswire (CNW, part of PR Newswire), Globe Newswire, Marketwired
- Professional bodies: PRSA (US), CPRS (Canada)
- Personalized pitches to 20-30 targeted journalists alongside wire distribution

### Best Send Times

| Market | Optimal window | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| US (general) | Tue-Thu, 10 AM-2 PM ET | Monday AM (inbox overload), Friday PM, major holidays |
| US (West Coast) | Adjust to 10 AM-2 PM PT for West Coast outlets | Same |
| US (earnings) | Before market open (pre-9:30 AM ET) or after close (post-4 PM ET) | NEVER during trading hours |
| Canada (EN) | Tue-Thu, 10 AM-1 PM ET | Coordinate with FR release simultaneously |
| Canada (FR) | Tue-Thu, 10h-13h ET | Same as EN — release simultaneously |

## Latin America

Covers Mexico, Central America, Brazil, and Spanish-speaking South America.

### Key Conventions

- **Style guides**: EFE style influences all Spanish-speaking markets. In Brazil, Agencia Brasil and major outlets follow their own conventions.
- **Language**: Spanish for most markets, Portuguese for Brazil. Do NOT use Spanish for Brazilian media — it is a distinct language and culture.
- **Tone**: Formal, institutional. Relationship-driven media culture across the region.
- **Structure**: Standard inverted pyramid. Longer releases (400-600 words) are more acceptable than in North America/Europe.

### Dateline Formats

- Spanish: `CITY, [day] de [month] de [year] --` (e.g., `CIUDAD DE MEXICO, 26 de marzo de 2026 --`)
- Portuguese (Brazil): `CITY, [day] de [month] de [year] --` (e.g., `SAO PAULO, 26 de marco de 2026 --`)

### Regulatory Landscape

- **Mexico (CNBV)**: Securities regulation for listed companies on BMV (Bolsa Mexicana de Valores)
- **Brazil (CVM)**: Listed companies on B3 must comply with CVM disclosure rules
- **Argentina (CNV)**, **Colombia (SFC)**, **Chile (CMF)**: National securities regulators

### Distribution Norms

- EFE and Reuters Latam are primary wire services for Spanish-speaking markets
- Agencia Brasil for Brazilian market
- Business Wire and PR Newswire have Latin America divisions
- Direct journalist relationships extremely important — personal connections matter more than wire services in many markets

### Best Send Times

| Market | Optimal window | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mexico | Tue-Thu, 10 AM-1 PM CST | Dia de Muertos week, Christmas-NY, Semana Santa |
| Brazil | Tue-Thu, 10h-13h BRT | Carnival week, year-end (Dec-Jan) |
| Argentina/Chile/Colombia | Tue-Thu, 10 AM-1 PM local | National holidays, Semana Santa, year-end |

### Common Mistakes

- Translating a US release into Spanish and expecting it to work across all Spanish-speaking markets (cultural adaptation required per country)
- Using Spain Spanish for Latin American markets (vocabulary and tone differ significantly)
- Ignoring the importance of personal relationships with journalists

## Middle East

Covers the Gulf Cooperation Council (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman), Levant (Lebanon, Jordan), Egypt, and North Africa.

### Key Conventions

- **Style guides**: WAM (Emirates News Agency) for UAE, SPA (Saudi Press Agency) for Saudi Arabia. Reuters and AFP Arabic editions are widely referenced.
- **Language**: Arabic + English bilingual releases are the norm for business and financial news. Some markets (UAE, Qatar) accept English-only for international business stories.
- **Tone**: Formal, respectful. Cultural sensitivity paramount. Honorifics and titles are important (Sheikh, His Excellency, Dr.).
- **Calendar**: Be aware of Islamic calendar dates — Hijri dates may be required alongside Gregorian dates for government-related releases.
- **Script direction**: Arabic is right-to-left (RTL). Ensure formatting accommodates this.

### Dateline Formats

- English: `CITY, Country (Month Day, Year) --` (e.g., `DUBAI, UAE (March 26, 2026) --`)
- Arabic: Follow WAM/SPA conventions with Hijri date optional

### Regulatory Landscape

- **UAE**: DFSA (Dubai Financial Services Authority) for DIFC-listed companies, SCA (Securities and Commodities Authority) for DFM/ADX-listed companies
- **Saudi Arabia (CMA)**: Capital Market Authority regulates Tadawul-listed companies. Vision 2030 initiatives may have specific announcement requirements.
- **Qatar (QFMA)**: Qatar Financial Markets Authority

### Distribution Norms

- WAM, SPA are primary government/official channels
- PR Newswire Middle East, Business Wire Middle East
- AETOSWire (Dubai-based, strong regional coverage)
- Al Jazeera, Al Arabiya, Arab News, Gulf News are key regional outlets
- Government communication offices are gatekeepers in many Gulf states

### Best Send Times

| Market | Optimal window | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Gulf states | Sun-Thu, 09:00-12:00 GST | Friday-Saturday (weekend). Ramadan working hours (shorter days). Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha holidays. |
| Egypt/Levant | Sun-Thu, 09:00-12:00 local | Same religious holidays. Egyptian national holidays. |

### Common Mistakes

- Ignoring the Friday-Saturday weekend in Gulf states (Sunday is a working day)
- Sending during Ramadan without adjusting for shorter business hours
- Failing to include Arabic version for domestic media
- Not using proper honorifics and titles
- Overlooking cultural sensitivities in imagery and language

## Africa

Covers Sub-Saharan Africa including South Africa, Nigeria, Kenya, Ghana, and East/West African markets.

### Key Conventions

- **Style guides**: No single dominant style. SAPA legacy (South Africa, now defunct but style lives on), AFP French Africa, Reuters Africa. Many outlets follow AP or PA style adapted locally.
- **Language**: Hugely diverse. English dominant in business press for East/Southern Africa. French for West/Central Africa. Portuguese for Mozambique, Angola, Cape Verde. Arabic for North Africa (see Middle East section).
- **Tone**: Professional but varies by market. South Africa is the most developed PR market on the continent.
- **South Africa specifics**: 11 official languages but English dominates business media. BBBEE (Broad-Based Black Economic Empowerment) context relevant for corporate announcements.

### Dateline Formats

Generally follow AP/PA style in English: `CITY, Country (Month Day, Year) --` or `CITY, Country, [Day] [Month] [Year] --`

For French-speaking Africa, follow AFP style.

### Regulatory Landscape

- **South Africa (FSCA/JSE)**: JSE Listings Requirements for listed companies. Strict continuous disclosure obligations.
- **Nigeria (SEC Nigeria/NGX)**: Securities and Exchange Commission and Nigerian Exchange regulations
- **Kenya (CMA Kenya/NSE)**: Capital Markets Authority regulations
- Markets with less developed capital markets have fewer formal requirements but professional standards are rising

### Distribution Norms

- APO Group: Pan-African distribution (50+ countries)
- Africa Newswire
- PR Newswire has growing African coverage
- Direct journalist outreach remains the most effective channel in most markets
- Social media (Twitter/X, WhatsApp) plays an outsized role in news distribution compared to other regions

### Best Send Times

| Market | Optimal window | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| South Africa | Tue-Thu, 09:00-12:00 SAST | December-January (holiday season), Heritage Day week |
| Nigeria | Tue-Thu, 09:00-12:00 WAT | National holidays, Eid celebrations |
| Kenya/East Africa | Tue-Thu, 09:00-12:00 EAT | National holidays, election periods |
| French-speaking Africa | Tue-Thu, 09:00-12:00 WAT/CET | Ramadan, August |

## Central Asia

Covers Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan, and Mongolia.

### Key Conventions

- **Style guides**: National wire agencies dominate — Kazinform (Kazakhstan), UzA (Uzbekistan). Russian-language media remains influential.
- **Language**: Local language + Russian for most markets. English for international-facing releases. Kazakhstan is transitioning from Cyrillic to Latin script.
- **Tone**: Formal, institutional. Government relationships are central to media access.
- **Structure**: Standard inverted pyramid. Government and institutional endorsements carry significant weight.

### Dateline Formats

English: `CITY, Country (Month Day, Year) --` (e.g., `ASTANA, Kazakhstan (March 26, 2026) --`)

### Regulatory Landscape

- **Kazakhstan (AFSA/KASE)**: Astana Financial Services Authority for AIFC-listed, Kazakhstan Stock Exchange for KASE-listed
- Markets are developing — regulatory requirements vary significantly
- Government approval may be needed for sensitive sectors (mining, energy, telecom)

### Distribution Norms

- National wire agencies are primary
- Interfax (Russian language) has regional coverage
- Direct outreach to key journalists — small media communities
- Government communication channels are often mandatory for major announcements

### Best Send Times

| Market | Optimal window | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| All Central Asia | Tue-Thu, 09:00-12:00 local | Nauryz/Nowruz (March 21-23), Ramadan/Eid, Russian New Year period (late Dec-early Jan) |

## East Asia

Covers China, Japan, South Korea, and Taiwan.

### Key Conventions

- **China**: Xinhua style. Strict regulatory environment. Government relationships essential. WeChat and Weibo distribution is as important as wire services. Self-censorship on sensitive topics is the norm.
- **Japan**: Kyodo/Jiji style. Extremely formal. Honorifics mandatory (-san, -sama). Kisha clubs (press clubs) are institutional gatekeepers — access is restricted and relationship-dependent.
- **South Korea**: Yonhap style. Formal, hierarchical. Naver and Daum news aggregators matter more than individual outlet websites. Chaebols dominate the business news cycle.
- **Taiwan**: CNA (Central News Agency) style. Traditional Chinese characters (not simplified). Media landscape is open and diverse.

### Dateline Formats

| Market      | Format                        | Example                       |
| ----------- | ----------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| China       | `CITY (Month Day, Year) --`   | `BEIJING (March 26, 2026) --` |
| Japan       | `CITY, Month Day, Year --`    | `TOKYO, March 26, 2026 --`    |
| South Korea | `CITY, Month Day (Yonhap) --` | `SEOUL, March 26 (Yonhap) --` |
| Taiwan      | `CITY (CNA) Month Day --`     | `TAIPEI (CNA) March 26 --`    |

### Regulatory Landscape

- **China (CSRC/CAC)**: China Securities Regulatory Commission for listed companies. Cyberspace Administration of China (CAC) regulates online content. Strict pre-approval may be required for certain announcements.
- **Japan (FSA/JPX)**: Financial Services Agency and Japan Exchange Group. TDnet system for timely disclosure.
- **South Korea (FSS/KRX)**: Financial Supervisory Service and Korea Exchange. KIND system for listed company disclosures.
- **Taiwan (FSC/TWSE)**: Financial Supervisory Commission. MOPS system for disclosures.

### Distribution Norms

- **China**: Xinhua, China News Service. PR Newswire China, Business Wire China. WeChat official accounts, Weibo, and Toutiao (ByteDance news aggregator) for digital reach.
- **Japan**: Kyodo, Jiji. PR Times (dominant domestic platform). Kisha club membership required for regular access to major outlets.
- **South Korea**: Yonhap, Newsis. Newswire.co.kr is the primary domestic platform. Naver/Daum placement is critical.
- **Taiwan**: CNA. Direct media relationships are effective — small, accessible media community.

### Best Send Times

| Market | Optimal window | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| China | Mon-Fri, 09:00-11:00 CST | Chinese New Year (1-2 weeks), Golden Week (Oct 1-7), politically sensitive dates |
| Japan | Mon-Thu, 10:00-15:00 JST | Golden Week (late Apr-early May), Obon (mid-Aug), year-end/New Year (Dec 28-Jan 3). Morning preferred for daily papers. |
| South Korea | Mon-Thu, 10:00-14:00 KST | Chuseok (3 days), Lunar New Year (3 days) |
| Taiwan | Tue-Thu, 10:00-14:00 CST | Lunar New Year, national holidays |

### Common Mistakes

- Assuming one release works for all East Asian markets (cultural, linguistic, and regulatory differences are enormous)
- Ignoring kisha clubs in Japan (you cannot simply pitch to a journalist — access is mediated)
- Using simplified Chinese for Taiwan or traditional Chinese for mainland China
- Underestimating the importance of Naver/Daum in South Korea
- Sending during Chinese New Year (media essentially shuts down for 1-2 weeks)

## Southeast Asia

Covers Singapore, Indonesia, Thailand, Vietnam, Philippines, Malaysia, and Myanmar.

### Key Conventions

- **Style guides**: No single dominant standard. Reuters, AFP, and AP style are widely referenced. National agencies: Bernama (Malaysia), Antara (Indonesia), PNA (Philippines), VNA (Vietnam), MCOT (Thailand).
- **Language**: English is the business language in Singapore, Philippines, and Malaysia. Local language required for domestic media in Thailand, Vietnam, Indonesia. Bilingual releases common.
- **Tone**: Generally formal. Hierarchy and respect for seniority reflected in how executives are referenced.
- **Singapore**: Functions as the regional PR hub. English-language releases distributed here often cover ASEAN-wide announcements.

### Dateline Formats

English: `CITY (Month Day, Year) --` or `CITY, Country (Month Day, Year) --`

### Regulatory Landscape

- **Singapore (MAS/SGX)**: Monetary Authority of Singapore and Singapore Exchange. Strict continuous disclosure.
- **Indonesia (OJK/IDX)**: Financial Services Authority and Indonesia Stock Exchange
- **Thailand (SEC Thailand/SET)**: Securities and Exchange Commission
- Regulatory maturity varies widely across the region

### Distribution Norms

- PR Newswire Asia, Business Wire Asia, GlobeNewswire
- Media OutReach (Hong Kong-based, strong ASEAN coverage)
- Direct journalist relationships critical in smaller markets
- Social media (Facebook, LINE in Thailand, Zalo in Vietnam) plays a significant news distribution role

### Best Send Times

| Market | Optimal window | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Singapore/Malaysia | Tue-Thu, 09:00-12:00 SGT | Chinese New Year, Hari Raya, Deepavali, Christmas |
| Indonesia | Tue-Thu, 09:00-12:00 WIB | Ramadan/Eid (major), Lebaran mudik period |
| Thailand | Tue-Thu, 09:00-12:00 ICT | Songkran (mid-April), Royal holidays |
| Vietnam | Tue-Thu, 09:00-11:00 ICT | Tet (Lunar New Year, 1-2 weeks) |
| Philippines | Tue-Thu, 09:00-12:00 PHT | Holy Week, Christmas season (Dec-Jan) |

## South Asia

Covers India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, and Nepal.

### Key Conventions

- **Style guides**: PTI (Press Trust of India) for India, APP (Associated Press of Pakistan) for Pakistan. Reuters and AFP widely referenced.
- **Language**: English dominant in business media across the region. Hindi and regional languages for domestic/regional outlets in India. Urdu for Pakistani domestic media.
- **Tone**: Formal. India has the most mature PR market in the region.
- **India specifics**: Massive, fragmented media landscape. National (English) + regional language media must both be addressed for broad reach. Bollywood, cricket, and politics dominate news cycles — business news competes for attention.

### Dateline Formats

English: `CITY (Month Day, Year) --` (e.g., `NEW DELHI (March 26, 2026) --`)

### Regulatory Landscape

- **India (SEBI/BSE/NSE)**: Securities and Exchange Board of India. Strict LODR (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) for listed companies.
- **Pakistan (SECP)**: Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan
- Regulatory sophistication varies — India is by far the most developed

### Distribution Norms

- PR Newswire India, Business Wire India
- India: PTI, IANS (Indo-Asian News Service). Regional language wire services for state-level coverage.
- Direct pitching remains the primary channel — journalist databases are less comprehensive than Western markets.

### Best Send Times

| Market | Optimal window | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| India | Tue-Thu, 10:00-13:00 IST | Diwali week, Holi, Independence Day, Republic Day, major cricket tournaments |
| Pakistan | Tue-Thu, 10:00-12:00 PKT | Ramadan/Eid, Independence Day |
| Bangladesh | Tue-Thu, 10:00-12:00 BDT | Ramadan/Eid, Pohela Boishakh |

## Australia / Oceania

Covers Australia, New Zealand, and Pacific Islands.

### Key Conventions

- **Style guide**: AAP (Australian Associated Press) style, similar to UK PA style. Australian English (colour, organisation, centre).
- **Tone**: Relaxed but professional. "Tall poppy syndrome" means excessive self-promotion is viewed negatively. Let results speak.
- **New Zealand**: NZPA legacy conventions. Maori cultural considerations for relevant announcements (Treaty of Waitangi context, te reo Maori greetings may be appropriate).

### Dateline Formats

`CITY, Country (Month Day, Year) --` (e.g., `SYDNEY (March 26, 2026) --`)

Australian/NZ cities that stand alone without country: Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, Auckland, Wellington.

### Regulatory Landscape

- **Australia (ASIC/ASX)**: Australian Securities and Investments Commission. ASX Listing Rules require continuous disclosure. Releases for listed companies go to ASX first, media second.
- **New Zealand (FMA/NZX)**: Financial Markets Authority and New Zealand Exchange

### Distribution Norms

- AAP MediaNet (primary Australian distribution)
- PR Newswire Asia-Pacific
- Scoop (New Zealand)
- Media landscape is concentrated — a small number of outlets (Nine, Seven, News Corp, ABC) dominate
- Direct pitching to journalists is highly effective

### Best Send Times

| Market | Optimal window | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Australia | Tue-Thu, 10 AM-1 PM AEST | Christmas-January lull (many journalists off Dec 20 - Jan 15), Easter, ANZAC Day |
| New Zealand | Tue-Thu, 10 AM-1 PM NZST | Same as Australia. Also Waitangi Day. |

### Common Mistakes

- Assuming Australia follows US PR conventions (it follows UK conventions more closely)
- Ignoring the December-January holiday period (Australian media effectively shuts down)
- Overly promotional tone (tall poppy syndrome — Australians distrust self-aggrandizement)

## International / Multilingual Releases

### Localization Principles

Localization means cultural adaptation, not just translation. A release for the US market cannot be simply translated for France or Japan.

### Key Adaptation Rules

- **Text expansion**: English to French ~20-25%, English to German ~20-30%, English to Japanese ~10-20% (but character width differs). Layouts must accommodate.
- **Date formats**: US (Month Day, Year), UK/Oceania (Day Month Year), France (day month year), Germany (Day. Month Year), East Asia (Year Month Day)
- **Measurement**: Metric for all non-US markets. Include conversions if targeting both.
- **Currency**: Local currency first, with USD/EUR equivalent in parentheses if relevant.
- **Visuals**: Color symbolism varies (red = luck in China, danger in South Africa). Verify cultural appropriateness.
- **Regulatory**: Global releases for listed companies must satisfy ALL applicable regulators.

### Practical Approach

1. Write the "master" release in the primary market's conventions
2. Create localized versions (not translations) for each target region
3. Use in-region PR professionals for localization, not translators
4. Adapt quotes — a quote that resonates in the US may fall flat in Germany or Japan
5. Maintain global messaging consistency while allowing local flexibility
6. Coordinate timing across time zones for simultaneous releases; if staggering, release first in the market where the news is most relevant
