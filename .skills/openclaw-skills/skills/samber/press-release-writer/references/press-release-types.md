## Product Launch {#product-launch}

**News angle**: What problem does this solve that couldn't be solved before? What's genuinely new?

**Mandatory elements:**

- Product name and what it does (one sentence)
- Specific pricing and availability (date, regions, channels). NEVER use "competitive pricing"
- The problem it solves, quantified if possible
- Technical specs or key differentiators (keep to 3-5 bullet points max)
- Customer or beta tester quote if available

**Timing**: Send under embargo 2-3 weeks before launch to give journalists prep time. Coordinate with wire release on launch day.

**Template skeleton:**

```
[Company] Launches [Product Name], [One-Sentence Value Prop]

[Subhead with key differentiator or data point]

CITY, Country -- (Date) -- [Company], [one-line descriptor], today
announced [Product Name], a [category] that [key benefit with specificity].
[Product] is available starting [date] at [price] through [channel].

[Problem paragraph: 2-3 sentences on the market gap or user pain point.
Include a data point: "X% of [users] currently face [problem]."]

"[Quote from CEO/CPO about vision and why this matters]," said [Full
Name], [Title] of [Company].

[How it works paragraph: 3-5 key features/capabilities, explained in
terms of user outcomes, not technical specs.]

[Optional: Beta/pilot results paragraph with specific metrics.
"During a 3-month pilot with [N] users, [Product] achieved [metric]."]

"[Quote from customer/beta tester about real-world results]," said
[Full Name], [Title] at [Customer Company].

[Availability paragraph: pricing tiers, supported platforms/regions,
how to get started. Include a URL.]

About [Company]
[Boilerplate]

Contact: [Name] | [Email] | [Phone]
###
```

## Funding Announcement {#funding-announcement}

**News angle**: What will this money enable? The funding itself is the hook, but the story is what comes next.

**Mandatory elements (ALL in the first paragraph):**

- Amount raised
- Round type (Seed, Series A/B/C, etc.)
- Lead investor name
- Use of funds (hiring, expansion, R&D, etc.)

**Additional required elements:**

- Investor quote explaining their investment thesis
- Total funding to date
- Key company metrics (ARR, users, growth rate)
- Specific hiring plans or expansion targets

**Timing**: Coordinate with investors. TechCrunch/Les Echos exclusives are common for Series A+. Announce early in the week (Tuesday preferred) for maximum pickup.

**Template skeleton:**

```
[Company] Raises [Amount] in [Round Type] Led by [Lead Investor]
to [Primary Use of Funds]

CITY, Country -- (Date) -- [Company], [descriptor], today announced
it has raised [amount] in [round type] funding led by [Lead Investor],
with participation from [other investors]. The round will fund
[primary use: e.g., "expansion into the European market and doubling
the engineering team to 50 by Q4 2026"].

[Company context paragraph: what the company does, key metrics.
"Since launching in [year], [Company] has grown to [X users/ARR/etc.],
representing [Y]% growth over the past [period]."]

"[CEO quote about what this funding means for the mission and
customers]," said [Full Name], [Title].

"[Lead investor quote explaining why they invested -- the thesis,
the market opportunity, what impressed them]," said [Full Name],
[Title] at [Investor Firm].

[Use of funds detail paragraph: specific plans, target metrics,
timeline. Be concrete: "open a Paris office in Q1 2026" not
"expand internationally."]

[Notable existing investors, advisors, or board changes if relevant.]

The round brings total funding to [amount]. [Company] was previously
backed by [prior investors] in its [prior round] in [year].

About [Company]
[Boilerplate]

Contact: [Name] | [Email] | [Phone]
###
```

## Partnership / Collaboration {#partnership}

**News angle**: What does this combination enable that neither party could do alone?

**Mandatory elements:**

- Both partners named in headline
- What the partnership delivers to end users/customers
- Scope (exclusive? geographic? time-limited?)
- Mutual approval of all content (verify with user)

**Format specifics:**

- Double dateline: "PARIS and SAN FRANCISCO -- (Date) --"
- Both company logos (mention in press kit)
- Both boilerplates
- Quotes from both organizations

**Template skeleton:**

```
[Company A] and [Company B] Partner to [Joint Value Proposition]

CITY_A and CITY_B -- (Date) -- [Company A] and [Company B] today
announced a [partnership type: strategic partnership / integration /
joint venture / distribution agreement] to [specific joint outcome].
[One sentence on scope or user impact.]

[Context paragraph: why this partnership, what market need it
addresses, with data.]

"[Company A spokesperson quote about what Company B brings]," said
[Full Name], [Title] at [Company A].

[Technical/operational detail paragraph: how it works, what changes
for customers, timeline.]

"[Company B spokesperson quote about strategic rationale and mutual
value]," said [Full Name], [Title] at [Company B].

[Availability/timeline: when customers can expect the joint offering,
how to sign up or learn more.]

About [Company A]
[Boilerplate A]

About [Company B]
[Boilerplate B]

Media Contacts:
[Company A]: [Name] | [Email] | [Phone]
[Company B]: [Name] | [Email] | [Phone]
###
```

## Event Announcement {#event}

**Mandatory elements:**

- Event name, date(s), location (physical address or virtual platform)
- Registration/ticket info with link and pricing
- Key speakers or attractions
- Who should attend (target audience)
- Agenda highlights

**Timing**: Send 4-6 weeks before for major events, 2 weeks for smaller ones. Follow up 1 week before with a reminder angle (new speaker, sold-out tier, etc.).

## Crisis Communication {#crisis}

**News angle**: Transparency, accountability, and corrective action. The story is what you're doing about it.

**Framework: Care-Control-Commitment**

The order is non-negotiable — lead with empathy, not facts. A crisis release that opens with operational details signals the company cares more about its reputation than the people affected.

1. **Care**: Acknowledge impact on affected people first. The very first sentence after the dateline must express concern for those affected — before any description of what happened or what you're doing about it.
2. **Control**: Describe what you know and what actions you've taken
3. **Commitment**: State specific next steps with timeline

**Critical rules:**

- Issue a holding statement IMMEDIATELY, then a detailed follow-up as facts emerge
- Never speculate or assign blame before investigation
- Never use "no comment" (say "we are investigating and will share updates as they become available")
- Never dump crisis news on Fridays hoping it will disappear (it backfires)
- All statements require legal review, but excessive legal delay worsens outcomes
- Include a timeline of events if relevant
- Provide a dedicated contact for media inquiries (not the general press line)

**Template skeleton:**

```
[Company] Statement Regarding [Brief Description of Incident]

CITY, Country -- (Date) -- [Company]'s thoughts are with [those affected — name the group
specifically: customers, employees, residents, passengers, etc.].

[What happened paragraph: factual description of the incident in
neutral terms. Be specific about what you know and transparent about
what you don't. Include scope and timeline.]

"[Senior leader quote — empathy and personal accountability first,
then what the company is doing. NOT operational details first]," said
[Full Name], [Title].

[Actions taken paragraph: immediate steps, timeline of response,
remediation already underway.]

[Next steps paragraph: what happens now, when the next update will
come, resources available to those affected (hotline, webpage, etc.).]

[If applicable: third-party investigation, regulatory notification,
or independent review.]

For ongoing updates, visit [URL]. Media inquiries: [dedicated
contact name, email, phone].

About [Company]
[Boilerplate -- keep short in crisis context]
###
```

## Executive Hire / Leadership Change {#executive-hire}

**Mandatory elements:**

- New executive's name, title, and start date
- Who they report to
- Brief career background (2-3 most relevant prior roles)
- What they will focus on (strategic mandate)
- Quote from CEO on why this hire
- Quote from the new executive on why they joined

## Award / Recognition {#award}

**Mandatory elements:**

- Award name and granting organization
- Why the award was given (criteria or category)
- What makes this recognition significant (selectivity, prestige)
- Quote from award-granting body if available

**Pitfall**: Awards releases are the most ignored type. They only work when the award is from a credible third party and the recognition maps to something the audience cares about. "Best Workplace" from a major publication is newsworthy. "Innovation Award" from an unknown body is not.

## Mergers & Acquisitions {#mergers-acquisitions}

**Mandatory elements:**

- Acquiring and target company
- Transaction value (or "undisclosed" with context on deal structure)
- Strategic rationale
- Impact on customers, employees, and products
- Expected closing timeline and regulatory conditions

**Regulatory requirements (public companies):**

- SEC Rule 425 legend (US)
- Forward-looking statement safe harbor under PSLRA
- "Additional Information and Where to Find It" section
- AMF notification requirements (France, Euronext-listed)

## Financial Results / Earnings {#earnings}

**Mandatory elements:**

- Revenue, net income/loss, EPS (current period + year-over-year comparison)
- GAAP figures first, with non-GAAP reconciliation
- Guidance or outlook for next period
- Key business metrics (subscribers, users, bookings, etc.)

**Regulatory requirements:**

- Must be furnished on SEC Form 8-K Item 2.02 within 4 business days (US)
- Non-GAAP measures require reconciliation to nearest GAAP measure (Regulation G)
- Safe harbor language under PSLRA
- Release timing: before market open or after market close, NEVER during trading hours

## Open Source Project Milestone {#open-source}

**News angle**: Community impact, adoption metrics, technical significance. Not marketing.

**Mandatory elements:**

- Project name, version number, and what changed
- Adoption metrics (GitHub stars, downloads, contributors, dependents)
- Key technical improvements (performance benchmarks, new capabilities)
- Community acknowledgment (name top contributors)
- Link to changelog, release notes, and migration guide

**Tone guidance (per Red Hat's guidelines):**

- Zero marketing language. Media outlets will immediately disregard hyperbolic claims.
- Thank the community explicitly and by name where possible — mention top contributors by GitHub handle or full name in the body text, not just in a generic "thanks to all contributors" line. Community recognition drives future contributions
- Include concrete benchmarks ("40% faster JSON parsing" not "significant performance improvements")
- If the project has corporate backing, be transparent about the relationship

**Template skeleton:**

```
[Project Name] [Version] Released: [Key Technical Achievement]

[Subhead with adoption metric or benchmark]

CITY, Country -- (Date) -- The [Project Name] maintainers today
released version [X.Y], featuring [top 1-2 changes]. [Project] is
used by [adoption metric: "over 15,000 projects on GitHub" / "50M+
monthly downloads on npm/pkg.go.dev"].

[Technical highlights paragraph: what's new, with benchmarks.
"[Feature X] reduces memory allocation by 35% in [benchmark scenario],
based on testing with [methodology]."]

"[Maintainer quote about the release, what it means for users,
and the community contributions that made it possible]," said
[Full Name], [maintainer role].

[Community paragraph: number of contributors to this release,
name the top 3-5 contributors by name/handle with their contributions,
and explain how to get involved.]

[Migration/compatibility paragraph: breaking changes, migration
path, supported platforms.]

[Project Name] is available under the [License] license at
[GitHub URL]. Documentation: [URL].

About [Project / Organization]
[Boilerplate]

Contact: [Name] | [Email]
###
```
