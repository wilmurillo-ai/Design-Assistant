## Print Media {#print}

### Format Expectations

- AP/AFP/PA style formatting depending on country
- Third-person, past tense for news events (present tense for ongoing situations)
- High-resolution images with **typed captions** (never stapled/attached to release)
- Print-ready PDF version often appreciated alongside plain text

### Lead Times

Plan distribution well in advance based on publication type:

- **National monthly magazines**: 4-6 months ahead
- **Weekly magazines**: 2-8 weeks ahead
- **Daily newspapers (features/sections)**: 2-3 weeks ahead
- **Daily newspapers (hard news)**: 1-3 days ahead (or same day for breaking news)
- **Sunday supplements**: 4-6 weeks ahead

### Embargo Protocol

Embargoes are an agreement, not a unilateral declaration.

- Write "EMBARGOED UNTIL [Date], [Time] [Timezone]" prominently at the top
- **Critical**: Wait for each journalist's explicit agreement BEFORE sending embargoed material
- If a journalist does not agree to the embargo before receiving the material, there is no embargo
- Follow up with a reminder 24 hours before embargo lifts
- If the embargo breaks (someone publishes early), release to all immediately

### What Print Journalists Need

- Clean, scannable text (no fancy formatting, no HTML)
- Factsheet or backgrounder as a separate document
- Professional headshots of quoted executives (300 dpi minimum)
- Product images (high-res, white background preferred for print)
- Permission/attribution requirements for all images

## Digital / Wire Service {#digital}

### Wire Service Requirements

Wire services (PR Newswire, Business Wire, GlobeNewswire) have specific formatting rules:

**Universal wire rules:**

- Headlines under 100 characters (ideal: 65 for Google SERP display)
- Subheadline under 160 characters
- Body between 300-500 words
- Include company ticker symbol for public companies: "[Company Name] (NYSE: TICK)"
- End with ### or -30-

**SEO optimization for digital:**

- Primary keyword in first 56 characters of headline
- Natural keyword distribution in first 250 words
- 1-3 hyperlinks (most important link in first paragraph)
- Implement NewsArticle schema markup if publishing on own newsroom
- Alt text for all images (descriptive, keyword-aware)

**Hyperlink best practices:**

- Maximum 3 links in the release body
- Use descriptive anchor text, not "click here"
- First link should point to the most important landing page
- Include UTM parameters for tracking: `?utm_source=press_release&utm_medium=wire&utm_campaign=[campaign_name]`
- Google's John Mueller has stated that press release links are "likely to be ignored" for direct link-building, but earned media links from coverage are valuable

**GEO (Generative Engine Optimization):** Press releases increasingly serve as source material for AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews). Optimize for AI citation:

- Clear entity definitions in the first paragraph ("X is a [category] company that [does what]")
- Answer-ready sections that stand alone as quotable passages
- Include a FAQ section at the end with 3-5 common questions and concise answers
- Use structured data and schema markup
- Descriptive alt text on all media assets

## Broadcast (TV and Radio) {#broadcast}

### Fundamental Differences

Broadcast writing is **writing for the ear**, not the eye. Every rule changes.

### Writing Rules for Broadcast

- **Sentence length**: 8-12 words maximum (vs 20+ for print)
- **Contractions**: Use them — always ("it's," "don't," "won't," "they're," "we've"). Broadcast copy without contractions sounds robotic and immediately signals a press release being read verbatim, which producers reject
- **Attribution first**: "The CEO said the company plans to..." NOT "The company plans to..., the CEO said"
- **No abbreviations**: Spell out everything. "The Federal Bureau of Investigation" on first reference, "the F-B-I" (hyphenated for reading) on second
- **Numerals**: Write out for readability. "about two-thousand" not "approximately 2,000"
- **Present tense preferred**: "The company says" not "The company said"
- **Active voice mandatory**: No passive constructions
- **Round numbers**: "nearly 50-thousand users" not "49,847 users"
- **Avoid direct quotes in body text**: Paraphrase instead, or use actuality/soundbite references

### Timing and Pacing

- Average speaking rate: 150-160 words per minute
- A 30-second story = ~75 words
- A 60-second story = ~150 words
- Time your script by reading aloud

### Soundbite Optimization

If you include a suggested quote for broadcast, make it:

- Under 15 seconds when spoken aloud (~35 words)
- Self-contained (makes sense without surrounding context)
- Conversational, not corporate
- Emotionally resonant or surprising

### Video News Releases (VNRs)

- Cost: $10,000-$80,000 to produce
- 90% of US newsrooms use supplied video
- FCC requires source disclosure (though compliance is low)
- Provide B-roll separately for stations that want to edit
- Include natural sound (factory floor, event ambiance, etc.)
- Provide multiple formats: broadcast-quality and web-optimized

### What Broadcast Journalists Need

- Media advisory with logistics (who is available for interview, where, when)
- Suggested interview questions
- B-roll footage or photo package
- Mobile-friendly materials (journalists file from the field)

## Social Media Press Release (SMPR) {#social}

### Origin and Philosophy

Todd Defren of SHIFT Communications created the SMPR in 2006, responding to Tom Foremski's "Die! Press Release! Die!" critique. The key insight: press releases should be modular, not monolithic. Each component should be individually shareable and remixable.

### SMPR Components

Instead of continuous prose, organize content as:

1. **Core facts**: Bulleted, under 400 words total
2. **Pre-approved quotes**: Each quote as a standalone, tweetable passage
3. **Links that matter**: Product page, demo, data source (not "learn more" generic links)
4. **Embedded media**: Images, video, podcast/audio clips, infographics
5. **Social sharing widgets**: Pre-composed tweets/posts with suggested hashtags
6. **RSS feed / permalink**: For content aggregation
7. **Comment/discussion section**: If published on own newsroom

### Social-Specific Adaptations

**For LinkedIn amplification:**

- Extract the most surprising data point for a standalone post
- Frame through the spokesperson's personal perspective
- Tag relevant company pages and people
- Use the press release as a "source" linked in the post

**For X/Twitter amplification:**

- Pre-write 3-5 tweet variations (each under 280 characters)
- Include relevant hashtags (max 2 per tweet)
- Create a thread version for complex announcements
- Tag relevant journalists and outlets

**For Instagram/TikTok:**

- Prepare a visual summary (carousel for Instagram, short video for TikTok)
- Behind-the-scenes content around the announcement
- Quote cards (spokesperson quote overlaid on branded image)

### Hybrid Approach (Most Common Today)

Most organizations now add social elements to conventional press releases rather than creating pure SMPRs:

- Standard press release structure
- Social sharing buttons at the top
- Pre-written social copy suggestions at the bottom
- Embedded multimedia
- Permalink on company newsroom

## Trade / Industry Press {#trade}

### How Trade Press Differs

Trade journalists operate in a fundamentally different context from consumer reporters. Understanding this changes everything about how you write.

**Key differences:**

- **Technical jargon is expected and signals expertise**: Where consumer releases avoid jargon, trade releases use it to demonstrate credibility
- **Editorial scope is extremely narrow**: A mining trade publication covering coal will NOT cover gold. A construction weekly covering cement will NOT cover concrete. Research each outlet's exact beat.
- **Depth over breadth**: Trade journalists want technical detail, methodology, and data that general reporters would skip
- **Longer acceptable length**: Trade releases can stretch to 500-700 words for technical announcements
- **Industry context is assumed**: No need to explain basic industry concepts

### Writing for Trade Press

- Lead with industry-specific impact, not general market trends
- Include technical specifications, benchmarks, and methodology
- Provide analyst or expert commentary from recognized industry voices
- Reference industry standards, certifications, or compliance frameworks
- Include comparison to existing solutions (specific, not vague)

### Distribution Strategy

- Wire services rarely produce deep placement in niche trade outlets
- **Tailored exclusives work best**: Offer an exclusive angle to one key trade publication
- Build relationships with specific beat reporters
- Provide analyst briefings as supplementary material
- Consider industry events as distribution moments (announce at the trade show)

### Feature Stories vs Press Releases in Trade

For trade media, feature stories (bylined articles, case studies, technical deep-dives) vastly outperform press releases for credibility and engagement. Consider:

- Offering a bylined article by your CTO/expert to the trade publication
- Providing a case study with a named customer and specific results
- Proposing a contributed piece that provides value to the trade publication's readers beyond your announcement

## Multimedia Enhancement {#multimedia}

### Why Multimedia is Mandatory in 2025-2026

The data is unambiguous:

- Releases with at least one image: **2x engagement**
- Releases with multiple images: **6x engagement**
- Releases with video: **3x engagement**
- Releases with any visual: up to **9.7x more views**
- 87% of journalists use multimedia assets supplied with pitches
- 36% of French journalists specifically report being more likely to study a release with multimedia

### Image Requirements

- **Resolution**: Minimum 300 dpi for print, 72 dpi for web
- **Formats**: JPEG or PNG. Provide both high-res and web-optimized versions
- **Captions**: Every image needs a caption and credit line
- **Alt text**: Descriptive (for accessibility and GEO). "Bar chart showing 15% YoY revenue growth for Q3 2025" not "chart.png"
- **Types to include**: Executive headshots, product shots, infographics, event photos, screenshots

### Video Requirements

- Keep under 90 seconds for social/web distribution
- Provide broadcast-quality (uncompressed) AND web-optimized versions
- Include subtitles/captions (required for social, helpful for broadcast)
- B-roll should be 2-3 minutes of varied footage with natural sound
- Include a transcript

### Infographics

- Summarize the key data points visually
- Brand-consistent design
- Provide as both embedded and downloadable high-res file
- Include source citations within the infographic itself

### Press Kit Composition

A complete press kit alongside the release includes:

1. The press release (plain text + formatted PDF)
2. High-res images with captions (zipped folder or media library link)
3. Executive bios and headshots
4. Company factsheet (founding date, HQ, headcount, key metrics)
5. Product screenshots or demo video
6. FAQ document
7. Previous relevant coverage (links to 2-3 top clips)
8. Brand guidelines / logo files
