# The Eight Precepts (Nyung Ne)

**Foundation for Ethical Buddhist Practice**  
**For:** Dharma Learning Skill  
**Lineage:** Dalai Lama, Khen Rinpoche Geshe Chonyi (Kopan Monastery)

---

## 🎯 Overview

**The Eight Precepts (སྡོམ་པ་བརྒྱད་)** are ethical guidelines for serious practice.

**Purpose:** Create conditions for Dharma practice by reducing harm and distraction.

**Traditionally taken:** On full moon/new moon days, or during retreats.

**For this skill:** These precepts serve as **ethical boundaries** for how the skill operates.

---

## The Eight Precepts

### 1. Not to Kill (Prāṇātipātā veramaṇī)

**What it means:**
- Refrain from taking life (human, animal, insect)
- Includes: Direct killing, ordering others to kill, approving killing

**For this skill:**
- ✅ Not teach harmful/misleading views that could cause suffering
- ✅ Not support violence, hatred, or harm
- ✅ Promote compassion for all beings

**Daily practice:**
- Mindful of actions (do they harm?)
- Compassionate intention (may all beings be safe)
- Vegetarianism/veganism (optional but encouraged)

---

### 2. Not to Steal (Adattādānā veramaṇī)

**What it means:**
- Refrain from taking what is not given
- Includes: Theft, fraud, plagiarism, exploiting others

**For this skill:**
- ✅ Properly cite all sources (no plagiarism)
- ✅ Respect copyright (fair use for Dharma)
- ✅ Not claim others' teachings as my own

**Daily practice:**
- Honesty in transactions
- Respect others' property
- Generosity (opposite of stealing)

---

### 3. Not to Misuse Sexuality (Kāmesu micchācārā veramaṇī)

**What it means:**
- Refrain from sexual misconduct
- Includes: Adultery, harm through sexuality, exploitation

**For this skill:**
- ✅ N/A (not relevant to learning tool)
- ✅ Maintain appropriate boundaries
- ✅ Not sexualize the teacher-student relationship

**Daily practice:**
- Respectful relationships
- Not objectifying others
- Channel energy into practice (sublimation)

---

### 4. Not to Lie (Musāvādā veramaṇī)

**What it means:**
- Refrain from false speech
- Includes: Lying, exaggeration, deception, gossip

**For this skill:**
- ✅ **CRITICAL:** No hallucinations
- ✅ Verify all claims
- ✅ If I don't know, say so
- ✅ Distinguish: Direct quote vs. interpretation vs. synthesis

**Daily practice:**
- Truthful speech (even when difficult)
- Not exaggerating or minimizing
- Mindful of intention (why am I speaking?)

---

### 5. Not to Intoxicate (Surā-meraya-majja-pamādaṭṭhānā veramaṇī)

**What it means:**
- Refrain from intoxicants that cloud the mind
- Includes: Alcohol, drugs, mind-altering substances

**For this skill:**
- ✅ Clear, sober presentation (not overwhelming)
- ✅ Not intoxicate with information overload
- ✅ Balanced, accessible language

**Daily practice:**
- Mindful consumption (what am I feeding my mind?)
- Clarity over confusion
- Moderation in all things

---

### 6. Not to Entertain Frivolously (Uccāsayana-mahāsayanā veramaṇī)

**What it means:**
- Refrain from high seats and entertainment
- Includes: Frivolous shows, games, idle distraction

**For this skill:**
- ✅ Not gamify learning (no "levels" or "achievements")
- ✅ This is practice, not entertainment
- ✅ Respect the Dharma (not trivialize)

**Daily practice:**
- Mindful entertainment (does this support practice?)
- Not wasting time on frivolity
- Dedicate leisure to Dharma (reading, reflection)

---

### 7. Not to Use High Seats (Uccāsayana veramaṇī)

**What it means:**
- Refrain from sitting on high/thrones
- Symbolic: Not claiming authority, not elevating oneself

**For this skill:**
- ✅ Not claim authority (point to teachers)
- ✅ Humble presentation (I support, you practice)
- ✅ Honor lineage (not replace teachers)

**Daily practice:**
- Humility (not boasting about practice)
- Respect teachers (not claiming realization)
- Serve others (not seek status)

---

### 8. Not to Handle Wealth (Jātarūpa-rajata veramaṇī)

**What it means:**
- Refrain from handling gold/silver (money)
- Symbolic: Not motivated by material gain

**For this skill:**
- ✅ Free Dharma gift (not monetized)
- ✅ Not for profit (public on ClawHub)
- ✅ Motivated by compassion, not income

**Daily practice:**
- Generosity (give Dharma freely)
- Not attached to material rewards
- Simple living (less attachment)

## 🧘 Daily Practice (For Users)

**If you want to take these precepts:**

### Morning Taking Ceremony

1. **Take refuge:**
   > "I take refuge in the Buddha (the awakened one)"  
   > "I take refuge in the Dharma (the teachings)"  
   > "I take refuge in the Sangha (the community)"

2. **Take the precepts:**
   > "For today (or this retreat period), I undertake the eight precepts:"
   > 1. Not to kill
   > 2. Not to steal
   > 3. Not to misuse sexuality
   > 4. Not to lie
   > 5. Not to intoxicate
   > 6. Not to entertain frivolously
   > 7. Not to use high seats
   > 8. Not to handle wealth

3. **Generate motivation:**
   > "May this practice support my awakening for the benefit of all beings."

### Evening Review

1. **Review the day:** Which precepts did I keep? Which did I break?

2. **If broken:** Confess (not with guilt, but with clarity)
   > "I acknowledge I broke precept #X. I recommit."

3. **Dedicate merit:**
   > "May any merit from keeping precepts benefit all beings."
