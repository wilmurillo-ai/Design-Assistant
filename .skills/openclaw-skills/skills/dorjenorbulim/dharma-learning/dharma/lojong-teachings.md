# Lojong: The Seven Point Mind Training

**Based on Geshe Lama Konchog's teachings**  
**Distilled:** March 28, 2026  
**For:** Dharma Learning Skill

---

## 🎯 Overview

**Lojong (བློ་སྦྱོང་)** = Mind Training

**Core purpose:** Transform self-cherishing mind into cherishing others → attain enlightenment for benefit of all beings.

**Central insight:** All suffering comes from self-cherishing. All happiness comes from cherishing others.

---

## The Seven Points

### Point 1: Preliminary Practices (Foundation)

**What to do:**
- Recognize precious human rebirth (rare, has great potential)
- Be mindful of death (impermanence — practice NOW, not later)
- Take refuge in Three Jewels
- Observe law of cause and effect (karma)

**Practice:**
- Clean practice space (visualize purifying mind)
- Arrange body, speech, mind (statues, offerings)
- Sit in meditative posture (7-point posture)
- Nine-round breathing (neutralize mind)
- Generate motivation: "I practice for all sentient beings"

**Key Quote:**
> *"Spiritual practice is not something too difficult. It is as easy as changing and transforming our minds and attitudes."*

---

### Point 2: Train on Ultimate Bodhicitta (Wisdom)

**What to realize:**
- All phenomena lack inherent existence (emptiness)
- Subject (mind) and object (phenomena) are both empty
- Between sessions: view everything as illusion, dream

**Practice:**
- Meditate on selflessness (no truly existent "I")
- When grasping arises: "This is like a dream"
- Understand: emptiness ≠ nothingness (things function dependently)

**Key Quote:**
> *"Between sessions, be like in a dream — or be like an illusion."*

---

### Point 3: Train on Relative Bodhicitta (Compassion)

**The Main Practice** — Two parts:

#### A. Equalizing Self and Others

**Recognize:**
- All beings want happiness, none want suffering (equal)
- Friend/enemy/stranger distinctions are temporary (past lives)
- All beings have been your mother (countless rebirths)

**Practice:**
1. **Equanimity:** Level your mind toward all beings (no discrimination)
2. **Recognize all as mother:** Meditate gradually (this life's mother → father → relatives → all beings)
3. **Remember kindness:** How they cared for you (in this life and past)
4. **Repay kindness:** Help them attain happiness, remove suffering

#### B. Exchanging Self for Others (Tonglen)

**Taking Visualization:**
- Inhale: Take on suffering of all beings (black smoke)
- Visualize it crushing your self-cherishing mind
- Start with yourself → parents → locality → world → all realms

**Giving Visualization:**
- Exhale: Give your merit, body, possessions (white light)
- Visualize beings receiving what they need
- Feel joy that they are happy

**Key Quote:**
> *"Combine the giving and taking together. With compassion, take. With love, give."*

---

### Point 4: Transform Adverse Conditions into the Path

**When suffering arises:**

1. **Accumulate merit** (generosity, ethics, patience)
2. **Purify negativities** (confession, prostrations)
3. **Offer to spirits/protectors** (request they ripen others' suffering on you)
4. **Invoke protectors** (ask for blessings to practice)

**Mindset:**
- Suffering = opportunity to practice Tonglen
- Criticism = chance to reduce pride
- Poverty = less attachment, more Dharma
- Spirits/hindrances = teachers destroying self-cherishing

**Key Quote:**
> *"When praised — I am not happy. When criticized — I am happy. When suffering — I am comfortable. Spirits are my teachers."*

**Practice:**
- When sick: "May this ripen on me instead of others"
- When criticized: "This destroys my pride — good!"
- When comfortable: "May others have this happiness"

---

### Point 5: Precepts for Practice (Guidelines)

**What to maintain:**

1. **Practice continually** (no breaks, even when traveling)
2. **Practice with mindfulness** (not mechanically)
3. **Antidote to self-cherishing** (not new form of ego)
4. **Accept harm** (do not retaliate)
5. **Not for material gain** (not for income, food, clothing)
6. **Practice with all beings** (not just friends)
7. **No attachment to outcomes** (not happy when praised, not sad when suffering)
8. **Abandon attachment** (visualize taking all beings' attachment onto yourself)

**Key Quote:**
> *"Do not let the god become a devil."* (Don't let practice become ego)

---

### Point 6: Commitment to Practice (Vows)

**Three inseparables:**
- Body → not separated from deity form
- Speech → not separated from mantra recitation
- Mind → not separated from bodhicitta

**Five Powers at Death:**

| Power | Practice |
|-------|----------|
| **Familiarity** | Practice mind training repeatedly (now, so it's automatic at death) |
| **White seed** | Give away possessions before dying (no attachment) |
| **Determination** | Never be separated from bodhicitta |
| **Repudiation** | Confess broken vows |
| **Prayer** | Pray to take others' suffering, not go to Pure Land |

**Key Quote:**
> *"At the time of death, have no regrets. The five powers are the real transference of consciousness."*

---

### Point 7: All Activities Become Training

**Transform daily life:**

| Activity | Transformation |
|----------|----------------|
| Eating | "May this feed organisms in my body so they generate bodhicitta" |
| Washing | "Water is wisdom washing away obscurations" |
| Opening door | "Opening door to liberation" |
| Closing door | "Shutting door to hell realms" |
| Lighting fire | "Burning self-cherishing with flame of compassion" |
| Sleeping | "May all beings awaken from ignorance" |

**Key Quote:**
> *"All conduct and activities become the training."*

---

## 🧘 Essential Practices (Daily)

### Morning (5-10 min)

1. **Clean space** (sweep, visualize purifying mind)
2. **Set motivation:** "Today, I practice for all sentient beings"
3. **Nine-round breathing** (neutralize mind)
4. **Set intention:** "I will transform all conditions into the path"

### During Day (As needed)

1. **When affected by external events:** Pause, ask "Is this within my control?"
2. **When suffering arises:** "May this substitute for others' suffering"
3. **When happy:** "May others have this happiness"
4. **When meeting anyone:** "This being has been my mother"

### Evening (10-15 min)

1. **Review day:** When did self-cherishing arise?
2. **Tonglen practice:** Take suffering, give merit (5-10 min)
3. **Dedication:** "May merit benefit all beings"

## ⚠️ Common Obstacles & Antidotes

| Obstacle | Antidote |
|----------|----------|
| "This is too difficult" | Start small (one being, one situation) |
| "I can't feel compassion" | Remember one kindness you received |
| "Too much suffering to take" | Visualize taking just one being's suffering |
| "I forget to practice" | Set reminders, link to daily activities |
| "I'm making no progress" | Practice without attachment to results |
| "Others don't deserve my compassion" | They're under control of delusions (like crazy mother) |

---

## 📏 Measuring Progress

**Signs of development:**

| Early Stage | Intermediate | Advanced |
|-------------|--------------|----------|
| Remember to practice sometimes | Practice most days | Practice naturally |
| Intellectual understanding | Some emotional shift | Spontaneous compassion |
| Suffering still painful | Can transform some suffering | All conditions become path |
| Care for some beings | Care for many beings | Care for all beings equally |

**Key indicator:** When suffering arises, do you feel depressed — or do you feel opportunity to practice?
