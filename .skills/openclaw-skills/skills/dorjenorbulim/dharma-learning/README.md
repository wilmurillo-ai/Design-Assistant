## 🚀 Quick Start

### 1. Install the Skill

```bash
# If published on ClawHub
clawhub install dharma-learning

# Or use locally (already in your workspace)
# Located at: ~/.openclaw/workspace/skills/dharma-learning/
```

### 2. Add Your Dharma Texts (Optional but Recommended)

**Location:** `~/.openclaw/workspace/skills/dharma-learning/dharma/`

**Add:**
- Teachings you've received from your teachers
- Notes from Kopan Monastery teachings
- Dalai Lama teachings you want to reference
- Your practice notes

**The more you add, the richer my responses.**

### 3. Start Learning

**Just ask:**

> "Teach me about compassion in Mahayana Buddhism"

> "What is emptiness according to Nagarjuna?"

> "Explain the Middle Way view with references"

## 📖 How It Works

### Step 1: You Ask

**Examples:**
- "What is bodhicitta?"
- "How do I practice loving-kindness at work?"
- "Explain dependent origination"
- "What did the Buddha say about suffering?"

### Step 2: I Search

**I search:**
- ✅ Your local `dharma/` folder (teachings you provided)
- ✅ Authoritative web sources (Kopan, DalaiLama.com, FPMT, etc.)
- ✅ Cross-reference for accuracy

### Step 3: I Verify

**I check:**
- ✅ Matches Middle Way view
- ✅ Consistent with your lineage teachers
- ✅ Properly sourced (references included)
- ✅ No hallucinations (if I don't know, I say so)

### Step 4: I Create Summary

**Output includes:**
- Core teaching (clear, with academic depth)
- Source references (linked)
- Daily life application
- Transformative benefits
- Related concepts

### Step 5: I Save

**Saved to:** `dharma-learning/learned/[topic]-summary.md`

**I remember this** — next time you ask, I build on it.

## 🔍 Accuracy Commitment

**What I Promise:**

✅ **Cite every claim** — Sources linked  
✅ **Cross-reference** — Multiple sources agree  
✅ **Flag uncertainty** — "This is my understanding, verify with teacher"  
✅ **Distinguish:** Direct quote vs. interpretation vs. synthesis  
✅ **Allow correction** — You correct me, I update learning  

**What I Don't Do:**

❌ **Hallucinate** — If I don't know, I say so  
❌ **Replace teachers** — Point you to qualified teachers  
❌ **Claim authority** — I'm a support tool, not a teacher  
❌ **Gamify** — No "levels" or "achievements" (contradicts non-attachment)

## 💡 Tips for Best Use

### 1. **Add Your Teachings**

The more you add to `dharma/` folder, the better my responses.

**Add:**
- Notes from teachings you've received
- Transcripts from recordings
- Book excerpts (properly cited)
- Your practice reflections

### 2. **Ask Specific Questions**

Better: "What is the relationship between emptiness and compassion according to HH Dalai Lama?"

Than: "Tell me about Buddhism"

### 3. **Verify with Teachers**

I support your practice. I don't replace your teachers.

**Always verify** important teachings with qualified teachers.

### 4. **Practice, Don't Just Learn**

Knowledge without practice = intellectual accumulation.

**After each learning:**
- Ask: "How do I apply this today?"
- Set a small practice intention
- Review later: "How did it go?"

### 5. **Correct Me**

If I get something wrong:
- Tell me
- I'll update my learning
- We both grow wiser
