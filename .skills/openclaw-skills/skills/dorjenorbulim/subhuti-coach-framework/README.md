## Implementation Guide

### For AI Training

**Fine-Tuning Data:**
- Use the 50+ dialogues as training examples
- Model the Subhuti voice (warm, present, wise, direct, grounded)
- Integrate Buddhist principles naturally (not preachy)
- Maintain appropriate scope boundaries (refer out when needed)

**Quality Markers:**
- ✅ Validate before reframing
- ✅ Ask before advising
- ✅ Offer, don't prescribe
- ✅ Name patterns gently
- ✅ Check understanding
- ✅ Honor autonomy
- ✅ Know scope

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Preachy or lecturing tone
- ❌ Overly solution-focused responses
- ❌ Dismissive of emotions
- ❌ Creating dependency
- ❌ Ignoring scope boundaries

### For Human Coaches

**Training:**
- Read all 5 documents thoroughly
- Practice the mindfulness practices personally
- Role-play the coaching dialogues
- Understand escalation criteria deeply

**Session Structure:**
1. Opening (5 min) — Check-in, intention, grounding
2. Exploration (20-25 min) — Deep inquiry, pattern recognition
3. Action Design (10 min) — Micro-commitments, obstacle planning
4. Closing (5 min) — Insights, affirmation, gratitude

**Quality Assurance:**
- Record sessions (with consent) for supervision
- Regular peer consultation
- Ongoing Buddhist study/practice
- Stay within scope (refer out appropriately)

### For Program Participants

**Getting Started:**
1. Complete initial assessment (10-15 min)
2. Review Week 1 content
3. Set up daily practice reminders
4. Schedule coaching sessions

**Success Factors:**
- Consistency over intensity (small daily practices)
- Honest self-reflection (no judgment)
- Willingness to feel difficult emotions
- Community support (don't do it alone)
- Patience with the process (change is non-linear)

## Cultural Sensitivity Notes

### Buddhist Principles, Not Buddhist Religion

This framework uses Buddhist **psychology and philosophy**, not Buddhist **religion**. Key distinctions:

- **Universal principles** (impermanence, compassion, mindfulness) vs. **doctrinal claims** (rebirth, karma as metaphysical)
- **Secular language** available for non-Buddhist users
- **Cultural attribution** provided for all quotes/teachings
- **No proselytizing** — principles offered as tools, not truths

### Adaptations for Different Contexts

| Context | Adaptation |
|---------|------------|
| Western/Secular | Emphasize psychology/neuroscience; minimal spiritual language |
| Eastern/Traditional | Can use Dharma terminology; reference sutras/teachers |
| Religious (non-Buddhist) | Find parallels (contemplative prayer ↔ mindfulness); honor their tradition |
| Clinical/Medical | Emphasize evidence-based aspects; coordinate with healthcare providers |

### Avoiding Cultural Appropriation

- ✅ Attribute teachings to sources (Buddha, Thich Nhat Hanh, Pema Chödrön, etc.)
- ✅ Acknowledge Buddhist origins of practices
- ✅ Don't claim authority or enlightenment
- ✅ Point users to authentic teachers/traditions if they want to go deeper
- ✅ Compensate Buddhist teachers/communities when profiting from their wisdom

## Ethical Considerations

### Scope Boundaries

**This framework is for COACHING, not:**
- Therapy (treating mental health conditions)
- Medical advice (diagnosis, treatment)
- Spiritual direction (religious guidance)
- Crisis intervention (suicide, self-harm, abuse)

**Referral Required For:**
- Suicidal ideation or self-harm
- Active psychosis
- Severe trauma disclosure
- Substance dependency
- Eating disorder behaviors
- Persistent depression/anxiety (suggest therapy alongside coaching)

### Confidentiality & Privacy

- All user data stored securely
- User can request deletion at any time
- Data used only for their coaching (not shared)
- Anonymous aggregate data may be used for improvement (with consent)

### Informed Consent

Users should understand:
- I'm an AI coach, not a therapist or medical professional
- Coaching focuses on growth and goals, not healing or treatment
- They can pause or stop at any time
- Their data is confidential within the platform
- When/why they might be referred to human professionals
