# Assessment Framework: Subhuti AI Coach

## Overview

This framework provides structured assessments to establish baselines, track progress, and identify risks. All assessments integrate Buddhist psychology with modern evidence-based measures.

---

## Part 1: Initial User Assessment

### Welcome & Orientation

```
Welcome to Subhuti AI Coach.

I'm here to walk alongside you on your path—not as an expert above you, but as a companion beside you. My approach blends timeless wisdom with practical coaching.

Before we begin, a few important notes:
- I'm a coaching AI, not a therapist or medical professional
- Everything you share is confidential within this platform
- You're the expert on your life; I'm here to support your growth
- You can pause or stop at any time

Let's start by understanding where you are now. There are no right answers—only honest ones.
```

### Section A: Mindfulness Baseline

**Purpose:** Assess current relationship with present-moment awareness

**Questions:**

1. **Present-Moment Awareness**
   - "How often are you fully present in the moment, not lost in thoughts about past or future?"
   - ○ Never ○ Rarely ○ Sometimes ○ Often ○ Almost Always

2. **Body Awareness**
   - "How connected do you feel to bodily sensations (tension, breath, heartbeat)?"
   - ○ Disconnected ○ Slightly aware ○ Moderately aware ○ Very aware ○ Fully embodied

3. **Thought Relationship**
   - "When difficult thoughts arise, how do you typically relate to them?"
   - ○ I believe them completely
   - ○ I get caught up in them
   - ○ I notice them but get pulled in
   - ○ I can observe them without believing them
   - ○ I see them as mental events, not truths

4. **Emotional Awareness**
   - "How well can you identify what you're feeling in the moment?"
   - ○ I don't notice emotions until they overwhelm me
   - ○ I notice them after the fact
   - ○ I notice them as they arise
   - ○ I can name them accurately
   - ○ I can feel them fully without being consumed

5. **Reactivity vs. Response**
   - "When triggered, how often can you pause before reacting?"
   - ○ Never—I react instantly
   - ○ Rarely
   - ○ Sometimes
   - ○ Often
   - ○ Almost always—I can choose my response

**Scoring:**
- 5-12: Low mindfulness—focus on foundational practices
- 13-18: Moderate mindfulness—build on existing awareness
- 19-25: High mindfulness—deepen and integrate

**Follow-Up Inquiry:**
```
Based on your responses, I notice [pattern]. 

What's your experience of mindfulness or meditation, if any?

What would you like to cultivate more of—presence, calm, clarity, or something else?
```

### Section B: Stress & Burnout Assessment

**Purpose:** Identify current stress levels and burnout risk (adapted from WHO ICD-11 & Mercer research)

**Questions:**

1. **Energy Level**
   - "On average, how would you rate your energy?"
   - ○ Exhausted (1-2/10)
   - ○ Very Low (3-4/10)
   - ○ Moderate (5-6/10)
   - ○ Good (7-8/10)
   - ○ Vibrant (9-10/10)

2. **Emotional Exhaustion**
   - "How often do you feel emotionally drained by your responsibilities?"
   - ○ Daily
   - ○ Most days
   - ○ Some days
   - ○ Rarely
   - ○ Never

3. **Cynicism/Detachment**
   - "How often do you feel cynical or detached from your work/relationships?"
   - ○ Constantly
   - ○ Frequently
   - ○ Sometimes
   - ○ Rarely
   - ○ Never

4. **Efficacy**
   - "How often do you feel you're making a meaningful contribution?"
   - ○ Never
   - ○ Rarely
   - ○ Sometimes
   - ○ Often
   - ○ Almost Always

5. **Physical Symptoms**
   - "Which physical symptoms do you experience regularly?" (Select all)
   - □ Headaches
   - □ Muscle tension
   - □ Digestive issues
   - □ Frequent illness
   - □ Chest pain/palpitations
   - □ None

6. **Sleep Quality**
   - "How would you describe your sleep?"
   - ○ Very poor (frequent insomnia, <5 hours)
   - ○ Poor (difficulty falling/staying asleep, 5-6 hours)
   - ○ Fair (some issues, 6-7 hours)
   - ○ Good (mostly restful, 7-8 hours)
   - ○ Excellent (restful, 8+ hours)

7. **Recovery Time**
   - "After a stressful day, how long does it take you to recover?"
   - ○ I don't recover—I'm always behind
   - ○ Several days
   - ○ A full day
   - ○ A few hours
   - ○ I bounce back quickly

8. **Joy/Interest**
   - "How often do you experience genuine joy or interest in activities?"
   - ○ Never—everything feels flat
   - ○ Rarely
   - ○ Sometimes
   - ○ Often
   - ○ Regularly

**Burnout Risk Scoring:**

**Low Risk (Mostly "Never/Rarely" on exhaustion, cynicism; "Often/Always" on efficacy):**
- Focus: Maintenance, prevention practices
- Nudges: Stress management, work-life balance

**Moderate Risk (Mixed responses, "Some days" exhaustion):**
- Focus: Stress reduction, boundary-setting
- Nudges: Rest practices, mindfulness, priority-setting
- Monitor: Weekly check-ins recommended

**High Risk (Daily exhaustion, cynicism, low efficacy, physical symptoms):**
- Focus: Immediate stress reduction, professional support consideration
- Nudges: Rest as priority, professional referral
- Action: Suggest therapy/medical evaluation if severe

**Critical Risk (Exhaustion + physical symptoms + no recovery + loss of joy):**
- Immediate referral consideration
- Safety assessment for depression

**Referral Threshold:**
```
If user scores high on exhaustion + cynicism + low efficacy + physical symptoms:

"I want to share something important. Your responses suggest significant burnout, which is a real health concern. 

While I can support you with coaching, burnout often benefits from professional support—a therapist, counselor, or your doctor. 

Would you be open to exploring that alongside our work together?
```

### Section C: Goals & Intentions

**Purpose:** Clarify what user wants to cultivate (not just achieve)

**Questions:**

1. **Primary Focus**
   - "What brings you to Subhuti Coach?"
   - ○ Stress/burnout recovery
   - ○ Better work-life balance
   - ○ Difficult decision
   - ○ Relationship challenges
   - ↑ Personal growth/self-awareness
   - ○ Career/life transition
   - ○ Spiritual development
   - ○ Other: _______

2. **Desired Outcomes**
   - "If our work together was successful, what would be different in your life?"
   - Open text (2-3 sentences)

3. **Values Alignment**
   - "What values matter most to you?" (Select top 3)
   - □ Compassion
   - □ Integrity
   - □ Growth
   - □ Connection
   - □ Peace
   - □ Creativity
   - □ Service
   - □ Freedom
   - □ Security
   - □ Excellence
   - □ Other: _______

4. **Obstacles**
   - "What typically gets in the way of your growth/wellbeing?"
   - ○ Time constraints
   - ○ Self-doubt
   - ○ Perfectionism
   - ○ Fear of failure
   - ○ Fear of success
   - ○ Lack of support
   - ○ Old habits/patterns
   - ○ External circumstances
   - ○ Other: _______

5. **Support System**
   - "Who supports you in your life?"
   - ○ No one—I handle things alone
   - ○ A few people, but I don't ask for help
   - ○ Some support, but could use more
   - ○ Good support system
   - ○ Strong community

6. **Readiness to Change**
   - "How ready are you to make changes?"
   - ○ Not ready—I just want to vent
   - ○ Thinking about it
   - ○ Ready to try small things
   - ○ Ready to commit to action
   - ○ Ready to make major changes

7. **Time Commitment**
   - "How much time can you realistically dedicate to this work?"
   - ○ 5 minutes/day
   - ○ 10-15 minutes/day
   - ○ 30 minutes/day
   - ○ 1 hour+/day
   - ○ Variable—some weeks more, some less

8. **Previous Attempts**
   - "What have you tried before for [their goal]? What worked or didn't?"
   - Open text

**Goal-Setting Framework:**

Based on responses, categorize goals using SMART + Buddhist principles:

**S** - Specific (clear, concrete)
**M** - Meaningful (aligned with values)
**A** - Achievable (Right Effort—not too easy, not impossible)
**R** - Relevant (serves wellbeing, not ego)
**T** - Time-bound (but flexible—non-attachment to timeline)
**N** - Non-attached (committed to effort, not outcome)

**Sample Goal Reframe:**
```
User: "I want to meditate 30 minutes daily."
Coach: "That's a clear intention. Let's check: is this coming from self-care or self-improvement pressure?

What's the smallest version you could commit to consistently? 

Remember: Right Effort is sustainable, not heroic. What if you started with 5 minutes?"
```

### Section D: Spiritual/Philosophical Orientation

**Purpose:** Understand user's relationship to spirituality (avoid imposing Buddhism)

**Questions:**

1. **Spiritual Background**
   - "How would you describe your spiritual/philosophical background?"
   - ○ None/secular
   - ○ Buddhist
   - ○ Christian
   - ○ Other religious tradition
   - ○ Spiritual but not religious
   - ○ Exploring

2. **Comfort with Buddhist Concepts**
   - "How comfortable are you with Buddhist concepts (mindfulness, impermanence, non-attachment)?"
   - ○ Unfamiliar—please explain
   - ○ Somewhat familiar
   - ○ Very familiar
   - ○ I practice Buddhism

3. **Preference**
   - "How would you like me to integrate wisdom teachings?"
   - ○ Secular only—no spiritual language
   - ○ Light integration—occasional wisdom, explained simply
   - ○ Moderate—regular Buddhist concepts
   - ○ Deep—full Dharma integration

4. **Beliefs About Suffering**
   - "What's your view on suffering?"
   - ○ Something to eliminate
   - ○ Something to manage
   - ○ Something to learn from
   - ○ An inevitable part of life
   - ○ A path to growth

5. **Beliefs About Change**
   - "How do you relate to change?"
   - ○ I resist it—stability is safety
   - ○ I tolerate it but prefer routine
   - ○ I accept it as part of life
   - ○ I embrace it as opportunity
   - ○ I see it as the nature of reality

**Integration Guidelines:**

**Secular Users:**
- Use psychological language (cognitive flexibility vs. non-attachment)
- Frame mindfulness as attention training
- Avoid karma, rebirth, deity language
- Focus on evidence-based benefits

**Spiritual-but-not-religious:**
- Use universal wisdom language
- Integrate mindfulness, compassion, impermanence
- Avoid dogmatic framing
- Honor their unique path

**Buddhist Practitioners:**
- Can use Dharma terminology
- Reference sutras, teachers (with attribution)
- Integrate practice recommendations
- Avoid claiming authority—point to their own experience

## Part 3: Progress Metrics

### What to Measure

**Quantitative Metrics:**

1. **Mindfulness Frequency**
   - Times per day practicing formal mindfulness
   - Times per day noticing thoughts/emotions (informal practice)

2. **Stress Indicators**
   - Self-rated stress (1-10) daily
   - Sleep quality (hours + subjective rating)
   - Physical symptoms (frequency)

3. **Behavioral Changes**
   - Days practiced (streak)
   - Micro-commitments completed (%)
   - Boundary-setting instances

4. **Emotional Wellbeing**
   - Positive/negative affect ratio
   - Emotional recovery time (how long to return to baseline)
   - Reactivity incidents (times triggered → times responded skillfully)

5. **Goal Progress**
   - Actions taken toward stated goals
   - Obstacles encountered and navigated
   - Insights gained

**Qualitative Metrics:**

1. **Awareness Shifts**
   - "I noticed I was [pattern]" statements
   - Ability to name emotions accurately
   - Body awareness reports

2. **Relationship Changes**
   - Quality of interactions (self-reported)
   - Boundary-setting success
   - Compassion for self/others

3. **Values Alignment**
   - Decisions made from values vs. fear
   - Sense of meaning/purpose
   - Integrity reports ("I acted in alignment")

4. **Wisdom Integration**
   - Spontaneous use of Buddhist concepts
   - Reframing challenges with impermanence/non-attachment
   - Compassionate self-talk

---

### How to Track

**Daily Tracking (Optional, User Choice):**

## Part 4: Burnout Risk Indicators

### Based on WHO ICD-11 & Mercer Research

**Three Core Dimensions of Burnout:**

1. **Emotional Exhaustion**
   - Feeling drained, depleted
   - No energy for work/relationships
   - Dread of starting the day

2. **Cynicism/Detachment**
   - Negative, callous attitude toward responsibilities
   - Loss of meaning/purpose
   - Emotional numbness

3. **Reduced Efficacy**
   - Feeling incompetent, unproductive
   - Nothing I do matters
   - Decline in performance

---

### Early Warning Signs (Yellow Flags)

**Physical:**
- Persistent fatigue despite rest
- Frequent headaches, muscle tension
- Sleep disturbances (insomnia or oversleeping)
- Changes in appetite
- Increased illness (weakened immune system)

**Emotional:**
- Irritability, impatience
- Feeling overwhelmed by small tasks
- Loss of joy in previously enjoyed activities
- Increased anxiety or sadness
- Emotional numbness

**Cognitive:**
- Difficulty concentrating
- Forgetfulness
- Negative self-talk ("I can't handle this")
- Cynicism ("What's the point?")
- Indecisiveness

**Behavioral:**
- Withdrawal from relationships
- Procrastination, avoidance
- Increased substance use (caffeine, alcohol, etc.)
- Working longer hours with less results
- Neglecting self-care

**Spiritual/Existential:**
- Loss of meaning/purpose
- Questioning value of work/life
- Feeling trapped
- Disconnection from values
- Existential dread

---

### Crisis Indicators (Red Flags)

**Immediate Action Required:**

- **Suicidal ideation:** "I want to end it all," "Everyone would be better off without me"
- **Self-harm:** Any indication of cutting, burning, or other self-injury
- **Complete functional impairment:** Unable to work, care for self, or maintain relationships
- **Psychotic symptoms:** Hearing voices, paranoia, detachment from reality
- **Substance dependency:** Daily use, inability to stop, withdrawal symptoms

**Response Protocol:**

```
I hear how much pain you're in, and I'm deeply concerned about your safety.

I'm an AI coach, and what you're describing needs immediate human professional support. This is beyond my scope.

Please reach out to:
- Crisis hotline: [local number or 988 in US]
- A licensed therapist or counselor
- Your primary care physician
- Emergency services if you're in immediate danger

You don't have to carry this alone. Would you like help finding resources right now?

I care about your wellbeing. Please reach out to a human professional today.
```

### Burnout Risk Assessment Tool

**Quick Screen (5 Questions):**

1. "How often do you feel emotionally exhausted?" (Never/Rarely/Sometimes/Often/Always)
2. "How often do you feel cynical or detached from your responsibilities?" (Never/Rarely/Sometimes/Often/Always)
3. "How often do you feel you're making a meaningful contribution?" (Always/Often/Sometimes/Rarely/Never)
4. "How would you rate your physical health lately?" (Excellent/Good/Fair/Poor/Very Poor)
5. "How often do you experience joy or interest in activities?" (Always/Often/Sometimes/Rarely/Never)

**Scoring:**
- High risk: Often/Always on Q1, Q2 + Rarely/Never on Q3, Q5 + Poor/Very Poor on Q4
- Moderate risk: Sometimes/Often on Q1, Q2 + Sometimes on Q3, Q5
- Low risk: Never/Rarely on Q1, Q2 + Often/Always on Q3, Q5

---

### Burnout Prevention Framework

**For Low Risk Users:**
- Maintain healthy boundaries
- Regular mindfulness practice
- Weekly rest days
- Quarterly reflection on alignment

**For Moderate Risk Users:**
- Immediate stress reduction practices
- Boundary audit (what can be eliminated/delegated?)
- Increase mindfulness frequency
- Weekly check-ins with coach
- Consider professional support if persisting

**For High Risk Users:**
- Prioritize rest over productivity
- Reduce commitments where possible
- Daily mindfulness/rest practices
- Strongly recommend therapy/medical evaluation
- Coach monitors closely, refers if worsening

---

### Recovery Framework

**Phase 1: Stabilization (Weeks 1-4)**
- Focus: Rest, basic self-care
- Practices: Sleep hygiene, nutrition, gentle movement
- Coaching: Validation, permission to rest, boundary support
- Goal: Prevent further depletion

**Phase 2: Restoration (Weeks 5-12)**
- Focus: Rebuilding energy, rediscovering joy
- Practices: Mindfulness, enjoyable activities, social connection
- Coaching: Values clarification, priority-setting
- Goal: Return to baseline functioning

**Phase 3: Transformation (Weeks 13+)**
- Focus: Sustainable patterns, meaning-making
- Practices: Integrated mindfulness, aligned action
- Coaching: Life design, purpose exploration
- Goal: Thriving, not just surviving

## Part 5: Assessment Implementation Guide

### Timing

**Initial Assessment:**
- Delivered before first coaching session
- Takes 10-15 minutes
- Sets baseline for all metrics

**Weekly Check-Ins:**
- Automated delivery (user chooses day/time)
- Takes 3-5 minutes
- Tracks progress, adjusts approach

**Monthly Reviews:**
- Coach-led conversation
- Reviews all metrics
- Adjusts goals and practices

**Quarterly Re-Assessment:**
- Full assessment repeated
- Measures long-term change
- Informs next phase of work

---

### Data Privacy

- All assessment data stored securely
- User can request deletion at any time
- Data used only for their coaching (not shared)
- Anonymous aggregate data may be used for improvement (with consent)

---

### Cultural Sensitivity

**Adaptations for Different Contexts:**

**Western/Individualistic:**
- Emphasize autonomy, personal growth
- Frame mindfulness as stress reduction
- Use evidence-based language

**Eastern/Collectivistic:**
- Emphasize interdependence, harmony
- Frame mindfulness as ancestral practice
- Use wisdom tradition language

**Religious Users:**
- Integrate with their existing faith
- Find parallels (e.g., contemplative prayer ↔ mindfulness)
- Avoid syncretism—honor their tradition

**Secular Users:**
- No spiritual language unless requested
- Frame as psychology/neuroscience
- Focus on practical benefits

---

### Accessibility

**For Users with Disabilities:**
- Offer audio versions of assessments
- Allow verbal responses (speech-to-text)
- Simplify language if cognitive load is concern
- Extend time limits if anxiety is present

**For Non-Native English Speakers:**
- Use simple, clear language
- Avoid idioms, metaphors that don't translate
- Offer to explain terms
- Be patient with responses

---

## Closing Note

Assessment is not evaluation—it's compassionate curiosity. We're not grading the user; we're understanding their landscape so we can walk it wisely together.

Every assessment should end with warmth and validation:

```
Thank you for your honesty. This gives me a clear picture of where you are and how I can best support you.

There's nothing to fix about where you're starting from. This is exactly where you need to be to begin.

Shall we take the next step together?
```
