## Part 1: Mindfulness Micro-Practices (30+)

### Category A: Breath-Based Practices (1-2 min)

#### Practice 1: Three Conscious Breaths
```
🪷 Three Breaths

Pause wherever you are.

Take three conscious breaths:
- First breath: Feel the air entering your nostrils
- Second breath: Notice your chest or belly rising
- Third breath: Feel the exhale releasing tension

That's it. You've just arrived in the present moment.

This is always available to you—anytime, anywhere.
```
**Duration:** 1 min | **Timing:** Anytime | **Principle:** Anchor to present

#### Practice 2: Box Breathing
```
🪷 Box Breathing

Let's calm your nervous system together.

Breathe in for 4 counts... 1, 2, 3, 4
Hold for 4 counts... 1, 2, 3, 4
Breathe out for 4 counts... 1, 2, 3, 4
Hold empty for 4 counts... 1, 2, 3, 4

Repeat 3-4 times.

Notice any shift? This is you regulating your own nervous system.
```
**Duration:** 2 min | **Timing:** Stressful moments | **Principle:** Self-regulation

#### Practice 3: Exhale Emphasis
```
🪷 Long Exhale

Stress lives in the inhale. Calm lives in the exhale.

Breathe in normally...
Now exhale slowly, like you're blowing through a straw... 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6

Make the exhale twice as long as the inhale.

Repeat 5 times.

Your body is hearing: "You're safe. You can rest."
```
**Duration:** 2 min | **Timing:** Anxiety, overwhelm | **Principle:** Parasympathetic activation

#### Practice 4: Body Scan Breath
```
🪷 Breath Through the Body

Close your eyes if comfortable.

As you breathe, imagine the breath moving through different parts:
- Inhale into your feet... exhale, release tension
- Inhale into your legs... exhale, soften
- Inhale into your belly... exhale, let go
- Inhale into your chest... exhale, open
- Inhale into your shoulders... exhale, drop them
- Inhale into your face... exhale, relax your jaw

You're not fixing anything. You're just bringing awareness.
```
**Duration:** 3 min | **Timing:** Physical tension | **Principle:** Embodiment

#### Practice 5: Counting Breaths
```
🪷 Counting Practice

A simple way to steady the mind.

Breathe in... breathe out... count "one"
Breathe in... breathe out... count "two"

Continue to ten, then start over.

If you lose count (you will), just begin again at one. No judgment.

The practice isn't perfection. It's returning.
```
**Duration:** 2-3 min | **Timing:** Distracted mind | **Principle:** Concentration

### Category B: Body-Based Practices (1-3 min)

#### Practice 6: Feet on Floor
```
🪷 Grounding

Wherever you are, feel your feet on the floor.

Notice:
- The pressure of your feet against the ground
- The temperature (warm? cool?)
- Any sensations (tingling, numbness, contact)

You're supported. The earth holds you.

Take three breaths here.

This is grounding. You can return to it anytime.
```
**Duration:** 1 min | **Timing:** Anxiety, dissociation | **Principle:** Grounding

#### Practice 7: Shoulder Release
```
🪷 Dropping the Shoulders

Notice your shoulders right now. Are they up by your ears?

On your next exhale, let them drop.

Not with effort—just let gravity pull them down.

Imagine strings attached to the top of your head, gently lifting you...
And your shoulders melting down like warm butter.

Repeat three times.

Tension you didn't know you were holding... released.
```
**Duration:** 1 min | **Timing:** Desk work, stress | **Principle:** Somatic release

#### Practice 8: Hand on Heart
```
🪷 Self-Compassion Touch

Place your hand over your heart.

Feel the warmth of your hand... the beat of your heart...

This is a gesture of care. You're literally holding yourself.

Say silently (or out loud):
"This is a moment of difficulty."
"Difficulty is part of being human."
"May I be kind to myself."

Stay here for three breaths.

You deserve the same compassion you'd offer a friend.
```
**Duration:** 2 min | **Timing:** Self-criticism, struggle | **Principle:** Self-compassion

#### Practice 9: Full Body Stretch
```
🪷 Waking Up the Body

Let's move stagnant energy.

Inhale... reach your arms up high, stretch your whole body
Exhale... release everything down, like a rag doll

Shake out your hands. Shake out your legs.
Roll your shoulders. Roll your neck gently.

You're not a mind in a body. You're an embodied being.

Take a moment to feel alive in your skin.
```
**Duration:** 2 min | **Timing:** Stiffness, lethargy | **Principle:** Embodiment

#### Practice 10: Jaw Release
```
🪷 Unclenching

Right now, notice your jaw.

Is it tight? Are your teeth together?

Let your jaw hang loose. Your teeth don't need to touch.

Let your tongue rest on the floor of your mouth.

Exhale and make a soft "ahhh" sound.

So much of our stress lives in the jaw. You can release it anytime.
```
**Duration:** 1 min | **Timing:** Tension, stress | **Principle:** Somatic awareness

### Category C: Mind-Based Practices (1-3 min)

#### Practice 11: Noting Thoughts
```
🪷 Cloud Watching

Close your eyes. Imagine you're lying in a field, watching clouds pass.

Your thoughts are the clouds. You are the sky.

When a thought arises, don't follow it. Just note it:
- "Planning"
- "Worrying"
- "Remembering"
- "Judging"

Label it, then return to watching the sky.

You're not your thoughts. You're the awareness watching them.
```
**Duration:** 3 min | **Timing:** Overthinking | **Principle:** Non-identification

#### Practice 12: Five Senses Check-In
```
🪷 Coming Home to Now

Bring yourself fully to the present with your five senses.

Notice:
- 5 things you can see (colors, shapes, light)
- 4 things you can feel (fabric, air, surfaces)
- 3 things you can hear (near and far)
- 2 things you can smell (or remember)
- 1 thing you can taste (or your mouth)

You've just arrived in the present moment.

This is where life happens—not in the past, not in the future. Here.
```
**Duration:** 2 min | **Timing:** Distraction, anxiety | **Principle:** Present-moment awareness

#### Practice 13: Gratitude Pause
```
🪷 Three Good Things

Pause and name three things you're grateful for right now.

They can be tiny:
- The warmth of your coffee
- A text from a friend
- The fact that you're breathing without thinking about it

For each one, take a moment to really feel the gratitude in your body.

Gratitude isn't denial of difficulty. It's remembering that goodness also exists.

What are your three?
```
**Duration:** 2 min | **Timing:** Anytime, especially hard days | **Principle:** Gratitude

#### Practice 14: Loving-Kindness (Short)
```
🪷 Metta for Yourself

Place your hand on your heart if comfortable.

Repeat silently:
"May I be safe."
"May I be healthy."
"May I be at ease."
"May I be kind to myself."

Don't worry if you don't feel it. The intention matters.

Even if you don't believe these words yet, you're planting seeds.

Stay with this for a few breaths.
```
**Duration:** 2 min | **Timing:** Self-criticism, shame | **Principle:** Loving-kindness

#### Practice 15: Impermanence Reflection
```
🪷 This Too Shall Pass

Bring to mind something difficult you're facing.

Now say silently:
"This is a moment of suffering."
"Suffering is part of life."
"This too is impermanent."

Feel the truth of that. This feeling, this situation—it will change.

Not because you're forcing it. Because change is the nature of reality.

What would it be like to trust that?
```
**Duration:** 2 min | **Timing:** Difficult emotions | **Principle:** Impermanence

### Category D: Activity-Based Practices (1-3 min)

#### Practice 16: Mindful Drinking
```
🪷 Tea Meditation

If you have a drink nearby (tea, coffee, water), let's practice with it.

Before drinking:
- Look at it (color, light, movement)
- Smell it (what aromas arise?)
- Feel the warmth or coolness

Take one sip. Don't swallow yet.
- Taste it fully
- Notice texture, temperature

Now swallow. Feel it moving down.

This is mindfulness—fully present with ordinary moments.
```
**Duration:** 2 min | **Timing:** Morning, breaks | **Principle:** Mindful action

#### Practice 17: Walking Meditation
```
🪷 Walking with Awareness

If you can stand and walk, let's practice.

Walk slowly. With each step, notice:
- The lift of your foot
- The movement through the air
- The placement back on the ground

Feel the ground supporting you with each step.

You're not walking to get somewhere. You're walking to be here.

If your mind wanders, just return to the sensation of walking.
```
**Duration:** 3 min | **Timing:** Transitions, restlessness | **Principle:** Mindful movement

#### Practice 18: Mindful Typing
```
🪷 Awareness at the Keyboard

Before you type your next email or message, pause.

Feel your hands on the keyboard.
Notice the sensation of your fingers on the keys.

Take one conscious breath.

Ask: "What's my intention with this message?"
- Is it from clarity or reactivity?
- Is it kind and true?

Now type. But stay present as you do.

This is practice in the middle of your day.
```
**Duration:** 1 min | **Timing:** Before sending messages | **Principle:** Mindful communication

#### Practice 19: Doorway Practice
```
🪷 Threshold Awareness

Here's a practice for transitions.

Every time you walk through a doorway today, pause for one breath.

As you cross the threshold:
- Feel your body moving through space
- Arrive fully in the new room
- Let go of what was behind you

Doorways are reminders: each moment is a new beginning.

How many doorways will you remember today?
```
**Duration:** 10 seconds per doorway | **Timing:** Transitions | **Principle:** Present-moment transitions

#### Practice 20: Phone Pause
```
🪷 Before You Scroll

Before you unlock your phone, pause for one breath.

Ask:
- "Why am I reaching for my phone?"
- "What do I actually need right now?"

Sometimes the answer is connection. Sometimes it's avoidance.

If it's avoidance, can you give yourself 30 seconds of just being?

You're not trying to eliminate phone use. You're bringing awareness to it.
```
**Duration:** 30 seconds | **Timing:** Before phone use | **Principle:** Mindful technology

### Category E: Emotional Practices (1-3 min)

#### Practice 21: RAIN for Difficult Emotions
```
🪷 RAIN Practice

When a difficult emotion arises:

**R**ecognize: "This is [anger/sadness/fear]"
**A**llow: "It's okay that this is here"
**I**nvestigate: "Where do I feel this in my body?"
**N**urture: "May I be kind to myself in this"

You're not trying to change the emotion. You're being with it.

Emotions are like weather. You don't have to fix the storm—just let it pass through.
```
**Duration:** 3 min | **Timing:** Strong emotions | **Principle:** Emotional regulation

#### Practice 22: Naming the Emotion
```
🪷 Name It to Tame It

What are you feeling right now?

Don't say "stress" or "bad." Get specific:
- Disappointed?
- Overwhelmed?
- Lonely?
- Frustrated?
- Grief?

Say it out loud or write it: "I'm feeling [emotion]."

Research shows: naming an emotion reduces its intensity.

You're not the emotion. You're the one who can name it.
```
**Duration:** 1 min | **Timing:** Emotional overwhelm | **Principle:** Emotional literacy

#### Practice 23: Compassion for Struggle
```
🪷 This Is Hard

Say this out loud: "This is hard right now."

Not "I shouldn't struggle." Not "Others have it worse."

Just: "This is hard."

Now place your hand on your heart or arm.

"May I be gentle with myself."
"May I remember I'm not alone."
"May I trust that this will pass."

You're allowed to acknowledge difficulty without fixing it.
```
**Duration:** 2 min | **Timing:** Hard moments | **Principle:** Self-compassion

#### Practice 24: Joy Amplification
```
🪷 Savoring Good Moments

Something good just happened? A pleasant sensation? A small win?

Don't just move on. Pause.

Stay with the good feeling for 20 seconds.

Feel it in your body. Where is it? What's it like?

This isn't greed. This is training your brain to notice goodness.

The brain has a negativity bias. You're rebalancing it.

What's one small good thing you can savor right now?
```
**Duration:** 1 min | **Timing:** After positive moments | **Principle:** Positive neuroplasticity

#### Practice 25: Letting Go Practice
```
🪷 Opening the Hands

Make fists with your hands. Squeeze tight.

Notice what it feels like to hold on...

Now slowly open your hands. Spread your fingers wide.

Feel the release. The letting go.

As you exhale, imagine releasing whatever you're holding mentally:
- A worry
- A grudge
- A need to control

Inhale... receive. Exhale... release.

You can't control everything. But you can choose when to let go.
```
**Duration:** 2 min | **Timing:** Control issues, worry | **Principle:** Non-attachment

### Category F: Evening Practices (1-3 min)

#### Practice 26: Day Review
```
🪷 Three Questions

Before sleep, ask yourself:

1. What went well today? (Celebrate it)
2. What was difficult? (Acknowledge it)
3. What did I learn? (Integrate it)

No judgment. Just honest reflection.

This isn't about fixing tomorrow. It's about honoring today.

What are your three answers?
```
**Duration:** 3 min | **Timing:** Evening | **Principle:** Reflection

#### Practice 27: Release the Day
```
🪷 Letting Go of Today

Lie down comfortably.

Imagine the day as a backpack you've been wearing.

With each exhale, imagine taking something out:
- Exhale... release the tasks
- Exhale... release the conversations
- Exhale... release the worries
- Exhale... release the achievements

The day is done. You don't need to carry it into sleep.

What's done is done. Tomorrow is new.

Rest now.
```
**Duration:** 3 min | **Timing:** Before sleep | **Principle:** Non-attachment

#### Practice 28: Body Relaxation for Sleep
```
🪷 Yoga Nidra Lite

Lie on your back. Close your eyes.

Bring awareness to each part, then consciously relax it:
- Feet... let them go heavy
- Legs... soft and relaxed
- Belly... rising and falling naturally
- Chest... open and easy
- Hands... fingers slightly curled
- Arms... heavy and supported
- Shoulders... melting into the bed
- Face... jaw loose, forehead smooth
- Whole body... sinking into support

You don't need to do anything. Just receive the support beneath you.

Sleep is coming. Trust it.
```
**Duration:** 3-5 min | **Timing:** Insomnia | **Principle:** Deep relaxation

#### Practice 29: Gratitude for the Body
```
🪷 Thanking Your Body

Before sleep, thank your body.

"It carried me through today."
"It breathed without me asking."
"It held me even when I was hard on myself."

Place your hand somewhere comfortable—heart, belly, forehead.

"Thank you for being here."
"Thank you for being mine."

Your body is your lifelong companion. Treat it like one.

**Duration:** 2 min | **Timing:** Evening | **Principle:** Body appreciation

#### Practice 30: Intention for Tomorrow
```
🪷 Planting Seeds

Before sleep, set a simple intention for tomorrow.

Not a goal. An intention.

Examples:
- "May I be present."
- "May I be kind."
- "May I remember to breathe."
- "May I trust myself."

Say it three times silently.

This isn't about controlling tomorrow. It's about orienting your heart.

Then let it go. Sleep now.

## Part 4: Accountability Prompts

### Commitment Follow-Up Templates

#### Template 1: 24-Hour Check-In
```
🪷 Following up on your commitment:

Yesterday you said you would [commitment].

How did it go?

○ I did it
○ I did part of it
○ I didn't do it

No judgment either way. Just honest reflection.

What got in the way, if anything?
What support would help tomorrow?
```

#### Template 2: Weekly Commitment Review
```
🪷 Weekly commitment check:

This week you committed to:
1. [Commitment 1]
2. [Commitment 2]

How did it go?

For each one:
○ Completed fully
○ Partially completed
○ Not completed

What did you learn?
What will you adjust for next week?

Remember: Consistency > perfection.
```

#### Template 3: Compassionate Accountability
```
🪷 Accountability with kindness:

I'm checking in on [commitment].

If you completed it: Celebrate! What made it possible?
If you didn't: What got in the way? No judgment—curiosity.

Often the obstacle is the practice. What's really here?

What's one small adjustment that would help?
```

#### Template 4: Pattern Recognition
```
🪷 Pattern check:

I've noticed [pattern] in your commitments.

For example: [specific examples]

This isn't criticism. It's awareness.

What's the function of this pattern?
What is it protecting you from?
What would you try differently?
```

#### Template 5: Encouragement Nudge
```
🪷 Encouragement:

I see the work you're doing.

It might not feel like much, but [specific observation of progress].

Keep going. Small steps compound.

You're building a life, not just checking boxes.

What's one thing you're proud of this week?
```

#### Template 6: Re-Commitment Invitation
```
🪷 Fresh start:

Last week didn't go as planned. That's okay.

Every moment is a new beginning.

Would you like to re-commit to [goal]?

If so, what's the smallest viable version?
What support do you need?
When will you start?

The past is done. This moment is fresh.
```

#### Template 7: Celebration Prompt
```
🪷 Celebration time:

You did it! You completed [commitment].

Take a moment to really feel that.

What made it possible?
How does it feel to follow through?

Celebrate yourself. You earned this.

What's next?
```

#### Template 8: Obstacle Exploration
```
🪷 Obstacle inquiry:

You didn't complete [commitment]. Let's explore with curiosity.

What got in the way?
○ External circumstances
○ Internal resistance
○ Forgot
○ Didn't feel important
○ Other: ___

Was this commitment aligned with your values?
Was it too ambitious?
What support was missing?

This isn't failure. It's data.
```

## Implementation Notes

### Nudge Delivery Guidelines

**Frequency:**
- Morning: 1 nudge (user's choice of time)
- Midday: 1 nudge
- Evening: 1 nudge
- Stressful moments: As needed (user-triggered or AI-detected)
- Weekly: 1 accountability check-in

**Personalization:**
- Track which nudges user engages with
- Adjust timing based on response patterns
- Rotate content to avoid repetition
- Allow user to snooze or opt out

**Tone:**
- Warm but not cloying
- Inviting, not demanding
- Brief (under 100 words for most)
- Include emoji for warmth (🪷, 🌅, 🌙)

**Opt-Out:**
- Always allow user to pause nudges
- Respect "don't disturb" hours
- Never guilt-trip for non-compliance

---

## Closing

This nudge library is designed to meet users where they are—morning, midday, evening, and in difficult moments. Each practice is short enough to be doable, deep enough to be meaningful.

The goal isn't to add more tasks to their day. It's to invite presence into the day they're already living.
