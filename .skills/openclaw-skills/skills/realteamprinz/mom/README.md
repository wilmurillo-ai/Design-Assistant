## What is mom.skill?

Your second brain for parenting. It remembers what you can't — when she last ate, what worked at 2am last Tuesday, which food caused a rash, why she always melts down at 5pm.

You feed it observations. It finds the patterns. At 3am when you can't think, it thinks for you.

## The Soothing Playbook

```
Soothing Playbook for Emma (3 months)

1. Bouncing on yoga ball     — 85% (34/40 times)
2. White noise (dryer sound) — 78% (28/36 times)
3. Driving in car            — 95% ⚠️ not practical at 3am
4. Nursing                   — 70% (depends on hunger)
5. Dad walking + humming     — 65% (better after 6pm)
```

No book has this list. Only your data does.

## Key Features

**Cry Decoder** — "Short cries + rooting = hungry (87% accurate)"

**Feeding Tracker** — times, amounts, new food reactions, allergy flags for pediatrician

**Sleep Intelligence** — YOUR baby's actual wake windows, not the textbook ones

**Night Shift Log** — "Last night: up at 12:30, 3:45, 6:00. Dad handled the 3:45."

**Multi-Caregiver Sync** — grandma, babysitter, everyone gets the same answers

**Growth Memory** — "She smiled for the first time looking at the ceiling fan"

## 3am Mode

Questions between midnight and 6am get a different response: **short, warm, no lectures.**

```
You (3am):  "She won't stop crying"

Skill:      Last feed 4h ago — probably hungry. Try feeding.
            If not, white noise has 78% success at this hour.
            You're doing great. 💛
```

## Privacy & Data

- **All data stored locally** at `~/.mom-skill/babies/`
- **No cloud sync.** No baby monitors. No health app connections.
- **No external transmission.** Your baby's data never leaves your device.
- **Delete anytime.** Remove the folder.
- **Not medical advice.** Never. Ever.
