# Soothing Techniques Reference

Common soothing methods to try and track. Every baby is different — the playbook learns which ones work for YOURS.

## Physical Movement
- **Bouncing on yoga ball** — rhythmic, adjustable intensity
- **Walking while holding** — motion + body contact
- **Car ride** — vibration + white noise combo
- **Stroller walk** — fresh air + motion
- **Rocking chair** — gentle, sustainable for long sessions
- **Baby swing** — hands-free option

## Sound
- **White noise (fan/dryer)** — continuous background
- **Shushing** — rhythmic, close to ear
- **Singing** — specific songs may work better than others
- **Heartbeat sounds** — mimics womb
- **Vacuum cleaner** — some babies love it

## Touch
- **Swaddling** — arms in vs arms out preference varies
- **Skin-to-skin** — chest contact
- **Gentle patting** — rhythmic, on back or bottom
- **Massage** — especially for gas (bicycle legs, tummy circles)

## Feeding-Related
- **Nursing/bottle** — hunger is the #1 cause of crying
- **Pacifier** — non-nutritive sucking soothes some babies

## Environment
- **Dim lights** — less stimulation
- **Fresh air** — step outside briefly
- **Bath** — warm water calms some babies
- **Change of scenery** — sometimes that's all it takes

## The Most Important One
- **Nothing works and that's okay** — sometimes babies cry. Witching hour (5-8pm), purple crying, overtiredness. It's not your fault. Put baby in a safe place, take 2 minutes to breathe, come back.

---

*Track what works for YOUR baby. After 2 weeks of logging, your personalized playbook will be more useful than any list.*
