# colleague.skill 👔

**Your colleagues, distilled.**

Their work style. Their thinking patterns. Their institutional knowledge. When they leave, what they contributed stays.

---

## What is colleague.skill?

colleague.skill captures and preserves the nuances of how your colleagues work — their communication preferences, decision-making patterns, problem-solving approaches, and the institutional knowledge they carry. Built as an OpenClaw skill, it helps you understand your team better while they're present and maintains that understanding after they move on.

Every colleague holds unique value: the undocumented processes they've built, the relationships they maintain, the historical context they carry. When people leave, that knowledge often disappears with them. colleague.skill ensures it doesn't.

---

## How It Works

### 1. Describe
Share what you know about a colleague — their role, your interactions, observed behaviors, or specific situations you've encountered together.

### 2. Learn
The skill extracts patterns across multiple dimensions: communication style, decision-making approach, collaboration preferences, and institutional knowledge. It builds a structured profile that deepens with every interaction.

### 3. Query
Ask questions about your colleagues:
- "How should I approach this conversation with Sarah?"
- "What's the best way to work with Marcus on this?"
- "Who on the team handles vendor relationships?"

### 4. Preserve
When colleagues leave, their profiles become institutional memory. Their thinking patterns, preferences, and knowledge remain available for future reference.

## Active Mode vs After They Leave Mode

### Active Mode (Colleague Present)

When colleagues are still part of the team, use their profile for:

**Meeting Preparation**
```
How should I approach asking Sarah to change the API design?
What does Marcus need to see before he can approve this?
```

**Behavior Decoding**
```
She's been declining all meetings this week, what usually causes that?
Why does he always push back on feature requests in standup?
```

**Conflict Navigation**
```
We disagree on the implementation approach, how do I find common ground?
What triggers tensions between these two?
```

**Onboarding Support**
```
What does the new developer need to know about working with the team?
How do I explain our code review process to someone new?
```

### After They Leave Mode

When colleagues depart, their profiles become critical institutional memory:

**Decision Support**
```
How would David have approached this technical debt problem?
What factors would Sarah weigh in this vendor evaluation?
```

**Historical Context**
```
Why did we choose PostgreSQL over MySQL?
Who made the call on the microservices migration?
```

**Knowledge Transfer**
```
Sarah owned the client onboarding process, what should we know?
Who handled the relationship with our primary vendor?
```

**Continuity Planning**
```
What institutional knowledge did Marcus carry?
How do we rebuild the context for this project?
```

## Privacy & Data

- **All data stored locally** on your machine at `~/.colleague-skill/`
- **No cloud sync.** No external transmission. Zero data leaves your device.
- **No surveillance.** This skill only records YOUR observations — it never accesses colleagues' emails, messages, or accounts.
- **You control everything.** Delete any profile anytime by removing its folder.
- **Plain text files.** Human-readable Markdown and JSONL. No vendor lock-in.
