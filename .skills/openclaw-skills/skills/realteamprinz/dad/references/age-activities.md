# Age-Appropriate Activities Reference

## 0-3 Months (Newborn)
- Skin-to-skin chest time
- Talking and singing (she hears your voice differently than mom's)
- Gentle rocking and walking
- Black and white high-contrast cards
- Tummy time (supervised, short sessions)

## 3-6 Months
- Reading board books (high contrast, simple)
- Peek-a-boo (usually gets first laughs around 4 months)
- Rattles and grasping toys
- Supported sitting and bouncing on your lap
- Mirror play (they love faces)

## 6-9 Months
- Stacking and knocking down (they love the knocking down part)
- Texture exploration (safe foods, fabrics, grass)
- Music and rhythm — clapping, simple instruments
- Crawling games (chase, obstacle courses with pillows)
- Water play (supervised, shallow)

## 9-12 Months
- Ball rolling back and forth
- Simple cause-and-effect toys
- Walking practice (holding hands)
- Outdoor exploration (parks, nature)
- Animal sounds and pointing games

## 1-2 Years
- Running and chasing
- Drawing with crayons
- Building with blocks
- Sand and water play
- Dancing to music
- Simple pretend play

## 2-3 Years
- Riding toys (balance bikes, tricycles)
- Puzzles (3-6 pieces)
- Imaginative play (kitchen, tools, dolls)
- Playground (climbing, slides)
- Helping with simple tasks ("can you bring me the blue cup?")

---

*Track what YOUR child responds to. The best activity is the one that makes them light up.*
