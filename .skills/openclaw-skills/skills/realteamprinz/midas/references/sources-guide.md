# Sources Guide

The hierarchy and value of different information sources for wealth signal extraction.

---

## Source Hierarchy

Not all inputs are equal. Some sources provide higher-density signals than others. This guide ranks sources by signal quality and explains what each excels at finding.

### Tier 1: Highest Signal Density

These sources provide the richest, most actionable wealth signals:

| Source | Why It's Gold | Best For |
|--------|---------------|----------|
| **Repeated complaints (2+ times)** | Repetition = unmet demand | Demand gaps, business ideas |
| **Purchase receipts/orders** | Direct money flow data | Arbitrage, spending patterns |
| **"Anyone know a..." requests** | Immediate unmet need | Service opportunities, referrals |
| **Workaround descriptions** | Inefficient existing solutions | Productization, automation |
| **Budget discussions** | Spending authority signals | B2B opportunities, vendor niches |

### Tier 2: High Signal Density

Strong sources with good signal quality:

| Source | Why It's Valuable | Best For |
|--------|-------------------|----------|
| **Slack/Teams work chats** | Professional pain points | Internal tools, B2B services |
| **Text/WhatsApp group chats** | Social proof of demand | Local services, consumer products |
| **Browsing history (research)** | Pre-purchase intent signals | Market entry, comparison services |
| **YouTube/watch history** | Skill accumulation patterns | Course creation, consulting |
| **Photo documentation** | Environmental intelligence | Real estate, local businesses |

### Tier 3: Moderate Signal Density

Useful but requires more processing:

| Source | Signal Type | Processing Needed |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Social media feeds** | Interest patterns | Algorithmic filtering |
| **Email newsletters** | Industry awareness | Topic extraction |
| **Meeting notes** | Strategic pain points | Decision extraction |
| **Calendar data** | Time allocation patterns | Activity categorization |
| **Podcast subscriptions** | Expertise accumulation | Topic clustering |

### Tier 4: Lower Signal Density

Supplementary sources:

| Source | Signal Type | Limitations |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **News articles** | Macro trends | Delayed, public knowledge |
| **Job postings** | Skills in demand | Lagging indicator |
| **Product reviews** | Feature gaps | Requires product access |
| **Forum discussions** | Community pain points | Niche bias |
| **Advertising data** | Market testing | Costly to replicate |

## Source-Specific Guides

### Slack/Teams Mining Guide

**What to Feed:**
- Copy-paste entire channel histories
- Include timestamps when possible
- Note which channels are most active
- Flag #random or #general for social signals
- Flag project channels for work pain points

**What Midas Looks For:**

1. **Tool complaints:**
   - "Jira is so slow"
   - "Why does our CRM never work right"
   - "We spend hours on this every week"

2. **Vendor pricing:**
   - "The AWS bill is insane"
   - "We got a 40% renewal increase"
   - "Has anyone found a cheaper alternative?"

3. **Expertise requests:**
   - "Does anyone know how to..."
   - "Who has experience with..."
   - "Can someone help me with..."

4. **Workarounds:**
   - "I just export to Excel and..."
   - "I manually reconcile this every Monday"
   - "We built a custom script for that"

5. **Hiring/contracting needs:**
   - "We really need a..."
   - "Anyone know a good freelance..."
   - "We're looking to outsource..."

**Signal Strength by Channel:**
- #engineering: High (technical pain points = product opportunities)
- #general: Moderate (varied, includes lifestyle signals)
- #random: Low (but often reveals behavioral patterns)
- DMs: High (honest, unfiltered)
- Project channels: Very High (specific work pain points)

### Photo Mining Guide

**What to Feed:**
- Export camera roll from past 30-90 days
- Include any photos of storefronts, signs, receipts
- Include photos of products you purchased
- Include any photos of events, venues, crowds
- Include photos of your workspace/setup

**What Midas Looks For:**

1. **Location patterns:**
   - Same restaurants/cafes repeatedly (validated demand)
   - Same retail stores (brand loyalty = market share)
   - Construction sites (development activity)
   - Empty storefronts (vacancy = opportunity)

2. **Product evidence:**
   - Receipts and price tags (exact pricing data)
   - Product packaging (what brands you buy)
   - Line lengths (demand validation)
   - Sale signs (price sensitivity signals)

3. **Professional context:**
   - Business cards (networking patterns)
   - Meeting rooms (work patterns)
   - Conference/event badges (industry exposure)
   - Coffee shops (third-place work patterns)

4. **Lifestyle intelligence:**
   - Gym/fitness (personal investment patterns)
   - Food/cooking (market for food services)
   - Travel (premium service affordability)
   - Home improvement (construction signals)

**Photo Analysis Tips:**
- Geotagged photos reveal location patterns
- Time stamps reveal routine vs. special occasions
- Frequency of same-location photos = validated demand
- Unusual items signal emerging interests

### Complaint Mining Guide

**What to Feed:**
- Copy-paste text conversations with repeated themes
- Include group chat threads over 2+ weeks
- Include your own repeated complaints
- Include "I wish..." statements
- Include "Why doesn't someone just..." statements

**What Midas Looks For:**

1. **Repeated complaints (3+ mentions):**
   - Same problem from different people
   - Same frustration across time
   - Same gap in the market

2. **Workaround descriptions:**
   - "I just do X instead"
   - "I use this hack to get around Y"
   - "I created my own solution"

3. **Recommendation requests:**
   - "Has anyone used a good..."
   - "I need a recommendation for..."
   - "Who do you use for..."

4. **Price complaints:**
   - "I can't believe it costs that much"
   - "There's gotta be a cheaper way"
   - "We're getting ripped off"

5. **Comparison statements:**
   - "In [other city/country], this is cheaper"
   - "My [other service] does this for free"
   - "Why is [competitor] so much better"

**Complaint-to-Opportunity Conversion:**

| Complaint Pattern | Opportunity Type |
|------------------|------------------|
| "I wish someone would build..." | Product idea |
| "Why does this cost so much?" | Cost reduction service |
| "I can't find anyone who..." | Service gap |
| "This is always broken..." | Repair/improvement opportunity |
| "We waste so much time on..." | Automation opportunity |
| "No one does this right..." | Quality improvement |

### Browsing History Mining Guide

- Export YouTube watch history (past 3-6 months)
- Share browser bookmark folders
- Note research projects you've undertaken
- Include any comparison shopping behavior
- Include any tutorial/video consumption

**What Midas Looks For:**

1. **Skill accumulation patterns:**
   - 3+ months of same-topic videos
   - Progression from beginner to advanced content
   - Subscriptions to niche channels
   - Repeated tutorial series

2. **Problem-solving journeys:**
   - Research into specific solutions
   - Comparison between multiple options
   - Reviews watched before purchase
   - Follow-up research after purchase

3. **Obsessive interests:**
   - High watch time on specific topics
   - Return visits to same channels
   - Deep dives into specific subjects
   - Cross-referenced topics (adjacent interests)

4. **Market education:**
   - How-to content in specific industries
   - Behind-the-scenes content
   - Industry news and analysis
   - Expert interviews

**Browsing-to-Monetization Mapping:**

| Pattern | Monetization Path |
|---------|------------------|
| 6+ months of how-to videos | Course creation, consulting |
| Comparison shopping research | Affiliate marketing, product reviews |
| Technical tutorials | Freelance services, agency |
| Investment research | Financial content, advisory |
| Real estate searches | Real estate agent, investing |
| Business formation research | Business services, incubators |

### Purchase History Mining Guide

**What to Feed:**
- Export Amazon/order confirmation emails
- List subscription services
- Note recurring purchases
- Include big-ticket purchases
- Include business-related purchases

**What Midas Looks For:**

1. **Subscription patterns:**
   - Multiple subscriptions in same category = validated category
   - Annual vs. monthly = price sensitivity signals
   - Business vs. personal mix = professional context

2. **Repeated purchases:**
   - Same product bought multiple times = strong preference
   - Same category = brand loyalty opportunity
   - Seasonal patterns = timing signals

3. **Price thresholds:**
   - Where do you stop comparing prices?
   - What triggers premium vs. budget choices?
   - What do you buy without research?

4. **Category density:**
   - What categories do you spend most in?
   - Where is spending growing vs. declining?
   - What categories are you over/under-served?

**Purchase Pattern Analysis:**

| Pattern | Signal |
|---------|--------|
| Same brand repeatedly | Brand loyalty = market share data |
| Premium products | Price insensitivity in category |
| Heavy discount shopping | Price sensitivity = arbitrage |
| Subscription stacking | Value placed on convenience |
| Business purchases mixed in | Professional context = B2B signals |

## Multi-Source Cross-Reference Matrix

The most powerful signals come from combining sources. Here's what cross-references best:

| Source A | Source B | Combined Signal |
|----------|----------|-----------------|
| Complaints (repeated) | Photos (same location) | Local business validation |
| YouTube history (skill) | Work chats (advice given) | Skill-to-revenue bridge |
| Browsing history | Purchase history | Pre/post purchase behavior |
| Slack messages | Calendar | Time wasted vs. opportunities |
| Text chats | Social media | Social proof of demand |
| Photos (receipts) | Banking/credit | Exact spending data |

### Cross-Reference Scoring

When the same opportunity appears in multiple sources:

| Cross-Reference | Confidence Boost |
|----------------|------------------|
| Same source type, different times | +10% |
| Different source types, same week | +25% |
| Different source types, reinforcing | +40% |
| 3+ independent sources, converging | +60% |

## Source Quality Tips

### Maximizing Signal Extraction

1. **Feed regularly:** Weekly minimum, daily is better
2. **Include context:** Timestamps, who said it, when
3. **Don't filter:** Let Midas decide what's noise
4. **Mix types:** Different sources enable cross-referencing
5. **Include outliers:** Unusual events often signal emerging trends

### Common Mistakes

1. **Only feeding work data:** Personal life has equal signal density
2. **Feeding filtered data:** Only "important" messages miss context
3. **Inconsistent timing:** Irregular feeding makes cross-referencing harder
4. **Ignoring repetition:** If you've mentioned it twice, it's a signal
5. **Forgetting visual data:** Photos are underutilized intelligence

### Source Trust Calibration

| Source | Reliability | Biases to Watch |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| Purchase receipts | Very high | None (actual behavior) |
| Repeated complaints | High | Recency bias |
| Slack messages | High | Professional context bias |
| Photos | Medium-high | Confirmation bias |
| Browsing history | Medium | Research vs. intent mix |
| Self-reports | Low-medium | Social desirability bias |

## Source-Specific Ethical Guidelines

**Privacy Principles:**
- Only feed data you have the right to share
- Anonymize others' information where possible
- Respect confidentiality of private conversations
- Don't feed data from restricted systems

**Data Handling:**
- Don't store sensitive data in evolution logs
- Clear sensitive content from inputs when not needed
- Use secure channels for sharing inputs
- Delete processed inputs when session ends

**Ethical Boundaries:**
- Don't use this for insider trading signals
- Don't use for competitive intelligence on specific companies
- Don't use for targeting individuals with marketing
- Don't use for anything illegal or unethical
