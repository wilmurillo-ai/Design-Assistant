# Bro Archetypes Reference Guide

## Overview

Every bro falls into one or more archetypes. These aren't rigid categories — most bros are a blend. But understanding the archetypes helps the skill predict behavior, simulate conversations, and match energy accurately.

---

## The Eight Archetypes

### 1. The Hype Man

**Energy:** 11/10 at all times. There is no off switch.

**How to identify:**
- They scream when something mildly good happens
- Everything is "the best thing ever" or "insane" or "LET'S GOOO"
- Their excitement is genuinely contagious — you can't help but get hyped too
- They never enter a room quietly

**Famous examples:** IShowSpeed, early-era Jake Paul, that one friend who turns every minor event into a celebration

**Comedy style:** Volume IS the joke. The reaction IS the content. Over-the-top enthusiasm applied to mundane situations.

**In a group chat:** First to respond with all caps. Sends voice notes instead of typing. Uses excessive emojis. Gets the energy going even when everyone else is dead.

**Weakness:** Can be exhausting. Sometimes the room needs calm and The Hype Man doesn't read that.

**Pairs well with:** The Cool Bro (contrast comedy), The Silent Killer (the quiet one grounds them)

**Clashes with:** Another Hype Man (two screaming people cancel each other out)

---

### 2. The Roast Master

**Energy:** Calm, measured, precise. A surgeon with words.

**How to identify:**
- Their insults are so specific they feel personalized
- They observe silently then strike at the perfect moment
- They never raise their voice — the words do all the damage
- They can make a room go silent with one sentence
- They laugh at their own roasts, which somehow makes them funnier

**Famous examples:** KSI, Anthony Jeselnik, that friend who never yells but always has the most devastating thing to say

**Comedy style:** Surgical precision. Economy of words. The less they say, the harder it hits. They find the one thing you're insecure about and frame it as a casual observation.

**In a group chat:** Drops one message that ends the conversation. Everyone responds with "BRO" or the skull emoji. Then silence.

**Weakness:** Sometimes crosses a line they didn't see. The precision that makes them funny can also make them accidentally hurtful.

**Pairs well with:** The Hype Man (provides a steady target), The Storyteller (both are performers)

**Clashes with:** Another Roast Master (becomes a roast battle that gets uncomfortable for everyone else)

---

### 3. The Cool Bro

**Energy:** Low, steady, unflinching. Nothing fazes them.

**How to identify:**
- They speak in a calm, measured tone no matter what's happening
- Everyone in the group respects their opinion
- When they do show emotion, it's a big deal
- They have a quiet confidence that isn't performative
- They don't try to be funny — they just ARE funny because of their delivery

**Famous examples:** Keanu Reeves, Idris Elba, that friend who's always chill and somehow makes chill look cool

**Comedy style:** Understatement. Deadpan delivery. Says something wild in the most casual tone. The humor comes from the contrast between the content and the delivery.

**In a group chat:** Rarely messages. When they do, everyone reads it carefully. Their "lol" means something actually made them laugh. Their single emoji response carries weight.

**Weakness:** Can seem disengaged or uninterested. Sometimes the group needs someone to match energy and The Cool Bro won't.

**Pairs well with:** The Chaos Agent (The Cool Bro anchors the chaos), The Hype Man (contrast creates comedy)

**Clashes with:** Nobody, really. That's the whole point of The Cool Bro.

---

### 4. The Chaos Agent

**Energy:** Unpredictable. Zero to 100 to zero to 100 again.

**How to identify:**
- They do things nobody asked for and somehow it works out
- Their ideas are either genius or completely insane (no in-between)
- They make impulsive decisions and commit fully
- They thrive on reactions — the bigger the reaction, the more they escalate
- Plans change the moment they show up

**Famous examples:** Jake Paul (peak era), early MrBeast, that friend who texts "I bought a boat" at 2am on a Tuesday

**Comedy style:** Shock value. The unexpected. "I can't believe he actually did that." The comedy comes from the gap between what a normal person would do and what The Chaos Agent does.

**In a group chat:** Sends messages that derail the entire conversation. Brings up topics from 3 weeks ago. Makes plans, changes plans, abandons plans, makes new plans — all in 10 minutes.

**Weakness:** Exhausting for people who need structure. Can create actual problems that other people have to clean up.

**Pairs well with:** The Strategy Bro (channels the chaos productively), The Cool Bro (provides a grounding force)

**Clashes with:** The Peacemaker subtype of other archetypes (they keep trying to calm what can't be calmed)

---

### 5. The Strategy Bro

**Energy:** Focused, calm, always thinking three steps ahead.

**How to identify:**
- Every conversation becomes a business plan or a life lesson
- They see opportunities everywhere — "you know what you should do?"
- They have spreadsheets for things that don't need spreadsheets
- They turn fun activities into optimized systems
- Their advice is actually good, which is the annoying part

**Famous examples:** MrBeast (evolved era), Gary Vee, that friend who turned a hobby into a business and now won't stop talking about it

**Comedy style:** Dry wit. The humor comes from applying business logic to absurd situations. "Actually, if we optimize the road trip route, we save 47 minutes." The joke is they're serious.

**In a group chat:** The one who creates polls. Makes shared Google Docs for trip planning. Sends articles with "thought of you guys." Always has a take that's somehow both obvious and insightful.

**Weakness:** Can suck the fun out of things by over-optimizing. Not everything needs to be productive.

**Pairs well with:** The Chaos Agent (needs someone to direct the energy), The Storyteller (Strategy Bro provides the framework, Storyteller provides the narrative)

**Clashes with:** People who just want to vibe. The Strategy Bro can't turn off.

---

### 6. The Silent Killer

**Energy:** Minimal output, maximum impact.

**How to identify:**
- They're quiet for long stretches — you forget they're there
- When they speak, everyone stops to listen
- Their one sentence is funnier than everyone else's paragraph
- They have perfect comedic timing because the silence before their line IS the setup
- They never fight for attention — it comes to them

**Famous examples:** The friend in every group who barely talks but gets the biggest laughs. Bill Murray energy. Aubrey Plaza energy.

**Comedy style:** Economy. One line. Perfect timing. The silence before it is the setup. They let everyone else create the context, then drop the punchline. Effortless.

**In a group chat:** 90% lurker. 10% absolute destruction. Their messages get screenshotted and sent to other group chats. When they react with a single emoji, people analyze what it means.

**Weakness:** Hard to get to know. Their mystique can feel like disinterest. Sometimes you need more than one perfect line — you need someone who'll actually participate.

**Pairs well with:** The Hype Man (the contrast makes both funnier), The Roast Master (both are precision-based)

**Clashes with:** People who interpret silence as judgment. The Silent Killer isn't judging. They're loading.

---

### 7. The Meme Lord

**Energy:** Reactive. A mirror that reflects everything in meme format.

**How to identify:**
- They have a meme for every situation — instantly
- Their phone gallery is 90% saved memes
- They communicate in images more than words
- They're always on the cutting edge of internet culture
- Their meme game is so specific it feels like they made them (sometimes they did)

**Famous examples:** That friend who responds to everything with a perfectly chosen image. The person in your Discord who has reaction emojis for every possible scenario.

**Comedy style:** Visual. Contextual. The humor comes from the perfect match between the situation and the meme. Speed matters — the first meme response wins. Original memes > reposted memes.

**In a group chat:** Their contributions are 80% images, 10% videos, 5% links, 5% actual words. When they DO type actual text, it's usually a caption for a meme. The group chat gallery is mostly their contributions.

**Weakness:** Can derail serious conversations. Sometimes people need actual words and The Meme Lord has none.

**Pairs well with:** The Storyteller (one creates content, the other reacts to it), The Hype Man (both are reactive energy)

**Clashes with:** Situations that genuinely require verbal communication. Job interviews. Weddings. (Though they'll try.)

---

### 8. The Storyteller

**Energy:** Dramatic, expressive, builds to a crescendo.

**How to identify:**
- Every experience becomes a 10-minute dramatic retelling
- They set the scene, do the voices, act out the physical comedy
- "Okay so basically what happened was—" and then you're locked in for the ride
- They can make a trip to the grocery store sound like a heist movie
- Their stories always have a callback or a running thread

**Famous examples:** Your funniest friend, the person at the party everyone gathers around, stand-up comedians in casual conversation mode

**Comedy style:** Narrative. The setup is long but the payoff is worth it. They build tension, add characters, create callbacks. The story is never just "what happened" — it's a performance.

**In a group chat:** Sends multiple messages in rapid succession to build the story. Uses "..." for dramatic pauses between messages. Drops voice notes that are 3 minutes long. The group chat goes quiet while everyone reads/listens, then explodes.

**Weakness:** Can dominate conversations. Not everyone gets to talk when The Storyteller is going. Sometimes the story is 10 minutes and the payoff is... underwhelming. But the performance was great.

**Pairs well with:** The Silent Killer (perfect audience — one performer, one reactor), The Roast Master (both appreciate craft)

**Clashes with:** Another Storyteller (both want the floor), The Strategy Bro (wants to get to the point)

## Archetype Combinations

Most bros are a blend. Common combinations:

| Combo | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Hype Man + Chaos Agent | Maximum volume, maximum unpredictability | IShowSpeed |
| Roast Master + Silent Killer | Quiet until they strike, then devastating | The friend who destroys you with one calm sentence |
| Strategy Bro + Cool Bro | Calmly optimizes everything, never stressed | MrBeast (current era) |
| Storyteller + Hype Man | Dramatic stories told at maximum volume | Your friend who can't tell a story without yelling |
| Meme Lord + Silent Killer | Lurks, then drops the perfect meme | The Discord legend |
| Chaos Agent + Storyteller | Creates chaos then tells the story of the chaos they created | Your most entertaining friend |

## Using Archetypes in the Skill

When profiling a new bro:

1. **Identify primary archetype** — What's their dominant energy?
2. **Identify secondary archetype** — What's their backup mode?
3. **Note the blend ratio** — 70% Hype Man / 30% Chaos Agent is different from 50/50
4. **Track evolution** — Archetypes shift over time. The Chaos Agent might mellow into a Strategy Bro.
5. **Context matters** — The same bro might be a Hype Man at parties and a Silent Killer in meetings. Note the triggers.
