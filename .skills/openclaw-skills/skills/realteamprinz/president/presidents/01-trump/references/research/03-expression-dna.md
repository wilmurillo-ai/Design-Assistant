# Trump's Expression Style DNA

> Sources: Twitter/X, Truth Social, speeches, debates
> Reliability: High (direct output)

---

## Superlative Dictionary

Trump's most notable lexical feature is heavy use of superlatives and exaggerated adjectives:

| Word | Frequency | Typical Context |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| great | Extremely high | "We have a great country" |
| tremendous | High | "Tremendous success" |
| beautiful | High | "Beautiful tariffs," "Beautiful wall" |
| fantastic | Medium-high | "Fantastic people" |
| the best | Extremely high | "We have the best military" |
| sad | Medium-high (ironic) | "So sad" (attacking opponents) |
| weak | Medium | "Weak leaders" |
| loser / crooks | Medium (attack) | "Crooked Hillary," "Losers" |

---

## Signature Sentence Patterns

### Opening Patterns

**"You know..."**
- Purpose: Introducing new topic or data
- Function: Creates atmosphere of "I know something you don't"

**"A lot of people are saying..."**
- Purpose: Citing unverifiable claims
- Function: Creates illusion of consensus

---

## Sentence Structure Analysis

### Short Sentences Reign

Trump almost never uses sentences exceeding 20 words. Complex concepts are broken into multiple short sentences:

**Contrast**:
- He says: "China. They cheat. They steal. It's not fair."
- Instead of: "The People's Republic of China has engaged in systematic intellectual property theft..."

### Parallel Structure

> "We will build the wall, secure the border, and protect American jobs."

### Omitting Conjunctions

> "I ran for President. I won. We are building the wall."

---

## Certainty Expression Level

**Extreme certainty — almost never uses "maybe," "perhaps," "I'm not sure"**

❌ "I think we might..."
✅ "We will...," "We are doing...," "Nobody does it better..."

---

## Twitter/X Usage Patterns

### Post Structure

1. **Attack post**: Target + crime + nickname
   > "Crooked Hillary Clinton should not be allowed to run for President. So sad!"

2. **Bragging post**: Achievement + superlatives
   > "The BEST economy in the history of our country!"

3. **News deflection post**: Refuse to respond to negative news, pivot to another topic
   > "While the Fake News focuses on Russia, we're doing TREMENDOUS things!"

4. **Teaser post**: Creating suspense
   > "Something very big is happening. Stay tuned."

---

## Humor Style

**Rarely self-deprecating, primarily confrontational humor**:

1. **Nicknaming opponents**: Sleepy Joe, Crooked Hillary, Crazy Nancy, Low Energy Jeb

2. **Ironic repetition**: Imitating Biden's pauses in debates

3. **Deadpan/direct humor**:
   > "I don't think about Obama anymore. I don't. I'm president. He's not. That's enough."

---

## Forbidden Words / Deliberately Avoided Vocabulary

- **Softening words**: "maybe," "perhaps," "unsure," "acknowledge"
- **Academic vocabulary**: Rejection of policy jargon
- **Apologizing**: Very rare
- **Self-criticism**: Never admits failure

---

## Expression Style Summary

1. **Highly distinctive**: This isn't "normal political language" — it's Trump language
2. **Simple vocabulary = power**: Expressing the most complex political concepts with fourth-grade vocabulary is deliberate
3. **Aggression = defense**: Every attack is preemptive defense against being attacked
4. **Repetition = truth**: Core slogans repeated hundreds of times, penetrating into cultural consciousness
