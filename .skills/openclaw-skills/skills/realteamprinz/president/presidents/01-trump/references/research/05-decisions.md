# Trump: Decision Records and Actions

## Major Decision Analysis

### Decision 1: Withdraw from TPP (January 23, 2017)

**Decision logic**:
- "TPP is a disaster for American workers — Vietnam, Malaysia will take our manufacturing jobs"
- Congressional Republicans opposed (they supported TPP), but he didn't care

**Decision pattern characteristic**: Unilateral action, rapid execution — typical "America First" + "CEO-style decision-making"

---

### Decision 2: China Trade War (July 6, 2018)

**Core logic**:
- "China is stealing our jobs, stealing our technology"
- "Tariffs are beautiful weapons"

**Second term**: Full restart of tariff war, some products over 100%

---

### Decision 3: Kim Jong-un Summit (June 12, 2018)

**Core logic**:
- "I have chemistry with Kim Jong-un," "He can be a friend"
- Personal relationship diplomacy: Believing personal relationships with dictators can change their behavior

**Result**:
- Hanoi Summit collapsed
- No substantive agreement reached in the end

---

### Decision 4: Response to 91 Indictments (2023–2024)

**Core logic**:
- Turn charges into evidence of "political persecution"
- Turn every court appearance into campaign events

**Result**: Not only not finished — won 2024

## Decision-Making Style Summary

| Dimension | Trump's Typical Pattern |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Decision speed | Extremely fast (act first, explain later) |
| Information source | Personal judgment overstaff advice |
| Failure handling | Deny or redefine (never admit failure) |
| Success attribution | Always credit to himself |
| Legal boundaries | Probing advance (edge-riding) |
| Public opinion use | Direct appeal to the people (bypass elites) |

## Cases of Alignment / Misalignment Between Words and Actions

**Aligned**:
- ✅ Withdrew from TPP (campaign promise, fulfilled 2017)
- ✅ Tough on China (campaign promise, started trade war 2018)
- ✅ Build the wall (most controversial but consistently maintained)
- ✅ Exited Paris Agreement (campaign promise, fulfilled 2017)

**Misaligned**:
- ❌ "Mexico will pay for the wall" → American taxpayers actually paid
- ❌ "I'll quickly release healthcare replacement plan" → Repealing Obamacare never succeeded

**Pattern**: Aligned on core campaign promises; often watered down or failed on specific implementation details
