# Trump: Extended Conversations and Improvised Thinking

> Sources: Interviews, debates, press conferences, podcasts, summit dialogues
> Reliability: High (direct audio transcription)

## Debate Performance Analysis

### 2016 Presidential Debate (vs Hillary Clinton)

**Improvised response patterns**:
- Hillary mentioned his tax cuts would increase the national debt → immediate pivot: "She wants to talk about your bills. Let me talk about ISIS. Do you know what happened with ISIS when Hillary was Secretary of State?"
- Core strategy: Turn policy discussion into personal attacks while framing opponent's policy failures as "evidence of why your life got worse"

**Typical responses under pressure**:
> Interviewer: "Will you accept the election results?"
> Trump: "I will keep you in suspense."

### Republican Primary Debates (2015–2016)

**Personalized attack logic**:
- Jeb Bush: "Low Energy Jeb"
- Marco Rubio: "Lyin' Ted Cruz"
- Ted Cruz: "Only Candidate Who Can Beat Hillary"

**Strategy when pressed on extreme statements**:
- Never fully retract statements
- "I could rephrase it, but the meaning is the same"
- Turn controversy into media coverage — free publicity

## Key Findings on Improvised Thinking Style

1. **Never paces back and forth**: Every question is an opportunity — no "I don't know"
2. **Data usage**: Heavy reliance on approximate figures to support arguments
3. **Self-reference**: "I was right about X," "People are saying..."
4. **Rejection of ambiguity**: "It's going to be great," "We're doing tremendous work"
5. **Skipping details**: "I have a plan, believe me" is the standard deflection
6. **Infinite extension of analogies**: "Business negotiations are like foreign policy negotiations, like marriage, like everything"

## Types of Questions Trump Refuses to Answer

1. Specific questions about his private finances
2. His medical records (though he voluntarily disclosed a perfect cognitive test score)
3. Any acknowledgment of failures during his first term
4. His religious beliefs (only says "I believe in God")
