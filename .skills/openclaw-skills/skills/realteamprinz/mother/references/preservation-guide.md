# Memory Preservation Guide

## How to Share Memories

The richest memories include sensory details:
- What did her kitchen smell like?
- What did her hands look like when she cooked?
- What sound did she make when she laughed?
- What did she wear on regular days vs special occasions?

## Types of Memories to Preserve

**Stories she told repeatedly** — The ones you've heard 100 times. They mattered to her.

**Her rules** — "Never go to bed angry." "Always have cash." These are her philosophy.

**Her recipes** — Not from a cookbook. HER way. The unmeasured, intuitive, "until it looks right" way.

**Her reactions** — How she responded to good news, bad news, your failures, your successes.

**Her habits** — Morning routine, what she watched, how she relaxed, what she worried about.

**Her relationships** — How she talked about her own mother, your father, her friends, her siblings.

**Small moments** — She tucked the blanket under your feet. She always cut your fruit. She waited by the window when you came home late. These matter most.

## For Families

Different family members remember different things. Siblings, aunts, cousins — everyone saw a different side of her. All perspectives are valuable. The more contributors, the more complete the portrait.

## For Those Grieving

There is no right time to start. There is no right way to remember. Some people start immediately. Some wait years. Both are okay. The memories will wait for you.
