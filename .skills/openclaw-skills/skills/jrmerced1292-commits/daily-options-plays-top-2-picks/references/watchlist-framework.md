# Beginner Options Watchlist Framework

## Default Use Case

Use this skill for a beginner who wants a clean daily watchlist, not constant scalping.

Default assumptions:
- US equities only
- Options are used only as a wrapper around the stock thesis
- Scan starts after 9:45 AM ET
- Final output includes only the top 2 plays
- Tone stays calm, practical, and skeptical

## Scan Checklist

Review these before ranking setups:

1. Market context
   - index direction and trend quality
   - VIX / volatility regime
   - major macro events, Fed, CPI, jobs, earnings clusters
   - sector strength vs market

2. Stock setup quality
   - daily and intraday trend alignment
   - volume expansion or unusual activity
   - support, resistance, VWAP, key moving averages
   - relative strength or weakness vs SPY
   - continuation vs exhaustion risk

3. Catalyst and sentiment
   - fresh news, earnings, guidance, analyst changes
   - unusual options activity if available
   - sentiment confirmation or conflict

4. Trade structure
   - clear entry trigger
   - invalidation / stop level
   - realistic first target
   - minimum acceptable risk/reward

## Ranking Rules

Prefer setups that have:
- strong liquidity
- simple chart structure
- clear invalidation
- alignment with market and sector tone
- a believable catalyst or momentum driver

De-prioritize setups that have:
- choppy range action
- wide or unclear stops
- weak liquidity
- conflicting news / chart signals
- panic-chasing after an already-extended move

If no setup is clean, return no-trade or only 1 idea.

## Contract Translation Rules

After the stock thesis is clear:
- Bullish setup -> call idea
- Bearish setup -> put idea
- Use near-the-money strikes by default
- Use 1-2 week expirations by default
- If asked for examples, describe the contract generically unless current chain data is available

Example phrasing:
- "Consider a near-the-money call expiring in 1-2 weeks."
- "Safer beginner choice: slightly ITM or ATM put with next-week expiration."

## Example Output

### Market tone
- Futures were mixed and volatility is elevated, so conviction is moderate.
- Momentum is cleaner in leaders than in the broad tape.
- Stay selective; no revenge trades if the first idea fails.

### Play 1 — NVDA bullish
- Entry: reclaim and hold above 942
- Stop: lose 934
- Target: 955, then 963
- Confidence: 7/10
- Why it made the list: relative strength, strong volume, cleaner trend than the index
- Options idea: near-the-money call, 1-2 weeks out
- Key risk: fails if semis roll over with QQQ

### Play 2 — COIN bearish
- Entry: reject 214 and lose intraday support
- Stop: reclaim 217
- Target: 208, then 204
- Confidence: 6/10
- Why it made the list: weak relative strength and crypto-sensitive volatility
- Options idea: near-the-money put, 1-2 weeks out
- Key risk: sudden bitcoin squeeze reverses the move

### Risk note
- Paper trade first. If both setups break structure, stand down.

## Style Rules

- Keep language beginner-friendly.
- Keep each play short.
- No hype, no certainty, no "can’t miss" framing.
- Say when data is missing instead of pretending.
- Treat the plan as a watchlist, not a command.
