## Launch Timeline Overview

```
Week -6:  ARC team recruitment begins
Week -4:  ARC copies distributed, Goodreads listing created
Week -3:  Email list teaser campaign begins, social media content starts
Week -2:  Pre-order live (optional), final metadata review
Week -1:  Launch week prep — all assets finalized, ARC readers reminded to post reviews
Week 0:   LAUNCH — KDP go-live, Author Central, community posts, email blast
Week +1:  Monitor BSR, solicit reviews, first Amazon Ads campaigns
Week +2:  Category optimization, A+ Content submission
Week +4:  30-day performance report, adjust pricing/keywords/categories
Week +12: 90-day review, decide on KDP Select renewal or go wide
```

## Pre-Launch (4-6 Weeks Before)

### ARC Campaign (Week -6 to -4)
See [ARC Reader Strategy](#arc-reader-strategy) below for full details.
- Recruit minimum 15-25 ARC readers (target 10+ day-one reviews)
- Distribute ARC copies 3-4 weeks before launch
- Follow up at 2 weeks and 1 week before launch

### Goodreads Setup (Week -4)
- Create the book listing on Goodreads
- Add book to relevant Goodreads shelves/lists
- Author profile linked to the listing
- Begin "Want to Read" campaign — share listing with network

### Email List Preparation (Week -3)
- Write 3-email pre-launch sequence:
  1. **Teaser email**: "Something big is coming" — build anticipation, share cover reveal or excerpt
  2. **Value email**: Share a useful insight or excerpt from the book — demonstrate the book's value
  3. **Launch email**: "It's live" — direct link, launch pricing, review request
- Segment list if possible: warm leads (engaged subscribers) get early access or bonuses

### Social Media Pre-Launch (Week -3)
- Create content calendar: 3-4 posts per week across relevant platforms
- Content types:
  - Cover reveal (high engagement)
  - Behind-the-scenes of the writing process
  - Excerpts or quotes from the book (design as shareable graphics)
  - "Why I wrote this book" personal story
  - Countdown posts in final week
- Platform priorities by genre — see [Social Media Marketing](#social-media-marketing)

### Pre-Order Decision (Week -2)
**Pre-order pros:**
- Accumulates sales that all count on launch day (BSR spike)
- Creates urgency ("pre-order now, releases [date]")
- Allows early marketing to drive to a live buy page

**Pre-order cons:**
- Manuscript must be final and uploaded before pre-order goes live
- If the book isn't ready, Amazon may penalize future pre-order eligibility
- No Kindle Unlimited page reads until the book is actually live

**Recommendation:** Use pre-orders only if you have an existing audience or email list that will actually pre-order. For first books with no audience, launch directly.

## Launch Week

### Day 0: Go-Live Checklist
- [ ] KDP shows "Live" status (can take 24-72 hours after upload)
- [ ] Verify correct pricing (ebook + paperback)
- [ ] Verify categories are correct
- [ ] Book description renders properly (check HTML formatting)
- [ ] "Look Inside" feature is working
- [ ] Author Central profile linked and updated
- [ ] Buy link tested (actually click through the purchase flow)

### Day 0-1: Activation
- [ ] Send launch email to full list (link directly to Amazon page)
- [ ] Post on all social media platforms
- [ ] Message ARC readers: "Book is live! Please post your review today if possible."
- [ ] Post to relevant communities (professional networks, Facebook groups, online communities)
- [ ] Share in relevant Discord/Slack communities where you're a genuine member
- [ ] Personal outreach to 10-20 supporters asking them to buy + review

### Day 1-3: Momentum Building
- [ ] Monitor BSR hourly on day 1 (note your best rank)
- [ ] Share BSR milestones on social media ("#1 New Release in [category]!")
- [ ] Thank early reviewers publicly
- [ ] Run any planned cross-promotions
- [ ] Submit to book promotion sites if pricing at $0.99 (BookBub, Freebooksy, BargainBooksy, Robin Reads)

### Day 3-7: Sustained Push
- [ ] Follow up with ARC readers who haven't posted reviews yet
- [ ] Second email to list: "Early reader reactions" (share positive reviews/feedback)
- [ ] Continue social media content (reader reactions, behind-the-scenes, Q&A)
- [ ] Begin Amazon Ads campaigns (see [Amazon Ads](#amazon-ads-for-authors))

## Post-Launch (30-90 Days)

### Week 2-4: Optimization
- **BSR tracking**: check daily, note trends
- **Review monitoring**: respond to questions, note recurring feedback
- **Keyword optimization**: update backend keywords based on actual search performance
- **Category adjustment**: request additional categories via KDP Support
- **A+ Content**: create and submit (see [Amazon A+ Content](#amazon-a-content))
- **Price adjustment**: raise from launch price to full price (week 2-3)

### Month 2-3: Scaling
- **Amazon Ads**: review ACOS, optimize campaigns, scale winners
- **Cross-promotion**: guest posts, podcast appearances, collaborations
- **Content marketing**: blog posts, YouTube videos, social media content derived from book content
- **Backlist planning**: if this is book 1, begin planning book 2 (series effect is the strongest indie marketing tool)

## ARC Reader Strategy

### What is an ARC?
Advance Review Copy — a free copy of the book sent to readers before or at launch, in exchange for an honest review posted on Amazon and/or Goodreads.

### Recruitment Sources
- **Personal network**: friends, family, colleagues who genuinely read the genre
- **Professional network**: colleagues in your field, community members, fellow authors
- **Social media followers**: ask in stories/posts who wants an early copy
- **ARC services**: BookSirens, BookSprout, NetGalley (for broader reach)
- **Email list**: offer ARC to engaged subscribers
- **Facebook groups**: genre-specific reader groups (follow group rules about promotion)

### ARC Campaign Timeline
| When | Action |
|---|---|
| Week -6 | Begin recruiting — post to social media, email list, personal outreach |
| Week -5 | Confirm ARC team list (minimum 15, target 25) |
| Week -4 | Distribute ARC copies (ebook format — EPUB or PDF, NOT final KDP file) |
| Week -2 | First reminder: "How's the reading going? Any early thoughts?" |
| Week -1 | Second reminder: "Launch is next week! Reviews posted on day one make a huge difference." |
| Day 0 | Final message: "It's live! Here's the Amazon link. Please post your review today." |
| Day +3 | Follow up with anyone who hasn't posted yet |
| Day +7 | Thank everyone, share review count milestone |

### ARC Best Practices
- **Never require positive reviews** — this violates Amazon TOS. Ask for "honest" reviews.
- **Provide a review template** to make it easy: "Here's the Amazon link. Even a 2-3 sentence review helps enormously."
- **Stagger reviews**: 5-10 reviews in the first 48 hours creates social proof without triggering Amazon's review fraud filters
- **Track who delivers**: maintain an ARC reader list with status (sent, reminded, reviewed)
- **Build your ARC team over time**: readers who review once reliably are worth recruiting again

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

## Amazon Ads for Authors

### Campaign Types

**1. Sponsored Products (Start Here)**
- Your book appears in search results and on competitor book pages
- Pay-per-click — you only pay when someone clicks
- Best for: driving sales and BSR, testing keywords

**2. Sponsored Brands**
- Banner ad at top of search results showing your author brand + multiple books
- Best for: authors with 3+ books, brand building

**3. Lockscreen Ads**
- Display ads on Kindle lockscreens and Fire tablets
- Best for: broad awareness, genre fiction, high-volume categories

### Setting Up Your First Campaign

**Budget:** Start with $5-$10/day. Scale after 2 weeks of data.

**Targeting Options:**
- **Keyword targeting**: your ads show when readers search these keywords
  - Use your 7 backend keywords + variations
  - Add competitor book titles and author names as keywords
  - Start with "broad match," refine to "exact match" as you learn what converts

- **Product targeting**: your ads show on specific competitor book pages
  - Target the top 20 books in your category
  - Target books with similar price/review profiles to yours

**Bid Strategy:**
- Start with Amazon's suggested bid
- Adjust after 1 week: increase bids on keywords with good ACOS, decrease or pause poor performers
- Target ACOS (Advertising Cost of Sale) below 70% for launch phase, below 40% for sustained profitability

### Amazon Ads Metrics to Track
| Metric | What It Means | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Impressions | How many times your ad was shown | Higher = better visibility |
| Clicks | How many people clicked your ad | CTR > 0.3% is decent |
| Orders | How many clicks converted to sales | Conversion > 10% is good |
| ACOS | Ad spend / ad-attributed revenue | < 70% launch, < 40% sustained |
| Spend | Total money spent | Stay within daily budget |

### Common Amazon Ads Mistakes
- Setting budget too high before understanding what converts
- Not giving campaigns 2 weeks of data before optimizing
- Using only broad keywords (too generic, wastes spend)
- Not targeting competitor ASINs (product targeting is often the highest ROI)
- Forgetting to pause losing keywords (check weekly)

## Social Media Marketing

### Platform Priorities by Genre

| Genre | Primary Platforms | Content Style |
|---|---|---|
| Health/Wellness Non-fiction | Instagram, YouTube, Facebook Groups | Educational carousels, short-form video tips, Q&A |
| Children's Books | Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest | Illustration reveals, read-aloud clips, parent community |
| Faith/Ministry | Facebook, Instagram, YouTube | Devotional content, Scripture graphics, testimony stories |
| AI/Technology | LinkedIn, Twitter/X, YouTube | How-to threads, demo videos, behind-the-scenes |
| Romance/Fiction | TikTok (BookTok), Instagram (Bookstagram) | Aesthetic mood boards, trope content, character reveals |
| Business/Self-Help | LinkedIn, Instagram, YouTube | Key insight carousels, short-form video, case studies |

### Content Framework: The 4-1-1 Rule
For every 6 posts:
- **4 value posts**: tips, insights, excerpts, educational content from the book
- **1 soft sell**: share the book with context ("If you've been struggling with X, I wrote this for you")
- **1 engagement post**: question, poll, reader story, behind-the-scenes

### BookTok/Bookstagram Strategy
- **BookTok**: short videos (30-60 seconds), trending sounds, emotional hooks
  - "POV: you finally understand why [topic A] and [topic B] are connected"
  - Book reveal videos, reading vlogs, "books that changed my perspective" trends
- **Bookstagram**: aesthetic flat-lays, quote graphics, story polls
  - Consistent visual brand (color palette, fonts)
  - Engage with other bookstagrammers in your niche
  - Use hashtags strategically: mix large (#BookTok), medium (#HealthBooks), and niche (#[YourTopic])

## Email Marketing

### The Author Email Funnel
```
Reader finds book → CTA in back matter → Signs up for newsletter
→ Welcome sequence (3-4 emails introducing author + free resource)
→ Regular content emails (2-4 per month)
→ Launch email sequence for next book
```

### Welcome Sequence (Automated, Triggered on Signup)
1. **Email 1 (Immediate)**: Welcome + deliver the free resource/bonus. Set expectations.
2. **Email 2 (Day 2)**: Share your story — why you write, who you help, what drives you.
3. **Email 3 (Day 5)**: Best content or insight — one email that delivers massive value.
4. **Email 4 (Day 8)**: Soft CTA — "If you enjoyed [book], here's how to stay connected / what's coming next."

### Launch Sequence (3 Emails Over 5 Days)
1. **Email 1 (Day -2)**: "It's almost here" — teaser, cover reveal, build anticipation
2. **Email 2 (Day 0)**: "It's live!" — direct Amazon link, launch pricing, review request
3. **Email 3 (Day +3)**: "Early reader reactions" — share positive reviews, remind about review CTA

### Newsletter Best Practices
- **Frequency**: 2-4 emails per month (enough to stay top-of-mind, not enough to annoy)
- **Value ratio**: 80% useful content, 20% promotion
- **Subject lines**: specific and curiosity-driven, not generic ("5 things your {{AUTHOR_CREDENTIALS}} wants you to know about [topic]" not "Monthly newsletter")
- **CTA**: every email has one clear call-to-action, not five
- **Platform**: ConvertKit (author-focused), Mailchimp, or Substack

## Amazon A+ Content

### What It Is
Enhanced product page content — branded images, comparison charts, and formatted text that appears below the book description on Amazon.

### Eligibility
- Must be enrolled in Amazon Brand Registry OR have an Amazon Author Central account
- Available for both KDP and traditionally published books
- Free to create and submit

### Content Modules to Use
1. **Brand Story**: Author photo + mission statement + "why this book exists"
2. **Comparison Chart**: Compare your book to competitors (or to other books in your series)
3. **Image with Text**: Excerpt or key insight presented with a professional graphic
4. **Feature Highlights**: 3-4 key benefits with icons or images

### A+ Content Best Practices
- **Design quality matters**: professional graphics, consistent brand colors
- **Focus on conversion**: every module should answer "why should I buy this book?"
- **Include social proof**: reader quotes, review excerpts, credentials
- **Mobile-first**: most Amazon browsing is mobile — test how modules render on phone

## Performance Tracking

### Metrics to Track Weekly
| Metric | Source | What It Tells You |
|---|---|---|
| BSR (Best Sellers Rank) | Amazon product page | Overall sales velocity relative to all books |
| Category rank | Amazon product page | How you rank in your specific categories |
| Review count + average | Amazon product page | Social proof momentum |
| KU page reads | KDP Dashboard | Kindle Unlimited engagement (if enrolled) |
| Units sold | KDP Dashboard | Actual purchase volume |
| Revenue | KDP Dashboard | Bottom line |
| Ad spend + ACOS | Amazon Ads Dashboard | Marketing efficiency |
| Email list growth | Email platform | Long-term audience building |

### BSR Interpretation Guide
| BSR Range | Approximate Daily Sales | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| < 5,000 | 25-50+ per day | Bestseller territory |
| 5,000-20,000 | 5-25 per day | Strong consistent seller |
| 20,000-50,000 | 2-5 per day | Decent performance |
| 50,000-100,000 | 1-2 per day | Moderate — needs marketing push |
| 100,000-500,000 | A few per week | Low but active |
| 500,000+ | Infrequent sales | Needs attention or may be in a slow niche |

## Long-Term Platform Building

### The Author Flywheel
```
Write great book → Readers discover it → CTA drives to email list
→ Email list grows → Launch next book to engaged audience
→ Day-one sales spike BSR → Algorithm pushes book to more readers
→ More readers → More reviews → More organic discovery → Repeat
```

### Key Long-Term Assets
1. **Email list**: the only audience you own (not dependent on any platform's algorithm)
2. **Backlist**: every new book sells old books. Series effect is the #1 indie growth driver.
3. **Author brand**: consistent voice, visual identity, and reader expectation across all books
4. **Review base**: books with 50+ reviews convert dramatically better than books with 5
5. **Amazon algorithm history**: books that sell consistently rank better over time

### The #1 Rule of Indie Marketing
**The best marketing for your current book is your next book.** Backlist drives more sustainable revenue than any ad campaign. Keep the pipeline moving.
