# Plant Problem Diagnosis Manual

This document provides diagnostic methods and solutions for common plant problems, helping users quickly identify and resolve issues encountered in plant care.

---

## 1. Water-Related Problems

### Overwatering

**Identifying Symptoms**
- Yellowing leaves, dull and lackluster
- Both new and old leaves turning yellow simultaneously
- Soil remains wet for extended periods
- Leaves wilting but soil is moist
- Soft or discolored stem base
- Standing water in drainage tray

**Possible Causes**
- Watering too frequently
- Poor drainage in pot
- Poor soil aeration
- Poor ventilation, slow water evaporation
- Continued regular watering during winter dormancy

**Solutions**
1. Stop watering immediately
2. Loosen surface soil to improve aeration
3. Place pot in well-ventilated area
4. Poke a few holes in soil with chopsticks to improve airflow
5. If root rot has occurred, repot with root trimming and fresh soil
6. Adjust watering frequency, follow "soak and dry" principle

**Prevention**
- Choose pots with good drainage
- Add perlite or coarse sand to soil mix
- Test soil moisture with finger before watering
- Reduce watering frequency in winter

### Underwatering

**Identifying Symptoms**
- Wilting, drooping leaves
- Leaf edges curling, browning
- Soil is dry and cracked
- Faded leaf color
- Soil pulling away from pot walls
- Leaf drop (in severe cases)

**Possible Causes**
- Forgotten to water
- Only watered the surface, didn't soak through
- Pot too large, roots can't reach moisture
- Environment too dry, water evaporating quickly

**Solutions**
1. Water thoroughly immediately, ensure water reaches the bottom
2. For extremely dry soil, use bottom watering method
3. Remove severely wilted leaves
4. Move to shaded area for recovery
5. Increase surrounding air humidity

**Bottom Watering Steps**
1. Prepare lukewarm water (room temperature)
2. Place pot in water, water level to one-third of soil height
3. Soak for 20-30 minutes
4. Remove and drain excess water in ventilated area
5. Resume normal care afterward

### Water Quality Issues

**Identifying Symptoms**
- Spots or burns on leaves
- White deposits on soil surface
- Slow growth
- Yellowing between leaf veins

**Possible Causes**
- Using untreated tap water
- High mineral or salt content in water
- Using dishwashing or laundry water

**Solutions**
1. Let tap water sit for 24 hours before use
2. Collect rainwater or use purified water
3. Periodically flush soil with clean water
4. Switch to well-aerating soil mix

## 2. Light-Related Problems

### Excessive Light / Sunburn

**Identifying Symptoms**
- Burn marks on leaves
- Leaf edges turning brown and crispy
- Faded, whitened leaf color
- Dehydrated, wilting leaves
- Stunted growth

**Possible Causes**
- Direct summer sunlight
- Sudden move from indoors to outdoors
- Glass focusing sunlight causing burns
- Grow lights placed too close

**Solutions**
1. Move to shaded area immediately
2. Remove severely burned leaves
3. Keep soil moist
4. Gradually increase light exposure to avoid further burns
5. Provide shade during summer

### Insufficient Light

**Identifying Symptoms**
- Faded, yellowing leaf color
- Elongated stems (leggy growth)
- Smaller leaves
- Slow or stunted growth
- Light-colored new leaves
- Flowering plants not blooming
- Leaves tilting toward light source

**Possible Causes**
- Placement too dark
- Not rotating plant position regularly
- Windows blocked
- Short daylight hours in winter

**Solutions**
1. Gradually move to brighter location
2. Rotate pot regularly for even light exposure
3. Use grow lights to supplement
4. Prune leggy growth
5. Reduce watering for leggy plants

**Grow Light Recommendations**
- Choose full-spectrum grow lights
- Supplement 4-6 hours daily
- Keep 8-16 inches (20-40 cm) from plants
- Avoid placing too close causing burns

## 3. Temperature-Related Problems

### Heat Damage

**Identifying Symptoms**
- Wilting, drooping leaves
- Brown leaf edges
- Brown spots on leaves
- Growth stagnation
- Deformed or dropping fruit

**Possible Causes**
- No shade during summer heat
- Near heating vents or air conditioning
- Pot placed near heat-emitting objects
- Temperature too high in enclosed vehicles

**Solutions**
1. Move to cool, ventilated area
2. Mist leaves to cool down
3. Increase watering frequency (only for heat-tolerant plants)
4. Use shade cloth or curtains
5. Keep away from heat sources

### Cold/Frost Damage

**Identifying Symptoms**
- Leaves becoming soft and weeping
- Darkened, blackened leaf color
- Stem softening and rotting
- Whole plant wilting
- New leaves falling off

**Possible Causes**
- Forgot to bring indoors during winter
- Forgot to close windows at night
- Placed near doors or windows with cold drafts
- Heating suddenly stopped

**Solutions**
1. Immediately move to warm area (above 50°F / 10°C)
2. Do not water immediately
3. Remove severely frost-damaged leaves and stems
4. Keep soil relatively dry
5. Avoid sun exposure, gradually adapt to warmth
6. Wait 24 hours before light watering

### Excessive Temperature Fluctuations

**Identifying Symptoms**
- Deformed leaves
- Damaged growing points
- Leaf drop
- Flower petals damaged during blooming

**Possible Causes**
- Day-night temperature difference exceeding 59°F (15°C)
- Intense sun during day, cold nights
- Direct hot or cold air drafts

**Solutions**
1. Adjust placement location
2. Avoid direct hot or cold air drafts
3. Move indoors at night during winter
4. Gradually adapt to temperature changes

## 4. Fertilization-Related Problems

### Over-Fertilization (Fertilizer Burn)

**Identifying Symptoms**
- Brown, crispy leaf edges or tips
- Water-soaked spots on leaf surface
- White salt deposits on soil surface
- Thickened, deformed leaves
- Whole plant wilting
- Roots turning brown

**Possible Causes**
- Fertilizer concentration too high
- Using uncomposted organic fertilizer
- Fertilizing too frequently
- Fertilizing when soil is dry

**Solutions**
1. Immediately flush soil with large amount of water
2. Repeat flushing 2-3 times
3. Place in ventilated area to dry
4. If severe, repot with fresh soil
5. Prune wilted portions
6. Reduce fertilizer amount afterward

### Under-Fertilization

**Identifying Symptoms**
- Faded leaf color
- Smaller leaves
- Slow growth
- Lower leaves turning yellow and dropping
- Smaller flowers on flowering plants
- Thinner stems

**Possible Causes**
- Long period without fertilizing
- Poor soil quality
- Vigorous plant growth consuming nutrients
- No base fertilizer added during repotting

**Solutions**
1. Supplement nitrogen to promote leaf growth
2. Light and frequent fertilization, avoid high concentrations
3. Increase fertilization frequency during growing season
4. Regularly repot with fresh soil to replenish nutrients

## 5. Pest and Disease Problems

### Pest Identification and Treatment

#### Aphids

**Identifying Features**
- Small green, black, or brown insects
- Clustering on new growth, flower buds, leaf undersides
- Curling and deforming leaves
- Sticky honeydew (ants love this)

**Solutions**
1. Rinse leaves with clean water
2. Wipe with cotton swab dipped in alcohol
3. Spray with diluted soap water
4. For severe cases, use imidacloprid
5. Introduce natural predators like ladybugs

#### Spider Mites

**Identifying Features**
- Tiny red mites
- Fine white dots appearing on leaves
- Webbing on leaf undersides
- Dry, falling leaves

**Solutions**
1. Increase air humidity
2. Rinse leaf undersides with water
3. Spray with neem oil or horticultural oil
4. Use abamectin or other miticides
5. Isolate affected plants to prevent spread

#### Scale Insects

**Identifying Features**
- Brown or white round insects
- Fixed on leaves or stems
- Yellow spots on leaves
- Sticky secretions

**Solutions**
1. Scrape off with toothbrush or cotton swab
2. Wipe with alcohol
3. Spray with horticultural oil or malathion
4. For severe cases, soak entire plant

#### Whiteflies

**Identifying Features**
- Small white flying insects
- Scatter when disturbed
- Eggs laid on leaf undersides
- Yellowing leaves, poor growth

**Solutions**
1. Use yellow sticky traps
2. Spray with diluted soap water
3. Use imidacloprid or acetamiprid
4. Maintain good ventilation

### Disease Identification and Treatment

#### Leaf Spot Disease

**Identifying Features**
- Brown, black, or gray spots on leaves
- Spots gradually expanding
- Leaves dying in severe cases
- May show concentric rings

**Solutions**
1. Remove and destroy affected leaves
2. Reduce environmental humidity
3. Improve ventilation
4. Spray with carbendazim or chlorothalonil
5. Avoid watering on leaves

#### Root Rot

**Identifying Features**
- Roots turning black and mushy
- Discolored stem base
- Wilting leaves
- Foul smell from soil

**Solutions**
1. Repot immediately to inspect
2. Trim away rotted roots
3. Soak with carbendazim for disinfection
4. Replace with fresh soil
5. Control watering, maintain ventilation

#### Powdery Mildew

**Identifying Features**
- White powdery substance on leaf surface
- Curling and deforming leaves
- Affecting photosynthesis
- More common in high humidity

**Solutions**
1. Remove severely affected leaves
2. Spray with baking soda solution (5g/L)
3. Use sulfur powder or chlorothalonil
4. Increase ventilation, reduce humidity

#### Viral Diseases

**Identifying Features**
- Mottled or striped patterns on leaves
- Deformed leaves
- Stunted growth
- Abnormal flowering

**Solutions**
1. No effective treatment
2. Destroy affected plants to prevent spread
3. Disinfect tools
4. Use healthy seedlings

## 6. Physiological Disorders

### Nutrient Deficiency

#### Nitrogen Deficiency
- Symptoms: Older leaves turn yellow first, pale color
- Solution: Apply urea or nitrogen fertilizer

#### Phosphorus Deficiency
- Symptoms: Dark green but dull leaves, slow growth
- Solution: Apply phosphorus fertilizer or bone meal

#### Potassium Deficiency
- Symptoms: Brown leaf edges, older leaves affected first
- Solution: Apply potassium fertilizer or wood ash

#### Iron Deficiency
- Symptoms: New leaves with green veins, yellowing leaf tissue
- Solution: Apply ferrous sulfate

#### Magnesium Deficiency
- Symptoms: Yellowing between veins, green veins
- Solution: Apply magnesium sulfate

#### Calcium Deficiency
- Symptoms: Deformed new leaves, dying growing points
- Solution: Apply calcium nitrate

### Element Toxicity
- Symptoms: Brown spots, deformed leaves
- Cause: Over-fertilization or improper use
- Solution: Flush with large amount of water, repot if severe

## 7. Environmental Adaptation Problems

### Repotting Stress

**Identifying Symptoms**
- Temporary leaf wilting
- Minor leaf drop
- Temporary growth stagnation

**Treatment**
1. Place in shaded area for recovery 1-2 weeks after repotting
2. Keep soil slightly moist
3. Do not fertilize immediately
4. Avoid direct sunlight
5. Wait for plant recovery before normal care

### Shipping/Transport Stress

**Identifying Symptoms**
- Leaf drop
- Wilting stems and leaves
- Falling flower buds

**Treatment**
1. Place in warm, bright area
2. Keep soil moist
3. Do not repot or fertilize
4. Be patient for recovery
5. Can gradually resume normal care after 1-2 weeks

### Seasonal Transition Stress

**Identifying Symptoms**
- Spring: Slow new growth
- Summer: Heat-induced dormancy
- Fall: Leaf discoloration or drop
- Winter: Growth stagnation

**Treatment**
1. Adjust care according to season
2. Avoid sudden environmental changes
3. Gradually adapt to temperature changes
4. Adjust watering and fertilization accordingly

## 8. Special Problems

### Flowering Problems

#### Not Blooming
- Insufficient light: Increase light
- Too much nitrogen: Reduce nitrogen, increase phosphorus-potassium
- Not yet mature: Be patient
- Improper pruning: Prune correctly to promote flower buds

#### Flower/Bud Drop
- Sudden environmental change: Maintain stability
- Improper watering: Adjust watering
- Insufficient nutrients: Supplement nutrients
- Poor ventilation: Improve ventilation

### Fruit Problems

#### Fruit Drop
- Sudden water changes: Maintain stability
- Nutrient deficiency: Supplement nutrients
- Unsuitable environment: Improve conditions

### Leggy/Stretched Growth

**Identifying Features**
- Elongated, thin stems
- Increased spacing between leaves
- Loose overall plant form
- Faded color

**Causes**
- Insufficient light
- Too much nitrogen
- Overwatering

**Solutions**
1. Increase light
2. Reduce nitrogen
3. Control watering
4. Prune stretched portions
5. If severe, consider taking cuttings for propagation

## 9. Emergency Flowcharts

### Leaf Wilting Emergency Flowchart

```
Leaf Wilting
    │
    ├── Soil Dry? ── Yes ──→ Water Thoroughly / Bottom Water
    │                      │
    │                      └── Observe 2-4 Hours
    │                          │
    │                          └── Recovered? ── Yes ──→ Normal Care
    │                                              │
    │                                              └── No ──→ Check Roots
    │
    └── Soil Moist? ── Yes ──→ Check for Root Rot
                             │
                             ├── Root Rot ──→ Repot with Root Trimming
                             │
                             └── No Rot ──→ Increase Ventilation, Reduce Watering
```

### Yellowing Leaves Emergency Flowchart

```
Yellowing Leaves
    │
    ├── Old Leaves Yellow, New Leaves Normal?
    │       │
    │       ├── Yes ──→ Natural Aging, No Action Needed
    │       │
    │       └── No ──→ Check New Leaves
    │                   │
    │                   ├── New Leaves Also Yellow ──→ Iron Deficiency or Overwatering
    │                   │                │
    │                   │                ├── Iron Deficiency ──→ Apply Ferrous Sulfate
    │                   │                │
    │                   │                └── Overwatering ──→ Control Water, Improve Ventilation
    │                   │
    │                   └── Young Leaves Wilting ──→ Possible Fertilizer Burn or Underwatering
    │                                   │
    │                                   └── Flush with Large Amount of Water
    │
    └── Leaf Edges Brown? ── Yes ──→ Excessive Light or Over-Fertilization
                            │
                            ├── Light Issue ──→ Move to Shaded Area
                            │
                            └── Fertilizer Issue ──→ Flush with Water
```

### Root Rot Emergency Flowchart

```
Signs of Root Rot (Wilting, Soft Stems, Foul Soil Smell)
    │
    ├── Remove from Pot Immediately
    │
    ├── Clear Old Soil
    │
    ├── Inspect Roots
    │   │
    │   ├── Rot < 50% ──→ Keep Healthy Roots
    │   │                │
    │   │                ├── Trim Rot
    │   │                ├── Soak in Carbendazim 20 Minutes
    │   │                ├── Air Dry
    │   │                └── Repot with Fresh Soil
    │   │
    │   └── Rot > 50% ──→ Keep Healthy Stems and Leaves
    │                       │
    │                       ├── Remove Rotted Roots and Most Leaves
    │                       ├── Soak in Fungicide
    │                       ├── Air Dry
    │                       └── Propagate via Cuttings
    │
    └── Post-Repotting Care
            │
            ├── Place in Shaded Ventilated Area
            ├── Control Watering
            ├── No Fertilizer
            └── Wait for New Root Growth (About 2-4 Weeks)
```
