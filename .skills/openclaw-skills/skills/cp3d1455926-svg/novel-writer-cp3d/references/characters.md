# Character Management Guide

## Character Profile Template

For each major character, track:

### Basic Information
- **Name**: Character name
- **Role**: Protagonist, antagonist, supporting, etc.
- **Age**: Character age
- **Occupation**: What they do
- **Appearance**: Physical description

### Personality
- **Traits**: Key personality characteristics
- **Strengths**: What they're good at
- **Weaknesses**: Flaws and vulnerabilities
- **Motivations**: What drives them
- **Fears**: What they fear

### Background
- **History**: Past experiences
- **Family**: Relationships
- **Key Events**: Defining moments

### Relationships
- **Allies**: Friends, supporters
- **Enemies**: Antagonists, rivals
- **Romantic**: Love interests
- **Mentors**: Teachers, guides

### Development Arc
- **Starting State**: Who they are at the beginning
- **Goal**: What they want
- **Obstacles**: What's in their way
- **Transformation**: How they change
- **Ending State**: Who they become

## Character Consistency Checklist

When writing a character, verify:
- [ ] Voice matches personality
- [ ] Actions align with motivations
- [ ] Dialogue fits their background
- [ ] Relationships are consistent
- [ ] Development is gradual and believable
- [ ] Weaknesses aren't forgotten
- [ ] Strengths aren't exaggerated

## Common Character Issues

1. **Inconsistent Voice**: Character sounds different in different chapters
2. **Sudden Changes**: Personality shifts without development
3. **Forgotten Traits**: Established characteristics disappear
4. **Plot Device**: Character acts only to advance plot
5. **Perfect Character**: No flaws or weaknesses
