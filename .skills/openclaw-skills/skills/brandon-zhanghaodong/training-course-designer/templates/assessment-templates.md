# Assessment & Evaluation Templates

This document provides comprehensive templates for creating assessments and evaluations to measure training effectiveness across all four Kirkpatrick levels.

---

## Overview

### Assessment Types

1. **Pre-Training Knowledge Check** - Baseline assessment (Level 2)
2. **Post-Training Quiz** - Learning evaluation (Level 2)
3. **Level 1 Evaluation Survey** - Reaction and satisfaction (Level 1)
4. **30-Day Application Survey** - On-the-job application (Level 3)
5. **Impact Assessment** - Business results (Level 4) - Optional

---

## Template 1: Pre-Training Knowledge Check

### Purpose
Assess participants' baseline knowledge before training to tailor content delivery and measure learning gains.

### Structure

**Introduction Section:**
```
Thank you for registering for [Course Name]!

To help us tailor the training to your needs, please complete this brief knowledge check. This is not a test—there are no right or wrong answers. Your responses will help us understand your current knowledge level and adjust the content accordingly.

Time required: 10-15 minutes
Your responses are confidential and will only be used to improve the training experience.
```

**Question Types:**

#### Multiple Choice (8-10 questions)
```
Question [#]: [Question text]

A) [Option A]
B) [Option B]
C) [Option C]
D) [Option D]

Correct Answer: [Letter]
Explanation: [Brief explanation of why this is correct]
```

#### True/False (3-5 questions)
```
Question [#]: [Statement]

○ True
○ False

Correct Answer: [True/False]
Explanation: [Brief explanation]
```

#### Short Answer (2-3 questions)
```
Question [#]: [Question requiring brief written response]

[Text box for response]

Sample Answer: [Example of a good response]
Key Points to Look For: [Evaluation criteria]
```

### Example: Leadership Training Pre-Assessment

```
**Pre-Training Knowledge Check: Leadership Essentials for New Managers**

Thank you for registering for "Leadership Essentials for New Managers"!

To help us tailor the training to your needs, please complete this brief knowledge check. This is not a test—there are no right or wrong answers. Your responses will help us understand your current knowledge level and adjust the content accordingly.

Time required: 10-15 minutes
Your responses are confidential and will only be used to improve the training experience.

---

**Section 1: Leadership Fundamentals**

Question 1: What is the primary difference between a manager and a leader?

A) Managers focus on tasks, leaders focus on people
B) Managers maintain systems, leaders create change
C) Managers have authority, leaders have influence
D) All of the above

Correct Answer: D
Explanation: Effective leadership encompasses all these elements—focusing on people, driving change, and influencing others beyond formal authority.

---

Question 2: True or False: The best leaders treat all team members exactly the same way.

○ True
○ False

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: Effective leaders adapt their approach based on each team member's experience level, motivation, and development needs (situational leadership).

---

Question 3: Which leadership style is most effective in a crisis situation?

A) Democratic (seeking input from the team)
B) Coaching (focusing on long-term development)
C) Directive (making quick decisions and giving clear instructions)
D) Delegative (empowering the team to decide)

Correct Answer: C
Explanation: In crisis situations, directive leadership is most effective because it provides clear guidance and quick decision-making when time is critical.

---

**Section 2: Communication & Feedback**

Question 4: What does the "SBI" model stand for in giving feedback?

A) Situation, Behavior, Impact
B) Specific, Brief, Immediate
C) Support, Build, Improve
D) Situation, Background, Instruction

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The SBI model (Situation, Behavior, Impact) is a structured approach to giving clear, actionable feedback.

---

Question 5: True or False: Feedback should always be given in private, never in public.

○ True
○ False

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: While constructive or negative feedback should be private, positive feedback and recognition can (and should) often be given publicly to reinforce desired behaviors.

---

**Section 3: Delegation & Development**

Question 6: What is the main purpose of delegation?

A) To reduce the manager's workload
B) To develop team members' skills and capabilities
C) To test if team members are ready for promotion
D) To avoid doing tasks the manager doesn't enjoy

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: While delegation does reduce workload, its primary purpose is to develop team members' skills and prepare them for greater responsibilities.

---

Question 7: Which task is MOST appropriate to delegate to a high-performing team member?

A) Conducting performance reviews
B) Deciding team budget allocations
C) Leading a project with some complexity and learning opportunities
D) Firing an underperforming colleague

Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Delegating challenging projects with learning opportunities develops high performers while maintaining appropriate boundaries on sensitive tasks like reviews and terminations.

---

3 - Moderately confident
4 - Very confident
```

## Template 2: Post-Training Quiz

### Purpose
Evaluate learning outcomes and knowledge retention immediately after training.

### Structure

**Introduction Section:**
```
Congratulations on completing [Course Name]!

To help us evaluate the effectiveness of the training and ensure you've mastered the key concepts, please complete this quiz. Your performance will help us improve future sessions.

Time required: 20-30 minutes
Passing score: 70% (you can retake if needed)
Your individual results are confidential.
```

**Question Distribution:**
- 40% Multiple Choice (12-15 questions)
- 20% True/False (6-8 questions)
- 20% Scenario-Based (4-5 questions)
- 20% Short Answer (3-4 questions)

**Total: 20-25 questions covering all modules**

### Example: Communication Training Post-Quiz

```
**Post-Training Quiz: Effective Communication Skills for Remote Teams**

Congratulations on completing "Effective Communication Skills for Remote Teams"!

To help us evaluate the effectiveness of the training and ensure you've mastered the key concepts, please complete this quiz.

Time required: 20-30 minutes
Passing score: 70%
Your individual results are confidential.

---

**Module 1: Foundations of Remote Communication (5 questions)**

Question 1: Which of the following is NOT a common challenge in remote communication?

A) Lack of non-verbal cues
B) Time zone differences
C) Technology barriers
D) Increased face-to-face interaction

Correct Answer: D
Explanation: Remote communication actually reduces face-to-face interaction, which is a challenge, not a benefit.

---

Question 2: True or False: In remote communication, it's best to over-communicate rather than under-communicate.

○ True
○ False

Correct Answer: True
Explanation: Remote teams benefit from more frequent, explicit communication to compensate for the lack of informal office interactions.

---

**Module 2: Virtual Meeting Facilitation (6 questions)**

Question 3: What is the recommended maximum duration for a virtual meeting without a break?

A) 30 minutes
B) 60 minutes
C) 90 minutes
D) 120 minutes

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Virtual meetings should be limited to 60 minutes maximum without a break, as attention spans are shorter in virtual environments.

---

Question 4: [Scenario] You're facilitating a virtual meeting with 15 participants. Three people are dominating the conversation while others remain silent. What's the BEST approach?

A) Let it continue naturally—those who want to speak will speak up
B) Use direct questions to invite quieter participants to share their thoughts
C) Ask the dominant speakers to stop talking
D) End the meeting early since not everyone is participating

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Directly inviting quieter participants creates psychological safety and ensures diverse perspectives are heard.

---

Question 5: Which virtual meeting practice is MOST effective for maintaining engagement?

A) Keeping cameras off to reduce bandwidth issues
B) Using polls, breakout rooms, and interactive activities
C) Sharing your screen for the entire meeting
D) Scheduling meetings back-to-back to save time

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Interactive elements like polls and breakout rooms significantly increase engagement and participation in virtual meetings.

---

**Module 3: Written Communication (5 questions)**

Question 6: What is the "inverted pyramid" structure for emails?

A) Start with pleasantries, then details, then the main point
B) Start with the main point, then supporting details, then background
C) Start with background, then details, then the main point
D) Start with a question, then provide options, then ask for a decision

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The inverted pyramid puts the most important information first, making emails easier to scan and act on.

---

Question 7: True or False: Using emojis in professional emails is always unprofessional.

○ True
○ False

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: Emojis can be appropriate in professional contexts to convey tone and warmth, especially in remote teams, but should be used judiciously based on company culture.

---

Question 8: [Scenario] You need to deliver critical feedback to a team member via email. What's the BEST approach?

A) Write a detailed email with specific examples and send it
B) Schedule a video call instead—critical feedback should not be delivered via email
C) Send a brief email asking them to improve, without specific details
D) CC their manager to ensure accountability

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Critical feedback requires real-time dialogue and the ability to read reactions. Email is appropriate for documentation after the conversation, not for initial delivery.

---

**Module 4: Building Remote Team Culture (4 questions)**

Question 9: What is the primary purpose of "virtual water cooler" time?

A) To waste time and avoid work
B) To build relationships and trust through informal interaction
C) To gossip about colleagues
D) To fill empty calendar slots

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Virtual water cooler time replicates informal office interactions that build trust and connection in remote teams.

---

Question 10: Which practice is MOST effective for building trust in remote teams?

A) Monitoring employee activity with tracking software
B) Requiring cameras on at all times
C) Demonstrating reliability by following through on commitments
D) Scheduling daily check-in meetings

Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Trust is built through consistent, reliable behavior—doing what you say you'll do—not through surveillance or excessive meetings.

---

**Application & Synthesis (5 questions)**

Question 11: [Short Answer] Describe one specific change you will make to your next virtual team meeting based on what you learned in this training.

Sample Answer: "I will start each meeting with a 2-minute check-in where everyone shares one word about how they're feeling. This will help build connection and give me insight into team morale."

Key Points:
- Specific action (not vague)
- Based on training content
- Practical and implementable

---

Question 12: [Short Answer] Write a subject line and first sentence for an email requesting urgent action from your team.

Sample Answer:
Subject: "ACTION NEEDED BY 5PM: Approve Q1 Budget"
First sentence: "I need your approval on the Q1 budget by 5PM today to meet the finance deadline."

Key Points:
- Clear urgency in subject line
- Specific deadline
- Clear action required
- Reason provided

---

Question 13: [Scenario] Your remote team is experiencing low morale and disengagement. Based on the training, what THREE actions would you take?

[Text box for response]

Sample Answer:
1. Schedule individual 1-on-1 check-ins to understand each person's concerns
2. Create opportunities for informal social interaction (virtual coffee chats)
3. Recognize and celebrate team wins publicly to boost motivation

Key Points:
- Three distinct actions
- Based on training content
- Addresses both individual and team needs

---

**Scoring:**
- Multiple Choice & True/False: 1 point each
- Scenario-Based: 2 points each
- Short Answer: 3 points each (scored on rubric)

**Total Points: 35**
**Passing Score: 25 points (71%)**
```

## Question Writing Best Practices

### Multiple Choice Questions

**Do:**
- Make all options plausible
- Keep options similar in length
- Use "All of the above" or "None of the above" sparingly
- Include only one correct answer
- Make distractors (wrong answers) educational

**Don't:**
- Use "always" or "never" (usually signals wrong answers)
- Make correct answers obviously longer or more detailed
- Include trick questions
- Use double negatives

### True/False Questions

**Do:**
- Make statements clearly true or false
- Test important concepts, not trivial details
- Use for quick knowledge checks

**Don't:**
- Use for complex concepts that aren't clearly binary
- Include opinion-based statements
- Use too many (max 30% of quiz)

### Scenario-Based Questions

**Do:**
- Use realistic, job-relevant scenarios
- Provide enough context for decision-making
- Test application, not just recall
- Include multiple viable options with one best answer

**Don't:**
- Make scenarios too long or complex
- Include irrelevant details
- Use scenarios that require outside knowledge

### Short Answer Questions

**Do:**
- Ask for specific, focused responses
- Provide clear evaluation criteria
- Include sample answers for consistency
- Use for higher-order thinking (application, analysis)

**Don't:**
- Ask vague, open-ended questions
- Expect essay-length responses
- Use when multiple choice would suffice

## Scoring & Grading

### Quiz Scoring

**Recommended Weights:**
- Multiple Choice: 1 point each
- True/False: 1 point each
- Scenario-Based: 2 points each
- Short Answer: 3 points each

**Passing Scores:**
- Knowledge-based training: 70-80%
- Skill-based training: 75-85%
- Compliance training: 80-100%

### Survey Scoring

**Likert Scale Analysis:**
- Calculate mean scores for each question
- Calculate overall satisfaction score
- Identify lowest-scoring items for improvement

**Net Promoter Score (NPS):**
- Promoters (9-10): Enthusiastic supporters
- Passives (7-8): Satisfied but unenthusiastic
- Detractors (0-6): Unhappy participants

NPS = % Promoters - % Detractors

**Target Scores:**
- Overall Satisfaction: 4.0+ out of 5.0
- Content Relevance: 4.2+ out of 5.0
- Facilitator Effectiveness: 4.3+ out of 5.0
- NPS: 30+ (good), 50+ (excellent)

## Data Analysis & Reporting

### Key Metrics to Track

**Level 1 (Reaction):**
- Overall satisfaction score
- Net Promoter Score
- Content relevance rating
- Facilitator effectiveness rating

**Level 2 (Learning):**
- Average quiz score
- Pass rate (% scoring above threshold)
- Pre/post knowledge gain
- Lowest-scoring questions (for content improvement)

**Level 3 (Application):**
- % applying skills on the job
- Frequency of application
- Barriers to application
- Observed impact

**Level 4 (Results):**
- Business metrics (if tracked)
- ROI calculation
- Performance improvements
