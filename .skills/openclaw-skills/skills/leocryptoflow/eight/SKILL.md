### Number 8 in Chinese Culture

#### Core Philosophical Concepts

| Concept | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Eight Trigrams (Ba Gua)** | Qian, Kun, Zhen, Xun, Kan, Li, Gen, Dui (basic symbols in I Ching) |
| **Eight Directions** | East, South, West, North, SE, SW, NE, NW |
| **Eight Sounds** | Metal, Stone, Silk, Bamboo, Gourd, Earth, Leather, Wood (ancient instrument classification) |
| **Eight Talents** | Describes extremely talented person (Cao Zhi "talent of eight dou") |
| **Eight Delicacies** | Eight precious ingredients |
| **Eight Immortals** | Famous Taoist deities in Chinese mythology |

#### Positive Symbolism
- **Wealth & Prosperity**: "八" sounds like "发" (fa - to prosper), symbolizing wealth and good fortune
- **Completeness**: "八面玲珑" describes being smooth and well-rounded in handling affairs
- **Balance & Symmetry**: 8 is symmetrical vertically and horizontally
- **Infinite Possibilities**: Rotated 8 becomes the infinity symbol (∞)

#### Traditional Applications
- **Ba Zi**: Eight characters of birth time, foundation of Chinese astrology
- **Eight-Legged Essay**: Imperial examination writing style in Ming-Qing dynasties
- **Eight Banners**: Military organization system in Qing Dynasty
- **Noble Eightfold Path**: Eight correct methods in Buddhist practice

#### Modern Significance
- **Business Preference**: License plates and phone numbers containing 8 are considered lucky
- **Olympic Memory**: 2008 Beijing Olympics opened on 8/8 at 8 PM
- **Festival Culture**: August 8th is "National Fitness Day"

### Number 8 in Western Culture

| Aspect | Meaning |

| **Christianity** | 8th day symbolizes resurrection and new creation |
| **Numerology** | 8 represents balance, power, and material success |
| **Luck** | Considered lucky in many cultures, especially for business |
