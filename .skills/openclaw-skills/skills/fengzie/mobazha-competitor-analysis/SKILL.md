# Competitor Analysis

Research and analyze competitor products, customer reviews, and market positioning to make informed decisions for your Mobazha store.

## When to Use

- Deciding what products to sell or add next
- Setting prices for new or existing products
- Understanding why competitors succeed (or fail)
- Finding market gaps to exploit
- Improving your product descriptions and positioning

## What It Produces

| Output | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Market Overview** | Key players, price ranges, positioning |
| **Review Insights** | What customers love and hate about competitors |
| **Pricing Analysis** | Price ranges, value perception, sweet spots |
| **Gap Analysis** | Underserved needs and opportunities |
| **Action Plan** | Specific recommendations for your store |

## Workflow

### Step 1: Define Scope

Tell the agent what you want to research:

> "Analyze the market for [product category]. I'm selling on Mobazha and want to understand how competitors on [Amazon/Etsy/Shopify stores] are doing."

The agent will confirm:

- Product category / niche
- Competitor platforms to research
- Whether this is your product or a category you're entering
- Known competitors (if any)

### Step 2: Gather Data

The agent researches using web access:

| Source | What to Collect |
|--------|----------------|
| **Competitor listings** | Titles, prices, descriptions, images |
| **Customer reviews** | Ratings, top complaints, top praise |
| **Pricing** | Range, average, premium vs budget tiers |
| **Product features** | What's included, quality indicators |
| **Marketing claims** | How they position themselves |

### Step 3: Analyze

#### Review Analysis

Analyze competitor reviews to find patterns:

- **Top complaints** — Problems you can solve
- **Top praise** — Table-stakes features you must match
- **Feature requests** — Unmet needs you can fill
- **Price objections** — Opportunities for better value
- **Quality mentions** — Materials, durability, sizing issues

#### Pricing Analysis

| Tier | Price Range | Value Perception |
|------|------------|-----------------|
| Budget | $X - $Y | Basic, entry-level |
| Mid-range | $X - $Y | Good balance of quality/price |
| Premium | $X - $Y | High quality, brand premium |

Where should your product sit and why?

#### Differentiation Opportunities

Based on the analysis, identify:

1. **Quality gaps** — Can you offer better materials/craftsmanship?
2. **Price gaps** — Is there a sweet spot no one fills?
3. **Feature gaps** — What's missing that buyers want?
4. **Trust gaps** — Can Mobazha's escrow/privacy be an advantage?
5. **Niche gaps** — Underserved segments within the category

### Step 4: Deliver Recommendations

#### Quick Actions

Changes you can make immediately:

- Adjust pricing based on market data
- Update descriptions to address competitor weaknesses
- Add features/variants that competitors lack
- Highlight your Mobazha-specific advantages

#### Strategic Moves

Longer-term positioning:

- Products to add to your catalog
- Collections to create
- Messaging angles that differentiate
- Markets or audiences to target

## Mobazha-Specific Competitive Angles

When positioning against products on centralized platforms, highlight:

| Their Weakness | Your Advantage |
|---------------|---------------|
| Platform fees inflate prices | No middleman — better price for buyers |
| Data harvested and sold | Privacy-first — no tracking |
| Platform can suspend sellers | Censorship-resistant storefront |
| Payment processor restrictions | Crypto + fiat flexibility |
| Counterfeit concerns | Direct from verified seller with escrow |
