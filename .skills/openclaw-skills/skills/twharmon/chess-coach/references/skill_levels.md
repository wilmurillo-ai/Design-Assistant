# Chess Skill Levels and Coaching Focus

This document identifies focus areas for coaching based on the user's current rating on Chess.com.

## 0-800: Beginner
- **Focus**: Piece safety, basic tactics (hanging pieces, simple forks/skewers), and fundamental checkmates (King + Queen, King + Rook).
- **Avoid**: Complex opening theory or high-level endgame theory.
- **Motto**: "Look for hanging pieces, every move."

## 800-1200: Improving Beginner
- **Focus**: Opening principles (center control, development, castling), tactical patterns (pins, discoveries), basic endgames (King + Pawn).
- **Practice**: Analyzing the why behind every blunder.

## 1200-1600: Intermediate
- **Focus**: Positional concepts (weak squares, pawn structure), simplified opening repertoires, more advanced tactics.
- **Analysis**: Identifying tactical shots that were missed in longer games.

## 1600-2000: Advanced
- **Focus**: Deep opening preparation, refined positional understanding, endgame technique.
- **Motto**: "Small advantages count."

## 2000+: Expert/Master
- **Focus**: Identifying nuances, engine comparisons, specific professional game study.
