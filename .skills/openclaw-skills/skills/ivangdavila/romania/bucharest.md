# Bucharest — Entry Hub and City Strategy

## Why It Works

Bucharest is best used as:
- the easiest international entry point
- a strong food city
- a two-night urban reset before or after Transylvania

It is not the prettiest part of Romania, but it is useful, lively, and much stronger than its reputation if the user likes city texture.

## Best Uses

### 1. First 24-48 Hours
- recover from arrival
- eat well
- visit Calea Victoriei, the Romanian Athenaeum area, and a museum
- leave Old Town for a short walk, not necessarily for sleeping

### 2. Food-Focused Stop
- Obor Market for mici and market energy
- Caru' cu Bere for the historic room more than quiet intimacy
- Lacrimi si Sfinti or Zexe for stronger Romanian food framing

### 3. Easy Day-Trip Launch Point
- Sinaia and Peles
- Snagov
- Mogosoaia

## Where to Stay

| Area | Best for | Watch out for |
|------|----------|---------------|
| Calea Victoriei | First-timers, walkability, museums | Higher hotel prices |
| Cotroceni | Quieter, leafy, local feel | Less nightlife |
| Floreasca | Dining and modern city vibe | Less classic sightseeing feel |
| Old Town | Nightlife and late energy | Noise, crowds, weak sleep |

## What To Prioritize

- Village Museum if the user wants Romanian vernacular culture fast
- Revolution Square for modern history context
- Parliament only if they care about state scale or communist-era legacy
- Therme if they want a leisure day and do not mind a polished, popular venue

## When To Cut It Short

If the user has only 4-5 nights total in Romania, do not let Bucharest eat the trip. One or two nights is usually enough before moving to Brasov or Sibiu.

## Local Traps

- staying in Lipscani and expecting quiet
- relying on street taxis instead of Bolt or Uber
- assuming every district is equally walkable for visitors
- treating airport-to-center timing as fixed during rush hour
