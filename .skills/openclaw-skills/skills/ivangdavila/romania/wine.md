# Romania Wine and Drinks

## Why Romania Drinks Better Than Many Travelers Expect

Romania is a real wine country, not just a cheap-beer stop. Good local bottles often overdeliver for the price.

## Grapes To Know

| Grape | Style |
|-------|-------|
| Feteasca Regala | Fresh, versatile white |
| Feteasca Alba | Light aromatic white |
| Feteasca Neagra | Signature red, spicy and dark-fruited |
| Tamaioasa Romaneasca | Floral, often sweeter white |
| Busuioaca de Bohotin | Fragrant rose-toned wine |

## Regions To Mention

- Dealu Mare for reds and accessible wine framing
- Dragasani for stronger modern winery interest
- Cotnari for traditional sweet-wine reputation
- Transylvanian plateau for cooler-climate styles
- Dobrogea and Murfatlar for coast-adjacent production

## Other Drinks

### Tuica and Palinca
- fruit brandies
- stronger than many visitors expect
- often offered in homes or rural guesthouses

### Visinata
- sour-cherry liqueur
- sweeter and easier for cautious drinkers

### Beer
- local lagers are everywhere
- craft beer is strongest in Bucharest and Cluj

## Ordering Guidance

- recommend Romanian wine before imported bottles at the same price point
- for food-heavy dinners, Feteasca Neagra or a local white often makes more sense than defaulting to international labels
- warn the user that homemade spirits can be much stronger than they feel
