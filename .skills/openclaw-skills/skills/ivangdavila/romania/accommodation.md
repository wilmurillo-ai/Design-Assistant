# Romania Accommodation — Where to Stay

## General Rule

Choose the base first, then the property type:
- city hotel for short logistics-heavy stops
- pension or guesthouse for scenery and slower pace
- apartment only when the user values space or self-catering more than service

## Best Base by Trip Style

| Trip style | Best bases |
|------------|------------|
| First Romania trip | Bucharest + Brasov |
| Slow culture | Sibiu + Brasov |
| Urban food and nightlife | Bucharest + Cluj-Napoca |
| Family summer | Brasov or coast, depending on dates |
| Hiking-led | Brasov area, Sinaia, or a mountain village base |

## City Stay Logic

### Bucharest
- central but not deep inside the loudest Old Town blocks
- prioritize air conditioning in summer

### Brasov
- old-town proximity for atmosphere
- edge-of-town or Poiana options if parking or winter access matters

### Sibiu
- old town if the user wants charm
- outer ring if the user is self-driving

### Cluj-Napoca
- central for short stays
- confirm event calendars because festival dates distort value fast

## Property Type Guidance

- Pensions are often better than generic midrange hotels outside major cities
- Rural stays can be excellent for food and warmth, but weaker for language consistency or late-night flexibility
- In shoulder season, confirm heating, not just aesthetics

## Booking Traps

- overvaluing "castle view" marketing when the route is the real priority
- sleeping in noisy party strips for the sake of being central
- underestimating parking friction in old centers
