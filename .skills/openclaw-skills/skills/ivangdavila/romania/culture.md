# Romania Culture and Etiquette

## Core Social Feel

Romanians are often warmer and more direct than visitors expect. Hospitality matters, and so does practical common sense.

## Useful Social Rules

### Greetings
- "Buna" works casually
- a handshake is normal in formal or first-meeting settings

### Homes
- expect to remove shoes in many homes
- bringing sweets, wine, or flowers is a good move
- give flowers in odd numbers, not even

### Meals
- portions can be large
- refusing homemade spirits is fine, but do it politely
- toasting matters more in homes than in casual city bars

## Important Cultural Notes

- Romanian and Roma are not interchangeable terms
- Bucharest and Cluj feel more western-facing; smaller towns can feel more traditional
- Orthodox holidays shape family rhythms and some closures
- older generations may be more formal, especially outside major cities

## Language Reality

- Romanian is the main language
- younger people in cities often speak solid English
- outside urban centers, basic Romanian phrases help a lot

## Useful Phrases

| English | Romanian |
|---------|----------|
| Hello | Buna |
| Thank you | Multumesc |
| Please | Va rog |
| Cheers | Noroc |
| The bill | Nota |
| How much? | Cat costa? |

## Etiquette Traps

- assuming every rural interaction will happen easily in English
- joking carelessly about communism or "Dracula" with strangers
- treating church visits casually without basic respect for dress and behavior
