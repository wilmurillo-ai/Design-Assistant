# Romania Transport — Realistic Route Tradeoffs

## The Core Rule

Romania transport is not one thing. The right answer changes by corridor.

## Best Defaults

### Rail
Best when:
- entering or exiting Bucharest
- connecting Bucharest and Brasov
- the user prefers not to drive

Reality check:
- some rail routes are slower than the map suggests
- comfort and speed vary

### Car
Best when:
- the route includes villages, mountain areas, or the Delta edge
- the user wants flexibility
- the trip leaves the easiest city-to-city spine

Reality check:
- roads can be slow
- overtaking culture can feel aggressive
- weather changes mountain driving fast

### Bus or Minivan
Useful when:
- rail is weak on a specific leg
- the user is price-sensitive
- the route is regional rather than cross-country

## Entry Points

| Airport | Best use |
|---------|----------|
| Bucharest OTP | Easiest first-time gateway |
| Cluj CLJ | West-side or urban-focused routes |
| Sibiu SBZ | Strong for southern Transylvania starts |

## Route Advice

- 3-5 days: stay on the Bucharest plus Brasov axis
- 7 days: add Sibiu or Cluj, not both plus the coast
- coast or Delta: simplify the rest of the trip

## Transport Traps

- thinking a short-distance road leg will be quick
- designing a route that depends on perfect train connections
- adding too many one-night stops because each town looks close on paper
