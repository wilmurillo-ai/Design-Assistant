# Recovery

Use this file when the user already bought a Groupon or the merchant experience has gone wrong.

## First classify the situation

Start with these questions:
- What kind of deal is it: local service, goods, getaway, or event?
- Is the voucher unused, booked, redeemed, shipped, or already disputed?
- Is the problem with Groupon policy, the merchant, the booking channel, or the quality of the service?

Do not promise a refund path until those three answers are clear.

## Common Recovery Paths

### Merchant cannot book or has no availability
1. Capture the attempted dates, channels used, and exact refusal.
2. Check whether the restriction was disclosed clearly.
3. If the merchant behavior conflicts with the published terms, prepare a support summary with dates, screenshots, and the specific mismatch.

### Merchant closed, moved, or looks inactive
1. Verify with current public signals, not one old review.
2. Save the evidence source and date.
3. Escalate through Groupon support with the merchant-status mismatch stated plainly.

### Deal value was overstated
1. Compare the claimed original value to the merchant's current or recent public pricing.
2. Separate "bad deal" from "misleading listing."
3. If the pricing looks misleading, document the comparison before contacting support.

### Quality problem after redemption
1. Record what was promised, what was delivered, and any on-site upsells.
2. Note whether the merchant tried to limit Groupon customers differently from regular customers.
3. Request remediation with a short fact pattern, not an emotional recap.

## Support Packet Template

```text
Deal:
Deal type:

Problem:
What the terms said:
What actually happened:

- screenshot
- booking attempt date
- merchant reply

```

## Refund and policy guidance

- Groupon policies can differ by deal type, timing, and whether the voucher has already been used or booked.
- Confirm the live help-page wording before telling the user that a refund is guaranteed.
- If the path is unclear, tell the user exactly what is known, what is not, and what evidence would strengthen the case.

## When to redirect away from Groupon

Recommend a non-Groupon alternative when:
- the merchant shows repeated redemption friction
- the same experience is similarly priced without voucher complexity
- the user's date window is tight and the deal adds too much uncertainty
- the support burden is already larger than the expected savings
