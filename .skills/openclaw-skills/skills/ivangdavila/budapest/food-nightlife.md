# Budapest Nightlife & Drinks

## The Ruin Bar Phenomenon

Budapest invented the ruin bar concept in early 2000s — abandoned buildings transformed into eclectic drinking spaces. Now world-famous.

### The Originals

**Szimpla Kert** (VII)
- The first, the legend
- Multi-room maze of eclectic furniture
- Now touristy but still essential
- Sunday farmers market
- Food: basic, overpriced
- **Go**: At least once, ideally weekday

**Instant-Fogas** (VII)
- Massive complex
- Multiple floors, themes, dance areas
- More club-like than Szimpla
- **Go**: If you want to party

**Ellátó Kert** (VII)
- More local crowd
- Less touristy
- Good vibes
- **Go**: Authentic ruin experience

**Anker't** (VII)
- Slightly upscale
- Good food alongside drinks
- Design-conscious
- **Go**: Elevated ruin bar

**Mazel Tov** (VII)
- Restaurant-bar hybrid
- Beautiful courtyard
- Excellent Middle Eastern food
- **Go**: Dinner + drinks

**Csendes** (V)
- Vintage ruin bar
- More quirky, less party
- Intellectual crowd
- **Go**: Chill alternative

### Beyond Ruin Bars

**Kuplung** (VII)
- Old parking garage
- More underground feel
- Less tourist

**Corvin Terasz** (VIII)
- Rooftop cinema vibes
- Great views
- More local

**Fekete Kutya** (VII)
- Older crowd
- No tourist horde
- Authentic neighborhood bar

## Wine Bars (Borozó)

Hungarian wine is excellent and underrated. Explore at these spots:

### Premium Wine Bars

**DiVino** (multiple locations)
- Best wine bar in city
- Excellent by-the-glass selection
- Good food too
- Perfect for learning Hungarian wine

**Doblo** (VII)
- Cellar atmosphere
- Focus on Hungarian wines
- Cheese + charcuterie
- Intimate, great for dates

**DROP** (VI)
- Modern, sleek
- Natural wines
- Food-forward

**Kadarka** (VII)
- Wine + food
- Named after grape variety
- Quality selection

### Budget Wine

**Borbiár** (V)
- Standing wine bar
- Very cheap
- Authentic experience

**Local borozós** (various)
- Traditional wine bars
- Old men drinking
- Very cheap, very authentic
- Find one, embrace it

### Wine 101

**Key Hungarian Wines**:
- Olaszrizling (dry white)
- Furmint (dry white, Tokaj)
- Kékfrankos (light red)
- Egri Bikavér (Bulls Blood blend)
- Tokaji Aszú (sweet dessert)
- Villányi reds (Cabernet, Merlot)

**Ordering**:
- "Fröccs" = wine spritzer (very Hungarian)
- "Száraz" = dry
- "Édes" = sweet

## Pálinka (Fruit Brandy)

Traditional Hungarian spirit. 40-70% alcohol. Take seriously.

### Types
- Barack (apricot) — most common
- Szilva (plum) — classic
- Körte (pear) — elegant
- Cseresznye (cherry) — fruity
- Ágyas (aged/on fruit bed) — premium

### Where to Try

**Rumpus Tiki Bar** (VII)
- Unexpected but good selection
- Fun atmosphere

**DiVino** (various)
- Good pálinka alongside wine

**Any traditional restaurant**
- After dinner tradition
- Ask for recommendation

### Etiquette
- Sipped, not shot
- Room temperature
- "Egészségedre!" (to your health)
- After meals traditionally

## Practical Tips

### Hours
- Bars: 6pm-2am typical
- Ruin bars: Open late (4-5am weekends)
- Clubs: 10pm-6am
- Wine bars: Close earlier (11pm-12am)

### Prices
- Beer (0.5L): 800-1,500 HUF (€2-4)
- Wine glass: 1,000-2,500 HUF (€2.50-6)
- Cocktail: 2,500-4,000 HUF (€6-10)
- Pálinka shot: 1,000-2,000 HUF (€2.50-5)

### Cover Charges
- Most bars: None
- Clubs: Usually 2,000-5,000 HUF (€5-12)
- Some waive with guestlist

### Safety
- Very safe generally
- Watch drinks (universal advice)
- Taxis: Use Bolt app
- Pickpockets: Aware in crowded bars
- Drink water — pálinka strong

### Getting Home
- Night buses (3-digit numbers)
- Bolt app
- Főtaxi (official)
- Never street hail taxis
