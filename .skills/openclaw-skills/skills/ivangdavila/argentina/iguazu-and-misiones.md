# Iguazu and Misiones — Playbook

## Best Use Case

Short high-impact nature block, family-friendly wow factor, or tropical contrast to city, wine, or Patagonia trips.

## Core Advantage

Massive payoff for relatively short stays when timing, heat management, and border decisions are handled well.

## Park Logic

- The Argentina side deserves a full day.
- Official hours run 8:00 to 18:00, with the last train to Garganta del Diablo at 15:30.
- Late starts are an avoidable mistake.

## Stay Strategy

- Puerto Iguazu base usually beats same-day in-and-out planning.
- Add a second or third night if Brazil side, slower pace, or weather flexibility matters.

## Health and Weather Notes

- Heat and humidity are real trip-limiters in warmer months.
- Repellent, hydration, and weather fallback matter more than in most city itineraries.
- If user is vaccine-sensitive or traveling with children, check tropical-health guidance early.

## Common Mistake

Treating Iguazu as a half-day stop and then discovering that park timing, queues, and border friction consume the whole schedule.
