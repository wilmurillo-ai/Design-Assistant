# Domestic Transport — Argentina

## Mode Selection Framework

| Route type | Default winner | Why |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| Buenos Aires to Patagonia / Iguazu / Mendoza | Flight | Distances are large and time savings are decisive |
| Buenos Aires urban movement | Public transit + walking | SUBE-supported network reduces car friction |
| Wine-country day structure | Car with driver or guided transport | Tasting days and mountain roads change driving logic |
| Patagonia scenic loops | Car | Flexibility matters once already inside the region |
| Mid-distance overnight corridor | Bus | Can work when traveler accepts slower pacing and terminal friction |

## Airport Reality

- Buenos Aires has airport friction that should be priced honestly.
- Same-day international arrival plus domestic repositioning needs conservative buffers.
- Weather-sensitive southern flights should not sit on a zero-buffer plan.

## Rail and Bus Reality

- Long-distance trains are limited and should be treated as selective experiences, not the country backbone.
- Long-distance buses are viable on some corridors, but seat class, overnight fatigue, and terminal timing matter.

## Urban Transport Notes

- Buenos Aires public transport uses the rechargeable SUBE card.
- City-only trips rarely benefit from renting a car.

## Transfer Buffers to Add

- International arrival to domestic onward: generous same-day margin or overnight
- Separate tickets: assume missed-connection risk is yours
- Park or excursion departure after flight day: only with explicit slack

## Tourist Trap to Flag

Trying to "save money" with transport choices that erase half a day every time you move.
