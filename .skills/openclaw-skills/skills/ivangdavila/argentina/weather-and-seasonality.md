# Weather and Seasonality — Argentina

## Macro Seasonal Strategy

| Season | Strong fits | Main risks |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| Spring | Buenos Aires, Mendoza, shoulder Patagonia, wine and city combinations | Volatile shoulder weather in the south |
| Summer | Patagonia, lakes, Atlantic coast, wildlife windows | Heat in north and cities, peak crowds, smoke risk |
| Autumn | Mendoza, Buenos Aires, northwest, softer shoulder travel | South Patagonia becomes more fragile as season turns |
| Winter | Ski trips, city escapes, some northwest routes, milder Iguazu visits | Limited Patagonia hiking, snow and shorter daylight |

## Region-First Rules

- Patagonia should be matched to month before flights are priced.
- Iguazu works year-round but feels completely different in hot and humid months.
- Mendoza can be a strong shoulder-season anchor.
- Salta and Jujuy need altitude and daylight pacing, not just clear skies.

## Tourist Trap to Flag

Using northern-hemisphere logic by default. Argentina should be planned with southern seasons first, destination wishlist second.
