# National Parks and Nature — Argentina

## Access Rules That Matter

- Argentina's national parks system centralizes ticketing and updates through official portals.
- Ticketed parks include major tourist anchors such as Iguazu, Los Glaciares, Nahuel Huapi, and Tierra del Fuego.
- The official system applies a 50% second-day discount within 72 hours; Tierra del Fuego limits that discount to web purchases.

## High-Value Practical Notes

- Los Glaciares north gateways around El Chalten are online-first and card-only; do not rely on cash.
- Iguazu is timing-sensitive: arrive early, respect entry timing, and remember the last train to Garganta del Diablo leaves well before closing.
- Aconcagua permits are season-based and more demanding than a casual national-park stop.

## Reservation Pressure

| Area | What creates friction |
|------|-----------------------|
| Los Glaciares | Peak weather windows, camp reservations, long transport days |
| Iguazu | Entry timing, heat, and cross-border temptation |
| Tierra del Fuego | Weather, cruise timing, web-discount logic |
| Aconcagua | Permit windows, insurance or evacuation requirements, altitude reality |

## Outdoor Safety Rule

Nature plans need a fallback. Wind, flood impacts, heat, smoke, altitude, and snow should change the daily plan before they become emergencies.
