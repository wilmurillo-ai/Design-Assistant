# Budget and Costs — Argentina

## Budget Reality by Trip Style

| Style | Cost driver | Typical mistake |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| City-focused | Dining, better neighborhoods, airport transfers | Thinking Buenos Aires daily spend predicts Patagonia |
| Wine-focused | Drivers, tastings, premium lunches, lodge location | Looking only at hotel rate |
| Nature-focused | Domestic flights, boats, guides, park access | Underpricing excursions and buffers |
| Family trip | Larger rooms, private transfers, pacing days | Packing too many destinations to save money |

## Cost Rules

- Internal flights can dominate total budget once Patagonia or multiple regions enter the plan.
- Premium nature trips usually jump in price because of transport and low-inventory activities, not because of food.
- Iguazu is often cheaper than Patagonia, but adding Brazil logistics can change the equation.
- Road trips look economical until fuel, one-way rentals, parking, and slow days are counted honestly.

## Smart Framing

- Separate fixed trip costs from daily variable spend.
- Use one "anchor splurge" logic: glacier boat, vineyard lunch, wildlife cruise, or premium estancia.
- Build one low-cost flex day after every expensive movement or excursion block.

## Tourist Trap to Flag

Calling Argentina "cheap" without separating Buenos Aires city math from Patagonia and wilderness math.
