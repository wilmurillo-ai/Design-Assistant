# Safety and Emergencies — Argentina

## Core Safety Model

Argentina travel is usually about managing friction, not panic. The biggest risks are petty theft, bad transfer choices, weather disruptions, and poor judgment around remote conditions.

## Urban Safety Rules

- Use stricter phone and bag discipline in big-city transit zones, terminals, and tourist-heavy areas.
- Night return planning matters more than daytime sightseeing risk.
- Protests and marches can affect movement in Buenos Aires even when violence is not the main issue.

## Nature and Weather Risks

- Patagonia: wind, cold, and sudden shifts
- Iguazu/Misiones: heat and humidity
- Mendoza: Zonda and mountain exposure
- Salta/Jujuy: altitude and long drives

## Emergency Stack

- 911 is the general emergency number in major cities.
- Buenos Aires tourism also lists 107 for medical emergencies, 100 for fire service, and 103 for Civil Defense.
- Official weather alerts should be checked through SMN references.

## Common Mistake

Treating security as a neighborhood list only, while ignoring transfers, exhaustion, and weather-driven bad decisions.
