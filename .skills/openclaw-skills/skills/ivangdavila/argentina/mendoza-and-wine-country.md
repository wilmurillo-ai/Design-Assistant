# Mendoza and Wine Country — Playbook

## Best Use Case

Wine, long lunches, Andes scenery, slower premium travel, and one of the easiest high-value contrasts to Buenos Aires.

## Core Advantage

Strong blend of city comfort, vineyard access, and day-trip mountain scenery without Patagonia-level logistics.

## Base Strategy

- Choose between city base, vineyard base, or split stay before booking tastings.
- City works better for flexible dining and mixed plans.
- Vineyard or Uco-style stays work better when the trip is explicitly slow and wine-led.

## Timing and Season

- Vendimia period brings major demand; book earlier if trip falls around early March.
- Zonda episodes and mountain weather can reshape Andes days quickly.

## Transport Rule

- Self-drive only works if user will not be drinking heavily and accepts longer rural movement.
- Otherwise, driver or guided transport is part of the winery budget.

## Common Mistake

Trying to combine too many wineries, a full mountain day, and city dining in a rushed 48-hour stop.
