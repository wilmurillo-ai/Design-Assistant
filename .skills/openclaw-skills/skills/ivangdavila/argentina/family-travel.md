# Family Travel — Argentina

## Best Family Fits

- Buenos Aires for museums, parks, and easy short blocks
- Iguazu for high-impact nature without extreme physical demands
- Peninsula Valdes for wildlife-led travel
- Patagonia Lakes for mixed-age scenery if pacing stays moderate

## Harder Fits

- El Chalten-heavy trips with very young children or low mobility
- Salta/Jujuy loops without enough rest or altitude awareness
- Border-hop days with toddlers and no buffer

## Family Rules

- One major activity block per day is often enough.
- Keep transfer days visibly lighter than inspiration-based itineraries suggest.
- Minor paperwork should be checked early, especially for binational or resident-family cases.
- Build weather fallback before kids hit fatigue, not after.

## Common Mistake

Copying an adult hiking or wine itinerary into a family context and hoping naps or snacks will fix it.
