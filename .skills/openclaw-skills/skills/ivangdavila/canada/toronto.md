# Toronto Complete Guide

## Neighborhoods — Where to Go

### St. Lawrence + Distillery District
**Vibe:** Historic core, market culture, walkable architecture.
**Best for:** First day orientation, food sampling, easy sightseeing.

**Do:**
- St. Lawrence Market (weekday mornings are calmer)
- Distillery District in late afternoon
- Flatiron Building and Old Town walk

**Eat here:**
- **Buster's Sea Cove** — market seafood classic, CAD 18-35
- **Paddington's Pump** — old-school pub feel, CAD 20-35

### Kensington Market + Chinatown
**Vibe:** Independent shops, global food, creative energy.
**Best for:** Street food, casual meals, vintage browsing.

**Eat here:**
- **Seven Lives** — tacos, short menu done well
- **Swatow** — Cantonese comfort food
- **Rasta Pasta** — Caribbean-Italian fusion

### Queen West + Ossington
**Vibe:** Boutique-heavy, restaurants and bars, younger crowd.
**Best for:** Dinner + drinks loop.

**Eat and drink:**
- **Bar Raval** — standing bar snacks and cocktails
- **Mamakas Taverna** — Greek, polished but lively
- **Bellwoods Brewery** — local beer stop

### Yorkville
**Vibe:** Upscale shopping and hotel zone.
**Best for:** Luxury stays, polished dining, museum proximity.

**Reality check:** Great location, but easy to overspend without higher quality return.

## Transport in City

- **TTC day use:** Tap credit/debit directly, no need for separate card unless frequent use
- **Streetcars:** Useful but slower in traffic-heavy windows
- **UP Express:** Fast airport link to Union Station
- **Walking:** Core neighborhoods are highly walkable

## Mistakes to Avoid

1. Spending all time around CN Tower area food chains.
2. Attempting too many distant neighborhoods in one day.
3. Assuming every top-rated place takes walk-ins at peak dinner time.
4. Ignoring weather layering needs even in shoulder seasons.
