# Measurement

Use this for campaign reviews, reporting, and budget decisions.

## KPI Tree

| Layer | Questions | Example metrics |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| Attention | are people stopping and clicking | hold rate, CTR, profile clicks, organic sessions |
| Consideration | are they engaging with the message | time on page, replies, form starts, email clicks |
| Conversion | are they taking the action | lead rate, purchase rate, booking rate |
| Economics | is the action worth the cost | CAC, payback, MER, ROAS, influenced pipeline |
| Retention | does value continue after the first action | repeat purchase, activation, retained usage, churn |

## Review Rules

- Do not decide from one metric alone.
- Pair attention with downstream quality.
- Compare channel performance by business outcome, not just by cheap clicks.
- Separate signal problems from economics problems.

## UTM Discipline

At minimum, make traffic distinguishable by:
- source
- medium
- campaign
- creative angle
- audience or segment when relevant

If traffic cannot be separated, learning cannot compound.

## Weekly Review Output

End each review with only four buckets:
- keep
- kill
- scale
- recycle

Each decision should cite one clear reason:
- stronger conversion quality
- better economics
- stronger proof response
- faster creative fatigue
- broken follow-up or landing path

## Alert Conditions

Investigate quickly if:
- spend rises while qualified conversions drop
- CTR rises but conversion rate collapses
- views rise but comments show mismatch or confusion
- organic traffic grows but branded search and leads stay flat
- repeat buyers or retained users weaken after acquisition pushes
