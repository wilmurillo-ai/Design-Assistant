# Experiments

Use this to keep digital marketing compounding instead of looping through random activity.

## Experiment Template


## What to Test

| Surface | Examples |
|---------|----------|
| Message | problem framing, promise, proof, objection handling |
| Creative | hook, opening visual, caption, CTA, format |
| Landing path | headline, proof block, form length, offer structure |
| Lifecycle | send timing, subject line, reminder sequence, reactivation angle |
| Channel mix | paid plus organic bundle, content plus retargeting, video plus email |

## Prioritization Rules

Prefer tests that are:
- fast to launch
- tied to a specific bottleneck
- reusable across more than one channel
- clear enough to kill quickly if they fail

## Winner Recycling

When something wins:
1. reuse the hook elsewhere
2. expand the angle into deeper content
3. update paid and organic variants
4. record the condition that made it win

## Kill Rules

Stop early when:
- the message is attracting the wrong audience
- economics are clearly worse than baseline
- comments or replies show trust damage
- the offer is mismatched and needs rewriting first

Good marketing systems do not only find winners. They also stop losers fast.
