# Playbooks

Use these when the user needs speed, not theory.

## 1. Launch Sprint

Best for a new product, feature, offer, or campaign window.

- Choose one headline promise and one proof source.
- Build one landing page angle, one short-form script set, one email sequence, and one retargeting path.
- Launch organic and paid from the same message, then review after 72 hours.
- Keep only the angle that produces qualified action, not just reach.

## 2. Trend Response Sprint

Best for reacting to an emerging topic, format, or market conversation.

- Verify relevance before speed. A trend that does not fit the buyer is noise.
- Create one contrarian angle, one proof-backed angle, and one practical angle.
- Publish fast in social and short-form first, then expand winners into longer content or landing copy.
- Stop if attention does not translate into comments, search lift, or pipeline quality.

## 3. Content-to-Revenue Sprint

Best when content exists but conversion does not.

- Pick one strong source asset: webinar, call, case study, or founder insight.
- Extract hooks, objections, proof points, and CTA language.
- Repurpose into SEO page, short-form cuts, social posts, and email.
- Route every asset into one capture path and one follow-up sequence.

## 4. Retention and Reactivation Sprint

Best when acquisition is running but users or customers do not stick.

- Segment active, dormant, and high-intent users first.
- Build messages around missed value, proof, and easiest next step.
- Use email, retargeting, and product or landing education together.
- Fix the weakest activation or repeat-use point before scaling acquisition again.
