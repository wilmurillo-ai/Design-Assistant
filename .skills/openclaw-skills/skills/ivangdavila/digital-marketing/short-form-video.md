# Short-Form Video

Use this when the user wants viral growth, creator-style marketing, or faster message testing.

## What short-form is for

- testing hooks and claims faster than long production cycles
- turning proof into attention
- mining comments for objections and new copy angles
- repurposing webinars, demos, customer calls, and founder opinions

## Core Structure

| Part | Job |
|------|-----|
| Hook | stop the scroll in the first seconds |
| Proof or tension | show why this matters now |
| Payoff | explain the insight, demo, or result |
| CTA | next action matched to funnel stage |

## Hook Sources

- painful problem stated directly
- unpopular but defensible opinion
- before and after result
- product demo with immediate payoff
- customer objection answered in plain language
- trend reframed through the user's niche

## Native Execution Rules

- Write for spoken rhythm, not blog rhythm.
- Burn in captions and keep them readable without sound.
- Show proof early: screen, data point, outcome, quote, or transformation.
- Keep one video focused on one promise or one objection.
- Turn strong comments into the next script.

## Repurposing Flow

1. Find winning hook
2. Turn it into ad headline variants
3. Expand it into landing page copy
4. Convert it into social text and email subject lines
5. Build SEO content only if the topic shows durable demand

## Failure Signs

- views without profile clicks or meaningful comments
- long setup before the first payoff
- polished brand look with no native energy
- too many ideas in one cut
- no bridge from attention to next step
