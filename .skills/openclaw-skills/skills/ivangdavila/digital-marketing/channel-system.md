# Channel System

Use this to turn one growth thesis into coordinated channel execution.

## Channel Roles

| Channel | Primary job | Core asset | Main metric | Handoff |
|---------|-------------|------------|-------------|---------|
| SEO | capture existing demand | intent-matched page | qualified organic sessions | page CTA or lead magnet |
| Long-form content | earn trust and proof | article, guide, case study | assisted pipeline or engaged visits | email, retargeting, short-form cuts |
| Social posts | distribute point of view | native post or thread | saves, replies, profile clicks | landing page, newsletter, short-form |
| Short-form video | test hooks fast | native vertical cut | hold rate, watch-through, click intent | landing page, longer content, ads |
| Email and lifecycle | nurture and reactivate | sequence or broadcast | reply rate, click rate, conversion rate | demo, purchase, retention action |
| Paid acquisition | scale proven messages | ad plus landing bundle | CAC, payback, qualified conversions | lifecycle and retargeting |

## Orchestration Rules

### One idea, many surfaces
- Start with one sharp angle.
- Adapt the same angle to search, social, video, landing copy, and email.
- Do not invent a new strategy for every channel.

### Organic informs paid, paid informs organic
- Use comments, replies, and search terms to improve ad hooks.
- Use ad winners to decide what deserves evergreen content and SEO coverage.

### Campaign bundle requirement

Before launch, define at least:
- message angle
- audience
- primary channel
- secondary distribution channel
- landing or CTA destination
- follow-up sequence
- review date

## When to reduce channel count

Cut channels if:
- message is not converting anywhere
- the landing path is still weak
- the team cannot review output quality fast enough
- too many channels are fragmenting the same budget or idea

Fewer channels with clear reuse beats broad weak execution.
