# Guest List and Seating

Use this when guest-count uncertainty is affecting venue, catering, invitations, or family dynamics.

## Keep Scenarios, Not One Fantasy Number

Maintain three counts until the list is stable:
- A list: must invite
- B list: would like to invite if space or budget allows
- C list: optional or politically sensitive additions

This keeps the plan adaptable while venue capacity, travel likelihood, and funding are still moving.

## Boundary Decisions to Lock Early

- adults only or child-friendly
- plus-one policy
- invited to ceremony only, reception only, or both
- whether family hosts get special override rights

## Seating Logic

- Build tables from relationship comfort, not just family labels.
- Keep conflict pairs and social dependencies visible.
- Leave buffer seats for late RSVP changes or delicate placements.

## RSVP Discipline

- Every RSVP deadline should map to a vendor or payment consequence.
- Follow up in waves: easy confirms first, uncertain guests second, sensitive cases last.
- Final headcount should become a protected number once catering or rental deadlines hit.
