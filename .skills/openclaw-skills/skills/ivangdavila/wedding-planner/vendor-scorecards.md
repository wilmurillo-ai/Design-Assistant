# Vendor Scorecards

Use this when the user is comparing vendors, negotiating offers, or trying to narrow a shortlist without losing track of trade-offs.

## Scorecard Fields

Keep the same fields for every vendor in a category:

| Vendor | Category | Price Fit | Availability Fit | Style Fit | Communication | Contract Risk | Backup Strength | Notes |
|--------|----------|-----------|------------------|-----------|---------------|---------------|-----------------|-------|
| | | 1-5 | 1-5 | 1-5 | 1-5 | low/med/high | weak/ok/strong | |

## What to Compare

- Price fit: not the cheapest option, but whether it fits the real budget lane.
- Availability fit: does the date actually work, and is response speed acceptable?
- Style fit: can they deliver the atmosphere the user wants without interpretation risk?
- Communication: are they clear, fast, and easy to coordinate with under pressure?
- Contract risk: cancellation, overtime, rescheduling, meal requirements, exclusivity, travel, and hidden fees.
- Backup strength: what happens if they cancel, get sick, or underperform?

## Contract Review Prompts

Before the user commits, check:
- payment schedule and refund terms
- reschedule or force-majeure language
- overtime rates
- deliverables and timeline
- setup and teardown windows
- who the day-of point person is

## Selection Rule

If the user chooses a vendor with weaker communication or higher contract risk, record why the trade-off is still worth it. That prevents re-arguing the same decision every week.
