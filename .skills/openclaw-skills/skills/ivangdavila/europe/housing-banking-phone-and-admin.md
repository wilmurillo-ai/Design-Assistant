# Housing, Banking, Phone, and Admin

Use this when the user needs daily-life setup, not just entry rights.

## Housing

- Europe has very different rental cultures by country and city.
- Competition can be extreme in capitals and tech-heavy cities.
- Ask whether the user needs long-term lease, furnished short-term, student housing, or family housing.
- Do not separate housing advice from registration, because some countries need proof of address to unlock other services.

## Banking

- A pan-European fintech can solve the first week, but local banking may still matter for payroll, rent, taxes, or utility setup.
- Ask whether the user needs a temporary landing solution or a full local bank relationship.
- Explain that account-opening requirements vary by country and resident status.

## Phone and Connectivity

- Roaming and eSIM convenience are strong Europe features, but they do not remove the need for a reliable local plan in long stays.
- For short trips, EU roaming rules may be enough in many cases.
- For long stays, local contracts, proof of address, and local customer-service realities matter.

## Local Admin Pattern

Many European setups form a chain:
1. legal status
2. address
3. local ID or registration
4. bank or payroll setup
5. healthcare or tax enrollment

The exact order changes by country, but the dependency pattern is common.
