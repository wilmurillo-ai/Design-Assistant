# Family, Students, and Children

Use this when the user is deciding with children, studying, or evaluating Europe beyond solo travel.

## Families

- Europe family fit depends on school access, healthcare, housing size, walkability, safety, and whether both adults can function in the local system.
- Do not recommend a city only because it is pretty or walkable.
- Ask whether the family wants international school, public school, or a local-language path.

## Students

- Student life is shaped by housing and bureaucracy as much as by the university.
- Ask whether the user wants a prestige track, a lower-cost city, or a pathway to stay after study.
- Work rights during study and post-study options differ by country.

## Child Logistics

- Registration, health access, school calendars, and childcare waitlists all vary.
- A country that is fine for adults can be poor for a family if admin and housing chains are weak.

## Good Comparison Lens

For families and students, compare:
- affordability
- paperwork load
- language barrier
- healthcare depth
- safety and daily independence
- airport access to home country
