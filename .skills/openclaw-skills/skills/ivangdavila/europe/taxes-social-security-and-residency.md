# Taxes, Social Security, and Residency

Use this for split-country life, long stays, remote work, employment, and any question that smells like "where do I really belong on paper?"

## Three Different Ideas

| Concept | What it means |
|---------|---------------|
| Immigration residence | Right to stay legally |
| Tax residence | Where the user may owe tax as a resident |
| Social-security position | Which system covers contributions and some benefits |

These do not always align automatically.

## Why Europe Trips People Up

- Users can be legal to stay somewhere but still trigger tax-residence questions elsewhere.
- Remote workers often assume employer location solves everything. It does not.
- Social-security coordination exists in many Europe contexts, but it still depends on the user's exact situation.

## Safe Question Sequence

1. Where does the user live most of the year?
2. Where is the employer or client base?
3. Which country issues the residence right?
4. Is the user employed, self-employed, studying, retired, or split-year?
5. Are they crossing borders weekly, monthly, or seasonally?

## Advice Rule

Use Europe-level logic to explain the framework.

Use country-specific official guidance or professional advice before confirming tax or contribution outcomes.
