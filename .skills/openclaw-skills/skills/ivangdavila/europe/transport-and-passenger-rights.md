# Transport and Passenger Rights

Use this for trains, flights, buses, ferries, missed connections, and cross-border travel friction.

## Europe Strengths

- Dense rail in many corridors
- Strong budget-air competition in some routes
- Relatively good bus and ferry coverage for specific corridors
- Passenger-right frameworks that are often stronger than users expect

## But Do Not Oversimplify

- Rail quality varies sharply by corridor and country.
- Cheap flights can destroy a short trip if airports are far, baggage rules bite, or strikes hit.
- Ferries and overnight options solve some geography problems but create new timing constraints.

## Rights-Aware Planning

- If a user is buying separate tickets, connection protection may be weaker.
- Air, rail, bus, and ferry rights differ.
- Use official passenger-right resources when delays, cancellations, or compensation matter.

## Routing Rule

Choose transport based on:
- total door-to-door time
- transfer risk
- border or ID checks
- luggage friction
- strike sensitivity
- whether the user is traveling light or moving their life
