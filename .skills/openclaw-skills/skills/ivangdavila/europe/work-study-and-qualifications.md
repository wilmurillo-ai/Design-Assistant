# Work, Study, and Qualifications

Use this file for jobs, universities, credentials, and business entry.

## Jobs

- Europe job logic is country-specific even when EURES gives a continent-wide starting point.
- Ask whether the user needs local-language work, English-first work, remote work, or a specialized regulated profession.
- Salary alone is not enough; compare tax, rent, healthcare, and social-security obligations too.

## Students

- Separate university choice from country fit.
- Housing, paperwork, language, and work rights during study often shape the real experience.
- Ask whether the user wants an international-student bubble, a lower-cost city, or long-term stay after graduation.

## Qualifications

- Some credentials move more easily than others across Europe.
- For regulated professions, formal recognition may be required.
- Use Europass and official qualification-recognition portals as starting points, then drop to the country level.

## Entrepreneurs and Freelancers

- Europe is not one company-formation zone.
- Ask where the user will actually live, where clients are, and where tax and invoicing will happen.
- Country-level registration, VAT exposure, and local compliance can matter more than the city brand.
