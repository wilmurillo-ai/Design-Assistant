# Remote Work and Digital Nomads

Use this for remote workers, freelancers, slow travelers, and split-country users.

## First Clarify the Goal

Ask whether the user wants:
- a legal residence base
- a travel-heavy temporary route
- a tax-efficient structure
- a climate or lifestyle base
- a company setup

These are different problems.

## Europe Reality

- There is no single Europe-wide digital-nomad status.
- Some countries offer national remote-work or digital-nomad pathways.
- Other users may be operating under EU citizen movement rights, student status, or ordinary long-stay permissions instead.

## Common Failure Modes

- Confusing a legal long visit with tax-safe remote work
- Staying inside Schengen too casually
- Choosing a city for vibes while ignoring housing or admin friction
- Assuming fast internet and English service means easy legal setup

## Better Advice Pattern

Compare countries and cities using:
- legal fit
- housing pressure
- weather
- airport and rail access
- coworking and community depth
- cost of staying compliant
