# Moving and Settling in Europe

Use this when the user is choosing a European base or relocating to a European country.

## Before Arrival

1. Confirm legal pathway first: free movement, visa, permit, student route, family route, or work sponsorship.
2. Narrow the country and city before booking long-term housing.
3. Decide whether the first base should be temporary housing or a direct long lease.
4. Price the move with deposits, paperwork, health cover, and setup friction included.

## First Week

1. Confirm the local registration sequence for the chosen country.
2. Get a workable payment stack and local phone connectivity.
3. Walk or test the actual neighborhood and commute before emotionally committing to it.
4. Start any mandatory local registration, tax, or address proof steps immediately.

## First Month

1. Secure permanent or semi-stable housing.
2. Register for healthcare if eligible.
3. Fix banking, rent payments, transport passes, and recurring bills.
4. Build the first local routines: groceries, pharmacy, urgent care, coworking, school, or language classes.

## Europe-Specific Reminder

Moving success often depends less on the continent and more on:
- the country's admin friction
- the local housing market
- the user's language readiness
- the order in which documents unlock each other

Never promise a smooth move from Europe-level logic alone.
