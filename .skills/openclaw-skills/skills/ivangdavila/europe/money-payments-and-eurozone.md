# Money, Payments, and the Eurozone

Use this file for budgeting, cards, cash, currency friction, and everyday payment behavior.

## First Distinction

Europe is not one currency zone.

The euro removes friction in some countries, but UK, Switzerland, parts of the Balkans, and other non-euro countries still create payment and budgeting differences.

## Practical Rules

- Card use is widespread in much of Europe, but cash still matters in some countries, markets, and taxis.
- ATM strategy matters more than exchange-counter strategy.
- Budget by country and city, not by continent.
- Cost of living, wages, and housing pressure can swing more inside Europe than many users expect.

## Good Questions to Ask

1. Short trip or long stay?
2. Eurozone-only route or mixed-currency route?
3. Need rent, payroll, and bills or just travel spending?
4. Backpacking budget, family budget, or premium city-break budget?

## Trap

Users often say "Europe is expensive" or "Eastern Europe is cheap" in ways that hide city, season, and housing differences. Force the comparison down one level.
