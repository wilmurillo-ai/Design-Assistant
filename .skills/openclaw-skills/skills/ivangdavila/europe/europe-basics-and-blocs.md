# Europe Basics and Blocs

Use this file before answering any Europe question that risks confusing geography with legal reality.

## The Four Layers That Matter Most

| Layer | Why it matters |
|-------|----------------|
| Europe (continent) | Geography, climate, culture, route logic |
| European Union | Citizen rights, consumer rights, roaming, social-security coordination, common regulation |
| Schengen Area | Border checks, 90/180 logic for many visitors, internal free movement |
| Eurozone | Currency and payment convenience |

## Core Distinctions

- A country can be in Europe but not in the EU.
- A country can be in the EU but not in the eurozone.
- A country can be in Schengen but not in the EU.
- UK, Switzerland, Balkans, and microstates often require separate logic even when the trip still feels "European."

## What Usually Changes by Layer

### EU layer
- Right to live and work for EU citizens
- Roaming and many consumer protections
- Coordination of some social-security and qualification frameworks

### Schengen layer
- Border-crossing experience inside the area
- 90/180 stay math for many non-EU visitors
- Need to think in area-wide time spent, not just one country

### Eurozone layer
- Currency friction and ATM/cash assumptions
- Easier price comparison and card use

### Country layer
- Visa categories
- Tax residence
- Healthcare enrollment
- Housing processes
- School systems
- Job market and salary reality

## Default Rule

If the answer changes based on nationality, country, or stay length, do not answer at "Europe" level only.

Use Europe as the framework, then drop to the correct country layer.
