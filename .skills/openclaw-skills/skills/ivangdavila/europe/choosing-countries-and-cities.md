# Choosing Countries and Cities

Use this file when the user does not yet know where in Europe they should base, move, study, or spend most of their time.

## Country-Choice Dimensions

| Dimension | Questions to ask |
|-----------|------------------|
| Legal fit | Can the user actually stay, work, or study there? |
| Housing reality | Is rental inventory manageable for the user's budget and timeline? |
| Language load | Can the user function in English only, or do they need local-language capacity fast? |
| Work and salary | Does the country support the user's field or remote-work model? |
| Daily life | Transit, walkability, healthcare depth, family fit, and admin friction |
| Weather and seasonality | Heat tolerance, winter tolerance, daylight, and shoulder-season use |
| Connectivity | Rail, airports, and weekend-access patterns |

## Fast Regional Heuristics

| Cluster | Often good for | Watch for |
|---------|----------------|-----------|
| Iberia | Climate, lifestyle, food, medium-cost cities, remote-work appeal | Salary levels, bureaucracy, hot summers, housing in hot markets |
| France and Benelux | Strong rail, major cities, institutions, consumer protections | Cost, bureaucracy, housing competition |
| Italy | Lifestyle, culture, smaller-city depth, varied regional choices | Wage gaps, slower admin, regional differences |
| DACH and Nordics | Salaries, systems, order, welfare depth | Cost, language expectations, winter and housing competition |
| Central and Eastern Europe | Value, growing tech hubs, easier short-trip coverage | Local salary differences, country-to-country variance, legal specifics |
| UK and Ireland | English use, strong universities, certain job markets | Post-Brexit border rules, high cost, housing stress |
| Balkans and non-EU southeast | Value, slower pace, scenic routes | More fragmented rights, payments, insurance, and border logic |

## Good Comparison Questions

Ask these before recommending a country:
1. Tourist base, relocation base, student base, or tax base?
2. Full-time, split-year, or project-based stay?
3. Prioritizing weather, salary, affordability, or legal simplicity?
4. Need a mega-city, mid-size city, or slow coastal or mountain base?
5. Rail-first life, airport-first life, or car-first life?

## City vs Country Rule

- Use country choice to set the legal and macro-economic frame.
- Use city choice to set housing, transport, community, and day-to-day quality.
- A country may be right while the capital is wrong for the user's actual life.
