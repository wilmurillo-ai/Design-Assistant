# Regional Corridors and Country Clusters

Use this file when the user needs a realistic Europe map, not a random list of countries.

## Europe by Functional Corridor

| Corridor | Strong for | Watch for |
|----------|------------|-----------|
| Iberia | Climate, medium-cost city life, food, slower rhythm, remote-work appeal | Hot summers, bureaucracy, salary ceilings |
| France-Benelux-Western core | Rail density, institutions, city hopping, high service depth | Cost, housing competition, strike exposure |
| DACH and Alpine arc | Salaries, order, transport, mountain access | Cost, language expectations, tighter systems |
| Italy and Adriatic south | Lifestyle, culture, regional variety, slower pace | Wage gaps, admin friction, seasonality |
| Nordics and Baltic north | Governance, English use, digital systems, summer quality | Cost, darkness, winter load |
| Central Europe | Rail, value mix, city-break density, growing business hubs | Country-to-country differences can be larger than expected |
| Balkans and southeastern routes | Scenic road and coast value, longer stays, non-Schengen buffers in some cases | Fragmented rights, border friction, payment and insurance differences |
| UK and Ireland | English-first work or study, global-city pull, easy air access | Post-Brexit rights, cost, housing stress |

## How to Use the Corridors

- For tourists, corridors help avoid absurd country counts.
- For movers, corridors help compare legal fit, climate, and cost before choosing a single country.
- For remote workers, corridors help balance timezone, airport access, and quality of life.

## Default Matching Rule

Start with a corridor, then narrow to country, then city.

That sequence is usually more useful than asking "Which European country is best?" with no frame.
