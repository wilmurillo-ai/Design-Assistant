# Weather, Seasons, and Trip Timing

Use this file whenever the user is timing a Europe trip or choosing a base partly for climate.

## Europe Timing Is Not Uniform

- Mediterranean summer, Alpine winter, Nordic darkness, shoulder-season cities, and stormy Atlantic coasts all behave differently.
- Saying "best time to visit Europe" is too broad to be useful.

## Useful Timing Buckets

| Pattern | Usually best for | Watch for |
|---------|------------------|-----------|
| Spring shoulder season | City breaks, mixed routes, gardens, lower heat | Holidays, rain variability |
| Peak summer | Coasts, festivals, mountains, school-break travel | Heat, crowds, expensive lodging |
| Autumn shoulder season | Cities, wine regions, lower crowd pressure | Shorter daylight, rain patterns |
| Winter | Christmas markets, skiing, museums, cheaper city breaks | Cold, snow, short daylight, closures |

## Planning Rules

- Southern Europe can be punishing in peak summer if the user wants city-heavy days.
- Northern Europe may be magical in summer but harder in dark winter for first-time visitors.
- Shoulder seasons are often the best answer for Europe beginners unless the trip is explicitly beach or ski driven.
