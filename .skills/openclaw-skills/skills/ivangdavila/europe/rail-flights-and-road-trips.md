# Rail, Flights, and Road Trips

Use this when the user is building a Europe route or comparing transport models.

## The Three Default Models

### Rail-first
- Best for dense Western and Central European corridors.
- Good for city-center to city-center movement.
- Often highest quality for 2-5 country loops with moderate pace.

### Flight-first
- Best when geography is wide, islands matter, or time is short.
- Strong for north-south jumps and non-contiguous routes.
- Weak if the trip is supposed to feel calm and urban.

### Road-first
- Best for rural regions, mountain routes, coastlines, Balkans, islands with ferries, and areas where public transport is weaker.
- Weak for old city centers, parking cost, and one-way cross-border rental complexity.

## Europe Pacing Rule

- One major base per 4-7 days is often a better Europe experience than checking off countries.
- Use the map plus transfer reality, not the border count, to decide trip shape.
- Night trains, ferries, and shoulder-season routes can improve both budget and pace if used deliberately.
