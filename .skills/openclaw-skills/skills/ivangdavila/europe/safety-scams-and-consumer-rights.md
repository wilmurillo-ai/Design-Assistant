# Safety, Scams, and Consumer Rights

Use this for safety, emergency planning, travel disruptions, and consumer problems.

## Europe-Level Safety Reality

- Many European destinations feel physically safe compared with other global regions.
- That does not remove the need to plan for pickpocketing, tourist scams, transport theft, rental scams, fake ticket sellers, or post-disruption stress.
- The safety pattern changes more by city zone and travel style than by continent branding.

## Practical Priorities

- Keep ID and payment resilience in mind for cross-border trips.
- Teach users to separate petty theft risk from violent-crime fear.
- For relocations, include housing scams, fake landlords, and deposit fraud.

## Rights Tools

- 112 is the cross-Europe emergency baseline to remember.
- Consumer-protection tools such as the European Consumer Centres and SOLVIT can matter when rights are cross-border.
- Passenger-right processes should be handled with official guidance when delays or cancellations create losses.

## Do Not Forget

- A scam problem in Europe is often administrative or transactional, not dramatic.
- The best prevention is often route design, booking hygiene, and contract skepticism.
