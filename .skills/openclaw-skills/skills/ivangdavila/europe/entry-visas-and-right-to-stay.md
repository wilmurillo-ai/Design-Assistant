# Entry, Visas, and Right to Stay

Use this file whenever the user asks about moving to Europe, staying beyond tourism, or working or studying legally.

## First Principle

Europe does not have one unified long-stay system.

The correct answer depends on:
- nationality
- target country
- purpose: tourism, study, work, family, retirement, or remote work
- stay length

## Three Big User Groups

### EU/EEA/Swiss citizens
- Often have the easiest pathway inside many European countries.
- The real work is usually registration, housing, tax, and healthcare, not a visa.
- Country-specific registration still matters.

### UK citizens
- Need separate post-Brexit treatment in many scenarios.
- Do not assume EU-style free movement.
- Tourism, work, study, and long stays must be handled separately.

### Non-EU citizens
- Need country-specific visa or permit logic for stays beyond tourism or for employment, study, or family reunification.
- Use the EU Immigration Portal as a Europe-level starting point, then confirm on the target country's official site.

## Safe Answer Pattern

1. Identify nationality and passport.
2. Identify target country.
3. Identify intended stay length.
4. Identify work vs study vs tourism vs family vs retirement.
5. Give the framework first.
6. Then verify the target country's current official requirements.

## Common Mistakes

- Saying "move to Europe" without naming a country.
- Confusing visa-free tourist access with work or residence rights.
- Assuming a digital-nomad route exists continent-wide.
- Planning a move before confirming right to stay.
