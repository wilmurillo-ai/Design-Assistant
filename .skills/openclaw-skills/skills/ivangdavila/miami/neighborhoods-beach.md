# Miami Beach & Barrier Islands

## South Beach (SoBe)

### Sub-Areas
| Area | Vibe | Rent (1BR) |
|------|------|------------|
| **South of Fifth (SoFi)** | Upscale, quieter, luxury condos | $3,500-5,000 |
| **Collins/Ocean (1st-15th)** | Tourist central, Art Deco | $2,500-4,000 |
| **Flamingo Park** | Residential, local feel | $2,200-3,200 |
| **Sunset Harbour** | Fitness/wellness hub, restaurants | $2,800-4,000 |

### Rent (2024-2025)
| Type | Range | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Studio | $1,800-2,500 | Older Art Deco buildings |
| 1BR | $2,500-4,500 | Wide range by location |
| 2BR | $3,500-6,500 | SoFi on high end |

### Vibe
- Tourists everywhere (Ocean Drive especially)
- Nightlife concentrated on Ocean, Collins
- Mix of young partiers and older residents
- LGBTQ+ friendly (Lincoln Road, 12th St)

### Pros
✅ **Walk to beach** — The main draw
✅ **Walkable neighborhood** — Restaurants, bars, shops
✅ **Art Deco architecture** — Beautiful buildings
✅ **Nightlife** — Clubs, rooftop bars
✅ **Lincoln Road** — Pedestrian mall, dining

### Cons
❌ **Tourists everywhere** — Gets old fast for residents
❌ **Parking nightmare** — $30-50/day, street parking brutal
❌ **Noise** — Ocean Drive clubs, music until 5am
❌ **Expensive groceries** — Limited options, premium pricing
❌ **Spring break chaos** — March is unbearable

### Best For
- Beach lovers who prioritize ocean access
- First year in Miami (to get it out of system)
- Nightlife enthusiasts
- Those without cars

## Mid-Beach (20th-63rd St)

### Overview
Quieter stretch between South Beach party zone and family-oriented North Beach. Home to the Fontainebleau and Eden Roc.

### Rent (2024-2025)
| Type | Range |
|------|-------|
| 1BR | $2,200-3,200 |
| 2BR | $3,000-4,500 |

### Vibe
- Mix of tourists and residents
- Less chaotic than SoBe
- Hotel row (Fontainebleau, Faena, Edition)
- Good restaurants without SoBe markup

### Pros
✅ Beach access without SoBe crowds
✅ More parking than South Beach
✅ Good restaurants (Carbone, Surf Club)
✅ Walking/biking path

### Cons
❌ Between two worlds — not quite anywhere
❌ Still touristy around hotels
❌ Need car for most errands

### Best For
- Families wanting beach access
- Those who want calmer version of SoBe
- Hotel industry workers

## North Beach (63rd+) / Surfside / Bal Harbour

### Overview
Quieter, more residential beach communities. Orthodox Jewish community concentrated in Surfside/Bal Harbour.

### Rent (2024-2025)
| Area | 1BR Range |
|------|-----------|
| North Beach | $1,800-2,500 |
| Surfside | $2,500-4,000 |
| Bal Harbour | $3,500-6,000 |

### Vibe
- Family-oriented
- Orthodox Jewish community visible (especially Shabbat)
- Quiet beaches, fewer tourists
- Bal Harbour Shops (ultra-luxury mall)

### Pros
✅ **Affordable** (North Beach)
✅ **Quiet beaches** — locals, not tourists
✅ **Family-friendly**
✅ **Kosher dining options**

### Cons
❌ Far from nightlife (30+ min to SoBe)
❌ Need car
❌ Fewer restaurant options
❌ Surfside condo collapse increased scrutiny on buildings

### Best For
- Families with children
- Jewish community members
- Beach lovers on a budget (North Beach)
- Ultra-wealthy seeking privacy (Bal Harbour)

## Key Biscayne

### Overview
Exclusive island connected by bridge (toll: $2). Resort community with Ritz-Carlton, great beaches, and high prices.

### Rent (2024-2025)
| Type | Range |
|------|-------|
| 1BR | $3,500-5,000 |
| 2BR | $5,000-8,000 |
| House | $8,000-15,000 |

### Vibe
- Wealthy families, many Latin American
- Resort lifestyle
- Excellent public beaches (Crandon, Bill Baggs)
- Tennis culture (Crandon Park, former Sony Open)

### Pros
✅ **Best beaches in Miami** — Crandon Park, Bill Baggs
✅ **Safe** — Island security
✅ **Nature access** — State parks, kayaking
✅ **Excellent schools**
✅ **Community feel**

### Cons
❌ **Expensive** — Everything costs more
❌ **Isolated** — One road in/out, bridge toll
❌ **Need car** — No transit
❌ **Limited nightlife** — One main strip

### Best For
- Wealthy families prioritizing schools + safety
- Beach lovers with money
- Those seeking island seclusion
- Tennis players
