# Delhi

Delhi is usually the right landing city for a first India trip, but only if you use the city by zone instead of trying to cross it all day.

## Best Areas to Stay

| Area | Best For | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Aerocity | Short stays, easy airport logistics | Safe, clean, business-hotel heavy |
| Connaught Place | Central sightseeing | Walkable core, older hotels vary a lot |
| South Delhi | Food, cafes, better day-to-day feel | Hauz Khas, Green Park, Defence Colony |
| Old Delhi | Only visit, do not base there | Incredible, intense, and noisy |

## What Is Worth It

- **Old Delhi morning** for Jama Masjid, Chandni Chowk lanes, and food
- **Humayun's Tomb + Lodhi Garden** for a calmer Mughal day
- **Qutub complex + Mehrauli** if you want monuments without Old Delhi intensity
- **Khan Market / Lodhi area** for a softer first-night introduction

## Food Moves

| Craving | Go To | Note |
|---------|-------|------|
| Mughlai classics | Karim's or Al Jawahar area | Go for the food, not the mood |
| Chaat | UPSC Lane or Bengali Market | Better than random tourist picks |
| Modern Indian splurge | Indian Accent | Book ahead |
| South Indian comfort food | Saravana Bhavan Janpath | Reliable vegetarian fallback |

## Good Delhi Logic

- Pair **Old Delhi + Red Fort side** on one early start
- Pair **Humayun's Tomb + Nizamuddin/Lodhi** on another
- Keep **New Delhi shopping and restaurants** for evenings

## Delhi Traps

- Spending hours in traffic to "see everything"
- Treating Old Delhi as a casual midday stroll in peak heat
- Staying in Paharganj unless the traveler explicitly wants backpacker chaos
- Booking monuments without checking closed days or light-and-heat timing
