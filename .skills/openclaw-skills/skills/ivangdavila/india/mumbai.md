# Mumbai

Mumbai works best when treated as a city of neighborhoods, sea-facing evenings, and strong food stops instead of a checklist city.

## Best Areas to Stay

| Area | Best For | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Colaba / Fort | First-time sightseeing | Best for Gateway, Kala Ghoda, old-city feel |
| Bandra West | Food, nightlife, creative crowd | Best all-round base if budget allows |
| Lower Parel | Business + modern hotels | Efficient but less charming |
| Juhu | Beachside hotels and easier airport access | Better for comfort than culture |

## What Is Worth It

- **Kala Ghoda + Fort walk** for colonial core, galleries, and cafes
- **Marine Drive at sunset** for classic Mumbai mood
- **Bandra lanes + sea face** for shops, bars, and a softer evening
- **Elephanta Caves** only if the traveler likes boat day trips and history

## Food Moves

| Craving | Go To | Note |
|---------|-------|------|
| Coastal seafood | Trishna or Mahesh Lunch Home | Reliable classics |
| Gujarati vegetarian | Swati Snacks | Great for lunch |
| Irani cafe mood | Britannia or Kyani area style spots | Go for nostalgia, not speed |
| Street food | Busy stalls at Chowpatty or Bandra | Choose turnover over aesthetics |

## Mumbai Logic

- South Mumbai is best done on foot + short cabs
- Do not bounce between Colaba and Bandra multiple times a day
- Pick one side of the city for day, another for evening only if needed

## Mumbai Traps

- Underestimating traffic by one full hour
- Choosing a cheap hotel far from the action
- Expecting the beach to be the main reason to visit Mumbai
- Planning museums, markets, and Bandra nightlife all in one day
