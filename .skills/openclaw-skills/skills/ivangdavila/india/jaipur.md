# Jaipur

Jaipur is the easiest Rajasthan entry point: heritage hotels, strong food, and enough sights for two solid days without feeling rushed.

## Best Areas to Stay

| Area | Best For | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| MI Road / C-Scheme | Balanced city base | Easy restaurants and transfers |
| Old City edge | Heritage feel | Better for atmosphere than convenience |
| Near Amer road | Resort stays | Good for slower luxury trips |

## What Is Worth It

- **Amer Fort early** before heat and crowds build
- **City Palace + Jantar Mantar** as the cleanest central pairing
- **Hawa Mahal photo stop** rather than a long visit
- **Panna Meena ka Kund** if the route already includes Amer side

## Food Moves

| Craving | Go To | Note |
|---------|-------|------|
| Rajasthani thali | LMB or a strong heritage hotel lunch | Better at lunch than dinner |
| Kachori breakfast | Rawat | Classic, go early |
| Stylish dinner | Bar Palladio or similar | For mood, not local everyday life |
| Casual modern Indian | C-Scheme restaurants | Better choice for repeat travelers |

## Jaipur Logic

- Jaipur is better as **2 nights minimum**
- Add **one strong shopping block** for textiles, block print, or jewelry
- Use Jaipur as the gentle part of a Delhi-Agra-Rajasthan route

## Jaipur Traps

- Visiting Amer late morning in hot months
- Assuming every "heritage" hotel is equally well run
- Buying heavy souvenirs before the rest of the trip
- Treating Jaipur only as photo backdrops and missing food and crafts
