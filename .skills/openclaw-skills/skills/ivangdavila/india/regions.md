# Regions — India

India works best when divided by travel style, climate, and pace instead of by a map alone.

## High-Level Regions

| Region | Best For | Best Window |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Delhi + North plains | First trips, monuments, food | Oct-Mar |
| Rajasthan | Heritage, textiles, easier routing | Oct-Mar |
| Mumbai + West coast | Food, city energy, short luxury trips | Nov-Feb |
| Goa | Beach and nightlife | Nov-Mar |
| Kerala | Slow travel, backwaters, spice, wellness | Dec-Mar, or monsoon for atmosphere |
| Himalayas | Hiking, cooler weather, landscapes | Varies by subregion, usually Apr-Jun or Sep-Oct |
| Varanasi / spiritual belt | Ritual, river culture, intense heritage | Oct-Mar |
| Northeast | Nature and depth | Best for repeat visitors with time |

## Strong Route Patterns

- **First trip:** Delhi + Agra + Jaipur
- **Balanced trip:** Delhi + Rajasthan + Goa
- **Urban + coast:** Mumbai + Goa
- **Slow trip:** Kerala or Himalayas, not both rushed together

## Region Traps

- North India in peak late-spring heat for first-timers
- Himalayas in active monsoon with tight deadlines
- Adding too many internal flights instead of building one coherent loop
- Treating all of South India as interchangeable
