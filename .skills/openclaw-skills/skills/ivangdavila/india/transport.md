# Transport — India

Choosing the right transport mode is often the difference between a good India trip and an exhausting one.

## Best Mode by Route

| Situation | Best Choice | Why |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Major city to major city | Flight or premium train | Saves huge time |
| Delhi-Agra-Jaipur | Car + driver or train mix | Easiest classic route |
| Mumbai within city | Cabs + local train only if useful | Traffic is real |
| Goa | Pre-booked car, taxi, or confident scooter use | Public transport is not the point |
| Mountains | Private car with buffer time | Roads decide the pace |

## Train Logic

- Use AC classes for comfort-first travel
- Trains are best when the route is direct and the station logistics are clean
- Overnight trains can work, but not as a default for first-timers

## Flight Logic

- Internal flights are often worth it on longer routes
- Add buffer for airport security and terminal changes
- Avoid same-day monument + airport + evening city transfer plans

## Transport Traps

- Treating road times as fixed
- Booking the cheapest train class for a comfort-first traveler
- Arriving at giant stations with no porter or platform plan
- Mixing too many flights, trains, and long drives in a short trip
