# Experiences — India

The best India experiences are usually about context, craft, food, and pace, not only headline monuments.

## High-Signal Additions

| Experience | Best Place | Why it works |
|------------|------------|--------------|
| Old-city food walk | Delhi | Strong first-day orientation |
| Textile / block-print workshop | Jaipur | Great souvenir logic and craft depth |
| Market + home meal or cooking class | Delhi, Jaipur, Goa | Better than generic "cultural show" |
| Sunset boat or sea-facing evening | Mumbai or Goa | Gives the trip breathing room |
| Wildlife safari | Ranthambore, Pench, Bandhavgarh | Worth it only with enough nights |
| Backwaters stay | Kerala | Best for slower routes |

## What to Skip Unless It Fits the Traveler

- Generic sound-and-light shows
- Elephant experiences that feel built for photos, not animal welfare
- Forced cultural village packages attached to big hotels
- Day trips added only because "everyone does it"

## Good Experience Logic

- Put immersive activities between heavy sightseeing days
- Choose one craft or food experience per region instead of five weak ones
- Use markets with a purpose: food, textiles, antiques, or architecture
