# Food Guide — India

India is not one cuisine. Route design should follow food just as much as monuments.

## What to Order Where

| Region | Go-To Dishes | Notes |
|--------|--------------|-------|
| Delhi / North | Butter chicken, kebabs, chaat, paratha | Street food is best when turnover is high |
| Rajasthan | Laal maas, ker sangri, gatte ki sabzi | Better at lunch or heritage dinners |
| Mumbai | Vada pav, pav bhaji, seafood, Irani cafe dishes | Great mix of street and classic dining rooms |
| Goa | Fish curry rice, cafreal, xacuti, bebinca | Goan Portuguese layer matters |
| South India | Dosa, idli, appam, Kerala fish, Chettinad dishes | Huge difference between states |
| Gujarat | Thali, farsan, snacks | Very strong vegetarian option |

## Strong First-Trip Food Logic

- Use **Delhi** for Mughlai, chaat, and modern Indian
- Use **Mumbai** for seafood, regional crossover, and cafe culture
- Use **Goa** for coastal lunches, pork dishes, and slower meals

## Street Food Rules

- Busy stalls beat pretty stalls
- Fried-to-order is safer than pre-cut fruit or exposed dairy in heat
- Ease into spice level on day one
- Keep one or two "recovery meal" options in every city

## Dining Traps

- Booking only famous tourist restaurants
- Doing heavy street food right after a long-haul arrival
- Assuming vegetarian means boring in India
- Ignoring regional breakfast, which is often one of the best meals of the day
