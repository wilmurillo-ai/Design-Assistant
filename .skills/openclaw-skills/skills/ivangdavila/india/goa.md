# Goa

Goa planning is mostly about choosing the right side, not just choosing "Goa".

## North vs South

| Area | Best For | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Assagao / Anjuna | Restaurants, bars, design-heavy stays | Best for stylish short trips |
| Vagator | Nightlife and villa groups | Easy for beach clubs, less for calm |
| Panjim / Fontainhas | Culture, food, old Portuguese layer | Smart non-beach base |
| South Goa | Quiet beach time, families, resort stays | Better sand-and-rest choice |

## What Is Worth It

- **Fontainhas + Panjim** for architecture and a softer day away from beach repetition
- **A strong beach-hopping half day** instead of trying every famous beach
- **A good Goan lunch** with fish curry rice, prawn balchao, or vindaloo done properly
- **One night area only**: choose Vagator/Anjuna OR calmer South Goa, not both every evening

## Food Moves

| Craving | Go To | Note |
|---------|-------|------|
| Classic Goan home-style | Vinayak Family Restaurant | Unflashy and reliable |
| Modern long lunch | Assagao restaurants | Better for scene and cocktails |
| Beachfront splurge | Good resort or serious beach restaurant | Choose quality over DJ volume |
| Bebinca and sweets | Panjim bakeries and old-school cafes | Best after lunch, not beachside |

## Goa Logic

- Monsoon Goa is green and atmospheric, not beach-classic Goa
- North Goa suits short energetic trips
- South Goa suits families, recovery, and slower pacing

## Goa Traps

- Booking a random "beach resort" far from the part of Goa you actually want
- Expecting all beaches to be swimmable or clean in all seasons
- Mixing nightlife-heavy North Goa with family-rest South Goa in one-night hops
- Assuming a scooter is stress-free for every traveler
