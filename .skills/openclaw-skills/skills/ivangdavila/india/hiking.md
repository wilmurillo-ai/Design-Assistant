# Hiking — India

Mountain planning in India is mostly a season-and-road question before it is a trek question.

## Good Bases

| Base | Best For | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Dharamshala / McLeod Ganj | Easy Himalayan intro | Good for first mountain add-on |
| Rishikesh | Short hikes + yoga/wellness | Easy with Delhi routing |
| Uttarakhand higher valleys | Serious trekking | Needs tighter season planning |
| Ladakh | Landscapes and altitude | Amazing, but not a casual add-on |
| Western Ghats | Monsoon green routes | Better for South India itineraries |

## Best Use Cases

- Add one mountain base after Delhi if the traveler wants cooler air
- Use the Himalayas for repeat visitors or slower trips
- Keep altitude jumps gentle when going to Ladakh or high Himalayan areas

## Hiking Traps

- Adding mountain roads in active monsoon without buffer days
- Treating Ladakh as a simple weekend extension
- Ignoring altitude, permits, or road-closure risk
- Booking treks without checking whether the traveler wants scenery, challenge, or comfort
