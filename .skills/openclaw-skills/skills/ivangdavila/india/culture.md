# Culture — India

India is usually forgiving if the traveler is respectful, patient, and observant.

## Practical Etiquette

- Remove shoes where expected in temples, some homes, and some heritage spaces
- Dress modestly at religious sites and in smaller towns
- Ask before photographing people, rituals, or worship
- Use the right hand when offering or receiving food, money, or prasad when possible

## Social Realities

| Topic | Good Default |
|-------|--------------|
| Bargaining | Expected in markets, not in malls or serious boutiques |
| Tipping | Common in restaurants and for drivers/porters, but keep it proportionate |
| Public affection | Better kept low-key outside nightlife or beach pockets |
| Time | Build extra buffer; urgency rarely speeds systems up |

## Cultural Traps

- Treating religious sites like photo sets
- Dressing beach-casual in conservative city contexts
- Pushing hard in negotiations when a light touch works better
- Reading direct "yes" as operational certainty every time
