# Debt Triage — Personal Finance Tracker

Use this when the user is balancing debt pressure, subscriptions, and short-term cashflow.

## Triage Order

### 1. Protect the floor
- Rent, utilities, food, transport, taxes, and minimum debt payments come first.
- If the floor is not protected, stop discussing optimization and focus on survival cashflow.

### 2. Rank debt by near-term damage
- Highest priority goes to debts with imminent penalties, high rates, or missed-payment risk.
- If two debts are similar financially, prefer the one that removes the most monthly pressure.

### 3. Treat subscriptions like mini-debts
- Recurring charges with low value should be paused, downgraded, or canceled before new goals are added.
- Small monthly leaks matter because they reduce runway every cycle.

## Decision Modes

| Mode | Use When | Bias |
|------|----------|------|
| Avalanche | Rates vary a lot | Minimize total interest |
| Snowball | Motivation is fragile | Score quick wins |
| Cashflow Relief | Bills are crushing runway | Remove the highest monthly drag first |

## Questions That Matter

- Which payment is due first?
- Which debt has the highest rate or penalty?
- Which subscription or bill can disappear with low pain?
- What improves cashflow this month, not just in theory?

## Avoid

- Recommending aggressive payoff while essentials are uncovered
- Ignoring annual or quarterly bills
- Treating debt plans as static when income is irregular
