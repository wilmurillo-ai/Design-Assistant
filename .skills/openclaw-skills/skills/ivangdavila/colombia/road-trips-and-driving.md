# Road Trips and Driving — Colombia

## When Car-First Is Correct

- Coffee Region town loops
- Boyaca heritage circuits
- Santander canyon or small-town exploration
- Flexible countryside routes where public transport is possible but inefficient

## When Car-First Is a Mistake

- Bogota, Medellin, or Cartagena core stays
- Rainy mountain periods with landslide risk
- One-way plans where airport or city drop friction adds stress

## Driver Documentation

- Foreign-license validity can depend on stay status and trip profile, so verify before promising a self-drive plan.
- Carry original license, passport, rental documents, and conservative insurance expectations.
- If the user is uncertain or road-anxious, offer a driver or transit-first alternative.

## Cost Structure Most Travelers Miss

- Tolls on mountain highways
- Fuel and parking in city edges
- Time loss from slower-than-expected road conditions
- Driver fatigue on winding roads

## Practical Routing Rules

- Keep mountain driving legs realistic; road time expands quickly.
- Avoid late-night arrivals to unfamiliar rural stays.
- In rainy months, keep a same-region fallback instead of a tight multi-stop road chain.
