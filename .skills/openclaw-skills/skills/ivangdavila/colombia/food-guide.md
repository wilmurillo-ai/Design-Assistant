# Food Guide — Colombia

## Regional Food Logic

| Region | Good anchors |
|--------|--------------|
| Bogota and central Andes | Ajiaco, modern Colombian tasting menus, bakery and coffee culture |
| Medellin and Antioquia | Bandeja paisa, grilled meats, neighborhood brunch, specialty coffee |
| Caribbean coast | Seafood rice, fried fish, coconut flavors, street fruit, ceviche-style dishes |
| Pacific | Deep Afro-Colombian seafood traditions, coconut, plantain, soups, and stews |
| Coffee Region | Trout, simple farm meals, coffee-estate pairings |
| Santander and Boyaca | Strong soups, arepas, meats, sweets, and market foods |

## Planning Rules

- Use lunch for heavier traditional dishes and dinner for lighter or modern options.
- In hot coastal cities, schedule big walking blocks away from midday heat.
- Users who care about food should stay in one neighborhood long enough to repeat good places, not just hit landmarks.

## Street-Food Rule

- Favor busy, high-turnover stalls.
- Keep hydration and stomach-risk tolerance in mind before beach or boat days.
- Do not build a packed transfer day around uncertain food experiments.

## Coffee Note

If the user wants coffee-country depth, move beyond one plantation tour and build a town + farm + landscape rhythm.
