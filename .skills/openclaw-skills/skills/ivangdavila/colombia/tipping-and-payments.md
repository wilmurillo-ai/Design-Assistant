# Tipping and Payments — Colombia

## Card and Cash Reality

- Cards work well in major cities, nicer restaurants, and organized tourism operators.
- Cash still matters for taxis, smaller shops, local food spots, boats, and some rural areas.
- Keep a backup physical card plus a moderate cash reserve instead of one large carry.

## Service-Charge Reality

- Restaurants may ask whether to include service.
- Do not assume U.S.-style mandatory tipping norms.
- If a service charge is already included, avoid accidental double tipping.

## Payment Traps

- Dynamic currency conversion can worsen rates.
- Street money changers create unnecessary risk.
- ATMs with poor withdrawal limits can force repeated fees.

## Practical Rule

Plan the real total in Colombian pesos where possible, but quote trip budgets in the user's home currency if that improves decision-making.
