# Regions — Colombia

## Region Selection Grid

| Region | Best for | Watch-outs |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Bogota and central Andes | Museums, food, urban base, easy add-ons | Altitude, traffic, cool weather |
| Medellin and Antioquia | Mild climate, nightlife, urban design, nearby pueblos | Holiday crowding, weekend pricing, rain |
| Caribbean coast | Beaches, history, island add-ons, heat-loving trips | Heat, humidity, aggressive upselling in tourist zones |
| Coffee Region | Scenic towns, soft-adventure, coffee estates | Rain, winding roads, dispersed lodging |
| Cali and Pacific | Salsa, Afro-Colombian food, surf, whale season logic | Rain intensity, longer transport chains |
| Santander and eastern Andes | Canyons, adventure, small-city Colombia | Heat in canyon zones, bus-heavy access |
| Boyaca and colonial Andes | Heritage towns, calmer rhythm, road loops | Cold nights, limited nightlife, holiday spikes |
| Amazon and Llanos | Wildlife and frontier travel | Vaccine planning, weather disruption, fewer fallbacks |
| San Andres and Providencia | Reef, beach, slower island pace | Wind, sea conditions, higher costs, limited seats |

## Planning Rule of Thumb

- 7 days: 1 region
- 10-14 days: 2 regions
- 21 days: 3 regions maximum, unless the user accepts multiple flights and light packing

## Transfer Reality

- Mountain corridors make bus times longer than map distance suggests.
- Domestic flights are often the correct answer between Andean cities and Caribbean or island segments.
- Island, Amazon, and Pacific trips need more buffer than city-to-city travel.

## First-Time Colombia Route Suggestions

- Cities + food + moderate pace: Bogota + Medellin
- History + beaches: Cartagena + one island or Caribbean add-on
- Scenery + smaller towns: Coffee Region + Boyaca or Medellin side trip
- Nature + frontier: Medellin or Bogota anchor + one harder region only
