# National Parks — Colombia

## Planning Reality

Colombia parks are not one uniform system. Access, closures, insurance rules, weather, and transport difficulty vary sharply by park.

## High-Value Park Types

| Type | Examples | Main planning issue |
|------|----------|---------------------|
| Caribbean coastal parks | Tayrona, Corales del Rosario | Sea conditions, closures, holiday crowding |
| Andean parks | Chingaza, Los Nevados | Weather volatility, altitude, access windows |
| Jungle parks | Amacayacu-style Amazon planning if open and permitted | Vaccine logic, transport complexity, guide dependency |
| Desert or canyon edge nature | Tatacoa side trips, regional reserves | Heat and long transfers |

## Booking and Access Rules

- Verify the park's current status before suggesting it. Closures and operational pauses happen.
- Some parks or operators require accident insurance or organized entry steps.
- If the user only has one weather window, give them one park primary and one lower-risk backup.

## Tayrona Rule

- Never promise Tayrona casually during peak or closure-sensitive periods.
- Check access rules, internal walking demands, and heat tolerance.
- Pair it only with users who accept early starts and basic movement constraints.

## Nature Safety Rule

- Start early, carry water, and respect closures.
- Jungle or island plans should not sit on the final day before an international departure.
- Remote parks require more buffer than urban travelers expect.
