# Bogota — Playbook

## Best Use Case

First-time urban base for museums, food, history, and onward connections across the country.

## Neighborhood Strategy

- Stay by purpose: historic core for culture, northern zones for hotels and dining, selected central zones for balanced movement.
- Avoid assuming a map-central hotel means faster days; traffic defines Bogota more than distance.

## Movement Rules

- Cluster days by zone instead of crisscrossing the city.
- Put Monserrate and skyline-sensitive plans on the clearest weather window.
- Use a lighter plan on the first day if the traveler is altitude-sensitive.

## Cost Traps

- Underestimating airport-to-hotel time
- Overusing taxis for every daytime movement
- Choosing a cheap stay that creates long unsafe returns

## 3-Day Skeleton

- Day 1: La Candelaria and museum core
- Day 2: Northern dining and cultural districts
- Day 3: Monserrate or major viewpoint plus one focused neighborhood block
