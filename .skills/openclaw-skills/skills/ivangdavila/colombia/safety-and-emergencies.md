# Safety and Emergencies — Colombia

## Essential Numbers

- `123` for emergency response in major cities.
- Use hotel, host, or operator emergency contacts for remote region logistics.
- Keep insurance and airline contacts easy to access offline.

## Risks That Matter for Tourists

- Petty theft in crowded urban and nightlife areas
- Overcharging and aggressive street selling in high-tourism coastal zones
- Weather or landslide disruptions on mountain routes
- Heat, dehydration, and sea-condition risks on coast or island trips

## City Safety Habits

- Use licensed transport or known app pickups at night.
- Keep phone and card handling discreet.
- Avoid designing late-night cross-city moves just to save hotel cost.

## Nature and Frontier Safety Habits

- Start early and respect closures.
- Do not improvise remote hikes, jungle routes, or boat transfers without local current info.
- Keep offline copies of IDs, bookings, and key addresses.

## Protest and Operational Disruption Rule

If a corridor becomes unreliable due to protests, road closures, or weather:
1. Stop chaining the original plan
2. Rebase in the nearest functional city
3. Rebuild the remaining itinerary around safe, reachable segments
