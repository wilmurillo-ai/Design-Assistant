# Telecoms and Apps — Colombia

## Connectivity Setup

- Prefer eSIM or a reliable local SIM plan before the first intercity jump.
- Download offline maps before mountain, island, or jungle segments.
- Keep screenshots of bookings, Check-Mig, and airport details.

## Urban and Travel Tools

| Use case | Recommended app or system |
|----------|---------------------------|
| Bogota transit | TuLlave-aware planning plus map backup |
| Medellin metro network | Civica-aware planning plus official metro info |
| Flights | Airline official apps for gate and baggage changes |
| Road or regional travel | Offline maps plus INVIAS road-status checks |
| Weather-sensitive routes | IDEAM and local operator updates |

## Battery and Device Hygiene

- Carry a power bank and cable access in the day bag.
- Keep a backup payment method separate from the phone.
- Save hotel addresses in Spanish-friendly format for drivers and check-ins.

## Practical Rule

No phone backup means fragile travel in Colombia. Build redundancy for maps, payments, and entry documents.
