# Weather and Seasonality — Colombia

## Climate Reality

Colombia is a microclimate country, not a single-season destination. Altitude and coast shape the trip more than broad national averages.

## High-Impact Patterns

| Zone | Typical feel | Main planning issue |
|------|--------------|---------------------|
| Bogota and high Andes | Cool to mild, rain swings | Layering, afternoon rain, altitude adjustment |
| Medellin valley | Spring-like but rainy cycles | Rain can disrupt day trips and viewpoints |
| Caribbean coast | Hot and humid | Midday fatigue, sea conditions, holiday peaks |
| Pacific | Heavy rain for much of the year | Operational reliability and backup value |
| Coffee Region | Mild but wet-prone | Green landscapes come with rain tradeoffs |
| Amazon and Llanos | Hot, humid, strongly seasonal | River, wildlife, and mosquito conditions vary |

## Planning Rules

- Use altitude for comfort design: Bogota first is not the same as Cartagena first.
- Caribbean and island plans need heat management, not just beach enthusiasm.
- Pacific and jungle routes need weather backup built into the schedule.

## Holiday and Crowd Notes

- Holy Week, year-end holidays, and long weekends distort prices and transport capacity.
- If the user needs lower friction, bias toward shoulder weeks and earlier bookings.
- Do not combine a weather-sensitive island segment with a fixed international departure the next morning.

## Practical Rule

For Colombia, "best time" is really "best region for this traveler in this window."
