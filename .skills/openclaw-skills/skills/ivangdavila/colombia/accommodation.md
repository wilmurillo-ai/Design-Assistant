# Accommodation Strategy — Colombia

## Base Selection Logic

Pick bases by movement friction and night safety, not by the cheapest pin on the map:

- Walkability to the trip's main daytime zones
- Reliable late-night return options
- Airport or terminal transfer simplicity
- Noise, heat, and hill access in real conditions

## Stay Patterns by Trip Type

| Trip type | Better pattern |
|-----------|----------------|
| First-time city visit | One strong neighborhood base and zone-clustered days |
| Beach or island trip | Fewer hotel changes and more weather buffer |
| Coffee or mountain loop | Two regional bases instead of daily hops |
| Family trip | Fewer check-ins, easier meals, and predictable transport access |

## Booking Filters That Matter

- Air conditioning where Caribbean heat is non-negotiable
- Reliable hot water and stairs reality in Andean or colonial properties
- Quiet-sleep tradeoff vs nightlife access
- Airport transfer clarity for late arrivals

## Neighborhood Rule

In major cities, one step better on safety and transport usually beats one step cheaper on nightly rate.

## Practical Trap

Do not book the absolute cheapest coastal or hill property if the user will lose an hour each day to taxis, stairs, or unsafe returns.
