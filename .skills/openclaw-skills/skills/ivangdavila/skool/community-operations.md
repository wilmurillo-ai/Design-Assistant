# Community Operations - Skool

## Start With the Group Job

Define the group in one line before changing anything:
- who it is for
- what recurring problem it solves
- what members get weekly that keeps them coming back
- what the paid or free boundary really means

If that line is fuzzy, approval, content, and automation decisions will drift.

## Approval and Onboarding

- Keep membership questions short enough to complete and sharp enough to filter obvious mismatches.
- Match instant approval to risk tolerance. High-volume and low-risk groups can be more permissive than premium, high-touch groups.
- Welcome flow should tell members the first action, the first win, and the first live date that matters.

## Posting and Engagement Cadence

- Run one flagship thread or event cadence that members can predict.
- Tie posts to member actions: introduce yourself, complete lesson one, attend event, share win, submit blocker.
- Avoid filling the feed with admin-only updates that do not change member behavior.

## Moderation and Trust

- Clarify what gets removed, who can remove it, and how edge cases are handled.
- Keep moderation standards consistent across free and paid members.
- Treat spam waves and repeated low-quality applicants as a funnel problem, not just a moderation problem.

## What Good Looks Like

- new members reach the first meaningful action fast
- active members know the next event or next unlock
- moderators can explain the rules without improvising
- retention work is tied to actual member milestones, not just content volume
