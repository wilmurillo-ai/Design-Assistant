# Costs and Taxes — New York (State)

Use this file for salary reality, relocation math, tax questions, or total-cost comparisons.

## New York Cost Rule

Do not reduce New York math to "high taxes" or "high salaries." The real answer depends on state tax, local property tax, commuting structure, heating, insurance, and whether the user is paying for access to New York City or avoiding it.

## Core Cost Buckets

### 1. Income and payroll reality
- New York salary range varies dramatically between NYC finance or tech, suburban professional work, state-government tracks, and smaller upstate markets.
- A higher salary only wins if the commute, tax, and housing system do not absorb it.

### 2. Housing and property-tax reality
- Some markets look cheaper monthly until property tax, insurance, heating, or necessary car ownership are added back in.
- Long Island and many suburban counties can shock buyers more with carrying cost than sticker price.

### 3. Transit, tolls, and car math
- In downstate corridors, rail passes, tolls, parking, and bridge or tunnel use often matter more than gas price alone.
- In upstate and rural regions, car ownership, winter maintenance, and insurance become baseline costs.

## Questions to Answer Before Recommending a Region

- Is the user optimizing for salary growth, family stability, commute simplicity, or lifestyle?
- Are they buying or renting?
- Do they need NYC access every day, sometimes, or almost never?
- Is winter driving and home maintenance acceptable?

## Common Mistakes

- Comparing New York to tax-free states without comparing school quality, transit, healthcare access, and pay bands.
- Calling an area "affordable" without including heating and commuting cost.
- Forgetting that local taxes and property carrying costs vary meaningfully inside the state.
- Using statewide averages when the user is making a borough, suburb, or county choice.
