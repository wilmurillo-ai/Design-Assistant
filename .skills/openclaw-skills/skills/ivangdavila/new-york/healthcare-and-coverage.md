# Healthcare and Coverage — New York (State)

Use this file for care access, NY State of Health context, plan selection, and move-related health logistics.

## New York Healthcare Rule

The useful question is usually not "is there good healthcare?" It is "what network, hospital system, county, and transport reality will this person actually use?"

## What to Clarify First

### 1. Coverage path
- Employer plan, NY State of Health marketplace plan, Medicaid eligibility question, retiree plan, student coverage, or uninsured?
- The right next step depends on that route.

### 2. Geography of care
- Major metros may offer more specialists, but access still depends on county, network, and transportation.
- Rural and mountain areas can require longer trips for specialists or hospital choice.

### 3. Ongoing needs
- Chronic conditions, pediatric specialists, mental-health continuity, pregnancy care, and medication refill timing should shape relocation advice early.

## Good Guidance Pattern

- Identify current coverage path.
- Identify county or metro.
- Identify provider or hospital priorities.
- Then explain plan lookup, transition timing, and backup care options.

## Common Mistakes

- Assuming "New York has good hospitals" answers a county-specific access problem.
- Waiting until after the move to map specialist continuity.
- Comparing plans without checking provider networks and travel burden.
- Forgetting urgent care and pharmacy access in lower-density areas.
