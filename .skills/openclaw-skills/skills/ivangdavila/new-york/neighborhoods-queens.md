# Queens Neighborhoods

## Astoria
- **Vibe:** Diverse (Greek roots, now global), neighborhood feel, beer gardens. Family-friendly with nightlife.
- **Cost:** $$ — 1BR $2,500-3,000
- **Transit:** Excellent — N/W, M/R. 20-30 min to Midtown. Near LaGuardia.
- **Safety:** Good (7.5/10)
- **Good For:** Young professionals, families, foodies seeking value
- **Food:** Taverna Kyclades (Greek seafood), Tufino's, Sugar Freak
- **Notable:** Museum of Moving Image, Astoria Park

## Long Island City (LIC)
- **Vibe:** Manhattan-lite. Glass towers, rooftop pools, waterfront luxury. Rapid development.
- **Cost:** $$$$ — Average ~$4,137
- **Transit:** Excellent — 7 (one stop to Grand Central!), E/M, G, N/W, Ferry
- **Safety:** Good (7.5/10)
- **Good For:** Professionals wanting Manhattan access, new construction, amenities
- **Food:** Casa Enrique (Michelin star), Mu Ramen
- **Notable:** Gantry Plaza State Park, MoMA PS1, skyline views

## Flushing
- **Vibe:** NYC's largest Chinatown (and US). Bustling, authentic, food paradise. Less English spoken.
- **Cost:** $$ — More affordable, diverse housing
- **Transit:** Good — 7 train terminus, LIRR. Further from Manhattan (40+ min)
- **Safety:** Medium (6.5/10) — watch belongings in crowds
- **Good For:** Food lovers, families, authentic Asian communities
- **Food:** Joe's Shanghai, Nan Xiang (soup dumplings), New World Mall food court, White Bear
- **Notable:** Diverse Asian cuisines (Sichuan, Dongbei, Xinjiang, not just Cantonese)

## Jackson Heights
- **Vibe:** Most diverse zip code in US. South Asian + Latin American hub. Historic garden apartments.
- **Cost:** $ — Most affordable. 1BR ~$2,200
- **Transit:** Good — 7, E/F/M/R. 20 min to Midtown. Near LaGuardia.
- **Safety:** Medium (6/10) — caution near Roosevelt Ave late night
- **Good For:** Budget-conscious, food lovers, embracing diversity
- **Food (South Asian):** Jackson Diner, Angel Indian, Phayul (Tibetan)
- **Food (Latin):** Arepa Lady, Birria-Landia, Cositas Ricas
- **Notable:** Historic district, Queens Pride Parade
