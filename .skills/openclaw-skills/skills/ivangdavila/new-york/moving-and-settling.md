# Moving and Settling — New York (State)

Use this file for relocations from another state or from outside the U.S.

## First 90 Days

### Days 1-7
- Confirm lease or closing documents, utility handoff, internet, renter or homeowner insurance, parking reality, and whether heating fuel or snow removal is on you.
- Map the essentials: grocery, pharmacy, urgent care, commute path, transit station, and the nearest winter, flood, or outage issues that actually matter.
- If schools matter, confirm the exact district and assignment logic before assuming the address works.

### Days 8-30
- Build the license and vehicle plan using `new-york-dmv-and-vehicles.md`.
- Update mailing address, employer records, bank, insurance, and voter registration in the right order.
- Create a winter-readiness kit before the next snow, freeze, or major rain event tests the home.

### Days 31-60
- Pressure-test the commute at the real hour, not a midday sample.
- Re-evaluate housing assumptions: heating cost, parking, commute, school fit, and whether the region still matches the user's actual routine.
- If buying, start tax, insurance, and flood or snow-load screening early rather than after emotions lock in.

### Days 61-90
- Lock in recurring care: primary doctor, pediatrician, dentist, and urgent care fallback.
- Decide whether the metro is still the right fit or whether the real problem is neighborhood, suburb, school district, or rail corridor choice.
- Capture unresolved loops in memory so future New York advice starts from reality.

## Documents to Keep Ready

- ID and proof of lawful presence if applicable
- Lease or deed
- Proof of insurance
- Vehicle title or registration
- New York residency documents when required
- School, vaccine, and care records if moving with children

## Relocation Rules

### 1. New York setup is sequence-sensitive
- The same document often unlocks the next one.
- Do not treat license, registration, utilities, school paperwork, insurance, and commute planning as separate tracks.

### 2. Ask "city, county, ZIP" early
- New York answers often go wrong because the user says only "upstate" or "near the city."
- School districts, utility setup, flood exposure, and commuting cost can change fast across nearby ZIPs.

### 3. Check the hidden recurring costs
- State income tax, local property tax, heating, tolls, parking, and rail passes can erase headline salary gains.

## Common Mistakes

- Moving without testing the real commute or winter travel path.
- Signing a lease before checking heating setup, flood history, and parking rules.
- Assuming all New York suburbs have the same school, tax, and transit logic.
- Waiting too long to organize DMV and vehicle workflows.
