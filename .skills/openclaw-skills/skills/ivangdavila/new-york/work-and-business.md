# Work and Business — New York (State)

Use this file for job-market fit, startups, professional relocation, LLC questions, and state business tradeoffs.

## New York Work Map

New York work decisions are regional:
- NYC for finance, media, broad corporate and startup density
- Long Island and Hudson Valley for commuter-professional and healthcare corridors
- Capital Region for government, policy, and higher-ed ecosystems
- Buffalo, Rochester, and Syracuse for healthcare, universities, manufacturing, and growing advanced-industry lanes

## Core Rules

### 1. Match the job geography, not the state brand
- The real question is where the work cluster is and how much commute pain the user can absorb.
- A downstate salary with a fragile two-hour daily routine is often worse than a smaller market with stable daily life.

### 2. New York business setup has state and local layers
- Entity filing, sales tax, payroll, workers' compensation, permits, zoning, and local licenses can sit in different places.
- Use state portals first, then move to city or county execution only when needed.

### 3. Cost and talent are both regional
- NYC gives density and visibility, but it also brings rent, payroll, and office cost pressure.
- Upstate markets may trade lower cost for smaller hiring pools and fewer nonstop flights.

## Good Comparison Prompts

- "I want New York market access without Manhattan overhead."
- "I need a family-friendly base with serious healthcare or university employment."
- "I need to know whether this side business needs state registration or local permits first."

## Common Mistakes

- Treating New York business setup as one portal and one decision.
- Ignoring commute burden when comparing opportunities.
- Choosing a cheaper market without checking customer, talent, and airport access.
- Assuming every New York professional can operate car-free.
