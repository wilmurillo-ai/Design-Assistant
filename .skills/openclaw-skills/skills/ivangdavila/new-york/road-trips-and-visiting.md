# Road Trips and Visiting — New York (State)

Use this file for statewide travel, park planning, leaf-peeping or summer routing, and multi-stop itineraries.

## Visiting Rule

New York travel works best when the user picks the right base and season. The state is too varied for one generic itinerary.

## Best Use Cases

### 1. City plus nature split
- Pair NYC with Hudson Valley, Catskills, Finger Lakes, Adirondacks, or Long Island depending on season and tolerance for driving.
- The right combo depends on rail access, car need, reservation timing, and weather.

### 2. Upstate loop
- Capital Region, Saratoga, Lake George, Adirondacks, Finger Lakes, Rochester, Buffalo, and Niagara can work well in a loop when daylight and driving tolerance are realistic.
- In colder seasons, reduce ambition and increase buffer.

### 3. Seasonal travel
- Fall foliage, summer lakes, ski season, holiday city trips, and shoulder-season wine-country weekends all need different timing.
- State parks, camping, and resort areas can become reservation problems rather than routing problems.

## Good Planning Questions

- Is the user flying in or driving from nearby?
- Do they want cities, food, lakes, hiking, skiing, family activities, or quiet towns?
- Are they comfortable with long drives, winter roads, or parking-heavy bases?

## Common Mistakes

- Treating New York as an NYC add-on instead of a full statewide trip canvas.
- Overstuffing the Hudson Valley, Catskills, and Adirondacks into one short weekend.
- Forgetting thruway tolls, seasonal closures, and reservation windows.
- Planning shoulder-season or winter trips without weather fallback options.
