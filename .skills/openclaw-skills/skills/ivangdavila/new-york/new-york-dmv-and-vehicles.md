# DMV and Vehicles — New York (State)

Use this file for driver licenses, REAL ID questions, vehicle registration, titles, inspections, and everyday driving reality.

## First Sequence for New Residents

### Do this first
- Decide whether the user is exchanging an out-of-state license, applying for a first New York license, or just updating address and records.
- Check whether the user needs REAL ID or Enhanced ID timing for domestic flights or border-crossing use.
- Gather proof of identity, lawful status if applicable, Social Security eligibility documentation, and New York residency proofs before scheduling office time.

### Do this next
- Plan vehicle insurance, registration, and plate workflow together rather than one by one.
- Confirm whether emissions and safety inspection timing applies right away after registration or transfer.
- If the user is moving from a car-light place, verify whether the new region really needs a car before buying one.

### Do this later
- Clean up toll tags, parking permits, and local snow-emergency rules once the home base is stable.
- For households with multiple cars, compare insurance, parking, and winter-storage cost before keeping everything.

## Core Rules

### 1. Residency starts the clock
- New York expects license exchange and vehicle paperwork after residency begins.
- The right answer depends on whether the user has already established residence, signed a lease, or moved the vehicle into regular New York use.

### 2. Registration is not the whole vehicle cost
- Registration, inspection, insurance, tolls, parking, winter tires, and snow removal all matter.
- In many downstate and college-town areas, the better answer may be fewer cars, not better cars.

### 3. Regional driving reality matters
- NYC and some rail-oriented suburbs punish casual car ownership with parking and insurance pain.
- Upstate and rural corridors often require a car even when housing is cheaper.

## What to Verify on Official Sources

- Out-of-state license exchange rules and residency timing
- REAL ID or Enhanced ID document requirements
- Registration, title transfer, and plate steps
- Inspection timing and emissions rules
- Office appointments, reservations, and closures

## Common Mistakes

- Waiting until travel is imminent to think about REAL ID timing.
- Treating New York car ownership like a flat statewide cost.
- Forgetting tolls, bridge or tunnel use, parking permits, and winter equipment.
- Assuming a suburban rail station makes every local errand car-free.
