# NYC Food Halls & Markets

## Chelsea Market
**Location:** Meatpacking District (75 9th Ave)
**Hours:** 7am-9pm daily
**Vibe:** Tourist-heavy but quality. Former Nabisco factory (first Oreo made here).

**Must-try:**
- **Los Tacos No. 1** — Best tacos in market ($4-5/taco)
- **Lobster Place** — Fresh seafood, raw bar
- **Mokbar** — Korean rice bowls
- **Very Fresh Noodles** — Hand-pulled noodles

**Price:** $$ — Expect $15-25 per person

---

## Smorgasburg
**Location:** Williamsburg (Sat), Prospect Park (Sun) — outdoor, seasonal (Apr-Oct)
**Hours:** 11am-6pm
**Vibe:** Brooklyn food festival. 100+ vendors. Crowded but worth it.

**Must-try:**
- Whatever's new/hyped
- Ramen burger (iconic)
- Rotating Malaysian, Filipino, Thai vendors

**Price:** $-$$ — $5-15 per item, plan $20-30 to graze

---

## Essex Market
**Location:** LES (88 Essex St, new building)
**Hours:** 8am-9pm daily
**Vibe:** Neighborhood market since 1818. Less touristy than Chelsea.

**Must-try:**
- **Puebla Mexican Food** — Solid tacos
- **Shopsin's** — Chaotic, legendary, random rules
- **Nom Wah Nolita** — Dim sum

**Price:** $-$$ — Cheaper than Chelsea Market
