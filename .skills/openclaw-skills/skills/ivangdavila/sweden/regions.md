# Regions - Sweden

## Region Selection Grid

| Region | Best for | Watch-outs |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Stockholm | First-time city base, museums, design, ferries, easy rail links | Premium lodging, summer crowding, old-town cobbles |
| Stockholm Archipelago | Island hopping, short summer escapes, nature with low friction | Seasonal service, weather, late-booking risk |
| Gothenburg and West Coast | Seafood, relaxed city pace, Bohuslan islands, road-rail mix | Summer demand and island timetable limits |
| Malmo and Skane | Bikeable city, easy Copenhagen pairing, castles, beaches | Cross-border logistics, wind, fewer late-night options |
| Dalarna and Central Sweden | Lake country, cabins, folk culture, midsummer, skiing | Car often improves reach, shoulder seasons can be quiet |
| Gotland | Medieval town base, beaches, cycling, summer events | Short peak season and fast sell-outs |
| Swedish Lapland | Aurora, winter activities, national parks, midnight sun | Internal flight timing, cold, darkness, remote logistics |

## Planning Rule of Thumb

- 5 days: one main base
- 7 days: one base plus one nearby add-on
- 10-14 days: two strong regions or one south-north pairing with a flight

## Transfer Reality

- Sweden is easy in the south and center by rail, but north-south distance is large.
- Stockholm, Gothenburg, and Malmo combine well; Lapland usually needs its own trip logic.
- Island and remote-nature plans should be treated as timetable problems, not just map-distance problems.

## First-Time Sweden Suggestions

- Classic first trip: Stockholm plus one archipelago or central-Sweden add-on
- Food and coast: Gothenburg plus Bohuslan
- City plus easy cross-border contrast: Malmo-Skane with Copenhagen access
- Winter signature trip: Stockholm plus Lapland by flight or sleeper train
