# Accommodation - Sweden

## Base Selection Rules

- Stay near the transport mode that dominates the trip: central station for rail plans, harbor access for island plans, airport shuttle access for one-night buffers.
- In Stockholm, location quality usually beats a cheaper room that adds long daily transit.
- In Lapland, choose lodging by activity pickup logic, not just room aesthetics.

## Region by Region

| Area | Best stay logic | Main trap |
|------|-----------------|-----------|
| Stockholm | Norrmalm, Sodermalm, or station-friendly zones for easy movement | Cheap outer areas that burn time every day |
| Gothenburg | Central tram network or near the station | Overpaying for waterfront without transport gain |
| Malmo | Walkable center or station access for Denmark links | Forgetting airport or bridge timing |
| Gotland | Visby for first trips, car or bike access for rural stays | Summer late booking |
| Lapland | Base by tours, rail station, or airport transfer | Remote lodging without confirmed pickups |

## Booking Pattern

1. Confirm the region and month first.
2. Lock the hardest inventory next: midsummer, west-coast summer weekends, Gotland peak season, or Lapland winter packages.
3. Only then optimize for charm, cabin style, or exact neighborhood.

## Practical Filters

- Elevator or step-free access if winter luggage or mobility matters
- Breakfast and supermarket distance in remote or family-heavy plans
- Flexible cancellation when weather is a major risk driver
- Parking only when the trip truly needs a car

## Tourist Trap to Flag

One-night churn looks efficient in Sweden but often wastes the strongest advantage the country offers: calm, well-connected bases with clean day-trip logic.
