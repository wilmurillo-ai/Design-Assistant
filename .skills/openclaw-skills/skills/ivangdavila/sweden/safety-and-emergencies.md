# Safety and Emergencies - Sweden

## Essential Numbers

- `112` for urgent police, fire, and ambulance emergencies
- `1177` for healthcare advice and non-emergency medical guidance
- `114 14` for many non-urgent police matters inside Sweden

## Alert Systems to Use

- SMHI for weather warnings
- Krisinformation for broader official crisis messaging
- Airline, rail, and airport apps for disruption updates

## City Safety Habits

- Use normal big-city awareness in stations, airport links, and nightlife areas.
- Keep the hotel address and departure details saved offline.
- Watch for pickpocket-style distraction risk in crowded transport nodes.

## Nature and Winter Safety

- Cold, wind, and wet conditions can escalate faster than users expect, especially in the north and on ferries.
- Check daylight and road conditions before remote drives.
- In Lapland or mountain contexts, downgrade early if forecast or clothing is weak.

## Incident Response Rule

If a route becomes unsafe due to weather, storm, snow, ferry disruption, or wildfire-style outdoor closures:
1. Stop movement
2. Rebase to the nearest strong transport node
3. Rebuild the remaining days around low-risk options
