# Telecoms and Apps - Sweden

## Connectivity Setup

- Prefer eSIM or roaming that is confirmed before arrival.
- Download offline maps for Lapland, islands, and rural cabin routes.
- Keep screenshots of bookings and transfer instructions for winter or ferry days.

## High-Value App Stack

| Use case | Recommended app or system |
|----------|---------------------------|
| Long-distance rail | SJ or relevant operator app |
| Stockholm transit | SL |
| Gothenburg transit | Vastrafik |
| Skane transit | Skanetrafiken |
| Flights and airport info | Swedavia and airline apps |
| Weather and alerts | SMHI and Krisinformation |

## Payment and Local Habits

- Contactless card readiness matters more than local cash access.
- Swish is common locally, but tourists should not assume they need it.
- Save one backup payment method outside the main wallet.

## Device Hygiene

- Carry a power bank on long rail, ferry, or winter activity days.
- Save passport, policy, and booking references offline.
- No phone means much weaker transport resilience, especially in remote or weather-sensitive trips.

## Practical Rule

In Sweden, the trip works best when maps, tickets, weather alerts, and payment all stay available from the same phone.
