# Customs and Border - Sweden

## What to Check First

- Whether arrival is from another EU country or from outside the EU customs area
- Whether the traveler is carrying alcohol, tobacco, food, large cash amounts, or prescription medication
- Whether the trip includes tax-free shopping plans on departure

## High-Impact Customs Areas

| Topic | Why it matters |
|-------|----------------|
| Alcohol and tobacco | Allowances differ sharply by origin and purpose |
| Cash declaration | EU cash-reporting rules can apply at EUR 10,000 or more |
| Food and plant products | Non-EU arrivals face stricter controls |
| Medicines | Original packaging and prescriptions reduce border friction |

## Practical Rules

- For alcohol and tobacco, ask where the traveler is arriving from before quoting limits.
- For large cash amounts, treat EUR 10,000 as a declaration threshold to verify early.
- If the traveler wants to bring meat, dairy, or plant material from outside the EU, tell them to check Tullverket before departure.
- Duty-free and tax-free shopping are useful only when the route and residency rules actually match the scheme.

## Border Comfort Checklist

- Passport or ID is easy to access on arrival
- Medication is in original packaging when possible
- Restricted goods have been checked against official customs guidance
- High-value purchases and receipts are organized if tax-free shopping is planned

## Tourist Trap to Flag

Users often assume Sweden border friction is zero because the country feels orderly. In practice, the mistakes are simple: wrong alcohol assumptions, cash not declared, or medicines carried without easy documentation.
