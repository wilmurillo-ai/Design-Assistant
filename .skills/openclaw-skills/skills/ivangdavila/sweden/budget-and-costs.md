# Budget and Costs - Sweden

## Useful Budget Bands (Per Person / Day)

| Style | Typical range | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Budget | SEK 900-1,600 | Hostels or simple hotels, supermarket meals, transit-first plans |
| Mid-range | SEK 1,600-3,000 | 3-star hotels, mixed dining, museums, some long-distance transport |
| Premium | SEK 3,000+ | Prime lodging, stronger dining, taxis, curated activities, Lapland add-ons |

## Major Cost Drivers

- Stockholm summer and design-heavy central stays
- Lapland winter packages and activity bundles
- Gotland or archipelago peak-season lodging
- Airport transfers, ferries, and last-minute domestic transport
- Restaurant alcohol and full-service dining

## Cost Control Levers

- Fewer bases, longer stays per base
- Rail-first planning in the south and center
- Lunch deals and supermarket strategy
- Shoulder-season timing when daylight still works
- Choosing one premium region instead of trying to sample all of Sweden

## Hidden Costs Checklist

- Airport train or shuttle costs
- Ferry tickets or island transport
- Car parking and one-way rental friction
- Winter clothing or gear rental
- Activity pickups in Lapland
- Alcohol and cafe spend drift

## Planning Rule

If the budget is tight, reduce region count before cutting every day down to transport-heavy bargain moves.
