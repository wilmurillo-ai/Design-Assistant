# Domestic Transport - Sweden

## Mode Selection Framework

| Route type | Default winner | Why |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| Stockholm-Gothenburg or Stockholm-Malmo | Rail | Strong city-center to city-center efficiency |
| South Sweden with Denmark access | Rail plus regional transit | Easy bridge and airport integration |
| Stockholm to Lapland | Flight or overnight train | Huge distance, strong tradeoff between time and experience |
| Island or scattered rural plans | Mixed transit or car | Last-mile access matters more than headline speed |

## Rail and Flight Reality

- Sweden rail works best when the user stays station-centric and books sensible times.
- Overnight trains are high-value for Lapland only when the user accepts sleeper logistics and limited flexibility.
- For far north winter trips, flights often simplify the route and preserve daylight hours for activities.

## Airport Logic

- Stockholm visitors often arrive through Arlanda; compare airport transfer cost and time before choosing the "cheapest" flight.
- South Sweden trips may start through Copenhagen Airport even when the destination base is Malmo or Skane.
- Final night should stay near the actual departure node, not the prettiest last stop.

## Local Transit Stack

- Stockholm: SL for metro, commuter rail, buses, and ferries
- Gothenburg: Vastrafik for trams, buses, and local boats
- Skane: Skanetrafiken for Malmo-Lund-regional movement
- National journeys: SJ and other operators for long-distance rail

## Transfer Buffers to Add

- International arrival to train onward: build slack for baggage and passport control
- Winter flights to activities: avoid same-minute pickups with no storm buffer
- Island connections: do not chain the last ferry of the day with a non-flex flight

## Tourist Trap to Flag

The classic Sweden mistake is mixing efficient intercity rail with one rural or island segment and assuming the whole route stays equally frictionless.
