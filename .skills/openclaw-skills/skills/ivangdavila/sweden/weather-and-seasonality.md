# Weather and Seasonality - Sweden

## Macro Seasonal Strategy

| Season | Strong fits | Main risks |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| Spring | Cities, south Sweden, shoulder-value routes | Unstable weather, cool water, patchy service |
| Summer | Archipelago, west coast, Gotland, long-day sightseeing | Peak prices, crowding, mosquitoes, limited late booking |
| Autumn | Cities, food trips, central Sweden | Shorter daylight, wind, rain, earlier closures |
| Winter | Stockholm city breaks, Lapland, festive travel, skiing | Ice, darkness, weather disruption, road risk |

## Daylight Logic

- Summer in Sweden can feel much longer and more active than users expect, especially in the north.
- Winter trips need mood, energy, and transport planning built around limited daylight or deep darkness.

## High-Impact Seasonal Patterns

- Lapland winter is signature Sweden, but only for users who accept cold, cost, and remote logistics.
- Gotland and the archipelago are strongest in summer and shoulder periods with service running.
- Midsummer and school-holiday windows create concentrated pressure on coast and cabin inventory.

## Weather Risk

- Wind and rain can change island and ferry days quickly.
- Winter roads in the north should never be treated like normal city driving.
- Shoulder seasons improve value but increase the need for flexible route logic.
