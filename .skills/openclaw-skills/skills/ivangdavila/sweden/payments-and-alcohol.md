# Payments and Alcohol - Sweden

## Payment Reality

- Sweden is one of the most card-friendly countries in Europe.
- Many businesses prefer or effectively expect card or contactless payment.
- Carry a backup physical card even if mobile wallet is the primary method.

## Cash Guidance

- Keep only a small fallback amount of cash if the traveler feels safer with it.
- Do not build the trip around ATM access or cash-heavy assumptions.
- Small operators and temporary festival settings can still vary, so a backup plan helps.

## Alcohol Buying Logic

| Channel | Typical use | Watch-out |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Supermarkets and convenience stores | Low-alcohol beer and basics | Stronger alcohol is not the main retail model |
| Bars and restaurants | Social drinking and nightlife | Expensive quickly |
| Systembolaget | Stronger take-away alcohol | Limited hours and no casual late-night fallback |

## Practical Rules

- If user cares about nightlife budget, explain that restaurant and bar alcohol changes total trip math fast.
- If user wants self-catering or cabin stays, remind them to plan alcohol purchases before Sunday or late-evening dead ends.
- If user asks about tax-free shopping, confirm residency and departure logic before promising a refund.

## Tourist Trap to Flag

Users from cash-heavy or low-alcohol-cost destinations often underestimate how much Sweden rewards card readiness and punishes last-minute assumptions about alcohol availability.
