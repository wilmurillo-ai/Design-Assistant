# Family Travel - Sweden

## Why Sweden Works Well

- Clean, structured cities with strong public space quality
- Good museum and playground mix
- Easy day-trip logic from major bases
- Nature access without extreme complexity in the south and center

## Planning Rules

- Keep transfers limited; families benefit more from stable bases than from chasing every region.
- In winter, ask about stroller, snow, darkness, and child cold tolerance.
- In summer, islands and beaches work best when weather backup exists.
- Book family rooms early in Stockholm summer and on Gotland.

## Good Family Patterns

- Stockholm with one museum day, one outdoor day, and one ferry day
- Gothenburg plus southern archipelago or amusement-style add-ons
- Dalarna cabin or lake trip for slower pacing

## Trap to Flag

Sweden looks child-friendly, but family trips still break on the same fundamentals: too many moves, weather blindness, and weak meal planning.
