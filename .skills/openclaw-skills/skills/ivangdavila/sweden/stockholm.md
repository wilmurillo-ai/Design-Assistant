# Stockholm - Playbook

## Best Use Case

First-time Sweden base with the strongest museum density, easiest public transport, and the cleanest mix of design, food, waterfront, and day-trip options.

## Neighborhood Strategy

- Norrmalm or around Central Station for efficient first trips and rail links
- Sodermalm for food, nightlife, and neighborhood feel
- Gamla Stan for atmosphere, not always for easiest luggage logistics
- Ostermalm or Djurgarden access for premium and museum-heavy stays

## Movement Rules

- Cluster days by island or district instead of crossing the city repeatedly.
- Use transit and ferries; a car is usually negative value.
- Keep one indoor-heavy museum day and one clear-weather waterfront day.

## Timing Moves

- Summer and Christmas periods increase pressure on central lodging and headline attractions.
- Restaurant reservations matter more on weekends than many visitors expect.
- If a user wants archipelago time, decide early whether it is a day trip or an overnight island stay.

## Cost Traps

- Paying for a cheap room far outside the core and losing time every day
- Using taxis for basic cross-city movement
- Booking every meal in tourist-heavy old-town zones

## 3-Day Skeleton

- Day 1: Gamla Stan, waterfront, and evening city view
- Day 2: Djurgarden museums plus food and cafe rhythm
- Day 3: Sodermalm or archipelago-style add-on depending weather
