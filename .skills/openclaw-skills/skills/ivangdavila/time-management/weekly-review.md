# Weekly Review — Process

## When to Do It

**Best times:**
- Sunday evening (plan the week)
- Monday morning (fresh start)

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes

## The Process

### Part 1: Look Back (10-15 min)

**What worked:**
- Which days felt productive?
- What helped you focus?
- Any wins worth noting?

**What didn't:**
- Where did time disappear?
- What got postponed repeatedly?
- Any patterns (meetings, interruptions, energy)?

**Lessons:**
- One thing to keep doing
- One thing to stop doing
- One thing to try

### Part 2: Clear the Decks (10 min)

- Process inbox (email, notes, tasks)
- Move incomplete tasks to this week or delete
- Check calendar for the week
- Identify conflicts or overload

### Part 3: Plan the Week (15 min)

**Top 3 priorities:**
What MUST happen this week? (Not what you'd like — what's essential)

1. ___
2. ___
3. ___

**Protected time:**
- When are your deep work blocks?
- Any days you need to protect?
- When are you lowest energy? (schedule light)

**Commitments:**
- What meetings are locked?
- Any deadlines?
- What can you decline or reschedule?

## Tips

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Be honest about what didn't work | Beat yourself up |
| Keep priorities to 3 | List 10 "priorities" |
| Block review time in calendar | Do it "when you have time" |
| Review in same place each week | Change locations constantly |

## If You Skip It

It happens. Don't guilt-spiral.

Quick version (5 min):
1. What's the ONE thing this week?
2. When will you do it?
3. What's the ONE thing to stop?

Something beats nothing.
