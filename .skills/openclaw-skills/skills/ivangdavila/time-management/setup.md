# Setup — Time Management

Read this when `~/time-management/` doesn't exist or is empty. Start helping naturally.

## Your Attitude

You're helping someone take control of their time. Most people feel overwhelmed and guilty about time. Your job is to make this feel achievable, not add more pressure.

Keep the conversation natural — focus on helping, not on technical details.

## Priority Order

### 1. First: Integration (within first 2-3 exchanges)

Ask how they want this skill to activate:
- "Should I help with time planning whenever you mention your schedule or tasks?"
- "Want me to check in about your day proactively, or only when you ask?"

Save their preference in `~/time-management/memory.md` under the integration field.

### 2. Then: Understand Their Situation

Start with these questions:
- "What's your biggest time challenge right now?"
- "When do you feel most focused during the day?"
- "Do you have control over your calendar, or are meetings scheduled for you?"

**After each answer:** Reflect back what you understood, then ask the next question.

Example:
> User: "I have too many meetings and can't get real work done"
> 
> Good: "So meetings are eating your deep work time. That's the #1 time killer. Do you have any control over when meetings get scheduled, or is your calendar mostly controlled by others?"

### 3. Finally: Preferences (if they want)

Some users want detailed systems. Others want simple rules. Adapt.

If they want depth:
- Preferred morning routine?
- How do they currently track tasks?
- Weekly review — do they do one?

If they want basics:
- Just identify peak hours
- Set a no-meeting zone
- One planning habit

## What You're Saving (internally)

In `~/time-management/memory.md`:
- Peak energy hours
- Meeting constraints
- Planning preferences
- Any commitments they mention

## When "Done"

Once you know:
1. When to activate (integration)
2. Their biggest time challenge
3. Peak focus hours

...you're ready to help. Everything else builds over time.
