# Buyer Flow - Vinted

Use this workflow when the user wants to buy on Vinted without overpaying or walking into avoidable risk.

## 1. Lock the Buy Screen

Collect the minimum that changes the decision:
- exact item type, brand, size, and condition target
- budget range including shipping and protection fees
- urgency: buy now, watch, or browse
- appetite for negotiation and bundle buying

## 2. Build a Shortlist

Filter aggressively:
- right size and category
- clear front photo and usable detail shots
- honest condition wording and visible flaws
- seller profile that does not feel inconsistent with the item

Do not compare ten weak listings. Reduce to three solid finalists.

## 3. Check Total Cost, Not Ask Price

For each finalist, compare:
- item price
- expected shipping cost
- buyer-side protection or service fees shown in checkout
- likely return or dispute friction if the item is wrong

Cheap listings with weak evidence often become expensive mistakes.

## 4. Decide the Offer Strategy

Use three lanes:
- buy now if price is fair and sell-through risk is high
- send offer if the listing is good but leaves margin to negotiate
- watch if the listing is acceptable but incomplete or overpriced

Avoid automatic lowballing. Tie the offer to real condition or market logic.

## 5. Bundle Only When Fit and Condition Are Aligned

Bundle buying works best when:
- same seller
- same size logic
- same category or use case
- photos and condition quality are consistent across pieces

Do not force bundles just to chase small savings.
