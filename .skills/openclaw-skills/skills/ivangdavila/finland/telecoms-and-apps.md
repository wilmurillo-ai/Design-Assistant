# Telecoms and Apps — Finland

## Connectivity Basics

- Mobile coverage is strong in cities and many main corridors, but remote parks and far-north areas still justify offline backups.
- eSIM or roaming-friendly plans are usually easier than hunting for local SIM value on a short trip.
- Download maps, tickets, and lodging instructions before leaving the city or station.

## Best App Categories

| Need | What to use |
|------|-------------|
| Helsinki transit | HSL app and journey planner |
| Trains | VR app or booking site |
| Long-distance buses | Matkahuolto |
| Weather and warnings | Finnish Meteorological Institute and 112 Suomi |
| Food delivery and convenience | Wolt when useful |
| Taxis and rides | Bolt, Uber, or local taxi apps where relevant |

## Planning Advice

- Keep QR codes and reservation emails available offline.
- Save accommodation addresses and station names before arrival.
- In winter, weather and transport apps matter more than discovery apps.

## Rule

Digital planning works well in Finland until weather, distance, or weak signal interrupts it. Make maps, tickets, and warnings resilient before leaving the main corridor.
