# Weather and Seasonality — Finland

## Seasonal Grid

| Period | Best for | Watch-outs |
|--------|----------|------------|
| May to June | Cities, shoulder travel, long daylight, early nature | Variable temperatures and some services still ramping up |
| July to August | Lakes, archipelago, cabins, family summer routes | Higher domestic demand, mosquitoes, and tourist pricing |
| September to October | Foliage, cooler city trips, early aurora in the north | Shorter days and more weather volatility |
| November to March | Aurora, snow, winter activities, Christmas travel | Darkness, ice, high Lapland prices, and harder self-drive conditions |
| April | Late winter north, transition south | Mixed conditions and uneven product quality by region |

## Winter Rule

- Choose winter for snow, atmosphere, aurora probability, and specific activities.
- Do not choose winter to replicate a summer multi-stop route with different scenery.
- Build around darkness, clothing, and energy management as much as around attractions.

## Summer Rule

- Summer gives long daylight and softer logistics for lakes and islands.
- Long daylight is helpful, but ferry schedules, weather, and fatigue still limit what is smart.
- Summer trips are often better outside a pure Helsinki-only model.

## Aurora and Daylight Logic

- Aurora improves with darkness, cloud luck, and location, not wishful thinking.
- Midnight sun is a meaningful travel feature in the far north, but it changes sleep and pacing.
- South Finland and Lapland can feel like different countries in the same month.

## Planning Rule

Pick Finland region from season first, then match the user to the correct corridor. If season and region fight each other, the trip loses.
