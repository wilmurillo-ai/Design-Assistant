# Regions — Finland

## Region Selection Grid

| Region | Best for | Watch-outs |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Helsinki and the south | First trip, city culture, design, food, easy transit | Can feel expensive and compact if user expects wilderness immediately |
| Lapland | Aurora, snow, winter activities, Christmas travel | High prices, darkness, and weather-dependent planning |
| Lakeland and Tampere | Summer cabins, sauna, lakes, slower pacing | Better with more time and lower urgency |
| Turku and archipelago | Ferries, bikes, island towns, summer light | Shoulder-season service reductions and weather exposure |

## Planning Rule of Thumb

- 4 days: one base, usually Helsinki or one Lapland base
- 5-7 days: one corridor or one base pair with explicit transfer logic
- 8-14 days: two corridors maximum, and only if season and budget support it

## Transfer Reality

- Helsinki works as the default entry and exit hub for most trips.
- Lapland is not a casual side trip if the user hates flights, night trains, or winter transfer days.
- Summer island and lake trips feel easy only when ferry schedules and daylight are already in the plan.

## First-Time Finland Route Suggestions

- City plus sauna: Helsinki and one light south-coast side trip
- Winter highlight: Helsinki plus one Lapland base
- Summer nature: Helsinki plus Lakeland or archipelago
- Slow and scenic: Tampere or Turku paired with cabin or national-park time
