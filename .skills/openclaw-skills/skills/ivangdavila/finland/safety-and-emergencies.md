# Safety and Emergencies — Finland

## Baseline

- Finland is generally a low-drama destination for visitors.
- The main safety risk is often environment and weather, not street crime.
- Winter darkness, ice, isolation, and cold can matter more than city safety headlines.

## Emergency Stack

- Use 112 for urgent emergencies.
- The 112 Suomi app is useful for location-aware emergency support and alerts.
- For health problems that are serious but less clear-cut, route users to local emergency-care guidance rather than guessing.

## Main Risks by Trip Type

| Trip type | Main risks |
|----------|------------|
| Helsinki city break | Ice, slips, heavy wind, normal urban caution |
| Lapland winter | Cold exposure, road conditions, fatigue, weather-dependent activities |
| Summer nature | Water safety, insects, fire restrictions, remote-area self-reliance |
| Archipelago | Ferry timing, weather shifts, bike or road exposure |

## Advice Pattern

- For outdoor trips, check warnings and conditions before departure.
- In winter, protect hands, feet, batteries, and transfer buffers.
- If the user drives, road conditions matter more than raw distance.
- For remote plans, make sure maps, lodging details, and emergency contact paths work offline.
