# Customs and Border — Finland

## Main Traveler Checks

- Customs rules matter most for food, alcohol, tobacco, medicines, and high-value shopping.
- Cash declaration rules can apply for large amounts entering or leaving the EU.
- Airport security and customs are different checks; do not merge them in advice.

## Border Reality

- Finland is easy for standard air arrivals when documents are correct.
- Overland border logic is less forgiving if the user assumes an eastern crossing is open without checking.
- Ferry arrivals still require the same passport and Schengen discipline as other external-border routes when relevant.

## Common Problem Areas

| Topic | Watch-out |
|------|-----------|
| Food and animal products | Import rules vary by origin and product type |
| Alcohol and tobacco | Quotas and taxes depend on where the user is arriving from |
| Medicines | Prescription and packaging rules matter for controlled items |
| Cash | Large sums may need declaration |

## Advice Pattern

- Ask where the traveler is arriving from before answering customs questions.
- Use official customs guidance for exact thresholds instead of guessing from another EU country.
- If the user is carrying gifts, specialty food, or expensive gear, warn them early that Finland and EU rules may not match their assumptions.
