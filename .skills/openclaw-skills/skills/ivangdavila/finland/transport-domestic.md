# Transport Domestic — Finland

## Main Modes

| Mode | Best use | Watch-outs |
|------|----------|------------|
| HSL local transit | Helsinki, Espoo, Vantaa, airport transfers | Zones matter; airport sits in ABC logic |
| VR rail | Helsinki to major cities and night trains north | Peak dates and sleepers can sell out |
| Buses | Regional links and places rail misses | Slower and more season-dependent |
| Domestic flights | Helsinki to Lapland or long-distance compression | Airport overhead reduces the gain on shorter routes |
| Ferries | Suomenlinna and Baltic add-ons | Weather and timetables still matter |

## Best Planning Patterns

- For Helsinki stays, default to HSL unless the user has a late-night or heavy-luggage edge case.
- For Lapland, compare day flight, evening flight, and sleeper train instead of assuming one is always best.
- For summer Lakeland or archipelago, check rail-plus-bus and ferry combinations before recommending a car.

## Airport Logic

- Helsinki Airport is easy to reach by rail, which often beats taxi for value and predictability.
- Tight same-day long-distance connections after landing can still be bad ideas, especially in winter.
- Protect the user from landing, changing terminals, and immediately forcing a northbound train or bus with no buffer.

## Common Mistakes

1. Treating airport-to-center taxi as the default Helsinki move.
2. Assuming night trains are always cheaper or always more restful than flights.
3. Building remote summer days without checking the last return bus or ferry.
4. Forgetting that winter weather can weaken a beautiful multi-leg plan faster than a compact one.
