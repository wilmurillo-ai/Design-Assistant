# Lapland

## Best Fit

- Aurora-focused trips
- Winter activities such as huskies, reindeer, snowmobiles, and easy snow play
- Travelers who want a high-contrast Finland experience instead of a generic city break
- Summer users who specifically want midnight sun, hiking, and northern quiet

## Base Selection Logic

| Goal | Better base |
|------|-------------|
| Easiest first Lapland trip | Rovaniemi |
| Resort and winter-activity rhythm | Levi or Yllas |
| Further north and darker-sky feel | Saariselka or Inari area |
| Summer hiking and slower north | One base with car or reliable local transport |

## Winter Rules

- Never promise aurora; promise probability management.
- Pick one base unless the user truly wants logistics-heavy movement.
- Protect the first evening after arrival and do not stack too many late nights in a row.

## Practical Friction

- Peak winter dates can be expensive and crowded.
- Activity pickups, luggage, and airport or rail transfers matter more than hotel glamour.
- Users without winter-driving confidence should default to one base plus guided add-ons.

## Trap Avoidance

- Do not combine multiple far-north bases in a short first trip.
- Do not treat glass-igloo marketing as route logic.
- Avoid forcing a northern plan in shoulder periods when the user's real goal would be better met in another season.
