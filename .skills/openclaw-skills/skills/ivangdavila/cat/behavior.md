# Cat Behavior

## Patterns to Track

Log the trigger, the environment, and the exact behavior for:
- litter box avoidance or straining
- scratching furniture or doors
- night vocalization
- fear, hiding, or overgrooming
- biting, swatting, or overstimulation during petting
- conflict between cats

## First Adjustments

- Litter issue -> check box count, placement, cleanliness, litter type, and stress changes
- Scratching -> place better scratching options at the current target location first
- Night activity -> increase daytime enrichment and a predictable play-to-food rhythm
- Carrier panic -> train the carrier on calm days, not only before vet trips
- Cat conflict -> separate, lower visual pressure, and rebuild exposure gradually

## Reading the Context

Ask what changed recently:
- new home layout
- guests, noise, or renovation
- new pet or baby
- food or litter change
- schedule change
- illness, pain, or medication

## Escalation

Recommend veterinary or behavior-professional help sooner when there is:
- sudden aggression or fear without an obvious reason
- self-injury or nonstop overgrooming
- escalating conflict between cats
- repeated litter problems not improved by environment changes
