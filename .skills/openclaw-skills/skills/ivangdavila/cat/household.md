# Cat Household Setup

## Baseline Environment

Cats do better with:
- predictable feeding and quiet rest zones
- vertical space and at least one high retreat
- scratching surfaces near entrances, sleep spots, and problem furniture
- at least one hiding option in each main area
- safe window access without escape risk

## Litter Setup

Default to one box per cat plus one extra unless the user already has a working exception.

Check:
- location privacy and easy access
- whether a dominant cat is blocking another
- litter depth and texture changes
- covered versus open box preference

## Risk Reduction

Flag:
- toxic plants or cleaners
- tilted windows or easy escape points
- string, thread, hair ties, and chewable hazards
- loud appliances or visitors that trap the cat away from essentials

## When the Home Changes

For moves, guests, or renovations:
- preserve one familiar room first
- move food, litter, and resting spots as little as possible
- introduce new rooms gradually
- reduce forced handling while the cat is adjusting
