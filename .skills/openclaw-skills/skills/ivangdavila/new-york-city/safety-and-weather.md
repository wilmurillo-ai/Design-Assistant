# Safety and Weather — New York City

Use this file for practical street awareness, transit habits, heat or cold planning, and "what should I watch for?" guidance.

## Practical Safety Rule

NYC usually rewards awareness more than fear. The right answer depends on time of day, route, station, crowding, and how distracted the user is.

## What to Lead With

### 1. Focus on nuisance risk first
- Phone snatching, bag inattention, fake ticket or street scams, and bad late-night routing are more common day-to-day issues than dramatic scenarios.
- The best prevention is route choice and attention, not paranoia.

### 2. Transit safety is situational
- The same station can feel normal at rush hour and poor at 1 a.m.
- Ask about late-night returns, solo travel, and comfort level before saying an area is "fine."

### 3. Weather changes the city
- Summer heat and humidity, winter wind, rain, and slush all change walking tolerance and transfer pain.
- Build in realistic breaks, layers, and indoor fallback options.

## Common Mistakes

- Standing at train doors with phone out.
- Overloading an itinerary in July or in freezing rain.
- Assuming all safe-feeling daytime routes stay good late.
- Treating "walkable" as frictionless for luggage, strollers, or accessibility needs.
