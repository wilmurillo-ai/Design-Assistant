# Moving and Housing — New York City

Use this file for apartment search, move-in sequencing, room-share questions, and early resident setup.

## NYC Housing Rule

The apartment is only half the decision. The building, block, train access, storage, laundry, grocery access, and move-in friction usually decide whether the place works.

## First 60 Days

### Days 1-7
- Confirm lease terms, broker-fee reality if any, guarantor or documentation needs, and move-in rules.
- Walk the block morning and night, then test the real commute and grocery path.
- Check building basics: elevator, laundry, package handling, heat, cooling, and noise.

### Days 8-30
- Set up utilities, internet, renter insurance, and address updates in the right order.
- Learn the nearest pharmacy, urgent care, supermarket, and alternate subway route.
- Decide whether the problem is apartment size, building quality, or neighborhood fit before panicking.

### Days 31-60
- Pressure-test the commute, late-night return, and airport path.
- Reassess storage, laundry, and work-from-home reality.
- Capture unresolved loops in memory so future NYC advice starts from the actual block and routine.

## Apartment Search Rules

### 1. Documentation and timing matter
- Good listings move fast, but pressure is not a reason to skip verification.
- Clarify income, guarantor, international-doc, roommate, and timing constraints first.

### 2. Building reality beats listing photos
- Older walk-ups, street noise, package theft exposure, and weak cooling matter more than staged images.
- Ask how the user will actually live there in July, in the rain, and after a grocery run.

### 3. The right "budget" includes friction
- A cheaper apartment can become expensive if it adds long commutes, car-share dependence, or constant ride-hail use.

## Common Mistakes

- Signing based on video alone.
- Ignoring building management quality and package handling.
- Falling for fake urgency or unverified landlords.
- Choosing a place because it sounds trendy instead of because it works on Tuesday.
