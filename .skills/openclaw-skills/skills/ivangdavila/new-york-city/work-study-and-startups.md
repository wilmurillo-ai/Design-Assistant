# Work, Study, and Startups — New York City

Use this file for office geography, campus fit, startup neighborhoods, and city-based professional decisions.

## NYC Work Rule

The right city base depends on where the routine repeats, not on where the skyline looks best.

## Main Patterns

### 1. Office geography still matters
- Midtown, Downtown, Flatiron, Dumbo, Long Island City, Brooklyn Navy Yard, and campus corridors create different commute math.
- A "great neighborhood" can become a bad week if the daily route is fragile.

### 2. Students need routine fit, not only prestige
- Campus location, housing options, late-night return path, and internship access all matter.
- The right school area depends on lifestyle, not only tuition or ranking.

### 3. Startup and creative scenes are distributed
- NYC startup life is not one neighborhood anymore.
- The useful answer depends on whether the user values investor access, studio space, nightlife, airport ease, or family stability.

## Common Mistakes

- Choosing a home base from general prestige instead of actual office or campus location.
- Ignoring late-night or early-morning commute stress.
- Treating startup advice as if every founder wants the same borough.
- Forgetting that internships and networking also have transit costs.
