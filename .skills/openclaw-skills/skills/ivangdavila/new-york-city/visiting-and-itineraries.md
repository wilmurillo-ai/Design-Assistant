# Visiting and Itineraries — New York City

Use this file for trip design, attraction filtering, day pacing, and booking strategy.

## Visiting Rule

A good New York trip usually has a clear base, a manageable daily footprint, and fewer cross-city hops than most first drafts.

## Trip Design Patterns

### 1. First-time visit
- Pair iconic sights with neighborhood time so the trip does not feel like a queue marathon.
- Put major timed tickets and one anchor meal in place early, then leave room for weather and energy changes.

### 2. Return visit
- Shift toward borough-specific days, food focus, museums, live events, parks, or day trips.
- Repeat visitors usually benefit more from better routing than from more landmarks.

### 3. Family or mixed-group travel
- Pace matters more than total count.
- The right itinerary often mixes one headliner, one flexible neighborhood block, and easy meal logistics.

## Booking Logic

- Reserve high-friction items early if they are must-do.
- Keep airport arrival and departure days intentionally light.
- Match the hotel base to the heaviest daily activity, not to generic centrality.

## Common Mistakes

- Overpacking three boroughs and ten landmarks into a short stay.
- Booking a cheap hotel base that adds daily transit drag.
- Treating Times Square as the center of every good plan.
- Ignoring weather, crowds, or museum-closure patterns.
