# Food and Dining — New York City

Use this file for where and how to eat well in NYC without wasting time or money.

## Food Rule

The best New York food advice is rarely the most famous list. It is the neighborhood, timing, and category fit that matches the user's day.

## Useful Patterns

### 1. Eat by neighborhood, not by attraction
- Tourist zones often punish convenience with bad value.
- Walking a few blocks or changing neighborhoods can improve quality dramatically.

### 2. Match the meal to the day
- Quick slice, solo lunch, late-night meal, reservation dinner, family brunch, and food-crawl day all need different advice.
- The right answer depends on pace, queue tolerance, and travel path.

### 3. NYC is strongest in categories, not only in famous names
- Bagels, pizza, deli, Chinese regional cooking, Korean, Caribbean, Middle Eastern, South Asian, Japanese, and many immigrant-neighborhood scenes reward specific routing.
- Good food advice should point to the right area, not just a brand name.

## Practical Watchouts

- Times Square and adjacent dining are usually convenience plays, not quality plays.
- Reservation-heavy places need planning or flexible timing.
- Some hyped spots are worth it only if they align with the user's route.

## Common Mistakes

- Sending users to famous but low-value stops.
- Building an itinerary around cross-city food hops with no transit logic.
- Ignoring dietary constraints until the last minute.
- Assuming Manhattan contains the best version of everything.
