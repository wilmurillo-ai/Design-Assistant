# Climate Guide — Barcelona

## Overview

Barcelona has a **Mediterranean climate** — mild winters, hot summers, low rainfall, lots of sunshine. More moderate than Madrid (no extremes). Humidity can make summer feel hotter.

**Key characteristics:**
- Warm/hot summers (26-32°C peaks July-August)
- Mild winters (rarely below 8°C)
- 2,500+ hours of sunshine/year
- Low rainfall, mostly autumn/spring
- Sea moderates temperatures
- Humidity higher than inland Spain

## Season Details

### Spring (March - May)

**Best time for sightseeing.** Pleasant temperatures, occasional showers, everything blooming.

| What to Expect | What to Pack |
|----------------|--------------|
| 17-22°C days | Layers |
| Occasional rain | Light jacket |
| Sunny most days | Sunglasses |
| Warm by May | Summer clothes for May |

**Highlights:**
- Sant Jordi (April 23) — books + roses, amazing
- Beach starts being possible late May
- Perfect for walking, sightseeing
- Fewer tourists than summer

### Summer (June - August)

**Beach season.** Hot, humid, crowded. Locals flee in August.

| What to Expect | What to Pack |
|----------------|--------------|
| 26-32°C days | Light, breathable clothing |
| Humidity | Sunscreen (essential) |
| Hot evenings (20-24°C) | Something for AC |
| UV very high | Hat, sunglasses |
| Sea warm (21-25°C) | Swimwear! |

**Tips:**
- Beach in morning or late afternoon
- Sightseeing: Early morning or evening
- AC is your friend
- Hydrate constantly
- Terrace culture peaks

**August Warning:**
- Locals leave for vacation
- City feels different (more tourists, fewer locals)
- Some local shops/restaurants close
- Beaches PACKED
- Festa Major de Gràcia mid-August (amazing if there)

### Autumn (September - November)

**Excellent shoulder season.** Warm start, gradual cooling. More rain than summer.

| What to Expect | What to Pack |
|----------------|--------------|
| September: 24-26°C | Summer clothes still |
| October: 19-22°C | Layers, light jacket |
| November: 14-17°C | Warmer jacket |
| More rain | Umbrella |

**Highlights:**
- La Mercè festival (late Sept) — city's biggest festa
- Beach still possible Sept, sometimes Oct
- Wine harvest season
- Bolets (mushrooms) in markets
- Fewer tourists

### Winter (December - February)

**Mild.** Rarely freezing. Christmas markets, fewer tourists, good for sightseeing.

| What to Expect | What to Pack |
|----------------|--------------|
| 12-16°C days | Medium jacket |
| 6-10°C nights | Layers |
| Mostly sunny | Sunglasses (yes, winter sun!) |
| Some rain | Umbrella |
| No snow | No heavy winter gear needed |

**Tips:**
- Christmas lights beautiful (late Nov-Jan 6)
- Three Kings parade January 5 (bigger than Christmas here)
- Great museum weather
- Terrace culture continues (with heaters)
- Fewer tourists = better deals

- Sunglasses

- Swimwear

## Weather Phenomena

### Llevant (East Wind)

Strong easterly wind bringing rain and rough seas. Can last days. More common in autumn.

### Tramuntana

Cold north wind from Pyrenees. Makes it feel colder, clear skies.

### Humidity

Barcelona is more humid than Madrid. Summer days can feel hotter than temperature suggests.

### Sea Breeze

Afternoon sea breeze (marinada) provides relief on hot days. Near beach = cooler.

---

## Air Quality

Generally good, better than Madrid. Some days in summer with higher pollution (heat inversions). Check AQICN.org if sensitive.

---

## Sun Safety

Mediterranean sun is strong, especially summer:
- UV index can reach 10+ in summer
- Sunscreen essential April-October
- Wear hat during midday
- Sunglasses recommended year-round
