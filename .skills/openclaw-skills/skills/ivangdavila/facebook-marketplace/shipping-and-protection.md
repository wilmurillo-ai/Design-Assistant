# Shipping and Protection - Facebook Marketplace

Use this when the user is deciding whether to ship, use checkout, or document a transaction properly.

## Shipping Is a Trade-Off, Not a Default

Shipping can expand the buyer pool, but it adds:
- fees
- packaging effort
- damage risk
- delivery disputes
- more room for fraud or confusion

Use shipping only when the item, margin, and platform protection still make sense after those costs.

## When Pickup Usually Wins

Prefer local pickup when the item is:
- bulky
- fragile
- low margin
- hard to describe remotely
- expensive to pack well

Pickup also reduces ambiguity for condition checks.

## Proof Chain for Sellers

When a shipped or high-risk local transaction matters, keep an evidence chain:
- item photos before packing
- photos of serials, labels, or included accessories when relevant
- packaging photos
- drop-off or delivery proof
- timeline notes when something goes wrong

Good proof turns a vague dispute into a documentable case.

## Protection Mindset for Buyers

Before paying:
- confirm what protection the platform flow actually offers for that transaction
- avoid ad hoc deposits or side-payment shortcuts
- know whether pickup means inspect first, then pay
- document the listing and the final agreement

If the protection path is unclear, default to the safer transaction structure.

## Shipping Questions That Matter

Ask these before committing:
- who is paying the shipping cost
- whether the item is packed already or needs new packaging
- what damage points matter most
- whether tracking, signature, or insurance are worth the margin hit

## Escalation Rule

If a transaction becomes confusing, document first and advise second.
Do not improvise around missing proof, vague addresses, or changed terms.
