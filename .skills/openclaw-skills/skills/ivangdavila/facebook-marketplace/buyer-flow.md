# Buyer Flow - Facebook Marketplace

Use this when the user is buying, scouting, or comparing listings.

## 1. Build the Search Spec Before Scrolling

Lock five fields first:
- target item
- maximum budget
- maximum travel radius
- minimum acceptable condition
- hard deal-breakers

Without this, browsing turns into random deal-chasing.

## 2. Score Each Listing on Value, Not Hype

Check these in order:
- asking price versus realistic local comps
- completeness: charger, accessories, packaging, documents, keys, parts
- condition signals from real photos, not claims
- pickup friction: distance, timing, parking, loading, testing
- resale or replacement downside if the item fails

If two listings are similar, the cleaner and easier pickup usually wins.

## 3. Force Evidence Before Commitment

Ask only the questions that change the decision:
- Is it still available today?
- What is the exact model or size?
- Are there defects, repairs, or missing parts?
- Can it be tested or shown working at pickup?
- Is the listed price firm, flexible, or already discounted?

Weak or evasive replies lower the listing score immediately.

## 4. Use Negotiation Bands, Not Improvised Offers

Set three numbers before messaging:
- ideal buy price
- acceptable close price
- walk-away price

Never reveal urgency.
Never negotiate against yourself before the seller responds.

## 5. Plan the Pickup Like a Risk Check

Before leaving:
- confirm address area and timing
- bring the right vehicle, tools, or test gear
- choose a payment method with the right protection for the situation
- know exactly what must be inspected before handing over money

For high-risk or high-value deals, safer meeting points beat convenience.

## Buyer Stop Signals

Skip or pause when:
- photos look generic or inconsistent
- the seller pushes deposits or off-platform payment
- the model, measurements, or condition stay vague
- the item is priced far below believable local value
- the meetup plan feels unstable or rushed
