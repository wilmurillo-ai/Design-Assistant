# Listing and Pricing - Facebook Marketplace

Use this when the user is creating, fixing, or relaunching a listing.

## Listing Package

The listing should answer the buyer's first doubts without sounding overbuilt:
- exact item name and useful keywords
- condition stated plainly
- what is included and what is not
- why the price makes sense
- pickup city and any real shipping constraint

The first photo must earn the click.
Do not let the title carry the whole listing.

## Price Triangle

Set three prices before publishing:
- ask price: leaves room for expected negotiation
- floor price: lowest acceptable close
- fast-sale price: use only when time matters more than margin

Choose based on:
- local comps, not dream listings
- item condition and completeness
- seasonality and urgency
- how annoying pickup or packaging will be

## Category and Condition Discipline

Wrong category or misleading condition creates bad leads and future disputes.
Pick the most accurate category even if a busier one feels tempting.
If damage exists, say it before the buyer asks.

## Improve Conversion Before Cutting Price

Fix this stack in order:
- cover photo clarity
- title specificity
- included parts and measurements
- condition honesty
- pickup or shipping terms

If those are weak, a markdown often just attracts lower-quality buyers.

## For Bulky or Local-Only Items

Price must account for pickup effort.
Furniture, appliances, materials, and large bundles usually need a stronger convenience discount than small shippable items.

## Refreshing a Stale Listing

Diagnose before editing:
- wrong price
- weak photos
- vague copy
- bad category
- pickup friction
- unrealistic availability

Do not jump straight to repetitive reposting, duplication, or spammy edits after warnings.

## Seller Output Shape

End with a concrete publishing plan:
- title
- price triangle
- what photos to retake
- what to add to the description
- whether to publish now, revise, or pause
