# Policy and Account Health - Facebook Marketplace

Use this when the user hits removals, warnings, suspicious buyer or seller behavior, or feature confusion.

## Surface and Access Reality

Marketplace advice must respect account and feature limits:
- some workflows require a signed-in main Facebook profile
- some features may differ between desktop web and the mobile apps
- shipping, checkout, and ratings should be treated as availability-dependent, not guaranteed

If the user cannot see a feature, verify the surface and account context before suggesting workarounds.

## Prohibited and Restricted Listings

Before posting, screen for obvious policy risk:
- illegal goods
- unsafe or recalled products
- misleading branded or counterfeit items
- listings whose condition or identity cannot be stated honestly

When unsure, become conservative instead of creative.

## Warning and Removal Workflow

If a listing is removed or a warning appears:
1. capture the exact message and timing
2. save screenshots, listing text, and photos
3. identify the most likely policy mismatch
4. revise facts, category, or item choice before reposting

Do not recommend duplicate relists, mass retries, or account evasion.

## Account-Health Stop Conditions

Stop normal selling advice and switch to recovery mode when:
- removals start repeating
- the user mentions account restrictions or feature loss
- identity or verification prompts appear
- high-risk categories or repeated scam contacts cluster together

Recovery mode means slower, cleaner, evidence-first actions.

## Appeals and Support

Appeals work best when the story is simple:
- what item was listed
- why it is allowed or accurately described
- what evidence proves that

Avoid emotional or speculative explanations.

## Automation Boundaries

Policy risk rises sharply when advice crosses into:
- mass messaging
- scraping behind login
- auto-posting
- multi-account behavior
- anti-detection or restriction bypass

Refuse those paths and keep the workflow manual, explicit, and account-safe.
