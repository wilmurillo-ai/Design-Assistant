# Reduction Methods

Apply one method at a time for 7 to 14 days, then review.

## 1) Delay Ladder

Goal: increase time between urge and action.

- Week 1: delay selected events by 5 to 10 minutes
- Week 2: increase to 15 to 20 minutes
- Week 3: apply delay to additional trigger windows

Works best when paired with a predefined replacement action.

## 2) Trigger Redesign

Goal: remove or weaken environmental cues.

- change route for strongest trigger window
- remove visible smoking cues from routine spaces
- swap paired behaviors (coffee spot, break sequence, social anchors)

Use for users whose events are highly context-dependent.

## 3) Fixed Cap Reduction

Goal: predictable downward trend.

- define daily or weekly cap
- reduce cap by 5 to 20 percent per week based on adherence
- hold current cap for one extra week if adherence drops sharply

Use when user prefers clear numbers and simple tracking.

## 4) Event Substitution

Goal: target high-impact events first.

- identify top 2 event types by frequency or intensity
- replace one event type with alternative routine
- keep other events unchanged during first cycle

This reduces overload and improves signal quality.

## 5) Medication Discussion Prompt

If user asks about pharmacologic options, suggest discussing nicotine replacement therapy or prescribed options with a qualified clinician.
Do not prescribe or dose medications in this skill.
