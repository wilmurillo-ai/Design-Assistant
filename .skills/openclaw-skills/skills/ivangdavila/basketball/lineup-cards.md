# Lineup Cards

Use these as basketball shorthand, then adapt to the user's actual level and roster.

## Primary Roles

| Role | What good looks like | Common mismatch |
|------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Primary creator | bends the paint, reads second defender, creates efficient shots | asked to score every trip with no spacing support |
| Secondary connector | keeps advantage alive, attacks tilted floors, makes quick decisions | over-dribbles and kills advantage |
| Spacer | punishes help and keeps corners or slot occupied | treated as stationary when relocation is needed |
| Rim pressure big | screens well, rolls hard, finishes or creates help collapse | paired with non-shooters that shrink the lane |
| Defensive stopper | absorbs hardest matchup, navigates actions, closes possessions | overburdened with creation too |

## Pairing Logic

- One primary creator plus one fast connector usually stabilizes decision flow
- Two non-shooters need elite screening or passing to avoid clogged paint
- Small lineups need gang rebounding and clear low-man rules
- Big lineups need enough handling and spacing to avoid late-clock stalls

## Lineup Verdict Formula

`Primary action -> spacing shape -> defensive trade-off -> rotation verdict`

Example:
`Spread pick-and-roll -> one corner spacer missing -> weak-side tags crowd roller -> useful versus switching teams, risky versus loaded gaps`

## Common Checks

- Who creates the first advantage?
- Who keeps the second action alive?
- Who guards point of attack?
- Who ends the possession with a rebound?
- Which player becomes the target if the opponent hunts?
