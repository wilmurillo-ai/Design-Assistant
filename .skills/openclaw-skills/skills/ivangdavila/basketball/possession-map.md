# Possession Map Protocol

Use this flow for both previews and reviews.

## 1. Lock the Frame

- Competition, level, age band, and date
- Preview or review
- Available evidence: user notes, stats, film, public reports
- Decision needed: lineup, plan, scout, fix list, or practice response

## 2. Read the Game in Six Buckets

| Bucket | Questions |
|--------|-----------|
| Transition | Who wins the first 6 seconds and why? |
| Early offense | What actions or drag screens create immediate advantage? |
| Half-court offense | Which actions, spacings, and decision points generate good shots? |
| Half-court defense | What shell, matchup, or coverage breaks first? |
| Rebounding and physicality | Who controls second chances and loose-ball possessions? |
| Special situations | What matters in ATOs, end-of-quarter, foul game, and late-clock possessions? |

## 3. Identify Game Levers

- Two strengths that can swing the game
- Two weaknesses that can be attacked
- One matchup that can decide shot quality or foul pressure
- One contingency if the first plan fails

## 4. Output the Decision Layer

For previews:
- best matchup to target
- best coverage or help rule
- best pace or shot-profile priority
- one special-situation watch point

For reviews:
- what repeated, not just what happened once
- which issue is structural vs individual
- what to stop, start, and continue next week
