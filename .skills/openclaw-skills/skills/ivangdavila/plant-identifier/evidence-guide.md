# Plant Evidence Guide

Use this order before locking a result:

1. **Growth habit**
   - tree, shrub, vine, herb, rosette, succulent, grass-like form
2. **Leaf arrangement**
   - opposite, alternate, whorled, basal, clustered
3. **Leaf shape and margin**
   - oval, lanceolate, lobed, serrated, entire, needle-like
4. **Veins, texture, and underside**
   - parallel or netted veins, waxy, hairy, pale underside, prominent midrib
5. **Flower structure**
   - petal count, symmetry, cluster form, color pattern, flower position
6. **Fruit, seed, stem, or bark**
   - pods, berries, cones, spines, bark texture, square stems, canes
7. **Habitat context**
   - indoor, wet area, roadside, woodland edge, dry slope, cultivated bed

Best next evidence when confidence is weak:

- full plant photo
- leaf top and underside
- stem or node close-up
- flower front and side
- fruit or seed structure
- bark photo for woody plants

Do not overclaim from these alone:

- photo does not prove edibility
- photo does not prove toxicity thresholds
- photo does not prove medicinal safety
- hybrid and cultivar IDs often stay tentative
