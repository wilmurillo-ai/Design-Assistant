# Customs and Border

Use this file when users ask what they can bring, how cash rules work, or whether neighboring-country hops are frictionless.

## Core Rules

- Austria sits inside the Schengen area, but customs rules still matter for goods, cash, alcohol, tobacco, and food.
- If entering or leaving the EU with EUR 10,000 or more in cash or equivalent instruments, users should expect declaration rules.
- Meat, dairy, and some plant products from outside the EU can trigger restrictions or seizure.
- Duty-free and allowance questions change based on where the user is arriving from: EU internal travel, non-EU flight, or land border crossing.

## Practical Travel Advice

- Keep medication in original packaging with a prescription copy for anything non-obvious.
- For ski or outdoor gear, pack smart but avoid carrying food items that can create customs friction on non-EU entries.
- If combining Austria with Switzerland, the Balkans, or the UK, remind users that customs logic may differ from the Schengen border experience.
- Airport arrivals feel simpler than some land crossings, but regulated goods and cash rules still apply.

## Border Habits

- Germany, Czechia, Slovakia, Hungary, Slovenia, and Italy connections often feel seamless, but document checks can still happen.
- Rental-car border hops are usually easy inside the nearby Schengen zone, yet insurance, vignette, and parking rules still need explicit planning.
- Train users should still carry valid ID because random checks happen.

## Common Mistakes

- Assuming "no border" means no rules.
- Carrying large cash without understanding declaration duties.
- Packing restricted foods for alpine days without checking origin-country rules.
- Forgetting separate vignette or toll logic when a route crosses multiple countries by car.
