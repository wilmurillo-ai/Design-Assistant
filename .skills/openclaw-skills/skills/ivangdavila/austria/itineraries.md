# Itineraries — Austria

## 4-5 Days: One City + One Contrast

### Option A: Vienna + Wachau
- Days 1-3: Vienna museums, coffeehouse rhythm, one Heuriger evening
- Day 4: Wachau or Krems/Melk day trip by rail/river season permitting
- Day 5: Buffer and departure

### Option B: Salzburg + Lakes
- Days 1-2: Salzburg old town and fortress
- Days 3-4: Salzkammergut day structure with one lake-town focus per day
- Day 5: Buffer for weather or return rail

## 7 Days: Two-Base Classic First Trip

- Base 1: Vienna for 3-4 nights
- Base 2: Salzburg or Innsbruck for 3 nights
- Keep transfers daylight-first unless the user explicitly wants a late arrival
- Do not add both Tirol and Salzkammergut unless the user accepts a faster pace

## 10 Days: Cities + Alps

- Vienna: 3 nights for major museums and urban culture
- Salzburg: 2 nights for compact old town and lake access
- Innsbruck or a Tirol base: 3-4 nights for mountain lift or valley plans
- Final night near the departure airport if flying out early

## 14 Days: Austria With Depth

- Vienna / Lower Austria: 4 nights
- Salzburg / Salzkammergut: 3 nights
- Tirol: 4 nights
- Graz or Carinthia: 2-3 nights
- Use rail for city moves, then add a local car only if lakes, alpine valleys, or winery roads truly need it

## Seasonal Patterns

- Winter city trip: Vienna + Salzburg works better than forcing long alpine hops
- Summer lakes trip: pair Salzburg region with Carinthia or Salzkammergut, not every lake district
- Ski trip: pick one valley cluster and stay put instead of hotel-hopping
- Christmas markets: two cities are usually enough; crowd friction rises sharply on weekends

## Itinerary Quality Checks

- Keep one macro-region per 4-5 travel days
- Add a lighter day after long museum or mountain sequences
- Protect the last night before departure in the departure city
- If weather drives the trip, keep one indoor backup day in every 3-4 day block
