# Domestic Transport — Austria

## Mode Selection Framework

| Route type | Default winner | Why |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| Vienna-Salzburg-Innsbruck-Graz | Rail | Fast station-to-station movement and low parking friction |
| Vienna city-only trip | Walking + transit | Dense core and strong U-Bahn/tram network |
| Salzburg with lake day trips | Rail/bus mix | Better than relocating the car constantly |
| Tirol valley trip | Rail + local bus, or local car if remote base | Depends on exact valley and winter gear load |
| Rural lake or wine route | Local car only where needed | Add after the city leg, not for the whole trip |

## Rail-First Rule

- Start with OBB for intercity planning before discussing cars.
- Austria is one of the easier European countries for city-to-city rail routing.
- For classic visitor routes, the car often adds parking cost and old-town stress without saving time.

## Transfer Reality

- Vienna airport to city or onward rail is easy, but protect buffer if using separate tickets.
- Salzburg and Innsbruck are compact, yet mountain weather can still affect the next leg.
- Do not promise tight same-day flight-to-ski or flight-to-hut transfers without slack.

## Buses and Regional Links

- Regional buses matter most for lakes, valleys, and shoulder-season villages.
- Frequency drops outside main corridors and outside peak visitor periods.
- Sunday and evening service can be the hidden constraint, not headline train time.

## Tourist Trap To Flag

Renting a car from arrival for the whole trip because "Austria is small." The better pattern is usually rail for the backbone, then a short car rental only where it clearly improves execution.
