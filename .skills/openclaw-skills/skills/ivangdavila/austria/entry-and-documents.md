# Entry and Documents

Use this file before booking anything non-refundable.

## Core Logic

1. Confirm nationality and residence status.
2. Check whether user enters Austria as an EU/EEA/Swiss traveler, a visa-free Schengen visitor, or a visa-required traveler.
3. For non-EU/EEA visitors, verify passport validity rules before the itinerary is locked.
4. If the route includes neighboring non-Schengen segments or long stays, re-check the whole legal path rather than Austria alone.

## Practical Rules

- Austria applies Schengen entry logic, so the legal answer often depends on the whole Schengen trip, not only the Austria dates.
- EU/EEA/Swiss travelers usually use a valid national ID card or passport.
- Many non-EU/EEA travelers need a passport issued within the last 10 years and valid for at least 3 months beyond planned Schengen departure.
- Visa-required travelers should work from the Austrian representation or official visa portal, not blog summaries.
- As of March 9, 2026, ETIAS is still not operational; official EU guidance points to a later 2026 start, so confirm status before travel.

## Ask These Before Advising

- What passport is being used?
- Is Austria the only Schengen country or part of a longer Schengen route?
- Total planned days in Schengen?
- Any previous visa refusal, overstay issue, or dual-nationality complexity?
- Is the user entering by air, rail, or car from a neighboring country?

## Common Mistakes

- Treating Austria entry as separate from the rest of the Schengen trip.
- Assuming visa-free means document-free.
- Discovering passport validity issues after booking ski hotels or summer lake stays.
- Ignoring that residency, work, or study questions stop being tourism advice.

## Official Direction

- Austria government portal for entry and residence basics
- Austrian foreign ministry / representation pages for visa-required cases
- EU travel portal for ETIAS status and Schengen updates
