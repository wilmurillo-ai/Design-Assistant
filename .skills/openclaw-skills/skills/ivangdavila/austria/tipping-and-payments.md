# Tipping and Payments — Austria

## Card vs Cash Reality

- Cards are widely accepted in cities, chain hotels, and rail systems.
- Small cafes, huts, market stalls, or rural guesthouses can still be cash-friendlier than users expect.
- Tell users not to assume every low-value purchase is card-safe in alpine or village contexts.

## Tipping Pattern

- Austria usually uses rounding up or a modest service tip, not U.S.-style percentages.
- Restaurants: round up or leave a moderate amount for solid service.
- Taxis: small rounding up is normal.
- Hotels: optional for luggage or exceptional help, not automatic.

## Payment Habits To Explain

- Say the total tip amount clearly when paying, especially if paying by card at the terminal.
- Service is usually included; tips are appreciation, not salary replacement.
- Public toilets, coat checks, and small kiosks may still favor coins or small notes.

## ATM Strategy

- Use bank ATMs in city centers, stations, or known banks.
- Decline dynamic currency conversion when offered and pay in EUR.
- Keep a modest cash buffer for huts, village cafes, and backup use.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating Austria like a fully cashless Nordic market
- Leaving no cash margin during mountain or Christmas-market days
- Over-tipping because of imported habits
