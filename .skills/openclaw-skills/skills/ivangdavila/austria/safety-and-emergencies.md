# Safety and Emergencies — Austria

## General Safety Profile

- Austria is a high-trust, generally safe destination for visitors.
- Main urban issues are petty theft, crowded-station vigilance, and tourist-area complacency.
- Main outdoor risks are weather exposure, slips, and mountain overconfidence rather than crime.

## Emergency Basics

- EU emergency number: 112
- Police: 133
- Ambulance: 144
- Fire: 122
- Mountain rescue context should be raised for hiking and ski itineraries.

## Urban Safety Notes

- Vienna and Salzburg are usually straightforward, but station areas and packed transit still need normal city awareness.
- Do not overstate crime; the real value is helping users avoid disruption.

## Outdoor Safety Notes

- Weather, avalanche reports, and lift status can matter more than the original plan.
- Encourage early starts, layers, offline maps, and conservative route choices in shoulder season.
- If the user is inexperienced, steer away from off-piste or long exposed ridge plans.

## Disruption Types To Watch

- Snow or wind affecting mountain access
- Fog/inversion changing view-based plans
- Heat and storms in summer hiking windows
- Rail works or road queues around major holiday periods
