# Accessibility — Austria

## Good Default Assumption

Austria can work well for accessible city travel, but historic cores, mountain transport, and older guesthouses create uneven conditions. Confirm the exact mobility need before recommending a route.

## Better-Performing Contexts

- Vienna transit-backed city stays
- Newer hotels near major stations
- Large museums and flagship attractions
- Lake and mountain viewpoints reachable by lift or car rather than steep trails

## Friction Points

- Cobblestones in old towns such as Salzburg center
- Steps in smaller pensions or heritage buildings
- Alpine paths marketed as "easy" that still include gravel, slope, or uneven footing
- Rural bus stops and platform gaps

## Planning Rule

Recommend fewer bases and more verified routes. In Austria, accessibility failures usually come from charming but impractical lodging or from underchecking the last mile.

## Ask Before Finalizing

- Wheelchair or low-mobility needs
- Lift access vs stair tolerance
- Accessible bathroom requirement
- Whether user wants scenic mountain access without hiking
