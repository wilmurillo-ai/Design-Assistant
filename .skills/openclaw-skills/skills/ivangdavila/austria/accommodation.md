# Accommodation — Austria

## Base Selection Logic

| Trip style | Best base pattern | Why |
|-----------|-------------------|-----|
| First-time city trip | Vienna or Salzburg center with rail access | Easier arrival and no car dependency |
| Alps without full ski focus | Innsbruck, Kitzbuhel area, or one valley town | Better than sleeping in a different village each night |
| Lakes summer trip | One lake base plus optional city arrival night | Cuts packing and parking friction |
| Christmas markets | Central hotels near station or tram/U-Bahn | Cold-weather movement matters more than views |

## Vienna

- Prioritize districts with easy U-Bahn or tram access over ring-road postcard views alone.
- The 1st district is convenient but expensive and crowded.
- The 6th-9th and parts of the 2nd and 7th often balance access, food, and calmer nights better.

## Salzburg

- Old town and station-adjacent zones both work; choose based on luggage tolerance and budget.
- For lake days, a station-friendly base is usually more useful than a pure romantic location.

## Tirol and Mountain Regions

- Choose lodging based on exact valley logistics, not generic "Tyrol" branding.
- Check whether lifts, ski buses, or trailheads are walkable or require a car.
- In winter, ask whether parking, ski storage, and sauna/wellness access actually matter to the user.

## Summer Lakes Regions

- Lakeside hotels book early and can be poor value for short daytime-only use.
- A slightly inland base often trades a better room and parking for only a small drive or bus ride.

## Common Cost Traps

- Paying premium old-town rates while taking day trips every day
- Booking remote alpine lodging without understanding evening dining and transport limits
- Choosing free-cancellation only in peak periods and losing the best-located stock
- Underpricing parking, breakfast, spa supplements, or local tax
