# Winter, Ski, and Christmas — Austria

## Winter Product Split

| Trip type | Better pattern |
|----------|----------------|
| Christmas markets + culture | Vienna + Salzburg, possibly Graz |
| Ski-first week | One resort or valley cluster only |
| Mixed city + snow | One city plus one alpine base, not three stops |

## Ski Planning Rules

- Ask ski level, rental needs, and whether the user wants a resort village or a real city base.
- Innsbruck-area stays can work well for mixed ski + city users.
- Dedicated ski villages win for slope-first users but can feel too quiet for non-ski partners.
- School holidays and Saturday turnover can reshape price and traffic dramatically.

## Christmas-Market Logic

- Austria does Christmas markets very well, but weekends can turn compact cities into queue-heavy experiences.
- Two market cities are usually enough for a short trip.
- Daylight is short, so indoor anchors matter more than in summer.

## Winter Transport Notes

- Rail is often the calmer choice for city-market trips.
- Driving makes more sense when carrying ski gear or staying in valley lodging with weak transfers.
- Same-day airport arrival plus mountain transfer should be treated conservatively.

## Common Mistakes

- Booking ski lodging without checking transfer day friction
- Combining too many Christmas-market cities in one week
- Ignoring inversion, fog, and short daylight in December
