# Regions

Use this file to choose the right Austria shape before suggesting cities.

| Region cluster | Best for | Main strengths | Main cautions |
|----------------|----------|----------------|---------------|
| Vienna and Lower Austria | First trip, museums, coffeehouses, Danube, wine, easy rail | Best arrival infrastructure, strongest year-round city product | Crowds, expensive central hotels, easy to overfill the schedule |
| Salzburg and Salzkammergut | Classic postcard route, music, lakes, compact old towns | Beautiful short-trip contrast of city plus scenery | Hallstatt crowd pressure, car-vs-rail decisions matter |
| Tyrol and western Alps | Ski, hiking, alpine villages, mountain sports | Strong scenery, Innsbruck hub, serious outdoors | Weather volatility, winter pricing, tunnel and pass logistics |
| Graz and Styria | Food, wine, design, slower city pace | Underrated city break, southern vineyards, less crowd stress | Weaker fit for ultra-short "must-see Austria only" trips |
| Carinthia and southern lakes | Summer swimming, active families, slower lake bases | Warm lakes, low-stress rhythm, border-friendly extensions | Strongest in summer; thinner value in shoulder and winter without a niche |
| Danube corridor and Upper Austria | Monasteries, cycling, Wachau, Linz culture stops | Rail-friendly, easy add-on from Vienna or Salzburg | Better as corridor or contrast region than first-ever standalone Austria trip |

## Fast Selection Logic

- **4-5 days:** Vienna only, Vienna plus Salzburg, or Salzburg plus lake district.
- **6-8 days:** Vienna plus Salzburg region, or one city plus one alpine or lake base.
- **9-14 days:** Add Tyrol, Styria, or Carinthia depending on season and transport style.
- **Winter:** Prioritize one ski zone or one city plus Christmas-market circuit, not both plus long alpine transfers.
- **Summer:** Choose whether the trip is city-led, lake-led, or mountain-led. Austria works better when one mode dominates.

## Transport Heuristic

- Rail-first works best for Vienna, Linz, Salzburg, Graz, and Innsbruck.
- Mixed rail plus short rental car works well for Salzkammergut, Wachau, and selected lake regions.
- Full car logic makes more sense for family lake holidays, remote alpine villages, or multi-country scenic loops.

## Good Combinations

- Vienna + Salzburg
- Vienna + Wachau + Salzburg
- Salzburg + Salzkammergut
- Innsbruck + Tyrol valleys
- Graz + South Styria
- Carinthian lakes + Ljubljana or northern Slovenia

## Weak Combinations

- Vienna + Salzburg + Hallstatt + Innsbruck as a rushed 4-5 day trip
- Vienna plus western ski resort with no weather buffer
- Summer lakes plus too many one-night city stops
