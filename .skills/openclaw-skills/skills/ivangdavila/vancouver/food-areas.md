# Best Areas for Dining in Vancouver

## Downtown & Central

### Yaletown
**Vibe**: Upscale, date night, see-and-be-seen
**Price range**: $$-$$$$
**Best for**: Seafood, cocktails, special occasions

**Top picks:**
- Blue Water Cafe (seafood)
- Minami (sushi)
- Cactus Club (upscale casual)
- Rodney's Oyster House (casual oysters)
- Homer St Cafe (brunch)

**Pro tip**: Reserve for weekends. Walk-in bar seating often available.

### Gastown
**Vibe**: Historic, cocktail-focused, trendy
**Price range**: $$-$$$
**Best for**: Drinks + dinner, atmosphere, groups

**Top picks:**
- L'Abattoir (French-Canadian)
- Bao Bei (Chinese brasserie)
- Tacofino (tacos)
- Pourhouse (cocktails)
- Alibi Room (craft beer)
- Nuba (Lebanese)

**Pro tip**: Start with drinks at Pourhouse or Diamond, then dinner. Best evening progression.

### Downtown Core
**Vibe**: Business, hotels, convenient
**Price range**: $$-$$$$
**Best for**: Business dinners, hotel guests, quick quality

**Top picks:**
- Hawksworth (fine dining)
- Miku (sushi)
- Chambar (Belgian)
- Medina (brunch)
- Jam Cafe (brunch)
- Joe Fortes (seafood)

## Main Street Corridor

**Price range**: $-$$
**Best for**: Craft beer, casual excellence, locals

**Top picks:**
- Savio Volpe (Italian)
- Anh and Chi (Vietnamese)
- Toshi Sushi (neighborhood gem)
- Burdock & Co (farm-to-table)
- Gene Cafe (coffee)

**Breweries (walkable):**
- Brassneck
- 33 Acres
- Main Street Brewing
- Faculty Brewing

### South Main (Main at King Edward to 41st)
**Vibe**: Residential, less trendy, solid
**Price range**: $-$$
**Best for**: Casual dining, value

**Top picks:**
- Sun Sui Wah (dim sum, Chinese)

- Phnom Penh (Cambodian-Vietnamese)

## Commercial Drive

### The Drive (Broadway to Venables)
**Vibe**: Bohemian, multicultural, coffee
**Price range**: $-$$
**Best for**: Coffee, Italian, Ethiopian, casual

**Top picks:**
- Via Tevere (pizza - best in city)
- Havana (Cuban)
- Kishimoto (Japanese)
- Prado (coffee)
- Caffe Calabria (coffee)
- Ethiopian restaurants (several)

**Coffee crawl route**: Start at Prado, walk to Calabria, end at JJ Bean. Compare styles.

**Pro tip**: Car Free Day (June) and Italian Day (June) are annual street festivals - plan around them or specifically for them.

## Kitsilano & West Side

### Kitsilano (4th Ave & Cornwall)
**Vibe**: Beach casual, healthy, brunch
**Price range**: $$
**Best for**: Brunch, health-conscious, dates

**Top picks:**
- AnnaLena (contemporary)
- Maenam (Thai - best in city)
- Sophie's Cosmic Cafe (brunch)
- Fable Kitchen (farm-to-table)
- Naam (vegetarian, 24hr)

**Beach day combo**: Morning at Kits Beach, lunch at Local on Kits Beach, coffee at 49th Parallel.

### South Granville (Granville & Broadway to 16th)
**Vibe**: Gallery district, upscale casual
**Price range**: $$-$$$
**Best for**: Pre/post gallery, fine dining

**Top picks:**
- Vij's (Indian - institution)
- Rangoli (Vij's casual)
- Seasons in the Park (views)
- West (contemporary)
- Thomas Haas (pastry)

**Pro tip**: Reserve Vij's line strategy: arrive at 4:30pm for 5pm seating, or 9pm for emptier.

## Richmond

### The Asian Food Capital
**Vibe**: Authentic Asian, food courts, suburban
**Price range**: $-$$
**Best for**: Dim sum, Chinese regions, Japanese, Korean

### By Area

**City Centre / Richmond Centre:**
- Dinesty (xiao long bao)
- Guu (izakaya)
- Multiple options in mall food courts

**Alexandra Road (Restaurant Row):**
- Dynasty Seafood (dim sum)
- Sea Harbour (Cantonese)
- Various regional Chinese

**No. 3 Road:**
- Crystal Mall food court
- Aberdeen Centre food court
- Parker Place Mall food court

**Steveston:**
- Pajo's (fish & chips)
- Dave's Fish & Chips
- Sockeye City Grill

### Food Court Strategy
Richmond mall food courts are legitimate dining:
1. **Crystal Mall** (Burnaby technically): Massive, diverse, local favorite
2. **Aberdeen Centre**: High quality, cleaner, wider variety
3. **Parker Place**: More local, less tourist

**How to do it**: Get a table first (busy), then take turns getting food from different stalls. Cheaper than restaurants, often comparable quality.

## Night Eating

### Late Night Options (After 11pm)

| Restaurant | Area | Closes | Known For |
|------------|------|--------|-----------|
| Naam | Kitsilano | 24 hrs | Vegetarian, post-bar |
| Congee Noodle House | Chinatown | 4am | HK-style comfort |
| Kintaro Ramen | West End | 12am | Late ramen |
| Gyoza King | West End | 2am | Japanese |
| Triple O's | Multiple | Varies | White Spot chain |
| Richmond food courts | Richmond | ~10pm | Some later |

**General rule**: Vancouver is not a late-night food city. Most kitchens close by 10pm. Plan accordingly.
