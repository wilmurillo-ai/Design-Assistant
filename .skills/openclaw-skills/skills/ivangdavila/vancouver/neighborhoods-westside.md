# Kitsilano, Point Grey & West Side Vancouver

## Kitsilano ("Kits")

### The Feel
Vancouver's beach lifestyle embodiment. Yoga studios, organic groceries, active people in Lululemon. Former hippie haven turned affluent but retained character.

### Boundaries
Burrard to Alma, 4th Ave to waterfront

### Rent Reality (Feb 2026)
| Type | Range | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| 1BR | $2,200-2,800 | Older buildings cheaper |
| 2BR | $2,800-3,600 | House shares common |
| Basement suite | $1,600-2,200 | Best value |

### Transit
- **Buses**: 4, 9, 99 (B-Line express), 2
- **No SkyTrain**: Bus to Broadway-City Hall station
- **Bike**: Excellent paths to Downtown (20 min)
- **Walk score**: 85

### Best For
- Beach and outdoor lifestyle
- Health/fitness focused people
- Families (Kits Beach, pools)
- Couples wanting space + character

### Beach Life
**Kits Beach:**
- City's most popular beach
- Volleyball courts, swimming pool (heated, outdoor)
- Boathouse restaurant
- Sunset watching central

**Jericho Beach:**
- More relaxed than Kits
- Sailing club
- Good for families
- Great bird watching

**Spanish Banks:**
- Further west, less crowded
- Longest beach at low tide
- Kite flying, picnics
- Best sunset views

### Street Life

**West 4th Avenue:**
- Main commercial strip
- Boutiques, restaurants, cafes
- Lululemon originated here
- Yoga studios every block

**Cornwall Avenue:**
- Beach-adjacent
- Restaurants with views
- Ice cream shops
- Bike rental

**West Broadway:**
- More practical services
- Less boutique, more grocery/hardware
- 99 B-Line bus route

### Restaurants
- **AnnaLena**: Contemporary Canadian, excellent
- **Sophie's Cosmic Cafe**: Brunch institution
- **Naam**: 24-hour vegetarian (Vancouver institution)
- **Fable Kitchen**: Farm-to-table
- **Maenam**: Thai (among city's best)

### Drawbacks
- No SkyTrain (bus commute to Downtown)
- Expensive for what it is
- Can feel suburban-ish
- Parking difficult near beaches in summer
- "Yoga mom" stereotype has truth to it

## Point Grey

### The Feel
Old money Vancouver. Stately homes, tree-lined streets, UBC adjacent. Quiet residential wealth.

### Boundaries
Alma to UBC, 4th to 16th (roughly)

### Rent Reality (Feb 2026)
| Type | Range | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| 1BR apartment | $2,000-2,600 | Limited supply |
| Basement suite | $1,800-2,400 | Common in houses |
| 2BR | $2,800-3,500 | Family units rare |

Most housing is owner-occupied single-family homes ($3M-10M+ to buy).

### Transit
- **Buses**: UBC routes frequent
- **No SkyTrain**: Future Broadway extension will help
- **Car**: Most residents own

### Best For
- UBC students/faculty
- Families wanting top schools
- People seeking quiet residential
- Those with UBC connection

### Education
**Schools in catchment are among Vancouver's best:**
- Lord Byng Secondary
- Point Grey Secondary
- University Hill Secondary (for UBC families)

### UBC Proximity
- Campus is 10-15 min drive
- Student renters common in basements
- Academic culture permeates
- Museum of Anthropology worth visiting

### Drawbacks
- Far from Downtown (30-45 min by bus)
- Very expensive if buying
- Limited nightlife/restaurants
- Need car for convenience
- Insular community

## Dunbar

### The Feel
Family-focused, village atmosphere. Coffee shops, good schools, parks. Upper-middle-class comfort.

### Boundaries
Roughly: Alma to Dunbar, 16th to 41st

### Rent Reality (Feb 2026)
| Type | Range | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| 1BR | $1,900-2,400 | Limited apartments |
| Basement suite | $1,600-2,200 | Main rental stock |
| 2BR | $2,500-3,200 | Family sizes rare |

### Transit
- **Buses**: Limited (41, 7, 25)
- **Walk score**: 65-75
- **Car**: Most families have one

### Best For
- Families with school-age kids
- People wanting village feel
- Those working from home
- Nature access (Pacific Spirit Park)

### Dunbar Village
Small commercial strip on Dunbar at 41st:
- Excellent bookstore
- Local cafes
- Restaurants
- Hardware, pharmacy, basics

### Parks
**Pacific Spirit Regional Park:**
- 763 hectares of forest
- Trails for walking, running, cycling
- UBC-adjacent
- One of largest urban forests in Canada

### Drawbacks
- Transit poor
- Far from Downtown
- Limited restaurant scene
- Can feel isolated

## Kerrisdale

### The Feel
Established, affluent. Wide streets, heritage homes, conservative vibe. Older demographic, well-maintained.

### Boundaries
Roughly: Arbutus to Dunbar, 41st to 49th

### Rent Reality (Feb 2026)
| Type | Range | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| 1BR | $1,800-2,300 | Some older buildings |
| 2BR | $2,400-3,000 | Limited |
| Basement suite | $1,500-2,000 | Good value |

### Transit
- **Bus routes**: 41, 43, Arbutus buses
- **Future Arbutus Greenway transit** may improve

### Best For
- Families wanting established neighborhood
- Retirees
- Those seeking quiet residential
- Access to Arbutus Village

### Village Area
41st and West Boulevard:
- Thomas Haas (best chocolate/pastries in city)
- Kerrisdale Cameras (institution)
- Local shops, restaurants
- Community centre

### Drawbacks
- Transit limited
- Skews older
- Less vibrant than other areas
- Far from Downtown

## South Granville (Fairview Slopes)

### The Feel
Gallery row and restaurant street. Good transit, urban convenience with residential calm behind main street.

### Boundaries
Roughly: Granville from Broadway to 16th

### Rent Reality (Feb 2026)
| Type | Range | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| 1BR | $2,100-2,700 | Newer buildings |
| 2BR | $2,800-3,600 | Good availability |

### Transit
- **Bus**: 10 (Granville), 99 B-Line (Broadway)
- **Canada Line**: Broadway-City Hall nearby
- **Walk score**: 90+

### Best For
- Young professionals
- Art/design lovers
- Good transit + restaurants
- Hospital workers (VGH nearby)

### South Granville Strip
**Galleries:**
- Douglas Reynolds Gallery (Indigenous art)
- Bau-Xi Gallery
- Multiple contemporary galleries

**Restaurants:**
- Vij's (Indian, reservations essential)
- West (fine dining)
- Seasons in the Park (views)

**Shopping:**
- Restoration Hardware flagship
- Design stores
- Boutique shopping

### Drawbacks
- Granville Street traffic noise
- Expensive for apartments
- Transitional feel (residential meets commercial)
