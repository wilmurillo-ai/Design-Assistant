# Emergencies - Paraguay

Emergency response quality changes by city and distance. In complex situations, stabilize fast and move toward major urban care rather than improvising.

## Emergency Numbers

| Service | Number | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| General / Police emergency | 911 | Primary emergency line |
| Firefighters | 132 | Fire and rescue response |
| Ambulance / SEME | 141 | Public emergency medical response |

## Medical Reality

| Situation | Better Move |
|-----------|-------------|
| Serious issue in Asuncion | Go toward major private or larger hospital care quickly |
| Minor issue | Pharmacy first can be efficient |
| Rural or Chaco problem | Stabilize locally, then escalate toward bigger centers if needed |

## Practical Safety Issues

- Heat exhaustion and dehydration are more common travel problems than dramatic crime scenarios.
- On border or shopping days, keep documents and backup cash separated.
- Do not drive tired after dark on longer rural routes.

## Documents and Theft

1. Contact card providers immediately if cards are stolen.
2. File the necessary report for insurance or document replacement.
3. Contact your embassy or consulate for passport problems.

## Health Notes

| Risk | Response |
|------|----------|
| Heat | Shade, hydration, slower midday pacing |
| Mosquitoes | Repellent and coverage, especially in warm months |
| Stomach issues | Hydration first, pharmacy if mild, medical care if persistent |

## Traps

- Assuming every town will have the same medical depth as Asuncion.
- Waiting too long to cool down or rehydrate in extreme heat.
- Treating a long rural drive as harmless when fatigue is already high.
