# Transport - Paraguay

Transport in Paraguay is manageable when the route respects geography and heat. Keep the plan simple and avoid stacking long road days on top of border friction.

## Core Transport Modes

| Mode | Best For | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Domestic flight | Limited time-saving on selected corridors | Check current routes before promising |
| Long-distance bus | Budget travel between main cities | Common but not always fast |
| Rental car | South routes, lake corridor, Chaco | Most flexible outside core cities |
| Ride-hailing / taxi | Urban movement | Best in Asuncion and larger cities |

## Arrival Logic

| Arrival Point | Best Use |
|---------------|----------|
| Asuncion / Silvio Pettirossi corridor | Default entry for most trips |
| East corridor / Guarani airport side | Useful if the trip is truly about the east |

## Driving Reality

- Self-drive adds major value outside the capital if the traveler is comfortable.
- Daylight driving is strongly preferable.
- Fuel and service planning matter more in the Chaco and on longer rural stretches.

## Route Advice

| Route | Practical Note |
|-------|----------------|
| Asuncion to Encarnacion | Worth doing if the traveler wants the south, not as a random add-on |
| Asuncion to Ciudad del Este | Better for tactical east-corridor plans than leisure loops |
| Asuncion to Chaco | Strong with car, weak if rushed |

## Public Transport Reality

- Buses solve movement, but they do not automatically create a pleasant trip.
- For short trips, fewer bases usually outperform "seeing more" by bus.
- Ride-hailing is often the easiest city solution when active locally.

## Traps

- Promising same-day overland transfers plus major sightseeing.
- Treating the east and south as quick side trips from Asuncion.
- Driving long rural stretches after sunset.
