# Telecoms - Paraguay

Connectivity is usually fine in the capital and other major urban zones, then weaker once the trip leans rural or Chaco-heavy.

## What to Expect

| Topic | Practical Read |
|-------|----------------|
| City coverage | Usually adequate for normal travel needs |
| Rural coverage | More variable, especially on longer road routes |
| Wi-Fi | Common in hotels and cafes, quality varies |
| eSIM or local SIM | Useful if the traveler depends on maps and messaging all day |

## Common Operators

Travelers will usually compare the main local carriers such as Tigo, Personal, and Claro. Recommend checking whichever has the strongest current package and coverage for the actual route.

## Setup Advice

- If the traveler only has a short city stay, roaming or a simple eSIM may be enough.
- If the route includes long drives, a local data plan is safer.
- Download maps before rural transfers even if signal looks fine at the hotel.

## Device Basics

| Topic | Note |
|-------|------|
| Plugs | Type C is common |
| Voltage | 220V is the normal assumption |
| Power backup | Helpful on longer road days and for border-heavy travel |

## Traps

- Assuming the Chaco will behave like a capital-city data environment.
- Depending on one phone battery for maps, tickets, and translation all day.
- Forgetting adapters or voltage compatibility on older devices.
