# Food Guide - Paraguay

Paraguay food works best when recommendations are tied to context: city lunch, roadside stop, family meal, market breakfast, or countryside grilling. Do not reduce the country to one bread or one soup.

## Core Dishes to Know

| Dish | What It Is | Best Context |
|------|------------|--------------|
| Sopa paraguaya | Dense cornbread-style dish with cheese and onion | Side dish, home-style meals |
| Chipa | Cheese bread ring or bun | Breakfast, road snack, bus snack |
| Mbeju | Cassava and cheese pancake-style staple | Breakfast or afternoon merienda |
| Vori vori | Cornmeal-and-cheese dumpling soup | Comfort food, rainy day, slower lunch |
| Asado | Grilled meat culture | Weekend meal, family setting |
| Pira caldo | Fish soup | Better near river zones or traditional menus |
| Chipa guazu | Corn casserole-style dish | Strong side dish in family-style meals |
| Lomito arabe | Local fast-food favorite | Late-night or casual stop |

## Where the Food Angle Changes

| Area | Food Strength |
|------|---------------|
| Asuncion | Best range from traditional to modern Paraguayan cooking |
| Central department towns | Casual bakeries, grill culture, snack foods |
| Encarnacion and south | Leisure dining, waterfront meals, mission-route lunches |
| Chaco | Ranch meals, grilled meats, practical hearty food |
| East border zone | Mixed menus, commerce-first eating, choose more selectively |

## How to Build Better Recommendations

- Offer one classic meal and one more contemporary meal when the traveler has 2+ nights in Asuncion.
- In summer, suggest lighter lunches and terere-compatible pacing.
- Use bakeries and chiperias to solve travel-day breakfasts.
- Save the strongest dining nights for Asuncion unless the route has a specific countryside or lodge angle.

## Food Rules

- Lunch can be the most useful meal for traditional dishes.
- Weekend grilling culture matters more than a long list of random restaurants.
- Ask whether the traveler wants traditional, polished, or street-casual food before naming places.

## Traps

- Recommending only grills to every traveler.
- Ignoring the role of breakfast snacks and merienda in the daily rhythm.
- Overpromising culinary density in pure transit zones like parts of Ciudad del Este.
