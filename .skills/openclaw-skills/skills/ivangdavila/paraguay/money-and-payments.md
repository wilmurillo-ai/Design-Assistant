# Money and Payments - Paraguay

Payment friction matters more in Paraguay than many travelers expect. Solve cash, cards, and border logic early so the itinerary runs smoothly.

## Core Rules

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| Carry local currency for everyday spending | Smaller places, tolls, and casual stops may prefer it |
| Use cards for hotels, nicer restaurants, and formal retail | Easier and safer when accepted cleanly |
| Keep small notes | Reduces change problems |
| Do not build the day around finding the "perfect" exchange | Wasted time is a hidden cost |

## Practical Reality

| Situation | Best Payment Approach |
|-----------|-----------------------|
| Asuncion restaurants and hotels | Card first, cash backup |
| Markets and smaller roadside stops | Cash |
| Border-shopping formal malls | Card or whichever quoted currency is clearly best |
| Chaco and remote road sections | More cash backup than in the capital |

## Border Caution

- In east-border zones, prices may appear in guaranies, dollars, or reais. Compare the effective rate before paying.
- Avoid casual street exchange recommendations unless the traveler already understands the risk and local context.
- If the route includes both Paraguay and neighbors, make the payment plan part of the route plan.

## ATM and Cash Strategy

- Withdraw in major urban zones when possible, not after you already need the cash.
- Do not assume every ATM outside main cities will be convenient or fully stocked.
- Keep separate emergency cash so one lost wallet does not end the trip logic.

## Tipping

- Tipping is lighter and less ritualized than in the United States.
- Small rounding up or a modest extra amount is usually enough in normal service settings.
- Do not over-tip because a guidebook implied US norms.

## Traps

- Arriving in a smaller town with only cards.
- Crossing a border for shopping without deciding which currency makes sense.
- Accepting poor card conversion choices just because checkout feels rushed.
