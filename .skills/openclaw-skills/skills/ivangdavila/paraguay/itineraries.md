# Itineraries - Paraguay

These route shapes keep Paraguay manageable. Good trips usually focus on one primary axis plus one contrast, not everything at once.

## 4 Days - Asuncion and Nearby

| Day | Plan |
|-----|------|
| 1 | Arrive Asuncion, settle in Carmelitas or Villa Morra, easy dinner |
| 2 | Historic center, museum, long lunch, Costanera sunset |
| 3 | Aregua or San Bernardino half-day, relaxed evening back in the city |
| 4 | Local shopping, cafe, depart |

Best for: first-timers, work trips with extra days, food-forward weekends.

## 6 Days - Asuncion plus Encarnacion

| Day | Plan |
|-----|------|
| 1-2 | Asuncion base |
| 3 | Travel south |
| 4 | Encarnacion waterfront and city |
| 5 | Jesuit missions day |
| 6 | Return or depart depending on transport chain |

Best for: balanced culture and leisure.

## 7-8 Days - South Route Done Properly

| Day | Plan |
|-----|------|
| 1-2 | Asuncion |
| 3-5 | Encarnacion base |
| 6 | Countryside or mission overflow day |
| 7-8 | Return buffer, optional San Bernardino stop if season fits |

Best for: travelers who do not want border stress.

## 7 Days - East Corridor

| Day | Plan |
|-----|------|
| 1 | Arrive Asuncion or east corridor |
| 2 | Transfer east |
| 3 | Itaipu |
| 4 | Monday Falls and shopping |
| 5 | Border-flex day |
| 6 | Buffer or Iguazu-side add-on |
| 7 | Depart |

Best for: travelers explicitly motivated by shopping or tri-border logic.

## 9-10 Days - Asuncion plus Chaco

| Day | Plan |
|-----|------|
| 1-2 | Asuncion |
| 3 | Move into the Chaco |
| 4-7 | Colony and lodge days |
| 8 | Return buffer |
| 9-10 | Flexible city close or onward travel |

Best for: road-trippers and wildlife-focused travelers.

## What Not to Do

- 5-6 days with Asuncion, Encarnacion, Ciudad del Este, and the Chaco.
- Same-day city arrival, long overland transfer, and border crossing.
- Building a trip around only transit hubs with no slow days.
