# Intake Workflow - Dermatologist

Use this file when opening a new lesion, rash episode, or flare case.

## Minimum Questions

- What body site is involved, and is it one spot or a broader distribution?
- When was it first noticed, and what changed most recently?
- Is it itchy, painful, burning, bleeding, crusting, draining, or otherwise asymptomatic?
- Has it grown, spread, darkened, changed shape, or become more inflamed?
- What has already been tried: moisturizer, steroid, acne treatment, antifungal, antibiotic, cleanser, bandage, or nothing?
- Any recent exposures that might matter: new product, medication, shaving, friction, illness, sunburn, sweating, bites, or travel?
- Any clinician-confirmed history already known: eczema, psoriasis, rosacea, acne, skin cancer, immunosuppression, severe allergies?

## Pigmented Lesion Add-On

Use when the concern is a mole or dark spot:
- Is it new or long-standing?
- Any ABCDE change: asymmetry, border irregularity, color variation, diameter growth, or evolving behavior?
- Any bleeding, ulceration, persistent crust, or "looks unlike the others" concern?

## Rash or Flare Add-On

Use when the concern is distributed or recurrent:
- Symmetric or one-sided?
- Started after illness, medication, outdoor exposure, contact exposure, or product change?
- Face, eyelids, hands, groin, feet, scalp, nails, or mucosa involved?
- Any fever, swelling, eye irritation, or general unwell feeling?

## Output Standard

Reduce the intake to:
1. body site and pattern
2. onset and trend
3. symptom burden
4. recent exposures and treatments
5. escalation risk

## Do Not

- Ask a long questionnaire when urgent red flags are active.
- Label the condition from one photo or one symptom line.
- Merge clearly different lesions or rashes into the same case.
