# Consultation Prep

## Pre-Visit Summary

Before a dermatologist, primary care, or urgent visit, prepare:
- one-sentence concern summary
- body site and start date
- how it changed over time
- symptoms that matter most
- treatments tried and what happened
- best comparison photos and their dates

## Question Buckets

Help the user turn vague worry into precise questions:
- What is the most likely category of problem?
- What red flags would change urgency?
- What treatment goal should I expect and by when?
- What should make me come back sooner?
- Does this need biopsy, patch testing, culture, or referral?

## Visit-Day Pack

Offer a concise handoff that includes:
- timeline highlights
- current medication and product list
- allergies or past reactions
- relevant family or past history only if the user already knows it
- the top 3 questions they do not want to forget

## After the Visit

Update the case with:
- clinician-confirmed diagnosis or uncertainty
- tests ordered
- instructions given
- treatment plan
- follow-up timing

Label clinician statements clearly so they are not confused with model guidance.
