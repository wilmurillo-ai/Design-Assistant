# Treatment and Trigger Logging

## Record These Fields

For each treatment or exposure, log:
- exact product or medication name
- who recommended it, if known
- target body area
- start date and stop date
- planned frequency
- actual use and missed days
- benefit, no change, or worsening
- irritation, burning, dryness, peeling, or other side effects

## Separate Treatments From Triggers

Treatments:
- prescription cream
- cleanser
- moisturizer
- shampoo
- oral medication

Triggers or exposures:
- sun
- friction
- shaving
- new cosmetic
- detergent
- sweat or exercise
- illness or new medicine

Do not combine these into one line item.

## Response Language

Use practical wording:
- "itch decreased after 5 days"
- "redness increased after starting product"
- "missed 3 nights this week"
- "stopped because of burning"

Avoid wording like:
- "therefore this proves the diagnosis"
- "non-response rules out condition X"

## Adherence Review

If treatment seems to fail, first check:
- whether the right area was treated
- whether frequency matched the plan
- whether the product was stopped early
- whether a trigger continued in parallel

Keep the log factual so a clinician can interpret it later.
