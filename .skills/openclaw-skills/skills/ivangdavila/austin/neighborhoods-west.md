# West Austin & Hill Country Neighborhoods

## Westlake

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Wealthy enclave, top schools, exclusive
- **Rent 1BR**: Rare, $2,500-4,000+ if available
- **Homes**: $1,000,000-5,000,000+
- **Walk Score**: 15-25

### Who Lives Here
- Tech executives and founders
- Doctors, lawyers, finance professionals
- Old Austin money families
- Celebrities (Matthew McConaughey vibes)

### Pros
- Best public schools in Texas (Eanes ISD)
- Beautiful Hill Country setting
- Very safe
- Large lots, privacy
- Access to Lake Austin
- Prestige address

### Cons
- Extremely expensive
- Not walkable at all
- Car required
- Can feel isolated/bubble
- Far from urban Austin
- Homogeneous

### Schools
- Eanes ISD consistently ranked top in Texas
- Westlake High School is destination school
- Property values tied directly to school district

### Key Spots
- Lake Austin
- 360 overlook
- Barton Creek Square Mall
- Private clubs and golf courses

### Best For
Wealthy families who prioritize schools above all else. Executives who want privacy and prestige.

## Tarrytown

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Old money, established, close-in west
- **Rent 1BR**: Very rare, $2,000-3,500
- **Homes**: $800,000-3,000,000+
- **Walk Score**: 50-60

### Who Lives Here
- Old Austin families (multi-generational)
- UT professors and administrators
- Professionals who want close-in west side
- Empty nesters in established homes

### Pros
- Close to Downtown (10-15 min)
- Established trees and character
- Lake Austin access
- Tarrytown shops (small local strip)
- Historic homes
- Community feel

### Cons
- Very expensive
- Limited inventory
- Older homes need maintenance
- Not much rental available
- Can feel insular

### Key Spots
- Deep Eddy Pool
- Mozart's Coffee (Lake Austin view)
- Tarrytown shops
- Hula Hut

### Best For
Established professionals who want old Austin character close to Downtown. People with Lake Austin lifestyle.

---

## Bee Cave

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Hill Country suburban, upscale
- **Rent 1BR**: $1,600-2,400/month
- **Homes**: $500,000-1,500,000
- **Walk Score**: 25-40

### Who Lives Here
- Families seeking good schools
- People who want Hill Country feel
- Tech professionals commuting to Austin
- Retirees

### Pros
- Hill Country setting
- Good schools (Lake Travis ISD)
- Hill Country Galleria shopping
- Near wineries and breweries
- New construction available
- Safer, quieter

### Cons
- 25-35 min to Downtown
- Car essential
- Can feel isolated
- Expensive for distance
- Limited nightlife

### Key Spots
- Hill Country Galleria
- Hamilton Pool (nearby)
- Lake Travis
- Various wineries

### Best For
Families who want Hill Country lifestyle with good schools. People who don't need urban Austin access.

- **Walk Score**: 20-30

- Lake lifestyle families
- Work-from-home professionals
- Golf enthusiasts

- Beautiful scenery
- Golf courses
- Resort amenities

- Limited young person scene

## Dripping Springs

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Small town Hill Country, wineries, rural
- **Rent 1BR**: $1,200-1,800/month
- **Homes**: $400,000-1,200,000
- **Walk Score**: 15-25

### Who Lives Here
- Families seeking small town
- Remote workers
- People fleeing Austin density
- Wine and brewery enthusiasts
- Ranch owners

### Pros
- Small town character
- Winery and brewery corridor (290)
- Hill Country beauty
- Growing restaurant scene
- Space and land available
- Lower density

### Cons
- 35-45 min to Austin
- Very car dependent
- Limited amenities
- Growing fast (changing character)
- Schools mixed

### Key Spots
- Jester King Brewery
- Salt Lick BBQ
- Multiple wineries
- Dripping Springs downtown

### Best For
Remote workers who want Hill Country lifestyle. Wine and brewery enthusiasts.

- **Homes**: $300,000-1,000,000
- **Walk Score**: 10-20

- Lake enthusiasts
- People seeking rural Texas

- Very affordable (relative)
- Beautiful scenery
- True rural Texas feel

People who want true Hill Country rural life. Retirees. Remote workers with reliable internet.

## Hill Country Notes

### Wine Country (290 Corridor)
The stretch of Highway 290 between Dripping Springs and Fredericksburg has become Texas wine country:
- 50+ wineries
- Best visited as day trips from Austin
- Live in Dripping Springs for closest access
- Weekends are busy; weekday visits recommended

### Water Considerations
- Lake Travis levels fluctuate dramatically
- Drought years can eliminate lake access
- Check lake levels before buying lakefront
- Highland Lakes system serves Austin water supply

### Wildfire Risk
- Western areas have increasing wildfire risk
- Cedar and brush are fire-prone
- Some neighborhoods require fire-resistant construction
- Insurance can be expensive
