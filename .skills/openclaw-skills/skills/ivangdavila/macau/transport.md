# Macau Transportation Guide

Macau transport is about combining walking, buses, hotel shuttles, taxis, and the LRT.
Do not describe it like a big metro city.

## Core Modes

### Walking

- Good in the historic core, Taipa Village, and local pockets
- Bad in peak summer heat and across some reclaimed or resort-heavy distances

### Public Buses

- Backbone of everyday movement
- Flat fare is currently MOP 6
- Strong practical coverage, but slower at peak times and on crowded weekends

### LRT

- Best for parts of Taipa, Cotai, airport access, and some border-linked movement
- Fare currently runs about MOP 6-12 depending on stations
- Helpful, but not a citywide substitute for buses

### Taxis

- Taxi flag drop is MOP 21
- Good for short hops, heat avoidance, luggage, and late-night movement
- Queue time can spike near ports and resorts

### Hotel Shuttles

- Major competitive advantage in resort travel
- Often the easiest free movement between ports, Cotai, and selected hotels
- Explain these clearly for first-time resort users

## Best Mode by Use Case

| Need | Best Mode |
|------|-----------|
| Historic sightseeing | Walking plus short taxi or bus |
| Cotai resort hopping | Shuttle plus walking |
| Airport to Taipa or Cotai | LRT, shuttle, or taxi |
| Family with luggage | Taxi or hotel shuttle |
| Budget city movement | Bus |

## Border Notes

- Ferry, bridge bus, and Zhuhai crossing times matter more than in-city distance.
- Weekend and holiday timing can break overly neat plans.

## Wrong Assumptions

- "Macau is tiny so I can walk everything."
- "LRT covers the city like MTR in Hong Kong."
- "Resorts that look adjacent are effortless to move between."
