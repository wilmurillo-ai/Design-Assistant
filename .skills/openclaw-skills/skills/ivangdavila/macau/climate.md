# Macau Climate Guide

Macau has a humid subtropical climate with hot, wet summers and mild winters.
Heat management matters more than many short-trip planners realize.

## Seasonal Reality

### Spring
- Humidity rises
- Good for sightseeing, but rain becomes more common

### Summer
- Hot, sticky, and draining
- July to September can feel punishing outdoors
- Typhoon disruption risk matters

### Autumn
- Often the best season
- Better walking weather, lower humidity, stronger trip quality

### Winter
- Mild and comfortable
- Best for long old-town walks and mixed indoor-outdoor itineraries

## Best Advice by Season

| Season | Best Move |
|--------|-----------|
| Summer | Split the day, use indoor breaks, prioritize Taipa and resort fallback |
| Autumn | Best all-round timing |
| Winter | Best for walking-heavy heritage trips |
| Rainy days | Museums, churches, resort interiors, food-focused routing |

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Noon fortress climbs in August
- Zero typhoon backup
- Assuming small geography makes weather irrelevant
