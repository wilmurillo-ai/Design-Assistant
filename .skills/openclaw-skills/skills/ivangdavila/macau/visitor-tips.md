# Macau Visitor Guide - Practical Tips

## Border Logic First

- Ask where the user enters from.
- Hong Kong ferry and HZMB bus plans behave differently on weekends and holidays.
- Zhuhai crossings can be efficient, but mainland visa context matters.

## Money

- MOP is official.
- HKD is widely accepted in tourist-facing businesses.
- Expect many places to return change in MOP.
- 1:1 acceptance for HKD is convenient, not favorable.

## Best First Actions on Arrival

1. Confirm port exit and transport options.
2. Get enough local cash for taxis, snacks, and small vendors.
3. Decide whether the first stop is historic core, Taipa, or hotel check-in.

## What Visitors Usually Underestimate

- Heat and humidity
- Port queues
- Walking distance inside huge resorts
- Restaurant split hours
- How fast a half-day disappears

## Good Defaults

- Comfortable walking shoes
- Umbrella or light rain layer
- Power bank
- Small cash stash
- Screenshot of hotel shuttle details if staying in Cotai

## Family Notes

- Resorts are stroller-friendly, old-town lanes less so.
- Coloane works better than Cotai if the family needs space and a calmer rhythm.
- Heat management matters more than ambitious routing.

## Casino Reality

- Casual curiosity is fine, but do not build the whole trip around gaming unless the user explicitly asks.
- Many people enjoy Macau more through food, old streets, and one resort night than through multi-casino hopping.

## Day Trips Beyond Macau

- Hong Kong is the obvious add-on.
- Zhuhai is practical for cost and mainland access, not for first-time romance.
- Keep expectations realistic: border friction can wipe out over-packed plans.
