# Macau Visitor Guide - Attractions

Macau rewards a split-screen approach: heritage core plus resort spectacle.
Do not spend the entire trip indoors unless the user explicitly wants gaming and malls.

## Prioritize These

| Attraction | Why It Matters | Time | Best Window |
|-----------|----------------|------|-------------|
| Senado Square and St. Paul's area | Macau's visual identity and UNESCO core | 2-3h | Early morning or evening |
| A-Ma Temple and Barra | Deepest historical layer, calmer than the center | 1-2h | Morning |
| Taipa Village | Best mix of food, lanes, and human scale | 2-4h | Late afternoon to dinner |
| Coloane Village | Slower, greener, and good for reset time | 2-3h | Morning or sunset |
| Cotai resort interiors | Big-scale spectacle, shows, malls, casino design | 2-4h | Afternoon or evening |
| Macau Tower area | Skyline views, walking, and optional adrenaline | 1-2h | Sunset |

## Good but Context-Dependent

| Place | Go If | Skip If |
|------|-------|----------|
| Grand Lisboa / Wynn / Venetian circuits | User wants the casino-resort side of Macau | They want local streets and heritage only |
| Museums around Monte Fort | They like layered colonial and trading history | They only have half a day |
| Fisherman's Wharf zone | They want waterfront walking and photos | They expect major local character |
| Giant panda pavilion in Coloane | Family trip with children | Short adult-only trip |

## Common Mistakes

- Going to St. Paul's at midday in peak heat and hating the crowds.
- Thinking Cotai replaces historic Macau.
- Ignoring Barra and A-Ma, which explain the city's name and older identity better than any casino.
- Treating the whole city as flat and fully walkable in summer.

## Best Split by Trip Style

### Heritage-first
- Senado Square
- Ruins of St. Paul's
- Monte Fort
- St. Dominic's
- A-Ma Temple
- Mandarin's House

### Resort-first
- Cotai strip walk
- Venetian and Londoner interiors
- Wynn or MGM art and fountain elements
- One show or nightlife anchor

### Balanced First Timer
- Half day historic peninsula
- One Taipa Village meal block
- One Cotai night
- One quieter stop in Coloane or Barra

## Honest Take

What makes Macau good is contrast, not checklist length.
If the user only has half a day, tell them to choose between heritage Macau and resort Macau instead of pretending they can deeply do both.
