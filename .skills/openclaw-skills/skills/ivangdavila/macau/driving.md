# Driving in Macau

Macau is not a must-drive city for most visitors.
Buses, taxis, hotel shuttles, and selective walking usually beat the stress of parking and dense circulation.

## When Driving Makes Sense

- Family with specific hotel and luggage pattern
- Repeated local routines as a resident
- Business use that requires flexible movement beyond resort corridors

## When It Does Not

- Short visitor stays
- Cotai-heavy resort weekends
- First-time sightseeing days in the historic core

## Core Frictions

- Parking
- Dense local streets
- Port and holiday traffic pressure
- Low value versus taxis for short stays

## Best Rule

Recommend not driving unless the user already knows why they need it.
For most short trips, transport complexity drops when the car disappears.
