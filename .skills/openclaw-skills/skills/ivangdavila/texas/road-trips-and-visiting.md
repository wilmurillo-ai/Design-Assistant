# Road Trips and Visiting — Texas

Use this file for tourism, city-hopping, scenic routes, and family travel inside Texas.

## Visitor Rule

Texas travel works best when you choose fewer bases and respect drive-time reality.

## Good Base Patterns

| Goal | Better base |
|------|-------------|
| Food, music, walkable pockets | Austin or San Antonio depending on pace |
| Big-city dining and museums | Houston |
| Big airport access and broad suburban options | Dallas-Fort Worth |
| History and family pace | San Antonio |
| Coast and beach | Corpus Christi or Galveston depending the trip |
| Nature and distance reset | Big Bend or Hill Country with buffers |

## Route Design Rules

- Price the trip in daylight, weather, fuel stops, and fatigue, not just miles.
- If children are involved, design around shade, bathrooms, and two-to-three-hour reset windows.
- If the trip touches Gulf Coast or border zones, include weather and documentation logic early.

## Tourism Reality Checks

- Texas is not a one-weekend state if the plan spans multiple metros plus nature.
- Summer heat can erase outdoor plans between late morning and evening.
- Event weekends can distort hotel value and traffic more than first-time visitors expect.
- The best Texas trip is usually narrower and deeper than the first draft.

## Common Mistakes

- Trying to do Austin, Houston, Dallas, San Antonio, and Big Bend in one short trip.
- Ignoring tolls, parking, and airport spread inside major metros.
- Planning outdoor-heavy days without heat or storm buffers.
- Treating every city as interchangeable instead of choosing by vibe and logistics.
