# Housing and Property — Texas

Use this file for renting, buying, property tax pressure, flood exposure, HOAs, and homeowner basics.

## Rent vs Buy Questions That Matter

Ask these before judging a market:
- How long is the user staying?
- Does school district quality matter?
- Can they handle hail, flood, or wind deductible risk?
- Do they need walkability, yard space, or low-maintenance living?
- Is the real budget based on monthly payment or total carrying cost?

## Texas Housing Reality

- Housing varies more by corridor and suburb than by city brand alone.
- Insurance and weather exposure matter more than many out-of-state movers expect.
- Flood logic matters in Houston and Gulf Coast, but flash flood and drainage issues show up elsewhere too.
- HOA rules can materially change daily life, parking, rentals, exterior work, and short-term flexibility.

## Buying Rules

### 1. Property tax is part of the purchase price
- Do not compare mortgage principal and interest without tax, insurance, and maintenance.
- Appraisal district behavior and local rates change the real monthly burden.

### 2. Homestead is a workflow, not trivia
- Owner-occupants should review homestead eligibility and filing timing soon after closing.
- Exemption rules and local overlays change, so point users to the appraisal district and Texas property tax guidance.

### 3. Weather insurance is part of the house
- Ask about flood zone, prior claims, wind and hail deductibles, roof age, foundation risk, and backup power comfort.

## Renting Rules

- Lease terms, deposit handling, move-out fees, renter insurance requirements, and HOA overlays deserve a checklist.
- Texas is generally not the place to assume renter protections match the strongest blue-state markets.
- If the property is in a long commute corridor, calculate time and toll cost before recommending it.

## Common Mistakes

- Choosing a home by list price or rent alone.
- Skipping flood, drainage, roof, and deductible questions.
- Ignoring school district until after narrowing the map.
- Assuming every suburb in the same metro has the same taxes, commute shape, or insurance stress.
