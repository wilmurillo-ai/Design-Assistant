# Weather and Emergencies — Texas

Use this file when weather, power risk, road safety, or home-readiness changes the answer.

## Hazard Map by Region

| Region | Main risks |
|--------|------------|
| Gulf Coast | Hurricanes, flood, prolonged outages, heat |
| Houston area | Flood, storm lines, humidity, power disruption |
| North Texas | Hail, tornado watches, winter swings, ice events |
| Central Texas | Heat, flash flood, severe storms, cedar and smoke periods |
| Hill Country | Flash flood, wildfire exposure, water stress |
| West Texas | Long-distance heat, wind, wildfire smoke, sparse services |
| South Texas and border | Heat, hurricane spillover, long driving exposure |

## Rules for Good Advice

### 1. Texas weather is operational, not decorative
- It changes route design, move dates, insurance needs, home choice, and backup planning.

### 2. Lead with the nearest real risk
- For Houston housing, think flood first.
- For North Texas roofs and cars, think hail first.
- For Central Texas summer routines, think heat and power load first.

### 3. Always give a fallback
- Alternative route
- Earlier departure window
- Indoor option
- Backup power or cooling plan
- Reschedule threshold

## Readiness Checklist

- Water, flashlights, chargers, and medication buffer
- Cooling plan during long outages
- Flood-safe parking and evacuation logic if relevant
- Documents and insurance photos stored safely
- Fuel margin before a storm line or long rural drive

## Common Mistakes

- Recommending an outdoor-heavy plan without season fit.
- Talking about Texas weather like it is the same statewide.
- Ignoring outages and road closures in both home and travel advice.
- Treating winter freeze risk as impossible just because the state is warm overall.
