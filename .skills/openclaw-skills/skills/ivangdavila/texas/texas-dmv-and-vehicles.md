# Texas DMV and Vehicles

Use this file for driver license, state ID, title, registration, insurance, tolls, and vehicle-readiness questions.

## Core Workflow for New Residents

### Driver license and ID
- New Texas residents are expected to get a Texas driver license within 90 days of moving.
- Use Texas DPS for license and ID workflows, not TxDMV.
- Prepare identity, lawful presence if applicable, Social Security evidence, and Texas residency documents before chasing an appointment.

### Vehicle title and registration
- Vehicle registration and title work runs through TxDMV and the county tax office, not DPS.
- New residents generally need Texas auto insurance first, then the title and registration workflow.
- Registration timing can change by scenario, but 30-day planning is the safe default for newcomers.

## Inspection Reality After 2025

- Most noncommercial vehicles no longer do the old annual safety inspection statewide.
- Some counties still require emissions testing before registration renewal.
- Commercial vehicles still follow inspection rules.
- Always verify the county-specific rule before telling the user to book an inspection.

## Insurance Baseline

Texas minimum auto liability is commonly summarized as 30/60/25:
- $30,000 bodily injury per person
- $60,000 bodily injury per crash
- $25,000 property damage

Do not recommend minimum-only coverage without discussing:
- hail exposure
- uninsured drivers
- deductible comfort
- whether the user depends on the car for work or school logistics

## Toll and Drive-Time Reality

- DFW, Austin, Houston, and parts of Central Texas use toll roads more than many newcomers expect.
- Ask whether the user's daily routine crosses toll corridors before calling a suburb affordable.
- Toll tags reduce friction, but they do not fix a bad commute shape.

## Good Answer Pattern

1. Identify whether the task belongs to DPS, TxDMV, county tax office, or insurer.
2. Ask city, county, and current origin state or country.
3. Give the sequence, documents, and verification portal.
4. Flag county emissions and toll realities if they apply.

## Common Mistakes

- Sending a user to the wrong agency.
- Giving old inspection advice without checking the 2025 change.
- Treating insurance minimums as adequate guidance.
- Ignoring toll roads when comparing suburbs or jobs.
