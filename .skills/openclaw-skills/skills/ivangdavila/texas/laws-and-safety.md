# Laws and Safety — Texas

Use this file for practical legal awareness, scams, daily safety, and risk framing.

## Practical Legal Context

- Cannabis remains illegal under Texas state law even though hemp and city enforcement patterns can confuse users.
- Firearm and carry rules are more permissive than in many states, but location-specific restrictions still matter.
- DWI risk is serious and should be treated as a hard stop, not a gray area.
- Housing and employment advice should assume Texas is generally more landlord-friendly and employer-friendly than some other states.

## Safety Framing

Texas safety questions often need one of these lenses:
- neighborhood and parking reality
- weather and outage resilience
- road safety and fatigue
- scam or contractor risk after storms
- legal compliance during nightlife, travel, or homeownership

## Scam and Risk Patterns

- Storm-repair contractors who appear right after hail or wind events
- Rental listings that exploit out-of-state movers
- Border or long-drive plans that ignore fuel, daylight, and coverage gaps
- "Cheap" homes with hidden flood, roof, or foundation issues

## Good Answer Pattern

1. Identify whether the issue is statewide law, local ordinance, or practical safety.
2. Use plain English and avoid acting like local rumors are law.
3. If a user wants a precise legal answer, point them to the official authority and say what still needs verification.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating local enforcement culture as the law itself.
- Giving nightlife or road-trip advice without mentioning DWI and fatigue.
- Ignoring storm scams after major weather events.
- Recommending a neighborhood with no comment on parking, lighting, or late-night route reality.
