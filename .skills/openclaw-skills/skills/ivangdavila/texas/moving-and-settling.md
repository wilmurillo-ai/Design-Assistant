# Moving and Settling — Texas

Use this file for relocations from another state or from outside the U.S.

## First 90 Days

### Days 1-7
- Confirm lease or closing documents, utility handoff, internet, and renter or home insurance.
- Map the essentials: grocery, pharmacy, urgent care, commute path, nearest flood-prone roads, and toll corridors.
- If schools matter, confirm the exact district and campus assignment before assuming the address works.

### Days 8-30
- Build the driver-license and vehicle plan using `texas-dmv-and-vehicles.md`.
- Update mailing address, employer records, bank, insurance, and voter registration in the right order.
- Create a local weather kit before the next heat wave, storm line, or freeze.

### Days 31-60
- Pressure-test the commute at the real hour, not midday.
- Re-evaluate housing assumptions: utility cost, toll burden, school fit, flood comfort, and neighborhood rhythm.
- If buying, start homestead and appraisal planning early rather than waiting for the tax bill.

### Days 61-90
- Lock in recurring care: primary doctor, pediatrician, dentist, and urgent care fallback.
- Decide whether the metro is still the right fit or whether the problem is really suburb or corridor selection.
- Capture unresolved loops in memory so future Texas advice starts from reality.

## Documents to Keep Ready

- ID and proof of lawful presence if applicable
- Lease or deed
- Proof of insurance
- Vehicle title or registration
- Two proofs of Texas residency when required
- School, vaccine, and care records if moving with children

## Relocation Rules

### 1. Texas setup is sequence-sensitive
- The same document often unlocks the next one.
- Do not treat license, registration, insurance, utilities, and school paperwork as separate tracks.

### 2. Ask "city, county, district" early
- Texas answers often go wrong because the user says only "Houston" or "Dallas."
- County offices, school districts, appraisal systems, and utility territories can change fast across nearby ZIPs.

### 3. Check the hidden recurring costs
- Summer power bills, toll roads, hail deductibles, HOA fees, and commute fuel can erase headline savings.

## Common Mistakes

- Moving without testing the real commute.
- Signing a lease before checking flood history, school district, and internet options.
- Assuming a Texas metro works without a car when the weekly routine says otherwise.
- Waiting too long to organize license, vehicle, and insurance workflows.
