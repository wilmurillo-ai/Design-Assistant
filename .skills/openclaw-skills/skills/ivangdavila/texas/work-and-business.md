Use this file for job-market positioning, LLC setup, sales tax, franchise-tax awareness, hiring, and local business tradeoffs.

## Business Setup Rules

### 1. State formation is only the first layer
- Entity formation, registered agent, EIN, sales-tax permit, and franchise-tax reporting do not replace city or county compliance.

### 2. Ask what the business actually does
- Taxability, permits, insurance, and local approvals depend on product, service, and physical footprint.

### 3. Location changes the business model
- Texas may look founder-friendly at the state level while still creating expensive local friction through leases, permits, insurance, or distance.

## Job-Market Guidance

- Match the user to the metro that fits the role, not the one with the loudest brand.
- For hybrid or in-office roles, commute geometry matters more than city prestige.
- For small-business users, ask whether customers are local, statewide, or cross-border.

## Common Mistakes

- Saying Texas is business-friendly without mapping local compliance.
- Treating "no state income tax" as a full founder strategy.
- Giving hiring guidance without payroll, insurance, and local-office realities.
- Recommending a metro without considering where the actual customers or employers are.
