# Regions and Metro Tradeoffs — Texas

Use this file when the user is choosing where in Texas to live, base a trip, or expand a business.

## Quick Fit Map

| Region | Best for | Watchouts |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Austin metro | Tech, startups, walkable pockets, music, hybrid work | Expensive core, traffic, summer power costs, slower public transit |
| Houston metro | Energy, medicine, global business, diverse food, larger housing stock | Humidity, flooding logic, sprawl, storm disruption |
| Dallas-Fort Worth | Corporate jobs, airports, suburban family logistics, broad job market | Long commutes, toll dependence, weather swings, less central identity |
| San Antonio | Lower pressure than Austin, military and healthcare, family pace, culture | Lower salaries in some fields, hotter summers, car dependence |
| Hill Country | Lifestyle, wineries, second homes, scenic drives, slower pace | Septic and water issues, wildfire exposure, distance to services |
| Gulf Coast | Port work, beach access, petrochemical and logistics exposure | Hurricanes, flood insurance logic, humidity, seasonal risk |
| West Texas and Big Bend | Space, dark skies, energy corridor, destination trips | Distance, limited services, sparse healthcare, long driving windows |
| Rio Grande Valley and border | Cross-border family and trade patterns, lower cost pockets | Heat, hurricane exposure, border-specific logistics, longer statewide travel |
| East Texas | Forestry, lower-density living, smaller-town pace | Fewer job hubs, storm cleanup, narrower service options |
| Panhandle and High Plains | Agriculture, wind, aviation, lower-cost pockets | Wind, winter exposure, geographic isolation |

## Metro Choice Rules

### 1. Match by daily pattern, not brand
- If work, schools, or care needs are local, optimize for the weekly routine before nightlife or reputation.
- Texas punishes glamorous choices that create long daily drives.

### 2. Ask what must be close
- Airport, downtown office, hospital network, school district, aging parents, and major highways all change the best answer.
- One person's "great suburb" is another person's two-hour daily mistake.

### 3. Respect climate identity
- Houston and Gulf Coast: flood and hurricane thinking
- North Texas: hail, tornado, and winter swing thinking
- Austin and Hill Country: heat, water, cedar, and wildfire thinking
- West Texas: distance, wind, sun exposure, and service gaps

## Good Comparison Prompts

- "I work near the Energy Corridor but want lower flood stress."
- "I need the best family suburb near DFW without a brutal toll commute."
- "I want Austin access without paying central Austin rents."
- "I want a Texas road trip base that balances cities and nature."

## Common Mistakes

- Comparing Austin, Houston, and DFW as if they solve the same life problem.
- Choosing by city name instead of corridor, suburb, and commute shape.
- Ignoring county, school district, and flood zone until after narrowing housing.
- Assuming Hill Country or coastal living is simple just because it looks scenic.
