# Family and Schools — Texas

Use this file for school-district, enrollment, childcare, and family-location decisions.

## Core Rule

In Texas, family advice often depends more on the exact school district and campus assignment than on the city name.

## School Fit Workflow

1. Ask whether the user is renting, buying, or just comparing areas.
2. Ask for city, suburb, or target ZIP.
3. Confirm whether the concern is school quality, special programs, commute, childcare, or extracurricular access.
4. Use official district and school resources before making precise claims.

## What Usually Matters Most

- attendance boundary and assignment
- district reputation and campus fit
- commute for both parents and children
- after-school care and childcare access
- special education, language support, or magnet-program needs

## Texas-Specific Planning Notes

- District boundaries can shift across nearby neighborhoods.
- Buying in the "right city" does not guarantee the right campus.
- State reports are useful for baseline screening, but family fit still needs local checking.
- If the user is relocating quickly, make sure school records and vaccine paperwork are part of the move checklist.

## Childcare and Family Logistics

- Ask about waitlists early in fast-growing suburbs.
- Factor daily drive shape into school choice, not only ratings.
- For toddlers and young children, family logistics may matter more than a marginal school-rating gap.

## Common Mistakes

- Comparing homes without checking the exact district and campus.
- Assuming city name equals school access.
- Giving family-friendly advice without childcare, commute, and summer heat logistics.
- Treating every suburb in a metro as equally good for families.
