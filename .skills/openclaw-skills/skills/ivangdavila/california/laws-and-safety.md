# Laws and Safety — California

Use this file for practical legal awareness, scams, daily safety, and risk framing.

## Practical Legal Context

- California is not just "more regulated." The useful question is which state rule matters and what local enforcement or logistics change the real outcome.
- Housing, labor, and consumer protection expectations are often different from lower-regulation states, but local enforcement still matters.
- Vehicle, parking, transit, and local quality-of-life rules often matter more in daily life than abstract legal summaries.

## Safety Framing

California safety questions usually need one of these lenses:
- neighborhood and parking reality
- wildfire, smoke, or earthquake readiness
- road safety and long-commute fatigue
- rental or moving scam risk
- travel safety in dense urban cores or isolated road segments

## Scam and Risk Patterns

- Rental listings targeting out-of-state movers
- Fake "too cheap" units in expensive markets
- Contractor scams after fires, floods, or storms
- Parking-theft and car break-in exposure in dense tourist or transit-heavy zones

## Good Answer Pattern

1. Identify whether the issue is statewide law, local ordinance, or practical safety.
2. Use plain English and avoid acting like local rumor is law.
3. If the user wants a precise legal answer, point them to the official authority and say what still needs verification.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating California as one legal or safety environment.
- Recommending a neighborhood without talking about parking, transit, and late-night route reality.
- Ignoring contractor and rental scams after hazard events.
- Giving lifestyle advice that ignores actual street-level quality-of-life differences.
