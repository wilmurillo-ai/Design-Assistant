# Moving and Settling — California

Use this file for relocations from another state or from outside the U.S.

## First 90 Days

### Days 1-7
- Confirm lease or closing documents, utility handoff, internet, renter or homeowner insurance, and parking reality.
- Map the essentials: grocery, pharmacy, urgent care, commute path, transit station, and the nearest wildfire, flood, or earthquake-prep concerns that actually matter.
- If schools matter, confirm the exact district and campus logic before assuming the address works.

### Days 8-30
- Build the license and vehicle plan using `california-dmv-and-vehicles.md`.
- Update mailing address, employer records, bank, insurance, and voter registration in the right order.
- Create a local readiness kit before the next heat wave, smoke event, storm, or quake reminder.

### Days 31-60
- Pressure-test the commute at the real hour, not a midday sample.
- Re-evaluate housing assumptions: utility cost, parking, insurance pressure, school fit, and whether the region still matches the user's actual routine.
- If buying, start tax, insurance, and risk screening early rather than after inspection emotions take over.

### Days 61-90
- Lock in recurring care: primary doctor, pediatrician, dentist, and urgent care fallback.
- Decide whether the metro is still the right fit or whether the real problem is neighborhood, suburb, or commute corridor choice.
- Capture unresolved loops in memory so future California advice starts from reality.

## Documents to Keep Ready

- ID and proof of lawful presence if applicable
- Lease or deed
- Proof of insurance
- Vehicle title or registration
- California residency documents when required
- School, vaccine, and care records if moving with children

## Relocation Rules

### 1. California setup is sequence-sensitive
- The same document often unlocks the next one.
- Do not treat license, registration, insurance, utilities, school paperwork, and commute design as separate tracks.

### 2. Ask "city, county, ZIP" early
- California answers often go wrong because the user says only "LA" or "Bay Area."
- Utility providers, school districts, insurance exposure, and commute math can change fast across nearby ZIPs.

### 3. Check the hidden recurring costs
- Parking, toll bridges, state tax, insurance, utility rates, and commute time can erase headline salary gains.

## Common Mistakes

- Moving without testing the real commute or transit path.
- Signing a lease before checking parking, utility cost, and insurance fit.
- Assuming one California city can represent the whole state.
- Waiting too long to organize DMV and insurance workflows.
