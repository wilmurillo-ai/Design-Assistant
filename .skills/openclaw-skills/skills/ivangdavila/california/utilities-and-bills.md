# Utilities and Bills — California

Use this file for electricity, water, gas, internet, trash, and recurring household-cost questions.

## Utility Reality

- California utility answers are local and provider-specific.
- The right question is not only "how much is power?" but also who serves the address, how stable the service is, and whether climate or outage risk changes the real monthly bill.

## Billing Reality

California monthly costs often swing because of:
- summer cooling or inland heat
- EV charging
- water pricing and drought behavior
- parking and transit costs tied to location choice
- internet competition or monopoly building situations
- smoke, outage, or public-safety shutoff preparedness

## Move-In Utility Checklist

1. Confirm electric and gas provider by address.
2. Confirm water, trash, and any city or HOA bundling.
3. Check fiber and upload reliability before signing a lease if remote work matters.
4. Ask about deposits, transfer timing, and outage history.

## Power Rules

- Compare expected total bill, not only a generic statewide average.
- Ask whether the home is old, shaded, inland-hot, coastal-cool, all-electric, EV-equipped, or exposed to outage risk.
- If outage tolerance is low, bring in backup planning from `hazards-and-preparedness.md`.

## Internet Rules

- Address-level availability beats brand reputation.
- For remote workers or students, ask about upload speed, outage history, and whether mobile coverage can serve as backup.

## Common Mistakes

- Assuming California utility cost is one statewide number.
- Recommending a cheaper suburb without estimating power, water, parking, and commute together.
- Ignoring outage risk and backup planning in wildfire or heat-sensitive areas.
- Forgetting to check fiber before signing a long lease.
