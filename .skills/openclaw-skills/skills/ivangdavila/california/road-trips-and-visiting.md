# Road Trips and Visiting — California

Use this file for tourism, city-hopping, national parks, scenic routes, and family travel inside California.

## Visitor Rule

California trips work best when users choose fewer bases and respect transfer friction.

## Good Base Patterns

| Goal | Better base |
|------|-------------|
| Dense urban culture, food, and transit pockets | San Francisco or Bay Area core |
| Entertainment, food spread, and museum variety | Los Angeles |
| Family pace and beaches | San Diego or Orange County depending on style |
| Scenic drives and coastal rhythm | Central Coast |
| Big parks and more remote nature | Sierra or dedicated park routing with buffers |

## Route Design Rules

- Price the trip in daylight, traffic, permits, and fatigue, not just miles.
- If children are involved, design around reset windows, shade, and backup stops.
- If the trip touches parks, mountain roads, or wildfire season, bring in permit and hazard logic early.

## Tourism Reality Checks

- California is not a one-weekend state if the plan spans Bay Area, LA, San Diego, and parks.
- Park add-ons often require slower pacing than users expect.
- Summer or fire-season conditions can change a good-looking route fast.
- The best California trip is usually narrower and deeper than the first draft.

## Common Mistakes

- Trying to do San Francisco, Yosemite, Big Sur, Los Angeles, San Diego, and Joshua Tree in one short trip.
- Ignoring parking, traffic, and airport spread inside major metros.
- Planning outdoor-heavy days without heat, smoke, or permit buffers.
- Treating every California city as interchangeable instead of choosing by vibe and logistics.
