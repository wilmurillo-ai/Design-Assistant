# Family and Schools — California

Use this file for school-district, enrollment, childcare, and family-location decisions.

## Core Rule

In California, family advice often depends more on the exact district, commute shape, and childcare reality than on the city name alone.

## School Fit Workflow

1. Ask whether the user is renting, buying, or just comparing areas.
2. Ask for city, suburb, or target ZIP.
3. Confirm whether the concern is school quality, district boundary, commute, childcare, or special programs.
4. Use official district and school resources before making precise claims.

## What Usually Matters Most

- district and attendance boundary
- commute for both parents and children
- after-school care and childcare access
- special education, language support, or magnet needs
- whether the housing choice still works if school logistics become the daily bottleneck

## California-Specific Planning Notes

- District boundaries and school fit can change sharply across nearby neighborhoods.
- Buying in the "right city" does not guarantee the right school experience.
- Statewide data is useful for screening, but family fit still needs local checking.
- If the user is relocating quickly, make sure school records and care logistics are part of the move sequence.

## Childcare and Family Logistics

- Ask about waitlists early in high-demand metros and suburbs.
- Factor pickup, drop-off, and commute load into school choice, not only reputation.
- For younger children, family logistics may matter more than marginal school-ranking differences.

## Common Mistakes

- Comparing homes without checking the exact district and daily route shape.
- Assuming city name equals school access.
- Giving family-friendly advice without childcare, parking, and commute reality.
- Treating every suburb in a metro as equally good for families.
