# Housing and Insurance — California

Use this file for renting, buying, property fit, wildfire insurance pressure, renter insurance, and homeowner decision support.

## Core Housing Rule

In California, housing is not only about rent or mortgage. It is also about commute, parking, state-tax-adjusted budget, insurance availability, and hazard exposure.

## Rent vs Buy Questions That Matter

Ask these before judging a market:
- How long is the user staying?
- Does school district quality matter?
- Can they handle wildfire, smoke, flood, or earthquake-readiness stress?
- Do they need walkability, transit, or guaranteed parking?
- Is the real budget based on monthly payment or total carrying cost?

## California Housing Reality

- Housing varies more by corridor and municipality than by region label alone.
- Insurance can be a gating factor, not a footnote, especially in wildfire-exposed areas.
- Parking, HOA restrictions, rent-control assumptions, and local permit friction can materially change fit.

## Buying Rules

### 1. Price the full carrying cost
- Principal and interest alone are not the California answer.
- Include taxes, insurance, utilities, parking, HOA, maintenance, and risk-related costs.

### 2. Hazard screening is part of home search
- Ask about wildfire exposure, smoke history, flood or mudslide patterns, roof age, defensible space, and insurance availability before calling a home "good value."

### 3. Earthquake readiness changes the decision
- Earthquake insurance may be optional, but preparedness is not.
- Ask whether the user wants the lowest monthly cost or the best resilience posture.

## Renting Rules

- Lease terms, parking, utility pass-throughs, renter insurance, building age, and neighborhood safety all deserve a checklist.
- California renters often assume legal protections solve a bad unit or bad commute. They do not.
- If the property sits in a hard commute corridor or high-risk zone, calculate that early.

## Common Mistakes

- Choosing a home by list price or rent alone.
- Ignoring insurance availability until after emotional commitment.
- Forgetting parking and commute burden.
- Assuming all California suburban markets are interchangeable.
