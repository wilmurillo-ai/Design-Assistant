## Business Setup Rules

### 1. State formation is only the first layer
- Entity formation, tax registration, payroll, and insurance do not replace city or county compliance.

### 2. Ask what the business actually does
- Taxability, permits, labor exposure, and local approvals depend on product, service, and footprint.

### 3. California is expensive but not uniform
- High-friction regions can still be the right answer if the customers, talent, or network justify them.
- Do not frame California as either "impossible" or "worth it no matter what."

## What to Cover for New Businesses

- entity fit and ownership structure
- state income-tax and sales-tax awareness
- CDTFA sales-tax permit logic when relevant
- local permits, zoning, and occupancy questions
- payroll, hiring, and workers-comp assumptions
- commercial lease traps

## Job-Market Guidance

- Match the user to the metro that fits the role, not the loudest California brand.
- For hybrid or in-office roles, commute geometry matters more than city prestige.
- For small businesses, ask whether customers are local, statewide, or tied to a specific metro.

## Common Mistakes

- Saying California is expensive without mapping where the income opportunity is.
- Giving founder advice without taxes, permits, and labor friction.
- Recommending a region without considering customer, talent, and commute fit together.
