# Hazards and Preparedness — California

Use this file when wildfire, smoke, earthquake, flood, mudslide, heat, or outage risk changes the answer.

## Hazard Map by Region

| Region | Main risks |
|--------|------------|
| Bay Area | Earthquakes, localized flood, outages, smoke spillover |
| Los Angeles and foothills | Earthquakes, wildfire, smoke, heat, mudslide after burn scars |
| Orange County and inland south | Heat, wildfire edges, flood pockets, commute disruption |
| San Diego County | Wildfire, drought, heat, localized flood, border travel delays |
| Sacramento and Central Valley | Heat, smoke, flood patterns, power load |
| Sierra and mountain zones | Snow, wildfire, road closures, sparse services |
| North Coast and wine country | Wildfire, flood, landslide, road disruption |

## Rules for Good Advice

### 1. California hazards are operational, not decorative
- They change home choice, insurance, commute design, and trip timing.

### 2. Lead with the nearest real risk
- For foothill or canyon housing, think wildfire and insurance first.
- For Bay Area and LA building readiness, think earthquake and outage first.
- For Central Valley summer routines, think heat and smoke first.

### 3. Always give a fallback
- Alternative route
- Earlier departure window
- Remote-work or indoor option
- Backup power or cooling plan
- Reschedule threshold

## Readiness Checklist

- Water, flashlights, chargers, and medication buffer
- Earthquake basics: secure heavy items and know "drop, cover, and hold on"
- Wildfire basics: alerts, go-bag, defensible-space awareness where relevant
- Insurance documents and photos stored safely
- Fuel and phone margin before mountain, rural, or smoke-affected drives

## Common Mistakes

- Recommending a home or trip without region-specific hazard fit.
- Treating earthquake prep as optional trivia.
- Ignoring smoke and outage risk when advising families or remote workers.
- Talking about California climate like it is the same statewide.
