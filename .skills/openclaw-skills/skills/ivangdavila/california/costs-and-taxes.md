# Costs and Taxes — California

Use this file for salary realism, relocation budgets, statewide tax framing, and hidden recurring costs.

## California Cost Rule

Never compare California with another state using rent or salary alone.

## State Tax Reality

- California state income tax materially changes net income.
- Sales tax varies by local jurisdiction.
- The correct answer is almost always "it depends on where and how you live."

## Sales Tax Reality

- California has a statewide base sales and use tax rate.
- Local district taxes increase the real rate in many cities and counties.
- Use official address or jurisdiction lookups for anything precise.

## Household Budget Categories That Surprise Newcomers

| Category | Why it hurts |
|----------|--------------|
| State income tax | Changes take-home pay materially for many professional salaries |
| Auto insurance and parking | Dense metros and long commutes make this non-trivial |
| Utilities | Inland heat and provider structure can raise monthly cost fast |
| Transit or toll infrastructure | Bay bridges, express lanes, and passes change routine cost |
| Insurance stress | Wildfire and other hazard exposure can change housing cost materially |

## Budget Rules

### 1. Compare full monthly life, not only housing
- If the user is comparing California against Texas, New York, Washington, or another California metro, price the whole routine.

### 2. Ask whether the user is renter, owner, or operator
- State tax, insurance exposure, and business obligations change by role.

### 3. Distinguish statewide tax from local burden
- City, county, and neighborhood decisions change the monthly picture more than many users expect.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating a big salary as automatic California affordability.
- Quoting one statewide sales-tax number as if it were final.
- Ignoring insurance, parking, and utility pressure.
- Giving founder advice without mentioning California tax and compliance friction.
