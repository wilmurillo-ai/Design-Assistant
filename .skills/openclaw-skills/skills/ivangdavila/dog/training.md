# Dog Training

## Foundation Rules

- One cue means one behavior
- Reward fast enough that the dog can connect action to outcome
- Lower difficulty before the dog starts failing repeatedly
- End before fatigue or frustration turns the session sloppy

## Core Skills to Track

- name response
- recall
- loose-leash walking
- sit, down, wait, place
- handling tolerance for paws, ears, mouth, and vet prep
- crate or settle behavior

## Threshold Thinking

When a dog barks, lunges, freezes, or spirals:
- increase distance first
- reduce duration
- use easier repetitions
- switch from correction ideas to management and recovery

## Useful Progress Fields

Log:
- current cue reliability
- best reward
- easiest environment where the skill works
- current trigger distance
- recent wins and recent failures

## Common Mistakes

- repeating the cue until it becomes background noise
- training through fear or over-arousal
- assuming the dog is being stubborn when the task is too hard
- expecting recall to hold off leash before proofing it under distraction
