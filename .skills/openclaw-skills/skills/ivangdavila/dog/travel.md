# Dog Travel and Absence

## Car and Vet Travel

- Know whether the dog travels best with a crate, harness, or barrier
- Pack water, cleanup gear, meds, and a fallback rest plan
- Keep travel load realistic for weather and recovery
- Do not plan long, hot, or chaotic trips around an already stressed dog

## Sitter or Boarding Pack

Prepare a short exportable pack with:
- feeding amounts and schedule
- walk routine and elimination habits
- meds and handling instructions
- trigger list and management rules
- emergency clinic and owner contact details

## Travel Decisions

Help the user choose between:
- dog stays home with sitter
- dog boards
- dog travels with the user

Bias toward the option the dog can actually handle safely and predictably.

## Moving House

- preserve a stable rest area first
- keep walk, meal, and potty timing as close to normal as possible
- expect threshold changes while the dog adjusts
- lower training demands until the dog is settled
