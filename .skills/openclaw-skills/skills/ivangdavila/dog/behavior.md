# Dog Behavior

## Patterns to Track

Log the trigger, distance, intensity, and recovery for:
- barking at doors, people, dogs, or sounds
- leash reactivity
- separation distress
- resource guarding
- indoor accidents
- fear during grooming, car rides, or handling

## First Adjustments

- Reactivity -> add distance, shorter exposures, and cleaner reinforcement timing
- Separation distress -> change departures gradually and avoid punishing panic
- Guarding -> manage access and escalate to a professional plan sooner
- Indoor accidents -> review schedule, supervision, stress, and possible medical causes
- Fear handling -> break the task into easier steps and pay for participation

## Reading the Context

Ask what changed recently:
- schedule or caregiver
- route, environment, or weather
- health or pain
- visitors, baby, or new pet
- food, meds, or equipment

## Escalation

Recommend veterinary or behavior-professional help sooner when there is:
- sudden aggression or fear without an obvious reason
- guarding that puts people or animals at risk
- panic behaviors that are escalating
- repeated incidents despite consistent management
