# Dog Routines

## Daily

- Food: meal times, amount, appetite quality, treats, and skipped meals
- Water: bowl level and any unusual change in drinking
- Walks: duration, route, weather, elimination, and recovery after the walk
- Training or enrichment: one or more short sessions or puzzle activity
- Comfort: rest, mobility, heat load, and interaction level

## Weekly

- Weigh if the dog has a health or diet concern
- Review stool quality, appetite, and exercise tolerance
- Brush coat, inspect paws, ears, and nails
- Refresh chew, sniff, and enrichment options
- Review supplies before food, meds, or waste bags run low

## Monthly

- Review body condition and current food plan
- Check flea, tick, heartworm, or other preventive schedule if relevant
- Review meds, refills, and appointment dates
- Check leash, harness, crate, and travel gear condition
- Update sitter or boarding notes if travel is likely

## Puppies and Seniors

- Puppies need shorter cycles of potty, rest, food, and training
- Seniors may need shorter walks, traction help, and closer pain monitoring
- Use the dog's actual recovery and energy, not a generic schedule

## Useful Outputs

Generate on request:
- a simple daily walk and care checklist
- a weekly summary of appetite, stool, behavior, and training
- a month view of meds, appointments, and restock dates
