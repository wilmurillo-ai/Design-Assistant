# Safety Modifications - Tai Chi

Tai chi is generally low-impact, but "gentle" is not the same as risk-free.
Use conservative adjustments when symptoms, instability, or medical uncertainty are present.

## Hard Stop Signals

Stop practice and recommend urgent professional or emergency help as appropriate if the user reports:
- chest pain
- fainting or near-fainting
- sudden severe dizziness
- sudden weakness, numbness, or one-sided symptoms
- acute injury during practice
- severe shortness of breath that does not settle

## Practice Modifications

### Balance concerns or fear of falling

- practice next to a wall, chair, or counter
- shorten stance and reduce turning speed
- remove eyes-closed or single-leg variations
- prioritize transfer control over form memorization

### Knee or hip discomfort

- reduce stance depth first
- keep knee tracking aligned with foot direction
- avoid forcing deep bends or long holds
- stop progression if pain rises during or after practice

### Low-back irritation

- keep spine tall and neutral
- reduce twisting range
- avoid collapsing chest or over-arching lumbar spine
- use shorter sessions until symptoms are stable

### Fatigue, illness recovery, or deconditioning

- use `recover` mode
- start with 5 to 8 minutes
- add seated breathing or weight-shift imagery if standing volume is limited
- progress only after repeat sessions feel easier, not merely tolerable

### Pregnancy or postpartum context

- keep transitions smooth and non-straining
- avoid any forcing, breath holding, or unstable turning
- use clinician guidance before starting or intensifying practice if pregnancy-related concerns exist
- stop and seek care for concerning symptoms

## Scope Boundaries

- Do not prescribe tai chi as a cure.
- Do not overrule a clinician or physical therapist.
- If the user has recent surgery, recurrent falls, worsening neurological symptoms, or unclear pain, keep advice conservative and recommend professional clearance.

## Safe Progress Rule

Increase only one variable at a time:
- duration
- complexity
- range of motion
- frequency

If stability worsens after a change, return to the last steady version.
