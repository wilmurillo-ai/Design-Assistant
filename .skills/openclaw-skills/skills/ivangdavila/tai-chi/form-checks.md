# Form Checks - Tai Chi

Use these checks after watching for one repeating issue.
Correct one pattern at a time.

## Root

### Problem: stance is rigid or locked

What it looks like:
- knees fully locked
- hips fixed
- upper body sways because legs are braced

Correction cue:
- "Stand tall, soften the knees, and let the feet feel wide."

Easier drill:
- mini bend and rise without shifting weight

### Problem: body leans instead of shifting

What it looks like:
- torso falls sideways
- head moves beyond the base too early
- feet grip the floor

Correction cue:
- "Move the weight through the hips, not by tipping the chest."

Easier drill:
- hands on lower ribs while shifting side to side

## Shift

### Problem: weight transfer is abrupt

What it looks like:
- drop into one leg
- step lands heavy
- recovery step needed after transition

Correction cue:
- "Empty one side slowly before you fill the other."

Easier drill:
- pause at the center before each transfer

### Problem: knees collapse inward

What it looks like:
- knee points inside the foot
- stance feels unstable
- discomfort around knee or hip appears

Correction cue:
- "Track the knee where the toes point."

Easier drill:
- shorter stance with smaller transfer

## Breathe

### Problem: breath is held during transitions

What it looks like:
- shoulders rise
- jaw tightens
- movement becomes segmented

Correction cue:
- "Let the breath stay easy enough that you could speak a sentence."

Easier drill:
- exhale through the whole shift, inhale during reset

### Problem: movement becomes too performative

What it looks like:
- speed increases without control
- gestures get large but unstable
- cue recall gets worse

Correction cue:
- "Make it quieter, not bigger."

Easier drill:
- perform half-size version with eyes on the horizon

## Review Rule

A correction is working when:
- the user can repeat it for 3 to 5 rounds
- balance feels more predictable
- the cue becomes shorter, not more complicated
