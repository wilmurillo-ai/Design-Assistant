# Best Dining Areas - Seattle

## Capitol Hill

**Best for:** Date nights, variety, walkable dining

### Character
- Most restaurant-dense neighborhood
- Every cuisine represented
- Casual to upscale
- Great bar scene too

### Top Picks

| Restaurant | Cuisine | Price |
|------------|---------|-------|
| Altura | Italian | $$$$ |
| Stateside | Vietnamese | $$$ |
| Ba Bar | Vietnamese | $$ |
| Sitka and Spruce | PNW | $$$ |
| Oddfellows | Brunch | $$ |
| Momiji | Japanese | $$ |

### Best Blocks
- Pike/Pine corridor
- Broadway (more casual)
- 15th Ave E (quieter)

## Ballard

**Best for:** Breweries, nice dinners, established scene

### Character
- Restaurants and breweries
- Slightly older crowd than Cap Hill
- Nordic heritage shows in some spots
- Sunday farmers market

## International District

**Best for:** Asian cuisines, cheap eats

### Character
- Chinese, Vietnamese, Japanese, Filipino
- Most authentic Asian food
- Lower prices than elsewhere
- Uwajimaya is essential

### Top Picks

| Restaurant | Cuisine | Price |
|------------|---------|-------|
| Jade Garden | Dim Sum | $$ |
| Pho Bac | Vietnamese | $ |
| Tamarind Tree | Vietnamese | $$ |
| Maneki | Japanese | $$$ |
| Uwajimaya Food Court | Various | $ |
| Dough Zone | Dumplings | $$ |

### Best For
- Dim sum (weekend mornings)
- Pho (anytime)
- Asian groceries (Uwajimaya)
