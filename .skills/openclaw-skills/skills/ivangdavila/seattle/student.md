# Students - Seattle

## Universities

### University of Washington (UW)

**Overview:**
- Top public research university
- Strong CS, engineering, medicine
- Beautiful campus
- 45,000+ students

**Strengths:**
| Program | Ranking |
|---------|---------|
| Computer Science | Top 10 |
| Medicine | Top 10 |
| Business (Foster) | Top 25 |
| Engineering | Top 20 |
| Public Health | Top 5 |

**Location:** U-District, north Seattle

**Cost (2024):**
- In-state: ~$12K/year tuition
- Out-of-state: ~$40K/year tuition
- Total COA: $25-45K/year

**For Tech Students:**
- Direct pipeline to Amazon, Microsoft
- Allen School (CS) is competitive to get into
- Strong internship opportunities
- Research opportunities

### Seattle University

**Overview:**
- Private Jesuit university
- Smaller, personal attention
- Good business and nursing programs
- Downtown-adjacent

**Strengths:** Business, nursing, law

**Location:** First Hill (Capitol Hill adjacent)

**Cost:** ~$50K/year tuition

### Seattle Pacific University

**Overview:**
- Private Christian university
- Smaller, community-focused
- Various programs

**Location:** Queen Anne

### Community Colleges

## Student Life

### U-District (UW Students)

**Pros:**
- Cheap food (Asian especially)
- Student energy
- Light rail to downtown
- Near campus

**Cons:**
- Not the most exciting neighborhood
- Can feel young
- Some safety concerns

**Living costs:**
- Shared room: $800-1,200
- Studio: $1,200-1,700
- Food: Cheap options everywhere

### Capitol Hill (Alternative)

**Pros:**
- More nightlife
- Walking distance to SU
- Better food/bar scene
- Light rail to UW

**Cons:**
- More expensive
- Less student-focused

## Student Budget

### Monthly Breakdown (Shared Housing)

| Expense | Cost |
|---------|------|
| Rent (shared) | $900-1,200 |
| Utilities | $50-100 |
| Food | $300-400 |
| Transit (U-Pass) | Included |
| Phone | $50 |
| Entertainment | $100-200 |
| **Total** | **$1,400-2,000** |

### Money-Saving Tips
1. **U-Pass:** Free unlimited transit for UW students
2. **Campus food:** Often cheapest options
3. **Student discounts:** Amazon Prime Student, Spotify, etc.
4. **U-District food:** Cheap Asian restaurants
5. **Library:** Free entertainment, study space
6. **Roommates:** Essential for affordability

## Internships

### Tech (Most Common Path)

**Timeline:**
- Freshman: Start LeetCode, CS fundamentals
- Sophomore: First internships (smaller companies)
- Junior: FAANG internships (Big Tech)
- Senior: Return offers or full-time recruiting

**Compensation (Summer, 12 weeks):**
| Company | Monthly | Housing |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Amazon | $8-10K | Yes |
| Microsoft | $8-10K | Yes |
| Meta | $9-11K | Yes |
| Google | $9-11K | Yes |
| Startups | $4-8K | Maybe |

### Where to Find
- Handshake (university platform)
- LinkedIn
- Company websites
- Career fairs
- Professor connections
- Referrals

## Post-Graduation

### Employment Rates (UW CS)
- 90%+ employed within 6 months
- Most stay in Seattle initially
- Average starting: $120-150K (Big Tech)

### Stay vs Leave
**Stay in Seattle:**
- Strong job market
- Already have network
- Quality of life
- No state income tax

**Leave for SF:**
- Broader startup ecosystem
- More variety
- Career FOMO

## International Students

### OPT/CPT
- UW has strong international student services
- CPT available for internships
- OPT (12 months, 36 for STEM) post-graduation
- H-1B sponsorship common at Big Tech

### Visa-Friendly Employers
- Amazon (heavy H-1B sponsor)
- Microsoft (heavy H-1B sponsor)
- Most Big Tech
- Many established startups

### Tips
- Start internship search early
- Prioritize CPT/OPT-eligible work
- Network with other international students
- Know your timelines
