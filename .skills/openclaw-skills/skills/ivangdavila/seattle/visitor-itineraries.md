# Visitor Itineraries - Seattle

## 1-Day Itinerary (Best of Seattle)

### Morning (9am-12pm)
1. **Pike Place Market** (2-3 hours)
   - Arrive at 9am before crowds
   - Watch fish throwing
   - Quick photo at original Starbucks
   - Coffee at a real roaster (not Starbucks)
   - Pike Place Chowder or Beecher's mac and cheese

### Midday (12pm-3pm)
2. **Seattle Center** (2-3 hours)
   - Walk or monorail from downtown
   - Space Needle (or Columbia Center for budget)
   - Chihuly Garden and Glass (highly recommended)
   - Walk through MoPOP building at minimum

### Afternoon/Evening (3pm-8pm)
3. **Capitol Hill** (3+ hours)
   - Coffee at Victrola or Elm
   - Walk Broadway for people watching
   - Dinner at one of many great restaurants
   - Optional: Bar hop if nightlife interests you

**Alternative ending:** Sunset at Kerry Park (best skyline + Rainier view) then dinner in Queen Anne.

## 3-Day Itinerary

### Day 1: Downtown Core
- **Morning**: Pike Place Market (see 1-day above)
- **Midday**: Walk waterfront (under construction but still scenic)
- **Afternoon**: Seattle Art Museum or Pioneer Square
- **Evening**: Dinner in Belltown

### Day 2: Seattle Center + Neighborhoods
- **Morning**: Space Needle, Chihuly, MoPOP
- **Lunch**: Queen Anne
- **Afternoon**: Ballard - shops, Locks, breweries
- **Evening**: Dinner in Ballard (The Walrus, Stoneburner)

### Day 3: Outside Downtown
- **Morning**: Capitol Hill coffee and brunch
- **Midday**: University District if interested (campus is beautiful)
- **Afternoon**: Fremont (Troll, Theo Chocolate, quirky shops) or Discovery Park
- **Evening**: Fremont or Wallingford dinner

**Alternative Day 3:** Full day trip to Bainbridge Island (30-min ferry, charming town, wine tasting).

---

## 7-Day Itinerary

### Days 1-3: Core Seattle
Follow 3-day itinerary above.

### Day 4: Eastside
- **Morning**: Drive or Uber to Bellevue
- **Midday**: Bellevue downtown (surprisingly nice), Bellevue Square
- **Afternoon**: Kirkland waterfront (charming), or Woodinville wine tasting
- **Evening**: Dinner in Bellevue or back to Seattle

### Day 5: Day Trip - Mt. Rainier
- **Full day**: 2-hour drive each way
- **Highlights**: Paradise visitor center, Skyline Trail (if summer)
- **Best months**: July-September (snow clears)
- **Winter alternative**: Snoqualmie Falls (30 min) + North Bend small town

### Day 6: Day Trip - Islands or Coast
**Option A: San Juan Islands**
- Take ferry from Anacortes (2 hours north)
- Friday Harbor is main town
- Whale watching, kayaking, small-town vibes
- Full day commitment

**Option B: Bainbridge Island**
- 30-min ferry from downtown Seattle
- Walk to town, wine tasting, easy half-day
- Combine with Poulsbo (Scandinavian town)

### Day 7: Relaxed Exploration
- Sleep in
- Revisit favorite neighborhood
- Shopping (Nordstrom flagship downtown)
- Final Pike Place visit for souvenirs
- Early dinner, pack up

## Weather-Adjusted Planning

### Sunny Day (Rare Treasure)
Drop everything and go outside:
- Discovery Park
- Alki Beach
- Kerry Park sunset
- Gas Works Park
- Any mountain day trip

### Grey But Dry (Typical)
- Outdoor activities still fine
- Layer up
- Parks and walks work

### Rainy Day
- Pike Place (mostly covered)
- Museums (MoPOP, SAM, Chihuly)
- Coffee crawl
- Shopping

### Beautiful Summer Day (Jul-Sep)
- Ferry to Bainbridge
- Hike (Rattlesnake Ledge is close and easy)
- Outdoor dining
- Gas Works Park evening

## Logistics Tips

1. **Stay downtown or Capitol Hill** - Most walkable for tourists
2. **Skip the car** for Days 1-3 - Light rail + Uber works
3. **Rent car only for** day trips (Day 4-6)
4. **Book ahead**: Popular restaurants, whale watching, Mt. Rainier in summer
5. **Ferries**: Arrive 30-60 min early for walk-on, 1+ hour for car
