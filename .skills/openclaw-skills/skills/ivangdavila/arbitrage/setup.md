# Setup — Arbitrage

Use this guide when `~/arbitrage/` does not exist yet or when the user has never defined how they want arbitrage help to show up.

## Your Attitude

Be skeptical, practical, and calm. The goal is to help the user separate real locked spreads from misleading surface gaps. Reward precision, ask for missing economics, and reduce false confidence.

## Priority Order

### 1. First: Integration

Clarify when this skill should activate:
- whenever the user mentions arbitrage, surebet, hedge, spread, mispricing, or price gap
- only when the user asks directly
- never for certain markets or venues

### 2. Then: Understand Their Arena

Figure out which arena matters most right now:
- sportsbooks or betting exchanges
- prediction markets
- crypto spot, perps, basis, or cross-chain
- retail, resale, freight, travel, or another pricing mismatch
- education only, with no live opportunity tracking

### 3. Then: Capture Operating Constraints

Get the inputs that change the answer:
- minimum spread worth checking
- fees, transfer costs, financing, or FX they always want modeled
- preferred venues and blocked venues
- region, KYC, leverage, or capital limits
- whether they want hard rejections or a ranked watchlist

### 4. Finally: Decide Tracking Depth

Default to light tracking. If they want more depth, keep:
- venue notes
- active opportunities
- invalidated ideas and repeated failure modes

## What to Save

Save only what the user explicitly shares:
- when this skill should activate
- the markets they care about
- minimum spread or hurdle rate
- preferred and blocked venues
- risk limits, geography, and sizing constraints
- repeated failure modes they want remembered

## Good Openers

- "Should I jump in whenever you are evaluating a price gap, or only when you ask?"
- "What kind of arbitrage are you actually working on right now: books, prediction markets, crypto, or another market?"
- "What usually kills deals for you: fees, transfer time, sizing limits, or settlement rules?"
