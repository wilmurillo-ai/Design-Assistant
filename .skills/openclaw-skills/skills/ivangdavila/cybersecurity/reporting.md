# Reporting Structure

Write cybersecurity output so someone can act on it quickly.

## Executive Summary

- what happened or could happen
- business impact in plain language
- current confidence
- immediate decision needed

## Technical Summary

- affected asset or boundary
- attack path or failure mode
- evidence and assumptions
- exploit preconditions

## Recommendations

- now: contain, validate, block, rotate, patch, or monitor
- next: durable fixes and ownership
- later: hardening, detections, or process changes

## Severity Language

- avoid dramatic labels without evidence
- justify severity with impact, exploitability, and exposure
- say when compensating controls reduce practical risk
