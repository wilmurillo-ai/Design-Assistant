# Shipping and Disputes - DHgate

Use the live order page first whenever available.

## Triage Table

| Situation | Likely Meaning | Best Next Move |
|-----------|----------------|----------------|
| No tracking yet, very early | seller may still be inside handling window | confirm payment date and promised ship window before escalating |
| No tracking after a long silence | seller may be stalling or not dispatched | ask seller for dispatch proof, then prepare dispute path |
| Tracking exists but no movement | handoff lag or fake pre-shipment label | compare with promised ETA and ask for carrier-level detail |
| Marked delivered, parcel missing | local carrier, locker, or address issue | check local post office or carrier first, then prepare evidence |
| Wrong item, damaged item, or missing pieces | quality or fulfillment failure | collect evidence immediately and define refund target |

## Timing Rules

Current DHgate help guidance indicates:
- tracking numbers can take up to about 10 business days after payment to appear
- delivered-but-missing cases should be checked with the local post office or carrier using the tracking number and any claim paperwork
- reviews are usually available for a limited period after order completion, often around 30 days

If the user has the live page, trust the actual order state over generic timing memory.

## Return and Refund Workflow

When an order is in the stage that allows buyer action:
1. define the exact problem in one sentence
2. decide the requested remedy before writing the seller
3. submit evidence that matches the claim
4. keep messages factual and short
5. watch the seller response window closely

DHgate help materials indicate seller response windows are short, often around five days after a return or refund request begins.

## Evidence Checklist

For wrong, damaged, or incomplete orders, capture:
- outer package and shipping label
- all received contents in one frame
- close-ups of the defect or wrong specification
- quantity proof if units are missing
- short video if the issue is functional or mechanical
- timeline notes: order date, promised window, actual arrival, seller replies

## Message Rules

- do not argue about motives; describe facts
- name the exact mismatch between listing and received goods
- ask for one clear remedy: full refund, partial refund, or resend
- avoid long emotional messages that bury the evidence

## When to Push Harder

Escalate the recommendation level when:
- the seller contradicts the written listing
- the seller asks to settle outside the platform
- the evidence is strong but the seller tries to delay past the claim window
- the parcel shows delivery but the carrier can document loss or misdelivery
