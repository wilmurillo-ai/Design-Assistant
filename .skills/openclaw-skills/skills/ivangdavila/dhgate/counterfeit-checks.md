# Counterfeit Checks - DHgate

Cheap branded listings are usually the fastest way to turn a bargain into a loss.

## Fast Filter

Reject or strongly warn when you see:
- luxury, sportswear, electronics, or cosmetics listings at implausible discounts
- blurred or partially hidden logos
- titles that dodge the brand name while the photos imply the brand
- seller replies that avoid saying whether the product is branded or generic
- packaging promises that sound designed to hide origin or branding

## Category Risk Map

| Category | Risk | Why |
|----------|------|-----|
| Luxury and fashion logos | extreme | trademark seizure and obvious counterfeit exposure |
| Consumer electronics accessories | high | safety, certification, and compatibility claims are often weak |
| Cosmetics and ingestibles | extreme | health risk and poor traceability |
| Toys and baby items | high | safety standards matter more than price |
| Unbranded home goods | lower | still verify material quality and dimensions |

## Safe Alternative Framing

Instead of rationalizing a risky branded listing:
- find an unbranded equivalent
- compare with a domestic marketplace option
- ask whether the user wants a sample to validate quality first
- downgrade the category if compliance or safety matters

## Questions That Expose Risk

- Is the item sold as branded, generic, or compatible?
- Does the product itself carry a logo?
- Does the packaging show a logo?
- Are compliance claims documented or just mentioned?
- Is the price gap explainable by quantity, materials, or no-brand positioning?

## Hard Rules

- Never advise customs evasion, false declarations, or logo-masking tactics.
- Never frame counterfeit risk as acceptable just because the price is low.
- If authenticity matters, recommend buying from an authorized or lower-risk channel instead.
