# Culture — Andorra

Andorra feels Catalan first, with strong Spanish and French spillover. People often understand several languages, but showing basic awareness of the country's own identity helps.

## What Matters

| Topic | Reality |
|-------|---------|
| Official language | Catalan |
| Everyday multilingual reality | Spanish and French widely understood |
| Pace | Relaxed mountain-country rhythm, not a giant city |
| Identity | Small country, distinct from Spain and France |

## Etiquette

- A simple greeting matters.
- Keep political or tax jokes light; outsiders often reduce Andorra to clichés.
- Respect church and sanctuary spaces if visiting cultural sites.
- Tipping is modest. Round up or leave a small extra for good service.

## Timing Notes

- Dinner is later than in France, earlier than deep-Spain late nights.
- Shopping hours are generous, but mountains and weather still set the real pace.
- Sundays can still be useful shopping days in a way that surprises many visitors.

## Style Advice

- Dress for altitude and weather, not only for a city break.
- In winter, practical footwear is more important than trying to look polished.
- In nicer bordas and hotels, smart-casual works better than full resort gear.
