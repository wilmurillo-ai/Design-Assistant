# Hiking — Andorra

Andorra's summer identity is alpine hiking. Snowpack, storms, and visibility matter more than people expect, so route choice should be matched to the user's real fitness and flexibility.

## Best Hikes by Payoff

| Route | Level | Why it stands out |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| Tristaina Lakes | Moderate | Classic alpine scenery with big payoff |
| Sorteny Valley routes | Easy to moderate | Strong flowers and variety |
| Juclar Lakes | Moderate | Good lake objective |
| Comapedrosa side | Hard | Best for stronger hikers |
| Madriu-Perafita-Claror routes | Moderate to hard | UNESCO landscape, slower feel |

## Smart Matching

| Traveler | Best fit |
|----------|----------|
| First-time summer visitor | Tristaina or Sorteny |
| Family with active kids | Shorter valley or lake routes |
| Strong hikers | Comapedrosa or longer Madriu plans |
| Bad-weather risk | Keep a low-commitment walk instead of a summit day |

## Timing Rules

- Start early in summer to avoid afternoon storm risk.
- Shoulder season can bring mixed snow or muddy sections high up.
- If visibility is bad, swap the big panoramic route for a valley day.

## Common Mistakes

- Picking a route by photo only, not by exposure or fitness
- Underestimating altitude and sun even on mild days
- Forgetting that a quick mountain weather shift can erase a scenic objective

## Packing Basics

- Layers, not one heavy jacket
- Water and sun protection even if the morning feels cool
- Backup route saved offline in case signal drops
