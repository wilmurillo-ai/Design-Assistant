# Transport — Andorra

There is no train into Andorra. Most trips arrive by road, usually from Barcelona, Toulouse, or nearby airports, so transport planning matters more here than in many small European destinations.

## Best Ways In

| Route | Best for | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Barcelona by road or bus | Most common access | Strong for weekend trips |
| Toulouse by road or bus | Good northern access | Useful for some ski travelers |
| Car from Spain or France | Maximum flexibility | Best for hiking and mixed bases |

## Bus Reality

- Buses are the cleanest option for car-free travelers.
- Central bases work much better than remote ones if the user depends on buses.
- For a short trip, spending a little more for a direct transfer usually pays off.

## Driving Reality

| Topic | Advice |
|-------|--------|
| Winter tires or chains | Check before every cold-season drive |
| Parking | Confirm at the hotel, especially in central areas |
| Border traffic | Leave margin on Friday evenings, Sundays, and holidays |
| Mountain roads | Short on the map, slower in weather |

## Best Base by Transport Style

| Style | Best base |
|-------|-----------|
| No car | Andorra la Vella or Escaldes |
| Car + skiing | Canillo, Encamp, or slope-oriented stays |
| Car + hiking | Ordino, La Massana, or Canillo |

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Booking the cheapest room without checking the daily transfer cost in time
- Forgetting that parking can be the hidden tax of a city stay
- Assuming a late-night winter drive is easy just because the distance is short
