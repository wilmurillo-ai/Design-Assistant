# Experiences — Andorra

Andorra works best when the trip mixes one headline attraction with one slower local plan each day. The country is compact, so overpacking is a bigger risk than missing out.

## Best Year-Round Experiences

| Experience | Best for | Notes |
|------------|----------|-------|
| Caldea | Couples, bad weather | Book peak slots early |
| Shopping Mile | Weekenders | Better with a list, not pure wandering |
| Roc del Quer | Scenic first-timers | Weather dependent |
| Sanctuary of Meritxell | Culture + architecture | Easy add-on stop |

## Winter Priorities

| Experience | Why it wins |
|------------|-------------|
| Grandvalira ski day | Biggest mainstream draw |
| Ordino Arcalis side plan | Stronger mountain feel |
| Snow activities for non-skiers | Lets mixed groups work |
| Spa after slopes | One of the best winter combinations |

## Summer Priorities

| Experience | Why it wins |
|------------|-------------|
| Tristaina or Sorteny day | Signature mountain scenery |
| Family mountain park plan | High payoff if kids need structure |
| Cycling or e-bike day | Popular and terrain-rich |
| UNESCO Madriu side | Best for slower, more contemplative day |

## Good Trip Design

- One active anchor per day is enough.
- Pair weather-sensitive plans with a city, shopping, or spa backup.
- For weekend trips, do not chase every famous spot. Pick the one that matches the base.

## Weak Trip Design

- Four headline stops in one day
- Driving across the country repeatedly just because distances look small on a map
- Booking only outdoors with no indoor fallback
