# Weather and Seasonality

## Seasonal Grid

| Period | Best for | Watch-outs |
|--------|----------|------------|
| April to June | City plus coast balance, cycling starts, longer days | Wind, mixed temperatures, some sea-cold disappointment |
| July to August | Classic summer islands, beaches, cycling, Bornholm, family travel | Higher prices, booked-out islands, wind, and uneven beach weather |
| September to October | Shoulder-season cities, food, culture, calmer routes | Shorter days, more rain and wind, reduced island energy |
| November to March | City breaks, hygge, museums, winter mood, Christmas season | Short daylight, colder coast exposure, weaker bike and beach logic |

## Summer Reality

- Summer in Denmark is high-value but not guaranteed heat.
- Long daylight helps, but wind and cool water still change the coast experience.
- Islands, Bornholm, and top beach zones book earlier than casual travelers expect.

## Shoulder-Season Advantage

- September can be one of the strongest months for adult travelers who like cities, food, and lighter crowds.
- Shoulder seasons reward users who can tolerate changing weather and a narrower swim or beach menu.
- Culture, bakery, museum, and coastal walks often still work very well.

## Winter Rule

- Choose winter for city mood, food, museums, and seasonal atmosphere, not for pretending it is a beach or bike holiday.
- Copenhagen and Aarhus usually outperform scattered coast routes in the darker months.
- If the user insists on coast or island travel in winter, simplify the route and strengthen indoor fallback plans.

## Planning Rule

Choose the region from the season and the user's weather tolerance, not from the prettiest saved photo.
