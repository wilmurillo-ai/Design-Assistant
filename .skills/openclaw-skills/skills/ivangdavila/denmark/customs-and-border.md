# Customs and Border

Use this file when users ask what they can bring, how cash rules work, or whether border hops feel frictionless.

## Core Rules

- Denmark is in Schengen, but customs rules still matter for goods, cash, alcohol, tobacco, and food.
- If entering or leaving Denmark with EUR 10,000 or more in cash or equivalent valuables, users should expect declaration rules.
- If arriving in Denmark from outside the EU, customs-free value thresholds differ by arrival mode.
- Meat, dairy, some plants, medicines, and protected products can trigger restrictions or seizure.

## Practical Travel Advice

- Current Danish Customs guidance says non-EU arrivals can face duties above DKK 3,250 by plane or ship and above DKK 2,250 by other transport modes.
- Keep medication in original packaging and carry a prescription copy for anything non-obvious.
- If the user is returning from outside the EU, tell them to keep receipts for goods, not just luxury shopping.
- If combining Denmark with Sweden, Germany, the UK, or Norway, remind the user that customs logic may change even when border checks feel light.

## Border Habits

- Sweden and Germany crossings can feel seamless, but random checks still happen and valid ID should stay accessible.
- Ferry arrivals and road crossings may feel informal, but regulated goods and cash rules still apply.
- Train users should still carry valid ID because transport and police checks can happen.

## Common Mistakes

- Assuming "no border" means no rules.
- Carrying large cash without understanding declaration duties.
- Packing restricted foods for the onward trip without checking origin-country rules.
- Confusing EU free movement for people with fully unrestricted movement of goods.
