# Telecoms and Apps

## Connectivity Basics

- EU roaming rules help many travelers already inside the EU.
- Non-EU travelers should check eSIM or local-SIM value if they need constant navigation and messaging.
- Coverage is usually good, but coast walks, ferries, islands, and outdoor days still justify offline backups.

## High-Value App Categories

- Rejseplanen for national public-transport planning
- DSB or Rejsekort products for rail and flexible ticketing
- DMI for weather and warning awareness
- Ferry or bridge operators when the route depends on booked crossings
- Regional visitor tools when the user is going deeper into Copenhagen, Funen, or Bornholm

## Practical Advice

- Download tickets, hotel confirmations, and maps offline.
- Save station, harbor, and ferry names exactly as used in local systems.
- Carry power during full transport days, especially when payment and tickets live on the phone.

## Payment and Connectivity Link

- Denmark's digital convenience becomes fragile if the phone dies.
- A dead battery can mean no ticket, no route, no hotel confirmation, and no easy payment backup.
- Keep one physical card and one non-phone path to the next destination.
