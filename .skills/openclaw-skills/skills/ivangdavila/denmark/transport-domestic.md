# Transport Domestic

## Choose the Dominant Mode First

Denmark rewards plans where one transport mode leads and the others support it.

## Best Mode by Trip Shape

- **Rail-first:** Copenhagen, Odense, Aarhus, and simple east-Denmark intercity routes
- **Car-first:** west coast, Thy, Mons Klint, deep north Jutland, or family routes with gear
- **Ferry-plus-base:** Bornholm and island-heavy trips where access is part of the planning
- **Bike-plus-rail:** Copenhagen, Funen, and selected island or coast users who want active travel without full touring commitment

## Core Tools

- Use Rejseplanen as the default journey planner when stitching different public-transport operators.
- Use DSB directly when national rail timing, tickets, or check-in matters to the decision.
- Use Rejsekort or DSB app products for flexible ticketing, and use Bornholmslinjen directly for Bornholm access.

## Rail Logic

- Denmark rail works best on the Copenhagen, Funen, Aarhus, and Aalborg spine.
- Rail is especially strong when the user wants city plus one regional extension without parking stress.
- Rail weakens when the user expects scattered coast stops, dunes, cliff viewpoints, or late-night remote movement.

## Ferry and Bridge Logic

- Ferries are sometimes transport, sometimes part of the experience, and sometimes the trip bottleneck.
- Bridge tolls matter on self-drive routes even when map distance looks simple.
- Bornholm access should be booked early in peak season and kept visible in the planning timeline.

## Urban Transport Logic

- Copenhagen works best by metro, S-train, bus, walking, and short bike use.
- Aarhus and Odense are easy to handle without a car unless the route immediately continues into countryside or coast.
- Airport-to-city transfers in Denmark are often easier than parking-centered arrival plans.

## Common Transport Errors

- Planning Denmark with a generic assumption that every coast and island hop is spontaneous.
- Renting a car for a city-heavy trip where the car then sits expensively.
- Using too many bases when rail plus one smart day trip would solve the brief.
- Ignoring check-in, ferry, and transfer friction on arrival and departure days.
