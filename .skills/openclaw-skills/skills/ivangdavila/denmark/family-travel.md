# Family Travel

## What Works Well for Families

- Copenhagen with parks, easy transit, and short daily movement
- Funen for slower pacing and easier nature days
- Bornholm for beaches, bikes, and one-destination family logic
- Aarhus for a manageable city plus regional add-ons

## Match Region to Family Style

- **Young children:** Copenhagen, Odense, or Bornholm with minimal hotel changes
- **School-age kids:** Bornholm, Funen, or north-Jutland coast routes with active days
- **Mixed ages:** city plus one nature extension beats a full-country loop

## Transport Rules

- Rail-first is often best when the trip is city-heavy and stroller or luggage management is easy.
- A car pays off when naps, beach gear, groceries, or coast-town flexibility matter more than station-to-hotel simplicity.
- Ferry and island timing should be child-friendly, not treated as an afterthought.

## Pacing Rules

- One main outing plus one easy backup usually beats all-day stacking.
- Families benefit from park, beach, or harbor downtime between attractions.
- Weather backup matters more in Denmark than the map suggests.

## Common Mistakes

- Too many hotel changes in a short family trip.
- Beach expectations that ignore wind and water temperature.
- Car rental for Copenhagen-only days.
- Building a route that depends on every transfer working perfectly.

## Planning Rule

Family Denmark trips should feel easy, not impressive. If the route looks clever but fragile, simplify it.
