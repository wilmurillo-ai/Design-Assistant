# Budget and Costs

## Useful Budget Bands (Per Person / Day)

| Style | Typical range | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Budget | DKK 700-1,200 | Hostels, budget rooms, transit focus, selective paid sights |
| Mid-range | DKK 1,200-2,200 | Good-location hotels, mixed transport, paid attractions and dining |
| Premium | DKK 2,200+ | Prime-location hotels, top dining, flexible transport, upgraded stays |

## Major Cost Drivers

- Copenhagen and peak-summer islands
- Last-minute summer accommodation
- Bridge tolls, ferries, parking, and one-way rentals
- Museum passes, bike rental, and family-ticket layering
- Dining in popular waterfront or high-design areas

## Cost Control Levers

- Fewer bases, longer stays per base
- Rail-first planning for city-heavy routes
- Shoulder-season timing where weather still supports the user's priorities
- Lunch-focused dining with simpler dinners outside special occasions
- One key island or coast experience instead of several transport-heavy add-ons

## Booking Order for Cost and Risk Control

1. Entry and season fit confirmed
2. High-risk inventory such as islands, ferries, or Bornholm access
3. Lodging near primary daily movement
4. Car rental, bike rental, and activity layers

## Hidden Costs Checklist

- Hotel breakfast differences and city tax or service-fee assumptions
- Bridge tolls and parking
- Ferry reservations or date changes
- Airport and station transfers
- Museum-card or attraction bundling
- Data, battery, and offline-map fallback costs

## Planning Rule

If the budget is tight, reduce region count first. Denmark punishes over-routing more than it rewards "seeing everything."
