# Safety and Emergencies

## Essential Numbers

- `112` for police, fire, and ambulance emergencies.
- `114` for non-emergency police contact and general police enquiries.

## Alert Systems To Use

- Public warnings can arrive by siren or mobile warning systems in major incidents.
- DMI warnings matter for dangerous weather, wind, flooding, and storm conditions.
- Keep phone notifications enabled while traveling through coast, island, or storm-exposed areas.

## City Safety Habits

- Denmark is generally low-friction, but bike theft, nightlife carelessness, and unattended luggage still matter.
- Use licensed transport or trusted late-night routes after bars or concerts.
- Keep hotel, station, and airport details saved offline.

## Coast and Nature Safety

- Wind, cold water, tides, and slippery coast paths create more real risk than many first-timers expect.
- Respect cliff edges, dune restrictions, and posted warnings at places such as Mons Klint and exposed coasts.
- In Wadden Sea areas, tide logic matters; do not improvise a route onto flats or marsh without local guidance.

## Incident Response Rule

If a segment becomes unsafe due to storm, flood, heavy wind, or transport disruption:
1. Stop the fragile plan.
2. Rebase to the nearest stronger town or city.
3. Rebuild the remaining days around lower-risk transport and indoor or urban options.
