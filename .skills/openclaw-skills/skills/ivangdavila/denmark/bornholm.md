# Bornholm

## Best Fit

- Best for a dedicated island trip with bikes, beaches, rocky coast, food, and family pace.
- Strong for users who want one self-contained destination with real character.
- Weak fit as a rushed add-on to a short Denmark overview trip.

## Access Model

- Treat access as part of the trip, not a last-minute detail.
- Bornholm works by ferry or flight, and either model changes pacing, luggage logic, and weather resilience.
- Peak-season bookings should be handled early, especially when the user wants vehicle space or fixed accommodation dates.

## Stay Strategy

| Goal | Better base |
|------|-------------|
| Rocky north and castles | North Bornholm |
| Sandy beaches and family rhythm | South or southeast Bornholm |
| Balanced island access | Central base near practical road reach |
| Car-free active stay | One well-served town base |

## What Works Well

- Bornholm rewards fewer bases and more on-the-ground time.
- The island can handle food, beaches, cycling, craft culture, and family travel without needing many hotel moves.
- It is one of the cleanest Denmark products for users who want "one destination, many good days."

## Trap Avoidance

- Do not mix Bornholm with a full mainland Denmark route unless the total trip length really supports it.
- Avoid leaving ferry or flight planning until late.
- Keep weather and wind in mind for bike-heavy or beach-heavy expectations.

## Planning Rule

Use Bornholm when the user wants an island trip, not when they are just afraid to choose. It wins when protected as the main event.
