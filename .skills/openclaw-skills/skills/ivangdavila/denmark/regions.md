# Regions

## Think in Corridors

Denmark is easier to plan when broken into corridors with one main travel logic each.

## 1. Copenhagen and the Capital Region

- Best for first-timers with 3-5 days, city breaks, museums, food, design, and easy transit.
- Strong when the user wants the simplest Denmark trip with optional day trips.
- Weak fit for users whose main goal is remote coast, dunes, or island-hopping.

## 2. North Zealand and South Zealand

- Best for castles, coast, Roskilde history, Mons Klint, and short Denmark extensions from Copenhagen.
- Strong for users who want one capital base plus one scenic contrast.
- Weak fit if the user expects a deep west-coast or rugged-nature product.

## 3. Funen and the Archipelago

- Best for slower Denmark, Odense, cycling, manor and village rhythm, and island-hopping without huge distances.
- Strong for users who want a softer, greener, more local-feeling trip.
- Weak fit for users demanding big-city density every day.

## 4. Aarhus and East Jutland

- Best for second-city culture, food, design, museums, and smart access to Djursland and short coast trips.
- Strong for 4-7 day users who want city plus light nature.
- Weak fit if the user expects raw dune-and-lighthouse scenery as the main event.

## 5. North Jutland and Skagen

- Best for beaches, dunes, Skagen light, Thy, and bigger-space nature trips.
- Strong in late spring through early autumn and for users who accept more wind and driving.
- Weak fit for winter city-break expectations or rail-only travelers with short timelines.

## 6. Southwest Jutland and the Wadden Sea

- Best for Ribe, Romo, UNESCO Wadden nature, birdlife, dark-sky and tide-aware travel.
- Strong for users who value landscape, slowness, and fewer crowds.
- Weak fit if the user wants a classic first Denmark trip with minimal logistics.

## 7. Bornholm

- Best for a dedicated island trip with bikes, beaches, rocky coast, food, and family pace.
- Strong when the user accepts ferry or flight planning and wants one self-contained destination.
- Weak fit as a rushed add-on to a short multi-base Denmark itinerary.

## Corridor Pairings That Usually Work

- Copenhagen plus North Zealand
- Copenhagen plus Odense
- Aarhus plus north Jutland
- Copenhagen plus Bornholm
- Funen plus Aarhus or east Jutland

## Pairings That Usually Fail on Short Trips

- Copenhagen plus Aarhus plus Skagen plus Bornholm in one week
- Copenhagen plus Wadden Sea plus Bornholm as a "quick overview"
- East Jutland plus west-coast dunes plus island-hopping with no car or buffers
