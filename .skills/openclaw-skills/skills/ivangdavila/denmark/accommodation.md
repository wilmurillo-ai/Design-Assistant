# Accommodation

## Base Strategy First

Denmark trips improve when the user sleeps multiple nights per base and chooses lodging around movement, not just room price.

## Best Base Types by Trip Shape

- **Copenhagen city break:** stay near a metro or S-train node, not just the cheapest outer suburb.
- **Capital plus day trips:** central Copenhagen or one well-connected outer district beats hotel-hopping.
- **Funen or island travel:** prefer one town with evening food options over an isolated summer cottage unless the user wants self-catering.
- **Jutland coast routes:** one strong town base can outperform multiple one-night beach stops.
- **Bornholm:** book early and decide whether the user wants rocky north, sandy south, or a central compromise.

## Location Logic

- In cities, proximity to station, metro, or walkable core usually beats parking convenience.
- On islands and coasts, parking and luggage friction matter less than access to dinner, groceries, and weather backup.
- In peak summer, flexible cancellation and early booking are often worth more than chasing the lowest nightly rate.

## Lodging Types That Fit Denmark Well

- City hotels or aparthotels for Copenhagen and Aarhus
- Guesthouses and inns for smaller towns
- Summerhouses when the user wants slow, self-catered family or coast time
- Simple island stays when ferry timing matters more than luxury

## Common Mistakes

- Booking the cheapest airport-adjacent room for a city trip with no real time savings.
- Choosing a remote coast stay without a dinner or grocery plan.
- Using a car-dependent lodge for a rail-first trip.
- Leaving Bornholm, Skagen, or peak-summer coast stays too late.

## Planning Rule

Choose accommodation from daily movement and evening comfort. If the user will resent long transfers after dinner or in bad weather, the "deal" is not a deal.
