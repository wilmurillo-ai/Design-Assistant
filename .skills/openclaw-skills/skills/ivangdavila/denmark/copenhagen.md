# Copenhagen

## Best Fit

- Use Copenhagen for first-timers, long weekends, food and design travelers, and users who want Denmark with minimal friction.
- Keep Copenhagen as the transport backbone when the trip mixes city time with short day trips or one extension.
- Do not over-inflate the city stay if the user really wants dunes, islands, or remote nature.

## Stay Strategy

| Goal | Better base |
|------|-------------|
| Walkable classic city break | Indre By, Vesterbro, or central Christianshavn |
| Museum and design focus | Central Copenhagen near metro access |
| Family pace and easier parks | Frederiksberg or calmer connected districts |
| Early flight convenience | One airport hotel night only when timing is ugly |

## What Works Well

- Copenhagen rewards compact planning: one museum cluster, one food or market window, one evening neighborhood.
- The city is easy by metro, walking, and selective bike use; a car usually adds no value.
- The capital pairs well with North Zealand, Roskilde, or a Copenhagen Card-heavy city break.

## Day-Trip Logic

- Kronborg and Louisiana work well for culture-focused users.
- Roskilde fits Viking, cathedral, and lighter-history days.
- Mons Klint is a stronger scenic add-on only when the user accepts a longer transport day or an overnight extension.

## Trap Avoidance

- Avoid renting a car just to "have flexibility" in Copenhagen.
- Do not stack every headline museum and canal zone into one day.
- Summer crowds and restaurant demand justify reservations earlier than users expect.

## Planning Rule

Use Copenhagen as the cleanest Denmark entry point and decision hub. If the user wants deeper coast or island value, move them onward with purpose instead of stretching the city segment.
