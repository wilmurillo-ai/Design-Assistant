# Accommodation — Switzerland

## Base Strategy by Trip Style

| Trip style | Best lodging pattern |
|-----------|----------------------|
| First-time classic trip | One city base plus one mountain or lake base |
| Mountain-first trip | Fewer bases, longer stays near lifts or trailheads |
| Scenic rail trip | Sleep near major stations, not far uphill from them |
| Family trip | Apartment or aparthotel near station and groceries |

## Good Value Patterns

- Staying just outside the most famous station-core streets
- Using Lucerne, Lausanne, or Bern as stable bases for selective day trips
- Booking mountain resorts early when the user is tied to summer or ski peaks

## Where Booking Errors Hurt

- Zermatt, Wengen, Grindelwald, and major ski or holiday weeks
- Lake Geneva congress or festival dates
- Tiny villages where the train station and hotel are not actually close in elevation

## Practical Lodging Rules

- Prioritize station access and luggage realism over postcard views.
- In car-free mountain villages, check the final transfer chain, not just the hotel address.
- Ask whether breakfast, resort tax, spa access, or parking meaningfully change the total.

## Tourist Trap To Flag

Choosing a cheap hotel that requires uphill walking, multiple bus changes, or a late last-mile transfer after a long train day.
