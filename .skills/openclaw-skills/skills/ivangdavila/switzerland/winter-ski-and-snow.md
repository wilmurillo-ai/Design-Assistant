# Winter, Ski, and Snow — Switzerland

## Best Trip Shapes

- One ski base plus one city or lake buffer
- A pure mountain week for users who genuinely want snow every day
- Family snow trip with short transfers and simple slopes

## What Changes the Answer

- School-holiday pressure
- Whether the user wants skiing, winter hiking, sledding, spas, or festive atmosphere
- Altitude tolerance and willingness to pay peak mountain prices

## Operational Rules

- December gives festive atmosphere, not guaranteed deep snow in every resort.
- January and February reward true winter users, not travelers who dislike cold logistics.
- March can be excellent, but lower-altitude assumptions need checking.

## Common Mistakes

- Booking a ski icon because the name is famous, not because it fits skill level and budget
- Mixing ski-heavy and scenic-rail-heavy days without rest
- Treating arrival-day skiing as automatic after a long-haul flight

## Planning Reminder

For winter trips, always include:

- Weather and slope-condition downgrade options
- Rental and lesson timing
- Transfer logic that still works if snow or queues slow the day
