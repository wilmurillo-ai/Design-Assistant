# Customs and Border — Switzerland

## What Changes Traveler Behavior

- Switzerland is outside the EU customs area, so shopping and duty-free assumptions often need correction.
- Spot checks can still happen on train or road crossings even when the route feels seamless.
- Cash, alcohol, tobacco, meat, and other restricted-goods rules should be verified from the official customs source when the user is bringing significant amounts.

## Practical Customs Guidance

- If the user is carrying expensive purchases, ask where they were bought and where they will exit.
- For cross-border shopping, remind them that both Swiss and neighboring-country rules can matter.
- QuickZoll or the official customs workflow is relevant when declared goods or allowances are in play.

## Common Border Mistakes

- Assuming Schengen means customs no longer exists
- Treating euro purchases and Swiss purchases as one tax environment
- Buying large amounts of food, alcohol, or luxury goods without checking declaration steps
- Forgetting that rural or train-based border moves can still face enforcement

## When To Slow Down the Plan

Pause and verify current rules if the user mentions:

- High-value shopping
- Carrying large cash amounts
- Food or alcohol imports
- Cross-border car rentals with goods in the trunk
