# Domestic Transport — Switzerland

## Mode Selection Framework

| Route type | Default winner | Why |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| Zurich, Lucerne, Interlaken, Geneva classic trip | Rail | Dense coverage and low parking friction |
| Airport arrival plus city stay | Rail + local transit | Strong station and airport links |
| Scenic mountain-first itinerary | Rail + mountain transport | Better than driving if the user wants views, not road stress |
| Rural Ticino, Jura, or valley exploration | Mixed or selective car | Depends on exact villages and flexibility needs |

## Rail-First Rule

- Start with SBB before discussing a rental car.
- Switzerland is one of the strongest rail-first tourist countries in Europe.
- Station-to-hotel execution usually beats parking and toll anxiety on classic routes.

## Transfer Reality

- Scenic lines are experiences, not shortcuts.
- Some mountain routes need train plus boat plus bus plus lift; protect buffers.
- Last-mile elevation can matter more than pure timetable length.

## Passes and Tickets

- Do not assume a pass automatically wins. Compare it with the actual route, mountain plans, and flexibility.
- Mountain lifts and panoramic trains may still need reservations or supplements.
- If the user hates admin, a simpler ticket strategy can beat theoretical savings.

## Tourist Trap To Flag

Treating every famous Swiss train as mandatory. Many users are happier with one iconic scenic leg plus efficient normal trains for everything else.
