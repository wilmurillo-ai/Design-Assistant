# Regions — Switzerland

## Region Selection Grid

| Region | Best for | Watch-outs |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Zurich and north-east | First trip, easy arrival, museums, lake, day trips | High hotel prices, easy to overday-trip |
| Lucerne and Central Switzerland | Classic postcard balance of city, lake, and mountain excursions | Popular and expensive in peak months |
| Bern and Fribourg | Slow culture, old towns, cheese, lighter tourism pressure | Less dramatic if user expects nonstop alpine scenery |
| Bernese Oberland | Interlaken, Jungfrau region, valley villages, iconic mountain views | Crowds, weather dependence, premium pricing |
| Lake Geneva | Geneva, Lausanne, Montreux, wine, softer climate | Strong urban-hotel pricing and event spikes |
| Valais | Zermatt, Matterhorn, glacier and high-Alps logic | Altitude, seasonal closures, strong winter pricing |
| Graubunden and Engadin | Scenic trains, St. Moritz, Davos, Swiss National Park | Long transfers, shoulder-season gaps |
| Ticino | Palm trees, lakes, Italian-speaking Switzerland, warmer feel | Summer heat, tunnel and holiday traffic |
| Basel and northwest | Art, Rhine, easy tri-border positioning, Jura | Weaker fit if the user wants a mountain-first trip |

## Planning Rule of Thumb

- 4 days: one base
- 7 days: one base plus one contrast base
- 10-14 days: two or three bases maximum

## Transfer Reality

- Scenic-train legs often consume the day and should not be treated like fast transfers.
- Mountain villages can be near on the map and still require multiple timed connections.
- Airport choice strongly changes the best corridor.

## First-Time Route Suggestions

- Classic Switzerland: Zurich or Geneva arrival plus Lucerne or Bernese Oberland
- Scenic rail and Alps: Zurich plus Graubunden or Valais
- Lake and city mix: Lake Geneva plus Bernese Oberland or Lucerne
- Warm-weather south: Zurich or Lucerne backbone plus Ticino
