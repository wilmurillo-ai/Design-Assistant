# Safety and Emergencies — Switzerland

## Core Risk Reality

- Switzerland is generally orderly and low-drama, but mountain risk is real.
- The main tourist dangers are weather exposure, altitude misjudgment, slippery paths, lake or river overconfidence, and rushed transfers.

## Practical Safety Rules

- Use official weather and hazard tools before committing to a summit or exposed hike.
- Do not sell mountain visibility as guaranteed.
- Keep one lower-altitude or indoor fallback for every weather-sensitive day.

## Emergency Basics

- 112 is the broad emergency fallback.
- For acute medical, police, fire, or mountain-rescue needs, route the user to the local official emergency channel immediately.
- If the user is doing serious alpine activity, make sure they know their rescue and insurance exposure before the trip.

## Tourist Trap To Flag

- Treating cable cars and marked paths as proof that all mountain plans are low risk.
- Assuming lake towns are automatically safe for spontaneous swimming in any weather.
- Leaving the last train or lift decision too late after a full mountain day
