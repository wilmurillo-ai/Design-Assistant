# Entry and Documents — Switzerland

## Fast Decision Tree

1. Confirm whether the traveler is visa-free for short Schengen stays or needs a visa.
2. Verify passport validity and whether the trip also includes wider Schengen day-count pressure.
3. If Switzerland is only one stop in a larger Europe trip, check the whole Schengen timeline before locking non-refundable bookings.
4. Verify current EES and ETIAS rollout status close to travel dates instead of assuming they are already live or irrelevant.

## Practical Entry Notes

- Switzerland is in Schengen, so many tourists arrive under standard short-stay Schengen rules.
- Switzerland is not in the EU customs or VAT system, so border and shopping assumptions can differ from neighboring countries.
- Airport arrival is usually friction-light, but mountain transfers still need buffer after long-haul flights.

## Cross-Border Reality

- Trips that combine Switzerland with France, Italy, Germany, or Austria feel easy and still need passport discipline.
- Rental-car permission, train-ticket coverage, and mobile-roaming assumptions can break a border-heavy route even when passport control is minimal.

## Checklist Before Locking Non-Refundables

- Correct visa or visa-free path confirmed
- Passport validity checked for the whole trip
- Wider Schengen day count checked if Europe trip is long
- EES and ETIAS status re-verified near travel
- Border-hop assumptions checked for trains, cars, and phone plans
