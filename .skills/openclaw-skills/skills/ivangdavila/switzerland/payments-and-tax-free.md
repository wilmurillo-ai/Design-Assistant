# Payments and Tax-Free — Switzerland

## Payment Reality

- Cards are widely accepted in cities, stations, and tourist infrastructure.
- Carry a small CHF backup for markets, small kiosks, rural honesty shops, or edge cases.
- Do not assume euro cash is welcomed everywhere, even in border regions.

## Tipping Rule

- Service is usually included, so tipping is modest.
- Small rounding or a light extra amount is normal when service feels strong.
- Do not import a U.S.-style tipping model into Switzerland.

## Tax-Free Shopping Basics

- Tax-free shopping only matters when purchase thresholds and export steps are actually met.
- If the user plans luxury shopping, remind them to confirm paperwork, export validation, and where the refund is processed.
- Border timing matters if the user shops in Switzerland and exits through another country soon after.

## Card and Currency Traps

- Dynamic currency conversion is usually a poor default.
- Border towns and airports tempt users into mixing CHF and EUR decisions badly.
- Bank and card fees can matter more than the tiny cash amount the user is trying to optimize.

## Good Default Advice

- Pay in CHF when card terms are acceptable.
- Keep one card, one backup card, and a small cash reserve.
- Slow down only when the user is doing high-value shopping or repeated cross-border purchases.
