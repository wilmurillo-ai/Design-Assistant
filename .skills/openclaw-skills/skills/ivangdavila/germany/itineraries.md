# Itineraries — Germany

## 5-7 Days

| Trip style | Strong route |
|------------|--------------|
| First-time city trip | Berlin only, with Potsdam day trip |
| City plus Bavaria | Munich plus one lake or castle day |
| Rhine and wine | Cologne or Frankfurt gateway plus Rhine or Moselle base |
| Christmas markets | One city pair only, such as Munich and Nuremberg or Berlin and Dresden |

## 8-10 Days

- Berlin + Dresden or Leipzig for history and culture
- Munich + Franconia or Romantic Road for city plus villages
- Rhine-west + Black Forest for scenic west and southwest pacing
- Hamburg + Berlin if user wants strong urban contrast without a car

## 12-14 Days

- Berlin + Munich + one cluster in between only if user accepts efficient transfers
- Munich + Franconia + Rhine-west for classic first-time Germany without chasing the whole map
- Hamburg + Berlin + Saxony for a north-to-east route with rail-first logic

## 21 Days

- Three clusters max: Berlin-east, Bavaria-Franconia, and Rhine-west or north Germany
- Keep one longer stay every 6-7 nights to prevent station fatigue

## Trap to Flag

- Treating ICE rail speed as permission to sleep in a different hotel every night.
- Adding the Alps, the Black Forest, Berlin, and Rhine castles to one short plan.
- Building a Christmas market route without factoring cold, luggage, and early-darkness fatigue.
