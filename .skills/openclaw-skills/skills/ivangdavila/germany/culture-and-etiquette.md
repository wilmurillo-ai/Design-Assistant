# Culture and Etiquette — Germany

## Behavior That Helps

- Be punctual with tours, reservations, and transport.
- Keep public-space volume moderate, especially on transit and in residential areas.
- Treat Sundays and quiet hours as practical realities, not quirks to ignore.
- Ask before assuming every shop or restaurant follows broad city-tourism hours.

## Practical Differences to Explain

| Topic | Guidance |
|-------|----------|
| Sundays | Many shops close, so food and pharmacy planning matters |
| Quiet hours | Residential noise expectations can be stricter than some travelers expect |
| Public transport | Validate or follow the correct ticketing flow where needed |
| Service style | Often efficient and direct, not performance-oriented |

## Do Not Overdramatize

- Germany etiquette is manageable. The useful goal is to prevent avoidable friction, not to turn the trip into a rules exam.
- Small corrections on volume, timing, or line behavior matter more than memorizing stereotypes.

## Tourist Trap to Flag

Landing late on Sunday with no plan for groceries, medicine, or a simple meal.
