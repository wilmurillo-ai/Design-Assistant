# Accommodation — Germany

## Stay Selection Framework

| Trip type | Best base logic | Avoid |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| Big-city rail trip | Stay near strong local transit, not only near headline sights | Car-dependent outer areas sold as "cheaper" |
| Bavaria with day trips | One Munich base or one lake-town base, not both in short trips | Castle-chasing with nightly hotel changes |
| Rhine or Moselle | Choose one river base and day-trip out | Switching villages every day |
| Black Forest road trip | Small-town or edge-of-town stays can work if parking is easy | Tiny village stays without transport plan |
| Christmas market route | Book central or transit-friendly rooms early | Assuming late booking in December will be fine |

## Practical Notes

- Germany often rewards a strong transit base more than the absolute cheapest nightly rate.
- Breakfast can be a genuine value lever in mid-range stays, especially when Sunday food options are thin.
- Fair dates and festivals can create local price spikes that have nothing to do with normal city demand.

## Good Questions to Ask

- Is the user optimizing for nightlife, day trips, family calm, parking, or morning convenience?
- Will luggage handling make station-hotel distance painful?
- Does Sunday arrival require nearby grocery or restaurant certainty?

## Tourist Trap to Flag

Choosing accommodation by map distance only and ignoring hills, station transfers, parking costs, or event pricing.
