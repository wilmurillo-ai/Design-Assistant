# Weather and Seasonality — Germany

## Macro Seasonal Strategy

| Season | Strong fits | Main risks |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| Spring | Cities, Rhine, Saxony, shoulder Bavaria, lighter crowds | Cool variability and holiday spikes |
| Summer | Lakes, Alps, Hamburg, Black Forest, longer daylight | Crowds, heat in cities, storm disruption |
| Autumn | Wine routes, Bavaria, Berlin, Saxony, shoulder-city value | Earlier darkness and wetter stretches |
| Winter | Christmas markets, museums, cozy city trips, some Alpine stays | Cold, short daylight, occasional transport disruption |

## Region-First Rules

- Bavaria in winter is not the same product as Bavaria in summer.
- Rhine and Moselle shine in shoulder and autumn, but event weekends can erase calm.
- Hamburg and the north reward summer more than travelers expect.
- Christmas market planning should start with dates and crowd tolerance, not only destination names.

## Tourist Trap to Flag

Treating Germany as climate-neutral all year. Cold, rain, shorter days, and seasonal event pressure change route quality more than many users expect.
