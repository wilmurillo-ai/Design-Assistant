# Safety and Emergencies — Germany

## Baseline

- Germany is generally straightforward for tourists, but practical safety still matters: station theft, protest disruptions, winter weather, and event crowds.
- The useful posture is calm and operational, not alarmist.

## Main Friction Points

| Situation | Why it matters |
|-----------|----------------|
| Large stations and tourist cores | Pickpocket and bag-loss patterns |
| Festivals and Christmas markets | Crowds, late-night transport strain, hotel pressure |
| Winter conditions | Delays, icy surfaces, shorter daylight |
| Demonstrations or strikes | Can reshape urban movement or rail plans |

## Emergency Basics

- 112 is the core emergency number for urgent help.
- For ordinary health, pharmacy, or minor disruption questions, users usually need practical local direction rather than emergency escalation.
- Keep hotel address, offline maps, and a charged phone ready for late arrivals.

## Planning Rule

- Build one backup movement option when a day depends on weather, strike risk, or a late-night return.
- For December trips or high-event windows, favor simpler routing and stronger hotel locations.
