# Domestic Transport — Germany

## Mode Selection Framework

| Route type | Default winner | Why |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| Berlin, Hamburg, Munich, Cologne corridor | Rail | Strong intercity links and low city-car value |
| City-only stays | Public transit + walking | Parking and traffic usually lose |
| Romantic Road or village-heavy routes | Car | Flexibility matters more than rail elegance |
| Rhine river towns with one base | Mixed | Rail can cover core moves, car only if user wants deep detours |
| Black Forest scenic loops | Car | Small-town connections can consume too much time |

## Rail Reality

- ICE is powerful, but users still need buffer for connections, platform changes, and separate bookings.
- Some scenic or village-heavy routes look easy on the map yet become awkward without a car.
- The right answer is often hybrid: rail between major clusters, then local rental only where it adds value.

## Local Transit Notes

- Berlin, Hamburg, Munich, and Cologne reward transit-first planning.
- Deutschlandticket can be useful for regional transport logic, but it should not be presented as a universal answer for every intercity trip.

## Airport and Transfer Buffers

- Long-haul arrival plus same-day onward rail is possible, but not with zero margin.
- Separate tickets or late last trains should always be treated as risk-bearing.
- Last night before departure should usually be in the departure city or airport-connected cluster.

## Tourist Trap to Flag

Renting a car for the whole trip when only one scenic segment actually benefits from it.
