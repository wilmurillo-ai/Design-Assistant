# Customs and Border — Germany

## What to Confirm Early

- Customs allowances if arriving from outside the EU
- Cash declaration rules for large amounts
- Food, plant, or animal-product restrictions
- Duty-free assumptions if the trip includes non-EU legs

## Practical Reality

- Germany is easy on paper until travelers mix EU and non-EU legs, especially Switzerland, the UK, or long-haul arrivals.
- Do not tell users to rely on old blog posts for customs allowances or restricted foods.
- If user plans shopping-heavy travel, tell them to verify both import allowances and airline baggage rules.

## High-Friction Cases

| Situation | Why it matters |
|-----------|----------------|
| Carrying large cash amounts | Declaration rules can apply |
| Bringing meat, dairy, or plant products | Restrictions vary by origin |
| Arriving with luxury goods from non-EU trips | Allowances and proof of purchase can matter |
| Entering Germany after Switzerland or UK stops | Customs context is different from pure EU travel |

## Guidance Rule

- For ordinary tourists, keep the advice simple: check official customs pages before departure and avoid carrying questionable food items or undeclared large cash.
- For shopping or high-value gear questions, route to official customs references instead of improvising.
