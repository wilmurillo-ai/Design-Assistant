# Food Guide — Germany

## Food Planning Framework

| Goal | Best region or city fit |
|------|--------------------------|
| Modern city dining | Berlin, Munich, Hamburg |
| Beer-hall and hearty classics | Munich and Bavaria |
| Wine taverns and lighter regional menus | Franconia, Rhine, Moselle |
| Bakery and market culture | Most cities, strongest when mornings are planned |
| Seasonal winter atmosphere | Christmas market cities and colder-weather comfort food zones |

## Practical Advice

- Germany rewards breakfast and bakery planning more than many travelers expect.
- Beer-hall meals, wine taverns, and market food each fit different trip moments; do not flatten them into "German food."
- Big tourist squares are rarely the best value-for-experience food strategy.

## Region Signals

- Bavaria: beer gardens, roast-heavy comfort food, Alpine-day pacing
- Berlin: strongest range, easiest for modern and international dining
- Franconia and the Rhine: better for wine and smaller-town tavern rhythm
- Hamburg and the north: seafood and port-city contrasts

## Tourist Trap to Flag

Eating only near landmarks and leaving bakeries, markets, and neighborhood spots out of the plan.
