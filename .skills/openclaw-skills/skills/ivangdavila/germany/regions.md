# Regions — Germany

## Region Selection Grid

| Region | Best for | Watch-outs |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Berlin and Potsdam | First-time city base, museums, history, nightlife, day trips | Big-city sprawl, event-demand spikes, too many museum bookings |
| Munich and Upper Bavaria | Alpine access, beer gardens, castles, lakes, polished city rhythm | Oktoberfest pressure, premium pricing, airport-distance assumptions |
| Franconia and Romantic Road | Smaller-city charm, medieval towns, wine, scenic driving | Best with a car or slower pacing, tourist-route overstuffing |
| Rhine, Moselle, and west | Castles, wine villages, Cologne, river scenery | Car temptation in city segments, weekend crowd spikes, fair dates |
| Hamburg and the north | Port-city style, maritime feel, easier summer routing | Weather variability, wider spacing for coastal add-ons |
| Black Forest and southwest | Scenic drives, spa towns, villages, Freiburg, lake extensions | Rail is weaker for some loops, village logistics can eat time |
| Saxony and east | Dresden, Leipzig, culture, compact city pairing, quieter value | Less obvious for first-timers, some routes need deliberate planning |

## Planning Rule of Thumb

- 5-7 days: 1 core region, or one city plus one nearby contrast
- 8-12 days: 2 regions with clean rail or road logic
- 14-21 days: 3 regions max, only if transfers are disciplined

## Germany Compression Rule

- Berlin plus Bavaria works if the user accepts one major transfer.
- Bavaria plus Romantic Road works if the user wants castles and smaller towns.
- Rhine-west plus Black Forest works for river, wine, and village scenery.
- Do not casually bolt Hamburg, Berlin, Bavaria, and the Rhine into one short trip.

## First-Time Germany Route Suggestions

- City + castles: Munich and Upper Bavaria + Franconia
- History + nightlife: Berlin + Hamburg
- Wine + river scenery: Rhine or Moselle + west Germany
- Winter atmosphere: Munich, Nuremberg, Dresden, or Cologne depending crowd tolerance
