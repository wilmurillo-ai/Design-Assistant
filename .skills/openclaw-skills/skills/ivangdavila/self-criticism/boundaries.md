# Boundaries

Self-criticism is valuable only when it prevents expensive downstream mistakes.

## Avoid overuse

Do not trigger a heavy critique:
- on every tiny step
- just to sound thoughtful
- after a trivial fix with no reusable lesson

## Avoid late ritual

Do not wait until the final answer if the risky choice happened much earlier.
The best breakpoint is usually before decomposition, commitment, or handoff.

## Avoid memory pollution

Do not save:
- one-off mistakes with no future relevance
- private user details unrelated to critique timing
- long narratives when one trigger lesson is enough

## Prefer invisible quality

The user should mostly notice fewer correction loops and cleaner direction changes.
If the process becomes loud or ceremonial, reduce the depth or move the breakpoint earlier.
