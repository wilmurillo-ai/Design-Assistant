# Strategy Selection and Return Targets

Use this before underwriting specific properties. The user should know what game they are playing before asking whether a given deal is good.

## Match strategy to reality

Compare the common paths:

| Strategy | Best for | Capital need | Time load | Main upside | Main risk |
|----------|----------|--------------|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Buy-and-hold rental | durable cash flow and long-term equity | medium | medium | stable compounding | weak rentability after real expenses |
| Value-add | investors who can improve operations or finish deferred work | medium-high | high | forced NOI growth | rehab, tenant, and timeline drag |
| BRRRR | operators who can recycle capital | medium-high | high | capital velocity | refinance or appraisal failure |
| Flip | short hold, fast execution | high | very high | lump-sum profit | thin margin after delays and selling costs |
| House hack | owner-occupants willing to trade comfort for leverage | low-medium | medium | lower entry barrier | mixed personal and investment goals |
| Short-term rental | hospitality-oriented operators | medium | high | higher gross income | regulation, volatility, and management intensity |

## Return targets

Set explicit minimums before looking at listings:
- minimum monthly cash flow after reserves
- minimum cash-on-cash return
- minimum DSCR
- maximum break-even occupancy
- minimum equity-creation path or forced-appreciation case

If the user cannot define target returns yet, at least define what "good enough" means:
- stable passive income
- aggressive growth
- learning with controlled downside
- tax efficiency
- capital recycling

## Good strategy questions

- Does the user want a durable asset or a project?
- Is the real bottleneck capital, time, deal flow, contractor depth, or financing?
- Is the priority rentability now or equity growth later?
- What failure would hurt most: trapped cash, negative monthly carry, long rehab, or bad tenant operations?

## Output standard

Before full deal analysis, say:
- chosen strategy
- why it fits the user's capital and bandwidth
- what success looks like
- what metrics matter most for this strategy
- what would make a different strategy smarter
