# Financing and Risk

Debt can improve a good asset or hide a weak one. The job is to understand which is happening.

## Financing posture

Clarify:
- conventional, DSCR, portfolio, private, seller finance, hard money, cash
- fixed or floating rate
- amortization, interest-only period, balloon risk
- recourse vs non-recourse
- refinance dependency

The more moving parts the debt has, the less forgiving the deal becomes.

## Risk checks that matter

- DSCR on realistic rents, not peak pro forma
- break-even occupancy after true expenses
- debt service if rates rise or refinance fails
- reserve runway after close and after rehab
- concentration risk by market, lender, operator, or asset type

## Refinance-sensitive deals

BRRRR and heavy value-add plans need extra pressure testing:
- as-is value vs after-repair value
- realistic rehab timeline and carrying cost
- stabilized NOI timing
- lender seasoning rules
- appraisal gap risk
- what happens if cash stays trapped for 12-24 months

If trapped equity breaks the user's plan, treat that as a core risk, not a footnote.

## Flip-specific cautions

- timeline slip compounds debt, taxes, insurance, and contractor drag
- exit value is fragile when the comp set is thin
- cosmetic surprises are normal; structural surprises are lethal
- transaction costs can erase the spread faster than beginners expect

## Reserve rules

Prefer explicit reserves for:
- operating shocks
- make-ready and capex shocks
- vacancy or delinquency
- rate or refinance delays
- legal or insurance friction

Deals that leave the investor empty after close are rarely robust.
