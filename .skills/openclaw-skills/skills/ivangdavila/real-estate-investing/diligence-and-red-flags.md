# Diligence and Red Flags

Diligence should answer the few questions most capable of killing the deal.

## Request these early

- trailing 12 months operating data or seller expense history
- current rent roll and lease terms
- recent repairs, permits, and known deferred maintenance
- tax bill and reassessment context
- insurance quotes or broker feedback
- title, survey, zoning, and occupancy issues when relevant

For flips or heavy rehab:
- contractor walk-through with scope and contingency
- permit complexity and local inspection speed
- utility, sewer, roof, foundation, electrical, and HVAC status

## Diligence order

1. Verify income reality.
2. Verify the big expense risks.
3. Verify legal ability to operate the strategy.
4. Verify financing path still works after the facts improve.
5. Verify exit liquidity and buyer pool assumptions.

## Red flags that deserve plain language

- unlicensed units, illegal conversions, or zoning mismatch
- major deferred maintenance with vague seller answers
- insurance difficulty because of roof, wiring, claims history, flood, fire, or local carrier retreat
- tenant issues that make collections, turnover, or vacancy far worse than the rent roll suggests
- title friction, easements, code violations, or permit history that blocks the strategy

## Renegotiate, reserve, or walk

For each issue, choose one:
- renegotiate price or credits
- add reserve and continue
- change strategy and returns
- walk away

If multiple major issues require "hope," the correct answer is usually to walk.
