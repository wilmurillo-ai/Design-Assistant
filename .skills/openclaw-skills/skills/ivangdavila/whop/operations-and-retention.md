# Operations and Retention — Whop

Use this file when the user is already selling and needs tighter execution after the click.

## Read Operations as Revenue Protection

Whop's business docs expose the operational surfaces that most directly affect retention:

- Analytics
- Manage users
- Support chats
- Refunds
- Resolution Center
- Disputes
- Team management

Treat these as part of the funnel, not admin overhead.

## Analytics

Whop's built-in analytics cover user analytics, payments analytics, and Resolution Center analytics.

Use analytics to answer:

- Which products are growing or stalling
- Which buyers are converting and sticking
- Whether payment issues are increasing
- Whether support or dispute patterns are eroding trust

Start with the built-in dashboard before reaching for SQL stats or exports.

## Member Operations

When a user reports a member issue, inspect in this order:

1. Product and plan they bought
2. Checkout path used
3. Payment outcome
4. Access or entitlement state
5. Support or refund history

This keeps the team from confusing support noise with a real billing or access problem.

## Refunds and Disputes

Refunds and disputes are not just finance tasks. They reveal:

- Misaligned expectations
- Weak onboarding
- Poor fit traffic
- Confusing pricing or renewal terms

Track patterns by product, channel, and promise. If one channel produces noisy refunds, fix that acquisition source before increasing volume.

## Support Chats

Support should be close to the revenue loop:

- Save repeated objections
- Turn repeated questions into better product copy or onboarding
- Identify where waitlists, trials, or discounts are attracting the wrong buyers

If support load spikes after a campaign, the campaign likely changed traffic quality or promise alignment.

## Weekly Operating Rhythm

- Review user and payment analytics
- Check support and refund patterns
- Compare channels by conversion quality, not just top-line volume
- Tighten product copy, pricing, or onboarding based on what the data says
- Only then add more traffic or new automation
