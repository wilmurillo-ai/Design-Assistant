# Offers and Checkouts — Whop

This is the default file for most Whop work. Start here when the user wants to make money on Whop, improve conversion, or launch something new.

## Offer Design Order

Work through the funnel in this order:

1. Product: what is included and who it is for
2. Pricing: free, one-time, recurring, or split-payment
3. Friction: instant access, free trial, or waitlist
4. Checkout path: direct checkout link, embedded checkout, or partner-specific path

Do not jump into ads or automation until this stack is coherent.

## Products First

Whop's business docs center the offer around products and included apps. Use product reviews to tighten:

- Title and description clarity
- Which apps or entitlements are included
- Whether separate price points deserve separate products
- Whether a new billing option is better than editing an existing one

If the user has one product serving multiple very different buyers, split the offer before scaling traffic.

## Pricing Modes

The docs expose these practical checkout shapes:

- Free checkout links
- One-time payment links
- Subscription or recurring links
- Split-payment checkout links

Pick based on buyer trust and payback speed:

- One-time works when the value is delivered quickly and clearly
- Recurring works when access or outcomes continue over time
- Split payments reduce sticker shock for higher-ticket offers
- Free links only work when downstream upsell or qualification is deliberate

## Free Trials

Whop supports free trials that grant temporary access and then automatically charge the standard price when the trial ends.

Use trials when:

- The product becomes sticky after hands-on use
- Activation can happen quickly inside the trial window
- The user has a plan to prevent low-intent trial abuse

Avoid trials when the onboarding is weak or the product takes too long to deliver a first win.

## Waitlists

Whop supports waitlists where applicants join first, then you approve or deny them later.

Use a waitlist when:

- Qualification matters more than volume
- Capacity is limited
- The offer needs manual review
- You want application answers before admitting people

Avoid a waitlist when the real problem is that the offer is still unclear.

## Checkout Link Strategy

The dashboard supports creating and editing checkout links. Use separate links for separate intents:

- Main direct-purchase link
- Content or social link
- Affiliate-specific link
- Campaign-specific discount path
- High-ticket or split-payment path

This keeps attribution and conversion debugging cleaner than reusing one generic link everywhere.

## Offer Audit Checklist

- Is the product promise obvious in one sentence?
- Does the pricing model fit the value cadence?
- Is friction intentional: direct, trial, or waitlist?
- Does each acquisition source have a matching checkout path?
- Is there a clean next step after purchase?

If any answer is weak, fix that before buying more traffic.
