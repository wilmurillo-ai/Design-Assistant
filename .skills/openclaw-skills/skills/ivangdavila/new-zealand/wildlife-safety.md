# Wildlife and Outdoor Safety — New Zealand

## Core Reality

New Zealand is safer than many wilderness destinations in terms of dangerous animals, but the main risks are weather, water, terrain, and underestimation.

## Main Outdoor Risks

| Risk | Reality |
|------|---------|
| Surf and rip currents | Bigger danger than animals |
| Fast weather changes | Common in alpine and Fiordland areas |
| Cold water | Can become serious quickly |
| Driving fatigue | Major road-trip risk |
| Sandflies | Severe annoyance in West Coast and Fiordland |
| Wasps | Seasonal issue in some forest areas |

## Wildlife Respect Rules

- Stay back from seals and sea lions.
- Do not feed kea, birds, or marine animals.
- Keep distance from penguin viewing areas.
- Follow local DOC signs instead of trying to get the perfect photo.

## Practical Outdoor Rules

1. The absence of snakes does not mean the outdoors is low-risk.
2. Bring layers even on sunny days.
3. Carry water, snacks, and a charged phone or offline map.
4. If visibility turns poor, simplify the plan.

## Sandfly Strategy

- Especially relevant on the West Coast, Fiordland, and some lake edges
- Carry repellent
- Do not linger outside the car after wet weather with no protection
