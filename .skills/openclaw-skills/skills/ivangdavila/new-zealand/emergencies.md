# Emergencies and Safety — New Zealand

## Emergency Numbers

| Need | Number |
|------|--------|
| Emergency police, fire, ambulance | 111 |
| Non-emergency police | 105 |
| Health advice by phone | 0800 611 116 |

## What 111 Is For

Use 111 for:
- serious medical emergencies
- fire
- active danger
- road crashes with injuries
- urgent rescue situations

## Health Basics

- Pharmacies are good first stop for minor issues.
- After-hours clinics exist in bigger towns and cities.
- Travel insurance matters if the user is not covered locally.
- Sunburn, dehydration, and motion fatigue are common avoidable problems.

## Outdoor Safety

- Check weather before hikes and long drives.
- Surf conditions can be dangerous even when the beach looks calm.
- Fiordland, alpine roads, and exposed tracks need conservative decisions.

## If Something Goes Wrong on a Road Trip

1. Move to safety first
2. Call 111 if there are injuries or active danger
3. Use rental-car roadside support after immediate safety is handled
4. Rework the itinerary instead of trying to "make up time"

## Common Emergency Friction Points

- poor signal in remote areas
- underestimating cold and rain
- driving tired after flights
- no backup plan when ferries or weather disrupt a key leg
