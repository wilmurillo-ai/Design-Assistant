# Hiking and Track Safety — New Zealand

## Main Rule

New Zealand hiking is excellent because nature is real, not curated. That means weather, terrain, and booking logistics matter.

## Best Hike Types

| Type | Good Choices |
|------|--------------|
| Easy scenic walk | Hooker Valley Track, Mount Maunganui base track, Lake Matheson loop |
| Big day hike | Tongariro Alpine Crossing, Roys Peak, Mueller Hut track section |
| Multi-day classic | Great Walks such as Milford, Routeburn, Abel Tasman Coast Track |
| Half-day alpine feel | Key Summit, Isthmus Peak, Ben Lomond lower sections |

## Booking Logic

- Great Walk huts and campsites book early for peak season.
- Shoulder season needs extra weather caution, not less planning.
- Do not treat DOC huts like casual walk-ins in high season.

## Safety Rules

1. Check forecast and track alerts before leaving.
2. Carry layers, rain protection, food, and water even on "easy" trails.
3. Start early for major day hikes.
4. Turn around if conditions deteriorate.
5. For Tongariro and alpine walks, ask about fitness and weather tolerance before recommending.

## Hike Matching

| User | Best Starting Point |
|------|---------------------|
| First-time hiker | Hooker Valley, Abel Tasman sections, Key Summit |
| Fit one-day challenge | Tongariro, Roys Peak |
| Serious outdoors traveler | Great Walks or tailored multi-day routes |
| Family | Short DOC walks, geothermal loops, lakefront tracks |

## Common Mistakes

- Packing for a city walk instead of an alpine day.
- Starting Tongariro late.
- Ignoring wind, temperature drop, or shuttle timing.
- Confusing social-media popularity with beginner-friendliness.
