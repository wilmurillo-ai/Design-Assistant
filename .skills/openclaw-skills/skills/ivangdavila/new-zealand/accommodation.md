# Accommodation Strategy — New Zealand

## Main Rule

Pick accommodation by base logic, not by "best hotel in the country" lists.

## Best Bases by Trip Style

| Trip Style | Best Bases | Why |
|------------|------------|-----|
| First trip, mixed interests | Rotorua, Wellington, Christchurch, Queenstown | Easy activity range |
| Beaches and summer | Bay of Islands, Coromandel, Mount Maunganui, Abel Tasman | Better coastal rhythm |
| Hiking | Te Anau, Wanaka, Aoraki area, National Park Village | Early starts matter |
| Food and wine | Wellington, Waiheke, Hawke's Bay, Marlborough, Central Otago | Strong dining plus tasting access |
| Family | Rotorua, Taupo, Christchurch, Nelson | Easier pacing, practical stays |
| Budget road trip | Motels, holiday parks, cabins, simpler town-edge stays | Parking and laundry help |

## What Works Best in New Zealand

### Motels
- Very common and often the most practical choice
- Good for parking, late arrivals, and road trips

### Holiday parks
- Great for families, campervan routes, and budget-conscious travelers
- Can range from basic cabins to resort-style sites

### Apartments
- Best in Auckland, Wellington, Queenstown, Christchurch
- Useful for longer stays or bad-weather days

### Lodges and luxury stays
- Strong in Queenstown, Hawke's Bay, Marlborough, and top-end wilderness areas
- Often require earlier booking than visitors expect

## Booking Rules

1. Book Queenstown, Te Anau, and Milford-adjacent nights early for summer.
2. If using the Interislander or Bluebridge ferry, book the ferry before locking the nearby night.
3. On road trips, prefer 2-night bases over too many one-night hops.
4. In small towns, choose parking convenience over theoretical centrality.

## Where to Stay Inside the Main Hubs

### Auckland
- **Britomart/Commercial Bay:** best for short stay and ferries
- **Ponsonby:** food and nightlife
- **Takapuna/Devonport:** calmer waterfront feel

### Wellington
- **Te Aro/Cuba Street:** best all-round base
- **Waterfront:** easy and polished
- **Thorndon:** quieter, government side, less nightlife

### Queenstown
- **Town center:** walkable, expensive, noisy
- **Frankton:** practical, better parking, airport access
- **Arrowtown:** slower and more charming if driving

### Christchurch
- **CBD/Riverside:** easiest without a car
- **Merivale:** polished, food-friendly
- **Sumner:** beach feel if staying longer

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Booking only one-night stops across the South Island.
- Staying far outside Queenstown to save money, then burning the savings on driving and parking.
- Treating holiday parks as backpacker-only; many are excellent for families.
- Ignoring laundry, kitchen, and parking needs on longer self-drive trips.
