# Seasonality and Climate Strategy — New Zealand

## Core Reality

New Zealand seasons are opposite to Europe and North America, and weather varies sharply by island and terrain.

## Seasonal Pattern

| Season | Months | Best For | Watch Out For |
|--------|--------|----------|---------------|
| Summer | Dec-Feb | Beaches, hiking, road trips | Peak prices, booking pressure, holiday traffic |
| Autumn | Mar-May | Wine, road trips, stable shoulder travel | Shorter daylight in late autumn |
| Winter | Jun-Aug | Ski, hot pools, low-season cities | Frost, alpine roads, short days |
| Spring | Sep-Nov | Shoulder value, wildflowers, mixed itineraries | Wind, changeable weather, snow lingering up high |

## Regional Differences

- **Northland/Bay of Islands:** warmest feel, strongest summer demand
- **Wellington:** can be windy any time, especially spring
- **West Coast and Fiordland:** high rainfall year-round
- **Central Otago and Mackenzie:** big temperature swings, cold nights
- **Tongariro and alpine South Island:** snow and ice can affect access outside deep winter too

## Best Timing by Goal

| Goal | Best Window |
|------|-------------|
| First-trip road trip | Feb-Mar or Nov |
| Great Walks and day hikes | Jan-Mar, then late spring depending on track |
| Wine trip | Mar-Apr |
| Ski trip | Jul-Sep |
| Low-crowd city plus nature | May or Oct |

## Planning Rules

1. Add one weather-flex day for Fiordland-heavy trips.
2. If the user wants beaches and alpine hiking, ask which matters more.
3. In winter, shorten driving days and plan for early sunset.
4. In summer, book Queenstown, Abel Tasman, Bay of Islands, and ferry legs earlier than visitors expect.
