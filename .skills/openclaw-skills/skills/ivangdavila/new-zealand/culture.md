# Culture, Etiquette, and Expectations — New Zealand

## General Vibe

New Zealand is relaxed, practical, and outdoors-oriented. People are usually helpful, but they dislike pushiness and performative tourist behavior.

## Cultural Basics

- **Maori place names matter.** Make a reasonable effort with pronunciation.
- **Treat Treaty and Maori topics with respect.** Curiosity is fine; lazy stereotypes are not.
- **No tipping culture.** Good service is appreciated, not tipped by default.
- **Dress is casual.** Even good restaurants are less formal than many visitors expect.
- **People start early.** Cafes open early, some kitchens close earlier than big-city visitors expect.

## Food and Cafe Culture

- Coffee quality is high almost everywhere.
- Flat whites are standard, not exotic.
- Farmers markets and brunch culture are stronger than late-night dining in many towns.
- In small towns, dinner booking windows can feel early.

## Practical Etiquette

- Take muddy boots off when appropriate indoors.
- Do not feed wildlife.
- Respect private farmland and signed access rules.
- Pack out rubbish on walks and beaches.
- If locals say the weather can turn fast, believe them.

## Tourist Behavior That Reads Poorly

- Demanding alpine or surf plans after being told conditions are unsafe.
- Treating cultural performances as theme park content instead of hosted experiences.
- Driving aggressively on unfamiliar roads and holding up traffic without pulling over.
- Asking for constant customizations in busy small-town cafes.
