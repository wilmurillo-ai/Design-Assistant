# Beaches and Coastal Planning — New Zealand

## Core Rule

New Zealand beaches are beautiful but not automatically safe. Wind, surf, rips, and cold water matter.

## Best Beach Areas by Style

| Style | Best Picks |
|-------|------------|
| Easy family beach | Mount Maunganui, Orewa, Kaiteriteri, Sumner |
| Big surf scenery | Piha, Muriwai, Raglan |
| Warm-weather classic | Bay of Islands, Coromandel, Mount Maunganui |
| Golden-sand South Island | Abel Tasman coast, Kaiteriteri |
| Island beach day | Waiheke |

## Beach Notes

### Piha and west-coast Auckland beaches
- spectacular, wild, and not beginner-water
- great scenery, not casual-swim logic

### Mount Maunganui
- one of the easiest all-round beach towns
- good for families, cafes, and a short summit walk

### Coromandel
- iconic summer New Zealand feel
- road traffic is the main downside in peak periods

### Abel Tasman
- best paired with kayak or water taxi and short walks
- water and weather usually feel gentler than exposed west coast beaches

## Safety Rules

1. Swim between the flags when surf lifesavers are present.
2. Assume west-coast surf is stronger than it looks.
3. Use more sunscreen than you think you need.
4. Wind can turn a pleasant beach stop into a short one very quickly.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Treating all beaches as relaxed Mediterranean swimming beaches.
- Taking children into exposed surf zones because locals are nearby.
- Planning Cathedral Cove-type stops without checking current access conditions.
