# Nelson, Abel Tasman, and Marlborough

## Why This Region Is High Value

The top of the South is one of the easiest places to combine:
- sunshine
- beaches
- kayaking and coastal walks
- wine and seafood
- a lower-stress pace than Queenstown

## Best Base Logic

| Base | Best For |
|------|----------|
| Nelson | city comforts plus Abel Tasman access |
| Kaiteriteri / Motueka side | beach-first Abel Tasman access |
| Blenheim | Marlborough wine focus |
| Picton | ferry logistics and sounds access |

## Abel Tasman Strategy

- Works best with 2-3 nights in the region
- Best combinations are water taxi plus walk, or kayak plus short walk
- Strong family and couple destination in good weather

## Marlborough Strategy

- Best for Sauvignon Blanc and seafood
- Easy after or before the Wellington ferry
- Better for slower wine travelers than for pure nightlife seekers

## Who Should Prioritize This Region

- summer travelers
- food and wine travelers
- people who want nature without nonstop alpine intensity
- families

## Who Might Skip It on First Trip

- users with only 7-8 days focused on classic South Island scenery
- users prioritizing Queenstown, Aoraki, and Fiordland over coastal softness

## Common Mistakes

- Trying to rush in and out on ferry timing alone
- Doing Abel Tasman as a one-night stop with no weather flexibility
- Building a self-drive day that includes ferry, wine tasting, and long transfer all together
