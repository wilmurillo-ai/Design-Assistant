# Wellington — Complete Guide

## Why Wellington Punches Above Its Size

Wellington is the strongest compact city stop in New Zealand for:
- food and coffee
- museums and culture
- walkability
- short stays with real character

## How Long to Stay

| Trip Type | Good Stay |
|-----------|-----------|
| Transit stop only | 1 night |
| Proper city stay | 2 nights |
| City plus wine day trip | 3 nights |

## Best Areas to Stay

### Te Aro / Cuba Street
- best all-round base
- walkable, lively, lots of dining

### Waterfront
- polished and easy
- good for short first-timer stays

### Thorndon
- quieter and practical
- better for government side and station access

## Best Things to Do

- **Te Papa** is worth real time, not just a quick hour
- Walk the **waterfront** and orient around it
- Spend time around **Cuba Street** for food and bar density
- Ride or walk up to **Mount Victoria** viewpoints
- Visit **Zealandia** if the user likes conservation and wildlife
- Add **Weta Workshop** only if film interest is real

## Food and Drink

Wellington is the easiest city for a compact food trip:
- strong cafe culture
- craft beer and natural wine density
- easy bar-hopping without needing a car

## Best Add-On

### Martinborough
- realistic wine day trip
- works best with a driver or organized transport
- good extension if the user has 3 nights in Wellington

## Weather Reality

- Wind is not a joke here
- Build indoor/outdoor flexibility
- A windy Wellington day can still be good if the plan is compact

## Common Wellington Mistakes

- Treating it as just a ferry terminal
- Overpacking the day with transit to far suburbs
- Ignoring weather and booking one exposed plan only
