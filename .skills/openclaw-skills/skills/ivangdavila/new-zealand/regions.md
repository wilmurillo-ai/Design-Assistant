# Regions and Route Logic — New Zealand

## The Main Planning Truth

New Zealand works best when you choose a shape, not a bucket list.

## Route Zones at a Glance

| Zone | Best For | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Northland and Bay of Islands | Beaches, sailing, slower summer routes | Warmest feel, weak public transport |
| Auckland and Waiheke | Gateway city, food, short urban-plus-island stays | Better as start/end than main event |
| Coromandel and Bay of Plenty | Beaches, road trip, summer feel | Holiday traffic spikes hard |
| Rotorua, Taupo, and Central Plateau | Geothermal, Maori culture, mountain access | Strong family and mixed-interest base |
| Hawke's Bay and East Coast | Wine, art deco, sunshine | Great second-trip region |
| Wellington and Wairarapa | Food, culture, compact city, wine day trips | Windy but high-value short stay |
| Nelson, Abel Tasman, Marlborough | Kayak, beaches, wine, sunshine | Excellent shoulder-season value |
| Canterbury and Mackenzie | Christchurch gateway, alpine lakes, Aoraki | Strong South Island backbone |
| West Coast | Rainforest, glaciers, wild scenery | Slow drives, weather matters |
| Queenstown and Central Otago | Adventure, Pinot, alpine scenery | Expensive, book ahead |
| Fiordland and Southland | Milford, Doubtful, deep nature | Use Te Anau as practical base |

## North Island vs South Island

| Island | Better For |
|--------|------------|
| North Island | First trip with mixed interests, culture, geothermal areas, easier winter driving |
| South Island | Scenery-first trips, alpine lakes, hiking, road trips, adventure |

## Strong Route Shapes

### 1. North Island Classic
Auckland -> Rotorua -> Taupo or Tongariro -> Wellington

Best for:
- first-timers
- families
- mixed culture plus nature

### 2. South Island Scenic
Christchurch -> Tekapo or Aoraki -> Queenstown -> Te Anau or Wanaka

Best for:
- scenery-first travelers
- photographers
- short high-impact road trips

### 3. Top of the South
Wellington -> ferry -> Marlborough -> Nelson -> Abel Tasman

Best for:
- summer shoulder
- food and wine
- lower-rush trips

## Regions That Need More Time Than People Expect

- **Fiordland:** Long access days, weather-driven activities
- **Coromandel:** Roads are bendy and slower than maps suggest
- **West Coast:** Scenic but transfer-heavy
- **Bay of Islands:** Better with a car and at least 2-3 nights

## Good First-Trip Logic

If the user has:
- **5-7 days:** one island only
- **8-10 days:** strong South Island or compact North Island
- **11-14 days:** both islands only if one island is kept light
- **15+ days:** both islands can work without feeling rushed
