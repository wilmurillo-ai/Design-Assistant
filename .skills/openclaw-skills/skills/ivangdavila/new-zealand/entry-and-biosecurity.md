# Entry and Biosecurity Planning — New Zealand

## Core Principle

New Zealand arrival is smooth if you prepare, annoying if you improvise. Confirm entry pathway and biosecurity obligations before booking non-refundable domestic pieces.

## What to Check Early

- Passport validity and nationality-specific entry rules
- Whether the traveler needs an NZeTA or visa
- International Visitor Conservation and Tourism Levy obligations
- New Zealand Traveller Declaration timing
- Medication paperwork if carrying prescriptions
- Hiking boots, camping gear, and food declaration risk

## Biosecurity Reality

New Zealand takes border biosecurity seriously.

Declare when relevant:
- food, snacks, fruit, seeds, honey, meat, dairy
- hiking boots with soil or seeds
- camping, fishing, and outdoor gear
- animal products

If gear is dirty, clean it before flying. "I forgot" is a bad strategy here.

## Practical Rules

1. Keep digital and offline copies of passport, approvals, insurance, and bookings.
2. Do not promise same-day domestic chains after landing if the user still has entry uncertainty.
3. Tell users to re-check official immigration pages before paying for flights or campervans.
4. If the user is arriving for hiking or camping, ask what gear they are carrying.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Assuming old NZeTA or declaration rules are unchanged.
- Packing food casually for the flight and deciding later whether to declare it.
- Landing after a long-haul flight and driving immediately to Rotorua, Tongariro, or beyond.
- Booking ferries and alpine lodges before border and arrival steps are confirmed.
