# Payments and Cost Planning — New Zealand

## Payments

- Cards are accepted almost everywhere.
- Contactless is common.
- Some places add a small surcharge for credit or tap payments.
- Cash matters less than in many countries, but a little helps in edge cases.

## Cost Reality

New Zealand is usually not cheap once you add transport and activities.

## Where Budgets Get Hit

| Category | Watch Out For |
|----------|---------------|
| Car rental or campervan | Peak season pricing and one-way fees |
| Ferries | Vehicle plus passenger cost |
| Queenstown and Fiordland | Accommodation and tours |
| Domestic flights | School holidays and late booking |
| Great Walks and premium activities | Low inventory |
| Dining in tourist towns | Convenience premiums |

## Good Value Patterns

- Bakeries and supermarkets help a lot on road trips
- Motels and holiday parks often beat trendier boutique stays on value
- Wellington and Christchurch can offer better dining value than Queenstown
- Shoulder season improves both price and crowd pressure

## Tipping

Not expected. Round up only if the user really wants to.

## Budget Framing

### Budget-conscious
- motels, holiday parks, bakery lunches, self-drive discipline

### Mid-range
- 2-night bases, a few paid excursions, good casual dining

### Higher-end
- wine lodges, scenic flights, premium Fiordland and Queenstown experiences

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Comparing only hotel rates and forgetting car, fuel, ferry, and activity costs
- Leaving Queenstown summer booking too late
- Assuming "simple road trip country" means low total spend
