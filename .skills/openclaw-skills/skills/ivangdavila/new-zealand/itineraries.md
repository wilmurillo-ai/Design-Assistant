# Core Itineraries — New Zealand

## 7 Days — North Island First Trip

### Route
1. Auckland — 1 night
2. Rotorua — 2 nights
3. Taupo or Tongariro area — 2 nights
4. Wellington — 2 nights

### Why it works
- minimal flight dependence
- good food plus culture mix
- geothermal and mountain contrast

## 7 Days — South Island Highlights

### Route
1. Christchurch — 1 night
2. Tekapo or Aoraki area — 1-2 nights
3. Queenstown or Wanaka — 3 nights
4. Te Anau — 1 night

### Why it works
- highest scenery density
- easy first South Island loop
- room for one big nature day

## 10 Days — South Island Without Rushing Too Hard

### Route
1. Christchurch — 1 night
2. Tekapo — 1 night
3. Aoraki/Mount Cook — 1 night
4. Wanaka — 2 nights
5. Queenstown — 3 nights
6. Te Anau — 2 nights

### Use this for
- hiking and viewpoints
- first-time drivers
- wine and adventure mix

## 14 Days — Both Islands, Practical Version

### Route
1. Auckland — 2 nights
2. Rotorua — 2 nights
3. Wellington — 2 nights
4. Ferry to Picton / Marlborough — 1 night
5. Nelson or Abel Tasman — 2 nights
6. Christchurch — 1 night
7. Queenstown or Wanaka — 3 nights
8. Te Anau — 1 night

### Why it works
- only one island transfer
- not every stop is one night
- keeps South Island impact high

## 21 Days — Full New Zealand

### Route Logic
- Northland or Waiheke optional at the start
- Rotorua and central plateau for North Island depth
- Wellington as cultural hinge
- ferry into top of the South
- Abel Tasman or Marlborough
- Christchurch to Mackenzie
- Wanaka, Queenstown, Fiordland, and one deep-south choice

### Key rule
Even with 3 weeks, do not add every famous stop. Keep at least 2 nights in major bases.
