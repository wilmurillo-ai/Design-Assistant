# Queenstown — Complete Guide

## What Queenstown Is Actually Best At

Queenstown is the easiest high-impact New Zealand base for:
- alpine scenery
- adventure activities
- wineries
- premium short trips

It is also one of the easiest places to overspend.

## How Long to Stay

| Trip Type | Good Stay |
|-----------|-----------|
| First trip | 3 nights |
| Adventure plus wine | 4 nights |
| Ski trip | 4-7 nights |

## Where to Stay

### Town center
- easiest without a car
- lively but expensive and noisy

### Frankton
- practical, cheaper, airport-friendly
- good for self-drive trips

### Arrowtown
- excellent if the user wants charm and is driving
- slower evening scene

## Best Things to Do

- Gondola and viewpoint if the user wants easy scenery
- **Gibbston** wine day
- **Arrowtown** for a softer half day
- **Glenorchy** drive for scenery
- One big activity: boat, heli, canyon, ski, or hike depending on season

## Food and Drink

- Book ahead in peak periods
- Famous spots like **Fergburger** are fine if the user accepts the queue and times it well
- Better overall dining rhythm comes from mixing town-center spots with winery lunches and Arrowtown meals

## Best Day Trips

| Day Trip | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| Arrowtown | easy and high reward |
| Gibbston | best for wine |
| Glenorchy | scenery-first |
| Te Anau / Milford | possible, but better as separate base if Fiordland matters a lot |

## What to Watch

- summer and ski-season pricing
- limited availability for better stays
- weather-sensitive premium activities

## Common Queenstown Mistakes

- Using Queenstown as the only South Island base
- Doing too many adrenaline bookings back-to-back
- Staying too far away to save money, then losing time and flexibility
