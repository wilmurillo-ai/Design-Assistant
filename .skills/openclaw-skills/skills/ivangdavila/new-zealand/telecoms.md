# Phone and Internet — New Zealand

## Main Providers

| Provider | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| One NZ | Strong mainstream option |
| Spark | Broad coverage and easy tourist compatibility |
| 2degrees | Often good value in main corridors |

## What to Expect

- Coverage is good in cities and major towns.
- Coverage gets patchy fast in remote South Island, West Coast, Fiordland, and some Northland or East Cape stretches.
- Do not rely on live signal for outdoor navigation.

## Best Practices

1. Use eSIM or local SIM before major road-trip days.
2. Download offline maps.
3. Screenshot boarding passes, ferry confirmations, and hut bookings.
4. If the user works remotely, steer them toward city bases or proven regional towns, not deep-nature lodges.

## Wi-Fi Reality

- Hotels, motels, and cafes usually have workable Wi-Fi.
- Holiday parks vary.
- Scenic remote stays may have weaker speeds than urban travelers expect.
