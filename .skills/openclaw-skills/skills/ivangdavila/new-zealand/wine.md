# Wine Regions and Tasting Logic — New Zealand

## Main Wine Regions

| Region | Best Known For | Best Base |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| Marlborough | Sauvignon Blanc | Blenheim or Picton side trip |
| Central Otago | Pinot Noir | Queenstown, Arrowtown, Cromwell, Wanaka |
| Hawke's Bay | Syrah, Bordeaux blends, Chardonnay | Napier or Havelock North |
| Martinborough | Pinot Noir | Wellington day trip or overnight |
| Waiheke Island | Syrah, Bordeaux blends, views | Day trip from Auckland or island stay |
| North Canterbury/Waipara | Riesling, Pinot Noir | Christchurch side trip |

## Best Wine Trip Styles

### 1. Easy First-Timer
Waiheke or Martinborough

### 2. Food Plus Wine
Hawke's Bay or Marlborough

### 3. Scenic Wine Trip
Central Otago

## Tasting Rules

- Book ahead in peak season and weekends.
- Do not stack too many cellar doors in one day; 3 is enough.
- In rural regions, designate a driver or book transport.
- Winery lunches are often the real highlight.

## Region Notes

### Marlborough
- Best added after Wellington ferry or before Nelson
- Strong for white wine focus
- Combine with seafood and sounds scenery

### Central Otago
- Good even for non-experts because the scenery is part of the value
- Gibbston is easiest from Queenstown
- Bannockburn and Cromwell make sense if driving

### Hawke's Bay
- Easier pace than Queenstown
- Great for couples and shoulder season
- Napier art deco plus Havelock North makes a strong base pair

### Waiheke
- Best if the user wants a soft-landing Auckland day
- Use ferry, do not bring a car unless there is a clear reason
