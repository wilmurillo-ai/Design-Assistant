# Transport Guide — New Zealand

## Main Rule

Transport choice shapes the trip. New Zealand rewards realistic routing more than heroic ambition.

## Driving

### Best For
- South Island scenic loops
- Coromandel and Northland
- Fiordland
- mixed winery and nature routes

### Reality
- Drive on the left
- Roads are usually one lane each way
- Average speed is lower than visitors expect
- One-lane bridges are common in some regions
- Rural night driving is tiring and often not worth it

### Good Planning Benchmarks

| Route | Realistic Drive |
|-------|-----------------|
| Auckland -> Rotorua | about 3 hours |
| Wellington -> Martinborough | about 1.5 hours |
| Christchurch -> Tekapo | about 3 hours |
| Queenstown -> Te Anau | about 2 hours |
| Te Anau -> Milford Sound | about 2.5 hours without scenic stops |

## Domestic Flights

- Best when linking Auckland, Wellington, Christchurch, Queenstown
- Strong choice for short trips using both islands
- Add buffer when connecting to weather-sensitive regions

## Ferries

### Interislander and Bluebridge
- Connect Wellington and Picton
- Critical if building a self-drive or top-of-the-South route
- Weather and sea conditions can disrupt schedules
- Book holiday and summer sailings early

## Trains

New Zealand trains are scenic products, not daily-core transport.

| Train | Best Use |
|-------|----------|
| TranzAlpine | Scenic Christchurch day or transfer |
| Coastal Pacific | Seasonal scenic corridor when operating |
| Northern Explorer | Leisure travel, not speed |

## Coaches

InterCity is the backbone for no-car travelers between major towns.

## City Transport

### Auckland
- Ferries, trains, and buses work best with a central base
- Traffic is worse than many visitors expect

### Wellington
- Compact center, many core areas walkable
- Good bus and rail for regional moves

### Christchurch
- Easy with a car, manageable without if staying central

## Toll Roads and Road Alerts

- New Zealand has only a small number of toll roads
- Check NZTA tools rather than assuming cash booths or on-the-spot payment
- For road closures, weather alerts, and travel times, use NZTA before long drives

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Driving off the international arrival without sleep
- Treating Milford from Queenstown as a casual easy day
- Building same-day ferry plus long-drive plans with no slack
- Assuming trains replace rental-car logic across the country
