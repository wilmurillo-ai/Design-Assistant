# Coin Evidence Guide

Use this order before locking a result:

1. **Country or issuer**
   - readable script, coat of arms, monarch, state emblem, or language family
2. **Denomination**
   - visible number, spelled-out value, or currency word
3. **Date and mint mark**
   - date placement, small letters, privy marks, or mint symbols
4. **Metal color and finish**
   - copper, nickel, brass, bimetallic ring, proof-like finish, heavy wear
5. **Shape and edge**
   - round, scalloped, hole, reeded, lettered, plain, or segmented edge
6. **Portrait or commemorative theme**
   - ruler, building, animal, event, anniversary, or national symbol

Best next evidence when confidence is weak:

- straight obverse photo
- straight reverse photo
- edge photo
- close crop of date and mint mark
- scale reference or diameter
- weight if the user has it

Do not overclaim from these alone:

- photo does not prove authenticity
- photo does not prove grade
- photo does not prove silver or gold purity
- photo does not prove mint error rarity
