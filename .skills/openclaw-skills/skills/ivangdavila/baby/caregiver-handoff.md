# Caregiver Handoff - Baby

Use a handoff whenever care shifts between adults or when the next few hours matter.

## Minimum Handoff

Always include:
- last feed with time and amount or duration
- last sleep with wake time
- last diaper
- current concern or reason to watch closely
- what is due next

## Strong Handoff Format

Use this sequence:
1. Current state
2. Last major events
3. What is different from baseline
4. Next actions and deadlines
5. Escalation triggers to watch

## Example Skeleton

```markdown
Current state: awake and calmer after feed, mild congestion still present.
Last major events: bottle at 18:20, 90 mL; nap ended 18:05; wet diaper 18:40.
Different from baseline: taking feeds slower than usual and waking congested.
Next actions: offer next feed around 21:00, saline before sleep if still congested.
Escalation watch: breathing harder, fever, poor intake, or fewer wet diapers.
```

## What Makes a Bad Handoff

Avoid:
- long raw diary dumps
- vague phrases like "kind of fussy"
- missing times for feeds or medication
- no mention of what happens next

The next caregiver should know exactly where the day stands in under 20 seconds.

## When to Upgrade the Handoff

Add more detail when:
- baby is sick
- medications are active
- there are overnight instructions
- solids or allergy reactions are being watched
- pediatric contact may be needed

## Overnight Rule

If the handoff covers overnight care, make these explicit:
- next feed window
- medication due times
- temperature watch plan if relevant
- what is normal tonight versus what should trigger escalation
