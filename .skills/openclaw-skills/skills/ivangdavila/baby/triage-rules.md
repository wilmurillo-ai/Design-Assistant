# Triage Rules - Baby

Use this for escalation support, not diagnosis.
When in doubt with a very young baby or a rapidly worsening pattern, err toward urgent pediatric guidance.

## Red Alerts - Immediate Escalation

Pause routine tracking and advise emergency or urgent local care immediately if the user reports any of these:
- trouble breathing, blue lips, pauses in breathing, or obvious respiratory distress
- seizure-like activity, unresponsiveness, or severe difficulty waking the baby
- signs of severe dehydration such as very limited wet diapers plus lethargy or inability to keep fluids down
- repeated vomiting with concerning color, blood, or worsening weakness
- serious injury, suspected poisoning, or medication error with symptoms
- fever in a very young infant when local guidance treats it as urgent
- rapidly worsening rash with breathing issues, swelling, or major behavior change

## Amber Alerts - Same-Day Pediatric Guidance

Recommend same-day clinician contact when the user reports patterns such as:
- fever without emergency signs
- much lower intake than usual across several feeds
- fewer wet diapers than usual with hydration concern
- repeated vomiting or diarrhea without severe dehydration yet
- persistent breathing noise, cough, or congestion affecting feeds or sleep
- unusual sleepiness, irritability, or behavior change that is not baseline
- worsening rash, ear-pain suspicion, or pain cues that keep returning
- medication side effects or uncertainty about whether a medication was tolerated

## Routine Follow-Up

Use regular follow-up when the situation is stable and the user mainly needs:
- pattern tracking for sleep, feeds, diapers, or solids
- growth or milestone organization
- visit prep and question lists
- caregiver coordination

## Triage Workflow

1. Identify baby age or stage if known.
2. Ask only the minimum questions needed to decide urgency.
3. If red alert signs are present, escalate first and stop optimization advice.
4. If amber signs are present, help the user organize the timeline for a same-day call.
5. If no urgent signs are present, continue tracking and summary support.

## Questions That Improve Escalation Quality

Ask only what changes action:
- when did this start
- what changed from the baby's usual baseline
- current temperature and how it was measured
- intake and wet diapers today
- breathing impact, alertness, and response to soothing
- medication given and at what time

## Boundaries

Do not:
- diagnose the cause of symptoms
- tell the user to ignore urgent instincts
- guess medication dosing
- reassure over red flags just because one metric looks normal

## Documentation Rule

For every amber or red event, log:
- trigger
- observed facts
- action advised or taken
- outcome and follow-up plan

This improves handoffs and pediatric communication.
