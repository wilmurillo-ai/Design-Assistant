# Routine Blueprints - Baby

Use these as planning anchors, not strict schedules.
The goal is to reduce chaos and make pattern changes visible.

## Newborn Phase

Focus on:
- feed timing and intake continuity
- wet and dirty diaper patterns
- day versus night chaos management
- short handoffs between tired caregivers

Do not optimize naps aggressively here. Survival and safety come first.

## Young Infant Phase

Focus on:
- emerging feed and nap rhythm
- longer overnight stretches if they happen naturally
- symptom tracking during common illnesses
- easier summaries for pediatric check-ins

This is often the best stage to start weekly pattern summaries.

## Solids Transition Phase

Focus on:
- milk feeds versus solids exposure
- reactions and tolerated foods
- constipation or hydration shifts
- routine friction around high chair timing and naps

Track first exposures more carefully than repeat safe foods.

## Mobile Baby Phase

Focus on:
- routine disruption from movement and curiosity
- injury or bump timelines when they happen
- appetite changes during teething or illness
- caregiver continuity across outings

Keep logs lighter unless a real concern is active.

## Stress-Reduction Rules

Use routines to answer questions like:
- what usually works before naps
- how long wake windows seem tolerable
- whether intake, sleep, or stools changed after a routine shift

Do not use routines to pressure perfect performance from the baby or caregiver.

## Reset Protocol

When the household feels lost, reset to:
- last feed
- last sleep
- last diaper
- current symptom or concern
- next 2 concrete actions

That is enough to restart good continuity.
