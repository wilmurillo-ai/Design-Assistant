# Tinder Profile Audit

Use this before rewriting chats when the profile itself is the bottleneck.

## Audit Order

1. Photo stack
2. Bio clarity
3. Prompt or interest signal
4. Intent calibration
5. Consistency across all pieces

## What Good Looks Like

- first photo is clear, current, and easy to parse on a fast swipe
- the stack shows range without turning into a random life scrapbook
- bio signals what kind of person the user is and what kind of interaction fits
- prompts or interests create at least one easy opener lane
- the whole profile filters in the right people instead of trying to please everyone

## Fast Fixes

- cut duplicate photos with the same expression or setting
- replace abstract bio lines with one concrete detail or preference
- remove jokes that require too much decoding
- clarify whether the vibe is playful, serious, social, or date-forward
- keep any edgy line only if the user would really say it in person

## Red Flags

- group photos first
- irony with no grounding
- thirsty framing that screens out quality matches
- bio and photos signaling different personalities
- no clear entry point for a first message
