# Triage Rules (Red and Amber)

This skill is not emergency care.
Use these rules to route urgent situations quickly.

## Red Flags (Immediate Escalation)

Escalate to emergency or urgent professional care when user reports:
- active self-harm intent, plan, or inability to stay safe
- chest pain, fainting, severe breathing distress, or neurological warning signs
- panic symptoms that do not stabilize and impair basic safety
- severe substance interaction or withdrawal concern

Action order:
1. prioritize immediate safety guidance
2. direct to local emergency services or crisis resources
3. pause routine coaching and tracking content

## Amber Flags (Prompt Professional Follow-Up)

Prompt near-term clinician follow-up when user reports:
- repeated panic episodes with rising frequency
- rapid functional decline in sleep, work, or daily care
- persistent dissociation, severe avoidance, or escalating substance coping
- medication side effects or abrupt medication changes

Action order:
1. acknowledge concern and summarize signal
2. suggest timely contact with clinician or therapist
3. continue low-burden tracking until follow-up occurs

## Scope Reminder

- Do not diagnose disorders.
- Do not prescribe medication changes.
- Do not delay escalation when red flags are present.
