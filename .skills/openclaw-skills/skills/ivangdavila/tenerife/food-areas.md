# Best Dining Areas — Tenerife

## La Laguna — Foodie Capital

The best dining scene on the island.

### Why La Laguna
- University town = affordable quality
- Historic setting
- Tapas culture strong
- Local clientele (not tourists)
- Best bar hopping

### Key Streets
- **Calle Herradores**: Tapas bars, wine bars
- **Plaza del Adelantado**: Terraces, upscale
- **Quadrilatero**: Student bars, late night

### Must-Visit

**For Tapas**
- La Sombrita: Legendary, tiny, queue-worthy
- Tasca 61: Local favorite, Canarian tapas
- El Tonique: Wine bar with food
- Bodega el Lagar: Traditional

**For Dinner**
- San Sebastián 57: Contemporary
- El Patio de la Noria: Elegant Canarian
- D'Platos: Creative, popular

**For Coffee**
- Café Teatro: Plaza vibes
- La Máquina: Specialty coffee
- Various historic cafes

### Budget Tip
Student area has €8-10 menú del día options. Follow the crowds.

## La Caleta — Fine Dining Village

Small fishing village punching above its weight.

### Why La Caleta
- Fishing harbor authenticity
- Concentration of quality restaurants
- Sunset position
- Relaxed upscale

### Must-Visit

**Fine Dining**
- NUB: Michelin star, must-book
- La Vieja: Modern Canarian
- El Molino Blanco: Classic fine

**Seafood**
- El Rincón del Barco: Fresh catches
- La Vieja: Also excellent seafood
- Various harbor restaurants

**Sunset**
- Any harborside table
- Book sunset slot

### When to Go
- Lunch for casual seafood
- Dinner for fine dining experience
- Sunset essential

## Puerto de la Cruz — Traditional Quality

Established dining scene with history.

### Why Puerto de la Cruz
- 130 years of tourism
- Diverse options
- Quality local ingredients
- Traditional and modern

### Must-Visit

**Traditional**
- Régulo: Historic house, special occasion
- El Patio: Canarian classics
- Compostelano: Seafood

**Casual Quality**
- Tito's Bodeguita: Tapas institution
- La Rosa di Bari: Italian
- Various along waterfront

**Local Scene**
- Calle Mequinez area
- Less touristy inland spots

## Wine Country — Tacoronte-Acentejo

Unique culinary experience.

### Why Go
- Guachinches (home wine restaurants)
- Wine with source
- Traditional food
- Off tourist track

### The Route

Drive through:
1. Tacoronte
2. El Sauzal (Casa del Vino)
3. La Matanza
4. La Victoria
5. Santa Úrsula

### How to Find Guachinches
- No signs usually
- Look for cars outside houses
- Ask at local bars
- Check "Guachinches de Tenerife" Facebook

### What to Expect
- House wine by bottle (€4-8)
- Simple food (carne fiesta, costillas, potatoes)
- Cash only
- Very casual
- Often seasonal

## Anaga — Remote Rewards

Worth the drive for food alone.

### Why Go
- Freshest seafood
- Village restaurants
- Zero pretension
- Adventure dining

### Where

**Taganana**
- Casa África: Fish, views
- El Frontón: Traditional

**San Andrés**
- Near Teresitas beach
- Several fish restaurants

**What to Eat**
- Whatever's fresh
- Fish of the day
- Traditional Canarian
- Very affordable

## Seasonal Considerations

### Winter (High Season)
- Book ahead for popular spots
- Some guachinches closed
- Hotels busier

### Summer
- Some places close August
- Guachinches less common
- Locals holiday

### Carnival (Feb)
- Many places packed or closed
- Book far ahead
- Street food focus

### Harvest (Sep-Oct)
- Best guachinche season
- Wine festivals
- Excellent produce
