# Visitor Attractions — Tenerife

## Must-See (Don't Miss)

### 1. Mount Teide National Park
Spain's highest peak (3,718m) and UNESCO World Heritage Site.
- **Cable car**: Ascends to 3,555m. Book online in advance (sells out). €39 return.
- **Summit permit**: Free but limited; apply 2+ months ahead for the final 163m hike.
- **Sunset/stargazing**: Some of the world's best stargazing. Book observatory tours.
- **Drive up**: The road itself is spectacular. Allow 3-4 hours round trip from coast.
- **When**: Clear mornings best. Clouds often roll in afternoon.

### 2. La Laguna (UNESCO World Heritage)
The island's original capital and most beautiful town.
- **Old town**: Colonial architecture, pedestrian streets, great cafes
- **University vibe**: Young, vibrant, real Canarian culture
- **Cathedral**: Historic heart of the town
- **Markets**: Thursday and Sunday morning flea markets
- **Best for**: History buffs, architecture lovers, authentic experience

### 3. Anaga Rural Park
Ancient laurel forests (laurisilva) in dramatic volcanic landscape.
- **Hiking**: Dozens of trails from easy to challenging
- **Best hikes**: Roque de Taborno, Punta del Hidalgo to Chinamada
- **Villages**: Taganana, Chamorga — step back in time
- **Weather**: Often misty (adds atmosphere but bring layers)
- **When**: Mornings usually clearer

### 4. Los Gigantes Cliffs
600m volcanic cliffs rising from the sea.
- **Boat trips**: Whale watching + cliffs (€30-60). Dolphins almost guaranteed.
- **Kayak**: More intimate experience at cliff base
- **Viewpoints**: Mirador de Archipenque best land-based view
- **Swimming**: Natural pools at Punta de Teno nearby

### 5. Masca Village & Hike
Iconic mountain village with famous gorge hike.
- **The hike**: 4.5km descent through gorge to beach, then boat back. Book ahead.
- **Reopened 2021**: Trail closed for years, now properly managed
- **Driving there**: Narrow winding road is an experience itself
- **Skip if**: Not comfortable with heights or basic hiking

### 6. Loro Parque
World-class zoo/aquarium, consistently rated Europe's best.
- **Highlights**: Orca shows, penguin habitat, gorillas, tropical aquarium
- **Duration**: Full day easily
- **Price**: €40 adult, combos with Siam Park available
- **Controversy**: Animal rights concerns about orcas (worth researching)

### 7. Siam Park
Europe's best water park (multiple awards).
- **Highlights**: Giant wave pool, Tower of Power, lazy river
- **Duration**: Full day
- **Price**: €42 adult
- **Tips**: Go early, bring waterproof phone case, queues build by noon

## Worth Visiting

### Beaches
- **Playa de las Teresitas**: Golden sand (imported from Sahara), calm waters, local favorite
- **El Médano**: Natural beach, windy, kitesurfing hub
- **La Tejita**: Nudist-friendly, wild, near Montaña Roja
- **Playa del Duque**: Upscale south beach, loungers, restaurants
- **Playa Jardín**: Puerto de la Cruz, designed by César Manrique

### Towns & Villages
- **Puerto de la Cruz**: Traditional tourist town, botanical gardens, great food scene
- **Garachico**: Historic port destroyed by 1706 eruption, natural pools, charming
- **Icod de los Vinos**: 1000-year-old dragon tree, wine region
- **La Orotava**: Historic mansions, beautiful valley views
- **Candelaria**: Pilgrimage site, patron saint basilica

### Nature & Viewpoints
- **Mirador de la Corona**: Watch planes land beneath you
- **Pico del Inglés**: Panoramic views of Anaga
- **Montaña Roja**: Easy volcanic cone hike, sunset spot
- **Cueva del Viento**: Volcanic tube, 5th largest in world

### Activities
- **Whale watching**: Year-round pilot whales and dolphins. Los Gigantes or Puerto Colón
- **Stargazing**: Teide is exceptional. Book guided tours with telescopes
- **Diving**: Warm waters, volcanic formations, sea turtles
- **Golf**: 9 courses, year-round play. Buenavista and Adeje standouts

## Skip (Tourist Traps)

### 1. Jungle Park
- Overpriced for what it is
- Better: Loro Parque or actual hiking in nature

### 2. Generic "Pirates" Shows
- Las Américas dinner shows
- Dated, cheesy, aimed at package tourists

### 3. Centro Comercial Malls
- Same shops as everywhere
- Better: La Laguna streets or Santa Cruz centro

### 4. Banana Plantations (Most)
- Generic tours, buy bananas in any supermarket
- Exception: Casa del Plátano if genuinely interested

### 5. Overpriced Beach Clubs
- South coast clubs with €15 cocktails
- Better: Local chiringuitos or hotel pools

### 6. "Typical Canarian Shows"
- Hotel entertainment culture shows
- Better: Actual local fiestas (check ayuntamiento calendars)

## Day Trip Combos

### Full Day: North Loop
Morning: La Laguna old town → Drive through Anaga (stop Mirador Cruz del Carmen) → Lunch Taganana → Playa de las Teresitas → Evening Santa Cruz

### Full Day: West Coast
Morning: Icod (dragon tree) → Garachico (swim in natural pools) → Lunch → Los Gigantes boat trip → Sunset at Los Gigantes

### Full Day: Teide Experience
Early start → Drive up via La Esperanza forest → Teide cable car (book 10am slot) → Lunch at Parador → Sunset at Roques de García → Stargazing

### Half Day: South Chill
Morning: Costa Adeje beach or Siam Park → Lunch La Caleta fishing village → Afternoon whale watching

## Seasonal Considerations

| Season | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| Dec-Feb | Teide snow + coast sun, whale watching peak |
| Mar-May | Hiking (wildflowers), pleasant everywhere |
| Jun-Aug | Beaches, water sports, local fiestas |
| Sep-Nov | Shoulder season, warm seas, fewer crowds |

**Carnival (Feb/Mar)**: Santa Cruz carnival is second only to Rio. Book far ahead.

**Religious festivals**: Corpus Christi (La Orotava flower carpets), Romería (pilgrimages), local patron saint days throughout year.
