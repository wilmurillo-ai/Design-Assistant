# Market Checks

Before comparing prices or calling something value, confirm the market is economically identical.

If the request implies underage betting, restricted jurisdictions, evasion of operator controls, or self-exclusion workarounds, stop and refuse that part.

## Identity checks

Match all of these:
- sport and event
- side, total, or prop definition
- exact line or threshold
- overtime or extra-time grading
- player participation rule
- void or push rule

## Line shopping checklist

| Check | Why It Matters |
|-------|----------------|
| Same line? | -110 at -3 is not the same as -110 at -3.5 |
| Same book rules? | Some books void or grade differently on props |
| Same max stake? | A better number with tiny limits may not matter |
| Same timestamp? | Old screenshots create fake value |
| Same market status? | Open, suspended, and re-opened prices are different states |

## Live betting checks

For live tickets, ask:
- when the quote was seen
- whether a key event just happened
- whether the book is known to suspend slowly or re-open wide
- whether the user can still place the number they saw

If latency is the edge, assume the edge may already be gone.

## Parlays and same-game products

Treat same-game parlays as separate products:
- books often price correlation internally
- boosts can hide extra margin
- leg-by-leg comparison is not enough

If the user wants parlay analysis, grade each leg first and then discuss correlation risk explicitly.

## Exchange or prediction-style markets

Check:
- fee schedule
- depth at the posted price
- resolution language
- time to exit or hedge

Thin liquidity or ambiguous resolution is a red flag even when headline odds look attractive.
