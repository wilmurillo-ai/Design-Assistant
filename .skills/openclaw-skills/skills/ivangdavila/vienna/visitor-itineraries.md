# Vienna Itineraries

## 1-Day Itinerary (Must-Sees Only)

> ⚠️ Skip Schönbrunn on 1 day — it's outside center and takes 3+ hours

### Morning (8:00 AM - 12:00 PM)

**8:00 AM — Viennese Breakfast**
- Café Central or Café Sacher
- Try: Melange + Sachertorte or Apfelstrudel

**9:00 AM — Hofburg Palace**
Choose 2-3:
- Sisi Museum & Imperial Apartments (75 min)
- Spanish Riding School training (10 AM, 1 hour)
- Austrian National Library (15-20 min)
- Imperial Treasury (30 min)

**12:00 PM — Kunsthistorisches Museum**
- Stunning architecture + world-class art
- Lunch at museum's dome café

### Afternoon (2:00 PM - 6:00 PM)

**2:00 PM — Walk to Stephansdom**
Via Kärntner Strasse past Vienna Opera

**2:30 PM — St. Stephen's Cathedral**
- Free nave access
- Optional: Tower climb for views
- Optional: Catacombs tour

**4:00 PM — Historic Center**
- Graben (Plague Column)
- Kohlmarkt (luxury shopping)
- St. Peter's Church
- Michaelerplatz

### Evening

**Dinner Options:**
- Traditional Beisl for Wiener Schnitzel
- Heuriger wine tavern
- Naschmarkt area

**Optional:**
- Opera standing room (from €13)
- Coffeehouse evening pastry
- Ringstrasse night stroll

## Money-Saving Tips

### Free Days
- **First Sunday:** Wien Museum locations free
- **First Thursday 6-9pm:** Leopold Museum free
- **October 26:** Austrian National Library museums free

### Cheap Eats
- Billa/Spar supermarket meals
- Naschmarkt stall food (avoid sit-down restaurants)
- Würstelstand (sausage stands) — iconic and cheap
- University Mensas (student cafeterias)

### Transport
- **24h pass:** €8 (worth it for 4+ rides)
- **Vienna → airport:** S7 train €4.40 (vs CAT €14.90)
- Walk — central Vienna very walkable

### Entertainment
- Opera standing room: €13-18
- Film Festival at Rathausplatz (July-August): FREE
- Donauinselfest (June): FREE

### Passes
- **Skip Vienna Pass** unless doing 3+ big attractions daily
- **Sisi Pass** (€57) saves money if doing all 3 palaces
- **Annual museum cards** worth it for 5+ visits

## Best Times to Visit

### By Season

| Season | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| **Spring (Apr-May)** | Mild weather, gardens blooming, fewer crowds | Unpredictable rain |
| **Summer (Jun-Aug)** | Long days, outdoor events, Film Festival | Hot, some Heurigen closed |
| **Fall (Sep-Oct)** | Wine harvest, mild weather, Heurigen peak | Some rainy days |
| **Winter (Nov-Feb)** | Christmas markets, New Year's concert, fewer tourists | Cold, short days |

### Best Months Overall
- **May-June:** Perfect weather, gardens peak, before summer crowds
- **September:** Wine harvest, warm, fewer tourists

### Christmas Markets
- Late November to December 23
- Best: Rathaus (City Hall), Schönbrunn, Spittelberg
- Avoid weekends in December (packed)

## Common Tourist Mistakes

1. **Booking Schönbrunn day-of** — Always book ahead in peak season
2. **Eating on Stephansplatz** — Walk 2 blocks for better value
3. **Skipping standing room at Opera** — Best deal in classical music
4. **Not bringing cash** — Some places (Stephansdom) cash only
5. **Underestimating walking distances** — Vienna is bigger than it looks
6. **Visiting only tourist restaurants** — Best food in neighborhoods
7. **Skipping Heurigen** — Unique Vienna experience, don't miss it
8. **Not checking free days** — First Sunday/Thursday save money

## Getting from Airport

| Method | Price | Time | Notes |
|--------|-------|------|-------|
| **S7 train** | €4.40 | 25min | Best value |
| **CAT (City Airport Train)** | €14.90 | 16min | Faster, not worth extra |
| **Bus** | ~€8 | 20-30min | Multiple routes |
| **Taxi/Uber** | €35-45 | 20-40min | Fixed fare zones |

**Tip:** S7 stops at Wien Mitte, Praterstern, Floridsdorf — may be closer to your hotel than CAT's Wien Mitte terminus.
