# Starting a Business in Las Vegas

## Why Nevada for Business

### Tax Advantages
- **No corporate income tax**
- **No personal income tax**
- **No franchise tax**
- **No inventory tax**
- Competitive sales tax (8.375% in Clark County)

### Business Climate
- Pro-business regulations
- Fast entity formation
- Privacy protections
- Strong LLC laws
- Landlord-friendly

## Business Entity Types

### Nevada LLC
**Most popular choice for small businesses and asset protection.**

**Benefits**:
- No state income tax on LLC income
- Strong charging order protection
- Privacy (can use registered agent)
- Pass-through taxation (federal)
- Flexible management structure

**Costs**:
- Formation: $75 (state) + registered agent
- Annual: $350 state business license
- Initial List: $150 (due within 30 days)
- Annual List: $150

**Process**:
1. Choose a name (check availability: nvsilverflume.gov)
2. File Articles of Organization
3. Get EIN from IRS
4. File Initial List of Managers/Members
5. Obtain state business license
6. Local business license (city/county)

### Nevada Corporation
**For larger operations or those seeking investment.**

**Benefits**:
- No corporate income tax
- Strong director/officer protections
- Can issue stock
- Established legal framework

**Costs**:
- Formation: $75 minimum (depends on authorized shares)
- Annual: $350 state business license
- Annual List: $150

### Sole Proprietorship
**Simplest but least protection.**

**Process**:
- File fictitious name (DBA) if using business name
- Obtain business license
- Get EIN if hiring employees

## Business Licensing

### State Level
**State Business License** (required for most businesses):
- Cost: $200/year (or $500/4 years)
- Apply: nvsilverflume.gov
- Due: Before commencing business

### Local Level (Clark County / Cities)

**Clark County**:
- Required for unincorporated areas
- Apply: clarkcountynv.gov
- Varies by business type

**City of Las Vegas**:
- Required for city limits
- Apply: lasvegasnevada.gov
- Varies by business type

**Henderson**:
- Similar requirements
- Apply: cityofhenderson.com

**North Las Vegas**:
- Own licensing requirements
- Check specific requirements

### Professional Licenses
Some professions require state licensing:
- Contractors (Nevada State Contractors Board)
- Real estate (Nevada Real Estate Division)
- Healthcare (various boards)
- Legal (State Bar)
- Accounting (Board of Accountancy)

## Industry-Specific Considerations

### Gaming-Related Business
Heavily regulated by Nevada Gaming Control Board.
- Gaming license required for any gaming involvement
- Extensive background checks
- High compliance requirements
- Don't start a gaming-adjacent business without legal counsel

### Cannabis
Legal in Nevada but highly regulated:
- State and local licenses required
- Limited licenses available
- Significant capital requirements
- Federal banking challenges persist

### Alcohol
- State license from Nevada Department of Taxation
- Local licenses required
- Different types (tavern, package, etc.)

### Food Service
- Health permit from Southern Nevada Health District
- Food handler cards for employees
- Health inspections

### Construction
- Nevada contractor's license required
- Different classifications for different work
- Bonding requirements

## Office Space Options

### Traditional Office
**Class A (premium)**:
- Strip-adjacent, Henderson, Summerlin
- $30-50/sq ft annually
- Full service

**Class B (standard)**:
- Various locations
- $20-35/sq ft annually

**Class C (budget)**:
- Older buildings
- $15-25/sq ft annually

### Coworking
- WeWork, Regus, IWG: Various locations
- Local options: Work in Progress, others
- $200-600/month for dedicated desk
- $50-200/month for hot desk

### Home Office
Legal for most businesses. Check:
- HOA restrictions
- Zoning (for customer-facing businesses)
- Business license requirements
- Insurance implications

### Industrial/Warehouse
- North Las Vegas: Best value
- Henderson: Growing
- Significant space available

## Hiring in Vegas

### Labor Market
- Large hospitality-experienced workforce
- Growing tech talent pool
- Bilingual workers (Spanish) readily available
- Lower wages than coastal cities

### Minimum Wage (2024)
- $10.25/hour (with health benefits)
- $11.25/hour (without health benefits)
- Increases scheduled

### Employment Considerations
- At-will employment state
- Workers' compensation required
- Unemployment insurance required
- No state income tax withholding

## Taxes

### What You Pay
| Tax Type | Rate |
|----------|------|
| Federal income | Standard federal rates |
| State income | 0% |
| Sales tax | 8.375% (Clark County) |
| Modified Business Tax | 1.17% (wages over $50K/quarter) |
| Commerce Tax | 0.051-0.331% (gross over $4M) |

### Sales Tax
- Must collect if selling taxable goods
- File monthly or quarterly
- Use: nevadatax.nv.gov

### Payroll Taxes
- Unemployment insurance (state)
- Workers' compensation
- Modified Business Tax (larger employers)

## Real Estate for Business

### Buying vs Leasing
**Leasing** (usually recommended for new businesses):
- Lower upfront costs
- Flexibility
- Landlord handles maintenance

**Buying**:
- Equity building
- More control
- Tax benefits (depreciation)
- Requires significant capital

### Commercial Lease Terms
- Triple net (NNN) common
- 3-5 year terms typical
- Negotiate tenant improvements
- Personal guarantee often required

## Common Mistakes

1. **Skipping business license**: Required and enforced
2. **Wrong entity type**: LLC is usually right, but not always
3. **Not understanding Modified Business Tax**: Kicks in at $50K/quarter in wages
4. **Ignoring local licensing**: Each city has requirements
5. **Assuming "no tax state" means no business taxes**: Sales tax, MBT, etc. exist
6. **Not using registered agent**: Required for LLC/Corp; privacy matters
