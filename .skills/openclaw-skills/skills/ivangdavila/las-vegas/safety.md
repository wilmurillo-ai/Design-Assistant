# Las Vegas Safety & Laws

## Overall Safety

Las Vegas is generally safe for visitors and residents, but like any major city, awareness matters.

**Crime rates**: Higher than national average, but concentrated in specific areas
**Tourist safety**: The Strip and major casinos are heavily monitored and secure
**Residential safety**: Varies dramatically by neighborhood

## Safety by Area

### Safest Areas
- Summerlin (consistently lowest crime)
- Henderson (ranked among safest US cities)
- Green Valley
- Southern Highlands
- MacDonald Highlands

### Average Safety
- Southwest Las Vegas
- Enterprise
- Spring Valley (varies by block)
- Downtown Summerlin area

### Higher Crime Areas
- Parts of North Las Vegas
- East Las Vegas / Sunrise Manor
- Some areas near the Stratosphere
- Parts of Downtown (late night)
- Paradise (property crime higher)

### The Strip
- Heavy security presence
- Camera surveillance everywhere
- Generally safe for tourists
- Petty crime exists (pickpockets, scams)

## Common Crimes

### Tourists Face
**Pickpocketing**:
- Crowded areas (Fremont Street, busy casino floors)
- Distracted photo-takers
- Prevention: Keep valuables secure, use front pockets, don't flash cash

**Scams**:
- Costumed characters demanding tips (legal but aggressive)
- CD sellers (aggressive sales)
- "Free" tickets with timeshare presentations
- Fake taxi meters (use rideshare apps)

**Vehicle break-ins**:
- Don't leave anything visible
- Use valet or secure parking
- Common at trailheads (Red Rock, etc.)

### Residents Face
**Property crime**:
- Car break-ins (don't leave valuables)
- Package theft (use secure delivery)
- Home break-ins (varies by area)

**Vehicle theft**:
- Higher than national average
- Certain models targeted
- Use steering wheel locks, trackers

## Legal Essentials

### Gambling
- **Legal age**: 21+
- **Where**: Licensed casinos only
- **Banned**: Can be banned from any casino for any reason
- **Cheating**: Felony, prosecuted aggressively
- **Card counting**: Not illegal but casinos will ban you

### Alcohol
- **Legal age**: 21+
- **Open container**: LEGAL on Strip and Fremont pedestrian areas
- **Open container in vehicle**: Illegal
- **DUI**: 0.08 BAC limit, heavily enforced
- **Public intoxication**: Can be arrested if causing disturbance

### Cannabis
- **Legal**: Recreational use 21+
- **Purchase**: Licensed dispensaries only
- **Where you can consume**: Private residences only
- **NOT legal**: On Strip, in casinos, in public, in hotels
- **DUI**: Impaired driving laws apply to cannabis
- **Employers**: Can still drug test and fire for use

### Prostitution
- **ILLEGAL in Clark County** (where Las Vegas is)
- Legal in some rural Nevada counties, NOT Vegas
- Solicitation is illegal
- Cards handed out on Strip advertise "entertainment" only

### Firearms
- **Open carry**: Legal without permit
- **Concealed carry**: Permit required (shall-issue)
- **Casinos**: Private property, can prohibit
- **Federal buildings, schools**: Prohibited
- **Background checks**: Required for dealer sales, not private

### Other Laws to Know
- **Jaywalking**: Technically illegal, rarely enforced on Strip
- **Trespassing**: Casinos can ban you; returning = trespassing
- **Loitering**: Enforced in some areas
- **Panhandling**: Regulated, aggressive panhandling illegal
- **Drones**: Prohibited near Strip, airport, events

## DUI Specifics

**Nevada takes DUI seriously.**

**Limits**:
- Standard: 0.08% BAC
- Commercial drivers: 0.04%
- Under 21: 0.02%

**Penalties (First Offense)**:
- 2 days to 6 months jail (or community service)
- $400-1,000 fine
- License suspension 185 days
- DUI school
- Ignition interlock possible

**Checkpoints**: Common, especially near Strip on weekend nights

**Best practice**: Uber/Lyft. Cheap compared to DUI consequences.

## Casino Security

### What Casinos Monitor
- Every table game (multiple cameras)
- Every slot machine
- Every entrance/exit
- Parking structures
- Hotel hallways

### What Gets You Watched
- Unusual betting patterns
- Winning too much
- Counting cards (not illegal but they'll stop you)
- Acting suspicious
- Intoxicated and causing issues

### What Gets You Removed/Banned
- Cheating (any form)
- Fighting
- Excessive intoxication
- Harassment
- Trespassing (if previously banned)

### Player Rights
- You can leave at any time
- You can refuse to show ID (but they can refuse service)
- Winnings are yours (with tax implications above $1,200)
- You can be banned without explanation

## Scam Awareness

### Common Tourist Scams

**Timeshare "gifts"**:
- "Free show tickets!" means 4-hour high-pressure sales
- Know what you're signing up for

**CD sellers**:
- Put CD in your hand, demand payment
- Just say no and keep walking

**Costumed characters**:
- Photos are "free," then aggressive tipping demands
- Decide tip amount before photo

**Fake taxis**:
- Unlicensed vehicles
- Use rideshare apps with tracked drivers

**"Dropped ring" scam**:
- Someone finds ring, offers to sell it to you
- It's worthless; classic street con

**Strip club bait-and-switch**:
- Promised one price, billed another
- Use reputable establishments only

### Protecting Yourself
- Don't flash cash
- Use cards when possible
- Keep phone/wallet secure
- Be skeptical of "free" offers
- Walk away from aggressive sales
- Trust your instincts

## Natural Hazards

### Heat
**The biggest danger in Vegas.**
- Heat exhaustion and heat stroke kill people every year
- Summer temps: 105-115°F
- Symptoms: Dizziness, nausea, confusion, stopping sweating
- Action: Get to shade/AC, hydrate, seek medical help if severe

### Flash Floods
- Desert doesn't absorb water
- Washes flood instantly during monsoons (July-August)
- **Never** drive through flooded washes
- Stay out of flood channels, even when dry

### Earthquakes
- Less common than California but possible
- Know basic earthquake safety

### Wildlife
- Scorpions (rare in developed areas, common in new construction)
- Rattlesnakes (hiking areas)
- Black widow spiders (garages, outdoor areas)

## Safety Tips by Situation

### Walking the Strip
- Stay on main sidewalks
- Use pedestrian bridges
- Don't engage with aggressive promoters
- Watch for distracted drivers
- Hydrate in summer

### Gambling
- Set a budget before starting
- Use player's card (helps track play)
- Don't chase losses
- Take breaks
- Don't leave drink unattended

### Nightlife
- Stay with your group
- Watch your drink
- Use rideshare home
- Don't accept drinks from strangers
- Know where you're staying

### Hiking
- **Tell someone your plans**
- Bring more water than you think
- Start early, finish before heat
- Don't hike alone in remote areas
- Check weather (flash flood warnings)

### At Home
- Lock doors and windows
- Get to know neighbors
- Package theft is common (secure delivery)
- Ring/security cameras help
- Motion-sensor lights
