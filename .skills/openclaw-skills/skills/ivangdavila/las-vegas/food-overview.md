# Las Vegas Dining Overview

## The Vegas Food Scene

Las Vegas has transformed from all-you-can-eat buffets into one of America's top dining destinations. Every celebrity chef has a restaurant here. The diversity ranges from $1.99 shrimp cocktails to $1,000 tasting menus.

## Understanding Vegas Dining

### The Strip
- Celebrity chef flagships
- Premium prices (20-40% above other cities)
- Tourist-heavy, reservations essential
- Consistent quality, proven concepts

### Off-Strip
- Hidden gems, local favorites
- 30-50% cheaper than Strip
- Chinatown = best Asian food outside coastal cities
- Requires knowing where to go

### The Buffet Reality
- Classic Vegas buffets declining
- Bacchanal (Caesars) and Wicked Spoon (Cosmo) survived and improved
- Not the deal they once were ($50-80+)
- Worth it once for the experience

## The Chinatown Phenomenon

**Location**: Spring Mountain Road, west of Strip (15-min drive)

**Why it matters**: This is one of America's best food destinations, hidden in strip malls.

**The experience**: Ugly strip mall exteriors, incredible food inside. Trust the crowds.

**Must-try restaurants:**
| Restaurant | Specialty |
|------------|-----------|
| Lotus of Siam | Northern Thai |
| Raku | Japanese robata |
| Chengdu Taste | Sichuan |
| China Mama | Hand-pulled noodles |
| Hwaro | Korean BBQ |
| EDO Gastro | Omakase |
| District One | Vietnamese |

**Strategy**: Go hungry, hop between 2-3 spots, share dishes.

## Dining Strategies

### Reservations

| Restaurant Type | How Far Ahead |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Ultra-fine dining | 4-8 weeks |
| Celebrity chef (dinner) | 2-4 weeks |
| Celebrity chef (lunch) | 3-7 days |
| Popular local spots | 1-3 days |
| Casual dining | Walk-in or same-day |

**Best booking tools**: OpenTable, Resy, SevenRooms, direct call

### Saving Money

**Happy Hours** (typically 4-7pm):
- Many celebrity restaurants offer happy hour
- 30-50% off drinks, discounted apps
- Great way to experience high-end venues affordably

**Lunch vs Dinner**:
- Same kitchens, same quality
- 20-40% lower prices
- Easier reservations

**Off-Strip**:
- Chinatown is 50-70% cheaper than Strip
- Downtown Arts District has great values
- Henderson has excellent options

### Tipping

| Service Level | Tip |
|---------------|-----|
| Counter service | $1-2 or 10-15% |
| Casual dining | 18-20% |
| Fine dining | 20-22% |
| Exceptional service | 25%+ |

Vegas servers often work harder than elsewhere. Tip well.

## Special Situations

### Late Night (After 11pm)
- **Strip**: Many hotel restaurants close early
- **Open late**: Peppermill (24hr), Chinatown spots (many 24hr)
- **Room service**: Available but expensive

### Vegetarian/Vegan
- Veggie/vegan options expanding
- **Best options**: Wynn properties, Cosmopolitan restaurants
- **Dedicated vegan**: VegeNation (Downtown), Violette's (Henderson)

### Dietary Restrictions
- High-end restaurants accommodate well
- Call ahead for severe allergies
- Kosher options limited (Hamada has kosher sushi)

### Business Dining
- **Power lunch**: Cut, Carbone, SW
- **Client dinner**: CUT, Le Cirque, Picasso
- **Private dining**: Available at most celebrity restaurants

## Value vs Splurge Guide

### Best Values
1. Chinatown anything
2. Happy hour at celebrity restaurants
3. Tacos El Gordo (Strip location)
4. Lotus of Siam (legendary and reasonable)
5. Arts District restaurants

### Worth the Splurge
1. Joel Robuchon (once-in-a-lifetime)
2. é by José Andrés (unique experience)
3. SW Steakhouse (Strip steakhouse done right)
4. Carbone (hype justified)
5. Bazaar Meat (creative and delicious)

### Tourist Traps to Avoid
- Generic Strip casino buffets (quality crashed)
- "Italian" restaurants with outdoor hawkers
- Overpriced basic food with Strip views
- Chain restaurants at Strip prices
