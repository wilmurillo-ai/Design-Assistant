# Las Vegas Healthcare

## Healthcare Landscape

Las Vegas healthcare is adequate but not exceptional. The city is still building medical infrastructure to match its population growth.

## Hospital Systems

### University Medical Center (UMC)
**Clark County's public hospital and only Level I trauma center.**

- Location: 1800 W Charleston Blvd
- Affiliation: UNLV School of Medicine
- Services: Trauma, burn center, transplant, children's hospital
- Reality: Busy, serves uninsured, can have waits

### Sunrise Hospital & Medical Center
**Major private hospital.**

- Location: 3186 S Maryland Pkwy
- Services: Cardiac, stroke, trauma (Level II), pediatrics
- Notes: High volume, established facility

### St. Rose Dominican Hospitals
**Dignity Health system with multiple locations.**

- St. Rose Siena (Henderson)
- St. Rose San Martin (South)
- St. Rose de Lima (Henderson)
- Notes: Catholic hospital system, quality care

### Valley Hospital Medical Center
**Established hospital in central Las Vegas.**

- Location: 620 Shadow Lane
- Services: General medical/surgical, heart, stroke

### Henderson Hospital
**Newer Henderson facility.**

- Location: 1050 W Galleria Dr
- Services: ER, general care
- Notes: Less crowded than Strip-area hospitals

### Summerlin Hospital
**Serving Summerlin community.**

- Location: 657 N Town Center Dr
- Services: ER, general care, maternity
- Notes: Newer facility, serves west valley

### MountainView Hospital
**North/Northwest Las Vegas.**

- Location: 3100 N Tenaya Way
- Services: ER, general care, orthopedics

## Finding Doctors

### Primary Care
- Wait times: 1-2 weeks for established patients
- New patients: 2-4 weeks or longer
- Many practices accepting new patients

### Specialists
**Vegas still developing specialist depth.**

- Dermatology: Long waits (months common)
- Psychiatry: Limited availability
- Certain subspecialties: May require travel to LA

### Resources
- Insurance provider directory
- Zocdoc
- Healthgrades
- UNLV Health (medical school practice)
- Southwest Medical (large practice)

## Urgent Care

Many options throughout the valley:
- UMC Quick Care
- Concentra
- CareNow
- Various independent urgent cares

**Cost**: $75-200 without insurance, copay with insurance

**When to use**: Non-emergency issues that can't wait for doctor appointment

## Emergency Care

### When to Go to ER
- Chest pain, difficulty breathing
- Severe injury
- Stroke symptoms
- Major trauma
- Life-threatening situations

### ER Realities
- Waits can be long (especially UMC, Sunrise)
- Cost is high without insurance
- Non-emergencies should use urgent care

### Free-Standing ERs
Several exist throughout valley. Same services as hospital ER, sometimes faster.

## Health Insurance

### Employer Coverage
Most common way to get insurance. Major employers (casinos, healthcare, government) offer plans.

### Nevada Health Link (ACA Marketplace)
State marketplace for individual coverage:
- Bronze: $250-350/month
- Silver: $350-500/month
- Gold: $450-600/month

Open enrollment: November-December
Special enrollment: With qualifying life event

### Medicaid (Nevada Medicaid)
For lower-income residents:
- Expanded eligibility under ACA
- Apply: dwss.nv.gov

### Medicare
For 65+:
- Standard Medicare A/B
- Medicare Advantage plans available
- Part D for prescriptions

## Healthcare Challenges

### Access Issues
- Specialist shortages (especially psychiatry, dermatology)
- Growing population outpacing provider growth
- Some specialties require travel to LA

### Cost
- Typical American healthcare costs
- Urgent care and ER can be expensive without insurance
- Insurance options exist but can be costly

### Quality
- Generally adequate
- Not a "destination" for healthcare
- Complex cases may require travel

## Mental Health

### Resources
- **Crisis hotline**: 988
- **Emergency**: UMC psychiatric services
- **Outpatient**: Various practices, but limited availability

### Challenges
- Psychiatrist shortage significant
- Wait times for new patients: Weeks to months
- Teletherapy expanding options

### Tips
- Start searching early
- Consider teletherapy options
- Check insurance network carefully
- Community mental health centers exist

Some Vegas residents travel for care:
- **Los Angeles**: Specialty care, second opinions

- **Mexico**: Dental, elective procedures (cost savings)

## Healthcare Tips

### Finding Care
1. Check insurance network first
2. Use online appointment booking when available
3. Ask about wait times for new patients
4. Consider UNLV Health (medical school, often accepting)

### Saving Money
- Use urgent care vs ER when appropriate
- Generic medications when possible
- Costco pharmacy for best prices
- Compare prices for procedures

### Staying Healthy
- Stay hydrated (desert climate)
- Sun protection essential
- Exercise early morning (summer)
- Mental health matters (invest in it)

## For Newcomers

### First Month
1. Find primary care doctor
2. Set up pharmacy accounts
3. Locate nearest urgent care and ER
4. Understand your insurance

### Ongoing
- Establish relationship with PCP
- Get preventive screenings
- Don't delay needed care
- Advocate for yourself
