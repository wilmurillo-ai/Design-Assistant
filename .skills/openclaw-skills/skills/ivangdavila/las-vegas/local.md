# Local Services & Setup

## DMV (Department of Motor Vehicles)

### What You Need to Do
Within 30 days of establishing residency:
- Get Nevada driver's license
- Register your vehicle

### Driver's License

**Required documents**:
- Proof of identity (passport, birth certificate)
- Proof of SSN
- Proof of Nevada residency (2 documents)
- Out-of-state license (if transferring)

**Residency proof** (need 2 different types):
- Utility bill
- Bank statement
- Lease/mortgage
- Vehicle registration
- Pay stub

**Cost**: ~$42 (8-year license)

**Process**:
1. Make appointment online (strongly recommended)
2. Bring all documents
3. Pass vision test
4. Take photo
5. Written test waived if transferring valid license

### Vehicle Registration

**Required**:
- Title or registration from previous state
- Proof of insurance (Nevada policy required)
- Smog check (Nevada emissions test)
- VIN inspection (form provided by DMV)

**Cost**: Based on vehicle value. Typical: $300-600 first year.

**Annual renewal**: Due on birthday, can renew online

### DMV Tips
- **Appointment essential**: Walk-in waits can be 2-4 hours
- Book at smaller offices (Henderson, Summerlin)
- Bring all documents — missing one = come back
- Online services work for renewals

**Locations**:
- Sahara (main, busiest)
- Henderson
- Summerlin
- North Las Vegas

## Utilities

### Electricity: NV Energy
- Monopoly provider for Las Vegas
- Create account online before move
- Budget billing available (evens out summer spikes)
- Solar rebates/programs available

**Contact**: 702-402-5555 | nvenergy.com

### Gas: Southwest Gas
- Natural gas for heating, water heater, stove
- Create account online

**Contact**: 877-860-6020 | swgas.com

### Water: Las Vegas Valley Water District
- One of most efficient water utilities in US
- Tiered pricing (higher usage = higher rates)
- Conservation rebates (grass removal, efficient fixtures)

**Contact**: 702-870-4194 | lvvwd.com

### Trash/Recycling
Provider depends on location:
- Republic Services (most areas)
- City of Las Vegas (city limits)
- Henderson (Henderson limits)

Typically included in HOA or billed directly.

### Internet

**Cox Communications**:
- Cable internet, most coverage
- 50-1000 Mbps options
- $50-100/month typical

**CenturyLink/Lumen**:
- DSL and fiber (limited areas)
- Competitive in fiber areas

**T-Mobile Home Internet**:
- 5G wireless option
- $50/month flat rate
- Coverage varies

**Tips**:
- Check what's available at your specific address
- Negotiate — both companies offer deals
- Bundle discounts available

## Voter Registration

### How to Register
- Online: nvsos.gov
- DMV (automatic offer when getting license)
- Mail-in form

### Requirements
- US citizen
- Nevada resident
- 18+ by election day

### Nevada Specifics
- Same-day registration available
- No-excuse absentee voting
- Early voting period (usually 2 weeks before election)

## Schools Enrollment

### Clark County School District

**Process**:
1. Determine zoned school (ccsd.net)
2. Gather documents:
   - Proof of residency
   - Child's birth certificate
   - Immunization records
   - Previous school records
3. Register at zoned school

**Magnet/Choice schools**: Separate application process, limited slots

**School year**: August to May (earlier than many states)

## Pet Setup

### Licensing
Clark County requires dog licensing.
- $14/year (spayed/neutered)
- $70/year (intact)
- Available online

### Vets
Many options throughout valley. Yelp reviews helpful.

### Emergencies
- Multiple 24-hour emergency vets
- Banfield, VCA networks

### Pet-Specific Concerns
- **Heat**: Pavement burns paws. Walk early morning only in summer.
- **Scorpions**: Possible in desert areas, dangerous to small pets
- **Pools**: Fence or supervise (drowning risk)
- **Coyotes**: Present in some areas (small pet risk)
