# Las Vegas Startup Scene

## Ecosystem Overview

Las Vegas has a small but growing startup ecosystem. It's not Austin or Denver yet, but there's real activity.

### Strengths
- Tax advantages (no state income tax)
- Lower operating costs than coastal cities
- Gaming/entertainment domain expertise
- CES brings global tech attention annually
- Growing remote worker population

### Weaknesses
- Limited local VC presence
- Smaller talent pool
- Less dense ecosystem
- Brain drain to larger markets
- Hospitality dominates economy

## Funding Landscape

### Local Investors

**Vegas Tech Fund**
- Early-stage focus
- Founded by Tony Hsieh's Downtown Project
- Entertainment/hospitality tech interest

**StartUpNV**
- Accelerator program
- Mentorship and connections
- Small funding amounts

### Regional Investors
Most significant funding comes from outside Nevada:
- Los Angeles VCs (proximity)
- Bay Area investors
- National generalist funds

### Typical Funding Path
1. **Bootstrapping/Friends & Family**: Common start
2. **Angel investors**: Local angels exist but limited
3. **Accelerators**: StartUpNV or out-of-state programs
4. **Seed/Series A**: Usually requires coastal VCs

### Funding Reality
For significant funding ($1M+), expect to pitch coastal VCs. Many Vegas startups relocate or establish dual presence.

## Legal & Incorporation

### Entity Formation
Nevada is LLC-friendly:
- No corporate income tax
- Strong asset protection
- Privacy features
- Fast formation

See `business.md` for detailed incorporation guide.

### Common Structure
Most Vegas startups:
- Nevada LLC or C-Corp
- Delaware C-Corp for seeking VC (industry standard)
- Registered agent in Nevada

### Startup Attorneys
Several firms specialize in startup law. Look for:
- Experience with tech companies
- Understanding of VC term sheets
- Pricing appropriate for early stage

## Realistic Expectations

### Timeline
Building a funded startup in Vegas typically takes:
- 6-12 months: Validate idea, build MVP
- 12-24 months: Seed funding (often requires coastal VCs)
- 24-36 months: Traction, possible Series A

### Likely Outcomes
- Many founders bootstrap longer than in SF/NY
- Coastal VC interest requires proven traction
- Gaming/entertainment adjacent has best local support
- Some successful founders relocate for scale

### What Works Best
1. **Gaming/entertainment focus**: Leverage local expertise
2. **B2B services**: Serving hospitality/gaming industry
3. **Remote-first**: Access broader talent and investor pools
4. **Bootstrapped growth**: Less pressure for VC funding

## Moving a Startup to Vegas

### Why Move Here
- Tax savings (founder income)
- Lower burn rate
- Quality of life for team
- Strategic for gaming/entertainment

### Considerations
- Investor access is harder
- Talent pool is smaller
- May need to maintain coastal presence
- Network needs rebuilding

### How to Do It
1. Research thoroughly (visit multiple times)
2. Establish Nevada entity
3. Move gradually (test the waters)
4. Build local network before fully committing
5. Keep investor relationships warm
