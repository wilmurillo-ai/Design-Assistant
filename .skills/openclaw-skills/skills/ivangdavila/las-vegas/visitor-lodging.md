# Las Vegas Lodging Guide

## Understanding Vegas Hotels

### The Resort Fee Reality
**Every major hotel charges resort fees** ($35-60/night) NOT included in advertised rates.

| What they claim it covers | Reality |
|---------------------------|---------|
| WiFi | Should be free anyway |
| Fitness center | You probably won't use it |
| Pool access | Pools are technically free to guests |
| Local calls | Who makes local calls? |
| Newspaper | Digital, worthless |

**True nightly cost** = Room rate + resort fee + tax (13.38% Clark County)
A "$99 room" actually costs ~$160-180/night.

### Location Strategy

The Strip is 4.2 miles long. Where you stay matters.

**South Strip (Mandalay Bay to Park MGM)**:
- Pros: Near airport, T-Mobile Arena, newer properties
- Cons: Far from Fremont, long walk to mid-Strip
- Best for: Concert-goers, families, quieter experience

**Mid-Strip (Aria to Caesars)**:
- Pros: Central to everything, walking distance to most attractions
- Cons: Most expensive, most crowded
- Best for: First-timers, those who want to walk everywhere

**North Strip (Venetian to Wynn)**:
- Pros: Upscale properties, near convention center
- Cons: Far from south Strip, some dead zones
- Best for: Convention attendees, luxury seekers

**Downtown (Fremont Street area)**:
- Pros: Cheaper, old Vegas vibe, better gambling odds
- Cons: 15-20 min Uber from Strip, grittier neighborhood
- Best for: Budget travelers, old Vegas fans, serious gamblers

## Hotels by Budget

### Ultra-Luxury ($400+/night)

**Wynn/Encore**
- The gold standard. Best service, most beautiful grounds.
- Rooms: Spacious, floor-to-ceiling windows
- Pool: Multiple pools, European pool available
- Worth it for: Special occasions, luxury seekers

**Bellagio**
- Classic Strip luxury, fountain views
- Rooms: Traditional luxury, recently renovated
- Pool: Mediterranean feel, adults-focused
- Worth it for: Romance, fountain experience

**Venetian/Palazzo**
- All-suite property (smallest = 650 sq ft)
- Rooms: Largest standard rooms on Strip
- Pool: Canyon Ranch spa, multiple pools
- Worth it for: Space, suite experience at "room" prices

**Cosmopolitan**
- Trendy, younger crowd, best mid-Strip location
- Rooms: Unique layouts, some with terraces
- Pool: Multiple pools, rooftop bar, ice rink in winter
- Worth it for: Nightlife lovers, Instagram aesthetics

### Upscale ($200-400/night)

**Aria**
- Modern, tech-forward, AAA Five Diamond
- Rooms: Automated controls, sleek design
- Pool: Three pools, great daybeds
- Worth it for: Modern aesthetic, convenience to T-Mobile Arena

**Mandalay Bay**
- Beach pool (sand!), Shark Reef, south Strip location
- Rooms: Solid quality, some with bay views
- Pool: 11-acre beach, wave pool, lazy river
- Worth it for: Families, pool lovers, concert venues

**Caesars Palace**
- Classic Vegas landmark, central location
- Rooms: Varies by tower (Forum Tower best)
- Pool: Garden of the Gods, multiple pools
- Worth it for: Classic Vegas experience, Forum Shops access

**MGM Grand**
- Massive property, great entertainment
- Rooms: Recently renovated, solid quality
- Pool: Multiple pools, lazy river
- Worth it for: Convention attendees, entertainment (David Copperfield, etc.)

### Mid-Range ($100-200/night)

**Park MGM**
- Boutique feel, quieter, good restaurants
- Rooms: Modern, clean, reasonable size
- Pool: Nice but small
- Worth it for: Those who want quality but hate mega-resort chaos

**New York-New York**
- Fun theme, roller coaster, central location
- Rooms: Smaller, some quirky layouts
- Pool: Rooftop, limited hours
- Worth it for: Budget mid-Strip, fun atmosphere

**The LINQ**
- Mid-Strip location, High Roller attached
- Rooms: Recently renovated, compact
- Pool: LINQ pool (seasonal)
- Worth it for: Location, price, young travelers

**Planet Hollywood**
- Central Strip, Miracle Mile Shops attached
- Rooms: Hollywood themed, varies by room type
- Pool: Decent, views of Strip
- Worth it for: Shopping lovers, central location on budget

### Budget ($50-150/night)

**Excalibur**
- Castle theme, connected to Luxor and Mandalay
- Rooms: Basic but clean
- Pool: Basic
- Worth it for: Families on budget, south Strip access

**Luxor**
- Pyramid icon, unique architecture
- Rooms: Pyramid rooms are slanted walls (weird), tower rooms normal
- Pool: Decent
- Worth it for: Unique experience, decent value

**Circus Circus**
- Old Vegas, Adventuredome theme park
- Rooms: Dated but functional
- Pool: Basic
- Worth it for: Families with kids, ultra-budget

**Off-Strip Properties** (Virgin, Palms, Rio):
- Usually $100-150 cheaper
- Uber/taxi costs add up
- Worth it if: Staying 4+ nights, have a car, want specific amenity (Palms pool)

### Downtown Options

**Circa**
- Newest downtown, adults-only, massive sportsbook
- Rooms: Modern, quality rivals Strip
- Pool: Stadium Swim (amphitheater pool)
- Worth it for: Sports fans, adults who prefer downtown

**Golden Nugget**
- Best quality downtown, shark tank pool
- Rooms: Rush Tower is excellent
- Pool: Shark tank, slides
- Worth it for: Downtown experience with Strip quality

**The D / Downtown Grand**
- Solid mid-range downtown options
- Worth it for: Budget downtown, walking to Fremont

## Room Type Strategy

### What Room Types Mean

| Room Type | What You Get |
|-----------|--------------|
| Standard/Deluxe | Basic room, interior or low floor |
| Strip View | Guaranteed view (worth $30-50 upgrade) |
| High Floor | Better views, less noise |
| Corner | More windows, more space |
| Suite | Separate living area (often worth it for groups) |
| Villa/Bungalow | Private, often poolside, ultra-luxury |

### When to Upgrade

**Worth the upgrade**:
- Strip view at Bellagio (fountain views)
- High floor at Wynn/Encore (floor-to-ceiling windows)
- Suite at Venetian (already huge, suite is absurd)

**Not worth the upgrade**:
- "Deluxe" rooms that are identical to standard
- Pool view (you'll be AT the pool, not looking at it)
- Premium rooms at budget properties

### The Art of the Free Upgrade

- **Join loyalty programs** before booking (MGM Rewards, Caesars Rewards, Wynn Rewards)
- **Book direct** (hotels prioritize direct bookers)
- **Check-in late afternoon** when rooms open up
- **Be nice** to front desk staff
- **Ask politely** "Is there any chance of an upgrade?" - worst they say is no
- **Tip $20-40** sandwiched with ID (the "$20 trick" - hit or miss, but sometimes works)

## Booking Strategy

### When to Book

| Timeframe | Room Rates |
|-----------|------------|
| Convention week | Sky-high |
| Holiday weekends | 2-3x normal |
| Summer weekdays | Cheapest |
| Weekend (any time) | 30-50% higher than weekday |
| Major events (F1, Super Bowl, NYE) | 3-5x normal |

### Where to Book

**Book direct if**:
- You want loyalty points
- You might need to cancel/modify
- You want to request specific room

**Use third parties if**:
- Price is significantly lower (10%+)
- You won't need to modify
- You're fine with non-refundable

**Best third-party sites**:
- Direct hotel sites (compare first)
- Expedia/Hotels.com (Marriott/Hilton alternative properties)
- Vegas.com (package deals)

### Hidden Costs Checklist

Before booking, add these to your budget:

| Fee | Typical Amount |
|-----|----------------|
| Resort fee | $35-60/night |
| Parking (self) | $0-18/day |
| Parking (valet) | $15-35/day |
| Tax | 13.38% of room + resort fee |

**Example**: "$150/night" room actually costs:
- $150 room + $50 resort fee = $200
- $200 x 13.38% tax = $227/night total

## Special Situations

### Convention Attendees
- Stay at convention host hotel if price reasonable
- LVCC is at north end of Strip (Wynn, Venetian, Encore closest)
- Monorail stops at LVCC
- Book 3-6 months ahead for major conventions

### Bachelor/Bachelorette Parties
- Need suite or connecting rooms for group
- Cosmopolitan, Venetian, Planet Hollywood popular
- Cabana rentals at pools (book weeks ahead)
- Some hotels have "party suite" packages

### Families
- Avoid Cosmopolitan (adults-focused)
- Excalibur, Circus Circus, Mandalay Bay best for kids
- Request rooms away from elevators and pool
- All-suite (Venetian) helps with kids

### Romantic Getaways
- Wynn, Bellagio, Cosmopolitan most romantic
- Request high floor, strip or fountain view
- Book spa treatments in advance
- Gondola suite at Venetian is cliche but works

### Accessibility
- All major properties ADA compliant
- Request accessible room when booking
- Pools vary in accessibility (check specific hotel)
- Monorail is accessible; some pedestrian bridges are not

## What to Avoid

- **Rooms facing construction** (check Trip Advisor for current projects)
- **Connecting rooms you didn't request** (noise from neighbors)
- **Ground floor "garden view"** (people walking by your window)
- **Newly renovated if you have allergies** (off-gassing from new materials)
- **Properties during major renovations** (always verify before booking)
