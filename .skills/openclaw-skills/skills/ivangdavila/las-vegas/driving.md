# Driving & Car Ownership in Las Vegas

## Car Ownership is Essential

Let's be clear: **you need a car in Las Vegas.** The city is designed around driving. Public transit is inadequate for most lifestyles.

## Buying a Car

### New vs Used
- **New**: Dealer network strong, financing available
- **Used**: Good selection, CarMax, Carvana, dealers
- **Private sale**: Craigslist, Facebook (be careful)

### Registration
Nevada registration is based on vehicle value:
- New cars: $300-600+ first year
- Depreciates over time
- Due annually on birthday
- Can renew online

### Sales Tax
- 8.375% in Clark County
- Applies to vehicle purchases

## Insurance

### Requirements
Nevada requires:
- 25/50/20 liability minimum
- Actually enforced

### Reality
- Vegas has high accident rates
- Insurance costs more than national average
- Typical cost: $150-250/month
- Uninsured motorist coverage: Recommended
- Comprehensive: Recommended (hail, theft)

### Getting Quotes
- Major carriers all present
- Compare multiple quotes
- Consider bundling with home/renters

## Driving Conditions

### Traffic Patterns
**Rush hour** (7-9am, 4-7pm):
- I-15: Brutal, especially near Strip
- I-215: Heavy but moves
- US-95: Bad near downtown
- Surface streets: Poorly timed lights

**Event days**:
- Raiders games: South Strip chaos
- UFC/concerts: T-Mobile area congestion
- Conventions: I-15 near LVCC backed up

### Driving Culture
- Fast drivers
- Aggressive lane changes
- Running red lights (common, dangerous)
- Distracted driving (tourists, phones)
- DUI risk (especially near Strip at night)

### Summer Driving
- AC is essential (non-negotiable)
- Fuel consumption higher (AC running constantly)
- Touch no metal surfaces
- Sunshade for windshield
- Park in shade when possible

## Gas

### Prices
Nevada gas is higher than national average:
- Regular: $3.50-4.50/gallon
- Premium: $4.00-5.00/gallon
- Diesel: Similar to premium

### Saving Money
- Costco/Sam's Club (membership pays off)
- GasBuddy app
- Avoid Strip and airport stations
- Off-brand stations (same quality)

## Maintenance

### Heat Takes a Toll
Vegas heat is brutal on vehicles:

| Component | Life Expectancy | Notes |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| Battery | 2-3 years | Heat kills batteries |
| Tires | Faster wear | Check pressure frequently |
| Wiper blades | 6-12 months | Sun degrades rubber |
| Coolant | Regular checks | Overheating risk |
| AC | Annual service | Don't skip this |

### Finding Mechanics
- Dealers: Expensive but reliable
- Independents: Good options exist
- Quick service: Jiffy Lube, Valvoline, etc.
- Mobile mechanics: Growing option

### Essential Maintenance
- AC servicing (annual, critical)
- Coolant flush (per manufacturer)
- Tire rotation (heat = uneven wear)
- Battery testing (frequent)

## Tips & Tricks

### Surviving Summer
- Remote start is worth it (cool car before entering)
- Windshield sunshade (essential)
- Tinted windows (reduces heat, legal limits apply)
- Never leave people/pets in car
- Carry water in vehicle always

### Commute Optimization
- Know alternate routes (I-15 backup = surface streets)
- Flexible hours help (avoid peak rush)
- Remote work is the best commute
- Podcasts/audiobooks for the time

### Parking Strategy
- Strip: Know free and cheap options
- Casino apps: Some offer free parking for rewards members
- Consider Uber for Strip if parking is expensive

## DMV Process

### Getting Nevada License
**Within 30 days of establishing residency.**

Required:
- Proof of identity (passport, birth certificate)
- Proof of SSN
- Proof of Nevada residence (2 documents)
- Out-of-state license (if transferring)

Process:
1. Make appointment online (essential)
2. Bring all documents
3. Vision test
4. Written test (waived if transferring valid license)
5. Receive temporary, card mailed

### Vehicle Registration
**Within 30 days of establishing residency.**

Required:
- Title or out-of-state registration
- Proof of Nevada insurance
- Smog check (Nevada emissions test)
- VIN inspection (form provided)

Cost: Based on vehicle value ($300-600+ typical for newer cars)

### Tips
- Appointments strongly recommended
- Bring all documents (missing one = another trip)
- Smaller offices (Henderson, Summerlin) often faster

## Electric Vehicles

### EV in Vegas
- Charging network growing
- Many apartments lack charging
- Tesla Superchargers numerous
- NV Energy has some incentives

### Considerations
- No EV state tax credit (Nevada)
- Federal credits may apply
- Battery performance in heat (factor to consider)
- Road trip charging infrastructure improving

## Rideshare vs Ownership

### Math
Car ownership: $600-1,000+/month all-in
Rideshare only: Possible but expensive for daily use

### Verdict
- **Own a car** if you live and work here full-time
- **Rideshare** if visiting or rarely need to travel
- **Hybrid approach** for some (cheap car + rideshare for nights out)
