# Celebrity Chef Restaurants in Las Vegas

## The Celebrity Chef Capital

Las Vegas has more celebrity chef restaurants per square mile than any city in the world. Nearly every famous chef has planted a flag here.

## Gordon Ramsay (5 Vegas Restaurants)

### Hell's Kitchen (Caesars Palace)
- **Concept**: The TV show experience
- **Must-order**: Beef Wellington, sticky toffee pudding
- **Price**: $$$$ ($100-150/person)
- **Vibe**: Red vs blue kitchen, theatrical
- **Reservations**: Essential, 2-3 weeks out
- **Worth it?**: Yes for fans of the show; food is legitimately good

### Gordon Ramsay Steak (Paris)
- **Concept**: High-end steakhouse
- **Must-order**: Beef Wellington, any steak
- **Price**: $$$$ ($150-200/person)
- **Vibe**: Elegant, tunnel entrance, Vegas views
- **Worth it?**: Among the better Strip steakhouses

### Gordon Ramsay Fish & Chips (LINQ)
- **Concept**: Casual British
- **Must-order**: Fish and chips (obviously)
- **Price**: $$ ($25-40/person)
- **Vibe**: Quick-service casual
- **Worth it?**: Good quick meal, not destination dining

### Gordon Ramsay Burger (Planet Hollywood)
- **Concept**: Upscale burgers
- **Must-order**: Hells Kitchen Burger
- **Price**: $$ ($30-50/person)
- **Worth it?**: Better than average chain, convenient

### Gordon Ramsay Pub & Grill (Caesars)
- **Concept**: British pub elevated
- **Must-order**: Shepherd's Pie, Scotch Egg
- **Price**: $$$ ($50-80/person)
- **Worth it?**: Good for British pub food cravings

## Wolfgang Puck (5+ Vegas Restaurants)

### CUT (Palazzo)
- **Concept**: Modern steakhouse
- **Must-order**: 35-day dry-aged ribeye, bone marrow flan
- **Price**: $$$$ ($150-250/person)
- **Vibe**: Sleek, Richard Meier-designed
- **Worth it?**: One of the best steakhouses in Vegas

### Spago (Bellagio)
- **Concept**: California cuisine flagship
- **Must-order**: Smoked salmon pizza, any seasonal special
- **Price**: $$$ ($80-120/person)
- **Vibe**: Patio overlooking fountains
- **Worth it?**: Classic Vegas dining, great views

### Wolfgang Puck Bar & Grill (MGM)
- **Concept**: Casual Puck
- **Price**: $$$ ($50-80/person)
- **Worth it?**: Good quality, accessible

## José Andrés (3 Vegas Restaurants)

### Bazaar Meat (SLS/Sahara)
- **Concept**: Avant-garde carnivore experience
- **Must-order**: Cotton candy foie gras, José's Wagyu, any fire-cooked meat
- **Price**: $$$$ ($150-250/person)
- **Vibe**: Theatrical, innovative, fun
- **Worth it?**: ABSOLUTELY — one of Vegas's best dining experiences

### é by José Andrés (Cosmopolitan)
- **Concept**: 8-seat counter, tasting menu
- **Must-order**: Whatever they're serving (no menu)
- **Price**: $$$$$ ($300-400/person)
- **Vibe**: Intimate, experimental, mind-blowing
- **Reservations**: Released monthly, sells out in minutes
- **Worth it?**: Bucket-list dining if you can get in

### Jaleo (Cosmopolitan)
- **Concept**: Spanish tapas
- **Must-order**: Jamón, paella, patatas bravas
- **Price**: $$$ ($60-100/person)
- **Worth it?**: Fun group dining, authentic tapas

## Reservation Strategy

### Hardest to Get
- é by José Andrés (releases monthly, gone in minutes)
- Hell's Kitchen (2-3 weeks, weekends tough)
- Carbone (not technically celeb chef, but same difficulty)

### Easier Than Expected
- CUT (weekday lunch)
- Bouchon (except brunch)
- Most Bobby Flay/Guy Fieri spots

### Tips
- Book direct through restaurant when possible
- OpenTable, Resy work for most
- Hotel concierge can sometimes help guests
- Lunch is ALWAYS easier than dinner
- Sunday-Tuesday easier than Fri-Sat
