# Las Vegas Itineraries

## Before Planning

**Key principles**:
- Vegas distances deceive. Walking the full Strip = 4+ miles in desert heat.
- Pace yourself. It's a marathon, not a sprint.
- Everything costs more and takes longer than expected.
- Summer itineraries must account for heat (outdoor activities early morning or skip).

## Weekend Warrior (2-3 Days)

### Day 1: Strip Immersion

**Morning (10am-1pm)**:
- Start at your hotel, explore the casino floor
- Walk to Bellagio Conservatory (free, seasonal botanical display)
- Coffee at a Strip cafe

**Afternoon (1pm-6pm)**:
- Lunch at a celebrity chef restaurant (see `food-celebrity.md`)
- Walk The Strip south: Paris (Eiffel Tower replica), Cosmopolitan, Aria
- Pool time at your hotel (most pools open until 6pm)

**Evening (6pm-late)**:
- Bellagio Fountains at sunset
- Dinner reservation 7-8pm
- Show (Cirque, residency, comedy - see `visitor-shows.md`)
- Late night: Wander casinos, gamble, bar hop

**Budget estimate**: $200-500 (excluding hotel, depending on dining/show choices)

### Day 2: Beyond The Strip

**Morning (8am-12pm)**:
- Brunch at Hash House A Go Go, The Egg & I, or similar
- Uber to Downtown (15 min)
- Mob Museum (2-3 hours) - speakeasy in basement for lunch drink

**Afternoon (12pm-5pm)**:
- Walk Fremont Street (daytime is calmer)
- Container Park (shops, bars, fire-breathing mantis)
- Neon Museum (book night tour if available, otherwise daytime)

**Evening (5pm-late)**:
- Return to Strip for dinner
- Different casino exploration: Wynn/Encore, Venetian
- Nightlife: Club or lounge (see `visitor-tips.md` for club strategy)

### Day 3: Choose Your Adventure

**Option A: Nature (cooler months only)**:
- Red Rock Canyon (leave 7am, return by 1pm)
- Afternoon pool recovery
- Final dinner splurge

**Option B: Day Party**:
- Sleep in
- Pool party/dayclub (Wet Republic, Encore Beach Club)
- Early dinner
- Late flight out

**Option C: Culture**:
- Springs Preserve (nature/history museum)
- Lunch in Arts District
- Seven Magic Mountains (outdoor art installation, 30 min from Strip)
- Final Strip evening

## Full Week Experience (5-7 Days)

### Days 1-2: Strip Deep Dive
Follow weekend itinerary above, but slower pace. Add:
- High Roller at sunset (Day 1)
- Gondola ride at Venetian (Day 2)

### Day 3: Downtown & History
- Morning: Neon Museum (night if possible, book ahead)
- Afternoon: Mob Museum
- Evening: Fremont Street Experience, Container Park, downtown bars
- Late night: Downtown casinos (looser slots, lower minimums)

### Day 4: Nature Day
Pick based on season and interest:

| Option | Distance | Best Season | Time Needed |
|--------|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Red Rock Canyon | 30 min | Oct-Apr | 4-6 hours |
| Valley of Fire | 1 hour | Oct-Apr | 4-6 hours |
| Hoover Dam | 45 min | Year-round | 3-4 hours |
| Grand Canyon West | 2.5 hours | Year-round | Full day |

Return by 5pm for pool/spa recovery, casual dinner.

### Day 5: Relaxation & Shopping
- Sleep in
- Hotel spa morning
- Lunch at Fashion Show Mall or Forum Shops
- Afternoon: Pool or shopping
- Evening: Nice dinner, show you haven't seen

### Day 6: Off-Strip Exploration
- Morning: Springs Preserve or Atomic Testing Museum
- Lunch: Local spot off-Strip (see `food-local.md`)
- Afternoon: Arts District (First Friday if timing works)
- Evening: Different Strip section, new restaurants/bars

### Day 7: Departure Day
- Brunch with friends
- Final casino walk-through
- Airport 3 hours before flight (security can be slow)

## Bachelor/Bachelorette Weekend

### The Classic Bach Experience

**Day 1 (Friday)**:
- Afternoon arrival, check-in
- Group dinner at celebrity chef spot
- Pregame at hotel suite/bar
- Club night (reserve table for groups)

**Day 2 (Saturday)**:
- Late brunch (11am-1pm)
- Pool party/dayclub (2pm-6pm)
- Recovery nap
- Group activity: Top Golf, escape room, ax throwing
- Dinner
- Second club night or bar crawl

**Day 3 (Sunday)**:
- Very late brunch
- Pool recovery
- Early departure

**Key bookings needed**:
- Club table reservation (essential for groups)
- Dayclub entry or cabana
- Dinner reservations for 8+ people (book way ahead)
- Group activity

**Budget reality**: $500-1500 per person for the weekend (not including flights/hotel)

### Less Intense Alternative
Skip clubs, focus on:
- Nice dinners
- Shows (comedy, magic, Cirque)
- Pool time
- Golf at a nice course
- Day trip (Red Rock, Hoover Dam)

## Family-Friendly Itinerary

### With Kids (All Ages)

**Day 1**:
- Circus Circus Adventuredome (indoor theme park)
- Excalibur Tournament of Kings dinner show
- Fremont Street (early evening, before it gets rowdy)

**Day 2**:
- Shark Reef at Mandalay Bay (morning)
- Pool time (Mandalay Beach or Excalibur pool)
- Gondola ride at Venetian
- Marvel Avengers STATION or Bodies exhibit

**Day 3**:
- Discovery Children's Museum (Downtown)
- Springs Preserve
- Early dinner, early night

**Best family hotels**:
- Excalibur (medieval theme, affordable)
- Circus Circus (theme park, carnival)
- Mandalay Bay (beach pool, Shark Reef)
- Wynn/Encore (upscale family experience)

**What to skip with kids**:
- Anything after 10pm on Strip
- Pool parties (adults only)
- Most shows (age restrictions)
- Extended casino floor time (boring for kids, not always allowed)

## Seasonal Itinerary Adjustments

### Summer (June-August)
**Modify all itineraries**:
- No outdoor activities after 10am or before 6pm
- Pool parties become primary daytime activity
- Indoor attractions only (museums, shopping, casinos)
- Hydrate constantly

### Winter Holidays (Dec-Jan)
- Add: Ice skating at Cosmopolitan
- Add: Holiday decorations (Bellagio Conservatory Christmas theme)
- Book everything way ahead (peak season)
- Expect higher prices and crowds

### March Madness
- Sportsbook watching is A-list activity
- Book tables/seats at sports-focused venues
- Circa downtown has best sportsbook
- Everything crowded with basketball fans

### F1 Weekend (November)
- Strip partially closed for race
- Walking access complicated
- Prices astronomical
- Book 6+ months ahead or skip entirely

## Transportation Strategy by Itinerary

| Activity | Best Transport |
|----------|----------------|
| Within Strip hotel cluster | Walk |
| Strip to Downtown | Uber/Lyft ($15-20) or RTC Deuce bus ($6 24-hr pass) |
| Red Rock/Valley of Fire | Rental car essential |
| Hoover Dam | Rental car or organized tour |
| Airport | Uber/Lyft ($15-25) or hotel shuttle |

**Rental car worth it if**:
- Doing 2+ day trips
- Staying at off-Strip hotel
- Group of 3+ (splitting costs)
- F1/major event weekend (Uber surge pricing brutal)

## Budgeting Reality

### Per Person Per Day (Average)

| Style | Daily Budget |
|-------|--------------|
| Budget | $150-200 |
| Moderate | $300-400 |
| Comfortable | $500-700 |
| Luxury | $1,000+ |

**What's included**: Food, entertainment, transport, gambling budget, tips. NOT hotel.

### Where Money Goes

| Category | Budget | Moderate | Luxury |
|----------|--------|----------|--------|
| Food | $50 | $150 | $300+ |
| Drinks | $30 | $75 | $150+ |
| Shows/Entertainment | $0-50 | $100-200 | $300+ |
| Gambling | $50 | $100 | Unlimited |
| Transport | $20 | $40 | $100+ |
| Tips | $20 | $40 | $100+ |

**Hidden costs to budget for**:
- Resort fees: $35-60/night
- Parking: $0-25/day
- Club cover/table: $50-500+
- Pool chair/cabana: $20-500
- ATM fees: $5-8 per withdrawal
