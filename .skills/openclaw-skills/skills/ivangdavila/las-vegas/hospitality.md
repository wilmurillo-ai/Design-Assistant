# Hospitality Careers in Las Vegas

## The Hospitality Economy

Las Vegas's economy is built on hospitality. This creates unusual career opportunities.

**Key facts**:
- ~30% of jobs are hospitality-related
- Tourism drives the economy
- Tips can be substantial
- Union jobs offer benefits
- Career paths to six figures exist

## Casino Jobs

### Dealers
**The iconic Vegas career path.**

**Earnings**:
- Base pay: $8-15/hour
- Tips: $20-40+/hour on busy tables
- Total: $60,000-100,000+/year for experienced dealers

**How to become a dealer**:
1. Attend dealing school (6-12 weeks, $500-2,000)
2. Learn 2-3 games (blackjack, poker, craps, roulette)
3. Audition at casinos
4. Start at locals casino or break-in house
5. Move up to Strip properties

**Best paying**:
- High-limit rooms
- Poker (tournament dealing)
- Baccarat
- Strip properties

**Dealing schools**:
- PCI Dealer School
- Casino Gaming School
- CEG Dealer School
- Various others

### Slot Technicians
**Maintaining slot machines.**

**Earnings**: $40,000-70,000/year

**Requirements**:
- Technical aptitude
- Training programs available
- Electronics background helpful

### Casino Hosts
**VIP relationship management.**

**Earnings**: $50,000-150,000+ (base + bonuses)

**Requirements**:
- Sales/relationship skills
- Often promoted from within
- Must be 21+
- Gaming license required

### Surveillance
**Security and monitoring.**

**Earnings**: $35,000-60,000/year

**Requirements**:
- Attention to detail
- Gaming knowledge helpful
- Background check extensive

### Casino Management
**Operations, marketing, finance.**

**Earnings**: $60,000-200,000+ (varies by role)

**Path**: Usually internal promotion or hospitality management degree

## Hotel Jobs

### Front Desk
**Guest services and check-in.**

**Earnings**: $30,000-45,000/year (tips variable)

**Path**: Entry-level, good starting point

### Concierge
**Guest assistance and recommendations.**

**Earnings**: $35,000-55,000/year + tips

**Requirements**:
- Local knowledge
- Service orientation
- Problem-solving

### Hotel Management
**Operations oversight.**

**Earnings**: $50,000-150,000+ (varies by property/level)

**Path**: Hospitality degree or internal promotion

### Housekeeping
**Room cleaning and maintenance.**

**Earnings**: $30,000-45,000/year

**Notes**: Union properties offer better wages and benefits

## Food & Beverage

### Bartenders
**Strip bartenders can earn well.**

**Earnings**:
- Base: $10-15/hour
- Tips: $30-60+/hour (busy shifts, good spots)
- Total: $60,000-120,000+/year at top venues

**Best positions**:
- High-end clubs (XS, Omnia)
- Celebrity chef restaurants
- Pool parties
- Busy Strip bars

### Servers
**Restaurant serving positions.**

**Earnings**:
- Fine dining: $70,000-150,000+/year
- Casual dining: $40,000-60,000/year
- Buffet: $30,000-50,000/year

**Best opportunities**:
- Celebrity chef restaurants
- Steakhouses
- High-end Asian restaurants

### Cocktail Servers
**Casino floor service.**

**Earnings**: $50,000-80,000+/year (tips)

**Requirements**:
- 21+
- Often appearance standards
- Stamina (walking all shift)

### Sommeliers
**Wine service in fine dining.**

**Earnings**: $60,000-100,000+/year

**Requirements**:
- Sommelier certification
- Extensive wine knowledge
- Sales ability

### Kitchen Positions
| Position | Annual Earnings |

## Nightlife

### Club Promoters
**Bringing guests to venues.**

**Earnings**: Commission-based, highly variable

**Notes**: Not as lucrative as it appears for most

### Bottle Service/VIP Hosts
**Managing VIP sections.**

**Earnings**: $50,000-150,000+ (tips/commission)

**Requirements**:
- Sales skills
- Network building
- Often appearance-focused

### DJs
**Resident and guest DJs.**

**Earnings**: Highly variable ($500-500,000+ per appearance)

**Notes**: Top DJs earn millions; most earn modest amounts

### Security
**Club and casino security.**

**Earnings**: $35,000-55,000/year

**Requirements**:
- Guard card
- Physical fitness
- De-escalation skills

## Union Membership

### Culinary Union (Local 226)
**The dominant hospitality union.**

**What it offers**:
- Higher wages
- Health insurance
- Pension benefits
- Job protection

**Industries**: Hotels, casinos, restaurants

**How to join**: Get hired at union property, then join

### Other Unions
- Bartenders Union (Local 165)
- Stagehand Union (IATSE)
- Various trade unions

## Career Paths

### Entry to High Earner
**Dealing example**:
1. Dealing school (6-12 weeks)
2. Break-in house/locals casino (1-2 years)
3. Mid-tier Strip property (2-3 years)
4. High-limit room or top property (ongoing)
5. Earnings: $40K → $60K → $80K → $100K+

**Server example**:
1. Casual dining experience
2. Fine dining host/food runner
3. Fine dining server (celebrity restaurant)
4. Head server or captain
5. Earnings: $30K → $50K → $80K → $120K+

### Management Track
**Hospitality management degree** + Entry role → Supervisor → Manager → Director

### The Hustle Reality
Many high-earning hospitality workers:
- Work multiple jobs
- Take extra shifts during conventions
- Build regular customer relationships
- Continually improve skills

## Getting Started

### With No Experience
1. Apply for entry-level positions
2. Target locals casinos (more entry-level friendly)
3. Get trained and gain experience
4. Move up to better properties

### With Experience
1. Research best employers (union, benefits, earnings)
2. Network with current employees
3. Apply during hiring pushes (new properties, expansions)
4. Negotiate based on experience

### Best Times to Apply
- New property openings
- Convention season staffing (fall/winter)
- After holiday turnover (January)

## Quality of Life

### Schedule Reality
- Nights, weekends, holidays are when money is made
- Irregular schedules common
- Seniority determines schedule preference
- Work-life balance challenging for some

### Physical Demands
- Standing for full shifts
- Walking miles daily
- Carrying heavy items (servers)
- Heat exposure (pool venues)

### Social Life
- Work when others play
- Build friendships with coworkers
- Daytime freedom (if working nights)
- Can feel isolated from "normal" schedule friends

## Making It Work

### Maximizing Earnings
- Work the best shifts (weekends, conventions)
- Build regular customer relationships
- Upsell naturally
- Stay current on industry knowledge
- Network constantly

### Sustainability
- Take care of your body
- Save during good times
- Develop backup skills
- Plan for career evolution
- Consider management track eventually

### Exit Strategies
Common transitions:
- Hospitality management
- Real estate
- Sales (other industries)
- Entrepreneurship
- Training/education
