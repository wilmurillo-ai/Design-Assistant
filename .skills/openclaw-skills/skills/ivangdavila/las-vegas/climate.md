# Las Vegas Climate & Weather

## The Desert Reality

Las Vegas has a hot desert climate (Köppen: BWh). It's one of the hottest cities in the United States.

**Key facts**:
- 300+ sunny days per year
- Less than 5 inches of rain annually
- Extreme temperature swings (day to night)
- Summer is genuinely dangerous heat
- Winter is mild and pleasant

## Understanding Vegas Heat

### Why It's Different
- **Dry heat**: Lower humidity makes it feel slightly more tolerable
- **Intense sun**: High altitude desert = stronger UV
- **Pavement heat**: Concrete/asphalt radiate heat up to 150°F
- **No shade**: Sparse vegetation, minimal natural cover

### Summer Reality (June-August)
- **Daily highs**: 100-115°F (38-46°C)
- **Morning lows**: 75-85°F (still hot)
- **Peak heat**: 2-6pm
- **Tolerable hours**: Before 9am, after 8pm

**What 110°F feels like**:
- Oppressive, heavy heat
- Metal surfaces too hot to touch
- Car interiors dangerous (140°F+)
- Outdoor exercise nearly impossible
- 5 minutes in sun = sweating

### Heat Dangers

**Heat exhaustion** (moderate):
- Heavy sweating
- Weakness, dizziness
- Nausea
- Headache
- **Action**: Get to AC, hydrate, rest

**Heat stroke** (emergency):
- Body temp above 103°F
- Confusion, altered mental state
- Hot, red, dry skin (stopped sweating)
- Rapid pulse
- **Action**: Call 911, cool person immediately

**Who dies from Vegas heat**:
- Hikers who underestimate
- Homeless individuals
- Elderly without AC
- Children/pets left in cars
- Workers without breaks

### Surviving Summer

**Hydration**:
- Drink water constantly (before you're thirsty)
- 8-12 glasses minimum on hot days
- Electrolytes if sweating heavily
- Avoid excessive alcohol/caffeine

**Behavior**:
- Stay indoors 11am-6pm when possible
- Exercise at dawn or late evening only
- Use AC liberally (not the time to save electricity)
- Park in shade; use sunshades
- Never leave people/pets in cars

**Dress**:
- Light colors
- Lightweight, breathable fabrics
- Wide-brimmed hat
- Sunglasses

**Sun protection**:
- SPF 30+ (reapply every 2 hours)
- Lip balm with SPF
- UV index often 10+ (extreme)

## Winter Weather

### What to Expect
- **Days**: 50-65°F, pleasant and sunny
- **Nights**: 35-45°F, sometimes near freezing
- **Snow**: Rarely in valley; common on Mt Charleston

**Packing for winter visit**:
- Light jacket or sweater
- One warmer layer for evening
- Closed shoes
- No heavy winter gear needed (usually)

### Cold snaps
Occasionally temperatures drop below freezing at night. Unusual but happens.

## Monsoon Season (July-August)

### The Arizona Monsoon Effect
Moisture from the Gulf of California moves into the Southwest, causing:
- Sudden thunderstorms
- Dramatic lightning
- Brief, intense rainfall
- Flash flooding

### Flash Flood Danger

**CRITICAL SAFETY**:
- Desert soil doesn't absorb water
- Washes flood within minutes
- Walls of water can be 6+ feet
- **Never drive through flooded roads**
- People die in flash floods every year

**The rule**: "Turn around, don't drown"

### Watching for Storms
- Check weather before hiking
- Storms build quickly in afternoon
- Dark clouds + thunder = get inside
- Flash flood warnings are serious

## Wind

### Average Conditions
- Generally light to moderate
- Spring tends to be windiest
- Dust storms possible

### High Wind Events
- Occasional gusts 40-60mph
- Pool furniture becomes projectile
- Outdoor events may cancel
- Dust reduces visibility

## Air Quality

### General
Las Vegas air quality is moderate. The valley can trap pollution.

**Best air**: Winter after rain
**Worst air**: Summer inversions, fire season

### Fire Season
California fires occasionally send smoke into the valley. Check air quality during fire season (late summer/fall).

### Dust
Construction and desert conditions create dust. Consider this if you have respiratory issues.

## Seasonal Living

### Summer Lifestyle (June-September)
- Indoor focus (AC everything)
- Pool as primary activity
- Early morning outdoor activities (before 9am)
- Late evening walks (after 8pm)
- Higher electricity bills
- Travel to cooler places popular

### Winter Lifestyle (November-February)
- Peak hiking season
- Outdoor dining possible
- Golf season
- Day trips to Red Rock, Valley of Fire
- Mt Charleston for snow
- Best time to explore

### Shoulder Seasons (March-May, October)
- Best weather of the year
- Perfect outdoor activity time
- Pool season begins/ends
- Tourism peaks in spring

## What to Expect If Moving Here

### First Summer
You will question your decision. The heat is shocking if you're not from a hot climate. It does get easier after the first year (sort of).

**Survival tips for new residents**:
- Keep your car fuel above 1/4 tank (AC uses gas)
- Remote start is worth it
- Park in garages when possible
- Join a gym with a pool
- Plan summer trips to cooler places

### Clothing Adjustments
- You'll own more shorts and sandals
- Office appropriate warm-weather clothing
- One jacket handles all of "winter"
- Sunglasses become essential accessory

### Home Considerations
- AC maintenance is critical (annual tune-up)
- Window tinting helps
- Blackout curtains in bedrooms
- Pool adds value and lifestyle

## Climate Change Considerations

### Observed Trends
- Summers getting hotter
- Record temperatures more frequent
- Extended heat seasons
- Water supply stress (Lake Mead)

### Future Projections
- More extreme heat days expected
- Continued water challenges
- May affect long-term livability
- Mitigation efforts ongoing

### What This Means
- Vegas will likely get hotter
- Water conservation will increase
- Energy costs may rise
- Good reason to prioritize energy-efficient housing
