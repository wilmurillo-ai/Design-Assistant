# Getting Around Las Vegas

## The Car-Centric Reality

**Let's be clear**: Las Vegas is designed for cars. Public transit exists but is inadequate for most lifestyles. If you live here, you need a car.

## Driving

### Traffic Patterns

**Rush hour** (7-9am, 4-7pm):
- I-15 (north-south): Brutal, especially near Strip
- I-215 (beltway): Heavy but moves
- US-95 (northwest): Bad near downtown
- Surface streets: Poorly timed lights, frustrating

**Best times to drive**:
- Before 7am
- 9:30am-3:30pm
- After 7pm

**Worst times**:
- Friday afternoon/evening (weekend arrivals)
- Sunday afternoon (departures)
- Event nights (UFC, concerts, Raiders games)
- Convention weeks (CES is the worst)

### Commute Realities

| From | To Strip | To Downtown | Notes |
|------|----------|-------------|-------|
| Summerlin | 20-40 min | 20-30 min | I-215 to I-15 |
| Henderson | 20-40 min | 25-35 min | I-215 or I-15 |
| North LV | 20-40 min | 15-25 min | I-15 or US-95 |
| Spring Valley | 10-25 min | 15-25 min | Surface streets or I-15 |
| Paradise | 5-20 min | 15-20 min | Varies greatly |

**Double these times during rush hour and events.**

### Parking

**Strip hotels**:
- Self-parking: $15-25/day
- Valet: $20-35/day
- Free: Circus Circus, Treasure Island (as of 2024)

**Downtown**:
- Casino parking: Often free with validation
- Street meters: $2-4/hour
- Lots: $5-15/day

**Residential**:
- Most have garage or parking spot
- Street parking in neighborhoods is usually fine
- Some areas have permit restrictions

### Gas Prices
Nevada gas is often above the national average because Clark County taxes and tourism demand add cost.
Budget with a range, not a single number, and check current stations before long drives.

Cheapest stations: Costco, Sam's Club, off-Strip locations

## Public Transit

### RTC (Regional Transportation Commission)

**The Deuce**:
- Double-decker bus running entire Strip + Downtown
- Fare: $6 (2-hour all-access), $8 (24-hour), $20 (3-day)
- Frequency: Every 15-20 minutes
- Hours: 24 hours
- Reality: Slow but covers Strip well. Tourists mostly.

**SDX (Strip & Downtown Express)**:
- Faster limited-stop service
- Same fare as Deuce
- Runs less frequently

**Local buses**:
- Cover metro area
- Fare: $2 single ride, $3 (2-hour), $5 (24-hour)
- Frequency: 15-60 minutes depending on route
- Reality: Adequate for some commutes, slow and infrequent for most

### Using RTC

**For tourists**: Deuce is fine for Strip exploration
**For residents**: Supplemental at best, not primary transport

**RTC app**: Real-time tracking, mobile fare

### Monorail

- Runs behind east side of Strip (MGM Grand to Sahara)
- Fare: about $6 single, with day-pass pricing changing by promo/channel
- Hours: 7am-midnight (later weekends)
- Reality: Stations are far from hotel entrances. Only useful for specific trips.

**Useful for**: Convention center, moving between east-side casinos
**Not useful for**: General Strip transportation, anything west of Strip

### Trams (Free)

**Free trams connect**:
- Mandalay Bay ↔ Excalibur (with Luxor stop)
- Bellagio ↔ Park MGM (via Aria, Crystals)

**Use these**: They save walking in the heat.

## Rideshare

### Uber/Lyft

**Availability**: Excellent throughout metro area

**Typical fares**:
| Route | Off-Peak | Surge |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Airport ↔ Strip | $15-25 | $30-50 |
| Strip ↔ Downtown | $15-20 | $25-40 |
| Strip ↔ Summerlin | $25-35 | $40-60 |
| Within Strip | $8-15 | $15-25 |

**Surge pricing**: Common during events, late night (2-4am), convention weeks

**Airport pickup**: Follow signs to rideshare area (not at terminal)

**Strip pickup**: Designated rideshare areas at hotels (ask staff)

### When Rideshare Makes Sense
- Airport transfers
- Drinking nights
- Quick Strip hops
- When parking would cost more

### When It Doesn't
- Daily commuting (adds up fast)
- During major events (surge insanity)
- Regular errands (get a car)

## Taxis

### Traditional Cabs
Still exist but largely replaced by rideshare.

**When to use**:
- Airport taxi line (sometimes faster than rideshare wait)
- If rideshare is surging extremely high
- Hotel taxi stands

**Rates**:
- $3.50 pickup + $2.76/mile
- Airport fee: $2
- Tip: 15-20%

**Reality**: Rideshare is usually cheaper and more convenient.

## Biking

### The Reality
Las Vegas is NOT a cycling city for transportation.

**Challenges**:
- Summer heat (literally dangerous)
- Distances (everything is spread out)
- Infrastructure (bike lanes limited)
- Drivers (not bike-aware)

**When biking works**:
- Cooler months (Oct-Apr)
- Recreational trails (River Mountains Loop, Red Rock)
- Very short neighborhood trips
- Downtown Arts District (flat, concentrated)

### Bike Share
RTC Bike Share exists but usage is minimal.

### E-Scooters
Lime, Bird, etc. scattered around Strip and Downtown.
Good for: Short Strip trips
Bad for: Summer, any distance

## Walking

### On The Strip
**Possible but understand**:
- The Strip is 4.2 miles long
- Casinos are HUGE (walking through one takes 10+ minutes)
- Pedestrian bridges are numerous (use them)
- Summer heat makes walking miserable

**Strip walk times** (casino to casino):
| From | To | Walking Time |
|------|-----|--------------|
| Mandalay Bay | Luxor | 10-15 min |
| Bellagio | Caesars | 15-20 min |
| Venetian | Wynn | 10-15 min |
| MGM | Venetian | 35-45 min |

### Downtown
More walkable than Strip. Fremont Street is pedestrian only.

### Neighborhoods
Most Vegas neighborhoods are NOT walkable for errands. Strip malls, wide roads, limited sidewalks.

**Exceptions**:
- Downtown Arts District
- Downtown Summerlin (small area)
- Some older neighborhoods

## Airport Transportation

### Harry Reid International (LAS)

**Location**: 5 miles from Strip (very close)

**Getting to/from**:

| Method | Cost | Time | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-------|
| Uber/Lyft | $15-25 | 10-20 min | Pickup at designated area |
| Taxi | $20-30 | 10-20 min | Taxi stand at arrivals |
| Shuttle | $10-15 | 30-45 min | Multiple stops |
| Rental car | Varies | 15-25 min | Rental center off-site, shuttle |
| RTC bus | $6 | 30-45 min | Route 109 (Westcliff) |

**Rental car tips**:
- Rental center is separate from terminal (free shuttle)
- Book ahead during conventions/events
- Fuel up before returning (airport gas is expensive)

## Transportation by Lifestyle

### Tourist (Short Visit)
- Stay on Strip: Walk + free trams
- Rideshare for downtown/off-Strip
- No car needed

### New Resident (No Car Yet)
- This is very hard
- Uber/Lyft will be expensive
- Live near work if possible
- Get a car ASAP

### Resident (Has Car)
- Car for most trips
- Rideshare for drinking nights
- Public transit rarely

### Commuter (Strip Worker)
- Car likely necessary
- Parking pass or free employee parking
- Some use bus from park-and-rides

### Remote Worker
- Car still needed for errands, life
- Can avoid rush hour (huge quality of life boost)
- Location flexibility is valuable

## Money-Saving Tips

**Gas**:
- Costco/Sam's Club membership pays off
- Apps: GasBuddy for cheap stations
- Avoid Strip/airport stations

**Parking**:
- Player's card often = free or validated parking
- Check which casinos offer free parking
- Meter apps help downtown

**Rideshare**:
- Compare Uber vs Lyft (prices vary)
- Walk to cheaper pickup spots (off Strip)
- Schedule rides when possible (avoids surge)

**Public transit**:
- 24-hour pass better than single fares
- Day passes on weekends for Strip exploring
