# Las Vegas Neighborhoods: Quick Reference

## Understanding Las Vegas Layout

Las Vegas is a sprawling metropolitan area of 2.3 million people across the Las Vegas Valley. The famous "Strip" is actually in Paradise, an unincorporated township — not technically in Las Vegas city proper.

**Key geographic facts**:
- Valley surrounded by mountains (Spring Mountains west, Black Mountains east)
- The Strip runs north-south through Paradise
- Downtown Vegas is 4 miles north of the Strip
- Master-planned communities expand in all directions
- Everything is driving distance; very little is walkable

## The Las Vegas Transformation

Understanding Las Vegas requires knowing its recent history:

**Pre-2010**: Affordable, boom-bust housing cycles, casino industry dominated

**2010-2015**: Recovery from 2008 crash (Vegas was ground zero), foreclosures, bargain real estate

**2016-2019**: Raiders announced (2016), tech migration begins, Golden Knights arrive (2017)

**2020-2021**: COVID devastates tourism/hospitality, but remote workers discover tax-free living

**2022-2024**: Housing prices up 50%+, California exodus, infrastructure struggling to keep pace

**2025-present**: F1 arrives, continued growth, water concerns emerge, traffic worsens

## Safety by Neighborhood

**Safest areas** (lowest crime per capita):
- Summerlin
- Henderson
- Green Valley
- MacDonald Highlands
- Southern Highlands

**Average safety**:
- Southwest
- Centennial Hills
- Spring Valley (varies by block)
- Paradise (varies significantly)

**Higher crime** (use caution):
- Parts of North Las Vegas
- East Las Vegas
- Near Stratosphere area
- Downtown late night (petty crime)

**Important**: Even "safer" areas have property crime. Lock cars, don't leave valuables visible.

## HOA Reality

Most Las Vegas suburbs have HOAs. This is inescapable for newer construction.

**Strictest HOAs**:
- Summerlin (master-planned, everything regulated)
- Henderson master-planned communities
- The Lakes, Spanish Trail

**HOA fees range**: $30-300/month depending on amenities

**What HOAs regulate**:
- Exterior paint colors
- Landscaping (desert plants required in many)
- RV/boat parking
- Basketball hoops
- Holiday decorations

Read CC&Rs carefully before buying.

## Commute Realities

**Rush hour** (7-9am, 4-7pm):
- I-15 (north-south): Brutal
- I-215 (beltway): Heavy but moves
- US-95 (northwest): Bad near downtown
- Surface streets: Signal timing is terrible

**Work from home advantage**: Vegas traffic is a major quality-of-life issue. Remote workers avoid the worst of it.

**From Summerlin to Strip**: 20 min off-peak, 40-60 min rush hour
**From Henderson to Strip**: 25 min off-peak, 45-60 min rush hour

## Newcomer Neighborhoods

**If you're moving to Vegas, consider**:

**Remote workers / WFH**:
- Summerlin (nice environment, quiet)
- Henderson (suburban quality, good internet)
- Southern Highlands (newer, good infrastructure)

**Young professionals**:
- Downtown (urban, arts scene, affordable)
- Southwest (near Strip but livable)
- Enterprise (newer, reasonable prices)

**Families**:
- Summerlin (schools, parks, safety)
- Henderson (schools, family-oriented)
- Green Valley (established, good schools)

**Budget-conscious**:
- North Las Vegas (emerging, affordable)
- Spring Valley (central, older)
- Sunrise Manor (cheap but sparse amenities)

**Retirees**:
- Sun City Summerlin (55+ community)
- Henderson (medical facilities, quiet)
- Boulder City (small-town feel, no casinos)
