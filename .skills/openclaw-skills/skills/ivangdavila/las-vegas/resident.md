# Moving to Las Vegas: The Resident's Guide

## Why People Move to Vegas

### The Pitch
- **No state income tax** — Keep more of what you earn
- **Lower cost of living** — vs California, New York, etc.
- **Sunshine** — 300+ sunny days per year
- **Entertainment** — World-class at your doorstep
- **Outdoor access** — Red Rock, Lake Mead, Mt Charleston

### The Reality
- **Heat** — Summer is genuinely brutal
- **Car required** — Public transit is inadequate
- **Water concerns** — Real long-term issue
- **Transient city** — Building community takes effort
- **Housing prices up** — Not as cheap as it used to be

## The Move Checklist

### Before You Move

**Financial preparation**:
- [ ] Save 3-6 months expenses (job market can be volatile)
- [ ] Research housing costs (higher than you think)
- [ ] Budget for car (essential)
- [ ] Check your credit (rental requirements are strict)

**Research**:
- [ ] Visit in summer (seriously — experience the heat)
- [ ] Tour neighborhoods (in person, not just online)
- [ ] Test your commute (rush hour)
- [ ] Talk to residents (Reddit r/vegas, Facebook groups)

**Job situation**:
- [ ] Job lined up? (Ideal)
- [ ] Remote work? (Research tax implications)
- [ ] Job hunting? (Hospitality hires quickly, tech takes longer)

### First Week in Vegas

**DMV and ID** (do this first):
- [ ] Nevada driver's license (within 30 days of establishing residency)
- [ ] Vehicle registration (within 30 days)
- [ ] Bring: Out-of-state license, proof of residency, proof of identity

**Utilities**:
- [ ] NV Energy (electricity)
- [ ] Southwest Gas (natural gas)
- [ ] Las Vegas Valley Water District
- [ ] Internet (Cox or CenturyLink in most areas)

**Other setup**:
- [ ] Bank account (if switching)
- [ ] Voter registration
- [ ] Update address (USPS, subscriptions, etc.)

## Establishing Residency

### For Tax Purposes
Nevada has no state income tax, but you must properly establish residency.

Treat residency as a full-facts test, not a magic threshold:
- Spend most of your year in Nevada
- Make Nevada your primary home and day-to-day base
- Move your driver's license, vehicle registration, voter registration, and financial footprint
- Keep records in case your former state questions the move

**Documentation needed**:
- Nevada driver's license
- Nevada vehicle registration
- Voter registration
- Bank accounts in Nevada
- Lease or mortgage in Nevada
- Utility bills in your name

**Why it matters**: If you came from a high-tax state (CA, NY), they may audit your residency. Document everything.

### What NOT to Do
- Don't keep your old state's license
- Don't maintain property/voting registration in old state
- Don't leave your practical life anchored in old state
- Don't claim residency just for tax purposes without actually moving

## Housing Search

### Renting

**Requirements** (typical):
- Income 2.5-3x monthly rent
- Credit score 620+ (many want 650+)
- No evictions
- First month + security deposit (usually 1 month)
- Application fee ($50-100)

**Where to search**:
- Zillow, Apartments.com
- Facebook Marketplace (be careful of scams)
- Drive neighborhoods (smaller landlords don't advertise online)
- Craigslist (lots of scams, but some legit)

**Red flags**:
- Asking for money before showing property
- Price way below market
- Unwilling to meet in person
- Pressure to sign immediately

### Buying

**Market reality (early 2026)**:
- Median home price in the mid-$400Ks
- Low inventory, competitive
- HOA-heavy market
- Cash offers still competitive

**What to know**:
- Property taxes are low by big-metro standards
- HOA fees common ($30-300/month)
- Home insurance rates increasing (climate risk)
- Flood zones exist — check FEMA maps

**First-time buyer programs**:
- Home Is Possible (down payment assistance)
- NV Housing Division programs
- Check current eligibility requirements

## Cost of Living Reality

### Monthly Budget (Median)

**Single person**:
| Expense | Range |
|---------|-------|
| Rent (1BR) | $1,200-1,600 |
| Utilities | $150-250 |
| Car payment + insurance | $500-700 |
| Gas | $150-200 |
| Food | $400-600 |
| Phone + Internet | $100-150 |
| Entertainment | $100-300 |
| **Total** | **$2,600-3,800** |

**Family of 4**:
| Expense | Range |
|---------|-------|
| Rent/Mortgage (3BR) | $2,000-3,000 |
| Utilities | $200-400 |
| Transportation (2 cars) | $900-1,200 |
| Food | $800-1,200 |
| Childcare/Activities | $500-1,500 |
| **Total** | **$4,400-7,300** |

### Compared to Other Cities
- **vs Los Angeles**: 30-40% cheaper
- **vs San Francisco**: 50%+ cheaper
- **vs Phoenix**: Similar
- **vs Denver**: 10-20% cheaper
- **vs Austin**: Similar

### Hidden Costs
- **Summer electricity**: $200-400/month (A/C runs constantly)
- **Car maintenance**: Heat destroys batteries, tires, interiors
- **Sunscreen/skincare**: Budget for it
- **Pool maintenance**: If you have one, $100-200/month

## Schools

### Clark County School District
Fifth-largest district in the US. Quality varies dramatically.

**Rating by area** (general):
- Summerlin: Above average to excellent
- Henderson: Good to excellent
- Southwest/Enterprise: Average to good
- North Las Vegas: Below average to average

**Before moving with kids**:
1. Research specific schools (GreatSchools.org)
2. Verify school zones (CCSD website)
3. Consider private options
4. Visit schools if possible

### Private Schools
- The Meadows School (Summerlin) — Premier
- Bishop Gorman (SW) — Catholic, strong athletics
- Faith Lutheran (NW) — Lutheran, solid academics
- Henderson International School — IB program

### Higher Education
- UNLV — State university
- CSN (College of Southern Nevada) — Community college
- NSC (Nevada State College) — Newer 4-year option

## Healthcare

### Hospital Systems
- **UMC** — County hospital, Level 1 trauma, UNLV affiliated
- **Sunrise Hospital** — Major private hospital
- **St. Rose** — Multiple locations, Dignity Health
- **Valley Hospital** — Established, central
- **Henderson Hospital** — Newer, Henderson area

### Healthcare Reality
- Specialist wait times can be long
- Many doctors from California (license reciprocity)
- Quality is good but not as deep as major metro areas
- Urgent care clinics everywhere

### Health Insurance
- Employer coverage most common
- Nevada Health Link for marketplace plans
- Medicaid expansion (Nevada participated)

## Settling In

### Making Friends
Vegas can feel transient. Strategies for building community:

**Join things**:
- Meetup groups (active Vegas scene)
- Sports leagues (kickball, softball, etc.)
- Gyms with communities (CrossFit, climbing)
- Volunteer organizations

**Places to meet people**:
- Dog parks (if you have a dog)
- Coffee shops in your neighborhood
- Downtown Arts District (creative community)
- Local casinos (regulars become friends)

### Things Locals Do
- **Red Rock picnics** — Sunset at Calico Basin
- **Pool parties** — At home, not just dayclub
- **Downtown** — Fremont, Arts District
- **Local casinos** — Station properties, Boyd properties
- **Day trips** — Lake Mead, Mt Charleston, Valley of Fire
- **Restaurant hopping** — Chinatown especially

### What to Expect
- **First summer**: Brutal. You'll question your decision.
- **First year**: Adjustment period. City feels different once you're not a tourist.
- **After 2 years**: You either love it or leave. Most stay.

## The Water Question

### The Reality
Lake Mead, Vegas's primary water source, has been at historic lows.

### What's Being Done
- Strict conservation measures
- Water recycling (most indoor water is recycled)
- New water sources (desalination, etc. in development)
- Grass removal incentives ($3/sq ft rebate)

### What It Means for Residents
- Water bills: Reasonable but rising
- Landscaping: Desert-friendly required in many HOAs
- Pools: Still legal, still common
- Long-term: Uncertainty, but not immediate crisis

## Is Vegas Right for You?

### Vegas Is Great For
- Remote workers who want tax savings
- Entertainment industry professionals
- Those who love outdoor activities (cooler months)
- People who thrive in 24/7 cities
- Hospitality career seekers
- Retirees who want sunshine and activities

### Vegas May Not Be Right For
- Those who hate driving
- People who can't handle extreme heat
- Those seeking walkable urban lifestyle
- People who need four seasons
- Those who dislike gambling culture

### The Test Run
**Highly recommended**: Rent for 6-12 months before buying. Vegas isn't for everyone, and you'll know within a year if it's your place.
