# Las Vegas Outdoor Activities

## The Desert Playground

Vegas is surrounded by exceptional outdoor recreation. The catch: seasonal timing is everything.

## Seasonal Reality

| Season | Outdoor Rating | Notes |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| Oct-Nov | ★★★★★ | Perfect hiking weather |
| Dec-Feb | ★★★★ | Cool, possible snow at elevation |
| Mar-Apr | ★★★★★ | Ideal before heat |
| May | ★★★ | Getting hot, early mornings only |
| Jun-Aug | ★ | Dangerous heat, avoid |
| Sep | ★★ | Still hot, late month improves |

## Red Rock Canyon

**The crown jewel of Vegas outdoor access.**

### Overview
- Distance: 17 miles west of Strip
- Entry: $15/vehicle
- Scenic drive: 13-mile one-way loop
- Open: 6am to varying close times

### Best Hikes

| Trail | Difficulty | Distance | Notes |
|-------|------------|----------|-------|
| Calico Tanks | Easy-Moderate | 2.5 mi RT | Best views, popular |
| Keystone Thrust | Moderate | 2.2 mi RT | Geological interest |
| Ice Box Canyon | Moderate | 2.5 mi RT | Shady canyon |
| Turtlehead Peak | Strenuous | 5 mi RT | Challenging summit |
| First Creek | Easy | 3.5 mi RT | Waterfall (seasonal) |

### Rock Climbing
- World-class climbing destination
- Hundreds of routes
- All difficulties
- Guide services available

### Tips
- Arrive at opening (fills up by 9am weekends)
- Bring more water than you think
- No services inside park
- Check for seasonal road closures

## Mt Charleston

**Escape the heat: 8,000+ feet elevation.**

### Overview
- Distance: 35 miles northwest
- Elevation: 8,000-11,000+ feet
- Temperature: 20-30°F cooler than valley
- Snow: Winter months

### Activities

**Hiking**:
- Mary Jane Falls: Moderate, waterfall
- Cathedral Rock: Strenuous, views
- South Loop to Charleston Peak: Strenuous, highest point in region

**Winter**:
- Lee Canyon Ski Resort
- Snowshoeing
- Sledding areas

### Why It Matters
When it's 110°F in Vegas, it's 80°F on Mt Charleston. Summer escape without leaving the area.

## Valley of Fire State Park

**Stunning red rock formations, petroglyphs.**

### Overview
- Distance: 50 miles northeast
- Entry: $10/vehicle
- Less crowded than Red Rock
- More remote feel

### Highlights
- Fire Wave Trail (easy, iconic photos)
- White Domes Loop (moderate, diverse scenery)
- Petroglyphs (ancient rock art)
- Elephant Rock (easy, unique formation)

### Photography
Best light at sunrise/sunset. Red rocks glow.

## Lake Mead National Recreation Area

**Water recreation in the desert.**

### Overview
- Distance: 30 miles southeast
- Entry: $25/vehicle (7 days)
- Activities: Boating, swimming, hiking, fishing

### Beaches
- Boulder Beach: Most popular
- Willow Beach: Below Hoover Dam
- Various coves accessible by boat

### Water Sports
- Boat rentals available
- Kayaking, paddleboarding
- Jet skiing
- Fishing (striped bass, catfish)

### Water Level Reality
Lake Mead has been at historic lows. Check current conditions. Some facilities affected.

## Hoover Dam Area

### The Dam
- Tours available ($15-30)
- Engineering marvel
- Photo opportunities
- Mike O'Callaghan Bridge viewpoint (free)

### Black Canyon
- Kayak tours from dam base
- Hot springs (accessible by water)
- Scenic paddling

## Sloan Canyon National Conservation Area

**Less crowded alternative to Red Rock.**

### Overview
- Distance: 15 miles south (Henderson)
- Entry: Free
- Petroglyphs: Over 300 rock art panels

### Why Go
- Fewer crowds than Red Rock
- Excellent petroglyphs
- Good hiking options

## Day Trip Destinations

### Death Valley National Park
- Distance: 2 hours
- Best: October-April (DANGEROUS in summer)
- Highlights: Badwater Basin, Zabriskie Point, dunes
- Entry: $30/vehicle

### Zion National Park
- Distance: 2.5 hours
- Best: Spring and fall
- Highlights: Angels Landing, The Narrows
- Very crowded; book permits early

### Grand Canyon (South Rim)
- Distance: 4.5 hours
- Best: Any time
- Highlights: The iconic rim views
- Book lodging far in advance

### Grand Canyon (West Rim)
- Distance: 2 hours
- Best: Any time
- Highlights: Skywalk (glass floor)
- Note: Not part of National Park; separate fees

### Joshua Tree National Park
- Distance: 3 hours
- Best: Spring
- Highlights: Joshua trees, rock formations, climbing

## Golf

**Vegas is a golf destination.**

### Public Courses
- Bear's Best (Summerlin)
- Las Vegas National
- Angel Park (36 holes)
- Desert Pines
- Various municipal courses

### Resort Courses
- Wynn Golf Club ($$$$)
- TPC Las Vegas
- Rio Secco
- Reflection Bay (Lake Las Vegas)

### Golf Season
Year-round, but:
- Winter: Perfect conditions
- Summer: Discounted rates, brutal heat (dawn tee times)

## Running

### Best Running Spots

- Floyd Lamb Park
- Various community trails

### Events

- Las Vegas Marathon

### Essentials for Vegas Outdoors
- Hydration pack (mandatory)
- Sun protection (hat, sunscreen, sunglasses)
- Proper footwear
- First aid basics
- Navigation (phone/GPS)

## Safety Essentials

### Heat Safety
- **Hydrate before, during, after**
- Start early, finish by 10am in summer
- Know heat exhaustion symptoms
- Turn back if feeling unwell
- Tell someone your plans

### Wildlife
- Rattlesnakes: Watch where you step
- Scorpions: Check shoes
- Coyotes: Keep distance
- Mountain lions: Rare but present

### Flash Floods
- Check weather before canyon hikes
- Never enter flooded washes
- Monsoon season: July-August

### Cell Service
Limited in many outdoor areas. Download offline maps.
