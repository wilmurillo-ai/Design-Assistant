# Las Vegas Education

## Overview

Clark County School District (CCSD) is the 5th largest in the nation. Quality varies dramatically by location.

## Clark County School District (CCSD)

### The Numbers
- 320,000+ students
- 360+ schools
- Massive geographic area

### The Reality
- Wide quality variation
- Funding challenges
- Teacher shortages
- Some excellent schools exist
- Location matters enormously

### Rating System
Schools rated 1-5 stars by Nevada. Generally:
- 5 stars: Excellent (rare)
- 4 stars: Good
- 3 stars: Average
- 2 stars: Below average
- 1 star: Struggling

## Best Public Schools by Area

### Summerlin
Consistently the best public school options.

**Elementary (4-5 star examples)**:
- Bonner Elementary
- Steele Elementary
- Rogich Middle School (6-8)

**High Schools**:
- Palo Verde High School (strong)
- West Career Technical Academy
- Various magnet programs

### Henderson
Also strong public school options.

**High Schools**:
- Coronado High School
- Green Valley High School
- Foothill High School
- Basic High School

### Enterprise/Southwest
Growing area with newer schools.

**Generally**: Average to above average, improving

### North Las Vegas
More challenged schools.

**Generally**: Below average to average, with exceptions

## School Zone Strategy

**Before buying or renting with kids:**
1. Research specific schools (not just area)
2. Check current ratings (GreatSchools.org, NDE)
3. Verify exact school zone (CCSD website)
4. Visit schools if possible
5. Talk to other parents

**Zone boundaries matter.** Two houses on the same street can have different schools.

## Magnet Schools

CCSD offers magnet programs with competitive admission:

### Notable Magnets
- **Advanced Technologies Academy (A-TECH)**: Tech focus
- **Las Vegas Academy**: Performing arts
- **West Career Technical Academy**: Career/technical
- **CSN High School**: Early college
- **Various IB programs**

### Magnet Application
- Application period: Usually December-February
- Lottery for oversubscribed programs
- Transportation may not be provided

## Private Schools

### Top Private Schools

**The Meadows School** (Summerlin)
- Type: College prep, Pre-K through 12
- Tuition: $25,000-30,000/year
- Notes: Most prestigious private school in Vegas

**Bishop Gorman High School**
- Type: Catholic, 9-12
- Tuition: ~$20,000/year
- Notes: Strong athletics, college prep

**Faith Lutheran**
- Type: Lutheran, Pre-K through 12
- Tuition: ~$15,000-20,000/year

**Henderson International School**

## Special Education

CCSD provides special education services:

- Quality varies by school

## Education Challenges

### What Vegas Struggles With
- Funding (Nevada ranks low nationally)
- Teacher recruitment and retention
- Large class sizes
- Achievement gaps
- Limited advanced options in some areas

### What's Working
- Magnet programs (when you can access them)
- Some excellent individual schools
- Charter school options
- Private school quality

## Making It Work

### Strategies for Families

1. **Research obsessively before choosing where to live**
   - School zone is critical
   - Don't assume area = good schools

2. **Consider private or charter if public options are weak**
   - Private school may justify Summerlin premium
   - Charters can be good free alternatives

3. **Get involved**
   - PTA/PTO participation
   - Volunteer
   - Advocate for your child

4. **Supplement as needed**
   - Tutoring (Kumon, Mathnasium, private)
   - Online resources
   - Summer programs

5. **Stay flexible**
   - Schools can change
   - Be willing to move if needed

## For Newcomers

### Process
1. Determine zoned schools (CCSD website)
2. Research those schools (ratings, reviews)
3. Consider alternatives if needed
4. Gather enrollment documents
5. Register at school (or apply for magnet/charter)

### Documents Needed
- Proof of residency (2 documents)
- Child's birth certificate
- Immunization records
- Previous school records
- Any IEP/504 documentation

### Timing
- Can enroll anytime (mid-year transfers common)
- Magnet applications have windows
- Charter lotteries have deadlines
