# Las Vegas Tech Industry

## Overview

Las Vegas has an emerging tech scene, though it's smaller than coastal tech hubs. The ecosystem is growing, driven by tax benefits, lower costs, and remote work migration.

## Tech Landscape

### Strengths
- No state income tax (attracts talent)
- Lower cost of living than Bay Area/LA
- Growing startup community
- Data center presence (Switch)
- Gaming technology expertise
- Convention/events tech

### Weaknesses
- Smaller talent pool
- Fewer established tech companies
- Less VC presence
- Brain drain to coastal cities
- Not a "tech destination" yet

## Remote Work Hub

### The Opportunity
Vegas has become attractive for remote workers because:
- No state income tax
- Lower cost of living
- Sunshine and lifestyle
- Airport connectivity

### Considerations
- Establish proper residency for tax benefits
- Move your primary legal and day-to-day ties, not just your mailing address
- Document your presence
- Some companies adjust pay for location

### Infrastructure
- Good internet availability (Cox, CenturyLink)
- Coffee shops and coworking spaces
- Libraries with wifi
- Most residential areas have adequate connectivity

## Breaking Into Vegas Tech

### From Out of State
1. Apply to major employers (Amazon, gaming companies)
2. Network at CES or other events
3. Consider remote role while establishing residency
4. Connect with local tech community online first

### Career Changers
1. Bootcamp or self-study
2. Target gaming companies (strong local presence)
3. Network actively (smaller community = easier access)
4. Consider hospitality tech crossover

### From Hospitality
Vegas has unique hospitality + tech crossover opportunities:
- Hotel technology systems
- Restaurant POS and tech
- Entertainment technology
- Casino systems

## Quality of Life for Tech Workers

### Pros
- No state income tax = more take-home pay
- Lower housing costs than coastal cities
- Sunshine and outdoor access
- Entertainment options endless
- Good airport connectivity

### Cons
- Smaller tech community
- Fewer career options locally
- May need to go remote for best opportunities
- Summer heat limits outdoor time
- Less "tech culture" than SF/Seattle

### Typical Tech Worker Life
- Work for Amazon, gaming company, or remote
- Live in Summerlin or Henderson
- Escape to mountains/beach for variety
- Take advantage of entertainment options
- Lower stress than coastal tech hubs
