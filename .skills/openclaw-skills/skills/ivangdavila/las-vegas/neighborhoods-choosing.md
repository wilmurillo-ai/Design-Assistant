# Choosing Your Las Vegas Neighborhood

## The Decision Framework

Choosing where to live in Vegas comes down to three core trade-offs:
1. **Price vs Quality**: More money = safer, better schools, nicer surroundings
2. **Location vs Space**: Central = smaller, suburban = larger
3. **Character vs Convenience**: Urban grit vs suburban polish

## Decision Trees by Profile

### Remote Worker / WFH Professional

```
Do you have children? 
├─ YES → Summerlin or Henderson
└─ NO → What matters more?
         ├─ Urban energy → Downtown Arts District
         ├─ Outdoor access → Summerlin (Red Rock) or Henderson (Lake Mead)
         ├─ Food scene → Spring Valley (Chinatown)
         └─ Budget → North Las Vegas (Aliante) or Enterprise
```

### Hospitality / Strip Worker

```
What shift do you work?
├─ Day shift → Anywhere with 30-min commute works
├─ Swing/Night → Near Strip for safety (no tired driving)
         ├─ Budget → Paradise, Spring Valley
         └─ Quality → Southwest, Enterprise
```

### Family with Kids

```
What's your budget?
├─ Premium ($500K+ buy, $1,600+ rent)
│   └─ Summerlin or Henderson (Anthem, Seven Hills)
├─ Mid-range ($350-500K buy, $1,300-1,600 rent)
│   └─ Henderson (Green Valley, Inspirada) or Enterprise
└─ Budget (under $350K buy, under $1,300 rent)
    └─ North Las Vegas (Aliante) or Henderson edges
```

### Young Professional

```
What's your priority?
├─ Nightlife access → Paradise (near Strip) or Downtown
├─ Career growth → Near where you work
├─ Saving money → North Las Vegas or Spring Valley
└─ Balance (quality + reasonable) → Southwest or Henderson
```

### Retiree

```
What lifestyle do you want?
├─ Active/Golf → Summerlin (Sun City) or Henderson (Sun City Anthem)
├─ Small-town → Boulder City
├─ Lake access → Lake Las Vegas or Henderson
└─ Urban/Cultural → Downtown Henderson or Arts District
```

## The Real Estate Agent Questions

Before touring, answer these:

1. **What's your monthly housing budget?** (Be honest)
2. **Do you have kids? Ages?** (School zones matter)
3. **Where do you work? Hours?** (Commute reality)
4. **How important is safety?** (Willing to trade for savings?)
5. **Car or no car?** (Car = opens all options)
6. **How long do you plan to stay?** (Rent vs buy calculation)
7. **What's non-negotiable?** (Pool? Garage? Yard? Gym?)
8. **What can you live without?** (Everything costs extra)

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake #1: Choosing Based on Strip Proximity Alone
**Reality**: Unless you work on the Strip, proximity doesn't matter much. You'll drive everywhere anyway.

### Mistake #2: Not Test-Driving the Commute
**Reality**: Rush hour traffic can double commute times. Drive the route at 8am and 5pm before committing.

### Mistake #3: Ignoring HOA Fees
**Reality**: $300/month HOA on a $300K condo = $108,000 over 30 years. Factor it into purchase decisions.

### Mistake #4: Prioritizing Amenities Over Location
**Reality**: That nice pool means nothing if the neighborhood is sketchy or commute is brutal.

### Mistake #5: Not Visiting at Night
**Reality**: Some areas feel fine at 2pm but different at 2am. Visit multiple times.

### Mistake #6: Assuming Vegas is Still Cheap
**Reality**: It's cheaper than CA/NY but not cheap. Budget realistically.

## The Rental vs Buy Decision

### Rent If
- New to Vegas (don't know areas yet)
- Uncertain about staying long-term
- Need to rebuild credit/savings
- Job is unstable
- Planning to relocate within 3 years

### Buy If
- Committed to Vegas long-term (5+ years)
- Have stable income
- Want to build equity
- Have down payment (3.5% FHA minimum)
- Found the right neighborhood

### The Vegas-Specific Factor
Vegas housing is volatile. 2008 crash devastated values. 2020-2024 saw 50%+ gains. Don't assume appreciation; buy what you can afford if values drop.

## Final Recommendations by Budget

### Under $1,200/month Rent
- **Best option**: North Las Vegas (Aliante) or Spring Valley
- **Trade-offs**: Longer commute, less prestige

### $1,200-1,500/month Rent
- **Best option**: Enterprise, Henderson (Green Valley), Southwest
- **Trade-offs**: Still not premium areas, but good value

### $1,500-1,900/month Rent
- **Best option**: Summerlin, Henderson (Anthem), Southern Highlands
- **Trade-offs**: You're paying for quality; expect to get it

### Under $350K Purchase
- **Best option**: North Las Vegas, older Henderson condos
- **Trade-offs**: Either location or property type compromise

### $350K-500K Purchase
- **Best option**: Henderson townhouse, Enterprise single-family
- **Trade-offs**: Good value; won't be premium neighborhoods

### $500K+ Purchase
- **Best option**: Summerlin, Henderson (good areas)
- **Trade-offs**: None significant; this is the sweet spot

## The One-Week Research Plan

**Day 1-2**: Online research, narrow to 3 neighborhoods

**Day 3-4**: Drive through neighborhoods (morning, afternoon, evening)

**Day 5**: Tour 3-5 properties in top 2 neighborhoods

**Day 6**: Test commute to work at rush hour

**Day 7**: Make decision, apply

This prevents the common mistake of committing too fast or researching forever.
