# Las Vegas Visitor Tips

## Money-Saving Strategies

### Gambling Smart (or Skipping It)
**The house always wins** — gambling is entertainment, not income.

**If you gamble**:
- Set a hard budget and put the rest in your room safe
- Sign up for player's cards (free) — comps add up
- Drink slowly — those "free" drinks impair judgment
- Table minimums: $15-25 on Strip, $5-10 Downtown
- Slot payback: Downtown ~94%, Strip ~90-92%
- Best odds: Blackjack, craps (pass/don't pass), baccarat

**If you don't gamble**:
- Walk through casinos — they're attractions themselves
- Sit at a slot machine briefly if you want free drinks
- Video poker at $1/hand while drinking beats $18 cocktails

### Getting Deals

**Shows**:
- Tix4Tonight booths: Same-day discounts (40-50% off)
- Hotel concierge: Sometimes has comps for shows with low attendance
- Weeknight performances: Cheaper than Friday/Saturday

**Food**:
- Happy hours: 4-7pm many restaurants, even celebrity chefs
- Lunch specials: Same food, lower prices than dinner
- Off-Strip: Half the price, often better food
- Buffets: Not the deal they once were; skip unless highly rated

**Rooms**:
- Myvegas app: Play free slots, earn real comps (rooms, shows, dining)
- Direct booking: Sometimes price-matches third parties plus benefits
- Last minute: Apps like HotelTonight for same-day deals

### Avoiding Tourist Traps

**Skip**:
- Strip ATMs ($5-8 fees) — use casino cage for no-fee cash advances
- Hotel convenience stores — bring snacks/water
- Photography "characters" on Strip — they demand tips
- Club promoters — deals usually have catches
- Timeshare presentations — not worth "free" show tickets

**Do**:
- Bring refillable water bottle (hydration is key)
- Pack comfortable shoes (walking = exercise)
- Download resort apps for mobile check-in, room keys

## Nightlife Strategy

### Getting Into Clubs

**For guys**:
- Guest list is essential (sign up online or through promoter)
- Dress code enforced: Dress shoes, collared shirt, no athletic wear
- Groups of only guys = tough. Mixed groups way easier.
- Bottle service guarantees entry but expensive ($500-5,000+)

**For women**:
- Guest list usually means free entry before a certain time
- Still need to dress up (heels encouraged but not required)
- Large groups of women = VIP treatment

**Best club nights**:
- Thursday-Saturday: All clubs open, best DJs
- Sunday: Pool party to club transition (Encore Beach Club)
- Monday-Wednesday: Hit or miss; check who's spinning

### Bar Hopping

**Best bar scenes by vibe**:
- **Upscale cocktails**: Chandelier Bar (Cosmo), Parasol Up/Down (Wynn)
- **Sports**: Circa's Stadium Swim, Caesars' sportsbook
- **Views**: Skyfall Lounge (Delano), Foundation Room (Mandalay)
- **Weird Vegas**: Atomic Liquors (downtown), Double Down Saloon
- **Budget drinks**: Downtown, off-Strip locals casinos

## Dining Strategy

### Reservations
- **Book ahead for celebrity chef restaurants** (2-4 weeks for prime time)
- **OpenTable, Resy, Yelp** all work
- **Hotel concierge** can sometimes get hard reservations
- **Walk-ins**: Try for early (5pm) or late (9:30pm+) seating

### Tipping Guide

| Service | Tip |
|---------|-----|
| Cocktail server | $1-2 per drink minimum |
| Restaurant server | 18-22% (Vegas service is usually excellent) |
| Valet | $2-5 when car returned |
| Bellman | $1-2 per bag |
| Housekeeping | $3-5 per night (leave daily) |
| Concierge | $5-20 for reservations/tickets |
| Dealers | Varies — common to tip on big wins |
| Uber/Lyft | 15-20% |

**Vegas runs on tips** — budget for them.

### Eating on a Budget

**Breakfast/Brunch**:
- Casino coffee shops: $15-20 with coffee
- Off-Strip: Egg Works, Blueberry Hill, Babystacks

**Lunch**:
- Food courts: Venetian, Aria, Fashion Show Mall
- Quick-service at casinos: Bobby's Burgers, Shake Shack

**Dinner**:
- Happy hour at celebrity restaurants
- Chinatown (15 min from Strip): Authentic, half the price
- Container Park downtown: Casual food hall vibe

## Transportation Tips

### Getting Around

**The Deuce (RTC Bus)**:
- $6 for 2-hour pass, $8 for 24-hour
- Runs entire Strip + Downtown
- Slow but cheap and air-conditioned

**Monorail**:
- $5 single, $13 day pass
- Runs behind east side of Strip only
- Stations far from some hotels

**Uber/Lyft**:
- Airport to Strip: $15-25
- Strip to Downtown: $15-20
- Surge pricing during events/late night

**Walking**:
- The Strip is 4.2 miles long
- Pedestrian bridges connect major properties
- Underground trams: Bellagio-Aria-Park MGM, Excalibur-Luxor-Mandalay

**Rental Car**:
- Only if doing day trips
- Parking at Strip hotels: $15-25/day
- Traffic on Strip is brutal

### Airport Tips
- **TSA PreCheck/Global Entry**: Worth it; security lines can be 30+ min
- **Terminal 3**: Southwest and international (newer, nicer)
- **Terminal 1**: Most other domestic
- **Uber/Lyft**: Level 0 arrivals area (follow signs)
- **Allow 3 hours before flight**: Weekends and holidays get crazy

## Health & Safety

### Hydration Is Not Optional
- Vegas average humidity: 10-20%
- You're sweating even when you don't feel it
- Symptoms of dehydration: headache, dizziness, fatigue
- **Drink water constantly** — one glass per alcoholic drink minimum

### Sun Protection
- Sunscreen even for short walks (desert UV is intense)
- Hat and sunglasses essential
- Seek shade during midday (11am-3pm)

### Alcohol Pacing
- Drinks are strong and often free (while gambling)
- Altitude + desert = faster intoxication
- Eat before drinking
- Know your limits

### Staying Safe
- **Pickpockets**: Active on Strip, especially crowded areas
- **Scams**: Characters who demand tips, "found" jewelry, CD sellers
- **At night**: Stick to well-lit, busy areas; skip shortcuts
- **Valuables**: Use room safe; leave expensive jewelry at home
- **Photography**: Costumed characters WILL demand money

### If Things Go Wrong
- **Emergency**: 911
- **Non-emergency police**: 311
- **Lost at casino**: Security desk in every casino
- **Medical**: UMC (closest major hospital to Strip), many urgent cares on/near Strip
- **Lost items**: Check hotel security/lost & found first

## What to Pack

### Essentials
- **Comfortable walking shoes** (you'll walk 5-10 miles daily)
- **Layers**: AC is aggressive; outside is hot
- **Refillable water bottle**
- **Portable phone charger**
- **Sunscreen, sunglasses, hat**

### For Nightlife
- **Dress clothes**: At least one nice outfit
- **Dress shoes for guys**: Required at clubs
- **Heels for women**: Optional but expected at upscale venues

### Skip
- **Excessive cash**: ATMs everywhere (casino cage for no-fee)
- **Expensive jewelry**: Just a headache to protect
- **Heavy jackets**: Even winter rarely drops below 40°F

## Vegas Etiquette

### Casino Floor
- **Don't touch cards in blackjack** (hand signals only)
- **Don't hand cash directly to dealer** — lay it on the table
- **Color up before leaving table** (exchange small chips for larger)
- **Tip dealers** on big wins or when leaving
- **Keep phone calls away from tables**

### Pools
- **No saving chairs with towels** then leaving
- **Topless is usually NOT allowed** (Vegas pools, unlike Europe)
- **Keep volume reasonable** even at party pools
- **Tip cocktail servers** — they work in the heat

### Shows
- **Arrive early** — late seating often restricted
- **No photography** in most shows
- **Silence phones** — seriously
- **Don't talk during performance**

### General
- **Keep it moving on escalators** — walk left, stand right
- **Don't block sidewalks** for photos
- **Tip generously** — Vegas service industry relies on tips
- **Don't be that person** who's too drunk too early

## First-Timer Mistakes

| Mistake | Reality |
|---------|---------|
| Trying to walk entire Strip in one day | It's 4+ miles; pace yourself |
| Eating only on Strip | Off-Strip is better and cheaper |
| Not booking show tickets ahead | Good shows sell out |
| Gambling too early in the trip | Save it for later when you know your limits |
| Not joining loyalty programs | Free comps for signing up |
| Checking bag at airport | Vegas bag claim is slow |
| Not budgeting for tips | Tips add 20%+ to everything |
| Planning too much | Vegas rewards spontaneity |

## Weather Reality Check

| Season | Temperature | What to Expect |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Summer (Jun-Aug) | 95-115°F | Brutal outdoor heat; pool and indoor only |
| Fall (Sep-Nov) | 70-90°F | Ideal; pool season winds down |
| Winter (Dec-Feb) | 45-65°F | Pleasant days, cold nights |
| Spring (Mar-May) | 65-95°F | Ideal; pool season begins |

**Summer warning**: Heat exhaustion and heat stroke are real dangers. People die every year from underestimating the heat. If hiking or spending time outdoors, early morning only.
