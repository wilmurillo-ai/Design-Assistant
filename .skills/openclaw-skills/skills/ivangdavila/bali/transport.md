# Bali Transport Guide 2026

> Last updated: March 2026

## Reality

Bali transport is road-dominant. Travel times are driven by congestion and weather, not just distance.

## Main Transport Modes

| Mode | Best Use | Trade-Off |
|------|----------|-----------|
| Scooter | Flexible local movement | Safety risk, weather exposure |
| App rides (car/bike) | Point-to-point convenience | Surge pricing and delays |
| Private driver | Full-day route efficiency | Higher cost |
| Taxis | Airport and urban rides | Needs meter/price clarity |
| Ferries/fast boats | Nusa and nearby islands | Weather-dependent reliability |

## Airport (DPS) Transfer Logic

| Destination | Typical Drive Window (traffic-dependent) |
|-------------|-------------------------------------------|
| Kuta/Seminyak | 25-75 min |
| Canggu | 45-120 min |
| Ubud | 60-120 min |
| Uluwatu | 35-90 min |
| Sanur | 25-60 min |

## App and Taxi Guidance

- Prefer app booking in high-traffic zones.
- Confirm pickup point clearly in airport and mall areas.
- Keep buffer before flights and timed activities.

## Day Trip Routing Rule

Maximize experience by limiting cross-island hops:
- One major geographic cluster per day.
- Start early for inland or east/north routes.
- Keep sunset plans near where you already are.

## Rain Season Transport Rule

During heavy rain periods:
- Expect substantial delays.
- Avoid last-minute inter-area reservations.
- Keep cancellation-friendly bookings.
