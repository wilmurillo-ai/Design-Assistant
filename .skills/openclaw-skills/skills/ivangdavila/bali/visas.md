# Bali Visas and Stay Compliance Guide 2026

> Last updated: March 2026 | Verify before purchase: rules can change fast.

## Most Common Entry Route: Visa on Arrival (B1)

Official immigration references list B1 as a common tourist/business-visit entry category for eligible nationalities.

| Item | Current Reference |
|------|-------------------|
| Fee | IDR 500,000 |
| Initial stay | Up to 30 days |
| Extension | One extension up to 30 days |
| Purpose | Tourism, meetings, medical, government visit, transit |

## Bali Tourist Levy

Separate from visa cost:
- IDR 150,000 per international visitor.
- Pay via official Bali tourism levy channel or designated payment points.

## Typical Stay Paths

| Stay Goal | Typical Path |
|-----------|--------------|
| 1-30 days | B1/VOA route |
| 31-60 days | B1 + extension |
| Longer purpose-specific stays | Use correct pre-arranged visa category |

## Compliance Rules

1. Do not assume tourism visa allows all business activity.
2. Keep digital and printed proof of entry and payment records.
3. Track expiration dates early; avoid last-minute extensions.
4. Never overstay intentionally; penalties and legal issues escalate quickly.

## Practical Document Checklist

- Passport with sufficient validity.
- Onward/return evidence.
- Accommodation details.
- Funds/means evidence if requested.
- Tourist levy proof for Bali arrivals.

## Risk Traps

- Using hearsay from forums as legal source.
- Confusing remote work for foreign clients with local work permissions.
- Assuming visa-agent advice without official cross-check.

## Verification Sources

Always validate with:
- Indonesia immigration official portals.
- Official e-visa platform references.
- Bali government tourism levy channels.
