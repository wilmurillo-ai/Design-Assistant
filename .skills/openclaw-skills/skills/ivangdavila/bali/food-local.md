# Balinese and Indonesian Food Essentials

> Last updated: March 2026

## Core Dishes to Know

| Dish | What It Is | Spice Level |
|------|------------|-------------|
| Nasi campur | Rice with mixed proteins/sides | Mild to medium |
| Babi guling | Roast suckling pig (Balinese) | Medium |
| Ayam betutu | Slow-cooked spiced chicken | Medium to hot |
| Sate lilit | Minced seafood/meat satay | Mild to medium |
| Lawar | Mixed vegetable/coconut/meat dish | Mild to medium |
| Nasi goreng | Fried rice staple | Mild to medium |
| Mie goreng | Fried noodles | Mild to medium |

## Best Use by User Type

- First-timers: nasi campur and sate lilit at clean, busy spots.
- Culinary explorers: babi guling and betutu with local recommendations.
- Sensitive stomachs: start mild, avoid uncertain raw add-ons.

## Local Dining Rules

- Choose busy kitchens with high turnover.
- Ask spice level explicitly.
- Prefer cooked-hot food over warm-holding trays when unsure.

## Budget Direction

- Local breakfast: IDR 15k-50k
- Typical local lunch: IDR 30k-80k
- Local dinner with drink: IDR 50k-120k
