# Bali Education Guide for Expat Families 2026

> Last updated: March 2026

## Education Landscape

Bali offers international schools, private local schools, and homeschooling-style alternatives. Choice depends on curriculum goals, budget, and commute tolerance.

## Common Curriculum Paths

| Path | Typical Profile |
|------|-----------------|
| International (IB/US/UK-influenced) | Families planning global mobility |
| National-plus private | Families balancing cost and English exposure |
| Alternative/project-based | Families prioritizing non-traditional formats |

## Cost Direction (Annual)

| Tier | Typical Annual Tuition |
|------|------------------------|
| Lower private range | IDR 50M-120M |
| Mid international range | IDR 120M-300M |
| Premium international range | IDR 300M-600M+ |

## Parent Decision Checklist

1. Curriculum continuity for future country transitions.
2. Door-to-door commute realism from chosen neighborhood.
3. Class-size and support services.
4. Enrollment waitlist and admissions timeline.

## Area Fit Notes

- Canggu/Berawa: strong density of international-family demand.
- Sanur: family-friendly pace with easier routines for some schools.
- Ubud: selective alternative education options.

## Common Mistakes

- Choosing home based only on rent before school commute testing.
- Underestimating non-tuition costs (transport, activities, uniforms).
- Starting applications too late for popular intakes.
