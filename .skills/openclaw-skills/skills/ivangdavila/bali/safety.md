# Bali Safety and Legal Guide 2026

> Last updated: March 2026

## Safety Snapshot

Indonesia is generally workable for travel and long stays, but official advisories emphasize caution for terrorism risk, petty crime, and natural hazards in specific contexts.

## Main Risk Categories

| Category | Practical Risk |
|----------|----------------|
| Road safety | Highest everyday risk for many foreigners |
| Petty theft/scams | Moderate in tourist-heavy zones |
| Natural hazards | Earthquakes, volcanic events, flooding periods |
| Legal exposure | Drug laws and visa misuse are high-severity |

## Legal Non-Negotiables

- Drug offenses in Indonesia carry severe penalties.
- Respect immigration rules and stay validity.
- Maintain respectful conduct at religious sites and ceremonies.

## Personal Safety Baseline

1. Use registered transport channels.
2. Avoid intoxicated scooter riding.
3. Keep copies of passport/visa data separately.
4. Use trusted ATMs and card practices.
5. Monitor local hazard alerts (weather/volcanic notices).

## Scam Patterns to Watch

| Pattern | Mitigation |
|---------|------------|
| Overpriced transport at high-pressure points | App booking or agreed fare before departure |
| Rental deposit disputes | Full condition photos and clear contract terms |
| Fake activity offers | Book via verified channels |
| Currency exchange tricks | Use reputable, transparent providers |

## Women and Solo Traveler Notes

- Bali is heavily touristed and generally manageable for solo travel with standard precautions.
- Maintain normal urban safety protocols for nightlife and isolated areas.

## Emergency Readiness

- Keep insurer emergency hotline accessible.
- Save local emergency contacts in phone and offline note.
- Share location plan for remote treks or water crossings.
