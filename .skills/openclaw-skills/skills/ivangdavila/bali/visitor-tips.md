# Bali Visitor Practical Tips

> Last updated: March 2026

## Arrival Checklist

1. Passport validity and onward travel proof.
2. Visa plan confirmed (VOA/B1 or pre-arranged visa).
3. Tourist levy readiness (IDR 150,000 per international visitor).
4. e-CD customs declaration completed before arrival.
5. Transfer plan from DPS airport (app ride, hotel transfer, or driver).

## Money and Payments

- Card works widely in tourist zones, but carry cash for small warungs/parking.
- Prefer ATM withdrawals from bank-attached machines.
- Keep multiple payment rails (two cards + some cash).
- FX kiosks vary in reliability; avoid aggressive street offers.

## Connectivity

| Need | Practical Choice |
|------|------------------|
| Fast setup on arrival | Airport or mall SIM/eSIM counter |
| Stable remote-work backup | Dual-SIM or second data profile |
| Route and traffic | Google Maps + local ride apps |

## Health and Comfort

- Drink sealed or filtered water.
- Use strong mosquito protection, especially at dawn/dusk.
- Pace heat exposure during midday hours.
- Pack reef-safe sunscreen and basic GI medication.

## Respect and Etiquette

- Temple visits require modest dress; use sarong/sash where required.
- Do not step on ritual offerings in streets.
- Keep respectful behavior during ceremonies and processions.

## Transport Reality

- Distances look short, but travel time can be long.
- Rain + rush hour can double ride time in hotspots.
- Keep margin before tours, reservations, and airport runs.

## Scams and Friction Points

| Situation | Prevention |
|-----------|------------|
| Airport transfer overpricing | Use app booking or fixed quote before departure |
| Scooter damage disputes | Take full pickup/dropoff photos and videos |
| Inflated activity pricing | Compare 2-3 providers first |
| Informal ticketing confusion | Buy from official counters or trusted channels |

## Day Trip Reliability

- Offshore trips (Nusa islands) depend heavily on sea and weather conditions.
- Keep cancellation fallback options for unpredictable water conditions.

## Minimum Safety Baseline

- Quality helmet every scooter ride.
- No driving after alcohol.
- Emergency contact and insurance details always accessible.
- Share itinerary with a trusted contact for remote areas.
