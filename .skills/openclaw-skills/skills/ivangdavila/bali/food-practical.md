# Bali Food Practical Guide

> Last updated: March 2026

## Water and Hygiene Baseline

- Drink bottled or filtered water.
- Use ice from reputable venues only.
- Keep oral rehydration support for GI disruption days.

## Dietary Coverage by Area

| Diet | Easiest Areas |
|------|---------------|
| Vegetarian/vegan | Ubud, Canggu |
| Gluten-aware | Canggu, Seminyak, Ubud |
| High-protein fitness meals | Canggu, Berawa |
| Halal-focused planning | Broadly possible with targeted venues |

## Bali Belly Risk Reduction

- Favor high-turnover kitchens.
- Avoid risky raw foods in uncertain settings.
- Wash hands frequently and carry sanitizer.
- Start with moderate spice if newly arrived.

## Tipping and Etiquette

- Service charge often included in mid/premium venues.
- Small extra tip is optional for excellent service.
- Respect local customs during religious periods and near temples.

## Ramadan and Religious Periods

Bali itself is majority Hindu, but Indonesia-wide religious calendars still influence staffing, logistics, and some business hours. Confirm opening times on major holidays.
