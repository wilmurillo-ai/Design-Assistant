# Customs and Arrival — Indonesia

Indonesia arrival friction is manageable when the first hour is planned. Most problems come from missing customs steps, weak cash strategy, or assuming transfers are faster than they are.

## First-Hour Priorities

1. Immigration and visa processing
2. Baggage collection
3. Customs declaration and screening
4. ATM, SIM, or eSIM activation
5. Confirmed transfer out of the airport

## Arrival Checklist

| Item | Why it matters |
|------|----------------|
| Customs declaration readiness | Avoid unnecessary queue confusion |
| Small amount of local currency | Useful for cash-only moments |
| Data before leaving airport | Critical for ride apps and hotel contact |
| Driver or app pickup plan | Airport taxi friction is common |
| Buffer after landing | Immigration plus baggage can take time |

## Customs Logic

- Carry medicine in original packaging when possible.
- Declare high-value goods or unusual equipment if required.
- Keep drone, camera, or dive-gear plans realistic: some operators or parks may ask questions even if customs does not.
- Avoid bringing anything that could create drug-law or prescription confusion.

## Airport Transfer Rules

| Context | Best default |
|---------|--------------|
| Bali first-timer | Pre-booked car or reliable app ride |
| Jakarta business or city hotel | App ride with clear terminal pickup |
| Late-night arrival | Hotel transfer or known driver |
| Family or heavy luggage | Car, not bike taxi |

## Cash and Connectivity on Arrival

- Use bank-attached ATMs or airport counters with clear receipts.
- Keep two payment rails: card plus cash.
- Download local apps before flying when possible.
- Screenshot hotel address and local contact in case data setup fails.

## Traps

- Booking a remote first night after a midnight arrival
- Leaving the airport without data or cash backup
- Assuming the fastest boat or domestic connection is safe the same day
- Using informal exchange counters or unmetered drivers without agreement
