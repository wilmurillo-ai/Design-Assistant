# Safety and Emergencies — Indonesia

Most Indonesia trips are fine when the user respects transport, sea, scooter, heat, and animal risks. The problem is not panic; it is underestimating friction.

## Emergency Baseline

| Need | Action |
|------|--------|
| General emergency | Call local emergency services or hotel support immediately |
| Clinic or hospital need | Use major-hub facilities when possible |
| Boat or diving incident | Contact operator and local authorities fast |
| Passport or theft issue | Police report plus embassy or consulate |

## Main Risk Categories

- Scooter crashes
- Rough seas and rushed boat transfers
- Dehydration and sun exposure
- Food or GI disruption
- Monkey, dog, or wildlife incidents
- Volcano closures, ash, or trail restrictions

## Practical Rules

1. Quality helmet every scooter ride.
2. No alcohol plus scooter or boat risk-taking.
3. Carry travel insurance that clearly covers motorbikes or diving if relevant.
4. Use sealed or trusted water sources.
5. Re-check volcano and sea conditions before committing to dawn starts or crossings.

## Scam and Friction Notes

- Airport and harbor pricing confusion
- Scooter damage disputes
- Informal tour upsells
- Weak safety standards from the cheapest operator

## Good Output Standard

For risky routes, always include:
- nearest realistic clinic or hospital logic
- whether the area is a weak fit for low-mobility users
- what to re-check the night before
