# Weather and Seasonality — Indonesia

Indonesia has year-round travel potential, but not year-round route symmetry. Monsoon timing, surf season, and sea conditions change which islands feel smart.

## Safe Planning Shape

| Period | Practical pattern |
|--------|-------------------|
| Apr-Oct | Stronger for many Bali, Lombok, Komodo, and surf-heavy routes |
| Nov-Mar | Rain and rougher-sea logic matter more; route choice needs more caution |

## Route Logic by Season

- Bali and Lombok are easiest in the drier months.
- Komodo and boat-heavy routes need stronger sea-condition awareness in rougher periods.
- Java culture routes still work, but volcano visibility and rain patterns can shift the payoff.
- Sumatra needs extra honesty around rain and road conditions.

## Month-Aware Guidance

| Goal | Best window |
|------|-------------|
| First easy trip | Shoulder to drier months |
| Surf focus | Match coast and swell season, not just one country season |
| Volcanoes | Drier periods with visibility buffer |
| Family smoothness | Avoid roughest boat windows and peak heat stacks |

## Traps

- Saying "Indonesia is good all year" without route nuance
- Ignoring sea state when recommending island hops
- Confusing good beach weather with good hiking or diving conditions
