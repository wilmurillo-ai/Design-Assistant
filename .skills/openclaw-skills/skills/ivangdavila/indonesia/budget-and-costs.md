# Budget and Costs — Indonesia

Indonesia can feel cheap or expensive depending on island, transport style, and how often the user pays tourist-convenience premiums.

## Budget Drivers

| Cost driver | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| Domestic flights and baggage | High on multi-island routes |
| Fast boats and private transfers | Adds up quickly |
| Resort or villa area | Bali south and Komodo can spike fast |
| Diving, boats, park fees | Major cost block |
| Family comfort | More car transfers, bigger rooms, easier logistics |

## Daily Budget Shape

| Style | Typical pattern |
|-------|-----------------|
| Backpacker | Simple stays, local food, slower routing |
| Mid-range | Most common sweet spot, especially Bali or Java |
| Premium | Fastest way to lose budget in Bali, boats, and luxury islands |

## Hidden Costs

- Visa or VOA fees
- Bali tourist levy
- Domestic baggage fees
- Harbor or island transfer charges
- Park or conservation fees
- Driver waiting time
- ATM and cash-withdrawal friction

## Budget Advice Rules

1. Keep the island count low if budget matters.
2. Use trains in Java where possible.
3. Budget more for Komodo, diving, and remote islands than first-timers expect.
4. Do not compare Bali south-coast pricing with inland Java or Sumatra.
5. Add a disruption buffer for boats, rain, and last-minute transport shifts.
