# Jakarta and West Java

Jakarta is usually a gateway, business stop, or food and shopping play, not the emotional center of a leisure-first Indonesia trip. West Java matters more when the user wants Bandung, tea-country scenery, or urban contrast before or after other islands.

## Best Use Cases

| Use case | Why it works |
|----------|--------------|
| Gateway night | Recover from long-haul flight |
| Business plus leisure | Strong hotels and dining |
| Food and shopping | Excellent urban depth |
| Add-on to Java route | Easy bridge before trains or flights onward |

## Planning Notes

- Stay near the real next-day priority: airport, business district, or station.
- Traffic is the dominant cost.
- Bandung is better as a targeted add-on than a rushed same-day detour.

## Good Advice

- Keep Jakarta short unless the user likes big-city energy.
- Use hotels with easy car access and reliable breakfast if arrival is late.
- Pair Jakarta with Yogyakarta or Bali only when flights and recovery pacing make sense.

## Traps

- Treating Jakarta like a walkable sightseeing city
- Underestimating airport-to-hotel time
- Forcing Bandung into a short first-time holiday
