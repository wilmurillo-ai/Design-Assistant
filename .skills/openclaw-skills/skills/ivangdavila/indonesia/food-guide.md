# Food Guide — Indonesia

Indonesia food advice should route by island and meal style, not by one national stereotype.

## What to Seek by Region

| Region | Strongest food angle |
|--------|----------------------|
| Bali | Balinese dishes plus polished international scene |
| Jakarta | Big-city range, seafood, malls, and late-night options |
| Yogyakarta | Sweet Central Java flavors and street-food culture |
| Padang and Sumatra | Powerful spice and rice-table style eating |
| Lombok | Spicier Sasak flavors |

## Safe Guidance Rules

- Ask about spice tolerance early.
- Explain that "local food" can mean very different styles across islands.
- Warn first-time travelers about heat, fried-food fatigue, and water or ice caution when relevant.
- Keep one light-food fallback for GI recovery days.

## Must-Know Dishes

| Dish | Why it matters |
|------|----------------|
| Nasi goreng | Reliable gateway comfort food |
| Mie goreng | Easy staple |
| Satay | Common, flexible, good first-order option |
| Nasi Padang | High-payoff regional experience |
| Babi guling | Bali-specific, not for all diets |
| Soto | Useful comfort category with many regional versions |

## Traps

- Assuming Bali dining reflects all Indonesia food culture
- Sending low-spice travelers straight into the deepest regional dishes
- Ignoring halal or pork constraints by region
