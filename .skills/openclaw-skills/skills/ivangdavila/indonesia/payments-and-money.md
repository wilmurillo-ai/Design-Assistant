# Payments and Money — Indonesia

Users need a mixed card-and-cash strategy. Generic "cashless travel" advice breaks quickly outside polished tourist zones.

## Safe Default

| Tool | Role |
|------|------|
| Primary card | Hotels, flights, larger restaurants |
| Backup card | Failure protection |
| Cash in IDR | Small food, parking, tips, ferries, local drivers |
| Banking app | Card freezes, withdrawal checks, fraud response |

## Payment Reality by Context

| Context | Card friendliness |
|---------|-------------------|
| Bali tourist core | High but not universal |
| Jakarta malls and hotels | High |
| Yogyakarta and Java cities | Mixed but workable |
| Small islands, harbors, local warungs | Cash often still needed |

## ATM Rules

- Prefer bank-attached machines.
- Withdraw in larger, safer chunks rather than tiny repeated amounts.
- Carry smaller notes for drivers and small purchases.
- Keep emergency cash separate from day wallet.

## Tipping and Small Payments

- Service charge may already appear at larger venues.
- Small rounding or modest cash tips are normal for drivers, porters, and helpful hotel staff.
- Avoid flashing large notes in small harbors or roadside stops.

## Traps

- Arriving late without cash or a working debit card
- Assuming island boat operators always take card
- Relying on one card only
- Withdrawing at sketchy standalone ATMs
