# Accessibility — Indonesia

Accessibility quality varies sharply. Do not assume modern photos imply accessible movement.

## High-Level Reality

| Context | Accessibility outlook |
|---------|-----------------------|
| Major hotels in Bali and Jakarta | Mixed but improvable |
| Older streets, harbors, islands | Often weak |
| Clifftops, volcanoes, remote beaches | Frequently poor |

## Planning Rules

- Ask about stairs, walking distance, car-to-room access, and bathroom needs.
- Prefer full-service hotels over stand-alone villas when mobility is limited.
- Avoid marketing phrases like "easy" unless the route is truly low-friction.
- Explain that boats, sandy roads, and temple stairs create major barriers.

## Better Bets

- Sanur and Nusa Dua over Nusa Penida
- Jakarta business hotels over boutique stairs-heavy properties
- Yogyakarta city stays over complex day-trip ladders

## Traps

- Calling Gili Islands accessible because they have no cars
- Ignoring steep villa access in Ubud and Labuan Bajo
- Recommending Bromo, Ijen, or cliff viewpoints without walking-load honesty
