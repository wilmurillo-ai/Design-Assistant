# Transport Domestic — Indonesia

Indonesia transport is about choosing the least fragile option, not the theoretically fastest one.

## Best Mode by Situation

| Situation | Best default |
|-----------|--------------|
| Long island-to-island move | Flight |
| Java cultural route | Train plus car transfers |
| Short Bali movement | Driver or app ride |
| Harbor or island hop | Boat only with time buffer |
| Early-volcano start | Driver near trailhead |

## Flights

- Best for Bali, Flores, Lombok, Jakarta, and bigger island jumps
- Add baggage cost and delay tolerance to every plan
- Keep a buffer before any liveaboard, international leg, or fixed-time activity

## Trains

Java is where trains add real value:
- Yogyakarta to Surabaya corridors
- Better comfort than long road transfers for many users
- Easier family pacing than buses

## Boats and Ferries

| Type | Use with caution |
|------|------------------|
| Fast boat | Tourist island hops, but weather and sea state matter |
| Public ferry | Slower, cheaper, less elegant |
| Day boat trips | Great only when the user accepts sun, motion, and schedule drift |

## Transfer Rules

1. Never promise same-day boat plus flight without a backup.
2. For families or older travelers, optimize for lower stress rather than lower cost.
3. In Bali, 10 km can still mean heavy traffic.
4. In Komodo or Gili routes, exact harbor timing matters more than map distance.

## App Guidance

- Use ride apps in Jakarta and Bali where coverage is strong.
- Screenshot tickets and hotel addresses.
- Keep one offline map option when heading to remote areas.
