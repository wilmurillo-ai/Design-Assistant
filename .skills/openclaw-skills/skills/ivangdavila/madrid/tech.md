# Tech Industry Guide — Madrid

## Tech Scene Overview

Madrid is Spain's largest tech hub and growing fast in European rankings. Strong in fintech, mobility, proptech, and enterprise software. Not Silicon Valley scale, but excellent quality of life + reasonable salaries + growing ecosystem.

**European ranking:** #33 globally, #5 in Europe for VC-backed startups (2025)
**Key sectors:** Fintech, mobility, AI, proptech, industrial digitization

## Visa for Tech Workers

### EU Citizens
No visa needed. Work freely.

### Non-EU Citizens

#### Digital Nomad Visa (2023+)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **For** | Remote workers for non-Spanish companies |
| **Income** | Min €2,520/month (2x min wage) |
| **Duration** | 1 year, renewable to 5 years |
| **Tax** | Special regime possible (24% flat for 5 years) |
| **Processing** | 2-4 weeks |

#### Highly Qualified Professional Visa

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **For** | Employed by Spanish company |
| **Salary threshold** | €42,000/year (varies by sector) |
| **Education** | University degree usually required |
| **Sponsorship** | Employer must apply |
| **Timeline** | 4-8 weeks |

#### Entrepreneur Visa

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **For** | Starting innovative business |
| **Endorsement** | ENISA or approved body |
| **Business plan** | Must show innovation + viability |
| **Timeline** | 8-12 weeks |

## Spain Hiring Process

### How It Works

| Aspect | Spanish Practice |
|--------|------------------|
| **Interviews** | 3-4 rounds typical |
| **Technical test** | Common, often take-home |
| **Timeline** | 2-4 weeks |
| **Negotiation** | Less aggressive than US |
| **Notice periods** | 15 days minimum, often 1 month |
| **Probation** | 6 months typical |

### Standard Benefits (Spain)

| Benefit | Standard |
|---------|----------|
| **Holiday** | 22 working days minimum (30 calendar days) |
| **Healthcare** | Public (Seguridad Social) covered |
| **Private health** | Common perk at tech companies |
| **Pension** | Social Security contributions |
| **Lunch vouchers** | €11/day common (tax-free) |
| **Training budget** | Often €1,000-2,000/year |
| **Remote work** | Hybrid common (2-3 days office) |

### Red Flags

- "We're like family" (often means overtime)
- No salary range in job post
- "Startup salary, equity later"
- Very long notice periods (3+ months = they struggle to retain)

## Tax Considerations

### Beckham Law (Flat Tax)

If you qualify:
- 24% flat tax on Spanish income (vs progressive up to 47%)
- 6-year eligibility
- For those who haven't been Spanish tax resident in 10 years
- Also applies to digital nomad visa holders

### Regular Tax Brackets

| Income | Rate |
|--------|------|
| Up to €12,450 | 19% |
| €12,450-20,200 | 24% |
| €20,200-35,200 | 30% |
| €35,200-60,000 | 37% |
| €60,000-300,000 | 45% |
| Over €300,000 | 47% |

**Note:** Regional variations in Madrid slightly favorable.
