# Transport Guide — Madrid

Practical transport guide for navigating Madrid. Covers all modes, fares (as of February 2026), and decision-making shortcuts.

---

## System Overview

Madrid has excellent public transport managed by CRTM (Consorcio Regional de Transportes de Madrid).

| Mode | Coverage | Best For |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Metro** | 13 lines, 302 stations | Central Madrid, cross-city |
| **EMT Buses** | 200+ urban routes | Local trips, areas without metro |
| **Cercanías** | Commuter rail, 9 lines | Airport, suburbs, day trips |
| **BiciMAD** | 600+ stations, e-bikes | Short trips, exercise |
| **Metro Ligero** | 3 light rail lines | Northern/western suburbs |

**Key Insight:** Metro is fastest and most reliable. EMT buses fill gaps. Cercanías essential for airport and suburbs.

## Tarjeta Multi vs Abono — Which to Use

### Quick Decision

| Situation | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| **Tourist (1-7 days)** | Tarjeta Turística or Tarjeta Multi |
| **Living in Madrid (under 26)** | Abono Joven (€10/month!) |
| **Living in Madrid (26-64)** | Abono Zona A (€32.70/month with discount) |
| **Living in Madrid (65+)** | FREE (Tarjeta Azul) |
| **Occasional use** | Tarjeta Multi (pay per trip) |

### Tarjeta Multi (Pay-As-You-Go)

Rechargeable card for metro, bus, and Cercanías.

| Ticket Type | Price | Notes |
|-------------|-------|-------|
| **Billete sencillo Metro** | €1.50-2.00 | Based on stations (€1.50 up to 5 stations) |
| **Metrobús 10 viajes** | €7.30 | 10 trips, metro or EMT bus |
| **Billete sencillo EMT** | €1.50 | Single bus ride |
| **Airport supplement** | €3.00 | Additional fee to/from airport metro |

**How it works:**
1. Buy Tarjeta Multi at any metro station (€2.50 card fee)
2. Load tickets or Metrobús onto it
3. Tap in/out at metro, tap on bus

**Requirements:**

## Zone System

Madrid uses concentric zones from center.

| Zone | Coverage |
|------|----------|

| **B1** | Near suburbs (Alcorcón, Leganés) |

| **B3** | Far suburbs |
| **C1-C2** | Regional (Toledo direction) |

**Most visitors and residents only need Zona A.**

## Getting from Airports

### Madrid-Barajas (MAD) — Main Airport

| Option | Time | Cost | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-------|
| **Metro (Line 8)** | 20-30 min | €4.50-5.00 (€1.50-2.00 + €3 supplement) | 06:00-01:30, connects at Nuevos Ministerios |
| **Cercanías (C1)** | 25 min | €2.60 | T4 only, to Atocha/Chamartín |
| **EMT Bus 200** | 40 min | €5.00 | 24/7, to Avenida de América |
| **Airport Express (Bus)** | 30-40 min | €5.00 | 24/7, to Atocha via Cibeles |
| **Taxi** | 20-30 min | €33 (fixed) | Fixed rate to/from center |
| **Uber/Cabify** | 20-30 min | €25-40 | Variable pricing |

**Recommendation:** 
- Day: Metro (cheapest if you have Abono, includes airport supplement free)
- Night: Airport Express bus (24/7)
- Groups: Taxi (€33 fixed is good value for 3-4 people)

**IMPORTANT:** Taxi has FIXED €33 rate to/from city center. Refuse any other price.

### Terminal Notes
- **T1, T2, T3**: Older terminals, connected by free shuttle
- **T4 & T4S**: Newer, separate metro stop, Cercanías stop

## BiciMAD — Electric Bike Share

Public e-bike system covering central Madrid.

### Pricing (2026)

| Option | Cost |
|--------|------|
| **Per trip** | €0.50 first 30 min, €0.50 each additional 30 min |
| **Monthly subscription** | €10/month (30 min free per trip) |
| **Annual subscription** | €25/year (trips still cost €0.50) |
| **Occasional user** | €2 activation + per-trip costs |

### How It Works
1. Download BiciMAD app
2. Register with credit card
3. Find station with available bikes
4. Unlock via app (QR code)
5. Ride (max 2 hours per trip)
6. Dock at any station

### Tips
- **Dock properly** — you'll keep being charged if bike not docked
- **Hills**: Madrid has them. Battery helps but plan accordingly
- **Availability**: Central stations often empty/full at rush hour
- **All electric**: Better for Madrid's hills than regular bikes

## Driving — Why NOT to Drive

### The Costs & Hassles

| Issue | Impact |
|-------|--------|
| **ZBE (Low Emission Zone)** | Older vehicles restricted in center |
| **SER Parking** | €1.50-3.90/hour in central zones |
| **Finding parking** | Nearly impossible in center |
| **Traffic** | Heavy during rush hours |

### ZBE Madrid (Low Emission Zone)

Madrid 360 environmental zone restricts vehicles:
- **Centro**: Most restricted, older vehicles banned
- **Rest of city**: Based on environmental label
- **Check:** madrid.es/zonadebajasemisiones

### When Driving Makes Sense
- Day trips outside Madrid
- Moving furniture
- Shopping at IKEA/Costco
- Airport with lots of luggage

## Late Night Transport

### Metro Hours
- **Weekdays**: 06:00-01:30
- **Weekends**: 06:00-02:00 (Fri-Sat nights)

### Night Buses (Búho)

When metro closes, "owl buses" (búhos) take over.

| Hub | Routes |
|-----|--------|
| **Plaza de Cibeles** | All night bus routes start here |
| **Frequency** | Every 20-35 min, 23:00-06:00 |
| **Fare** | Same as day bus (€1.50) |

**Key routes:**
- N1, N2: Northern neighborhoods
- N3: Eastern (Ventas, Salamanca)
- N4, N5: Southern neighborhoods

### Taxi/Uber at Night

| Service | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| **Taxi** | White cars, green light = available |
| **Uber** | Works in Madrid |
| **Cabify** | Spanish alternative, often cheaper |
| **Bolt** | Also available |

**Night supplement:** Taxis charge extra 22:00-06:00 (built into meter).

## Quick Decision Rules

| Situation | Do This |
|-----------|---------|
| Airport → Center (day) | Metro Line 8 + €3 supplement |
| Airport → Center (night) | Airport Express bus (24/7) |
| Airport → Center (group) | Taxi (€33 fixed) |

| Short trip (<2km) | Walk or BiciMAD |
