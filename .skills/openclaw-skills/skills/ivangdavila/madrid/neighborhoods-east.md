# East Madrid Neighborhoods

## 1. Salamanca

**Best for**: High-net-worth individuals, luxury seekers, business executives, expats

**Character**: Madrid's most prestigious address. 19th-century grid layout, designer boutiques on Calle Serrano and Ortega y Gasset. Old money meets new wealth. Extremely elegant, somewhat snobbish.

| Rent | 1BR: €1,800–2,500 | 2BR: €2,500–4,000+ |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Safest neighborhood, prestigious address, excellent shopping, beautiful architecture, Michelin restaurants
**Cons**: Very expensive, can feel stuffy, limited nightlife, less diverse

**Transit**: Serrano, Velázquez, Núñez de Balboa, Goya
**Must-do**: Shopping on Calle Serrano, Goya's paintings at Fundación Carlos de Amberes, brunch at El Perro y La Galleta

### Sub-areas within Salamanca:
- **Recoletos**: Most upscale, near Paseo de Recoletos, museums
- **Goya**: More accessible, good metro connections
- **Lista**: Residential, families, slightly more affordable
- **Castellana**: Business corridor, corporate

---

## 2. Retiro

**Best for**: Families, nature lovers, those wanting green space with central access

**Character**: Named for the magnificent Retiro Park. Elegant residential area with tree-lined streets. Mix of families and professionals. Peaceful but not boring.

| Rent | 1BR: €1,400–1,800 | 2BR: €1,800–2,400 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Retiro Park at your doorstep, very safe, family-friendly, good schools, beautiful streets
**Cons**: Can feel quiet, limited nightlife, fewer young people

**Transit**: Retiro, Ibiza, Pacífico, Atocha
**Must-do**: Row boats on Retiro lake, Crystal Palace, Sunday walk through Rosaleda

### Sub-areas within Retiro:
- **Jerónimos**: Near Prado, most prestigious
- **Ibiza**: More residential, younger
- **Pacífico**: Near Atocha, well-connected
- **Niño Jesús**: Next to park, very quiet

---

## 3. Chamartín

**Best for**: Business professionals, corporate expats, those working in business district

**Character**: Madrid's business district. Modern offices, AZCA financial center, Santiago Bernabéu stadium. Professional atmosphere, international restaurants.

| Rent | 1BR: €1,500–2,100 | 2BR: €2,000–2,800 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Near business district, excellent transport (Chamartín train station), Real Madrid stadium, modern amenities
**Cons**: Less authentic Madrid feel, corporate atmosphere, quiet weekends

**Transit**: Chamartín, Plaza de Castilla, Santiago Bernabéu, Nuevos Ministerios
**Must-do**: Santiago Bernabéu tour, aperitivo at AZCA rooftops, Cuatro Torres skyline view

---

## 4. Ventas-Guindalera

**Best for**: Middle-class families, those wanting authentic barrio life

**Character**: Traditional working/middle-class Madrid. Las Ventas bullring is the landmark. Less polished than Salamanca but genuine local life.

| Rent | 1BR: €1,000–1,400 | 2BR: €1,300–1,800 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Affordable for east side, authentic Madrid, good local markets, decent transport
**Cons**: Less fashionable, limited international dining, older buildings

**Transit**: Ventas, Manuel Becerra, Diego de León
**Must-do**: Las Ventas (even without bullfight, architecture is stunning), Mercado de Ventas, local tapas bars

---

## 5. Prosperidad

**Best for**: Young families, first-time buyers, those wanting balance of price and location

**Character**: Pleasant residential neighborhood north of Salamanca. Good schools, parks, local shops. Not glamorous but comfortable and practical.

| Rent | 1BR: €1,100–1,500 | 2BR: €1,400–1,900 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: More affordable than Salamanca, family-friendly, good transport, local feel
**Cons**: Not exciting, limited nightlife, unremarkable architecture

**Transit**: Prosperidad, Alfonso XIII, Concha Espina
**Must-do**: Local bakeries, weekend markets, Parque Berlín

---

## 6. Moratalaz

**Best for**: Families seeking safety and quiet, retirees, budget-conscious

**Character**: One of Madrid's safest districts. Residential, quiet, with parks and local shops. Not trendy but excellent quality of life. Strong community feel.

| Rent | 1BR: €900–1,200 | 2BR: €1,100–1,500 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Very safe, affordable, quiet, good for families, parking easier
**Cons**: Disconnected from central nightlife, limited metro coverage, older architecture

**Transit**: Vinateros, Artilleros, Pavones (Line 9)
**Must-do**: Parque de la Cuña Verde, local mercados, peaceful evening walks
