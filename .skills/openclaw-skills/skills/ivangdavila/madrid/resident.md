# Resident Guide — Moving to Madrid

## Before Moving

### Visa Requirements

| Visa Type | For Whom | Key Requirements |
|-----------|----------|------------------|
| **EU/EEA citizens** | Europeans | No visa. Register after 3 months (NIE) |
| **Digital Nomad** | Remote workers | €2,520/month income, non-Spanish employer |
| **Highly Qualified** | Employed by Spanish company | €42,000+ salary, degree |
| **Entrepreneur** | Starting business | ENISA endorsement, business plan |
| **Non-Lucrative** | Retirees, wealthy | €28,800/year income, no work |
| **Student** | Studying | University acceptance, €600/month funds |

### EU/EEA Citizens

No visa required. After 3 months:
1. Get NIE (Número de Identidad de Extranjero)
2. Register at Registro Central de Extranjeros
3. Takes 2-4 weeks with appointment

### Non-EU Process

1. Apply for visa at Spanish consulate (home country)
2. Arrive in Spain
3. Apply for TIE (Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero) within 30 days
4. Process NIE simultaneously

### Timeline for Moving

| Phase | Timeframe | Actions |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Planning** | 3-6 months before | Job search (if needed), visa application |
| **Pre-arrival** | 1-2 months before | Arrange temporary housing, flights |
| **Arrival week** | Days 1-7 | Temporary housing, phone SIM, bank app |
| **First month** | Week 1-4 | NIE/TIE, empadronamiento, permanent housing |
| **Settling** | Month 1-3 | Social Security, health, routines |

## Finding Housing

### The Rental Process

**Typical timeline:** 1-3 weeks from search to move-in

| Step | What Happens | Duration |
|------|--------------|----------|
| 1. Search | Idealista, Fotocasa, Badi | 1-2 weeks |
| 2. Viewing | Contact via platform, schedule | 1-3 days |
| 3. Application | Provide documents | Same day |
| 4. Contract | Review, sign (usually Spanish) | 1-2 days |
| 5. Move-in | Pay deposit + first month | Agreed date |

### Documents Required to Rent

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| **Passport/ID** | Identity |
| **NIE** | Tax ID (or promise to obtain) |
| **Employment contract** | Proof of income |
| **Last 3 payslips** | Income verification |
| **Bank statements** | Financial health |
| **Previous landlord contact** | Reference (optional) |

**Newcomer challenges:**
- No NIE yet → some landlords accept promise to obtain
- No Spanish payslips → offer larger deposit, guarantor
- No bank statements → international statements accepted sometimes

### Deposit Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Standard deposit** | 1-2 months rent |
| **Maximum legal** | 2 months for residential |
| **Return timeline** | Within 1 month of leaving |
| **Deposit protection** | Not legally required (unlike UK) |

### Typical Upfront Costs

| Cost | Amount |
|------|--------|
| **Deposit (fianza)** | 1-2 months |
| **First month** | Due on signing |
| **Agency fee (if used)** | 1 month + 21% IVA |
| **Total** | 3-4 months rent |

## Essential Registrations

### NIE (Número de Identidad de Extranjero)

**What:** Your tax/ID number for everything in Spain
**Who needs it:** Everyone planning to stay long-term
**Where:** Policía Nacional (Extranjería office)

| Requirement | Notes |
|-------------|-------|
| Appointment | Book via sede.administracionespublicas.gob.es |
| Form EX-15 | Downloaded and filled |
| Passport + copy | |
| Proof of reason | Job contract, rental, etc. |
| Fee | ~€16 (paid at bank) |

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks from appointment

### Empadronamiento (Padrón)

**What:** Municipal registration proving your address
**Why needed:** Healthcare, NIE, voting (EU), schools
**Where:** Local Ayuntamiento (city hall) office

| Requirement | Notes |
|-------------|-------|
| Appointment | Madrid.es |
| Passport/ID | |
| Proof of address | Rental contract or utility bill |
| Form | Provided at office |

**Timeline:** Same day (usually)

### Social Security Number

**What:** Needed for healthcare and employment
**Where:** Tesorería General de la Seguridad Social
**When:** After NIE, before starting work

| Path | Process |
|------|---------|
| **Employed** | Employer registers you |
| **Self-employed (autónomo)** | Register yourself |
| **Non-working (Convenio)** | Pay €60/month for healthcare |

## Setting Up Life

### Opening a Bank Account

**Traditional banks:**

| Bank | Account Type | Requirements |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Santander** | Various | NIE + passport |
| **BBVA** | Cuenta Negocios | NIE + passport |
| **CaixaBank** | imagin (app) | Just passport |
| **Bankinter** | Various | NIE + address |

**Digital banks (easier for newcomers):**

| Bank | Features | Requirements |
|------|----------|--------------|
| **N26** | EU-wide, free | Passport |
| **Revolut** | Multi-currency | Passport |
| **Openbank** (Santander) | Spanish, full features | NIE |
| **Evo Banco** | Good interest | NIE |

**Recommendation for newcomers:**
1. Open N26 or Revolut immediately
2. Open Spanish bank after NIE (needed for some things)

### Healthcare Registration

**Public Healthcare (Seguridad Social):**

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Get Social Security number (via work or Convenio) |
| 2 | Apply for Tarjeta Sanitaria at Centro de Salud |
| 3 | Present: NIE, empadronamiento, Social Security doc |
| 4 | Receive card (weeks) or temporary document |

**Access while waiting:**
- Urgencias (ER) treats everyone regardless
- Private insurance for routine needs

**Private Healthcare:**

| Insurer | Monthly Cost | Notes |
|---------|--------------|-------|
| **Sanitas** | €50-100 | Extensive network |
| **Adeslas** | €45-90 | Good coverage |
| **Asisa** | €40-80 | Budget option |
| **DKV** | €50-90 | German company |

## Cultural Adaptation

### Spanish Schedule

| Activity | Time |
|----------|------|
| **Work** | 9am-2pm, 4pm-7pm (traditional) or 8am-5pm (modern) |
| **Lunch** | 2-4pm (MAIN meal) |
| **Dinner** | 9-11pm |
| **Going out** | Starts 11pm+ |
| **Shops** | Many close 2-5pm (less common now) |

### Social Norms

| Spanish Trait | Reality |
|---------------|---------|
| **Time flexibility** | 10-15 min late is normal socially |
| **Physical contact** | Two kisses greeting (cheek to cheek, right first) |
| **Loudness** | Spaniards are loud. It's normal. |
| **Directness** | More direct than UK, less than Germany |
| **Work-life balance** | Sacred. Don't expect 60-hour weeks |

### Making Friends

| Method | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| **Intercambio** | Language exchange = friend making |
| **Expat groups** | Meetup, Facebook, InterNations |
| **Sports clubs** | Padel, running groups |
| **Work colleagues** | After-work drinks (cañas) |
| **Neighbors** | Slower but authentic |

**Reality:** Making Spanish friends takes time. They have established friend groups. Persistence + language helps.

## Practical Services

### Post Office (Correos)

- Yellow buildings/signs
- For packages: use lockers or Correos app
- DHL, UPS, SEUR for international

### Driving License

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **EU license** | Valid indefinitely |
| **UK/US/etc.** | Valid 6 months, then must exchange/take test |
| **Exchange** | DGT appointment, ~€30 |
| **Full test** | €90 + driving school |

### Pets

| Need | Where |
|------|-------|
| **Vet** | Many local veterinarios |
| **Registration** | Ayuntamiento (microchip required) |
| **Pet-friendly housing** | Many rentals allow, ask first |
| **Parks** | Dog areas in most parks |
