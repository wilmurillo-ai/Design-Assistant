# Scene Design

Use this file when a story needs emotional impact, credibility, or better pacing.

## Scene Recipe

A high-quality scene usually includes:
- place and time anchor
- one active character objective
- one constraint or conflict
- one action with consequence
- one concrete sensory or operational detail

## Scene Compression Pattern

When space is limited, keep:
- one setting cue
- one decisive action
- one outcome detail

Remove decorative context that does not affect meaning.

## Escalation Ladder

Escalate in this order:
1. inconvenience
2. repeated friction
3. explicit risk
4. forced decision
5. irreversible consequence

Skipping directly to high drama often feels unearned.

## Scene Editing Checks

- Can the audience infer stakes without extra explanation?
- Does each sentence move action, context, or consequence?
- Is there any sentence that only sounds good but adds no meaning?
- Is the scene length proportional to its strategic importance?
