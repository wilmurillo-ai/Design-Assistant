# Valencia Visitor Itineraries

## 1-Day Itinerary: Essential Valencia

Best for: Cruise passengers, day-trippers, very short stays.

### Morning (9:00-13:00)
**Centro Histórico**
- 09:00 — Mercado Central: coffee and pastry, explore the market
- 10:00 — La Lonja de la Seda (30 min)
- 10:45 — Plaza de la Virgen: Cathedral exterior, Basílica de la Virgen
- 11:15 — Cathedral + Micalet tower (1 hour)
- 12:30 — Walk through El Carmen, Torres de Serranos

### Lunch (13:30-15:30)
**El Carmen or Ruzafa**
- Paella at Casa Roberto (Centro) or La Salvaora (Ruzafa)
- Or tapas crawl in El Carmen

### Afternoon (16:00-19:00)
**Ciudad de las Artes y las Ciencias**
- Walk or bike through Jardín del Turia (30 min)
- Arrive at CAC for photos and L'Umbracle gardens
- Optional: Oceanogràfic if time permits (2+ hours needed)

### Evening (20:00+)
**Dinner & Drinks**
- Sunset drinks at rooftop bar (Ático, Hotel Caro)
- Dinner in Ruzafa (international options)
- Bar hop in El Carmen if energy remains

## 3-Day Itinerary: Complete Valencia

### Day 1: Historic Center & Culture

**Morning**
- 09:00 — Mercado Central (arrive before crowds)
- 10:30 — La Lonja de la Seda
- 11:15 — Cathedral + Santo Cáliz + Micalet tower
- 12:30 — Plaza de la Reina, coffee break

**Lunch**
- Menú del día in El Carmen (€12-15)

**Afternoon**
- 15:00 — Barrio del Carmen walking exploration
- 16:00 — Torres de Serranos (climb for views)
- 17:00 — IVAM contemporary art museum
- 18:30 — Horchata at Horchatería Santa Catalina

**Evening**
- Dinner in Ruzafa
- Cocktails: Olhöps (craft beer) or Café de las Horas (eclectic)

### Day 2: Science, Art & Beach

**Morning**
- 10:00 — Ciudad de las Artes y las Ciencias
- Focus on Oceanogràfic (3 hours) OR Museo de las Ciencias + L'Umbracle

**Lunch**
- Quick lunch at CAC or head to beach

**Afternoon**
- 14:30 — Malvarrosa Beach
- Swim, sunbathe, chiringuito drinks
- 17:00 — Walk to El Cabanyal neighborhood
- Explore this gentrifying fisherman's quarter

**Evening**
- Sunset on Patacona beach
- Dinner at La Pepica (paella, historic) or Casa Montaña (tapas, 1836)

### Day 3: Ruzafa, Art & Jardín del Turia

**Morning**
- 10:00 — Brunch in Ruzafa (Federal, Bluebell, Copenhagen)
- 11:30 — Explore Ruzafa: vintage shops, cafes, street art
- 13:00 — Mercado de Ruzafa (smaller, more local than Central)

**Lunch**
- Tapas in Ruzafa: Bar Ricardo, Casa Guillermo

**Afternoon**
- 15:30 — Museo de Bellas Artes (free, 1.5 hours)
- 17:30 — Rent Valenbisi bikes
- Ride through Jardín del Turia end to end
- Stop at Gulliver Park if with kids

**Evening**
- Sunset drinks at L'Umbracle terrace (summer) or rooftop bar
- Farewell dinner at a nice restaurant (Lienzo, Fierro, Canalla Bistro)

## 7-Day Itinerary: Deep Dive

### Days 1-3
Follow 3-day itinerary above.

### Day 4: La Albufera Day Trip

**Morning**
- 10:00 — Bus 25 or rent car to La Albufera
- 11:00 — Explore El Palmar village
- 12:00 — Walk rice paddies, birdwatching

**Lunch**
- 13:30 — Authentic paella in El Palmar (Nou Racó, Bon Aire)
- This is THE place for real Valencian paella

**Afternoon**
- 16:00 — Boat ride on the lagoon
- 18:00 — Return to Valencia

**Evening**
- Light dinner (you're full of paella)
- Jazz at Jimmy Glass or live music at Loco Club

### Day 5: Bioparc & Western Valencia

**Morning**
- 10:00 — Bioparc Valencia (2-3 hours)
- Amazing modern zoo, arrive when it opens

**Lunch**
- Eat at Bioparc or nearby in Campanar

**Afternoon**
- 15:00 — IVAM if not done yet
- 16:30 — Jardines de Monforte (romantic formal gardens, free)
- 17:30 — Jardines del Real (Viveros) — larger park

**Evening**
- Sunset from Jardines del Real
- Dinner in Benimaclet (student neighborhood, cheap eats)
- Alternative: flamenco show at El Loco del Pelo

### Day 6: Day Trip Options

**Option A: Sagunto (30 min by train)**
- Roman theater and castle
- Charming old town
- Half-day trip, back for late lunch

**Option B: Peñíscola (1.5 hours)**
- "Gibraltar of Valencia"
- Medieval castle on peninsula
- Beautiful beaches
- Full day trip

**Option C: Xàtiva (45 min by train)**
- Hilltop castle with stunning views
- Birthplace of the Borgia popes
- Peaceful old town
- Half-day trip

**Option D: Requena (1 hour)**
- Wine region (cava and reds)
- Bodega visits and tastings
- Underground caves
- Half to full day

### Day 7: Relaxation & Departure

**Morning**
- Sleep in
- Brunch at favorite Ruzafa spot
- Last-minute shopping: souvenirs at Mercado Central

**Midday**
- 12:00 — Beach time or revisit favorite spot
- Pack

**Afternoon**
- Depending on departure time:
  - Final paseo by the river
  - One more horchata
  - Metro to airport (30 min from Centro)

## Practical Planning Tips

### Best Months
- **April-May, September-October**: Perfect weather, manageable crowds
- **Avoid**: August (locals leave, heat) and March 15-19 (Fallas chaos unless you want it)

### Book Ahead
- Oceanogràfic: summer weekends
- Top restaurants (Ricard Camarena, El Poblet): 2-4 weeks
- Fallas period: hotels 3-6 months ahead

### Valencia Card
- €15-25 for 24-72 hours
- Unlimited metro/bus
- Museum discounts
- Worth it for 2+ days of active sightseeing

### Pace Yourself
- Valencia rewards slow exploration
- Don't rush between attractions
- Take the Spanish pace: long lunch, late dinner
- Siesta time (14:00-17:00) = shop closures, embrace it
