# Regions — Chile

## Macro-Region Selection Grid

| Region | Best for | Watch-outs |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Santiago and Central Valley | First-time entry point, city, wine, museums, easy day trips | Urban theft risk, heat in summer, smog periods |
| Valparaiso and Vina coast | Color, sea air, food, weekend contrast with Santiago | Hills, stairs, parking friction, weekend price spikes |
| Atacama and Norte Grande | Desert landscapes, astronomy, lagoons, altiplano scenery | Altitude, cold nights, long transfers, dry climate |
| Lake District and Chiloe | Volcano-lake scenery, softer nature, family-friendly routes | Rain, car dependence outside core towns |
| Patagonia and Magallanes | Torres del Paine, glaciers, wildlife, iconic trekking | Wind, high costs, reservations, weather disruption |
| Aysen and Carretera Austral | Deep road trip, fjords, ferries, remote landscapes | Ripio, ferry timing, limited services, slower travel |
| Rapa Nui | Archaeology, island culture, big-sky Pacific isolation | Extra entry rules, high prices, limited flexibility |

## Planning Rule of Thumb

- 5-7 days: Santiago plus coast, or one macro-region only
- 8-10 days: Central Chile plus one major region
- 12-14 days: One major region done properly, or two only if flights are accepted
- 21 days: Desert plus central, or lakes plus Patagonia, with real transfer buffers

## First-Time Chile Pairings That Work

- Cities + wine + coast: Santiago + Valparaiso/Vina
- Desert focus: Santiago + San Pedro de Atacama
- Softer nature: Santiago + Puerto Varas/Lake District
- Big trekking trip: Santiago + Puerto Natales/Torres

## Combos That Usually Underperform

- Atacama + Patagonia in under 12 days
- Rapa Nui attached to an already full mainland trip
- Carretera Austral without extra weather slack
- Lake District + Torres del Paine as if they were interchangeable
