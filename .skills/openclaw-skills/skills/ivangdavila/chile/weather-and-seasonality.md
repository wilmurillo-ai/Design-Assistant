# Weather and Seasonality — Chile

## Season Matrix

| Zone | Best windows | Watch-outs |
|------|--------------|------------|
| Santiago / Central | Spring and fall | Summer heat, winter smog and rain spells |
| Coast | Spring through summer | Cool marine air, weekend crowds |
| Atacama | Year-round with planning | Hot sun, cold nights, altitude, occasional winter effects at altitude |
| Lake District | Late spring to early fall | Rain can reshape plans anytime |
| Patagonia | Late spring through early fall | Wind, volatile weather, shorter shoulder logistics |
| Rapa Nui | Broad year-round appeal | Humidity, wind, and limited flexibility on a small island |

## Rule of Thumb

- November to March is strongest for southern Chile and classic Patagonia.
- Shoulder months often win for Santiago, wine country, and the coast.
- Atacama works most of the year if the traveler respects altitude and temperature swings.
- Lakes are greener and softer than Patagonia, but rain still drives the plan.

## Trip-Shaping Hazards

- Patagonia wind can erase long-hike quality even on technically open days.
- Rain and lower visibility matter more in the lake district than raw temperature alone.
- Atacama sun and dehydration can feel harder than the forecast suggests.
- Coastal and volcanic zones should always include emergency-awareness basics.

## Month Logic

- If the user has only one winter window: favor city, wine, and selected desert rather than classic Patagonia.
- If the user has peak summer: Patagonia becomes viable, but reservation pressure rises.
- If the user has flexible shoulder dates: central Chile plus one macro-region usually gives the best crowd-value balance.
