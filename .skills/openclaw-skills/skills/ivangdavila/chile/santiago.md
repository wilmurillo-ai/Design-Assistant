# Santiago — Chile

## Best Use Case

Santiago works as entry buffer, city break, museum-and-food base, wine-country gateway, and transfer stabilizer before a bigger region.

## Stay Strategy

| Style | Best fit |
|-------|----------|
| First-time city stay | Providencia or Las Condes for smoother logistics |
| Walkable culture and food | Lastarria / central-cultural areas with hotel quality checks |
| Budget | Near strong transit, not just the cheapest address |

## Good 2-4 Day Structure

- Day 1: arrival, neighborhood orientation, low-friction city center
- Day 2: historic center + museum or hill viewpoint + strong dinner
- Day 3: wine valley, Cajon del Maipo, or market/food day
- Day 4: buffer or transfer day

## What Santiago Is Good At

- Easy adjustment day before or after long domestic routes
- Museums, food, urban parks, and day trips
- Pairing with Valparaiso/Vina without much friction

## What Santiago Is Not

- The best place to overcommit nightlife and next-day flights without sleep buffer
- A city where every cheap central location is equally comfortable for tourists
- A reason to skip Chile's nature completely if the user actually wants landscapes

## Practical Warnings

- Urban theft risk exists in busy transit and tourist zones.
- Summer heat can flatten ambitious city walking.
- Demonstrations can change route logic in the center.
