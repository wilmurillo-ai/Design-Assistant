# Rapa Nui — Chile

## Why Rapa Nui Is Different

Rapa Nui is not "just another Chile stop." It has separate entry frictions, limited flexibility, and a distinct archaeological visit logic.

## Entry and Stay Rules

- Chile Travel currently states a maximum island stay of 30 days for visitors.
- Travelers need a round-trip ticket and lodging reservation or qualifying invitation.
- The FUI is part of the current pre-arrival process.
- Park access needs its own ticket beyond the flight.

## Trip Design

- 3-5 nights is the normal first-time sweet spot.
- Do not attach it to an already overloaded mainland itinerary.
- Respect archaeology-site rules and guide requirements where stated.
- Treat it as an island trip with less plan-B capacity than mainland Chile.

## Good Expectations

- Culture and archaeology over nightlife
- Scenic coastline and sky
- Higher costs and fewer substitution options
- Easier pace if the traveler accepts the island rhythm

## Mistakes to Prevent

- Forgetting the extra entry layer until after buying flights
- Treating park access like a casual same-day purchase
- Expecting low prices or abundant last-minute choices
