# National Parks — Chile

## Park Planning Rule

Check access, ticketing, and season window before building the itinerary around the park. In Chile, park logistics are often the trip.

## Reservation Reality

- Many protected areas now use `pasesparques.cl`.
- Torres del Paine is the biggest planning trap: park entry is only one layer, while refugio or camping reservations may be the real bottleneck.
- High season means earlier sellout pressure for Torres and some marquee routes.
- Weather, trail conditions, and transport timing can invalidate a fragile day plan.

## Park Types by Planning Pressure

| Type | Examples | Main pressure |
|------|----------|---------------|
| Iconic high-demand | Torres del Paine | Tickets, lodgings, weather, transport chain |
| Easy-access scenic | Vicente Perez Rosales | Vehicle flow, weather, day-trip pacing |
| Altitude and desert landscapes | Atacama protected areas | Acclimatization, tour timing, sun exposure |
| Remote road-trip parks | Aysen / Patagonia corridors | Fuel, ferry, daylight, weak fallback options |

## Torres del Paine Notes

- Base the plan around Puerto Natales or lodge/refugio availability.
- W and O circuits require much more commitment than a scenic park visit.
- Strong wind, shoulder-season weather, and transport timing deserve explicit contingency space.
- Use `patagonia-and-torres-del-paine.md` for trip architecture, not only park facts.

## Park Workflow

1. Choose season.
2. Choose park difficulty level.
3. Confirm access portal and ticket method.
4. Lock transport and overnight plan.
5. Add a weather backup.

## Mistakes to Prevent

- Assuming "I'll buy it there" works in peak windows.
- Mixing high-altitude desert parks with an overpacked same-day schedule.
- Booking Torres entry without solving the nights and transfers around it.
- Forgetting that some "easy scenic" parks still require long driving days.
