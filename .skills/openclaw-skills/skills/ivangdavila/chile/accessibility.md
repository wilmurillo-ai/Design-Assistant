# Accessibility — Chile

## Accessibility Reality

Chile can work well, but accessibility quality varies a lot between modern urban infrastructure and historic or nature-heavy areas.

## Easier Zones

- Newer hotels in Santiago
- Main airports and formal transfer services
- Selected metro and urban-service corridors
- Some lake-district stays built for leisure travel

## Harder Zones

- Valparaiso hills and uneven surfaces
- Historic areas with stairs and steep streets
- Rural or park-heavy routes
- Budget accommodation that does not clearly document adapted access

## Planning Rules

1. Base accessibility trips around the smoothest overnight base.
2. Verify elevator, bathroom, and transfer details before recommending a property.
3. Do not assume scenic routes are mobility-friendly.
4. Separate "city-accessible" from "park-accessible."

## Good Output Style

Recommend:
- Best base neighborhood
- Transfer method
- Hotel verification checklist
- Realistic day-trip ceiling
