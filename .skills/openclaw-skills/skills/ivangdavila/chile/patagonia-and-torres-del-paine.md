# Patagonia and Torres del Paine — Chile

## Scope Clarifier

Most users who say "Patagonia" really mean one of three things:
- Torres del Paine with Puerto Natales
- A broader Magallanes trip via Punta Arenas
- A deeper self-drive expedition that should maybe be Aysen instead

## Base Strategy

| Base | Better for |
|------|------------|
| Puerto Natales | Torres logistics, scenic gateway, easier first Patagonia trip |
| Punta Arenas | Flights, buffer nights, penguins, broader Magallanes routing |
| Park lodges/refugios | Maximum park immersion, minimum flexibility |

## Trip Architecture

- Add a Santiago buffer on the way in or out when possible.
- Solve park entry plus overnight logistics early.
- Distinguish scenic-visit Patagonia from trekking Patagonia.
- If weather is ugly, the backup plan matters more than the perfect map route.

## What to Warn About

- Wind is not cosmetic.
- Costs rise fast once park lodgings or guided trekking are involved.
- Even non-hikers need reservation discipline in peak season.
- "Open" does not mean comfortable or photogenic.

## Good Minimums

- 4-5 nights for a scenic Torres/Natales block
- More if trekking is the real goal
- More again if Punta Arenas wildlife or long transfers are part of the same plan

## Weak Designs

- Patagonia plus another far-away macro-region in a short trip
- Late booking in peak months
- Same-day flight plus long trek plus no weather cushion
