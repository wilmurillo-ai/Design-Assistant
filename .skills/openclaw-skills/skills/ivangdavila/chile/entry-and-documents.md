# Entry and Documents — Chile

## Fast Decision Tree

1. Check whether the traveler can enter as a standard visitor or needs a consular visa.
2. Confirm passport or national ID eligibility for the traveler's nationality and route.
3. Assume most tourists receive a `Permanencia Transitoria` stay of up to 90 days, but verify the current rule and any nationality-specific exception.
4. If going to Rapa Nui, apply the island's extra requirements on top of Chile entry rules.

## Visitor Pathway

| Path | Typical use | Core limit |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Visa-free or regular tourist entry | Short tourism visits | Usually up to 90 days |
| Consular visa before travel | Nationalities that require it | Rules depend on nationality |
| Temporary-stay extension | Longer tourism stay | One extension of up to 90 days |

## Practical Chile Notes

- SERMIG describes tourist entry under the `Permanencia Transitoria` framework.
- The standard tourist stay is up to 90 continuous days from entry.
- Extension is generally possible once, for up to another 90 days, if requested before expiry.
- The current published SERMIG fee for that extension is USD 100.

## What to Verify Before Booking Non-Refundables

- Passport validity for the whole trip
- Whether the traveler's nationality needs a prior visa
- Return or onward proof when relevant
- Sufficient funds and accommodation logic if border officers ask
- Rapa Nui extra documents if the island is part of the route

## Rapa Nui Add-On Rules

Chile Travel currently states that Rapa Nui visitors must also have:
- A round-trip ticket with a maximum island stay of 30 days
- A completed FUI within 48 hours before the flight
- A reservation in registered accommodation or a qualifying invitation letter
- A separate national park ticket for park access

For archaeological sites such as Orongo or Rano Raraku, Chile Travel says an accredited guide is required.

## Overstay and Work Warnings

- Tourist status is not the same as a residence permit.
- Paid work is not automatically allowed under tourist entry.
- Overstaying can trigger fines and exit problems.
- If timing looks tight, use `sources.md` and refresh the exact rule before advising.
