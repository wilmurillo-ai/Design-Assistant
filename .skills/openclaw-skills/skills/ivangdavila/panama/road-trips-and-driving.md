# Road Trips and Driving

## When Driving Helps

- Best for Azuero, Pacific beach corridors, and flexible inland trips.
- Useful when the user wants stop freedom, photography, or smaller-town lodging.

## When Driving Hurts

- Usually unnecessary in central Panama City.
- Weak fit for San Blas and island-based trips.
- Adds fatigue when the trip already includes boats, flights, and early starts.

## Road Reality

- Main corridors are easier than small coastal or rural access roads.
- Rain can turn short drives into slower, higher-stress segments.
- Parking, hotel access, and night driving quality matter more than map distance.

## Self-Drive Rules

- Avoid late-night unfamiliar coastal drives after landing.
- For surf or beach trips, confirm whether the final road is standard-car friendly.
- If the user is hesitant about aggressive urban traffic, pick up the car after leaving Panama City.
