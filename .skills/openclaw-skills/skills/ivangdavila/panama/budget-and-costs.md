# Budget and Costs

## Cost Pattern

- Panama City is usually the priciest everyday base.
- Highlands can offer better value than the city.
- Islands often look cheap at first, then get expensive through transfers, tours, and cash-only extras.

## Main Budget Levers

### 1. Transfer Style
- Domestic flights cut fatigue but raise total cost.
- Shared shuttles and buses can save money, but they limit flexibility.

### 2. Island Logistics
- Bocas and San Blas budgets rise fast when adding water taxis, tours, baggage handling, and late bookings.
- Coiba trips are usually defined by tour cost more than hotel cost.

### 3. Trip Shape
- One-base or two-base trips usually outperform fast multi-stop routes on both cost and quality.

## Practical Heuristics

- Budget travelers do better by reducing bases and keeping one premium activity per corridor.
- Mid-range trips should reserve budget for transfers first, then upgrade rooms if money remains.
- Premium trips gain more from smarter logistics and fewer lost days than from paying for the highest nightly rate.

## Common Hidden Costs

- Airport transfers
- Boat transfer surcharges
- Cash-only day tours
- National park or island fees
- High wet-season laundry needs
- Last-minute flight changes after weather disruptions
