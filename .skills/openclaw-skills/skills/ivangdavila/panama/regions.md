# Regions

## Best Route Shapes

### Panama City + Canal Corridor
- Best for first trips, short breaks, mixed culture and nature, and low-friction logistics.
- Works well for 3 to 5 nights.

### Bocas del Toro
- Best for island hopping, surf, laid-back social energy, and Caribbean water activities.
- Better when the user accepts boat logistics and wet-weather flexibility.

### Guna Yala / San Blas
- Best for postcard island scenery and one or two simple nights.
- Not ideal for travelers needing comfort, strong Wi-Fi, or fully independent logistics.

### Boquete + Chiriqui Highlands
- Best for cooler air, coffee, hiking, birding, and a calmer pace.
- Strong pairing with a city stay or Pacific coast stop.

### Santa Catalina + Coiba
- Best for diving, snorkeling, wildlife, and a more remote Pacific feel.
- Strong for nature-first travelers who tolerate longer transfers.

### Azuero / Playa Venao / Pedasi / Cambutal
- Best for surf, beach downtime, fishing, folklore, and slower road-trip rhythm.
- Works better by car or private transfer than by fragmented public transport.

## Fast Matching

| User priority | Best base |
|---------------|-----------|
| Short first trip | Panama City |
| Social island trip | Bocas del Toro |
| Simple tropical postcard | Guna Yala |
| Coffee + hiking | Boquete |
| Dive trip | Santa Catalina |
| Surf and quieter Pacific coast | Playa Venao / Pedasi |

## Pairings That Usually Work

- Panama City + Boquete
- Panama City + San Blas
- Panama City + Bocas del Toro
- Panama City + Santa Catalina
- Boquete + Bocas only if time is generous

## Pairings That Often Fail

- San Blas + Bocas + Boquete in one week
- Panama City as a day-trip base for everything
- Bocas + Coiba in a short trip without weather buffers
