# Weather and Seasonality

## The Big Rule

- Ask for month first. Panama plans that ignore rain and sea conditions look fine on paper and fail in real life.

## Seasonal Pattern

- The drier months are usually easiest for multi-stop travel and island-heavy plans.
- The wetter months bring greener landscapes, but also more transfer uncertainty, rougher seas, and more need for backup plans.

## Region Effects

### Panama City
- Heat and humidity shape walking comfort more than travelers expect.

### Bocas Del Toro
- Rain and sea conditions matter every day, even if the trip still works overall.

### San Blas
- Sea state can be a bigger issue than rainfall itself.

### Highlands
- Cooler temperatures are a feature, but rain and cloud can change hiking quality quickly.

### Pacific Marine Trips
- Coiba and coastal boat days depend on marine conditions, not just sunshine.

## Planning Rule

- The more boats and remote transfers in a route, the more weather buffer the itinerary needs.
