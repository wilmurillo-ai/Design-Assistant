# Safety and Emergencies

## Baseline

- Panama works best when users stay alert about transport, weather, water conditions, and ordinary urban petty theft rather than imagining high drama everywhere.

## Practical Safety Priorities

- Use trusted transport at arrival and late night.
- Keep phones and wallets controlled in busy urban or transit areas.
- Respect sea conditions and operator calls on island or dive days.
- Do not normalize unplanned remote movement after dark.

## Where Trips Break

- Rough water on boat-heavy itineraries
- Overconfident luggage handling on docks and water taxis
- Heat, dehydration, or wet-weather gear failure
- Late transfer chains with no fallback

## Emergency Mindset

- Keep accommodation details and one offline map available.
- Know the next morning's transport before going out at night.
- For diving, surfing, hiking, or offshore plans, use operator briefings as the final go or no-go signal.
