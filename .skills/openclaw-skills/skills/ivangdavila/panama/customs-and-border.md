# Customs and Border

- Panama tourism guidance says the traveler affidavit is only needed when entering with cash, objects, or documents valued above US$10,000.
- If there is nothing to declare, travelers generally do not need to complete the affidavit.
- Migration guidance also stresses baggage and document inspection as part of entry control, so keep luggage straightforward and easy to explain.

## Arrival Strategy

- Treat Tocumen as an efficient regional hub, but still budget time for immigration, baggage, and the final transfer into the city.
- Keep one place to stay locked before arrival, even for short city breaks.
- If landing late and heading onward the next morning, stay close to the airport corridor or keep the overnight in Panama City simple.

## Money and Border Friction

- Carry some small cash for taxis, snacks, or cards not accepted on arrival, but do not overcarry cash.
- For high-cash trips such as San Blas or remote beach areas, split cash storage instead of keeping everything in one pouch.
- If a user plans to enter with photo gear, drones, or expensive equipment, advise them to keep invoices or a simple equipment list.

## Border Mindset

- Answer questions briefly and consistently.
- Keep proof of onward travel easy to show.
- Do not promise fast transfers after landing without a time buffer.
