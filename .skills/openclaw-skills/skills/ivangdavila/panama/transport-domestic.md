# Transport Domestic

## Choosing the Right Model

### Flight-Heavy
- Best for short trips combining Panama City with Boquete, David, or Bocas.
- Reduces fatigue and protects quality when time is limited.

### Ground-and-Boat
- Best for slower trips or budget-conscious users with transfer tolerance.
- Works for El Valle, Azuero, Santa Catalina, and some westbound routes.

## Panama City Mobility

- The metro and bus network are useful for selected city moves, but many travelers still depend on ride-hailing or taxis for speed and door-to-door simplicity.
- The airport branch on Metro Line 2 can be useful when luggage is light and the final destination matches the corridor.

## Islands

- Bocas movement depends on water taxis and weather-aware timing.
- San Blas access is usually package-based or prearranged rather than improvised.
- Coiba access depends on departure point, marine conditions, and operator schedules.

## Planning Rules

- Never promise a tight same-day international arrival plus remote island connection without an overnight buffer.
- If a user dislikes transfer stress, choose fewer bases and use flights where it matters most.
- For rural or beach stops, solve the last mile before confirming the hotel.
