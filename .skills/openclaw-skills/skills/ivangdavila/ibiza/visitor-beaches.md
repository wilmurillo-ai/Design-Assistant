# Ibiza Beaches Guide

## Beach Categories

### World-Famous Calas (Worth the Hype)

**Cala Comte (Cala Conta)**
- **Vibe**: Sunset paradise, crystal clear turquoise water
- **Best for**: Sunset watching, swimming, Instagram shots
- **Parking**: Limited and chaotic; arrive before 11am or taxi
- **Facilities**: Beach bars, sunbeds (€€€), restaurants
- **Crowd level**: Very high in summer
- **Local tip**: Walk 5 minutes north for quieter Cala Escondida

**Cala Salada**
- **Vibe**: Pine-fringed cove, natural beauty
- **Best for**: Families, snorkeling, escaping mega-crowds
- **Parking**: Restricted access road; shuttle bus from San Antonio in summer
- **Facilities**: One chiringuito, limited sunbeds
- **Crowd level**: High but more manageable than Cala Comte
- **Local tip**: The smaller Cala Saladeta (5-minute walk) is quieter

**Cala Bassa**
- **Vibe**: Beach club meets natural beauty
- **Best for**: Party people who want daytime luxury
- **Parking**: Large car park (€)
- **Facilities**: CBbC beach club, full restaurant, sunbeds, water sports
- **Crowd level**: Very high; mix of families and clubbers
- **Local tip**: Book sunbeds in advance through app

**Cala Tarida**
- **Vibe**: Family-friendly, wide sandy beach
- **Best for**: Families with kids, swimming
- **Parking**: Adequate
- **Facilities**: Multiple restaurants, sunbeds, water sports
- **Crowd level**: Moderate to high
- **Local tip**: Western end is quieter

### Hidden Gems (Local Knowledge)

**Cala Xarraca**
- **Location**: North coast
- **Vibe**: Secluded, natural, therapeutic mud
- **Best for**: Nature lovers, mud bath experience
- **Access**: Easy road access, small car park
- **Facilities**: One restaurant
- **Local tip**: The natural mud on the rocks is famous for skin benefits

**S'Illot des Renclí (Pou des Lleó)**
- **Location**: Northeast
- **Vibe**: Rocky cove, turquoise pools, off-the-beaten-path
- **Best for**: Adventurous swimmers, escaping crowds
- **Access**: Dirt road, some walking
- **Facilities**: None
- **Local tip**: Bring everything you need; no shops nearby

**Cala Mastella**
- **Location**: East coast
- **Vibe**: Tiny fishermen's cove, authentic
- **Best for**: Lunch at El Bigotes (legendary fish restaurant, cash only, no reservations)
- **Access**: Short walk from parking
- **Facilities**: Just the restaurant
- **Local tip**: Arrive at 13:30 for lunch queue; worth the wait

**Benirràs**
- **Location**: North coast
- **Vibe**: Bohemian, famous Sunday drumming ceremony
- **Best for**: Sunset (faces west-ish), hippie culture
- **Parking**: Very limited; arrive early on Sundays
- **Facilities**: Beach bars
- **Local tip**: Sunday drum circle starts 2 hours before sunset; arrive early for spots

**Aigües Blanques**
- **Location**: Northeast
- **Vibe**: Long sandy beach, nudist-friendly north section
- **Best for**: Naturists, families (south section), beach day
- **Access**: Easy parking
- **Facilities**: Beach bars, sunbeds
- **Local tip**: Natural division — clothed south, nude north

### Party Beaches

**Playa d'en Bossa**
- **Vibe**: Party central, Ushuaïa/Hï territory
- **Best for**: Clubbers, people-watching, daytime parties
- **Length**: 2.5km of sand
- **Facilities**: Everything — clubs, restaurants, beach clubs, hotels
- **Crowd level**: Extreme in summer
- **Local tip**: Nassau Beach Club for calmer vibe; south end near Torre de Ses Portes is quieter

**Las Salinas (Ses Salines)**
- **Vibe**: See-and-be-seen, beautiful people
- **Best for**: Beach club scene, glamour, people watching
- **Facilities**: Sa Trinxa, Jockey Club, Malibu Beach
- **Parking**: €€ and fills up by noon
- **Local tip**: Walk south toward Es Cavallet for more space

**Es Cavallet**
- **Vibe**: Gay-friendly, nudist sections, natural beauty
- **Best for**: LGBTQ+ scene, nudism, long walks
- **Facilities**: Chiringay (iconic beach bar)
- **Parking**: Shared with Las Salinas
- **Local tip**: Protected natural park; keep to paths

## Beach Etiquette

- **Topless**: Legal and common everywhere
- **Nudism**: Only at designated beaches (Aigües Blanques north, Es Cavallet, Aguas Blancas)
- **Umbrellas**: Bringing your own is fine at public beaches
- **Beach clubs**: Reserved sections cost €€-€€€€; free sections exist
- **Trash**: Take it with you; fines for littering
- **Music**: Personal speakers increasingly frowned upon; some beaches ban them

## Practical Tips

**Arriving Strategy**
- **Before 10am**: Easy parking at most beaches
- **10am-12pm**: Parking fills; may need to wait for spots
- **After 12pm**: Consider taxi or scooter; driving around looking for parking is frustrating

**What to Bring**
- Cash (some chiringuitos don't take cards)
- Water (beach bar prices are €5-8 per bottle)
- Reef-safe sunscreen
- Water shoes for rocky entries
- Snorkel gear (clear water everywhere)

**Beach Club Reservations**
Major beach clubs (CBbC, Nassau, Blue Marlin, Nikki Beach) fill up quickly in summer. Book sunbeds through their apps or websites, especially for weekends.

**Water Conditions**
- Generally calm Mediterranean
- Posidonia seagrass: Not dirt — it's a UNESCO-protected ecosystem
- Jellyfish: Occasional; medusa warnings posted at beaches
- Water temperature: 22-26°C in summer
