# Local Ibicenco Cuisine & Markets

## Traditional Dishes

### Main Courses

**Bullit de Peix**
The island's signature dish. Fish stew served in two courses:
1. First: The fish itself with potatoes and aioli
2. Second: The broth used to cook rice (arroz a banda)
- Best at: Es Boldado, Can Pujol, traditional restaurants
- Price: €25-40 per person
- Authenticity marker: Uses local rockfish, not imported

**Sofrit Pagès**
Hearty meat stew with potatoes and local sausages.
- Traditionally made with lamb, chicken, sobrasada, botifarra
- Winter/comfort food
- Best at: Ca N'Alfredo, traditional countryside restaurants
- Price: €18-25

**Guisat de Peix**
Fish stew similar to bullit but served together rather than two courses.
- More casual version
- Common in chiringuitos
- Price: €18-28

**Frit de Polp**
Fried octopus with potatoes and peppers.
- Tapas or main course
- Best with local white wine
- Price: €15-22

### Appetizers & Sides

**Ensalada Payesa**
"Peasant salad" with dried fish (peix sec), tomatoes, peppers, onion.
- Unique to Ibiza
- The dried fish is key — don't skip it
- Price: €10-14

**Coca de Pimientos**
Flatbread with peppers.
- Similar to mainland coca but local twist
- Great appetizer
- Price: €8-12

**Sobrasada**
Soft, spreadable cured sausage (paprika-heavy).
- Balearic specialty
- On bread, with honey, or in dishes
- Buy at markets for home

### Desserts

**Flaó**
Cheese and mint tart.
- Sweet but not cloying
- Made with fresh cheese (similar to ricotta)
- Mint is essential, don't accept substitutes
- Price: €5-8

**Greixonera**
Bread pudding with cinnamon and lemon.
- Dense, sweet, traditional
- Not on every menu
- Price: €5-7

**Orelletes**
Fried pastries, anise-flavored.
- Festival food
- Easter and Christmas traditions
- Price: €3-5

## Where to Find Authentic Ibicenco Food

### Restaurants

**Ca N'Alfredo (Santa Eulalia)**
- Family-run, decades of tradition
- Bullit de peix, sofrit pagès done right
- Reservations recommended
- €€€

**Es Boldado (Cala d'Hort)**
- Cliffside views of Es Vedrà
- Excellent bullit de peix
- Book for sunset
- €€€

**Can Pujol (Ibiza Town)**
- Port location, seafood focus
- Traditional preparations
- Tourist-friendly but authentic
- €€€

**Ca's Pagès (San Rafael)**
- Interior location
- Traditional country cuisine
- Local crowd
- €€

**Can Domo (Ibiza Town)**
- Traditional recipes
- Less touristy
- Good value
- €€

### Finding Authenticity

Look for:
- "Cocina ibicenca" on menu
- Dried fish visible
- Local wines on offer
- Spanish being spoken by most customers
- Off the main tourist strips

Avoid:
- "Paella" as the only Spanish dish
- International menu with one "local" section
- Tourist-strip locations

## Markets

### Mercat Vell (Ibiza Town)
- **Location**: Old town
- **Hours**: Mon-Sat 7:00-14:00
- **What**: Fresh produce, fish, meat, cheese
- **Vibe**: Local, authentic, morning shoppers
- **Tip**: Go early for best fish selection

### Santa Eulalia Market
- **Location**: Town center
- **Hours**: Mon-Sat mornings
- **What**: Fresh produce, local products
- **Vibe**: Smaller, local

### Supermarkets for Local Products

**What to look for:**
- Sobrasada (various grades)
- Hierbas ibicencas (local herb liqueur)
- Local olive oil
- Flor de sal (salt from Ses Salines)
- Ibizan almonds
- Local wines (improving yearly)

**Best supermarkets:**
- Eroski: Wide selection, reasonable prices
- Mercadona: Best prices, good quality
- Can Mimós (Santa Gertrudis): Premium local products

## Hippie Markets (Food Sections)

### Las Dalias (San Carlos)
- **When**: Saturday (all day), Thursday night (summer)
- **Food**: Organic produce, baked goods, specialty items
- **Vibe**: Hippie, artisanal, international-influenced

### Punta Arabí (Es Canar)
- **When**: Wednesday
- **Food**: Fresh produce, snack stalls, local crafts
- **Vibe**: Tourist-friendly, oldest market

### San Juan Sunday Market
- **When**: Sunday morning
- **Food**: Local farmers, organic, authentic
- **Vibe**: Most local, least touristy

## Local Drinks

### Hierbas Ibicencas
Herbal liqueur, digestif.
- Made from local herbs (thyme, rosemary, etc.)
- Sweet or dry versions
- After-dinner tradition
- Brands: Familia Marí Mayans, Hierbas Ibicencas Ibiza

### Frígola
Thyme liqueur.
- Drier than hierbas
- Digestif

### Local Wine
- **Ibizkus**: Local winery, improving quality
- **Can Rich**: Another local producer
- Small production, worth trying

### Pomada
- Gin + lemonade
- Originally from Menorca but popular here
- Summer daytime drink

## Seasonal Specialties

### Spring
- Wild asparagus (esparecs silvestres)
- First almonds
- Easter pastries (orelletes)

### Summer
- Fresh figs
- Tomatoes at peak
- Watermelon, melon

### Autumn
- Grape harvest
- Mushrooms (after rain)
- Pomegranates

### Winter
- Oranges, lemons
- Hearty stews
- Sobrasada dishes
