# Santa Eulalia & East Coast

## Overview

Santa Eulalia del Río is Ibiza's third-largest town and the family-friendly capital of the island. Beautiful promenade, calm bay, upscale restaurants, and access to the quieter east coast beaches. No mega-clubs, no rowdy bars — just quality Mediterranean life.

## Sub-Areas

### Santa Eulalia Town
- **Vibe**: Upscale family resort, beautiful promenade
- **Pros**: Beach promenade, excellent restaurants, yacht marina, services
- **Cons**: Limited nightlife, can feel quiet for party-seekers
- **Best for**: Families, couples, foodies
- **Rent**: €1,600-2,800/month

### Es Canar
- **Vibe**: Resort area, hippie market home
- **Pros**: Sandy beach, famous Wednesday market (Punta Arabí), facilities
- **Cons**: Resort-feeling, can be touristy
- **Best for**: Families, market lovers
- **Rent**: €1,400-2,200/month

### Cala Llenya / S'Argamassa
- **Vibe**: Residential, quiet beaches
- **Pros**: Beautiful cove beaches, pine forests, family-friendly
- **Cons**: Car needed, limited services
- **Best for**: Peace-seekers, nature lovers
- **Rent**: €1,200-2,000/month

### Siesta / Residential Area
- **Vibe**: Local residential
- **Pros**: Affordable, real island life, near town
- **Cons**: Not scenic, no beach
- **Best for**: Long-term stays, budget-conscious
- **Rent**: €1,000-1,600/month

## Practical Info

### Getting Around
- **Walking**: Town very walkable
- **Cycling**: Popular; bike paths along promenade
- **Car**: Needed for beaches beyond Es Canar
- **Bus**: Connections to Ibiza Town, other areas
- **Taxi**: Available but limited vs. Ibiza Town

### Nightlife

Santa Eulalia is not a nightlife destination. That said:

**Evening Drinks**
- Promenade bars: Cocktails at sunset
- **Guaraná**: Beach club, DJ sessions
- **Nikki Beach** (if it's your thing): Day club scene

**Clubs**
- None in Santa Eulalia proper
- Taxi to Ibiza Town clubs: €25-35
- Taxi to Playa d'en Bossa: €30-40

**Live Music**
- Various restaurants have live music in summer
- Jazz and acoustic sessions

### Dining

**Seafood & Mediterranean**
- **Proyecto Santa Eulalia**: Modern Mediterranean, excellent
- **Es Caliu**: Traditional, excellent fish, institution
- **Ca N'Alfredo**: Family-run, traditional Ibicenco
- **La Noria**: Fine dining, romantic

**International**
- **Amante**: Clifftop restaurant, stunning views (Sol d'en Serra beach)
- **Babylon Beach**: Beach restaurant, eclectic menu
- **ME Ibiza**: Hotel restaurant, modern

**Casual**
- **Passion Café**: Healthy brunch, vegetarian-friendly
- **La Belle Ibiza**: Brunch spot
- **Promenade cafés**: Various, sea views

### Services
- **Supermarkets**: Mercadona, Eroski, Spar
- **Medical**: Centro de Salud Santa Eulària, pharmacies
- **Banks**: All major Spanish banks
- **Shopping**: Boutiques along promenade, marina shops
- **Gym**: ME Ibiza (day passes), local gyms

## Living Here

### Pros
- Family-friendly environment
- Beautiful promenade and marina
- Excellent restaurants
- Calm, safe atmosphere
- Good base for east coast exploration
- Year-round life

### Cons
- Far from club scene
- Taxis expensive to nightlife
- Can feel too quiet for some
- Slightly "resort" feel in peak season

### Best For
- Families with children
- Couples wanting romance without noise
- Foodies
- Older travelers
- Digital nomads seeking calm
- Long-term residents

### Avoid If
- Clubbing is your priority
- You want buzzing nightlife
- You're a budget backpacker (slightly pricier)

## Insider Tips

1. **Wednesday market (Punta Arabí, Es Canar)**: Oldest hippie market on island. Go early to avoid coach crowds.

2. **Amante Beach Club**: Book for sunset dinner — the drive down is dramatic, the views incredible.

3. **El Bigotes (Cala Mastella)**: Legendary fish restaurant. Cash only, no reservations, arrive 13:30 for queue. Worth it.

4. **River walk**: The "river" in Santa Eulalia del Río is now mostly dry, but the walk along it is pleasant.

5. **Cycling**: The promenade extends for km in both directions. Perfect for morning/evening bike rides.

6. **Sunday brunch**: Multiple spots do excellent brunch; book ahead.

7. **Off-season**: Santa Eulalia stays alive in winter. Good restaurants remain open.
