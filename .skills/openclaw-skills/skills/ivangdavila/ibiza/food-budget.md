# Budget Eating in Ibiza

## Reality Check

Ibiza is expensive. Budget eating requires strategy. The good news: it's absolutely possible to eat well cheaply if you know where to look.

## Daily Budget Guide

| Strategy | Daily Food Cost | How |
|----------|-----------------|-----|
| **Survival** | €15-25 | Supermarket, self-catering |
| **Budget** | €25-40 | Mix of supermarket + cheap eats |
| **Moderate** | €40-70 | Local restaurants, avoid tourist traps |
| **Comfortable** | €70-100 | Mix of quality restaurants |

## Supermarkets

### Main Chains

**Mercadona**
- Best prices overall
- Good quality
- Fresh bread, deli counter
- Locations: Ibiza Town, Santa Eulalia, San Antonio
- Tip: "Hacendado" brand is good quality

**Eroski**
- Wider selection
- Slightly higher prices
- Good for specialty items
- Good wine selection

**Lidl**
- Cheapest for basics
- Limited locations
- German efficiency

**Spar/Small Markets**
- Convenient (late hours)
- Premium prices (20-40% more)
- Use for emergencies

### What to Buy

**Breakfast**
- Bread (€1-2)
- Jamón/cheese (€3-5)
- Fruit (€2-3)
- Coffee from apartment

**Lunch/Picnic**
- Pre-made sandwiches (€2-4)
- Salads (€3-5)
- Empanadas (€2-3)
- Fruit, snacks

**Dinner Prep**
- Pasta + sauce (€3-4)
- Rotisserie chicken (€5-7)
- Fresh fish (cheaper than restaurant!)
- Vegetables (€2-4)

### Picnic Strategy
Pack cooler bag for beach days:
- Saves €30-50 vs beach club lunch
- Bring wine (€5-10 bottle)
- Sandwiches, fruit, snacks
- Freeze water bottles for cool

## Menú del Día Strategy

Spanish tradition: fixed lunch menu with:
- Starter
- Main
- Dessert or coffee
- Often includes bread and drink

**Price**: €12-18 (vs €30-40 à la carte)
**When**: Lunch only (13:00-16:00)
**Where**: Local bars/restaurants (not tourist spots)

Look for handwritten boards with "menú" outside.

## Beach Day Budget

### DIY Beach Day
- Bring food from supermarket
- Bring water (lots)
- Use free beach sections
- Total: €0-10

### Budget Beach Day
- Skip sunbed (use towel)
- One drink at chiringuito: €5-8
- Snack/sandwich: €8-12
- Total: €15-20

### vs. Beach Club Day
- Sunbed: €50-150
- Lunch: €40-70
- Drinks: €30-50
- Total: €120-300+

## Markets for Cheap Fresh Food

### Mercat Vell (Ibiza Town)
- Fresh produce, fish, meat
- Cheaper than supermarket
- Quality better
- Morning only

### Hippie Markets (food stalls)
- Mixed value
- Some cheap eats
- Some expensive
- Good for one-off treats

### Village Markets
- San Juan Sunday
- Local produce
- Very reasonable

## Accommodation with Kitchen

Most important budget decision:
- **With kitchen**: Save €30-50/day on food
- **Without kitchen**: Captive to restaurants

Even a simple kitchenette allows:
- Breakfast at home
- Packed lunches
- Simple dinners

## Drinking on Budget

### Supermarket vs. Bar

| Drink | Supermarket | Bar/Club |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Beer (330ml) | €1 | €5-8 |
| Wine (bottle) | €5-15 | €25-60 |
| Spirits (bottle) | €12-25 | €15-25/drink |

### Pre-drinking Strategy
Common and acceptable:
- Drinks at apartment before going out
- Supermarket alcohol
- Save €50-100 on club night

### Happy Hours
- Some bars offer 2-for-1 or discounts
- Usually 17:00-20:00
- San Antonio sunset strip has options

### Club Survival
- Drink water (yes, expensive but necessary)
- One drink per hour = pace yourself
- Skip bottle service (€€€€)

- 30-50% cheaper

- Better negotiating position
