# Ibiza Activities & Hidden Gems

## Beyond Beaches & Clubs

### Boat Experiences

**Boat Charters**
- **Full-day charter** (8h): €800-3,000+ depending on boat size
- **Half-day** (4h): €400-1,500
- **Sunset cruise**: €200-500 per person
- **Best route**: West coast (Cala Salada → Cala Comte → Es Vedrà)
- **Book through**: CharterAlia, Ibiza Boat Club, local marinas
- **Group tip**: Split cost with 6-10 people

**Boat Parties**
- **Cirque de la Nuit**: Popular floating party, DJ + drinks included (~€100)
- **Float Your Boat**: Various themes, includes club entry (~€85)
- **Oceanbeat**: Younger crowd, energetic (~€80)
- **Warning**: Quality varies wildly. Research reviews carefully.

**Formentera Day Trip**
- Ferry from Ibiza Town: 30-60 min, €25-60 round trip
- Rent scooter on arrival: €30-50/day
- Must-see: Ses Illetes beach (consistently rated top 10 in world)
- Day plan: Morning ferry → scooter → beaches → lunch → sunset ferry
- Book: Trasmapi, Balearia, Aquabus

### Land Activities

**Hiking**
- **Es Vedrà viewpoint**: Easy 20-min walk from parking; mystical rock offshore
- **Sa Talaia**: Highest point (475m); moderate 2h round trip; panoramic views
- **Cap des Falcó**: Cliffside walk; sunset spot
- **Torre des Savinar**: Old watchtower; Es Vedrà views
- **Best season**: Oct-May (summer too hot)

**Cycling**
- Road cycling popular with locals
- Mountain biking in interior
- E-bike rentals increasing
- Routes: Interior villages circuit, coastal roads

**Yoga & Wellness**
- **Ibiza Retreats**: Various programs, north coast
- **Six Senses**: Day spa packages available
- **Drop-in classes**: Ibiza Yoga (Santa Gertrudis), various beach sessions
- **Full retreats**: Search Retreat Guru or BookYogaRetreats
- **Peak season**: May and October (weather pleasant, fewer crowds)

### Cultural Experiences

**Dalt Vila (UNESCO Old Town)**
- Walled citadel overlooking port
- Phoenician, Roman, Moorish history
- Best at sunset (empty of day-trippers)
- Free to wander; guided tours available
- Must-see: Cathedral, city walls walk, Portal de ses Taules entrance

**Hippy Markets**
- **Las Dalias (San Carlos)**: Saturday all day, Thursday night (summer)
- **Punta Arabí (Es Canar)**: Wednesday, oldest market
- **San Juan**: Sunday morning, most authentic local market
- What to buy: handmade jewelry, clothing, Adlib fashion, leather goods

**Museums**
- **Museo de Arte Contemporáneo (MACE)**: Contemporary art, Dalt Vila
- **Museu Puget**: Local painters, historic house
- **Museo Arqueológico**: Phoenician artifacts
- Small but quality; good for hot afternoon breaks

**Historic Sites**
- **Necròpolis del Puig des Molins**: UNESCO Phoenician burial site
- **Ses Salines salt flats**: Historic, still operational, beautiful
- **Churches**: Whitewashed churches throughout interior villages

### Villages Worth Exploring

**Santa Gertrudis**
- Interior village, expat favorite
- Great restaurants, boutiques, galleries
- Famous: Bar Costa (local institution)
- Sunday market vibes

**San Carlos**
- Hippie heritage
- Las Dalias market
- Anita's Bar (legendary since 1970s)

**San Juan**
- Authentic north village
- Sunday market (local produce, hippie goods)
- Gateway to north beaches

**Sant Josep (San José)**
- Southern interior
- Gateway to Es Vedrà area
- Growing foodie scene

**Sant Mateu d'Albarca**
- Remote, few tourists
- Wine country (small vineyards)
- Authentic island life

### Hidden Gems (Local Secrets)

**Punta Galera**
- Flat rock formations perfect for sunbathing
- Cliff jumping spots (be careful)
- No sand, no facilities
- Bring everything; leave nothing
- Sunset spectacular

**Atlantis (Sa Pedrera)**
- Abandoned quarry with mystical carved pools
- Challenging to reach (rocky path)
- Not officially promoted (fragile site)
- Go early morning, bring water shoes
- Instagram-famous but still magical

**S'Espalmador**
- Small island between Ibiza and Formentera
- Accessible by boat or wading at low tide
- Natural mud baths
- Private island vibes
- Day trips from Formentera

**Torre de Portinatx**
- Old watchtower
- Less visited than Es Vedrà viewpoint
- North coast views
- Short walk from Portinatx beaches

## Seasonal Activities

### Summer (Jun-Sep)
- Beach all day
- Clubs all night
- Boat trips
- Sunset worship
- Pool time

### Shoulder (May, Oct)
- Hiking (perfect weather)
- Cycling
- Yoga retreats
- Cultural exploration
- Fewer crowds at everything

### Winter (Nov-Apr)
- Hiking and walking
- Cycling (locals' favorite season)
- Wellness retreats
- Local food scene (restaurants stay open)
- Creative/art scene
- Running events

## Booking Tips

- **Boat charters**: Book 2-4 weeks ahead in summer
- **Wellness retreats**: Book 1-2 months ahead
- **Popular restaurants**: Book 1-2 weeks ahead (La Paloma, El Bigotes, Atzaró)
- **Formentera ferry**: Book day before or morning of (usually fine)
- **Scuba diving**: Book 2-3 days ahead
