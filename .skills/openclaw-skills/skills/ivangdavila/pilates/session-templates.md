# Session Templates - Pilates

Build the session around one purpose.
Do not add extra exercises unless they improve the main purpose.

## 5-Minute Reset

Use when adherence is at risk or the user feels too busy to practice.

1. Easy breathing and setup: 45 seconds
2. Pelvic clock or imprint awareness: 1 minute
3. One core-control drill: 2 minutes
4. One simple mobility or extension drill: 1 minute
5. Closing note: 15 to 30 seconds

Target outcome:
- maintain continuity
- leave with one clear cue for next time

## 10-Minute Beginner Mat Session

1. Breathing and rib setup
2. Pelvic tilt or imprint check
3. Marching or tabletop preparation
4. Bridge or hip stability drill
5. Easy spinal mobility or chest-opening finish
6. Quick review: what felt controlled, what felt rushed

Use when:
- starting from zero
- rebuilding confidence after time away

## 15-Minute Form Repair Session

1. Name the target issue
2. Practice reduced-range version
3. Add one cue from `form-checks.md`
4. Repeat 3 to 5 steady rounds
5. Compare before and after

Use when:
- neck tension dominates abdominal work
- ribs flare during leg movement
- lower back takes over bridges or extensions
- wrists or shoulders complain during support work

## 20-Minute Standard Home Session

1. Warm-in: 2 to 3 minutes
2. Foundational control drill: 4 minutes
3. Main exercise block: 8 to 10 minutes
4. Cool-down or decompression: 2 minutes
5. Log one cue, one win, one next step

Use when:
- the user already practices at least weekly
- no current safety concerns are active

## Low-Energy or Pain-Aware Session

Adjust any session with these rules:
- reduce range before removing control
- shorten leverage before dropping the exercise entirely
- use props, wall support, or bent-knee versions if needed
- stop if symptoms escalate instead of settling

Good finish:
- user feels more organized or calmer than at the start
- no pressure to push through discomfort
