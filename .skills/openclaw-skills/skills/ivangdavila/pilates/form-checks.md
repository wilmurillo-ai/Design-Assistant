# Form Checks - Pilates

Use these checks after noticing one repeating issue.
Correct one pattern at a time.

## Stack

### Problem: ribs flare and chest leads the exercise

What it looks like:
- front ribs pop up
- low back arches early
- abs feel disconnected even when effort is high

Correction cue:
- "Soften the ribs toward the mat and keep the sternum quiet."

Easier drill:
- exhale first, then move one limb at a time

### Problem: neck takes over abdominal work

What it looks like:
- jaw clenches
- shoulders creep up
- strain appears before the target muscles work

Correction cue:
- "Keep the back of the neck long and let the ribs start the curl."

Easier drill:
- hands behind thighs or head support with a smaller lift

## Brace

### Problem: pelvis swings instead of staying organized

What it looks like:
- rocking side to side
- marching or leg lowers feel jerky
- control disappears when leverage increases

Correction cue:
- "Move the legs only as far as the pelvis can stay quiet."

Easier drill:
- toe taps or bent-knee marching

### Problem: glutes grip and lower back drives bridges

What it looks like:
- hamstrings cramp
- ribs lift at the top
- descent drops instead of rolls down

Correction cue:
- "Press through the feet and peel up one segment at a time."

Easier drill:
- smaller bridge hold with exhale on the lift

## Breathe

### Problem: breath is held during effort

What it looks like:
- face tightens
- movement gets choppy
- the user can only complete reps by bracing harder

Correction cue:
- "Exhale through the hardest part and keep the breath smooth enough to speak."

Easier drill:
- one rep per full breath cycle

### Problem: tempo gets faster as control drops

What it looks like:
- reps speed up
- end range gets messy
- cue recall gets worse mid-set

Correction cue:
- "Make the movement quieter, not bigger or faster."

Easier drill:
- halve the reps and add a short pause at the hardest point

## Review Rule

A correction is working when:
- the user can repeat it for 3 to 5 rounds
- tension drops in the non-target area
- the cue gets shorter, not more complicated
