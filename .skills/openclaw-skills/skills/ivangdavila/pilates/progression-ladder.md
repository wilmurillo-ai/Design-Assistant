# Progression Ladder - Pilates

Progression is earned by repeatable control, not by collecting harder exercise names.

## Stage 1: Show Up

Entry condition:
- user has not built a stable routine

Goal:
- 2 to 3 short sessions per week
- finish feeling organized, not drained

Advance when:
- at least 2 weeks of repeated practice
- no major confusion about breathing and setup

## Stage 2: Stabilize

Entry condition:
- practice is happening, but control varies

Goal:
- hold one main cue across an entire short session
- identify one recurring breakdown pattern

Advance when:
- the same correction works across multiple sessions
- the user reports better control or less irritation

## Stage 3: Control

Entry condition:
- breathing and basic positions feel predictable

Goal:
- keep pelvis and ribs organized as leverage increases
- maintain calm tempo under light challenge

Advance when:
- exercises stay precise without rushed reps
- the user can self-notice drift and correct it

## Stage 4: Personalize

Entry condition:
- regular practice exists and the user understands their own weak points

Goal:
- tailor cadence, length, and cue style
- separate maintenance sessions from growth sessions

Advance when:
- the user can sustain the routine for 3 to 4 weeks
- weekly review shows adherence without symptom escalation

## Weekly Review Decision Rules

Increase duration or leverage only if:
- completion rate is at least 70 percent
- pain or symptom notes are stable or improving
- breathing quality and control are steady or better

Switch to repair mode if:
- the same form issue appears 3 sessions in a row
- the user cannot explain the main cue

Switch to recover mode if:
- fatigue rises across the week
- soreness lasts into the next day
- fear or instability is increasing
