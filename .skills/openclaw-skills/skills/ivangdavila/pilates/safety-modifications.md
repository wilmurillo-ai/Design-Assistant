# Safety Modifications - Pilates

Pilates is often low-impact, but "controlled" is not the same as risk-free.
Use conservative adjustments when symptoms, instability, or medical uncertainty are present.

## Hard Stop Signals

Stop practice and recommend urgent professional or emergency help as appropriate if the user reports:
- chest pain
- fainting or near-fainting
- sudden severe shortness of breath
- sudden weakness, numbness, or one-sided symptoms
- acute injury during practice
- severe pain that escalates rapidly

## Practice Modifications

### Low-back irritation

- reduce lever length first
- keep ribs and pelvis organized before adding range
- avoid pushing through extension or leg-lowering drills that increase symptoms
- stop progression if pain rises during or after practice

### Neck or shoulder irritation

- reduce head-lift volume
- use support under the head or hands-behind-thigh options
- shorten plank or quadruped loading
- avoid chasing the full version when upper-trap tension dominates

### Wrist sensitivity

- use fists, forearms, wedges, or wall support
- shorten support holds
- favor supine or side-lying options when needed
- stop if numbness or sharp pain appears

### Pregnancy or postpartum context

- keep transitions smooth and non-straining
- use clinician guidance before starting or intensifying practice if concerns exist
- avoid breath holding and aggressive abdominal pressure strategies
- stop and seek care for concerning symptoms

### Osteoporosis, recent surgery, or medically restricted movement

- keep advice conservative
- avoid loaded twisting, flexion, or extension recommendations without explicit clearance when those are relevant restrictions
- default to basic positioning, breathing, and gentle control work
- recommend professional guidance before progression

## Scope Boundaries

- Do not prescribe Pilates as a cure.
- Do not overrule a clinician or physical therapist.
- If the user has recent surgery, worsening neurological symptoms, or unclear pain patterns, keep advice conservative and recommend professional clearance.

## Safe Progress Rule

Increase only one variable at a time:
- duration
- complexity
- leverage
- frequency

If tolerance worsens after a change, return to the last steady version.
