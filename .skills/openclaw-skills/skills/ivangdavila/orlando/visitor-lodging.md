# Orlando Visitor Guide: Where to Stay

## Main Hotel Zones

| Zone | Best For | Strengths | Watchouts |
|------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Disney / Lake Buena Vista | Disney-first families | Resort transport, themed hotels, easier Disney rhythm | Less convenient for Universal or Downtown |
| Universal / International Drive North | Universal trips, older kids, short stays | Walkable pockets, easier nightlife, strong hotel mix | Traffic and parking complexity |
| International Drive / Convention Center | Conferences, mixed itineraries | Hotel density, dining, convention access | Feels touristy, not local |
| Downtown Orlando | Urban weekends, events | Better local feel, bars, Lake Eola access | Weak for theme parks, parking varies |
| Winter Park area | Boutique, local, polished stays | Best city texture, dining, walkability | Limited hotel inventory |
| Airport / Lake Nona | Early flights, business travel | Clean, efficient, close to MCO | Not a leisure base |

## Best Strategy by Trip Type

### Disney-First
- Stay on Disney property or very nearby if the trip is mostly Disney.
- The transport and mid-day reset options often justify the higher rate.

### Universal-First
- Stay near Universal or in the northern I-Drive zone.
- This reduces wasted time and makes CityWalk evenings easy.

### Split Trip
- Consider two hotels if doing multiple Disney days plus Universal.
- It sounds annoying, but it can save hours and lower stress.

### Conference Trip
- Stay near the venue unless the conference is light and leisure is the real priority.

## Common Hotel Fee Traps

- Resort fees on off-site hotels
- Paid parking even at non-luxury properties
- Weak "free shuttle" schedules that force rideshare anyway
- Breakfast that is technically included but operationally poor
- Rooms that look close to parks but are actually in traffic-heavy corridors

## Lodging Questions to Ask Before Booking

1. Which park or venue matters most?
2. Will the group rest mid-day?
3. Is parking included?
4. Is there a real shuttle, or only limited departures?
5. Does the group need a kitchen, laundry, or suite layout?

## Good Default Picks

| Trip Style | Default Move |
|------------|--------------|
| First Disney family trip | Disney-area hotel with transport |
| Teens and thrill rides | Universal-area hotel |
| Conference with one park day | Convention Center or I-Drive hotel |
| Adult city weekend | Downtown or Winter Park area |
