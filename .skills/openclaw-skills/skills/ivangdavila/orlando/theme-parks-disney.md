# Walt Disney World Strategy

## When Disney Is the Right Answer

- First family trip
- Kids who care more about characters and atmosphere than pure thrill rides
- Multi-day travelers who want an all-in-one ecosystem
- Guests who value resort transport and easier mid-day resets

## Best Planning Principles

1. Pick your 1-2 non-negotiables for each park before arrival.
2. Stack the morning well, because that is when standby lines are most humane.
3. Leave room for one pool or hotel reset on longer trips.
4. Do not pay for every premium add-on by default.

## Park Personality

| Park | Best For | Note |
|------|----------|------|
| Magic Kingdom | Small kids, nostalgia, fireworks | Highest classic family value |
| EPCOT | Adults, food, older kids | Great as a half-relaxed day |
| Hollywood Studios | Star Wars and thrill mix | Needs stronger ride prioritization |
| Animal Kingdom | Slower pace, animals, visuals | Best early day and can end early |

## Disney Mistakes

- Treating EPCOT like a full thrill day
- Paying deluxe prices without using resort benefits
- Doing park hopper moves that spend more time on buses than on rides
- Ignoring weather and stroller fatigue

## When to Stay On-Site

- Disney is the trip spine
- The group wants easy transport and mid-day breaks
- Early entry and resort flow matter more than absolute room value

## When Off-Site Is Fine

- The group is driving everywhere anyway
- The trip mixes Disney, Universal, and non-park days
- Kitchen or villa space matters more than themed immersion
