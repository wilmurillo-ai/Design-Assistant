# Orlando Climate and Seasonality

## Core Reality

Orlando weather is not a side note. Heat, humidity, and storms shape how the city works.

## Seasonal Pattern

| Season | Reality | Best Use |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Jan-Feb | Best weather, strong demand | Peak tourist comfort |
| Mar-Apr | Warm and busy | Good if crowds are acceptable |
| May | Hotter but still workable | Strong shoulder month |
| Jun-Aug | Hot, humid, stormy | Toughest period for long park days |
| Sep-Oct | Hurricane watch but often lower crowd pressure | Good value with weather awareness |
| Nov | Strong balance month | Great for many travelers |
| Dec | Festive and expensive | Good if budget allows |

## What This Means

- Summer park days need early starts and indoor backup.
- Afternoon thunderstorms are common, not surprising.
- Hurricane season matters even inland because flights, roads, school, and power can still be disrupted.

## Resident Notes

- Electricity costs spike in heat.
- Mold, humidity control, and A/C reliability matter in housing.
- Storm supplies are still worth keeping even though Orlando is inland.
