# Orlando Immigration Context

## Core Framing

Immigration is U.S.-level, not Orlando-specific, but Orlando can still be a good landing city for certain profiles because of UCF, healthcare, hospitality, aviation, and niche professional sectors.

## Common Paths Seen Around Orlando

| Profile | Typical Path |
|---------|--------------|
| International student | F-1 through UCF or other institutions |
| Specialty worker | H-1B or employer-sponsored role |
| Healthcare professional | Employer-led sponsorship plus licensing |
| Investor or founder | U.S. entity setup plus visa-specific legal planning |

## Good Guidance Rules

- Never present Orlando as if the city itself creates a visa path.
- Use immigration counsel for exact filing strategy.
- Treat job offer quality and employer sophistication as more important than city marketing.

## Why Orlando Can Still Fit

- Lower living cost than major gateway cities in some categories
- Large airport and decent domestic connectivity
- Real employer base in healthcare, education, and specialized industry niches
