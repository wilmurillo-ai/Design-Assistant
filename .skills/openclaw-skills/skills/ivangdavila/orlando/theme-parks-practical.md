# Theme Parks: Tickets and Logistics

## Big Budget Levers

| Lever | When It Helps | Risk |
|-------|---------------|------|
| On-site hotel | Best when one resort family dominates | Can overpay if splitting parks |
| Lightning Lane | Helps when time matters and the group is disciplined | Easy to overspend |
| Universal Express | Best on high-crowd days or short trips | Weak value on light-crowd weekdays |
| Park hopper / park-to-park | Useful when the user truly wants flexibility | Often wasted on tired families |
| Rental car | Best for split-resort or day-trip itineraries | Parking and toll costs add up |

## Ticket Reality

- Orlando tickets are date-based, dynamic, and harder to compare than they look.
- Disney one-day admission starts around USD 119.
- SeaWorld promotional entry often starts around USD 79.99.
- Universal pricing changes by day, park mix, and Express choices.
- Annual passes make sense only for repeat regional users or two-trip households.

## Parking and Arrival Rules

- Disney standard parking runs around USD 30 per day for cars.
- SeaWorld self-parking regularly starts around USD 35.
- Universal parking varies by season and event pressure.
- If the user is driving, parking cost must be part of the hotel comparison.

## Best Practical Defaults

1. One park family per day.
2. One premium line-skipping tool only when crowd pressure justifies it.
3. One true recovery block every two demanding days.
4. One weather backup per day in summer.

## What to Verify Live

- Exact park hours
- Virtual queue or reservation changes
- Current parking prices
- Premium add-on policies
- Early-entry perks tied to specific hotels
