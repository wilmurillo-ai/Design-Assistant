# Orlando Visitor Itineraries

## 1 Day: Conference or Add-On Trip

### Park-First
1. Pick one park only.
2. Arrive before opening.
3. Take a mid-day rest or indoor break.
4. Finish with a nearby dinner instead of cross-town driving.

### City-First
1. Morning in Winter Park or Downtown.
2. Lunch in Mills 50 or Audubon Park.
3. Afternoon at Leu Gardens or resort pool.
4. Evening on Park Avenue, Disney Springs, or a show.

## 3 Days: Best First-Time Split

### Family Version
- Day 1: Magic Kingdom or Epic Universe
- Day 2: Rest morning + pool + Disney Springs or CityWalk
- Day 3: Second park or Kennedy Space Center

### Adult Version
- Day 1: Universal or EPCOT
- Day 2: Winter Park + Mills 50 + nice dinner
- Day 3: Kennedy Space Center or springs day trip

## 5 Days: Balanced Orlando

- Day 1: Arrival, grocery stop, easy evening
- Day 2: Full park day
- Day 3: Half-day park plus rest
- Day 4: Non-park day trip or local city day
- Day 5: Second major park day or shopping and departure

## Do Not Overload These Combinations

- Disney and Universal on back-to-back rope-drop days without rest
- Airport arrival plus evening park ticket unless staying on-site
- Springs or beach day after a late theme-park close
- Kennedy Space Center on a very rainy forecast day

## Strong Pairings

| Trip Goal | Good Pairing |
|-----------|--------------|
| Little kids | Disney resort + one non-park recovery day |
| Teens | Universal + Epic Universe + outlet or NBA / soccer event |
| Couples | EPCOT or Universal + Winter Park + chef-driven dining |
| Mixed generations | Disney-area stay + one relaxed off day near the hotel |

## Best Seasonal Fit

- Jan-Feb: best weather, high demand
- Mar-Apr: strong weather but spring-break crowds
- May: hot but manageable, good for weekdays
- Jun-Aug: toughest heat and storm rhythm
- Sep: hurricane watch and lower crowds
- Oct-Nov: best balance for many visitors
- Dec: festive but expensive
