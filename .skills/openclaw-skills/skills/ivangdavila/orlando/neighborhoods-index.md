# Orlando Neighborhoods: Quick Comparison

## Metro Snapshot

| Area Cluster | Best For | Price Feel | Car Need | Vibe |
|--------------|----------|------------|----------|------|
| Downtown core | Young professionals, events, short urban stays | Medium-high | Medium | Urban, nightlife, lakes |
| Winter Park / College Park belt | Families, polished lifestyle, remote workers | High | Medium | Tree-lined, upscale, walkable pockets |
| Lake Nona / southeast | Airport users, healthcare, clean master-planned feel | High | High | New-build, polished, planned |
| Tourism corridor | Visitors, conference travel, service workers | Medium-high | Medium | Resort, chain-heavy, convenience-first |
| Western and northern suburbs | Families wanting space | Medium-high | High | Suburban, school-driven |
| Budget outer ring | Price-sensitive commuters | Low-medium | High | Practical, less charming |

## Fast Recommendations

- Best true local feel: Winter Park, Audubon Park, Mills 50, Thornton Park
- Best for airport-heavy life: Lake Nona, Conway
- Best for Disney access: Celebration, Lake Buena Vista side, Horizon West corridor
- Best for families and schools: Winter Garden, Windermere, Winter Park, Oviedo
- Best for nightlife without full tourism chaos: Downtown / Thornton Park / Mills 50

## Core Truth

Orlando neighborhoods are less about postcard beauty and more about what friction the user wants to remove:
- parks
- airport
- schools
- commute
- walkability
- budget
