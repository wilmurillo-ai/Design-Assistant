# Orlando Tech and Professional Work

## What Orlando Is Actually Good At

- Simulation and modeling
- Defense and aerospace-adjacent work
- Hospitality tech and travel-tech operations
- Healthcare systems and health-adjacent roles
- Remote work for people who want Florida residency and airport connectivity

## Why Orlando Works

The metro is anchored by UCF talent, a large visitor economy, and a professional-services ecosystem that is broader than outsiders assume.

## Salary Reality

| Role Type | Typical Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| Mid-level software or product role | Often lower than big coastal hubs |
| Senior software role | Competitive only in select employers or remote roles |
| Simulation / defense niche role | Often better than generic local tech |

## Best Advice

- For pure salary maximization, remote work or niche employers often beat generic local roles.
- For lifestyle-plus-airport balance, Orlando can work well.
- For early-career growth, UCF and the metro network create real but not massive opportunity density.
