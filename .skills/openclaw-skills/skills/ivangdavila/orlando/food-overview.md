# Orlando Food Scene Overview

## What Orlando Does Well

- Chef-driven dining in Winter Park, Mills 50, Audubon Park, and parts of Dr. Phillips
- Latin American depth, especially Puerto Rican and broader Central / South American options
- Strong Asian pockets, especially Vietnamese and Filipino influence
- Theme-park dining that can be excellent if chosen deliberately

## What People Get Wrong

1. Thinking Orlando food equals chains plus park snacks.
2. Eating every dinner near a hotel corridor.
3. Missing the local east-of-Downtown neighborhoods.

## Best Dining Lanes

| Lane | Best Area |
|------|-----------|
| Special-occasion dinner | Winter Park, Dr. Phillips |
| Casual local eats | Mills 50, Audubon Park, Milk District |
| International range | Mills 50, Colonial corridor, west Orlando pockets |
| Family-friendly convenience | Celebration, Winter Garden, Lake Nona |

## Best Rule

If the user is in Orlando more than two nights, move at least one dinner outside the tourism corridor.
