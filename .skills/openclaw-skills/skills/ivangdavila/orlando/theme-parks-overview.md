# Orlando Theme Parks: Overview

## Core Portfolio

| Resort | Best For | Strength | Weak Spot |
|--------|----------|----------|-----------|
| Walt Disney World | Families, first-timers, immersive theming | Breadth, service rhythm, resort ecosystem | Expensive, complex planning |
| Universal Orlando Resort | Older kids, teens, adults, Potter fans | Better thrill density and simpler multi-day trip | Less broad for tiny kids |
| Epic Universe | Repeat Orlando visitors and high-hype seekers | Newest park, strongest novelty factor | High demand, can dominate trip budget |
| SeaWorld Orlando | Coaster fans and mixed-value ticket shoppers | Good coaster lineup, lighter commitment | Less essential for many first-timers |
| Discovery Cove | Premium low-intensity day | Strong service and relaxation | Expensive, niche use |

## Best Resort Choice by Trip Goal

- Small children and first Orlando trip: Disney
- Teens and thrill rides: Universal plus Epic Universe
- Adults with 2-3 days: Universal-area base plus one city evening
- Short budget trip: one flagship park family only

## Planning Truths

1. Most bad Orlando trips come from trying to do too many parks, not too few.
2. Hotel choice is part of park strategy, not a separate decision.
3. New or premium add-ons only pay off if the user values time more than money.

## Good Splits

| Length | Strong Split |
|--------|--------------|
| 2 days | Universal + Epic or Disney + rest evening |
| 3 days | Two park days + one soft day |
| 5 days | Two to three hard park days + one city or recovery day |

## Do Not Pretend These Are Equal

- Disney is better at all-ages family immersion.
- Universal is better at teen/adult momentum.
- Epic Universe is the new draw, but it can crowd out the rest of the trip if over-prioritized.
- SeaWorld is optional, not mandatory.
