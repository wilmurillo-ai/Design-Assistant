# Orlando Culture and Social Norms

## Core Identity

Orlando is friendlier, more transient, and more suburban than many visitors expect. The metro mixes tourism workers, families, Latin American communities, students, healthcare professionals, and new arrivals from other U.S. states.

## What Feels Different

- The tourist city and resident city overlap but are not the same thing.
- Car culture shapes social life.
- The weather influences schedules, fitness, and patience.

## Social Expectations

- Casual dress is normal almost everywhere outside luxury settings.
- Service interactions are generally warm and efficient.
- Family scheduling drives a lot of local rhythm.

## Good Cultural Reads

- Winter Park and College Park feel more established and polished.
- Mills 50 and nearby districts feel more local and creative.
- Southwest Orlando feels more practical and convenience-driven.
