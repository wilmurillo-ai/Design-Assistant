# Orlando Resident Guide

## First 30 Days

1. Pick the correct county and commute pattern before signing a lease.
2. Confirm renter or homeowner insurance pricing before assuming a place is affordable.
3. Set up SunPass or a toll strategy if driving will be regular.
4. Get utilities, internet, and primary-care planning in motion quickly.
5. Build a heat and storm routine before summer or hurricane season arrives.

## What New Residents Underestimate

- Commutes that cross I-4 or major park corridors
- Toll accumulation
- Lower local wages relative to headline housing prices
- Childcare and after-school costs
- Insurance premiums in Florida

## Good Resident Anchors

| Priority | Better Move |
|----------|-------------|
| Family setup | Choose school fit and commute before chasing house features |
| Remote work | Pay for neighborhood quality, not just square footage |
| Southwest jobs | Live on the same side of the metro if possible |
| Frequent flying | Do not ignore airport access |

## Weak Default Moves

- Picking a cheap outer-ring apartment without commute testing
- Assuming a tourist-zone lease means easier daily life
- Moving in June or August without budgeting for utility spikes and schedule stress
