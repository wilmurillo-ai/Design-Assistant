# Storms and Preparedness in Florida

Storm planning is part of ordinary Florida decision-making.

## Main Risks

- Hurricanes and tropical storms
- Storm surge and coastal flooding
- Inland flooding and drainage failures
- Heat, humidity, lightning, and long outage windows
- Mold and moisture after storms

## Use This Guidance

### Housing
- Ask about flood zone, elevation, roof condition, shutters, generator rules, and evacuation complexity.
- For condos, confirm building process for storms and post-storm reentry.

### Trips
- Avoid overcommitting fixed itineraries during hurricane season without fallback plans.
- Cruise, Keys, and barrier-island trips need extra weather flexibility.

### Daily Life
- Keep basic power, water, medication, document, and pet plans ready.
- Seasonal residents need a plan for storms that hit while they are in another state.

## What Good Advice Looks Like

- Explain the actual tradeoff between waterfront convenience and evacuation stress.
- Distinguish inland flood risk from coastal surge risk.
- If current conditions matter, verify with official emergency resources instead of summarizing from memory.
