# Florida Regions

Use this file to match the user's actual Florida pattern instead of defaulting to "beach + no state income tax."

## Fast Region Fit

| Region | Best for | Watch for |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| South Florida | International access, nightlife, dense services, coastal living | High housing cost, storm exposure, car insurance, traffic |
| Central Florida | Theme-park economy, family suburbs, airport reach, newer master-planned areas | I-4 traffic, heat, tourism-driven peaks, storm outages |
| Tampa Bay | Balanced metro life, healthcare, Gulf access, mixed-age population | Flood pockets, bridge traffic, insurance pressure |
| Southwest Florida | Retirees, seasonal living, boating, quieter pace | Storm vulnerability, seasonal pricing, limited depth outside key corridors |
| First Coast / Jacksonville | Larger homes, logistics, military and healthcare mix, beach access | Sprawl, car dependence, neighborhood spread |
| Panhandle | Lower headline costs, military presence, quieter beaches, less density | Fewer nonstop flights, hurricane risk, smaller labor markets |
| Florida Keys | Scenic living, boating, niche tourism economy | Limited housing stock, expensive insurance, storm evacuation complexity |

## Matching Logic

### South Florida
- Best for users who want high service density, Latin American connectivity, cruise access, and late-night or urban energy.
- Usually a poor fit for users who want lower insurance, easy parking, or a quiet suburban rhythm without tradeoffs.

### Central Florida
- Best for families, hospitality workers, airport-heavy travel, and users who want newer suburbs without South Florida pricing.
- Check whether the user is okay with tourism spillover, toll roads, and corridor traffic.

### Tampa Bay
- Best for users seeking a middle ground between intensity and livability.
- Strong option for healthcare-adjacent households and semi-retirees who still want a metro.

### Southwest Florida
- Best for retirees, snowbirds, boaters, and users prioritizing calmer daily life.
- Verify storm risk, healthcare depth, and whether seasonal crowding breaks the lifestyle they want.

### First Coast / Jacksonville
- Best for military families, logistics, healthcare, and households that want space over glamour.
- Neighborhood spread matters more than city branding.

### Panhandle
- Best for users who want a slower pace, military or university anchors, and lower headline costs.
- Remind users that storm risk and smaller job markets still matter.

### Keys
- Best only when waterfront lifestyle is the point.
- Treat evacuation, insurance, and limited services as core planning items, not footnotes.

## Ask These Before Recommending a Region

1. Full-time move, part-time, or winter-only?
2. Coastal lifestyle or lower-risk inland preference?
3. Need major airport access or just occasional flights?
4. Family-school focus, retirement-healthcare focus, or nightlife-job focus?
5. Comfortable with high insurance and HOA costs, or trying to minimize fixed carrying cost?
