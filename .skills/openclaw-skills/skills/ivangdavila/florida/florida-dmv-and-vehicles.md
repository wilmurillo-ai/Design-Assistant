# Florida DMV and Vehicles

Use official Florida Highway Safety and Motor Vehicles guidance first, then county tax collector pages when local execution differs.

## Core Agencies

- FLHSMV handles driver licenses, IDs, titles, and statewide rules.
- County tax collectors often handle local registration transactions and appointments.
- Toll systems and local parking programs are separate from licensing.

## Resident Sequence

### Today
1. Confirm whether the user is a new resident, returning resident, or seasonal resident.
2. Check the local office path for the user's county.
3. Gather required identity, residency, insurance, and vehicle documents before sending them into an appointment maze.

### This Week
1. Handle license or ID first if deadlines are tight.
2. Then sequence title, registration, plate, and any county-specific visit.
3. Set up toll transponders if the user will drive in South Florida, Central Florida, or Tampa corridors.

### After Completion
1. Recheck auto insurance and roadside coverage for Florida pricing.
2. Review parking permits, condo parking rules, and airport access patterns.

## Practical Traps

- Users confuse FLHSMV with the county tax collector and lose time booking the wrong office.
- Seasonal residents may not need the same sequence as permanent movers; ask first.
- Toll roads matter fast in many metros. Budget for convenience, not just mileage.
- Florida car insurance can be materially higher than expected, especially in South Florida.
