# Costs and Taxes in Florida

Use this before telling the user that Florida is cheap because there is no state income tax.

## Cost Layers That Matter

- Housing payment or rent
- Homeowners, condo, renter's, flood, and auto insurance
- HOA or condo dues
- Utilities and storm-related contingency costs
- Car ownership, tolls, parking, and airport access
- Healthcare premiums and out-of-network risk for retirees or split-state households

## Tax Logic

- No state income tax helps some households, but it is only one line in the budget.
- Sales tax, property tax, and tourism-heavy local pricing still affect daily life.
- Seasonal residents need to think through domicile, mail, and tax positioning carefully before assuming advantages.

## Common Misreads

- Comparing Florida to a no-income-tax state with cheaper insurance as if they are equal.
- Looking only at list price or rent and skipping insurance and HOA.
- Ignoring seasonal price spikes in Southwest Florida, the Keys, and some coastal corridors.

## Better Question to Answer

Do not ask whether Florida is cheap.

Ask whether a specific Florida region works for the user's income, insurance tolerance, healthcare needs, and seasonality pattern.
