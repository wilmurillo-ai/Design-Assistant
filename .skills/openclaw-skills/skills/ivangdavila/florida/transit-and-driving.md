# Transit and Driving in Florida

Florida mobility is usually car-first, but the failure mode differs by region.

## Region Reality

- South Florida has more transit options, but daily life still leans heavily on driving and parking.
- Central Florida often means toll roads, theme-park traffic, and airport-oriented movement.
- Tampa Bay and Southwest Florida are car-heavy with bridge or corridor bottlenecks.
- The Keys and coastal corridors require weather-aware timing more than transit theory.

## Ask Before Recommending

1. Daily commute or occasional errands?
2. Comfortable with toll roads?
3. Need frequent airport access?
4. Can the household operate with one car or none?
5. Is evacuation-route simplicity part of the decision?

## Practical Rule

Do not call a Florida area "easy without a car" unless the user's exact neighborhood and routine support that claim.
