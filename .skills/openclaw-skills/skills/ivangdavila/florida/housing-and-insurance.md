# Housing and Insurance in Florida

Florida housing advice is incomplete without insurance, flood exposure, and building health.

## Renters

- Ask whether the unit is inland or coastal, condo or apartment, older or recently renovated.
- Check commute, parking, storm-shutter expectations, generator policy, and internet reliability.
- Explain that renter's insurance may not cover flood losses by default.

## Buyers

- Treat roof age, four-point inspections, wind mitigation, flood zone, claims history, and HOA or condo reserves as first-order issues.
- Condo buyers should ask about reserves, deferred maintenance, and special assessments before assuming monthly dues are stable.
- Homeowners should stress-test carrying cost with insurance, flood coverage, HOA, and utility load included.

## Coastal vs Inland Logic

- Coastal lifestyle may justify higher fixed cost and storm complexity for some users.
- Inland locations can reduce surge risk but still carry flood, heat, and storm-outage exposure.
- The correct comparison is resilience plus quality of life, not just miles to the beach.

## Insurance Reality

- Homeowners insurance availability can shape which homes are realistic.
- Flood insurance may matter even outside the user's mental model of "high risk."
- Condo and homeowner policies have different gaps; do not blur them.
- For address-specific decisions, direct the user to official insurance and flood resources before overcommitting.
