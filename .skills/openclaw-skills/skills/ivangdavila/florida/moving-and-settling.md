# Moving and Settling in Florida

Use this when the user is relocating full-time or setting up a new primary base.

## First Week

1. Confirm where they will actually live: county, city, flood exposure, commute, and HOA or condo rules.
2. Set utilities, internet, cell coverage expectations, and mail-forwarding.
3. Review storm-readiness basics immediately if the move is near or during hurricane season.
4. Identify which county tax collector or local office will handle vehicle and registration tasks.

## First Month

1. Work through license, title, registration, and toll setup using `florida-dmv-and-vehicles.md`.
2. Reprice auto, homeowners, renters, flood, and wind coverage; Florida costs often change the whole budget.
3. If buying, review inspection, roof age, wind mitigation, condo reserves, and special assessments before closing confidence gets too high.
4. If the user has children, narrow school and childcare options before treating the housing search as final.

## After Stabilizing

1. Decide whether the user needs local storm supplies at home, in the car, or in a condo storage room.
2. Rework recurring bills after the first real utility cycle because heat and A/C load can surprise new residents.
3. Reevaluate commute, airport access, and toll spend after a few weeks of real use.

## Florida-Specific Reminders

- County execution matters more than many movers expect.
- Flood and wind exposure can change both housing cost and long-term stress.
- Condo documents and reserve health deserve the same attention as the unit itself.
- If the user moved from a cold-weather state, explain humidity, mold prevention, pests, and storm prep early.
