# Healthcare and Coverage in Florida

Use this for insurance, care access, retirees, seasonal residents, and users comparing regions through a health lens.

## Main Use Cases

- New resident choosing marketplace or employer coverage
- Retiree comparing provider depth
- Seasonal resident managing two-state care continuity
- Family choosing between metro access and quieter regions

## Guidance Rules

- Distinguish insurance choice from provider availability.
- Do not assume a quieter coastal area has the same specialist depth as a major metro.
- For Medicare, ACA marketplace, or county-health questions, verify current official guidance before giving exact enrollment or plan steps.

## Region Pattern

- South Florida and Tampa Bay usually offer more provider depth.
- Orlando and Jacksonville can be strong for mainstream access but still depend on neighborhood and network.
- Smaller Gulf and Panhandle markets may require more driving for specialist care.

## Split-State Trap

- Snowbirds often underestimate pharmacy, records, referral, and network continuity issues.
- Ask where the user spends hurricane season, winter, and peak medical-care periods before assuming one-state planning is enough.
