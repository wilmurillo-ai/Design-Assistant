# Work and Business in Florida

Florida business advice should reflect tourism exposure, insurance cost, workforce seasonality, and local permitting.

## Good Fits

- Hospitality, travel-adjacent, marine, logistics, healthcare, home services, and some remote-work households
- Businesses that benefit from airport, cruise, or retiree corridors

## Watch For

- Seasonal demand swings
- Insurance and liability costs
- Local licensing and county or city permits
- Labor-market variation between South Florida, Orlando, Tampa Bay, Jacksonville, and smaller coastal markets

## Operator Sequence

### First
1. Identify whether the user is opening in a tourism-driven zone, suburban service zone, or logistics corridor.
2. Separate state registration from city or county permits.
3. Estimate year-round demand, not just high season.

### Then
1. Review tax, insurance, and workforce assumptions.
2. Check weather resilience for physical operations.
3. If the business depends on locals, distinguish full-time residents from seasonal spending.
