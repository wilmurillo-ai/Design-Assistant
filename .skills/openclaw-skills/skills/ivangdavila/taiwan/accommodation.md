# Accommodation — Taiwan

Pick the city and district first, then the hotel. In Taiwan, good location often beats a better room because food, transit, and weather flexibility are worth more than a marginal upgrade.

## Best Base by Goal

| Goal | Best base |
|------|-----------|
| First trip, transit, museums, nightlife | Taipei |
| Slower city pace and central branching | Taichung |
| Food and old-town atmosphere | Tainan |
| Southern weather and harbor evenings | Kaohsiung |
| Hot-spring split stay | Taipei + Beitou or another dedicated hot-spring stop |

## District Logic

| City | Best area |
|------|-----------|
| Taipei | Daan, Zhongshan, Xinyi depending on style |
| Taichung | West District or modern Xitun side |
| Tainan | West Central District |
| Kaohsiung | Yancheng, Central Park, or Formosa Boulevard side |

## Budget Guidance

| Budget | Best move |
|--------|-----------|
| Budget | Near transit, small room, better location |
| Mid-range | Prioritize district quality and breakfast |
| Premium | Use higher-end stays where the neighborhood supports the price |

## Booking Rules

- Holiday weekends and Lunar New Year distort price and availability quickly
- If the trip is rail-heavy, stay near the right transit node, not just "city center"
- For Tainan, old-town access matters more than flashy facilities
- For Kaohsiung, harbor-adjacent neighborhoods often beat generic mall zones

## When to Split Stays

- Taipei plus a hot-spring night
- Taiwan city trip plus one beach or lake stay
- Long first trip where the user wants a different pace in the second half

## Common Mismatches

- Airport-adjacent hotels for travelers who actually want city nights
- Cheap remote stays that create daily taxi friction
- Ximending or Fengjia chosen by hype instead of traveler fit
- One-night luxury splurges that create too much packing overhead
