# Emergencies — Taiwan

Taiwan is generally easy to travel, but weather, traffic, and outdoor plans mean emergency basics are still worth knowing.

## Emergency Numbers

| Service | Number | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Police | 110 | Main police emergency line |
| Fire / ambulance | 119 | Fire and medical emergencies |
| Tourist hotline | 0800-011-765 | 24-hour travel information support |
| Foreigners in Taiwan hotline | 1990 | Multilingual support for foreigners in Taiwan |

## Medical Reality

- Big cities have strong hospital access
- Pharmacies and convenience stores solve minor issues fast
- For serious heat, traffic, or outdoor incidents, escalate early rather than late

## Weather Emergencies

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Typhoon alert | Re-check trains, flights, ferries, and outdoor plans immediately |
| Earthquake | Protect first, then move carefully once safe |
| Heat stress | Get indoors, hydrate, cool down, seek help fast if symptoms escalate |

## Outdoor and Transit Reality

- Mountain and coastal plans fail harder in bad weather than urban plans do
- Ferry and scenic-route disruptions happen; do not improvise blindly
- Save hotel details and key transport info offline

## Lost Documents or Theft

- File a police report before trying to solve insurance or replacement issues
- Contact the embassy or consular office if passport loss is involved
- Freeze cards quickly through banking apps or hotlines

## Common Mistakes

- Treating typhoon or mountain warnings as optional suggestions
- Waiting too long to reroute a weather-sensitive day
- Assuming a scenic area has the same support level as a central city
