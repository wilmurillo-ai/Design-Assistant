# Hiking — Taiwan

Taiwan hiking ranges from urban sunset climbs to serious alpine logistics. The main skill is matching the user's fitness, weather tolerance, and transport reality to the right trail class.

## Best Hikes by Difficulty

| Level | Best fit | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Easy | Elephant Mountain, urban ridge paths, short tea-country walks | High payoff with low planning |
| Moderate | Yangmingshan loops, coastal ridge half-days | Good for day hikers |
| High but accessible | Hehuanshan-type alpine viewpoints | Better with transport planning and weather care |
| Serious multi-day | Central mountain routes | Only for prepared hikers with permits and proper gear |

## Best Hiking Bases

| Base | Best For |
|------|----------|
| Taipei | Easy urban and north-coast hiking |
| Taichung / central Taiwan | Mountain branching and alpine launches |
| Hualien / east routes | Scenic nature-heavy trips when conditions allow |
| Kaohsiung south | Heat-sensitive planning and lighter winter hiking |

## Rules That Matter

- Start earlier than the user thinks
- Check current weather, closures, and permit needs
- Match trail ambition to transport and return time
- Do not force mountain heroics into a city-first itinerary

## Good Hiking Pairings

| Pairing | Why it works |
|---------|--------------|
| Taipei city stay + easy morning hike | Minimal friction |
| Hot spring + shorter trail day | Great shoulder-season mix |
| Central Taiwan stay + alpine scenic drive | Strong for repeat visitors |

## Common Hiking Mistakes

- Treating weather as a minor variable
- Underpacking water, layers, and battery
- Assuming a "famous trail" is automatically suitable for beginners
- Adding a hard hike on a same-day long-transfer schedule
