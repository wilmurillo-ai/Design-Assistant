# Itineraries — Taiwan

Taiwan rewards routes with one clear spine. The fastest way to ruin the trip is to over-sample the whole island without enough time for weather or food pacing.

## 4 Nights — Taipei-Only First Trip

- Base in Taipei all 4 nights.
- Use 2 city days, 1 easy day trip, and 1 flexible food or hot-spring day.
- Best for travelers who want a clean, low-friction intro.

## 6 Nights — Taipei + Tainan or Kaohsiung

- 3 nights Taipei
- 2 nights Tainan for food and history or 2 nights Kaohsiung for southern pace
- 1 flexible final night based on flight logic

Best when the user wants north plus one contrasting southern stop.

## 7 Nights — Classic West Coast First-Timer

- 3 nights Taipei
- 1 night Taichung
- 1 night Tainan
- 2 nights Kaohsiung

This works when the user likes movement but still wants the HSR to do the heavy lifting.

## 9 Nights — Better West Coast Version

- 3 nights Taipei
- 2 nights Taichung
- 2 nights Tainan
- 2 nights Kaohsiung

This is the stronger answer for travelers who want food, neighborhoods, and a less rushed city experience.

## 10-12 Nights — Add East Coast Carefully

- Build the west coast first
- Add the east coast only if the user has enough time and weather flexibility
- Keep east-coast nights meaningful instead of tagging on a stressful one-night sprint

## Family Version

- Fewer hotel moves
- More Taipei or Kaohsiung time
- Keep one weather-proof option each day
- Prefer MRT-friendly or easy-taxi cities over ambitious rural chains

## Bad Route Patterns

- Taipei + Sun Moon Lake + Alishan + Tainan + Kaohsiung + east coast in one short trip
- Same-day long transit plus packed night plan
- No backup for rain, typhoon, or low-energy days
- Constant one-night stops that kill morning and evening food windows
