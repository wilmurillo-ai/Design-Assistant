# With Kids — Taiwan

Taiwan is very workable for families when the trip has structure, shade, and backup options. Big wins come from easy transit, convenience stores, parks, food courts, and not overmoving.

## Best Family Bases

| Base | Why it works |
|------|--------------|
| Taipei | Easiest transit, broad food options, museums, parks |
| Kaohsiung | More space, broad avenues, easier southern pace |
| Taichung | Softer city rhythm and family-friendly pacing |

## Good Family Activities

| Activity | Best for | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| MRT-linked city day | First-timers | Lowest stress |
| Zoo, museum, or science center | Rain backup | High payoff on tired days |
| Easy bike or riverside outing | Older kids | Works best in good weather |
| Hot-spring stop | Families who want a reset | Check family rules in advance |
| Beach or harbor day | Mixed-age groups | Better with short transfer logic |

## Family Rules

- Reduce hotel changes
- Use convenience stores as support, not as a last resort
- Keep one indoor fallback every day
- Plan around naps, heat, and snack windows, not adult fantasy itineraries

## Best Family Food Strategy

- Mix known winners with one or two local experiments per day
- Use department-store food courts or easy malls for resets
- Night markets work best in shorter, more focused passes

## Common Mistakes

- Treating kids as if they can handle an adult rail-and-market sprint
- Overdoing night markets after long transit
- Underestimating heat and humidity
- Booking small inconvenient hotels because the photos were cute
