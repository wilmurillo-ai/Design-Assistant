# Tea — Taiwan

Tea is one of Taiwan's highest-value souvenirs and one of its best slow-travel experiences. The key is matching style, elevation, and budget instead of buying a generic "Taiwan tea" box.

## Core Styles

| Tea | Best known area | What it tastes like |
|-----|-----------------|---------------------|
| High-mountain oolong | Alishan, Lishan, Shan Lin Xi | Clean, floral, sweet, lifted |
| Dong Ding oolong | Nantou | More roasted, deeper, classic |
| Baozhong | Pinglin | Light, floral, elegant |
| Muzha tieguanyin | Taipei tea tradition | Roasted, nutty, heavier |
| Oriental Beauty | Hsinchu / Miaoli | Honeyed, fruity, bug-bitten character |
| Sun Moon Lake black tea | Nantou | Malty, smooth, approachable gift tea |

## Best Tea Experiences

| Experience | Best for |
|------------|----------|
| Taipei tea house or tea district stop | First-timers who want zero logistics |
| Maokong tea day | Easy Taipei add-on |
| Pinglin tea day | Lighter, less famous-feeling tea outing |
| Mountain tea purchase | Serious tea drinkers with time and transport |

## Buying Logic

- Ask whether the user wants drinking tea or gifting tea
- For gifting, clean packaging and recognizable region names help
- For drinking, buy by style and freshness, not only by altitude hype
- Do not oversell the most expensive high-mountain tea if the user actually likes roasted or black tea

## How to Order or Talk About Tea

| Goal | Best language |
|------|---------------|
| Easy cafe order | Ask for house tea or tea set |
| Tea shopping | Focus on style, roast level, and budget |
| Gift advice | Choose a famous region plus simple brewing notes |

## What Makes Taiwan Tea Special

- Strong regional identity
- Great range from floral to roasted
- Easy combination of scenic travel plus tasting
- Good souvenir-to-baggage ratio

## Common Mistakes

- Buying only by "highest mountain" language
- Treating tea as one-note when styles are radically different
- Skipping tea entirely because the user is not a tea nerd
- Buying fragile premium tea for a traveler who wants convenience over ceremony
