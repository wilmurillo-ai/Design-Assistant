# Accommodation - Turkey

## Stay-Type Grid

| Base type | Strong for | Watch-outs |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| Historic core hotel | Walkable landmark access | Noise, steps, transfer friction |
| Modern central district | Better nightlife, cafes, easier taxis | Less postcard atmosphere |
| Cave hotel | Cappadocia experience and views | Steps, uneven floors, weather exposure |
| Resort strip | Family and beach convenience | Isolation from local food and old towns |
| Old-town guesthouse | Character and evening walkability | Parking, luggage access, room size |

## Istanbul Neighborhood Logic

- **Sultanahmet / Eminonu:** strongest for first landmark-heavy trip, weakest for nightlife and hill comfort.
- **Karakoy / Galata / Beyoglu:** better for mixed city life, food, and evening range.
- **Besiktas / Nisantasi / Kadikoy:** stronger for repeat visitors or users who want local-city rhythm over monuments.

## Cappadocia Stay Logic

- Goreme is the easiest first-time base.
- Uchisar feels quieter and more upscale.
- Urgup works well for travelers who care less about dawn walk-out views and more about hotel comfort.

## Coast Logic

- Selcuk is tactical for Ephesus; Izmir is stronger for bigger-city comfort.
- Antalya old town is best for short cultural stays; Lara and Belek are different resort products.
- Kas and Fethiye are better for scenic coast travelers than for travelers wanting large full-service resorts.

## Booking Rules

- In peak summer and holiday windows, book small boutique or cave properties early.
- Ask whether stairs, cobbles, or parking matter before recommending a romantic old-town stay.
- Prioritize transfer ease on arrival night and last night, not just charm.
