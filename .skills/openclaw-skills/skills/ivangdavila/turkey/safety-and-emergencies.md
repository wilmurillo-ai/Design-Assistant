# Safety and Emergencies - Turkey

## Main Safety Stack

| Risk | Where it matters most | Response |
|------|-----------------------|----------|
| Heat and dehydration | Southeast, Mediterranean, inland summer routes | Shift heavy activity to early and late hours |
| Wildfire or smoke | Summer coast and forested regions | Keep route flexibility and monitor official alerts |
| Earthquake awareness | Urban and historic-core stays | Know exits and basic response, avoid panic myths |
| Tourist scams | Dense city and airport zones | Use clear prices, official transport, and booking proof |
| Road fatigue | Long coast or mountain drives | Reduce night driving and overpacked transfer days |

## Practical Safety Rules

- Save 112 emergency access and hotel address details on the phone.
- Arrival-night transfers are smoother when planned before landing.
- Solo travelers should match nightlife and transport advice to the district and hour, not just to the city.
- If current local events or protests matter, refresh sources close to the travel date.

## Health and Comfort

- Heat, dust, and smoke can change sightseeing tolerance fast.
- Shade, water, and realistic pacing matter more than heroic attraction counts.
- Food-heavy routes need moderation if the user has a sensitive stomach or strict dietary needs.
