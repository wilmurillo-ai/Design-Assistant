# Payments and Tipping - Turkey

## Payment Reality

- Cards are common in cities, airports, chain hotels, and most tourist-facing restaurants.
- Cash still matters for small cafes, taxis, local markets, and some rural or beach situations.
- Keep some Turkish lira available even if the trip is mostly card-driven.

## ATM and Exchange Logic

- Use bank ATMs in well-lit, normal locations.
- Airport exchange desks are for convenience, not best rate.
- If user is already in Europe, compare card FX terms before carrying large cash.

## Tipping Rule of Thumb

| Context | Typical move |
|---------|--------------|
| Restaurant | Round up or tip around 5-10% if service charge is not already included |
| Hotel porter / housekeeping | Small cash tip if service level warrants it |
| Taxi | Round up on normal city rides |
| Guide / driver | Tip for full or excellent service, not automatically at every micro-service |

## Card Practical Notes

- Dynamic currency conversion should usually be declined in favor of local currency.
- Contactless works well in many urban places but do not assume every cab or kiosk accepts it.
- On coastal road trips, cash resilience matters more than in central Istanbul.
