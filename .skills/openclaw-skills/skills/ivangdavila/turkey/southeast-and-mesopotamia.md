# Southeast and Mesopotamia - Turkey

## Best Fit

Best for travelers who prioritize food, early history, old stone cities, and a different cultural texture from the west coast and resort corridor.

## Region Selection Grid

| Base | Strong for | Watch-outs |
|------|------------|------------|
| Gaziantep | Food capital logic and museum value | Heat and intense eating pace |
| Sanliurfa | Gobeklitepe and deep-history focus | Midday heat and slower rhythm |
| Mardin | Architecture, views, old-city atmosphere | Hills, steps, and accessibility limits |
| Diyarbakir | Walls, food, layered city history | Needs deliberate current-context planning |

## Planning Rules

- Use this region for users who want substance, not checklist tourism.
- Summer heat is the main trip-shaping factor; mornings and evenings matter.
- Flights usually beat overland entry unless the user explicitly wants the road journey.
- Keep local-context awareness current and do not assume every nearby city has the same visitor feel.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating it like a low-friction extension to a beach holiday.
- Planning heavy walking in the hottest part of the day.
- Missing the food logic by hopping too fast.
- Ignoring accessibility limits in old stone urban cores.
