# Domestic Transport - Turkey

## Transport Selection Grid

| Mode | Best for | Limits |
|------|----------|--------|
| Domestic flight | West-east compression, Istanbul-Cappadocia, coast jumps | Airport overhead and baggage fees |
| Intercity bus | Value routes and many city pairs | Night comfort varies and trip times are long |
| Rail | Selected corridors such as Ankara-centered links | Not the backbone for most tourist circuits |
| Ferry | Bosphorus, urban sea moves, selected coastal logic | Weather and timetable limits |
| Car | Coast, archaeology clusters, rural viewpoints | Parking, tolls, fatigue, and city traffic |

## Istanbul Transport Reality

- Metro, tram, bus, and ferries make Istanbul workable without a car.
- Airport transfer from IST is a different problem than from SAW; never treat them as interchangeable.
- A one-neighborhood hotel choice can save more time than a faster-looking cross-city transit plan.

## Domestic Flight Rule

- Flights are strongest when the route spans Istanbul, Cappadocia, the southeast, or the far east.
- If the user hates airport overhead, one compact west-coast or southeast cluster is better than repeated flights.
- Same-day international arrival plus separate domestic ticket needs real slack.

## Surface Travel Rule

- Buses cover far more of the country than rail.
- High-speed rail is useful for selected Ankara and Konya links, not for most coast or Cappadocia tourism.
- Ferries are tactical urban or short-sea tools, not a national backbone.
