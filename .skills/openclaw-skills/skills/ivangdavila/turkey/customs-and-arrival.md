# Customs and Arrival - Turkey

## Arrival Reality

Turkey arrival friction is usually about airport choice, transfer timing, and first-hour logistics more than formal border complexity.

## Airport Logic

| Airport | Best for | Watch-outs |
|--------|----------|------------|
| IST | Most international arrivals and strong network reach | Long internal walking and long city transfer |
| SAW | Many low-cost and Asia-side flows | Not interchangeable with IST |
| AYT | Antalya coast trips | Resort transfer times can still be long |
| ADB | Izmir, Ephesus, west coast | Ground transfer still needed for Cesme or Selcuk |
| ASR / NAV | Cappadocia access | Late arrivals shrink next-day balloon flexibility |

## First-Hour Priorities

- Confirm which airport you actually landed at before planning the city transfer.
- Use official airport transport or prebooked transfer logic, not parking-lot touts.
- Keep a small cash backup for arrival-day edge cases even if cards are the default.
- If the trip starts with a separate-ticket domestic leg, leave generous buffer time.

## Customs and Baggage Practical Notes

- Pack medication in original packaging if there is any doubt.
- High-value gear, drones, or unusual electronics should be checked against airline and customs guidance before departure.
- If carrying large cash or valuables, use official customs guidance instead of assumptions.

## Same-Day Transfer Rule

- International arrival plus same-day Cappadocia flight is workable only with real buffer.
- International arrival plus same-day long-distance bus is usually a bad first-night experience.
- Istanbul old-city arrival days improve when the first hotel has easy transfer access, not just postcard value.
