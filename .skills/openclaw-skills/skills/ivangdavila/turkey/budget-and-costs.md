# Budget and Costs - Turkey

## Cost Shape by Trip Type

| Trip style | Budget | Mid-range | Premium |
|------------|--------|-----------|---------|
| Istanbul city break | Lower room cost if outside prime zones | Strong value in shoulder season | Waterfront and design hotels climb fast |
| Istanbul plus Cappadocia | Internal flight raises floor | Good balance for first-time trips | Cave suites and private tours raise spend sharply |
| Summer coast route | Peak season compresses budget value | Mid-range books up early | Premium beach and yacht products spike hard |
| East or southeast history route | Hotels can be cheaper | Flights and private transfers matter more | Comfort premium is mostly in transport quality |

## Practical Daily Math

- Budget travelers can still spend more than expected on airport transfers and entrance fees.
- Mid-range Turkey often beats western Europe on room value but not always on transport convenience.
- Premium spend rises fastest on coast resorts, private transfers, and high-demand cave or boutique stays.

## Biggest Hidden Costs

- Domestic flight baggage and airport transfer stacking
- Fuel, tolls, valet, and parking on road trips
- Resort or beach-zone dining premiums
- Site tickets, museum bundles, and guided-tour upsells
- Last-minute room spikes around holidays and long weekends

## Budget Rules

- Keep a city-heavy route if the user wants lower transfer burn.
- Price one domestic flight against one long bus or car day, not just ticket price.
- Treat coastal August pricing as its own budget season.
- If user wants balloons, private gulets, or beach clubs, expose those as optional luxuries, not core assumptions.
