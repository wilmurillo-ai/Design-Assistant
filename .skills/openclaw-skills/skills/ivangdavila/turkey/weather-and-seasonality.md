# Weather and Seasonality - Turkey

## Seasonal Grid

| Season | Strongest fit | Main watch-outs |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Spring | Istanbul, Aegean, Cappadocia, southeast | Variable wind, rain pockets, holiday demand |
| Summer | Coast trips, evening city life, long daylight | Extreme heat inland and in the southeast |
| Autumn | Istanbul, Cappadocia, Aegean, food routes | Storm swings and shorter daylight later in season |
| Winter | Istanbul city breaks, Cappadocia snow mood, Kars | Snow, flight disruption, cold morning logistics |

## Region-Specific Reality

- Istanbul is year-round but wind, rain, and hills change daily comfort.
- Cappadocia is spectacular in multiple seasons, but balloons are always weather-dependent.
- The Mediterranean and southeast can be punishing in peak summer midday hours.
- The Black Sea stays greener and wetter than the classic coast image of Turkey.

## Planning Rules

- Put exposed ruins and valley walks in the coolest hours.
- Shoulder season is often the best balance for first-time mixed routes.
- Build one backup indoor or lower-exposure option for every outdoor-heavy day.
- If beaches are central, match coast choice to the month instead of defaulting to one famous town.

## Timing Shortcuts

- Best broad first trip months: spring and autumn
- Best pure beach logic: late spring to early autumn
- Best snow-mood and rail stories: winter with buffer
- Worst overreach pattern: inland archaeology plus coast plus southeast in peak summer
