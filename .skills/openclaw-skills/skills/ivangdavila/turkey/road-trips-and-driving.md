# Road Trips and Driving - Turkey

## Best Driving Use Cases

- Aegean archaeology plus small coastal towns
- Antalya to Kas or Fethiye style scenic routes
- Cappadocia viewpoints and valleys if user wants sunrise and sunset flexibility
- Black Sea mountain or eastern routes where public transport is thin

## When Not To Drive

- Istanbul stays
- One-night city hops
- Historic cores with parking friction
- Any plan where the user is already transfer-fatigued or anxious about aggressive traffic

## Practical Rules

- Confirm rental policy for foreign licenses and whether an IDP is recommended for the traveler's document type.
- Tolls often run through rental-car systems; ask how HGS charging is handled before leaving the lot.
- Fuel and parking matter more than many first-time visitors expect.
- Mountain, coast, and old-town roads look short on maps but drive slowly.

## Region Notes

- Cappadocia roads are easy to cover but dawn and winter conditions change confidence.
- The D400 Mediterranean coast is scenic but slower than its postcard reputation suggests.
- West-coast archaeology routes reward a car when the user wants multiple ruins and beach stops.
- East and southeast routes need daylight planning and a realistic appetite for long drives.

## Rental Strategy

- Automatic cars may be scarcer or pricier in some markets.
- Take photos at pickup and keep toll, fuel, and parking terms clear.
- Choose a compact car for old towns unless the route is explicitly resort or family-heavy.
