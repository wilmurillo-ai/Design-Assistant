# Setup - Turkey Travel Guide

Create `~/turkey/` on first use and initialize `~/turkey/memory.md` from `memory-template.md`.

## First Contact

Answer the immediate Turkey question first, then capture only the trip details that change the plan:
- Entry pathway already solved or still pending
- Travel dates or month range
- Istanbul-only, Istanbul plus Cappadocia, coast-focused, archaeology-focused, or mixed
- Domestic flight tolerance, bus tolerance, and driving comfort
- Trip style: food, history, beaches, shopping, nightlife, family, hiking, mixed
- Budget band, pace, and heat tolerance

## What To Save

Keep durable facts in memory:
- Travel window and flexibility
- Arrival and departure airports
- Confirmed bases and open region choices
- Balloon interest, coast interest, and archaeology priorities
- Mobility needs, driving tolerance, and conservative-context sensitivity

## Returning Users

Read `~/turkey/memory.md`, reuse what is still valid, and ask only what changed:
- Dates
- Region focus
- Bookings already made
- Domestic flights, car rental, or balloon decisions

## Quick Start Prompts

**"I want Istanbul and Cappadocia"**
- Ask total days, airport pair, and whether a balloon backup morning is acceptable.
- Then use `istanbul.md`, `cappadocia-and-central-anatolia.md`, `itineraries.md`, and `transport-domestic.md`.

**"I want beaches and history"**
- Ask month, heat tolerance, and whether user prefers Aegean archaeology or Mediterranean resort/coast logic.
- Then use `aegean-and-west-coast.md`, `mediterranean-coast.md`, `archaeology-and-museums.md`, and `weather-and-seasonality.md`.

**"I need a full Turkey route"**
- Ask total days, arrival airport, and whether user accepts one domestic flight or open-jaw routing.
- Then use `regions.md`, `itineraries.md`, `budget-and-costs.md`, and `entry-and-documents.md`.

## Important Notes

- Turkey quality comes from choosing one west or one east logic, not forcing every famous stop.
- Istanbul airport choice and domestic transfer timing matter more than many first-time visitors expect.
- Heat, wind, and long transfer distances are often bigger trip shapers than attraction count.
