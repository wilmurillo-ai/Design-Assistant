# Regions - Turkey

## Region Selection Grid

| Region | Best for | Watch-outs |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Istanbul | First-time city trips, history, food, shopping, Bosphorus | Airport transfer friction, dense crowds, steep hills |
| Cappadocia and Central Anatolia | Landscapes, balloons, cave stays, easy 2-4 night add-on | Wind cancellations, early starts, weak public transit on site |
| Aegean and West Coast | Ephesus, Izmir, Bodrum, Cesme, archaeology plus coast | Summer crowds, long coast transfers, car value rises fast |
| Mediterranean Coast | Antalya, Kas, Fethiye, beach and resort mixes | Peak heat, traffic on scenic roads, resort sprawl |
| Black Sea and Eastern Anatolia | Green mountains, yaylas, remote scenery, winter rail stories | Weather volatility, slower logistics, fewer direct links |
| Southeast and Mesopotamia | Gaziantep food, Mardin, Sanliurfa, Gobeklitepe, deep history | Extreme summer heat, stronger local-context planning needed |

## Planning Rule of Thumb

- 4-5 days: Istanbul only or Cappadocia only
- 7-9 days: Istanbul plus one second cluster
- 10-14 days: Two clusters with one domestic flight
- 16+ days: West route or east route, not full-country overreach

## Transfer Reality

- Istanbul is the domestic air hub, but not every route should touch it twice.
- Cappadocia works best as a compact insert, not as part of a five-base sprint.
- The west and south coasts look close on a map but drive slower than users expect.
- The east is stronger with targeted flights than with accidental marathon overland days.

## First-Time Turkey Route Suggestions

- Culture plus food: Istanbul plus Gaziantep or Izmir-Selcuk
- First classic route: Istanbul plus Cappadocia
- Beach plus ruins: Aegean west coast or Antalya plus nearby coast
- Scenic greenery: Black Sea cluster, not a rushed add-on
