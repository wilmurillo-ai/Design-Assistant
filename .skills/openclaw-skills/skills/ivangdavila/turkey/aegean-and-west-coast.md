# Aegean and West Coast - Turkey

## Best Fit

Best for travelers who want Ephesus, Izmir, boutique coast time, beach clubs, food, and archaeology without full-resort dependence.

## Route Logic

| Cluster | Strong for | Watch-outs |
|---------|------------|------------|
| Izmir plus Selcuk | Ephesus and efficient archaeology base | Less beach atmosphere |
| Cesme / Alacati | Wind, style, weekend coast | Summer pricing and nightlife premiums |
| Bodrum Peninsula | Premium coast, dining, boating | Traffic, cost, and fragmented geography |
| Datca / quieter coast | Slower pace and lower-density scenery | Longer access time |

## Planning Rules

- Keep one culture base and one coast base instead of chasing every peninsula.
- Selcuk is the tactical archaeology play; Izmir is the comfort and flight-connectivity play.
- Aegean summer wind can help with heat but also changes sea and ferry mood.
- Coast weekends can feel very different from weekdays in price and crowd level.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating Izmir, Ephesus, Cesme, Bodrum, and Marmaris as one quick road loop.
- Choosing Bodrum for value when the user really wants lower-key coast.
- Doing major ruins at midday in peak summer.
- Underestimating the cost jump between local-town rhythm and premium beach products.
