# Quality Gates and Risk Controls

Run this checklist before sharing recommendations.

## Gate 1: Logic Integrity

- Problem statement matches the stated objective
- Recommendation addresses root causes, not only symptoms
- Claims are supported by explicit evidence or labeled assumptions

Fail condition:
- Recommendation relies on unstated assumptions or vague claims

## Gate 2: Feasibility

- Required resources exist or are clearly sourced
- Timeline matches team capacity
- Critical dependencies are visible and owned

Fail condition:
- Plan assumes capabilities the team does not have

## Gate 3: Financial Sanity

- Expected benefit and downside range are explicit
- Cost and effort are not hidden
- Breakeven or payback logic is clear when relevant

Fail condition:
- Recommendation has no economic logic or downside framing

## Gate 4: Stakeholder Viability

- Decision rights and approval path are clear
- Likely objections are anticipated
- Pre-wire plan exists for high-influence stakeholders

Fail condition:
- Recommendation ignores known blockers or governance

## Gate 5: Execution Readiness

- First 2-4 weeks have concrete actions
- Each action has owner, deadline, and metric
- Review cadence and escalation path are defined

Fail condition:
- Output ends at strategy with no executable next steps

## Final Risk Label

Assign one label before delivery:
- Green: ready to execute with normal monitoring
- Amber: viable but with notable unresolved risk
- Red: do not execute until blocking risks are resolved

Include the label and rationale in the final recommendation.
