# Customs and Border — United Kingdom

## What to Declare

Declare goods, restricted items, and large amounts of cash when required. Border problems often come from assumptions about "personal use" that do not match the rule set.

## Cash and Monetary Instruments

- Cash declaration rules can apply when entering Great Britain from outside the UK with large sums.
- If a traveler is carrying a high amount of currency or equivalents, route them to the official declaration guidance before travel day.

## Food, Animal, and Plant Restrictions

- Rules are stricter for meat, dairy, plants, and agricultural goods than many tourists expect.
- The exact restriction depends on where the traveler is arriving from.
- If the user wants to bring gifts or food, tell them to verify before departure instead of relying on airline assumptions.

## Duty and Personal Allowance Context

- Duty-free rules depend on origin, quantity, and whether goods are for personal use.
- Alcohol, tobacco, and luxury purchases can trigger separate thresholds or taxes.

## Medicines and Controlled Items

- Keep prescription medicines in original packaging when possible.
- Carry a prescription copy or doctor note for anything unusual, high-volume, or controlled.
- Do not assume medicines legal at home are frictionless at the UK border.

## Practical Border Hygiene

- Keep receipts for high-value purchases.
- Separate documents, medicines, and valuables into an easy-to-show section of luggage.
- If uncertain about an item, declare it and ask rather than guessing.

## Common Border Mistakes

- Treating gifts or snacks as automatically exempt
- Carrying large cash without checking declaration rules
- Assuming Ireland and UK customs logic are identical in every routing scenario
- Packing restricted food or plant items in checked baggage without checking first
