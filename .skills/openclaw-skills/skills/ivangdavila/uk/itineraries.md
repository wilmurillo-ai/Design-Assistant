# Itineraries — United Kingdom

## 7 Days: One Capital Plus One Strong Extension

### Option A: London plus Bath and Cotswolds
- Days 1-4: London by zones, one museum-heavy day, one market or neighborhood day
- Day 5: Rail to Bath, light Roman/Georgian core
- Days 6-7: Cotswolds villages by tour or rental car, then return

### Option B: Edinburgh plus Highlands sampler
- Days 1-3: Edinburgh Old Town, New Town, and one castle or Arthur's Seat block
- Days 4-6: Inverness or Fort William base with one scenic route day
- Day 7: Return buffer and departure

## 10 Days: Two-Base United Kingdom

### Option A: London plus Edinburgh
- Base 1: London for 5-6 nights with one day trip maximum
- Rail transfer: daytime train to Edinburgh
- Base 2: Edinburgh for 3-4 nights, with one Highlands or Stirling-style add-on

### Option B: London plus North England
- London for 4-5 nights
- York or Manchester for 2-3 nights
- Lake District or Liverpool extension for 2 nights if traveler accepts a faster pace

## 14 Days: Capitals Plus Scenery

### London plus Edinburgh plus one scenic region
- London 5 nights
- Edinburgh 3 nights
- One of: Cotswolds, Lake District, Highlands, or North Wales for 4-5 nights
- Final night near departure airport or rail hub

### Great Britain Deep Route
- London 4 nights
- York 2 nights
- Edinburgh 3 nights
- Highlands or Lake District 3 nights
- One buffer night before outbound flight

## 21 Days: Three Clusters With Restraint

- Cluster 1: London and South England
- Cluster 2: North England or Wales
- Cluster 3: Scotland or Northern Ireland
- Keep at least 3 nights in each cluster to avoid spending the trip in transit

## Quality Checks

- No same-day transatlantic arrival plus long rail transfer unless traveler explicitly prefers it
- Every 3-4 active days should include one lighter day
- Scenic road regions need weather flexibility and earlier sunset awareness outside summer
- If the trip includes Ireland and Northern Ireland, confirm border and entry assumptions before locking plans
