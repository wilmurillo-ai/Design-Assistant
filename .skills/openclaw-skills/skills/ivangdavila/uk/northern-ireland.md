# Northern Ireland — Playbook

## Best Use Case

Compact road trip or short-break destination combining Belfast, political history, coastal scenery, and one of the easiest scenic loops in the British Isles.

## Core Anchors

- Belfast for Titanic Quarter, murals, food, and nightlife
- Causeway Coast for Giant's Causeway, Dunluce, and cliff scenery
- Derry/Londonderry for walls, history, and northwest base logic
- Mourne Mountains for hiking add-ons

## Planning Rules

- Belfast works well as a city break without a car; the wider region does not.
- Causeway Coast deserves at least one overnight, not only a rushed bus-day itinerary.
- If the user is combining Northern Ireland with the Republic of Ireland, confirm border, entry, and insurance assumptions before recommending a loop.
- Weather and coastal visibility change the quality of the route more than first-time travelers expect.

## Good 5-Day Pattern

- 2 nights Belfast
- 2 nights Causeway Coast or Derry base
- 1 final Belfast or airport-adjacent night depending on flight time

## Failure Modes

- Trying to do Belfast, Causeway Coast, and Donegal day trips from one base
- Ignoring limited evening transport outside Belfast
- Treating political-history areas as generic photo stops instead of context-rich visits
