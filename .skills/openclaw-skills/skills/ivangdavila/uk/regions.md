# Regions — United Kingdom

## Region Selection Grid

| Region | Best for | Watch-outs |
|--------|----------|------------|
| London | First-time UK trips, museums, markets, major rail links | High lodging costs, timed-entry pressure, airport sprawl |
| South England and Cotswolds | Villages, gardens, Bath/Oxford, softer-paced road or rail trips | Car can help outside main rail towns, summer weekend crowds |
| North England and Lake District | Rail-city culture plus hiking and scenic drives | Weather volatility, slower rural transfers |
| Scotland | Edinburgh plus Highlands, whisky, castles, rail or self-drive touring | Long distances north of the Central Belt, midges in warm months |
| Wales | Coast, castles, hikes, compact road routes | Public transport weak in rural areas, weather changes quickly |
| Northern Ireland | Belfast plus Causeway Coast and compact road trips | Car is often best outside Belfast, weather can reshuffle coastal plans |

## Planning Rule of Thumb

- 4 to 6 days: London only, or one non-London region with depth
- 7 to 10 days: London plus one additional region
- 11 to 14 days: Two clusters maximum unless traveler enjoys frequent hotel changes
- 14 plus days: Great Britain plus Northern Ireland only if flights or ferries are accepted

## Route Logic

- Rail works best for London, Edinburgh, York, Bath, Oxford, Manchester, and Liverpool.
- A car becomes more valuable in the Cotswolds, Lake District, Scottish Highlands, rural Wales, and Causeway Coast.
- Do not combine too many scenic regions in one week; countryside travel is slower than map distance suggests.
- Keep the final night near the departure airport or rail hub when flying home.

## First-Time United Kingdom Suggestions

- Classic first trip: London plus Edinburgh
- City plus village contrast: London plus Bath/Oxford/Cotswolds
- Rail culture route: London, York, and Edinburgh
- Nature-led trip: Edinburgh plus Highlands, or North Wales plus Lake District
- Compact island feel: Belfast plus Causeway Coast, or South England only
