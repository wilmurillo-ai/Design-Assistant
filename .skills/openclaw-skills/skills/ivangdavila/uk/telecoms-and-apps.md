# Telecoms and Apps — United Kingdom

## Connectivity Setup

- eSIM or prepaid SIM is the easiest visitor setup.
- Coverage is usually sufficient in cities and transport corridors, but rural valleys, coasts, and highlands can still be weak.
- Download key tickets and maps offline before countryside segments.

## Practical App Stack

| Use case | Best tool type |
|----------|----------------|
| National rail planning | National Rail or operator apps |
| London transit | TfL Go, contactless payment, step-free planning |
| Big-city routing | City navigation app with live transit |
| Walking and rural navigation | Offline-capable maps |
| Weather and warnings | Met Office and local alert tools |

## Ticketing Rule

- Keep screenshots or offline access for rail tickets, hotel addresses, and airport transfers.
- QR or app-based tickets are normal, but battery failure can still break the day.

## Payments and Device Hygiene

- Assume the phone may be used for boarding, payments, and navigation on the same day.
- Carry a power bank and cable.
- Keep at least one payment method independent from the phone.

## Connectivity by Trip Type

| Trip type | Connectivity priority |
|-----------|-----------------------|
| London city break | Transit, payments, museum tickets |
| Rail-heavy UK loop | Rail updates and platform changes |
| Highlands/Wales/Cornwall drive | Offline maps and weather |
| Family trip | Shared ticket access and battery redundancy |

## Practical Rule

No phone does not need to mean no trip, but users should build enough redundancy that a dead battery does not block payments, transport, or lodging access.
