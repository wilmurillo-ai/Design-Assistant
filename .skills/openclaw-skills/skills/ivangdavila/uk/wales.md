# Wales — Playbook

## Best Use Case

Castle-heavy, coast-and-mountain trips for travelers who want a quieter UK region with strong scenery and less big-city pressure.

## Main Clusters

- Cardiff and south coast heritage for a compact short break
- North Wales for Snowdonia, Conwy, and castle routes
- Pembrokeshire for coastal walking and slower summer travel
- Mid Wales for scenic drives and low-density countryside

## Planning Rules

- Cardiff works by rail; most other Wales itineraries improve sharply with a car.
- North Wales and Pembrokeshire are different trips unless the user has 10+ days.
- Mountain days need weather backup and modest daily expectations.
- Castle touring is strongest when paired with one scenic base, not a different hotel every night.

## Good 6-Day Pattern

- 2 nights Cardiff or Chester gateway
- 3 nights North Wales base around Conwy, Llandudno, or Betws-y-Coed
- 1 flexible return night based on departure logistics

## Failure Modes

- Treating Snowdonia/Eryri hikes as guaranteed in wet or windy conditions
- Overestimating rural public transport for village-to-village movement
- Packing south and north Wales into one short trip and losing days to transit
