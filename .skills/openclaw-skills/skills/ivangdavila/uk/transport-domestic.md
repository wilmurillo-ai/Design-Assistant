# Domestic Transport — United Kingdom

## Mode Selection Framework

| Route type | Default winner | Why |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| London and nearby cities | Rail | Fast center-to-center movement |
| Rural loops and national parks | Car | Last-mile access and timetable freedom |
| Budget long-distance | Coach | Lower cost, slower time |
| Island or water-linked routes | Rail + ferry mix | Depends on exact destination and weather |

## Rail-First Reality

- Rail is the highest-value default for many England and Scotland city pairs.
- Advance booking often changes the fare more than the route changes the time.
- Strikes, engineering works, and Sunday reductions can reshape a plan, so check the date-specific service.

## Best Use Cases by Mode

| Mode | Strongest fit |
|------|---------------|
| National Rail | London, York, Edinburgh, Manchester, Bath-type city chains |
| Coach | Budget travel, airport connections, flexible travelers |
| Domestic flight | Long north-south hops when time matters more than center access |
| Ferry | Islands, Northern Ireland links, scenic crossings |

## Urban Transit Rule

- In London, use contactless or mobile wallet on TfL by default.
- In other major cities, verify whether tap-to-pay, app ticketing, or operator tickets are best.
- Do not recommend renting a car for London-first trips.

## Transfer Buffers to Add

- Air to rail same day: leave explicit recovery time
- Separate-ticket rail changes: build enough margin for platform shifts
- Ferry segments: add weather and boarding slack
- Sunday travel: assume less frequency, not normal frequency

## Rail Booking Strategy

1. Fixed dates and long routes: check advance fares early
2. Flexible dates: compare off-peak options
3. Multi-stop trip: compare point-to-point tickets versus pass products only if the traveler is moving often

## Tourist Trap to Flag

Buying a car-centered UK itinerary by habit often backfires for London, Edinburgh, York, Bath, Oxford, or Cambridge style trips where parking and access are the real cost.
