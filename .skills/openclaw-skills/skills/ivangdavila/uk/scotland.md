# Scotland — Playbook

## Best Use Case

Users wanting the strongest Britain-style contrast in one country: Edinburgh urban heritage, whisky and castle interest, and optional Highlands scenery with real dramatic payoff.

## Regional Split

| Zone | Core strengths | Watch-outs |
|------|----------------|------------|
| Edinburgh and the Lothians | Walkable history, festivals, museums, easy rail access | August crowd and price spikes |
| Glasgow and west-central belt | Music, design, food, museum value | Less postcard-obvious for first-timers |
| Highlands | Scenic drives, lochs, hiking, whisky routes | Long driving days, weather and daylight constraints |
| Islands | Distinct landscapes and slower pace | Ferry or flight planning, weather exposure |

## Planning Rules

- Edinburgh is the best first base for most short Scotland trips.
- Glasgow is a strong second-city add-on for food, music, and local atmosphere, not mandatory for every itinerary.
- Highlands routes work best with 3 or more nights and realistic daily mileage.
- Do not promise Isle of Skye as a quick add-on unless the trip is built around it.

## Good 8-Day Pattern

- 3 nights Edinburgh
- 1 transit night or stop in Pitlochry/Inverness if self-driving
- 3 nights Highlands base
- 1 final night near Edinburgh or Glasgow for departure

## Failure Modes

- Treating Edinburgh Fringe season like a normal city-break period
- Trying to do Loch Ness, Skye, Glencoe, and whisky touring in 2-3 days
- Ignoring weather, single-track roads, and early darkness outside summer
