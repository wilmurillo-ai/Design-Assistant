# London — Playbook

## Best Use Case

First-time United Kingdom base with the strongest museum density, best long-distance rail connections, and enough neighborhoods to fill a week without forcing rushed day trips.

## Neighborhood Strategy

- West End and Covent Garden: best for first-time short stays, expensive but efficient
- South Bank and Waterloo: strong transport plus evening skyline value
- Kensington and Paddington: easier museum access and Heathrow links
- Shoreditch or King's Cross: better for repeat visitors, dining, and rail flexibility

## Movement Rules

- Plan by zone clusters, not attraction lists spread across the whole city.
- Use contactless payment on TfL; Oyster is rarely necessary for short visits.
- Heathrow is operationally easier via Elizabeth line unless the hotel is directly on the Piccadilly line.
- Avoid car rental for city-only London stays.

## High-Value Planning Moves

- Reserve British Museum side priorities, West End shows, and timed-entry attractions early in busy periods.
- Keep one rainy-day museum block and one clear-sky skyline or park block.
- If using London as a base for day trips, cap it at one or two strong outings, not daily commuting.

## Common Cost Traps

- Cheap-looking hotels with 45-60 extra minutes of daily transit
- Airport transfer choices that save little money but waste the first day
- Overusing rideshare late at night instead of staying closer to the planned neighborhood

## Good 4-Day Skeleton

- Day 1: Westminster, St James's, and South Bank
- Day 2: Museum-heavy day in Bloomsbury or Kensington
- Day 3: Market and neighborhood day, such as Shoreditch, Borough, or Notting Hill
- Day 4: One headline reservation plus a lighter park or river segment
