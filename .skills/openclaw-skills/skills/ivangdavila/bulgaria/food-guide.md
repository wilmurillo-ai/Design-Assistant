# Food Guide - Bulgaria

## What to Eat First

| Dish | When | Why it matters |
|------|------|----------------|
| Banitsa | Breakfast | The classic quick start |
| Shopska salad | Lunch or dinner | The default Bulgarian salad |
| Tarator | Summer lunch | Cold yogurt-cucumber soup |
| Kebapche / kyufte | Casual meal | Grill culture, easy everywhere |
| Kavarma | Cooler weather | Rich clay-pot comfort food |
| Mekitsa | Breakfast or snack | Fried dough, nostalgic and heavy |

## How Meals Tend to Flow

- Salad often comes first.
- Rakia before or with the first plates is normal.
- Grilled meats and shared plates show up often.
- Service is usually straightforward, not highly performative.

## Best Food Contexts by Region

| Region | Look for |
|--------|----------|
| Sofia | Modern Bulgarian restaurants, bakeries, wine bars |
| Plovdiv | Wine-focused meals, traditional taverns, cafes |
| Black Sea | Mussels, grilled fish, fried sprats, tomato-heavy summer dishes |
| Bansko / mountains | Hearty grills, beans, stews, mountain taverns |
| Melnik and southwest | Wine, local meats, broad bean dishes |

## Breakfast Rules

- Bakeries are often the strongest value breakfast.
- Banitsa plus ayran or boza is the classic fast option.
- Sit-down brunch exists in Sofia and Plovdiv, less so elsewhere.

## Ordering Notes

- Bulgarian menus often lean meat-heavy.
- Vegetarian meals are possible, but vegan travelers need more deliberate filtering outside big cities.
- Fish quality jumps on the coast, especially away from the loudest resort strips.

## Tourist-Trap Signals

- Oversized menu with everything from sushi to pizza to "traditional folklore dinner"
- Beachfront restaurant with aggressive host and photo menu
- Old-style decor used as a substitute for actual quality

## Good User Guidance

- Foodies should prioritize Sofia and Plovdiv over generic resort areas.
- On the coast, eat where locals go after the beach, not directly on the main strip.
- In ski towns, favor busy taverns over novelty-theme venues.
