# Transport - Bulgaria

## First Principle

In Bulgaria, the best intercity answer is not always rail. Buses are often more practical, and a car becomes more useful once the trip turns coastal, rural, or mountain-heavy.

## Arrival

### Sofia
- Sofia Airport is the main entry point.
- Metro is the easiest budget link into the center when staying near a useful stop.
- App taxi or official taxi ranks are better than accepting random offers.

## Intercity

| Mode | Best for | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Bus | Most city-to-city travel | Often faster and more frequent than rail |
| Train | Scenic or slower travel | Fine when timetable works, not always first choice |
| Car | Coast, villages, mountains | Best for flexibility, worst inside Sofia |

## City Use

| City | Best move |
|------|-----------|
| Sofia | Metro + walking + ordered taxi |
| Plovdiv | Mostly walking |
| Varna | Walking + short taxi or bus |
| Bansko | Walking in town, gondola or shuttle pattern in season |

## Driving Rules

- Driving is useful for the south coast, Rhodopes, and smaller mountain bases.
- Winter mountain driving needs caution, especially around ski areas.
- Sofia does not reward having a car unless the route immediately leaves the city.

## Good Advice

- Use Sofia as the main air hub, not as a road-trip burden.
- Keep transfer days realistic; Bulgaria looks small but mountain and coast movement takes time.
- If a schedule is critical, verify current operator timetables before locking the plan.
