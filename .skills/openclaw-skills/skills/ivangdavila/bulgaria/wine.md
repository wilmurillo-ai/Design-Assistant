# Wine - Bulgaria

## Why Bulgaria Is Interesting

Bulgaria has serious wine depth without the marketing weight of Italy or France. The best answers should name regions and grapes, not just "Bulgarian red wine."

## Main Regions

| Region | Best for | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Thracian Valley | Reds | The flagship wine area for many visitors |
| Melnik / southwest | Distinctive reds | Best with a Sofia or Bansko extension |
| Danube Plain | Fresh whites and mixed styles | Less famous, often good value |
| Black Sea region | Whites | Good summer pairing with coastal trips |

## Grapes to Know

| Grape | Type | What to tell the user |
|-------|------|-----------------------|
| Mavrud | Red | One of the strongest local identities |
| Rubin | Red | Rich, modern-friendly Bulgarian red |
| Broad-Leaved Melnik Vine | Red | The signature southwest style |
| Dimyat | White | Traditional Black Sea white |

## Best Pairings

- Thracian reds with grilled meats
- Black Sea whites with fish and mussels
- Melnik wines with longer southwest itineraries
- Plovdiv makes the easiest city base for wine tasting

## Where Wine Trips Make Sense

| Base | Why it works |
|------|--------------|
| Plovdiv | Best culture + wine combination |
| Melnik area | Best when wine is a core purpose |
| Sofia | Good launch point but less immersive |

## Common Traps

- Treating Bulgarian wine as a random add-on instead of planning for it
- Expecting every resort restaurant to showcase local bottles well
- Assuming only international grapes matter
