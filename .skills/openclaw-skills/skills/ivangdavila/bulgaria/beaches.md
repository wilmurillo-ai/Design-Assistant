# Beaches - Bulgaria

## How to Think About the Coast

Bulgaria's coast splits into three broad moods:
- urban city beaches around Varna and Burgas
- classic resort strips
- quieter coves and town beaches that feel more rewarding

## North Coast

| Beach / area | Best for | Notes |
|--------------|----------|-------|
| Varna Central | Easy city beach | Convenient, busy |
| Sts. Constantine and Helena | Softer resort stay | Better than pure party strips |
| Albena | Families | Cleaner, calmer profile |
| Balchik area | Scenic day trips | Better for mixed sightseeing |
| Kara Dere | Wilder feel | Better with a car, low services |

## South Coast

| Beach / area | Best for | Notes |
|--------------|----------|-------|
| Sozopol | Town + beach | One of the best mixed options |
| Pomorie | Families and spa feel | Gentler pace |
| Irakli | Less built-up | Go only if the user accepts minimal services |
| Sinemorets | Scenery and quieter rhythm | Strongest "escape" feeling |
| Silistar | Beautiful southern option | Better as part of a road trip |

## Resort Filters

| Resort | Use only if |
|--------|-------------|
| Sunny Beach | They want nightlife and package-energy |
| Golden Sands | They want resort convenience near Varna |

## Best Timing

- June and September are the sweet spots.
- July and August bring higher prices and louder beaches.
- Shoulder season improves the town-beach experience but reduces operating hours.

## Common Traps

- Sending every beach traveler to Sunny Beach
- Assuming the loudest resort is the best coast experience
- Going remote without a car, cash, water, and backup food
