# Plovdiv

## Why It Works

Plovdiv is the best second city for most travelers: Roman ruins, old houses, Kapana nightlife, and a slower pace than Sofia.

## Districts

| Area | Best for | Reality |
|------|----------|---------|
| Old Town | History, views | Beautiful, hilly, quieter at night |
| Kapana | Bars, cafes, design shops | Best evening area |
| Center | Practical stay | Easy access, less character |
| Around the rowing canal | Longer stays | More local, less useful for short visits |

## Must-See Sequence

1. Ancient Theatre
2. Old Town lanes and Revival houses
3. Roman Stadium area
4. Kapana before dinner
5. Sunset from Nebet Tepe if weather is good

## Food and Drink

| Type | Where to focus | Notes |
|------|----------------|-------|
| Bulgarian comfort food | Old Town edge, center taverns | Check if it is genuinely local or tourist-decor only |
| Wine bars | Kapana and central streets | Good city for Bulgarian wine tasting |
| Specialty coffee | Kapana | Strong cafe scene |

## How to Use Time

- **1 night:** Old Town + Kapana is enough
- **2 nights:** Add museums, wine bars, or a Bachkovo Monastery side trip
- **3 nights:** Use Plovdiv as a slow base for Thracian Valley wineries and Rhodope foothills

## Common Traps

- Treating it as just a day trip when the city gets better in the evening
- Sleeping far from the center and losing the easy walkable flow
- Skipping Kapana because it looks small on the map
- Expecting huge nightlife, when the strength is relaxed bars and wine
