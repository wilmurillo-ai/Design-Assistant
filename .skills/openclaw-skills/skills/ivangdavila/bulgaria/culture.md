# Culture - Bulgaria

## Communication

- English is common in tourism-facing places, much less so in rural areas.
- A few Bulgarian phrases help more outside Sofia and resort zones.
- Some older locals may use head gestures in ways that confuse visitors; listen for the spoken yes/no, not only the motion.

## Meal Rhythm

| Moment | Typical pattern |
|--------|-----------------|
| Breakfast | Bakery, banitsa, coffee, quick starts |
| Lunch | Practical and often earlier than southern Europe |
| Dinner | Relaxed, but not as late as Spain |

## Payment Reality

- Cards are normal in cities, malls, chain hotels, and many restaurants.
- Cash is still useful in villages, buses, markets, and some beach or mountain spots.
- Keep small cash for transport friction and rural areas.

## Service Style

- Service is usually direct rather than performative.
- Do not expect constant table check-ins.
- Ask clearly if you need the bill or a menu explanation.

## What Visitors Often Misread

- Bulgaria is hospitable but not always smile-first in the same way as southern resort cultures.
- "Traditional" decor does not automatically mean quality food.
- Cheap prices do not mean low standards everywhere; the best places often look unflashy.

## Good Advice

- Dress city-casual in Sofia and Plovdiv, not beachwear.
- Mountain and monastery visits need more practical clothing.
- If the user is sensitive to smoking, check terrace and older venue culture in advance.
