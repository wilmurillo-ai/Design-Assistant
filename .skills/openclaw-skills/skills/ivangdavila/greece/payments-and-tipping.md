# Payments and Tipping - Greece

## Card and Cash Reality

- Cards are widely accepted in cities and tourist zones.
- Keep some cash for taxis, small cafes, beach kiosks, village shops, and remote areas.
- Split payments are not always smooth in small family-run places.

## Tip Norms

| Situation | Practical norm |
|-----------|----------------|
| Casual cafe or bakery | Round up or leave small change |
| Restaurant with table service | Small extra amount if service was good |
| Taxi | Round up or add a modest amount |
| Premium guide or private driver | Tip only if service quality clearly justified it |

## Payment Traps

- Confirm whether beach setup or service fees are separate.
- Ask whether card minimums exist in tiny businesses.
- Do not assume every island taverna wants large bills near closing time.

## ATM and Card Hygiene

- Use bank-linked ATMs where possible.
- Decline dynamic currency conversion if card options appear in home currency.
- Keep one backup payment method in case island connectivity is weak.

## Rule

In Greece, over-tipping like the United States is unnecessary; under-carrying cash on smaller islands is the more common mistake.
