# Accessibility - Greece

## Core Reality

Greece can be excellent for culture and scenery, but old towns, ports, stairs, and uneven surfaces create real accessibility limits. Confirm specifics early.

## What To Check First

| Topic | Why it matters |
|-------|----------------|
| Hotel stairs and elevator access | Island stays often have many steps |
| Port boarding support | Ferry accessibility varies by vessel and operator |
| Archaeological site terrain | Stone, slopes, and uneven paths are common |
| Transfer method | Airport, port, and old-town links may involve stairs or cobbles |

## Better Patterns

- Athens can work if hotel, metro access, and daily pacing are chosen carefully.
- One accessible base is usually better than frequent hops.
- Museum-heavy days can be easier than exposed ruin-heavy days.

## Ask Explicitly About

- Walk distance tolerance
- Wheelchair or scooter needs
- Bathroom access frequency
- Heat sensitivity and shade requirements

## Trap Avoidance

- Do not assume "central" means accessible.
- Do not book cliffside or village stays from photos alone.
- Do not promise universal beach accessibility across islands.
