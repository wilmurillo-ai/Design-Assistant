# Accommodation - Greece

## Base Selection Rules

- In Athens, stay by neighborhood logic, not by cheapest central pin.
- On islands, prioritize port access, beach access, or village atmosphere, but rarely all three at once.
- For mainland loops, use 2-3 strong bases instead of one-night hops.

## What To Optimize By Destination

| Destination type | Best accommodation logic |
|------------------|--------------------------|
| Athens | Walkable neighborhood plus metro or tram access |
| Small Cyclades island | Port or village base if no car, beach side if staying put |
| Santorini | Pick caldera view only if user values the room experience enough to pay for it |
| Crete | Choose one coast zone first, then hotel second |
| Peloponnese | Parking and easy road access matter more than old-town novelty |

## Practical Filters

- Late ferry arrival or early departure may justify one night near port or airport.
- Stairs are common in island stays; confirm mobility needs early.
- Many attractive budget rooms trade away blackout, soundproofing, or luggage ease.

## Peak Season Advice

- July and August pricing jumps fast on headline islands.
- Shoulder season often allows better-value boutique stays.
- For sunset-view areas, decide whether view is the trip or just one dinner.

## Trap Prevention

- Do not book remote villas without a transport plan.
- Do not choose Santorini or Mykonos by price alone; location friction matters.
- Do not assume all "sea view" rooms are walkable to swimming or dining.
