# Food Guide - Greece

## Meal Logic

- Breakfast can stay light; lunch and dinner do more of the cultural work.
- Long seafood lunches, meze dinners, and bakery breakfasts often outperform formal three-course planning.
- Good tavernas usually win on location, freshness, and rhythm rather than on polished branding.

## Regional Differences

| Region | Food angle |
|--------|------------|
| Athens | Strong modern Greek and market-led variety |
| Cyclades | Seafood, local cheeses, simpler island menus |
| Crete | Deep local products, greens, lamb, olive oil strength |
| North | Richer meat, pastries, and stronger city cafe culture |
| Peloponnese | Produce, olive oil, citrus, and road-trip tavernas |

## Practical Advice

- Book one or two destination dinners, not every meal.
- Use bakeries, cafes, and market lunches to keep archaeology or beach days flexible.
- Ask about catch of the day and portion sizing before over-ordering seafood.

## Trap Avoidance

- Menus with photo overload in the busiest landmark rows often signal weak value.
- Sunset-view dining can charge for the view more than the food.
- A "free beach transfer" sometimes means pressure to spend heavily once there.

## Rule

In Greece, the best food plans leave room for place-driven meals rather than rigid reservation stacks.
