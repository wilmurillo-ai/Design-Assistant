# Road Trips and Driving - Greece

## When a Car Helps

- Peloponnese loops
- Crete outside one single resort zone
- Mainland archaeology and beach combinations
- Villages, monasteries, and mountain routes with weak transit coverage

## When a Car Hurts

- Athens center
- Hydra and other no-car or low-car style destinations
- Short island stays where parking and pickup waste time
- Mykonos or Santorini if user mainly wants walkable bases and taxis

## Practical Road Reality

- Narrow village streets and parking constraints are normal.
- Manual cars are common; check transmission early if automatic is required.
- Google Maps time estimates can be optimistic on mountain or coastal roads.

## License and Rental Logic

- Verify whether traveler needs an International Driving Permit for the rental company and nationality involved.
- Confirm ferry permissions before assuming a rental car can move between islands.
- Photograph the car and tire condition before departure from small ports or airports.

## Route Quality Rule

If the road trip is mainly about archaeology and scenic stops, keep daily drive loads moderate. Greece roads are more tiring than the map suggests.
