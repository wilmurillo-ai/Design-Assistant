# Island Hopping and Ferries - Greece

## Core Rule

Island hopping only improves the trip when the islands are geographically compatible and the traveler accepts schedule risk. More islands does not mean more Greece.

## Good vs Bad Hops

| Pattern | Verdict | Why |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Paros plus Naxos | Good | Short hop, similar routing logic |
| Santorini plus Naxos plus Paros | Usually good | Manageable if time is enough |
| Mykonos plus Crete plus Corfu | Weak | Too much transfer friction |
| Athens arrival plus same-day essential ferry | Fragile | Border, baggage, and delay risk |

## Ferry Planning Rules

- Prefer morning or midday crossings when possible.
- Build at least one cushion before a fixed international departure.
- Check whether the ticket is for seat, cabin, or car, not just departure time.
- Port choice matters in Athens; not every ferry leaves from the same place.

## Meltemi and Disruption Logic

- Summer wind is not a rare edge case in the Cyclades.
- High-speed ferries are often less comfortable in rough conditions.
- If trip quality depends on one exact crossing, the route is too brittle.

## Best Use Cases

- 8 to 12 day trip with one gateway city plus one neighboring island pair
- Travelers who like movement and accept that weather may reshape timing
- Shoulder season routes with fewer crowd bottlenecks

## Avoid

- Three ferry transfers in four days
- Last-night-on-an-island before a morning international flight
- Car ferry bookings left to the last moment in peak season
