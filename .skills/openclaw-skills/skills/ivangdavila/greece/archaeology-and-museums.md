# Archaeology and Museums - Greece

## Planning Rule

Do not stack too many ancient sites into one midday summer block. Greece rewards fewer major sites with better timing.

## Highest-Friction Stops

| Site type | Why planning matters |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Acropolis zone | Heat, cruise spillover, timed-entry pressure |
| Delphi and Epidaurus | Better as route anchors than rushed add-ons |
| Knossos | Often paired badly with overlong Cretan day plans |
| Museum-heavy Athens days | Need pacing, shade, and neighborhood logic |

## Practical Strategy

- Use the official ticketing platform from `sources.md` when advance purchase makes sense.
- Start exposed archaeological sites early.
- Pair one headline site with one museum or shaded neighborhood, not with three more ruins.
- Use museum days as wind or heat backups.

## Better Combinations

- Acropolis plus Acropolis Museum plus Plaka or Koukaki
- Nafplio base plus Epidaurus and Mycenae on separate pacing
- Heraklion Museum plus Knossos with heat-aware sequencing

## Traps

- Chasing "all ancient Greece" in one trip
- Ignoring site-closing days or seasonal schedules
- Treating museum time as optional after an exhausting outdoor site
