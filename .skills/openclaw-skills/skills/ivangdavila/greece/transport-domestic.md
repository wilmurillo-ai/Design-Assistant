# Domestic Transport - Greece

## Transport Selection Grid

| Mode | Best for | Limits |
|------|----------|--------|
| Domestic flight | Long jumps, island-mainland compression | Airport overhead, bag fees |
| Ferry | Island connections, scenic travel | Wind sensitivity, schedule changes |
| KTEL bus | Mainland value routes | Operator fragmentation by region |
| Car | Peloponnese, Crete, inland routes | Parking, narrow roads, fatigue |
| Rail | Selected mainland legs only | Limited network relevance for many trips |

## Athens Transport Reality

- Athens has workable metro, bus, tram, and airport links.
- Airport access can be cheaper by metro or bus and faster by taxi only in some time windows.
- Cruise-port, airport, and central-Athens moves are different problems; do not treat them as one.

## Mainland Rule

- KTEL buses matter more than many first-time visitors expect.
- Rail can help on some corridors, but it is not the backbone for a full Greece trip.
- Car is strongest when route is archaeology-heavy or beach-heavy outside major cities.

## Island Rule

- On smaller islands, the key transport question is not public transit quality but whether the chosen base makes a car unnecessary.
- Airport islands may still need a ferry mindset if user wants multi-stop routes.
