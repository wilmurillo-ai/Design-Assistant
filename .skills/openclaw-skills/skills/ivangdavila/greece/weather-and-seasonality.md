# Weather and Seasonality - Greece

## Seasonal Grid

| Period | Best for | Watch-outs |
|--------|----------|------------|
| April to early June | Sightseeing, hiking, mixed routes | Water may still be cool in some areas |
| Late June to August | Beaches, nightlife, long daylight | Heat, meltemi, crowds, price spikes |
| September to early October | Warm sea, better balance, strong value | Some late-season wind and schedule taper |
| Late October to March | Cities, culture, quieter mainland trips | Reduced island services, cooler sea, variable weather |

## Summer Reality

- Midday archaeology can become low-quality or unsafe in hot periods.
- Cyclades wind is a planning factor, not a trivia note.
- Premium islands become expensive and crowded fast.

## Shoulder Season Advantage

- May, June, September, and early October are often the best all-round periods.
- Sea temperature, opening calendars, and ferry frequency still need checking.
- Mainland loops often outperform island-hopping in cooler months.

## Winter Rule

- Athens and Thessaloniki still work.
- Some islands feel sleepy or under-serviced unless the user explicitly wants quiet.
- Big islands such as Crete remain more viable than tiny islands.

## Planning Rule

Choose trip style from season, not the other way around.
