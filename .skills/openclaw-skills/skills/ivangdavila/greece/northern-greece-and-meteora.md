# Northern Greece and Meteora - Greece

## Best Use Case

Northern Greece works for travelers who want Thessaloniki, monasteries, mountain scenery, food-led city time, and a cooler-shoulder-season route.

## Core Pairings

| Pairing | Why it works |
|---------|--------------|
| Thessaloniki plus Meteora | City plus landmark contrast |
| Thessaloniki plus Halkidiki | City food base plus beach add-on |
| Zagori or Epirus routes | Nature-first trip with mountain logic |

## Strengths

- Better fit for repeat visitors who already did Athens and Cyclades
- Strong food and cafe culture in Thessaloniki
- Meteora gives one of the most dramatic mainland experiences in Greece

## Logistics

- Better as a dedicated mainland block than as an afterthought to a busy island trip
- Train, bus, or car choices depend heavily on the exact north route
- Weather can swing more in shoulder and winter periods

## Trap Avoidance

- Do not treat Meteora as a trivial day trip unless the user truly accepts the transit load.
- Do not combine far north and far south Greece in one short itinerary.
- Do not assume beach weather in the north matches the islands on the same dates.
