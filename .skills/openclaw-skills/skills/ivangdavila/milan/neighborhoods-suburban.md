# Suburban and Value-Oriented Milan Areas

## Lambrate

### Strengths
- Better rent-to-space ratio than central zones.
- Good access for students and early-career residents.

### Considerations
- Not all blocks have the same residential quality.
- Social life depends on specific micro-areas.

## Affori and Dergano Belt

### Strengths
- Solid metro access in key pockets.
- Better affordability for long-term residents.

### Considerations
- Fewer premium dining and nightlife options nearby.
- Commute quality depends on exact station distance.

## Niguarda

### Strengths
- Family-friendly and calmer day-to-day rhythm.
- Larger apartment stock compared to center.

### Considerations
- Longer travel for central nightlife and tourism.
- Car ownership can still be useful in some pockets.

## San Siro Outer Zones

### Strengths
- Larger housing inventory.
- Event access for football-focused users.

### Considerations
- Match-day crowd and transport pressure.
- Some blocks feel less active outside event days.

## Who Should Prioritize Suburban Districts

- Families optimizing space and monthly burn.
- Students or early professionals seeking value.
- Long-term residents with hybrid work patterns.
