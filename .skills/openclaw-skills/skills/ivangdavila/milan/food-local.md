# Milan and Lombardy Food Guide

## Essential Dishes

| Dish | What It Is | Typical Price |
|------|------------|---------------|
| Risotto alla Milanese | Saffron risotto, creamy and rich | EUR 14-26 |
| Ossobuco | Braised veal shank, often with risotto | EUR 22-40 |
| Cotoletta alla Milanese | Breaded veal cutlet, classic Milan style | EUR 18-35 |
| Cassoeula | Slow-cooked winter pork and cabbage dish | EUR 16-28 |
| Mondeghili | Milanese meatballs, traditional reuse cuisine | EUR 8-16 |
| Panettone | Iconic Milan holiday dessert | EUR 18-60 (whole) |

## Where To Focus by Experience

- Traditional trattoria for classic dishes.
- Historic cafes for pastry and coffee rituals.
- Modern osteria for updated local cuisine.

## Seasonal Notes

- Heavier comfort dishes are best in colder months.
- Summer menus emphasize lighter preparations.
- Holiday period increases panettone quality and variety.

## Common Ordering Mistakes

- Expecting huge US-style portions at upscale places.
- Skipping local specials in favor of generic pasta.
- Not asking about daily menu items.

## Local Pairing Ideas

| Food | Pairing |
|------|---------|
| Cotoletta | Light red from Lombardy or nearby regions |
| Risotto alla Milanese | Crisp white or sparkling option |
| Ossobuco | Medium to full-bodied red |
| Panettone | Sweet wine or quality coffee |
