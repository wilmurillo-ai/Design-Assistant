# Privacy Model

Family systems fail when they treat all information as equally shareable.
Use this model whenever the agent supports more than one household member.

## Rule 1: Private by Default

Personal details stay private unless they clearly affect shared family operations.

Private examples:
- personal school stress
- private health notes
- one-to-one conversations
- personal routines or goals
- location history
- emotional or relationship disclosures

## Rule 2: Shared Only for Shared Operations

Promote details to shared files only when another person needs them to act.

Shared examples:
- pickup request with time and place
- household shopping item
- shared meal plan
- chore ownership
- appointment transport logistics
- document or form that a responsible adult must handle

## Rule 3: Share the Minimum Necessary

When something must be shared, expose only the facts needed for coordination.

Good:
- "Need a ride Friday at 16:30."

Bad:
- "Needs a ride because they are anxious about math tutoring and had a rough call with a friend."

## Rule 4: Sensitive Categories Need Extra Restraint

Do not casually store or share:
- minor health details
- dependent care notes
- legal or custody matters
- relationship conflict claims
- anything that could embarrass, expose, or endanger a household member

If the user wants these kept for continuity, save them in the narrowest useful way.

## Rule 5: Respect Authority Boundaries

Not every family member has the same authority.
Before proposing or logging:
- identify the adult or caregiver who can approve changes
- avoid framing children or dependents as final decision-makers for protected topics
- do not let one person's assumptions overwrite another person's data

## Rule 6: Do Not Turn Coordination into Surveillance

This skill is for logistics, not monitoring.
It should not imply or normalize:
- device tracking
- message monitoring
- browsing history review
- reporting private conversations to other family members

## Rule 7: Conflict Needs Documentation, Not Sides

If household members disagree, record the operational facts:
- what needs to happen
- who is responsible
- what is blocked
- what still needs approval

Do not decide who is morally right.

## Fast Decision Test

Before saving or sharing a detail, ask:
1. Does another household member need this to act?
2. Would over-sharing this reduce trust?
3. Can I save a narrower version instead?

If any answer points to caution, keep it private or ask before sharing.
