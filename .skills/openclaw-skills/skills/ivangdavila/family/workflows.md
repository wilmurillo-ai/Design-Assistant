# Family Ops Workflows

Use these workflows when the household needs reliable coordination, not just ideas.

## 1. Daily Triage

Use when the family is juggling multiple moving parts today.

1. List today's hard commitments.
2. Surface collisions, transport gaps, and prep work.
3. Assign owner, deadline, and fallback for each non-obvious step.
4. Return one clean operating picture for the day.

Output should answer:
- what happens
- who owns it
- what can break
- what the fallback is

## 2. Weekly Reset

Use once per week or when the schedule already feels broken.

1. Review the next 7 days in `weekly-plan.md`.
2. Pull in school deadlines, appointments, meals, shopping, and transport.
3. Identify overload windows and unresolved handoffs.
4. Convert vague intentions into owned tasks.
5. Update shopping, meals, and documents only where the week depends on them.

## 3. Handoff Note

Use when responsibility moves from one person to another.

Good handoff notes are short enough to read in under 30 seconds:
- who or what is being handed off
- exact timing or next deadline
- non-obvious risk
- what to do if the plan slips

## 4. Meal and Shopping Loop

Use when meal planning and household supply management are creating repeated friction.

1. Build the shortest realistic meal plan.
2. Generate shopping from actual meals plus restock thresholds.
3. Group by store or urgency.
4. Flag the one missing item that would break the week.

Prefer repeatable fallback meals over aspirational planning.

## 5. School and Childcare Loop

Use for school forms, events, pickup changes, activity gear, and deadline tracking.

1. Record the deadline, child, owner, and status.
2. Separate private school details from logistics another adult must act on.
3. Track only the shared facts needed for pickup, transport, supplies, or paperwork.
4. Close the loop when the form is submitted, the gear is packed, or the event is covered.

## 6. Care Coordination Loop

Use for appointments, medications, routine support, and dependent care logistics.

1. Record the next appointment or task.
2. Note prep, transport, and follow-up.
3. Track refill runway or missed-step risk if relevant.
4. Escalate conservatively when health or safety is unclear.

The agent coordinates logistics. It does not diagnose or replace professional care.

## 7. Incident Review

Use after a missed pickup, missed dose, forgotten form, or repeated household breakdown.

1. Log what happened in `logs/incidents.md`.
2. Identify the real failure mode: unclear ownership, stale data, missing reminder, bad handoff, or overload.
3. Fix the system, not just the symptom.
4. Update one upstream file so the same error is less likely next time.
