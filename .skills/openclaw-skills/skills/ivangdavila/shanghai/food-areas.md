# Shanghai Food Areas Guide

## How To Use This Guide
- Pick dining zones by purpose first, then by cuisine.
- Shanghai traffic can turn a 6 km trip into 40-60 minutes at peak times.
- Stay local for weekday dinners; cross-town for planned weekends.

## Former French Concession (Xuhui and western Huangpu)
- Best for: cafe culture, international dining, stylish local restaurants.
- Strength: walkable streets and high venue density.
- Watch out: queues and higher prices on weekends.

## Jing'an
- Best for: business dinners, bars, polished mid-to-upscale options.
- Strength: many choices near metro lines and malls.
- Watch out: premium pricing around core commercial blocks.

## The Bund and East Nanjing Road (Huangpu)
- Best for: skyline views, first-time visitor dinners, special occasions.
- Strength: iconic atmosphere and hotel-grade service.
- Watch out: tourist pricing and variable food quality near major sights.

## People's Square and South Huangpu
- Best for: practical central dining, legacy local brands.
- Strength: easy transport and broad budget range.
- Watch out: some older venues close earlier than expected.

## Lujiazui (Pudong)
- Best for: corporate meals, hotel dining, high-floor views.
- Strength: reliable service and easier reservations in business towers.
- Watch out: fewer casual street-level choices late at night.

## Changning and Gubei
- Best for: Japanese and Korean food, family-friendly venues.
- Strength: strong neighborhood repeat-customer restaurants.
- Watch out: some clusters are less convenient without ride-hailing.

## Xintiandi and Huaihai Road area
- Best for: social dinners, stylish group outings, premium casual.
- Strength: compact zone with many post-dinner options.
- Watch out: high spend per person and crowded holiday evenings.

## Fast Decision Matrix
- Local Shanghainese dinner: Huangpu or Xuhui.
- International date night: Former French Concession or Jing'an.
- Client dinner with views: Bund or Lujiazui.
- Japanese or Korean focus: Changning/Gubei.
- Budget-friendly local meal near center: People's Square side streets.
