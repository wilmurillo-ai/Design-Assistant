# Santo Domingo

## Best Fit

- Use Santo Domingo when the user wants history, restaurants, nightlife, and the country's strongest city identity.
- Strong for 1-3 nights before or after a beach segment.
- Weak fit as a substitute for a beach holiday.

## Stay Strategy

| Goal | Better base |
|------|-------------|
| Colonial atmosphere and walkability | Zona Colonial |
| Business-style comfort and modern dining | Piantini or Naco |
| Nightlife and social energy | Central districts with easy ride access |

## What Works Well

- Colonial Zone for first-time history and evening walks
- Modern city districts for better hotel inventory and more polished dining
- Food and nightlife users who want to see the country beyond resort walls

## Trap Avoidance

- Do not oversell the city as a same-day side trip from far east-coast resorts.
- Keep airport, hotel, and evening plans geographically tight because traffic can eat the schedule.
- Use the city for contrast, not for beach time.

## Planning Rule

Santo Domingo is the best Dominican Republic stop for depth, but only if the user gives it enough time to breathe.
