# Safety and Emergencies

## Core Risks

| Risk | Why it matters |
|------|----------------|
| Road behavior and night transfers | Travel friction and accident exposure rise fast after dark |
| Water and current conditions | Not every beach suits casual swimmers every day |
| Petty theft in urban or transit settings | More relevant outside controlled resort environments |
| Storm disruption | Hurricane-season trips need buffer and flexibility |
| Remote-stay logistics | Help, food, and transport can be slower in isolated areas |

## Practical Rules

- Use 911 for police, medical, or fire emergencies where service is available.
- In remote areas, resorts, hotels, and tour operators may still be the fastest first layer of help.
- Respect beach flags, surf warnings, and rough-water days, especially on Atlantic-facing beaches.
- Keep passports, cards, phones, and transport details controlled in cities, beaches, and transit points.
- Avoid casual late-night road moves after long flights if a transfer or hotel night solves the risk better.

## Health and Execution

- Hydration, sun protection, and heat pacing matter more than many first-timers expect.
- Drinkable-water assumptions should follow hotel or bottled-water logic, not improvisation.
- Travel insurance matters more once the trip includes boats, diving, road trips, or remote stays.

## Rule

Dominican Republic safety is usually a logistics-and-conditions problem before it becomes a crime problem.
