# Budget and Costs — Dominican Republic

Tourism pricing often appears in USD, while many local transactions still happen in Dominican pesos. Budget honestly from route shape, not from hotel ads alone.

## Fast Budget Bands

| Trip style | Typical reality |
|------------|-----------------|
| Budget | Guesthouses, buses, simple local meals, fewer private transfers |
| Mid-range | Mix of decent hotels, selective transfers, local meals plus some resort or beach-club spend |
| Upscale | Better beachfront hotels, private transfers, curated excursions, premium dining |
| Luxury | Branded resorts, villas, butlers or drivers, premium excursions, and low-friction airport handling |

## The Hidden Cost Drivers

- Airport transfers and long one-way road moves
- Resort meal plans versus eating outside compounds
- Boat excursions with add-on fees and tips
- Rental car, fuel, tolls, and parking
- Weather forcing a paid backup activity
- Late booking in winter-sun dates or holiday periods

## Budget Logic by Route

| Route shape | Cost behavior |
|-------------|---------------|
| One resort base | Easy to control if transfers are solved once |
| City plus beach | Moderate, but transfer math matters |
| Peninsula or north-coast self-routed trip | Lodging can be fair, movement adds up |
| Villa trip | Great for groups, wasteful for short solo or couple trips |

## Practical Advice

- Quote the user a daily cost range plus transfer and excursion assumptions.
- Separate "room rate" from "trip cost" so they can see where the money goes.
- If the user is price-sensitive, cut base changes before cutting every meal.
- If they care about peace of mind, pay for the clean airport and arrival chain first.
