# Food Guide

## What To Explain Early

- Resort food and Dominican food are not the same category.
- The user gets the country's real flavor in city dining, roadside stops, beach restaurants, and neighborhood breakfast spots.

## Core Dishes

| Dish | Why it matters |
|------|----------------|
| La bandera | Everyday plate of rice, beans, meat, and salad |
| Mangu | Dominant breakfast reference point |
| Sancocho | Celebration and comfort dish |
| Fried fish by the beach | Core coastal eating pattern |
| Coconut-forward seafood in the northeast | Strong Samana identity |
| Tostones and yaniqueques | Common side or snack logic |

## Food Strategy by Region

- Santo Domingo for the broadest dining range and strongest modern restaurant scene.
- Samana and Las Terrenas for seafood and beach-town dining.
- North coast for a mix of Dominican, surf-town, and international casual food.
- Resort zones for convenience, with better local food often found outside the property walls.

## Practical Rules

- Breakfast and lunch are often the easiest times to find more local everyday food.
- Ask whether the user wants polished restaurants, street-level local food, seafood, or all-inclusive convenience before giving food advice.
- If the user is staying in a resort, separate "best local food nearby" from "best food inside the property."

## Rule

Food advice should locate the user in the country's real eating rhythm, not just upgrade their buffet.
