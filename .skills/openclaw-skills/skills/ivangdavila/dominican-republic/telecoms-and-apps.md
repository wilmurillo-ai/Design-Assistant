# Telecoms and Apps

## Connectivity Basics

- Roaming or eSIM is usually the easiest answer for short resort trips.
- A local line from major operators such as Claro or Altice becomes more useful for longer stays, remote work, or independent multi-stop routes.
- Download offline maps before mountain drives, remote beaches, or cross-country transfer days.

## Best App Categories

| Need | What to use |
|------|-------------|
| Messaging with hotels, drivers, hosts, and tours | WhatsApp |
| Driving and traffic | Waze plus offline map backup |
| General navigation | Google Maps with saved hotel and airport pins |
| Delivery in bigger cities | PedidosYa or hotel concierge fallback |
| Ride-hailing in major cities | Uber where available, while checking local pickup reality |

## Practical Advice

- Save lodging addresses and transfer contacts before landing.
- Keep e-ticket QR, hotel confirmations, and return-flight details available offline.
- Ride-hailing works best in major urban areas, but airport and resort pickup rules can change, so always confirm the exact meeting point.

## Rule

Digital convenience is good in the Dominican Republic until the route leaves the main city or resort corridor, so make maps, contacts, and confirmations resilient before moving.
