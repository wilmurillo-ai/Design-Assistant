# Weather and Seasonality

## Broad Timing Logic

- December to April is the easiest window for most users: lower humidity, strong demand, and more predictable beach weather.
- May to August is hotter and more humid but still works well for many beach trips.
- Hurricane season runs from June through November, with higher storm risk later in the season.

## What Season Changes

| Period | What changes |
|--------|--------------|
| Winter peak | Best broad weather, highest pricing, strongest demand |
| Spring shoulder | Good balance for many first trips |
| Summer | Hotter, more humid, family travel, north-coast wind sports logic improves |
| Late summer to fall | Storm flexibility matters more than destination count |

## Special Seasonal Windows

- Samana whale watching is a winter feature, not a year-round promise.
- North-coast wind and kitesurf logic is strongest when the user actually wants wind, not protected calm swim water.
- Mountain add-ons help users who want a break from peak coastal heat.

## Planning Rule

- In hurricane season, choose routes with flexible cancellation logic, fewer fragile transfers, and properties that still work well in mixed weather.
- For short premium trips, pay extra attention to the season fit because one storm-affected day can change the value of the whole trip.

## Rule

For the Dominican Republic, month matters almost as much as destination.
