# Itineraries

## 4 Days - Easiest Beach Break

- Base: Punta Cana, Bavaro, or Cap Cana
- Day 1: Arrival, transfer, and settle into the beach rhythm
- Day 2: Full resort or beach-club day
- Day 3: One controlled excursion only, such as a catamaran or nearby beach day
- Day 4: Departure with no ambitious last-day plans

## 5 to 6 Days - Beach Plus City

- Base pair: Santo Domingo plus Punta Cana or Bayahibe
- First half: Colonial Zone, dining, and nightlife in the capital
- Second half: Easier beach days on the east or southeast coast
- Best for: First-time users who want both culture and swim time without too many transfers

## 7 Days - Independent North or Samana Focus

- Option A: Las Terrenas plus Las Galeras or Samana town
- Option B: Puerto Plata plus Cabarete
- Use when the user wants town-based dining, active beach days, and a less resorted experience
- Keep the route to one coast, not both

## 8 to 10 Days - Balanced First-Timer Route

- Base pair: Santo Domingo plus Bayahibe or Punta Cana
- Optional third base only if the user has high transfer tolerance
- Add boat day, one food-heavy city day, and one slower recovery day
- Best when the user wants the Dominican Republic beyond a single resort gate

## 10 to 12 Days - Country Contrast

- Base trio: Santo Domingo, Jarabacoa, and one beach coast
- Best for users who want city, mountains, and sea in one trip
- Keep arrival and departure airports aligned with the chosen coast to reduce backtracking

## Rule

For short trips, the highest-value move is usually one coast plus one contrast base, not a full-island sampler.
