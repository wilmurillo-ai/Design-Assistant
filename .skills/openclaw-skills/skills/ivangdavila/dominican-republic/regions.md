# Regions

## Think in Coast Types

Dominican Republic is easier to plan when broken into coast and trip identities rather than city names alone.

## 1. Punta Cana, Bavaro, and Cap Cana

- Best for easiest first trip, direct resort logistics, family convenience, and users who want beach time with low planning friction.
- Strong for all-inclusive stays, golf, pool-heavy trips, and short Caribbean holidays.
- Weak fit for users who want the most local, walkable, independently exploratory version of the country.

## 2. Bayahibe and La Romana

- Best for calmer Caribbean water, shorter boat access to Saona or Catalina, diving, and a quieter resort-plus-town balance.
- Strong for couples, divers, and travelers who want less mega-resort sprawl than Bavaro.
- Weak fit if the user wants big nightlife or a fully self-contained east-coast resort machine.

## 3. Santo Domingo

- Best for history, food, nightlife, and travelers who want the country beyond beach branding.
- Strong as a 1-3 night city add-on before or after a coast stay.
- Weak fit as the only stop for users who mainly want postcard beach relaxation.

## 4. Samana, Las Terrenas, and Las Galeras

- Best for independent travelers, greener scenery, beaches with personality, whale season, and a beach-town feel.
- Strong for boutique stays, mixed nature days, and users comfortable with slightly more logistics.
- Weak fit for users who want a frictionless short resort break.

## 5. Puerto Plata, Sosua, and Cabarete

- Best for north-coast energy, kitesurf or surf, active travelers, and mixed beach-town nightlife.
- Strong when the user values wind sports and a more town-based trip over pure resort insulation.
- Weak fit for users who need calm-water certainty or dislike breezier Atlantic conditions.

## 6. Jarabacoa and the Central Highlands

- Best for rafting, mountain air, weekend escapes, and travelers who want a break from beach weather.
- Strong as a contrast add-on with Santo Domingo or the north coast.
- Weak fit for users whose dream trip is mostly sea, boats, and beachfront leisure.

## 7. Southwest and Far South

- Best for repeat visitors, road-trip users, and travelers chasing a less-developed, more remote side of the country.
- Strong for high-commitment nature users.
- Weak fit for first-timers with short trips or anyone who wants easy logistics.

## Pairings That Usually Work

- Punta Cana or Bayahibe plus Santo Domingo
- Santo Domingo plus Jarabacoa
- Samana plus Las Terrenas
- Puerto Plata plus Cabarete

## Pairings That Usually Fail on Short Trips

- Punta Cana plus Samana plus Santo Domingo plus Puerto Plata in one quick holiday
- East-coast resort stay plus same-day deep city detours
- Remote southwest added as a casual side trip
