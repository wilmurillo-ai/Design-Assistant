# Culture — Dominican Republic

The Dominican Republic rewards warmth, flexibility, and basic social awareness. The vibe is generally expressive and social, but good trip advice should turn that into practical expectations.

## What Matters

| Topic | Reality |
|-------|---------|
| Language | Spanish dominates daily life |
| Rhythm | Social, warm, and often more relaxed than rigid visitor schedules |
| Dress | Beachwear belongs at the beach; cities and nicer dinners skew more put-together |
| Music and nightlife | Merengue, bachata, and loud environments are normal in many contexts |

## Etiquette

- Greetings matter.
- A little patience goes further than acting rushed or entitled.
- In city and local settings, dressing with some intention often reads better than staying in full beach mode all day.
- Basic Spanish helps, especially outside resort bubbles.

## Timing Notes

- Heat changes the pace of midday.
- Dinners and evenings can start later than some visitors expect.
- Sundays and holidays can reshape shopping, traffic, and family outing patterns.

## What Outsiders Misread

- Friendly conversation is not the same as formal efficiency.
- Resort behavior and local urban behavior are not identical.
- "Island time" is not an excuse for bad planning, but users should expect a more flexible rhythm than in tightly scripted destinations.
