# Puerto Plata, Cabarete, and Sosua — Dominican Republic

The north coast works best for active travelers who want beaches plus movement, not just passive resort time.

## Best For

- Surf or kite travelers
- Repeat visitors
- Travelers who want a beach base with more day-to-day variation
- Mixed beach and inland add-on routes

## Base Logic

| Base | Best For | Reality |
|------|----------|---------|
| Puerto Plata | Easier arrivals, mixed resort and city access | Good anchor, not the most stylish beach-town feel |
| Cabarete | Wind sports, active scene, beach-town rhythm | Best for movement, not for total calm |
| Sosua | Convenient north-coast base with easy access | More practical than aspirational |

## What the North Coast Does Well

- Wind and activity
- Easier pairing with inland mountain days
- More varied trip texture than a single resort strip

## What It Does Less Well

- Perfectly calm-water beach expectations every day
- Hyper-polished resort consistency
- Very short trips where airport simplicity matters most

## Practical Advice

- Put Cabarete first only if wind sports or active beach life really matter.
- Use Puerto Plata when the user wants an easier anchor with excursions around it.
- Keep expectations honest: this coast is fun because it is varied, not because it feels like a controlled resort postcard.
