# Road Trips and Driving

## When a Car Helps

- Samana peninsula, Jarabacoa, and more remote beach or mountain combinations gain real value from a car.
- North-coast multi-base trips can also justify a rental if the user wants flexibility and is comfortable driving by day.

## When a Car Hurts

- Santo Domingo city stays
- Short Punta Cana resort trips
- Users arriving late and facing a long unfamiliar drive
- Travelers who do not want road ambiguity, parking friction, or toll stops

## Practical Rules

- Prefer daylight driving, especially on first arrival and in mountain or less-developed areas.
- Treat toll roads, speed variation, and local driving style as real friction, not small details.
- Keep fuel and cash/card readiness simple before longer road days.
- If the user only needs one scenic outing, a driver is often smarter than renting for the whole trip.

## Best Use Cases

- Santo Domingo to Jarabacoa with flexible mountain stops
- North-coast trip mixing Puerto Plata, Sosua, and Cabarete
- Samana-area beaches where the user wants freedom to move between bases

## Rule

Recommend a car only when it improves the full trip more than it increases risk.
