# North Bucharest — Floreasca, Dorobanți, Primăverii, Herăstrău

## Floreasca

### Overview
The "it" neighborhood of Bucharest. Once industrial, now home to the best restaurants, trendy bars, and young professionals. Think Brooklyn or Shoreditch vibes, but with more parking.

### The Vibe
- **Day**: Brunch crowds, fitness people, workers
- **Night**: Best restaurant scene in city
- **Character**: Trendy, upscale, energetic

### Living Here

| Aspect | Rating | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Walkability | ★★★★☆ | Good within area |
| Nightlife | ★★★★☆ | Quality over quantity |
| Restaurants | ★★★★★ | Best in Bucharest |
| Groceries | ★★★★★ | Mega Image, plus gourmet |
| Quiet | ★★★☆☆ | Busy streets, quieter sides |
| Safety | ★★★★★ | Very safe |
| Value | ★★★☆☆ | You pay for trendy |

### Rent
- **Studio**: €550-800
- **1BR**: €700-1,200
- **2BR**: €1,000-1,800

### Who Lives Here
- Young professionals
- Tech workers
- Entrepreneurs
- Fitness enthusiasts
- Foodies

### Key Spots
- **Floreasca Lake**: Small park area
- **Promenada Mall**: Shopping, dining
- **Bucharest Tower**: Landmark building
- **Restaurant row**: Bulevardul Barbu Văcărescu

### Pros
- Best food scene in Bucharest
- Young, dynamic atmosphere
- Safe and clean
- Good gyms and fitness
- Near park access

### Cons
- Expensive
- Can feel "bubble-ish"
- Traffic on main streets
- Limited historic charm

### Verdict
**Great for**: Foodies, young professionals, those who want "modern Bucharest"
**Avoid if**: Budget-conscious, want traditional atmosphere

## Dorobanți

### Overview
Old-money Bucharest. Tree-lined streets, embassies, expensive villas. More established than Floreasca, less flashy. The neighborhood where Romanian elites have lived for decades.

### The Vibe
- **Day**: Quiet, diplomatic cars, dog walkers
- **Night**: Very quiet, residential
- **Character**: Elegant, discreet, wealthy

### Living Here

| Aspect | Rating | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Walkability | ★★★★☆ | Pleasant but spread out |
| Nightlife | ★★☆☆☆ | Go to Floreasca |
| Restaurants | ★★★★☆ | Quality, not quantity |
| Groceries | ★★★★☆ | Good options |
| Quiet | ★★★★★ | Very peaceful |
| Safety | ★★★★★ | Safest area |
| Value | ★★☆☆☆ | Premium prices |

### Rent
- **Studio**: €600-900
- **1BR**: €800-1,500
- **2BR**: €1,200-2,500

### Who Lives Here
- Diplomats
- Business executives
- Old Romanian families
- Embassy staff
- Wealthy expats

### Key Landmarks
- Multiple embassies
- Zambaccian Museum
- High-end villas
- Dorobanți Church

### Pros
- Extremely safe
- Beautiful architecture
- Quiet and prestigious
- Near Floreasca dining
- Green streets

### Cons
- Very expensive
- Can feel isolated
- Limited nightlife
- "Established" can mean stuffy

### Verdict
**Great for**: Executives, diplomats, families wanting prestige
**Avoid if**: Young and social, budget matters

## Primăverii

### Overview
The most exclusive residential area in Bucharest. Where Ceaușescu's government officials lived, now home to diplomats, billionaires, and elite. Villa district with security and privacy.

### The Vibe
- **Day**: Gardeners, security guards, luxury cars
- **Night**: Silent, private
- **Character**: Elite, gated, discreet

### Living Here

| Aspect | Rating | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Walkability | ★★★☆☆ | Residential only |
| Nightlife | ☆☆☆☆☆ | None |
| Restaurants | ★★☆☆☆ | Very limited local |
| Groceries | ★★★☆☆ | Need to drive |
| Quiet | ★★★★★ | Dead quiet |
| Safety | ★★★★★ | Highest security |
| Value | ★☆☆☆☆ | Ultra-premium |

### Rent
- **Apartments rare**: Mostly villas
- **1BR** (if available): €1,000-1,500
- **Villas**: €3,000-10,000+

### Who Lives Here
- Billionaires
- Ambassadors
- Top politicians
- Oligarchs
- A-list celebrities (Romania)

### Key Features
- Former Ceaușescu residence (museum)
- Herastrău Park edge
- Ultra-security presence
- Massive villas with gardens

### Pros
- Ultimate privacy
- Ultimate safety
- Beautiful properties
- Park access

### Cons
- Isolating
- No local amenities
- Need car for everything
- Unaffordable for most

### Verdict
**Great for**: Ultra-wealthy, diplomats, those needing maximum security
**Avoid if**: You're not in the 0.1%

## Herăstrău / Aviatorilor

### Overview
The neighborhoods surrounding Bucharest's largest park. Mix of residential buildings, offices, and park lifestyle. Popular with fitness enthusiasts and families who want green space.

### The Vibe
- **Day**: Runners, cyclists, families in park
- **Night**: Quiet residential, some restaurants
- **Character**: Active, green, balanced

### Living Here

| Aspect | Rating | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Walkability | ★★★★☆ | Park is amazing |
| Nightlife | ★★☆☆☆ | Some restaurants near park |
| Restaurants | ★★★☆☆ | Park restaurants + nearby |
| Groceries | ★★★★☆ | Good supermarkets |
| Quiet | ★★★★☆ | Peaceful |
| Safety | ★★★★★ | Very safe |
| Value | ★★★☆☆ | Medium-high |

### Rent
- **Studio**: €500-700
- **1BR**: €600-900
- **2BR**: €900-1,400

### Who Lives Here
- Fitness enthusiasts
- Families
- Professionals who value work-life balance
- Dog owners
- Nature lovers

### Key Features
- **Herăstrău Park**: 187 hectares, lake, running paths
- **Village Museum**: In the park
- **Boat rentals**: On the lake
- **Cycling paths**: Throughout

### Aviatorilor Specifics
- Named after Aviation Square
- Near museums (Village, contemporary art)
- Good metro access (M2)
- More residential than park edge

### Pros
- Best park access in Bucharest
- Great for fitness and outdoor life
- Safe and clean
- Good metro connection
- Balanced lifestyle

### Cons
- Far from center action
- Park can get crowded weekends
- Some areas feel impersonal
- Winter is very quiet

### Verdict
**Great for**: Active lifestyle, families, nature lovers, dog owners
**Avoid if**: Want nightlife, prefer urban energy
