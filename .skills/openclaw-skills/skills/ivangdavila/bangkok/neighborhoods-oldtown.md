# Old Town, Chinatown & Rattanakosin

## Rattanakosin (Old Town)

### Overview
- **Historic heart**: Grand Palace, major temples, original Bangkok
- **Character**: Tourist attractions, backpackers, traditional Bangkok
- **Who lives here**: Backpackers, temple enthusiasts, some long-term expats

### Pros
- Walking distance to all major temples
- Historic atmosphere
- Cheapest area to stay
- Authentic old Bangkok feeling
- River access
- Best areas for photography

### Cons
- **No BTS/MRT** — dependent on taxis, tuk-tuks, boats
- Tourist-trap restaurants
- Can feel isolated from modern Bangkok
- Limited modern amenities

### Key Spots
- **Grand Palace / Wat Phra Kaew**: Thailand's most sacred site
- **Wat Pho**: Reclining Buddha
- **Wat Arun**: Across the river
- **Sanam Luang**: Royal field, ceremony site
- **National Museum**: Thai history/art
- **Thammasat University**: Historic campus

### Getting There
- **Boat**: Chao Phraya Express to Tha Chang or Tha Tien
- **Taxi/Grab**: 30-60 min from Sukhumvit depending on traffic
- **MRT**: Blue line to Sanam Chai (nearest, still 10 min walk)

### Rent
- **Guesthouses**: ฿300-800/night
- **Apartments**: ฿8,000-18,000/month (limited options)
- Mostly short-term accommodation

## Khao San Road Area

### Overview
- **Backpacker mecca**: Budget accommodation, party street
- **Character**: Chaotic, cheap, young travelers, bucket drinks
- **Who lives here**: Gap year travelers, party backpackers

### Pros
- Cheapest beds in Bangkok
- Social atmosphere
- Walk to temples
- Travel agencies for onwards trips
- 24/7 energy

### Cons
- Loud, chaotic, party noise until 4 AM
- Tourist-trap pricing on food
- Far from anywhere by transit
- Not authentic Bangkok
- Can feel like a theme park

### Key Spots
- **Khao San Road**: Main strip
- **Rambuttri Road**: Slightly calmer parallel street
- **Phra Athit Road**: Nicer bars along the river
- **Brick Bar**: Decent music venue
- **Roof Bar**: Views over the chaos

### Who It's For
- 20-somethings on a budget
- Solo travelers wanting to meet people
- First-night-in-Bangkok transition
- Temple-focused visitors

### Who Should Avoid
- Anyone over 30
- Families
- People wanting sleep
- Digital nomads (no WiFi café culture)

### Rent
- **Dorm beds**: ฿150-300/night
- **Private rooms**: ฿400-1,000/night
- **Guesthouses**: ฿500-1,500/night

## Chinatown (Yaowarat)

### Overview
- **Best street food in Bangkok**: Period.
- **Character**: Old Chinese district, gold shops, traditional commerce
- **Who lives here**: Chinese-Thai families (generations), food lovers

### Pros
- **Food**: The main attraction — seafood, dim sum, roasted meats
- Authentic old Bangkok atmosphere
- Gold shops (if you're buying gold)
- Night photography paradise
- MRT access now (Wat Mangkon station)

### Cons
- Dirty, crowded, chaotic
- Daytime is less interesting
- Limited accommodation
- Very hot (narrow streets, no breeze)

### Key Spots
- **Yaowarat Road**: Main strip, neon lights at night
- **Soi Texas (Soi Phadungdao)**: Seafood heaven
- **T&K Seafood**: Famous seafood restaurant
- **Wat Traimit**: Golden Buddha
- **Sampeng Lane**: Wholesale market
- **Talat Noi**: Hipster cafés meeting old Chinatown

### Best Time to Visit
- **6 PM - midnight**: When it comes alive
- Daytime is just regular commerce (gold, wholesale)

### Street Food Must-Trys
- **Pad Thai at Thip Samai** (technically nearby)
- **T&K Seafood**: Tom yum, crab
- **Yaowarat roasted duck/pork**
- **Bird's nest soup** (expensive)
- **Mango sticky rice stalls**
- **Dim sum** (morning only)

### Rent
- **Limited options**: ฿10,000-22,000/month
- Better as a food destination than living area

## Talat Noi

### Overview
- **Chinatown's hipster pocket**: Heritage buildings, cafés, street art
- **Character**: Old shophouses converted to cafés and galleries
- **Who lives here**: Artists, café owners, heritage enthusiasts

### Pros
- Best street art in Bangkok
- Excellent cafés in heritage buildings
- Walk to Chinatown food
- Authentic atmosphere
- Near MRT Hua Lamphong

### Cons
- Very small area
- Limited accommodation
- Hot walking

### Key Spots
- **Soy Sauce Factory**: Heritage café
- **Here Hai**: Popular café
- **Street art walks**: Alex Face murals
- **Hong Sieng Kong**: Shrine and atmosphere

---

## Pahurat (Little India)

### Overview
- **Indian district**: Fabrics, Indian food, Sikh temple
- **Character**: Textile markets, samosas, chai
- **Who lives here**: Indian-Thai community

### Adjacent to Chinatown
- Walk from Yaowarat
- MRT Hua Lamphong or Sam Yot

### Key Spots
- **Pahurat Market**: Fabric wholesale
- **Gurdwara Siri Guru Singh Sabha**: Sikh temple (free meals)
- **Royal India**: Oldest Indian restaurant
- **ATM Tea Corner**: Cheap excellent chai

## Nang Loeng Market Area

### Overview
- **Forgotten Bangkok**: Old neighborhood tourists miss
- **Character**: 100-year-old market, local life
- **Who lives here**: Old Bangkok families

### Why Visit
- Most authentic Bangkok experience possible
- Zero tourists
- Incredible street food
- Walking historic streets

### Key Spots
- **Nang Loeng Market**: Morning market, unchanged for decades
- **Old shophouses**: Photography heaven
- **Local noodle shops**: Some of Bangkok's best

---

## Living in Old Town: Verdict

### For Short Stays (1-7 days)
- ✅ Temple-focused visitors
- ✅ First Bangkok visit wanting culture
- ✅ Budget backpackers
- ✅ Photographers

### For Long Stays (1+ month)
- ❌ Digital nomads (no cafés, no transit)
- ❌ Nightlife seekers
- ❌ Anyone needing BTS/MRT
- ❌ Modern amenity dependent

### Compromise
Stay in Sukhumvit/Silom, day trip to Old Town
- Take boat for temple days
- Return to modern Bangkok for sleep
