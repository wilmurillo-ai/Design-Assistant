# Silom, Sathorn & Riverside

## Silom (BTS Sala Daeng / MRT Silom)

### Overview
- **Bangkok's CBD**: Office towers, banks, business lunches
- **Character**: Day = corporate. Night = Patpong, LGBT scene.
- **Who lives here**: Business travelers, office workers, LGBT community

### Pros
- Central business district access
- Walk to Lumphini Park
- BTS + MRT connection
- Excellent street food at lunch
- LGBT-friendly (Soi 2, Soi 4)

### Cons
- Dead on weekends
- Expensive for what you get
- Patpong area is sleazy

### Key Spots
- **Silom Complex**: Office mall
- **Lumphini Park**: Bangkok's central park
- **Patpong Night Market**: Tourist market + red light
- **Soi 2 & 4**: LGBT bars, clubs (DJ Station, G Bangkok)
- **Vertigo**: Famous rooftop bar (Banyan Tree)

### Rent
- **1BR condo**: ฿25,000-50,000/month
- **Serviced apartments**: ฿40,000-80,000/month

### LGBT Scene
Silom Soi 2 and Soi 4 are Bangkok's main gay entertainment streets:
- **DJ Station**: Biggest gay club
- **G Bangkok**: Popular club
- **Telephone Pub**: Classic bar
- **Maggie Choo's**: Glam bar (mixed)

## Sathorn

### Overview
- **Upscale CBD extension**: Embassies, luxury condos, corporate HQs
- **Character**: Bangkok's most "international" feeling area
- **Who lives here**: Executives, embassy staff, wealthy expats

### Pros
- High-end condos with views
- BTS Chong Nonsi, Sathorn
- Near Lumphini Park
- Upscale restaurants and hotels
- Some of Bangkok's best rooftop bars

### Cons
- Expensive rent
- Can feel soulless/corporate
- Limited local character

### Key Spots
- **W Bangkok**: Hip hotel/bar
- **Sathorn Square**: Office complex
- **Embassies**: Australian, Irish, nearby
- **The House on Sathorn**: Heritage restaurant
- **Saffron**: Rooftop fine dining

### Rent
- **1BR condo**: ฿28,000-60,000/month
- **Luxury condos**: ฿60,000-200,000/month

---

## Lumphini Area

### Overview
- **Park-adjacent**: Bangkok's only real green space
- **Character**: Upscale, quiet, morning joggers, monitor lizards
- **Who lives here**: Health-conscious expats, families wanting green space

### Pros
- Lumphini Park access (58 hectares)
- Morning exercise culture
- Monitor lizards to watch
- Paddle boats on lake
- MRT + BTS access

### Cons
- Premium prices
- Not walkable to much else
- Can be boring

### Park Activities
- **5-6 AM**: Tai chi, aerobics classes
- **6-8 AM**: Jogging, walking
- **Evening**: Cooler walking, exercise
- **Weekend**: Paddle boats, picnics

### Rent
- **1BR condo (park view)**: ฿35,000-70,000/month

---

## Chong Nonsi (BTS Chong Nonsi)

### Overview
- **Office tower central**: Between Silom and Sathorn
- **Character**: Very corporate, modern high-rises
- **Who lives here**: Office workers, business travelers

### Pros
- Modern condo buildings
- Quick walk to both Silom and Sathorn offices
- Some good restaurants below towers

### Cons
- No neighborhood feel
- Dead at night and weekends
- Just office buildings

### Rent
- **1BR condo**: ฿22,000-45,000/month

---

## Riverside (BTS Saphan Taksin + boats)

### Overview
- **Luxury hotel strip**: Mandarin Oriental, Peninsula, Shangri-La
- **Character**: Tourist riverside, upscale hotels, Asiatique
- **Who lives here**: Luxury seekers, short-term visitors

### Pros
- Stunning river views
- World-class hotels
- Boat access to temples
- Asiatique night market
- Romantic dinner options

### Cons
- Isolated from rest of Bangkok
- Depends on boats for old town
- Very tourist-facing
- Expensive

### Key Spots
- **Mandarin Oriental**: The legendary hotel
- **Peninsula Bangkok**: Classic luxury
- **Asiatique**: Night market/mall
- **Supanniga Eating Room**: Excellent Thai riverside
- **Icon Siam**: Mega mall with amazing food court

### Getting Around
- **Chao Phraya Express boats**: Orange flag to Grand Palace area
- **Free hotel boats**: Many 5-stars have shuttles to BTS
- **BTS Saphan Taksin**: Connection point

### Rent
- **1BR condo**: ฿30,000-80,000/month
- **Riverside luxury**: ฿80,000-200,000/month

---

## Icon Siam Area

### Overview
- **Newest mega development**: Opened 2018
- **Character**: Massive mall + luxury condos
- **Who lives here**: New wealthy residents

### Icon Siam Mall
- Best food hall in Bangkok (Sook Siam)
- Floating market inside mall
- Luxury brands
- Free boat from BTS Saphan Taksin

### Rent (The Residences at Mandarin Oriental)
- **1BR**: ฿100,000-300,000/month
- Ultra-luxury territory

## Silom vs Sathorn vs Sukhumvit

| Factor | Silom | Sathorn | Sukhumvit |
|--------|-------|---------|-----------|
| **Vibe** | Corporate + LGBT | Upscale corporate | Young expats + diverse |
| **Transit** | BTS + MRT | BTS | BTS (best coverage) |
| **Food** | Lunch spots, Silom Soi 20 | Hotel restaurants | Everything |
| **Nightlife** | LGBT scene, Patpong | Hotel bars | Best selection |
| **Rent** | ฿25-50K | ฿28-60K | ฿15-55K |
| **Best for** | Business, LGBT | Executives | Everyone else |

## Silom Street Food Spots

### Silom Soi 20
- **When**: Lunch only (11 AM - 2 PM)
- **What**: Massive lunch market for office workers
- **Must try**: Khao man gai, boat noodles, fried rice

### Convent Road
- **When**: Lunch
- **What**: Upscale street food
- **Vibe**: Slightly pricier, cleaner

### Sala Daeng / Silom Road
- **When**: Evening
- **What**: Night food stalls appear
- **Warning**: More tourist-priced
