# Bangkok International & Fine Dining

## International Cuisine Scene

Bangkok's expat population has created excellent international food options at every price point.

## Japanese

Bangkok has the best Japanese food outside Japan.

### Why So Good
- Large Japanese expat community (100,000+)
- Direct imports from Tsukiji/Toyosu markets
- Japanese chains and independent restaurants

### Where to Go

**High-End**
- **Sushi Masato** (Sukhumvit 31) — Omakase, reservations essential
- **Ginza Sushi Ichi** (Erawan) — Tokyo import, Michelin
- **Isao** (Sukhumvit 31) — Kappo-style

**Mid-Range**
- **Sushi Hiro** (Sukhumvit 53) — Great value omakase
- **Kensaku** (Silom) — Izakaya favorite
- **Bankara Ramen** (multiple) — Rich tonkotsu

**Budget**
- **Ootoya** (malls) — Set meals, quality chain
- **Fuji** (malls) — Reliable chain
- **Food courts** — Sashimi, donburi surprisingly good

### Price Range
- **Omakase**: ฿2,500-10,000+
- **Mid-range dinner**: ฿500-1,500
- **Casual**: ฿200-500

## Indian

Strong South Asian community means excellent Indian food.

### Best Areas
- **Sukhumvit Soi 3 (Soi Arab)**: Arab/Indian mix
- **Pahurat (Little India)**: Traditional Indian
- **Silom**: Upscale Indian options

### Restaurants
- **Gaggan Anand** — 2 Michelin stars, progressive Indian (฿6,000+)
- **Indus** (Sukhumvit 26) — Upscale North Indian
- **Dosa King** (Sukhumvit 11) — South Indian dosas
- **Mrs. Balbir's** (Sukhumvit 11) — Classic North Indian
- **Royal India** (Pahurat) — Old-school, massive portions

### Price Range
- **Upscale**: ฿800-2,000
- **Mid-range**: ฿250-600
- **Pahurat stalls**: ฿80-150

## Middle Eastern

### Best Areas
- **Sukhumvit Soi 3-11**: Arab Quarter
- **Nana area**: Concentrated options

### What's Good
- **Shawarma**: ฿80-150
- **Hummus, falafel**: Ubiquitous
- **Lebanese**: Full mezze

### Restaurants
- **Nasir Al-Masri** (Soi 3/1) — Institution, 24/7
- **Beirut** (Soi 3) — Upscale Lebanese
- **Al Hussain** (Soi 3) — Good value Egyptian

## Chinese

Bangkok has significant Chinese heritage, but "Chinese food" is often Thai-ified.

### Cantonese/Dim Sum
- **Man Ho** (JW Marriott) — Upscale Cantonese
- **Mei Jiang** (Peninsula) — Elegant
- **Canton House** (Ambassador) — Classic

### Chinatown
- **T&K Seafood** — Must-visit
- **Shark fin restaurants**: Yaowarat Road (ethical concerns noted)
- **Dim sum shops**: Morning only

## Korean

## Fine Dining (Michelin & Beyond)

### 2+ Stars
- **Gaggan Anand** (2★) — Progressive Indian
- **Le Normandie** (2★) — French classic
- **Sühring** (2★) — German fine dining
- **R-Haan** (2★) — Royal Thai

### 1 Star Notable
- **Jay Fai** (1★) — Street food legend, crab omelette
- **Sorn** (1★) — Southern Thai
- **Paste** (1★) — Modern Thai

### Worth the Splurge (No Stars)
- **Nahm** — David Thompson's Thai (was starred)
- **Bo.Lan** — Sustainable Thai
- **Issaya Siamese Club** — Thai in heritage house

### Reservations
- Book 2-4 weeks ahead for starred restaurants
- Jay Fai: Join waiting list, expect 2-3 hour wait
- Smart casual dress code minimum

## Rooftop Dining

### With Views
- **Vertigo** (Banyan Tree) — Outdoor, dramatic
- **Sirocco** (Lebua) — Hangover 2 famous
- **Breeze** (Lebua) — Asian seafood
- **Char** (Hotel Indigo) — Steakhouse

### Tips
- Dress code: No shorts, sandals, sleeveless
- Sunset arrival recommended
- Prices 2-3x ground level
