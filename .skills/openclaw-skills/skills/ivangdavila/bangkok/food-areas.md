# Best Food Areas in Bangkok

## Chinatown (Yaowarat) — Best Street Food

### When to Go
- **Evening (6 PM - midnight)**: Prime time, stalls fully open
- **Weekends**: Most vibrant
- **Avoid**: Daytime (just regular shops)

### Getting There
- **MRT**: Wat Mangkon station (direct into Chinatown)
- **MRT**: Hua Lamphong station (10-min walk)

### Must-Try Stalls

**Soi Texas (Phadungdao)**
- Seafood heaven
- Grilled shellfish, prawns
- ฿100-400 per portion

**T&K Seafood**
- Institution, red plastic chairs
- Tom yum, crab, prawns
- ฿200-500 per person

**Yaowarat Toasted Bread**
- Late-night staple
- Condensed milk and pandan custard

**Nai Ek Roll Noodles**
- Rolled rice noodles
- Quick, cheap, delicious

**Guay Jub Ouan Pochana**
- Rolled noodle soup
- Peppery pork broth

### Tips
- Walk the main Yaowarat Road first
- Duck into sois for less touristy options
- Share dishes to try more
- Expect standing/plastic stool eating
- Budget: ฿200-500 for feast

## Sukhumvit Soi 38 — Night Food Street

### When to Go
- **Evening (6 PM - midnight)**
- Some stalls open until 2-3 AM

### Getting There
- **BTS**: Thong Lo station, walk to Soi 38

### What's There
- Pad thai
- Khao man gai
- Seafood
- Isaan food
- Desserts

### Vibe
- Slightly touristy but quality maintained
- Good intro to Bangkok street food
- More expensive than local streets (฿60-120)

---

## Silom Soi 20 — Office Worker Lunch

### When to Go
- **Lunch only (11 AM - 2 PM)**
- Closes after lunch rush

### Getting There
- **BTS**: Chong Nonsi
- Walk into Soi 20

### What's There
- Every Thai dish imaginable
- Made-to-order stalls
- Massive variety in small space

### Vibe
- Authentic office worker canteen
- Fast, efficient, cheap (฿40-70)
- No English, point and nod

---

## Victory Monument — Boat Noodles

### What's Special
- Boat noodle capital of Bangkok
- Small bowls (฿15-25 each)
- Stack bowls to count
- Eat 5-15 bowls

### When to Go
- All day, but lunch/dinner peak

### Getting There
- **BTS**: Victory Monument

### Notable Spots
- **Boat Noodle Alley**: Walk around the monument
- Multiple stalls with slight variations

---

## Ari — Hipster Food Scene

### When to Go
- **All day**: Cafés morning, restaurants evening

### Getting There
- **BTS**: Ari

### Vibe
- Thai hipster paradise
- Great cafés (Casa Lapin, Ceresia)
- Local restaurants at local prices
- Less English but friendly

### Good For
- Coffee + brunch
- Authentic Thai without tourists
- Budget dining

---

## Thonglor — Expat Food Hub

### When to Go
- Evening prime, but cafés all day

### Getting There
- **BTS**: Thong Lo

### What's There
- Every cuisine imaginable
- Excellent Japanese cluster
- High-end Thai
- Cafés everywhere

### Notable Streets
- **Thonglor main road**: Restaurants
- **Sukhumvit Soi 55**: Thonglor itself
- **Thonglor Soi 9-11**: Restaurant concentration

### Budget
- Mid to high (฿200-800 per meal typical)

---

## Pratunam — 24/7 Food

### What's Special
- Never closes
- Cheap, local, authentic
- Post-clubbing destination

### When to Go
- Anytime — literally 24/7

### Getting There
- **BTS**: Ratchathewi, then walk
- Or taxi to Pratunam area

### Must-Try
- **Pratunam Chicken Rice** (Soi 30)
- Best khao man gai in Bangkok debate
- Pink shirt, green sauce

---

## Or Tor Kor Market — Premium Food Market

### What's Special
- Highest quality produce in Thailand
- Prepared foods section
- Air-conditioned
- Clean, organized

### When to Go
- **Morning - afternoon**
- Best 8 AM - 2 PM

### Getting There
- **BTS**: Mo Chit
- **MRT**: Kamphaeng Phet

### What to Get
- Tropical fruits (best in city)
- Prepared Thai dishes (฿80-150)
- Fresh seafood
- Regional specialties

---

## Train Market Ratchada — Night Market Food

### When to Go
- **Evening (5 PM - midnight)**
- Thursday-Sunday

### Getting There
- **MRT**: Thailand Cultural Centre (Exit 3)

### What's There
- Hundreds of food stalls
- Instagram-famous colored tents
- Mix of Thai and fusion
- Drinks, desserts

### Vibe
- Touristy but fun
- Great for photos
- Slightly inflated prices (฿80-150)

---

## Chatuchak Weekend Market — Market Food

### When to Go
- **Saturday-Sunday only**
- 9 AM - 6 PM

### Getting There
- **BTS**: Mo Chit
- **MRT**: Kamphaeng Phet / Chatuchak Park

### Food Spots
- Section 2-3: Basic food stalls
- Section 23-26: More food options
- **Viva 8**: Famous for coconut ice cream
- Multiple cafés inside

### Tips
- Eat early before crowds peak
- Hydrate (it's hot)
- Or Tor Kor next door is better for food

---

## Bang Rak / Charoen Krung — Old Bangkok Eating

### What's Special
- Historic district, heritage buildings
- Mix of old Chinese, Thai, international
- Hipster bars and cafés arriving

### When to Go
- Evening for street food
- Daytime for cafés

### Getting There
- **BTS**: Saphan Taksin
- Walk along Charoen Krung

### Notable Spots
- **Jua**: Excellent Thai, Michelin-recommended
- **El Chiringuito**: Spanish wine bar
- **80/20**: Modern Thai, tasting menu
- **Heritage restaurants**: Colonial-era atmosphere
