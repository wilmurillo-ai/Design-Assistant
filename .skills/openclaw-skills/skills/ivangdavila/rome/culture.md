# Culture & Entertainment — Rome

## Overview

Rome is a **living museum** with 3,000 years of cultural history, but it's also a modern city with active arts, music, and entertainment scenes.

---

## Museums & Galleries

### Major Museums

**Vatican Museums**
- One of world's greatest collections
- Sistine Chapel, Raphael Rooms
- Book ahead: weeks/months
- Friday nights less crowded (summer)
- EUR 17-25

**Borghese Gallery**
- Bernini sculptures, Caravaggio
- Mandatory timed reservation
- Book 2+ weeks ahead
- 2-hour slots
- EUR 15

**Capitoline Museums**
- World's oldest public museum
- Roman sculptures, paintings
- Piazza designed by Michelangelo
- EUR 15

**National Roman Museum**
- Four locations (Palazzo Massimo best)
- Roman antiquities
- Often overlooked by tourists
- EUR 12 (all four)

### Modern & Contemporary

**MAXXI**
- Contemporary art and architecture
- Zaha Hadid building
- Rotating exhibitions
- EUR 12

**MACRO**
- Municipal contemporary art
- Two locations
- Edgier exhibitions
- EUR 10

**Centrale Montemartini**
- Ancient sculpture in power plant
- Unique juxtaposition
- Usually empty
- EUR 10

### Free Options
- **Pantheon**: Always free
- **Churches**: All free (donations welcome)
- **First Sunday**: State museums free monthly
- **Piazzas and fountains**: Free outdoor museum

## Performing Arts

### Opera

**Teatro dell'Opera di Roma**
- Main opera house
- Season: November-June
- Summer: Baths of Caracalla (spectacular)
- Tickets: EUR 25-200
- Book ahead for popular productions

### Classical Music

**Auditorium Parco della Musica**
- Renzo Piano design
- Orchestra dell'Accademia Nazionale di Santa Cecilia
- Various concert series
- Modern facility
- EUR 20-80

**Churches**
- Many free or cheap concerts
- Baroque settings
- Quality varies, check reviews
- Beautiful acoustics often

### Theater

**Teatro Argentina**
- Historic theater
- Italian productions
- Limited English
- Beautiful venue

**Teatro Sistina**
- Musical productions
- Italian language
- Popular shows

**English Theater**
- Limited options
- Miracle Players (English)
- Some expat productions

## Annual Events

### Spring
- **Easter**: Vatican ceremonies
- **Rome's Birthday** (April 21): Celebrations
- **May Day**: Concert at San Giovanni
- **Open House Roma**: Architecture openings

### Summer
- **Estate Romana**: City-wide festival
- **Opera at Caracalla**: Outdoor opera
- **Outdoor cinema**: Various locations
- **Gay Village**: LGBTQ+ festival

### Fall
- **Rome Film Fest**: October
- **RomaEuropa Festival**: Performing arts
- **Cultural season begins**: Theater, opera

### Winter
- **Christmas markets**: Piazza Navona
- **New Year**: Circus Maximus concert
- **Epiphany** (Jan 6): Piazza Navona fair
- **Carnevale**: February (smaller than Venice)

## Sports

### Football (Calcio)

## Practical Tips

### Tickets
- Book museums ahead (especially Vatican, Borghese)
- Opera/concerts: official sites best
- Football: be careful of resellers
- Free Sundays: arrive early (crowded)

### Timing
- Many museums closed Monday
- Churches closed midday (12-3pm often)
- Nightlife starts late (11pm+)
- Dinner theater after 21:00

### Dress
- Churches: covered shoulders/knees
- Opera: smart casual to formal
- Clubs: depends on venue
- Italians dress up
