# Visas & Immigration — Rome/Italy

## Overview

Italy is part of the **Schengen Area**. Visa requirements depend on your nationality, purpose, and duration.

### Quick Check

| Nationality | Duration | Purpose | Need Visa? |
|-------------|----------|---------|------------|
| EU/EEA/Swiss | Any | Any | No (free movement) |
| US/UK/Canada/Aus/NZ | ≤90 days | Tourism | No |
| US/UK/Canada/Aus/NZ | >90 days | Any | Yes |
| Most others | Any | Any | Usually yes |

---

## Tourist Visits (≤90 Days)

### Schengen Rule
- 90 days within any 180-day period
- Applies to ALL Schengen countries combined
- Not 90 days per country

### Visa-Exempt Countries (Tourism)
- USA, Canada, Australia, New Zealand
- UK (post-Brexit)
- Japan, South Korea, Singapore
- Many Latin American countries
- Check MFA website for full list

### Requirements
- Passport valid 3+ months beyond stay
- Return/onward ticket
- Proof of accommodation
- Proof of funds (€50-100/day)
- Travel insurance recommended

### At Border
- Immigration may ask questions
- Have accommodation proof ready
- Rarely problematic for Western tourists
- Stamp in passport (check you got it)

## Longer Stays (>90 Days)

### You Need a Visa

For stays over 90 days, you must apply for a **national visa (Type D)** BEFORE arriving in Italy.

### Common Visa Types

**Elective Residence Visa**
- For people with passive income
- Retirement, remote work (for non-Italian company)
- Requires proof of income (~EUR 31,000/year single)
- No work for Italian employers
- Popular with retirees, digital nomads

**Student Visa**
- Enrolled in Italian institution
- University, language school, etc.
- Duration matches course
- Can work limited hours

**Work Visa**
- Employer-sponsored
- Requires job offer from Italian company
- Quota system (limited numbers)
- Complex process

**Family Visa**
- Joining Italian/EU family member
- Spouse, dependent children
- Different from other visa types

**Self-Employment Visa**
- Starting business in Italy
- Freelancers technically here
- Complex requirements
- Need to show business plan, funds

## Digital Nomad Considerations

### Current Situation (2024-2026)
- Italy introduced Digital Nomad Visa (2024)
- Allows remote work for non-Italian companies
- Requirements still being clarified
- Income threshold: ~EUR 28,000/year
- Health insurance required

### Alternative: Elective Residence
- Technically for "passive" income
- Some nomads use this
- Gray area — not "working" in Italy
- Consult immigration lawyer

### Reality Check
- Many nomads stay on tourist (≤90 days)
- Reset clock in non-Schengen country
- Not technically legal long-term
- Risk: entry denial, fines

## EU/EEA Citizens

### Your Rights
- Free movement within EU
- No visa needed
- Can live and work freely
- Register residence after 3 months

### Registration
- Go to Comune (municipality)
- Register as resident
- Get Codice Fiscale (tax code)
- Access Italian services

### UK Post-Brexit
- Now treated as non-EU
- 90-day tourist rule applies
- Need visa for longer stays
- Settled UK residents pre-2021: different rules

## The Permesso di Soggiorno

### What It Is
- Residence permit
- Required for stays >90 days
- Different from visa (visa gets you in, permesso lets you stay)

### Process
1. Enter Italy with correct visa
2. Apply for Permesso within 8 days
3. Kit at post office (Poste Italiane)
4. Appointment at Questura
5. Wait (weeks to months)
6. Collect card

### Timeline Reality
- Appointment: weeks to months wait
- Processing: weeks to months
- Receipt valid while waiting
- Renewals: start 60 days before expiry

### Documents Needed
- Application form
- Passport + copies
- Visa + copies
- Passport photos
- Proof of accommodation
- Proof of income/funds
- Health insurance
- Marca da bollo (tax stamp)
- Others depending on type

---

## Codice Fiscale

### What It Is
- Italian tax identification number
- 16-character code
- Needed for almost everything

### What You Need It For
- Opening bank account
- Signing rental contract
- Healthcare registration
- Employment
- Utilities
- Phone contract
- Many purchases

### How to Get It
- Agenzia delle Entrate (tax office)
- Italian consulate (before arrival)
- Some can apply online
- Free, same day usually
- Bring passport

---

## Healthcare Access

### EU Citizens
- European Health Insurance Card (EHIC)
- Access to emergency care
- Register with SSN for full access

### Non-EU with Permesso
- Can register with SSN (national health)
- Need Permesso and Codice Fiscale
- Annual fee: ~EUR 400 if not employed/student

### Tourists
- Travel insurance recommended
- Emergency care available
- Must pay without insurance
- Private healthcare accessible

---

## Residenza (Legal Residence)

### What It Is
- Official registration with Comune
- Different from Permesso (immigration status)
- Required after 3 months
- Needed for many services

### Why You Need It
- Full SSN access
- Voting (EU citizens)
- Driver's license
- Various bureaucratic needs
- Proof of address

### Process
- Apply at Comune
- Vigili (municipal police) may verify
- Visits your address
- Adds to registry
- Takes weeks

---

## Common Mistakes

### Don't
- Overstay your visa/90 days
- Work on tourist visa
- Forget to apply for Permesso on time
- Assume rules are loosely enforced
- Lose your receipt while waiting

### Do
- Keep all documents
- Make copies of everything
- Apply for Codice Fiscale early
- Get health insurance
- Consult a lawyer for complex cases
- Start bureaucracy EARLY
