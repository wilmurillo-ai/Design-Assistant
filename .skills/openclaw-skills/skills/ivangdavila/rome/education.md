# Education — Rome

## Overview

Rome has extensive educational options from international schools to prestigious universities. Language is the key consideration for most expats.

---

## School System Structure

### Italian System

| Level | Ages | Duration |
|-------|------|----------|
| Asilo nido | 0-3 | 3 years |
| Scuola materna (Infanzia) | 3-6 | 3 years |
| Scuola primaria (Elementary) | 6-11 | 5 years |
| Scuola media (Middle) | 11-14 | 3 years |
| Scuola superiore (High) | 14-19 | 5 years |

### High School Types
- **Liceo Classico**: Humanities, Latin, Greek
- **Liceo Scientifico**: Science, math focus
- **Liceo Linguistico**: Languages
- **Istituto Tecnico**: Technical/vocational
- **Liceo Artistico**: Arts

---

## International Schools

### Why International
- English (or other) language instruction
- Curriculum familiar to expats
- Easier transitions if relocating again
- International student body
- Generally excellent facilities

### Major International Schools

**American Schools**
- **St. George's British International School**: British curriculum
- **Rome International School**: IB curriculum
- **Marymount International School**: American + IB
- **American Overseas School of Rome (AOSR)**: American curriculum

**British Schools**
- **St. George's**: British curriculum through A-levels
- **New School Rome**: British + IB

**Other Languages**
- **Lycée Chateaubriand**: French
- **Deutsche Schule Rom**: German
- **Istituto Svizzero**: Swiss

### Costs (Annual)
| Level | Range |
|-------|-------|
| Preschool | EUR 8,000-15,000 |
| Primary | EUR 12,000-25,000 |
| Middle | EUR 15,000-28,000 |
| High | EUR 18,000-32,000 |

Plus: Registration fees, uniforms, activities, lunch

### Application Process
- Apply 1+ year ahead for popular schools
- Waiting lists common
- Assessment/interview required
- Previous school records needed
- Some require language tests

## Italian Public Schools

### Pros
- Free
- Italian language immersion
- Integration with local community
- Cultural experience

### Cons
- All instruction in Italian
- Different curriculum
- Less support for non-Italian speakers
- Varying quality

### Enrolling
- Register at local school (by residence)
- Present documents (passport, vaccinations, etc.)
- Academic records from previous school
- Codice fiscale

### Language Support
- Some schools have Italian L2 support
- Mediatore culturale sometimes available
- Varies greatly by school
- Consider private tutoring supplement

---

## Italian Private Schools

### Catholic Schools
- Many in Rome (Vatican connection)
- Quality varies
- Religious instruction
- Lower cost than international
- EUR 3,000-10,000/year

### Bilingual Schools
- Italian + English instruction
- Growing category
- Cultural bridge option
- Various curricula

## Universities

### Major Universities

**La Sapienza**
- Largest in Italy
- Founded 1303
- Wide range of programs
- Some English programs
- Lower cost

**Tor Vergata**
- Suburban campus
- Science and engineering strength
- Some English programs
- Growing reputation

**Roma Tre**
- Younger university
- Various faculties
- Some English options
- Ostiense area

**LUISS Guido Carli**
- Private, business-focused
- International programs
- English language options
- Higher cost, strong network

**John Cabot University**
- American university in Trastevere
- All English instruction
- American degrees
- Study abroad students

**American University of Rome**
- American curriculum
- English instruction
- Liberal arts focus
- Smaller community

### Tuition

**Public Universities**
- EUR 1,000-4,000/year
- Income-based
- EU and non-EU same

**Private Universities**
- EUR 5,000-15,000+/year
- LUISS: ~EUR 10,000-14,000
- John Cabot: ~EUR 25,000-30,000

### Admission
- Public: National test (some faculties)
- Private: Individual applications
- Language requirements (Italian or English depending on program)
- Recognition of foreign qualifications needed

## Language Learning

### Italian Language Schools

**Popular Schools**
- **Torre di Babele**: Well-established
- **Dilit International House**: Reputable
- **Centro Linguistico Italiano Dante Alighieri**: Traditional
- **Scuola Leonardo da Vinci**: Multiple locations

### Course Types
| Type | Duration | Cost |
|------|----------|------|
| Intensive (20hr/week) | 1-4 weeks | EUR 200-400/week |
| Standard (10hr/week) | 1-12 weeks | EUR 150-250/week |
| Private lessons | As needed | EUR 30-60/hour |
| University courses | Semester | EUR 500-1,500 |

### Free/Cheap Options
- Tandem language exchange
- Community centers (CTP)
- University free courses for residents
- Online resources (Duolingo, etc.)

## Childcare

### Asilo Nido (0-3 years)

**Public**
- Limited availability
- Long waiting lists
- Income-based fees
- EUR 100-400/month

**Private**
- More availability
- Higher cost
- EUR 500-1,200/month
- International options exist

### Finding Childcare
- Apply early (before birth for public)
- Ask other parents
- Check credentials
- Visit in person

## Practical Tips

### Choosing a School
1. Define priorities (language, curriculum, location)
2. Visit schools
3. Talk to current parents
4. Consider commute
5. Budget realistically
6. Apply early

### Documents Needed
- Birth certificate (apostilled)
- Previous school records (translated)
- Vaccination records
- Passport copies
- Proof of residence
- Codice fiscale

### Calendar
- School year: September - June
- Christmas break: ~2 weeks
- Easter break: ~1 week
- Summer: June-September
- Various holidays

### Transportation
- Some schools provide buses
- Public transport for older students
- Parent carpools common
- Consider location when choosing
