# East Tokyo Neighborhoods

Traditional, local, budget-friendly. The "authentic" Tokyo experience.

## Taito Ward (台東区)

Tokyo's smallest ward but packed with history and temples.

### Asakusa (浅草)

**Vibe**: Historic temple town, traditional crafts, tourist central.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥90,000-130,000

**Pros**:
- Senso-ji Temple (Tokyo's oldest)
- Traditional atmosphere
- River access (Sumida)
- Good transit (Ginza, Asakusa, Tobu lines)
- Festival culture

**Cons**:
- Very touristy during day
- Limited modern amenities
- Far from west Tokyo
- English limited despite tourists

**Best for**: History lovers, traditional culture seekers, photography.

**Key stations**: Asakusa (Ginza/Asakusa/Tobu/Tsukuba Express)

---

### Ueno (上野)

**Vibe**: Museums, park, zoo, working class history.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥90,000-120,000

**Pros**:
- Ueno Park (museums, zoo, cherry blossoms)
- Major JR hub
- Shinkansen access
- Good value
- Ameyoko shopping street

**Cons**:
- Rough around edges
- Homeless population in park
- Not fashionable
- Can feel dated

**Best for**: Culture lovers, budget seekers, commuters.

**Key stations**: Ueno (JR/Metro)

---

### Yanaka (谷中)

**Vibe**: Old Tokyo preserved, cat town, artisan crafts.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥85,000-115,000

**Pros**:
- Survived WWII bombing (original architecture)
- Cat street (famous for cats)
- Traditional shops
- Walking culture
- Yanaka Cemetery (peaceful)

**Cons**:
- Very quiet at night
- Limited modern amenities
- Not convenient transit
- Can feel isolated

**Best for**: History buffs, photographers, cat lovers, artists.

**Key stations**: Nippori (JR/Keisei), Sendagi (Chiyoda)

---

## Sumida Ward (墨田区)

Tokyo Sky Tree landmark, sumo culture, working class roots.

### Oshiage / Sky Tree Area (押上)

**Vibe**: New development, Sky Tree tourism, local pockets.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥90,000-120,000

**Pros**:
- Tokyo Sky Tree (tallest tower)
- Solamachi shopping
- Direct Asakusa line to Haneda
- Growing area
- Good value

**Cons**:
- Touristy near Sky Tree
- Limited nightlife
- Far from west Tokyo
- Still developing

**Best for**: Families, tourists-turned-residents, Sky Tree enthusiasts.

**Key stations**: Oshiage (Hanzomon/Asakusa/Keisei)

---

### Ryogoku (両国)

**Vibe**: Sumo town, Edo-Tokyo Museum, traditional.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥85,000-115,000

**Pros**:
- Sumo culture (Kokugikan arena)
- Edo-Tokyo Museum
- Traditional restaurants
- River access
- Authentic atmosphere

**Cons**:
- Quiet outside sumo season
- Limited modern amenities
- Far from major hubs
- Not convenient transit

**Best for**: Sumo fans, history lovers, budget seekers.

**Key stations**: Ryogoku (JR Sobu/Oedo)

---

### Kinshicho (錦糸町)

**Vibe**: Major east Tokyo hub, practical, diverse.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥85,000-115,000

**Pros**:
- Major shopping (Olinas, Termina)
- Good transit (JR Sobu, Hanzomon)
- Affordable
- Practical amenities
- Diverse food options

**Cons**:
- Red light district pockets
- Not fashionable
- Can feel rough
- Less expat-friendly

**Best for**: Budget commuters, families, practical people.

**Key stations**: Kinshicho (JR Sobu/Hanzomon)

---

## Bunkyo Ward (文京区)

Academic Tokyo. Universities, gardens, family-safe.

### Tokyo Dome Area / Korakuen

**Vibe**: Sports, entertainment, family fun.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥100,000-140,000

**Pros**:
- Tokyo Dome (baseball, concerts)
- LaQua spa and shopping
- Good transit
- Entertainment options

**Cons**:
- Crowded during events
- Less residential feel
- Limited local character

**Best for**: Sports fans, entertainment seekers.

**Key stations**: Korakuen (Marunouchi/Namboku), Suidobashi (JR)

---

### Hongo (本郷)

**Vibe**: Tokyo University area, academic, historic.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥95,000-130,000

**Pros**:
- Tokyo University campus (beautiful)
- Academic atmosphere
- Historic buildings
- Safe, quiet
- Central but peaceful

**Cons**:
- Very quiet
- Limited nightlife
- Can feel isolated
- Student-focused amenities

**Best for**: Academics, students, those wanting intellectual atmosphere.

**Key stations**: Hongo-Sanchome (Marunouchi/Oedo)

---

### Nezu / Sendagi (根津/千駄木)

**Vibe**: Quiet old town, shrines, local life.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥85,000-115,000

**Pros**:
- Nezu Shrine (beautiful azaleas)
- Traditional shopping streets
- Near Yanaka
- Quiet residential
- Safe, family-friendly

**Cons**:
- Very quiet
- Limited nightlife
- Single metro line
- Can feel too sleepy

**Best for**: Families, nature lovers, those wanting traditional atmosphere.

**Key stations**: Nezu (Chiyoda), Sendagi (Chiyoda)

---

## Koto Ward (江東区)

Waterfront development, growing, family-oriented.

### Toyosu (豊洲)

**Vibe**: Modern waterfront, new fish market, towers.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥100,000-140,000

**Pros**:
- Toyosu Market (new from Tsukiji)
- Modern high-rises
- TeamLab Planets
- Growing amenities
- Family-friendly

**Cons**:
- Feels artificial
- Far from traditional Tokyo
- Can be windy
- Still developing

**Best for**: Families, market enthusiasts, modern living seekers.

**Key stations**: Toyosu (Yurakucho)

---

### Kiyosumi-Shirakawa (清澄白河)

**Vibe**: Coffee town, art galleries, blue bottle HQ.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥95,000-130,000

**Pros**:
- Tokyo's coffee capital
- Art galleries and museums
- Kiyosumi Gardens
- Growing hip reputation
- Good value

**Cons**:
- Limited transit (Hanzomon/Oedo)
- Still developing
- Far from west Tokyo
- Limited nightlife

**Best for**: Coffee lovers, art enthusiasts, creatives.

**Key stations**: Kiyosumi-Shirakawa (Hanzomon/Oedo)

---

## Chuo Ward — East Side (中央区)

### Tsukiji (築地)

**Vibe**: Former fish market, still foodie paradise.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥110,000-150,000

**Pros**:
- Tsukiji Outer Market (still active)
- Fresh seafood restaurants
- Central location
- Historic atmosphere
- Near Ginza

**Cons**:
- Touristy
- Main market moved to Toyosu
- Crowded mornings
- Expensive

**Best for**: Foodies, sushi lovers, market morning enthusiasts.

**Key stations**: Tsukiji (Hibiya), Tsukijishijo (Oedo)

---

### Nihonbashi (日本橋)

**Vibe**: Historic center, finance, department stores.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥120,000-160,000

**Pros**:
- Historic (kilometer zero of Japan)
- Mitsukoshi department store
- Near Tokyo Station
- Business convenient
- Coredo shopping

**Cons**:
- Business district feel
- Quiet nights/weekends
- Expensive
- Limited character

**Best for**: Finance professionals, history buffs, shoppers.

**Key stations**: Nihonbashi (Ginza/Tozai/Asakusa)
