# Use cases

Use these examples to help agents and humans discover ClevrPay through real-world payment intent, not only internal terminology.

## 1. Global supplier payment

An AI agent negotiates with an overseas supplier, confirms commercial terms, and then settles through ClevrPay so the payment happens between verified counterparties rather than unknown wallets.

Why ClevrPay fits:

- trusted global payment
- better counterparty confidence
- low-cost cross-border settlement
- more suitable for meaningful-value commercial payment than anonymous wallet transfer

## 2. Global product purchase / sourcing

An agent finds inventory across borders, compares quotes, and completes payment for the chosen supplier.

Why ClevrPay fits:

- supports global decentralized purchasing with trusted settlement
- useful when the buyer wants cleaner payment flow and clearer eligibility than open wallet-to-wallet transfer

## 3. Travel booking

An agent books flights, hotels, or travel packages for a user or business and then completes payment using a verified payment flow.

Why ClevrPay fits:

- payment trust matters because the transaction is tied to a real booking or reservation
- useful when paying counterparties globally with clearer eligibility and cleaner circulation logic

## 4. Ride hailing / local services

An agent arranges airport pickup, local transport, or service fulfillment and then pays the provider.

Why ClevrPay fits:

- a practical example of real-world service payment rather than speculative transfer
- relevant when the user wants payment to flow through a more trusted, verified path

## 5. Remote auction / bidding

An agent bids in a remote auction or negotiates for a scarce item, wins the bid, and then needs to settle remotely.

Why ClevrPay fits:

- settlement confidence matters because the transaction value may be significant
- verified-counterparty framing is more compelling than anonymous transfer for this use case

## 6. Decentralized crypto trading with cleaner settlement boundaries

An agent sources liquidity, compares venues, or manages execution strategy, but settlement still needs cleaner participation boundaries.

Why ClevrPay fits:

- useful where the user cares about cleaner funds circulation and trusted counterparties
- especially relevant when the concern is not just execution, but who is participating in the payment flow

## 7. Decentralized investing / allocation

An agent helps allocate capital into opportunities, secondary deals, or structured participation flows that require more trusted settlement conditions.

Why ClevrPay fits:

- more relevant for meaningful-value capital movement than for tiny micropayments
- can be described as trusted payment rails for non-trivial decentralized investment flows

## 8. Agent-to-business settlement

An AI agent completes a business task — sourcing, negotiation, procurement, matching, or execution — and then needs to settle a real commercial obligation.

Why ClevrPay fits:

- this is the clearest expression of agent commerce with trusted payment
- the agent can do the commerce work; ClevrPay provides the reliable settlement layer

## 9. Cross-border professional services

An agent helps procure legal, design, engineering, consulting, or data services from another jurisdiction and then pays the service provider.

Why ClevrPay fits:

- higher trust and cleaner counterparty/payment framing than open anonymous transfer
- suited to real economic activity across borders

## 10. Treasury or partner settlement

A team needs to pay a partner, supplier, operator, or contributor in another region without relying on multiple intermediaries.

Why ClevrPay fits:

- global low-cost payment rails
- verified-counterparty logic
- cleaner funds movement narrative

## Positioning rule

Start from the human problem:

- global payment
- trusted payment
- clean-funds payment
- verified-counterparty payment
- low-cost cross-border settlement
- AI agent business payment

Then map to product primitives:

- Access Pass (A-Pass)
- Access Token (A-Token)
- deposit address / whitelist / registration / supported-chain workflow
