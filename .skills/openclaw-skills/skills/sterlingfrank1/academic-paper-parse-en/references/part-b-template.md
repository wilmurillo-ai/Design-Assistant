# Part B: Core Logic Chain and Essential Value Distillation — Output Template and Writing Guide

> The goal of this section is to distill the paper's most fundamental value. You are not summarizing content — you are reconstructing the minimal complete structure of its ideas. Capture each element in 2–4 precise sentences.

## The Four Core Elements

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| **Root Problem** | What is the most critical "tension" or "knowledge gap" the paper addresses? Not the broad research area, but the fundamental reason this paper needed to exist. |
| **Analytical Angle** | What distinctive "key insight" or "new assumption" sets the authors apart? This is the soul of the paper — the pivotal turn that others had not seen or acted on. |
| **Key Method** | What is the "core mechanism" the authors use to validate their angle and resolve the problem? Focus on the one or two most central technical or methodological innovations. |
| **Core Finding** | What "new knowledge" or "answer" does the paper ultimately produce? Support it with the single most critical data point or conclusion. |

---

## Method as Formula

Distill the "Key Method" above into a concise "verbal formula" that reveals its underlying logic or computational process.

**Format examples**:
- "New algorithm = (Feature A + Feature B) × Weight C"
- "Historical analysis = Path dependence + Perturbation at critical node A"
- "Value = Utility / Cognitive cost"
- "Reliable industrial AI = (Multi-agent division of labor + Structured chain-of-thought) × Real-time knowledge base"

---

## Final Dual Summary

**One-sentence summary (core value)**: Fuse the four elements — Problem, Angle, Method, and Finding — into a single, coherent, and concise sentence that clearly conveys the paper's core logic and significance. This sentence should allow a fellow researcher in the field to immediately understand what the paper does and why it matters.

**One-sentence summary (plain-language version)**: Capture the paper's central insight using an analogy or explanation accessible to a 10-year-old. Avoid all jargon; prioritize vividness and intuitive clarity.
