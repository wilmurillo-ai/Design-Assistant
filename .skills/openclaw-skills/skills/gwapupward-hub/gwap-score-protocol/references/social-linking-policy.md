# Social Linking Policy

## Purpose
Use public/social signals as supporting evidence without letting them dominate trust. Social data can confirm identity continuity and public legitimacy but must not override behavioral evidence or inflate scores without limit.

## Allowed social uses
- **Identity continuity**: linking wallet to a consistent social presence
- **Public business or creator legitimacy**: verifying a business or creator account exists and matches wallet identity
- **Posting consistency**: demonstrating ongoing activity over time
- **Profile age**: older accounts inspire more confidence
- **Verified status**: if the platform offers official verification, this may add weight
- **Engagement authenticity**: measuring quality of interaction rather than raw volume
- **Protocol‑linked creator credibility**: attestations from marketplaces or partners
- **Community vouches**: endorsements with caps

## Prohibited or weak uses
- Raw follower count as primary trust factor
- Viral reach as trust proof
- Popularity replacing behavioral reliability
- Weak identity guesses across platforms
- Scraping private or deceptive data

## Linking standards
A social link should be considered stronger when:
- The user self‑links it and signs proof with their wallet
- The wallet signs a message proving control over the social handle
- The handle is repeatedly associated with the subject across official channels
- Business or creator identity is consistent across surfaces

## Score cap
Positive impacts originating from social attestations are capped and subordinate to behavioral evidence. Social signals can increase confidence and provide modest score lift, but cannot be the sole basis for high trust.
