# Registers & Differences (CN/EN)

This reference summarizes **how human writing differs from typical AI writing** across common domains, and what to do to produce convincing human text.

> Principle: Humans write to *a situation* (relationship, incentives, time pressure). AI often writes to *a rubric*.

## 0) Cross-cutting differences: Human vs AI

### 0.1 Discourse + intent
**Human**
- Has an implied situation: "I’m late", "I’m persuading", "I’m documenting for audit".
- Uses pragmatic cues: what to say vs what to skip.
- Leaves some things unsaid (shared context).

**AI (common failure modes)**
- Over-explains basics; repeats; tries to be universally correct.
- Uses "neutral helpful" tone even when inappropriate.
- Adds symmetrical structure everywhere (too tidy).

### 0.2 Rhythm (句式节奏)
**Human**
- Mix of short/medium/long sentences.
- Uses fragments, parentheticals, interruptions, rhetorical questions.
- Paragraphing mirrors thinking.

**AI**
- Even sentence lengths; uniform paragraphs.
- Too many lists or too many perfectly balanced clauses.

### 0.3 Specificity vs genericness
**Human**
- Uses concrete anchors: time, numbers, small constraints.
- Adds realistic tradeoffs: "I can do X today, but Y needs tomorrow".

**AI**
- Generalities: "significant", "various", "robust", "comprehensive".
- Abstract nouns and vague claims.

### 0.4 Stance + uncertainty
**Human**
- Confidence tracks evidence and social risk.
- Chooses modality: 可能/大概/基本/很可能/倾向于.

**AI**
- Either overly certain or overly hedged everywhere.

## 1) 日常用语（聊天/短信/私信）

### What humans do
- Front-load intent: "你现在方便吗？我想问个事" / "Quick question—"
- Use relationship markers: "麻烦你" / "兄弟" / "老师" / "hey"
- Allow informality: fragments, quick clarifications, small self-corrections

### Common AI tells
- Too polite + too complete: "希望你一切都好" when it’s not that relationship.
- Over-structured: 3 bullet points for a 1-line ask.

### Templates
**CN — casual ask**
- 版本A（直接）: "我这边想确认下 X，方便给我个 Y 吗？今天/明天都行。"
- 版本B（带原因）: "我在弄 X，卡在 Y 这一步了。你那边知道怎么处理吗？"

**EN — casual ask**
- "Quick check: can you share X? If it’s easier, just send a screenshot."

### Micro-techniques
- Add 1 human constraint: "我现在在路上" / "I’m in between meetings".
- Use 1 conversational connector: "对了" / "顺便" / "btw".

## 2) 书面表达（邮件/公告/说明/工作文档）

### What humans do
- Make the ask unmistakable.
- Separate facts vs decisions.
- Respect reader time (scannable).

### Common AI tells
- Inflated politeness: "非常感谢您的时间与支持" (when internal email).
- Too much framing: "在当今快速变化的环境中..."

### Template: internal email (CN)
**Subject:** [主题：一句话]

1) **背景**（1–2句）
2) **结论/决定**（1–2句）
3) **需要你做什么**（清单，带截止时间）
4) **附件/链接**

### Template: short written notice (EN)
- Context (1 line)
- What’s changing (2–3 lines)
- Who’s impacted
- Timeline
- Contact

## 3) 论文表述（学术/技术论文）

### What humans do
- Define terms.
- Narrow claims to evidence.
- Use cautious, conventional phrasing.

### Common AI tells
- Lots of adjectives, few operational definitions.
- Claims novelty without citations.

### Canonical structure
- Abstract-like summary (problem → method → results → limitations)
- Introduction + related work
- Method
- Experiments/Results
- Discussion/Limitations

### Phrase bank (use sparingly)
**CN**: "我们观察到" "结果表明" "在……条件下" "尚存在局限".
**EN**: "We find that" "Results suggest" "under the assumption that" "a limitation is".

---

## 4) 新闻（报道/新闻稿/媒体通稿）

### What humans do
- Lead with the most newsworthy, verifiable fact.
- Attribute claims: who said it, where, when.
- Avoid editorial adjectives.

### Common AI tells
- Opinionated tone: "groundbreaking" "revolutionary".
- No attribution; vague sources.

### Template: straight news
1) Lead (5W1H)
2) Key details (numbers, location)
3) Attribution/quote
4) Background/context
5) What happens next

---

## 5)生活常见其他场景（快速指引）
- **客服/支持**: 明确道歉点 + 复述问题 + 下一步 + 时间预期。
- **法律/合规**: 术语一致；避免口语；保留责任边界。
- **营销**: 读者利益优先；少形容词堆砌；多证据/案例。
- **社交媒体**: 强钩子；观点明确；短句；适度口语。
