# AI Tells (and how to remove them)

Use this when text reads like an assistant instead of a person.

## 1) Overhelpful scaffolding
**Tells**: "以下是..." "总之" "希望对你有帮助" "当然可以".
**Fix**: Remove meta commentary. Start with the actual message.

## 2) Excessive symmetry / perfect structure
**Tells**: every paragraph same length; 3 bullets for everything.
**Fix**: Mix structures. Allow one short paragraph, one list, one longer sentence.

## 3) Vague intensifiers
**Tells**: "significant" "various" "robust" "comprehensive".
**Fix**: Replace with concrete nouns/numbers or delete.

## 4) Generic hedging everywhere
**Tells**: "may" "might" "could" in every sentence.
**Fix**: Be confident on facts; hedge only where uncertainty is real.

## 5) Lack of situation
**Tells**: sounds like it could be sent to anyone.
**Fix**: Add 1–2 situational anchors (time, reason, constraint) without inventing facts.

## 6) No real tradeoffs
**Tells**: everything is "best practice".
**Fix**: Add realistic constraints: timeline, resource limits, prioritization.

## 7) Mis-calibrated politeness
**Tells**: formal tone to close friends; casual tone to executives.
**Fix**: Match relationship + culture. In CN, calibrate 您/你, 麻烦/请, and sign-off.

## 8) Sanitized emotions
**Tells**: never sounds annoyed, rushed, excited.
**Fix**: Add mild emotional temperature when appropriate (0–3), e.g., "有点赶" / "I’m a bit slammed".

## 9) News/press boilerplate (especially in quotes + closers)
**Tells**: generic PR/news closers that add no new information.
Examples:
- “adds fresh capital”, “looks to grow”, “as it seeks to…”, “product offering”, “expand its footprint”
- generic quotes: “This funding will help us…”, “We’re excited/thrilled…”, “We’re committed to…”, “We’re on a mission to…”
**Fix**:
- If you don’t have additional facts, **end early**.
- Make quotes **specific to the provided facts** (what, for whom, where) without inventing.
- Prefer concrete next steps over vague “additional safeguards” where the prompt allows.
- Avoid repetitive openers like “The company said…” in every paragraph.

## 10) Marketing-template voice
**Tells**: copy that sounds like a generic landing page regardless of product.
Examples:
- “If you strip away the buzzwords…”, “unlock”, “seamlessly”, “next-generation”, “transform your workflow”
- broad claims with no concrete scenario
**Fix**:
- Add one specific scenario + one specific pain point.
- Use simpler, more direct sentences; allow one imperfect line.
- Remove grand claims and replace with observable outcomes (less screenshot ping-pong, fewer status pings, clearer handoffs).
