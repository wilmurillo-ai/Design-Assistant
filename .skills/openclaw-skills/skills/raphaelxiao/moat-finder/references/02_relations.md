# Relation Monopoly Interview Guide - DETAILED BOUNDARIES

**CRITICAL**: Each relation has STRICT boundaries. Do NOT mix categories.

---

## 1. Government Relations (与政府机构的关系)

### STRICTLY INCLUDES:
- **Licenses & Permits**: Payment licenses, banking licenses, telecom licenses, gaming licenses
- **Regulatory moats**: Industries where regulation restricts new entrants (tobacco, finance, utilities)
- **State monopolies**: Officially sanctioned monopolies (salt, tobacco, lotteries)
- **Policy protection**: Tax advantages, subsidies, or protections through government relationships
- **Zoning & approvals**: Exclusive rights to operate in certain areas

### STRICTLY EXCLUDES:
- General legal compliance (this is baseline, not a moat)
- **鈫?If discussing payment licenses or regulatory permits, it goes HERE, NOT under Land or Technology.**

### Interview Questions:
- "Does the business require a specific government license that is restricted, expensive, or frozen?"
- "Is the industry protected by state monopoly or heavy regulation that limits competition?"
- "Are there policy barriers that prevent new entrants from competing (e.g., tobacco, finance)?"

---

## 2. Peer/Competitor Relations (与同行的关系)

### STRICTLY INCLUDES:
- **Price alliances**: OPEC-like cartels that control supply and pricing
- **Standard setting**: Industry consortia that set technical standards (Type-C, 5G)
- **Geographic indications**: Protected regional names (Champagne, Scotch)
- **Patent pools**: Cross-licensing agreements among industry players

### STRICTLY EXCLUDES:
- Normal market competition
- General industry associations

### Interview Questions:
- "Is the company part of an alliance that controls industry pricing or supply?"
- "Does the company help set industry standards that others must follow?"
- "Are there geographic or brand protections that the industry collectively enforces?"

---

## 3. Supplier Relations (与供应商的关系)

### STRICTLY INCLUDES:
- **Exclusive supply agreements**: Long-term contracts for scarce materials
- **Bargaining power**: Scale-based discounts that competitors cannot match (Walmart)
- **Vertical integration**: Owning or controlling key suppliers
- **Lock-in mechanisms**: Suppliers dependent on the company for revenue

### STRICTLY EXCLUDES:
- Normal procurement relationships
- Short-term supply contracts
- **鈫?If discussing exclusive access to raw materials through contracts, that's Supplier Relations. If discussing owning the mine itself, that's Land.**

### Interview Questions:
- "Does the company have exclusive, long-term agreements with key upstream suppliers?"
- "Is the company's procurement volume large enough to force supplier discounts?"
- "Would suppliers suffer significantly if they lost the company's business?"

---

## 4. Customer Relations (与客户的关系)

### STRICTLY INCLUDES:
Three mechanisms to lock in customers:

**A. Brand (品牌) - Lowers Decision Cost**
- Premium brand allowing price premiums (Hermes, Maotai)
- High repurchase rates due to brand trust
- Reduced customer decision fatigue

**B. Channel (渠道) - Lowers Purchase Cost**
- Dominant distribution networks (Haitian in gas stations)
- Exclusive retail partnerships
- Direct-to-consumer control

**C. Switching Costs (转换成本) - Raises Exit Cost**
- **Social graphs**: WeChat (your network is here)
- **Data lock-in**: Cloud services, notes, playlists
- **Learning curves**: Complex software (Photoshop, CAD)
- **Membership programs**: Airline status, hotel loyalty
- **Long-term contracts**: SaaS subscriptions, enterprise software

### STRICTLY EXCLUDES:
- One-time transactions
- General customer satisfaction
- **鈫?If discussing why customers stay even when competitors offer lower prices, investigate Switching Costs here.**

### Interview Questions:
- **Brand**: "Can the company charge 20-30% premium solely due to brand reputation?"
- **Channel**: "Is the product available in places competitors cannot access?"
- **Switching Costs**: "If a competitor offered the same product 20% cheaper, what would prevent customers from leaving? What is the actual cost (time, money, risk, social connections) of switching?"
