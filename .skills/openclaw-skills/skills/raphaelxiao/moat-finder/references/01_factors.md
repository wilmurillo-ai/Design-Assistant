# Factor Monopoly Interview Guide - DETAILED BOUNDARIES

**CRITICAL**: Each factor has STRICT boundaries. Do NOT mix categories.

---

## 1. Land & Endowment (土地与资源禀赋)

### STRICTLY INCLUDES:
- **Natural resources**: Mines, oil reserves, forests, water resources
- **Geographic uniqueness**: Ports, choke points (Singapore), irreplaceable locations
- **Climate/microclimate**: Unique weather patterns (Maotai town), soil conditions
- **Resource quality**: High-grade ore, rare earth elements

### STRICTLY EXCLUDES (Do NOT discuss here):
- Government licenses or permits
- Zoning approvals
- Regulatory protections
- **鈫?These belong to: Government Relations**

### Interview Questions:
- "Does the company own or control any scarce natural resources (mines, oil, forests)?"
- "Does the company benefit from a unique geographic location that cannot be replicated (ports, choke points)?"
- "Does the company rely on unique climate or environmental conditions (like Maotai's weather)?"

---

## 2. Labor (劳动力)

### STRICTLY INCLUDES:
- **Scarce talent**: Top celebrities, irreplaceable researchers, key executives
- **Specialized skills**: Unique craftsmanship, rare technical expertise
- **Binding mechanisms**: Long-term contracts, equity stakes (not just salaries)

### STRICTLY EXCLUDES:
- General workforce availability
- Labor cost advantages (this is a result, not a moat)
- **鈫?If discussing executive contracts with equity, that's still Labor**

### Interview Questions:
- "Is the company's core revenue driven by 1-2 highly scarce, irreplaceable individuals?"
- "How are these key individuals bound to the company-contracts, equity, or culture?"
- "If these individuals left, would the company suffer immediate, severe damage?"

---

## 3. Capital (资本)

### STRICTLY INCLUDES:
- **Matthew Effect**: Access to massive funding that competitors cannot match
- **Survival advantage**: Cash reserves to outlast competitors in price wars
- **Acquisition power**: Ability to buy competitors or key assets

### STRICTLY EXCLUDES:
- Capital efficiency ratios (ROE, ROIC)-these are results
- Normal business financing
- **鈫?These belong to: Financial performance analysis**

### Interview Questions:
- "Is this a capital-intensive industry where only well-funded players survive?"
- "Does the company have access to funding sources that new entrants cannot replicate?"
- "Can the company sustain losses longer than competitors to capture market share?"

---

## 4. Technology (技术)

### STRICTLY INCLUDES:
- **Patents**: Blocking patents that competitors cannot bypass
- **Copyrights**: Long-lasting IP (Disney's characters, software copyrights)
- **Trade secrets**: Hidden know-how, proprietary formulas (Coca-Cola recipe, battery material ratios)
- **Proprietary algorithms**: Unique technical solutions (but NOT general data accumulation)

### STRICTLY EXCLUDES:
- Data accumulation over time (this is Factor 5: Data)
- General technology adoption
- Open-source technology

### Interview Questions:
- "Does the company hold patents that competitors cannot work around without infringement?"
- "Does the company have trade secrets or 'know-how' that took years to develop?"
- "Is there a proprietary formula or process that is kept secret (like Coca-Cola's recipe)?"

---

## 5. Data (数据)

### STRICTLY INCLUDES:
- **Accumulated user behavior**: Historical interactions that train algorithms
- **Proprietary datasets**: Information that cannot be scraped or bought
- **Self-reinforcing loops**: More users → more data → better product → more users

### STRICTLY EXCLUDES:
- Publicly available data
- One-time data purchases
- **鈫?If discussing how data improves algorithms over time, that's Data. If discussing the algorithm itself, that's Technology.**

### Interview Questions:
- "Does the product get better automatically as more users generate data?"
- "Does the company possess historical data that new entrants cannot access or replicate?"
- "If a competitor launched today with the same features, would they lack the data to match your performance?"
