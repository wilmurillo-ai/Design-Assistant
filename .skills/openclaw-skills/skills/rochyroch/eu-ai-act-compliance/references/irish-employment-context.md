# Irish Employment and Regulatory Context

## Overview

This document provides specific guidance for AI compliance in the Irish employment context, including relevant Irish legislation, regulatory bodies, and practical considerations.

## Irish Employment Legislation

### Employment Equality Acts 1998-2021

**Key Provisions**:

| Ground | Protection |
|--------|------------|
| Gender | Discrimination prohibited |
| Civil status | Discrimination prohibited |
| Family status | Discrimination prohibited |
| Sexual orientation | Discrimination prohibited |
| Religion | Discrimination prohibited |
| Age | Discrimination prohibited |
| Disability | Discrimination prohibited |
| Race, colour, nationality | Discrimination prohibited |
| Traveller community | Discrimination prohibited |

**AI Implications**:

- AI systems must not discriminate on these grounds
- Objective justification required for differential treatment
- Positive action permitted in limited circumstances
- Reasonable accommodation required for disability

**Relevant Case Law**:
- *Grace v. DPSA* [2014] — Burden of proof in discrimination cases
- *Croker v. Minister for Finance* [2015] — Statistical evidence of discrimination

---

### Unfair Dismissals Acts 1977-2015

**Key Provisions**:

- Employees with 12 months' service have protection
- Dismissal must be for fair reasons
- Fair procedures must be followed
- Redress includes reinstatement, re-engagement, or compensation

**AI Implications**:

- AI-assisted dismissal decisions must follow fair procedures
- Human oversight required for termination decisions
- Documentation of AI's role in decision-making
- Appeal mechanisms required

---

### Terms of Employment (Information) Acts 1994-2014

**Key Provisions**:

- Employees must receive written statement of terms
- Changes must be notified in writing
- Core terms must be provided within 5 days

**AI Implications**:

- Privacy policies must cover AI processing
- Employee monitoring policies required
- Notification of AI use in performance management

## SI 284/2016: Public Sector Procurement

### European Union (Award of Public Authority Contracts) Regulations 2016

**Application**: Public sector bodies in Ireland procuring services.

**Relevance to AI**:

| Requirement | AI Implication |
|-------------|----------------|
| Competitive procurement | AI systems must be competitively tendered |
| Selection criteria | Technical capability, compliance evidence |
| Award criteria | Quality, price, social considerations |
| Contract conditions | Data protection, confidentiality, security |

**Key Provisions for AI Procurement**:

1. **Technical specifications** (Regulation 42)
   - Must be non-discriminatory
   - May include accessibility requirements
   - Should reference standards where available

2. **Selection criteria** (Regulation 58)
   - Suitability to pursue professional activity
   - Economic and financial standing
   - Technical and professional ability
   - For AI: data protection compliance, security certifications

3. **Award criteria** (Regulation 67)
   - Lowest price or most economically advantageous
   - Quality, technical merit, accessibility
   - For AI: accuracy, explainability, human oversight

4. **Contract performance conditions** (Regulation 70)
   - May include social, environmental, accessibility conditions
   - For AI: bias testing requirements, transparency obligations

**Practical Implications**:

- Public bodies must conduct competitive processes for AI systems
- Evidence of compliance with EU AI Act required
- Due diligence on AI vendors essential
- Contract terms must address data protection and security

## Irish Data Protection Requirements

### Data Protection Impact Assessments

**When Required**:

- AI processing in HR context (high-risk by default)
- Automated decision-making with legal effects
- Processing on a large scale
- Use of new technologies

**Irish DPC Guidance**:

- Publish DPIA template and guidance
- Encourage early consultation
- Require documentation of all processing activities

**DPIA Structure**:

1. Description of processing
2. Assessment of necessity and proportionality
3. Assessment of risks
4. Measures to address risks
5. Consultation with DPO

---

### Subject Access Requests

**Rights under GDPR Articles 15-22**:

| Right | AI Implications |
|-------|-----------------|
| Access | AI decisions must be documented |
| Rectification | AI training data must be accurate |
| Erasure | Right to deletion from training data |
| Restriction | Limit AI processing during disputes |
| Portability | Transfer AI-derived data |
| Object | Object to AI processing |
| Automated decision-making | Right to human intervention |

**Response Time**: 30 days (extendable to 90 days for complex requests)

## Relevant Irish Case Law

### Employment Equality Cases

1. **Grace v. DPSA** [2014] EDA 1414
   - **Issue**: Statistical evidence of discrimination
   - **Holding**: Statistical disparities can shift burden of proof
   - **AI Relevance**: AI systems producing discriminatory outcomes may be challenged statistically

2. **Croker v. Minister for Finance** [2015] EDA 54
   - **Issue**: Age discrimination in recruitment
   - **Holding**: Objective justification required for age criteria
   - **AI Relevance**: AI systems must not use age as proxy for other characteristics

3. **A Company v. A Worker** [2017] EDA 27
   - **Issue**: Disability discrimination
   - **Holding**: Reasonable accommodation required
   - **AI Relevance**: AI systems must accommodate candidates with disabilities

### Data Protection Cases

1. **DPC v. Meta** (2023)
   - **Issue**: Data processing for advertising
   - **Holding**: Consent mechanisms insufficient
   - **AI Relevance**: AI systems must have valid lawful basis

2. **DPC Enforcement Actions** (Ongoing)
   - **Issue**: Transparency in algorithmic processing
   - **AI Relevance**: AI systems must be transparent about processing
