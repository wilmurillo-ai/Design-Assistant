# EU AI Act Article 14: Human Oversight Checklist

## Overview

Article 14 of the EU AI Act requires that high-risk AI systems be designed to allow effective human oversight during use. This checklist helps HR directors document their human oversight arrangements.

**Legal Reference**: Regulation (EU) 2024/1689, Article 14 — Human oversight

## Specific HR Context Requirements

### Recruitment Systems

- [ ] Human reviews all shortlisting decisions before candidates are rejected
- [ ] Scoring criteria are documented and available to candidates on request
- [ ] Bias testing results are reviewed by HR professionals
- [ ] Final hiring decisions are made by humans, not AI

### Performance Management Systems

- [ ] Performance scores are reviewed by managers before becoming final
- [ ] Employees can appeal AI-influenced assessments
- [ ] Human managers explain AI contributions to assessments
- [ ] AI recommendations are advisory only

### Workforce Planning Systems

- [ ] Redundancy or dismissal decisions involve human review
- [ ] Workforce planning outputs are validated by HR
- [ ] Employee representatives consulted where appropriate
- [ ] Fair procedure followed for affected employees

## Documentation Requirements

Under Article 14(3), deployers must keep records of:

1. **Date and time of AI system use**
2. **Reference to the AI system used**
3. **Input data provided to the system**
4. **Output generated by the system**
5. **Human oversight actions taken**
6. **Decisions made based on AI output**
7. **Any incidents or anomalies observed**
