# Founder Story Brief: Stone & Stitch
## Snapshot
- **Category**: Handcrafted leather goods
- **Products**: Wallets, Bags, Belts
- **Founder**: Alex (Austin, TX) — sole maker
- **Audience**: people who value longevity over trends
- **Brand voice**: warm, direct, craft-forward, no hype
- **Primary CTA**: Shop the collection

## Narrative spine (one sentence)

> We started Stone & Stitch because I couldn't find leather pieces that aged beautifully and lasted for years, so I began making my own after hours in a small studio.. Today we make heirloom-quality leather goods that get better with time for people who value longevity over trends by cut, skive, and stitch each piece by hand in our studio.

## Origin (why it started)

I couldn't find leather pieces that aged beautifully and lasted for years, so I began making my own after hours in a small studio.

## Mission (what you exist to do)

make heirloom-quality leather goods that get better with time

## Differentiators (concrete, verifiable)

- cut, skive, and stitch each piece by hand in our studio
- use full-grain leather and solid brass hardware
- made-to-order to reduce waste and improve consistency

## Values (2–4 principles)

- no shortcuts
- transparent materials
- repair over replace

## Proof / credibility (if any)

- 8 years making leather goods
- 1,200+ orders fulfilled

## Constraints (what not to claim or mention)

- avoid health claims
- avoid competitor mentions

## Placement plan

- **About / Brand page**: Full origin + mission + values + proof. (300–800 words, scannable sections)
- **Homepage hero**: One headline + one sub + one CTA. No long paragraphs.
- **PDP brand block**: 2–4 sentences: origin + process + for-whom. Avoid repeating the full About.
- **Packaging / insert**: 1–2 sentences with one memorable line.

## Notes for copywriting

- Replace generic claims with concrete process/origin/proof.
- Keep hero copy short; let product and CTA do the work.
- Keep origin and mission consistent across placements; only length changes.
