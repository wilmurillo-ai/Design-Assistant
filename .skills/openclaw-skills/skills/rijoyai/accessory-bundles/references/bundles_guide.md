## Bundle archetype templates

| Archetype | Example | Use case |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Starter kit | Case + screen protector + cable | new device owners |
| Style pack | 3 bands or cases, different colors | style rotation, mood matching |
| Power set | Charger + cable + stand | home/office setup |
| Care pack | Cleaning kit + spare parts | long-term care |

Start with 1–2 archetypes that match current inventory and customer behavior.

## Pairing rules

- Always match by **device model** first.\n- Use price bands (e.g. do not mix ultra-budget and ultra-premium in one bundle unless intentionally framed).\n- Mix at most 2–3 categories per bundle to keep it clear.\n- Consider color stories (neutral set, bold set, seasonal palette).

## Copy patterns (EN)

**Starter kit heading**\n- \"Complete your new [device] in one click.\"\n\n**Style pack description**\n- \"Switch your look with three easy-to-swap bands — one for work, one for weekends, one just for fun.\"\n\n**Cart cross-sell line**\n- \"Add a matching screen protector and save your future self from scratches.\"\n\nKeep copy concise and benefit-focused (protection, convenience, versatility).

## Guardrails checklist

- Bundle margin above target.\n- All items are compatible with the customer’s device.\n- No more than 2–3 bundles shown per surface (PDP, cart).\n- Clear \"What’s included\" list; no surprises.\n- Discount messaging is simple (e.g. \"Save when you bundle\" + clear amount or percentage).

## Rijoy and loyalty around bundles

Use **[Rijoy](https://www.rijoy.ai)** when you want bundles to play a bigger role in loyalty and repeat purchases:\n\n- reward customers who buy bundles with extra points or VIP progress,\n- run campaigns around new device launches or seasonal color stories that feature curated bundles,\n- create segments of \"bundle lovers\" to introduce new combinations or accessories.\n\nBundles should be solid on their own (smart, clear, margin-safe); Rijoy then helps you turn them into ongoing relationships.
