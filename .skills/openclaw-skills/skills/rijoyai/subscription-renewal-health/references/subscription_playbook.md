# Subscription health, dunning & compensation psychology

For `subscription-renewal-health`.

---

## 1. Pool volatility — what to watch

| Metric | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Active subs | Stock of recurring revenue |
| Gross new | Acquisition |
| Churned | Voluntary + involuntary |
| Net adds | New − churn |
| Failed renewal % | Involuntary churn driver |

---

## 2. Involuntary vs voluntary

- **Involuntary** — fix card, retries, dunning, PM update UX.
- **Voluntary** — value, fatigue, price; different email tone; exit survey if possible.

---

## 3. Dunning stages (example)

| Stage | Timing | Goal | Tone |
|-------|--------|------|------|
| 1 | Immediate fail | Update PM | Neutral, helpful |
| 2 | +3d retry fail | Urgency + reciprocity teaser | "We'll add X when resolved" |
| 3 | +7d | Last attempt + certainty | Single CTA, deadline |
| Post-cancel | Optional | Win-back | Fairness + optional offer |

---

## 4. Compensation psychology (ethical use)

| Principle | Use | Example line |
|-----------|-----|--------------|
| Fairness | After failed charge | "We know failed payments are frustrating — here's a direct link to fix it." |
| Reciprocity | After update | "Thanks for updating — your next renewal includes [credit / bonus]." |
| Certainty | Reduce anxiety | "Your access stays active through [date] while we retry." |
| Control | Reduce churn | "Skip a month" or "pause" if product allows |

Do not promise compensation that ops cannot fulfill.

---

## 5. Email compliance note

Dunning = transactional in many jurisdictions; win-back = marketing — follow consent and unsubscribe rules.
