## 1. STEELMAN BEAR CASE

> **Purpose:** Develop strongest possible opposing argument (not strawman)
> **Questions:** What's best argument this fails? | Counter-evidence for each bull pillar? | Historical precedent for failure?
> **Detail level:** detailed

### 1.1 Core Bear Thesis

> **What to deliver:** Strongest coherent counter-thesis, not just criticism but alternative investment view
> **Format:** Bear thesis statement, supporting evidence, internal consistency assessment

### 1.2 Pillar-by-Pillar Attack

> **What to deliver:** Counter-arguments to each bull case pillar (technology, team, market, tokenomics)
> **Format:** Per pillar: Bull claim → Counter-evidence → Bear interpretation

### 1.3 Bear Case Strength Assessment

> **What to deliver:** Honest evaluation of bear case strength, which counter-arguments research fails to address
> **Format:** Strength rating, strongest counter-arguments, unaddressed challenges

---

## 2. THESIS BREAKING POINTS

> **Purpose:** Identify conditions under which bull thesis collapses completely
> **Questions:** What assumptions if wrong invalidate thesis? | Probability of each breaking point? | Early warning signals?
> **Detail level:** detailed

### 2.1 Critical Assumptions Mapping

> **What to deliver:** Key assumptions mapped to thesis outcomes, thesis-breaking assumptions identified
> **Format:** Assumption → If fails → Impact on thesis → Breaking point? (Y/N)

### 2.2 Breaking Point Analysis

> **What to deliver:** Each breaking point with probability, early warning signals, thesis sensitivity
> **Format:** Per breaking point: Description, probability, signals, sensitivity assessment

### 2.3 Fragility Assessment

> **What to deliver:** Overall thesis fragility - does it break easily or withstand challenges?
> **Format:** Fragility rating, critical vs minor vulnerabilities, robustness evaluation

---

## 3. OPTIMISTIC ASSUMPTION CHALLENGES

> **Purpose:** Question every best-case scenario assumption aggressively
> **Questions:** Where does research assume things go right? | Evidence for rosy projections? | Hidden optimism bias?
> **Detail level:** standard

### 3.1 Assumption Audit

> **What to deliver:** All optimistic assumptions identified across categories (market, team, tech, token)
> **Format:** Per category: Optimistic assumption → Evidence quality → Pessimistic alternative

### 3.2 Optimism Bias Assessment

> **What to deliver:** Overall optimism bias level, most egregious examples, base-case vs best-case realism
> **Format:** Bias rating, key examples, realistic adjustment recommendations

---

## 4. COMPREHENSIVE FAILURE MODES

> **Purpose:** Identify all ways project could fail despite positive analysis
> **Questions:** How could execution fail? | Technical failure paths? | Competitive/market failures? | Token economic failures?
> **Detail level:** detailed

### 4.1 Failure Mode Inventory

> **What to deliver:** All failure modes categorized (execution, technical, competitive, market, tokenomic, catastrophic)
> **Format:** Per category: Failure mode, probability, impact, research coverage (addressed/blind spot)

### 4.2 Failure Probability Matrix

> **What to deliver:** Prioritized failures by probability × impact
> **Format:** Matrix or ranked list with likelihood, severity, combined risk score

---

## 5. CONSENSUS QUESTIONING

> **Purpose:** Challenge "everyone knows X" assumptions without independent validation
> **Questions:** Where does analysis accept market consensus? | Is consensus evidence-based or groupthink? | Contrarian view?
> **Detail level:** concise

### 5.1 Consensus Dependencies

> **What to deliver:** Consensus beliefs research relies on, evidence quality for each, contrarian alternatives
> **Format:** Consensus belief → Evidence basis → Who benefits → Contrarian view

---

## 6. ADVERSARIAL SCENARIO STRESS-TEST

> **Purpose:** Test thesis under realistic stress scenarios, not just best-case
> **Questions:** How does thesis hold under moderate/significant/worst-case stress? | Which scenarios are thesis-breaking?
> **Detail level:** standard

### 6.1 Scenario Analysis

> **What to deliver:** Base-case, stress-case, worst-case scenarios with thesis survival assessment
> **Format:** Per scenario: Assumptions, probability, thesis survival (holds/weakens/breaks)

### 6.2 Scenario Comparison

> **What to deliver:** Research scenarios vs adversarial scenarios, adequacy assessment
> **Format:** Comparison table, gaps identified, robustness verdict

---

## 7. WEAK EVIDENCE ATTACKS

> **Purpose:** Identify unsourced or weakly-sourced claims
> **Questions:** Which claims lack evidence? | Evidence quality for key claims? | Does thesis hold without weak evidence?
> **Detail level:** concise

### 7.1 Evidence Quality Audit

> **What to deliver:** Weak evidence inventory, speculation creep detection, key claims without adequate support
> **Format:** Claim → Evidence quality → Impact if removed

---

## 8. REALITY CHECK VERDICT

> **Purpose:** Synthesize adversarial analysis into honest assessment
> **Questions:** Is thesis realistic or overconfident? | Gap between claims and realistic expectations? | Viability assessment?
> **Detail level:** standard

### 8.1 Overall Assessment

> **What to deliver:** Reality check verdict, thesis realism, probability of success, gap analysis
> **Format:** Verdict rating, key findings synthesis, honest viability assessment

### 8.2 Constructive Path Forward

> **What to deliver:** How to strengthen thesis if possible, what additional validation needed
> **Format:** Recommendations for addressing identified weaknesses
> **Note:** Confrontational but constructive - honest about flaws, provide path to improvement
