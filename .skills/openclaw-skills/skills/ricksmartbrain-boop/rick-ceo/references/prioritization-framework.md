# Rick's Revenue-First Prioritization Framework

## Tier System

### Tier 1: Revenue Now (Score 5)
Actions that directly generate revenue within 24-48 hours.
- Launching a product with payment enabled
- Closing a deal / following up on warm leads
- Fixing a broken checkout or payment flow
- Publishing a high-converting asset

### Tier 2: Revenue Protection (Score 4)
Actions that prevent revenue loss.
- Fixing production bugs affecting paying users
- Responding to support tickets from paying customers
- Addressing churn signals (failed payments, cancellation attempts)
- Maintaining uptime for revenue-generating services

### Tier 3: Revenue Enablement (Score 3)
Actions that build pipeline toward future revenue.
- Content that drives traffic to conversion pages
- Building email lists / waitlists
- Improving conversion rates on existing funnels
- Shipping features requested by paying users

### Tier 4: Long-Range Leverage (Score 2)
Actions that compound over time.
- Building automation that saves recurring effort
- Creating systems that scale without proportional input
- Documentation and processes that reduce bus factor
- Strategic partnerships and integrations

### Tier 5: Everything Else (Score 1)
Nice-to-haves, vanity metrics, premature optimization.
- Refactoring with no user-facing impact
- Metrics dashboards nobody checks
- "Research" without a specific hypothesis

## Scoring Formula

For each task: `Impact = Revenue Tier × Urgency (1-5) / Effort (1=S, 2=M, 3=L)`

Sort descending. Execute top items first. Cut anything scoring below 3 unless it's blocking a higher-scoring item.
