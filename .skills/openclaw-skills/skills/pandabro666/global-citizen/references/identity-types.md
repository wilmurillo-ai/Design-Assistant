# Identity Type Taxonomy

Structural knowledge about the hierarchy of overseas identity types. This file provides
the conceptual framework for understanding different pathways and their fundamental
characteristics. All specific numbers (prices, timelines) are illustrative baselines —
verify through real-time queries before use.

---

## The Identity Spectrum

```
Visa → Temporary Residency → Permanent Residency (PR) → Citizenship / Passport
  ↑                                                   ↑
Most flexible, fewest rights              Most stable, most rights
```

Understanding where a pathway sits on this spectrum is the foundation of all analysis.
Moving right on the spectrum generally requires more time, money, or commitment —
but also confers more rights and stability.

---

## Category 1: Visas

### Digital Nomad Visa
- **What it is**: Legal residence permit for remote workers earning income from outside the host country
- **Typical duration**: 6 months to 2 years, often renewable
- **Residence requirement**: Usually minimal or none
- **Core conditions**: Proof of foreign-source income (thresholds vary widely by country); remote work contract or self-employment evidence
- **Path to permanence**: Some programs (e.g., Portugal D8) can transition to longer-term residency
- **Known markets**: 50+ countries as of 2026; quality varies significantly
- **Best for**: Remote workers, freelancers, content creators who want geographic flexibility

### Student Visa → Work Visa Pathway
- **What it is**: Enter as international student, transition to post-graduation work visa, then to PR
- **Typical total timeline**: 5-10 years from entry to citizenship
- **Residence requirement**: Must be physically present throughout; typically full-time study + work
- **Key advantage**: Structured, well-understood pathway with clear milestones
- **Key risk**: High total cost (tuition + living expenses); age points may diminish over time
- **Best for**: Those willing to commit years to a single country's education system

### Skilled Work Visa / Talent Visa
- **What it is**: Residence permit granted based on professional skills, qualifications, or talent
- **Sub-categories**: Points-based (Canada EE, Australia 189), employer-sponsored (482, H-1B), talent-based (UK Global Talent, Germany Blue Card, Singapore ONE Pass)
- **Special advantage for PhD/researchers**: Many countries offer accelerated processing, points bonuses, or dedicated streams for doctoral degree holders and STEM researchers
- **Typical challenge**: Often requires a job offer (except points-based independent programs); competitive selection
- **Best for**: Highly educated professionals, especially in STEM/AI fields

## Category 2: Residency (Residence Permit)

### Golden Visa / Residence by Investment
- **What it is**: Legal residency obtained through qualifying capital investment in the host country
- **What it is NOT**: Citizenship. Holders remain citizens of their original country with their original passport.
- **Core rights**: Residence, work, education access, healthcare (in EU: Schengen-area free travel)
- **Residence requirement**: Varies from none (Greece) to minimal (Portugal ~7-14 days/year) to significant
- **Common investment types**:
  - Real estate purchase (historically most popular; increasingly restricted)
  - Fund or bond investment
  - Business investment / job creation
  - Bank deposit
- **Path to citizenship**: Available in most programs, typically 5-10 years, with language and civics exams
- **Key distinction**: Residency ≠ passport. Visa-free travel does NOT improve until citizenship is obtained.

### Passive Income / Non-Lucrative Residency
- **What it is**: Residency granted based on proof of sufficient passive income or savings
- **Representative programs**: Portugal D7, Spain Non-Lucrative Visa, Malaysia MM2H
- **Key advantage**: No investment required; lower cost than golden visas
- **Key challenge**: Income thresholds vary and can be substantial; may restrict local employment
- **Best for**: Those with rental income, investment returns, pensions, or remote freelance income

## Category 3: Permanent Residency (PR)

- **What it is**: Indefinite right to reside, work, and access social services in the host country
- **How obtained**: Usually through cumulative years of legal residence (2-10 years varies by country)
- **What it provides**: Stability, full work rights, social benefits, no renewal焦虑 (may require periodic confirmation of residence)
- **What it does NOT provide**: Passport/visa-free travel improvement (use original passport), voting rights (usually)
- **Key insight**: PR is often the "sweet spot" — most practical benefits of citizenship without giving up original nationality

## Category 4: Citizenship / Second Passport

### Citizenship by Descent (Bloodline)
- **What it is**: Claim citizenship through ancestral lineage
- **Cost**: Minimal (legal fees, document retrieval, apostilles)
- **Timeline**: 1-3 years typically (depends on document availability)
- **Key markets**: Italy (no generational limit), Poland, Ireland, Lithuania, Germany (for descendants of those stripped of citizenship)
- **Hidden value**: Extremely high ROI — essentially free passport if eligible
- **Action**: Always check for ancestral nationality eligibility first; it costs almost nothing to investigate

### Citizenship by Investment (CBI)
- **What it is**: Direct acquisition of citizenship and passport through qualifying investment or donation
- **What it provides**: Full citizenship immediately (no residency period required); new passport with its visa-free benefits
- **What it does NOT require**: Physical residence, language exams, or cultural integration
- **Caribbean programs**: St. Kitts, Grenada, Dominica, Antigua, St. Lucia — similar structure with varying pricing; **verify current unified thresholds**
- **Key differentiation**: Grenada uniquely provides access to US E-2 investor visa (verify current treaty status)
- **Non-Caribbean options**: Turkey (real estate), Vanuatu (donation), others — verify current availability
- **Risk**: EU has been pressuring Caribbean nations on CBI programs; visa-free access to Schengen could theoretically be restricted

### Naturalization
- **What it is**: Citizenship obtained through years of legal residence, language proficiency, and civic knowledge
- **Typical timeline**: 3-10 years of residence depending on country
- **Common requirements**: Sustained physical presence, language exam (varying levels), civics/culture exam, clean criminal record
- **Fast-track options**: Some countries reduce timelines for spouses of citizens, refugees, or exceptional contributors
- **Key insight**: This is the "slow but legitimate" path — no shortcuts, but also no large investment required

## Cross-Category Comparison

| Dimension | Visa | Residency (Golden Visa) | PR | CBI Passport |
|-----------|------|------------------------|----|----|
| Legal status | Temporary visitor | Resident | Permanent resident | Full citizen |
| Passport benefit | Original passport only | Original passport only | Original passport only | **New passport** |
| Residence requirement | Varies | Usually minimal | Must genuinely reside | Usually none |
| Work rights | Restricted | Usually allowed | Full rights | Full rights |
| Path to citizenship | Indirect or none | Yes (5-10 years) | Yes (additional years) | Already citizen |
| Typical cost | Low | Medium-high | Accumulated living costs | High ($100K-600K+) |
| Typical timeline | Weeks-months | Months-years | Years | Months (CBI) or years (naturalization) |
| Family inclusion | Varies | Usually yes | Usually yes | Usually yes |
