# Pathway Analysis Template

Standardized template for comparing immigration pathways. Every pathway analysis MUST cover
all three dimensions. Use this template as a checklist — if information for any field is
missing, mark it as "requires verification" and initiate a real-time search.

---

## Guidance: How to Fill Each Dimension

### Dimension 1: Eligibility Requirements

Focus on **gatekeepers** — the non-negotiable requirements that determine whether the user
can even apply:

- **Language**: Is a language test required? What level (A2, B1, B2, IELTS)? Can it be waived
  for PhD holders? Can it be learned within the timeline?
- **Technical/Skill**: Does the program require specific qualifications? Is a job offer needed?
  Are there points for age/education/experience?
- **Financial Proof**: What income or asset level must be demonstrated? How far back must
  financial records go? Is the bar higher for families?
- **Background**: What disqualifies an applicant? Are there countries with stricter screening?
- **Age**: Is there an age limit? Do younger applicants get points advantages?

**Key insight**: Eligibility requirements are the most stable dimension — they change less
frequently than prices or processing times. Still, verify against official sources.

### Dimension 2: Economic Cost

Adopt the **total cost of ownership (TCO)** mindset:

- **Do not quote only the headline investment amount** — this is the most common mistake
- Always include at minimum: investment + government fees + professional fees + ancillary costs
- Flag which costs are **recoverable** (e.g., real estate can be sold) vs **sunk** (e.g., donation)
- Estimate ongoing costs for at least 5 years
- Separate **DIY cost** from **agency-assisted cost** where applicable
- Note any **cost escalation risks** (e.g., property price inflation, fee increases)

**Cost verification priority**: Prices are the most volatile data point. Always search for
the latest official fee schedule before quoting numbers.

### Dimension 3: Time Cost

Time is often the most underestimated dimension:

- **Processing time**: Varies wildly by country and program. Can range from 2 months (CBI)
  to 3+ years (skilled migration). Search for recent applicant reports, not just official estimates.
- **Residence requirement (移民监)**: This is the hidden time cost. "7 days/year in Portugal"
  sounds easy, but "183 days/year" in some programs makes it incompatible with a China-based life.
- **Cumulative path to citizenship**: Map out the full journey — residency → PR → citizenship —
  with realistic timelines at each stage.
- **Flexibility**: Can the applicant maintain the identity while primarily living in China?
  This is a critical factor for users who want a "plan B" without relocating.

**Key insight**: A cheap program with high residence requirements may cost more in terms of
career opportunity cost than an expensive program with no residence requirements.

## Common Evaluation Pitfalls

1. **Comparing apples to oranges**: A $200K CBI donation (sunk cost, immediate passport) is
   fundamentally different from a €500K golden visa investment (recoverable, 5-year path to
   citizenship). Always normalize comparisons across the three dimensions.
2. **Ignoring opportunity cost**: Money spent on a CBI passport could earn returns elsewhere.
   Account for this in the economic analysis.
3. **Underestimating time for language exams**: B2 Portuguese or A2 Greek may take 6-18 months
   of study, depending on the learner's starting point and available time.
4. **Assuming policies won't change**: A pathway that exists today may close or become more
   expensive. Factor in policy risk when recommending long-term strategies.
5. **Not accounting for family**: Adding a spouse and children can double or triple total costs
   and significantly complicate processing. Always ask about family situation early.
