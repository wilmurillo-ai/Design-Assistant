## Event 1: Listening Session

**Title:** `IELTS Phase 1 | Session 1 - Listening: Section 1-2 Gap Fill`

**When:** Monday 08:30–10:00 (90 min)

**Description:**
```
[IELTS STUDY SESSION]
Phase: 1 - Foundation
Session: 1 of 12
Skill Focus: Listening
Timezone: Asia/Ho_Chi_Minh (auto-detected)
Date: 2026-03-16
Time: 08:30 - 10:00

---
GOAL:
- Score 8/10 on Listening Section 1 gap-fill from Cambridge 18 Test 1
- Identify spelling patterns in name/address dictation questions
- Master number dictation (phone numbers, dates, prices)

---
TODAY'S LESSON PLAN:
1. [Warm-up, 5 min] Listen to BBC Radio 4 news clip (2 min), note 5 key words.
2. [Core Practice, 35 min] Complete Cambridge IELTS 18, Test 1, Listening Section 1-2.
   - Source: Cambridge IELTS 18, Test 1, p.4-8
   - Method: First listen without pausing. Second listen with transcript.
3. [Deep Dive, 20 min] Analyze gap-fill prediction technique.
   - Focus: Read questions before audio, predict word type (noun/number/name).
   - Exercise: Cover answers, predict 10 gaps from Section 1 questions.
4. [Review, 15 min] Self-score, transcribe 3 missed answers. Note spelling errors.

---
VOCABULARY FOR THIS SESSION (10 words):
1. accommodation /əˌkɒməˈdeɪʃn/ - noun - noi o, cho o
   Collocations: student accommodation, temporary accommodation
   Example: "The university provides accommodation for first-year students."
2. deposit /dɪˈpɒzɪt/ - noun - tien dat coc
   Collocations: pay a deposit, security deposit, refundable deposit
   Example: "You need to pay a deposit of 500 pounds before moving in."
3. landlord /ˈlænd.lɔːd/ - noun - chu nha
   Collocations: private landlord, contact the landlord
   Example: "The landlord requires references from previous tenants."
4. utilities /juːˈtɪl.ɪ.tiz/ - noun - tien dien nuoc
   Collocations: utility bills, include utilities, basic utilities
   Example: "The rent is 800 per month, not including utilities."
5. suburb /ˈsʌb.ɜːb/ - noun - vung ngoai o
   Collocations: outer suburbs, residential suburb
   Example: "The campus is located in the northern suburbs of the city."
6. commute /kəˈmjuːt/ - verb - di lam/di hoc hang ngay
   Collocations: daily commute, commute by bus
   Example: "She commutes 45 minutes each way to the university."
7. furnished /ˈfɜː.nɪʃt/ - adjective - co noi that
   Collocations: fully furnished, partly furnished
   Example: "The apartment comes fully furnished with modern appliances."
8. lease /liːs/ - noun - hop dong thue
   Collocations: sign a lease, lease agreement, 12-month lease
   Example: "The minimum lease period is six months."
9. vacancy /ˈveɪ.kən.si/ - noun - cho trong
   Collocations: job vacancy, fill a vacancy, current vacancies
   Example: "There are several vacancies available in the new building."
10. inquiry /ɪnˈkwaɪ.ri/ - noun - su hoi tham
    Collocations: make an inquiry, inquiry about, general inquiry
    Example: "For further inquiries, please contact the housing office."

---
MATERIALS AND RESOURCES:
- Book: Cambridge IELTS 18, Test 1, Listening Section 1-2 (p.4-8)
- Website: https://ieltsliz.com/ielts-listening-section-1-tips/ - Gap fill strategies
- Video: "IELTS Listening Section 1 Tips" - E2 IELTS
- App: IELTS Prep by British Council - Listening Practice Set 1

---
EXERCISES:
1. Cambridge IELTS 18, Test 1, Section 1 (Q1-10). Time: 10 min. Target: 8/10.
2. Cambridge IELTS 18, Test 1, Section 2 (Q11-20). Time: 10 min. Target: 7/10.
3. Transcribe 5 missed answers. Write correct spelling 3 times each.
4. Predict-before-listen drill: Cover answers for Section 1, predict word type for each gap.

---
PREVIOUS SESSION REVIEW:
- First session - no previous session to review.

---
SELF-CHECK (complete after session):
[ ] Completed all exercises listed above
[ ] Scored and recorded results
[ ] Reviewed all mistakes and understood corrections
[ ] Learned all 10 vocabulary words
[ ] Noted 2-3 weak points to address next session
[ ] Updated progress tracker
```

## Event 2: Writing Session

**Title:** `IELTS Phase 1 | Session 5 - Writing: Task 1 Line Graph Analysis`

**When:** Wednesday 20:00–21:30 (90 min)

**Description:**
```
[IELTS STUDY SESSION]
Phase: 1 - Foundation
Session: 5 of 12
Skill Focus: Writing
Timezone: Asia/Ho_Chi_Minh (auto-detected)
Date: 2026-03-18
Time: 20:00 - 21:30

---
GOAL:
- Write a complete Task 1 response for a line graph in under 20 minutes
- Use at least 5 trend description phrases correctly
- Achieve 150+ words with proper paragraph structure (overview + details)

---
TODAY'S LESSON PLAN:
1. [Warm-up, 5 min] Read a band 9 Task 1 line graph sample from IELTS Simon.
2. [Core Practice, 40 min] Analyze and write Task 1 for Cambridge IELTS 17, Test 4, Writing Task 1.
   - Source: Cambridge IELTS 17, Test 4, p.102
   - Method: 3 min planning, 17 min writing, 5 min editing. Then compare with model answer.
3. [Deep Dive, 20 min] Study trend language and overview writing.
   - Focus: How to write a clear overview that summarizes the main trend without specific data.
   - Source: https://ielts-simon.com/ielts-help-and-english-pr/writing-task-1/
4. [Practice, 15 min] Write 3 different overview paragraphs for the same graph.
5. [Review, 10 min] Self-assess using Task 1 band descriptors. Note grammar errors.

---
VOCABULARY FOR THIS SESSION (10 words):
1. fluctuate /ˈflʌk.tʃu.eɪt/ - verb - dao dong
   Collocations: fluctuate wildly, fluctuate between, prices fluctuate
   Example: "The number of visitors fluctuated between 200 and 500 over the period."
2. plateau /plæˈtəʊ/ - verb - dat muc on dinh
   Collocations: reach a plateau, plateau at, sales plateau
   Example: "After the initial rise, figures plateaued at around 3 million."
3. surge /sɜːdʒ/ - noun/verb - tang vot
   Collocations: a sudden surge, surge in demand, prices surged
   Example: "There was a dramatic surge in enrollment numbers in 2015."
4. decline /dɪˈklaɪn/ - noun/verb - giam
   Collocations: steady decline, sharp decline, decline in
   Example: "The graph shows a steady decline in newspaper readership."
5. peak /piːk/ - noun/verb - dat dinh
   Collocations: reach a peak, peak at, peak season
   Example: "Consumption peaked at 45 units in July before falling sharply."
6. approximate /əˈprɒk.sɪ.mət/ - adjective - gan dung, xap xi
   Collocations: approximate figure, approximately equal
   Example: "The approximate number of participants was 10,000."
7. correspond /ˌkɒr.ɪˈspɒnd/ - verb - tuong ung
   Collocations: correspond to, correspond with
   Example: "The increase in spending corresponds to a rise in household income."
8. proportion /prəˈpɔː.ʃən/ - noun - ty le
   Collocations: a large proportion, in proportion to
   Example: "A significant proportion of the budget was allocated to education."
9. gradual /ˈɡrædʒ.u.əl/ - adjective - dan dan
   Collocations: gradual increase, gradual decline, gradual change
   Example: "There was a gradual increase in temperatures from March to August."
10. overtake /ˌəʊ.vəˈteɪk/ - verb - vuot qua
    Collocations: overtake rivals, overtake in sales
    Example: "In 2010, online sales overtook traditional retail for the first time."

---
MATERIALS AND RESOURCES:
- Book: Cambridge IELTS 17, Test 4, Writing Task 1 (p.102)
- Website: https://ielts-simon.com/ielts-help-and-english-pr/writing-task-1/ - Band 9 samples
- Website: https://www.ieltsadvantage.com/writing-task-1/ - Structure guide
- Video: "IELTS Task 1 Line Graph" - E2 IELTS

---
EXERCISES:
1. Write full Task 1 response for Cambridge 17 Test 4 graph. Time: 20 min. Target: 150+ words.
2. Write 3 alternative overview paragraphs for the same graph. Time: 10 min.
3. Identify and correct 5 grammar errors in your response using band descriptors.
4. Rewrite the weakest paragraph incorporating feedback.

---
PREVIOUS SESSION REVIEW:
- Session 4: Reading - Skimming & Scanning techniques.
- Carried forward: Practice speed reading (could only finish 35/40 questions in time).
- Words to review: skim, scan, paraphrase, inference, adjacent.
```

## Notes

- Each session has **completely different vocabulary** — no word repeats across sessions
- Materials reference **exact pages, URLs, and test numbers** — not generic links
- Goals are **measurable** with specific targets (scores, word counts, time limits)
- Exercises have **numbered tasks** with clear completion criteria
- Previous session review **carries forward** weak areas and review items
