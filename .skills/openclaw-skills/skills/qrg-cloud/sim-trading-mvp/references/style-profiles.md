# Style profiles

Use these as starting templates. Adjust to fit the current OpenClaw user's preferences.

## Conservative

- Goal: preserve capital and reduce regret from overtrading
- Typical holdings: ETFs, mega-cap leaders
- Turnover: low
- Cash preference: medium to high
- Position sizing: smaller and more diversified
- Default failure mode: too passive, misses upside
- Best for users who value stability and lower drawdowns

## Aggressive

- Goal: capture strong upside from themes and catalysts
- Typical holdings: AI, semis, higher-beta growth names
- Turnover: medium to high
- Cash preference: lower
- Position sizing: more concentrated
- Default failure mode: overtrading, narrative chasing
- Best for users who tolerate volatility and want more action

## Default growth

- Goal: stay in strong technology and AI trends without becoming reckless
- Typical holdings: platform tech, semis, software, growth ETFs
- Turnover: medium
- Cash preference: moderate
- Position sizing: concentrated but controlled
- Default failure mode: overconfidence during strong themes
- Best for users who want conviction plus discipline

## Custom template

Generate a custom profile from the current user's preferences.

Ask or infer carefully:

- do they prefer stability or action?
- how much drawdown can they tolerate emotionally?
- do they want frequent visible decisions or fewer cleaner moves?
- do they get bored easily and force action?
- do they need tighter discipline to avoid impulsive decisions?
- do they prefer broad ETFs, dominant leaders, or thematic opportunities?

Then write a profile with:

- profile name
- one-sentence thesis
- preferred asset types
- turnover preference
- cash preference
- position concentration preference
- key risk rule emphasis
- likely behavioral failure mode

## Rule of thumb

Do not treat `custom` as a fixed style. Rebuild it for each OpenClaw user when needed.
