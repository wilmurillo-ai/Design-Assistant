# Diagnostic Questions: Unlocking the Author's Insider Perspective

These five questions are asked of the thesis author BEFORE any rewriting begins.
The answers are the raw material for the entire thesis improvement.

---

## Q1: Your Role and Access

**Question:** What is your role at the company? Which business units or functions
have you directly worked in or closely observed?

**Why it matters:** Your unique perspective defines the scope of what's credible
to write. The thesis should be anchored in what YOU know that others don't.

**What to listen for:** Specific functions, teams, decisions, or time periods
where the author had direct visibility.

---

## Q2: The Internal vs. External Story Gap

**Question:** In the past few years, has your company made a major strategic
decision where the public/official explanation was quite different from the
real internal reason?

**Why it matters:** This gap between internal truth and external narrative is
typically the most valuable research insight. It reveals the actual mechanisms
driving company behavior.

**Examples to prompt if needed:**
- "We announced a 'strategic upgrade' but internally everyone knew it was because..."
- "The official story was market expansion, but actually we were retreating from..."
- "Leadership said it was about innovation, but the real driver was regulatory pressure..."

---

## Q3: The "This Makes No Sense" Observation

**Question:** Is there something at your company — a strategy, a process, a
decision — where you thought "this is pointless" or "we're doing this backwards"?

**Why it matters:** These observations often point to structural problems, misaligned
incentives, or path dependencies that are analytically interesting. They become the
basis for the thesis's critical argument.

**Scope:** Can be product strategy, channel decisions, organizational design,
pricing, talent management — anything where you sensed a fundamental logic problem.

---

## Q4: The Structural Disadvantage (Specific, Not Generic)

**Question:** Compared to domestic competitors, what is your company's most
significant structural disadvantage in the Chinese market — specifically at the
operational level, not just "localization"?

**Why it matters:** The textbook answer ("insufficient localization") is useless.
The author should know the specific operational bottleneck: Is it approval timelines?
Distribution channel access? Pricing authority? Talent acquisition? Government relations?

**Push for specifics:** "Which exact step in which process is where you lose to
domestic players?"

---

## Q5: The Non-Textbook Solution

**Question:** If you were the China CEO of your company for one year, what is
one thing you would do that is NOT in any MBA textbook — something that you
believe would actually work based on what you've seen?

**Why it matters:** This reveals the author's genuine judgment and lived wisdom.
Even if unconventional or controversial, it becomes the basis for a distinctive
conclusion that stands out from generic recommendations.

---

## How to Use the Answers

After collecting answers, do the following:

1. **Identify the core tension:** What is the fundamental contradiction the
   company faces that the author has witnessed firsthand?

2. **Name a mechanism:** What causal chain explains this tension?
   Template: "[Condition] → [Behavior] → [Outcome] because [mechanism]"

3. **Find the original contribution:** What can be said here that couldn't be
   said without the author's insider perspective?

4. **Map to thesis structure:** Which chapter does each insight belong in?
   - Q1 → establishes credibility (Chapter 1 research method section)
   - Q2 → Chapter 4 critical analysis
   - Q3 → Chapter 4 problem diagnosis
   - Q4 → Chapter 3 or 4 competitive analysis
   - Q5 → Chapter 5 managerial implications
