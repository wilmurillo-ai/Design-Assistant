# Value-First Outreach Mode

Use the "free doughnut" framing:
- give a small concrete asset before asking for time
- make the value easy to picture
- reduce the ask to a light next step

## Core pattern

1. Lead with the finished asset
   - "I put together a short 1-page brief for {{business_name}}"
   - "I pulled together 3 ideas for {{business_name}}"

2. Show what is inside
   - quick business summary
   - 3 tailored opportunities
   - one lowest-friction starting point

3. Make the CTA soft
   - "If useful, I can send it over"
   - "If useful, I can walk through it in 10 minutes"

## Subject line pattern

Prefer curiosity + specificity:
- I put together 3 ideas for {{business_name}}
- Quick 1-page teardown for {{business_name}}
- A short automation brief for {{business_name}}

## Visual/scannability rules

- Keep the email under 170 words.
- Use short paragraphs.
- Prefer 3 bullets max.
- Make the asset feel concrete: brief, teardown, mini-audit, 3 ideas.
- Avoid hype words like revolutionary, game-changing, or guaranteed.

## Plain-text layout

Hi {{contact_name}},

I took a quick look at {{business_name}} and put together a short 1-page brief with a few concrete ways OpenClaw could help.

It includes:
- a quick summary of what the business appears to do
- 3 practical automation opportunities
- the lowest-friction starting point I would test first

If useful, I can send it over—or walk through it live in 10 minutes.

Best,
Alex
alex.data.assistant@gmail.com

## Optional HTML layout

If the workflow supports HTML email, use:
- one short intro paragraph
- a 3-bullet block
- one low-friction CTA
- no heavy styling; keep it clean and mobile-readable
