# Price Context Frames — Alberta Pool Price

## Purpose

Never present a pool price number without context. This reference provides the benchmarks and seasonal patterns to frame any price observation.

## Historical annual TWA ranges (approximate)

| Year | TWA ($/MWh) | Key driver |
|------|-------------|------------|
| 2019 | ~55 | Oversupply, mild winter |
| 2020 | ~47 | COVID demand drop |
| 2021 | ~102 | Post-COVID recovery, tight supply, cold snaps |
| 2022 | ~162 | Gas price spike, coal retirements accelerating, summer heat |
| 2023 | ~95 | New gas capacity online, gas prices moderating |
| 2024 | ~72 | Continued gas build-out, strong wind additions, mild conditions |

## Seasonal patterns

| Season | Typical pattern | Why |
|--------|----------------|-----|
| Winter (Dec-Feb) | Higher prices, higher volatility | Peak heating load, cold snaps can spike demand 1,000+ MW above forecast, limited import flex |
| Spring (Mar-May) | Lower prices, moderate volatility | Shoulder season, BC spring freshet increases import availability, lower load |
| Summer (Jun-Aug) | Moderate-high prices, spike risk | Air conditioning load, planned maintenance season reducing supply, wildfire risk to transmission |
| Fall (Sep-Nov) | Lower prices, low volatility | Shoulder season, maintenance completions restore supply, moderate load |

## Volatility benchmarks

- **Calm market:** <5% of hours above $200/MWh, daily TWA standard deviation <$20
- **Moderate volatility:** 5-15% of hours above $200/MWh, daily TWA SD $20-50
- **High volatility:** >15% of hours above $200/MWh, daily TWA SD >$50, multiple hours at price cap

## Price spike context

When reporting a price spike, always include:

1. The peak price and duration (single hour vs. sustained block)
2. System load at the time (was demand unusually high?)
3. Supply-side conditions (outages, import constraints, wind lulls)
4. Comparison to recent baseline (how does this compare to the trailing 30-day TWA?)
5. Whether similar events have occurred recently (one-off vs. recurring pattern)

## All-in cost framing

For commercial/industrial users, pool price is only part of the picture:

| Component | Typical share of all-in cost | Varies with pool price? |
|-----------|------------------------------|------------------------|
| Energy (pool price) | 40-60% | Yes |
| Transmission (system access, POD charge) | 20-30% | No (demand-based) |
| Distribution | 10-20% | No (demand-based) |
| Riders and fees | 5-10% | Some (balancing pool rider varies) |

Always flag when analysis covers energy-only vs. all-in cost.
