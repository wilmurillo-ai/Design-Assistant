# Commercial Translation

Use this reference when the brief needs to help a client-facing operator decide what to do next.

## Core question set

After synthesizing the evidence, answer these questions:
1. What changed?
2. Why does it matter commercially?
3. What should the operator do next?
4. What can the operator say to a client or prospect this week?

## Commercial implication patterns

Translate developments into operator-relevant implications such as:
- positioning shifts
- pricing pressure or packaging changes
- emerging demand signals
- category confusion or education burden
- new objections likely to appear in sales conversations
- new opportunities for messaging, offers, or service packages

## Recommended next 3 actions

Prefer actions that are:
- practical within a week
- proportional to the evidence strength
- useful in client or prospect workflows

Examples:
- update one client briefing note or weekly update
- test one revised outreach or positioning angle
- monitor one specific signal over the next two weeks
- prepare one FAQ or objection-handling note

## Client-facing talk track

Keep the talk track short and usable.
It should sound like something an operator could adapt quickly, not a grand analyst monologue.

Pattern:
- what changed
- why it matters
- what to watch
- what we recommend next

If evidence is mixed, use conditional language and make the uncertainty visible.
