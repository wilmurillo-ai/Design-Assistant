# Source and Claim Rubric

## Source preference order

Prefer, in descending order when available:
1. Primary company materials: earnings, filings, pricing pages, product docs, release notes, official blogs
2. High-quality reporting with named sources or document support
3. Credible analyst or research synthesis with direct sourcing
4. Secondary commentary and community discussion
5. Anonymous rumor and weak aggregation

## Claim labels

Use clear distinctions such as:
- Confirmed: directly supported by strong primary or well-corroborated reporting
- Reported: stated by a credible external source but not directly confirmed by a primary source
- Rumored / weakly supported: appears in low-confidence or weakly corroborated sources
- Inferred: reasoned interpretation based on available evidence

## Handling uncertainty

When evidence is thin:
- say so explicitly
- reduce the confidence of implications
- prefer open questions over strong recommendations

## Anti-patterns

- Multiple low-quality posts do not equal confirmation
- A press release does not automatically confirm market impact
- Hiring signals suggest priorities but rarely prove them
- Messaging changes can matter, but do not overread them without supporting evidence
