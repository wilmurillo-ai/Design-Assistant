# Copywriting Frameworks

> Reference for structuring landing page copy using proven persuasion frameworks.
> Load with `read_file("references/copywriting-frameworks.md")` during the Copy phase.

---

## Framework Selection Guide

| Framework | Best For | Core Structure |
|-----------|----------|----------------|
| **PAS** | Pain-driven products, problem solvers | Problem → Agitate → Solution |
| **AIDA** | Products needing awareness building | Attention → Interest → Desire → Action |
| **BAB** | Transformation products | Before → After → Bridge |
| **4Ps** | Products with clear proof points | Promise → Picture → Proof → Push |
| **StoryBrand** | Complex or unfamiliar products | Character → Problem → Guide → Plan → Action → Success → Failure |

**Default for solo entrepreneur products**: PAS — it's the most direct and converts well for specific problem-solution products.

---

## PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution

### Structure

1. **Problem** — Name the specific pain. Be precise, not generic.
2. **Agitate** — Amplify the consequences. What happens if they don't solve this?
3. **Solution** — Present your product as the clear fix.

### Application to Landing Page Sections

| Section | PAS Element | What to Write |
|---------|-------------|---------------|
| Hero headline | Problem | Name the pain in the reader's own words |
| Hero subheadline | Agitate | "You've tried X, but..." or "Every day without this costs you..." |
| Hero CTA | Solution | "Start [solving the problem] now" |
| Features section | Solution details | How each feature addresses a specific aspect of the problem |
| Testimonials | Problem + Solution | "I used to struggle with [problem]. Now [solution result]." |

### Example

```
PROBLEM: "You're spending 20 hours a week on invoicing instead of building your product."
AGITATE: "Every hour on admin is an hour your competitor spends shipping features. And late payments are eating your runway."
SOLUTION: "AutoInvoice handles your entire billing cycle — create, send, follow up, collect — in 10 minutes a week."
```

## AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

### Structure

1. **Attention** — Stop the scroll. Bold claim, surprising stat, provocative question.
2. **Interest** — Build curiosity. "Here's why this matters..."
3. **Desire** — Make them want it. Benefits, social proof, transformation.
4. **Action** — Tell them exactly what to do next.

### Application to Landing Page Sections

| Section | AIDA Element | What to Write |
|---------|--------------|---------------|
| Hero headline | Attention | Bold, specific claim or surprising stat |
| Problem/benefits section | Interest | "Here's why 10,000 founders switched" |
| Features + testimonials | Desire | Concrete benefits + social proof |
| CTA sections | Action | Clear, specific, low-friction CTA |

---

## BAB — Before, After, Bridge

### Structure

1. **Before** — Current painful state (the world without your product)
2. **After** — Desired future state (the world with your product)
3. **Bridge** — Your product is the bridge between the two

### Best For

Transformation products where the before/after contrast is vivid:
- Productivity tools (chaos → organized)
- Design tools (ugly → beautiful)
- Learning products (confused → confident)

---

## 4Ps — Promise, Picture, Proof, Push

### Structure

1. **Promise** — The big claim / main benefit
2. **Picture** — Paint the scenario of life with the product
3. **Proof** — Evidence: testimonials, stats, case studies, logos
4. **Push** — Urgency + CTA

## StoryBrand (Simplified for Landing Pages)

### Structure

1. **Character** — The visitor (hero of the story, not your product)
2. **Problem** — External problem, internal frustration, philosophical wrong
3. **Guide** — Your product/brand as the trusted guide (empathy + authority)
4. **Plan** — Simple 3-step plan to get started
5. **Call to Action** — Direct ("Buy now") and transitional ("Learn more")
6. **Success** — What their life looks like after
7. **Failure** — What happens if they don't act (stakes)

### Application to Landing Page

| Section | StoryBrand Element |
|---------|-------------------|
| Hero | Character + Problem |
| "How It Works" | Plan (3 steps) |
| Features | Guide showing capability |
| Testimonials | Success stories |
| Final CTA section | Failure avoidance + CTA |

## Headline Formulas

### Proven Patterns

1. **[Desired outcome] without [feared obstacle]**
   → "Send professional invoices without learning accounting"

2. **[Desired outcome] in [specific timeframe]**
   → "Build your landing page in 30 minutes"

3. **The [adjective] way to [desired outcome]**
   → "The fastest way to get paid by your clients"

4. **Stop [painful activity]. Start [desired activity].**
   → "Stop chasing payments. Start growing your business."

5. **[Number] [type of people] use [product] to [desired outcome]**
   → "12,000 solo founders use InvoiceBot to get paid 2x faster"

6. **What if you could [dream scenario]?**
   → "What if every client paid on time, every time?"

7. **[Product] helps [audience] [outcome] so they can [bigger goal]**
   → "PayFlow helps freelancers automate billing so they can focus on the work they love"

### Headline Quality Checklist

- [ ] Specific (not generic — "grow your business" is too vague)
- [ ] Benefit-led (not feature-led)
- [ ] Addresses the reader ("you/your"), not the company ("we/our")
- [ ] Under 12 words for primary headline
- [ ] Subheadline expands with specifics (how, for whom, proof point)

## CTA Copy Rules

### Do

- **Be specific**: "Start your free trial" > "Submit"
- **Use first person**: "Start my free trial" > "Start your free trial"
- **Include the benefit**: "Get paid faster" > "Sign up"
- **Reduce risk**: "Try free for 14 days — no credit card" > "Buy now"
- **Create micro-commitment language**: "See how it works" > "Learn more"

### Don't

- Generic: "Submit", "Click here", "Learn more" (without context)
- Demanding: "Buy now" on a cold visitor
- Vague: "Get started" without saying what they're starting

### CTA Hierarchy

| Position | CTA Type | Example |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Hero (primary) | Direct action | "Start building your page — free" |
| After features | Reinforce | "See it in action" |
| After social proof | Conversion | "Join 5,000+ founders" |
| Final section | Urgency/recap | "Start your free trial today" |

## Tone Guidelines for Solo Entrepreneur Products

### Default Tone: Professional-casual

- Conversational but not sloppy
- Confident but not arrogant
- Specific but not jargon-heavy
- Direct but not pushy

### Tone Spectrum (user can choose)

| Tone | Voice | Best For |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Professional** | Corporate, polished, formal | B2B SaaS, enterprise tools, financial products |
| **Professional-casual** | Friendly expert, clear, warm | Most solo entrepreneur products (DEFAULT) |
| **Casual** | Conversational, playful, relatable | Consumer products, creative tools, communities |
| **Bold/provocative** | Challenging, opinionated, edgy | Disruptive products, personal brands |

## Section-by-Section Copy Length Guide

| Section | Recommended Length | Notes |
|---------|-------------------|-------|
| Hero headline | 5–12 words | Punchy, specific |
| Hero subheadline | 15–25 words | Expands on headline |
| Feature blocks | 20–40 words each | Benefit-led, one idea per block |
| Testimonials | 20–50 words each | Specific outcomes, not generic praise |
| FAQ answers | 30–60 words each | Direct, no filler |
| Final CTA section | 15–30 words | Recap value + urgency |
