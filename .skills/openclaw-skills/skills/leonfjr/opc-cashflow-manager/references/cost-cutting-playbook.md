# Cost-Cutting Playbook for Solo Founders

Ordered guide for reducing expenses when cash is tight. Cut in order — optional first, critical never.

---

## Essentiality Classification

Every outflow must be classified. When in doubt, classify one level higher (more essential).

### Critical — Cannot Defer

These keep the business legally operational and personally solvent. Cutting them creates immediate legal, contractual, or personal risk.

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Housing/office | Rent, mortgage (if business address) |
| Taxes | Estimated quarterly tax, sales tax, payroll tax |
| Insurance | Business liability, health insurance, E&O |
| Loan payments | Business loan, line of credit minimum |
| Core infrastructure | Domain renewal, primary hosting, business bank fees |
| Legal obligations | Court-ordered payments, regulatory fees, licenses |

### Important — Should Pay on Time

These keep the business running effectively. Deferring them degrades capability but doesn't create immediate legal risk.

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Key software | Primary dev tools, production database, email service |
| Active contractors | Current project contractors (check contract terms for cancellation) |
| Utilities | Internet, phone, electricity |
| Professional services | Accountant (during tax season), lawyer (active matter) |
| Client-facing tools | CRM, support desk, analytics |

### Optional — Can Defer or Cancel

These are nice-to-have. Cutting them reduces comfort or future growth but doesn't impact current operations.

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Premium tiers | Paid plans when free tier exists |
| Marketing spend | Paid ads, sponsored content, conference tickets |
| Learning/growth | Course subscriptions, book purchases, conference fees |
| Convenience tools | AI assistants (premium), design tools (if not core), music streaming |
| Coworking | Coworking membership (work from home instead) |
| Hardware | Non-urgent upgrades, peripherals, equipment |

## Ordered Cut List

Cut from top to bottom. Stop when you've achieved your target savings.

### Tier 1: Immediate Cuts (Optional Items)

| # | Action | Typical Monthly Savings | Recovery Time |
|---|--------|------------------------|---------------|
| 1 | Cancel unused subscriptions | $20–200 | Instant re-subscribe |
| 2 | Downgrade premium → free tiers | $30–150 | Instant upgrade |
| 3 | Pause marketing/ad spend | $100–2000+ | 1–2 weeks to restart |
| 4 | Cancel coworking membership | $100–500 | 1 month waitlist typical |
| 5 | Cancel learning subscriptions | $20–100 | Instant re-subscribe |

**Expected total Tier 1 savings: $200–3000/month**

### Tier 2: Deferral Actions (Important Items)

| # | Action | Typical Monthly Savings | What Breaks |
|---|--------|------------------------|-------------|
| 6 | Reduce contractor hours (not quality) | $500–5000 | Slower delivery |
| 7 | Downgrade software to cheaper alternatives | $50–300 | Feature loss, migration effort |
| 8 | Defer professional services | $200–1000 | Risk if legal/tax matter pending |
| 9 | Negotiate payment terms with vendors | $0 (cash flow relief) | Relationship strain |
| 10 | Switch annual → monthly billing (short-term) | Net negative long-term | Lose annual discount |

**Expected total Tier 2 savings: $500–5000/month**

### Tier 3: Never Cut These

| Item | Why |
|------|-----|
| Domain renewals | Losing your domain is catastrophic |
| Core hosting (production) | Downtime = lost revenue and trust |
| Business insurance | One lawsuit without insurance = game over |
| Tax payments | IRS/tax authority penalties compound fast |
| Health insurance | Personal health > business optimization |
| Minimum loan payments | Default destroys credit and triggers acceleration clauses |

## Survival Mode Protocol

**Activate when conservative runway < 3 months.**

1. **Immediately**: Cut all Tier 1 items. No exceptions.
2. **Within 48 hours**: Review all Tier 2 items. Cut or defer everything possible.
3. **Within 1 week**: Contact all clients with outstanding invoices. Offer 5% discount for payment within 7 days.
4. **Revenue focus**: Stop spending time on anything that doesn't directly generate cash within 30 days.
5. **Consider**: Raising prices on new work (existing contracts honored). Adding a rush fee option. Offering prepaid packages at a discount.

---

## Revenue-Side Alternatives

Before cutting expenses further, consider increasing income:

| Action | Timeline | Impact |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Raise prices (new clients) | Immediate | 10–30% more per project |
| Collect outstanding AR faster | 1–4 weeks | Accelerates cash timing |
| Offer prepaid discount | 1–2 weeks | Cash now vs later |
| Add rush/priority pricing | Immediate | 20–50% premium |
| Productize a service | 2–4 weeks | Recurring revenue |
| Subcontract excess capacity | 1–2 weeks | Lower margin but positive |

## Monthly Subscription Audit Checklist

Run this monthly. It takes 15 minutes and typically finds $50–300 in waste.

1. Export bank/credit card statement for last month
2. Highlight every recurring charge
3. For each charge, ask: "Did I use this in the last 30 days?"
4. If no → cancel immediately
5. If yes but rarely → check if free tier exists
6. If yes and regularly → keep, but check if annual billing saves money
7. Update recurring_commitments in snapshot to match reality
