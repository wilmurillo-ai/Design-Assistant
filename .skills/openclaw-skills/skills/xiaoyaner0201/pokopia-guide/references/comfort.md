# Comfort System & Roommate Guide (舒适度系统)

## What is Comfort?

Every Pokémon has a **Comfort Level** (0–5 stars). Higher comfort = happier Pokémon = better performance with Specialties.

Comfort is affected by:
1. **Habitat match** — Are they in the right habitat?
2. **Preferences** — Do they like nearby items/environment?
3. **Roommates** — Do they get along with neighbors?
4. **Weather** — Is the weather what they prefer?
5. **Time of day** — Some Pokémon prefer day or night

## How to Check Comfort

- Open the **Pokédex** and select a Pokémon
- Look at their **Comfort Level** (star rating)
- The game shows hints about what would improve comfort

## Raising Comfort

### 1. Build the Right Habitat
Each Pokémon has preferred habitats listed in the Pokédex. Build at least one.

### 2. Add Preferred Items
Pokémon like certain items near their habitat:
- **Fire-types** → Campfire, Firepit, warm items
- **Water-types** → Water features, hydrated ground
- **Grass-types** → Flowers, plants, trees
- **Electric-types** → Lights, vending machines, generators
- **Fairy-types** → Pretty flowers, dolls, cute items
- **Ghost-types** → Gravestones, eerie candles, dark spaces
- **Fighting-types** → Exercise equipment, training areas
- **Rock/Ground-types** → Boulders, rocks, sandy areas
- **Psychic-types** → Books, desks, quiet study areas
- **Normal-types** → Comfortable furniture, beds, sofas

### 3. Match Weather Preferences
Some Pokémon only appear or gain comfort in specific weather:
| Weather | How to Trigger | Pokémon Examples |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| Sunny | Burn Pokémon at Sunny Day Site | Cacnea, Charmander line |
| Rain | Water Pokémon at Rain Dance Site | Goomy, Sliggoo, Azumarill, Empoleon |
| Cloudy | Default weather cycle | Most Pokémon |
| All | No restriction | Majority of Pokémon |

### 4. Respect Time Preferences
| Time | Real Clock | Pokémon Examples |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| Morning/Day/Evening | ~6AM–6PM | Pidgey, Chatot, Fidough, Espeon |
| Night | ~6PM–6AM | Oddish, Zubat, Hoothoot, Clefairy, Umbreon |
| Always | 24/7 | Most Pokémon |

### 5. Good Roommates
Pokémon in close proximity affect each other's comfort. **Matching preferences = higher comfort**.

## Comfort Level Effects

| Stars | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| ⭐ | Basic — Pokémon stays but minimal engagement |
| ⭐⭐ | Pokémon uses Specialty occasionally |
| ⭐⭐⭐ | Pokémon uses Specialty regularly |
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Pokémon very active, may produce bonus items |
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Max comfort — highest Specialty output, special animations |

## Quick Tips

- **Don't overcrowd** — Too many Pokémon in one area reduces comfort
- **Variety helps** — Mix different types for environmental bonuses
- **Feed them** — Give Pokémon their preferred food for temporary comfort boost
- **Talk to them** — Regular interaction slowly raises comfort
- **Clean up** — Remove trash and debris near habitats
- **Light it up** — Many Pokémon prefer well-lit areas (use streetlights, lamps)
- **Water nearby** — Water sources boost comfort for many Pokémon, not just Water-types
