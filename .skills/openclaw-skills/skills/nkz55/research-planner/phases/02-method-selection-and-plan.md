# Phase 2: Method Selection and Study Plan

Goal of this phase: turn the clarified research need from Phase 1 into a **concrete research design and documented plan**.

Inputs:

- Phase 1 summary (business context, decisions, objectives, key questions, users, constraints)

Outputs:

- Chosen research approach(es)
- A written research plan that stakeholders can review and approve

---

## 1. Map Objectives to Research Types

Start by deciding the **research type** for each objective:

- **Exploratory / generative**
  - When the problem space is poorly understood
  - Focus on discovering needs, motivations, mental models
- **Descriptive**
  - When you need to describe current behaviors, attitudes, or segments
- **Evaluative**
  - When you need to assess a concept, design, or experience
- **Causal / comparative (lightweight)**
  - When you want to understand relative performance or preference between options

**Output (example table):**

```markdown
| Objective                               | Research type   | Notes                           |
|-----------------------------------------|-----------------|---------------------------------|
| Understand how X currently do Y        | Exploratory     | Early‑stage discovery           |
| Evaluate usability of the new Z flow   | Evaluative      | Post‑prototype / pre‑launch     |
| Estimate interest in pricing model A   | Descriptive     | Survey suitable if constraints  |
```

---

## 2. Select Methods (Single or Mixed)

Choose 1–2 **primary methods** and any **secondary methods** that complement them, based on:

- Research type (from above)
- Timeline and budget
- Access to participants
- Depth vs. breadth requirements

Use a matrix similar to the one in `SKILL.md`:

```markdown
| Goal / need                          | Candidate method(s)                               | When it fits                               |
|--------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| Explore unknown problem space        | 1:1 interviews, contextual inquiry, diary study  | Early‑stage, unclear problem               |
| Evaluate usability of an experience  | Moderated / unmoderated usability tests          | Design iterations, pre‑/post‑launch        |
| Measure attitudes at scale           | Survey, poll                                     | Need quantitative signal across segments   |
| Compare concepts                     | Concept testing, preference test, card sort      | Multiple options, need evidence to choose  |
| Understand real‑world context        | Field visits, observation, shadowing             | Critical workflows, high‑risk contexts     |
```

When you propose a design:

- Explain **why** the method(s) are appropriate
- Note trade‑offs (e.g., depth vs. speed, sample size vs. representativeness)

**Output (example format):**

```markdown
### Recommended Research Approach
- Primary method(s): [...]
- Secondary method(s): [...]
- Rationale:
  - Links to objectives: [...]
  - Fits constraints (time, budget, access): [...]
```

---

## 3. Decide on Mixed‑Methods Designs (If Needed)

For more complex questions, consider combining:

- **Qualitative first, then quantitative**
  - Use qual to explore and generate hypotheses
  - Use quant to validate and size
- **Quantitative first, then qualitative**
  - Use quant to identify patterns or outliers
  - Use qual to explain the “why” behind them
- **Parallel mixed methods**
  - Run qual and quant in parallel when time is limited

When proposing mixed methods:

- Clarify the **sequence** (which comes first)
- Clarify what each method contributes

**Output (example format):**

```markdown
### Mixed‑Methods Design (if applicable)
- Sequence: [e.g., Qual → Quant]
- Role of Method 1: [...]
- Role of Method 2: [...]
```

## 4. Draft the Research Plan Structure

Use this standard structure for the research plan. Each section can be short but must be concrete and decision‑oriented.

### 4.1 Background & Objectives

Summarize the key points from Phase 1:

- Business context and decision(s)
- Research objectives and key questions

### 4.2 Methodology Overview

Describe:

- Chosen method(s) and research type(s)
- Study design (exploratory / descriptive / evaluative; formative vs. summative)
- Any mixed‑methods sequencing

### 4.3 Participants

Specify:

- Target population and segments
- Inclusion / exclusion criteria
- Sample size and rationale (e.g., usability heuristic of 5–8 per segment; survey power trade‑offs)

### 4.4 Procedure

Outline:

- Study phases (pilot, main fieldwork)
- Session format (remote / in‑person, moderated / unmoderated)
- High‑level session or survey flow

### 4.5 Logistics and Timeline

Include:

- Key milestones (plan sign‑off, recruitment, fieldwork, debrief)
- Dependencies (tooling, stakeholder availability)

### 4.6 Risks and Ethical Considerations

Note:

- Data privacy and consent requirements
- Sensitive topics, vulnerable populations
- Any mitigation steps (e.g., anonymization, opt‑out options)

**Output (skeleton):**

```markdown
# Research Plan – [Project Name]

[...]

## 4. Procedure
[...]

## 5. Logistics and Timeline
[...]

[...]
```

## 5. Align with Stakeholders

Encourage the user to:

- Share the draft plan with key stakeholders
- Confirm:
  - Objectives and key questions
  - Methods and sample strategy
  - Timeline and responsibilities

When updating the plan, keep a short **change log** so that decisions remain transparent.
