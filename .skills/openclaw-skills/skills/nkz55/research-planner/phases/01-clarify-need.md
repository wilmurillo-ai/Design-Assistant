# Phase 1: Clarify the Research Need

Goal of this phase: turn a vague request into a **crisp, decision‑focused research brief** that can drive method selection and study design.

Use this phase whenever the user’s request sounds like:

- “We want to understand…”
- “We’re not sure why users…”
- “Before we build X, we need to know…”

---

## 1. Business Context and Decision

Always start from the **decision**, not the method.

Ask or infer:

- **Business context**
  - What product, service, feature, or market are we talking about?
  - What has already happened (e.g., launch, beta, pilots, complaints)?
- **Decision(s) to be made**
  - What concrete decision(s) will this research inform?
  - What options are on the table (e.g., launch vs. delay, A vs. B concept)?
- **Success criteria**
  - How will stakeholders judge that the research was useful?
  - Are there metrics or outcomes they care about (e.g., adoption, conversion, NPS, retention)?

**Output (example format):**

```markdown
### Business Context
- Product / domain: [...]
- Current situation: [...]

### Decision(s) to Inform
- Primary decision: [...]
- Secondary decisions (if any): [...]

### Success Criteria
- For the business: [...]
- For the research: [...]
```

---

## 2. Research Objectives and Key Questions

Translate the decision into **research objectives** and **key questions** that are as MECE as possible.

- **Research objectives**
  - 2–4 high‑level statements of what the research should achieve.
  - Focus on what to learn or understand, not what method to use.
- **Key questions**
  - 3–7 questions that, if answered, would enable the decision.
  - Avoid method language (“via survey”, “via interviews”) here.

**Checklist for good objectives:**

- Are they linked to the decisions?
- Are they outcome‑oriented (what we learn), not activity‑oriented (what we do)?
- Are they understandable to non‑research stakeholders?

**Output (example format):**

```markdown
### Research Objectives
1. [...]
2. [...]

### Key Questions
1. [...]
2. [...]
3. [...]
```

---

## 3. Target Users and Context

Define **who** the research must represent and **in what context**.

Clarify:

- **User segments or personas**
  - Roles (e.g., admin, end‑user, decision‑maker)
  - Experience level (e.g., new vs. power users)
  - Market / geography (if relevant)
- **Usage context**
  - Where and how they interact with the product or problem
  - Devices, channels, environments, constraints
- **In / out of scope audiences**
  - Who is explicitly *not* the focus for this study

**Output (example format):**

```markdown
### Target Users
- Primary segment(s): [...]
- Secondary segment(s): [...]

### Usage Context
- Typical environment(s): [...]
- Devices / channels: [...]

### Explicitly Out of Scope
- [...]
```

---

## 4. Constraints and Practical Considerations

Capture the **constraints** that will shape method choice and design.

Consider:

- **Timeline**
  - When are decisions due?
  - Hard deadlines (e.g., release date, board meeting)?
- **Budget and capacity**
  - Any limits on incentives, tools, or external vendors?
  - Available internal research / design / PM capacity?
- **Access to participants**
  - Existing user lists, CRM, panel providers, intercept possibilities?
  - Legal / compliance constraints on contacting users?
- **Risks and sensitivities**
  - Regulatory, privacy, or ethical concerns?
  - Topics that require extra care (e.g., health, finance)?

**Output (example format):**

```markdown
### Timeline
- Decision needed by: [...]
- Desired research window: [...]

### Budget and Capacity
- Budget constraints: [...]
- Team capacity: [...]

### Participant Access
- Available channels: [...]
- Constraints / approvals needed: [...]

### Risks and Sensitivities
- [...]
```

---

## 5. Phase 1 Summary (Hand‑off to Method Selection)

End this phase by producing a **one‑page brief** that can be read quickly by stakeholders and used as input to Phase 2.

Recommended summary structure:

```markdown
# Phase 1 Summary – Clarified Research Need

## 1. Business Context and Decisions
[Short summary]

## 2. Research Objectives and Key Questions
[Bulleted list]

## 3. Target Users and Context
[Bulleted list]

## 4. Constraints and Practical Considerations
[Bulleted list]
```

Use this summary directly as the starting point for Phase 2: Method Selection and Study Plan.
