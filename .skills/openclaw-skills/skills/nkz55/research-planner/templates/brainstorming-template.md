# Brainstorming Template

Template for planning and running structured brainstorming or ideation sessions with stakeholders and/or users.

## How to Use

- Use to generate a broad set of ideas **after** you have clarity on the problem and user needs.
- Combine individual and group activities to balance depth and diversity.

## Brainstorming Session Template

```markdown
# Brainstorming Session – [Topic]

Date:
Facilitator:
Participants:

## 1. Objectives

- What problem or opportunity are we ideating around?
  - [...]
- How will ideas be used after the session?
  - [...]

## 2. Participants

- Roles and perspectives represented:
  - [...]
- Group size:
  - [e.g., 4–8 people]

## 3. Constraints and Framing

- Timebox:
  - [e.g., 60–90 minutes]
- Any constraints (technical, regulatory, brand):
  - [...]
- "How might we..." framing (if helpful):
  - "How might we [...desired change...] for [...user group...] in the context of [...constraints...]?"

## 4. Activities

- Warm-up exercise:
  - Quick icebreaker focused on creativity or the theme.
- Idea generation rounds:
  - Individual silent ideation (e.g., 10 min sticky-note storm).
  - Group sharing and building on ideas.
- Clustering and voting:
  - Group similar ideas.
  - Use dot-voting or other methods to identify themes or favorites.

## 5. Capture and Next Steps

- How ideas will be documented:
  - Photos of physical boards, exports from digital tools, summarized lists.
- Criteria and process for follow-up:
  - Who will review and prioritize ideas?
  - How and when will decisions be communicated back to participants?
```
