# UXR Consent Form Template

Template for plain-language consent information used in UX and market research studies (interviews, usability tests, surveys, diary studies, etc.).

**Note:** This template is not legal advice. Always adapt to local laws, organizational policies, and review by legal/compliance teams.

## How to Use

- Keep wording simple, non‑technical, and respectful.
- Tailor examples and descriptions to the specific study.
- Ensure translations maintain meaning, not word‑for‑word phrasing.

## Consent Information Template

```markdown
# Research Participation Information and Consent

Study title: [...]
Organization: [...]
Contact: [...]
Date: [...]

## 1. Purpose of the Study

We are conducting a research study to learn more about how people [brief description of topic, e.g., "use our product to manage their finances"]. Your feedback will help us understand what works well and what can be improved.

## 2. What Participation Involves

If you agree to take part:

- You will be asked to [e.g., "participate in a one-on-one interview", "complete a usability test of our website", "fill in an online survey", "keep a short diary about your daily activities related to X"].
- The session will take about [X] minutes.
- With your permission, we may [record audio / video / your screen] so our team can review the session later.

## 3. Voluntary Participation

Your participation is completely voluntary.

- You may choose not to answer any question you do not want to answer.
- You may stop participating at any time, for any reason, without penalty.
- Your decision about whether or not to participate will not affect your relationship with [organization] or access to our products or services.

## 4. Risks and Discomforts

We do not expect any major risks from taking part in this study. However:

- Some questions may feel personal or sensitive.
- You can skip any question or stop at any time if you feel uncomfortable.

If we discuss or show anything that you find upsetting, please let us know and we will move on or end the session.

## 5. Benefits

There may be no direct benefit to you from taking part in this study. However:

- Your feedback will help us improve [product/service] for you and others in the future.

## 6. Incentives

If you complete the study, you will receive [description of incentive, e.g., "a $XX gift card", "a voucher", "no incentive will be provided"]. We will explain how and when you will receive this.

## 7. Privacy and Data Use

We will treat your information carefully:

- We will store your data securely and limit access to authorized team members.
- When we share results, we will not include your name or contact details.
- We may use quotes or examples from the session, but we will remove information that could identify you.
- Data will be kept for [X months/years] and then [deleted / anonymized], according to our policies.

If we use any third‑party tools (such as video‑conferencing or survey platforms), your data may also be processed by those services according to their privacy policies.

## 8. Contact Information

If you have questions about this study, or if you wish to withdraw your consent after the session:

- Contact name: [...]
- Email: [...]
- (Optional) Phone: [...]

If you have concerns about your rights as a participant, you may contact [ethics/complaints contact, if applicable].

## 9. Consent Statement

By [checking the box / signing below], you confirm that:

- You have read and understood the information above.
- You are at least [minimum age, if applicable].
- You voluntarily agree to take part in this study.

[ ] I agree to take part in this research study.

Optional:
[ ] I agree to audio recording.
[ ] I agree to video/screen recording.

Name: ________________________________

Date: _________________________________
```

## Key References for Consent and Ethics

- American Psychological Association (APA) Ethical Principles of Psychologists and Code of Conduct – sections on informed consent.
- British Psychological Society (BPS) Code of Human Research Ethics – guidance on consent and participant rights.
- UXPA International and related professional organizations – general UX research ethics guidelines.
- Nielsen Norman Group articles on UX research ethics and participant consent (`https://www.nngroup.com/articles/` – search for “ethics”, “consent”).
