## 3. Introduction (Script)

Use or adapt this script; read in a natural tone.

```markdown
Hi [participant name], thanks for taking the time to speak with us today.

My name is [...], and I’m a [role, e.g., researcher / product manager] working on [product / area].

**Purpose**
Today, I’d like to learn about how you [brief description of topic, e.g., "manage your team’s projects" / "use our product to do X"]. This will help us improve [product / service] so it better meets your needs.

**What to expect**
The session should take about [X] minutes. There are no right or wrong answers—we’re interested in your honest experiences and opinions.

With your permission, we’ll [audio / video] record this session so we don’t miss anything. The recording will be used only by our internal team for research purposes.

**Confidentiality**
Anything you share will be kept confidential. When we share findings, your responses will be anonymized and not linked to your name.

**Questions?**
Before we start, do you have any questions?

[Pause and answer questions]

If at any point you’d like to skip a question or stop the interview, that’s completely fine—just let me know.

Shall we get started?
```

## 4. Warm‑up and Background

Goal: build rapport and understand high‑level context.

```markdown
### Warm‑up
1. To start, can you tell me a bit about your role and what you do day‑to‑day?
   - Probe: How long have you been in this role?
   - Probe: What does a typical day look like?

2. How, if at all, does [topic or product area] fit into your work or life?
   - Probe: When do you typically deal with [topic]?
   - Probe: How important is [topic] relative to other things you do?
```

Add or adjust warm‑up questions to match the domain.

---

## 5. Core Sections (Themes)

Create 3–5 main sections aligned to the **key questions** from the research plan. For each section, include primary questions and example probes.

### 5.1 Theme A – Current Behaviors and Workflows

```markdown
1. Can you walk me through the last time you [did the focal activity]?
   - Probe: Where were you? Who else was involved?
   - Probe: What tools or products did you use?
   - Probe: What, if anything, was frustrating or slow?

2. How do you usually decide [key decision related to the topic]?
   - Probe: What information do you look at?
   - Probe: Who else is involved in the decision?
```

### 5.2 Theme B – Needs, Pain Points, and Opportunities

```markdown
1. When you think about [topic], what are the biggest challenges you face?
   - Probe: Can you give an example?
   - Probe: How do you handle that today?

2. If you could wave a magic wand and change anything about how you [do topic], what would you change?
   - Probe: Why that, specifically?
```

### 5.3 Theme C – Reactions to Concepts / Ideas (optional)

Use this if you are showing concepts, prototypes, or ideas.

```markdown
1. I’m going to show you [concept / prototype]. Please think aloud as you look at it.
   - Probe: What stands out to you first?
   - Probe: What do you think this is for? Who is it for?

2. What do you like, if anything, about this?
3. What concerns do you have, if any?
4. How, if at all, would this fit into your current workflow or life?
```

Add additional themes as needed (e.g., adoption, pricing, comparison with alternatives).

---

## 6. Closing and Wrap‑up

End by giving participants space for anything you missed and thanking them.

```markdown
### Wrap‑up
1. Is there anything we didn’t cover that you think is important about [topic]?
2. If you could give one piece of advice to the team working on [product / area], what would it be?

Thank you very much for your time and insights today. Your feedback will help us improve [product / service].

If you have any questions after the session, you can reach us at [contact].
```
