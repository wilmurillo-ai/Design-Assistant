# Research Plan Template

Detailed template for documenting a complete research plan that stakeholders can review, comment on, and approve.

Use this as the primary structure when the user asks for a “research plan”, “research proposal”, or “study design document”.

## How to Use

- Keep language concise and decision‑oriented.
- Fill in all `[...]` sections with project‑specific details.
- Link to or attach detailed instruments (guides, surveys) rather than pasting them in full, unless required.

## Key References for Research Plans

- Erika Hall, *Just Enough Research* – chapters on research planning and stakeholder alignment.
- Naresh K. Malhotra, *Marketing Research* – classical structure of research design and data collection planning.
- Nielsen Norman Group, “Research Plans: Organize, Document, Inform” – guidance on essential components of UX research plans (`https://www.nngroup.com/articles/pm-research-plan/`).
- User Interviews, “The Ultimate Guide to Research Strategy” – practical framing of research strategy pillars (`https://www.userinterviews.com/blog/the-ultimate-guide-to-ux-research-strategy`).
