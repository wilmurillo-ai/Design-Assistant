# Focus Group Template

Template for planning and moderating focus groups or group discussions with multiple participants.

## How to Use

- Use when group dynamics and shared discussion will surface attitudes, language, or ideas efficiently.
- Avoid using focus groups for usability testing of detailed interaction flows (1:1 sessions are better for that).

```markdown
  - Keep participants roughly homogenous within groups to encourage open discussion (e.g., similar roles or contexts).

## 3. Logistics

- Location / platform:

- Session length:
  - [e.g., 60–90 minutes]
- Recording setup:
  - [Audio/video recording, consent requirements, backup plan]
- Room setup (for in-person):

## 4. Discussion Guide Overview

### 4.1 Introduction and Ground Rules

- Introduce yourself and purpose.
- Ground rules:
  - One person speaks at a time.
  - All opinions are valid; we’re not looking for consensus.
  - Encourage everyone to share, but it’s okay to pass.
  - Respect confidentiality within the group (as much as is realistic).

### 4.2 Warm-up

- Short introductions:
  - Name, role, quick relevant context.
- Light, non‑threatening starter question about the topic.

### 4.3 Core Topics and Activities

- Topic 1:
  - Opening question:
  - Follow‑ups and probes:
- Topic 2:
  - Opening question:
  - Optional exercises (e.g., card sort, prioritization, sketching):
- Topic 3:
  - [...]

### 4.4 Wrap-up

- Final reflections:
  - "If you could give one piece of advice to the team working on [topic], what would it be?"
- Thank participants and explain next steps and incentives.

## 5. Roles

- Moderator:
  - Guides discussion, keeps time, encourages participation.
- Note-taker / observer:
  - Captures key quotes, themes, non‑verbal reactions.
- Backroom stakeholders (if any) and observation rules:
  - Stay off camera/mic in remote sessions.
  - Avoid influencing participants during the session.
```

## Key References for Focus Groups

- Richard A. Krueger & Mary Anne Casey, *Focus Groups: A Practical Guide for Applied Research* – widely cited methodology text.
- David L. Morgan, *Focus Groups as Qualitative Research* – foundational overview of focus group methods.
- Nielsen Norman Group and other UX research authorities on the pros/cons and appropriate uses of focus groups (`https://www.nngroup.com/articles/`; search for “focus groups”).
