# Stakeholder Walkthrough Template

Template for structured walkthrough sessions with internal stakeholders (e.g., PMs, sales, support) to understand internal perspectives and constraints.

## How to Use

- Use before or alongside user research to gather internal knowledge and constraints.
- Keep sessions focused; avoid turning them into design reviews unless that is the explicit goal.

## Stakeholder Walkthrough Template

```markdown
# Stakeholder Walkthrough – [Project / Area]

Date:
Facilitator:
Participants (roles):

## 1. Objectives

- What do we need to learn from internal stakeholders?
  - [...]
- How will this inform the research or product strategy?
  - [...]

## 2. Participants

- Stakeholder roles (PM, Sales, Support, Legal, etc.):
  - [...]

## 3. Materials

- Product demos, dashboards, internal docs to review:
  - [...]
- Any pre-reading or data summaries:
  - [...]

## 4. Discussion Guide

### 4.1 Current Understanding of Users and Problems

- Who do you see as the primary users or customers?
- What problems are we solving for them?
- Where do you believe users struggle the most today?

### 4.2 Known Issues and Pain Points

- What issues are you hearing about (from customers, sales, support)?
- Which of these feel most urgent or impactful?

### 4.3 Success Metrics and Constraints

- How will we know this initiative or product is successful?
- What metrics do you personally care about?
- What constraints (technical, legal, organizational) should we keep in mind?

### 4.4 Assumptions and Open Questions

- What assumptions are we currently making about users or the market?
- What are your biggest open questions that you’d like research to help answer?
```

## Key References

- Erika Hall, *Just Enough Research* – sections on stakeholder interviews and aligning expectations.
- General product discovery and strategy literature emphasizing stakeholder engagement (e.g., Marty Cagan, *Inspired*).
