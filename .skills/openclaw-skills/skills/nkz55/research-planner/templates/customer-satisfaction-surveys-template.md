# Customer Satisfaction Surveys Template

Template for designing structured customer satisfaction (CSAT) surveys to measure perceptions of products or services.

## How to Use

- Use when stakeholders want a **repeatable, quantitative** measure of satisfaction at key touchpoints.
- Keep surveys short and focused; combine with qualitative research if you need deep “why” insights.

## Customer Satisfaction Survey Template

```markdown
# Customer Satisfaction Survey – [Product / Service]

Version: v[...]
Date: [...]
Owner: [...]

## 1. Survey Goal

- Decisions or improvements this survey should inform:
  - [...]
- Customer touchpoints or journeys in scope:
  - [e.g., "post-purchase delivery experience", "support interaction", "overall product usage"]

## 2. Target Population and Sampling

- Who should receive this survey?
  - [e.g., "customers who completed a purchase in the last 7 days"]
- When should it be sent?
  - [e.g., "24 hours after support ticket closure", "after completing onboarding", "monthly to active users"]
- Sampling plan:
  - [e.g., "random 10% sample of eligible users per week", "all users with constraint of max 2 invites/year per user"]

## 3. Core Metrics

Include a small set of standard questions for tracking over time.

### 3.1 Overall Satisfaction (CSAT)

> "Overall, how satisfied are you with [product/service/experience]?"

Scale (example 5‑point):
- Very dissatisfied
- Dissatisfied
- Neither satisfied nor dissatisfied
- Satisfied
- Very satisfied

### 3.2 Optional Complementary Metrics

- Likelihood to recommend (NPS‑style, used carefully and in context):
  - "How likely are you to recommend [product/service] to a friend or colleague?"
  - 0–10 scale (Not at all likely → Extremely likely)
- Other drivers:
  - Perceived quality
  - Perceived value for money
  - Ease of use
  - Reliability and performance
  - Support or service experience

## 4. Question Sections (Example Layout)

### Section 1 – Usage and Context

- How often do you use [product/service]?
  - Options (e.g., "Multiple times a day", "Daily", "Weekly", "Less often")
- In what context do you mostly use it?
  - [e.g., "At work", "At home", "On mobile", "On desktop"]

### Section 2 – Overall Satisfaction (CSAT)

- Overall satisfaction question (see above).

### Section 3 – Drivers of Satisfaction

Ask about specific aspects that you can influence:

- "How satisfied are you with the following aspects of [product/service]?"
  - Ease of getting started
  - Reliability and performance
  - Features and capabilities
  - Customer support
  - Value for money

Use a consistent Likert scale (e.g., 5‑point from "Very dissatisfied" to "Very satisfied").

### Section 4 – Open-Ended Feedback

Provide at least one open question:

- "What is the main reason for your rating?"
- "What is one thing we could improve to make your experience better?"

## 5. Response Scales

- Choose 5‑ or 7‑point scales and **label all points clearly**.
- Avoid mixing scale types too much in one survey.

Example satisfaction scale (5‑point):
- Very dissatisfied
- Dissatisfied
- Neither satisfied nor dissatisfied
- Satisfied
- Very satisfied

Example agreement scale:
- Strongly disagree
- Disagree
- Neither agree nor disagree
- Agree
- Strongly agree

## 6. Distribution and Length

- Channel(s):
  - [email / in‑product modal / in‑app banner / SMS / support follow‑up]
- Triggering:
  - Event‑based (e.g., after a purchase or support interaction)
  - Time‑based (e.g., quarterly pulse survey)
- Target completion time:
  - Aim for < 5 minutes.
- Avoid over‑surveying:
  - Use frequency caps or sampling to protect user experience.
```

## Key References for Customer Satisfaction Surveys

- Fred Reichheld, writings on Net Promoter Score (NPS) – for understanding loyalty metrics (used carefully and in context).
- Jeff Sauro & James R. Lewis, *Quantifying the User Experience* – guidance on questionnaire design, satisfaction metrics, and scale selection.
- Industry best-practice guides on CSAT survey design from UX and service design organizations (e.g., NN/g articles on satisfaction surveys and UX metrics; `https://www.nngroup.com/articles/`).
