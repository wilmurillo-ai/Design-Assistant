# Ethnography Template

Template for planning ethnographic or deep qualitative field research to understand cultures, practices, and lived experiences.

## How to Use

- Use when you need **deep contextual understanding** of people’s lives, work, or cultures.
- Typically medium‑ to long‑term; plan carefully for ethics, access, and researcher role.

## Ethnographic Study Template

```markdown
# Ethnographic Study – [Context / Community]

Version: v[...]
Date: [...]
Owner: [...]

## 1. Objectives

- What cultural practices, norms, or environments are we trying to understand?
  - [...]
- What decisions or designs will these insights influence?
  - [...]

## 2. Participants / Field Sites

- Communities or groups in scope:
  - [...]
- Field sites and access considerations:
  - [...]
- Criteria for selecting sites and participants:
  - [...]

## 3. Methods

- Observation:
  - Types of events, routines, or spaces to observe.
- Informal interviews and conversations:
  - When and how to approach participants.
- Artifact collection (where appropriate):
  - Photos (with consent), documents, tools, or other relevant artifacts.

## 4. Timeframe

- Duration of fieldwork:
  - [e.g., several weeks, months, or a series of shorter visits]
- Frequency of visits:
  - [e.g., weekly visits, embedded days, occasional follow‑ups]

## 5. Ethical Considerations

- Informed consent and confidentiality:
  - How consent will be obtained and recorded.
- Power dynamics and researcher role:
  - How you will present yourself, manage expectations, and avoid harm.
```

## Key References for Ethnography

- Classic ethnography texts (e.g., Spradley’s *Participant Observation*).
- Applied anthropology and design research works that adapt ethnography for product and service design.
- NN/g and UX research field guides on ethnographic methods and field studies (`https://www.nngroup.com/articles/`; search for “ethnography”).
