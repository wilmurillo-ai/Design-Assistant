## Normalize

The user likes the current design overall. Sections were pulled from different websites, so styling is not fully consistent — fonts, spacing, colors, and component sizes do not always match across sections. Go through the page, identify inconsistencies, and normalize everything so it looks and feels like one cohesive, polished page.

## Research

Review the existing website structure: go through every component and identify what content and information each section needs. Then do thorough research on the topic (e.g. humanoid robots, Tesla Optimus–style, or the user’s own version) and gather everything required to fill those sections: naming, branding, specs, pricing, use cases, and competitive positioning.

## Fill content

Review the entire website again and populate every section with realistic, polished content based on the research. Keep the design clean — no walls of text or clutter. The copy should be concise and impactful, consistent with the brand identity and positioning established.
