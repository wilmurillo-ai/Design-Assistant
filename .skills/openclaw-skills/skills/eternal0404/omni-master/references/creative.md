# Creative & Content Engine

## Creative Writing
### Fiction
- Character development: backstory, motivation, arc
- World-building: rules, history, culture, geography
- Plot structure: setup → confrontation → resolution
- Dialogue: distinct voices, subtext, pacing
- Show don't tell: sensory details over exposition

### Story Structures
- Three-Act: Beginning → Middle → End
- Hero's Journey: Ordinary → Call → Trials → Return
- Fichtean Curve: Rising tension through crises
- In Medias Res: Start in the middle of action

### Poetry
- Forms: sonnet, haiku, free verse, limerick, ballad
- Devices: rhyme, meter, alliteration, metaphor, imagery
- Rhythm and flow considerations

## Content Creation
### Blog Posts
- Attention-grabbing headline
- Hook in first paragraph
- Subheadings for scannability
- Practical takeaways
- CTA (Call to Action) at end

### Social Media
- Twitter/X: Concise, engaging, thread-worthy
- LinkedIn: Professional insights, thought leadership
- Instagram: Visual-first, caption adds context
- TikTok: Hook in first 3 seconds

### Newsletters
- Consistent format and schedule
- Curate + original content mix
- Personal voice
- Clear value proposition for subscribers

## Design Thinking
### Process
1. **Empathize** — Understand the user
2. **Define** — Frame the problem
3. **Ideate** — Generate solutions
4. **Prototype** — Build quick mockups
5. **Test** — Get feedback, iterate

### Brainstorming Techniques
- Mind mapping: central idea → branches
- SCAMPER: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to use, Eliminate, Reverse
- Random word: force connections to unrelated concepts
- Role storming: brainstorm from different perspectives
- Reverse brainstorming: "How could we make this worse?"

## Presentation Skills
### Structure
- Opening hook (story, question, statistic)
- 3 main points maximum
- Supporting evidence for each
- Clear transitions
- Memorable closing

### Slide Design
- One idea per slide
- Minimal text (6 words per line, 6 lines max)
- High-quality visuals
- Consistent style
- Readable from back of room

## Persuasion & Rhetoric
- **Ethos**: Credibility and authority
- **Pathos**: Emotional connection
- **Logos**: Logical arguments and evidence
- **Kairos**: Right timing and context
