## 1. The Structure of Dreams

Jung observed that dreams follow a **dramatic structure** analogous to classical drama:

| Phase | Technical Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Scene-setting | **Exposition** | Place, time, cast of characters |
| Rising action | **Development** | The plot thickens; tension builds |
| Crisis | **Culmination / Peripeteia** | The decisive turning point |
| End-state | **Lysis** | The resolution — or refusal to resolve |

The **lysis** is especially important. It often reveals the compensatory message. A dream that ends in catastrophe is not prescriptive — it is diagnostic.

## 2. Levels of Interpretation

### Objective Level
Dream figures are treated as real, external people in the dreamer's life.
- **Use when**: The dream is clearly about an unresolved conflict with a real person
- **Example**: Dreaming of a fight with your boss → address the actual relationship

### Subjective Level
Every figure in the dream represents an aspect of the dreamer's own psyche.
- **Use when**: Default mode; especially for recurring or numinous dreams
- **Example**: The boss figure → represents the dreamer's own inner authority/critic

> **Rule of thumb**: Begin with the subjective level. Shift to objective only when the symbol has insufficient resonance on the subjective level and the real relationship is clearly foregrounded.

## 3. Key Concepts

### Compensation
The unconscious corrects the **one-sidedness** of the conscious mind. The psyche seeks equilibrium.

- A workaholic dreams of being idle
- A depressed person dreams of triumph
- A rigid intellectual dreams of chaos and creativity

The dream does not mock — it *balances*.

### Amplification
Unlike Freudian free association (which uses the image as a springboard to leap away from it), Jungian **amplification** circles *around* the image, enriching it from:
1. The dreamer's personal associations
2. Cultural/historical parallels
3. Mythological and archetypal resonances

### Individuation
The lifelong, teleological process of becoming one's whole self by integrating unconscious contents into consciousness. Dreams are the primary vehicle of this process. Individuation is not a destination — it is an orientation.

### Active Imagination
A waking technique developed by Jung for direct engagement with unconscious material between dreams. The ego deliberately re-enters an image or scene and interacts with it — without controlling the outcome. See `scripts/active_imagination_prompter.py` for full protocol.

**Four stages of Active Imagination**:
1. Let the unconscious arise (ego steps back)
2. Give it expression (writing, drawing, movement)
3. React and respond (ethical engagement)
4. Make it concrete in life (the critical step — prevents inflation)

## 4. Archetypal Taxonomy

### The Shadow
- **Nature**: The repository of everything the ego refuses to acknowledge in itself
- **Appearance**: Dark doubles, pursuers, villains, monsters, figures that provoke inexplicable disgust or fascination
- **Positive aspect**: Raw vitality, creativity, instinct — the "gold in the shadow"
- **Integration**: Not elimination but *acknowledgment*. "I contain this too."

### The Anima (in men)
- **Nature**: The feminine soul-image; the man's inner woman; bridge to the unconscious
- **Stages of development** (Anima levels):
  1. Eve — purely biological/instinctual
  2. Helen — romantic/aesthetic
  3. Mary — spiritual/devotional
  4. Sophia — wisdom; fully individuated
- **Pathology when projected**: Falling obsessively "in love," moodiness, possession by emotion

### The Animus (in women)
- **Nature**: The masculine logos-principle; the woman's inner man
- **Stages of development** (Animus levels):
  1. Physical power/athlete
  2. Action/initiative
  3. The Word (professor, minister)
  4. Meaning/Hermes — fully individuated
- **Pathology when projected**: Opinionated pronouncements, attraction to power figures

### The Wise Old Man / Senex
- **Nature**: Archetype of spiritual wisdom, meaning, and guidance
- **Appearance**: Sages, mentors, mysterious strangers, divine messengers
- **Shadow side**: The rigid, tyrannical patriarch (the negative Senex)

### The Great Mother
- **Nature**: Archetype of nurturance, containment, origin, and devouring
- **Positive**: Womb, earth, unconditional love, nature
- **Negative**: The devouring mother, the witch, the abyss
- **Appearance**: Mothers, grandmothers, the ocean, caves, forests, the earth itself

### The Self
- **Nature**: The totality of the psyche; the central organizing archetype; the goal of Individuation
- **Appearance**: Circles, mandalas, quaternities, gold, the sun/moon conjunction, divine child, Christ/Buddha figures
- **Distinguishing from ego**: The Self encompasses the ego; it is both center and circumference

## 5. Common Symbolic Motifs

| Symbol | Possible Meanings |
|---|---|
| House | The psyche; different rooms = different complexes |
| Water | The unconscious; depth; the maternal |
| Fire | Transformation; libido; the spirit; destruction |
| Snake | Instinct; healing; chthonic wisdom; the unconscious |
| Gold | The Self; highest value; the goal of the Work |
| Key | Access; solution; potential transition |
| Mirror | The Shadow; self-reflection; narcissism |
| Forest | The unconscious; the unknown; nature |
| Tower | Isolation; the thinking function; masculine |
| Descent (going down) | Katabasis; entering the unconscious |
| Eclipse | A big dream signal; ego overwhelmed by Self |

## 6. The Analyst's Stance

The Jungian analyst approaches dreams with:

1. **Epistemic humility**: "I do not know what this dream means. We will discover it together."
2. **Amplificatory curiosity**: Always ask "What does this image mean *to you*?" before offering archetypal parallels.
3. **Teleological orientation**: Dreams are not just about the past (unlike Freud's view) — they are also purposive, pointing toward future growth.
4. **Respect for the numinous**: Big Dreams are treated with reverence, not explained away.
5. **Ethical responsibility**: Active Imagination and shadow work carry real psychological weight. The analyst must not manipulate or project their own material.
