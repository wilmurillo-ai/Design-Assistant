# Harvard Office of Career Services — Resume & Cover Letter Rules

Source: Official Harvard OCS Resume & Cover Letter Guide (2025)

---

## RESUME LANGUAGE RULES

Language MUST be:
- Specific rather than general
- Active rather than passive
- Written to express, not impress
- Articulate rather than "flowery"
- Fact-based (quantify and qualify wherever possible)
- Written for people/systems that scan quickly

## TOP 5 RESUME MISTAKES TO AVOID
1. Spelling and grammar errors
2. Missing email and phone information
3. Using passive language instead of action words
4. Not well organized, concise, or easy to skim
5. Not demonstrating results

## DON'T
- Use personal pronouns (I, We, My)
- Abbreviate
- Use a narrative style
- Use slang or colloquialisms
- Include a picture
- Include age or gender
- List references
- Start each line with a date

## DO
- Be consistent in format and content
- Make it easy to read and follow, balancing white space
- Use consistent spacing, underlining, italics, bold, and capitalization for emphasis
- List headings (e.g., Experience) in order of importance
- Within headings, list information in reverse chronological order (most recent first)
- Avoid information gaps such as a missing summer
- Ensure formatting translates properly if converted to PDF

## RESUME STRUCTURE (STANDARD ORDER)

1. **Header** — Name (bold, centered), contact info line
2. **Education** — reverse chronological; include degree, concentration, GPA (optional), date, thesis (optional), relevant coursework (optional)
3. **Experience** — reverse chronological; org name, city/state, title, dates, bullets or paragraph
4. **Leadership & Activities** — can be moved above Experience if more relevant
5. **Skills & Interests** (optional) — Technical, Language, Laboratory, Interests

### Education Formatting Options (Harvard degree example):
- A.B. in Biomedical Engineering with a joint concentration in Computer Science
- A.B. in History with a double concentration in Statistics
- A.B./S.M. Computer Science, GPA 3.6 (concurrent degree)

## COVER LETTER RULES

### General Rules:
- Address to a specific person whenever possible
- Tailor to specific organizations — do research before writing
- Keep concise and factual — **maximum one page**
- Avoid flowery language
- Give concrete examples supporting skills and qualifications
- Don't overuse the pronoun "I"
- Use action words throughout
- Reference skills/experiences from the job description and draw connections to credentials
- Resume and cover letter must use the same font type and size
- Check that formatting translates correctly when converting to PDF

### Structure:
1. **Date** (written out: September 1, 2025)
2. **Recipient block**: Name, Title, Organization, Address
3. **Salutation**: "Dear [Name]:"
4. **Opening paragraph**: Who you are, what position you're applying for, where you found it, brief statement of excitement/fit
5. **Body paragraph(s)**: Most relevant experience connected to the organization's mission; quantify achievements; show you've researched the org
6. **Closing paragraph**: Thank the reader; express desire to discuss further
7. **Sign-off**: "Sincerely," followed by full name

### ⚠️ HALLUCINATION WARNING FOR COVER LETTERS:
DO NOT invent organizational details, the applicant's connection to the mission, quantified results, or any experience not explicitly provided by the person. Every claim in the cover letter must come from information the user gave you.
