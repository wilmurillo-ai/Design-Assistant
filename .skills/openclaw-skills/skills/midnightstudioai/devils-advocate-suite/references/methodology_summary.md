# Technical Summary: Adversarial Methodologies

## 1. Dissent Research (Charlan Nemeth)

- **Authenticity**: Natural dissent is more effective than contrived (assigned) dissent.
- **Cognitive Effect**: Dissent "opens" the mind, causing people to search for more information and detect solutions that would otherwise go undetected.
- **The "Bolstering" Trap**: Assigned Devil's Advocates often cause the majority to become *more* rigid and resistant to change because they believe they have "already considered" the other side.

## 2. The Pre-Mortem (Gary Klein)

- **Prospective Hindsight**: Shifting from "what might go wrong" to "what did go wrong" increases risk identification by 30%.
- **Virtue**: It legitimizes doubts and overcomes the social cost of being a "naysayer" by assuming the failure is already a fact.
- **Risk Scoring**: Use the 1–25 matrix (Likelihood × Impact) to prioritize. Scores ≥20 = CRITICAL, ≥12 = HIGH, ≥6 = MEDIUM, below 6 = LOW.

## 3. Working Backwards (Amazon Protocol)

- **Document Length**: Maximum 6 pages.
- **Structure**: Introduction, Goals, Tenets, State of Business, Lessons Learned, Strategic Priorities, Appendix.
- **The Ritual**: 20–30 minutes of silent reading at the start of the meeting prevents charismatic leader bias from anchoring the discussion.
- **The "So What" Test**: Every technical detail must justify its business value. Every strategic goal must map to a concrete action.

## 4. Groupthink (Irving Janis)

- **Symptoms**: Illusion of invulnerability, collective rationalization, self-censorship, pressure on dissenters, illusion of unanimity.
- **Prevention**:
  - Leaders remain impartial at the start of discussion.
  - Assign every member a "critical evaluator" role.
  - Invite outside experts to challenge the group.
  - Assign a different Devil's Advocate for each meeting (rotation prevents the role from becoming ritualized).
  - Use silent individual generation before group discussion.

## 5. Dialectical Inquiry (DI)

- **Origin**: Based on Hegelian dialectic (thesis / antithesis / synthesis).
- **Process**: Split into Proposal Team and Counter-Proposal Team. Counter-team invents plausible assumptions that contradict every premise of the original plan. Both teams then search for a "Third Path" synthesis.
- **Best for**: Ill-structured, complex problems with no clearly correct solution.

## 6. TRIZ for Business Trade-offs

- **Goal**: Resolve "Technical Contradictions" where improving parameter A worsens parameter B.
- **40 Inventive Principles**: Patterns such as Segmentation (1), Taking Out (2), Preliminary Action (10), The Other Way Round (13), Feedback (23), Intermediary (24).
- **Common Business Contradictions**:

  | Contradiction | Recommended Principles |

  | Capacity vs. Complexity | 1, 2, 24 |
  | Profitability vs. Sustainability | 13, 23, 2 |
  | Speed vs. Cost | 1, 10, 25 |
  | Customization vs. Scalability | 1, 2, 35 |
  | Innovation vs. Stability | 13, 10, 23 |
