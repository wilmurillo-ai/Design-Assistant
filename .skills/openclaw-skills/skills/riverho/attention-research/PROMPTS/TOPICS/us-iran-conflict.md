# Topic: us-iran-conflict

> US-Iran tensions, Hormuz strait, nuclear negotiations, ceasefire dynamics, sanctions enforcement, and regional proxy conflict.

---

## Methodology

This is a coercive bargaining domain where both sides maintain escalation capacity as leverage, not as intent. The critical distinction is between military posture and political intent — signals about bombing and signals about negotiation are not opposites; they are the same bargaining architecture from different angles.

The error analysts make: treating Hormuz as a shipping lane. It is a political instrument that functions as a shipping lane when convenient and a threat when not.

Key insight: neither side faces an extinction-level constraint. Both are optimizing for leverage preservation and narrative dominance. What looks like irrationality is usually calculated ambiguity.

## Signal Criteria

**Real signals:**
- Hormuz physical state: vessel traffic, AIS data, insurance premiums — not rhetoric about opening/closing
- Military posture changes: troop rotations, carrier deployments, drone activity — observable, not announced
- Sanctions enforcement: oil export data, ship tracking, secondary sanctions actions
- Negotiation mechanics: who is in the room, preconditions set or dropped, deadline framing
- Internal Iranian signals: IRGC vs civilian foreign ministry divergence

**Noise filters:**
- "Talks are progressing" without specifics
- "Both sides want de-escalation" — rhetorical theater
- US threat language without behavioral follow-through
- Iranian legal arguments about international law — theatrical
- European expressions of concern — no enforcement capacity

---

## Confidence Calibration

- **Confirmed:** Physical shipping data, confirmed military movements, actual sanctions enforcement, direct statements with follow-through evidence
- **Likely:** Cross-source confirmation of negotiation progress, pattern consistent with historical behavior, Gulf state private signals
- **Possible:** Single-source back-channel reports, unnamed official quotes
- **Speculative:** Interpretation of Khamenei speeches without behavioral confirmation

---

## Watch Items

1. Hormuz AIS shows sustained commercial traffic normalization → real de-escalation
2. IRGC or military media contradicts civilian diplomatic statements → internal pressure building
3. US secondary sanctions against third-country companies → enforcement is real
4. Pakistan mediation goes silent → back-channel broken, risk of miscalculation rises
5. Oil price spike with no physical disruption confirmed → market pricing political risk

## Source Hierarchy

1. Shipping / maritime: Lloyd's List, TradeWinds — physical reality
2. US government statements: State Dept, Pentagon briefings — often theatrical, weight by verification
3. Iranian state media: Fars, Tasnim, IRNA — mostly domestic audience management
4. Arabic press (Al Jazeera Arabic, Al Quds Al Arabi): Strong on regional dynamics
5. Western wire services: Reuters, AP — factual events, weaker on incentive interpretation
