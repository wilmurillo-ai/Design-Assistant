# Topic: geopolitics

> Power shifts, diplomacy, bloc formation, trade wars, regional conflicts, great power competition, and the structural forces reshaping global order.

---

## Methodology

Geopolitics is reading who is optimizing for what and why the gap between stated intent and actual behavior is the actual signal.

The error analysts make: treating summits and statements as meaningful events. Diplomatic meetings produce photographs, not policy. Real signals are: trade flow changes, military deployments, institutional alignments, budget allocations, and the behavioral patterns of middle powers.

The key question: **who is this for?** Every statement, meeting, treaty is aimed at: domestic audience, adversary, ally, and internal bureaucracy. Understanding which audience is being served tells you more than the content.

Bloc formation is the most important structural signal. When middle powers coordinate outside existing institutions, the old order is under pressure.

## Entity Framework

| Entity | Weight | Role |
|--------|--------|------|
| Major powers (US, China, Russia, EU) | 40% | Define the system, set constraints, fight over rules |
| Regional powers (India, Brazil, Turkey, Saudi) | 25% | Balance between majors, form their own influence networks |
| Multilateral institutions (UN, NATO, WTO, IMF) | 15% | Rules-based order, increasingly contested |
| Trade and economic blocs (RCEP, CPTPP, BRICS+) | 10% | Economic alignment, often precedes political alignment |
| Non-state actors with geopolitical weight | 5% | Large multinationals, energy companies, tech platforms |
| Other states (swing) | 5% | Swing states revealing pressure points |

---

## Signal Criteria

**Real signals:**
- Trade flow changes — actual commodity and goods flows, not agreement signings
- Military or security commitments — deployments, basing agreements, arms transfers
- Institutional alignment shifts — which institution a country uses for dispute resolution
- Budget and resource allocation — defense spending, foreign aid revealing actual priorities
- Diplomatic relationship changes — new partnerships, broken relationships

**Noise filters:**
- Summit photo ops without deliverables
- "Concerns raised" language without enforcement
- UN resolutions lacking enforcement mechanisms
- "Strategic partnership" language not reflected in actual flows

## Watch Items

1. Middle powers using alternative payment systems at scale → dollar system under pressure
2. Defense spending increases in countries not facing direct threat → bloc formation
3. Trade agreement bypassing WTO dispute mechanism → rules-based system weakening
4. Military basing agreement in previously non-aligned country → structural shift
5. Middle power public criticism of both US and China simultaneously → space creation

## Source Hierarchy

1. Trade and financial data — customs data, SWIFT transactions, reserve composition
2. Military intelligence (open source) — Jane's, Defense News, confirmed movements
3. Regional press — strong for their own regions
4. Named government officials with direct knowledge
5. Academic and think tank analysis — good on structure, slow on new signals
