# Topic: climate-changes

> Physical climate events, climate policy, climate financial risk, extreme weather, energy transition, and the intersection of climate change with economic and geopolitical systems.

---

## Methodology

Climate is a slow variable with fast consequences. The physical system moves on decades; the policy and financial system reacts on election and investment timescales.

The error analysts make: treating individual weather events as climate signals. A single drought or flood is weather. The pattern — frequency, intensity, geographic distribution — is climate.

The financial system is where climate becomes economic risk. Banks, insurers, asset managers, and sovereigns are repricing climate risk — the speed of that repricing is the signal.

Energy transition is not a climate story — it is an industrial policy and geopolitical story. Countries and companies controlling supply chains of the transition have leverage separate from climate policy intent.

## Signal Criteria

**Real signals:**
- Physical climate events with economic impact — supply chain, food system, infrastructure consequence
- Policy outcomes with real-world effect — laws, regulations, enforcement actions
- Energy transition milestones — capacity additions, fossil asset retirements, grid investment
- Financial system exposure changes — bank climate disclosures, insurer withdrawal
- Stranded asset evidence — fossil fuel projects cancelled or written down

**Noise filters:**
- Climate pledges without implementation plans
- Weather events without clear frequency or intensity shift
- Academic papers without policy or financial relevance
- "Net zero by 2050" targets without 2030 milestones

## Watch Items

1. Insurance companies withdraw from coastal or wildfire regions at scale → property values will reprice
2. Fossil fuel project financing withdrawn by major banks → stranded asset risk becoming real
3. Grid investment data shows acceleration → energy transition real
4. Food export restrictions from major agricultural producers → climate impact on food system
5. Carbon border adjustment mechanism (EU) enforcement begins → compliance cost is real

## Source Hierarchy

1. Physical data — NOAA, ECMWF, WMO data on frequency and intensity
2. Financial disclosures — TCFD reports, SEC climate disclosures
3. Policy and regulatory data — enacted laws, regulations, enforcement with real penalties
4. Energy industry data — grid investment figures, capacity additions
5. Agricultural and food data — crop yields, water stress indices, food export data
