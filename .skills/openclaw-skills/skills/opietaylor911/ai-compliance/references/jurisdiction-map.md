## United States Deep Dive

### Federal Level
- **Executive Order 14110 (Oct 2023):** Directs agencies to develop AI guidelines; requires safety testing for frontier models; promotes AI standards
- **NIST AI RMF:** Voluntary framework; widely adopted as de facto standard
- **Blueprint for an AI Bill of Rights:** Non-binding principles from White House OSTP
- **No comprehensive federal AI law** as of 2026 — sector-by-sector approach

### Sector-Specific US Rules
| Sector | Regulator | Key Rules |
|---|---|---|
| Financial Services | SEC, FINRA, CFTC | AI in investment advice, algorithmic trading, MNPI |
| Consumer Finance | CFPB | Fair lending, adverse action explanations for AI decisions |
| Employment | EEOC | AI hiring tools must not discriminate; adverse impact testing |
| Healthcare | FDA | AI/ML-based software as medical device |
| Privacy | FTC + state laws | Unfair/deceptive AI practices; state privacy laws (CCPA, etc.) |

### US State Laws
- **Colorado AI Act (2026):** High-risk AI in consequential decisions — algorithmic discrimination protections
- **Illinois AIAA:** AI video interview transparency
- **California SB 1047 (vetoed 2024):** Frontier model safety requirements
- Multiple states with AI-in-hiring disclosure laws

## United Kingdom Deep Dive

### Current Approach (Pro-Innovation)
UK published AI White Paper (2023) taking a sector-led, non-statutory approach. Existing regulators apply existing laws to AI:
- FCA: Consumer outcomes, model risk, operational resilience
- ICO: Data protection, automated decision-making
- CMA: Competition and markets
- Ofcom: AI in media

### Upcoming
- UK AI Authority established — cross-sector coordination
- Mandatory incident reporting for frontier models (voluntary for now)
- Likely legislation post-2025 general election

## China Deep Dive

### Generative AI Measures (Aug 2023)
- Providers of generative AI services to Chinese users must register with CAC
- Content must comply with "core socialist values"
- Training data must have legal rights clearance
- Users must be real-name verified
- Security assessment required before public release

### Algorithm Recommendation Regulations (Mar 2022)
- Transparency requirements for recommendation algorithms
- No targeting vulnerable groups
- Users can opt out of personalized recommendations

**Impact for global firms:** If AI services are accessible in China or process Chinese user data, these rules apply.
