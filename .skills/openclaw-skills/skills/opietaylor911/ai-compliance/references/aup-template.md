# [FIRM] Artificial Intelligence Acceptable Use Policy

**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** [DATE]
**Owner:** [TEAM] — Information Security AI (ISAI)
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the rules for acceptable use of artificial intelligence (AI) tools and services at [FIRM]. It protects the firm, its clients, and employees from risks associated with AI use including data leakage, regulatory violations, and reputational harm.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who use AI tools in connection with [FIRM] business, on any device, in any location.

## 3. Approved AI Tools

Only AI tools approved by ISAI may be used for firm business. The current approved list is maintained at [LINK].

Using non-approved AI tools with firm data is prohibited.

## 4. Mandatory Training

All employees must complete **Responsible AI Usage Training** before accessing approved AI tools. Access will be blocked until training is confirmed complete. Training must be renewed annually.

## 5. Prohibited Inputs

The following categories of data must **never** be entered into any AI tool, including approved tools, unless specifically authorized in writing by ISAI:

### 5.1 Financial & Regulatory
- Material non-public information (MNPI)
- Investment strategies, recommendations, or decisions under active consideration
- Client portfolio information
- Unannounced financial results, M&A activity, or transactions
- Trading positions or strategies
- Regulatory filings not yet public

### 5.2 Client & Personal Data
- Client names, account numbers, or identifying information
- Employee personal data (SSNs, DOB, salary, health information)
- Any data subject to GDPR, CCPA, or other privacy regulation
- Biometric data

### 5.3 Firm Confidential
- Source code (proprietary or licensed)
- Internal passwords, API keys, credentials, or secrets
- Unreleased product plans, roadmaps, or strategies
- Legal advice, litigation strategy, or privileged communications
- Internal audit findings or risk assessments
- Merger, acquisition, or restructuring information

### 5.4 Operational Security
- Network architecture, IP addresses, or infrastructure details
- Security controls, vulnerabilities, or pen test results
- Incident details or active investigations

## 6. Required Behaviors

- **Verify outputs:** AI-generated content must be reviewed and verified by a qualified human before being acted upon, published, or shared
- **Disclose AI use:** Clearly identify AI-generated content when sharing internally or externally
- **Use enterprise tiers only:** Consumer/free tiers of AI tools do not provide data protection guarantees and are prohibited for firm data
- **Report incidents:** Any suspected data leakage to an AI tool must be reported to ISAI immediately at [CONTACT]

## 7. Monitoring

[FIRM] monitors AI tool usage including prompts and outputs for policy compliance. By using firm-approved AI tools, employees consent to this monitoring. Monitoring is conducted by the ISAI team using automated DLP controls.

## 8. Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in:
- Immediate suspension of AI tool access
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination
- Regulatory reporting where required by law
- Personal liability for regulatory violations (e.g., MNPI misuse)

## 9. Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy require written approval from ISAI and the CISO. Submit exception requests to [CONTACT].

## 10. Acknowledgment

All employees must sign acknowledgment of this policy before AI tool access is granted. Records of acknowledgment are maintained by [TEAM].
