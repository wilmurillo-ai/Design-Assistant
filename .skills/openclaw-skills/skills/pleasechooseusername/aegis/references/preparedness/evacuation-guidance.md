# Evacuation Guidance

**Evacuate only when:** official authority orders it, OR staying is more dangerous than leaving.

## Decision Framework

```
Is there an official evacuation order?
  YES → Follow it immediately. Don't wait.
  NO  → Is your location directly threatened (active fire, structural damage, hostile activity)?
          YES → Evacuate to nearest safe zone
          NO  → Shelter in place and monitor
```

**Rule: Premature evacuation during chaos can be MORE dangerous than sheltering.**

## When You Decide to Leave

### Before You Go (5-10 minutes max)
- [ ] Grab your go-bag (it should already be ready)
- [ ] Charge phone to 100% if time allows
- [ ] Tell your out-of-area contact where you're going
- [ ] Leave a written note at home with your destination
- [ ] Lock up, turn off gas, grab keys

### Route Planning
1. **Know multiple routes** in advance — the obvious one may be blocked
2. **Check road status** via Waze, Google Maps, or local traffic apps (if they're working)
3. **Avoid crowds and choke points** — bridges, tunnels, highways during mass evacuation
4. **Consider off-peak times** — 3-5 AM may be safer than daytime
5. **Have a destination confirmed** — don't drive without knowing where you're going

### Transportation Priority
1. **Private vehicle** — Most flexible, most fuel-efficient for your route
2. **Taxi/ride-share** — If no vehicle. Book early, prices surge.
3. **Public transport** — May be suspended. Check official sources.
4. **On foot** — Last resort for short distances (under 5km)

## UAE-Specific Evacuation Routes

### Air (Primary)
- **DXB (Dubai International):** Monitor Emirates, flydubai, Air Arabia for schedule
- **AUH (Abu Dhabi International):** Etihad, Air Arabia Abu Dhabi
- **DWC (Al Maktoum):** Check for charter/emergency flights
- **SHJ (Sharjah):** Air Arabia and LCCs
- **Tip:** If UAE airports close, Bahrain (BAH) or Oman (MCT) may remain open

### Land (If Air Unavailable)
- **Dubai → Hatta → Oman:** E44 road, Hatta border crossing (~1.5hr from Dubai)
- **Abu Dhabi → Al Ain → Oman:** Via Buraimi crossing
- **Timing:** Cross at night or very early morning to avoid queues
- **Requirements:** Valid passport, vehicle documents (if driving your own car)
- **Oman is neutral:** Historically safer corridor during Gulf conflicts

### Sea (Emergency)
- Port Rashid and Jebel Ali — watch for official evacuation ships
- Monitor embassies for organized civilian evacuations by sea

## At Airports During Crisis

- **Arrive early** — 4+ hours minimum
- **Buy ticket on-site only if** you have no other option — prices may be extreme
- **If crowds are unmanageable** — Wait it out at airport, it's generally safe
- **Contact your embassy** — They may have emergency charter arrangements
- **Keep boarding essentials accessible** — Passport, ticket, cash, phone

## What NOT to Do

- ❌ Don't wait for "just 5 more minutes" to pack more stuff
- ❌ Don't take heavy/excess luggage — it slows you down
- ❌ Don't rely on GPS in conflict zones (may be spoofed/jammed)
- ❌ Don't post your route or location on social media
- ❌ Don't ignore official checkpoints — comply, be cooperative
- ❌ Don't run out of fuel — fill up before crisis or at every opportunity
- ❌ Don't separate from family at checkpoints/crowds without a confirmed meeting plan

## Register With Your Embassy NOW

Most embassies have a citizen registration system for emergencies:
- **US citizens:** [STEP program](https://step.state.gov) — get alerts + extraction help
- **UK citizens:** FCDO registration via gov.uk
- **Other nationalities:** Check your embassy's website for citizen registration

**Register before you need it. Registration is free.**
