# Family Communication Plan

When crisis hits, communication infrastructure may be overloaded or damaged. Plan NOW.

## Designate

1. **Primary contact person** — One family member everyone checks in with
2. **Out-of-area contact** — Someone in a different city/country (local lines may be overloaded, but international calls may work)
3. **Meeting point A** — Near home (e.g., neighbor's house, landmark)
4. **Meeting point B** — Outside neighborhood (if area is unsafe)
5. **Meeting point C** — Outside city (worst case evacuation)

## Communication Methods (Priority Order)

1. **SMS/Text** — Uses less bandwidth than calls, more likely to get through
2. **Messaging apps** — WhatsApp, Signal, Telegram (uses data, not cellular voice)
3. **Voice call** — May be congested; keep calls short
4. **Social media** — Facebook Safety Check, Twitter
5. **Email** — Low bandwidth, may work when other services don't
6. **Physical note** — Leave a note at Meeting Point A if you must leave

## Rules

- **Check in immediately** when something happens — even if you're fine
- **Keep messages short** — "I'm safe at [location]. Heading to [destination]."
- **Don't call repeatedly** — Send one text, wait 15 minutes before trying again
- **Agree on a check-in schedule** — e.g., every 2 hours during an event
- **Designate a code word** — If someone is in danger but can't speak freely

## Template Message
```
"I'm [safe/injured]. Currently at [location].
Moving to [destination/staying put].
Battery at [X]%. Next check-in at [time].
[Any specific needs]."
```
