# Shelter-in-Place Guidance

When told to shelter in place — or when going outside is dangerous.

## When to Shelter
- Official government alert says to shelter
- Explosions, gunfire, or sirens nearby
- Missile/drone alert (you have seconds to minutes)
- Chemical/biological/radiological threat
- Severe weather during crisis (compounds the danger)

## Immediate Actions (Under 60 Seconds)

1. **Get inside** — Nearest solid building. NOT a vehicle, tent, or mobile structure.
2. **Go low and interior** — Basement > ground floor > upper floors. Interior rooms > exterior rooms.
3. **Away from windows** — Glass is lethal in explosions. Move to hallways, bathrooms, stairwells.
4. **Close everything** — Windows, doors, vents, fireplace dampers. Seal if chemical threat.
5. **Stay off the phone** — Unless you're calling emergency services. Networks will be jammed.

## Best Shelter Locations (Ranked)

1. Underground shelter / bunker / basement parking
2. Basement of concrete/steel building
3. Interior ground-floor room of concrete building
4. Interior hallway or stairwell (no windows)
5. Bathroom (small, interior, structural walls)

## Worst Locations
- ❌ Near windows or glass walls
- ❌ Top floors of buildings
- ❌ Vehicles (unless nothing else available)
- ❌ Open areas, balconies, rooftops
- ❌ Elevators (may lose power)

## During Shelter

- **Listen for official updates** — Radio, phone alerts, NCEMA/civil defense
- **Do NOT go outside** to investigate sounds
- **Conserve phone battery** — Reduce brightness, close apps, airplane mode between check-ins
- **Account for everyone** — Check family, neighbors, especially elderly/disabled
- **Stay calm** — Panic spreads and causes bad decisions
- **Drink water** — Stress dehydrates you faster than you think

## Chemical/Radiological Threat (Additional Steps)

- **Seal the room** — Wet towels under doors, plastic sheeting and tape over windows/vents
- **Turn off HVAC** — All ventilation, air conditioning, heating
- **Cover mouth/nose** — Wet cloth as improvised filter
- **Remove contaminated clothing** — If exposed, remove outer layer, bag it, wash skin with soap and water

## When to Leave Shelter

**Only when:**
- Official all-clear from government/civil defense
- Emergency responders tell you it's safe
- Immediate life threat inside shelter (fire, structural collapse)

**Do NOT leave because:**
- It's been quiet for a while
- You're bored or uncomfortable
- Social media says it's over (wait for OFFICIAL sources)

## If Building is Damaged

- Check for injuries, provide first aid
- Check for gas leaks (smell), electrical hazards (sparks), structural damage (cracks, leaning walls)
- If building is unsafe → evacuate carefully, watching for debris
- Use stairs, NEVER elevators
- Cover mouth/nose (dust from concrete/steel is harmful)

---

**Know your shelter location BEFORE an emergency. Walk the route. Time it.**
