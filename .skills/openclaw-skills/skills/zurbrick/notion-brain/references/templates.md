# Notion Brain — Templates

Use these as **content-shaping templates**, not rigid forms. Keep what matters, delete the rest.

## Shared formatting rules

- Start with a 1-2 sentence summary
- Use H2/H3-equivalent section blocks via headings where useful
- Prefer bullets over dense prose
- Keep links, dates, owners, and decisions explicit
- Add `Next steps` only when there are real follow-ups

---

## 1) Research summary

**Title:** `Research Summary — {topic} — {date}`

**Template**
- Summary
- Question or objective
- Key findings
- Implications
- Recommendations
- Sources

**Use when** the output synthesizes articles, docs, videos, tools, or market intelligence into something reusable.

---

## 2) Decision memo

**Title:** `Decision Memo — {decision}`

**Template**
- Decision
- Why this matters
- Options considered
- Tradeoffs
- Chosen path
- Risks
- Next steps

**Use when** the content records a real choice with rationale.

---

## 3) Project plan

**Title:** `Project Plan — {project}`

**Template**
- Goal
- Success criteria
- Scope
- Constraints
- Milestones
- Risks or blockers
- Immediate next actions

**Use when** planning execution, especially work-facing projects.

---

## 4) Project status

**Title:** `Project Status — {project} — {date}`

**Template**
- Status at a glance
- Progress since last update
- Current blockers
- Decisions needed
- Next milestones
- Owner(s)

**Use when** capturing progress or preparing stakeholders.

---

## 5) Article draft

**Title:** `Draft — {working title}`

**Template**
- Thesis
- Audience
- Working outline
- Draft body
- Open questions
- Sources or examples

**Use when** the content is intended for publishing, refinement, or repurposing.

---

## 6) Security audit report

**Title:** `Security Audit — {system or scope} — {date}`

**Template**
- Executive summary
- Scope reviewed
- Key findings
- Severity breakdown
- Recommended fixes
- Residual risks
- Verification notes

**Use when** the note should preserve security posture, findings, and remediation advice.

---

## 7) Financial snapshot

**Title:** `Financial Snapshot — {period}`

**Template**
- Summary
- Account or portfolio highlights
- Changes since last snapshot
- Risks or watch items
- Decisions or recommendations
- Next review date

**Use when** preserving a durable money state, not raw transaction noise.

---

## 8) Weekly rollup

**Title:** `Weekly Rollup — {year-week}`

**Template**
- Week summary
- Wins
- Misses or blockers
- Important decisions
- Key metrics or signals
- Focus for next week

**Use when** creating a high-signal operating recap.

---

## 9) Contact note

**Title:** `Contact Note — {person}`

**Template**
- Snapshot
- Relationship context
- Recent interactions
- Opportunities or risks
- Follow-up ideas
- Useful details

**Use when** a person-specific note will help future conversations or decisions.

---

## 10) Meeting prep

**Title:** `Meeting Prep — {meeting or account} — {date}`

**Template**
- Purpose
- Desired outcome
- Context
- Talking points
- Questions to ask
- Risks or sensitive areas
- Follow-up items to capture during/after the meeting

**Use when** preparing the owner for an internal or external meeting.

---

## 11) Quick capture

**Title:** `Capture — {short subject} — {date}`

**Template**
- What this is
- Why it matters
- Raw notes
- Suggested destination or follow-up

**Use when** the note is worth saving now but can be structured later.

## Compression rule

If the content is weak or half-baked, do **not** force a full template.
Use the quick capture template or skip Notion entirely.
