# Product Hunt Launch Playbook

## When to Use
Use this skill when planning a Product Hunt launch for a new product or major feature.

## Core Rules

### 1. Build Your Supporter Base Before Launch Day
Product Hunt rankings are determined by upvotes in the first 24 hours. Your first-hour performance determines whether you make the front page. Build a list of 50–100 people (customers, friends, network) who will upvote and comment within the first hour of launch.

### 2. Launch on Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday
Weekday launches get more visibility than weekend launches because the Product Hunt community is most active Tuesday through Thursday. Avoid Monday (busy) and Friday/weekend (low engagement). Tuesday tends to produce the highest upvote counts.

### 3. Craft a Compelling Tagline, Not a Category Description
Your tagline has 60 characters to answer "why should I care?" "Find why visitors leave your landing page in 28 minutes" is compelling. "AI-powered user analytics platform" is a category description. Outcome-focused taglines get more clicks.

### 4. Respond to Every Comment on Launch Day
Responsiveness on launch day signals to the community that you care about their feedback. Respond to every comment with a thoughtful, non-defensive reply. The founder being active in the thread is a strong upvote signal for undecided visitors.

### 5. Follow Up With an Email to Non-Converting Visitors
Set up a retargeting audience from Product Hunt traffic on launch day. Send an email follow-up to newsletter subscribers and supporters 48 hours after launch with a "thank you" and any early traction numbers. Keep the momentum going beyond the launch day spike.

## Quick Reference

| Launch Day Timeline | Action |
|-------------------|--------|
| 12:01 AM PST | Launch goes live |
| 12:01–6 AM | Inner circle upvotes |
| 6–9 AM | Broader network outreach |
| 9 AM–12 PM | Social media posts |
| All day | Respond to every comment |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Asking people to "vote" — Product Hunt prohibits this; ask people to "check out" your launch
- Launching without a compelling demo GIF or video — visuals drive click-through
- Not having a dedicated landing page for Product Hunt traffic with a specific offer
