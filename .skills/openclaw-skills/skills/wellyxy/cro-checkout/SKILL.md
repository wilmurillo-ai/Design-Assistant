# Checkout Conversion Optimization

## When to Use
Use this skill when checkout abandonment rate is high or when users are dropping off during the payment flow.

## Core Rules

### 1. Offer Guest Checkout Prominently
Forcing account creation before purchase is the second biggest driver of checkout abandonment. "Continue as Guest" should be offered at the same visual level as "Create Account" — not hidden below it. After purchase, offer account creation with one click using the data already submitted.

### 2. Show the Total Cost Before the Final Step
Unexpected fees revealed at the final checkout step cause 48% of abandonment (Baymard Institute). Show the complete total — including tax and shipping — as early as step one. Price transparency reduces abandonment more than almost any other checkout optimization.

### 3. Add Trust Signals at the Payment Form
The payment form is peak anxiety. Place SSL indicators, accepted payment method logos, and a one-line refund policy directly next to or below the payment form. "Secure checkout · 30-day refund · Questions? Email us" on one line near the submit button consistently reduces abandonment.

### 4. Minimize Steps Without Hiding Information
Single-page checkouts outperform multi-step for most products under $500. But don't hide important information to appear simpler — combine billing and shipping on one page, use accordions for optional fields, and show order summary always visible.

### 5. Follow Up Abandoned Checkouts Within 1 Hour
Users who abandon checkout after entering their email have shown strong buying intent. An email within 1 hour — "You left something behind" with a direct link back to their cart — recovers 5–15% of abandoned checkouts. After 24 hours, recovery rate drops significantly.

## Quick Reference

| Checkout Fix | Expected Lift |
|-------------|--------------|
| Add guest checkout | 15–35% |
| Show total cost upfront | 10–20% |
| Add payment trust signals | 5–15% |
| Reduce to single page | 5–20% |
| Abandon cart email | 5–15% recovery |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Requiring phone number at checkout — it's the field most likely to cause abandonment
- Using a multi-page checkout for digital products with no shipping complexity
- Not testing checkout on mobile — majority of e-commerce traffic is mobile
