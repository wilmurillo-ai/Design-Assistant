# Growth Tactics for Indie Hackers

## When to Use
Use this skill when growing an indie project or bootstrapped SaaS product without a marketing budget.

## Core Rules

### 1. Find 5 Watering Holes Where Your Users Congregate
Your target users are already gathered somewhere: a subreddit, a Slack community, a Discord server, an Indie Hackers group. Identify 5 communities where they spend time, become an active member for 2–4 weeks before mentioning your product, then share it with genuine context.

### 2. Build in Public for Compound Distribution
Sharing your weekly progress (MRR updates, lessons learned, technical challenges) attracts an audience that roots for your success and tries your product. Building in public on Twitter/X creates a warm audience before you have a marketing budget.

### 3. Do Things That Don't Scale First
Send personalized emails to 100 people who have the exact problem you solve. Cold DM potential users who mention the problem on social media. Post in niche communities yourself. These non-scalable tactics are the fastest path to your first 100 customers.

### 4. Create One Piece of Genuinely Useful Free Content
A free tool, template, dataset, or guide that solves one specific problem in your target market creates inbound leads, backlinks, and community mentions. The free offering surfaces people who have the problem your product solves.

### 5. Leverage Other People's Audiences
Guest post on newsletters that serve your target audience. Appear on podcasts for indie founders. Co-create content with tools that serve adjacent audiences. Other people's audiences are your cheapest distribution channel.

## Quick Reference

| Tactic | Cost | Speed | Scale |
|--------|------|-------|-------|
| Community posting | Free | Fast | Low |
| Build in public | Free | Slow | High |
| Cold outreach | Free | Fast | Low |
| Free tool | Low | Medium | High |
| Podcast appearances | Free | Slow | Medium |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Spending on paid ads before finding organic distribution that works
- Posting promotional content in communities without building reputation first
- Trying 10 tactics at once instead of doing one well
