# Build in Public Strategy

## When to Use
Use this skill when using transparency and public progress-sharing as a growth and audience-building strategy.

## Core Rules

### 1. Share Numbers, Not Just Milestones
"We launched" is forgettable. "We hit $1,247 MRR in our first month with 23 paying customers" is a post people share. Specific numbers make your progress real, relatable, and memorable. Share the actual data — people respect honesty over polished PR.

### 2. Share Failures as Prominently as Wins
Build-in-public audiences grow fastest when founders share setbacks with the same openness as wins. "We lost 8 customers this month. Here's what we learned" generates more engagement, trust, and shares than win posts alone.

### 3. Create a Weekly Update Ritual
Consistency is the compounding factor in build-in-public. A weekly update — even brief — keeps your audience engaged, creates a public record of your journey, and gives you a regular forcing function to reflect on your progress.

### 4. Tag Tools and Products You Use
Mentioning tools you use in your build-in-public posts (with @mentions) gets you reshared by those tools' accounts, exponentially expanding your reach. This is free distribution: tag the tools, platforms, and communities that are part of your story.

### 5. Convert Your Audience With Soft CTAs
Build-in-public audiences are warm leads. Every few posts, include a soft CTA: "If you're building a SaaS landing page, I built this tool that might help." Never hard sell. Warm communities buy from people they follow and trust.

## Quick Reference

| Content Type | Frequency | Format |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| Weekly metrics update | Weekly | Thread or post |
| Lesson learned | As needed | Short post |
| Behind-the-scenes | Weekly | Photo/screenshot |
| Milestone celebration | At milestones | Thread |
| Community question | Weekly | Engagement post |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Only sharing positive updates — your audience will disengage without authenticity
- Sharing so frequently you flood followers' feeds and get muted
- Building in public without a clear product or CTA — audiences without a conversion path don't grow your business
