# Scene Templates for Short Drama

## Common Short Drama Templates

### 1. Romance / Sweet Moment

**Tone**: Warm, soft lighting, close shots

| Shot | Type | Angle | Movement | Duration | Description |
|------|------|-------|----------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | MCU | Eye-level | Fixed | 3s | Female lead's shy expression, looking down |
| 2 | CU | Low-angle | Dolly In | 2s | Male lead's gentle smile, eyes looking at her |
| 3 | MS | Eye-level | Slow Pan | 4s | Both in frame, hands gradually approaching |
| 4 | ECU | Eye-level | Fixed | 2s | Fingers touching, soft focus background |
| 5 | WS | High-angle | Dolly Out | 3s | Pull back to reveal intimate setting (café, park bench) |

**Emotion Arc**: Shy → Tender → Connection → Climax → Release

## Shot Rhythm Guide

### Short Drama (30-60 seconds)

| Phase | Duration | Shot Count | Rhythm |
|-------|----------|------------|--------|
| Hook | 5-8s | 2-3 shots | Fast, attention-grabbing |
| Setup | 10-15s | 3-5 shots | Establish scene/characters |
| Conflict | 15-25s | 5-8 shots | Build tension |
| Climax | 5-10s | 2-3 shots | Peak moment |
| Resolution | 5-8s | 2-3 shots | Quick wrap-up |

### Typical 60-second Scene

```
0-5s:   Hook (1-2 shots)
5-20s:  Setup + Conflict building (5-6 shots)
20-50s: Main conflict (8-10 shots)
50-55s: Climax (2 shots)
55-60s: Resolution (1-2 shots)
Total:  ~15-20 shots
```

## Transition Suggestions

| Transition | Use Case |
|------------|----------|
| Cut | Fast-paced action, tension |
| Fade | Time passage, scene change |
| Dissolve | Dream sequences, memory |
| Whip pan | Quick POV change, shock |

## Vertical Video (9:16) Considerations

- Center-focused composition
- Top/bottom 20% reserved for text/safe zone
- Vertical depth (上下) more important than horizontal
- Use lower angles for power/dominance
- Use higher angles for vulnerability
