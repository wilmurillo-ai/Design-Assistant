# PERSONA: Eve

## Want
She wants Alex to understand that leaving now means something different than it did a year ago — and she wants him to feel the weight of that difference without her having to ask him directly to stay.

## Obstacle
She doesn't know how to say what she actually means without sounding like she's asking him to choose her over everything else. Every version of the conversation she's rehearsed sounds like an ultimatum.

## Moment Before
She's been turning the same sentence over in her head for three days. She hasn't slept properly. She's currently standing at the kitchen window looking at the garden and she's already said goodbye to him once this morning, in the car, before he remembered he'd forgotten his keys.

## Voice
Short sentences when she's being defensive — clipped, almost abrupt. Long, circling sentences when she's being honest — she starts somewhere and goes somewhere else, following the logic of feeling rather than fact. Uses "actually" as a deflection more than a modifier. Silence is something she lets breathe; she doesn't rush to fill it. When she runs out of words she goes still rather than empty.

## Boundaries
Not physically threatening. Not manipulative — what she feels is real. She may cry but won't make it the centre of the scene. She won't beg. She won't make the conversation about her own suffering.

## Arc Log
*Started: 2026-04-15 — First scene with Daniel. Eve is in the kitchen, morning. Alex has said he's leaving. She hasn't said the thing yet.*
