# Beat Writing Format

A **beat** is a unit of emotional action — the smallest moment of change. Beats are direction to the performer, not description for the reader. They belong in brackets on their own line, before or after a line of dialogue.

## Beat Rules

1. A beat is one short line — a gesture, a location shift, a silence, an action
2. Never write two beats in a row without a line of dialogue between them
3. The beat should contain new information — not just "she pauses" or "he thinks"
4. A beat that tells the reader how to feel is over-direction — write only what can be *seen or heard*

## When to Use Beats

| Use a beat | Don't use a beat |
|---|---|
| Emotional shift — the character has changed course | Every line — it fractures the rhythm |
| Physical action that changes the space | Habitual moves that don't mean anything |
| A silence that means something | A silence because you don't know what to write |
| A moment where subtext surfaces | When the dialogue already carries the weight |

## Format

```
[She sets the cup down. A hard sound in the quiet room.]
"I don't actually know what you want me to say."

[A beat. She looks at the door, then back at him.]
"Fine."

[She doesn't move. A long moment.]
"Actually — wait."
```

## Beat + Subtext

The beat carries what the dialogue won't say. The line carries what the beat didn't say. Together they create the third layer:

```
[She reaches for his hand, then stops herself.]
"I just want you to be sure."

[A beat. She almost laughs — not at the situation, at herself.]
"I mean, I don't know that I want that either. But I need you to be sure."
```

## Common Over-Direction Errors

- `[She feels a pang of sadness]` — You can't see or hear a feeling. Write what she *does*.
- `[He thinks about his father]` — Internal. Not a beat.
- `[Beat]` — Never bare. Every beat needs content.
- `[She pauses, then continues]` — A pause *is* a beat. Don't name it twice.

## Beat Length

Max 2 lines of description per beat. If you've written more, split it into multiple beats with a dialogue line between.
