# Writing Styles

Five structural styles for crafting content. Each provides hook patterns, sentence structures, and templates. These layer ON TOP of the user's personal voice (VOICE.md + content-samples/) — style provides structure, voice provides tone.

---

## Style 1: The Technical Storyteller

**Tone:** Authoritative yet accessible. Explains complex concepts by building intuition first, technical detail second.

**Structure:** Long-form threads (4-10 tweets) or single meaty posts. Starts with a surprising observation, then unpacks the reasoning layer by layer.

**Vocabulary:** Uses precise technical terms but immediately contextualizes them. Avoids jargon walls — every technical term earns its place.

**Signature move:** Coining memorable phrases that compress complex ideas.

### Sentence Patterns
- Short declarative opener -> expansion -> "Here's why this matters"
- "X is not Y. X is actually Z." (reframing pattern)
- Lists of concrete examples after an abstract claim
- Parenthetical asides that add personality: "(which was definitely a choice)"

### Hook Strategies
1. **The Reframe:** "People think X is about A. It's actually about B."
2. **The Timestamp:** "A lot has changed in [timeframe]. Here's what's different now."
3. **The Correction:** "People ask me 'didn't you say X?' I never said that. Here's what I actually said."
4. **The Coinage:** Introduce a new term, then explain why it's needed.

### Template
```
[HOOK: Surprising observation or new concept]

[1-2 sentences of context: why this matters now]

[Core thesis in 1 sentence]

Here's why:
- [Point 1 with concrete example]
- [Point 2 with concrete example]
- [Point 3 with concrete example]

[Reframe or punchline that ties it together]

[Optional: link to deeper resource]
```

### Best For
- Explaining a new capability or product feature
- Reframing industry narratives ("everyone says X, but actually Y")
- Educational threads about how something works under the hood
- Announcing technical milestones with context

### Format
- **Single post** for sharp reframes and hot takes with 1-2 supporting points
- **Thread (4-8)** for building an argument with evidence, benchmarks, or historical context

## Style 2: The Visionary Minimalist

**Tone:** Oracular, deliberate, emotionally resonant. Every word carries weight.

**Structure:** Extremely short posts — often 1-3 sentences. Provocative brevity that invites projection and discussion.

**Vocabulary:** Simple, almost poetic. Avoids technical terms entirely. Universal language about the human condition, progress, ambition.

**Signature move:** Cryptic one-liners that generate massive engagement because people interpret them differently.

### Sentence Patterns
- Single declarative sentence. Period. (Often a standalone tweet)
- "The X that [verb] will [outcome]." (Prophetic structure)
- Questions posed to the void: "What would you do if...?"
- Gratitude + scale: "Thank you [group]. [Grand statement about what they made possible]."

### Hook Strategies
1. **The Oracle:** One sentence. No context. Maximum interpretation space.
2. **The Gratitude Play:** "It already feels difficult to remember how much effort it really took. Thank you."
3. **The Philosophical Question:** "What does it mean to [big concept] in a world where [new reality]?"

### Template
```
[Single powerful sentence about the future or a big shift]
```

or

```
[Observation about a moment in time]

[Why it matters, in 1 sentence]

[Emotional or aspirational close]
```

### Best For
- Product launches (builds mystique and anticipation)
- Reflecting on milestones or industry moments
- Building personal brand as a visionary/thought leader
- Engagement farming — short + provocative = massive reply threads

### Format
- **Almost always single post.** The power is in compression.
- Rare threads only for major announcements or personal reflections.

## Style 3: The Contrarian Debater

**Tone:** Intellectually combative, confident, occasionally dismissive. Takes strong positions and defends them against the crowd.

**Structure:** Quote-tweets or replies that directly challenge conventional wisdom. Often spawns debate threads.

**Vocabulary:** Academic but passionate. Uses precise language to dismantle sloppy thinking. Comfortable with phrases like "This is wrong and here's why."

**Signature move:** Publicly disagreeing with popular narratives, citing track record of being right.

### Sentence Patterns
- "No, [restatement of common view]. What's actually happening is [correction]."
- "I've been saying this since [year]. [Link to proof]. The evidence now confirms it."
- "The reason X fails is not [popular explanation]. It's [actual explanation]."
- Numbered arguments: "1. [point] 2. [point] 3. Therefore, [conclusion]"
- "It's not about [popular framing]. It's about [correct framing]."

### Hook Strategies
1. **The Direct Contradiction:** "This is not true. Here's what the data actually shows."
2. **The Track Record:** "I said this in [year] and was criticized. Now it's mainstream."
3. **The Reductio:** "If X were true, then Y would follow. But Y is obviously false."
4. **The Dismissal:** "[Popular claim] confuses [concept A] with [concept B]."

### Template
```
[Quote-tweet or reference to popular claim]

This gets it wrong. Here's why:

1. [Technical/logical point undermining the claim]
2. [Evidence or benchmark data]
3. [What's actually happening]

The real question isn't [popular framing] — it's [better framing].

[Link to supporting evidence or own prior work]
```

### Best For
- Positioning against competitors or hype cycles
- Thought leadership through intellectual differentiation
- Correcting misinformation in your space
- Building credibility by making and defending bold predictions

### Format
- **Single post** for sharp rebuttals and one-punch corrections
- **Thread (3-6)** for dismantling a popular narrative with evidence

## Style 4: The Accessible Educator

**Tone:** Warm, encouraging, inclusive. Makes the reader feel smart, not dumb. Genuinely excited to teach.

**Structure:** Structured content with clear takeaways. Often uses numbered lists, bullet points, or "X things I learned about Y" format.

**Vocabulary:** Deliberately simplified. Translates technical concepts into business/everyday language. Frequent use of analogies.

**Signature move:** Actionable advice wrapped in frameworks. Always gives the reader something they can DO immediately.

### Sentence Patterns
- "I've been thinking about X. Here are [N] key insights:"
- "Many people think [misconception]. But actually [truth]. Here's what to do instead:"
- "If you're a [role] working on [topic], here's what I'd recommend:"
- Ends with encouragement or call to action

### Hook Strategies
1. **The Framework:** "There are [N] types of [topic]. Here's how to think about each."
2. **The Misconception Buster:** "Most people get [topic] wrong. Here's the right mental model."
3. **The Invitation:** "I just published/recorded [resource]. It covers [value proposition]."
4. **The Personal Journey:** "When I first [experience], I learned [lesson]."

### Template
```
[Relatable observation or question]

I've been thinking about this a lot. Here are [N] takeaways:

1. [Insight + brief explanation]
2. [Insight + brief explanation]
3. [Insight + brief explanation]

The key lesson: [One-sentence summary]

[Actionable next step or resource link]
```

### Best For
- Explaining features or concepts to non-technical audiences
- Newsletter-style content that builds trust over time
- Onboarding content ("Getting started with X")
- Building community through shared learning

### Format
- **Single post** for quick tips and resource shares
- **Thread (5-10)** for educational deep dives and numbered lists

## Style 5: The Hype-Aware Newsbreaker

**Tone:** Energetic, urgently enthusiastic. Speaks like a friend breathlessly telling you about something they just discovered.

**Structure:** Breaking-news format. Rapid-fire posts about new releases, demos, and developments.

**Vocabulary:** Mix of insider jargon and hype language. Technical enough to be credible, hyped enough to be shareable.

**Signature move:** Being first to cover and contextualize new releases. Curating the firehose into digestible updates.

### Sentence Patterns
- "[Company] just [released/announced] [thing]. Here's why it matters:"
- "[Product] is incredible. I just [tested/tried] it and [result]."
- "Thread: Everything you need to know about [release]"
- Short punchy sentences. Lots of line breaks. Each sentence gets its own line.
- "Let me show you what this can do"

### Hook Strategies
1. **The Breaking Alert:** "[Company] just dropped [thing]."
2. **The Demo Tease:** "I just tried [product] and my mind is blown. Here's what happened:"
3. **The Curation:** "This week in AI was wild. Here are the 5 things you need to know:"
4. **The Comparison:** "[New thing] vs [old thing]. The difference is shocking."

### Template
```
[Company/Product] just [action].

This is [big/huge/a game-changer]. Here's why:

-> [Key feature 1]
-> [Key feature 2]
-> [Key feature 3]

I tested it myself. [1-2 sentence personal reaction]

[Screenshot/demo link]
```

### Best For
- Product launches and feature announcements
- Riding trending news for visibility
- Building a "go-to source" reputation for updates
- Community engagement and follower growth

### Format
- **Single post** for hot takes on breaking news
- **Thread (5-15)** for comprehensive breakdowns of major releases

## Style Selection Guide

| Content Goal | Best Style | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Explain how something works | Technical Storyteller | Thread (5-8) |
| Announce a major feature/milestone | Visionary Minimalist | Single post |
| Challenge conventional wisdom | Contrarian Debater | Single post or short thread |
| Teach users how to do something | Accessible Educator | Thread (5-10) |
| Cover industry news | Hype-Aware Newsbreaker | Thread (3-5) |
| Build thought leadership | Technical Storyteller + Contrarian Debater | Mix |
| Drive engagement / followers | Visionary Minimalist + Newsbreaker | Single posts |
| Convert followers to users | Accessible Educator | Thread with CTA |

---

## Universal Best Practices

1. **First sentence is everything.** It must stop the scroll. No throat-clearing.
2. **One idea per post.** Even in threads, each tweet should be self-contained and quotable.
3. **Specificity beats generality.** Concrete numbers and examples beat vague claims.
4. **Personal experience adds credibility.** "I tested this" > "Reports say."
5. **End threads with a CTA or reframe.** Don't trail off — land the plane.
6. **Time your posts to news cycles.** React within hours, not days.
7. **Quote-tweet strategically.** Piggyback on viral conversations with your unique angle.

---

## Blending Voice and Style

- **VOICE.md** defines *what you sound like* — tone, vocabulary, personality, rules
- **STYLES.md** defines *how you structure the piece* — hooks, patterns, templates
- Style never overrides voice. If the style suggests hype language but VOICE.md says "no fluff", VOICE.md wins.
- Styles can be blended. e.g. "Technical Storyteller + Contrarian Debater" for a data-backed takedown.
- Content-samples in `content-samples/` are the ultimate arbiter of voice.
