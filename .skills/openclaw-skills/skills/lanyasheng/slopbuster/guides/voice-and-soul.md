# Voice and Soul Guide

Avoiding AI patterns is only half the job. Sterile, voiceless writing is just as detectable as slop — it's just detected by a different classifier. Good writing has a human behind it.

---

## Signs of Soulless Writing (Even If "Clean")

You've stripped all the AI patterns. But the text still feels generated because:

- Every sentence is the same length and structure
- No opinions, just neutral reporting
- No acknowledgment of uncertainty or mixed feelings
- No first-person perspective when it would be natural
- No humor, no edge, no personality
- Reads like a Wikipedia article or a press release
- Too smooth — every paragraph resolves neatly
- Abstract industry analysis without personal stakes

**Removing AI patterns without adding soul produces text that's technically clean but obviously synthetic.** The goal isn't just subtraction — it's replacement.

---

## How to Add Voice

### Have Opinions
Don't just report facts — react to them. "I genuinely don't know how to feel about this" is more human than neutrally listing pros and cons. Real writers have takes.

### Vary Your Rhythm
Short punchy sentences. Then longer ones that take their time getting where they're going. Mix it up. A paragraph of identically structured sentences is a dead giveaway even after you've removed every AI vocabulary word.

### Acknowledge Complexity
Real humans have mixed feelings. "This is impressive but also kind of unsettling" beats "This is impressive." Ambivalence is human; clean resolution is algorithmic.

### Use "I" When It Fits
First person isn't unprofessional — it's honest. "I keep coming back to..." or "Here's what bugs me..." signals a real person thinking. Not every context allows it, but when it fits, use it.

### Let Some Mess In
Perfect structure feels algorithmic. Tangents, asides, and half-formed thoughts are human. Not every paragraph needs a topic sentence. Not every argument needs to resolve.

### Be Specific About Feelings
Not "this is concerning" but "there's something unsettling about watching metrics climb while nobody's sure what's actually happening." Specificity is the antidote to AI vagueness.

### Open With Friction
Stakes should be clear by paragraph one. A lived moment of doubt, failure, surprise, or frustration. Not background context. Not a definition. Not "In today's..."

### End by Extending, Not Summarizing
Never recap what you just said. Leave the reader with a new angle, a question, a forward push. The worst thing a conclusion can do is collapse the energy you just built.

## Before and After

### Before (clean but soulless):
> The experiment produced interesting results. The agents generated 3 million lines of code. Some developers were impressed while others were skeptical. The implications remain unclear.

### After (has a pulse):
> I genuinely don't know how to feel about this one. 3 million lines of code, generated while the humans presumably slept. Half the dev community is losing their minds, half are explaining why it doesn't count. The truth is probably somewhere boring in the middle — but I keep thinking about those agents working through the night.

## Voice Injection Checklist

After removing AI patterns, check each paragraph:

- [ ] Does it sound like a specific person wrote it, or could any AI have produced it?
- [ ] Is there at least one opinion, reaction, or moment of uncertainty?
- [ ] Does sentence rhythm actually vary? (Count word lengths — if three consecutive sentences are within 2 words of each other, fix it)
- [ ] Are examples specific (company names, numbers, dates) or vague ("many companies," "various industries")?
- [ ] Would you actually say this out loud to a colleague?
- [ ] Does the paragraph resolve too neatly? (If yes, let some tension carry forward)

## Context-Specific Voice

### Marketing / Blog Posts
Conversational. Contractions. Short paragraphs. Personal anecdotes welcome. Opinions expected. Humor appreciated.

### Technical Documentation
Clear and direct. Skip the personality — prioritize accuracy and findability. But still: use active voice, vary sentence length, and cut filler.

### Thought Leadership
Strong takes required. "Here's what I think and here's why." Personal experience as evidence. Contrarian positions welcome if well-argued.

### Business / Corporate
More formal, but not robotic. Contractions are fine. "We" instead of passive constructions. Specific numbers instead of "significant growth."

### Academic
See `rules/academic.md` for the full treatment. Voice comes through methodology choices, interpretation framing, and honest acknowledgment of limitations — not through casual language.

## The Two-Pass Soul Check

**Pass 1:** Remove AI patterns using the rule files.

**Pass 2:** Read the result aloud. Ask:
- "What still makes this obviously AI-generated?"
- List the remaining tells (brief bullets).
- Then revise to eliminate those tells.

This second pass catches the patterns that emerge FROM the cleanup — the sterile, committee-written feel that replacing "delve" with "explore" doesn't fix. The second pass is where voice actually enters.
