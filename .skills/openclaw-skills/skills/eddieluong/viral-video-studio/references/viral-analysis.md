# Viral Video Analysis Framework

## Why Videos Go Viral — The 7 Triggers

Every viral video hits at least 3 of these 7 psychological triggers:

| # | Trigger | What it does | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Surprise** | Breaks expectation | Tiny cat beats giant lion |
| 2 | **Relatability** | "This is me" feeling | Overconfident plan that fails |
| 3 | **Curiosity Gap** | Must know what happens | "Watch until the end..." |
| 4 | **Social Currency** | "I want to share this" | Makes sender look funny/smart |
| 5 | **Empathy** | Feel for the character | Character tries so hard, fails |
| 6 | **Awe** | Impressed/amazed | Stunning AI art quality |
| 7 | **Identity** | "This is my group" | Tag someone who does this |

## Deep Analysis Protocol

When given a reference video, analyze ALL of the following:

### 1. First 3 Seconds Analysis
```
- What is shown in frame 1? (visual hook)
- What text/caption appears? (text hook)
- What sound plays? (audio hook)
- Which of the 7 triggers is activated immediately?
- Why would a thumb STOP scrolling here?
```

### 2. Structural Deconstruction
```
Timestamp | What's happening | Emotional beat | Algorithm metric served
0-3s      | [describe]       | [emotion]      | [completion/rewatch/share/comment]
3-8s      | [describe]       | [emotion]      | [metric]
8-15s     | [describe]       | [emotion]      | [metric]
15-25s    | [describe]       | [emotion]      | [metric]
25-35s    | [describe]       | [emotion]      | [metric]
35-end    | [describe]       | [emotion]      | [metric]
```

### 3. The "Why" Layer Analysis
```
SURFACE LEVEL (what you see):
  "[Tiny hamster tries to rob a bank]"

EMOTIONAL LEVEL (what you feel):
  "[Rooting for the underdog + anticipating failure = tension + release]"

PSYCHOLOGICAL LEVEL (why you share):
  "[Everyone has felt overconfident then failed — deeply relatable]"
  "[Tagging friends = social bonding through shared humor]"

IDENTITY LEVEL (why you follow):
  "[Character represents 'me' — always trying, always failing, never giving up]"
```

### 4. Retention Mechanics
```
- LOOP FACTOR: Does the end connect back to the beginning? (rewatchable)
- CLIFFHANGER: Does it end before resolution? (forces Part 2 watch)
- REVELATION: Is info revealed gradually? (keeps watching to find out)
- PATTERN INTERRUPT: Does something unexpected break the flow? (resets attention)
```

### 5. Comment Bait Analysis
```
- What question does the video implicitly ask?
- What camps will viewers naturally fall into? (debate driver)
- What experience does it invite viewers to share?
- Is there a "tag someone" natural moment?
```

### 6. Share Mechanics
```
- Who would you send this to? (target relationship)
- What does sharing this say about the sender? (social currency)
- Is there a specific moment that's "screenshot-able" or "clip-able"?
```

## Viral Pattern Library

Common patterns found in top-performing AI comedy videos:

### Pattern 1: The Impossible Mission
- Tiny/weak character takes on impossible challenge
- Confident start → escalating problems → spectacular failure
- Emotional core: **Overconfidence is relatable (we've all been there)**
- Key to success: failure must be EARNED (build tension before releasing it)

### Pattern 2: The Role Reversal
- Weak character ends up in position of power over strong character
- Setup: strong character unaware of danger
- Payoff: power dynamic flipped unexpectedly
- Emotional core: **Wish fulfillment for underdogs (everyone roots for the little guy)**

### Pattern 3: The Misunderstanding
- Character confidently does something completely wrong
- Viewer sees the mistake, character doesn't
- Tension builds as disaster approaches
- Emotional core: **Dramatic irony — viewer feels smart + nervous + amused**

### Pattern 4: The Unexpected Competence
- Character expected to fail → surprisingly succeeds
- But success causes a new, bigger problem
- Emotional core: **Subverted expectations twice = double surprise**

### Pattern 5: The Dignity Collapse
- Dignified/serious character maintains composure
- Increasingly absurd things happen
- Final loss of composure = cathartic release
- Emotional core: **We all have to maintain composure sometimes — release is satisfying**

## Red Flags — Why Videos Fail

If your video has any of these, revise before posting:

❌ **Hook is too slow** — viewer knows what the video is about before 3s
❌ **No tension** — things happen but nothing is at stake
❌ **Predictable twist** — viewer sees it coming → no dopamine hit
❌ **CTA is generic** — "comment below" drives no specific response
❌ **Character has no personality** — just an animal doing things (not relatable)
❌ **Emotional arc is flat** — same energy throughout, no peaks/valleys
❌ **Too long for the content** — pacing feels slow, completion rate drops
❌ **Text overload** — too many words per frame, viewer skips
❌ **Inconsistent character** — looks different between frames (breaks immersion)
❌ **No shareable moment** — nothing "clip-able" or "tag-a-friend" worthy

## Platform-Specific Viral Patterns (2025-2026)

### What's Currently Dominating TikTok
1. **AI Animal Drama Soap Opera** — serialized AI-generated animal stories (Fat Orange Cat format)
   - Key: same character every video, escalating drama, no dialogue needed
   - Monetization: brand recognition → merch + affiliate

2. **Cute Failure Loops** — 7-15 second clips of cute animal/character failing instantly
   - Key: completion rate near 100%, high rewatch
   - Best for: early channel growth (algorithm loves completion rate)

3. **"POV: You are [character]"** — first-person perspective AI imagery
   - Key: identity trigger is very strong
   - Best for: niche audiences (gamers, workers, students)

4. **Before/After AI transformation** — character goes from normal to epic/ridiculous
   - Key: awe trigger + curiosity gap
   - Best for: broad audiences, high share rate
