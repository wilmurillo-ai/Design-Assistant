## Company Overview

OpenAI is the leading AI research company, creator of GPT models, ChatGPT, and the GPT API platform. Founded in 2015 as a nonprofit, transitioned to capped-profit structure in 2019.

**Key Products:**
- ChatGPT (consumer: Free/Plus/Pro/Team/Enterprise)
- GPT API (developer platform)
- GPT-5 family (GPT-5, GPT-5.3, GPT-5.3-Codex, GPT-5 Mini)
- o-series reasoning models (o3, o3-pro, o4-mini)
- DALL-E (image generation)
- Sora (video generation)
- Whisper (speech recognition)
- Azure OpenAI Service (enterprise via Microsoft partnership)

**Leadership:** Sam Altman (CEO), Greg Brockman (President)

## SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
- Dominant market position (ChatGPT: 180M+ users)
- Full model stack: GPT-5.4, o3-pro, GPT-5.3-Codex
- Massive consumer user base with Plus/Pro monetization
- Microsoft Azure partnership (enterprise distribution)
- Deep Research and Agentic AI capabilities
- GPT-5 batch pricing: $0.625/$5 (cheaper than Claude Sonnet)

### Weaknesses
- Plus tier no longer includes GPT-5 Pro (Pro tier at $200/mo required)
- o1-pro at $600/1M output tokens remains extreme premium
- Sora cancelled/IPO halted (strategic uncertainty)
- EU AI Act regulatory compliance costs

### Opportunities
- Agentic AI expansion (o3-pro for enterprise)
- Walmart partnership for agentic shopping
- Government/defense contracts (Palantir demos)
- Voice and multimodal integration

### Threats
- Anthropic gaining ground on safety narrative
- Google ecosystem integration (Workspace + Gemini)
- Open-source competition (Llama, DeepSeek price war)
- Cursor and other coding agents competing with Codex
