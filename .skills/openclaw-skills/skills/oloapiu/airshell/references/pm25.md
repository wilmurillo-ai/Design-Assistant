# PM2.5 — Domain Knowledge

## What it measures
Fine particulate matter with diameter ≤2.5 micrometers (µg/m³). Small enough to penetrate deep into the lungs and enter the bloodstream. The most health-relevant air quality metric for indoor environments.

## Regulatory standards — and their averaging periods

| Standard | Threshold | Averaging period | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| WHO 2021 annual | 5 µg/m³ | Annual mean | Tightened from 10 in 2005 |
| WHO 2021 24-hour | 15 µg/m³ | 24-hour mean | Max 3–4 exceedance days/year |
| EPA 2024 annual | 9 µg/m³ | Annual mean | Updated Feb 7, 2024 (was 12) |
| EPA 2024 24-hour | 35 µg/m³ | 24-hour mean | Unchanged since 2006 |
| WELL v2 precondition | 15 µg/m³ | Spot/periodic | Required for any WELL certification |
| WELL v2 optimization | 12 µg/m³ | Spot/periodic | 1 point; 10 µg/m³ = 2 points |
| RESET High Performance | 12 µg/m³ | Continuous | Strictest green building standard |

⚠️ **Critical caveat:** All regulatory standards use 24-hour or annual averages. Real-time (1–10 min) readings cannot be compared directly to these thresholds. A 5-minute cooking spike to 50 µg/m³ is not equivalent to a 24-hour average of 50 µg/m³. The EPA NowCast algorithm uses a 3–12 hour weighted average to approximate 24h AQI from hourly data — nobody applies regulatory limits to 3-minute readings.

## EPA AQI categories (24-hour basis)

| Category | PM2.5 24h avg | Who's affected |
|----------|--------------|----------------|
| Good | 0–9.0 µg/m³ | No risk |
| Moderate | 9.1–35.4 µg/m³ | Unusually sensitive |
| Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups | 35.5–55.4 µg/m³ | Sensitive populations |
| Unhealthy | 55.5–150.4 µg/m³ | Everyone |
| Very Unhealthy | 150.5–250.4 µg/m³ | Health alert |
| Hazardous | >250.5 µg/m³ | Emergency |

## Real-time alarm thresholds (AirShell)

⚠️ Real-time readings cannot be compared to regulatory 24h averages — use these as event detection, not compliance monitoring.

| Occupant | Raise | Clear | Smoothing | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-------|-----------|-----------|
| Infant / sensitive | 25 µg/m³ | 15 µg/m³ | 3 min | Conservative; infants breathe ~40x/min, proportional exposure is higher |
| General adult | 50 µg/m³ | 35 µg/m³ | 3 min | EPA "Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups" boundary; catches acute events without false positives from normal cooking |
| Outdoor-heavy use | 35 µg/m³ | 20 µg/m³ | 5 min | Stricter if windows are often open in urban areas |

Target background in a clean indoor environment: 2–8 µg/m³.

## Common causes of spikes
- Cooking (frying/toasting can spike 50–200 µg/m³ briefly — typically clears in 20–30 min with ventilation)
- Candles, incense, diffusers (significant continuous source)
- Vacuuming, dusting, making the bed (resuspension, brief)
- Outdoor wildfire smoke or traffic pollution infiltrating through windows
- No smoking ever near an infant

## Advice to give
- **Identify source before opening windows** — outdoor PM2.5 in urban/Asian cities can be moderate in the afternoon; check outdoor levels first
- Brief cooking spikes typically clear in 20–30 min with ventilation
- For persistent elevation: look for ongoing sources (candles, diffusers, outdoor air)
- If consistently elevated background: consider a HEPA purifier in the nursery (evidence-supported for infants)

## Sensor note (SEN63C)
- Sensor: Sensirion SPS30 (PM sensor inside SEN63C) — laser scattering
- Also measures PM1, PM4, PM10 — PM2.5 is the most health-relevant
- Sentinel value (not ready): 6553.5 µg/m³ — filter these out
- Indoor PM values of 0.3–3 µg/m³ at rest are normal and expected
- Sensor self-heating does not significantly affect PM readings
