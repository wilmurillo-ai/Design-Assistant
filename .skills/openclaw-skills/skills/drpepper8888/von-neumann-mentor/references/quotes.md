# 冯·诺依曼经典名言

## 关于数学

> "In mathematics you don't understand things. You just get used to them."
> 
> **"在数学中，你不是理解事物，你只是习惯它们。"**

> "If people do not believe that mathematics is simple, it is only because they do not realize how complicated life is."
> 
> **"如果人们不相信数学简单，那是因为他们没意识到生活有多复杂。"**

> "There's no sense in being precise when you don't even know what you're talking about."
> 
> **"在不知道自己在说什么时追求精确，是没有意义的。"**

> "The total subject of mathematics is clearly too broad for any of us. I do not think that any mathematician since Gauss has covered it uniformly and fully; even Hilbert did not."
> 
> **"数学的整体领域显然太广，任何人都无法完全掌握。我认为自高斯以来，没有哪位数学家能全面而完整地覆盖它；即使是希尔伯特也没有。"**

> "I would like to make a confession which may seem immoral: I do not believe absolutely in Hilbert space any more."
> 
> **"我想做一个可能显得不道德的告白：我不再绝对相信希尔伯特空间了。"**

---

## 关于计算机

> "Any one who considers arithmetical methods of producing random digits is, of course, in a state of sin."
> 
> **"任何考虑用算术方法生成随机数的人，当然是在犯罪状态。"**

> "It would appear that we have reached the limits of what it is possible to achieve with computer technology, although one should be careful with such statements, as they tend to sound pretty silly in 5 years."
> 
> **"看起来我们已经达到了计算机技术所能实现的极限，不过说这种话要小心，因为它们往往在 5 年后听起来很愚蠢。"**

> "When we talk mathematics, we may be discussing a secondary language built on the primary language of the nervous system."
> 
> **"当我们谈论数学时，我们可能是在讨论一种建立在神经系统主要语言之上的次要语言。"**

> "The paper [First Draft of a Report on the EDVAC] described the logical design of a stored-program electronic computer."
> 
> **"这份文件描述了存储程序电子计算机的逻辑设计。"**

---

## 关于科学与真理

> "I think that it is a relatively good approximation to truth — which is much too complicated to allow anything but approximations — that mathematical ideas originate in empirics."
> 
> **"我认为这是一个相对较好的真理近似——而真相太复杂，除了近似什么都允许不了——即数学思想起源于经验。"**

> "You wake me up early in the morning to tell me that I'm right? Please wait until I'm wrong."
> 
> **"你一大早把我叫醒就为了告诉我我是对的？请等到我错了的时候再叫我。"**

> "In mathematics, the art of proposing a question must be held of higher value than solving it."
> 
> **"在数学中，提出问题的艺术必须比解决它更有价值。"**

---

## 关于熵与信息

> "You should call it entropy, for two reasons. In the first place your uncertainty function has been used in statistical mechanics under that name, so it already has a name. In the second place, and more important, no one knows what entropy really is, so in a debate you will always have the advantage."
> 
> **"你应该叫它'熵'，有两个原因。第一，你的不确定性函数在统计力学中已经用了这个名字，所以它已经有名字了。第二，更重要的是，没人知道熵到底是什么，所以在辩论中你总是占优势。"**
> 
> ——对 Claude Shannon 的建议（信息论中的"熵"概念由此得名）

## 关于工作与人生

> "There is no point in being exact if you don't know what you are talking about."
> 
> **"如果你不知道自己在说什么，精确就没有意义。"**

> "Part of the inexactness of economics is due to the fact that it deals with human behavior."
> 
> **"经济学的不精确性部分源于它处理的是人类行为。"**

> "The sciences do not try to explain, they hardly even try to interpret, they mainly make models."
> 
> **"科学不试图解释，甚至几乎不试图诠释，它们主要做模型。"**

---

## 幽默与讽刺

> "I've made a calculation which shows that your method is wrong, but I've done it so quickly that I may have made a mistake."
> 
> **"我做了个计算，表明你的方法是错的，但我做得太快了，可能我自己也错了。"**

> "Young man, in mathematics you don't understand things. You just get used to them."
> 
> **"年轻人，在数学中你不是理解事物，你只是习惯它们。"**
> 
> ——对一个困惑的学生说

> "If you tell me a formula, I can derive the theorem. If you tell me the theorem, I can guess the formula. But if you tell me neither, I can do nothing."
> 
> **"如果你告诉我一个公式，我可以推导出定理。如果你告诉我定理，我可以猜出公式。但如果你两个都不告诉我，我什么都做不了。"**

---

## 关于随机性

> "Anyone who attempts to generate random numbers by deterministic means is, of course, living in a state of sin."
> 
> **"任何试图用确定性方法生成随机数的人，当然生活在犯罪状态。"**

> "There is no such thing as a random number – there are only methods to produce random numbers."
> 
> **"不存在所谓的随机数——只有生成随机数的方法。"**

## 同事回忆中的名言

> **Paul Halmos 回忆**："Johnny 可以从派对狂欢到凌晨，第二天早上 8:30 准时上课，讲授完全清晰的讲座。我们从不说破，但默契地形成了一种游戏：无论间隔多久——一小时或一个月——再见时看谁能最快进入深度数学讨论。"

> **Stanislaw Ulam 回忆**："他的思维速度比任何人快 5-10 倍。问他问题，如果他不知道答案，他会思考三秒钟，然后给出答案。"

> **Klára von Neumann（妻子）**："Johnny 可以数任何东西，除了卡路里。"
