# American Express Dispute Reference

> Amex is both the card network AND the issuing bank for most cardholders.
> This means disputes are handled entirely in-house — faster, simpler, and more consumer-friendly than Visa/Mastercard.
> Source: American Express Cardmember Agreement and Dispute Resolution Policy

---

## Filing deadline
**60 days** from the statement date on which the charge appeared.
Some dispute types allow up to **120 days** — Amex representatives have discretion.
If you're close to 60 days, file immediately and ask about extensions.

**Important:** Amex's deadline is shorter than Visa/Mastercard. Act faster.

---

## Why Amex disputes are different

Amex does not use a rigid reason code system the way Visa and Mastercard do. Instead:
- You describe what happened in plain language
- Amex's disputes team categorizes it internally
- Amex has a strong pro-cardholder reputation — they often rule in the cardholder's favor, especially for established cardmembers
- No "pre-arbitration" stage — Amex makes decisions in-house and they're generally final

For high-value disputes (usually $5,000+), Amex may require more documentation and escalate to their formal investigation team.

## Amex Dispute Categories (informal — what to tell them)

| Situation | What to say | Amex internal handling |
|---|---|---|
| Unauthorized charge | "I did not make this transaction" | Fraud investigation |
| Item not received | "I paid for goods/services that were never delivered" | INR dispute |
| Item not as described | "What I received was materially different from what was described" | Quality dispute |
| Duplicate charge | "This transaction was charged twice" | Processing error |
| Cancelled subscription | "I cancelled this subscription and was charged anyway" | Recurring billing dispute |
| Credit not processed | "The merchant agreed to issue a credit that never appeared" | Credit dispute |
| Services not rendered | "I paid for a service that was never provided" | Services dispute |

## Evidence requirements

Amex evidence is less rigid than Visa/Mastercard but the same underlying proof still helps:

**For all disputes:**
- Transaction details (date, amount, merchant — Amex already has this)
- Your account of what happened (clear, factual, chronological)
- Evidence of attempt to resolve with merchant (except for unauthorized charges)

**For INR:**
- Order confirmation with delivery date
- Tracking showing non-delivery (or absence of any tracking)
- Merchant communication showing they didn't resolve it

**For SNAD:**
- Original listing / description
- Photos of what you received showing the difference
- Any merchant refusal to accept a return

**For unauthorized:**
- Simple statement that you didn't make the purchase
- No merchant contact needed

**For credit not processed:**
- Merchant's written promise of refund
- Return tracking if applicable
- Statement showing no credit appeared

## How to file with Amex

**Online (fastest):**
1. Log in at americanexpress.com
2. Go to Account → Activity & Statement
3. Find the transaction → click "Dispute Charge"
4. Select the dispute reason
5. Upload supporting documents
6. Submit — you'll get a case number immediately

**By phone:**
- Call the number on the back of your card
- Ask for "Disputes" or "Billing Inquiries"
- Amex reps have more discretion than most bank reps — explain clearly and calmly

**Amex app:**
- Tap the transaction → "Report an issue" → Follow the prompts

## Amex dispute timeline

| Stage | Timeframe | What's happening |
|---|---|---|
| Provisional credit | 3–5 business days | Amex typically credits quickly |
| Investigation | 30–60 days | Amex contacts merchant, reviews evidence |
| Merchant response | Up to 20 days given to merchant | Merchant provides their side |
| Resolution | 45–90 days typical | Amex decides; provisional credit confirmed or reversed |

## Amex-specific tips

- **Amex Buyer Protection / Purchase Protection:** Separate from chargebacks — this is insurance-like coverage for eligible purchases. If your dispute is about damage or theft of a purchased item, check if Purchase Protection applies before filing a chargeback.
- **Amex Pay It Plan It:** If you used a Plan It installment plan, disputes work slightly differently — contact Amex directly for guidance.
- **Business cards vs. personal cards:** The dispute process is similar, but Amex may apply slightly different standards for business cardmembers.
- **Charge cards (no preset spending limit):** Same dispute process as credit cards.
- **Premium cards (Platinum, Centurion):** Cardmembers at these tiers often report faster and more favorable resolution. Mentioning your card tier isn't necessary but these accounts receive dedicated dispute teams.
- **The Amex advantage:** If a Visa/Mastercard dispute requires you to prove the negative (prove you didn't receive something), Amex tends to give cardmembers more benefit of the doubt. Their pro-cardholder stance is genuine — lean on it.
