# IRS Publications & Official Sources

> When citing sources or directing users to authoritative information, use this reference.
> All links point to IRS.gov — the authoritative source for US federal tax law.
> Always direct users to official sources rather than third-party summaries for anything they'll rely on for filing.

## When to Refer to State Tax Authorities

Always remind users that state tax rules can differ significantly. Key state tax authority websites:

| State | Authority | Website |
|---|---|---|
| California | Franchise Tax Board (FTB) | https://www.ftb.ca.gov |
| New York | Department of Taxation and Finance | https://www.tax.ny.gov |
| Texas | No state income tax | https://comptroller.texas.gov |
| Florida | No state income tax | https://floridarevenue.com |
| Washington | No state income tax (has capital gains tax) | https://dor.wa.gov |
| Illinois | Department of Revenue | https://tax.illinois.gov |
| All states | Federation of Tax Administrators | https://www.taxadmin.org/state-tax-agencies |
