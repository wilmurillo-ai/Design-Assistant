## Capital Gains Tax Rates (Long-Term)

### Single Filers
| Rate | Taxable Income |
|---|---|
| 0% | $0 – $47,025 |
| 15% | $47,026 – $518,900 |
| 20% | Over $518,900 |

### Married Filing Jointly
| Rate | Taxable Income |
|---|---|
| 0% | $0 – $94,050 |
| 15% | $94,051 – $583,750 |
| 20% | Over $583,750 |

**Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT):** Additional 3.8% on investment income when AGI exceeds $200,000 (single) / $250,000 (MFJ). These thresholds are NOT inflation-adjusted.

**Short-term capital gains:** Taxed at ordinary income rates (same brackets as above).

## Self-Employment
| Item | 2024 Value |
|---|---|
| SE tax rate | 15.3% (12.4% SS + 2.9% Medicare) |
| SE tax deduction | 50% of SE tax paid (above-the-line) |

| Additional Medicare Tax | 0.9% on wages/SE income over $200,000 (single) / $250,000 (MFJ) |

| QBI phase-out begins (MFJ) | $364,200 |
| Standard mileage rate (business) | 67¢ per mile |
| Standard mileage rate (medical/moving) | 21¢ per mile |

## Key Filing Deadlines (Calendar Year Taxpayers)
| Deadline | What's Due |
|---|---|
| January 15 | Q4 estimated tax payment (for prior year) |
| January 31 | W-2s and most 1099s must be issued to recipients |
| April 15 | Individual returns (Form 1040) OR extension request (Form 4868) |
| April 15 | Q1 estimated tax payment |
| April 15 | IRA contribution deadline for prior year |
| June 17 | Q2 estimated tax payment |
| September 16 | Q3 estimated tax payment |
| October 15 | Extended individual returns due |
| December 31 | Last day to make most retirement contributions, harvest tax losses, make charitable donations for current tax year |
