# Source Selection for Lodging Guides

Use the source that matches the decision.

## Hard data

Use maps, official scenic pages, or structured listings for:

- origin → hotel distance and duration
- hotel → scenic distance and duration
- tolls
- scenic latest entry / opening hours
- hotel address
- hotel phone
- hotel parking if the trip depends on self-drive
- restaurant hours if they affect departure timing

Do not let social posts determine schedule-critical numbers.

## Hotel selection

Use structured listings and review pages for:

- rating and review count
- area tag / town location
- parking / breakfast / chain-standard clues when visible
- per-capita or price band when available

Use social posts and note platforms for:

- new renovation / old facilities
- noise risk
- whether the hotel feels convenient in practice
- whether old-town walking access is truly useful

## Dining and local fit

Use structured review sites for:

- review count
- category
- per-capita spend
- recommended dishes

Use notes and local posts for:

-避坑
- queue risk
- whether a place is worth detouring for

## When hotel changes mid-plan

Immediately re-check:

- origin → new hotel
- new hotel → every scenic anchor
- new hotel → dinner / breakfast anchor
- the Day 1 walking radius

A lodging swap is not a find-and-replace task.
