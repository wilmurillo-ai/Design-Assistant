# Humanize Rules

Goal: make the review read like a real person wrote it, not like a polished assistant expanding a template.

Use these rules for both Chinese and English. Chinese output is the main target.

## Hard Bans

- Do not use em dashes: `——` or `—`.
- Do not lean on contrast templates such as:
  - `不是……而是……`
  - `并不是……而是……`
  - `与其说……不如说……`
- Do not stack transition words every paragraph:
  - `首先`
  - `其次`
  - `此外`
  - `同时`
  - `另外`
  - `总的来说`
  - `值得一提的是`
  - `需要注意的是`
  - `换句话说`
- Do not use empty abstract fillers such as:
  - `某种程度上`
  - `从某种意义上说`
  - `本质上`
  - `某种维度`
  - `某种层面`
  - `不难发现`
  - `可以发现`
- Do not build the article from repeated sentence molds like:
  - definition sentence
  - soft qualification
  - buyer segment sentence
  and then repeat that rhythm paragraph after paragraph.

## Blacklist Signals

If two or more of these appear in the same review, rewrite the passage:

- too many paired abstractions in one line, for example `解析、密度、层次、结像、定位`
- repeated `如果你是……，它适合你；如果你不是……，它不适合你`
- every paragraph having nearly the same length and same three-sentence rhythm
- generic opener lines that could fit any product
- clean but empty summary lines that do not change the buyer decision

## Preferred Style

- Vary sentence length on purpose. Mix short, medium, and long sentences.
- Let some sentences be blunt.
- Use direct spoken Chinese when it helps clarity.
- Prefer one concrete judgment over two safe qualifiers.
- Tie audio words to actual listening consequences.
- Write local transitions, not formal essay transitions.
- Keep the prose a little uneven in a good way. Human writing has friction and emphasis.

## Audio Review Guidance

- Do not pile up audiophile nouns without saying what the listener will hear.
- Do not praise balance, refinement, texture, or musicality unless you explain the audible result.
- When saying a model is `杂食`, explain what it still does badly.
- When saying a model is `自然`, explain whether that means smoother treble, more believable timbre, less edge, or something else.
- In the disagreement section, name the shared view first, then the split, then the likely reason for the split.
- Keep the disagreement section plain. It should feel like a sharp editor's note, not a second essay.

## Quick Self-Check

Before finalizing, check:

1. Remove one transition word from each paragraph that does not need it.
2. Replace one abstract phrase with a concrete hearing consequence.
3. Cut one sentence that sounds too perfect or too balanced.
4. If the piece reads like it could describe five other headphones, rewrite the weakest paragraph.
