### {Comprehensive Review — use localized header, e.g. "综合乐评" for Chinese, "Comprehensive Review" for English}

{A long-form essay of 1500–3000 words in the user's chosen language. This is the core of the review.

Structure guidance:

**Opening (1–2 paragraphs):** Set the scene. What was the world — or the genre, or the artist's life — like when this album appeared? Don't start with scores or data. Start with a moment, a context, a feeling.

**The listening experience (bulk of the essay):** Walk through the album as a listener would experience it. Discuss specific tracks, production details, lyrical moments, and emotional shifts. Don't just list track names — describe what happens in the music, how it feels, what choices the artist made and why they matter. Let the prose breathe. This section should feel immersive, like accompanying someone through the album for the first time.

Weave in critical perspectives naturally as you discuss tracks:
- "AllMusic (encyclopedic stance) singled out this track as..."
- "Pitchfork (indie/art-rock leaning) noted that..."
- When citing scores, mention them conversationally, not in tables.

**Counterargument (1 paragraph, mandatory):** Present the strongest critique of this album honestly. Steelman it. Don't dismiss it.

**Temporal trajectory (1–2 paragraphs):** How has the album's reputation changed over time? Was it an instant classic or a slow burner? Has it been re-evaluated? By whom?

**Closing (1–2 paragraphs):** Situate the album in the artist's catalog and in broader music history. Who should listen to it? What does it mean now? This replaces the old standalone "Summary" — the essay's ending IS the summary.}
