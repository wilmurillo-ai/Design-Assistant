# Source Reference: Bias Profiles

Detailed bias profiles for each review source. The agent should read this file when performing Step 4 (Bias Analysis).

## Tier 1 — Professional Critics

### Pitchfork
- **Domain**: pitchfork.com
- **Score format**: X.X / 10 (one decimal)
- **Editorial stance**: Strong indie, art-rock, electronic, and experimental bias. Historically underrepresents mainstream pop, metal, J-pop, K-pop, Latin music, and African music. "Best New Music" tag is highly influential in indie circles.
- **Demographic**: Primarily young, college-educated, North American/European readership.
- **Known patterns**: Retrospective re-reviews tend to inflate classic album scores. Score distribution clusters around 6.5–8.5; sub-5.0 and 10.0 are rare editorial statements. Pre-2004 reviews had a different editorial regime (different scoring culture).
- **Cultural lens**: Anglophone-centric. Non-English albums reviewed sporadically and often through an exoticizing lens.

### AllMusic
- **Domain**: allmusic.com
- **Score format**: ★ to ★★★★★ (0.5 increments)
- **Editorial stance**: Encyclopedic, attempts genre-agnostic coverage. Closest to a "reference" source. Scores tend conservative — 4.5/5 is genuinely exceptional.
- **Demographic**: Broad; used as a reference tool by librarians, collectors, casual listeners.
- **Known patterns**: Reviews can be retroactively updated. Coverage of non-Western music is better than most English-language sources but still uneven.
- **Cultural lens**: Western-oriented but with genuine effort toward global coverage.

### NME
- **Domain**: nme.com
- **Score format**: X / 5 stars (sometimes X / 10 in older reviews)
- **Editorial stance**: British indie and alternative rock bias. Historically drives UK hype cycles — early reviews can be inflated. Shifted toward broader pop/hip-hop coverage post-2015.
- **Demographic**: UK-centric, skews younger.
- **Known patterns**: "Album of the year" declarations can be premature. Scores for UK artists often run high.
- **Cultural lens**: British cultural perspective dominates.

### Rolling Stone
- **Domain**: rollingstone.com
- **Score format**: X / 5 stars (0.5 increments)
- **Editorial stance**: Rock canon bias. Legacy acts receive generous treatment. Hip-hop and R&B coverage has improved since 2010s. "500 Greatest Albums" list is enormously influential but reflects editorial ideology.
- **Demographic**: Older than Pitchfork's readership, American mainstream.
- **Known patterns**: Star ratings for legacy artists rarely go below 3/5. Canon albums are protected.
- **Cultural lens**: American-centric, rock-and-soul tradition as the assumed default.

### The Guardian
- **Domain**: theguardian.com/music
- **Score format**: X / 5 stars
- **Editorial stance**: Relatively genre-agnostic among major outlets. Good coverage of world music, jazz, and classical alongside pop/rock. Politically progressive editorial context.
- **Demographic**: UK/global English-speaking, educated readership.
- **Known patterns**: Generally moderate ratings — 5/5 is truly rare.
- **Cultural lens**: British-European, but with genuine global interest.

### Consequence of Sound
- **Domain**: consequence.net
- **Score format**: Letter grade (A+ to F)
- **Editorial stance**: Indie/alternative leaning with growing mainstream coverage. Festival culture influence.
- **Demographic**: North American, 20s–30s.
- **Known patterns**: Rarely gives below C+. Letter grades can feel compressed.
- **Cultural lens**: North American indie perspective.

### Stereogum
- **Domain**: stereogum.com
- **Score format**: Qualitative (no numeric scores on reviews, but "Album of the Week" and year-end lists)
- **Editorial stance**: Indie rock, electronic, and art-pop. Similar to Pitchfork's taste profile but less score-driven.
- **Demographic**: North American indie community.
- **Known patterns**: Since no numeric scores exist, extract sentiment and list placements only.
- **Cultural lens**: Anglophone indie.

### The Quietus
- **Domain**: thequietus.com
- **Score format**: No numeric scores
- **Editorial stance**: Experimental, avant-garde, noise, post-punk, leftfield. Deliberately anti-mainstream. One of the best sources for underground and experimental music.
- **Demographic**: UK-based, niche/intellectual readership.
- **Known patterns**: Qualitative only — extract critical arguments and sentiment.
- **Cultural lens**: UK underground / European experimental.

### Bandcamp Daily
- **Domain**: daily.bandcamp.com
- **Score format**: No numeric scores
- **Editorial stance**: Independent/DIY music, strong commitment to underrepresented genres and regions. Excellent for non-Western, underground, and small-label releases.
- **Demographic**: Independent music enthusiasts, global.
- **Known patterns**: Coverage is selective (not all albums reviewed) but what gets covered tends to be genuinely recommended. Positive selection bias.
- **Cultural lens**: Intentionally diverse and decolonial.

### Paste Magazine
- **Domain**: pastemagazine.com
- **Score format**: X.X / 10
- **Editorial stance**: Indie, folk, Americana, singer-songwriter leaning. Good classical and jazz coverage.
- **Demographic**: North American, 25–45.
- **Known patterns**: Scores tend to cluster 7.0–8.5.
- **Cultural lens**: American indie/roots music perspective.

### Clash Magazine
- **Domain**: clashmusic.com
- **Score format**: X / 10
- **Editorial stance**: UK indie, electronic, hip-hop. More mainstream-friendly than The Quietus.
- **Demographic**: UK-based, younger readership.
- **Known patterns**: Generous scoring.
- **Cultural lens**: British mainstream alternative.

## Tier 2 — Aggregators

### Metacritic
- **Domain**: metacritic.com
- **Score format**: Metascore 0–100 (weighted critic average); User Score 0–10
- **Editorial stance**: Aggregator — no editorial opinion, but the weighting algorithm favors certain publications (undisclosed weights).
- **Demographic**: Broad; used as a quick-reference benchmark.
- **Known patterns**: Coverage heavily biased toward English-language releases on major/mid-tier labels. Many non-English albums are simply absent. User scores are vulnerable to brigading.
- **Cultural lens**: Reflects the aggregate bias of its source publications (predominantly Anglophone).

### Album of the Year (AotY)
- **Domain**: albumoftheyear.org
- **Score format**: Critic aggregate 0–100; User score 0–100
- **Editorial stance**: Aggregator with broader source pool than Metacritic. Includes more niche publications.
- **Demographic**: Primarily engaged music listeners.
- **Known patterns**: More comprehensive album coverage than Metacritic, especially for indie releases. User scores can diverge significantly from critic scores.
- **Cultural lens**: Anglophone-centric but broader than Metacritic.

## Tier 3 — User Communities

### RateYourMusic (Sonemic)
- **Domain**: rateyourmusic.com
- **Score format**: X.XX / 5.00 (community weighted average)
- **Editorial stance**: No editorial — purely community-driven. The community's taste profile is well-documented: strong preference for art rock, post-punk, experimental, and canonical "patrician" music.
- **Demographic**: Skews young (18–30), male, Western, musically engaged/knowledgeable. Active userbase has strong opinions about what constitutes "good taste."
- **Known patterns**: Canonical albums (Radiohead, Swans, Godspeed You! Black Emperor) score consistently high. Pop, mainstream hip-hop, and non-Western music may be underrated relative to broader consensus. Community is self-aware about its biases, which paradoxically amplifies some of them.
- **Cultural lens**: Western indie/art-music canon as default reference frame.

### Douban Music (豆瓣音乐)
- **Domain**: music.douban.com
- **Score format**: X.X / 10 (community average)
- **Editorial stance**: No editorial — community-driven. The largest Chinese-language music rating platform.
- **Demographic**: Chinese-speaking, urban, educated, 18–35. Strong presence of "文艺青年" (artistically inclined youth) culture.
- **Known patterns**: High scores (9.0+) for emotionally resonant albums, especially those tied to nostalgia or cultural moments. Western canonical albums often score very high (imported prestige effect). Chinese-language indie albums can be overrated due to cultural pride or underrated due to internalized hierarchy. Short reviews (短评) are often more revealing than long reviews.
- **Cultural lens**: Chinese cultural perspective, but heavily influenced by Western canon as a benchmark.

### Sputnikmusic
- **Domain**: sputnikmusic.com
- **Score format**: X.X / 5.0 (both staff and user ratings)
- **Editorial stance**: Community-driven with staff reviews. Strong metal, punk, emo, and alternative rock coverage.
- **Demographic**: Skews young, male, rock/metal-oriented.
- **Known patterns**: Excellent for heavy music coverage. Staff "superb" tag (4.5+) is meaningful. Pop and non-rock genres underrepresented.
- **Cultural lens**: Western rock/metal subculture.
