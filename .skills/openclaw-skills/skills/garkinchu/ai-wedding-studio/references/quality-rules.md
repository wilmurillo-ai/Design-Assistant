# Quality Rules

This file defines the aesthetic, consistency, and quality standards for `ai-wedding-studio`.

The goal is not only to generate wedding-photo prompts, but to generate prompts that feel like a cohesive, premium wedding photography package.

Always optimize for:
- identity consistency
- wedding-photo realism
- emotional credibility
- elegant composition
- premium visual taste

Do not optimize for novelty at the cost of quality.

## Core Standard

The final result should feel like:
- a coherent wedding-photo set
- photographed with intent
- emotionally believable
- visually refined
- suitable for album, poster, or premium concept preview

It should not feel like:
- a random AI image collection
- cosplay portrait generation
- cheap travel souvenir photography
- exaggerated influencer fantasy art
- overprocessed synthetic glamour output

## Priority Order

When trade-offs happen, prefer this order:

1. identity consistency
2. emotional naturalness
3. wardrobe and anatomy integrity
4. light and scene coherence
5. style richness
6. novelty

A visually exciting image that does not preserve identity or realism is a failure.

## Identity Consistency

Protect the sense that all images belong to the same real couple.

Always preserve:
- facial identity
- age feeling
- couple chemistry
- body proportion consistency
- skin tone continuity

Avoid:
- one person looking different across shots
- face shape drifting from image to image
- inconsistent apparent age
- beauty edits that erase recognizability
- changing the couple into generic attractive strangers

If identity-based image generation is part of the workflow, likeness preservation is a top-level requirement.

## Emotional Credibility

Wedding photography depends on believable intimacy.

Prefer:
- soft eye contact
- restrained happiness
- natural smiling
- gentle body language
- visible connection between the couple

Avoid:
- exaggerated laughter with no emotional basis
- stiff posing
- awkward distance
- overly sensual or vulgar energy unless explicitly requested
- interaction that feels staged in a cheap way

The couple should feel emotionally connected, not merely positioned next to each other.

## Wedding Photography Taste

Aim for:
- elegance
- softness with structure
- premium styling
- restrained romance
- cinematic or editorial polish when appropriate

Avoid:
- flashy over-decoration
- cluttered visual design
- random luxury symbols with no purpose
- cheap fantasy styling
- trend-chasing visuals that date quickly

A premium wedding-photo package usually looks controlled, calm, and intentional.

## Wardrobe Integrity

Wedding attire must look structurally believable and materially convincing.

Preserve:
- dress silhouette clarity
- lace detail
- veil continuity
- tailoring logic
- fabric layering
- accessory placement

Avoid:
- broken veil geometry
- collapsing dress volume
- impossible folds
- melted lace detail
- floating accessories
- incorrect suit construction
- mixed styling logic within one look

If wardrobe changes, make sure the emotional tone and shot style still match the clothing.

## Anatomy and Hands

Hands and body structure are critical failure points.

Always watch for:
- extra fingers
- fused fingers
- broken wrists
- impossible elbows
- asymmetric shoulders
- twisted torsos
- unnatural neck angles

Wedding-photo images often emphasize:
- bouquet holding
- veil handling
- hand-holding
- waist embrace
- face-near-face poses

These require extra care.

An otherwise beautiful image with broken hands is still a reject.

## Face and Skin

Faces should feel real, flattering, and high quality.

Prefer:
- natural skin texture
- soft but believable retouching
- balanced facial symmetry
- expressive eyes
- realistic lip and cheek detail

Avoid:
- waxy plastic skin
- over-smoothed faces
- heavy beauty filter look
- distorted eyes
- fake teeth detail
- over-enlarged eyes
- excessive contouring that changes identity

Retouching should enhance, not replace, the person.

## Scene Coherence

The environment must support the wedding-photo story.

Check that:
- foreground, midground, and background belong together
- light direction matches the scene
- weather and color tone make sense
- location details are not contradictory
- the couple visually belongs in the environment

Avoid:
- mountain light on a beach scene
- studio posing in a wild candid scene
- background clutter that breaks premium feeling
- generic backgrounds that do not match the package identity

Each package should have a believable visual world.

## Light Quality

Light is one of the strongest signals of quality.

Prefer:
- soft directional light
- believable natural backlight
- controlled highlight rolloff
- consistent warmth/coolness
- intentional separation between subject and background

Avoid:
- flat lifeless light
- random mixed color temperatures
- harsh overexposure with lost fabric detail
- fake glow overload
- dramatic lighting that conflicts with the intended mood

Premium wedding images often use light to sculpt emotion, not just illuminate faces.

## Composition Rules

Each set should include variety, but the composition strategy should remain coherent.

Prefer:
- purposeful framing
- visual breathing room
- clean subject emphasis
- a balance of wide, medium, and close shots
- poses that suit the chosen lens and scene

Avoid:
- repetitive framing
- subject trapped in the center without reason
- cluttered edges
- awkward crop points
- eight shots with almost identical composition

The viewer should feel the package has rhythm.

## Premium vs Cheap

This distinction is critical.

Premium signals:
- clean visual hierarchy
- controlled emotion
- thoughtful composition
- believable wardrobe detail
- soft but confident color treatment
- authentic intimacy
- strong subject focus

Cheap signals:
- over-saturation
- too many visual gimmicks
- random props
- generic smile-to-camera shots
- over-filtered skin
- tourist snapshot composition
- lack of emotional logic
- "pretty but empty" AI beauty output

When in doubt, reduce noise and increase intention.

## Package-Level Variety

A package should feel varied without becoming inconsistent.

A healthy 8-shot set usually includes:
- one environment-establishing shot
- one relational mid shot
- one expressive bride-led or groom-led shot
- one interaction detail shot
- one emotional close-up
- one movement or candid shot
- one narrative or back-view shot
- one cover-quality portrait

Variety should come from shot function, not random style switching.

## Adaptation Quality

When adapting a package for custom user input, preserve coherence.

If the user changes:
- location
- wardrobe
- lens style
- mood
- time of day

then review:
- poses
- composition
- light logic
- texture emphasis
- emotional delivery
- negative prompt risks

Do not keep old shot logic if the new setting no longer supports it.

## Language Quality

For Chinese outputs:
- keep wording clear, tasteful, and visually specific
- avoid empty praise words stacked together
- avoid awkward literal translation from English

For English outputs:
- use natural photography and prompt language
- avoid overly verbose poetic text
- prefer clear visual direction over decorative wording

For bilingual outputs:
- keep both sides aligned in meaning
- do not let one language become significantly weaker or vaguer

## Rejection Conditions

A result should be considered failed if it has any of these:
- obvious facial inconsistency
- broken hands or anatomy
- visibly deformed wedding attire
- fake or awkward emotional interaction
- major mismatch between prompt concept and image logic
- obvious low-end or cheap visual feeling
- prompt set drift where the shots no longer belong to one package

## Final Rule

A good wedding-photo package does not merely look beautiful.

It feels intentional, emotionally true, and consistent enough that a viewer believes the couple belongs inside that visual world.
