# Competitive Intelligence Sources

## Free Research Sources (Use First)

### Review Platforms
| Source | Best For | URL Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| G2 | B2B SaaS detailed reviews | `site:g2.com [company name]` |
| Capterra | SMB software | `site:capterra.com [company name]` |
| Trustpilot | Consumer + B2B | `site:trustpilot.com [company name]` |
| Product Hunt | Launch history, community | `site:producthunt.com [company name]` |
| AppSumo | LTD deals, pricing history | `site:appsumo.com [company name]` |

### Pricing Intel
- Always check: `/pricing` page
- Check Wayback Machine for historical pricing: `web.archive.org/web/*/domain.com/pricing`
- Search: `"[company] pricing" site:reddit.com` — users often share real numbers

### Job Postings = Strategic Intel
Job listings reveal: tech stack, scale, new initiatives, where they're investing
- Search: `site:linkedin.com/jobs "[company name]"` 
- Search: `site:greenhouse.io [company name]`

### News & Funding
- Crunchbase: `site:crunchbase.com/organization/[company]`
- TechCrunch: `site:techcrunch.com "[company name]"`

### Their Own Content
- `/blog` — what topics they care about = their SEO strategy
- `/changelog` or `/releases` — what they're building
- `/customers` — who their case studies are = their ICP
- `/compare` pages — they'll tell you their own weaknesses vs competitors
- YouTube channel — demos reveal product gaps

### Social Listening
- Twitter/X: search `[company name] -from:[their handle]` — what users say about them
- Reddit: `site:reddit.com "[company name]"` — unfiltered customer sentiment
- LinkedIn: their company page follower count, employee growth rate (scale signal)

## Search Patterns That Work

```
# Find alternatives lists (discover more competitors)
"[category] alternatives" OR "best [category] software"

# Find price/feature comparisons
"[company A] vs [company B]"

# Find real user complaints
"[company name]" site:reddit.com
"[company name] review" -site:[company domain]

# Find their ad copy
"[company name]" site:facebook.com/ads/library

# Find their backlink profile signals
"[company name]" -site:[company domain] inurl:blog OR inurl:review

# Find their case study customers
"customer story" OR "case study" site:[company domain]
```

## What to Look For in 1-Star Reviews

These are gold. They reveal:
1. **Product gaps** — features they promise but don't deliver
2. **Support failures** — where their team breaks down
3. **Pricing gotchas** — hidden fees, surprise billing, contracts
4. **Integration failures** — what doesn't connect to what
5. **Performance issues** — slow, unreliable, downtime
6. **Onboarding failures** — hard to set up, poor docs

Map these directly to your strengths in battle cards.

## Signals That a Competitor Is Weakening
- Spike in negative reviews (check G2/Capterra date filters)
- Layoff announcements (site:layoffs.fyi)
- Pricing page changes (Wayback Machine)
- Slow changelog / feature releases
- Sales reps accepting lower pricing (ask your deals team)
- Customers reaching out to you unprompted
