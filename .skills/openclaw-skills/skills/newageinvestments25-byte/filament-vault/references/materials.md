# Filament Materials Guide

Quick reference for common 3D printing filament types.

---

## PLA (Polylactic Acid)
- **Difficulty:** Beginner-friendly
- **Print Temp:** 190–220°C
- **Bed Temp:** 0–60°C (no heated bed required)
- **Strengths:** Easy to print, low warp, wide color selection, biodegradable
- **Weaknesses:** Low heat resistance (~60°C), brittle, not UV-stable outdoors
- **Best for:** Prototypes, decorative prints, general use

---

## PETG (Polyethylene Terephthalate Glycol)
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Print Temp:** 230–250°C
- **Bed Temp:** 70–85°C
- **Strengths:** Tough, slightly flexible, decent heat resistance (~80°C), good layer adhesion
- **Weaknesses:** Strings easily, glossy finish can hide layer lines, hygroscopic
- **Best for:** Functional parts, containers, mild outdoor use

---

## ABS (Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene)
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate–Advanced
- **Print Temp:** 220–250°C
- **Bed Temp:** 100–110°C (enclosure recommended)
- **Strengths:** Good heat resistance (~100°C), tough, machinable, acetone-smoothable
- **Weaknesses:** Warps badly without enclosure, fumes (ventilate!), shrinkage
- **Best for:** Mechanical parts, heat-exposed components, post-processing

---

## TPU (Thermoplastic Polyurethane)
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Print Temp:** 220–240°C
- **Bed Temp:** 30–60°C
- **Strengths:** Flexible, rubber-like, impact-resistant, wear-resistant
- **Weaknesses:** Slow print speeds needed, stringing, difficult with Bowden setups
- **Best for:** Phone cases, gaskets, wheels, wearables, vibration dampeners

---

## Nylon (Polyamide)
- **Difficulty:** Advanced
- **Print Temp:** 240–270°C
- **Bed Temp:** 70–90°C (PEI or Garolite bed)
- **Strengths:** Very strong, fatigue-resistant, somewhat flexible, chemical-resistant
- **Weaknesses:** Extremely hygroscopic (must dry before printing), warps, needs dry storage
- **Best for:** Gears, hinges, functional mechanical parts, living hinges

---

## ASA (Acrylonitrile Styrene Acrylate)
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate–Advanced
- **Print Temp:** 230–260°C
- **Bed Temp:** 90–110°C (enclosure helpful)
- **Strengths:** UV-stable, weather-resistant, good heat resistance (~100°C)
- **Weaknesses:** Similar warp tendency to ABS, fumes
- **Best for:** Outdoor parts, garden/vehicle components, long-term exterior use

---

## Storage Tips

- **Keep sealed:** Most filaments absorb moisture from air, especially Nylon, PETG, TPU.
- **Dry filament:** Use a food dehydrator or filament dryer at appropriate temps before printing.
- **Vacuum bags + desiccant:** Best long-term storage method.
- **PLA** is the least hygroscopic but benefits from dry storage in humid climates.

## Low-Stock Threshold

Default alert threshold: **100g remaining**. This is roughly enough for a few small prints. Consider reordering below 200g if you print frequently with a material.
