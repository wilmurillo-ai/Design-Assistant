# PDF Checkpoint Example

## Stage Range
- Book: The Art of Doing Science and Engineering
- Segments: 1-5
- Pages: 1-60

## Stage Theme
This stage is mainly about how Hamming thinks about greatness, learning, education, and the development of scientific judgment.

## Core Findings
1. Greatness is treated as a practice that can be learned rather than a mysterious innate trait.
2. Education is framed as understanding what, when, and why to do something, not just how to do it.
3. The long-term value of learning comes from building a style of thought that survives technical change.

## Reconstructed Structure
1. The early sections redefine greatness and scientific contribution.
2. The middle sections explain Hamming's teaching philosophy and the role of style.
3. The later sections connect that philosophy to long-term learning and professional development.

## Key Insights
- What lasts is not only specific knowledge, but a trained pattern of judgment.
- The distinction between education and training is critical for understanding why some learning compounds.

## Follow-Up Actions / Reading Guidance
1. Write down your own definition of great work and compare it with your current projects.
2. Break one active learning goal into `what / when / why / how` and see which layer is weakest.
3. Continue reading while tracking patterns of thought rather than isolated facts.

## Reusable Checkpoint Takeaway
Long-term achievement depends not only on what you know, but on whether you have trained a style of learning, judgment, and creation that survives change.
