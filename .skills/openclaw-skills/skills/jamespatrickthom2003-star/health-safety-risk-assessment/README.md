# UK Health & Safety Risk Assessment Generator

HSE-compliant risk assessments from a plain English description. Describe the work, get a complete assessment with hazards, controls, risk ratings, and action plans.

## What It Does

Describe a workplace or activity. Get back a legally referenced UK risk assessment ready for review and implementation:

- Hazard identification with specific, named controls
- Risk ratings using the standard likelihood x severity matrix
- Action plans with owners and deadlines
- Correct legal references (HSWA, MHSWR, COSHH, CDM, and more)
- Output formatted to the HSE 5-step method

## Quick Start

```
/health-safety-risk-assessment small cafe kitchen with 3 staff, gas hobs, deep fryer
```

Or for a specific assessment type:

```
/health-safety-risk-assessment COSHH assessment for hairdresser using hair dye and bleach
```

```
/health-safety-risk-assessment fire risk assessment for a 2-storey office, 15 staff
```

## Assessment Types

1. **General Workplace Risk Assessment** -- offices, shops, salons, gyms, any workplace
2. **COSHH Assessment** -- chemicals, dust, fumes, biological agents
3. **Fire Risk Assessment** -- premises fire safety under the RRO 2005
4. **Manual Handling Assessment** -- lifting, carrying, pushing, pulling
5. **DSE/Workstation Assessment** -- computer and screen work
6. **Lone Working Risk Assessment** -- people working alone or in isolation
7. **Working at Height Risk Assessment** -- ladders, scaffolding, roofs, platforms
8. **Construction Method Statement** -- construction, demolition, refurbishment (CDM 2015)
9. **New & Expectant Mothers Risk Assessment** -- pregnant and breastfeeding workers
10. **Young Workers Risk Assessment** -- under-18s, including work experience

## How It Works

**Quick mode (default):** Describe the activity or workplace. Get a complete assessment immediately.

**Detailed mode:** Say "detailed assessment" and the skill asks up to 5 guided questions before generating. Best for high-risk activities (construction, COSHH, working at height).

Every assessment includes:
- Hazard descriptions
- Who is at risk and how
- Existing controls
- Risk rating (likelihood x severity)
- Additional controls needed (using the hierarchy of control)
- Action owner and deadline placeholders
- Residual risk after controls
- Review date and trigger events
- Legal references to the relevant UK regulations

## Legal Framework

All assessments reference actual UK legislation:

- Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974
- Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999
- Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005
- Control of Substances Hazardous to Health Regulations 2002
- Work at Height Regulations 2005
- Construction (Design and Management) Regulations 2015
- Manual Handling Operations Regulations 1992
- Health and Safety (Display Screen Equipment) Regulations 1992
- Provision and Use of Work Equipment Regulations 1998

## Important

This generates risk assessment templates based on HSE guidance. A competent person should review all assessments before implementation. For high-risk activities, consult a qualified health and safety professional. This does not constitute legal advice.
