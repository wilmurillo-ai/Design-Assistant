## Description

**The problem:** 5.5 million UK businesses employ staff. The Employment Rights Act 2025 overhauled the rules — day-one unfair dismissal protection, new paternity rights, mandatory sexual harassment prevention, fire-and-rehire restrictions, zero-hours reforms. HR consultants charge £75-150/hour to answer questions that have fixed, calculable answers.

**What this skill does:** Give it employee details or describe an HR situation. Get back either an exact statutory calculation with full working shown, or a professional, legally-aware HR letter ready to send.

**Calculations:**
- Statutory holiday entitlement (5.6 weeks, pro-rata, 12.07% accrual for irregular hours)
- Statutory Sick Pay (£116.75/week with qualifying day and waiting day rules)
- Statutory notice periods (1-12 weeks based on service)
- Redundancy pay (age-banded, capped at £700/week, max 20 years)
- Statutory Maternity Pay (90% then £187.18/week)
- Statutory Paternity Pay (£187.18/week — now day-one right)
- Shared Parental Leave/Pay
- National Minimum/Living Wage by age band

**Letter templates generated from your situation:**
- Disciplinary hearing invitations (ACAS Code compliant)
- Written warnings (first and final)
- Dismissal letters (conduct, capability, redundancy, SOSR)
- Grievance acknowledgement and outcome letters
- Flexible working request responses (day-one right under ERA 2025)
- Redundancy consultation letters (individual and collective)
- Settlement agreement covering letters
- Factual references
- Probation review letters
- Right to work check records

**Why it's better than Google:** Every calculation uses the exact 2025/26 statutory rates. Every letter references the correct legislation and follows the ACAS Code of Practice. ERA 2025 changes are flagged wherever relevant — so you know what changed and what it means for your business.

**Who it's for:** Small business owners, office managers handling HR, startup founders, bookkeepers advising clients, anyone employing staff in the UK without a dedicated HR department.
