## Category

## Description

**The problem:** Section 21 no-fault evictions are abolished from 1 May 2026. 2.65 million UK landlords face a 37-ground possession system, six new mandatory obligations, and fines starting at £7,000 — escalating to £40,000 for repeat offences. Rent Repayment Orders now cover 24 months of rent. A single compliance failure (unprotected deposit, missing gas certificate, no tenant information sheet) can block a possession claim entirely.

**What this skill does:** Describe your situation in plain English. Get back the exact document you need — completed, legally referenced, and ready for review by your solicitor:

- Section 8 notices with correct grounds, notice periods, and particulars for all 37 grounds
- Tenancy agreements compliant with the Renters' Rights Act 2025 (periodic tenancies, Tenant Fees Act caps, all required clauses)
- Section 13 rent increase notices with market rent guidance
- Deposit prescribed information for all three approved schemes
- Right to Rent check records with List A/B document tracking and follow-up scheduling
- Gas, electrical, smoke, and CO alarm certification trackers
- EPC Band C compliance tracking with exemption guidance
- Property condition reports aligned to the Decent Homes Standard
- HMO and selective licensing checklists
- Full eviction procedure walkthroughs from notice to enforcement

**Why it's better than a template pack:** It handles the logic. Tell it your tenant owes four months' rent — it selects Ground 8 (mandatory, 3+ months' arrears), calculates the correct four-week notice period, generates the Section 8 notice with proper particulars, and runs a pre-notice compliance check to make sure your deposit is protected, your gas certificate is current, and your tenant information sheet was served. A static template cannot do that.

**Who it's for:** Private residential landlords (single or portfolio), HMO landlords, letting agents, property managers, and anyone who needs to stay compliant under the most significant change to English tenancy law in thirty years.
