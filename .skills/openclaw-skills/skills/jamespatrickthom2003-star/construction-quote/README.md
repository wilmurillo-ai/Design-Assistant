# Construction Quote & Job Costing Calculator

Itemised construction quotes from a plain English job description. Describe the work, get a complete material list, labour breakdown, additional costs, and a client-ready quote document.

## What It Does

Describe a construction job. Get back a professional quote with:

- Full material list with quantities, unit prices, and totals
- Labour breakdown by trade with day rates and durations
- Additional costs (skips, scaffolding, Building Control, planning, Party Wall)
- Summary with waste allowance, profit margin, and VAT
- Client-ready quote document with payment schedule, terms, and exclusions

## Quick Start

```
/construction-quote 4m x 3m single-storey rear extension, flat roof, bifolds across the back, mid-range spec, Midlands
```

Or for smaller jobs:

```
/construction-quote strip and re-tile a 60m2 roof, concrete tiles, includes new felt and battens, Sheffield
```

```
/construction-quote bathroom refit, remove bath and replace with walk-in shower, re-tile walls and floor, London
```

## What's in a Quote

Every quote includes four sections:

**1. Material list** -- every material with quantity, unit, unit price range, and total
**2. Labour breakdown** -- each trade with number of days and day rate range
**3. Additional costs** -- skips, scaffolding, Building Control, planning, Party Wall, structural engineer, tool hire
**4. Summary** -- subtotals, 10% waste allowance, 20% profit margin, VAT (if applicable)

Plus a **client-ready quote document** formatted for sending directly to the homeowner, with payment schedule, inclusions, exclusions, and terms.

## Who It's For

- Builders pricing jobs before site visits
- Sole traders and micro firms who quote from experience but want a structured breakdown
- Contractors preparing tender submissions
- Project managers benchmarking subcontractor quotes
- Homeowners sense-checking quotes they have received
- Quantity surveyors doing preliminary estimates

## Important Notes

- All prices are UK 2025/26 estimates and vary by region
- London prices are typically 20-40% higher than national averages
- Always conduct a site visit before issuing a final quote
- Material prices fluctuate -- get supplier quotes for accuracy
- CIS (Construction Industry Scheme) guidance included for subcontracting scenarios
