# Agent37 Listing -- Contract Review & Risk Flagger

## Skill Name
Contract Review & Risk Flagger

## Tagline
Paste a contract. Get plain-English risk flags, missing clause warnings, and negotiation talking points -- ready to use in minutes.

## Category
Legal & Compliance

## Description

**The problem:** Freelancers and SMEs sign contracts without proper review because solicitor fees (GBP 200-500 per contract) don't make sense for smaller deals. They get burned by unlimited liability clauses, full IP assignments, 90-day payment terms, and non-competes buried on page 12.

**What this skill does:** Paste a contract or describe an agreement. Get back a structured review in plain English:

- Key terms summary — what you're actually agreeing to, without the legalese
- Risk flags rated High / Medium / Low with clear explanations and suggested amendments
- Missing clause warnings for standard protections that competent solicitors always include
- Unfair terms compliance check against UCTA 1977, Consumer Rights Act 2015, and Late Payment of Commercial Debts Act 1998
- Negotiation talking points you can use word-for-word when pushing back

**14 automatic red flag checks** covering unlimited liability, IP assignment, non-competes, auto-renewal, unilateral variation, payment terms, jurisdiction, indemnification, penalty clauses, GDPR compliance, termination, assignment, exclusivity, and force majeure.

**10 missing clause warnings** covering liability caps, termination rights, data protection, dispute resolution, confidentiality, IP ownership, scope definition, change procedures, insurance, and payment terms.

**9 contract types supported** including freelance agreements, employment contracts, NDAs, SaaS licences, commercial leases, and partnership agreements.

**Why freelancers pay for this:** A single unfair clause can cost thousands. Catching an unlimited liability term or a non-compete that restricts your livelihood is worth far more than the cost of a review. It won't replace a solicitor for a six-figure deal, but for everyday contracts it stops you signing something dangerous.

## Example Prompts
- "Here's the contract my client sent — can you review it?" [paste contract text]
- "Client wants me to sign a 2-year non-compete covering all of Europe"
- "Review this NDA before I sign — is there anything I should push back on?"
- "Is it normal for a freelance contract to have unlimited liability?"
- "My landlord sent a new commercial lease — what should I watch out for?"
- "SaaS vendor agreement — are these terms fair or am I getting locked in?"
