# Contract Review & Risk Flagger

Paste a contract. Get back a plain-English breakdown of what you're signing, what's risky, what's missing, and exactly what to say when you push back.

## What It Does

Give it a contract (pasted text or a description of the agreement). Get back a structured review:

- Plain-English summary of key terms — no legalese
- Risk flags rated High / Medium / Low with explanations
- Missing clause warnings for standard protections that should be there
- Unfair terms check against UCTA 1977 and Consumer Rights Act 2015
- Negotiation talking points you can use word-for-word with the other party

## Quick Start

```
/contract-review [paste your contract text here]
```

Or describe the situation:
```
/contract-review client wants me to sign a 2-year non-compete covering all of Europe
```

## Why It Exists

Freelancers and SMEs sign contracts regularly but skip solicitor reviews because they cost GBP 200-500 per contract. They get burned by unfair terms — unlimited liability, full IP assignment, 90-day payment terms, non-competes that are unenforceable but still intimidating.

This skill catches the common traps. It won't replace a solicitor for a six-figure deal, but for everyday freelance contracts, it stops you signing something dangerous.

## What It Catches

**Red flags (always flagged):**
- Unlimited liability (should be capped)
- Full IP assignment (vs licensing)
- Non-competes (duration, scope, geography)
- Auto-renewal without notice
- Unilateral variation rights
- Payment terms beyond 60 days
- Jurisdiction outside England & Wales
- Uncapped indemnification
- Penalty clauses
- Missing GDPR terms
- One-sided termination
- Assignment without consent

**Missing clause warnings:**
- Limitation of liability
- Termination rights for both parties
- Data protection / GDPR compliance
- Dispute resolution mechanism
- Confidentiality (mutual)
- IP ownership clarity
- Scope of work definition
- Change request procedure
- Insurance requirements
- Payment terms and late payment interest

## Contract Types Supported

- Freelancer / consultant service agreements
- Employment contracts
- Commercial supply agreements
- NDA / confidentiality agreements
- Software / SaaS licence agreements
- Partnership / joint venture agreements

## Who It's For

- Freelancers reviewing client contracts before signing
- Sole traders who can't justify GBP 400 for a solicitor on a GBP 3,000 project
- SME owners dealing with supplier or partnership agreements
- Anyone who needs to understand what they're actually agreeing to

## Important

This is a risk-flagging tool, not legal advice. For high-value contracts (above GBP 10,000) or complex arrangements, consult a qualified solicitor.
