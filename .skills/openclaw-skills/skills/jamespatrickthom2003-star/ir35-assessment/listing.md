# Agent37 Listing -- IR35 Status Assessment

## Skill Name
IR35 Status Assessment

## Tagline
Structured IR35 employment status assessments using actual case law and tribunal-tested factors. Risk ratings, contract recommendations, and Status Determination Statements -- not a simplified checklist.

## Category
Tax & Contracting

## Description

**The problem:** Over 2 million UK contractors need to know whether their engagements fall inside or outside IR35. HMRC's CEST tool is widely criticised as unreliable -- it does not adequately assess mutuality of obligation, produces frequent "unable to determine" results, and its outputs are not binding on tribunals. Specialist IR35 advisers charge GBP 200-500 per assessment. Getting IR35 wrong costs contractors 20-30% of their contract value in additional tax, and costs businesses backdated PAYE, interest, and penalties.

**What this skill does:** Describe your engagement or paste your contract. Get back a proper IR35 status assessment built on the actual legal framework:

- Factor-by-factor analysis across all 10 employment status indicators (the three key tests from Ready Mixed Concrete [1968] plus seven secondary factors)
- Risk rating (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW) with confidence score and plain-English summary
- Case law citations -- Ready Mixed Concrete, Autoclenz, Pimlico Plumbers, Atholl House, Hall v Lorimer, and more -- cited where relevant to your specific facts
- Contract amendment recommendations with specific clause changes
- Working practice changes that shift the balance toward outside IR35
- Status Determination Statements for medium/large businesses under the off-payroll rules (Chapter 10, ITEPA 2003)
- CEST comparison explaining where and why this assessment differs from HMRC's tool
- Dispute response support for contractors challenging inside-IR35 determinations

**Why it's better than CEST:** It applies the full legal framework that tribunals actually use. It assesses mutuality of obligation properly. It scrutinises substitution clauses the way Pimlico Plumbers requires. It paints the whole picture the way Hall v Lorimer demands. And it gives you actionable contract and working practice recommendations -- not just a status label.

**Why it's better than a spreadsheet checklist:** IR35 status is a question of fact and degree. No single factor is determinative. The weight of each factor depends on the specific engagement. This skill applies the nuanced, case-law-driven analysis that specialist advisers use -- not a points-based scoring system.

**Who it's for:** UK contractors and PSCs, recruitment agencies, end clients (medium/large businesses), accountants, and tax advisers.
