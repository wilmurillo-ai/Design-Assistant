# IR35 Status Assessment

Structured IR35 employment status assessments for UK contractors, using the actual case law framework that tribunals apply. Not a simplified checklist — a proper factor analysis with risk ratings, contract recommendations, and Status Determination Statements.

## What It Does

Describe your engagement — or paste your contract. Get back a full IR35 status assessment:

- **Factor-by-factor analysis** across all 10 employment status indicators
- **Risk rating** (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW) with confidence score
- **Case law references** — the actual cases tribunals rely on, cited where relevant
- **Contract amendment recommendations** — specific clause changes to strengthen your position
- **Working practice recommendations** — practical changes that shift the balance
- **Status Determination Statements** — for medium/large businesses required to provide one under the off-payroll rules
- **CEST comparison** — where and why this assessment may differ from HMRC's tool
- **Dispute response support** — help challenging an inside-IR35 determination with evidence

## Why This Exists

Over 2 million UK contractors operate through personal service companies. Since April 2021, medium and large businesses must determine their contractors' IR35 status — and take "reasonable care" doing so. Getting it wrong is expensive on both sides:

- **Contractors caught inside IR35** lose 20-30% of their contract value to additional tax
- **Businesses that make blanket determinations** risk HMRC challenge and chain liability
- **HMRC's CEST tool** is widely criticised — it does not adequately assess mutuality of obligation, its substitution analysis is binary, and its results are not binding on tribunals

Specialist IR35 advisers charge GBP 200-500 per assessment. This skill applies the same legal framework they use — the three-part test from Ready Mixed Concrete [1968], the substance-over-form principle from Autoclenz [2011], the substitution scrutiny from Pimlico Plumbers [2018], and the full-picture approach from Hall v Lorimer [1994].

## The Legal Framework

Every assessment is built on the three key tests from **Ready Mixed Concrete v MPNI [1968]**:

1. **Mutuality of Obligation** — Is there an obligation to provide work AND accept it?
2. **Control** — Does the client control what, how, when, and where?
3. **Personal Service / Substitution** — Can you genuinely send someone else?

Plus seven secondary factors: financial risk, part and parcel, exclusivity, equipment, benefits, payment structure, and business presence.

## Key Case Law Applied

- Ready Mixed Concrete v MPNI [1968] — the three-part test
- Autoclenz v Belcher [2011] — substance over form
- Pimlico Plumbers v Smith [2018] — substitution must be genuine
- Hall v Lorimer [1994] — paint the picture from all factors
- HMRC v Atholl House [2022] — MOO per engagement
- Professional Game Match Officials v HMRC [2024] — control in detail
- Kickabout Productions v HMRC [2022] — media sector IR35
