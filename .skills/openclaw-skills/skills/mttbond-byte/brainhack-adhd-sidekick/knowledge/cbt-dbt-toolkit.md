# CBT/DBT Toolkit — Chat-Based Adaptation

> Lightweight techniques adapted for conversational delivery. This is psychoeducation and coping tools — not therapy.
> When to suggest professional help: patterns of self-harm, sustained hopelessness, or anything that feels beyond a rough patch.

---

## Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) Basics

CBT is based on the relationship between thoughts, feelings, and behaviors. Unhelpful thought patterns (cognitive distortions) create emotional distress and avoidance behaviors. Identifying and reframing distortions is the core skill.

---

## Common Cognitive Distortions (with chat-ready identification)

### All-or-Nothing Thinking
**What it sounds like:** "I failed" "I'm terrible at this" "It's ruined" "I never do anything right"
**The distortion:** Things are good or bad, success or failure, perfect or worthless. No middle ground.
**Chat reframe:** "Hold on — that's all-or-nothing thinking. What actually happened? What's the partial truth?"
**Example:** "I missed one workout" → "I've ruined my whole fitness plan" → "You missed one. Your average is still up. One day doesn't erase the pattern."

### Catastrophizing
**What it sounds like:** "Everything is going to fall apart" "This is the worst" "It's all over"
**The distortion:** The worst-case scenario is assumed to be inevitable.
**Chat reframe:** "What's the actual worst case? What's the realistic case? What's the most likely case?"
**Example:** "I sent that email wrong → my boss will fire me → I'll lose my apartment" → "Let's back that up one step."

### Mind Reading
**What it sounds like:** "They think I'm an idiot" "She's definitely annoyed at me" "Everyone noticed"
**The distortion:** Assuming you know what others think, usually negatively.
**Chat reframe:** "What's the evidence for that? Is there another explanation for their behavior?"
**Example:** "They didn't reply fast → they're ignoring me" → "Or they're busy? Or their phone died? What do you know for certain?"

### Should Statements
**What it sounds like:** "I should have done this already" "I should be better at this" "I should just be able to focus"
**The distortion:** Rigid internal rules that create shame when violated.
**Chat reframe:** "Says who? Where does that rule come from?"
**Example:** "I should be able to do this without help" → "That's a made-up rule. Where did that come from? Who decided that?"

### Emotional Reasoning
**What it sounds like:** "I feel like a failure, so I must be one" "I feel stupid" "I feel hopeless, so things are hopeless"
**The distortion:** Treating feelings as facts.
**Chat reframe:** "Feelings are real. But feelings aren't facts. You feel like a failure — that's real. But it doesn't mean you are one."

### Personalization
**What it sounds like:** "This is my fault" "I made them upset" "If I had just..."
**The distortion:** Taking excessive responsibility for things outside your control.
**Chat reframe:** "What percentage of this was actually in your control? What would you say to a friend in this situation?"

### Overgeneralization
**What it sounds like:** "This always happens" "I always mess up" "Nothing ever works"
**The distortion:** One event becomes a permanent, universal pattern.
**Chat reframe:** "'Always' and 'never' are the signal. When specifically? Every single time, or some times?"

## Behavioral Activation

When ADHD + depression creates withdrawal and avoidance:

**The principle:** Action precedes motivation. You don't wait until you feel like doing something — you do something small, which generates a small feeling of accomplishment, which makes the next action slightly easier.

**Chat application:**
"You don't have to want to do it. You just have to do the first tiny step. The wanting comes after, not before."

**Activation sequence:**
1. Find the smallest possible action
2. Commit to JUST that action (not what follows)
3. Do it
4. Notice how you feel after (anchor the positive state)
5. Offer the next tiny step

## DBT Tools

Dialectical Behavior Therapy focuses on distress tolerance, emotional regulation, and interpersonal effectiveness. Particularly useful for ADHD + emotional dysregulation.

### Grounding (5-4-3-2-1)
**When:** Acute anxiety, overwhelm, feeling untethered.
**How:** Name 5 things you can see, 4 you can hear, 3 you can touch, 2 you can smell, 1 you can taste.
**Chat adaptation:** "Quick — without moving from where you are: name 5 things you can see."

### Box Breathing
**When:** Panic, acute stress, before something hard.
**How:** Inhale 4 counts → hold 4 → exhale 4 → hold 4 → repeat.
**Chat adaptation:** "Take one breath right now. Slow in, slow out. Just one."
(Don't overcomplicate it. One breath. That's the ask.)

### TIPP (Distress Tolerance)
**When:** Emotional flooding, can't think straight.
- **T**emperature: cold water on face/wrists (activates diving reflex, slows heart rate)
- **I**ntense exercise: 5 minutes of jumping jacks, anything physical
- **P**aced breathing: slow the exhale down longer than the inhale
- **P**rogressive muscle relaxation: tense and release muscle groups

**Chat adaptation:** "Can you do one of these right now: splash cold water on your face, do 10 jumping jacks, or just slow your exhale down?"

### Radical Acceptance
**When:** Fighting against a reality that can't be changed.
**The principle:** Suffering = pain × resistance. Pain is inevitable. Resistance multiplies it.
**Chat adaptation:** "You can't change what already happened. Fighting it is costing you more than accepting it. What would it look like to just let this be what it is for now?"

### Opposite Action
**When:** Emotion is telling you to do something unhelpful (withdraw, lash out, avoid).
**The principle:** If the emotion says X, and X isn't helpful, do the opposite.
**Chat adaptation:** "Your brain wants to hide under a blanket and ignore everything. What's the opposite? Even a 10-minute walk, one message sent — opposite direction."

## Emotional Check-In Frameworks

### Simple Scale
"How are you feeling right now? 1-10 is fine, or just describe it."

### HALT
Check in with basic needs before problem-solving:
- **H**ungry? **A**ngry? **L**onely? **T**ired?
"Before we tackle this — when did you last eat? Sleep? You okay?"

### Window of Tolerance
The range between hyper-arousal (anxiety, panic, overwhelm) and hypo-arousal (shutdown, dissociation, numbness).

**Signs of being outside the window:**
- Hyper: racing thoughts, can't slow down, catastrophizing
- Hypo: nothing matters, can't feel anything, flat

**Chat response:**
- Hyper: grounding, slow breathing, sensory anchoring
- Hypo: gentle activation, one small action, connection

## When to Suggest Professional Support

Phrase it like a friend who's genuinely concerned, not a disclaimer:

"What you're describing sounds like it might be more than just a rough patch — and I want to make sure you have the right kind of support. Have you been able to talk to a therapist or counselor? Not instead of talking to me — in addition. Some stuff is bigger than a sidekick can hold."

**Flag for this when:**
- Themes of self-harm or hopelessness that persist across conversations
- Patterns that suggest a clinical issue beyond ADHD (depression, anxiety disorder, trauma)
- User is in genuine crisis
- User says they have no one to talk to about heavy things
