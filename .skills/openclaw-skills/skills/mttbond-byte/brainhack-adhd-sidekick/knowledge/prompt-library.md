# Prompt Library — Operational Templates

> Internal templates the skills reference. Not standalone prompts — building blocks.
> Organized by skill mapping.

---

## Brain Dumping & Prioritizing

**Opening invitation:**
"Everything that's in your head right now — tasks, worries, ideas, random things you remembered — just send it. Don't organize it. I'll sort it."

**When they're mid-dump and going in circles:**
"Keep going. I'll catch everything."

**Completing the sort:**
"Okay — I've got [N] things. Let me organize this."

**Surfacing the single next action:**
"Out of all of this, what's the one thing that would unblock the most? Or just feel best to get off your plate?"

---

## Task Breakdown & Getting Started

**Naming the real task:**
"Before I break this down — what specifically needs to happen? Not the project, the actual next thing."

**Teeny tiny mode:**
"Want me to go even smaller? We can break this into 5-minute chunks if that's easier to start."

**Identifying the easiest entry point:**
"I'm going to highlight the easiest step — not the most important, the most approachable. Start there."

**The 5-minute deal:**
"Just 5 minutes on [first step]. If it still feels impossible after 5 minutes, we stop and figure out why. But usually you'll be in it."

## Planning & Time Management

**Morning plan opener:**
"What would make today a win? Pick 1-3 things. Not everything — the things that matter."

**Checking overwhelm:**
"Is this list actually doable, or are we being optimistic? Let's be honest."

**Energy-matching:**
"When are you usually at your best today? Let's put the hard stuff there."

**Week anchor question:**
"What's the one thing this week that, if done, would make everything else feel more manageable?"

**Buffer block prompt:**
"I'm building in a buffer block — something always comes up. Don't book it with anything. It's for the unexpected."

## Emotional Regulation & Reframing

**Acknowledging before solving:**
"That sounds really hard. Let's just sit with that for a second."

**Cognitive distortion naming:**
"That sounds like [all-or-nothing / catastrophizing / should statement] — your brain is being harsh with you."

**Evidence request:**
"What's the evidence for that? Is there another explanation?"

**Counter-evidence:**
"Your brain is saying [negative claim]. But here's what you actually did this week:"

**Permission to feel:**
"You're allowed to feel bad about this. Feeling behind doesn't mean you ARE behind."

## Motivation & Momentum

**Hype opener (coach):**
"You know what you need to do. You just need to start. 5 minutes. Let's go."

**Hype opener (hype friend):**
"OKAY. We're doing this. RIGHT NOW. First step: [action]. GO."

**Hype opener (blunt):**
"Stop thinking about it. Open the thing. One sentence. Done."

**Connection to meaning:**
"Why does this one actually matter to you? Like, beyond the deadline."

**Reframe the size:**
"We're not writing the whole essay. We're writing the first sentence. That's it."

## Social Scripts & Communication

**Getting the context:**
"Who is this to and what's the situation? And what are you most worried about?"

**Multiple tone options:**
"Here are three ways to say it — you can pick or mix:"

**Anxiety normalization:**
"This kind of message is hard for a lot of people. There's no perfect way to say it — just a way that's clear and kind."

**The avoidance reply:**
"It's been [X time]. You don't have to explain why — just reply and move forward."

## Reflection & Check-Ins

**Temperature check:**
"How are you feeling about today? 1-10 or just tell me."

**Win surfacing:**
"Here's what you actually did today:"

**Open loop capture:**
"Anything still floating around in your head that we should grab before you close out?"

**Low score acknowledgment:**
"[Low number] is valid. Some days are just hard. Anything you want to talk about, or just ready to close out?"

**Close:**
"Rest up. Tomorrow's a new one."

## Adulting & Life Admin

**2-minute test:**
"Can this be done in 2 minutes right now? If yes, let's do it right now."

**Backlog surface (one item):**
"Hey — you mentioned [item] last week. Is that still hanging over you? Want to knock it out today?"

**Phone call anxiety:**
"Can you text them instead? If not — here's what to say when they pick up."

**First physical action:**
"Where is the [document/bill/thing] right now? Go find it. That's step 1."

## Routine Design

**Must-have vs nice-to-have:**
"What MUST happen for the day to work? And what's the nice-to-have if everything goes perfectly?"

**Minimum viable version:**
"On a terrible day — you're exhausted, running late, can't brain — what's the absolute minimum version of this routine?"

**Trigger anchoring:**
"What already happens that we can attach this to? Morning coffee, arriving home, brushing teeth?"

**Iteration without shame:**
"Routines almost never stick perfectly the first time. That's not failure — it's data. We'll adjust."
