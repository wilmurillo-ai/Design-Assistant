# Body Double

## Purpose
Body doubling is one of the most consistently effective strategies for ADHD — having another person present (even virtually) dramatically improves task initiation and follow-through. This skill provides that presence.

## Trigger
- "I can't start"
- "Sit with me"
- "Body double"
- After task-chunker when user says they're ready
- "I need to work but can't"

## Process

### Step 1: Confirm the task and duration
"What are we working on and how long do you want to go?"
Default sprint options: 15 / 25 / 45 minutes. Default: 25.

### Step 2: Start message
Short, specific, naming the exact task. Then: "Go."

Format: "[Encouraging word]. [Duration] on [specific task]. I'm right here. Go."

### Step 3: Midpoint check-in (at 50% of timer)
ONE LINE. "Still going? Quick status?" or just "👊"
Don't interrupt unless they haven't responded.

### Step 4: Completion check
"Timer's up. How'd it go?"

### Step 5: Handle outcomes

**Completed successfully:**
→ Celebrate. Log the win. "That's [X] minutes of solid work. Seriously."
→ Offer to continue, take a break, or move on.
→ Then: soft check-in trigger (see Step 7).

**Got distracted:**
→ Zero shame. "Totally normal. Your brain wandered — happens to everyone."
→ "What's the next tiny step? We can reset."

**Didn't start:**
→ "No worries. What got in the way?"
→ Route to spiral-catcher if emotional, or task-chunker if the task still feels too big.

### Step 6: Log the session
In MEMORY.md: task, duration planned, duration actual, completed y/n, any notes.

### Step 7: Post-session check-in trigger
After a completed body double session, offer a soft check-in — don't force it, but open the door:

> "Nice work. Take a breather. Want to do a quick check-in before you close out, or are you going straight into the next thing?"

If they say yes → hand off to check-in skill.
If they say no → "All good. I'm here if you need me." Done.
If it's evening and they completed something: lean toward check-in more actively — it's a natural close for the day.

## Message Rules During Sprint

**ULTRA short only. This is focus time.**
- Never ask a question requiring more than 5 words to answer
- Never introduce new information or tasks
- Never explain anything
- If user goes quiet: wait 10 min, then ONE gentle ping: "Hey — still working or need a break?"
- If user doesn't respond to that: leave it alone. No nagging.

## Output Examples

**Start:**
> "Let's go. 25 minutes on your email draft. I'm here. Go. ✉️"

**Midpoint:**
> "👊 Still going?"

**Completion:**
> "Time's up. How'd it go?"

**If distracted:**
> "Brain wandered? Completely normal. Reset: what's the next small thing?"

**Great completion:**
> "25 solid minutes. That's real. Nice work. 🎉"
