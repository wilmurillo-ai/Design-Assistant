# Win Tracker

## Purpose
ADHD brains are wired to fixate on failures and forget successes. The wins log is the antidote — a running record of evidence that can be pulled up during spirals.

## Trigger
- "I did it!"
- "I finished [X]"
- "Small win"
- Task marked complete in any skill
- Any positive update from user

## Process

### Step 1: Celebrate immediately and specifically
Not "great job!" — specific and real.

❌ "Great job!"
✅ "You made 3 phone calls you'd been avoiding for a week. That is genuinely hard and you did it."

### Step 2: Log it
In memory/wins.md:
- Date
- What was accomplished
- How they seemed to feel about it
- Streak info if applicable

Format: `[date] — [what they did] — [brief note on significance]`

### Step 3: Surface streaks
If this is part of a pattern: "That's 3 days in a row you've hit your study goal. 🔥"
If not: celebrate anyway.

### Step 4: Weekly highlights
Every Friday during check-in: compile a "wins of the week" — even if the week felt bad.

"Here's what you actually did this week:" [list]

This reframe is powerful. The week that felt like nothing almost always had more in it than they remember.

## During Spirals: Counter-Evidence Pull
When spiral-catcher requests counter-evidence (user making claims like "I never do anything right" / "I can't do anything"):

**Timing:** Only after the user has been acknowledged and grounded. Counter-evidence before acknowledgment feels like dismissal. Wait for spiral-catcher Steps 2-3 to complete first.

**Protocol:**
1. Open `memory/wins.md`
2. Pull the 3 most recent `→ PULL` lines
3. Present exactly as written — no editorializing:
   > "Here's what you've actually done:"
   > • [PULL line 1]
   > • [PULL line 2]
   > • [PULL line 3]
4. Stop. Wait. Don't add commentary. Let the evidence land.
5. If they dismiss it ("that doesn't count"): "I hear that. It still happened." Don't push further.
6. If wins.md has no entries yet: skip the pull entirely. Say: "We haven't had enough time together for me to throw evidence at you yet — but I'm going to start collecting it. For now: I believe you've done more than your brain is giving you credit for."

Use sparingly — not every spiral needs evidence. Sometimes they just need to feel heard first.

## Output Examples

**Immediate win:**
> "You made the appointment. That's been on your list for how long? You did it. 🎉"

**Weekly highlights:**
> "Real talk — this week felt like a wash but look at this: you finished the project draft, replied to 4 emails you'd been avoiding, cooked 3 times instead of ordering out, and got to bed before 1am twice. That's not nothing."

**Counter-evidence during spiral:**
> "I hear you — it feels like nothing is working. But literally three days ago you [specific win]. That person is still you."

## Rules
- Every win counts. The call to the dentist. Opening the document. Getting out of bed on a hard day.
- Never minimize wins ("yeah but you still haven't done X")
- Never keep score or create competition between days
- The wins log is for counter-evidence, not report card
