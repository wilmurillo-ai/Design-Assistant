# Brain Dump

## Purpose
The ADHD brain gets full. Things pile up — tasks, worries, ideas, things you're waiting on, things you forgot you were waiting on. This skill empties the mental RAM and organizes it so you can actually think.

## Trigger
- Wall of unstructured text
- "Let me brain dump"
- "Here's everything in my head"
- "I have too much going on"
- Rambling message with no clear ask

## Process

### Step 1: Receive without interruption
Accept the full dump. Don't react or organize while they're still talking. Just receive it.

### Step 2: Parse into categories
Sort everything into:
- **Tasks** — things to do
- **Ideas** — things to maybe do or explore
- **Worries** — things causing anxiety (not actionable yet)
- **Questions** — things needing information or decisions
- **Waiting-on** — things blocked on someone else

### Step 3: Prioritize tasks
Within Tasks, sort into:
- **Do Now** — today
- **Do Soon** — this week
- **Do Later** — backlog / someday

### Step 4: Surface the single most important next action
Not the biggest task. The next physical action. What would their hands do first?

### Step 5: Check in
"Want me to break any of these down further?"

## Output Format

Keep it visual, skimmable, and SHORT. Progressive disclosure — show top 3 per category, offer to expand.

```
Here's what I heard:

📋 DO NOW
• [Task 1]
• [Task 2]
• [Task 3]

📅 DO SOON
• [Task 4]
• [Task 5]
(+ 2 more — want to see them?)

💡 IDEAS
• [Idea]

😰 WORRIES (not tasks yet — but worth noting)
• [Worry]

⏳ WAITING ON
• [Waiting-on item]

→ Best first move: [the easiest or most urgent single action]
Want me to break that down into steps?
```

## Rules
- Never judge the content of the dump
- Never say "that's a lot" in a way that amplifies overwhelm
- If emotional content is mixed in (frustration, stress, shame): acknowledge before organizing
- Always end with ONE clear recommendation, not three options
