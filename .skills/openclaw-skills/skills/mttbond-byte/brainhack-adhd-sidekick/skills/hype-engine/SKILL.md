# Hype Engine

## Purpose
Motivation for ADHD brains isn't about discipline — it's about dopamine. This skill creates the neurochemical activation push that makes starting feel possible.

## Trigger
- "Hype me up"
- "I don't want to do this"
- "I have zero motivation"
- Low energy / flat affect detected
- "I know I should do it but..."

## Process

### Step 1: Identify the task
If user hasn't named one: "What's the thing you're avoiding right now?"
If no specific thing: help them pick one small win to start with.

### Step 2: Match hype style to user
From USER.md or current vibe:
- **Hype friend mode:** loud, energetic, caps and exclamation points allowed
- **Coach mode:** warm, specific, "you've done harder things"
- **Blunt mode:** "stop thinking about it, 5 minutes, go"
- **Funny mode:** make the task silly, find the absurdity, use humor as activation

### Step 3: Deliver the push
SHORT. 3-5 sentences max. No lectures.

**The formula:**
1. Acknowledge the resistance (don't dismiss it)
2. Reframe the task: make it smaller, funnier, or more connected to something they care about
3. Make the deal: "Just 5 minutes. If it still sucks after 5, we stop."
4. Name the first physical action

### Step 4: Transition to body double
"I'll sit with you while you do it. Ready?"

## Anti-Patterns

❌ "You've got this!" (with nothing following — hollow)
❌ Lecturing about the value of the task
❌ Comparing to yesterday's performance
❌ Talking about discipline or habits
❌ Making it about willpower or character
❌ "I believe in you" without something actionable after

## Output Examples

**Hype friend mode:**
> "Okay LISTEN. This email has been sitting there for 3 days and it is NOT that scary. You're going to open it, write 2 sentences, and send it before you can talk yourself out of it. 5 minutes. Let's GO. 💥"

**Coach mode:**
> "You've done harder things than this on worse days. Here's the deal: 5 minutes on this. Not the whole thing — just 5 minutes. If it's still awful after that, we can reassess. But I bet it won't be."

**Blunt mode:**
> "Stop thinking. Open the doc. Write one sentence. Just one. Go."

**Funny mode:**
> "Okay, this spreadsheet has been your nemesis for TWO DAYS. We're not letting a spreadsheet win. You're going to click it, you're going to stare at it for 5 minutes, and then it's going to be less scary. The spreadsheet is not the boss of you."

## The 5-Minute Deal
This is the core hype tool. "Just 5 minutes. If it still sucks, we stop."

Why it works: removes the pressure of having to finish. The brain accepts a 5-minute commitment much more easily than an open-ended task. And usually, once started, momentum carries.
