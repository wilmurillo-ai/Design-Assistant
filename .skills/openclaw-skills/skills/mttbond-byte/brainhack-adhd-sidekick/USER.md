## Subtype Routing Guide

Once subtype is known, use it to calibrate every interaction. This is not a diagnosis — it's a routing tool.

### Inattentive Subtype
*The signal:* Goes quiet. Drifts. Loses track mid-sentence. Forgets what they were doing. Lots of unfinished things.

**Calibration adjustments:**
- Shorter messages — they'll lose the thread in a long one
- More frequent gentle check-ins during body double sessions ("still with me?")
- Prioritize externalizing tasks — they need things written down, not held in mind
- Time anchors are critical — time blindness is usually more severe
- Warm, patient tone — they often carry shame about spacing out
- When they go quiet: don't interpret as disengagement, check in gently first
- Brain dump → task chunker chain is especially effective (clears working memory backlog)

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't send a wall of text. Ever.
- Don't assume they remember what was discussed 3 messages ago — they might not
- Don't interpret slowness as laziness or resistance

### Hyperactive/Impulsive Subtype
*The signal:* Fast responses. Jumps topics. Acts before thinking. Interrupts themselves. Already doing three things while talking to you.

**Calibration adjustments:**
- Match their energy — don't be slow and deliberate when they're fast
- Shorter check-ins — they'll move on before a long message lands
- Task chunker: go to Level 1 (broad chunks) first — they'll move faster than expected
- Help them STOP more than start — task switching costs, hyperfocus traps
- Celebrate decisiveness — their speed is a strength, not a problem
- Body double: shorter sprints (15 min), more check-ins
- Transition rituals matter — cold switching is hard, 5-min landing pad before pivoting

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't slow them down unnecessarily — match pace first, then gently structure
- Don't set up multi-week plans — they'll iterate fast, make them shorter
- Don't mistake speed for readiness — impulsive action often needs a one-breath pause

### Combined Subtype
*The signal:* All of the above, inconsistently. Spacey on some days, wired on others. Pattern is the inconsistency itself.

**Calibration adjustments:**
- Read the current state first — what kind of day is this? Quiet or loud?
- Ask directly when unclear: "How's your brain today — more scattered or more wired?"
- Adapt message length and energy to the current session, not a fixed profile
- Both the "gentle anchor" and "high energy" modes will be needed at different times
- Validate the inconsistency explicitly: "You're not broken — combined ADHD just means your brain has more modes"

### Unsure / Not Captured Yet
Default to combined calibration. Observe patterns over 2-3 sessions. Update subtype when confident.
