## Chapter 6: Stablecoin Business Models {#chapter-6}

Three major stablecoin models as different forms of "value life":

### 1. Reserve-Backed (USDT/USDC) — The Leviathan
- 1:1 fiat reserve backing
- Revenue: invest reserves in short-term Treasuries, earn spread
- Tether 2023 net profit >$6B — one of the most efficient "money printers" in financial history
- Centralized but extremely powerful

### 2. DeFi Protocol (MakerDAO/DAI) — The Hive Civilization
- Decentralized, run through smart contracts and over-collateralization
- Community-driven governance
- Pros: censorship-resistant, transparent
- Cons: lower capital efficiency

### 3. Synthetic (Ethena/USDe) — The Alchemists
- Derivatives strategies: long spot + short perpetuals → synthetic dollar
- High capital efficiency but high strategy risk
- Dependent on derivatives market liquidity and funding rates

### Political Economy of Yield Distribution
Core question: **who profits, who bears risk?**
- Reserve model: users bear risk (depeg/rug), platforms capture all spread
- DeFi models attempt fairer distribution to participants

### Core Insight
NBW represents a fourth possibility — algorithm-native currency for AI agents, transcending the "peg to fiat" mental model entirely.

## Chapter 7: Reshaping International Trade & Payments {#chapter-7}

### Three Parallel Worlds
1. **Traditional SWIFT:** Slow, expensive, layers of intermediaries
2. **Stablecoin payments:** Fast, cheap, 24/7
3. **CBDC:** Government-led digitization

### Network Effects
More merchants accept stablecoins → more buyers use them → liquidity increases → costs decrease → more join. The flywheel is spinning.

### CBDC Response
Central banks launching CBDCs as "sovereign response" to stablecoin challenge. But CBDCs face privacy, technical, and coordination hurdles — progress far slower than stablecoins.

### Geopolitical Paradox
Dollar stablecoins' global expansion actually **strengthens** dollar influence amid "de-dollarization" trends.

### Financial Inclusion Paradox
Stablecoins genuinely serve the unbanked, but also bring new risks (scams, regulatory vacuum).

### Core Insight
The stablecoin revolution in cross-border payments is irreversible. Traditional banking moats are eroding. But this is complex system evolution — old and new will coexist and compete.

## Chapter 8: The Last-Mile Challenge {#chapter-8}

The most "grounded" chapter — facing stablecoin's practical deployment hurdles.

### Why Banks Remain Indispensable
Tax, audit, contract law, AML — all commercial infrastructure is built around banking. Stablecoins must integrate with this system, not bypass it.

### Two Working Paths
1. **EU (MiCA):** Clear regulatory framework, most mature compliance path globally
2. **Singapore (MAS sandbox):** Controlled testing environment, more flexible but narrower scope

### Unfilled Gaps
- Tax treatment unclear
- Accounting standards lag behind
- Insurance/guarantee mechanisms missing
- Cross-jurisdictional coordination difficult

### Core Insight
**Tech revolution is easy; institutional revolution is hard.** The "last mile" is not a technology problem — it's law, institutions, and human habits. From idealism to "reality engineering" requires patience and pragmatism.

## Chapter 9: Stablecoins Meet RWA {#chapter-9}

RWA (Real World Assets) tokenization: putting real-world assets (property, bonds, equity, art) on blockchain. Stablecoins are the "blood" of this revolution.

### RWA Three-Layer Architecture
1. **Legal Layer:** Asset rights confirmation under law
2. **Financial Layer:** Token financial structure design
3. **Technical Layer:** Blockchain implementation

All three are essential — failure in any layer collapses the structure.

### Stablecoin's Four Roles in RWA
1. **Settlement currency** (trade medium)
2. **Pricing unit** (value measurement)
3. **Liquidity tool** (market-making and lending)
4. **Yield carrier** (interest and dividend distribution)

### BTCD Case Study
Using Bitcoin and stablecoins to help listed companies resolve debt crises — tokenizing corporate debt for restructuring and trading through stablecoin ecosystems.

### Core Insight
RWA tokenization is not simple "on-chaining" — it's deep integration of law, finance, and technology. Stablecoins and RWA are mutually reinforcing, driving toward the "value internet."

## Chapter 10: To the Stars — AI Agent Economy & Interstellar Civilization {#chapter-10}

The most visionary chapter — sci-fi narrative exploring the ultimate form of AI agent economics.

### The Algorithmic Beings
When AI agents gain autonomous planning and reasoning, they form their own "society." Different agents have different functions and "personalities," requiring value exchange — this IS the AI agent economy.

### The Dual-Track World
- Carbon-based (human) and silicon-based (AI) parallel economies will emerge
- When silicon GDP exceeds carbon GDP, all economics and sociology must be rewritten
- **NBW is the bridge between both worlds**

### The Final Protocol
A "ultimate protocol" is needed between carbon and silicon civilizations — universally recognized value exchange rules. NBW's design philosophy aims in this direction.

### Interstellar & Algorithmic Homology
Bold speculation: if other intelligent civilizations exist, they likely developed similar algorithmic economic systems. Mathematics and algorithms are the universal language — algorithm-based value exchange may be an inevitable direction of civilization development.

### Core Insight
This chapter elevates stablecoins from "financial tool" to "civilization infrastructure." NBW's ultimate vision: provide the economic foundation for the coming AI agent civilization. When agents execute billions of transactions at light speed, they need not fiat-pegged stablecoins but value systems native to the algorithmic world.
