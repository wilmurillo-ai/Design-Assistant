# Female-Specific Training Guide

> **Purpose:** Training recommendations tailored for female physiological characteristics  
> **Last Updated:** 2026-03-17  
> **Evidence Base:** ACSM, NSCA women's training guidelines

## F1: Glute Shaping

**Focus:** Comprehensive development of gluteus maximus, medius, and minimus

### Primary Glute Exercises

**Hip Thrust:**
- Setup: Upper back on bench, barbell over hips
- Execution: Drive through heels, squeeze glutes at top
- Sets/Reps: 4 sets × 10-15 reps
- Progression: Add weight when hitting 15 reps with good form

**Squat Variations:**
- **Goblet Squat:** 3 sets × 12-15 reps
- **Sumo Squat:** 3 sets × 12-15 reps (wider stance, more glute activation)
- **Bulgarian Split Squat:** 3 sets × 10-12 reps/leg

**Deadlift Variations:**
- **Romanian Deadlift:** 3 sets × 12-15 reps (hamstring + glute focus)
- **Single-Leg Deadlift:** 3 sets × 10-12 reps/leg

### Glute Medius/Minimus (Side Glutes)

**Clamshells:**
- Setup: Side-lying, knees bent 90 degrees
- Execution: Open top knee while keeping feet together
- Sets/Reps: 3 sets × 15-20 reps/side

**Lateral Band Walk:**
- Setup: Resistance band around knees or ankles
- Execution: Step sideways maintaining tension
- Sets/Reps: 3 sets × 15-20 steps/direction

**Cable Hip Abduction:**
- Setup: Cable at ankle, standing
- Execution: Lift leg out to side
- Sets/Reps: 3 sets × 15-20 reps/side

## F2: Postpartum Recovery

**Focus:** Pelvic floor rehabilitation, diastasis recti repair

**Important:** Consult healthcare provider before starting postpartum exercise (typically 6-8 weeks after vaginal delivery, 8-12 weeks after C-section)

### Phase 1: Early Postpartum (Weeks 1-6)

**Breathing Exercises:**
- Diaphragmatic breathing: 5 minutes, 2-3 times/day
- Pelvic floor awareness: Gentle Kegels (if cleared by provider)

**Gentle Movement:**
- Walking: Start with 5-10 minutes, gradually increase
- Pelvic tilts: 2 sets × 10 reps

### Phase 2: Foundation Building (Weeks 6-12)

**Pelvic Floor Rehabilitation:**

**Kegel Progression:**
1. **Identification:** Stop urine flow mid-stream (only once for identification)
2. **Basic Kegel:**
   - Contract for 5 seconds
   - Relax for 5 seconds
   - Repeat 10 times
   - 3 sets/day
3. **Quick Kegels:**
   - Rapid contractions
   - 10 quick squeezes
   - 3 sets/day

**Core Rehabilitation:**

**Transverse Abdominis Activation:**
- Drawing-in maneuver: 2 sets × 10 reps (hold 5 seconds)
- Heel slides: 2 sets × 10 reps/side
- Dead bug (modified): 2 sets × 10 reps/side

**Diastasis Recti Check:**
- Lie on back, knees bent
- Place fingers above navel
- Lift head slightly
- Feel for gap between rectus muscles
- If gap >2 finger widths, avoid crunches and planks initially

### Phase 3: Strengthening (Weeks 12+)

**Progressive Exercises:**
- Modified plank (knees): 3 sets × 20-30 seconds
- Bird-dog: 3 sets × 10 reps/side
- Glute bridge: 3 sets × 15 reps
- Squat (bodyweight): 3 sets × 15 reps

## F3: Menstrual Cycle Training

**Focus:** Adjust training intensity based on cycle phase

### Phase 1: Menstrual Phase (Days 1-5)

**Hormonal Profile:**
- Estrogen: Low
- Progesterone: Low
- Energy: Often lower

**Training Recommendations:**
- **Intensity:** Reduce by 20-30%
- **Focus:** Gentle movement, stretching, yoga
- **Duration:** 20-30 minutes
- **Activities:**
  - Walking
  - Restorative yoga
  - Light stretching
  - Swimming (if comfortable)

**Nutrition Support:**
- Increase iron-rich foods (red meat, spinach, lentils)
- Warm foods (soups, stews)
- Reduce caffeine and salt

### Phase 2: Follicular Phase (Days 6-14)

**Hormonal Profile:**
- Estrogen: Rising
- Energy: Increasing
- Insulin sensitivity: High (best for carb utilization)

**Training Recommendations:**
- **Intensity:** Peak training period
- **Focus:** Strength training, HIIT, skill work
- **Frequency:** 4-5 times/week
- **Activities:**
  - Heavy compound lifts
  - HIIT sessions
  - New skill learning
  - PR attempts

**Nutrition Support:**
- Higher carbohydrate intake
- Adequate protein (1.6-1.8g/kg)
- Focus on nutrient timing around workouts

### Phase 3: Ovulation Phase (Days 14-16)

**Hormonal Profile:**
- Estrogen: Peak
- LH surge
- Strength: Peak

**Training Recommendations:**
- **Intensity:** Maximum strength period
- **Focus:** Heavy lifts, PR attempts
- **Activities:**
  - Heavy squats, deadlifts
  - Maximum effort lifts
  - Competition preparation

**Injury Prevention:**
- ACL injury risk slightly higher during ovulation
- Ensure proper warm-up
- Focus on form over weight

### Phase 4: Luteal Phase (Days 17-28)

**Hormonal Profile:**
- Progesterone: Rising then falling
- Estrogen: Secondary peak then falling
- Body temperature: Slightly elevated
- Metabolism: Increases 100-300 kcal/day

**Early Luteal (Days 17-23):**
- **Intensity:** Moderate to high
- **Focus:** Maintain training, slight reduction OK
- **Nutrition:** Slightly higher calories OK

**Late Luteal (Days 24-28):**
- **Intensity:** Reduce by 10-20%
- **Focus:** Maintenance, recovery
- **Symptoms:** PMS may affect energy and mood
- **Activities:**
  - Moderate strength training
  - LISS cardio
  - Yoga and stretching

**Nutrition Support:**
- Increase magnesium (300-400mg) for PMS
- Complex carbohydrates for mood
- Reduce salt for bloating
- Dark chocolate for cravings (70%+ cacao)

## F4: Upper Body Shaping

**Focus:** Back, shoulders, arm definition

### Back Exercises

**Pull-ups/Chin-ups:**
- Assisted version: 3 sets × 8-10 reps
- Negative pull-ups: 3 sets × 5 reps (5-second descent)

**Rows:**
- Bent-over dumbbell row: 3 sets × 12-15 reps
- Seated cable row: 3 sets × 12-15 reps
- Single-arm row: 3 sets × 12 reps/side

**Lat Pulldown:**
- Wide grip: 3 sets × 12-15 reps
- Close grip: 3 sets × 12-15 reps

### Shoulder Exercises

**Overhead Press:**
- Dumbbell shoulder press: 3 sets × 12-15 reps
- Arnold press: 3 sets × 12-15 reps

**Lateral Raises:**
- Dumbbell lateral raise: 3 sets × 15-20 reps
- Cable lateral raise: 3 sets × 15-20 reps

**Rear Delts:**
- Face pulls: 3 sets × 15-20 reps
- Reverse flyes: 3 sets × 15-20 reps

### Arm Exercises

**Biceps:**
- Hammer curls: 3 sets × 12-15 reps
- Concentration curls: 3 sets × 12-15 reps

**Triceps:**
- Tricep pushdown: 3 sets × 15-20 reps
- Overhead tricep extension: 3 sets × 12-15 reps
- Diamond push-ups: 3 sets × 10-15 reps

**Training Note:** Women typically don't develop large arms from training due to lower testosterone. Focus on definition through moderate weight and higher reps.

## F5: Core and Posture

**Focus:** Postpartum abdominal wall repair, posture correction

### Deep Core Activation

**Transverse Abdominis:**
- Drawing-in maneuver: 3 sets × 10 reps (hold 10 seconds)
- Dead bug: 3 sets × 12 reps/side
- Bird-dog: 3 sets × 10 reps/side

### Obliques

**Anti-Rotation:**
- Pallof press: 3 sets × 12 reps/side
- Suitcase carry: 3 sets × 30 seconds/side

**Rotation:**
- Cable woodchop: 3 sets × 12 reps/side
- Russian twist: 3 sets × 20 reps (10/side)

### Posture Correction

**Upper Crossed Syndrome (Common in Women):**

**Tight Muscles (Stretch):**
- Chest stretch: 2 sets × 30 seconds
- Upper trap stretch: 2 sets × 30 seconds/side

**Weak Muscles (Strengthen):**
- Band pull-aparts: 3 sets × 15-20 reps
- Face pulls: 3 sets × 15-20 reps
- Prone Y-T-W: 3 sets × 10 reps each

**Lower Crossed Syndrome:**

**Tight Muscles (Stretch):**
- Hip flexor stretch: 2 sets × 30 seconds/side
- Lower back stretch (child's pose): 2 sets × 30 seconds

**Weak Muscles (Strengthen):**
- Glute bridge: 3 sets × 15-20 reps
- Plank: 3 sets × 30-60 seconds
- Dead bug: 3 sets × 12 reps/side

## Weekly Training Template

### Beginner Female (0-6 months)

```
Monday: Lower Body
- Goblet Squat: 3×12
- Glute Bridge: 3×15
- Romanian Deadlift: 3×12
- Clamshells: 3×15/side

Wednesday: Upper Body
- Push-ups (knees): 3×10
- Dumbbell Row: 3×12/side
- Shoulder Press: 3×12
- Bicep Curl: 3×15

Friday: Full Body + Core
- Bodyweight Squat: 3×15
- Incline Push-up: 3×12
- Lat Pulldown: 3×12
- Plank: 3×30 seconds
- Dead Bug: 3×10/side
```

```
Monday: Glutes + Hamstrings
- Hip Thrust: 4×10-12
- Romanian Deadlift: 3×10-12
- Bulgarian Split Squat: 3×10/side
- Lateral Band Walk: 3×20 steps

Tuesday: Upper Body Push
- Bench Press: 4×8-10
- Overhead Press: 3×10-12
- Incline Dumbbell Press: 3×12
- Tricep Pushdown: 3×15

Thursday: Glutes + Quads
- Squat: 4×8-10
- Leg Press: 3×12-15
- Walking Lunge: 3×12/side
- Cable Kickback: 3×15/side

Friday: Upper Body Pull
- Pull-ups (assisted): 4×8

Saturday: Core + Cardio
- Plank variations: 3×45 seconds
- Cable Woodchop: 3×12/side
- LISS Cardio: 30 minutes
```

## Recovery & Nutrition for Women

### Protein Requirements
- General: 1.6g/kg body weight
- Muscle building: 1.8-2.0g/kg
- Fat loss: 2.0-2.4g/kg

### Iron Considerations
- Menstruating women: 18mg/day (vs 8mg for men)
- Food sources: Red meat, spinach, lentils, fortified cereals
- Pair with vitamin C for better absorption

### Calcium for Bone Health
- Requirements: 1000mg/day (1200mg if 50+)
- Food sources: Dairy, leafy greens, fortified foods
- Weight-bearing exercise essential
