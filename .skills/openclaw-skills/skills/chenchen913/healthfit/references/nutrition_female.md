# Female-Specific Nutrition Guide

> **Purpose:** Nutrition recommendations tailored for female physiological characteristics  
> **Last Updated:** 2026-03-17  
> **Evidence Base:** Chinese Nutrition Society 2023, ISSN position stands

## 1. Menstrual Cycle Nutrition (4-Phase Approach)

### Phase 1: Menstrual Phase (Days 1-5)

**Hormonal Profile:**
- Estrogen: Low
- Progesterone: Low
- Energy: Often lower

**Nutrition Focus:**
- **Iron:** 20mg/day (Chinese Nutrition Society 2023)
  - Red meat: 2-3 servings/week
  - Spinach, lentils, fortified cereals
  - Pair with vitamin C for better absorption
- **Warm Foods:**
  - Soups, stews, warm grains
  - Ginger tea, warm water
- **Reduce:**
  - Cold/raw foods (may worsen cramping)
  - Caffeine (can increase anxiety)
  - Excess salt (reduces bloating)

**Sample Foods:**
- Bone broth soup
- Warm oatmeal with cinnamon
- Steamed vegetables
- Lean red meat

### Phase 2: Follicular Phase (Days 6-14)

**Hormonal Profile:**
- Estrogen: Rising
- Energy: Increasing
- Insulin sensitivity: High (best for carb utilization)

**Nutrition Focus:**
- **Higher Carbohydrates:**
  - 4-6g/kg body weight
  - Complex carbs (oats, brown rice, sweet potato)
  - Time around workouts
- **Adequate Protein:**
  - 1.6-1.8g/kg body weight
  - Distribute across meals
- **Phytoestrogens (Optional):**
  - Soy products (tofu, tempeh, edamame)
  - May support estrogen balance

**Sample Foods:**
- Quinoa bowls
- Sweet potato with chicken
- Brown rice with vegetables
- Fruit smoothies

### Phase 3: Ovulation Phase (Days 14-16)

**Hormonal Profile:**
- Estrogen: Peak
- LH surge
- Strength: Peak

**Nutrition Focus:**
- **Maintain Balanced Intake:**
  - No special adjustments needed
  - Continue follicular phase approach
- **Hydration:**
  - Extra important during peak performance
  - 2.7 liters water/day minimum
- **Antioxidants:**
  - Berries, dark leafy greens
  - Support recovery from intense training

### Phase 4: Luteal Phase (Days 17-28)

**Hormonal Profile:**
- Progesterone: Rising then falling
- Estrogen: Secondary peak then falling
- Body temperature: Slightly elevated
- Metabolism: Increases ~100-300 kcal/day

**Early Luteal (Days 17-23):**
- **Slightly Higher Calories:**
  - Add 100-200 kcal/day
  - Don't fight increased hunger
- **Complex Carbohydrates:**
  - Support mood and energy
  - Whole grains, legumes

**Late Luteal (Days 24-28 / PMS):**
- **Magnesium:**
  - 300-400mg/day
  - Reduces PMS symptoms
  - Sources: Almonds, spinach, dark chocolate
- **B Vitamins:**
  - Support mood
  - Sources: Whole grains, eggs, leafy greens
- **Reduce:**
  - Caffeine (increases anxiety)
  - Alcohol (worsens mood swings)
  - Salt (reduces bloating)

**Cravings Management:**
- Dark chocolate (70%+ cacao): Satisfies chocolate craving, provides magnesium
- Complex carbs: Stabilizes blood sugar
- Don't restrict too much: May worsen cravings

## 2. Iron Management for Women

### Iron Requirements

**RDA (Chinese Nutrition Society 2023):**
- Premenopausal women: 20mg/day
- Postmenopausal women: 12mg/day
- Pregnant women: 29mg/day

**Why Women Need More:**
- Monthly blood loss during menstruation
- Higher risk of deficiency
- Affects energy, performance, immunity

### Iron-Rich Foods

**Heme Iron (Better Absorption):**
- Beef: 2-3mg per 100g
- Lamb: 2-3mg per 100g
- Chicken liver: 9mg per 100g
- Sardines: 2mg per 100g

**Non-Heme Iron (Plant Sources):**
- Spinach: 3mg per cup (cooked)
- Lentils: 6mg per cup
- Tofu: 3mg per 100g
- Fortified cereals: 4-18mg per serving

### Enhancing Iron Absorption

**With Vitamin C:**
- Add lemon juice to spinach
- Bell peppers with lentils
- Orange juice with fortified cereal
- Strawberries with oatmeal

**Avoid With Iron:**
- Calcium supplements (compete for absorption)
- Coffee/tea (tannins reduce absorption)
- Take iron supplements away from these

### Signs of Iron Deficiency

- Fatigue, weakness
- Pale skin
- Shortness of breath
- Cold hands and feet
- Decreased performance
- Frequent infections

**If Suspected:**
- Consult healthcare provider
- Get blood test (ferritin, hemoglobin)
- Supplement if deficient (under guidance)

## 3. Bone Density Protection

### Calcium Requirements

**RDA (Chinese Nutrition Society 2023):**
- 18-50 years: 800mg/day
- 51+ years: 1000mg/day

**Why Important for Women:**
- Higher osteoporosis risk than men
- Estrogen protects bone density
- Post-menopause: Rapid bone loss

### Calcium-Rich Foods

**Dairy:**
- Milk: 300mg per cup
- Yogurt: 400mg per cup
- Cheese: 200-300mg per 30g

**Non-Dairy:**
- Tofu (calcium-set): 350mg per 100g
- Sardines (with bones): 325mg per 100g
- Bok choy: 160mg per cup (cooked)
- Almonds: 75mg per 30g
- Fortified plant milks: 300mg per cup

### Vitamin D Synergy

**Function:**
- Essential for calcium absorption
- Bone health
- Immune function

**Requirements:**
- RDA: 400 IU/day
- If deficient: 1000-2000 IU/day

**Sources:**
- Sun exposure: 15-20 minutes midday
- Fatty fish: 400-600 IU per serving
- Egg yolks: 40 IU per yolk
- Fortified foods: 100 IU per serving

### Weight-Bearing Exercise

**Essential for Bone Health:**
- Strength training: 2-3 times/week
- Impact exercises: Running, jumping
- Resistance exercises: Squats, deadlifts

**Avoid:**
- Excessive cardio without strength training
- Very low calorie intake (reduces bone density)

## 4. Preconception/Pregnancy Nutrition (Informational)

**Important:** This section is for informational purposes only. Pregnant women should consult healthcare providers for personalized nutrition advice.

### Preconception (3 Months Before)

**Folic Acid:**
- **Recommendation:** 400-600 mcg/day
- **Start:** At least 3 months before conception
- **Purpose:** Prevents neural tube defects
- **Sources:** Leafy greens, fortified grains, supplements

**Iodine:**
- **Recommendation:** 150 mcg/day (preconception)
- **Sources:** Iodized salt, seafood, dairy

### During Pregnancy

**Calorie Needs:**
- First trimester: No additional calories
- Second trimester: +340 kcal/day
- Third trimester: +450 kcal/day

**Key Nutrients:**
- **Protein:** 1.1g/kg body weight
- **Iron:** 29mg/day
- **Calcium:** 1000mg/day
- **DHA:** 200-300mg/day

**Foods to Avoid:**
- Raw or undercooked meat/fish
- Unpasteurized dairy
- High-mercury fish (shark, swordfish, king mackerel)
- Excessive caffeine (<200mg/day)
- Alcohol (avoid completely)

## 5. Common Supplement Myths for Women

### "Women-Specific" Supplements

**Reality:**
- Most "women's" formulas are marketing
- Men and women need same nutrients
- Dosage may differ, not type

**Exceptions:**
- Prenatal vitamins (for pregnancy)
- Iron (if menstruating and deficient)

### Iron Supplements

**When Needed:**
- Confirmed deficiency via blood test
- Heavy menstrual bleeding
- Vegetarian/vegan athletes

**When Not Needed:**
- Normal iron levels
- Postmenopausal (unless deficient)
- Can cause constipation, nausea

**Best Practice:**
- Get tested before supplementing
- Take with vitamin C for absorption
- Away from calcium, coffee, tea

### Collagen Supplements

**Claims:**
- Improves skin elasticity
- Reduces wrinkles
- Supports joint health

**Evidence:**
- Limited human studies
- Some evidence for skin hydration
- Body breaks down collagen into amino acids

**Better Approach:**
- Adequate protein intake
- Vitamin C for collagen synthesis
- Sun protection for skin health
