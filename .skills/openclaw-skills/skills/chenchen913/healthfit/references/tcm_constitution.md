# Nine Body Constitutions Wellness Plans (TCM) - Complete Version

> **Purpose:** Complete wellness plans for all nine TCM constitutions  
> **Evidence Base:** TCM Constitution Classification and Diagnosis (GB/T 2009/2024)  
> **Last Updated:** 2026-03-18

---

## 1. Balanced Constitution (平和质 Píng Hé Zhì)

**Population:** 5-10%

### Characteristics
- **Core:** Yin-yang balanced, qi-blood harmonious
- **Appearance:** Healthy complexion, good energy
- **Tongue:** Pale red, thin white coating
- **Pulse:** Regular, moderate strength

### Wellness Principles
- **Goal:** Maintain balance, prevent disease
- **Approach:** Moderate in all things

### Dietary Therapy
**Recommended:**
- Balanced, varied diet
- All food groups in moderation
- Seasonal foods

**Avoid:**
- Excessive consumption of any one food
- Extremely cold or hot foods

### Exercise
**Recommended:**
- Moderate intensity
- Mix of cardio and strength
- 150 minutes/week

**Activities:**
- Jogging, swimming, cycling
- Tai Chi, Baduanjin
- Team sports

### Lifestyle
- Regular sleep schedule
- Stress management
- Maintain healthy weight

## 2. Qi Deficiency Constitution (气虚质 Qì Xū Zhì)

**Population:** 15-20%

### Characteristics
- **Core:** Insufficient vital energy
- **Symptoms:** Fatigue, shortness of breath, spontaneous sweating
- **Tongue:** Pale, tooth-marked, thin white coating
- **Pulse:** Weak, especially right guan position

### Dietary Therapy
**Recommended Foods:**
- **Grains:** Rice, millet, oats, sweet potato
- **Proteins:** Chicken, beef, fish, eggs
- **Vegetables:** Carrot, pumpkin, Chinese yam (Shānyào)
- **Fruits:** Jujube (Dàzǎo), longan, cherry

**Herbal Tea:**
- **Astragalus Tea:** Huángqí 10g + Dàzǎo 3 pieces
- **Ginseng Tea:** Rénshēn 3g (or Dǎngshēn 10g as substitute)

**Avoid:**
- Raw, cold foods
- Bitter, cooling foods (bitter melon, mung bean)

### Exercise
**Recommended:**
- Low to moderate intensity
- Avoid excessive sweating
- Focus on consistency

**Activities:**
- Walking, Tai Chi, Qigong
- Baduanjin (especially "Regulate the Spleen and Stomach")
- Gentle yoga

**Avoid:**
- High-intensity interval training
- Excessive cardio
- Exercising to exhaustion

### Acupressure
**Key Points:**
- **Zúsānlǐ (ST36):** 3 cun below ST35, one finger-width lateral to tibial crest
  - Tonifies Qi, strengthens Spleen and Stomach
  - Massage 2-3 minutes, twice daily
- **Qìhǎi (CV6):** 1.5 cun below navel
  - Tonifies original Qi
  - Moxibustion 10-15 minutes daily

### Lifestyle
- Avoid overexertion
- Take regular breaks
- 7-8 hours sleep
- Keep warm, avoid wind

## 3. Yang Deficiency Constitution (阳虚质 Yáng Xū Zhì)

**Population:** 10-15%

### Characteristics
- **Core:** Insufficient warming Yang Qi
- **Symptoms:** Cold intolerance, cold limbs, prefers warm drinks
- **Tongue:** Pale, swollen, white coating
- **Pulse:** Deep, weak, especially chi position

### Dietary Therapy
**Recommended Foods:**
- **Proteins:** Lamb, beef, shrimp, eel
- **Spices:** Ginger, cinnamon, cloves, fennel
- **Nuts:** Walnuts, chestnuts
- **Vegetables:** Leek, chives, onion, garlic

**Herbal Tea:**
- **Ginger Jujube Tea:** Shēngjiāng 3 slices + Dàzǎo 3 pieces
- **Cinnamon Tea:** Ròuguì 3g + Hóngchá 5g

**Avoid:**
- Cold nature foods: Cucumber, watermelon, mung bean
- Cold drinks and ice cream
- Raw foods

### Exercise
**Recommended:**
- Warming exercises
- Morning exercise (embrace Yang Qi)
- Moderate intensity

**Activities:**
- Tai Chi, Taijiquan
- Baduanjin (especially "Separate Heaven and Earth")
- Brisk walking in sunshine
- Dancing

**Avoid:**
- Swimming in cold water
- Exercising in cold, windy weather
- Night exercise

### Acupressure
**Key Points:**
- **Mìngmén (GV4):** Between L2-L3 spinous processes
  - Tonifies Kidney Yang
  - Moxibustion 15-20 minutes daily
- **Guānyuán (CV4):** 3 cun below navel
  - Warms lower burner
  - Moxibustion 15 minutes daily

### Lifestyle
- Keep warm, especially lower back and feet
- Hot foot bath before bed (40-45°C, 20 minutes)
- Avoid air conditioning
- Early to bed, early to rise

## 4. Yin Deficiency Constitution (阴虚质 Yīn Xū Zhì)

**Population:** 10-15%

### Characteristics
- **Core:** Insufficient nourishing Yin fluids
- **Symptoms:** Heat intolerance, dry mouth, night sweats
- **Tongue:** Red, little or no coating, cracks
- **Pulse:** Thready, rapid

### Dietary Therapy
**Recommended Foods:**
- **Proteins:** Duck, pork, turtle, clam
- **Grains:** Black sesame, wheat, millet
- **Vegetables:** Spinach, tomato, cucumber, lotus root
- **Fruits:** Pear, watermelon, mulberry

**Herbal Tea:**
- **Lily Bulb Tea:** Bǎihé 15g + Bīngtáng 10g
- **Dendrobium Tea:** Shíhú 10g + Gòuqǐzǐ 10g

**Avoid:**
- Warming foods: Lamb, chili, ginger, garlic
- Fried, grilled foods
- Alcohol, coffee

### Exercise
**Recommended:**
- Moderate intensity
- Avoid excessive sweating
- Cooling activities

**Activities:**
- Swimming
- Tai Chi
- Yoga (gentle, restorative)
- Walking in cool environments

**Avoid:**
- High-intensity exercise
- Exercising in hot weather
- Midday exercise (11 AM - 3 PM)

### Acupressure
**Key Points:**
- **Sānyīnjiāo (SP6):** 3 cun above medial malleolus
  - Nourishes Yin, benefits Kidney, Liver, Spleen
  - Massage 3 minutes, twice daily
- **Tàixī (KI3):** Between medial malleolus and Achilles tendon
  - Tonifies Kidney Yin
  - Massage 2-3 minutes, twice daily

### Lifestyle
- Avoid staying up late
- Manage stress (depletes Yin)
- Cool, humid environment
- Moderate sexual activity

## 5. Phlegm-Dampness Constitution (痰湿质 Tán Shī Zhì)

**Population:** 15-20%

### Characteristics
- **Core:** Impaired fluid metabolism
- **Symptoms:** Overweight, abdominal obesity, sticky mouth sensation
- **Tongue:** Swollen, white greasy coating
- **Pulse:** Slippery

### Dietary Therapy
**Recommended Foods:**
- **Grains:** Coix seed (Yìyǐrén), barley, corn
- **Vegetables:** Radish, winter melon, celery, seaweed
- **Proteins:** Carp, clam
- **Tea:** Puerh tea, Oolong tea

**Herbal Tea:**
- **Coix Seed Tea:** Yìyǐrén 30g + Chìxiǎodòu 30g
- **Chenpi Tea:** Chénpí 10g + Fúlíng 15g

**Avoid:**
- Greasy, fried foods
- Sweets, desserts
- Dairy products
- Alcohol
- Cold drinks

### Exercise
**Recommended:**
- Regular, consistent exercise
- Moderate to vigorous intensity
- Promote sweating

**Activities:**
- Brisk walking, jogging
- Cycling
- Dancing
- Baduanjin (especially "Regulate the Spleen and Stomach")

**Frequency:** At least 5 times/week, 30+ minutes

### Acupressure
**Key Points:**
- **Fēnglóng (ST40):** Midpoint between ST35 and ST41
  - Resolves Phlegm
  - Massage 3-5 minutes, twice daily
- **Zhōngwǎn (CV12):** 4 cun above navel
  - Strengthens Spleen, resolves Dampness
  - Moxibustion 10-15 minutes daily

### Lifestyle
- Avoid damp environments
- Regular meal times
- Don't overeat
- Maintain healthy weight

## 6. Damp-Heat Constitution (湿热质 Shī Rè Zhì)

**Population:** 10-15%

### Characteristics
- **Core:** Dampness combined with Heat
- **Symptoms:** Oily face, bitter taste, acne, yellow urine
- **Tongue:** Red, yellow greasy coating
- **Pulse:** Slippery, rapid

### Dietary Therapy
**Recommended Foods:**
- **Vegetables:** Bitter melon, cucumber, winter melon, mung bean
- **Grains:** Mung bean, red bean, barley
- **Proteins:** Duck, clam, crab

**Herbal Tea:**
- **Mung Bean Soup:** Lǜdòu 50g, boil until soft
- **Honeysuckle Tea:** Jīnyínhuā 10g + Júhuā 10g

**Avoid:**
- Spicy, pungent foods
- Warming foods: Lamb, chili, ginger
- Alcohol
- Fried, grilled foods

### Exercise
**Recommended:**
- Vigorous exercise
- Promote sweating
- Outdoor activities

**Activities:**
- Running, basketball, football
- Swimming
- High-intensity training

### Acupressure
**Key Points:**
- **Yīnlíngquán (SP9):** Below medial condyle of tibia
  - Drains Dampness
  - Massage 3-5 minutes, twice daily
- **Qūchí (LI11):** At lateral end of elbow crease
  - Clears Heat
  - Massage 3 minutes, twice daily

### Lifestyle
- Avoid humid environments
- Wear loose, breathable clothing
- Regular bowel movements
- Manage stress

## 7. Blood Stasis Constitution (血瘀质 Xuè Yū Zhì)

**Population:** 5-10%

### Characteristics
- **Core:** Poor blood circulation
- **Symptoms:** Dark complexion, spots, stabbing pain
- **Tongue:** Dark purple, petechiae, sublingual vein distension
- **Pulse:** Choppy

### Dietary Therapy
**Recommended Foods:**
- **Proteins:** Black fungus, hawthorn, vinegar
- **Vegetables:** Eggplant, carrot, onion
- **Fruits:** Peach, hawthorn berry

**Herbal Tea:**
- **Hawthorn Tea:** Shānzhā 15g + Hóngtáng 10g
- **Rose Tea:** Méiguīhuā 10g + Hóngzǎo 3 pieces

**Avoid:**
- Cold, congealing foods
- Excessive salt

### Exercise
**Recommended:**
- Dynamic movements
- Promote circulation
- Moderate intensity

**Activities:**
- Dancing
- Tai Chi
- Aerobics
- Walking

### Acupressure
**Key Points:**
- **Xuèhǎi (SP10):** 2 cun above medial condyle of femur
  - Invigorates Blood
  - Massage 3-5 minutes, twice daily
- **Sānyīnjiāo (SP6):** 3 cun above medial malleolus
  - Benefits Blood
  - Massage 3 minutes, twice daily

### Lifestyle
- Keep warm
- Avoid emotional stagnation
- Regular exercise
- Massage and stretching

## 8. Qi Stagnation Constitution (气郁质 Qì Yù Zhì)

**Population:** 5-10%

### Characteristics
- **Core:** Impaired Qi flow
- **Symptoms:** Emotional depression, sighing, chest tightness
- **Tongue:** Normal or slightly red, thin white coating
- **Pulse:** Wiry

### Dietary Therapy
**Recommended Foods:**
- **Foods with aromatic properties:** Orange peel, rose, chrysanthemum
- **Grains:** Wheat, oats
- **Vegetables:** Celery, spinach, broccoli

**Herbal Tea:**
- **Rose Tea:** Méiguīhuā 10g + Fóshǒu 10g
- **Chrysanthemum Tea:** Júhuā 10g + Gòuqǐzǐ 10g

**Avoid:**
- Astringent foods
- Excessive caffeine

### Exercise
**Recommended:**
- Social activities
- Outdoor activities
- Expressive movements

**Activities:**
- Group sports
- Dancing
- Hiking with friends
- Tai Chi

### Acupressure
**Key Points:**
- **Tàichōng (LR3):** On dorsum of foot, between 1st and 2nd metatarsals
  - Soothes Liver, regulates Qi
  - Massage 3-5 minutes, twice daily
- **Hégǔ (LI4):** Between 1st and 2nd metacarpals
  - Regulates Qi
  - Massage 3 minutes, twice daily

### Lifestyle
- Social interaction
- Emotional expression
- Hobbies and interests
- Avoid isolation

## 9. Inherited Special Constitution (特禀质 Tè Bǐng Zhì)

**Population:** 5%

### Characteristics
- **Core:** Congenital abnormality or allergic tendency
- **Symptoms:** Allergies (food, drug, pollen), asthma, hives
- **Tongue:** Variable
- **Pulse:** Variable

### Dietary Therapy
**Recommended:**
- Identify and avoid allergens
- Balanced, simple diet
- Cooked foods over raw

**Foods to Consider:**
- Rice, millet (hypoallergenic)
- Cooked vegetables
- Lean proteins

**Avoid:**
- Known allergens
- "Histamine-rich" foods if sensitive
- Processed foods with additives

### Exercise
**Recommended:**
- Moderate intensity
- Avoid triggering environments
- Indoor exercise during high pollen

**Activities:**
- Swimming (if no chlorine sensitivity)
- Indoor cycling
- Yoga
- Tai Chi

### Lifestyle
- Identify and avoid triggers
- Clean environment
- Stress management
- Adequate sleep
