## General Principles

### 1. Balanced Nutrition

**Three Macronutrients:**
- **Protein:** Building blocks for muscle, hormones, enzymes
- **Carbohydrates:** Primary energy source
- **Fats:** Essential for hormone production, vitamin absorption

**Recommended Ratios:**
- Protein: 15-25% of total calories
- Carbohydrates: 45-65% of total calories
- Fats: 20-35% of total calories

### 2. Micronutrients

**Vitamins:**
- Vitamin A: Vision, immune function
- B Complex: Energy metabolism
- Vitamin C: Antioxidant, collagen synthesis
- Vitamin D: Bone health, immune function
- Vitamin E: Antioxidant
- Vitamin K: Blood clotting

**Minerals:**
- Calcium: Bone health, muscle contraction
- Iron: Oxygen transport
- Magnesium: Muscle function, energy production
- Zinc: Immune function, protein synthesis
- Potassium: Fluid balance, nerve function
- Sodium: Fluid balance (don't overconsume)

### 3. Hydration

**Daily Water Intake:**
- Male: 3.7 liters/day (about 15 cups)
- Female: 2.7 liters/day (about 11 cups)

**During Exercise:**
- Light exercise (<1 hour): Water is sufficient
- Intense exercise (>1 hour): Consider electrolyte drinks

**Signs of Dehydration:**
- Dark yellow urine
- Dry mouth
- Fatigue
- Dizziness

## Meal Timing

### Pre-Workout Nutrition

**Timing:** 2-3 hours before workout

**Composition:**
- Carbohydrates: 1-4g/kg body weight
- Protein: 0.2-0.3g/kg body weight
- Low fat and fiber (for easier digestion)

**Example:**
- Oatmeal with banana and protein powder
- Rice with chicken breast and vegetables

### Post-Workout Nutrition

**Timing:** Within 2 hours after workout (anabolic window)

**Composition:**
- Protein: 20-30g (or 0.3-0.4g/kg)
- Carbohydrates: 1-1.2g/kg (if another session within 24 hours)

**Example:**
- Whey protein shake with banana
- Chicken breast with rice and vegetables

### Meal Frequency

**Options:**
- 3 meals/day: Traditional approach
- 4-6 meals/day: May help with hunger control
- Intermittent fasting: 16:8, 5:2, etc.

**Key Point:** Total daily intake matters more than meal frequency

## Special Considerations

### Vegetarian/Vegan

**Protein Sources:**
- Legumes (beans, lentils, chickpeas)
- Soy products (tofu, tempeh, edamame)
- Seitan (wheat gluten)
- Quinoa
- Nuts and seeds

**Nutrients to Watch:**
- Vitamin B12: Supplement recommended
- Iron: Pair with vitamin C for better absorption
- Calcium: Fortified plant milks, leafy greens
- Omega-3: Flaxseed, chia seeds, algae oil

### Food Allergies

**Common Allergens:**
- Milk/Dairy
- Eggs
- Peanuts
- Tree nuts
- Soy
- Wheat/Gluten
- Fish
- Shellfish

**Substitution Strategies:**
- Dairy allergy: Plant-based alternatives (almond, oat, soy milk)
- Egg allergy: Flax eggs, commercial egg replacers
- Nut allergy: Seeds (sunflower, pumpkin) as alternatives

## Supplement Guidelines

### Evidence-Based Supplements

| Supplement | Evidence Level | Recommended For | Dosage |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|--------|
| Whey Protein | Grade A | Inadequate protein intake | 20-30g/serving |
| Creatine Monohydrate | Grade A | Strength/power athletes | 3-5g/day |
| Vitamin D3 | Grade B | Inadequate sun exposure | 1000-2000 IU/day |
| Omega-3 (Fish Oil) | Grade B | Cardiovascular health | 1-2g EPA+DHA/day |
| Caffeine | Grade A | Pre-workout energy | 3-6mg/kg |

### Not Recommended (Lack of Evidence)

- Fat burners (mostly stimulants)
- BCAAs (if adequate protein intake)
- Testosterone boosters (most ineffective)
- Detox/cleanse products

## Behavior Change Strategies

### Goal Setting

**SMART Goals:**
- **S**pecific: Clear and well-defined
- **M**easurable: Track progress
- **A**chievable: Realistic
- **R**elevant: Aligns with values
- **T**ime-bound: Has deadline

**Example:**
❌ "I want to lose weight"
✅ "I will lose 5kg in 10 weeks by exercising 4 times/week and reducing daily calories by 500kcal"

### Self-Monitoring

**Effective Methods:**
- Food diary (app or notebook)
- Weekly weigh-ins (same time, same conditions)
- Progress photos (monthly)
- Workout logs

### Habit Formation

**Strategies:**
- Start small (one change at a time)
- Stack habits (after X, I will do Y)
- Remove barriers (prepare meals in advance)
- Reward progress (non-food rewards)
