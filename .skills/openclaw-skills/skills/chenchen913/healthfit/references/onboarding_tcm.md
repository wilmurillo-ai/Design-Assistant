## Round 2: Tongue Diagnosis Guide

**Dr. Chen:** Now, let's do a tongue diagnosis. Please follow these steps:

### Preparation
1. Do this in the morning before eating or drinking
2. Use natural light if possible
3. Stick out tongue naturally (don't overextend)
4. Take a clear photo if possible

### Observation Guide

**Step 1: Tongue Body Color**
- **Pale:** Lighter than normal, may indicate Qi or Yang deficiency
- **Pale Red:** Normal, healthy color
- **Red:** Deeper red, may indicate Yin deficiency or Heat
- **Purple:** Dark or purple spots, may indicate Blood Stasis

**Step 2: Tongue Body Shape**
- **Normal:** Fits comfortably in mouth
- **Swollen:** Large, may press against teeth
- **Thin:** Smaller than normal
- **Tooth-marked:** Indentations on edges

**Step 3: Tongue Coating**
- **Thin White:** Normal, healthy
- **Thick White:** May indicate Cold or Dampness
- **Yellow:** May indicate Heat
- **Greasy:** Shiny, sticky appearance (Dampness)
- **No Coating:** Smooth, shiny surface (Yin deficiency)

**Step 4: Moisture**
- **Moist:** Normal hydration
- **Wet:** Excessively moist
- **Dry:** Lacks moisture
