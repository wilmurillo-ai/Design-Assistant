## A. Training Core Parameters (Source: NSCA)

**Source:** National Strength and Conditioning Association (NSCA) Official Textbooks and Position Statements  
**Website:** https://www.nsca.com/about-us/position-statements/

### Training Frequency Recommendations

| Training Level | Full Body Training Frequency | Split Training Frequency |
|---------------|-----------------------------|-------------------------|
| Beginner (<3 months experience) | 2-3 times/week | Not recommended |
| Intermediate (3-12 months experience) | 3 times/week | 3-4 times/week |
| Advanced (>12 months experience) | 4 times/week | 4-6 times/week |

### Recommended Training Parameters by Goal

| Goal | Intensity (% 1RM) | Rep Range | Rest Between Sets |
|------|------------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Muscular Endurance | <70% | 12-20+ | 30-60 seconds |
| Hypertrophy | 70-85% | 6-12 | 60-90 seconds |
| Maximum Strength | 85-100% | 1-5 | 3-5 minutes |
| Power | 30-60% (speed priority) | 1-6 | 3-5 minutes |

### Supercompensation Principle

- **Overload Principle:** Training stimulus must exceed current adaptation level
- **Progression Rate:** Beginners recommended weekly increase not exceeding 5-10%
- **Deload Week:** Every 4-8 weeks, arrange 1 week with training volume reduced 40-60%, promoting recovery and supercompensation

### Movement Technique Reference

- Movement classification system reference: ExRx.net Exercise Library (https://exrx.net/Lists/Directory)
- This platform has been operating since 1999, recognized by NSCA, ACSM and other institutions
- Contains 2100+ exercises, is the most authoritative free exercise reference database in fitness field

## B. Nutrient Reference Intakes (Source: Chinese Nutrition Society 2023 Edition)

**Source:** Chinese Nutrition Society "Dietary Reference Intakes for Chinese Residents (2023 Edition)"  
**Publisher:** People's Medical Publishing House, September 2023 (9th Edition)  
**Editor-in-Chief:** Yang Yuexin (President of Chinese Nutrition Society)  
**Website:** https://www.cnsoc.org

### Protein Recommended Nutrient Intake (RNI) for Adults (18-50 years)

- **Male:** 65g/day (light physical labor)
- **Female:** 55g/day (light physical labor)

> **Note:** Above is basic requirement for general population. Exercise population protein requirements are higher, see specific recommendations in each agent file.

### Exercise Population Protein Reference Range (International Society of Sports Nutrition ISSN Position Stand)

| Population | Protein Requirement |
|------------|--------------------|
| Endurance Athletes | 1.2-1.4g/kg/day |
| Strength Trainers (Bulking Phase) | 1.6-2.2g/kg/day |
| Strength Trainers (Cutting Phase) | 2.2-3.1g/kg/day |

### Calcium (2023 Edition Revised Value)

- **18-50 years adults:** 800mg/day (RNI)
- **51+ years:** 1000mg/day
- **Note:** Chinese population dietary calcium intake is generally insufficient (survey shows daily average about 400mg), calcium supplementation has practical significance

### Vitamin D (2023 Edition Note)

- **18-65 years:** 400 IU/day (RNI)
- **Note:** Chinese Nutrition Society notes: vitamin D deficiency rate is high in Chinese residents, initial supplementation recommendation for deficient individuals is 1000-2000 IU/day

### Iron

| Population | Recommended Intake |
|------------|-------------------|
| Adult Male | 12mg/day (RNI) |
| Adult Female (non-pregnant) | 20mg/day (2023 edition adjusted from 2013 edition) |
| Pregnant Women | 29mg/day |

### Zinc (2023 Edition Revised Value)

| Population | Recommended Intake | Note |
|------------|-------------------|------|
| Adult Male | 12.5mg/day | 2023 edition decreased from previous edition |
| Adult Female | 7.5mg/day | 2023 edition increased from previous edition |

> **Note:** New edition revised based on latest zinc metabolism data, male-female gap narrowed

### Mifflin-St Jeor Formula (Basal Metabolic Rate Estimation)

**Formula:**
- Male: BMR = (10 × weight kg) + (6.25 × height cm) - (5 × age) + 5
- Female: BMR = (10 × weight kg) + (6.25 × height cm) - (5 × age) - 161

**Source:** Mifflin MD et al., JADA 1990  
**Status:** Confirmed by AND (Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics) as most accurate static metabolic rate estimation formula

## C. TCM Constitution Classification Authoritative Basis

**Source:** "TCM Constitution Classification and Diagnosis"

### Standard Evolution

| Time | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2009 | China Association of Chinese Medicine officially promulgated (ZYYXH/T157-2009), presided by Academician Wang Qi |
| 2024 | Upgraded to National Standard (GB/T), hosted by National Traditional Chinese Medicine Standardization Technical Committee |
| Application Scale | 1700+ secondary and above TCM hospitals nationwide, cumulative application over 570 million person-times |

### Legal Basis

TCM constitution identification was included in Ministry of Health "National Basic Public Health Service Specification" in 2009, is the only TCM physical examination content included in national public health system.

### Standard Query

- **National Standard Information Public Service Platform:** https://std.samr.gov.cn
- **Beijing University of Chinese Medicine Constitution Research Institute:** https://tizhi.bucm.edu.cn

### AI Constitution Identification Limitations Declaration

> ⚠️ **Important:** This Skill's TCM constitution identification is based on text questionnaires and self-reported symptoms, compared to licensed TCM practitioner's face-to-face diagnosis (inspection, listening/smelling, inquiry, pulse-taking), diagnostic dimensions are incomplete. Identification results are for reference only, do not replace professional TCM diagnosis.
