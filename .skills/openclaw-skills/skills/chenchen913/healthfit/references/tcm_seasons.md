## Spring Season (6 Solar Terms)

### 1. Lìchūn (Start of Spring) - February 3-5

**Solar Term Characteristics:**
- **Climate:** Yang qi begins to rise, temperature gradually warms
- **TCM Interpretation:** Liver qi flourishes, yang emerges from yin

**General Wellness Recommendations:**

**Daily Routine:**
- Early to bed, early to rise (follow yang qi rising)
- Sleep: 21:00-22:00, Wake: 6:00-6:30
- Keep warm (avoid cold wind)

**Diet Focus:**
- Pungent-warm foods (help yang qi spread)
  - Recommended: Leek, cilantro, onion, ginger, garlic
  - Avoid: Too much sour (constrains Liver)
- Seasonal vegetables: Spring bamboo shoots, spinach, Chinese toon

**Exercise:**
- Morning exercise, embrace yang qi
- Recommended: Tai Chi, Baduanjin, walking
- Intensity: Low to moderate

**Emotion:**
- Keep mood pleasant
- Avoid anger (Liver dislikes stagnation)
- Plan for new year

**Constitution-Specific Reminders:**

| Constitution | Key Points |
|-------------|------------|
| Yang Deficiency | Good time to tonify Yang, eat more warming foods |
| Qi Stagnation | Regulate Liver qi, avoid emotional suppression |
| Yin Deficiency | Don't overconsume pungent, may damage Yin |
| Qi Deficiency | Avoid excessive sweating, protect qi |

**Representative Wellness Recipe:**

**Spring Onion and Ginger Porridge**
```
Ingredients: Rice 100g, Spring onion 3 stalks, Ginger 3 slices
Method: Cook rice, add onion and ginger in last 5 minutes
Benefits: Disperses cold, supports Yang
Frequency: 3-4 times/week
```

**Tea: Chrysanthemum Goji Tea**
```
Ingredients: Chrysanthemum 5g, Goji berry 10g
Method: Steep in hot water for 5 minutes
Benefits: Nourishes Liver, clears eyes
Frequency: Daily 1 cup
```
