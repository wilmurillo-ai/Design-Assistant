## 1. Lower Body Push Exercises

### 1.1 Squat

**Target Muscles:** Quadriceps, gluteus maximus, hamstrings, adductors

**Equipment:** Barbell / Dumbbell / Bodyweight

#### Technique Points

**Starting Position:**
- Feet shoulder-width apart or slightly wider, toes turned out 15-30°
- Barbell on trapezius (high bar) or below scapular spine (low bar)
- Core tight, chest up, spine neutral

**Movement Execution:**
1. Inhale, hip and knee flexion, sit hips back and down
2. Knees track over toes, avoid valgus collapse
3. Squat until thighs parallel to ground or lower (maintain spine neutral)
4. Exhale, drive through feet, extend hips and knees to stand
5. Return to starting position, repeat

**Breathing:** Inhale down, exhale up

#### Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Correction |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Knee valgus | Knee joint injury | Strengthen gluteus medius, use resistance band cue |
| Rounded back | Lumbar injury | Reduce weight, strengthen core, keep chest up |
| Heels lifting | Ankle mobility issue | Wear weightlifting shoes, elevate heels, improve ankle mobility |
| Excessive forward lean | Increased lumbar stress | Reduce weight, check ankle mobility, adjust stance |
| Half reps | Reduced training effect | Reduce weight, complete full range of motion |

#### Regression/Progression

**Regressions:**
1. Box squat: Place box behind, sit to box then stand
2. Goblet squat: Hold dumbbell at chest, easier to maintain upright torso
3. Bodyweight squat: No external load

**Progressions:**
1. Front squat: Barbell on front delts, more quad emphasis
2. Overhead squat: Barbell overhead, requires mobility and stability
3. Pistol squat: Single-leg squat, advanced progression

#### Recommended Parameters

| Goal | Sets | Reps | Intensity | Rest |
|------|------|------|-----------|------|
| Muscular Endurance | 2-3 | 15-20 | 60% 1RM | 30-60s |
| Hypertrophy | 3-5 | 8-12 | 70-85% 1RM | 60-90s |
| Maximum Strength | 3-6 | 1-5 | 85-100% 1RM | 3-5min |

### 1.2 Leg Press

**Target Muscles:** Quadriceps, gluteus maximus, hamstrings

**Equipment:** Leg press machine

#### Technique Points

**Starting Position:**
- Sit in machine, back and head against pad
- Feet on platform shoulder-width apart
- Release safety handles

**Movement Execution:**
1. Lower platform by flexing knees and hips
2. Knees track over toes
3. Lower until knees at 90° (or comfortable depth)
4. Press through heels to extend legs
5. Don't lock knees at top

**Breathing:** Inhale down, exhale up

#### Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Correction |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Locking knees | Knee joint stress | Keep slight knee bend at top |
| Lifting hips off pad | Lumbar injury | Keep back pressed against pad |
| Knee valgus | Knee injury | Strengthen glutes, cue knees out |
| Too deep | Lower back rounding | Reduce range, maintain neutral spine |

---

### 1.3 Bulgarian Split Squat

**Target Muscles:** Quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings (unilateral)

**Equipment:** Dumbbells / Bodyweight

#### Technique Points

**Starting Position:**
- Stand 2-3 feet in front of bench
- Place top of one foot on bench behind
- Hold dumbbells at sides (or bodyweight)

**Movement Execution:**
1. Lower body by flexing front knee and hip
2. Keep torso upright, front knee over toes
3. Lower until front thigh parallel to ground
4. Drive through front heel to return
5. Complete reps, switch legs

**Breathing:** Inhale down, exhale up

## 2. Lower Body Pull Exercises

### 2.1 Deadlift

**Target Muscles:** Hamstrings, glutes, erector spinae, traps

**Equipment:** Barbell

#### Technique Points

**Starting Position:**
- Bar over mid-foot
- Feet hip-width apart
- Grip bar just outside legs
- Hips lower than shoulders, chest up
- Spine neutral, lats engaged

**Movement Execution:**
1. Take slack out of bar
2. Drive through feet, extend hips and knees
3. Keep bar close to body
4. Stand tall, shoulders back
5. Lower with control, hip hinge first

**Breathing:** Inhale and brace before lift, exhale at top

#### Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Correction |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Rounded back | Lumbar injury | Engage lats, chest up, reduce weight |
| Bar away from body | Increased shear force | Keep bar close, engage lats |
| Hips rise first | Becomes good morning | Drive through feet, extend together |
| Hyperextension at top | Lumbar stress | Stand tall, don't lean back |

---

### 2.2 Romanian Deadlift (RDL)

**Target Muscles:** Hamstrings, glutes, erector spinae

**Equipment:** Barbell / Dumbbells

#### Technique Points

**Starting Position:**
- Stand with bar at hip level
- Feet hip-width apart
- Soft knee bend (don't lock)

**Movement Execution:**
1. Hinge at hips, push hips back
2. Keep back flat throughout
3. Bar close to legs
4. Feel hamstring stretch at bottom
5. Drive hips forward to return

**Breathing:** Inhale down, exhale up

## 3. Glute-Specific Exercises

### 3.1 Hip Thrust

**Target Muscles:** Gluteus maximus

**Equipment:** Barbell / Bodyweight / Resistance Band

#### Technique Points

**Starting Position:**
- Upper back on bench
- Bar over hips (use pad)
- Feet flat, knees at 90°

**Movement Execution:**
1. Drive through heels
2. Extend hips, squeeze glutes at top
3. Body forms straight line at top
4. Lower with control
5. Don't hyperextend at top

**Breathing:** Exhale up, inhale down

---

### 3.2 Glute Bridge

**Target Muscles:** Gluteus maximus, hamstrings

**Equipment:** Bodyweight / Resistance Band / Barbell

#### Technique Points

**Starting Position:**
- Lie on back, knees bent
- Feet flat, hip-width apart
- Arms at sides

**Movement Execution:**
1. Drive through heels
2. Lift hips, squeeze glutes
3. Body forms straight line
4. Hold 1-2 seconds at top
5. Lower with control

## 4. Upper Body Push Exercises

### 4.1 Bench Press

**Target Muscles:** Pectoralis major, anterior deltoids, triceps

**Equipment:** Barbell / Dumbbells

#### Technique Points

**Starting Position:**
- Lie on bench, eyes under bar
- Feet flat on floor
- Grip slightly wider than shoulder-width
- Retract scapulae

**Movement Execution:**
1. Unrack bar, hold over chest
2. Lower to mid-chest with control
3. Elbows at 45° to body
4. Press bar up and slightly back
5. Lock out at top

**Breathing:** Inhale down, exhale up

---

### 4.2 Overhead Press

**Target Muscles:** Deltoids, triceps, upper chest

**Equipment:** Barbell / Dumbbells

#### Technique Points

**Starting Position:**
- Stand with feet shoulder-width
- Bar at shoulder level
- Core tight, glutes engaged

**Movement Execution:**
1. Press bar overhead
2. Head moves back slightly
3. Lock out at top
4. Lower with control to shoulders

**Breathing:** Inhale down, exhale up

## 5. Upper Body Pull Exercises

### 5.1 Pull-up

**Target Muscles:** Latissimus dorsi, biceps, rear delts

**Equipment:** Pull-up bar

#### Technique Points

**Starting Position:**
- Hang from bar, palms away
- Hands slightly wider than shoulders
- Dead hang position

**Movement Execution:**
1. Pull body up
2. Lead with chest
3. Chin over bar
4. Lower with control

**Breathing:** Exhale up, inhale down

#### Regression Options:
- Assisted pull-up machine
- Band-assisted pull-up
- Negative pull-ups (jump up, lower slowly)

---

### 5.2 Bent-Over Row

**Target Muscles:** Lats, rhomboids, rear delts, biceps

**Equipment:** Barbell / Dumbbells

#### Technique Points

**Starting Position:**
- Hinge at hips, torso at 45°
- Knees slightly bent
- Bar hanging at arm's length

**Movement Execution:**
1. Pull bar to lower chest
2. Squeeze shoulder blades
3. Lower with control
4. Keep back flat throughout

## 6. Core Exercises

### 6.1 Plank

**Target Muscles:** Transverse abdominis, rectus abdominis, obliques

**Equipment:** Bodyweight

#### Technique Points

**Starting Position:**
- Forearms on ground
- Body forms straight line
- Core engaged, glutes tight

**Hold Position:**
- Maintain neutral spine
- Don't let hips sag or pike
- Breathe normally

**Duration:** 30-60 seconds per set

---

### 6.2 Dead Bug

**Target Muscles:** Deep core, transverse abdominis

**Equipment:** Bodyweight

#### Technique Points

**Starting Position:**
- Lie on back
- Arms extended toward ceiling
- Knees bent 90° (tabletop)

**Movement Execution:**
1. Lower opposite arm and leg
2. Keep lower back pressed to floor
3. Return to start
4. Alternate sides

## 7. Cardio Programs

### 7.1 LISS (Low-Intensity Steady State)

**Target:** Cardiovascular endurance, fat oxidation

**Protocol:**
- Intensity: 60-70% maximum heart rate
- Duration: 30-60 minutes
- Frequency: 2-3 times/week

**Activities:**
- Jogging
- Cycling
- Swimming
- Brisk walking

---

### 7.2 HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training)

**Target:** VO2max, anaerobic capacity, EPOC

**Protocol:**
- Work: 30 seconds at 85-95% maximum effort
- Rest: 30-60 seconds active recovery
- Repeat: 8-12 rounds
- Frequency: 1-2 times/week

**Example Workout:**
```
Warm-up: 5 minutes light jogging

Main Set:
- Sprint 30 seconds
- Walk 30 seconds
- Repeat 10 times

Cool-down: 5 minutes walking
Total: 20 minutes
```

## 8. Traditional Exercises

### 8.1 Baduanjin (Eight Brocade Exercises)

**Source:** Traditional Chinese Qigong

**Benefits:** Regulate qi and blood, strengthen organs, improve flexibility

#### Eight Movements:

**1. Two Hands Hold up the Heavens**
- Regulates: Triple Burner (San Jiao)
- Repetitions: 6-8 times

**2. Draw the Bow to Shoot the Eagle**
- Regulates: Lungs, Large Intestine
- Repetitions: 3-5 times each side

**3. Separate Heaven and Earth**
- Regulates: Spleen, Stomach
- Repetitions: 3-5 times each side

**4. Wise Owl Gazes Backwards**
- Regulates: Heart, Small Intestine
- Repetitions: 3-5 times each side

**5. Sway the Head and Shake the Tail**
- Regulates: Heart, Kidneys
- Repetitions: 3-5 times each side

**6. Two Hands Hold the Feet**
- Regulates: Kidneys, Bladder
- Repetitions: 6-8 times

**7. Clench the Fists and Glare Fiercely**
- Regulates: Liver, Gallbladder
- Repetitions: 3-5 times each side

**8. Bounce on the Toes Seven Times**
- Regulates: All meridians
- Repetitions: 7 times

#### Practice Recommendations:
- **Time:** 15-20 minutes for complete form
- **Frequency:** Daily 1-2 times
- **Best Time:** Morning 6-8 AM or Evening 5-7 PM
- **Notes:** Movements slow and continuous, coordinate with breathing

## Additional Exercises (Supplement)

### Lower Body Push - Additional Variations

#### 1.4 Lunge

**Target Muscles:** Quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings

**Equipment:** Bodyweight / Dumbbells

**Technique Points:**
- Step forward with one leg
- Lower hips until both knees bent at 90°
- Front knee over ankle, not past toes
- Back knee hovers above ground
- Push back to starting position

**Common Mistakes:**
- Knee valgus → Strengthen glutes
- Front knee past toes → Shorten stride
- Torso leaning forward → Keep upright

**Sets/Reps:** 3 sets × 10-12 reps per leg

---

#### 1.5 Calf Raise

**Target Muscles:** Gastrocnemius, soleus

**Equipment:** Bodyweight / Machine / Dumbbells

**Technique Points:**
- Stand with balls of feet on elevated surface
- Lower heels below platform level
- Rise up on toes, contract calves
- Hold 1-2 seconds at top
- Lower with control

**Sets/Reps:** 4 sets × 15-20 reps

---

**Target Muscles:** Obliques, rectus abdominis

**Equipment:** Bodyweight / Medicine ball

**Technique Points:**
- Sit on floor, lean back 45°
- Lift feet off ground

**Target Muscles:** Lower abs, hip flexors

**Equipment:** Pull-up bar

**Technique Points:**
- Hang from bar

- Lower with control

**Equipment:** Ab wheel

- Continuous running with varied pace
- Alternate fast and slow segments

- 5 min warm-up jog

## Exercise Selection by Goal

### For Fat Loss
- Compound movements (squat, deadlift, bench)
- HIIT 2-3 times/week
- Moderate weights, higher reps (12-15)
- Short rest periods (30-60 seconds)

### For Muscle Building
- Progressive overload on compound lifts
- 8-12 rep range for hypertrophy
- 3-5 sets per exercise
- 48-72 hours rest per muscle group

### For Strength
- Heavy compound lifts (85-100% 1RM)
- Low reps (1-5)
- Long rest (3-5 minutes)
- Focus on technique

### For Endurance
- Higher reps (15-20+)
- Lower weights (<65% 1RM)
- Short rest (30-60 seconds)
- Cardiovascular training

## Safety Guidelines

### Before Training
1. Warm up for 5-10 minutes
2. Dynamic stretching
3. Start with light weights

### During Training
1. Maintain proper form
2. Don't hold breath (breathe continuously)
3. Stop if pain occurs

### After Training
1. Cool down for 5 minutes
2. Static stretching
3. Hydrate and refuel
