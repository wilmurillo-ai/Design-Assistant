# Male-Specific Nutrition Guide

> **Purpose:** Nutrition recommendations tailored for male physiological characteristics  
> **Last Updated:** 2026-03-17  
> **Evidence Base:** ISSN, Chinese Nutrition Society 2023

---

## 1. Testosterone Support Nutrition

### Key Nutrients

**Zinc:**
- **Function:** Essential for testosterone production
- **RDA:** 12.5mg/day (Chinese Nutrition Society 2023)
- **Food Sources:**
  - Oysters: 74mg per 100g (highest source)
  - Beef: 4-8mg per 100g
  - Pumpkin seeds: 7-8mg per 100g
  - Crab: 6-7mg per 100g
- **Supplementation:** 15-30mg/day if deficient

**Vitamin D:**
- **Function:** Hormone production, bone health
- **RDA:** 400 IU/day (1000-2000 IU if deficient)
- **Food Sources:**
  - Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel): 400-600 IU per serving
  - Egg yolks: 40 IU per yolk
  - Fortified milk: 100 IU per cup
- **Sun Exposure:** 15-20 minutes midday sun

**Magnesium:**
- **Function:** Supports testosterone, muscle function
- **RDA:** 400-420mg/day
- **Food Sources:**
  - Spinach: 157mg per cup (cooked)
  - Almonds: 80mg per 30g
  - Black beans: 120mg per cup
- **Supplementation:** 200-400mg before bed

**Omega-3 Fatty Acids:**
- **Function:** Reduces inflammation, supports hormone production
- **Recommendation:** 1-2g EPA+DHA/day
- **Food Sources:**
  - Salmon: 2g per 100g
  - Sardines: 1.5g per 100g
  - Fish oil supplement: 1g per capsule

### Foods to Limit

**Alcohol:**
- Chronic heavy consumption reduces testosterone
- Limit: ≤2 drinks/day, ≤14 drinks/week
- Avoid binge drinking

**Trans Fats:**
- Found in: Fried foods, processed snacks
- Effect: Increases inflammation, reduces testosterone
- Recommendation: Minimize or eliminate

**Soy Products (Controversial):**
- Contains phytoestrogens (plant estrogens)
- Moderate consumption OK (1-2 servings/day)
- Avoid excessive intake (>3 servings/day)

## 2. Muscle Building Nutrition (Bulking)

### Calorie Requirements

**Calculation:**
- Maintenance calories (TDEE) + 300-500 kcal surplus
- Example: TDEE 2500 kcal → Bulking: 2800-3000 kcal

### Macronutrient Targets

**Protein:**
- **Target:** 1.8g/kg body weight
- **Timing:** Distribute across 4-5 meals
- **Sources:** Chicken, beef, fish, eggs, whey protein

**Carbohydrates:**
- **Target:** 4-6g/kg body weight
- **Timing:** Higher around workouts
- **Sources:** Rice, oats, potatoes, fruits

**Fats:**
- **Target:** 1.0-1.2g/kg body weight
- **Focus:** Quality sources
- **Sources:** Nuts, olive oil, avocado, fatty fish

### Pre-Workout Nutrition

**Timing:** 2-3 hours before training

**Meal Composition:**
- Carbohydrates: 1-2g/kg
- Protein: 0.2-0.3g/kg
- Low fat (for faster digestion)

**Example:**
- Rice 200g + Chicken breast 150g + Vegetables
- Oatmeal 80g + Whey protein 1 scoop + Banana

### Post-Workout Nutrition

**Timing:** Within 2 hours after training

**Meal Composition:**
- Protein: 20-30g (or 0.3-0.4g/kg)
- Carbohydrates: 1-1.2g/kg (if training again within 24 hours)

**Example:**
- Whey protein shake + Banana
- Chicken breast 200g + Rice 300g + Vegetables

## 3. Fat Loss Nutrition (Cutting)

### Calorie Requirements

**Calculation:**
- Maintenance calories (TDEE) - 500 kcal deficit
- Maximum deficit: 0.5-1% body weight per week

### Macronutrient Targets

**Protein:**
- **Target:** 2.2-2.5g/kg body weight (higher to preserve muscle)
- **Importance:** Critical during calorie deficit

**Carbohydrates:**
- **Target:** 2-3g/kg body weight
- **Strategy:** Time around workouts
- **Rest days:** Reduce to 1-2g/kg

**Fats:**
- **Target:** 0.8-1.0g/kg body weight
- **Don't go too low:** Essential for hormone production

### Cutting Strategies

**Carb Cycling:**
- **Training days:** Higher carbs (3g/kg)
- **Rest days:** Lower carbs (1-2g/kg)
- **Benefit:** Maintains training performance while losing fat

**Refeed Days:**
- **Frequency:** 1 day per 7-10 days
- **Purpose:** Boost leptin, reduce metabolic adaptation
- **Implementation:** Increase carbs by 50-100g

## 4. Post-Workout Recovery Nutrition

### Anabolic Window

**Current Understanding:**
- Window is larger than previously thought (up to 2 hours)
- Total daily intake more important than timing
- Still beneficial to eat within 2 hours post-workout

### Recovery Meal Components

**Protein:**
- 20-30g high-quality protein
- Leucine-rich sources (whey, eggs, meat)

**Carbohydrates:**
- Replenish glycogen stores
- 1-1.2g/kg if training again within 24 hours
- 0.5g/kg if next training is >24 hours away

**Fluids:**
- Replace 150% of fluid lost through sweat
- Add electrolytes if session >90 minutes

## 5. Evidence-Based Supplements for Men

### Grade A (Strong Evidence)

**Creatine Monohydrate:**
- **Benefits:** Strength, power, muscle mass
- **Dosage:** 3-5g/day (no loading necessary)
- **Timing:** Any time, with meal for better absorption
- **Safety:** Extensively researched, safe for healthy individuals

**Whey Protein:**
- **Benefits:** Convenient protein source, high leucine
- **Dosage:** 20-30g per serving
- **Timing:** Post-workout or when convenient
- **Types:** Concentrate (good value), Isolate (lower lactose)

**Caffeine:**
- **Benefits:** Pre-workout energy, focus
- **Dosage:** 3-6mg/kg, 30-60 minutes before training
- **Tolerance:** Cycle to prevent tolerance buildup

### Grade B (Moderate Evidence)

**Vitamin D3:**
- **Benefits:** Bone health, immune function, testosterone support
- **Dosage:** 1000-2000 IU/day
- **Timing:** With meal (fat-soluble)

**Omega-3 (Fish Oil):**
- **Benefits:** Cardiovascular health, inflammation reduction
- **Dosage:** 1-2g EPA+DHA/day
- **Timing:** With meals

**Beta-Alanine:**
- **Benefits:** Muscular endurance (1-4 minute efforts)
- **Dosage:** 3-5g/day
- **Note:** Causes tingling sensation (harmless)

### Not Recommended (Lack of Evidence)

**Testosterone Boosters:**
- Most ingredients ineffective
- Save money on whole foods instead

**Fat Burners:**
- Mostly stimulants
- No long-term fat loss benefit
- Potential side effects

**BCAAs:**
- Unnecessary if adequate protein intake
- Whole protein sources superior

## 6. Sample Meal Plans

### Bulking Day (3000 kcal)

```
Breakfast (600 kcal):
- Oatmeal 100g
- Whey protein 1 scoop
- Banana 1 large
- Almond butter 2 tbsp

Lunch (700 kcal):
- Chicken breast 200g
- Brown rice 300g
- Mixed vegetables 200g
- Olive oil 1 tbsp

Pre-Workout (400 kcal):
- Greek yogurt 200g
- Berries 150g
- Granola 50g

Post-Workout (600 kcal):
- Whey protein 2 scoops
- Banana 1 large
- White rice 300g

Dinner (700 kcal):
- Salmon 200g
- Sweet potato 300g
- Asparagus 200g
- Avocado 1/2
```

### Cutting Day (2000 kcal)

```
Breakfast (400 kcal):
- Eggs 3 whole
- Egg whites 3
- Spinach 100g
- Whole wheat toast 1 slice

Lunch (500 kcal):
- Chicken breast 200g
- Quinoa 150g
- Broccoli 200g
- Olive oil 1 tsp

Snack (200 kcal):
- Greek yogurt (non-fat) 200g
- Berries 100g

Dinner (600 kcal):
- Cod 200g
- Mixed vegetables 300g
- Sweet potato 200g
- Olive oil 1 tsp

Evening (300 kcal):
- Cottage cheese 200g
- Almonds 15g
```
