# Investment Analysis Configuration

Supplements the generic five-step framework with investment-specific falsification criteria.

## Step 1 Additions: Value Node Decomposition for Investments

When analyzing an investment thesis, the value chain typically looks like:

```
Raw Materials / IP → Core Technology → Product / Service → Channel → End Customer
```

For each link, ask:
- Who captures the most margin? (Usually not the most visible company)
- Is this a commodity or a bottleneck?

Special categories:
- **必需品** (must-have): Will sell regardless of hype cycle
- **可选项** (optional): Nice but substitutable
- **锦上添花** (window dressing): Pure narrative, no revenue tie

## Step 2 Additions: Investment-Specific Falsification Checklist

For each investment target, specifically check:

| Falsification Angle | Red Flags |
|---------------------|-----------|
| Concept substitution | Claiming "AI company" but AI is <10% of revenue |
| Causal chain break | "Industry grows → this company benefits" without showing how |
| Peer comparison | Are there better/cheaper alternatives doing the same thing? |
| Timing | Is the thesis already priced in? Are we late? |
| Management quality | Are they executing or storytelling? |
| Financial evidence | Revenue acceleration? Margin improvement? Order book? |

## Step 3 Additions: Investment Beneficiary Hierarchy

1. **卖铲人** (Infrastructure/tooling/data) — Highest certainty
   - Examples: Chip designers, cloud platforms, training frameworks, data providers
   - Thesis: "Regardless of who wins the end application, they all need X"

2. **挖金人** (Core tech + commercialization) — Highest upside
   - Must have BOTH proprietary tech AND a path to monetization
   - Validate: Are they licensing? Selling products? Or just doing R&D?

3. **用金人** (Application layer) — Often overvalued
   - "Using AI to improve X" — sounds good but value capture is limited
   - Validate: Is the AI component truly defensible, or is it a thin wrapper?

## Step 4 Additions: Investment Stress Tests

- **Valuation anchor**: What multiple are you paying? Is it justified by growth?
- **Catalyst timeline**: What event will prove/disprove the thesis, and when?
- **Downside scenario**: If the bull case is wrong, what's the floor?
- **Liquidity / market structure**: Can you actually exit? Small-cap trap?

## Step 5 Additions: Investment Output

Add to the standard output table:
- **Position sizing suggestion**: Heavy / Medium / Light / Watch-only
- **Key catalysts to watch** (with dates if possible)
- **What would make you change your mind**

## A-Specific Noise Filters

When analyzing Chinese A-share targets, apply extra skepticism:

- **概念炒作** (concept hype): Default assumption for any "概念股"
- **关联度造假**: Company mentions AI/semiconductor/新能源 in filings but has zero related revenue
- **大股东减持**: Insiders selling while the narrative is hot
- **研报一致看好**: When every analyst is bullish, question who's left to buy
- **业绩不达预期**: Narrative stocks live on expectations; one miss can collapse the thesis

**Rule: In A-shares, "good company" ≠ "good investment thesis". The logic matters more than the company.**
