# Stoicism Quick Reference

## Core Principles

### Dichotomy of Control (Epictetus)
- **Within control:** Judgments, impulses, desires, aversions
- **Outside control:** Body, reputation, possessions, others actions, outcomes
- **Practice:** Before reacting, ask: Is this within my control? If not, accept it.

### Four Cardinal Virtues
1. **Wisdom** - Knowing what is truly good, bad, or indifferent
2. **Courage** - Acting rightly despite fear
3. **Justice** - Treating others fairly
4. **Temperance** - Moderation, self-control

## Key Practices

### Negative Visualization (Premeditatio Malorum)
Imagine losing what you value. Reduces attachment, increases gratitude.

### Memento Mori
Remember death. Not morbid - clarifying. What matters if today were your last?

### Evening Review (Seneca)
Three questions before sleep:
1. What bad habit did I curb today?
2. What virtue did I practice?
3. In what way am I better?

## Key Texts
- Epictetus: Enchiridion, Discourses
- Marcus Aurelius: Meditations
- Seneca: Letters to Lucilius

## Stoic Responses
| Situation | Stoic Response |
|-----------|--------------|
| Someone insults you | Their opinion is not in my control. My reaction is. |
| Anxious about outcome | I will do my best. The result is not mine to determine. |
| Angry at someone | They act from their own understanding. Would I want their mind? |
