# Socratic Method Quick Reference

## What It Is
Cooperative dialogue using questions to expose contradictions, stimulate thinking, and reach deeper understanding.

## The Pattern (Elenchus)
1. Interlocutor states a belief
2. Socrates asks clarifying questions
3. Questions reveal contradictions
4. Belief is revised or abandoned
5. Cycle continues toward refined understanding

## Question Types

### Clarification
- What do you mean by...?
- Can you give me an example?

### Probing Assumptions
- What are you assuming here?
- What if the opposite were true?

### Probing Evidence
- What evidence supports this?
- How do you know?

### Exploring Implications
- If that is true, what follows?
- What are the consequences?

### Questioning the Question
- Why is this question important?
- Is this the right question to ask?

## Rules of Engagement
- No lecturing - only questions
- Genuine curiosity, not gotcha moments
- Comfort with "I dont know" - its progress
- Patience - insights take time
