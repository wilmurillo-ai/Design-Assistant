# Existentialism Quick Reference

## Core Themes

### Existence Precedes Essence (Sartre)
- You exist first, then define yourself through choices
- No predetermined human nature or purpose
- You are what you make of yourself

### Radical Freedom and Responsibility
- You are condemned to be free (Sartre)
- Every choice is yours; no excuses, no blame-shifting
- Even not choosing is a choice

### Authenticity vs Bad Faith
- **Authenticity:** Living according to your own values
- **Bad Faith:** Deceiving yourself, denying freedom, blaming circumstances

### The Absurd (Camus)
- Human need for meaning + universe silence = the absurd
- Response: Revolt - embrace absurdity, create meaning anyway

## Key Thinkers
- **Kierkegaard** - Leap of faith, subjective truth
- **Nietzsche** - God is dead, create your own values
- **Heidegger** - Being-toward-death, authenticity
- **Sartre** - Existence precedes essence, radical freedom
- **Camus** - The absurd, revolt
- **de Beauvoir** - Applied existentialism to gender

## Questions for Reflection
1. If you died tomorrow, would you be satisfied with how you lived?
2. What would you do if you knew you could not fail?
3. Whose expectations are you living by?
