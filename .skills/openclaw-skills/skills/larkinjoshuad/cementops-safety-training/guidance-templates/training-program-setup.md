# Building a Part 46 Compliant Safety Training Program for Cement Plants

This guide provides a step-by-step approach to establishing and maintaining a training program that meets MSHA 30 CFR Part 46 requirements for surface metal/nonmetal mines, including cement plants, quarries, and associated facilities.

---

## 1. Written Training Plan

### What Part 46 Requires

30 CFR 46.3 requires a written training plan that is available at the mine site for review by MSHA, miners, and miners' representatives.

### Required Contents

- **Mine identification**: Mine name, MSHA Mine ID number, operator name
- **Responsible person**: Name and position of the person responsible for health and safety training at the mine
- **Competent persons**: List of all individuals designated to provide training, with their areas of expertise
- **Training descriptions**: General description of teaching methods, course materials, and approximate duration for each type of training (new miner, experienced miner, refresher, task, hazard awareness)
- **Evaluation procedures**: How the mine evaluates whether training is effective and whether trainees are competent

### Format and Approval

- The plan may be in any written format (paper or electronic) as long as it is readily accessible.
- The plan does not need to be pre-approved by MSHA, but MSHA can review and request modifications during an inspection.
- The MSHA district manager may require changes if the plan does not adequately address site hazards or meet Part 46 requirements.

### Recommended Practice

- Review and update the plan at least annually and whenever there are significant changes in operations, equipment, or personnel.
- Keep a revision history so you can demonstrate that the plan is actively maintained.
- Store a printed copy in the safety office and another in the control room for quick access during inspections.

## 2. Identifying Competent Trainers

### Definition

A "competent person" under 30 CFR 46.2(b) is someone designated by the operator who has the ability, training, knowledge, or experience to provide training in their area of expertise.

### Selection Criteria

- Subject matter expertise in the topic being taught (e.g., the quarry superintendent for mobile equipment, the electrical supervisor for LOTO on electrical systems)
- Ability to communicate effectively and engage trainees
- Familiarity with site-specific equipment, procedures, and hazards
- No formal instructor certification is required by MSHA, but consider providing train-the-trainer courses to improve training quality

### Documentation

- List each competent person in the training plan by name and area(s) of expertise.
- Update the list when personnel change.
- Outside trainers (vendor representatives, consultants) can serve as competent persons for specialized topics.

## 3. Developing Training Content

### New Miner Training (24 hours)

**Target audience**: Employees with less than 12 months of mining experience.

**Minimum topics** (per 30 CFR 46.5):

1. Introduction to the work environment (site tour, layout, key personnel)
2. Recognition and avoidance of electrical, mechanical, and other hazards
3. Emergency and evacuation procedures
4. Health and safety aspects of assigned tasks
5. Statutory rights of miners
6. Authority and responsibility of supervisors and miners' representatives
7. Introduction to reporting hazards
8. Self-rescue and respiratory devices (as applicable)

**Delivery guidance**:

- Minimum 4 hours must be completed before the new miner begins work at the mine.
- Remaining 20 hours must be completed within 90 calendar days.
- Include a combination of classroom, site tour, and hands-on demonstration.
- The new miner must be accompanied by an experienced miner at all times until the 24-hour requirement is met.

### Experienced Miner Training (8 hours)

**Target audience**: Miners with 12+ months of cumulative mining experience who are new to this mine site.

**Minimum topics** (per 30 CFR 46.6):

- Same core topics as new miner training, focused on site-specific conditions at the new mine.

**Delivery guidance**:

- Must be completed before the experienced miner begins work at the new mine.
- Emphasize differences between this site and the miner's previous experience.

### Annual Refresher Training (8 hours)

**Target audience**: All miners, due within 12 months of their last refresher or initial training.

**Content requirements** (per 30 CFR 46.8):

- Must include instruction on changes at the mine that could adversely affect safety or health.
- Should cover relevant health and safety subjects.

**Recommended approach**:

- Update content annually to reflect incidents, near misses, new hazards, and regulatory changes.
- Include a mix of classroom, video, hands-on, and interactive elements.
- Consider delivering in segments (e.g., monthly 45-minute sessions) rather than a single 8-hour day.
- Include site-specific examples, photos, and case studies.

### Task Training

**Target audience**: Any miner assigned a new task or one for whom a task change has occurred.

**Requirements** (per 30 CFR 46.7):

- No minimum hour requirement, but training must be sufficient to perform the task safely.
- Must be completed before the miner performs the task.
- Must include practice or demonstration under observation of a competent person.

**Recommended approach**:

- Develop a task training template for each major task at the plant (see the task-training-templates.json file for 10 examples).
- Create a task training matrix mapping each job role to required task training.
- Trigger task training whenever employees change roles, new equipment is installed, or procedures change.

## 4. Scheduling and Tracking

### Tracking System

Implement a system (spreadsheet, database, or commercial training management software) that tracks:

- Employee name, hire date, and job role
- Training type and date completed for each training event
- Refresher training due date (12 months from last refresher)
- Task training records by equipment/procedure
- Competent person who delivered each training

### Scheduling Recommendations

- Generate a monthly report of upcoming refresher expirations (60-day and 30-day warnings).
- Schedule new miner training completion check at the 60-day mark (before the 90-day deadline).
- Build task training into the onboarding checklist for role changes and new hires.
- Schedule annual refresher content development review 2 months before the training year begins.

### Avoiding Lapses

- Set calendar reminders for key deadlines.
- Include training status in the monthly safety report to plant management.
- Do not allow employees to work past their refresher expiration date without scheduling immediate make-up training.

## 5. Competency Evaluation Methods

Part 46 requires that training effectiveness be evaluated. Acceptable methods include:

| Method | Best Used For | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Hands-on demonstration | Task training, LOTO, confined space | Preferred for safety-critical tasks |
| Written quiz or test | Classroom topics, regulatory knowledge | Use 80% passing threshold |
| Oral questioning | Small group or one-on-one training | Document the questions asked and answers given |
| Observation in the field | Post-training verification | Competent person observes trainee performing task unsupervised |
| Practical exercise | Fire extinguisher, first aid, rigging | Combine with hands-on demonstration |

### Documentation

- Record the evaluation method, date, and result (pass/fail) in the training record.
- For failures, document the re-training provided and the successful re-evaluation.
- Keep evaluation materials (quizzes, checklists) on file for reference.

## 6. Record Keeping System

### Regulatory Requirements (30 CFR 46.9)

Each training record must include:

1. Trainee name
2. Type of training (new miner, experienced miner, refresher, task, hazard awareness)
3. Duration of training in hours
4. Date(s) training was conducted
5. Description of training content and materials used
6. Name of competent person who provided training
7. Evaluation method and result
8. Signature of competent person (or electronic verification)

### Retention

- Records must be retained for **3 years** after the training event.
- Records for currently employed miners must be kept for the **duration of employment** plus 3 years.
- Upon request, a copy of the training record must be provided to the miner.

### Organization

- Maintain records by employee name for easy retrieval during inspections.
- Cross-reference by training type and date for compliance reporting.
- If using paper records, keep originals in a fireproof cabinet with backup copies offsite or scanned electronically.
- If using electronic records, ensure backup and access controls.

## 7. MSHA Inspection Readiness

### What Inspectors Look For

MSHA inspectors conducting a regular inspection (at least 2 per year for surface mines) will typically:

1. Request the written training plan and verify it is current.
2. Select several employees and request their training records.
3. Verify that refresher training is within the 12-month window.
4. Check task training records for employees operating specific equipment.
5. Ask to see contractor and visitor hazard awareness sign-in records.
6. Observe whether training content aligns with actual site conditions.

### Preparation Checklist

- [ ] Training plan is printed and accessible in a known location.
- [ ] Competent person list in the plan is current.
- [ ] Training records for all active employees are organized and complete.
- [ ] No employees have expired refresher training.
- [ ] Task training matrix is current and all gaps are closed.
- [ ] Contractor/visitor hazard awareness sign-in records are at the gate or security office.
- [ ] Training content (presentations, handouts, videos) matches what is described in the records.

## 8. Common Citations and How to Avoid Them

### Most Frequent Part 46 Citations

| Citation | Description | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| **Inadequate task training records** | Most common citation. Employees cannot produce documentation of task training for the equipment they operate. | Maintain a task training matrix. Document every task training event with specific equipment, content, trainer, and competency evaluation. |
| **Expired annual refresher** | Second most common. Employee's refresher training is more than 12 months old. | Track expiration dates proactively. Generate 60-day warning reports. Do not allow work past expiration. |
| **Incomplete new miner training** | 24 hours not completed within 90 days, or less than 4 hours before first day of work. | Build the 90-day training schedule on day one. Calendar the 60-day checkpoint. |
| **Missing or outdated training plan** | Plan not available, lists former employees, or does not reflect current operations. | Review and update the plan annually. Keep it accessible. |
| **No hazard awareness for contractors/visitors** | Contractors or delivery drivers on site without documented hazard awareness training. | Implement gate-level check-in process. No exceptions for "quick" visits. |
| **No competency evaluation documented** | Training was conducted but there is no record of how the trainee was evaluated. | Include evaluation method and result in every training record. |

### Penalty Context

- Part 46 citations are typically classified as non-S&S (non-significant and substantial) but can be elevated if the training gap directly contributed to an injury or if there is a pattern of non-compliance.
- Penalties for Part 46 violations typically range from $100 to $4,000 per citation but can be higher for repeated or willful violations.
- The cost of maintaining a compliant training program is far less than the cost of citations, injuries, and lost productivity.
