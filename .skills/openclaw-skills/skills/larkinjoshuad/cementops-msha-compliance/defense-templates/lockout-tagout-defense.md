# Citation Defense Template: Lockout/Tagout
## 30 CFR 56.12016 — Electrically Powered Equipment — Deenergization

**Use this template when:** The plant received a citation for failure to deenergize and lock out/tag out electrically powered equipment before mechanical work.

**WARNING: LOTO citations are almost always S&S and carry the highest penalties in cement plant enforcement ($10,000 - $70,000+). Unwarrantable failure findings are common. If the citation includes "unwarrantable failure" or if the penalty exceeds $20,000, engage legal counsel immediately. Do not attempt to handle this alone.**

---

## Defense Strategy Framework

### Step 1: Assess Severity — This Determines Your Approach

| Factor | Low Stakes | High Stakes |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| S&S finding? | No | **Yes** |
| Unwarrantable failure? | No | **Yes — get a lawyer** |
| Penalty amount | Under $10,000 | **Over $10,000** |
| Injury involved? | No | **Yes — get a lawyer** |
| Repeat violation? | No | **Yes — pattern of violations risk** |

**If ANY high-stakes factor applies: engage qualified legal counsel. This template helps you prepare — it does not replace an attorney.**

### Step 2: Assess Whether the Citation Is Contestable

Answer honestly:

- [ ] Was LOTO actually applied but not visible to the inspector?
- [ ] Was the work being performed diagnostic/testing that required energized equipment per manufacturer procedure?
- [ ] Was the equipment deenergized through an alternative verified method?
- [ ] Was the work "mechanical work" as defined by the standard, or was it observation/inspection only?
- [ ] Does the plant have a documented, comprehensive LOTO program?
- [ ] Are all involved employees trained and training records current?

**If you cannot answer YES to at least one of the first four questions, the citation is likely valid. Focus on penalty reduction and S&S contestation rather than vacating the citation.**

### Step 3: Gather Evidence (Do This IMMEDIATELY — Within 24 Hours)

**LOTO evidence degrades fast. Locks get removed. People forget. Act now.**

- [ ] Photographs of the disconnect/breaker where LOTO should have been applied
- [ ] Copy of the LOTO permit/tag (if one was issued)
- [ ] Interview statements from: the person doing the work, the person who applied LOTO (if different), the supervisor who authorized the work
- [ ] The plant's written LOTO program/procedure
- [ ] Training records for all involved employees (LOTO-specific training)
- [ ] LOTO audit records (does the plant audit LOTO compliance? How recently?)
- [ ] The specific work being performed — what task, what equipment, what was the purpose?
- [ ] Manufacturer documentation if the task required energized equipment
- [ ] Equipment-specific LOTO procedure (does one exist for this equipment?)
- [ ] Photos of all energy isolation points for the cited equipment
- [ ] Records of any previous LOTO citations (establishes or refutes pattern)

### Step 4: Draft the Defense Letter

---

**[PLANT LETTERHEAD]**

[Date]

Mine Safety and Health Administration
[District Office Address]

Re: Citation No. [CITATION NUMBER]
    30 CFR 56.12016 — Deenergization / Lockout-Tagout
    Mine ID: [MINE ID]
    Date of Citation: [DATE]

Dear Conference Officer:

[PLANT NAME] respectfully contests the above-referenced citation and requests a conference. Given the serious nature of this citation, we present the following factual record and legal arguments.

**ARGUMENT 1: LOTO Was Properly Applied But Not Observed by Inspector**
*(Use if applicable — this is the strongest defense)*

At the time of the inspection, lockout/tagout was properly applied to [EQUIPMENT] at [DISCONNECT/BREAKER LOCATION]. Lock number [X], assigned to [EMPLOYEE NAME], was applied at [TIME] before work commenced. Zero-energy verification was performed by [METHOD].

The inspector observed [EMPLOYEE] performing [WORK] on [EQUIPMENT] from [LOCATION/VANTAGE POINT]. The lock was applied at [DISCONNECT LOCATION], which is [X] feet from the work location and not visible from the inspector's vantage point. The inspector did not ask the employee about LOTO status before writing the citation.

[Attach: photo of lock on disconnect with timestamp, LOTO permit, employee statement, photo showing the distance between the work location and the disconnect.]

**ARGUMENT 2: Work Did Not Require Deenergization**
*(Use if applicable — narrow defense, requires strong evidence)*

The work being performed — [DESCRIBE TASK] — did not constitute "mechanical work" requiring deenergization under the standard. [EMPLOYEE] was performing [diagnostic testing / visual inspection / adjustment] that, per the manufacturer's procedure [Attachment X], requires the equipment to be energized.

The plant's LOTO program [Attachment Y, Section Z] specifically addresses tasks that require energized equipment and prescribes alternative protective measures, which were followed: [describe measures — e.g., insulated tools, safety observer, reduced voltage, specific PPE].

**ARGUMENT 3: Alternative Deenergization Method Was Used**
*(Use if applicable)*

The equipment was deenergized through [METHOD — e.g., remote disconnect, VFD stop command with physical verification, pneumatic isolation]. While a physical lock was not applied to the [specific disconnect], the energy isolation was verified by [METHOD] and the equipment could not be reenergized without [ACTION].

The standard requires that "power switches shall be locked out or other measures taken to prevent the equipment from being energized without the knowledge of the persons working on it." The alternative measures taken met this requirement.

**ARGUMENT 4: Contest S&S Finding**
*(Almost always applies for LOTO — prepare this argument)*

For a violation to be S&S under the Mathies test, MSHA must establish a reasonable likelihood that the hazard contributed to by the violation will result in a reasonably serious injury. [PLANT NAME] contests this element based on:

- The actual exposure duration was [X minutes/hours] — brief exposure reduces likelihood
- [X] employees were potentially exposed — limited exposure scope
- The equipment involved operates at [describe speed, force, energy level] — [argue why injury severity may be lower than assumed, if factually supportable]
- Engineering controls beyond LOTO were in place: [describe — e.g., equipment was in manual mode, VFD was at zero speed command, pneumatic pressure was bled off]
- The employee performing the work had [X] years of experience and [describe training/qualifications]

**Note: Do NOT argue that an experienced worker doesn't need LOTO. That argument always fails and makes you look worse.**

**ARGUMENT 5: Contest Unwarrantable Failure Finding**
*(Use ONLY if the citation includes unwarrantable failure — this requires legal counsel)*

An unwarrantable failure requires aggravated conduct constituting more than ordinary negligence. MSHA considers: (1) extensiveness of the violation, (2) length of time it existed, (3) whether the operator was placed on notice, (4) the operator's efforts to abate, (5) whether the violation was obvious, and (6) the operator's knowledge of the violation. (IO Coal Co., 31 FMSHRC 1346, 2009).

[Address each factor with evidence. This section should be drafted with legal counsel.]

**DEMONSTRATION OF GOOD FAITH:**

Regardless of the outcome of this contest, [PLANT NAME] has taken the following corrective actions:

1. [CORRECTIVE ACTION 1 — e.g., retrained all affected employees on LOTO within 48 hours]
2. [CORRECTIVE ACTION 2 — e.g., conducted plant-wide LOTO audit]
3. [CORRECTIVE ACTION 3 — e.g., installed additional lockout stations at cited equipment]
4. [CORRECTIVE ACTION 4 — e.g., updated equipment-specific LOTO procedures]

**REQUEST:**

Based on the above, [PLANT NAME] respectfully requests that Citation No. [CITATION NUMBER] be [vacated / modified to remove S&S finding / modified to remove unwarrantable failure finding / reduced in penalty].

Sincerely,

[NAME]
[TITLE]
[PLANT NAME]
[MINE ID]

## Timeline Reference

| Deadline | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Day 0 | Citation issued |
| Day 1-2 | Gather all evidence (photos, permits, statements, records) |
| Day 3-5 | Draft defense with plant management and safety team |
| Day 10 | Review with legal counsel (if S&S or unwarrantable) |
| Day 30 | Deadline to file notice of contest with MSHA |
| Day 30-60 | Safety and health conference with MSHA district manager |
| If unresolved | Hearing before Federal Mine Safety and Health Review Commission |

**DISCLAIMER:** This template provides a regulatory defense framework based on published MSHA standards, enforcement patterns, and Federal Mine Safety and Health Review Commission precedent. It is NOT legal advice. LOTO citations carry severe consequences including high penalties and potential pattern-of-violations findings. Consult qualified legal counsel before submitting any formal response to MSHA.
