# Citation Defense Template: Housekeeping
## 30 CFR 56.20003 — Housekeeping

**Use this template when:** The plant received a citation for workplaces, passageways, storerooms, or service rooms not being clean and orderly.

**This is the single most frequently cited MSHA standard across all mining operations.** It is also the most defensible because cement plants generate continuous dust and material spillage as a normal part of operations. The key defense: show you have a program and were executing it.

---

## Defense Strategy Framework

### Step 1: Assess the Citation

- [ ] What specific condition was cited? (material accumulation, blocked walkway, spilled material, etc.)
- [ ] Was the condition from current-shift operations or accumulated over time?
- [ ] Was a cleanup in progress or scheduled?
- [ ] Was the area barricaded or coned off?
- [ ] Is this S&S? (Housekeeping is rarely S&S unless the accumulation created a secondary hazard like a fall, fire, or equipment damage.)
- [ ] Was anyone actually exposed to a hazard from the condition?

### Step 2: Gather Evidence (Do This Within 48 Hours)

- [ ] Photographs of the cited area as it was AND after cleanup
- [ ] Housekeeping schedule showing assigned responsibilities and frequencies
- [ ] Cleanup crew shift assignment records for the day of the citation
- [ ] Pre-shift inspection records (did the examiner note the condition?)
- [ ] If cleanup was in progress: photo of cleanup equipment staged, testimony of cleanup crew
- [ ] Production records showing the operation that generated the material (proves it was current-shift)
- [ ] History: any prior housekeeping citations in the same area? (Pattern matters.)

### Step 3: Draft the Defense Letter

**[PLANT LETTERHEAD]**

[Date]

Mine Safety and Health Administration
[District Office Address]

Re: Citation No. [CITATION NUMBER]
    30 CFR 56.20003 — Housekeeping
    Mine ID: [MINE ID]
    Date of Citation: [DATE]

Dear Conference Officer:

[PLANT NAME] respectfully contests the above-referenced citation and provides the following information for your consideration.

**CONTEXT: Continuous Process Operations**

[PLANT NAME] operates a continuous cement manufacturing process that inherently generates material spillage and dust as part of normal operations. The plant operates [X] hours per day, [X] days per week. Material handling, grinding, conveying, and packaging operations produce ongoing accumulation that requires continuous and cyclical cleanup — not a one-time effort.

The plant maintains a documented housekeeping program [Attachment A] that assigns cleanup responsibilities by area, shift, and crew. This program is reviewed [quarterly/annually] and updated as operations change.

**ARGUMENT 1: Accumulation Was From Current-Shift Operations**
*(Use if applicable)*

The cited material accumulation in [AREA] was generated during the current operating shift. Production records [Attachment B] show that [OPERATION] was running from [TIME] to [TIME], which is the source of the cited material. The scheduled cleanup for this area occurs at [TIME] — the inspection occurred [X hours] before the scheduled cleanup window.

It is unreasonable to require that a continuous process operation maintain zero accumulation at all times. The standard requires areas be "kept clean and orderly" — the plant's documented program demonstrates ongoing compliance with this requirement through its cleanup cycle.

**ARGUMENT 2: Active Cleanup Was In Progress**
*(Use if applicable)*

At the time of the inspection, cleanup of the cited area was actively in progress. [EMPLOYEE NAME/CREW] was assigned to clean [AREA] beginning at [TIME]. [Cleanup equipment — bobcat, vacuum truck, shovel crew] was staged at the location. The inspection interrupted the cleanup activity.

[Attach: crew assignment, photos of cleanup equipment staged, employee statement.]

**ARGUMENT 3: Area Was Barricaded Pending Cleanup**
*(Use if applicable)*

The cited area was barricaded with [cones/barriers/caution tape] and signed to redirect personnel away from the accumulation pending scheduled cleanup. No personnel were exposed to the condition as a result of the barricading. The plant's procedure is to barricade significant accumulations until they can be safely cleaned, rather than allowing traffic through the area.

**ARGUMENT 4: Contest S&S Finding**
*(Use if applicable — rare for housekeeping but it happens)*

The cited housekeeping condition did not create a discrete safety hazard with a reasonable likelihood of resulting in a reasonably serious injury. The accumulation of [MATERIAL] on [SURFACE] did not obstruct egress, create a fall hazard, or pose a fire or health risk. The standard requires clean and orderly conditions — the cited condition was a temporary accumulation from normal operations, not a systemic failure.

**REQUEST:**

Based on the documented housekeeping program, the operational context of continuous cement manufacturing, and the evidence that cleanup was [in progress / scheduled / barricaded], [PLANT NAME] respectfully requests that Citation No. [CITATION NUMBER] be [vacated / reduced in penalty].

Sincerely,

[NAME]
[TITLE]
[PLANT NAME]
[MINE ID]
