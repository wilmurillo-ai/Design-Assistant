# Citation Defense Template: Fall Protection
## 30 CFR 56.15005 — Safety Belts and Lines

**Use this template when:** The plant received a citation for persons working where there is a danger of falling without safety belts/lines (personal fall arrest), or for inadequate fall protection systems.

**Cement-specific context:** Preheater towers (200-400 feet), silo tops, kiln hood platforms, elevated conveyor galleries, and clinker cooler roofs are the most common locations for fall protection citations in cement plants. This citation is almost always S&S due to the potential severity of a fall injury.

---

## Defense Strategy Framework

### Step 1: Identify the Defense Angle

Fall protection citations have limited but specific defensible arguments:

- [ ] Were adequate guardrails in place? (If yes, personal fall arrest may not be required.)
- [ ] Was the person using a properly rated aerial lift/man-lift with built-in fall restraint?
- [ ] Was the person transiting (walking through) rather than working at the location?
- [ ] Was the person tied off but the anchor point was not visible to the inspector?
- [ ] Was the cited location actually below 6 feet from the lower level?
- [ ] Were there mitigating factors (e.g., safety net, partial guardrail system)?

### Step 2: Gather Evidence (Within 48 Hours)

- [ ] Photographs of the cited location showing: height from lower level, guardrail system (or lack thereof), anchor points, access path
- [ ] Measurements: actual height from the working surface to the lower level
- [ ] If guardrails present: photos showing top rail, mid rail, and toe board with measurements (42" +/- 3" top rail, mid rail at ~21")
- [ ] If tied off: photo of the anchor point, the lanyard/SRL, the harness — with employee statement
- [ ] If man-lift: model and rating of the lift, fall restraint features, employee training records for aerial lift operation
- [ ] Employee statement: what task they were performing, what fall protection they were using
- [ ] Plant fall protection program documentation
- [ ] Training records: fall protection training for the cited employee
- [ ] Harness inspection records (annual inspection requirement)

### Step 3: Draft the Defense Letter

**[PLANT LETTERHEAD]**

[Date]

Mine Safety and Health Administration
[District Office Address]

Re: Citation No. [CITATION NUMBER]
    30 CFR 56.15005 — Safety Belts and Lines
    Mine ID: [MINE ID]
    Date of Citation: [DATE]

Dear Conference Officer:

[PLANT NAME] respectfully contests the above-referenced citation and presents the following.

**ARGUMENT 1: Adequate Guardrails Were In Place**
*(Use if applicable — strongest defense)*

The cited work location at [LOCATION] was protected by a guardrail system consisting of a top rail at [HEIGHT — should be 42" +/- 3"], a mid rail, and a toe board. The guardrail system was structurally sound and met the requirements of 30 CFR 56.11027. When an adequate guardrail system is in place, personal fall arrest (safety belts and lines) is not required under 56.15005.

[Attach: photographs of the guardrail system with measurements, showing the guardrail was adequate to prevent falls.]

**ARGUMENT 2: Personnel Were Using Fall Restraint in an Aerial Lift**
*(Use if applicable)*

The cited employee was performing [TASK] from a [MODEL] aerial lift / man-lift rated for the task. The lift is equipped with a manufacturer-provided fall restraint system. The employee was connected to the lift's anchor point with [DESCRIBE — lanyard, SRL] at all times. The employee's training record for aerial lift operation is attached [Attachment X].

Fall protection requirements are met when personnel use a properly rated aerial lift with integral fall restraint connected.

**ARGUMENT 3: Fall Arrest Was Properly Applied But Not Observed**
*(Use if applicable)*

The cited employee was wearing a [BRAND/MODEL] full-body harness connected to a [SRL/retractable lanyard/fixed lanyard] anchored at [ANCHOR POINT LOCATION]. The anchor point is rated for [5,000 lbs per OSHA/MSHA requirement].

The inspector observed the employee from [LOCATION], which is [X] feet from the work area. The [lanyard/SRL/anchor point] was [behind the employee / above the employee / obscured by equipment] and not visible from the inspector's vantage point. The inspector did not inquire about fall protection status before issuing the citation.

[Attach: photo of anchor point, harness inspection record, employee statement confirming they were tied off.]

**ARGUMENT 4: Person Was Transiting, Not Working**
*(Use if applicable — narrow defense)*

The cited individual was transiting through [AREA] via the designated access route to reach [DESTINATION]. The access route is protected by [guardrails/other]. The individual was not performing work at the cited elevated location — they were passing through.

**ARGUMENT 5: Contest S&S Finding**
*(Use if applicable)*

While [PLANT NAME] takes fall hazards with the utmost seriousness, we contest the S&S determination based on:

- The actual fall distance was [X] feet — [if relatively low, argue reduced severity]
- Exposure duration was [X minutes] — the task was brief
- [If partial protection existed]: while the cited fall protection was not complete, [describe mitigating factors — e.g., guardrails on three sides, low work height, employee experience]
- The employee had [X] years of experience working at heights and current fall protection training [Attachment X]

**CORRECTIVE ACTIONS TAKEN:**

1. [E.g., installed additional anchor points at the cited location]
2. [E.g., retrained all personnel who work at height]
3. [E.g., conducted plant-wide fall protection audit]
4. [E.g., installed additional guardrail sections to eliminate the need for personal fall arrest]

**REQUEST:**

Based on the above, [PLANT NAME] respectfully requests that Citation No. [CITATION NUMBER] be [vacated / modified to remove S&S finding / reduced in penalty].

Sincerely,

[NAME]
[TITLE]
[PLANT NAME]
[MINE ID]
