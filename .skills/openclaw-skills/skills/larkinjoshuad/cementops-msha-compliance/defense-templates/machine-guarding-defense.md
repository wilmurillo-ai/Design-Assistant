# Citation Defense Template: Machine Guarding
## 30 CFR 56.14107(a) — Moving Machine Parts

**Use this template when:** The plant received a citation for unguarded moving machine parts (gears, sprockets, chains, pulleys, shafts, couplings, fan blades, or similar).

**This is the #1 most-cited standard in cement manufacturing.** Every conveyor, mill, fan, and pump is a potential citation. Inspectors walk conveyor galleries first and look for any rotating part within 7 feet of a walking/working surface.

---

## Defense Strategy Framework

### Step 1: Assess Whether the Citation Is Contestable

Answer these questions before spending time on a defense:

- [ ] Was the moving part actually within reach of personnel? (7-foot rule from walking/working surface)
- [ ] Was there a walking/working surface adjacent to the cited equipment?
- [ ] Was the guard removed for active maintenance with LOTO in place?
- [ ] Was the area barricaded or otherwise inaccessible?
- [ ] Is this an S&S (Significant & Substantial) finding? If yes, the stakes are higher — consider legal counsel.

**If the answer to any of the first 4 questions supports your case, proceed with the defense.**

### Step 2: Gather Evidence (Do This Within 48 Hours)

- [ ] Photographs of the cited equipment from the inspector's vantage point AND from the nearest walking/working surface
- [ ] Measurements: distance from the moving part to the nearest walking/working surface
- [ ] If LOTO was applied: copy of the LOTO permit, photos of the lock on the disconnect
- [ ] If barricaded: photos of the barricade, interview with the person who set it up
- [ ] Maintenance work order if the guard was removed for maintenance
- [ ] Pre-shift inspection records for the cited area (from the shift the citation was issued)
- [ ] Guard purchase order or fabrication work order if a replacement was in progress
- [ ] Manufacturer specifications for the guard (if the inspector claims it was inadequate)

### Step 3: Draft the Defense Letter

**[PLANT LETTERHEAD]**

[Date]

Mine Safety and Health Administration
[District Office Address]

Re: Citation No. [CITATION NUMBER]
    30 CFR 56.14107(a) — Moving Machine Parts
    Mine ID: [MINE ID]
    Date of Citation: [DATE]

Dear Conference Officer:

[PLANT NAME] respectfully contests the above-referenced citation and requests a conference to present the following information.

**ARGUMENT 1: Guard Was Temporarily Removed for Active Maintenance Under LOTO**
*(Use if applicable)*

At the time of the inspection, the guard on [EQUIPMENT DESCRIPTION] had been removed to facilitate [MAINTENANCE ACTIVITY]. Lockout/tagout procedures were properly applied per the plant's LOTO program. Lock number [X] was applied to disconnect [X] by [EMPLOYEE NAME], a qualified maintenance person. The equipment was de-energized and verified zero-energy before the guard was removed.

The guard removal was temporary and part of a planned maintenance activity that required access to the [COMPONENT]. The guard was staged nearby for reinstallation upon completion of the work. [Attach: LOTO permit, photos of lock on disconnect, maintenance work order.]

**ARGUMENT 2: Moving Part Was Not Within Reach of Personnel**
*(Use if applicable)*

The cited moving part on [EQUIPMENT] is located [X] feet from the nearest walking/working surface. The standard requires guarding to "protect persons from contacting" moving parts. At [X] feet of separation, with no platform, ladder, or other means of reaching the moving part during normal operations, contact was not reasonably possible.

[Attach: photographs with measurements showing distance from walking/working surface to the moving part.]

**ARGUMENT 3: Area Was Barricaded and Inaccessible**
*(Use if applicable)*

At the time of the inspection, the area surrounding [EQUIPMENT] was barricaded with [TYPE OF BARRICADE] and signed to prevent personnel access. The barricade was established by [NAME/ROLE] at [TIME] as part of [REASON]. No personnel were observed within the barricaded area.

[Attach: photos of barricade, interview statement from person who established it.]

**ARGUMENT 4: Condition Was Identified and Corrective Action Was In Progress**
*(Use if applicable)*

The condition cited was identified during [pre-shift inspection / routine PM / operator report] on [DATE/TIME], prior to the MSHA inspection. Work order [NUMBER] was generated at [TIME] and [MAINTENANCE RESOURCE] was assigned to [install/repair] the guard. The corrective action was in progress at the time of the inspection.

[Attach: pre-shift inspection record, work order with timestamp, maintenance assignment.]

**ARGUMENT 5: Contest S&S Finding**
*(Use if citation was marked S&S)*

[PLANT NAME] contests the Significant & Substantial finding. For a violation to be S&S, MSHA must establish: (1) an underlying violation, (2) a discrete safety hazard contributed to by the violation, (3) a reasonable likelihood that the hazard will result in an injury, and (4) a reasonable likelihood that the injury will be of a reasonably serious nature. (Mathies Coal Co., 6 FMSHRC 1, 3-4, 1984).

[Explain why one or more of the four Mathies elements is not met — typically element 3 (reasonable likelihood of injury) based on the actual conditions, exposure frequency, and controls in place.]

**REQUEST:**

Based on the above, [PLANT NAME] respectfully requests that Citation No. [CITATION NUMBER] be [vacated / modified to non-S&S / reduced in penalty].

Sincerely,

[NAME]
[TITLE]
[PLANT NAME]
[MINE ID]
