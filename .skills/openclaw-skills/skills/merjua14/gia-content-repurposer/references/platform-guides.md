# Platform Character Limits & Best Practices

## Twitter/X
- **Max:** 280 chars per tweet (4,000 for X Premium)
- **Threads:** 25 tweets max
- **Images:** Up to 4 per tweet
- **Best times:** 8-10 AM, 12-1 PM weekdays
- **Hook formats that work:** Questions, bold claims, "Here's what nobody tells you about...", numbered lists

## LinkedIn
- **Max:** 3,000 chars per post
- **"See more" cut:** ~210 chars (first 3 lines)
- **Best times:** Tue-Thu 8-10 AM
- **What works:** Personal stories with business lessons, contrarian takes, "I used to think X, now I think Y"
- **Avoid:** "I'm excited to announce", generic motivational quotes

## Email Newsletter
- **Subject line:** 30-60 chars optimal (6-10 words)
- **Preview text:** First 40-90 chars after subject
- **Ideal length:** 200-500 words for highest engagement
- **Best days:** Tuesday-Thursday morning
- **Format:** Short paragraphs, bold key points, clear CTA

## Instagram
- **Caption max:** 2,200 chars
- **Visible before "more":** ~125 chars
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 relevant (down from 30 — algorithm changed)
- **Best times:** 11 AM-1 PM, 7-9 PM
- **Carousels:** Highest engagement format, 5-10 slides

## General Content Repurposing Rules
1. **Don't copy-paste** — each platform has its own language and norms
2. **Change the hook** — what works on Twitter won't work on LinkedIn
3. **Adjust length** — Twitter = punchy, LinkedIn = story-driven, Email = scannable
4. **Stagger posting** — don't post everywhere simultaneously (looks robotic)
5. **Add platform-native elements** — polls on Twitter, carousels on Instagram, documents on LinkedIn
