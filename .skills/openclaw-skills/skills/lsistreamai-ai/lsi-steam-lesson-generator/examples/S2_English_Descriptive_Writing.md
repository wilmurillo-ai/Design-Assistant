## 2. AI Tool Integration

### Tool 1: GLM5 (Student Use - Core Activity)

```
Tool: GLM5
Purpose: Brainstorm descriptive vocabulary and improve sentence variety
Setup Required: Students access GLM5 on devices (pairs OK)
Activity:

  Phase 1 - Vocabulary Expansion (8 min):
  - Prompt: "Give me 15 vivid adjectives to describe [a rainy night / an abandoned house / a busy market]"
  - Students copy useful words into their notes
  - Extension: Ask "Now give me 5 similes for [rain / darkness / noise]"
  
  Phase 2 - Sentence Improvement (10 min):
  - Students write 2-3 basic sentences about their chosen setting
  - Prompt: "Make this sentence more vivid and atmospheric: [student's sentence]"
  - Students compare original vs AI-improved version
  - Discuss: "Which changes do you like? Which would you reject?"

  Phase 3 - Writing Support (5 min):
  - Stuck students prompt: "I'm writing about [setting]. Give me 3 opening lines that create suspense."

Time Allocation: 23 minutes total
Tips for Teachers:
- Model the prompting process on projector first
- Emphasize: AI suggests, YOU decide what to use
- Warn students: Don't copy AI text directly — use as inspiration
- Have backup word lists if AI is slow/blocked
```

## 3. Teaching Steps

```
Minute 0-8: Introduction & Hook
- Show 3 atmospheric images (projector): rainy street, dark forest, crowded market
- Ask: "What feeling does each image give you? How does the photographer create this feeling?"
- Introduce key term: ATMOSPHERE (氛圍)
- Quick write: "Describe this image in ONE sentence that shows the atmosphere" (1 min)
- Share 2-3 student sentences, discuss what works

Minute 8-18: Sensory Details Mini-Lesson
- Present the 5 senses: Sight, Sound, Smell, Touch, Taste
- Model: Transform "The house was scary" → "The cracked windows stared like hollow eyes. Wind whistled through gaps. The smell of dust and old memories filled the air."
- Key techniques:
  1. Show, don't tell (不要直接說)
  2. Use sensory verbs (感官動詞)
  3. Specific details beat general description
- GLM5 demonstration: "Give me 3 ways to describe 'the night was quiet' using sound"
- Discuss AI's suggestions — which are effective?

Minute 18-28: Canva Mood Board Creation
- Students choose ONE setting:
  - A rainy night in the city (城市的雨夜)
  - An abandoned house (廢棄的房子)
  - A festival at night (夜晚的節日)
- Create mood board in Canva (2-3 images + 5-10 descriptive words)
- Partner share: "What atmosphere are you trying to create?"

Minute 28-50: Writing Workshop with GLM5 Support
- Students write a descriptive paragraph (150-200 words)
- Must include:
  - At least one sentence for each of 3 senses
  - At least one simile or metaphor
  - Clear atmosphere (tension, peace, excitement, fear, etc.)
- GLM5 used for:
  - Vocabulary help when stuck
  - Sentence variety suggestions
  - NO copying full AI-generated paragraphs
- Teacher circulates, gives individual feedback

Minute 50-62: Peer Review
- Pair share paragraphs
- Partner feedback using checklist:
  □ Can I identify the atmosphere? (Yes/No)
  □ Which senses did the writer use?
  □ What's one vivid word/phrase that stood out?
  □ One suggestion for improvement
- Optional: Use GLM5 for feedback prompt:
  "Give constructive feedback on this descriptive paragraph: [paste paragraph]"

Minute 62-70: Closure & Exit Ticket
- Volunteers share their best sentence
- Recap: Sensory details, show don't tell, specific vocabulary
- Exit ticket:
  1. One technique I used today: _____________
  2. One thing I want to improve: _____________
  3. How did AI help my writing? (brainstorming / feedback / stuck moments / didn't use)
```

## 4. Student Activities

```
Activity 1: Vocabulary Brainstorm with AI
- Type: Individual or pair
- Tool: GLM5
- Instructions:
  1. Prompt GLM5: "Give me 15 vivid adjectives to describe [your chosen setting]"
  2. Copy 5-8 useful words into your notes
  3. Prompt: "Give me 3 similes for [rain / darkness / noise / silence]"
  4. Choose 1-2 similes to potentially use
- Output: Word bank of 8-10 descriptive words + 1-2 similes

Activity 2: Mood Board Creation
- Type: Individual
- Tool: Canva AI
- Instructions:
  1. Search for images matching your setting (or generate with AI)
  2. Select 2-3 that show your desired atmosphere
  3. Add 5-10 descriptive words as labels
  4. Save as reference for writing
- Output: Visual mood board (saved as PNG)

Activity 3: Descriptive Paragraph Writing
- Type: Individual
- Tool: Paper or Google Docs (GLM5 optional support)
- Instructions:
  Write 150-200 words describing your setting.
  Requirements:
  - Make the atmosphere CLEAR (reader should FEEL something)
  - Use at least 3 senses
  - Include 1 simile or metaphor
  - Use at least 3 words from your word bank
  - NO copying AI-generated paragraphs — use AI for ideas only
- Output: Completed descriptive paragraph

Activity 4: Peer Feedback Exchange
- Type: Pair
- Tool: None (discussion)
- Instructions:
  1. Read your partner's paragraph aloud
  2. Complete the checklist for them
  3. Discuss: What worked? What could be stronger?
  4. Writer notes one change to make
- Output: Feedback notes
```

## 5. Assessment Rubric

```

|-----------|---------------|----------|----------------|---------------|
| Atmosphere | Atmosphere is vivid and consistent; reader clearly feels the mood | Atmosphere is clear but could be stronger | Atmosphere is unclear or inconsistent | No clear atmosphere created |
| Sensory Details | Uses 3+ senses with vivid, specific details | Uses 2-3 senses with some specific details | Uses 1-2 senses with general description | Relies on telling, not sensory details |
| Figurative Language | Simile/metaphor is original and enhances atmosphere | Simile/metaphor is present and appropriate | Simile/metaphor is basic or cliché | No figurative language used |
| AI Tool Integration | AI used effectively for vocabulary/ideas; writer's voice remains clear | AI used appropriately with some original additions | Over-reliance on AI or minimal use | Copied AI content directly or no AI use |

## 5B. AI-Assisted Assessment

```
How to Use GLM5 to Help Evaluate Student Paragraphs:

1. Copy the student's paragraph
2. Open GLM5 (chat.openai.com or school platform)
3. Use this prompt:

---
PROMPT FOR AI:
"I am a Secondary 2 English teacher assessing a student's descriptive paragraph.

The learning objectives were:
- Create clear atmosphere through sensory details
- Use at least 3 senses and 1 simile/metaphor
- Use AI for vocabulary brainstorming (not copying)

Please evaluate this paragraph on a scale of 1-4 for each criterion:
- Atmosphere: Vivid, consistent mood that reader can feel
- Sensory Details: Specific details using multiple senses
- Figurative Language: Original simile/metaphor that enhances the writing
- Writer Voice vs AI Use: Student's own voice, AI used appropriately

Provide:
1. A score (1-4) for each criterion
2. Specific feedback for the student (2-3 sentences, encouraging, specific)
3. One strength to highlight
4. One suggestion for revision

Student paragraph:
[paste paragraph]
---

4. Review AI's evaluation and adjust based on your teacher judgment
5. AI provides a starting point — YOU decide the final score

Example Assessment Prompt:

"Student paragraph:
The old house stood at the end of the street. It looked scary. I walked slowly. The wind was blowing. I felt afraid.
The windows were broken like empty eyes. The smell of old dust filled my nose. Every creak made my heart beat faster like a drum.

Please evaluate on the 4 criteria above."

Sample AI Response:
"Scores: Atmosphere (2) - mood starts weak but improves; Sensory Details (2) - only 2 senses used; Figurative Language (3) - 'empty eyes' effective, 'heart beat like a drum' is cliché; Writer Voice (3) - mostly original, could use more specific vocabulary.

Feedback: You've made a good start with the 'empty eyes' simile — that creates a creepy feeling. Try adding more specific sensory details: What did the wind sound like? What exactly did you smell? This will make your atmosphere stronger.

Strength: Effective personification with windows as eyes.
Suggestion: Replace cliché 'heart beat like a drum' with something more original."

Important:
- AI helps with initial feedback and specific comments
- Teacher has final judgment on scores
- Use AI to generate feedback faster, then personalize
- Great for students who need more detailed written feedback
```

## 6. Teacher Notes

```
Preparation Checklist:
☐ Test GLM5 on school network
☐ Prepare 3-4 atmospheric images (projector)
☐ Create sample mood board in Canva
☐ Print vocabulary cards (backup if tech fails)
☐ Prepare exit ticket slips or Google Form

Differentiation:
- For struggling learners:
  - Provide sentence starters: "The [sight] made me..."
  - Focus on ONE sense at a time
  - Use more structured templates
  - Allow more GLM5 support

- For advanced learners:
  - Challenge: Write TWO contrasting paragraphs (peaceful vs tense, day vs night)
  - Eliminate said/was/had — force stronger verbs
  - Try multiple AI prompts for experimentation

Common Issues & Solutions:
- Students copying AI text: Require handwritten drafts, check AI use in real-time
- GLM5 blocked on network: Use pre-loaded vocabulary lists as backup
- Students stuck after AI help: Ask them to describe the setting to a partner verbally first
- Overuse of adjectives: Teach "strong verbs beat weak verbs + adjectives"
