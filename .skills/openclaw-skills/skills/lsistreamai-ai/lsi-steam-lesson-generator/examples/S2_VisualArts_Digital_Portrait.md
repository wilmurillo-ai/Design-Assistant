## 2. AI Tool Integration

### Tool 1: HeyGen (Teacher Demonstration + Student Observation)

```
Tool: HeyGen
Purpose: Generate diverse portrait avatars to study facial proportions and styles
Setup Required: Teacher logs into HeyGen, prepares 3-4 avatar examples
Activity:
  1. Teacher generates avatars with different:
     - Ethnicity/skin tones
     - Ages (child, teenager, adult, elderly)
     - Expressions (happy, serious, surprised)
  2. Students observe and discuss: "What makes a portrait look realistic?"
  3. Compare AI-generated faces to classical portraits (e.g., Mona Lisa, Van Gogh self-portrait)
Time Allocation: 10 minutes
Tips for Teachers:
- Use diverse examples to discuss representation in art
- Pause generation to show students the "uncanny valley" effect
- Discuss ethics: AI portraits vs real portrait photography
```

### Tool 2: Canva AI (Student Use)

```
Tool: Canva AI
Purpose: Students create their own digital portrait with AI assistance
Setup Required: Students log into Canva, access AI image generator
Activity:
  Phase 1 (Exploration - 10 min):
  - Students experiment with prompts: "portrait of a teenager, anime style"
  - Compare styles: realistic, cartoon, abstract, watercolor
  
  Phase 2 (Creation - 20 min):
  - Students create original portrait using Canva's design tools
  - Use AI to generate background, textures, or enhancement
  - Apply knowledge of facial proportions from earlier discussion
  
  Phase 3 (Refinement - 10 min):
  - Use Canva AI editing tools to adjust lighting, colors
  - Add personal artistic touches
Time Allocation: 40 minutes total
Tips for Teachers:
- Encourage students to iterate: "First attempt is exploration, not final product"
- Remind students to apply traditional art principles (composition, balance, emphasis)
- Have students save both AI-generated versions and their manual edits for comparison
```

### Tool 3: Tripo3D.ai (Extension/Demonstration)

```
Tool: Tripo3D.ai
Purpose: Transform 2D portrait into 3D bust (show new possibilities)
Setup Required: Teacher demonstrates on projector
Activity:
  - Teacher selects one completed student portrait
  - Upload to Tripo3D.ai
  - Generate 3D model
  - Discuss: "How does dimension change the portrait?"
Time Allocation: 5 minutes (end of lesson, if time permits)
Tips for Teachers:
- This is a "wow moment" to inspire students
- Discuss future of digital art: 2D→3D, virtual galleries, AR/VR display
- No need for every student to try if time is limited
```

## 3. Teaching Steps

```
Minute 0-10: Introduction & Hook (引入)
- Question: "What do you think AI can and cannot do in art?"
- Show 3 images: 1 classical portrait, 1 digital portrait, 1 AI-generated portrait
- Quick poll: "Which was made by AI?" (Reveal answers)
- Introduce today's topic: Creating digital portraits with AI tools

Minute 10-20: Portrait Fundamentals & AI Demonstration (基礎知識與演示)
- Quick review: Face proportions (eyes at midpoint, 5-eye width rule)
- HeyGen demonstration: Generate 3 diverse avatars
- Discussion: "What makes these faces look realistic or artificial?"
- Compare to classical portrait paintings (Mona Lisa, Frida Kahlo)
- Introduce key vocabulary: 比例 (proportion), 特徵 (features), 風格 (style)

Minute 20-30: Canva AI Exploration (探索)
- Students log into Canva
- Task 1: Generate 3 different portrait styles using AI prompts
- Prompt examples:
  - "Portrait of a confident young woman, oil painting style"
  - "Abstract portrait using geometric shapes"
  - "Portrait of elderly person, watercolor style"
- Students screenshot and save their 3 experiments

Minute 30-55: Portrait Creation (創作)
- Task: Create your own original digital portrait
- Requirements:
  - Must include a face (can be realistic OR stylized)
  - Must use at least one AI-generated element (background, texture, or enhancement)
  - Must show understanding of facial proportions
  - Must include both digital AND manual elements (not 100% AI)
- Teacher circulates, gives individual feedback
- Encourage students who finish early to try different styles

Minute 55-65: Critique & Reflection (評賞與反思)
- Pair share: Show your portrait to a partner
- Discussion questions:
  1. "How did you use AI in your portrait?"
  2. "What decisions did YOU make vs what the AI made?"
  3. "What would you change if you had more time?"
- Select 3-4 portraits to show on projector (student volunteers)
- Brief Tripo3D.ai demonstration (if time permits)

Minute 65-70: Closure & Exit Ticket (總結)
- Recap: Portrait proportions, AI as a tool (not replacement)
- Exit ticket (written):
  - One thing I learned: _____________
  - One question I still have: _____________
  - My portrait score: 1-5 (how happy am I with my work?)
```

## 4. Student Activities

```
Activity 1: AI Style Exploration
- Type: Individual
- Tool: Canva AI
- Instructions:
  1. Open Canva AI image generator
  2. Create 3 portraits using these prompts:
     - "Portrait of [type], [style] style"
  3. Screenshot and save to your folder
  4. Write 1 sentence: "I chose this style because..."
- Output: 3 AI-generated portrait experiments + 1 reflection sentence

Activity 2: Original Portrait Creation
- Type: Individual
- Tool: Canva (full platform)
- Instructions:
  1. Start with a blank canvas (portrait orientation)
  2. Use Canva's AI tools for ONE of these:
     - Background generation
     - Texture/pattern enhancement
     - Color palette suggestions
  3. Draw/paint the portrait yourself using Canva's design tools
  4. Apply what you learned about facial proportions
  5. Add at least 3 personal artistic choices (style, colors, expression, accessories)
- Output: Completed digital portrait (PNG export)

Activity 3: Partner Critique
- Type: Pair work
- Tool: None (discussion)
- Instructions:
  1. Show your portrait to your partner
  2. Answer: How did AI help you? What did you do yourself?
  3. Give your partner one compliment and one suggestion
  4. Write down your partner's feedback
- Output: Written feedback note
```

## 5B. AI-Assisted Assessment

```
How to Use GLM5 to Help Evaluate Student Portraits:

1. Take a screenshot of the student's digital portrait
2. Open GLM5 (chat.openai.com or your school's AI platform)
3. Use this prompt:

---
PROMPT FOR AI:
"I am a Visual Arts teacher assessing a Secondary 2 student's digital portrait.

The learning objectives were:
- Understand facial proportions
- Use AI tools thoughtfully as ONE tool among many
- Create an original portrait with personal artistic decisions

Please evaluate this student work on a scale of 1-4 for each criterion:
- Portrait Technical Skills: Face shows accurate proportions; clear use of digital tools
- AI Integration: AI used thoughtfully as ONE tool; clear artistic decisions
- Creativity & Originality: Unique style; personal artistic voice evident
- Reflection Quality: Thoughtful self-evaluation shown in design choices

Provide:
1. A score (1-4) for each criterion
2. Specific feedback for the student (2-3 sentences, encouraging tone)
3. One suggestion for improvement

Student work description: [Describe: style, colors, expression, use of AI, proportions observed]
---

4. Review AI's evaluation and adjust based on your teacher judgment
5. AI provides a starting point — YOU decide the final score

Example Assessment Prompt (copy-paste):

"Student created a semi-realistic portrait of a teenager with blue hair. 
Proportions: Eyes slightly too high on face (should be at midpoint). 
Used Canva AI for background (sunset gradient) and texture overlay. 
Student drew the face and added accessories (earrings, glasses) manually. 
Style is anime-inspired with personal color choices. 
Expression shows confidence. 
Please evaluate on the 4 criteria above."

Important:
- AI assists, not replaces, teacher judgment
- Always review AI suggestions before giving to students
- Use AI for initial feedback, especially for detailed written comments
- Final grades remain the teacher's decision

```
Preparation Checklist:
☐ Test HeyGen on school network (may need VPN)
☐ Prepare Canva class accounts or ensure students have access
☐ Prepare 3 comparison images (classical, digital, AI-generated)
☐ Create sample Canva file to show as example
☐ Test Tripo3D.ai (optional but recommended for demonstration)
☐ Prepare exit ticket slips or digital form

Differentiation:
- For struggling learners: 
  - Provide template with face outline already drawn
  - Focus on ONE AI tool (Canva AI only)
  - Allow reference images to trace
  
- For advanced learners:
  - Challenge to create series of 3 portraits (different emotions/ages)
  - Try Tripo3D.ai to turn portrait into 3D
  - Research and present on AI art ethics

Common Issues & Solutions:
- Canva AI blocked on school network: Use personal hotspots or prepare offline examples
- Students copy AI output entirely: Require clear annotation of "AI parts" vs "my parts"
- Students uncomfortable drawing faces: Allow stylized/cartoon approach; focus on creativity over realism
- HeyGen generating inappropriate content: Pre-generate examples; don't use live generation
