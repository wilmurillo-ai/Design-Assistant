## Retirement Letter (2010)

**Source:** Financial Times — August 18, 2010  
**Link:** https://www.ft.com/content/6f1ca310-aaf8-11df-9e6b-00144feabdc0

### Core Message

Druckenmiller announced the closure of Duquesne Capital Management after 30 years of managing money, citing the "heavy personal toll" of continuous trading.

> "I have decided to retire from managing client funds and I wanted to give you prompt notice of my intentions and explain the reasons for this."

### Key Themes
- **Performance record:** Generated ~30% annualized returns over 30 years with no losing years
- **AUM growth:** From $900k to $12 billion
- **Personal cost:** "The joy of winning for clients is immense... the disappointment of each mistake is magnified by the fact that I know I am going to be living with it for a long time"

### Legacy
One of the most successful hedge fund managers in history, Drucenmiller shut down while still at the top of his game — a rarity in the industry.

## Lost Tree Club Speech (2015)

**Source:** TSOH Investment Research — Speech delivered January 2015  
**Link:** https://thescienceofhitting.com/p/im-here-to-tell-you-i-was-a-pig-4a9  
**Original PDF:** https://wtkthecontrarian.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/Stanley-Druckenmiller-Lost-Tree-Club.pdf

### Introduction (by Sam Reeves)

Performance comparison over 30 years:
| Investor | $1,000 Becomes (pre-tax) | Down Years |
|----------|--------------------------|------------|
| S&P 500 | $27,000 (~11.3% p.a.) | 5 |
| Warren Buffett | $177,000 (~20% p.a.) | 6 |
| Stanley Druckenmiller | $2,600,000 (~30% p.a.) | **0** |

### "I Was A Pig" — The Core Philosophy

> "The first thing I heard in this business was bulls make money, bears make money, and pigs get slaughtered. I'm here to tell you I was a pig. And I strongly believe the only way to make superior long-term returns in our business is by being a pig."

**Interpretation:**  
Diversification is logical for passive investors, but for active investors seeking alpha, concentrated bets based on conviction are essential.

### The Soros Lesson: When You See It, Bet Big

Druckenmiller describes how Soros taught him about sizing:

> "When I read 'The Alchemy of Finance', I understood very quickly that he was already employing an advanced version of the philosophy I was developing in my fund… I had a higher batting average, but Soros had a much higher slugging percentage… He just had more guts. He was betting more money with my ideas than I was."

### The British Pound Trade (1992)

> "Nothing explains our relationship and what I've learned from him more than the British pound… In August 1992, there was $7 billion in Quantum. I put $1.5 billion short the British pound… I wake up one morning and the head of the Bundesbank… basically said 'the British pound is crap and we don't want to be united with this currency'. I thought, well, this is my opportunity… I go in and I say, 'George, I'm going to sell $5.5 billion worth of British pounds tonight. Here's why I'm doing it, and that means we'll have 100% of the fund in this one trade.'
> 
> I think he's about to blow away my thesis, and he says, 'That is the most ridiculous money management I've ever heard. What you described is an incredible one-way bet. We should have 200% of our net worth in this trade, not 100%. Do you know how often something like this comes around? Like once every 20 years.'
> 
> We didn't get the whole $15 billion on, but we got enough that I'm sure some people in this room have read about it in the financial press."

**Result:** The trade generated approximately $1 billion profit. Soros later noted their total position was worth almost $10 billion by Black Wednesday.

### On Cutting Losses

> "I've thought a lot of things when I was managing money with great, great conviction, and a lot of times I was wrong. When you're betting the ranch and the circumstances change, you have to change – and that's how I've always managed my money."

## My First Million Podcast (2021)

**Source:** The Acquirer's Multiple — August 9, 2021  
**Link:** https://acquirersmultiple.com/2021/08/stanley-druckenmiller-what-makes-a-great-investor/

### What Makes A Great Investor

**On concentration vs. diversification:**

> "When I've looked at all the investors of very large reputations, Warren Buffett, Carl Icahn, George Soros, they all only have one thing in common and it's exact opposite of what they teach in business school. It is they make large concentrated bets where they have a lot of conviction.
> 
> They're not buying 35 or 40 names and diversifying. I don't know whether you remember Icahn, a few years ago, put $5 billion into Apple. And I don't think he was worth more than 10 billion when he did that. When I went in to tell Soros that I was going to short a hundred percent of the fund in the British pound against the Deutsche Mark, he looked at me with great disdain because he thought the story was good enough that I should be doing 200%."

**On why concentration decreases risk:**

> "Concentration, this is very counterintuitive, it really gets your intention. So it actually, in my thinking, decreases your overall risk because where you tend to be in trouble is if you have 35 or 40 names and you stopped paying attention to one.
> 
> If you have big, massive positions, it has your attention."

**Mark Twain quote he loves:**

> "Put all your eggs in one basket and watch the basket carefully."

**On stop losses:**

> "I've been in business since 1976 as a money manager, I've never used the stop loss, not once. Dumbest concept I've ever heard. It goes down 15%, I'm automatically out."

**On when to sell:**

> "I've also never hung onto a security if the reason I bought it has changed. And that's when you need to sell. If I buy X security for A, B, C, and D reasons, and those no longer are valid, whether I have a loss or a gain. That stock doesn't know whether you have a loss or a gain, it is not important. Your ego is not what this is about. What this is about is you're making money."

**On being wrong:**

> "If I have a thesis and it doesn't bear out, which happens often with me. I'm often wrong. Just get out and move on."

## Selected Quotes & Principles

### On Compounding

> "The way to build superior long-term returns is through preservation of capital and home runs." — Stanley Druckenmiller

> "I have this gift for compounding." — Yahoo Finance interview, March 2026

### On the Role of Liquidity

> "Stanley Druckenmiller's macro investing philosophy emphasizes that liquidity drives markets more than earnings." — Scribd, January 2026

### On Pattern Recognition

> "There's no silver bullet, and I'm the great beneficiary of 40 years of scars and successes that I can go back on and a lot of pattern recognition because there's not much I haven't seen in this business."

### On Knowing When To Stop Analyzing

> "The hardest skill to teach in investing is… knowing when to stop analyzing… At some point, the analysis becomes counterproductive. Sometimes, when the opportunity is so big, and you just kind of know it, you've just got to plunge in without the proper information."

### On Emotional Discipline

> "Everyone knows how I played the NASDAQ melt-up in '99, sold it perfectly in January, and then bought the exact top… I learned not to do that 20 years before, but I got emotional, which I fight every day."

> "The joy of winning for clients is immense, for me the disappointment of each mistake is magnified by the fact that I know I am going to be living with it for a long time."
