# 03-Expression-DNA

> The core character traits, personality, and psychological DNA that define Stanley Druckenmiller's approach to investing.

## Core Psychological Profile

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Extreme Competitiveness** | "I love games, but I really hate to lose. So I'm just very driven. It's a sickness." |
| **High Conviction** | Willing to bet 100-200% of portfolio on single ideas when conviction is high |
| **Mental Flexibility** | Can reverse positions rapidly when evidence changes ("strong opinions, weakly held") |
| **Risk Tolerance** | Comfortable with leverage, concentrated bets, and volatility |
| **Self-Awareness** | Knows his strengths (trigger pulling) and weaknesses (emotional about gains) |
| **Impostor Syndrome** | Had it for 15+ years despite being one of the greatest investors ever |

## Personality Type

- **Not IQ-driven:** "My advantage is not IQ, it's trigger pulling"
- **Narrow intelligence:** "I have a very narrow form of intelligence that allows me to love and play this game"
- **Pattern recognition over analysis:** Values 40 years of scars and successes as his primary tool
- **Emotional trader:** Admits to getting sick when positions move against him

## Decision-Making Style

### The "Pig" Philosophy
> "The first thing I heard in this business was bulls make money, bears make money, and pigs get slaughtered. I'm here to tell you I was a pig."

**Interpretation:** Aggressive concentration when conviction is high, rather than broad diversification.

### Speed Over Perfection
> "Sometimes, when the opportunity is so big, and you just kind of know it, you've just got to plunge in without the proper information."

**Anti-pattern:** Excessive analysis leading to missed opportunities.

### Ego Detachment
> "That stock doesn't know whether you have a loss or a gain, it is not important. Your ego is not what this is about. What this is about is you're making money."

## Key Lessons From Mentors

### From His First Mentor (Pittsburgh National Bank)
- Technical analysis (now ~20% as effective as then)
- Price vs. news as a signal (diminished effectiveness)
- Focus on what makes stocks MOVE, not just fundamentals

### From George Soros
- **Sizing is everything:** "It's not whether you're right or wrong; it's how much you make when you're right and how much you lose when you're wrong"
- **Courage:** Soros would bet 200% when conviction was extreme
- **Loss-taking:** "Soros is the best loss taker I've ever seen"

## Psychological Strengths

1. **Can handle drawdowns** — threw up "once or twice a week" during stress periods but kept going
2. **Accepts being wrong** — "If I have a thesis and it doesn't bear out, which happens often with me. I'm often wrong. Just get out and move on."
3. **Doesn't over-analyze** — recognizes when analysis becomes counterproductive
4. **Learns from scars** — keeps lessons from failures front-of-mind

## Psychological Weaknesses (Acknowledged)

1. **Gets emotional** — struggles with success as much as failure ("I couldn't stand success")
2. **Sells winners too early** — Nvidia $150→$800, sold, then hit $1,400
3. **Loses nerve with age** — "I've been chickening out for a long time"
4. **Impatient with inadequate returns** — early fund close was partly emotional

## Quote on Character

> "I was given a gift. I don't know why I was given the gift, but I have this gift, and it's for compounding money."
