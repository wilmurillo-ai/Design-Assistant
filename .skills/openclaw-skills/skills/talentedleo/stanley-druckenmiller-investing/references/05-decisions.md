# 05-Decisions

> Major investment decisions, trades, and strategic choices that defined Stanley Druckenmiller's career.

## Iconic Trades

### 1. British Pound Short (1992) — "Breaking the Bank of England"

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Position** | Short British pound vs Deutsche Mark |
| **Initial size** | $1.5 billion (Quantum Fund had $7B total) |
| **After Bundesbank signal** | Increased to $5.5 billion (100% of fund) |
| **Soros wanted** | 200% of net worth |
| **Result** | ~$1 billion profit |
| **Soros total position** | ~$10 billion by Black Wednesday |

**Thesis:** UK couldn't maintain ERM membership with unsustainable currency; raising interest rates would be politically impossible.

**Lesson:** When the trade is right, size aggressively.

| Detail | Value |

## Strategic Business Decisions

### 1. Closing Duquesne Capital (2010)

**Reason:** "Heavy personal toll" — stress from managing $12B with expectations of perfection

**Outcome:** One of few hedge fund managers to close at peak while still performing well

**Lesson:** Even the best can walk away when the personal cost is too high

**Reason:** After tech crash losses, needed to refocus on Duquesne

## Decision-Making Framework (How He Makes Decisions)

### 1. Identify Conviction
- Look for once-in-a-generation opportunities
- Only 1-2 times per year "something really, really excites you"

### 2. Size Accordingly
- 100% concentration when conviction is extreme
- 200% if Soros-level confidence

### 3. Monitor Thesis
- Original reasons for buying must remain valid
- If thesis changes → exit regardless of P&L

### 4. Cut Losses Emotionally
- Don't let ego hold losing positions
- "The stock doesn't know if you're up or down"

### 5. Take Profits (Known Weakness)
- Admits to selling winners too early
- Working on regaining "nerve"
