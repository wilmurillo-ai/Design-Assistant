# 05-Decisions

> Major investment decisions, trades, and strategic choices that defined Bill Ackman's career.

## Iconic Trades

### 1. Target (2007)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Position** | Significant stake in Target Corporation |
| **Thesis** | Retailer with undervalued real estate and underperforming operations |
| **Outcome** | Target fought back; Ackman eventually exited with profit |
| **Lesson** | Sometimes companies resist activist pressure |

---

### 2. Canadian Pacific Railway (2011–2017)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Position** | Became largest shareholder, led proxy contest |
| **Thesis** | Operational underperformance; needed leadership change |
| **Action** | Won proxy fight; replaced CEO and board majority |
| **Result** | Stock doubled; Hunter Harrison installed as CEO |
| **Recognition** | Globe and Mail named Ackman "CEO of the Year" for his activism |

---

### 3. J.C. Penney (2010–2013)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Position** | ~18% of company at $22/share average |
| **Thesis** | Department store with valuable real estate; needed reinvention |
| **Action** | Installed new CEO (Ron Johnson) |
| **Result** | Stock dropped sharply; CEO fired; Ackman eventually exited |
| **Lesson** | Retail transformation thesis was correct but timing/CEO choice was wrong |

---

### 4. Herbalife Short (2012–2020)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Position** | ~$1 billion short bet |
| **Thesis** | Pyramid scheme; FTC would shut it down |
| **Campaign** | PR war, lobbying, protests, Congressional testimony |
| **Carl Icahn** | Opened large long position; public feud ensued |
| **Outcome** | FTC ruled Herbalife deceived consumers (2016), paid $200M |
| **Trade result** | Short underperformed; Ackman eventually closed position |
| **Lesson** | Being right about a company ≠ profitable trade |

---

### 5. Valeant Pharmaceuticals (2013–2016) — THE DISASTER

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Position** | ~$3.8 billion at prices up to $196/share |
| **Thesis** | Drug company with aggressive pricing + roll-up strategy |
| **What happened** | Accounting fraud revealed; stock crashed from $260 to $8 |
| **Loss** | Estimated ~$4 billion loss for Pershing Square |
| **Congressional testimony** | Ackman testified alongside Valeant leadership |
| **Lesson** | Business model based on price hikes was fundamentally broken; board oversight failures |

---

### 6. Chipotle Mexican Grill (2016–2024)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Position** | Significant stake starting 2016 |
| **Thesis** | Food safety crisis was temporary; digital transformation opportunity |
| **Action** | Helped hire Brian Niccol as CEO (2018) |
| **Result** | Stock recovered strongly; Chipotle became top performer |
| **Role** | Credit for helping find right CEO and supporting digital strategy |

---

### 7. Universal Music Group (2021–present)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Position** | 10% stake (2021); one of Pershing Square's largest positions |
| **Personal connection** | Ackman's grandfather wrote a hit song on Tin Pan Alley in 1926 |
| **Thesis** | Dominant music company with streaming growth; long-term compounder |
| **Board seat** | Joined UMG board in 2022; stepped down in 2025 |

### 8. Pershing Square USA IPO (2024) — WITHDRAWN

## Strategic Business Decisions

### 1. Leave Gotham Partners → Start Pershing Square (2004)

**Reason:** Personal capital + backing from Leucadia National ($54M)

### 2. Fee Innovation

Pioneered 0% management fee + performance fee structure to align manager/investor interests.

### 3. List Pershing Square Holdings (London, 2014)

Structured as closed-end fund on London Stock Exchange (PSH, PSHD) — giving retail investors access to his strategy.

### 4. Pershing Square USA Attempt (2024)

Tried to bring strategy to US market via IPO — failed due to insufficient capital.

---

## Notable Errors

| Error | What Happened | Lesson |
|-------|----------------|--------|
| **Valeant** | ~$4B loss; backed wrong business model | Due diligence on business model is critical |
| **J.C. Penney** | Wrong CEO hire; stock dropped | Management quality matters |
| **Herbalife** | Right about company, wrong about trade | Thesis correctness ≠ profitable trade |
| **PSH USA IPO** | Withdrew due to insufficient demand | Market conditions matter for fund-raising |
