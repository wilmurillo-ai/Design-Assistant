## Congressional Testimony — Valeant (April 27, 2016)

**Source:** U.S. Senate Special Committee on Aging  
**Link:** https://www.businessinsider.com/valeant-congress-testimony-2016  
**Participants:** Bill Ackman, Valeant CEO J. Michael Pearson, CFO Howard Schiller

### Context

Valeant Pharmaceuticals was one of Ackman's worst investments. Pershing Square owned a significant stake and Ackman was a board member. The company later collapsed due to:
- Aggressive drug pricing practices
- Accounting irregularities
- Stock dropping from $260 to $8

### Ackman's Position at Valeant

- Pershing Square invested ~$3.8 billion in Valeant at prices up to $196/share
- Ackman defended Valeant's business model early on
- Later acknowledged mistakes in backing the company
- Congressional testimony sought to explain his due diligence and what went wrong

### Key Lesson from Valeant

From Ackman's retrospective analysis:
- Valeant's business model was fundamentally flawed (price hikes as core strategy)
- Board oversight was insufficient
- Ackman admitted: "I should have done better"

## Herbalife vs. Icahn Feud (2013–2014)

### The Setup (December 2012)

Ackman announced a $1 billion short bet against Herbalife, calling it a pyramid scheme.

### Icahn's Response (January 2013)

Carl Icahn bought a large stake and publicly defended Herbalife, creating the famous feud.

### The Battle

- **Ackman's weapons:** PR campaign, lobbying, protests, congressional testimony
- **Icahn's weapons:** Counter-research, media appearances, accumulating shares

### Outcome

- **Ackman:** Partial vindication via FTC ruling (2016) requiring Herbalife to pay $200M and restructure
- **Icahn:** Profited significantly from his long position
- **Ackman's short:** Did NOT generate expected profits despite being right about the company
- Herbalife stock eventually recovered significantly

## Political Engagement — Trump Endorsement (2024)

**Date:** 2024 Presidential Election

### Background

Ackman was a long-time Democrat donor and supporter. In a significant political shift, he endorsed Donald Trump for president in 2024.

### Why the Shift?

Ackman cited concerns about:
- Economic policies under the Biden administration
- Direction of the country
- Support for Israel

### Significance

The endorsement was notable because:
- Ackman had been a major Democratic donor
- Many in the finance world were anti-Trump
- It reflected broader realignment among some Wall Street figures

## Israel Support & Campus Protests (2024)

**Context:** October 7, 2023 Hamas attacks on Israel

### Ackman's Position

Ackman has been an outspoken supporter of Israel following the October 7 attacks. He:
- Publicly criticized pro-Palestinian protests on university campuses in 2024
- Used his platform to advocate for strong US support of Israel
- Argued that campus protests were anti-Semitic and dangerous

### His Criticism of Campus Protests (2024)

> "University presidents and administrators have failed to protect Jewish students. The campus protests are not about free speech — they are about harassing Jewish students and celebrating terrorism."
