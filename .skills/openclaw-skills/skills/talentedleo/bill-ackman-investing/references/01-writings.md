## Investment Philosophy

### Core Principles

**1. High-Conviction, Concentrated Bets**

Ackman runs a highly concentrated portfolio — typically 8-12 large positions. He believes in owning fewer companies deeply rather than diversifying broadly.

**2. Long-Term Time Horizon**

Pershing Square's investment horizon is typically 3-5 years. Ackman despises short-term noise and quarterly earnings reports.

**3. Activist Engagement**

Ackman takes significant stakes and seeks to influence company strategy — board seats, management changes, strategic pivots. He wants to be an active partner, not a passive holder.

**4. Deep Due Diligence**

Before investing, Ackman conducts exhaustive research. Famous for reading thousands of pages of legal documents (e.g., 725,000 pages for MBIA).

**5. Focus on Intrinsic Value**

Seek businesses trading below intrinsic value with a clear path to unlocking that value through operational improvements, capital allocation, or corporate governance changes.

**6. Aligned Incentives**

Ackman's fee structure is designed to align manager and investor interests: no management fee, earn only when investors profit.

## Pershing Square USA IPO Attempt (2024)

**Date:** Early 2024 → Withdrawn August 1, 2024

**What happened:**
- Ackman attempted to take Pershing Square USA public
- Goal: raise capital for a new concentrated, long-only vehicle
- Secured only ~$2 billion → withdrew the IPO on August 1, 2024
- Announced plans to relaunch with a "new transaction structure"

**Why it matters:**
- Shows Ackman's willingness to innovate on fund structure
- Also shows market skepticism of large, late-stage hedge fund IPOs
- The IPO withdrawal was widely covered as a high-profile setback

## Fee Structure Philosophy

Ackman is known for championing investor-aligned fee structures:

**The Ackman Model:**
- **Management fee:** 0% (no fee charged simply for managing money)
- **Performance fee:** Standard hedge fund rate (industry typical ~20%, verify exact PSH structure)
- **High-water mark:** Ensures managers only earn performance fees on new profits

**Additional innovation:**
- In some structures: investors share in a portion of the performance fee at the end of the fund's life (the "waterfall" or "profit sharing" element)
- Ackman has advocated publicly for this model as the right way to align hedge fund managers with their investors

**His argument:**
> If I can't make money for investors without charging them to manage their money, I don't deserve to be paid.

## Herbalife Short Campaign (2012–2020)

**Duration:** December 2012 – 2020  
**Position:** ~$1 billion short bet  
**Thesis:** Herbalife is a pyramid scheme

### The Thesis

**On announcing the short (December 2012):**
> "We believe Herbalife is a pyramid scheme. It is a company that rewards distributors for recruiting new distributors rather than for selling product to end consumers."

### The Campaign

Ackman ran an aggressive, multi-year campaign:
- **Short position:** $1 billion notional short bet
- **PR war:** Funded a persistent PR campaign against Herbalife
- **Lobbying:** Hired numerous lobbying firms to pressure state and federal regulators
- **Protest organizing:** Organized protests and letter-writing campaigns
- **Political lobbying:** Personally lobbied senators (Linda Sánchez, Ed Markey)
- **Congressional testimony:** Testified before Senate committee in 2016 alongside Valeant CEO

### Carl Icahn Rivalry

Billionaire Carl Icahn took the opposite position — bought a large stake in Herbalife and defended the company. The Ackman-Icahn feud became legendary on Wall Street, with public insults traded on CNBC.

### Outcome

**Partial vindication in 2016:**
- FTC ruled Herbalife deceived consumers and had to pay $200 million and restructure
- Found that "the small minority of distributors who do make a lot of money are compensated for recruiting new distributors"

**However:**
- Ackman's short position did not generate the massive profit he expected
- Herbalife stock initially dropped but later recovered significantly
- The campaign was costly and consumed years of resources
- By ~2020, Ackman had closed the position (exact timing unconfirmed)

**Lesson:** Being right about a company's faults does not always translate to profitable trades.

## Selected Quotes & Principles

### On Activist Investing

> "We are not passive investors. We are trying to change the trajectory of companies."

> "The best investments often come when a great business is going through a temporary problem that the market overreacts to."

### On Concentration

> "We think it's better to own a few great businesses than many mediocre ones."

> "Our philosophy is to make a small number of very high-conviction investments and then actively engage with management to create value."

### On Due Diligence

> "We read everything. Every contract, every filing, every legal document. The details matter."

### On Herbalife

> "Herbalife is a pyramid scheme. It's that simple."

### On Long-Term Thinking

> "We don't think in quarters. We think in years. The market short-termism is an opportunity for patient, long-term investors."

### On Fee Alignment

> "If I can't make money for my investors, I don't deserve to be paid."

### On His Investment Edge

> "We're not smarter than everyone else. We just do more work, we concentrate more, and we stay longer."
