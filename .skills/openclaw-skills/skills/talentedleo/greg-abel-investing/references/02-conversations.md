# Greg Abel - Interviews & Conversations

*Last updated: 2026-04-10*

## Executive Summary

Greg Abel is one of the most private executives in American business. Unlike Warren Buffett, who grants numerous interviews and writes detailed shareholder letters, Abel has given only a handful of documented interviews in his entire career. Most public knowledge about him comes from secondhand accounts and Berkshire's official communications.

---

## Direct Interviews

### 1. 2025 Berkshire Annual Meeting (May 3) - First Statement as CEO-Designate

**Context:** First public statement after Buffett's retirement announcement. Abel was caught off-guard by the timing.

**Abel's Statement:**
> "It's really the investment philosophy and how Warren and the team have allocated capital for the past 60 years. Really, it will not change. And it's the approach we'll take as we go forward."

**Key Detail:**
Only Buffett's children had advance knowledge of the retirement decision. Abel had no prior warning.

**Source:** BBC News, May 3, 2025
**Link:** https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cqj4nev7p70o

---

### 2. FOX Business Interview (September 7, 2018) - First TV Interview

**Context:** First business network interview. Conducted at Berkshire 5K run in Omaha. Abel was 56, vice chairman for less than a year.

**On his new role:**
> "Nothing's changed. Business as usual. It's just great. We've got great people to be working with… couldn't be better."

**On current business conditions:**
> "Well, things are good right now. So our businesses are in a great place. And we've got great managers and like I said we are moving forward."

**Source:** FOX Business, September 7, 2018
**Link:** https://www.foxbusiness.com/business-leaders/greg-abel-holding-a-key-to-buffetts-kingdom

---

### 3. Horatio Alger Association Speech (May 2018)

**Context:** Abel received the Horatio Alger Award, given to individuals who overcome adversity to achieve success.

**On childhood in Edmonton:**
> "I remember getting together with other neighborhood kids as soon as we could after school. We played hockey nearly every day in summer and winter and stayed out until we were called in for dinner."

**On making money as a kid:**
> "Growing up, Abel made money through odd jobs, like delivering advertising flyers and filling fire extinguishers for the company where his father worked, Levitt Safety."

**On career motivation:**
> "I want to have an impact. I want to roll up my sleeves and be actively engaged in making our company successful."

**On work ethic:**
> "I think hard work leads to good outcomes. In my schooling, in sports, and in my business positions, I learned that if I put in a lot of work and was well-prepared, then success would be more likely."

**Source:** Horatio Alger Association, May 2018
**Link:** https://horatioalger.org/members/member-detail/gregory-e-abel/

## Notable Absences

**Abel does NOT typically:**
- Grant media interviews (very rare exceptions - see above)
- Write public letters or articles
- Speak at conferences outside Berkshire events
- Comment on specific investment decisions publicly
- Discuss his investment decision-making framework

This privacy is in stark contrast to Buffett and makes research on Abel's specific views significantly more challenging.

---

## What We Know About His Communication Style

From all available evidence:

1. **Direct and economical** - Few words, no elaboration
2. **Understated** - "Couldn't be better" echo of Buffett
3. **Deflects praise** - Credits others, especially "great people"
4. **Practical focus** - Emphasizes operations over theory
5. **Humble** - Rarely talks about personal achievements
6. **Reactive** - Doesn't proactively seek publicity
