# Greg Abel - Key Decisions

*Last updated: 2026-04-10*

## Executive Summary

Unlike Warren Buffett, whose investment decisions are well-documented through shareholder letters, Greg Abel's decision-making is less visible. Most documented decisions relate to operational management of BHE rather than public equity investments. The BYD investment is the most famous case where Abel's role can be identified.

## BYD Investment (2008) - The Most Famous Abel-Adjacent Decision

### The Decision

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Date** | September 2008 |
| **Initial Investment** | ~$230 million for 9.89% stake |
| **Price** | HK$8 per share |
| **Who Made the Call** | Charlie Munger identified the opportunity |
| **Abel's Role** | As CEO of MidAmerican/BHE, Abel oversaw the subsidiary making this investment |
| **Berkshire Exit** | Completed September 2025 |
| **Holding Period** | 17 years |
| **Total Return** | ~3,890% (reported) |

### Context

**Timing:** Made during the 2008 financial crisis — a classic Buffett/Munger countercyclical move.

**Munger's Vision:** Munger identified BYD's vertically integrated model (batteries + autos) as distinctive. Buffett credited Munger with the original insight.

**Source:** Wikipedia
**Link:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BYD_Company

---

### The Investment Thesis (Inferred from Munger's Known Preferences)

1. **Vertically integrated** — BYD made batteries AND cars
2. **China's emerging EV market** — Early bet on Chinese consumer adoption
3. **Cost advantage** — Manufacturing capabilities
4. **Management** — Wang Chuanfu's technical background

### The Exit

| Year | Action |
|------|--------|
| 2022 | Berkshire began gradually reducing BYD stake |
| June 2024 | Stake reduced to 6.9% |
| September 2025 | Complete exit announced |

**Rationale for Exit:** Unclear publicly. Munger (who died 2023) was the primary BYD bull. Valuation concerns may have prompted trimming.

**Source:** Wikipedia - BYD Company
**Link:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BYD_Company

### Results

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Initial Investment | ~$230 million |
| Final Exit Value | Estimated ~$5-6 billion at peak |
| Holding Period | 17 years |
| Return | ~3,890% |

## Berkshire Hathaway Energy - Operational Decisions

### BHE Growth Strategy (Abel's Leadership)

Under Abel, BHE transformed from a regional utility into a diversified energy conglomerate:

| Year | Action | Significance |
|------|--------|--------------|
| 1999 | Berkshire acquires controlling interest | Entry point for Abel |
| 2000s | Expansion into wind, solar, geothermal | Diversification beyond coal |
| 2008 | Abel becomes CEO of MidAmerican | Full operational control |
| 2014 | Renamed Berkshire Hathaway Energy | Brand alignment |
| Ongoing | Renewable energy investment | Major wind/solar expansion |
| Ongoing | Regulatory strategy | Multi-state utility operations |

**Source:** Business Standard, May 2025
**Link:** https://www.business-standard.com/world-news/who-is-greg-abel-warren-buffet-successor-berkshire-hathaway-ceo-125050400113_1.html

### Key Characteristics of Abel's BHE Approach

1. **Long-term infrastructure focus** — Regulated utilities with stable returns
2. **Decentralized management** — Local managers run their businesses
3. **Minimal interference** — "Let managers manage" philosophy
4. **Reinvestment discipline** — Cash reinvested vs. returned

### BHE Scale Under Abel

- **2014:** ~$92 billion in assets
- **8%** of Berkshire's total revenue and pretax earnings
- **One of largest** regulated utility groups in North America

**Source:** CNBC, 2021
**Link:** https://www.cnbc.com/2021/05/03/who-is-greg-abel-warren-buffetts-successor-at-berkshire-hathaway.html

## Leadership Decisions

### 2018 Vice Chairman Appointment

**The Decision:** Buffett elevates both Abel and Ajit Jain to Vice Chairman roles.

**Abel's Portfolio:**
- All non-insurance operations
- BNSF Railway
- Berkshire Hathaway Energy
- See's Candies, Dairy Queen, etc.

**Significance:**
- Most visible step in succession process
- Gained visibility into ALL Berkshire subsidiaries
- Buffett mentorship intensified

**Source:** CNBC, January 2018
**Link:** https://www.cnbc.com/2018/01/10/berkshire-hathaways-warren-buffett-appoints-greg-abel-and-ajit-jain-as-vice-chairmen.html

---

### 2022 Share Purchase Decision

**The Decision:** Abel purchased $68 million of Berkshire Class A shares (168 shares at ~$404,761 each)

**Context:** After selling his BHE stake for $870 million

**Significance:**
- Personal confidence in Berkshire
- Aligns his wealth with shareholders
- Buffett said he wanted successors who were "already very rich"

**Source:** CNBC, August 2022
**Link:** https://www.cnbc.com/2022/08/06/berkshire-ceo-designate-abel-sells-stake-in-energy-company-he-led-for-870-million.html

## What We Don't Know

Abel is notably private about specific decision-making frameworks. Unlike Buffett's detailed explanations, Abel has rarely articulated:

- Specific criteria for evaluating businesses
- How he thinks about capital allocation
- Views on specific industries or companies
- His investment decision-making process
- When he would vs. wouldn't do a deal

**Most information comes from Buffett's public statements about Abel, not from Abel directly.**

---

## Key Insight: Operational vs. Investment Focus

| Aspect | Buffett | Abel |
|--------|---------|------|
| **Primary expertise** | Capital allocation | Operational management |
| **Track record** | 60+ years of documented picks | BHE utilities |
| **Public philosophy** | Extensive writings | Virtually none |
| **Decision style** | Long-term value investing | Decentralized operations |
| **Risk approach** | Concentrated bets | Steady, regulated growth |
