# Greg Abel - External Views & Criticisms

*Last updated: 2026-04-10*

## Executive Summary

Greg Abel has received near-universal praise from Berkshire insiders, but external analysts and media have raised legitimate questions about his lack of public communication, limited independent investment track record, and the challenge of succeeding the most famous investor in history.

## Criticisms & Concerns

### 1. Lack of Public Communication

**Concern:** Unlike Buffett, Abel doesn't articulate his philosophy publicly.

**Implication:** Shareholders and analysts cannot gauge his thinking on key decisions.

**Mitigation:** Buffett's 60-year track record and stated confidence provides reassurance.

---

### 2. No Independent Investment Track Record

**Concern:** Abel has never publicly explained his investment decision-making at Berkshire scale.

**Detail:** BHE's investments were made under Berkshire oversight. Abel hasn't made standalone investment decisions at Berkshire's $1 trillion+ scale.

**Implication:** Unknown how he would handle the equity portfolio.

**Mitigation:** Buffett explicitly said Abel would handle capital allocation, suggesting confidence.

---

### 3. Succession Timing

**Concern:** Some questioned whether Buffett waited too long.

**Timeline:**
- 2021: Abel confirmed as successor
- 2025: CEO change effective
- 5-year transition

**Perspective:** Some view as too long; others view as prudent given Buffett's longevity.

---

### 4. BYD Exit Timing

**Concern:** Berkshire exited BYD gradually 2022-2025, missing some peak.

**Context:** Munger was the BYD champion; unclear if Abel shared that conviction.

---

### 5. Scale Question

**Concern:** Managing $1 trillion+ conglomerate vs. running utilities.

**Note:** BHE is regulated utilities; very different from Berkshire's equity investments and diverse subsidiaries.

---

## External Assessment Summary

### Strengths Recognized Externally

1. **Proven operator** — Successfully ran BHE for 15+ years
2. **Culture keeper** — Understood and preserved Berkshire's decentralized model
3. **Work ethic** — Known for engagement and preparation
4. **Trusted by Buffett** — Most important endorsement possible
5. **Deal maker** — "Astute" per WSJ

### Concerns Acknowledged

1. **Limited public philosophy** — Hard to assess independent thinking
2. **No direct investment record** — BHE's regulated utility model differs from equity investing
3. **Communication style** — Less transparent than Buffett
4. **Scale challenge** — $1T+ vs. utility operations
5. **Inheriting legend** — "Shoes to fill" concern
