## Cassandra Unchained Debut (November 2025)

**Date:** November 23–24, 2025  
**Platform:** Substack ($379/year) + X (Twitter)  
**Subscriber count:** 23,000+ within days

### Key Statements on X (October–November 2025)

**October 30, 2025 — Return to X (first post in 2+ years):**
> Diagnosed the AI boom as a bubble. "The only winning move is not to play."

**On "give-and-take deals" among AI companies:**
> Accused AI companies of mutual deals to inflate revenues, overinvesting, and dragging out depreciation schedules to mask earnings quality.

**On Palantir feud with CEO Alex Karp:**
- Karp called Burry's $1.1B put options on Nvidia and Palantir "batshit crazy"
- Burry responded: "Karp couldn't crack a 13F" (meaning Karp doesn't understand filings)
- Burry later revealed Scion exited Palantir puts in October — before the stock dropped 26%
- Closed Scion Asset Management's SEC registration days later

**November 25 teaser:**
> "On to much better things Nov 25th"

## Fannie Mae / Housing Market (March 2026)

**Date:** March 2026  
**Platform:** X (Twitter)  

### Agrees with Bill Ackman on Fannie Mae / Freddie Mac

Burry agreed with Bill Ackman's bullish call on Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac — both stocks that Ackman has championed.

### On US Housing Market

Burry pushed back against the "housing shortage" narrative:

> "The issue is misallocation, not supply. The US housing problem is not a supply problem — it's a misallocation problem."

He called for reform to Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac's role in the mortgage market.

## GameStop — Before the Meme Stock Era

**Year:** 2021 (before the Reddit/WSB frenzy)

**What happened:**
- Burry had been an investor in GameStop before it became a meme stock
- He sold before the dramatic ascent driven by Reddit's r/wallstreetbets

**What he said at the time:**
- Warned about meme stock hype
- Raised concerns about electric vehicles, SaaS companies, and bitcoin — comparing them to the dot-com bubble
- These markets would later collapse

**January 2026:** Burry said he was **buying GameStop shares again**, but explicitly NOT betting on meme-stock mania — treating it as a value play this time.

## Bitcoin / Crypto Stance (2021–2026)

### 2021: Initial Criticism

Burry compared bitcoin and meme stocks to the dot-com bubble, warning of an imminent crash.

### Post-2022 Crypto Collapse

After the crypto market collapsed in 2022, Burry continued to criticize cryptocurrencies.

### February 2026: Bearish Bitcoin Chart

Burry released a chart comparing the bearish bitcoin state to the 2021–2022 plunge — suggesting the possibility of a deeper reset.

**Note:** Burry has never been a crypto bull. He views it as a speculative bubble, not a store of value.

## COVID Lockdowns Stance (2020)

Burry was **highly critical of the COVID-19 lockdowns** in the United States. He believed the economic damage was predictable and that the policy response was disproportionate to the actual risk.

This criticism is consistent with his broader worldview: he favors personal responsibility, minimal government intervention, and is skeptical of consensus-driven policy responses.
