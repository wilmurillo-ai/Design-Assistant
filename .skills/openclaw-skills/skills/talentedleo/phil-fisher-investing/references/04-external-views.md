# 04-External-Views

> How the world sees Phil Fisher — media coverage, peer views, and public perception.

## Media Characterization

### Common Themes in Coverage
- "Pioneer of growth investing" — the founder of growth investing category
- "Quality over price" — great businesses at fair prices
- "Motorola investor" — bought 1955, held until death
- "Scuttlebutt pioneer" — originated the competitive intelligence approach
- "The private investor" — very selective, few interviews

## Peer Assessments

### Warren Buffett
> "85% Benjamin Graham + 15% Philip Fisher" — John Train's famous description of Buffett

> "Common Stocks and Uncommon Profits is a very, very good book." — 2018 Berkshire Hathaway meeting

> Fisher's scuttlebutt technique "continues to be a good way of investing" — 2018 Berkshire Hathaway meeting

### Charlie Munger
> Pushed Buffett toward Fisher's quality approach; influenced See's Candy investment

### Kenneth Fisher (Son)
> Carried forward his father's principles in founding Fisher Investments (1979)

## Public Reputation

| Aspect | Reputation |
|--------|------------|
| **Investment approach** | Pioneer of growth investing; GARP pioneer |
| **Books** | Common Stocks and Uncommon Profits is a classic |
| **Privacy** | Very private; few public appearances |
| **Longevity** | Held Motorola 49 years (1955-2004) |

## How He's Viewed by Different Groups

| Group | View |
|-------|------|
| **Value Investors** | Graham purists see him as too expensive; synthesis with Buffett is the bridge |
| **Growth Investors** | See him as the original growth investor; a pioneer |
| **Academic Finance** | Teaches the Graham + Fisher + Schloss framework |
| **General Public** | Not well known until his book in 1958 |

## Key External Quotes About Fisher

> "Philip Fisher is one of the great investors of all time." — Morningstar

> "The way he taught us to think about businesses was transformational." — Berkshire Hathaway perspective
