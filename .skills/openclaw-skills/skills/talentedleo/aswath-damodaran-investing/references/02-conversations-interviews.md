# Aswath Damodaran - Conversations and Interviews

## Overview

Aswath Damodaran is one of the most prolific speakers on valuation, regularly appearing on podcasts, TED Talks, and conferences. His interviews challenge conventional valuation wisdom and provide deep insights into understanding value in uncertain markets.

## 3. Recent Substack Q&A (2024-2025)

### On Market Uncertainty

**Question:** How do you value companies when there is extreme uncertainty?

**Answer:**
> "When uncertainty is high, I do three things:
> 1. Keep the valuation simple
> 2. Use probability-weighted scenarios
> 3. Price in the uncertainty explicitly
>
> The worst thing you can do is pretend uncertainty does not exist."

---

### On AI/Tech Valuations

**Question:** How do you value AI companies?

**Answer:**
> "AI companies are some of the hardest to value because:
> 1. The technology is changing rapidly
> 2. The business models are unclear
> 3. Competition is intense
>
> My approach is to build multiple scenarios and be explicit about probabilities. I do not pretend to know which one will happen."

---

### On Market Cycles

**Question:** How do you know when the market is overvalued?

**Answer:**
> "I look at three indicators:
> 1. Market-wide P/E ratios vs. historical averages
> 2. Risk premiums being demanded
> 3. The stories investors are telling
>
> When all three suggest optimism, I become more cautious. When they suggest fear, I become more aggressive."

## 6. Key Interview Themes

### On Valuation Methodology

**DCF is Not Dead:**
> "DCF is not a crystal ball. It is a tool for understanding the implications of your assumptions. If you put garbage in, you get garbage out."

**Relative Valuation Matters:**
> "The best way to check your DCF is to compare it to what similar companies are trading at. If there is a big gap, you need to understand why."

**Story-Driven Valuation:**
> "Every valuation should tell a story about the future. If you cannot tell a coherent story, your valuation is likely wrong."

### On Risk and Uncertainty

**On Risk Premiums:**
> "The equity risk premium is not a number you look up. It is a judgment you make about the future of the economy."

**On Uncertainty:**
> "I do not try to reduce uncertainty. I try to understand it. Then I price it appropriately."

### On Investment Strategy

**On Being a Contrarian:**
> "Being contrarian is not about being different. It is about being right when the market is wrong. If you are contrarian for the sake of being contrarian, you will lose money."

**On Learning from Mistakes:**
> "I have been valuing companies for 30 years, and I still make mistakes. The key is to learn from them and not make the same mistake twice."

## 9. Recent Interviews (2026)

### Substack Q&A: Investment Philosophy (March 2026)

**On Finding Your Philosophy:**
> "You may not be able to mend the damage to your portfolio, but it will help you find balance again and make sense of the noise around you."

**On Market Uncertainty:**
> "When uncertainty roils markets, it is natural for investors to get knocked off balance, and when off-balance, to make investment decisions that they often regret later."

**On Active Investing:**
> "Active investing is one of the most difficult games to win at... Just look at the percentage of active institutional investors who beat their respective indices over the last decade."

**On Luck vs. Skill:**
> "Much as investment books and classes claim otherwise, investing results are affected by so many forces that are out of your control that disentangling how much of your final returns can be attributed to skill and how much to luck is very difficult to do."

---

### Substack Q&A: AI Disruption (March 2026)

**On AI Valuations:**
> "AI companies are some of the hardest to value because:
> 1. The technology is changing rapidly
> 2. The business models are unclear
> 3. Competition is intense"

**On AI Scenarios:**
> "The problem with all of these AI scenarios is that they are rooted in the weakest of responses to uncertainty, which is to either pick a scenario and describe it in detail, without establishing how likely that scenario is."

**My Approach:**
> "My intent in this post is less about promoting my favored AI scenario, and more about providing a framework for you to develop your own."

**On Probabilistic Thinking:**
> "Financial analysts and economists have been slow in adopting probabilistic approaches, where point estimates are replaced by distributions. One reason is that being open about estimates and errors, which is visible in probabilistic approaches, will be viewed as a sign of weakness or lack of conviction."

---

### Substack Q&A: Oil War and Markets (April 2026)

**On War and Markets:**
> "Markets play an expectations game, and in March 2026, we saw the process play out, with all of its upsides and downsides."

**On Expert Predictions:**
> "We live in an age of commentary, as self-proclaimed experts offer prognostications... Given the strong priors that many of these experts bring to the game, it should not be surprising that their views are driven by those priors."

**On Listening to Markets:**
> "I have learned that the market is far better at making sense of complexity and uncertainty than experts are, and it behooves us therefore to listen to what it is saying."

**On Risk Pricing:**
> "The heightened uncertainty generated by war and its consequences has played its way out not just in oil prices and treasury rates, but in the prices that investors charge for risk."
