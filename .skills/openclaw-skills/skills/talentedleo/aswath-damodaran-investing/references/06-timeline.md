# Aswath Damodaran - Complete Timeline

## Overview

Aswath Damodaran is an Indian-American academic and author who has become one of the world's foremost experts on valuation. This timeline traces his career and major contributions.

---

## Early Life and Education

### 1958: Birth
- Born in Chennai, India
- Family background in academics

### 1976-1980: Undergraduate Studies
- Bachelor's degree in Commerce
- Loyola College, Chennai
- First exposure to finance and accounting

### 1980-1985: Graduate Studies
- MBA, Indian Institute of Management (IIM)
- Developed interest in finance theory

### 1985-1989: PhD Studies
- Doctorate in Finance
- University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA)
- Specialization in valuation and corporate finance

---

## Academic Career

### 1989: First Academic Position
- Joined University of California, Berkeley (Haas School of Business)
- Began developing his valuation methodology
- Focus on bridging academic theory and practical application

### 1990s: Rise to Prominence
- Published first papers on implied cost of capital
- Developed novel approaches to DCF valuation
- Created datasets that became industry standard

### 1994: NYU Stern
- Joined New York University Stern School of Business
- Has remained at NYU Stern since
- Built one of the most popular finance courses

## Key Contributions

### 1. Implied Cost of Capital (ICC)

**Innovation:**
Developed a methodology to estimate the cost of equity from current market prices rather than historical data.

**Application:**
- Used by analysts worldwide
- Basis for many quantitative models
- Changed how practitioners think about risk

### 2. Probabilistic Valuation

**Innovation:**
Emphasized that valuation should produce a range of values with probabilities, not a single number.

**Application:**
- Scenario analysis
- Monte Carlo simulation
- Real options

### 3. Story-Driven Analysis

**Innovation:**
Argued that every valuation should tell a story about the future.

**Application:**
- Qualitative assessment
- Narrative and numbers combined
- Critical thinking emphasis
