## Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meetings (with Buffett)

**Role:** Vice Chairman of Berkshire Hathaway (1978–2023)  
**Co-speaker:** Warren Buffett (Chairman and CEO)

### The "Woodstock of Capitalism"

The annual Berkshire Hathaway meeting is famously called "Woodstock of Capitalism." Munger and Buffett sit on stage for 5-6 hours taking questions from shareholders and reporters.

**Key themes in their joint appearances:**

| Year Range | Key Theme |
|-----------|-----------|
| 1978–1990s | Early Berkshire years, partnership formation |
| 1990s–2000s | Quality businesses at fair prices, See's Candy acquisition |
| 2000s | Crisis of 2008, financial system, bailouts |
| 2010s | Innovation, technology, Costco, Apple |
| 2020–2023 | COVID, changing world, final appearances |

### Famous Munger Moments at Berkshire Meetings

**On index funds:**
> "Index funds are the best thing for most investors — it's a demonstrable fact."

**On Bitcoin:**
> "It's a poison. It's a gambling contract with a 100% spread for the house."

**On Tesla:**
> "I think Elon Musk is probably the world's most overestimated individual."

**On the role of vice chairman:**
> "I'm the guy who sits next to Warren and occasionally says something useful."

## Daily Journal Corporation Annual Meetings

**Role:** Chairman of Daily Journal Corp (1984–2023)

Munger ran Daily Journal as a personal investment vehicle, using the company's excess cash to invest in a concentrated portfolio of high-quality businesses.

### Annual Meeting Philosophy

Munger would take questions for hours at Daily Journal meetings. His answers were known for being:
- Direct and often irreverent
- Full of historical references
- Willing to criticize academics and professionals
- Consistent with Berkshire principles

### Key Positions Discussed at Daily Journal Meetings

**Core holdings over the years:**
- Costco Wholesale (held for decades, major position)
- Wells Fargo (held for decades)
- Bank of America
- US Bancorp
- Other financial institutions

### Famous Daily Journal Quotes

**On sitting on cash:**
> "It takes character to sit with all that cash and do nothing. I didn't get to where I am by going after mediocre opportunities."

**On waiting:**
> "It's waiting that helps you as an investor, and a lot of people just can't stand to wait."

**On the value of concentration:**
> "The way to get rich is to concentrate your holdings. Most people should be buying index funds, but for those who want to pick stocks — diversification is for people who don't know what they're doing."

## Final Public Statements (2023)

### Munger's Last Public Remarks

**Date:** 2023 (months before passing November 28, 2023)  
**Source:** Various press appearances

Munger gave a small number of interviews in 2023. These were notable because he was 99 years old and rarely gave interviews.

### Key 2023 Themes

**On longevity:**
> "I've been very lucky. I had a father who lived to 94 and a mother who lived to 92."

**On Berkshire's future:**
> "Berkshire will do fine without me. It's already fine."

**On regrets:**
> "I should have bought more Costco. And I should have been more willing to go against consensus."

## On Buffett

### How Munger Described Buffett

> "Warren is the most rational man I know. He has an instinct for business that borders on genius."

> "Warren is a learning machine. He never stops."

### Buffett on Munger

**From Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting:**

> "Charlie is the most rational man I know. He comes equipped for rationality. And it's been the greatest partnership I could ever imagine."

> "Charlie has never tried to copy anyone. He has his own mold."

> "He is the best partner I could ever have."

### The Munger-Buffett Dynamic

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Buffett's role | Decision-maker on capital allocation |
| Munger's role | Devil's advocate, psychological pressure, multi-disciplinary thinking |
| How they disagree | Munger pushes back harder than most |
| How they agree | Always on the big picture — quality businesses, patience, rationality |

## On Specific Topics

### On Investing

**On index funds:**
> "Index funds are the best thing for most investors. It's a demonstrable fact."

**On stock picking:**
> "If you're going to be an active investor, the time to be very concentrated is when you have a lot of conviction. Most people don't have that much conviction."

**On market timing:**
> "We're trying to invest always. We never say we're out of the market."

**On crypto:**
> "Cryptocurrency is a poison. I wouldn't take all the bitcoin in the world for $25."

### On Business

**On Costco:**
> "Costco is one of the greatest businesses I have ever seen. The way they've built it — it's a phenomenon."

**On Costco CEO Jim Sinegal:**
> "He's one of the great businessmen of the century."

**On Apple (as investment):**
> "Apple is a hell of a business. Tim Cook did a magnificent job."

### On Psychology

**On human misjudgment:**
> "We are all influence machines. The question is whether you influence for good or bad."

**On overconfidence:**
> "Smart people aren't exempt from professional disasters from overconfidence."

### On Academic Economics

**On economics education:**
> "Economics has too few models and too much pride. It's like the village blacksmith who learned one way to shoe a horse and never learned any other."

## Notable Interview Themes

| Theme | Munger's Position |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Multidisciplinary thinking** | Essential — read across disciplines |
| **Patience** | The ultimate virtue; waiting is the game |
| **Rationality** | "I'm rational" — most important trait |
| **Inversion** | Solve problems backwards; know where you'll fail |
| **Avoiding stupidity** | More important than seeking brilliance |
| **Circle of competence** | Know what you know; stay in your circle |
| **Lollapalooza effects** | Multiple tendencies combine; outcomes are multiplicative |
| **Berkshire model** | Capital allocation to highest-return opportunities |
