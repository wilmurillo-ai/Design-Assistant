# 03-Expression-DNA

> The core character traits, personality, and psychological DNA that define Charlie Munger's approach to thinking and investing.

## Core Psychological Profile

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Rational** | "I'm rational" — primary self-description; every decision grounded in logic |
| **Multidisciplinary** | Reads across disciplines — psychology, economics, physics, biology, history |
| **Patient** | "You make your money by the waiting" — willingness to do nothing for years |
| **Inverted thinker** | Approaches problems backwards — "Invert, always invert" |
| **Ironic/sardonic** | Dry humor, irreverent toward authority, willing to offend |
| **Independent** | "Marches to the beat of his own music" — Buffett description |
| **Principled** | Strong moral compass; won't compromise on ethics |
| **Retrospective** | Studies failure more than success; collects instances of bad judgment |

## Personality Type

- **Renaissance polymath** — Refused to be confined to one discipline
- **Devil's advocate** — Always arguing the other side; tests ideas by attacking them
- **Stoic** — Accepts reality even when painful; doesn't deny inconvenient truths
- **Lifelong learner** — "Spend each day trying to be a little wiser"

## Decision-Making Style

### Inversion First

> "I always knew from when I was a little boy that the opportunities that were important were few and that the trick was to prepare myself for seizing the few that came."

**The inversion method:**
1. Define the problem
2. Ask: "How would I make this worse?"
3. Avoid those outcomes
4. Then optimize forward

### Collect Failure Cases

Munger would collect instances of bad judgment the way others collect butterflies. He believed understanding failure was more valuable than studying success.

### Multidisciplinary Synthesis

> "I paid no attention to the territorial boundaries of academic disciplines and I just grabbed all the big ideas that I could."

He believed most people are "one-tool" thinkers — specialists in one field who apply it everywhere. The antidote is collecting mental models from many disciplines.

## Psychological Strengths

1. **Rationality above all** — Never lets emotions drive decisions
2. **Extreme patience** — Can wait years for the right opportunity
3. **Self-awareness** — Knows the limits of his knowledge (circle of competence)
4. **Failure focus** — Studies mistakes systematically
5. **Inversion** — Solves problems backwards before forwards

## Psychological Weaknesses (Acknowledged)

1. **Limited social warmth** — Not warm or effusive; described himself as "difficult"
2. **High standards for others** — Little patience for people who are confidently wrong
3. **Intensity** — Can be overwhelming to colleagues and employees

## Quote on Character

> "I'm rational." — His most repeated self-description

> "Spend each day trying to be a little wiser than you were when you woke up. Discharge your duties faithfully and well. Systematically you get ahead, but not necessarily in fast spurts."
