# 05-Decisions

> Major investment decisions, business choices, and strategic moves that defined Charlie Munger's career.

## Iconic Decisions

### 1. Join Berkshire Hathaway (1978)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Decision** | Become Vice Chairman of Berkshire Hathaway |
| **Context** | Had been running his own fund, Wesco Financial |
| **Impact** | Transformed Berkshire from cigar-butt investing to quality businesses |

**Why it mattered:** Munger's influence shifted Berkshire's strategy from "fair businesses at wonderful prices" to "wonderful businesses at fair prices." This single decision created hundreds of billions in additional wealth.

---

### 2. Costco — The "Hell Yes" Decision (Early 2000s)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Decision** | Invest heavily in Costco at Daily Journal |
| **Thesis** | Exceptional business with strong culture and pricing power |
| **Result** | Costco became one of Munger's best investments ever |
| **Held for** | Decades |

**Why it mattered:** Munger called Costco "one of the greatest businesses I have ever seen." The patience paid off massively as Costco became one of the world's great companies.

---

### 3. Daily Journal — Patience on Cash (1984-2023)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Decision** | Hold massive cash at Daily Journal waiting for opportunities |
| **Context** | Most companies would deploy cash; Munger waited |
| **Result** | Avoided bad investments; waited for right opportunities |

**Why it mattered:** The discipline to do nothing — to sit on cash and wait — is Munger's signature. Most professionals can't handle the psychological pressure of not acting.

---

### 4. Wesco Financial — Building Before Selling (1974-2000)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Decision** | Run Wesco Financial as Munger's insurance/holding company |
| **Eventually** | Berkshire acquired Wesco completely in 2000 |
| **Result** | Full integration into Berkshire family |

**Why it mattered:** Wesco was Munger's vehicle for deploying his own investment ideas before fully merging with Berkshire.

---

### 5. No Bitcoin — The "Poison" Decision (Multiple years)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Decision** | Refused any cryptocurrency involvement |
| **Quote** | "It's a poison. I wouldn't take all the bitcoin in the world for $25." |
| **Consistency** | Maintained this view until his death |

**Why it mattered:** Showed Munger's commitment to fundamental value vs. speculative assets.

---

## Strategic Business Decisions

### 1. The Partnership with Buffett (1975-1978)

After running Munger Partnership (1962-1975) with exceptional returns, Munger dissolved it and sent investors to Buffett. This created the legendary Berkshire partnership.

---

### 2. Choosing Law Over Academia

Early career choice: Munger chose law practice and real estate development over academia. This gave him practical experience in business before turning to investing full-time.

---

### 3. Refusing to Leverage

Unlike many hedge fund operators, Munger rarely used significant leverage. Berkshire Hathaway under Munger/Buffett almost never borrowed.

---

## Notable Errors

| Error | What Happened | Lesson |
