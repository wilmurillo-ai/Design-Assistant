# Benjamin Graham - Complete Timeline

## Overview

Benjamin Graham (born May 8, 1894 - died September 21, 1976) was one of the most influential investors and educators in the history of finance.

---

## Early Life and Education

### 1894: Birth
- Born in London, England
- Family emigrated to New York when he was young
- Father died when Graham was young

### Early Education
- Graduated from Columbia University at age 19
- Majored in literature and philosophy
- Initially planned to pursue a career in writing or teaching

---

## Career Beginnings

### 1914-1920: Early Career
- Took a job on Wall Street after graduation
- Worked at various finance-related positions
- Served in the Army during World War I

### 1920s: Rising on Wall Street
- Became a research director for a New York brokerage firm
- Developed expertise in financial analysis
- Began writing articles on investment strategy

---

## Academic Career

### 1928: Columbia Business School
- Began teaching at Columbia Business School
- Developed course on security analysis
- Combined academic rigor with practical Wall Street experience

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Systematic approach to stock analysis
- Emphasis on intrinsic value vs. market price
- Risk management through margin of safety

---

## Major Works

### 1934: Security Analysis (First Edition)
- **Co-author:** David Dodd
- **Publisher:** McGraw-Hill
- **Significance:** Became the standard textbook for security analysis
- **Context:** Written after the 1929 market crash, during the Great Depression

**Evolution of the Book:**
- 1934: First Edition
- 1940: Second Edition (significantly revised)
- 1951: Third Edition
- 1962: Fourth Edition

The book evolved from detailed company analysis to a more systematic "group approach" in later editions.

---

### 1949: The Intelligent Investor
- **Publisher:** Harper & Brothers
- **Significance:** Became the definitive book on value investing
- **Influence:** Warren Buffett called it "the best book on investing ever written"

**Key Concepts Introduced:**
- Mr. Market allegory
- Defensive vs. Enterprising investor
- Systematic approach for defensive investors

---

## Investment Partnership

### 1936-1956: Graham's Partnership
- Managed money for friends, family, and colleagues
- Achieved average annual return of ~20%
- Beat the Dow Jones in almost every year
- Dissolved the partnership in 1956

---

## Notable Relationships

### Warren Buffett
- **First Meeting:** 1950 at Columbia Business School
- **Relationship:** Teacher-student, later business partners
- **Buffett's Tribute:** "The second most influential person in my life, after my father"

**Buffett's Learning:**
- Studied under Graham at Columbia
- Worked at Graham's partnership for two years
- Later adapted Graham's approach into his own style

---

## Later Years

### 1950s-1960s: Later Career
- Continued writing and teaching
- Served on boards of directors
- Advised institutional investors

### 1970s: Final Years
- Died September 21, 1976 in New York
- Left behind a legacy that shaped modern value investing

## Legacy

### Influence on Future Generations
- Warren Buffett (student and greatest follower)
- John Neff
- Peter Lynch
- Seth Klarman
- Glenn Greenberg
- Many others who adopted value investing

### Institutions
- Columbia Business School's value investing program
- CFA Institute's curriculum
- MBA programs worldwide

### Impact on Investment Industry
- Created the concept of value investing
- Established security analysis as a profession
- Influenced institutional investing practices
