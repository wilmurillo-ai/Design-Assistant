# Benjamin Graham - Primary Writings

## Overview

Benjamin Graham is the author of two foundational texts in value investing:
1. Security Analysis (1934, with David Dodd)
2. The Intelligent Investor (1949)

---

## 1. The Intelligent Investor (1949)

**Full Text Reference:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Intelligent_Investor

### Background
- First published in 1949 by Harper & Brothers
- Based on value investing approach Graham taught at Columbia Business School since 1928
- Warren Buffett read this book at age 20 and called it "the best book on investing ever written"

### Core Philosophy
The book is built on value investing principles:

**Key Concept: Mr. Market**
Graham introduced the allegory of "Mr. Market" - a fictional market participant who offers to buy or sell shares at varying prices. The key insight is that investors should treat Mr. Market's daily price quotations as a service rather than a guide. When prices are irrationally low, investors can buy; when prices are irrationally high, they can sell or hold. The rational investor ignores short-term market movements and focuses on fundamental values.

**Key Concept: Margin of Safety**
The margin of safety principle states that an investor should only purchase a security when its market price is significantly below its intrinsic value. This difference provides a cushion against losses if the investment doesn't perform as expected. Graham believed this concept was the cornerstone of prudent investing.

**Investment Approach: Defensive vs. Enterprising**
- **Defensive Investor**: Focuses on broad diversification through low-cost index funds and large-cap stocks with strong balance sheets
- **Enterprising Investor**: Willing to put more effort into analyzing individual securities to find undervalued opportunities

### Historical Significance
- Voted "the best book on investing ever written" by Financial Times
- Has been continuously in print since 1949
- Warren Buffett's primary influence for value investing

## 2. Security Analysis (1934)

**Full Text Reference:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Security_Analysis_(book)

### Background
- First published in 1934 with co-author David Dodd
- Written during the aftermath of the 1929 market crash
- Became the standard reference for security analysis on Wall Street

### Evolution of Approach
In later editions, Graham shifted from detailed company-by-company analysis toward a simpler "group approach":

> "To try to buy groups of stocks that meet some simple criterion for being undervalued -- regardless of the industry and with very little attention to the individual company... The results were very good for 50 years. They certainly did twice as well as the Dow Jones."

This represented a pragmatic evolution from pure fundamental analysis to systematic screening.

## 3. Other Writings

### "The Future of Financial Analysis" (1945)
- Presented at Columbia Business School
- Explored limitations of financial analysis during wartime

### "Some Thoughts on Defensive Investment" (1950s)
- Guidelines for the defensive investor
- Systematic approach to stock selection

### "The Intelligent Investor" Revisions
- Multiple editions published over decades
- Jason Zweig wrote commentary for later editions

## Key Quotes

> "The intelligent investor is one who focuses on the intrinsic value of a business rather than the daily movements of the stock market."

> "In the short run, the market is a voting machine. In the long run, it is a weighing machine."

> "The investor's chief problem -- and even his worst enemy -- is likely to be himself."

> "The Margin of Safety is always dependent on the price paid."
