# Peter Lynch - Timeline

## Overview

Peter Lynch is one of the most successful mutual fund managers in history. His journey from caddie to Wall Street legend spans decades.

---

## Early Life

### 1944: Birth
- Born January 19, 1944
- Boston, Massachusetts
- Grew up in a working-class family

### 1955-1960: Caddying
- Started caddying at age 11
- Met corporate executives on the golf course
- Learned about stocks from investors

---

## Education

### 1960s: College Years
- Attended Boston College
- Worked as caddie for scholarship
- Developed interest in investing

### Graduate Studies
- Served in Army (2 years)
- MBA from Wharton School of Finance
- University of Pennsylvania

---

## Career at Fidelity

### 1966: First Summer Job
- Summer student at Fidelity
- Market near 1,000 on Dow
- 75 applicants for 3 jobs

### 1969: Full-Time Position
- Joined Fidelity as research analyst
- Covered textiles, metals, retail
- Learned the business from ground up

### 1974: Director of Research
- Promoted to director of research
- Continued as analyst
- Built research methodology

---

## Magellan Fund (1977-1990)

### 1977: Takes Over Magellan
- Became manager of Magellan Fund
- Fund had $20 million in assets
- Only the 33 years old

### Early Years (1977-1982)
- One-third of shares redeemed
- Market did little
- Patiently built portfolio

### Growth Years (1982-1990)
- Market took off
- Magellan consistently outperformed
- Assets grew to $14 billion

### Key Statistics (13-Year Record)
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Annual Return | 29.2% |
| Assets Growth | $20M → $14B |
| Beat S&P 500 | 11 of 13 years |
| Stocks Owned | 1,000+ |

### 1990: Retirement
- Retired at age 46
- Full of capital
- Devoted time to philanthropy

---

## Post-Fidelity Career

### 1990s: Philanthropy
- Founded Lynch Foundation
- Teaching individual investors
- Speaking engagements

### 2000: First Book Published
- "One Up on Wall Street"
- Became bestseller
- Translated into multiple languages

### 1993: Second Book
- "Beating the Street"
- Practical investing guide

### 1995: Third Book
- "Learn to Earn"
- Investment basics for beginners

---
