# Peter Lynch - Conversations and Interviews

## Overview

Peter Lynch is known for his candid interviews and speeches that reveal his investment philosophy. His interviews span decades, from his early days at Fidelity to his recent appearances discussing lessons learned. In 2025, he made several rare appearances providing timeless wisdom.

---

## 1. The Compound and Friends Podcast (October 2025)

**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YIlurDigZcY

**Date:** October 2025

**Co-Host:** Josh Brown (Ritholtz Wealth Management)

**Key Quotes:**

### On AI Investments
> "I have zero AI stocks. I literally couldn't pronounce Nvidia until about eight months ago."

> "I'm the lowest tech guy ever. I can't do anything with computers. I just have yellow pads."

### On Knowing What You Own
> "If you don't understand what you own, you're toast."

> "If you can't explain to an 11-year-old in a minute or less why you own it—not 'this sucker is going up'—you should buy a fund."

### On Market Volatility
> "The average range for a stock on the New York Stock Exchange, the average high, average low every year, is 100%. There's a 100% move every year. Most stocks you're going to buy, they're probably going to go down."

### On "Playing the Market"
> "Somebody invented this awful term called play the market. Play the market is not what you do. You buy good companies and some work, and you have to know what they do."

### On Preparing for Corrections
> "Far more money has been lost by investors preparing for corrections or trying to anticipate corrections than has been lost in corrections themselves."

### On Economic Forecasting
> "If you spend more than 13 minutes analyzing economic and market forecasts, you've wasted 10 minutes."

### On Selling Winners
> "Selling your winners and holding your losers is like cutting the flowers and watering the weeds."

### On American Resilience
> "We had many opportunities to have a 'big one.' We've had some probably bad presidents, some bad congresses, we've had bad economists, and we've made it through. It's a pretty good system."

## 4. PBS Frontline Interview - "Betting on the Market"

**URL:** https://www.pbs.org/wgbh/pages/frontline/shows/betting/pros/lynch.html

**Date:** 1990s

### Key Quotes:

**On First Stock (Flying Tiger):**
> "I put a thousand dollars in it and I remember I thought this air cargo was going to be a thing of the future. And I bought it and it got really lucky because it went up for another reason. The Vietnam War started and they basically hauled a lot of troops to Vietnam in airplanes and the stock went up, I think, nine- or ten-fold and I had my first ten bagger."

**On Ten Baggers:**
> "You don't need a lot in your lifetime. You only need a few good stocks in your lifetime. I mean how many times do you need a stock to go up ten-fold to make a lot of money? Not a lot."

**On Letting Winners Run:**
> "The secret is if you have a lot of stocks, some will do mediocre, some will do okay, and if one of two of 'em go up big time, you produce a fabulous result."

**On Market Timing:**
> "In this business if you're good, you're right six times out of ten. You're never going to be right nine times out of ten."

**On Research:**
> "If you looked at ten companies, you'd find one that's interesting, if you'd look at 20, you'd find two, or if you look at hundred you'll find ten. The person that turns over the most rocks wins the game."

## 5. Fortune Interview (April 2023)

**URL:** https://fortune.com/2023/04/26/legendary-investor-peter-lynch-biggest-mistakes-best-advice/

**Date:** April 2023

### Key Quotes:

**On Missing Apple:**
> "Apple was not that hard to understand. I mean, how dumb was I? I should have done some work on Apple... it's not a complicated company."

**On the Story Approach:**
> "Look for thriving businesses at 'cut-rate prices,' and do your homework to understand the firms' operations fully before investing."

**On Investment Mistakes:**
> "Even the best investors make mistakes, and for Lynch, his most glaring errors are the opportunities that got away."

**On Recessions:**
> "We've had 13 recessions since World War II and 13 recoveries. I cannot predict the future, but this recession is so expected, so predicted, maybe it's coming, but I don't know."

## 6. Famous Speech: "The Person Who Turns Over the Most Rocks Wins"

### Core Message

> "If you looked at ten companies, you'd find one that's interesting, if you'd look at 20, you'd find two, or if you look at hundred you'll find ten. The person that turns over the most rocks wins the game."

### Key Themes

1. **Research is Key** - Lynch spent hours researching companies
2. **Small Companies Have Advantages** - Professionals ignore small companies
3. **Diversification Matters** - 10-30 stocks across categories

## 10. Moneyweb Interview (2026)

**URL:** https://www.moneyweb.co.za/in-depth/sasfin-wealth/peter-lynchs-simple-secret-why-behaviour-matters-more-than-brilliance-in-investing/

**Date:** January 2026

### Key Quotes:

**On Temperament:**
> "In the stock market, the most important organ is the stomach. It's not the brain."

**On Behavior:**
> "Successful investing is largely about behaviour, patience and temperament."

**On Understanding:**
> "If you can't explain to an 11-year-old, in under a minute, what a company does and why it's a good business, you probably shouldn't own it."

**On Fridge Research:**
> "Lynch often joked that many people do more research before buying a new fridge than they do before buying a share."

**On Barbara Streisand:**
> "She could name the stocks but admitted she had no idea what the businesses actually did. The problem wasn't the market. It was that she owned stocks, not businesses."

**On Flowers and Weeds:**
> "Selling your winners and holding your losers is like cutting your flowers and watering the weeds."

**On Compounding:**
> "His biggest successes often took three to 10 years to fully play out. That's the power of compounding – but it only works if you don't interrupt it."

**On Optimism:**
> "History shows that economies adapt and grow over time. Over 10, 30, or 100 years, equities have rewarded those who remained invested."

**On Success:**
> "His success wasn't about being the smartest person in the room; it was about being the most disciplined."

**On the Real Question:**
> "Are you managing your portfolio, or is your portfolio managing you?"
