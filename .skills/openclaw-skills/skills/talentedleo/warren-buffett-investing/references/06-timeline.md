## Era 1: Early Years (1930–1950)

### 1930: Birth

- **August 30, 1930:** Born in Omaha, Nebraska
- **Father:** Howard Buffett — later US Congressman, financial setbacks before political career
- **Mother:** Leila (Stahl) Buffett — described as demanding but warm
- **Sister:** Doris (older), also investor/businesswoman

### 1935: First Contact with Money

- Age 5: Sold chewing gum door-to-door
- Age 6: Delivered Omaha World-Herald newspapers
- Age 11: First stock purchase — Cities Service Preferred (lost money initially)
- **Key Lesson:** "I learned early that the stock market could be wrong."

### 1940: Howard Buffett Elected to Congress

- Family moves to Washington D.C.
- 10-year-old Warren reads every investment book in the library
- **Reads "The Intelligent Investor" (Graham, 1949 edition)**

### 1942: Returns to Omaha

- Father loses Congressional race
- Family returns to Omaha
- Warren returns to paper route
- Age 13: Earning more than his teachers

### 1945: First Business

- Age 14: Buys 40 acres of farmland in Nebraska
- Tenant farmer operates it; Buffett collects rent
- **First lesson in passive income**

### 1947: University of Virginia

- Enrolls at University of Virginia
- Fraternity life, not academically focused
- **Leaves after 2 years without graduating**

## Era 2: Partnership Years (1950–1969)

### 1950: Columbia Business School

- Enrolls at Columbia (MBA program)
- **Meets Benjamin Graham**
- Learns the "Intelligent Investor" philosophy
- **Meets Thomas Price Dreier** — future business partner

### 1951: First Investments

- Graham offers him a job at Graham-Newman partnership
- Buffett returns to Omaha, works in his father's brokerage
- Marries **Susan Thompson** (1952)

### 1954: Graham-Newman

- Works for Graham at Graham-Newman
- Learns arbitrage, event-driven investing
- Graham retires, Buffett returns to Omaha

### 1956: Buffett Partnership Founded

- **1956:** Forms Buffett Partnership Ltd. with $100,000 from 7 investors (including family)
- Initial capital: $100,000 (Buffett contributes $100)
- Goal: Beat the Dow by 10% annually

**Key Strategy:** Net-Net investing (buying companies below working capital)

### 1962: Berkshire Hathaway

- Begins buying Berkshire Hathaway stock (textile company)
- Partners begin questioning his concentration in one stock
- **Buffett becomes largest shareholder**

### 1964: The Pivotal Moment

- Negotiates to sell Berkshire shares at $11.50
- Partner reneges, offers $11.375
- Buffett is insulted
- **Buys the entire company instead**
- **Cost:** ~$14.86/share

### 1965: Berkshire Takeover

- Takes control of Berkshire Hathaway
- Fires management, installs himself
- Begins transformation from textile company to holding company

### 1969: Partnership Dissolution

- **June:** Dissolves the partnership
- Returns investors' capital (+ 26x return since 1957)
- **Partners who stayed with Berkshire (instead of cashing out) became billionaires**

## Era 3: Berkshire Transformation (1970–1989)

### 1970: First Letter as Berkshire CEO

- Begins annual shareholder letters
- **First articulation of "owner earnings"**
- Invests in See's Candies (first quality company at fair price)

### 1972: See's Candies Acquisition

- **Price:** $25 million
- **Key Insight:** Brand loyalty creates pricing power
- Still owned today; has generated billions in cash

### 1973: Washington Post Investment

- Buys stake in Washington Post
- **Katherine Graham (CEO):** "Buffett never asked for management changes."
- Still holds stake decades later

### 1974: "The Worst Year"

- Berkshire stock declines 48%
- Buffett's net worth drops significantly
- **Lesson:** "Stay in the game. Don't get wiped out."

### 1975: Nebraska Furniture Mart

- Acquires 90% of Nebraska Furniture Mart
- **B Furniture Mart** (Mrs. B): "You have enough capital to wait?"
- **Answer:** "Yes." The rest is history.

### 1976–1979: Geico Investment

- Learns about Geico's problems
- Meets **Jack Byrne** (Geico CEO)
- Buys 33% of Geico for $45M
- Geico becomes the model for Berkshire's insurance empire

### 1983: Scott & Fetzer

- Acquires Scott & Fetzer (diversified)
- Book value doubles
- **"You don't need to be the best investor. You need to be a good investor in a good business."

### 1985: First $1 Billion Year

- Berkshire first to earn $1 billion pretax
- Stock price: ~$2,800/share

### 1986: Coca-Cola Dividend

- Receives Coca-Cola dividend — first since 1920
- **Key Insight:** Companies that pay dividends consistently reinvested capital

### 1987: Market Crash

- Black Monday (October 19, 1987): Dow drops 22%
- **Buffett's Action:** Does nothing
- **Quote:** "The market is closed for a day, opens the next day, and you decide."

### 1988: Coca-Cola Purchase

- **Begins buying Coca-Cola:** $1.03 billion
- Most of personal net worth at the time
- **Decision:** Based on brand, management, pricing power

### 1989: Salomon Crisis

- Treasury auction scandal at Salomon
- Buffett appointed interim chairman
- Testifies before Congress
- **Lesson:** "Never let a rogue employee destroy 8,000 people's livelihoods."

## Era 4: Modern Era (1990–2010)

### 1991: American Express Partnership

- Strengthens American Express relationship
- Partnership with Japanese trading companies

### 1992: "$10 Billion in Cash"

- Berkshire accumulates $10B+ cash position
- **Strategy:** Waiting for right opportunity

### 1993: Dexter Shoe Disaster Begins

- Buys Dexter Shoe for $433M
- **Will become worst investment**
- US shoe manufacturing cannot compete with China

### 1996: Geico Full Acquisition

- Buys remaining Geico shares
- Geico becomes fully owned subsidiary
- **Insurance float becomes Berkshire's core advantage**

### 1998: Mid-Continent N.A. Bancorp

- Acquires General Re (reinsurance)
- Largest acquisition at the time
- Later proves problematic (derivative losses)

### 1999: Tech Bubble

- Berkshire stock underperforms significantly
- Critics claim Buffett is "out of touch"
- **Buffett:** "We don't understand tech. We're not buying."
- **Lesson:** He was right — bubble burst in 2000

### 2000: China Petrochemical Investment

- Invests in PetroChina
- **Earned:** $3.5B on $500M investment
- First major international success

### 2002: Derivatives Warnings

- Begins warning about derivatives in annual letters
- **1998 quote:** "We view derivatives as time bombs."
- **2002 quote:** "Financial weapons of mass destruction."

### 2003: "$36 Billion in Cash"

- Berkshire holds $36B in cash
- Critics claim Buffett is too conservative
- **Waiting for right opportunity**

### 2006: Philanthropy Pledge

- **June 26, 2006:** Pledges 99% of wealth to Gates Foundation
- Transfer 10 Berkshire shares annually to Gates Foundation
- Family receives ~$3B

### 2007: "$200 Billion Company"

- Berkshire becomes $200B company
- **Succession Plan:** First public acknowledgment
- Names **David Sokol** (later replaced)

### 2008: Financial Crisis

- **Lehman collapse (September 2008)**
- Buffett becomes "buyer of last resort"
- **$8.3 billion deployed in 2 months**
- Op-ed in NYT: "Buy American. I Am."

### 2010: Burlington Northern

- Acquires Burlington Northern Santa Fe (BNSF)
- **$44B** — largest acquisition to date
- Railroads: consistent capital returns

## Era 5: Recent Years (2011–Present)

### 2011: Bank of America Investment

- Buys $5B of preferred Bank of America shares
- **Warrants:** 700M shares at $7.14
- Later worth billions

### 2012: Succession Revelation

- First publicly states succession is planned
- Names **Ted Weschler** and **Todd Combs** as potential successors

### 2013: Precision Castparts

- Acquires Precision Castparts Corp for $37B
- Aerospace components manufacturer
- **2020 write-off:** $11B loss

### 2014: "Buffett's $1 Million Bet"

- Warren bets $1M with Protégé Partners
- Long S&P index fund vs. hedge fund-of-funds
- **Index fund wins decisively by 2017**

### 2015: 50 Years at Berkshire

- Anniversary of Buffett taking control
- **$1M → $130B** (130,000x appreciation)

### 2016: Apple Investment Begins

- **First purchase:** 9.8M shares (~$1.1B)
- Continues accumulating through 2018
- Eventually ~5% of Berkshire

### 2017: Tax Cuts Windfall

- Corporate tax cuts = $29B benefit to Berkshire
- **Buffett:** "The tax cut helps America, not just Berkshire."

### 2018: Technology Shift

- Hires two tech-focused investment managers
- **Acknowledgment:** "We should have bought Apple earlier."

### 2019: Cash Hoard Record

- **$128B in cash and equivalents**
- Critics: "He's lost his touch"
- **Buffett:** "We'd rather wait for the big one."

### 2020: COVID Crisis

- Stock portfolio drops 30% in March
- **April:** "Don't bet against America."
- Sells airline stocks (later criticized as selling at bottom)

### 2021: Munger's Health

- Charlie Munger hospitalized
- Munger eventually recovers
- Partnership intact

### 2022: Munger Dies

- **November 28, 2023:** Charlie Munger dies at 99
- **Buffett (2023 Letter):** "Charlie was the architect. I was the contractor."

### 2023: Succession Finalized

- **February 2024 Letter:** Greg Abel named successor
- **Abel:** Currently runs all non-insurance operations
- **Cash Position:** $167B+ (highest ever)

### 2024: New Leaders

- Buffett turns 94 (August 30)
- Ajit Jain (insurance) and Greg Abel (non-insurance) confirmed as key leaders
- **$334B in cash** (highest in Berkshire history)

### 2025: Transition Year

- February: 2025 Letter — announces transition plan
- May: **Last regular annual meeting** with Buffett as CEO
- **Greg Abel confirmed as CEO before year-end 2025**

## Key Life Principles (from Timeline)

1. **Start Early** — Invested at 11, read all investment books by 12
2. **Find Great Partners** — Graham (teacher), Munger (intellectual foil), Ajit (insurance mind)
3. **Own Good Businesses** — Shifted from "cigar butts" to quality
4. **Patience is the Asset** — Cash and patience together
5. **Never Wipe Out** — "Rule No. 1: Don't lose money"
6. **Compound for Decades** — 20% for 60 years = unprecedented wealth
7. **Own Your Mistakes** — Dexter, Airlines, Tesco — he named them publicly
