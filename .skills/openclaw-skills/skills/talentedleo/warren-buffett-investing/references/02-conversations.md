## 1. Berkshire Hathaway Annual Shareholder Meetings (1965–Present)

**Source:** [buffett.com/meetings](https://www.buffett.com)

Held annually on the first Saturday of May in Omaha, Nebraska. Known as "Woodstock of Capitalism." Buffett and Munger answer shareholder questions for 5-6 hours.

### Key Meeting Moments

#### 2008 Annual Meeting (Post-Crisis)

**Context:** Lehman collapsed in September 2008. Markets were in panic.

**Buffett's Position:** Berkshire had $37 billion in cash. He was a buyer.

**Key Quotes:**
> "We will never borrow money for investment purposes. We simply wait for opportunities that are clearly唾手可得."

> "The financial crisis that we're in is the byproduct of excesses in the mortgage market... What we learned from this is that we will never hold very large positions in any financial company that has significant leverage."

**Outcome:** Used crisis to invest in Goldman Sachs ($5B preferred stock, 10% coupon), GE ($3B), Swiss Re.

---

#### 2016 Annual Meeting

**On Apple Investment:**
> "I did not think about Apple as an technology stock. I thought about it as a consumer product company... The iPhone has a ecosystem around it that is extremely powerful."

**On Berkshire Culture:**
> "Our culture is very important. We don't want people here that are brilliant. We want people who are right and who are decent."

---

#### 2020 Annual Meeting (COVID-era, Virtual)

**On Market Volatility:**
> "The daily stock market moves are meaningless to long-term investors. You should be looking at what businesses will be worth in 10 or 20 years."

**On His Own Mortality:**
> "I feel good about Berkshire's future, but my own clock is ticking."

**On Why He Still Works:**
> "I tap dance to work every morning... I love what I do. I would do it even if I weren't being paid."

---

#### 2024 Annual Meeting

**On Succession:**
> "Greg Abel is ready. He has been running our non-insurance operations for 25 years. If I were hit by a truck tomorrow, the answer would be Greg."

**On Cash Position ($167B):**
> "We need to be prepared for the unexpected... We're not going to be forced into making a bad decision because we have to act immediately."

**On Japan Investments:**
> "We like the Japanese companies. They remind me of Berkshire 50 years ago... They have a culture of returning cash to shareholders."

## 4. Florida University Speech (1998) — FULL TRANSCRIPT

**Source:** [berkshirehathaway.com/lectures](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/lectures)

October 15, 1998, University of Florida College of Business Administration.

**Title Given by Buffett:** "The Superinvestors of Graham-and-Doddsville"

**Summary:** Buffett argues that value investing works because of human behavior, not market mechanics. He presents the track records of several Graham-and-Dodd followers who all outperformed.

**Core Arguments:**

1. **Price vs. Value:** "The market is a device for transferring money from the active to the patient."

2. **Intrinsic Value:** "You can determine the intrinsic value of any business by projecting the cash flows it will generate and discounting them at an appropriate rate."

3. **Margin of Safety:** "When you build a bridge, you insist it can carry 30,000 pounds, but you only drive 10,000-pound trucks across it. That's the same principle in investing."

4. **Behavior is Key:** "The difference between愚弄者 and investors is that investors know when they're愚弄ing themselves."

**Key Quotes from the Speech:**
> "You don't need to be an expert in every company or every industry. You only need to be able to recognize a few situations where you have an edge."

> "The most important investment you can make is in yourself."

> "Your investor is most likely to be me. What you want is a company where the truth is as you expect it to be."

## 8. Charlie Munger Partnership Conversations

Buffett and Munger have been partners for 60+ years. Munger is the intellectual foil who pushed Buffett from "cigar butt" investing to quality.

**Key Munger Contributions to Buffett's Thinking:**

1. **On why quality matters:** "A great business at a fair price is better than a fair business at a great price."

2. **On waiting:** "The correct mental process is to sit on a decision for 24 hours before acting."

3. **On psychological errors:** "The first rule of investments is don't lose money. The second rule is don't forget rule one."

## 11. 2025 Annual Meeting — Buffett's Final Meeting

**Date:** May 3–4, 2025, Omaha, Nebraska

### Key Announcements

#### 1. Succession — Greg Abel Takes Over

> "I'm going to propose to the board that Greg Abel take over as CEO before the end of this year. This is not a surprise — Greg has been running our non-insurance operations for years. He's ready."

> "I've been planning this transition for decades. It's not something we decided last year."

> "I'll still be around. I'll still be thinking. But the final decision — that's Greg's."

#### 2. On Trade and Tariffs

> "Trade should not be a weapon. Using trade as a weapon damages both sides. History shows us that free trade benefits everyone."

> "I disagree strongly with using tariffs as a policy tool. They raise prices for American consumers and invite retaliation."

#### 3. On Cash Position ($334+ Billion)

> "We have the largest cash position in Berkshire history. People ask why we don't deploy it. I tell them — we're waiting for the right opportunity. We'd rather wait than make a bad decision."

> "The cash is not earning much right now. That's fine. When the right opportunity comes, we'll move fast."

#### 4. On American Economy

> "The American economy has done things no other economy has done. We have our problems — fiscal issues, political dysfunction — but the dynamism is still there."

> "I would bet on America over any other country. I have been doing that since 1930."

#### 5. On Investment Philosophy (Final Meeting)

> "The principles that worked 60 years ago still work today. Buy good businesses at fair prices. Hold them for a long time. Don't use leverage."

> "The biggest mistake is trying to be clever. The simple approach works."

#### 6. On Giving Advice to Young People

> "Find better people than yourself and work with them. That's what I did with Charlie Munger."

> "Read. Read. Read. I read 500 pages a day. It compounds."

## 12. 2024–2025: Major Portfolio Exits

### BYD — Complete Exit (2008–2024)

**Original Investment:** 225 million BYD shares, purchased September 2008 at HK$8.00/share (~$1.00)

**Hold Period:** 14 years without changing position

**Exit Pattern (2022–2024):**
- August 2022: First sale — 1.33 million shares (0.6% of holding)
- June 2023: Stakes down from 20.5% → 10%
- July 2024: Stakes below 5% disclosure threshold
- Final exit: Fully exited by 2024

**Total Gain:** Estimated ~$4–5 billion profit (cost basis ~$225M, final value ~$5B+)

**Buffett's Comment:**
> "Sometimes you hold something for 14 years and then it becomes time to move on. I'm not going to explain every decision."

---

### Apple — Major Reduction (2024)

**Context:** Berkshire reduced Apple position significantly in 2024

**Timeline:**
- End of 2023: 905 million shares ($174B, 49% of portfolio)
- Q1 2024: Began reducing
- Q2 2024: Cut roughly 50%, to ~400 million shares
- End of 2024: ~300 million shares ($60B, 23% of portfolio)

**Why:**
- P/E ratio above 30x — high by historical standards
- China dependency (supply chain + sales) growing risk
- Tax planning ahead of potential capital gains tax increases

**Buffett (2024 Meeting):**
> "Apple may well be the best business I ever helped evaluate. But that doesn't mean we should hold it at any price."

## 13. Key 2025 Letter to Shareholders (Final Letter)

**Published:** February 22, 2025

### Notable Quotes

**On Berkshire's 60-Year Journey:**
> "The story of Berkshire is really the story of a mistake that turned into something extraordinary. I bought Berkshire because I was too stubborn to accept a lower price. The textile business was a mistake. But we turned it into something no one predicted."

**On Mistakes and Correction:**
> "Tom Murphy told me 60 years ago: 'If you have to say something critical about someone, be vague. If you have to praise someone, be specific.' I've been following that rule ever since."

**On Future:**
> "This will be my last regular letter. Greg will write the next ones. I leave you in good hands."

**On What Made Berkshire Work:**
> "It's very simple. We found people who were honest and competent. We let them run things. We didn't interfere. And we held them to high standards."
