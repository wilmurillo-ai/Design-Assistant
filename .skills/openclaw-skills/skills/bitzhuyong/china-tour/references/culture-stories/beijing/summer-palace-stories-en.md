## 🏛️ Renshou Hall

### L1 Basic
> "Renshou Hall was where Empress Dowager Cixi handled state affairs, like her 'office'. The hall is lavishly decorated, showing royal grandeur."

### L2 Standard
> "Renshou Hall was the political center of Summer Palace. Cixi handled court affairs and received ministers here. The name comes from Confucian 'The benevolent live long.'
>
> In the center is Cixi's throne, with 'Great Round Mirror' plaque above. Bronze dragon and phoenix in front symbolize imperial power, but phoenix before dragon suggests Cixi's rule."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Construction History**:
> Originally named Qinzheng Hall, built during Qianlong's reign. Destroyed in 1860, rebuilt by Cixi in 1886, renamed Renshou Hall.
>
> "**Political Center**:
> Cixi lived in Summer Palace April-October yearly, handling state affairs here. Important decisions like Hundred Days' Reform failure and declaration of war against Eight-Nation Alliance happened here.
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Phoenix before dragon: Suggests Cixi's rule
> - Heating: Underfloor heating system
> - Throne material: Rosewood with gems"

## 🏛️ Long Corridor

### L1 Basic
> "The Long Corridor is the world's longest gallery, 728 meters long, with over 14,000 painted decorations including landscapes, flowers, birds, and stories."

### L2 Standard
> "The Long Corridor extends from Yaoyue Gate to Shizhang Pavilion, 728 meters long with 273 sections. It's the longest veranda in Chinese gardens.
>
> Over 14,000 painted decorations include landscapes, flowers, birds, and character stories. In 1990, it was listed in Guinness World Records as 'world's longest gallery.'
>
> The corridor is not just a passage, but also the best viewing position for Kunming Lake."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Construction History**:
> Built during Qianlong's reign, destroyed in 1860. Rebuilt by Cixi in 1886, 28 meters longer than original.
>
> "**Paintings**:
> 14,000+ paintings divided into three categories:
> 1. Landscapes: West Lake scenes, famous sites
> 2. Flowers and birds: Plum, orchid, bamboo, chrysanthemum
> 3. Stories: Romance of Three Kingdoms, Water Margin, Journey to the West
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Length: 728 meters, 273 sections
> - Paintings: 14,000+
> - Restoration: 2-3 years each time
> - Painters: 50+ masters needed"

## 🏛️ Tower of Buddhist Incense

### L1 Basic
> "The Tower of Buddhist Incense is the landmark of Summer Palace, 41 meters high, octagonal, three stories with four eaves. Houses Thousand-Hand Guanyin."

### L2 Standard
> "Built on Longevity Hill, 41 meters high, octagonal, three stories with four eaves. It's the highest point and landmark of Summer Palace.
>
> Inside houses a bronze Thousand-Hand Guanyin statue, 5 meters tall, weighing 5 tons. The name comes from Buddhism, meaning 'Buddha's fragrance shines everywhere.'
>
> Standing on the tower, you can overlook entire Kunming Lake and Beijing, excellent view."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Construction History**:
> Original site was Great Temple of Gratitude built by Qianlong for his mother's birthday. Destroyed in 1860. Rebuilt by Cixi in 1886, renamed Tower of Buddhist Incense.
>
> "**Architecture**:
> 41 meters high, octagonal, three stories, supported by 8 large pillars. Internal staircase to top, 114 steps total.
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Height: 41 meters, about 13 floors
> - Steps: 114
> - View: Can see 30km away
> - Restoration cost: 2 million yuan in 1980s"

## 🏛️ Seventeen-Arch Bridge

### L1 Basic
> "Seventeen-Arch Bridge is the longest stone arch bridge in Summer Palace, 150 meters long, with 17 arches. Has 544 stone lions on railings."

### L2 Standard
> "East from South Lake Island, west to East Embankment, 150 meters long, 8 meters wide. It's the longest stone arch bridge in Summer Palace.
>
> 17 arches, during winter solstice sunlight can pass through all arches, creating 'Golden Light Through Arches' spectacle. 544 stone lions on railings, each with different posture.
>
> Modeled after Lugou Bridge, but longer and more exquisite."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Construction History**:
> Built during Qianlong's reign (1750), for his mother's birthday. Named for 17 arches.
>
> "**Architecture**:
> 150m long, 8m wide, 17 arches. Largest arch spans 10m, smallest 3m. Bridge surface paved with white marble.
>
> "**Stone Lions**:
> 544 lions on railings, more than Lugou Bridge. Each lion has different posture: playing, holding ball, watching moon.
>
> "**Golden Light Spectacle**:
> During winter solstice, sunset passes through all 17 arches. Perfect combination of optics and architecture.
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Lions: 544
> - Arches: 17
> - Best photo: Winter solstice 3-4 PM
> - Restoration: Major restoration in 1990s"

## 🏛️ Marble Boat

### L1 Basic
> "Marble Boat is Western-style stone boat, 36 meters long. Built with marble, unique landscape in Summer Palace."

### L2 Standard
> "Originally Qingyan Boat, built during Qianlong's reign, 36 meters long, built with marble. Hull is stone, but upper structure is wooden.
>
> Symbolizes 'Eternal empire, calm seas.' Destroyed in 1860, rebuilt in 1893 in Western style.
>
> Only Western-style building in Summer Palace, reflecting Western influence at that time."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Construction History**:
> Built in 1755, originally Qingyan Boat. Destroyed in 1860. Rebuilt by Cixi in 1893, changed to Western style with glass windows and Western railings.
>
> "**Symbolism**:
> Symbolizes 'Eternal empire.' Marble hull suggests stability. Ironically, Qing dynasty was not stable.
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Length: 36 meters
> - Weight: About 1000 tons
> - Glass windows: Imported from Germany
> - Restoration cost: 500,000 yuan in 1980s"
