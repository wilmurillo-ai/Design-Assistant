## 🏛️ Pit 1

### L1 Basic
> "Pit 1 is the largest of the Terracotta Army pits, 230 meters long, 62 meters wide, with over 14,000 terracotta warriors. This is the right army of Qin forces, mainly infantry."

### L2 Standard
> "Pit 1 was discovered in 1974, the largest of the three pits, covering 14,260 square meters. It contains over 14,000 terracotta warriors and 40 chariots, forming a rectangular military formation.
>
> The formation consists of vanguard, main body, wings, and rear guard. The vanguard consists of 210 lightly armed infantry with bows and crossbows. The main body consists of heavily armed infantry with long weapons. The wings are guard troops.
>
> Each warrior is 1.8-2.0 meters tall, with different facial features, made based on real Qin soldiers."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Discovery History**:
> Discovered in March 1974 by villagers digging a well. Initially found terracotta fragments and bronze arrowheads. Archaeological team excavated, shocking the world.
>
> "**Military Formation**:
> Pit 1 is the right army of Qin forces, mainly infantry. Divided into:
> - Vanguard: 3 columns, 210 men, with bows
> - Main body: 38 columns, with long weapons
> - Wings: Guard troops, preventing surprise attacks
> - Rear guard: Preventing pursuit
>
> "**Craftsmanship**:
> Warriors made using molds and hand-sculpting. Bodies made with molds, faces hand-carved. Fired at 950-1050 degrees. Originally had colored paint, oxidized after excavation.
>
> "**Historical Value**:
> - Shows Qin army organization and equipment
> - Reflects Qin Dynasty sculpture art level
> - Provides physical data for Qin history research
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Restoration difficulty: Several months per warrior
> - Restored: 1,000+, still 5,000+ fragments
> - Height secret: 1.8-2.0 meters, real Qin soldier height
> - Facial features: Each warrior is different, real models"

## 🏛️ Pit 2

### L1 Basic
> "Pit 2 covers 6,000 square meters, with over 1,300 warriors, 89 chariots. This is a multi-branch mixed formation, including infantry, cavalry, chariots, and archers."

### L2 Standard
> "Pit 2 is located northeast of Pit 1, covering 6,000 square meters, with over 1,300 warriors, 89 chariots, and 400 horses.
>
> Pit 2 is a multi-branch mixed formation, divided into four units:
> 1. Archer formation
> 2. Chariot formation
> 3. Cavalry formation
> 4. Infantry formation
>
> The most famous kneeling archer was found in Pit 2, one of the best-preserved warriors."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Formation Features**:
> Pit 2 is a multi-branch mixed formation, showing Qin army's coordinated combat capability. Four units can fight independently or cooperate.
>
> "**Kneeling Archer**:
> The kneeling archer is the star exhibit of Pit 2. Height 1.2 meters, kneeling posture, right hand holding bow, left hand holding arrow. Armor, shoe soles and other details are clearly visible.
>
> "**Cavalry Formation**:
> Cavalry formation has 116 cavalry warriors and 116 horse warriors. Cavalry holds short weapons, horses equipped simply. This is the earliest physical data of Chinese cavalry.
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Kneeling archer: Best preserved, originally colored
> - Cavalry warriors: Hold short swords, horses are short
> - Chariots: Wood decayed, only bronze parts remain
> - Discovery time: 1976, 2 years after Pit 1"

## 🏛️ Pit 3

### L1 Basic
> "Pit 3 is the smallest, only 520 square meters, with 68 warriors. This is the army's command headquarters, the highest level."

### L2 Standard
> "Pit 3 is located northwest of Pit 1, covering 520 square meters, with 68 warriors and 1 chariot.
>
> Pit 3 is the army's command headquarters, all warriors are high-ranking general warriors. They wear armor, wear headdresses, hold long swords, with majestic expressions.
>
> The headquarters has a chariot and guards in front, and a conference room behind, a complete command system."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Headquarters Layout**:
> Pit 3 is 'concave' shaped, divided into main hall, chariot room, and side rooms. Main hall is where generals work, chariot room parks chariots, side rooms are conference rooms.
>
> "**General Warriors**:
> General warriors are the highest level warriors. Height 1.9-2.0 meters, wear double-layer armor, wear headdresses (double-curl tail), hold long swords. Majestic expression, slightly protruding belly, showing high status.
>
> "**Historical Value**:
> Pit 3's discovery proves Qin army had a complete command system. General warriors' clothing and equipment provide physical data for studying Qin military rank system.
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Smallest area: Only 520 square meters
> - Fewest warriors: Only 68
> - Highest level: All general warriors
> - Discovery time: 1976, discovered with Pit 2"

## 🏛️ Bronze Chariots and Horses

### L1 Basic
> "Bronze chariots and horses were Emperor Qinshihuang's imperial carriages, excavated west of the mausoleum. There are two carriages, each composed of over 3,000 bronze parts, known as 'Crown of Bronze'."

### L2 Standard
> "Bronze chariots and horses were excavated in 1980, 20 meters west of the mausoleum mound. There are two carriages: No.1 is standing carriage (high carriage), No.2 is sitting carriage (warm carriage).
>
> Each carriage consists of over 3,000 bronze parts, using casting, welding, inlaying, painting and other crafts. Horses, drivers, carriage covers and other details are lifelike.
>
> Bronze carriages are made at 1/2 scale of real carriages, showing the highest level of Qin bronze casting."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Discovery History**:
> Discovered in 1980 by archaeological team west of the mausoleum. Broken into over 3,000 pieces when excavated, took 8 years to restore.
>
> "**No.1 Carriage (Standing)**:
> No.1 is standing carriage, also called high carriage. Length 2.25 meters, height 1.52 meters. Driver stands driving, holding reins and weapons. This is the pilot car when Emperor Qinshihuang traveled.
>
> "**No.2 Carriage (Sitting)**:
> No.2 is sitting carriage, also called warm carriage. Length 3.17 meters, height 1.06 meters. Driver sits driving, enclosed carriage with windows. This is Emperor Qinshihuang's carriage.
>
> "**Craft Technology**:
> Bronze carriages used multiple crafts:
> - Casting: Horses, carriage bodies and other large parts
> - Welding: Part connection
> - Inlaying: Gold and silver decoration
> - Painting: Originally had colored paint
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Number of parts: Over 3,000
> - Restoration time: 8 years
> - Scale: 1/2 of real carriage
> - Weight: No.1 1,041 kg, No.2 1,241 kg"

## 🐉 Emperor Qinshihuang and Terracotta Army

### L1 Basic
> "Emperor Qinshihuang was China's first emperor, unified six states, established Qin Dynasty. Terracotta Army is his mausoleum burial object, purpose is to continue protecting him after death."

### L2 Standard
> "Emperor Qinshihuang (259-210 BC), surname Ying, name Zheng, was China's first emperor. He unified six states, established Qin Dynasty, unified writing, currency, weights and measures, built Great Wall.
>
> Emperor Qinshihuang ascended throne at 13, began building mausoleum. Mausoleum is located at north foot of Lishan Mountain, took 39 years to complete. Terracotta Army is mausoleum's burial pits, purpose is to continue protecting Emperor Qinshihuang after death.
>
> Terracotta Army's discovery provides precious data for studying Qin history, military, and art."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Emperor Qinshihuang's Life**:
> - 259 BC: Born in Handan, Zhao State
> - 246 BC: Ascended throne as King of Qin at 13
> - 221 BC: Unified six states, became First Emperor
> - 210 BC: Died during eastern tour, aged 49
>
> "**Historical Achievements**:
> 1. Unified six states, established China's first centralized country
> 2. Unified writing (small seal script), currency (round square hole coin), weights and measures
> 3. Built Great Wall, defending against Xiongnu
> 4. Built imperial roads, facilitating transportation
> 5. Dug Ling Canal, connecting Yangtze and Pearl River
>
> "**Mausoleum Construction**:
> - Start time: 246 BC (ascended throne at 13)
> - Completion time: 208 BC (took 39 years)
> - Workers: Over 700,000
> - Mausoleum area: 56.25 square kilometers
>
> "**Unsolved Mysteries**:
> 1. Was the underground palace stolen?
> 2. Are there mercury rivers?
> 3. Are there traps and weapons?
> 4. Is Emperor Qinshihuang's body intact?
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Terracotta Army is just the tip of the iceberg
> - Main mausoleum not excavated yet
> - Over 180 burial pits
> - Listed as World Heritage in 1987"
