## 🏞️ Yuanjiajie

### L1 Basic
> "Yuanjiajie is Zhangjiajie core scenic area, named because Yuan family lived here. This is movie 'Avatar' filming location, Qiankun Pillar is landmark scenic spot."

### L2 Standard
> "Yuanjiajie is located at Zhangjiajie National Forest Park north, altitude 1,000+ meters. Named because Ming Dynasty Yuan family lived here.
>
> Yuanjiajie is Zhangjiajie most essence scenic area, has 'Avatar filming location' title. Qiankun Pillar 150 meters high, diameter 10 meters, is Yuanjiajie landmark scenic spot.
>
> 2010, movie 'Avatar' filmed here, 'Hallelujah Mountain' prototype is Qiankun Pillar."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Name Origin**:
> Yuanjiajie named because Ming Dynasty Yuan family lived here. Yuan ancestors to avoid war, hid here, multiplied descendants.
>
> "**Geological Features**:
> Yuanjiajie is quartz sandstone peak forest landform, formed 380 million years ago. At that time this was ocean, later crust uplift, seawater retreated, quartz sandstone dissolved by water, formed stone pillars.
>
> "**Qiankun Pillar**:
> Qiankun Pillar 150 meters high, diameter 10 meters, vertical stands. Pillar body red-brown, is iron oxide dyed.
>
> 2010, movie 'Avatar' filmed here, 'Hallelujah Mountain' prototype is Qiankun Pillar. After Qiankun Pillar renamed 'Avatar Mountain'.
>
> "**Avatar Filming**:
> 2008, movie 'Avatar' crew came to Zhangjiajie film. Director Cameron was shocked by Zhangjiajie peak forest, used Qiankun Pillar as 'Hallelujah Mountain' prototype.
>
> 2010 January 25, Zhangjiajie city government officially renamed Qiankun Pillar 'Avatar Mountain'.
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Altitude: 1,000+ meters
> - Qiankun Pillar height: 150 meters
> - Formation time: 380 million years ago
> - Avatar filming: 2008"

## 🏞️ Tianzi Mountain

### L1 Basic
> "Tianzi Mountain is Zhangjiajie highest peak, altitude 1,262 meters. Named because Ming Dynasty Tujia leader Xiang Dakun called himself 'Tianzi'."

### L2 Standard
> "Tianzi Mountain is located at Zhangjiajie National Forest Park north, altitude 1,262 meters, is Zhangjiajie highest peak.
>
> Tianzi Mountain named because Ming Dynasty Tujia leader Xiang Dakun called himself 'Tianzi'. Xiang Dakun here rebelled against Ming, failed sacrificed.
>
> Tianzi Mountain main scenic spots has Helong Park, Yubi Peak, Fairy Scattering Flowers etc."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Name Origin**:
> Tianzi Mountain named because Ming Dynasty Hongwu period (1368-1398) Tujia leader Xiang Dakun called himself 'Tianzi'.
>
> Xiang Dakun here rebelled against Ming, established regime, called himself 'Xiang Wang Tianzi'. Later army defeated sacrificed, later generations to commemorate him, named this mountain 'Tianzi Mountain'.
>
> "**Helong Park**:
> Helong Park is Tianzi Mountain main scenic spot, to commemorate Marshal Helong built. Helong is Zhangjiajie Sangzhi County person, Chinese People's Liberation Army founder one of.
>
> Park has Helong bronze statue, 6.5 meters high, 9 tons heavy.
>
> "**Yubi Peak**:
> Yubi Peak is Tianzi Mountain landmark scenic spot, composed by three stone pillars, shape like imperial brush. Legend Xiang Wang Tianzi here reviewed memorials.
>
> "**Fairy Scattering Flowers**:
> Fairy Scattering Flowers is Tianzi Mountain famous scenic spot, stone peak shape like fairy holding flower basket, scattering flowers.
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Altitude: 1,262 meters
> - Name origin: Xiang Dakun called himself 'Tianzi'
> - Helong statue: 6.5 meters high, 9 tons
> - Yubi Peak: Three stone pillars"

## 🏞️ Yangjiajie

### L1 Basic
> "Yangjiajie named because Northern Song Yang General descendants lived here. Main scenic spots has Natural Great Wall, Wulong Village etc."

### L2 Standard
> "Yangjiajie is located at Zhangjiajie National Forest Park northwest, altitude 1,000+ meters. Named because Northern Song Yang General descendants Yang Zaixing lived here.
>
> Yangjiajie main scenic spots has Natural Great Wall, Wulong Village, Tianbo Mansion etc. Natural Great Wall is quartz sandstone peak forest, shape like Great Wall.
>
> Wulong Village is bandit den, terrain dangerous, easy defend difficult attack."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Name Origin**:
> Yangjiajie named because Northern Song Yang General descendants Yang Zaixing lived here. Yang Zaixing is Yang General descendant, to avoid war, hid here.
>
> "**Natural Great Wall**:
> Natural Great Wall is Yangjiajie landmark scenic spot, composed by multiple stone pillars, shape like Great Wall. Stone pillars 100-200 meters high, vertical stand.
>
> Natural Great Wall formed 380 million years ago, is quartz sandstone peak forest landform.
>
> "**Wulong Village**:
> Wulong Village is Yangjiajie famous scenic spot, once was bandit den. Terrain dangerous, easy defend difficult attack.
>
> Wulong Village has 'Three Ghost Passes':
> 1. One Step Difficult: Narrow stone crack
> 2. Who Dare Not Bow Head: Bow head to pass
> 3. One Line Sky: Only allow one person pass
>
> "**Tianbo Mansion**:
> Tianbo Mansion is Yang General mansion site, legend Yang Zaixing here lived. Existing site and monument.
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Name origin: Yang General descendants
> - Natural Great Wall: Stone pillars 100-200 meters
> - Wulong Village: Bandit den
> - Formation time: 380 million years ago"

## 🏞️ Golden Whip Stream

### L1 Basic
> "Golden Whip Stream is Zhangjiajie longest stream, total length 7.5 kilometers. Named because stream side has Golden Whip Rock."

### L2 Standard
> "Golden Whip Stream is located at Zhangjiajie National Forest Park south, total length 7.5 kilometers, is Zhangjiajie longest stream.
>
> Golden Whip Stream named because stream side has Golden Whip Rock. Golden Whip Rock 380 meters high, shape like golden whip.
>
> Golden Whip Stream is hiking route, along way has Guanyin Sending Son, Pigsy Carrying Wife etc scenic spots."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Name Origin**:
> Golden Whip Stream named because stream side has Golden Whip Rock. Golden Whip Rock 380 meters high, shape like golden whip, legend is Qin Shi Huang mountain-driving whip.
>
> "**Geological Features**:
> Golden Whip Stream is canyon landform, two sides is quartz sandstone peak forest. Stream water clear, negative oxygen ion content high, is natural oxygen bar.
>
> "**Hiking Route**:
> Golden Whip Stream hiking route total length 7.5 kilometers, walk about 2-3 hours. Along way has:
> 1. Guanyin Sending Son: Stone peak shape like Guanyin holding son
> 2. Pigsy Carrying Wife: Stone peak shape like Pigsy
> 3. Golden Whip Rock: 380 meters high, shape like golden whip
> 4. Thousand Miles Meeting: Two stone pillars opposite
>
> "**Wildlife**:
> Golden Whip Stream wildlife rich:
> - Plants: Dove tree/Ginkgo/Red bean fir
> - Animals: Macaque/Giant salamander/Pangolin
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Total length: 7.5 kilometers
> - Hiking time: 2-3 hours
> - Golden Whip Rock height: 380 meters
> - Negative oxygen ions: 100,000 per cubic centimeter"

## 🏔️ Zhangjiajie History Overview

### L1 Basic
> "Zhangjiajie 1982 became China's first National Forest Park, 1992 listed as World Natural Heritage."

### L2 Standard
> "Zhangjiajie is located at Hunan Province northwest, area 9,800 square kilometers. Zhangjiajie landform is quartz sandstone peak forest landform, world rare.
>
> 1982 September 25, Zhangjiajie became China's first National Forest Park. 1992 December, listed as World Natural Heritage.
>
> 2010, movie 'Avatar' filmed here, Zhangjiajie famous worldwide."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Geological History**:
> - 380 million years ago: This was ocean
> - 200 million years ago: Crust uplift, seawater retreated
> - 100 million years ago: Quartz sandstone dissolved by water, formed peak forest
> - Modern: Zhangjiajie landform formed
>
> "**Development History**:
> - Ming Dynasty: Tujia people lived
> - 1958: Discovered Zhangjiajie
> - 1982: Became China's first National Forest Park
> - 1992: Listed as World Natural Heritage
> - 2001: Rated as National 5A scenic area
> - 2010: 'Avatar' filmed, famous worldwide
>
> "**Landform Features**:
> Zhangjiajie landform is quartz sandstone peak forest landform, world rare. Main features:
> - Stone pillars: 3,000+
> - Height: 100-300 meters
> - Diameter: 5-20 meters
> - Color: Red-brown (iron oxide dyed)
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Discovery time: 1958
> - Forest Park: 1982
> - World Heritage: 1992
> - Stone pillar count: 3,000+"
