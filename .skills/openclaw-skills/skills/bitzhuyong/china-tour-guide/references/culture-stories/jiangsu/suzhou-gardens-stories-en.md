## 🏛️ Humble Administrator's Garden (Zhuozheng Yuan)

### L1 Basic
> "Humble Administrator's Garden is Suzhou largest classical garden, area 78 mu. Built in Ming Dynasty, with Lingering Garden, Summer Palace, Chengde Mountain Resort called China's Four Great Gardens."

### L2 Standard
> "Humble Administrator's Garden is located at Suzhou Northeast Street, first built Ming Zhengde fourth year (1509), is Censor Wang Xianchen resigned returned home built.
>
> Humble Administrator's Garden area 78 mu, is Suzhou largest classical garden. Garden name from Jin Dynasty Pan Yue 'Idle Living Fu' in 'clumsy person's governance', meaning clumsy person governing.
>
> Humble Administrator's Garden divided into East, Middle, West three parts, Middle is essence location."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Construction History**:
> Humble Administrator's Garden first built Ming Zhengde fourth year (1509), Censor Wang Xianchen resigned returned home built. Garden name from Jin Dynasty Pan Yue 'Idle Living Fu' in 'clumsy person's governance'.
>
> "**Layout Structure**:
> Humble Administrator's Garden divided into East, Middle, West three parts:
> - East: Pingyuan Hall main, open spacious
> - Middle: Yuanxiang Tang center, water mountains surround
> - West: Thirty-Six Mandarin Duck Hall main, buildings exquisite
>
> "**Garden Techniques**:
> Humble Administrator's Garden uses borrowed scenery/opposite scenery/framed scenery/leaked scenery techniques:
> - Borrowed scenery: Beisi Pagoda borrowed
> - Opposite scenery: Yuanxiang Tang and Xuexiang Yunwei Pavilion opposite
> - Framed scenery: Through doors windows frame scenery
> - Leaked scenery: Through flower windows leak scenery
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Area: 78 mu (5.2 hectares)
> - Build time: 1509
> - Garden name meaning: Clumsy person's governance
> - World Heritage: 1997"

## 🏛️ Lingering Garden (Liu Yuan)

### L1 Basic
> "Lingering Garden is China's Four Great Gardens one, area 30 mu. Built in Ming Dynasty, famous for buildings arranged exquisite, strange rocks numerous."

### L2 Standard
> "Lingering Garden is located at Suzhou Changmen Outside, first built Ming Wanli twenty-first year (1593), is Taipu Temple Shaoqing Xu Taishi private garden.
>
> Lingering Garden area 30 mu, divided into East, Middle, West, North four parts. Middle water mountains main, East buildings main, West natural scenery main.
>
> Lingering Garden Guan Yun Peak is Jiangnan largest Taihu stone, 6.5 meters high."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Construction History**:
> Lingering Garden first built Ming Wanli twenty-first year (1593), Taipu Temple Shaoqing Xu Taishi built. Qing Jiaqing period, Liu Nu purchased this garden, renamed Hanbi Mountain Villa. Later because surname Liu, commonly called Liu Garden, later renamed Lingering Garden.
>
> "**Layout Structure**:
> Lingering Garden divided into East, Middle, West, North four parts:
> - East: Buildings main, has Guan Yun Peak etc
> - Middle: Water mountains main, has Hanbi Shanfang
> - West: Natural scenery main
> - North: Pastoral scenery main
>
> "**Guan Yun Peak**:
> Guan Yun Peak is Jiangnan largest Taihu stone, 6.5 meters high, weighs about 5 tons. Stone shape like cloud, hence name Guan Yun Peak.
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Area: 30 mu (2 hectares)
> - Build time: 1593
> - Guan Yun Peak: 6.5 meters high
> - World Heritage: 1997"

## 🏛️ Master of Nets Garden (Wangshi Yuan)

### L1 Basic
> "Master of Nets Garden is Suzhou smallest garden, area only 0.6 hectares. Built in Southern Song, famous for small exquisite."

### L2 Standard
> "Master of Nets Garden is located at Suzhou Daichengqiao Road, first built Southern Song Chunxi period (1174-1189), is Shi Zhengzhi Wanjuan Hall site.
>
> Master of Nets Garden area only 0.6 hectares, is Suzhou smallest garden. Garden name 'Wangshi' means fisherman, expressing garden owner reclusion intention.
>
> Master of Nets Garden night tour is feature project, can appreciate garden night scenery and traditional performances."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Construction History**:
> Master of Nets Garden first built Southern Song Chunxi period (1174-1189), Shi Zhengzhi Wanjuan Hall site. Qing Qianlong period, Song Zongyuan purchased this garden, renamed Master of Nets Garden.
>
> "**Layout Structure**:
> Master of Nets Garden although small, but layout exquisite:
> - East: Residential area
> - Middle: Main garden area, has Moon Comes Wind Arrives Pavilion
> - West: Inner garden, has Dianchun Yi
>
> "**Moon Comes Wind Arrives Pavilion**:
> Moon Comes Wind Arrives Pavilion is Master of Nets Garden landmark building, built facing water. Mid-Autumn moon viewing best.
>
> "**Dianchun Yi**:
> Dianchun Yi is Master of Nets Garden boutique, American Metropolitan Museum of Art replicated Dianchun Yi, named Mingxuan.
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Area: 0.6 hectares
> - Build time: Southern Song
> - Dianchun Yi: American Metropolitan Museum has replica
> - Night tour: Feature project"

## 🏛️ Lion Grove Garden (Shizi Lin)

### L1 Basic
> "Lion Grove Garden famous for rockeries, praised as 'Rockery Kingdom'. Built in Yuan Dynasty, garden rockeries like lions, hence name Lion Grove Garden."

### L2 Standard
> "Lion Grove Garden is located at Suzhou Garden Road, first built Yuan Zhizheng second year (1342), is Tianru Chan Master to commemorate master Zhongfeng Chan Master built.
>
> Lion Grove Garden famous for rockeries, garden rockeries like lions, hence name Lion Grove Garden. Rockeries area about half garden, praised as 'Rockery Kingdom'.
>
> Lion Grove Garden is 'Dream of the Red Chamber' Grand View Garden prototype one of."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Construction History**:
> Lion Grove Garden first built Yuan Zhizheng second year (1342), Tianru Chan Master to commemorate master Zhongfeng Chan Master built. Because Zhongfeng Chan Master once at Tianmu Mountain Lion Rock practiced, hence name Lion Grove Garden.
>
> "**Rockery Features**:
> Lion Grove Garden rockeries area about half garden, has 'Rockery Kingdom' title:
> - Rockery group: Nine Lion Peak/Zhibai Pavilion
> - Stone caves: 21 caves, complicated
> - Stone peaks: Various shapes, like lions
>
> "**Cultural Significance**:
> Lion Grove Garden is Zen garden representative, embodies Zen thought. Also is 'Dream of the Red Chamber' Grand View Garden prototype one of.
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Area: 1.1 hectares
> - Build time: 1342
> - Rockery count: 21 caves
> - 'Dream of the Red Chamber': Grand View Garden prototype"

## 🏛️ Suzhou Gardens History Overview

### L1 Basic
> "Suzhou Gardens 1997 listed as World Cultural Heritage. Suzhou has 100+ classical gardens, is China garden art representative."

### L2 Standard
> "Suzhou Gardens is located at Jiangsu Province Suzhou City, existing 100+ classical gardens. Suzhou Gardens started Spring Autumn, flourished Five Dynasties, peak Ming Qing.
>
> 1997, Humble Administrator's Garden/Lingering Garden/Master of Nets Garden/Huanxiu Mountain Villa listed as World Cultural Heritage. 2000, Canglang Pavilion/Lion Grove Garden/Yipu/Ou Garden/Tuisi Garden extended listed.
>
> Suzhou Gardens is China garden art representative, praised as 'within short distance recreate universe'."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**History**:
> - Spring Autumn: Wu King built palaces gardens
> - Five Dynasties: Wuyue King built gardens
> - Song Dynasty: Literati gardens rose
> - Ming Qing: Suzhou Gardens peak
> - 1997: Listed as World Heritage
>
> "**Garden Techniques**:
> Suzhou Gardens uses multiple garden techniques:
> 1. Borrowed scenery: Borrow outside garden scenery
> 2. Opposite scenery: Two scenery opposite
> 3. Framed scenery: Through doors windows frame scenery
> 4. Leaked scenery: Through flower windows leak scenery
> 5. Blocked scenery: Use stones trees block scenery
>
> "**Art Features**:
> Suzhou Gardens art features:
> - Small see big: Within short distance recreate universe
> - Water mountains depend: Mountains because water alive, water because mountains turn
> - Buildings exquisite: Pavilions terraces, scattered
> - Plant configuration: Four seasons scenery
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Garden count: 100+
> - World Heritage: 9 gardens
> - Largest garden: Humble Administrator's Garden (78 mu)
> - Smallest garden: Master of Nets Garden (0.6 hectares)"
