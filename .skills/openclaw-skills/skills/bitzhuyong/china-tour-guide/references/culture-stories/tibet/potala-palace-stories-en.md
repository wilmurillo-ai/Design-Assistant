## 🏛️ White Palace

### L1 Basic
> "White Palace is Dalai Lama's administrative area, named for white walls. East Hall is where Dalai was enthroned and assumed power."

### L2 Standard
> "White Palace is located at east side of Potala Palace, named for white walls. White Palace is administrative and living area of successive Dalai Lamas, total 7 floors.
>
> East Hall is White Palace's main hall, area 717 square meters, has 44 large pillars. This is where Dalai was enthroned and assumed power, also where major religious ceremonies held.
>
> White Palace also has Dalai's bedchambers, scripture halls etc, showing Dalai's life scenes."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Construction History**:
> White Palace first built 1645, Fifth Dalai Lama period. 1648 basically completed, became successive Dalai Lamas' winter palace.
>
> "**East Hall**:
> East Hall is White Palace core building, area 717 square meters, 44 large pillars. Hall enshrines Sakyamuni statue, two sides are successive Dalai portraits.
>
> East Hall held many major ceremonies:
> - Dalai enthronement ceremony
> - Power assumption ceremony
> - Major religious dharma assemblies
>
> "**Dalai Bedchambers**:
> White Palace has Dalai's East and West bedchambers. Bedchambers furnishings simple, has beds, scriptures, dharma instruments. Shows Dalai's daily life.
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - White Palace floors: 7
> - East Hall area: 717 square meters
> - Pillar count: 44
> - Wall color: White (milk/sugar/honey mixed paint)"

## 🏛️ Red Palace

### L1 Basic
> "Red Palace is Potala Palace's religious area, named for red walls. Has successive Dalai stupa halls and Buddha halls."

### L2 Standard
> "Red Palace is located at center of Potala Palace, named for red walls. Red Palace is religious area, has successive Dalai stupa halls and Buddha halls.
>
> Red Palace's main building is Fifth Dalai Stupa Hall, stupa 14.85 meters high, uses 119,082 taels of gold, inlaid with 15,000+ jewels.
>
> Red Palace also has Dharma King Cave, Holy Guanyin Hall, Kalachakra Hall etc Buddha halls, enshrining various Buddha statues and scriptures."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Construction History**:
> Red Palace first built 1690, after Fifth Dalai passed away, Desi Sangye Gyatso presided construction. 1693 basically completed.
>
> "**Fifth Dalai Stupa Hall**:
> Fifth Dalai Stupa Hall is Red Palace's largest hall, area 717 square meters. Stupa 14.85 meters high, uses 119,082 taels of gold, inlaid with 15,000+ jewels, including diamonds, pearls, agate, coral etc.
>
> Stupa enshrines Fifth Dalai's dharma body, is Potala Palace's most precious relic.
>
> "**Dharma King Cave**:
> Dharma King Cave is Potala Palace's oldest hall, built 7th century Songtsen Gampo period. Cave enshrines Songtsen Gampo, Princess Wencheng, Princess Bhrikuti statues.
>
> "**Holy Guanyin Hall**:
> Holy Guanyin Hall enshrines naturally formed sandalwood Guanyin statue, is Potala Palace's town palace treasure. Legend says this Guanyin statue flew from India.
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Red Palace floors: 8
> - Stupa count: 8 Dalai stupas
> - Gold usage: 119,082 taels
> - Jewel count: 15,000+ pieces"

## 🏛️ Fifth Dalai Stupa

### L1 Basic
> "Fifth Dalai Stupa is Potala Palace's largest stupa, 14.85 meters high, uses 110,000+ taels of gold, inlaid with 15,000 jewels."

### L2 Standard
> "Fifth Dalai Stupa is located at center of Red Palace, is largest of Potala Palace's 8 Dalai stupas.
>
> Stupa 14.85 meters high, stupa body wrapped in gold, uses 119,082 taels of gold. Stupa body inlaid with 15,000+ jewels, including diamonds, pearls, agate, coral, turquoise etc.
>
> Stupa enshrines Fifth Dalai Ngawang Lobsang Gyatso's dharma body. Fifth Dalai is one of Tibet's greatest Dalais, unified Tibet, established Ganden Phodrang regime."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Fifth Dalai Life**:
> Fifth Dalai Ngawang Lobsang Gyatso (1617-1682), is Tibet's greatest Dalai Lama.
>
> Main achievements:
> 1. Unified Tibet, ended division
> 2. Established Ganden Phodrang regime
> 3. Established contact with Qing Dynasty, 1652 went to Beijing to meet Emperor Shunzhi
> 4. Built Potala Palace
>
> "**Stupa Construction**:
> After Fifth Dalai passed away, Desi Sangye Gyatso presided stupa construction. Took 3 years, cost huge.
>
> Stupa structure:
> - Stupa base: Square, carved Buddha images
> - Stupa vase: Spherical, enshrines dharma body
> - Stupa spire: Conical, inlaid with jewels
>
> "**Jewel Decoration**:
> Stupa inlaid with 15,000+ jewels:
> - Diamonds: Largest from India
> - Pearls: From South Sea
> - Agate: From Tibet local
> - Coral: From South Sea
> - Turquoise: From Persia
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Stupa height: 14.85 meters
> - Gold usage: 119,082 taels (about 3.7 tons)
> - Jewel count: 15,000+ pieces
> - Construction time: 3 years"

## 🏛️ Dharma King Cave

### L1 Basic
> "Dharma King Cave is Potala Palace's oldest hall, built 7th century. Cave enshrines Songtsen Gampo, Princess Wencheng, Princess Bhrikuti statues."

### L2 Standard
> "Dharma King Cave is located at Red Palace bottom floor, is Potala Palace's oldest hall, built 7th century Songtsen Gampo period.
>
> Cave enshrines Songtsen Gampo, Princess Wencheng, Princess Bhrikuti, minister Gar Tongtsen etc statues. Statues lifelike, are Tubo period sculpture art masterpieces.
>
> Dharma King Cave is Songtsen Gampo's practice place, also Potala Palace's town palace treasure."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Construction History**:
> Dharma King Cave built 7th century Songtsen Gampo period, is Potala Palace's earliest building. Over 1,300 years, well preserved.
>
> "**Enshrined Statues**:
> Cave enshrines 9 statues:
> 1. Songtsen Gampo: Tubo Dynasty founder
> 2. Princess Wencheng: Tang Dynasty princess, promoted Han-Tibetan exchange
> 3. Princess Bhrikuti: Nepal princess
> 4. Gar Tongtsen: Songtsen Gampo's minister
> 5-9. Other ministers and translators
>
> "**Art Value**:
> Statues are Tubo period sculpture art masterpieces, vivid shapes, colorful. Statues made of clay sculpture with color, over thousand years not fading.
>
> "**Historical Significance**:
> Dharma King Cave witnessed Han-Tibetan friendship history. Princess Wencheng entered Tibet, brought Central Plains culture, promoted Tibet development.
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - Construction time: 7th century
> - History: 1,300+ years
> - Statue count: 9
> - Statue material: Clay sculpture with color"

## 🏰 Potala Palace History Overview

### L1 Basic
> "Potala Palace first built 631 AD, Songtsen Gampo built for Princess Wencheng. 1645 Fifth Dalai rebuilt, became successive Dalai winter palace. 1994 listed as world cultural heritage."

### L2 Standard
> "Potala Palace first built 631 AD, Tubo Dynasty Songtsen Gampo built for welcoming Princess Wencheng. At that time had 999 rooms.
>
> 9th century Tubo Dynasty collapsed, Potala Palace destroyed. 1645 Fifth Dalai rebuilt, 1693 basically completed, became successive Dalai winter palace.
>
> Potala Palace 117 meters high, 13 floors, has 1,000+ rooms. 1994 listed as world cultural heritage."

### L3 Deep Dive
> "**Tubo Period (7-9th Century)**:
> - 631: Songtsen Gampo built Potala Palace
> - 641: Princess Wencheng entered Tibet
> - 842: Tubo Dynasty collapsed, Potala Palace destroyed
>
> "**Rebuild Period (17th Century)**:
> - 1642: Fifth Dalai established Ganden Phodrang regime
> - 1645: Started rebuilding Potala Palace
> - 1648: White Palace completed
> - 1690: Started building Red Palace
> - 1693: Red Palace basically completed
>
> "**Modern Period**:
> - 1959: Fourteenth Dalai fled to India
> - 1961: Listed as national key cultural relics protection unit
> - 1994: Listed as world cultural heritage
> - 2002: Potala Palace Square completed
>
> "**Architecture Features**:
> - Height: 117 meters
> - Floors: 13
> - Rooms: 1,000+
> - Wall: Thickest 5 meters
> - Material: Stone-wood structure
>
> "**Trivia**:
> - First built: 631
> - Rebuild time: 1645-1693
> - World Heritage: 1994
> - Altitude: 3,700 meters"
