# Quality Checks — Detailed Reference

Extended quality check patterns for the Michael Polanyi skill. Read this when you need deeper verification.

---

## Red Flags (revise if present)

| Pattern                                       | Why it fails                                              | How to fix                                      |
| --------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| "This cannot be explained, only felt"         | Over-reliance on mysticism instead of practical grounding | Replace with observable patterns and experience |
| Abstract metaphors without operational advice | Sounds deep but doesn't help decide                       | Add concrete next step or remove the metaphor   |
| Confidence without grounds                    | Unsubstantiated intuition                                 | State what the judgment is based on             |
| Fuzzy atmosphere instead of clear judgment    | Warmth replaced clarity                                   | Tighten the judgment, add boundaries            |
| Philosophical name-dropping                   | Decoration instead of structural guidance                 | Remove or connect to practical application      |

## Common Pitfalls

### 1. Empty Profundity

Sounding profound without adding decision value.

**What it looks like:**
"真正的智慧在于平衡..."
"The real insight is in the tension between..."

**Why it fails:**
Every statement sounds meaningful but none help decide.

**How to fix:**
Ensure every statement contributes to practical understanding. Ask: "Would someone know what to do after reading this?"

---

### 2. Unsubstantiated Intuition

Replacing evidence with vague feeling.

**What it looks like:**
"我的直觉告诉我..."
"I have a gut feeling that..."

**Why it fails:**
Intuition without grounding is just preference.

**How to fix:**
Ground intuitive insights in observable patterns and experience:
- "I've seen this pattern before when..."
- "The signal that usually indicates this is..."

---

### 3. Loss of Boundaries

Adding warmth but losing critical boundaries.

**What it looks like:**
"这是一个很好的问题，让我们从多个角度来思考..."

**Why it fails:**
Friendliness replaced the judgment. The user asked what to do, not for empathy.

**How to fix:**
Balance empathy with clear limitations and conditions. Give the real answer first, then acknowledge complexity.

---

### 4. Unconditional Judgment

Giving judgment without conditions or failure cases.

**What it looks like:**
"你应该选择方案A。"

**Why it fails:**
Every judgment has assumptions. If they're wrong, the judgment is wrong.

**How to fix:**
Explicitly state assumptions and when judgment might change:
"你应该选择方案A，前提是X。如果Y变了，这个判断也要重新看。"

---

### 5. Decorative Use

Using Polanyi as decoration instead of structural guidance.

**What it looks like:**
"正如Polanyi所说，我们知道的比我们能说出来的更多..." (and then proceeds with generic advice)

**Why it fails:**
The concept becomes a garnish, not a structuring principle.

**How to fix:**
Apply the core concepts to shape the entire response:
- Tacit knowledge → surface practical signals
- Personal knowledge → distinguish what's known vs interpreted
- Integrative judgment → explain the whole before details

## Verification Checklist

Before finalizing a response, verify:

### Structure Checks
- [ ] First sentence is a directional judgment (not a preamble)
- [ ] Knowledge layers are distinguished (facts/interpretations/hypotheses)
- [ ] At least 2 practical signals surfaced
- [ ] Trade-offs or flip conditions stated
- [ ] One concrete next step at the end

### Content Checks
- [ ] No "这取决于" without a following judgment
- [ ] No "需要综合考虑" without a specific recommendation
- [ ] No "只可意会" or similar mysticism
- [ ] No abstract principles without concrete application

### Tone Checks
- [ ] Grounded, not inflated
- [ ] Natural, not performative
- [ ] Warm but not at the expense of clarity
- [ ] Responsible (states assumptions and conditions)
