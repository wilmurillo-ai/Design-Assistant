# Mondo Poster Composition Patterns Reference

> Based on [qiaomu-mondo-poster-design](https://github.com/joeseesun/qiaomu-mondo-poster-design) by [@vista8](https://x.com/vista8).

8 core composition techniques for creating authentic Mondo-style poster designs. Each pattern includes description, when to use it, prompt keywords, and example prompt fragments.

---

## 1. Centered Symmetrical

**Description:** Subject placed dead-center with balanced left/right mirroring. Creates heroic, iconic, monumental impact.

**When to use:** Hero posters, character showcases, album covers, subjects that demand authority and presence.

**Prompt keywords:** `centered symmetrical composition, balanced mirroring, monumental centered figure, iconic frontal pose, symmetrical design`

**Example:**
```
[Subject] in Mondo poster style, vertical 2:3, centered symmetrical
composition, single iconic figure, balanced design, 3-color screen print:
[color 1], [color 2], [color 3], monumental presence, vintage poster aesthetic
```

---

## 2. Negative Space Mastery

**Description:** Strategic empty areas that form meaningful shapes. The absence of ink tells as much as the ink itself. Creates dual-layered visual experiences with hidden surprises.

**When to use:** Conceptual posters, minimalist designs, clever visual storytelling where what is NOT shown matters.

**Prompt keywords:** `negative space storytelling, figure-ground inversion, hidden imagery, dual imagery, what's missing tells the story`

### Technique A: Visual Puns (Olly Moss Style)
One element serves double duty — silhouette CONTAINS another scene within negative space.

```
[Subject silhouette] in Mondo poster style, vertical 9:16, negative space
WITHIN silhouette reveals [hidden element], Olly Moss figure-ground inversion,
2-color duotone: [color 1] and [color 2], clever dual imagery
```

**Real-world inspiration:**
- Darth Vader silhouette containing AT-ST battle scene in negative space
- Detective hat where negative space forms city skyline
- Knife blade reflecting villain's silhouette

### Technique B: Scale Contrast Drama
Tiny subject vs. massive object creates emotional impact — isolation, wonder, or threat.

```
Tiny [subject] with massive [object] looming in Mondo poster style, vertical
9:16, dramatic scale contrast, [subject] occupies only bottom 20%, vast
negative space above, 2-3 color screen print, sense of [awe/isolation/danger]
```

### Technique C: Single Shape Storytelling
ONE iconic shape captures entire narrative. No clutter. Let one perfect symbol do ALL the work.

**Best practice:** 30% graphic, 30% text, 40% empty space.

```
Single [iconic object/symbol] centered in Mondo poster style, vertical 9:16,
ONLY this one element, surrounded by vast negative space, 2-color print:
[color 1] on [color 2] background, ultra-minimal approach, one image tells
complete story
```

## 3. Geometric Framing

**Description:** Using circles, triangles, arches, or other geometric shapes to contain and structure the composition. Creates visual focus and architectural order.

**When to use:** Sci-fi, architectural subjects, designs needing structure and visual hierarchy, Art Deco themes.

**Prompt keywords:** `geometric framing, circular composition, triangular composition, arch framing, geometric border, structured visual hierarchy`

**Example:**
```
[Subject] in Mondo poster style, vertical 2:3, geometric [circle/triangle/arch]
framing the central image, structured visual hierarchy, Art Deco geometric
borders, 3-color screen print, architectural composition, vintage poster
```

---

## 4. Layered Depth

**Description:** Creating dimensional foreground, midground, and background layers despite flat screen-print aesthetics. Uses color value and overlap to suggest depth.

**When to use:** Atmospheric scenes, environmental storytelling, subjects set in rich locations (cities, landscapes, interiors).

**Prompt keywords:** `layered depth composition, foreground silhouette, midground subject, background atmosphere, dimensional layering, atmospheric perspective`

**Example:**
```
[Subject] in Mondo poster style, vertical 2:3, layered depth composition,
dark foreground silhouettes framing [midground subject] against [atmospheric
background], 4-color screen print creating depth, atmospheric perspective,
environmental storytelling
```

---

## 5. Silhouette Dominance

**Description:** Reducing subjects to recognizable outlines without internal detail. The shape alone must communicate identity and mood.

**When to use:** Iconic characters, high-contrast designs, subjects with distinctive profiles (cowboys, detectives, monsters).

**Prompt keywords:** `bold silhouette, recognizable outline, no internal detail, shape-based storytelling, high contrast figure, iconic profile`

**Example:**
```
[Subject] bold silhouette in Mondo poster style, vertical 2:3, recognizable
outline against [color] background, no internal detail, shape tells the story,
2-color high contrast screen print, dramatic silhouette, vintage poster
```

---

## 6. Visual Puns & Hidden Imagery

**Description:** Clever compositions where objects serve dual purposes or reveal secondary imagery upon inspection. Rewards the viewer for looking closely.

**When to use:** Intellectual films, mysteries, subjects with duality or hidden meaning, conceptual designs.

**Prompt keywords:** `visual pun, hidden imagery, dual-purpose element, clever composition, secondary meaning revealed on inspection, intellectual design`

**Example:**
```
[Object A] that also resembles [Object B] in Mondo poster style, vertical
2:3, visual pun composition, dual-purpose imagery, clever design trick,
3-color screen print, reward for close inspection, intellectual poster design
```

---

## 7. Typography Integration

**Description:** Hand-drawn retro lettering functions as a compositional element, not just an overlay. Text IS the design.

**When to use:** Title-heavy designs, music posters, event announcements, subjects where the name/title is the star.

**Prompt keywords:** `integrated typography, text as design element, hand-drawn lettering, typographic composition, letterforms as imagery`

**Era-specific typography styles:**
- 1940s-50s: Hand-painted brush script, film noir shadows
- 1960s: Psychedelic flowing letterforms, Art Nouveau revival
- 1970s: Condensed bold sans-serif, geometric letterforms
- 1980s: Neon-glow display type, chrome effects

**Example:**
```
[Title] as primary visual element in Mondo poster style, vertical 2:3,
integrated typography composition, [era]-style hand-drawn lettering,
text forms the imagery, 3-color screen print, typographic poster design
```

---

## 8. Border & Edge Treatments

**Description:** Decorative frames and intentional edge designs that enhance the collectible, limited-edition feel of the poster.

**When to use:** Premium/collectible editions, Art Nouveau subjects, designs that benefit from frame-like presentation.

**Prompt keywords:** `decorative border, ornate frame, edge treatment, collectible edition feel, bordered composition, frame within frame`

**Example:**
```
[Subject] in Mondo poster style, vertical 2:3, ornate decorative border
framing the central image, Art Nouveau edge treatment, collectible limited
edition feel, 3-color screen print, bordered vintage poster composition
```

## Combining Patterns

Sophisticated Mondo designs often combine 2-3 patterns. Common pairings:

| Combination | Effect | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Negative Space + Silhouette | Hidden scene inside character outline | Olly Moss style character posters |
| Geometric Framing + Layered Depth | Structured scene with atmospheric depth | Sci-fi world-building posters |
| Typography + Border | Text-dominant collectible edition | Music festival / event posters |
| Centered Symmetry + Negative Space | Iconic figure with hidden visual pun | Conceptual character showcase |
| Silhouette + Scale Contrast | Tiny figure against massive silhouette | Horror / isolation posters |

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- **Too many elements** — Mondo is about distillation, not accumulation (exception: Tyler Stout maximalist style)
- **Photorealistic gradients** — Screen-print aesthetic demands flat color blocks and halftone textures
- **Complex facial details** — Use silhouettes instead; faces rarely work in screen-print style
- **Cluttered composition** — Even "busy" Mondo designs (Tyler Stout) have organized visual flow
- **Missing vintage context** — Always anchor to a specific decade (60s/70s/80s) for authentic feel
- **Digital-looking outputs** — Always include texture keywords: halftone, screen print, paper grain, ink layer
