# Crypto, Vehicles & Other Special Cases

## Cryptocurrency

### Where to declare
- Declared in **État des titres** (same as securities) as wealth/fortune
- NOT a separate section — treated like any other asset

### Valuation
- Use AFC fiscal values at 31.12: `https://www.ictax.admin.ch/extern/en.html#/ratelist/{YEAR}`
- For coins not listed on AFC: use the 31.12 price from a major exchange (Coinbase, Kraken, Binance)
- Source: ge.ch — "il est possible d'utiliser le cours au 31 décembre disponible sur les plateformes de négociation"

### Income from crypto
- **Capital gains from trading (private):** TAX-FREE (same as shares — private asset management)
- **Staking/mining rewards:** Taxable as income (rendement de fortune)
- **Airdrop/hard fork tokens:** Taxable as income when received
- **Professional trading:** If you trade at high frequency/volume, authorities may classify you as professional → gains become taxable income. Criteria: volume, frequency, leverage, proportion of total income

### What to declare per coin
- Name (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum)
- Quantity held at 31.12
- Value per unit at 31.12 (AFC fiscal value or exchange rate)
- Total fortune value
- Any staking/mining income during the year

### Tip
If the user has many different coins, they can attach a separate list as an annex rather than entering each individually in GeTax.

## Vehicles
- Cars are generally NOT declared as fortune in Geneva (personal use items)
- Car loans ARE declared as debts (dette chirographaire)
- Leasing: lease payments are NOT deductible; the interest portion within leasing payments is also NOT deductible

## Inheritance & Gifts Received During Year
- Inherited assets must be declared from the date of inheritance
- Gift tax in Geneva: exempt between spouses and direct-line relatives
- If you inherited property or securities mid-year, declare them in the relevant section with acquisition date

## Subletting Income
- If you sublet part of your apartment, that income goes in "Autres revenus" (Annexe C2)
- Declare the net income (rent received minus any costs)

## Alimony / Child Support
- **Paid:** Deductible (Annexe C4 — Pensions, contributions d'entretien versées)
- **Received:** Taxable income (Annexe C1)
- Only applies to court-ordered or legally agreed amounts

## Professional Training / Formation Continue
- Costs for professional development are deductible
- Max IFD: CHF 13'000
- Must be related to maintaining/improving current professional skills
- NOT deductible: first degree, career change education

## Donations (Dons)
- Deductible up to 20% of net income (ICC and IFD)
- IFD minimum: CHF 100 total
- Must be to recognized Swiss charitable organizations
- Keep receipts — the tax office may request them

## Source Tax (Impôt à la Source) / TOU / Quasi-Résident

### Who is source-taxed
- Foreign nationals without C permit (B, L permits)
- Tax deducted directly from salary by employer

### TOU (Taxation Ordinaire Ultérieure)
- Mandatory if gross salary ≥ CHF 120'000 or other income ≥ CHF 3'000
- Allows claiming ALL deductions (like a regular resident)
- Must file full GeTax return
- Source tax already paid is credited against final tax bill

### Quasi-Résident (for cross-border workers, G permit)
- Must have ≥90% of worldwide household income taxable in Switzerland
- Allows filing like a resident to claim full deductions
- Application via DRIS/TOU form

### Key difference
- TOU = mandatory for high earners, file full return
- Quasi-résident = optional for cross-border workers meeting 90% threshold

## Deadline & Extensions
- **Standard deadline:** March 31
- **Extension:** Can request until June 30 (or sometimes September)
- Request extension via ge.ch e-démarches BEFORE the deadline
- No penalty for requesting extension, but file on time to avoid default assessment

## Submission Checklist
Before submitting GeTax, verify:
1. All income declared (both spouses if married)
2. All bank accounts worldwide listed in F2
3. All securities with ictax values in F3
4. All deductions claimed (compare forfait vs effectifs)
5. Foreign property declared with correct exchange rates
6. Debts and interest entered
7. Health insurance premiums entered
8. Medical expenses claimed
9. Pillar 3a certificates attached
10. Required justificatifs uploaded (salary certs, rental statements, etc.)
