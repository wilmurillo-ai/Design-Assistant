# Insurance & Medical Expenses

## Health Insurance (Section 52.21)

### What to enter
- **Assurance-maladie ICC / IFD:** Total premiums paid (LAMal base + LCA complémentaire - any taxes/credits)
- Enter same amount for both ICC and IFD — GeTax applies the caps automatically
- Enter for each spouse separately (Contribuable A / B)

### Caps
- **ICC:** Adult (26+): max 17'122 / Young adult (19-25): max 12'842 / Child: max 3'965
- **IFD:** Flat-rate (auto-calculated, much lower)

### Source document
Request "Attestation pour l'autorité fiscale" from your insurer — shows premiums, subsidies, and unreimbursed medical costs for the year.

## Medical Expenses (Frais médicaux)

### Thresholds — IMPORTANT
- **ICC:** Deductible above **0.5%** of net income (low threshold — almost always worth claiming!)
- **IFD:** Deductible above **5%** of net income (high threshold — harder to reach)

### What counts
- Unreimbursed costs shown on insurer's attestation (franchise, quote-part)
- **Dental work** — often the biggest item, usually NOT covered by base insurance
- **Foreign dental/medical** — fully deductible, no territoriality restriction (UK dentist, France dentist, etc.)
- **Foreign dental insurance premiums** — can be declared as medical expense (simpler than trying to add to insurance section)
- Glasses/contacts and eye exams
- Physiotherapy, prescriptions not covered
- Any medical expense NOT reimbursed by insurance
- Over-the-counter medication prescribed by a doctor

### Tips
- Dental invoices are often paper-only — ask the user to check for them
- Even small amounts add up — ICC threshold is very low
- Enter the total of ALL unreimbursed costs (insurer attestation + dental + other)
- If user can't find dental receipts immediately, enter what's known and update later (before submission)

## Accident Insurance (Assurance-accidents)
- Usually covered by employer (LAA) — enter 0 unless user pays separate accident insurance
- Only enter premiums the user actually pays themselves

## Pillar 3b / Life Insurance (Geneva-specific!)
Geneva is one of few cantons allowing Pillar 3b deduction:
- Single: CHF 2'345
- Couple: CHF 3'518
- Self-employed: CHF 4'690
- **Don't forget to ask** if the user has any life insurance (assurance-vie) policies
