# Debts & Interest (Intérêts et Dettes)

## Overview
Declare all debts as of 31.12 and interest paid during the tax year.
- Debts reduce **taxable wealth** (fortune)
- Interest reduces **taxable income**

## Types of Debt

### Dettes hypothécaires (mortgage/secured debt)
- Secured by property (hypothèque)
- Enter each mortgage separately with full details

### Dettes chirographaires (unsecured debt)
- Car loans, personal loans, credit cards, margin loans
- GeTax may auto-create some from e-relevés (shown as non-editable)

## What to Enter Per Debt

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Créancier | Full name of lender |
| Domicile/Siège | Address of lender |
| Type de dette | Hypothécaire / Consommation / etc. |
| Quote-part | % attributed to Contribuable A vs B |
| Date de constitution | When the loan was taken out |
| Montant des intérêts | Total interest paid during tax year (in CHF) |
| Montant de la dette | Remaining balance at 31.12 (in CHF) |

## Currency Conversion for Foreign Debts
- **Debt amount (31.12 balance):** Use **year-end rate** (31.12)
- **Interest paid during year:** Use **annual average rate**
- These are different rates! Common mistake to use the same.

## Interest Deduction Cap
Interest on private debts is deductible up to:
**Gross wealth income + CHF 50'000**

This rarely limits people with mortgages, but be aware for large investment loans.

## NOT Deductible
- Interest on construction credits
- Interest included in leasing payments
- Capital repayment amounts (only interest is deductible)

## International Debt Distribution
When you have both Swiss and foreign assets with debts:
- Debts are proportionally allocated between Swiss and foreign assets
- Part of your debt/interest gets "allocated" to the foreign property
- This reduces what you can deduct against Swiss income
- GeTax handles this calculation automatically based on your total assets/debts

## Common Scenarios

### French Mortgage
- Créancier: French bank (e.g., Caisse d'Épargne)
- Type: Hypothécaire
- Get the "tableau d'amortissement" or "relevé annuel de prêt" from online banking
- Sum all monthly interest payments for the year (don't include capital repayment)
- Remaining debt = capital restant dû after the December payment

### Car Loan
- Type: Chirographaire (or Consommation)
- Often from the same bank or a specialized lender (e.g., CREDIPAR for PSA/Stellantis)
- Small interest amounts still worth declaring
- Check amortization table for exact 31.12 balance

### Multiple Loans with Same Bank
- Enter each loan as a separate line item
- Use the loan/credit number to distinguish them
