# Salary & Professional Expenses (Annexe A)

## Salary Entry (fields 11.10–11.92 / 21.10–21.92)
- Enter gross salary, net salary, and all fields from the salary certificate (certificat de salaire)
- **Source tax (impôt à la source):** If source-taxed, enter the amount withheld
- **RSU/ESPP:** RSU income goes in field 5. ESPP discount is employment income.
- **Field 9 (AVS/AI/APG/AC):** Include Amat (assurance maternité GE) even if employer lists it separately in field 15. Check field 15 remarks carefully.
- **Field 10.1 (LPP):** Employee's 2nd pillar contribution

## Forfaitaire vs Effectifs — CRITICAL DECISION

These are **mutually exclusive** — pick one or the other, but ALWAYS compare both.

### Forfaitaire (flat-rate)
One number covers ALL professional expenses (meals, transport, other):
- **ICC:** 3% of net salary, min 640, max 1'812
- **IFD:** 3% of net salary, min 2'000, max 4'000

### Effectifs (itemized)
Each expense declared separately:

| Expense | ICC | IFD |
|---|---|---|
| Meals (no employer contribution) | 15/day, max 3'200/yr **⚠️ ONLY if commute >2h/day by public transport** | 15/day, max 3'200/yr (no commute condition) |
| Meals (employer contributes, case G) | 7.50/day, max 1'600/yr **⚠️ same 2h condition** | 7.50/day, max 1'600/yr |
| Transport (public) | Max 534 | Max 3'300 |
| Transport (car, if justified) | 0.70/km | 0.70/km |

**⚠️ CRITICAL ICC RULE:** For ICC, meal deductions are ONLY allowed if commuting by public transport takes more than 2 hours per day round-trip. If the commute is shorter, NO meal deduction for ICC (but IFD meal deduction still applies with no such restriction). This means you can have forfaitaire for ICC and effectifs for IFD — GeTax allows separate choices.

### Quick Rule
If meals alone (3'200) > ICC forfait cap (1'812) AND commute >2h by public transport → **effectifs wins for ICC.**
If commute <2h → **no ICC meal deduction, forfaitaire almost always wins for ICC.** But effectifs can still win for IFD (meals allowed regardless of commute).

### Case F / Case G on salary certificate
- **Case F checked:** Employer provides free meals → NO meal deduction
- **Case G checked:** Employer contributes to meals → reduced deduction (7.50/day)
- **Neither checked:** Full meal deduction (15/day)

## Télétravail (Work From Home)
Geneva is very strict:
- Need a **dedicated room used ONLY for work** (not a corner of the living room)
- Employer must **NOT provide a workspace** (rare for office workers)
- On WFH days: can't claim meals or transport
- **Tip:** If you WFH part-time but don't have a dedicated room, declare going to office every day. You lose the WFH deduction (which is 0 anyway) but keep the full meal deduction.

## Dual-Income Deduction (Déduction gain époux)
Auto-calculated by GeTax once both spouses' incomes are entered:
- **ICC:** CHF 1'051
- **IFD:** 50% of lower net income, min 8'600, max 14'100

## Transport Tips
- Public transport pass: declare **actual cost paid** (after any commune subsidy)
- If commune subsidizes your TPG pass, only the amount you paid is deductible
- Max ICC transport deduction: CHF 534 (public transport >2h daily)
- Max IFD transport deduction: CHF 3'300
