# Research Brief — Deep Mode Protocol

When Depth = Deep, the Moderator executes this phase before any Fellow speaks.

## Purpose

Ground the debate in real data before it starts. Fellows should not argue from assumptions when actual information is available. The Research Brief ensures the debate is about interpretation of facts, not invention of them.

## Execution

### Step 1 — Identify Key Questions

From the topic and mode, identify 3–5 questions whose answers would meaningfully affect the debate. Prioritize:
- Market/competitive landscape questions (who else is doing this, what happened to them)
- Data questions (size, growth, benchmarks, unit economics)
- Technical questions (what's known about feasibility, prior attempts)
- Customer behavior questions (what do real users actually do)

### Step 2 — Research

Run web searches for each question. Prioritize:
- Specific data points with dates and sources
- Case studies of similar decisions (what happened when someone tried this)
- Recent developments (last 12 months) that change the context
- Dissenting views or failure modes that are documented

### Step 3 — Compile the Brief

Format:

```
## 📋 RESEARCH BRIEF
**Topic:** [one-sentence summary]
**Depth:** Deep | **Mode:** [Deliberate/Brainstorm/Review]
**Research conducted:** [timestamp]

### Key Findings
- [specific finding] — [source/date]
- [specific finding] — [source/date]
- [specific finding] — [source/date]
- [specific finding] — [source/date]

### What's Contested
[Areas where data conflicts, experts disagree, or the evidence is genuinely mixed]

### What's Unknown
[Important questions that couldn't be answered from available data — these often become crux conditions]

### Domain Recon (if user is outside their domain)
[3–5 things insiders know that outsiders always get wrong in this space]
```

## Quality Rules

- Every finding must have a source/date. "Studies show" is not acceptable.
- If you can't find data on something, say so explicitly — don't fill gaps with plausible-sounding estimates.
- The brief should take a position on which findings are most relevant to the debate, not just list everything found.
- Maximum length: 400 words. Dense, no filler.
