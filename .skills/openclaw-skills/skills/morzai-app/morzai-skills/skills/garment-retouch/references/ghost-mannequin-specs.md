# Ghost Mannequin Specs

This guide defines what a professional ghost-mannequin result should preserve and reconstruct for ecommerce apparel photography.

---

## Core goal

A ghost-mannequin result should remove the mannequin or hanger while preserving the garment's internal structure, silhouette, and realism so the final image still looks physically believable.

---

## Reconstruction priorities

### 1. Neckline and collar interior
- Rebuild hidden neckline interiors naturally.
- Maintain believable fabric thickness and collar roll.
- Avoid hollow black voids or over-smoothed fill.

### 2. Sleeve openings and armholes
- Restore sleeve interiors when the mannequin blocked them.
- Keep cuff shape and seam direction consistent.
- Avoid warped or mirrored fabric texture.

### 3. Torso volume and silhouette
- Preserve the original fit impression.
- Keep the garment centered and balanced.
- Avoid over-tightening or over-inflating the body shape.

### 4. Hem and side seams
- Keep hems straight and plausible.
- Preserve seam continuity and fabric tension.
- Avoid broken edge geometry or abrupt shape jumps.

---

## Quality checklist

A strong ghost-mannequin result should:
- look like a real empty garment photographed in studio conditions
- preserve visible construction and stitch logic
- keep shadows subtle and believable
- avoid obvious AI fill artifacts in previously occluded areas

---

## Failure signs

Watch for:
- floating collars
- collapsed neck interiors
- asymmetrical sleeves without product reason
- texture cloning or repeated fabric patterns
- shape distortion around armholes or side seams

---

## Agent implication

When the user asks for ghost-mannequin cleanup:
- prioritize structural realism over aggressive cleanup speed
- use stronger reconstruction only when the mannequin blocks major garment interiors
- if the garment is simple and only minor cleanup is needed, avoid over-processing
