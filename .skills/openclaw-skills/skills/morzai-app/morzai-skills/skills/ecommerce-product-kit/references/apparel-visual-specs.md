# Apparel Visual Specs

This guide defines the visual standards that should be preserved when generating ecommerce images for apparel, accessories, and fashion-adjacent product categories.

---

## 1. Core visual rules

| Element | Preferred direction | Anti-patterns |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lighting** | Use controlled side light, soft diffused light, or balanced studio light that preserves real garment color and surface detail. | Harsh direct light, flat shadowless lighting, extreme color casts that distort the product. |
| **Texture** | Preserve visible fabric character such as silk sheen, cotton grain, knit softness, and wool texture. | Plastic-looking fabric, oversmoothed surfaces, stretched or broken texture detail. |
| **Wrinkles** | Keep natural folds that communicate drape and structure while removing chaotic or distracting wrinkles. | Paper-flat garments, deep folding damage, or excessive creasing that looks unprepared. |
| **Cutout edges** | Edges must feel clean and natural. Shadows should support realism, not create halos. | Jagged edges, white fringe, floating garments, unrealistic hard-edge shadows. |

---

## 2. Category mapping

### Premium business / tailoring
- Low-saturation, cool-neutral, or darker palettes work well.
- Stable compositions and generous negative space reinforce quality.
- Typography should feel refined, serious, and editorial.

### Outdoor / athletic
- Use natural light, fresh contrast, and energetic directional framing.
- Functional details such as waterproofing, breathability, or motion-readiness should read clearly.
- Scenes can feel active, but the garment still needs to remain legible.

### Lifestyle / minimalist fashion
- Warm neutral palettes, soft contrast, and gentle daylight work well.
- Domestic or natural lifestyle context can support emotional resonance.
- The overall result should feel calm, tactile, and credible rather than overly staged.

---

## 3. Model and try-on guidance

- Body proportions, garment fit, and fold behavior must remain believable.
- Skin, fabric, and silhouette should all feel realistic in the same frame.
- Avoid over-retouched beauty styling that makes the garment feel less trustworthy.
- If the user wants fit confidence, show shape and drape clearly rather than hiding them with props or extreme poses.

---

## 4. Agent implications

When the product is apparel-related:
- prioritize realism of fabric, cut, and drape
- keep product color accurate
- avoid overdesigned effects that weaken trust
- use this file together with `platform-best-practices.md` and `output-spec.md`
