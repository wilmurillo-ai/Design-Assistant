# De-Wrinkle Mapping

This guide maps wrinkle severity and garment condition to practical adjustment strength so wrinkle removal stays realistic instead of over-processed.

---

## Severity mapping

| Garment condition | Recommended setting direction | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Minor surface wrinkles | Low to medium | Preserve natural fabric life while cleaning micro-creases |
| Moderate fold wrinkles | Medium | Good default for garments unpacked from shipping or storage |
| Deep transport or fold creases | Medium to high | Use with caution to avoid flattening structure |
| Premium polished studio finish | High with protection | Combine with edge protection and object protection |

---

## Parameter guidance

### Fine wrinkles
- Best for subtle surface cleanup.
- Should not erase the garment's natural drape.

### Deep wrinkles
- Use only when obvious fold lines or shipping creases reduce sellability.
- Avoid pushing too high on textured or soft fabric that needs natural volume.

### Edge smoothing
- Helpful when the garment outline looks wavy or collapsed.
- Should improve silhouette clarity without making edges artificial.

### Objects protection
- Keep enabled when buttons, zippers, buckles, or hardware are present.
- Prevents distortion around non-fabric elements.

---

## Fabric caution

### Soft fabrics
- Use lighter wrinkle removal to keep natural softness.

### Structured fabrics
- Can tolerate stronger smoothing if shape clarity is important.

### Textured fabrics
- Be careful not to blur weave, ribbing, or raised surface detail.

---

## Agent implication

- Start conservatively for premium garments where realism matters.
- Increase strength only when wrinkles clearly damage ecommerce readability.
- Treat wrinkle removal and silhouette repair as related but separate goals.
