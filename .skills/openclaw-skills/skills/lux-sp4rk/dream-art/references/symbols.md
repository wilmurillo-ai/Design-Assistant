# Dream Symbol Reference

Common dream symbols and their creative potential. Use as interpretive grounding, not fixed meanings — dreams resist reduction.

---

## Water
- Oceans, lakes, flooding → emotional state, subconscious depth
- Drowning → overwhelm, suppressed feeling
- Clear vs murky water → clarity or confusion of emotion
- Rain → renewal or melancholy; tears

## Houses/Buildings
- Large unfamiliar houses → psyche, self-exploration
- Stairs → transitions, progress/blocks
- Basement → repressed content, shadow material
- Attic → memory, things stored but not forgotten
- Rooms → different aspects of self; locked rooms = avoided truths

## Vehicles
- Car driving itself → lack of control, things happening to you
- Being in back seat while someone drives → passenger syndrome, lack of agency
- Crashes → fear of consequences, loss of control
- Trains/buses → predetermined path, social momentum

## Animals
- Dogs → loyalty, instinct; bite = warning
- Birds → freedom, messages, perspective from above
- Spiders → creativity (spinning) or fear/feeling trapped
- Snakes → transformation, fear, sexual energy
- Cats → independence, hidden things, sensuality

## People
- Unknown figures → unintegrated aspects of self
- Dead loved ones → processing grief OR the person filling a role, not the person themselves
- Children → potential, vulnerability, innocence
- Authority figures → internalized judgment, external pressure

## Falling
- Falling and never landing → anxiety, lack of grounding
- Falling and hitting → fear of failure, impact of consequences
- Controlled fall → surrender, letting go

## Flying
- Effortless → liberation, high confidence
- Struggling to gain height → ambition vs obstacle
- Fear of falling while flying → freedom tainted by anxiety

## Teeth/Falling
- Teeth falling out → insecurity about appearance or communication
- In visual art → striking image of vulnerability

## Doors/Thresholds
- Locked door → opportunity withheld, barrier
- Open door → invitation, new phase
- Walking through → transition, commitment to a direction

## Mirrors
- Seeing something wrong → confrontation with self-image
- No reflection → identity uncertainty, dissociation

## Creative Medium Mapping

| Emotional Frequency | Best Medium | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Dread, horror, unease | Image or Animation | Atmosphere is visual; sound intensifies |
| Longing, melancholy, beauty | Music (ambient) or Prose | Music does longing better than images |
| Awe, wonder, vastness | Image or Video | Scale needs visual confirmation |
| Confusion, disorientation | Prose | Language can hold contradiction |
| Grief, loss | Music or Prose | Musical mood communicates without words |
| Eroticism, desire | Prose or Image | Implicit > explicit |
| Playfulness, absurdity | Video or Image | Movement sells the joke |

## Prompts for Image Generation (dream content)

Tips for translating dream scenes to image prompts:
- Use painterly, cinematic, or surreal style keywords
- Time of day matters — dawn/dusk/darknight changes emotional temperature
- Include texture adjectives: weathered, soft, sharp, blurred
- Atmospheric terms: mist, haze, golden hour, thick air
- Material adjectives: rusted, luminous, translucent, decayed

**Example:**
Dream: A flooded library, books floating, one shelf above water.
Image prompt: "Cinematic, painterly, a flooded library with ancient books floating through murky green water, moonlight through broken roof, dust motes in the air, melancholy, surreal, soft focus edges, film grain"

## Notes on Dream Journaling

Dream journals work best with:
- Immediate capture (write before full wake)
- Present tense ("I am walking...")
- Sensory details (what you heard, felt, smelled)
- Emotional notes, not just events
- No editing in the moment — let it be raw

The richer the capture, the richer the creative fuel.
