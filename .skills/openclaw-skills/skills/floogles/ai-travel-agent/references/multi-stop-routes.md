# Multi-Stop Route Templates

Use when a user wants to travel around a country or region. These are starting points — always adapt to the user's vibe, duration, and preferences.

## How to Use
1. Match destination + duration to a template below
2. Adjust nights per stop based on total trip length
3. Note logical transport between stops (flight vs train vs bus)
4. Check if open-jaw makes sense (fly into A, out of B)

## Europe

### Japan (10–14 days)
**Classic route:** Tokyo → Kyoto → Osaka (→ Hiroshima day trip)
- Tokyo: 3–4 nights (city, food, neighbourhoods)
- Kyoto: 3 nights (temples, Arashiyama, Nara day trip)
- Osaka: 2–3 nights (food, nightlife, Hiroshima/Nara day trips)
- Transport: Shinkansen (bullet train) — book JR Pass in advance
- Fly into: TYO (NRT/HND) · Fly out: KIX (Osaka) — open-jaw recommended

**Extended (14–21 days):** Add Hakone (Mount Fuji views), Hiroshima/Miyajima, or Kanazawa

### Italy (7–14 days)
**Classic:** Rome → Florence → Venice
- Rome: 3–4 nights (Vatican, Colosseum, food)
- Florence: 2–3 nights (Uffizi, Tuscany day trips)
- Venice: 2 nights (walk, canals — don't over-stay)
- Transport: Trenitalia/Italo high-speed trains (1.5–2h each leg)
- Fly into: FCO (Rome) · Fly out: VCE (Venice) — open-jaw works well

**South Italy variant:** Naples → Amalfi Coast → Sicily
**North Italy variant:** Milan → Lake Como → Verona → Venice

### Spain (7–14 days)
**Classic:** Madrid → Seville → Granada → Barcelona
- Madrid: 2–3 nights (Prado, tapas, day trip to Toledo)
- Seville: 2 nights (Alcázar, flamenco)
- Granada: 1–2 nights (Alhambra — book tickets months ahead)
- Barcelona: 2–3 nights (Gaudí, food, beaches)
- Transport: AVE high-speed trains; consider internal flight Madrid→Barcelona
- Fly into: MAD · Fly out: BCN — open-jaw

**Shorter (7 days):** Madrid + Seville only, or Barcelona + Seville (fly between)

### Portugal (7–10 days)
**Classic:** Lisbon → Sintra (day trip) → Porto → Douro Valley
- Lisbon: 3 nights (Alfama, Belém, pastéis de nata)
- Sintra: day trip from Lisbon
- Porto: 2–3 nights (wine, bridges, tiles)
- Douro Valley: 1–2 nights if wine touring
- Transport: Comboios de Portugal trains (Lisbon→Porto ~3h); car hire for Douro

### Greece (7–14 days)
**Island hop:** Athens → Santorini → Mykonos (→ Crete)
- Athens: 2 nights (Acropolis, food)
- Santorini: 3–4 nights (caldera views, sunsets)
- Mykonos: 2–3 nights (beaches, nightlife)
- Transport: Aegean/Olympic Air internal flights (cheap, ~45min); or ferries (scenic, 2–5h)
- Fly into: ATH · Fly out: ATH or JMK (Mykonos)

**Alternative:** Athens + Crete only (less rushing)

### Croatia (7–10 days)
**Dalmatian Coast:** Dubrovnik → Split → Plitvice Lakes → Zagreb
- Dubrovnik: 2–3 nights (Old Town, Game of Thrones, beaches)
- Split: 2 nights (Diocletian's Palace, Hvar day trip)
- Plitvice: 1 night (waterfalls — worth the detour)
- Zagreb: 1 night (if flying out)
- Transport: Bus/car along coast; Plitvice requires car or organised tour
- Fly into: DBV (Dubrovnik) · Fly out: ZAG (Zagreb) — open-jaw

## Long Haul

### Thailand (10–14 days)
**Classic:** Bangkok → Chiang Mai → Islands (Koh Samui / Krabi)
- Bangkok: 2–3 nights (temples, street food, markets)
- Chiang Mai: 3 nights (temples, elephant sanctuary, cooking class)
- Islands: 4–5 nights (beach, relax — Nov–Feb dry season)
- Transport: Domestic flights (Air Asia, Nok Air — cheap); overnight trains Bangkok→Chiang Mai

### Vietnam (10–14 days)
**North to South:** Hanoi → Ha Long Bay → Hoi An → Ho Chi Minh City
- Hanoi: 2 nights (Old Quarter, street food)
- Ha Long Bay: 2 nights (overnight cruise — worth it)
- Hoi An: 3 nights (lanterns, tailors, beaches, food)
- Ho Chi Minh City: 2 nights (history, buzzing streets)
- Transport: Domestic flights (Vietnam Airlines, Vietjet — very cheap); VietnamAirlines Hanoi→Danang (near Hoi An)
- Fly into: HAN (Hanoi) · Fly out: SGN (Ho Chi Minh) — open-jaw essential

### Morocco (7–10 days)
**Classic circuit:** Marrakech → Fes → Sahara Desert → back
- Marrakech: 2–3 nights (medina, souks, food)
- Fes: 2 nights (oldest medina in the world)
- Sahara (Merzouga): 1–2 nights (camel trek, desert camp)
- Chefchaouen: 1 night (blue city — detour-worthy)
- Transport: CTM buses or private driver; hire car gives most flexibility
- Fly into/out of: RAK (Marrakech) — or RAK in, FEZ out

### USA — East Coast (10–14 days)
**Classic:** New York → Washington DC → Philadelphia (→ Boston)
- New York: 4–5 nights (Manhattan, Brooklyn)
- Philadelphia: 1 night (Liberty Bell, cheesesteak)
- Washington DC: 2–3 nights (museums, monuments — all free)
- Boston: 2 nights (history, seafood, Harvard)
- Transport: Amtrak Northeast Corridor (fast, scenic, no airport hassle)
- Fly into: JFK/EWR · Fly out: BOS — open-jaw

### Australia (14–21 days)
**East Coast:** Sydney → Melbourne → Cairns (→ Great Barrier Reef)
- Sydney: 4 nights (Opera House, beaches, Blue Mountains)
- Melbourne: 3 nights (coffee, food, art, day trip to Yarra Valley)
- Cairns: 4 nights (Great Barrier Reef, rainforest, Cape Tribulation)
- Transport: Domestic flights (Qantas, Jetstar, Virgin Australia — book early)
- Fly into: SYD · Fly out: CNS or MEL

## Itinerary Design Principles

- **Rule of 2 nights minimum:** One night in a place is rarely enough — recommend 2+ unless it's a day trip
- **Don't over-pack:** 3 stops in 10 days is comfortable; 5+ stops gets exhausting
- **Open-jaw is often cheaper and better:** Flying into one city and out of another avoids retracing steps
- **Build in buffer:** Don't schedule travel on the last day before flying home
- **Internal flights vs trains:** For legs under 3h, trains are often better (city centre to city centre, no airport faff). For 4h+, flights usually win.
- **Suggest day trips over extra stops:** Sometimes a day trip from a base is better than moving hotel (e.g. Nara from Kyoto, Sintra from Lisbon)
