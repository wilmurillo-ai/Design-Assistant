# People & Culture Playbook — Full Reference

## 1. Introduction and Philosophy

Principles:

- People first
- Psychological safety
- Continuous feedback
- Data-informed judgement
- Equity and belonging by default

Lifecycle:

- Attract, Onboard, Perform, Reward, Engage, Develop, Belong, Resolve, Include, Retain.

## 2. Recruiting and Talent Acquisition

### Core system

- Employer brand and EVP clarity
- Skills-based hiring and role competency maps
- Structured interviewing with scorecards
- Candidate experience SLAs and feedback loops
- Proactive sourcing and talent communities

### Recruiting KPIs

- Time to fill
- Quality of hire (90-day outcome)
- Offer acceptance rate
- Candidate NPS
- Source effectiveness and cost-per-quality-hire
- Pipeline diversity and referral mix

## 3. Onboarding Excellence

### 4C model

- Compliance
- Clarification
- Culture
- Connection

### 30/60/90 outcomes

- 30: absorb context and deliver first scoped output
- 60: contribute independently in team workflows
- 90: own outcomes and present forward plan

### Onboarding KPIs

- New hire satisfaction
- Time-to-productivity
- 90-day retention
- Manager and buddy completion rates

## 4. Performance Management

### Operating system

- Weekly 1:1s
- Quarterly growth reviews
- Goal clarity via OKRs/SMART
- Competency + outcomes assessment
- Calibration and bias safeguards

### Performance KPIs

- Goal attainment
- Feedback frequency and quality
- Calibration variance
- Performance fairness perception

## 5. Compensation and Benefits

### Design components

- Job architecture and leveling
- Salary bands and market benchmarks
- Pay equity audits
- Total rewards portfolio (health, flexibility, wellbeing, growth)
- Transparent communication on pay philosophy

### Compensation KPIs

- Pay equity ratios
- Offer competitiveness
- Benefits utilization
- Compensation fairness sentiment

## 6. Employee Engagement

### Drivers

- Meaning
- Autonomy
- Growth
- Recognition
- Belonging

### Engagement system

- Pulse surveys + annual deep survey
- Manager action plans per cycle
- Recognition rituals and manager capability programs

### Engagement KPIs

- Engagement score / eNPS
- Manager action completion
- Team-level variance and trend velocity

## 7. Training and Development

### Learning architecture

- 70-20-10 model
- IDP for all employees
- Role-based learning paths
- Leadership pipeline development
- Internal mobility and rotations

### Development KPIs

- IDP completion
- Internal mobility rate
- Learning hours and completion quality
- Promotion readiness and pipeline depth

## 8. Culture Building

### Cultural DNA

- Purpose
- Values
- Behaviours
- Rituals
- Stories

### Hybrid culture essentials

- Ritualized communication
- Deliberate social connection
- Transparent context sharing
- Periodic in-person relationship resets

## 9. Conflict Resolution

Framework:

1. Acknowledge
2. Listen
3. Understand interests
4. Co-create options
5. Commit and follow up

Manager enablement:

- difficult conversation training
- mediation protocols
- escalation paths and documentation standards

## 10. Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion

Embedding points:

- inclusive JDs
- structured interviews
- transparent promotion criteria
- inclusive meeting norms
- ERG support with business linkage

DEI KPIs:

- representation by level
- promotion equity
- pay equity
- belonging index
- retention by demographic

## 11. Retention Strategies

### Retention architecture

- stay interviews
- manager-quality interventions
- internal mobility
- proactive first-year support
- predictive attrition analytics
- alumni/boomerang strategy

Retention KPIs:

- voluntary attrition
- regrettable attrition
- first-year retention
- retention by manager/team segment

## 12. Measurement and Continuous Improvement

Quarterly loop:

1. Measure
2. Analyze root causes
3. Act on top 2–3 priorities
4. Review impact and iterate

Business-case lens:

- turnover cost
- productivity impact from engagement
- onboarding acceleration impact
- recruiting efficiency gains

## Practical Rule

Great cultures are not declared.
They are built through repeated, intentional operating decisions.
