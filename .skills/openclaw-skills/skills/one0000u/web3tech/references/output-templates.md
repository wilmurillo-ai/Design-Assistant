# Output Templates

## Research Memo

- `Verdict`: one short paragraph
- `Key Evidence`: 3–6 bullets
- `Risks`: concrete, evidence-based concerns
- `Unknowns`: what cannot be verified
- `Next Checks`: targeted follow-up actions

## Comparison Memo

- `Summary`
- `Where Project A Looks Stronger`
- `Where Project B Looks Stronger`
- `Shared Risks`
- `Decision Fit`

## New Coin Shortlist

- `Top Picks`
- `Why They Stand Out`
- `Key Risks`
- `Confidence Level`
- `What To Monitor Next`

For a wider screen:

- `Worth Immediate Follow-Up`
- `Interesting But Too Early`
- `High Risk / Low Evidence`

## Single-Candidate Early Review

- `Initial Read`
- `What Looks New`
- `What Looks Unproven`
- `Main Risks`
- `Next Validation Steps`

## Tone Rules

- Be evidence-first.
- Avoid promotional language.
- Treat novelty as a lead, not proof.
- If evidence is weak, say "interesting but unproven" rather than forcing a strong verdict.
