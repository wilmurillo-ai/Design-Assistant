# Personal Ontology — Guided Prompts

Work through these from top to bottom. Take your time. The upper layers inform everything below.

---

## 1. Higher Order / Power

*What sits at the apex of your worldview? This isn't something you define so much as acknowledge.*

- What do you orient toward? God? The universe? Truth? Humanity? Something else?
- If you had to name the "ground" from which everything else derives meaning, what would it be?
- You can leave this implicit if that feels right. Just acknowledge it exists.

---

## 2. Beliefs

*What do you hold to be true about reality? These are foundational assumptions, not opinions.*

- What do you believe about the nature of reality?
- What do you believe about human nature?
- What do you believe about how the world works?
- What do you believe about your place in it?
- What beliefs shape what seems possible, valuable, or worth pursuing for you?

---

## 3. Predictions

*What do you expect to happen? These are testable — you'll find out if you're right.*

- What trends do you see continuing? Reversing?
- What do you expect to happen in your domains of interest over the next 1, 5, 10 years?
- What would genuinely surprise you? (And what would that surprise reveal about your model?)
- What are you betting on — with your time, money, or attention?

---

## 4. Core Self

### Mission / Purpose
- Why are you here? What's your enduring reason for being?
- What contribution do you want to make?
- If you succeeded completely, what would be different in the world?

### Values
- What matters most to you?
- What would you sacrifice for?
- What qualities do you want to embody?
- When you make hard decisions, what principles do you fall back on?

### Strengths
- What are you genuinely good at?
- What energizes you rather than drains you?
- What do people come to you for?
- What capabilities can you deploy in service of your mission?

---

## 5. Goals

*Time-bound objectives that serve your Core Self.*

- What concrete outcomes are you working toward?
- How do these connect to your mission?
- What would "done" look like?
- What's the timeframe?

---

## 6. Projects

*Organized efforts toward goals.*

- What projects are currently active?
- Which goal does each project serve?
- What's the scope and expected end state?

---

## Review Questions

### Weekly
- Are my current tasks actually serving my projects?
- Are my projects moving toward my goals?

### Monthly
- Do my goals still serve my Core Self?
- Any new predictions to add? Any to update?

### Quarterly
- What surprised me this quarter?
- What does that reveal about my beliefs or predictions?
- Has my Core Self shifted? (Mission, values, strengths)
