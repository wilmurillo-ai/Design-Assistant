# The eBay Seller's Reference Guide

**A practical, no-fluff reference for listing and selling on eBay profitably.**

This guide covers fee math, title optimization, listing format decisions, return policy strategy, pricing methodology, and common mistakes that cost sellers money. It is designed for individual sellers and small businesses — not enterprise merchants. Whether you are listing your first item or your hundredth, use this as a checklist before every listing.

---

## Fee Structure

Understanding fees is essential for pricing correctly. eBay charges a Final Value Fee (percentage of total sale price including shipping) plus a fixed per-order fee.

| Category | Final Value Fee |
|---|---|
| Most categories | 13.25% |
| Clothing & Accessories | 12% |
| Guitars & Basses | 6% |
| Heavy Equipment | 3% |

**Per-order surcharge:** $0.30 per order, regardless of category.

### Quick Net-Profit Estimate

After fees, payment processing, and incidentals, assume you will keep roughly **83-85% of your sale price**. For fast mental math: **subtract 15-17% from the sale price to estimate your net proceeds.**

Example: You sell a camera lens for $300 total (item + shipping).
- Final Value Fee (13.25%): $39.75
- Per-order fee: $0.30
- **Net to you: ~$259.95**

Always factor in your actual shipping cost, packaging materials, and the cost of the item itself to determine true profit.

## Title Optimization

eBay titles have an **80-character maximum**. Every character counts because eBay's search engine (Cassini) matches buyer queries against your title. A well-crafted title is the single biggest driver of visibility.

### Title Formula

```
[Brand] [Model] [Key Specs] [Condition] [Notable Features] [Accessories Included]
```

### Rules

1. **Front-load searchable terms.** Put the brand and model number first. Buyers search by brand and model more than anything else.
2. **Include the model number exactly as buyers would type it.** "Sony SEL85F18" not just "Sony 85mm lens."
3. **Include color and size when applicable.** These are common search filters.
4. **No filler words.** Never use WOW, LOOK, L@@K, AMAZING DEAL, MUST SEE, or similar. They waste characters and make listings look unprofessional.
5. **No special characters for attention.** Asterisks, excessive punctuation, and all-caps hurt more than they help.
6. **Use every relevant character.** If you have space left, add keywords buyers might search for (e.g., "bundle," "with case," "mint").
7. **Spell everything correctly.** Misspellings mean your item will not appear in search results for the correct term.

### Good vs. Bad Examples

| Bad Title | Good Title |
|---|---|
| WOW!! Amazing iPad for sale L@@K | Apple iPad Air 2 64GB Space Gray Wi-Fi Tablet - Good Condition |
| Sony Lens - Great Deal!!! | Sony SEL85F18 85mm f/1.8 FE Mount Lens - Very Good w/ Hood |
| Nice Used Camera | Canon EOS R6 Mark II Mirrorless Camera Body - Low Shutter Count |

## Auction vs. Buy It Now Decision Guide

Choosing the wrong listing format leaves money on the table. Use this decision framework:

### Use Auction When

- The item is **rare, collectible, or one-of-a-kind** (vintage watches, limited edition sneakers, rare coins).
- You are **genuinely uncertain about market value** and want the market to set the price.
- You are a **new seller building feedback** — auctions at low starting prices attract buyers and build your review count.
- The item has **emotional appeal** that could drive competitive bidding (sports memorabilia, art).

### Use Buy It Now (Fixed Price) When

- The item is **common with a clear, established market price** (current-gen electronics, popular books, standard parts).
- You need a **guaranteed minimum price** — you are not willing to risk a low sale.
- The item is **high value (over $500)** — auction risk is too great; you do not want a $1,200 item selling for $400 because of a slow bidding week.
- You want **consistent, predictable sales** for inventory you restock regularly.

### Hybrid: Buy It Now with Best Offer

This is often the best of both worlds for used items in the $50-$500 range. Set your BIN price at the high end of recent sold comps, enable Best Offer, and set an auto-accept threshold at your minimum acceptable price. This lets motivated buyers negotiate while protecting your floor.

## Return Policy Impact on Visibility and Sales

eBay's search algorithm rewards seller-friendly return policies with higher placement in search results.

| Return Policy | Search Ranking Impact | Conversion Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 30-day free returns | Highest visibility | Highest conversion rate |
| 30-day buyer-pays returns | Moderate visibility | Moderate conversion |
| No returns | Lowest visibility | Lowest conversion |

### Recommendation

**Default to 30-day free returns.** This sounds risky, but in practice:

- Return rates on eBay are low (typically 5-10% for most categories).
- The conversion boost from higher search ranking and buyer confidence far outweighs the occasional return.
- eBay's algorithm actively suppresses no-return listings in search results.
- Buyers who see "free returns" are significantly more likely to purchase and less likely to open disputes.

The only exception: extremely niche, fragile, or hard-to-return items where a return would cost you more than the visibility benefit.

## Pricing Strategy

**The single most important pricing rule: base your price on SOLD listings, never on current asking prices.**

### Why Asking Prices Are Misleading

Active listings show what sellers *hope* to get, not what buyers actually pay. An item with 50 active listings at $200 and sold listings consistently at $120 is a $120 item — the $200 listings are just sitting there unsold.

### How to Research Pricing

1. **Search for your item on eBay.**
2. **Filter by "Sold Items"** (checkbox on the left sidebar, or use the advanced search).
3. **Look at the last 10-20 sold listings** in comparable condition.
4. **Calculate the average and note the range.**
5. **Price at or slightly below the average** for a quick sale, or at the average for a standard-timeline sale.

### Pricing Checklist

- Did you check SOLD prices, not asking prices?
- Did you filter for the same condition as your item?
- Did you account for eBay fees (15-17% off the top)?
- Did you include your actual shipping cost (or build it into the price)?
- Is your price competitive with the most recent 5-10 sold comps?

## Common Seller Traps to Avoid

These are the mistakes that cost new sellers the most money and cause the most frustration.

### 1. Underestimating Shipping Costs

**Weigh and measure your item BEFORE listing.** Use a kitchen scale and a tape measure. Include the weight of packaging materials. A 2-pound item in a box with bubble wrap and packing peanuts might ship as a 3-pound, dimensional-weight package. Check rates on pirateship.com or eBay's shipping calculator before setting your price.

### 2. Skipping Item Specifics

eBay's search engine uses item specifics (brand, model, size, color, etc.) for filtering. **Listings without item specifics get buried in search results.** Fill in every applicable field, even the optional ones. This is not optional in practice — it is required for visibility.

### 3. Pricing From Asking Prices Instead of Sold Listings

Covered above, but worth repeating: if you price based on what other sellers are asking, you will overprice your item and it will sit unsold. Always check sold comps.

### 4. Shipping Before Payment Clears

Never ship an item until the payment has fully cleared. Scammers use fake payment confirmations, unauthorized payment methods, and other tactics. Wait for eBay to confirm the funds are available before printing a label.

### 5. Using Stock Photos for Used Items

Buyers want to see the actual item they are buying, especially for used goods. Take clear photos of every side, any flaws, serial numbers, and included accessories. Stock photos on a used listing are a red flag for buyers and will reduce your conversion rate.

### 6. Ignoring Messages and Offers

Respond to buyer questions within a few hours. Quick response time builds trust and eBay tracks your response rate. Auto-decline offers that are insultingly low, but counter reasonable offers — most sales happen through negotiation on Best Offer listings.

### 7. Vague or Incomplete Descriptions

Describe the actual condition honestly. Mention every scratch, dent, missing accessory, and functional issue. Undisclosed flaws lead to returns, negative feedback, and eBay siding with the buyer in disputes. Honest descriptions protect you.

## Quick-Reference Checklist: Before You List

- [ ] Checked SOLD comps for pricing (not asking prices)
- [ ] Weighed and measured item with packaging
- [ ] Wrote title using all 80 characters with searchable keywords
- [ ] Took clear photos of the actual item (all sides, flaws, accessories)
- [ ] Filled in all item specifics (brand, model, size, color, etc.)
- [ ] Set 30-day free return policy
- [ ] Calculated net profit after fees (15-17%) and shipping
- [ ] Chose correct listing format (auction vs. BIN)
- [ ] Described condition honestly and completely
