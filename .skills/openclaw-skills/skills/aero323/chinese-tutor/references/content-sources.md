# Chinese Content Sources Guide

## Video Platforms

### Bilibili (B站)
- **URL**: bilibili.com
- **Best for**: HSK3+ learners. Educational content, vlogs, documentaries, tech explainers
- **Search strategy**: Use topic keywords + "科普" (popular science) or "教学" for educational content
- **Recommended channels/series**:
  - 科普类: 回形针PaperClip style explainers
  - 生活类: Daily vlogs with natural spoken Chinese
  - 美食类: 日食记, cooking channels with clear narration
  - 知识类: 半佛仙人, 硬核理财 style commentary
- **Learner tip**: Enable 弹幕 (danmu/bullet comments) OFF for beginners, ON for advanced learners to see colloquial reactions

### Douyin (抖音) / TikTok China
- **URL**: douyin.com
- **Best for**: HSK2+ learners. Short-form content (15s-3min), colloquial speech, trending expressions
- **Search strategy**: Use hashtag topics: #学中文 #中文教学 #日常生活
- **Content types**: Street interviews, food reviews, life hacks, comedy skits
- **Learner tip**: Great for picking up 口语 (spoken language) and internet slang, but speech is fast — suggest 0.75x speed for HSK2-3

### YouTube (Chinese creators)
- **Best for**: All levels. Many Chinese creators publish with subtitles
- **Search strategy**: "[topic] 中文" or look for channels like 李子柒 (cultural), CGTN (news)
- **Learner tip**: Use auto-generated Chinese subtitles + pause frequently

## Audio / Podcast Platforms

### 小宇宙 (Xiaoyuzhou)
- **URL**: xiaoyuzhoufm.com
- **Best for**: HSK4+ learners. Long-form conversation, intellectual topics
- **Recommended genres**: 商业 (business), 科技 (tech), 文化 (culture), 心理 (psychology)
- **Notable shows**: 
  - 《随机波动》— Culture & society commentary
  - 《商业就是这样》— Business stories
  - 《忽左忽右》— History & politics deep-dives
  - 《不合时宜》— Current affairs
- **Learner tip**: Podcast speech is slower and clearer than video — good bridge from textbook to real content

### 喜马拉雅 (Ximalaya)
- **URL**: ximalaya.com
- **Best for**: All levels. Audiobooks, storytelling, children's content for beginners
- **Search strategy**: Use level-appropriate keywords: "儿童故事" (HSK1-2), "有声书" (HSK3+), "新闻" (HSK4+)

### Apple Podcasts (Chinese shows)
- **Best for**: Learners outside China who can't access mainland apps
- **Search**: "学中文", "Chinese learning", or browse Chinese-language categories

## Reading Platforms

### 微信公众号 (WeChat Official Accounts)
- **Best for**: HSK3+ learners. High-quality long-form articles
- **How to access**: Search within WeChat app → 搜一搜 → 公众号
- **Recommended account types**:
  - 科技: 36氪, 极客公园, 虎嗅
  - 生活: 一条, 新世相, GQ实验室
  - 文化: 单读, 三联生活周刊, 看理想
  - 商业: 晚点LatePost, 商业周刊中文版
- **Learner tip**: Articles are well-edited — cleaner Chinese than social media posts

### 小红书 (Xiaohongshu / RED)
- **URL**: xiaohongshu.com
- **Best for**: HSK2+ learners. Short, casual posts with images
- **Content types**: Lifestyle tips, food reviews, travel guides, beauty, daily life
- **Search strategy**: Visual topics work best — 美食探店, 旅行攻略, 好物推荐
- **Learner tip**: Great for learning 网络用语 (internet slang) and casual writing style. Posts are short (200-500 chars) — perfect lesson size

### 微博 (Weibo)
- **URL**: weibo.com
- **Best for**: HSK2+ learners. Hot topics, celebrity culture, news commentary
- **Content types**: Hot search (热搜) topics, celebrity posts, news threads
- **Learner tip**: Comments section is goldmine for colloquial expressions. Hot search titles teach headline-style Chinese

### 知乎 (Zhihu)
- **URL**: zhihu.com
- **Best for**: HSK4+ learners. Q&A format, analytical writing
- **Content types**: In-depth answers on any topic, personal stories, professional insights
- **Search strategy**: Browse 热榜 (trending) or search specific topics
- **Learner tip**: Excellent for learning 书面语 (written/formal Chinese) and logical argumentation style

### 澎湃新闻 (The Paper)
- **URL**: thepaper.cn
- **Best for**: HSK5+ learners. Journalism, current affairs
- **Content types**: Investigative reporting, editorials, society news
- **Learner tip**: News Chinese uses specific vocabulary and sentence patterns — great for HSK5-6 prep

### 豆瓣 (Douban)
- **URL**: douban.com
- **Best for**: HSK3+ learners. Book/movie/music reviews, cultural discussion groups
- **Content types**: Reviews (书评/影评), group discussions (小组), personal diaries (日记)
- **Learner tip**: Reviews tend to be thoughtful and well-written — good model for Chinese composition

## Music

### 网易云音乐 (NetEase Cloud Music)
- **URL**: music.163.com
- **Best for**: All levels. Song lyrics as learning material
- **How to use**: Search songs → read lyrics panel → many songs have 热评 (top comments) worth reading
- **Genre guide for learners**:
  - HSK1-2: Children's songs (儿歌), simple pop
  - HSK3-4: Mainstream pop (周杰伦, 陈奕迅, 毛不易)
  - HSK5-6: Indie/folk (民谣), rap with wordplay, classical poetry set to music

### QQ音乐
- **URL**: y.qq.com
- **Best for**: Same as NetEase — different catalog, some exclusive content

## News

### General news for learners
- **Level-appropriate sources**:
  - HSK2-3: 人民网 headlines only, with vocab support
  - HSK4: BBC中文, VOA中文 (written for non-native audience — cleaner)
  - HSK5-6: 澎湃新闻, 财新, 南方周末 (authentic mainland journalism)

## Content Selection Criteria

When choosing content for a lesson:
1. **Relevance**: Match learner's stated interests
2. **Length**: Stay within level-appropriate character count (see Difficulty Calibration in SKILL.md)
3. **Freshness**: Prefer recent content (within last 6 months) to keep cultural context current
4. **Authenticity**: Must be real content from real platforms — never fabricate
5. **Richness**: Choose pieces that naturally contain 2-3 teachable grammar points and 5-8 new vocabulary items
6. **Cultural value**: Prefer content that opens a window into contemporary Chinese life, thinking, or humor

## Southeast Asian Learner Bridges

For learners from SEA countries, leverage linguistic and cultural connections to Chinese:

### Vietnamese (Tiếng Việt)
- **Sino-Vietnamese vocabulary**: ~60% of Vietnamese academic/formal vocabulary has Chinese roots
- Use cognate mapping: 学习 (xuéxí) → học tập, 大学 (dàxué) → đại học, 历史 (lìshǐ) → lịch sử
- Vietnamese is tonal (6 tones) — learners already understand tone concepts; compare tone systems directly
- Content bridge: Chinese-Vietnamese food culture overlap (phở vs 粉, bánh mì street food culture)

### Thai (ไทย)
- Thai has 5 tones — use this as advantage for learning Chinese tones
- Many Thai words have Chinese (Teochew/Hokkien) origins: ก๋วยเตี๋ยว (guǎy tiǎw) from 粿條
- Large Chinese-Thai community — cultural familiarity with 春节, 中秋 etc.
- Content bridge: Thai-Chinese drama crossovers, Chinese food in Thailand

### Indonesian / Malay (Bahasa Indonesia / Melayu)
- Chinese loanwords in Bahasa: tahu (豆腐), bakso (肉粽 variant), teh (茶)
- Large Chinese-Indonesian community with cultural knowledge
- Non-tonal language — tones need extra attention; more drilling on tone pairs
- Content bridge: Chinese-Indonesian food culture, business connections

### Filipino / Tagalog
- Hokkien-origin words in Filipino: kuya (哥), ate (阿姊), siopao (烧包), pancit (便食)
- Non-tonal — same tone drilling focus as Indonesian learners
- Content bridge: Chinese-Filipino cuisine, Chinatown culture

### Khmer, Lao, Burmese
- Less direct linguistic connection but strong cultural ties through Buddhism and trade
- Non-tonal (Khmer) or tonal (Burmese has 4 tones, Lao has 6) — adjust accordingly
- Content bridge: Mekong region shared culture, Chinese infrastructure/business presence in SEA
