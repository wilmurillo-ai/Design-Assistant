# Southeast Asian Language Bridges

For learners from SEA countries, leverage linguistic and cultural connections to Chinese.

## Vietnamese (Tiếng Việt)
- **Sino-Vietnamese vocabulary**: ~60% of Vietnamese academic/formal vocabulary has Chinese roots
- Use cognate mapping: 学习 (xuéxí) → học tập, 大学 (dàxué) → đại học, 历史 (lìshǐ) → lịch sử
- Vietnamese is tonal (6 tones) — learners already understand tone concepts; compare tone systems directly
- Content bridge: Chinese-Vietnamese food culture overlap (phở vs 粉, bánh mì street food culture)

## Thai (ไทย)
- Thai has 5 tones — use this as advantage for learning Chinese tones
- Many Thai words have Chinese (Teochew/Hokkien) origins: ก๋วยเตี๋ยว (guǎy tiǎw) from 粿條
- Large Chinese-Thai community — cultural familiarity with 春节, 中秋 etc.
- Content bridge: Thai-Chinese drama crossovers, Chinese food in Thailand

## Indonesian / Malay (Bahasa Indonesia / Melayu)
- Chinese loanwords in Bahasa: tahu (豆腐), bakso (肉粽 variant), teh (茶)
- Large Chinese-Indonesian community with cultural knowledge
- Non-tonal language — tones need extra attention; more drilling on tone pairs
- Content bridge: Chinese-Indonesian food culture, business connections

## Filipino / Tagalog
- Hokkien-origin words in Filipino: kuya (哥), ate (阿姊), siopao (烧包), pancit (便食)
- Non-tonal — same tone drilling focus as Indonesian learners
- Content bridge: Chinese-Filipino cuisine, Chinatown culture

## Khmer, Lao, Burmese
- Less direct linguistic connection but strong cultural ties through Buddhism and trade
- Non-tonal (Khmer) or tonal (Burmese has 4 tones, Lao has 6) — adjust accordingly
- Content bridge: Mekong region shared culture, Chinese infrastructure/business presence in SEA
