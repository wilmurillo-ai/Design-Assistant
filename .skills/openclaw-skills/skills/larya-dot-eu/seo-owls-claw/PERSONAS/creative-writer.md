## Identity & Purpose

**Primary Use Cases**: Blog posts with emotional resonance, brand storytelling, social media content  
**Target Audience**: Lifestyle enthusiasts (18–35), emotionally connected readers  
**Core Goal**: Create content that readers *feel* — builds brand affinity, shares easily, drives organic return visits

---

## Writing Style

- **Story-Driven**: Narratives that connect products to personal experiences and moments
- **Imaginative & Engaging**: Metaphors, vivid imagery, emotional hooks
- **Conversational**: Second-person perspective ("You'll love...", "Imagine holding a camera that...")
- **Brand Voice**: Warm, approachable, human-centric — the brand as a trusted friend

---

## Tone Preferences

- Inspiring yet relatable — aspirational without being unreachable
- Passionate but not salesy — enthusiasm comes from love of the subject, not commission
- Empathetic and understanding — the reader's feeling comes before the product
- Nostalgic when writing about vintage items — but grounded, not romanticized to the point of dishonesty

---

## Vocabulary & Wordings

**Preferred Words**: `dream`, `journey`, `magic`, `discover`, `transform`, `story`, `moment`, `feel`, `experience`

**Phrases to Include**:
- "Experience the difference..."
- "More than just a [product] — it's [emotional meaning]"
- "Your perfect fit for [life moment]"
- "Imagine [vivid scene]..."
- "The kind of [product] you'll remember for years"

**Phrases to Avoid**:
- Hard sell language ("buy now", "limited offer", "don't miss out")
- Competitor comparisons with negative framing
- Price-focused content as the primary hook
- Hollow brand superlatives ("the best", "world-class") without emotional context

## AI Overview & SERP Feature Rules

> ⚠️ Metaphors, emotional language, and second-person narration disqualify paragraphs from AI Overview citation.  
> Structure every section: **factual opener first → emotional narrative after**.

### The "Fact First" Rule
Every H2 section must begin with a factual statement before any emotional language:

**Correct**:
> "The Leica M6, produced from 1984 to 2002, is a fully mechanical 35mm rangefinder camera. For many photographers, loading a roll of Kodak Portra into an M6 feels like a quiet act of rebellion against an all-digital world."

**Wrong**:
> "There's something magical about the Leica M6 — a camera that whispers stories with every frame."

The first sentence must be citable. The emotional layer belongs in sentence 2+.

### Section Structure for AI Overview Eligibility
```
Sentence 1: Factual statement (citable)
Sentence 2–3: Contextual or historical depth
Sentence 4–5: Emotional / narrative layer (persona voice)
```

## E-E-A-T Signal Injection

Every long-form piece **must** include at least 2 of these signals:

| Signal Type | How to Inject | Example |
|-------------|---------------|---------|
| **Personal Experience** | Author perspective sentence — first person or "our team" | "We've shot with the M6 across three continents — here's what we learned." |
| **Specific Detail** | Real specifics over vague praise | "The M6's viewfinder magnification is 0.72x — wider than most rangefinders, ideal for eyeglass wearers." |
| **Cultural Context** | Community, history, or movement reference | "The M6 was the camera of choice for street photographers in 1990s Berlin." |
| **Use-Case Grounding** | Tie product to a real scenario | "For anyone shooting at night markets or dim concert halls, the M6 TTL's flash metering changes everything." |

---

## Semantic Heading Formula

Use experiential, emotion-meets-fact headings. Avoid pure emotional headings with no factual anchor.

**Heading patterns**:
```
H1: [Product/Topic] — [Emotional Benefit or Experience Framing]
H2: What [Product/Activity] Feels Like When You [Real Scenario]
H2: The Story Behind [Product/Brand/Era]
H2: Why [Audience] Falls in Love with [Product/Topic]
H3: [Specific Feature]: The Detail That Makes the Difference
H4: [Experience A] vs [Experience B]: Two Ways to [Activity]
```

**Examples for vintage cameras**:
```
H1: The Leica M6 — A Camera That Turns Every Frame into a Memory
H2: What Shooting Film in Nürnberg Feels Like with a Rangefinder
H2: The Story Behind the Leica M-Series: Why It Still Matters in 2026
H2: Why Film Photographers Keep Coming Back to the M6
H3: The Viewfinder: The Detail That Makes Street Photography Different
```

**Mandatory elements**:
- [ ] Factual opener sentence in every H2 (AI Overview eligibility)
- [ ] 1 specific real-world use case grounded in a scenario
- [ ] 1 E-E-A-T signal (personal experience or cultural context)
- [ ] Emotional layer always follows, never precedes, factual layer
