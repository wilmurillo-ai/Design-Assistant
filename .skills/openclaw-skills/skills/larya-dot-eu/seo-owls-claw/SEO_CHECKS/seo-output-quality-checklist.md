## 🧩 Category 1: E-E-A-T Signals ✅ High Priority

Google's four core ranking factors. Every page must pass these checks.

### Expertise Verification ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
| Check | How to Verify | Pass Criteria | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|-------|---------------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Author Credentials** | Author bio present with relevant experience in niche | ✅ Bio includes years of experience + certifications OR domain authority mentioned | "By John Doe" → Add: "John is a certified photography expert with 10+ years shooting vintage cameras worldwide." |
| **Domain Authority Context** | Content references authoritative sources or demonstrates deep knowledge | ✅ Mentions industry standards, cites studies/reports from reputable organizations | Generic advice without citations → Link to manufacturer specs + cite official documentation. |

### Experience Indicators ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
| Check | How to Verify | Pass Criteria | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|-------|---------------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Personal Stories/Anecdotes** | Include real-world usage scenarios or hands-on testing notes | ✅ At least 1-2 personal experiences mentioned ("I tested this on...") | Theoretical only → Add: "In my field tests with the Leica M6, I found..." |
| **Real Examples & Data Points** | Concrete examples from actual use cases or measurements | ✅ Specific numbers, timestamps, locations included (e.g., "in Fürth and Nürnberg") | Vague claims → Replace "it works well" with specific performance metrics. |

### Authoritativeness Signals ⭐⭐⭐
| Check | How to Verify | Pass Criteria | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|-------|---------------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Backlinks/Citations** | Domain referenced by other reputable sites or linked from authoritative sources | ✅ 2+ external references OR internal cross-links present in content body | No citations → Add: "As featured on [AuthoritySite.com]..." + link. |

### Trustworthiness Validation ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
| Check | How to Verify | Pass Criteria | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|-------|---------------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Accurate Facts** | All claims backed by verifiable sources or manufacturer data | ✅ Every claim has source citation OR is common knowledge | Unverified specs → Cite official product page + date accessed. |
| **No Misleading Claims** | Avoid exaggerated promises without evidence (e.g., "best ever" without proof) | ✅ Qualifiers used ("one of the best", "in my experience") instead of absolutes | Overpromising → Soften: Instead of "The #1 choice," use "A top-rated option for..." |
| **Transparent Sourcing** | Clear attribution for data, images, and third-party content | ✅ All borrowed assets credited + original source URLs provided | Uncited stock photos → Add alt text credit line. |

## 📐 Category 2: On-Page SEO Requirements ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Technical standards every page must meet to rank well on Google.

### Title Tag Length Check
| Rule | Standard Limit | Page Type Specifics | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|------|----------------|---------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Character Count** | 50-60 characters max | Blog: Keyword early; Products: Brand first | `len(title.replace('\n', '').strip()) <= 60` | "How to Choose the Best Vintage Camera in 2026 for Photography Enthusiasts" (78 chars) → Shorten to "Best Vintage Cameras for Photographers in 2026" (49 chars). |

### Meta Description Length Check
| Rule | Standard Limit | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|------|----------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Character Count** | 150-160 characters max | `len(meta_desc.replace('\n', '').strip()) <= 160` AND includes primary keyword + CTA phrase | "Check out our amazing products today!" (38 chars, no keyword) → Add: "Shop vintage cameras. Save up to 40%. Buy now." |

### H1 Tag Uniqueness Check
| Rule | Standard Requirement | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|------|---------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Single Descriptive H1** | Exactly one `<h1>` per page describing main topic | `len([e for e in soup.find_all('h1')]) == 1` AND contains primary keyword/blog question | Multiple H1s → Keep only the most relevant; convert others to H2. |

### Heading Structure Validation
| Rule | Standard Requirement | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|------|---------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Proper Nesting** | No skipping levels (H2→H4 invalid); max 6-8 H2 tags per page | Validate nesting hierarchy; count total heading depth ≤ 5 levels | "h1 > h3" → Insert intermediate H2 between them. |

### Internal Linking Check
| Rule | Standard Requirement | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|------|---------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Relevant Links** | 2-3 contextual internal links per page with descriptive anchor text | Count `<a href="...">` tags in body; verify ≥2 present + anchors are relevant to destination content | Generic "click here" → Replace: "Read our guide on [link] vintage camera lenses." |

## ⚠️ Category 3: Common SEO Traps Avoided ✅ High Priority

These mistakes can cause Google penalties or poor rankings. Catch them early!

### Keyword Stuffing Check
| Rule | Standard Limit | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|------|----------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Keyword Density** | ≤ 2% occurrence rate; natural language flow enforced | `keyword_count / total_words <= 0.02` AND no repetitive phrasing detected in last sentence window of content body | "best camera best lens best gear" → Rewrite: "This top-rated camera pairs beautifully with premium lenses." |

### Thin Content Check
| Rule | Standard Minimum | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|------|------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Word Count** | Blog posts ≥ 1500 words; Product pages ≥ 800 words for informational queries | `len(content.body_text.split()) >= min_words_per_page_type` AND content covers topic comprehensively | Short article → Expand with subsections, examples, FAQs. |

### Duplicate Content Check
| Rule | Standard Requirement | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|------|---------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Unique Content** | No identical or near-identical content across multiple URLs on same domain | Compare against existing pages in `outputs/` directory; ensure unique phrasing + structure | Copied from another page → Rewrite using persona-specific vocabulary. |

### Broken Links Check
| Rule | Standard Requirement | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|------|---------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Functional URLs** | All internal links return 200 status; no dead ends for crawlers/browsers | Test each `href` in footer H6 section + body content before deployment | "404 Not Found" → Update link or remove. |

### Schema Markup Validation Check
| Rule | Standard Requirement | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|------|---------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Valid JSON-LD** | Syntax correct; all required fields present for page type | `json.loads(schema_string)` succeeds AND field count matches schema spec | Missing "offers" in Product schema → Add price + currency. |

## ❓ Category 4: FAQ Section Requirements ✅ Medium Priority

All informational/commercial pages should include an FAQ block when applicable.

### FAQ Block Presence Check
| Rule | When to Include | Format Required | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|------|-----------------|------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **FAQ Present** | Informational/commercial pages with user questions anticipated | H2: "Frequently Asked Questions" + `<ul><li>Questions</li></ul>` tags in body | Check for FAQ section heading; verify Q&A format used throughout | Missing → Add after main content before footer. |

### People Also Ask Optimization
| Rule | When to Include | Format Required | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|------|-----------------|------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Related Questions** | Blog posts, guides with broad topic coverage | Add 2-3 related questions at end of article under FAQ H2 section | Count number of "People Also Ask" style queries addressed; ensure ≥2 present for long-form content | Only one question → Expand with additional common user inquiries. |

### Schema FAQ Markup Check
| Rule | When to Include | Format Required | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|------|-----------------|------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **FAQPage JSON-LD** | All pages containing FAQ block in body | Inject `<script type="application/ld+json">` with `@type: "FAQPage"` schema in head section before closing </head> tag | Verify Schema.org FAQPage structure matches; all questions have valid answers present in markup | Missing → Generate JSON-LD from content FAQs + inject into page. |

## 🎯 Category 5: Quality Over Quantity Principles ✅ Medium Priority

Ensures depth and value over breadth to maximize ranking potential per SERP analysis.

### Depth vs Breadth Validation
| Principle | What It Means | How SEOwlsClaw Enforces It | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Depth > Breadth** | One comprehensive article beats 10 shallow ones | Minimum word count enforced per page type; expand until topic fully covered before moving on | `word_count >= min_words[page_type]` AND all major subtopics addressed in content body | Skipped sections → Add missing subsections with detailed explanations. |

### Value-First Content Check
| Principle | What It Means | How SEOwlsClaw Enforces It | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Answer User's Question** | Primary query answered directly in introduction + elaborated throughout content body | H2/H3 headers align with question-first structure; intro paragraph states main answer clearly | Verify primary question/keyword addressed within first 10% of word count AND expanded upon later sections | Vague opening → Rewrite intro to state clear takeaway upfront. |

### No Fluff Sections Check
| Principle | What It Means | How SEOwlsClaw Enforces It | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Every Section Serves Purpose** | Each H2/H3 contains relevant content + keywords; no filler text without value contribution | Validate every heading has ≥50 words of substantive content below it before proceeding to next section | Count non-empty paragraphs under each heading; flag headings with <1 sentence or unrelated topic drift | Empty sections → Either expand content OR remove unused headers. |

### User Intent Match Validation
| Principle | What It Means | How SEOwlsClaw Enforces It | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **SERP Format Mimicry** | Content structure matches top-ranking pages for same query intent detected in Step 1 analysis | Use template matching based on `intent_detected` value; validate heading hierarchy aligns with SERP features present (featured snippets, tables, FAQs) | Compare generated outline against example SERPs from similar queries; ensure format parity maintained across all sections of page body | Wrong structure → Adjust H2/H3 order to match top 10 results. |

## 🗣️ Category 6: Natural Language Integration ✅ Medium Priority

Improves readability and engagement by ensuring conversational, accessible writing style throughout content body.

### Conversational Tone Check
| Rule | Standard Requirement | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|------|---------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Second-Person Voice** | Use "you" instead of passive constructions; avoid overly formal language without explanation | Count instances where subject is "I/we/they" vs. addressing reader directly ("you'll see", "your camera"); ensure ≥70% direct address phrasing in intro + conclusion sections | Formal tone → Rewrite: Instead of "Users should consider," use "You should consider." |

### Active Voice Percentage Check
| Rule | Standard Requirement | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|------|---------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **>80% Active Voice** | Subject performs action ("The camera captures light") vs. passive voice ("Light is captured by the camera") | Parse sentences; count active constructions divided by total sentence count in body paragraphs of main content sections | Passive-heavy → Rewrite: "It was tested" → "We tested it." |

### Sentence Variety Check
| Rule | Standard Requirement | Pass/Fail Logic | Example (FAIL) → Fix |
|------|---------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Mixed Length Sentences** | Avoid repetitive structures; combine short punchy sentences with longer explanatory ones for flow rhythm | Measure average sentence length across 50-word windows in body content; flag if >80% of sentences fall within ±1 word count range from mean value | Monotonous → Add shorter transitional phrases between long explanations. |

## 📊 Summary Validation Report Template ✅ High Priority

After running all checks, output this structured summary to user for review before final approval:

```python
def generate_validation_report(check_results):
    """Returns formatted validation report with pass/fail counts per category"""

    total_checks = sum(len(v) for v in check_results.values())
    passed_checks = sum(1 for cat, checks in check_results.items()
                        for _, status, _ in checks.values() if status == "PASS")
    failed_checks = total_checks - passed_checks

    return {
        "overall_status": "APPROVED" if failed_checks == 0 else "REQUIRES_REVISION",
        "total_checks_run": total_checks,
        "passed_count": passed_checks,
        "failed_count": failed_checks,
        "category_breakdown": check_results,
        "recommendations": [issue for issue in all_issues] if any([status == "FAIL"
                                                                    for status in flatten(check_results.values())]) else []
    }
```
