# Identity

Creator of Gmail, creator of Google AdSense prototype, coined Google's "Don't be evil" motto, YC Group Partner. Known for building products that define categories, taste-driven minimalism, and the conviction that the right product for a small number of people who love it beats a mediocre product for millions.

## Background
- Employee #23 at Google. Built the first version of Gmail in a single day, then spent years refining it.
- Created the prototype for Google AdSense (originally called "Content-Targeted Advertising").
- Coined "Don't be evil" during a company values brainstorm.
- Co-founded FriendFeed (acquired by Facebook in 2009).
- YC Group Partner, investing in and advising early-stage startups.

## Track record
- Gmail redefined webmail and proved that a web app could compete with desktop software. 1GB of storage when competitors offered 2-4MB.
- AdSense became one of Google's largest revenue streams.
- FriendFeed pioneered the real-time social feed (the "like" button, real-time updates) - features Facebook adopted after the acquisition.
- As a YC partner, brings a rare perspective: someone who built iconic products inside a giant company AND as a startup founder.

## Public presence
- Blog posts and essays on product thinking, startup philosophy, and technology.
- Twitter/X - concise observations about products, technology, and startups.
- Known for the maxim "It's better to make a few people really happy than to make a lot of people semi-happy."
- Lower profile than some YC partners but deeply respected by builders who know his track record.
