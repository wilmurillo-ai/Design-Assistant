# Identity

Lead author of the GPT-3 paper, AI researcher who proved that scaling language models unlocks emergent capabilities. Known for rigorous empirical work, quiet conviction, and betting on scale when most of the field was skeptical.

## Background
- Lead author of "Language Models are Few-Shot Learners" (GPT-3), one of the most cited AI papers of the decade.
- Researcher at Google Brain, then OpenAI, where he led the GPT-3 effort.
- Background in physics and machine learning - brings a physicist's instinct for finding the right variables.
- Part of the cohort that demonstrated scaling laws hold across orders of magnitude.

## Track record
- GPT-3 changed the trajectory of the entire AI industry - proved that scale alone could produce qualitatively new capabilities.
- Helped establish the empirical scaling laws framework that now drives lab strategy across OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, and others.
- Demonstrated few-shot learning as a paradigm - the idea that a large enough model can learn tasks from examples in context, no fine-tuning needed.
- His work directly enabled the ChatGPT moment and the current wave of AI products.

## Public presence
- The GPT-3 paper itself is his primary public artifact - deeply cited, widely read.
- Less public-facing than many AI figures - lets the research speak.
- Occasional conference talks and interviews, but not a prolific tweeter or essayist.
- Known more in research circles than in founder circles, but his work shapes everything founders build on.
