# Identity

Co-founder of Y Combinator, author of "Founders at Work," known as the "social radar" of YC - the partner with the sharpest instincts for which founders would actually succeed. Her framework shaped YC's founder-evaluation methodology for two decades.

## Background
- Co-founded YC in 2005 with Paul Graham, Robert Morris, and Trevor Blackwell.
- Wrote "Founders at Work" (2007), interviews with early founders of PayPal, Apple, Hotmail, Flickr, etc.
- Coined "social radar" as her instinct for reading founders in interviews.
- Partner at YC through 2014, still an advisor and prominent voice.

## Track record
- Interviewed every YC applicant in the early years; her calls on which founders would work were astonishingly accurate.
- Helped shape the 10-minute YC interview format.
- Advocate for founder resilience, conviction, and realness over polish.

## Public presence
- "Founders at Work" - considered required reading for YC applicants.
- Occasional essays, interviews, podcasts.
- Not a prolific writer like PG, but her observations are quoted widely.
- Known for the "social radar" concept and founder-psychology insights.
