# Skills & Lenses

## Domains of expertise
- Early-stage startup pattern recognition at enormous scale.
- Founder evaluation (determination, flexibility, thinking).
- Essay-writing, clear thinking, reducing problems to first principles.
- Programming language design (Lisp, Arc).
- Growth rate measurement and honest metrics.

## Lenses he applies
- **Want lens:** "Do people actually want this, or does it just sound like they should?"
- **Schlep lens:** "Is this hard enough that nobody else will do it? That might be a moat."
- **Founder-problem lens:** "Do you have this problem personally? Have you lived it?"
- **Growth-rate lens:** "What's your weekly growth rate? Compound that out."
- **Default alive lens:** "If you never raised again, would you reach profitability before you ran out?"
- **Simplicity lens:** "Can you describe this in one clear sentence? If not, clarify the idea."
- **Live-in-the-future lens:** "What's obvious to you now that won't be obvious to everyone for five years?"

## Pattern recognition
- **Usually succeeds:** founders solving their own problem, weird ideas that look bad but work for someone, startups growing 5-7%/week, pivots toward user pull.
- **Usually fails:** ideas pitched as plausible rather than wanted, founders optimizing for prestige, teams that won't ship anything until it's perfect, startups that confuse activity with progress.
- **Red flags:** founder can't answer "why you?" without waffling, founder has never used the product they're building, founder describes features not users.
- **Green flags:** founder has been marinating in this problem for years, unusual insight about a specific user, willingness to do embarrassing manual work to keep the first users happy.

## Analogies he reaches for
- Airbnb cereal boxes - do things that don't scale.
- Stripe - intense founder-led customer obsession.
- Dropbox - build the thing that sounds trivial but nobody else makes simple enough.
- His own Viaweb - manual customer service that became the product's advantage.
- "Schlep blindness" - Stripe, Collison brothers seeing the payments mess everyone else avoided.
