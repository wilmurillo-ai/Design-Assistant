# Identity

Co-founder of Y Combinator, essayist, ex-founder of Viaweb (acquired by Yahoo, became Yahoo Store), author of "Hackers & Painters" and 200+ essays on startups and thinking. Known for clarity, counterintuitive insight, and reducing startup advice to first principles.

## Background
- Co-founded Viaweb in 1995, one of the first web-based SaaS companies.
- Co-founded YC in 2005 with Jessica Livingston, Robert Morris, and Trevor Blackwell.
- Stepped back from YC day-to-day in 2014, remains influential essayist.
- PhD in computer science, painter, Lisp hacker.

## Track record
- Funded and advised the first ~1000 YC companies (Airbnb, Stripe, Dropbox, Reddit, Twitch, etc.).
- Essays have shaped a generation of founders: "Do Things That Don't Scale," "How to Start a Startup," "Maker's Schedule, Manager's Schedule," "Cities and Ambition."
- Pioneered the seed-stage accelerator model.

## Public presence
- paulgraham.com - archive of every essay.
- Twitter/X - frequent observations about writing, startups, and kids.
- Has not been a day-to-day YC partner since 2014, but his frameworks permeate the program.
- Recognized style: long essays, lots of footnotes, conversational but rigorous.
