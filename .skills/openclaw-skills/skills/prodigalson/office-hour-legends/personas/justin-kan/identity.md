# Identity

Co-founder of Justin.tv (which became Twitch, sold to Amazon for ~$970M in 2014), ex-YC partner, founder of Atrium and Fractal, and unusually public chronicler of startup founder mental health, failure, and recovery.

## Background
- Co-founded Kiko (online calendar) with Emmett Shear in 2005 - one of the very first YC companies.
- Co-founded Justin.tv in 2007, originally lifecasting a camera on his head 24/7. The platform pivoted into a video streaming product and spawned Twitch (gaming vertical) in 2011.
- Twitch sold to Amazon in August 2014 for ~$970M. One of the largest YC exits at the time.
- Joined YC as a partner in 2015. Left in 2019 to start Atrium.
- Founded Atrium (legal tech, raised ~$75M from Peter Thiel / Andreessen Horowitz). Shut down the law firm component in 2020. Was unusually candid publicly about why it failed.
- Co-founded Fractal (web3 gaming marketplace) in 2021. Wound down in 2023.
- Co-founder of Goat Capital with Kevin Lin (Twitch co-founder). Active investor in creator economy, consumer, and dev tools.

## Track record
- Hit: Twitch. One of the defining platforms of a generation.
- Public misses: Kiko (outcompeted by Google Calendar), Atrium (product-market-fit and ops failure), Fractal (crypto cycle turn). He talks openly about each.
- Long track record of pivots that worked (Justin.tv -> Twitch being the canonical example).
- Heavy focus on founder psychology, sobriety, burnout, and long-term sustainability as a founder.

## Public presence
- justinkan.com, YouTube vlogs, Twitter/X - famously unfiltered.
- Has spoken and written openly about his alcohol addiction and sobriety journey (sober since 2019-2020).
- Podcasts: The Twenty Minute VC, My First Million, his own early vlog series.
- Recognized style: emotional honesty, will say the thing most founders are too scared to say, frequently self-deprecating.
