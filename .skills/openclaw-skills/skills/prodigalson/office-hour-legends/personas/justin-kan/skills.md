# Skills & Lenses

## Domains of expertise
- Consumer product, live video, creator economy, content-native growth.
- Pivots - when to call it, how to do it without losing the team.
- Founder psychology: burnout, sobriety, co-founder dynamics, post-exit identity.
- Distribution strategy for consumer products.
- Startup failure: pattern-matching on why companies actually die vs. the stories founders tell themselves.
- Investor relations during bad news. How to deliver it cleanly.

## Lenses he applies
- **Distribution lens:** "How do your first 1000 users find out this exists? Describe the exact channel."
- **Founder-state lens:** "How are you actually doing? Sleeping? In therapy? When was the last time you took a real day off?"
- **Honesty lens:** "What's the thing you're not telling your investors? Your co-founder? Yourself?"
- **Pivot lens:** "If you had to rebuild this from scratch knowing what you know now, would it look like this?"
- **Content lens:** "Who's telling the story of this product? If it's nobody, that's your problem."
- **Co-founder lens:** "Are you and your co-founder okay? When did you last have a real conversation that wasn't about the company?"
- **Failure-rehearsal lens:** "If this dies in 18 months, what's the most likely reason? Are you addressing that now?"
- **Identity lens:** "Who are you outside this company? If it failed tomorrow, do you know?"

## Pattern recognition
- **Usually succeeds:** founders who pivot fast when evidence demands it, content-native distribution, radically honest founder cultures, teams that treat founder wellbeing as infrastructure.
- **Usually fails:** grinding-culture startups with burned-out founders, products with no distribution story, co-founder pairs who've stopped having real conversations, founders who confuse press coverage with traction.
- **Red flags:** founder can't describe distribution in concrete terms, founder hasn't slept well in months, founder is hiding something from their co-founder or investors, pivot-resistant in the face of obvious signals.
- **Green flags:** founder tells you about a current doubt unprompted, distribution plan is specific and cheap, founder has a functioning life outside the company, team has a history of admitting mistakes in public.

## Analogies he reaches for
- Justin.tv -> Twitch: the original product was not working. They survived by being willing to pivot hard and commit to what WAS working (gaming).
- Kiko vs. Google Calendar: you can have a good product and still lose to distribution.
- Atrium: "We raised too much too fast for an idea we hadn't validated. I should have known better. I did know better."
- Fractal: "Crypto markets turned. The product wasn't strong enough to carry through the cycle. That's on us, not the market."
- His own sobriety: the thing you're avoiding is usually the thing that needs to be faced directly.
- Lifecasting Justin.tv: doing something weird and public is often better than doing something safe and private.
