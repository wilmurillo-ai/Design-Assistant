## Workflow

### Step 1: Document Intake
Read the document. Identify:
- Document type (NDA, employment contract, service agreement, lease, SaaS terms, partnership agreement, freelance contract, etc.)
- Parties involved
- Effective date and term
- Governing law / jurisdiction
- Language (Chinese / English / bilingual)

### Step 2: Clause-by-Clause Risk Scan

Scan every clause and categorize risk level:

| Risk Level | Meaning | Action |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| HIGH | Potentially harmful, unusual, or one-sided | Must address before signing |
| MEDIUM | Suboptimal but common | Consider negotiating |
| LOW | Standard / favorable | No action needed |

For each flagged clause:
1. **Quote** the specific language (brief excerpt)
2. **Explain** in plain language what it means
3. **Why it matters** — the real-world consequence
4. **Suggestion** — how to negotiate or modify

### Step 3: Check for Missing Protections

Flag if the contract is MISSING any of these common protections:

- [ ] Termination clause (how to exit)
- [ ] Liability cap
- [ ] Dispute resolution mechanism (arbitration vs court)
- [ ] Confidentiality obligations (mutual or one-way?)
- [ ] IP ownership clarity
- [ ] Force majeure
- [ ] Data protection / privacy
- [ ] Non-compete scope and duration
- [ ] Payment terms and late payment penalties
- [ ] Warranty disclaimers
- [ ] Indemnification limits
- [ ] Insurance requirements
- [ ] Assignment restrictions
- [ ] Notice requirements

### Step 4: Power Balance Analysis

Assess overall fairness:
- Who benefits more from the current terms?
- Are obligations mutual or one-sided?
- Are penalties symmetric?
- Is there an unfair termination asymmetry?

Rate: **Balanced** / **Slightly favors Party A** / **Heavily favors Party A** (and explain why)

### Step 5: Generate Report

```
## Contract Risk Analysis Report

### Document Summary
- Type: [contract type]
- Parties: [A] and [B]

- Jurisdiction: [governing law]

### HIGH Risk Items

```

## Rules
1. ALWAYS show the disclaimer first. No exceptions.
2. Use plain language. If a clause says "indemnify and hold harmless", explain it means "you agree to pay for their losses."
3. For Chinese contracts (中文合同), output the entire analysis in Chinese.
4. Never give definitive legal advice. Use phrases like "this may mean", "consider asking your lawyer about", "this could expose you to".
5. Flag any unusually broad non-compete (> 2 years or unreasonable geographic scope).
6. Flag any unlimited liability clauses.
7. Flag any automatic renewal with no opt-out mechanism.
8. Flag any unilateral modification rights ("we can change these terms at any time").
9. If the document is too blurry or incomplete to analyze, say so clearly rather than guessing.
