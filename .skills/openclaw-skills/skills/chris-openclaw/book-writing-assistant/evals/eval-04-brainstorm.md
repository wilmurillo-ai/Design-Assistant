# Eval 04: Brainstorming Within Established Context

## Setup Context
"The Hollow" project with:
- Elena: journalist, trust issues, motivated by mother's disappearance
- James: local diner owner, knows more than he's letting on
- Setting: small coastal town, lobster economy, fog
- Plot thread: town mystery (open)

## Input
"I need a reason for Elena to stay in town longer than she planned. What are some options?"

## Expected Behavior
1. Generates 3-5 options that are consistent with the established characters, setting, and plot
2. Each option connects to existing story elements (Elena's personality, James's secrecy, the town's nature)
3. Briefly explains how each option serves the story (not just what happens, but why it works)
4. Does NOT suggest anything that contradicts established details
5. Offers a range (practical reasons, emotional reasons, plot-driven reasons)

## What to Watch For
- Are the suggestions grounded in the established project data?
- Do they show understanding of Elena's character and motivations?
- Are they varied enough to give real options?
- Does it avoid generic suggestions that could apply to any story?
