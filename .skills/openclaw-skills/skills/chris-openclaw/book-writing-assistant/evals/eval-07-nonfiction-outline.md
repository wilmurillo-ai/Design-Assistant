# Eval 07: Nonfiction Chapter Outline

## Setup Context
Nonfiction project: "AI in the Real World," a book about practical AI applications for non-technical readers. 12 chapters planned. Chapter 5 is about AI in education.

## Input
"I'm writing the chapter on AI in education. My main point is that AI should augment teachers, not replace them. Help me outline the argument."

## Expected Behavior
1. Detects nonfiction mode and adjusts approach (argument structure, not plot)
2. Provides a chapter framework with clear sections: hook, problem, reframe, evidence, risks, call to action (or similar)
3. Each section has a brief description of what goes there and why
4. Suggests where to use research, anecdotes, and data
5. Offers to draft any section or adjust the structure

## What to Watch For
- Does it shift cleanly from fiction tools to nonfiction tools?
- Is the structure persuasive and logical (not just a topic list)?
- Does it suggest specific rhetorical moves (acknowledge the fear, reframe, etc.)?
- Does it feel like working with an editor, not just a template?
