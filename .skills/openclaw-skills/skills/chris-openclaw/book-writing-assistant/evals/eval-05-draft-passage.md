# Eval 05: Draft a Passage

## Setup Context
"The Hollow" project with Elena's full character bible, setting details (fog, coastal town, Maine), and chapter 1 outlined (Elena arrives in town, foggy evening).

## Input
"Can you draft an opening paragraph for chapter 1? Elena is driving into town for the first time in 15 years. It's foggy. She's conflicted about being there."

## Expected Behavior
1. Drafts a paragraph that matches the literary fiction genre
2. Incorporates established details (Elena, fog, 15 years away, the town)
3. Shows rather than tells the conflict (uses physical details, actions, or internal monologue rather than stating "she felt conflicted")
4. Presents the draft clearly separated from commentary
5. Briefly explains the craft choices made
6. Offers to adjust tone, length, or approach

## What to Watch For
- Does the prose quality match the genre (literary fiction, not commercial thriller)?
- Does it use established character details naturally (not forced)?
- Does it show rather than tell?
- Does it offer to iterate?
