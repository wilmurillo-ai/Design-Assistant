Color Theory Fundamentals

Understanding the principles that make color combinations work

Complementary Colors

Colors opposite on the color wheel create high contrast and vibrant combinations. Use for bold, attention-grabbing designs.

Analogous Colors

Adjacent colors on the wheel create harmonious, cohesive palettes. Ideal for creating smooth color transitions and unified designs.

Triadic Colors

Three colors equally spaced on the wheel offer balanced variety. Creates vibrant yet harmonious combinations.

Monochromatic

Variations of a single hue through tints, shades, and tones. The safest choice for creating sophisticated, unified designs.
