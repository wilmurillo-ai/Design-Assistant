# Review Rubric For Chain-Referenced Shortform

Use this file when gating candidates or explaining why a shot failed.

## Scoring order

Do not start from aesthetics. Start from survivability.

1. continuity
2. readability
3. cinematic strength
4. polish

## Master scene rubric

Pass only if all are true:

- identity is stable
- layout is legible
- lighting is stable
- fixed props are present
- the frame can support multiple follow-up shots
- the composition is not visually dead

Reject if the master scene is pretty but unusable as a continuity anchor.

## Shot-delta rubric

Pass only if all are true:

- the allowed delta is clear
- all locked facts remain locked
- the shot has one readable dramatic center
- the viewer can tell where the actor and key props are
- the camera choice matches the beat
- the shot can cut from the previous shot without confusion

Common fail patterns:

- new prop sneaks in
- pose changes but screen direction flips
- movement exists but has no staging logic
- the frame is technically sharp but narratively empty

## Bridge-frame rubric

Prefer frames with:

- low blur
- clear face or silhouette
- complete key props
- stable center of action
- usable eyeline
- end pose that can plausibly continue

Avoid frames where:

- limbs are mid-transition in unreadable ways
- the actor is occluded
- the prop is half visible
- the camera is turning too hard

## Final clip rubric

Review the clip for:

- identity stability across sampled frames
- prop continuity
- lighting continuity
- movement plausibility
- camera consistency
- editability

Do not approve a clip just because one frame looks good.

## Triage priorities

- If continuity fails, fix the packs or shot card.
- If readability fails, fix framing or blocking.
- If cinematic energy fails, fix shot size or movement.
- If polish fails, try model or settings changes last.
