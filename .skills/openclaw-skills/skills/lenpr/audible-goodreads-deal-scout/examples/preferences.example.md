I usually like fiction that is idea-driven, formally interesting, and emotionally controlled rather than sentimental.

Authors and books I tend to like:
- Ursula K. Le Guin
- George Orwell
- Kazuo Ishiguro
- David Mitchell
- literary science fiction
- political satire
- speculative fiction with philosophical weight

What I usually do not like:
- generic airport thrillers
- fantasy that is mostly lore and worldbuilding
- romance-forward plots
- books that rely on shock value more than ideas

When in doubt, I prefer:
- originality over comfort
- structure and voice over plot mechanics
- books that leave something to think about afterward
