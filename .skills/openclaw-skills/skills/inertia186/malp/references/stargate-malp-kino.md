# Stargate M.A.L.P. Reference

Source: https://stargate.fandom.com/wiki/Mobile_Analytic_Laboratory_Probe

## Overview

**Mobile Analytic Laboratory Probe (M.A.L.P.)** — a reconnaissance probe employed by Stargate Command and the Atlantis expedition to determine conditions on the other side of an active wormhole before sending a team through.

- Remote-controlled drone with instruments to determine safety of Stargate travel
- Monitors atmospheric conditions, transmits audio/video, confirms presence of functioning DHD
- Function originally held by F.R.E.D., but M.A.L.P. has superior scanning/analytical equipment

### Manufacturer
Tau'ri

### Affiliation
- Stargate Command
- Atlantis expedition

## Variants

### Mark I
- Small, tread-equipped vehicle with short grapple arm
- Used during first mission to Abydos and first three years of SGC
- Eventually phased out in favor of Mark II

### Mark II
- Much larger, longer grapple arm, conventional wheels (three per side) instead of treads
- More unwieldy but tougher than Mark I
- Used by Atlantis expedition

## Notable Uses

- Standard pre-mission reconnaissance through the Stargate
- Used as communication relay (Hammond quote: "We've been trying to raise him using the M.A.L.P as a relay")
- Atlantis team used a "MALP on a stick" (camera duct-taped to a stick) to see past a time dilation field (SGA: "Epiphany")
- Sent to P3W-451 during black hole incident; video came through seconds at a time due to time dilation (SG1: "A Matter of Time")
- Carter used wiring from abandoned MALP on P3W-451 to temporarily fix anti-gravity shield device
- Wraith Commander Todd gained access to a MALP to contact Atlantis (SGA: "Enemy at the Gate")

## Related Devices

- **F.R.E.D. (Field Remote Expeditionary Device)** — predecessor, less capable
- **UAV (Unmanned Aerial Vehicle)** — used when conditions are too hostile for MALP or team
- **Goa'uld reconnaissance probe** — their version of a MALP; equipped with plasma repeaters, defense shield, subspace transmitter
- **Kino** — Ancient equivalent, used on Destiny (SGU)

## Key Episodes
- SG1: "Enigma", "Urgo", "Entity", "Revisions", "The Sentinel", "Heroes Part 1", "A Matter of Time"
- SGA: "Rising", "Hide and Seek", "Epiphany", "Enemy at the Gate"

# Stargate Kino Reference

Source: https://stargate.fandom.com/wiki/Kino

## Overview

**Kino** — named by Eli Wallace after the Russian word "кино" (cinema). A sleek, softball-sized, self-levitating orb designed and built in mass quantities by the Ancients for use on the starship Destiny.

- Contains vast variety of sensors: atmospheric scanning, audio/video recording
- Controlled via Ancient remotes or Destiny's computers; also capable of self-guidance
- ~3.5" diameter, 11" outer circumference, 8" inner circumference

### Manufacturer
Ancients

### Affiliation
- Ancients
- Destiny expedition (Tau'ri)

## Comparison to MALP

"A Kino is comparable to a Mobile Analytic Laboratory Probe in its use as a probe sent through the Stargate to an unexplored world, but far more sophisticated."

Key advantages over MALP:
- Much smaller — Destiny carries significant supply without taking much space
- Can maneuver in areas a MALP could not
- Self-levitating — greater mobility
- Harder to target (smaller, harder to see)
- Capable of self-guidance (MALP is entirely remote-operated)

## Capabilities

- Automatically links with Destiny's computers when deployed through Stargate
- Records audio/video with finite memory
- Can follow preprogrammed course or standard search pattern
- Recognizes landmarks it's set to search for
- Withstands vacuum of space, close proximity to a star, extreme cold (< -47°C)
- Can carry several times own weight (dozens of Kinos + board = several hundred pounds)
- Night vision mode
- Susceptible to moisture (choppy video) and projectile weapon fire

## Kino Dispenser

Located in the Kino room on Destiny. As one Kino is taken, a new one is automatically generated (provided sufficient power and resources).

## Notable Uses

- Standard planetary reconnaissance through the Stargate
- Recording crew messages for posterity
- Exploring decompressed sections of Destiny
- **Kino sled** — Eli strapped dozens to a table to create anti-gravity cart for transporting ice from "Hoth" (SGU: "Water")
- **Time travel Kino** — damaged Kino found on jungle planet containing recording from alternate future; sent back through wormhole via solar flare (SGU: "Time")
- Used as surveillance during Col. Young's hearing (SGU: "Justice")
- Spying on Lucian Alliance after their takeover (SGU: "Incursion"/"Intervention")
- Col. Young used a Kino to move himself from Destiny's airlock to a derelict Ursini ship (SGU: "The Greater Good")

## Behind the Scenes

- Originally called **"HOVERMALP"** in the pilot's design
- Joseph Mallozzi suggested it should stand for "Kinetic Interactive Navigational Orb" (not officially adopted)
- Kino webisodes: 34 brief segments accompanying SGU Season 1

## Key Episodes
- SGU: "Air" Parts 1-3, "Darkness", "Light", "Water", "Time", "Earth", "Justice", "Incursion", "Intervention", "Awakening", "The Greater Good"
