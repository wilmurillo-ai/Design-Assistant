# Affiliate Performance Benchmarks

Use these benchmarks when analyzing program performance. Based on industry data and Affitor case studies.

## KPI Benchmarks by Channel

| Metric | Blog (SEO) | LinkedIn | X/Twitter | Reddit | Email | Paid Ads |
|--------|-----------|----------|-----------|--------|-------|----------|
| CTR (click-through) | 3-8% | 1-3% | 0.5-2% | 0.3-1% | 2-5% | 1-3% |
| Conversion Rate | 2-5% | 1.5-3% | 1-2% | 0.5-1.5% | 3-8% | 1-4% |
| EPC (earnings/click) | $0.50-2.00 | $0.30-1.50 | $0.10-0.80 | $0.10-0.50 | $0.50-3.00 | varies |

## Program Label Thresholds

Use these to assign Star/Cash Cow/Question Mark/Dog labels:

| Label | EPC | Volume (clicks/mo) | Action |
|-------|-----|---------------------|--------|
| **Star** ⭐ | ≥$0.50 | ≥200 | Double down — more content, more channels |
| **Cash Cow** 🐄 | $0.20-0.50 | ≥500 | Maintain — optimize CTAs, don't neglect |
| **Question Mark** ❓ | ≥$0.50 | <200 | Scale up — more traffic to this program |
| **Dog** 🐕 | <$0.20 | <200 | Phase out — redirect effort to Stars |

### Edge cases:
- **High volume, zero conversions**: Likely wrong audience or broken link. Investigate before labeling as Dog.
- **New program (<30 days data)**: Label as "New" — insufficient data for reliable labeling.
- **Only revenue data (no clicks)**: Cannot calculate EPC. Rank by revenue only, flag data gap.

## Conversion Rate by Product Type

| Product Type | Typical CR | Why |
|-------------|-----------|-----|
| Free trial SaaS | 3-8% | Low barrier — user tries for free |
| Paid SaaS ($20-50/mo) | 1-3% | Price resistance |
| Premium SaaS ($100+/mo) | 0.5-1.5% | High price needs nurture |
| One-time purchase | 2-5% | No commitment anxiety |
| Course/education | 1-3% | Impulse vs considered |

## S1 Scoring Feedback Loop

Performance data should calibrate S1 scoring weights over time:

- If programs with high Earning Potential score but low actual EPC → Earning Potential weight may be too high
- If programs with high Content Potential consistently outperform → Content Potential weight may need increase
- If Competition Level is the best predictor of actual success → increase Competition weight

**How to apply**: After running performance-report, compare S1 scores vs actual results. If scoring consistently over/under-predicts, note the pattern for future S1 runs.
