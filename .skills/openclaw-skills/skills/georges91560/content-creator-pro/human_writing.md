# Authentic Voice Reference — Content Creator Pro

The complete editorial craft guide for producing content with
genuine human voice and natural rhythm. Based on 2025-2026
journalistic standards and analysis of what separates authentic
writing from generic, flat content.

---

## What Makes Writing Feel Authentic (What the Agent Must Understand)

Readers and quality systems don't measure intent. They measure patterns.

```
PERPLEXITY — How predictable is the next word?
  AI text: low perplexity (each word is the statistically "safest" choice)
  Human text: higher perplexity (humans make unexpected word choices)
  Fix: choose surprising-but-correct words, not obvious ones

BURSTINESS — How much does sentence length vary?
  AI text: low burstiness (sentences are uniformly medium-length)
  Human text: high burstiness (short. Then a longer sentence that develops
              the idea across more space. Then short again.)
  Fix: deliberately alternate short and long sentences

STRUCTURAL REGULARITY — Does the pattern repeat?
  AI text: paragraph 1 looks like paragraph 2 looks like paragraph 3
  Human text: paragraphs have different shapes, starting points, lengths
  Fix: consciously break structural repetition
```

## The Complete Quality Blacklist

### Weak Words (generic vocabulary to eliminate)

**Overused generic vocabulary (still common):**
delve, tapestry, pivotal, underscore, testament, landscape,
meticulous, intricate, interplay, garner, bolstered, nuanced,
vibrant, foster, crucial, key, valuable, leverage, paradigm

**Recently overused vocabulary:**
align with, emphasizing, enhance, fostering, highlighting,
pivotal, showcasing, underscore, bolstered, enduring

**Currently overused vocabulary:**
emphasizing, enhance, highlighting, showcasing

**Always eliminate (timeless voice killers):**
it is worth noting, it is important to note, in conclusion,
in the realm of, when it comes to, having said that,
on the other hand, needless to say, as previously mentioned,
furthermore, moreover, additionally (as a paragraph opener),
this raises an important question, let's explore this further,
a comprehensive overview, robust framework, seamless integration,
game-changer, cutting-edge, revolutionize, streamline, harness,
unlock potential, empower, synergy

### Weak Structural Patterns

```
PATTERN: The Three-Part Parallel
  Bad: "This approach saves time, reduces costs, and increases revenue."
  Fine once. Never twice in the same piece.
  AI defaults to triples because it's clean. Humans don't structure
  their thoughts in perfect trios.

PATTERN: The Balance Structure
  Bad: "While X has advantages, it also has disadvantages. On one hand...
        On the other hand..."
  This "presenting both sides" structure is an AI tell because it's
  how models avoid taking positions. Take a position.

PATTERN: The Inserted Transition
  Bad: "This raises an important question. Let's explore this further."
  These sentences exist only to transition. They add nothing.
  Cut them. Just go to the next point.

PATTERN: The Summary Closer
  Bad: "In conclusion, we can see that X, Y, and Z all contribute to..."
  Never summarize at the end. The reader just read it.
  End with something that opens, not closes.

PATTERN: The Hedged Claim
  Bad: "It could be argued that..." / "Many experts suggest..."
  AI hedges everything because it can't be wrong.
  Make the claim directly. Own it. "This is wrong." "I disagree."

PATTERN: Uniform Paragraph Length
  Every paragraph being 3-4 sentences is an AI tell.
  One-sentence paragraphs are human.
  Eight-sentence paragraphs that build a complex point are human.
  Four sentences, four sentences, four sentences is not.
```

## What Human Writing Actually Looks Like

### Sentence-level signals

```
FRAGMENTS: Complete thoughts don't require complete sentences.
  "The solution? Stop posting more. Post better."
  Fragments feel like a thinking brain. Use them.

DASHES: The em dash — used mid-sentence — signals a real thought process.
  It shows the mind moving. AI uses commas. Humans use dashes.

CONTRACTIONS: "Don't" not "do not." "It's" not "it is."
  Formal writing without contractions feels institutional.
  Content should feel personal.

STARTING WITH "AND" OR "BUT":
  Teachers said not to. Good writers do it constantly.
  It sounds like conversation. And conversation converts.

REPETITION FOR EMPHASIS:
  "Work. Just work. Everything else is a distraction."
  AI avoids repetition (trained to vary vocabulary).
  Humans repeat when something matters.
```

### Paragraph-level signals

```
STRUCTURAL VARIETY:
  → One-sentence paragraph for impact.
  → Then a long paragraph that develops the nuance of that impact
     through a specific example drawn from real experience.
  → Then medium length.

EARNED TRANSITIONS:
  AI: "Additionally, there are other factors to consider."
  Human: "Here's what I didn't expect though:"
  Human: "Which is where it gets interesting:"
  Human: "The part that took me three months to figure out:"
  These feel like a real person making a real point, not a connector.

THE ASIDE:
  Using dashes or parentheses to add a thought mid-sentence (like this)
  is a human tell. AI rarely uses asides because they break the clean
  logical flow the model is trained to maintain.
```

### Article-level signals

```
TONAL VARIATION:
  AI maintains consistent neutral tone throughout.
  Humans get excited, get serious, get frustrated, get funny.
  The tone should shift with the subject matter.

INTELLECTUAL HONESTY:
  "I'm not sure about this yet."
  "I've changed my mind on this twice."
  "This worked for me — it might not work for you."
  AI is always confident. This is its biggest tell.
  Uncertainty signals a real human brain.

SPECIFICITY OF EXPERIENCE:
  "At 3am, after the third client cancellation that week..."
  This detail — the time, the number, the context — cannot be
  fabricated without it reading as fabricated.
  Real specificity is the hardest thing to fake.

OPINION WITH STAKES:
  "Most content advice is wrong."
  "This approach doesn't work and here's why:"
  Taking a position that someone could argue against is human.
  Presenting balanced perspectives on everything is AI.
```

## The Rewrite Protocol

When a piece feels like AI, the agent applies this protocol:

```
STEP 1 — SENTENCE AUDIT
  Read every sentence. Mark the ones that feel "clean" or "smooth."
  Smooth = AI. Rewrite marked sentences with more friction.

STEP 2 — TRANSITION HUNT
  Find every transition word: Additionally, Moreover, Furthermore,
  In conclusion, This raises, Let's explore.
  Delete them. Replace with earned transitions or nothing.

STEP 3 — OPINION INJECTION
  Find every hedged claim. "It could be argued" → "I argue."
  Find every balanced statement. Pick a side.
  One strong opinion per piece is the minimum.

STEP 4 — SPECIFICITY PASS
  Find every general statement. Add a specific number, time, or detail.
  "Many clients" → "11 of the 14 clients I spoke with last quarter"
  "Recently" → "Three weeks ago on a Tuesday"

STEP 5 — RHYTHM CHECK
  Count syllables of consecutive sentences.
  If they're all roughly the same → rewrite for variation.
  Read it aloud. Where does it sound robotic? Rewrite that part.

STEP 6 — FRAGMENT TEST
  Find the 3 most important sentences in the piece.
  Can one of them become a fragment for emphasis?
  Apply. The rhythm change signals a human hand.
```

## Platform Voice Adaptation

The agent adapts the same core idea to different platforms:

```
TWITTER / X
  Voice: punchy, direct, slightly informal
  Sentence length: very short in the hook, can expand in body
  Tone: confident, sometimes provocative
  What works: data, contrarian takes, personal results
  What fails: corporate-speak, hedging, being nice

LINKEDIN
  Voice: professional but human, not corporate
  Sentence length: hook is short, body can breathe
  Tone: honest, experienced, occasionally vulnerable
  What works: personal stories, lessons, real numbers
  What fails: promotional content, generic advice, fake positivity

REDDIT
  Voice: peer-to-peer, genuinely helpful, no ego
  Sentence length: normal prose
  Tone: like talking to someone who has more experience than you
        but isn't trying to impress you
  What works: specific answers, admitting what you don't know,
              actually helping without pitching
  What fails: any promotional intent (even subtle)
              anything that reads like it was written for a blog post

SUBSTACK / NEWSLETTER
  Voice: like a smart friend writing you a letter
  Sentence length: full prose, allowed to breathe
  Tone: intimate, direct, assumes the reader is intelligent
  What works: deep dives, original takes, personal experience
  What fails: listicles, surface-level advice, generic overviews
```

## The Voice.md File — How to Fill It

The agent reads this file before every piece of content.
If it doesn't exist → creates it with the default template.
The principal should customize it to match their actual voice.

```markdown
# Brand Voice — [Name or Business]

## Tone
[2-3 sentences: how do you actually sound? Direct? Warm? Analytical?]

## Identity pillars (what we own)
- [Topic 1]
- [Topic 2]
- [Topic 3]

## What we never say
- [Thing 1 — specific, not generic]
- [Thing 2]

## Signature moves
- [Something specific about how you write]
- [A recurring theme or reference point]

## Proof over claims
- [How you typically show evidence]
- [What your proof looks like — screenshots? Numbers? Stories?]

## Recent context
[Any recent events, results, or developments the agent should know about
when writing. Update this weekly.]
```

## Editorial Review — Before Any Post Goes Live

```
MINIMUM STANDARD:
  The piece must pass the "stranger test":
  If someone who doesn't know you read this, would they think
  "this is a real person with actual experience" or
  "this is generated content"?

  If the answer is the second → do not publish.
  Rewrite until it passes.

THE READ-ALOUD TEST:
  Read it out loud. Slowly.
  Any sentence where you stumble or where it sounds unnatural:
  Rewrite it.
  Your mouth knows before your brain does.

THE DELETE-ONE-SENTENCE TEST:
  What's the weakest sentence?
  Delete it. Does the piece improve?
  If yes → it should have been deleted.
  Do this until nothing can be cut without losing meaning.
```
