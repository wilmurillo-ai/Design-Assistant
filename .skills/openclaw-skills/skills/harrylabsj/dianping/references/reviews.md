# Review Analysis

Use this file when the user wants help reading Dianping reviews, judging whether a store is overhyped, or spotting review manipulation.

## Fast review-reading order

Read in this order:
1. recent 1–2 star reviews
2. recent 3-star reviews
3. photo reviews
4. recent 4–5 star reviews

This order reveals friction faster than reading praise first.

## What to trust more

Higher-value signals:
- specific dish names, room names, staff behavior, or concrete incidents
- mentions of queue time, waiting process, seating, booking friction
- follow-up comments after actual use
- user photos from different dates and angles
- comparisons with nearby stores

Lower-value signals:
- very short praise with no detail
- generic “很好/不错/推荐” comments
- repeated template wording across accounts
- praise focused on gifts or incentives instead of experience

## Red flags by category

### Restaurant
- recent complaints about queue chaos
- dishes inconsistent between visits
- service collapse at peak hours
- hygiene complaints
- big gap between menu photos and actual portions

### Coffee / dessert
- all praise is about environment and photos, not taste
- frequent complaints about crowded seating or poor drink quality
- high rating but low repeat-visit language

### Massage / beauty / hair salon
- strong upselling / forced add-ons
- hidden fees after arrival
- different treatment for group-buy customers
- staff attitude turns cold after refusing add-ons

### Entertainment / KTV / escape rooms
- old equipment not shown in listing photos
- poor soundproofing / dirty rooms
- booking disputes on weekends
- hidden minimum spend or time restrictions

## Signs of “刷评感”

Be cautious if you see:
- many short five-star comments posted close together
- nearly identical sentence structure across reviewers
- almost no mid-score reviews
- photos that look like provided marketing shots
- comments that mention benefits for reviewing

## How to summarize reviews

When answering, compress reviews into 3 buckets:
- **Strengths**: what consistently appears as good
- **Friction**: what repeatedly causes annoyance
- **Risk**: what could ruin the user’s specific use case

## Good output example

> 这家店不是不能去，而是“低峰期体验明显好于高峰期”。  
> 评论里真正稳定的优点是环境和招牌菜；真正反复出现的问题是排队、上菜慢和服务波动。  
> 如果你是专门去约会或请客，建议谨慎；如果只是普通吃一顿，错峰去问题不大。
