# Merchant Guide

Use this reference to compare merchant types on Chinese e-commerce platforms.

## Official flagship store
Best when:
- authenticity matters a lot
- after-sales support matters a lot
- the product is expensive or brand-sensitive

Trade-off:
- often not the absolute cheapest

## Brand-authorized store
Best when:
- the user wants stronger trust than a normal marketplace seller
- price is somewhat lower than official flagship channels

Trade-off:
- support quality may vary more than official flagship stores

## Self-operated retail
Best when:
- the platform itself handles fulfillment or service more directly
- the user values logistics and return convenience

Trade-off:
- not always the cheapest

## Marketplace seller
Best when:
- the product is low-risk or standardized
- the price gap is meaningful
- the user can tolerate more seller variation

Trade-off:
- trust and consistency vary more

## Factory-direct or wholesale-style seller
Best when:
- the user is price-sensitive
- the product is simple and not authenticity-sensitive
- minor packaging or service differences are acceptable

Trade-off:
- quality consistency and after-sales may be weaker

## Discount outlet or clearance channel
Best when:
- the user prioritizes savings on branded goods
- stock or seasonality variation is acceptable

Trade-off:
- size, variant, and return flexibility may be worse

## Final rule
The higher the product risk, the more merchant trust should matter.
