# Risk Thresholds

Use this reference to judge when paying more for a stronger merchant is worth it.

## Strongly prefer higher-trust merchants when
- the item is expensive
- authenticity matters
- warranty matters
- gifting or reliability matters
- return friction would be costly

## Lower-trust merchants may still be acceptable when
- the item is low-cost
- the product is standardized
- replacement risk is tolerable
- after-sales support is not very important
- the price gap is meaningful

## Final rule
A small price premium is often justified when failure, counterfeit risk, or after-sales friction would be costly.
