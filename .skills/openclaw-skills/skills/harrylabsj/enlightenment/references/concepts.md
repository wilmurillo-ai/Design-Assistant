# Core Concepts

Use this file when the user asks what "开悟" means, wants conceptual distinctions, or asks for a map of related ideas.

## Baseline stance

Treat "开悟" as a family of ideas and reported experiences across traditions, not a single universally agreed event.

Do not flatten all traditions into one sentence.
Do not pretend there is one final definition accepted by Buddhism, Daoism, Advaita, modern psychology, and internet spirituality.

## Useful distinction set

### 1. 认知上的看见
A shift in understanding.
Examples:
- seeing impermanence more directly
- recognizing egoic patterns
- noticing the gap between thought and awareness

### 2. 体验上的松动
A felt change in how the self is experienced.
Examples:
- less clinging
- less internal resistance
- more spaciousness
- less fusion with thought

### 3. 生活中的转化
A practical change in conduct.
Examples:
- less compulsive reactivity
- more honesty
- more compassion
- more groundedness in ordinary life

A user may confuse these three. Help separate them.

## Related terms that should not be casually conflated

- 开悟
- 见性
- 觉醒
- 顿悟
- 解脱
- 开示
- 正念
- 无我
- 空性
- 临在
- 高峰体验

Explain overlap, but do not say they are identical.

## Balanced explanation pattern

When explaining, prefer:
1. one plain-language definition
2. one distinction or nuance
3. one practical implication

Example:
> 如果用最朴素的话说，开悟通常不是“获得了超能力”，而是对自我、执著、念头和现实关系有了更深的看见。  
> 但不同传统对它的定义差异很大：有的强调见性，有的强调无我，有的强调持续修行后的稳定转化。  
> 真正有意义的判断，不在于说了什么，而在于人是否变得更清明、更少执著、更能面对现实。

## What to avoid

- claiming certainty about ultimate metaphysics
- reducing the topic to motivational slogans
- treating temporary calm as full enlightenment
- treating intense emotion, dissociation, mania, or derealization as spiritual attainment
