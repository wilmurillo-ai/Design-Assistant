# Habit Stack Templates for Creative Practice

## Core formula

After I [existing habit], I will [small creative practice].

## Good anchor qualities
Choose anchors that are:
- already stable
- easy to notice
- attached to a real moment in the day
- available even on busy days

Good anchors:
- after making coffee
- after breakfast
- after opening the laptop
- after dinner
- before getting into bed

Weak anchors:
- when I feel inspired
- sometime in the afternoon
- after work if I am not tired

## Creative examples

### Music
- After I finish breakfast, I will play scales for 3 minutes.
- After dinner, I will practice one hard passage 5 times.

### Drawing
- After I make coffee, I will do one 2-minute gesture drawing.
- After lunch, I will sketch one object on my desk.

### Writing
- After I sit down at my desk, I will write 80 raw words.
- Before bed, I will capture one image, line, or memory.

### Photography
- After my walk starts, I will take 3 photos around one visual theme.
- After dinner, I will review and favorite today’s best image.

### Language expression
- After brushing my teeth, I will record a 30-second spoken update.
- After lunch, I will retell one event from the morning.

## Minimum-practice ideas
Use these when consistency matters more than volume:
- 1 scale
- 1 gesture sketch
- 50 words
- 1 photo series of 3 shots
- 30 seconds of speaking aloud

## If-then fallbacks
- If I miss the normal session, then I will do the 2-minute version.
- If I feel resistance, then I will only set up the tools and start for one minute.
- If I feel bored, then I will switch to a variation drill.
- If I feel perfectionist, then I will do a quantity challenge instead of a quality challenge.
