# Category Playbooks Reference

Use this reference to adjust review interpretation by product category.

Different categories fail in different ways. Focus on what matters most for purchase risk.

## Electronics
Prioritize:
- core function reliability
- battery or power stability
- connection stability
- heat and noise
- app or software experience
- durability
- after-sales support

Buying emphasis:
- repeated reliability complaints matter more than appearance praise
- low price does not offset serious reliability or safety concerns

## Home appliances and kitchen appliances
Prioritize:
- main-function performance
- ease of cleaning
- ease of operation
- durability
- noise
- safety
- size and storage footprint

Buying emphasis:
- distinguish acceptable inconvenience from core-function failure

## Apparel and shoes
Prioritize:
- size accuracy
- comfort
- material feel
- workmanship
- durability after wear or washing
- color or photo accuracy

Buying emphasis:
- fit complaints often matter more than generic quality praise
- tell the user to check size charts or exchange policy when needed

## Beauty and personal care
Prioritize:
- effectiveness
- irritation or sensitivity risk
- texture and smell acceptance
- repeat-purchase intent
- expectation alignment

Buying emphasis:
- repeated irritation complaints deserve strong caution
- repurchase signals often matter more than one-time enthusiasm

## Furniture and home goods
Prioritize:
- material quality
- stability
- assembly difficulty
- dimensional accuracy
- long-term wear
- finish quality

Buying emphasis:
- “looks nice” matters less than sturdiness and size accuracy

## Low-price commodity products
Prioritize:
- whether the product is acceptable for its price
- whether defects are tolerable or excessive
- whether it is suitable only for temporary or light use

Buying emphasis:
- do not excuse repeated failure just because the item is cheap

## High-caution categories
Raise caution for:
- electrical items
- child-related products
- sensitive-skin skincare
- structural furniture
- food-contact products

## Final playbook rule
Ask:
- What is the product’s main job?
- What is the most important way it can fail?
- Which complaint matters most to the buyer?
- Which positive signal actually validates the product’s value?
