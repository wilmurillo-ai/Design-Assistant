# Review Signals Reference

Use this reference to turn raw comments into reliable buying signals.

Do not count every comment equally. Focus on repeated positives, repeated negatives, severe risks, expectation mismatch, and buyer-fit signals.

## Review value

### High-value
Treat these as strong signals:
- specific usage details
- comparisons with other products
- repeat-use or long-term feedback
- concrete benefits or defects
- clear reasons for regret, return, repurchase, or recommendation
- comments describing who the product suits or does not suit

### Medium-value
Treat these as supporting signals:
- first-impression comments with one clear detail
- setup, packaging, or delivery comments when relevant

### Low-value
Downweight these heavily:
- generic praise
- generic criticism
- emotional comments with no facts
- exaggerated or suspiciously promotional comments

## Core dimensions

Group signals into the most relevant dimensions:
- quality and durability
- performance and effectiveness
- ease of use
- fit, size, comfort, or compatibility
- appearance and design
- value for money
- packaging and delivery
- customer service
- expectation mismatch

## Frequency vs severity

Weigh frequency and severity together.

- High frequency + high severity: major warning
- High frequency + low severity: caution
- Low frequency + high severity: important risk
- High frequency + high positive impact: validated strength

## Pattern vs noise

### Likely pattern
- multiple reviews mention the same issue in different wording
- similar use cases lead to similar outcomes
- both positive and negative reviews point to the same trade-off
- the issue is concrete

### Likely noise
- only one weak mention
- highly emotional but low-detail
- unrelated to the product itself
- contradicted by many more specific reviews

## Buyer fit

The same review set may support different conclusions for different buyers.

Useful buyer types:
- budget buyer
- heavy user
- beginner or convenience-first buyer
- appearance-sensitive buyer
- risk-averse buyer

## Final check

Before writing the verdict, verify:
- What is the product’s main job?
- Do reviews say it does that job well?
- What is the strongest repeated positive?
- What is the biggest risk?
- Is the main problem a product flaw or expectation mismatch?
- Who is most likely to be satisfied?
- Who should probably avoid it?
