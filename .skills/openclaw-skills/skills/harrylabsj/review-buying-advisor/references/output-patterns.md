# Output Patterns Reference

Use these patterns to give practical buying advice after reading reviews.

The final answer must help the user decide, not merely summarize comments.

Choose the pattern that matches the current mode:
- **Mode 1** when public review evidence is available
- **Mode 2** when review access is blocked, weak, or incomplete

## Default output pattern

### Overall Verdict
Choose one:
- Recommend
- Recommend with caveats
- Depends on use case
- Not recommended
- Not enough evidence for a strong recommendation

### Why
2-4 short bullets with the strongest evidence.

### Best For
Who is most likely to be satisfied.

### Main Positives
Most credible repeated strengths.

### Main Risks
Most important risks, including repeated issues or severe but less frequent ones.

### Watch Before Buying
What to verify before ordering.

### Final Advice
A direct recommendation in plain language.

### Confidence
High / Medium / Low, with a brief reason.

## Mode 2 limited-access pattern

### Overall Verdict
Usually:
- Not enough evidence for a strong recommendation
- Depends on use case

### Why
1-3 short bullets explaining what evidence was not accessible.

### Current Limitation
State the limiting factor plainly.
Examples:
- platform verification page
- login wall
- public review text too weak
- product match still ambiguous

### Watch Before Buying
State what remains unverified.

### Final Advice
Give a cautious next-step recommendation without pretending the review picture is known.

### Confidence
Usually Low.

## Short decision pattern

### Verdict
[one line]

### Main reason
1-2 sentences with the biggest positive and biggest risk.

### Best for
One short line.

### Watch out
One short line.

### Final take
One direct sentence.

## Deep analysis pattern

### Verdict
State the conclusion clearly.

### What buyers consistently like
Repeated positives with short explanation.

### What buyers repeatedly complain about
Repeated negatives with short explanation.

### What looks like noise
Isolated or low-information complaints.

### What looks like a real pattern
Strong recurring signals.

### Who should buy it
Suitable buyer profiles.

### Who should skip it
Unsuitable buyer profiles.

### What to confirm before ordering
Practical checks.

### Final recommendation
Clear buying advice.

### Confidence
High / Medium / Low with a short justification.

## Verdict wording guide

### Recommend
Use when the main function is repeatedly validated and risks are manageable.

### Recommend with caveats
Use when the product works for some buyers but has meaningful trade-offs.

### Depends on use case
Use when suitability depends strongly on buyer type or expectations.

### Not recommended
Use when core complaints repeat or risk is too high.

### Not enough evidence for a strong recommendation
Use when review quantity, quality, access, or product identity is too weak for a firm call.
