# Product Matching Reference

Use this reference when deciding whether listings from different Chinese shopping platforms are comparable.

## Match on the most decision-relevant attributes
Prefer:
- brand
- exact model or series
- size, capacity, or color when they affect price materially
- quantity or pack count
- version or generation
- included accessories or bundle contents

## High-confidence match
Treat listings as highly comparable when:
- brand matches
- model or series matches
- core specs match
- quantity and bundle contents match

## Medium-confidence match
Proceed cautiously when:
- the title wording differs but model/spec clues align
- one listing is slightly less specific but still likely the same product
- seller wording is noisy but the product identity is mostly clear

## Low-confidence match
Do not present listings as directly comparable when:
- model is unclear
- one listing is a bundle and another is not
- quantity differs materially
- accessory set differs
- platform listing may refer to another generation or variant

If pack size, bundle contents, or included accessories differ materially, do not treat the listings as directly price-comparable.

## Clarification rule
If the product name is too broad, ask for the single most important missing detail first, such as brand, model, or size.

## Final rule
A narrower but honest comparison is better than a broad but wrong cheapest-price claim.
