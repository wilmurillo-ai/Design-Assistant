# Output Patterns Reference

Use these patterns to present a cross-platform cheapest-price result clearly.

Choose the pattern that matches the current mode:
- **Mode 1** when enough price evidence is accessible
- **Mode 2** when platform access, product matching, or price conditions are too weak

## Mode 1 default output

### Cheapest Found
State:
- platform
- visible price
- product or listing label

### Comparable Options
List 2-5 relevant alternatives with platform and visible price.

### Match Confidence
Choose one:
- High
- Medium
- Low

### Caveats
List important caveats such as:
- coupon-only or member-only pricing
- bundle differences
- uncertain variant match
- seller quality differences
- incomplete platform coverage

### Final Advice
Give a direct practical recommendation.

## Mode 2 limited-access output

### Current Best Signal
State the best still-usable price clue, if any.

### Why No Strong Cheapest Claim
Explain the limiting factor plainly.

### What Remains Unverified
State what cannot yet be confirmed.

### Final Advice
Give a cautious practical suggestion without pretending a full cheapest-price comparison succeeded.

## Short answer pattern
Use when the user only wants a quick answer.

- Cheapest found: ...
- Next best options: ...
- Caveat: ...
- Advice: ...
