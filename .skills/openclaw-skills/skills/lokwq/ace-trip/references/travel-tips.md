## Australian Open — Melbourne

### Visa
- Chinese passport holders need an Australian ETA (subclass 601) or visitor visa (subclass 600).
- Apply at least 4 weeks in advance. Use flyai: `flyai keyword-search --query "澳大利亚签证"`

### Weather (January)
- Southern hemisphere summer: 25–40°C, dry heat with occasional extreme heat days.
- **Must bring**: sunscreen SPF50+, hat, sunglasses. The venue has limited shade on outer courts.
- Heat policy: play may be suspended if temperatures exceed 40°C (organizers use a heat stress scale).

### Transport to venue
- Melbourne Park is walkable from the CBD (15 min from Flinders Street Station).
- Tram routes 70 and 75 stop at the venue. Trams are free within the CBD zone.
- **Do NOT drive** — parking is extremely limited during the tournament.

### Match-day tips
- Ground pass gives access to all courts except Rod Laver Arena, Margaret Court Arena, and John Cain Arena. Best value in early rounds.
- Gates open at 10:00 AM for day sessions, 5:00 PM for night sessions.
- Bring your own water bottle — free refill stations throughout the venue.
- The "Garden Square" area has live screenings, food trucks, and fan activities.

### Food & drink
- The venue food is expensive (AUD 15–25 per item). Eating in the CBD before entering saves money.
- Melbourne is Australia's coffee capital — flat whites are a must-try.
- Lygon Street (Little Italy) is 10 min from the venue for affordable dining.

### Best add-on experiences
- Great Ocean Road day trip (1 day, best done before or after the tournament).
- Philip Island penguin parade (half-day evening trip).
- Yarra Valley wine region (half-day).

## French Open — Paris

### Visa
- Chinese passport holders need a Schengen visa.
- Apply at the French consulate 6–8 weeks in advance. Book appointment early — peak season demand.
- Use flyai: `flyai keyword-search --query "法国签证"`

### Weather (late May – early June)
- Spring: 15–25°C, unpredictable rain showers.
- **Must bring**: light rain jacket, layers. Clay court play continues in light rain (unlike grass).
- Philippe Chatrier court has a retractable roof (installed 2020), but outer courts do not.

### Transport to venue
- Stade Roland Garros is in the 16th arrondissement.
- Métro line 9 (Porte d'Auteuil) or line 10 (Michel-Ange Auteuil).
- RER C to Avenue du Président Kennedy, then walk through Bois de Boulogne (scenic but 20 min).

### Match-day tips
- "Billet Découverte" (Discovery ticket) is the French equivalent of a ground pass — gives access to all courts except the three main show courts.
- Night sessions were introduced in 2021 — they start at 8:00 PM on Court Philippe Chatrier only.
- The venue is compact — you can walk between all courts in under 10 minutes.
- French crowds are knowledgeable and passionate. Expect cheering for French players to be very loud.

### Food & drink
- Venue food includes French options (crêpes, croque-monsieur) but is overpriced.
- The Bois de Boulogne park adjacent to the venue is perfect for a pre-match picnic. Buy supplies at a nearby boulangerie.
- Rue de Passy (5 min walk) has excellent cafés and bistros.

### Best add-on experiences
- Louvre and Musée d'Orsay (rainy day alternatives if matches are postponed).
- Versailles day trip (RER C, 40 min).
- Seine river cruise (evening, romantic option).
- Bois de Boulogne stroll — the park literally borders the venue.

## Wimbledon — London

### Visa
- Chinese passport holders need a UK Standard Visitor visa.
- Apply 8–12 weeks in advance — biometric appointment required.
- **Note**: UK is NOT part of Schengen. A Schengen visa does NOT cover the UK.
- Use flyai: `flyai keyword-search --query "英国签证"`

### Weather (late June – mid July)
- English summer: 15–25°C, frequent brief showers. Can change rapidly.
- **Must bring**: waterproof jacket (essential), umbrella, layers. Even on sunny days, evenings are cool.
- Centre Court has a retractable roof. Court No.1 also has a roof (since 2019). Outer courts have no cover.

### Transport to venue
- Wimbledon station: District line, or South Western Railway from Waterloo (20 min).
- From Wimbledon station, shuttle buses run to the grounds, or walk 15 min uphill through Wimbledon Village.
- Southfields station (District line) is slightly closer to Gate 3.

### Match-day tips
- **The Queue**: Wimbledon's famous tradition. Fans camp overnight for day-of-play tickets.
  - Arrive by 6:00 AM for a reasonable chance at show court tickets.
  - Arrive by 9:00 AM for ground pass only.
  - You receive a Queue Card with your position number. DO NOT lose it.
  - Camping equipment: tent, sleeping bag, folding chair (all allowed).
- Debenture tickets (premium, resold) are the only guaranteed way to get Centre Court seats without queueing. They are expensive (£500–2000+).
- **Dress code**: no specific dress code for spectators (that's for players), but the atmosphere is notably more formal than other Slams.
- Strawberries and cream is the iconic Wimbledon food — expect to pay ~£2.50 per portion.
- Play starts at 1:30 PM on Centre Court, 11:00 AM on other courts.
- Henman Hill / Murray Mound: the famous hill with a giant screen. Free to sit on with any ticket.

### Food & drink
- Pimm's and lemonade is THE Wimbledon drink.
- Bring your own picnic — it's welcomed and common (no glass bottles).
- Wimbledon Village has upscale restaurants and pubs, but they fill up fast during the tournament.

### Best add-on experiences
- British Museum, Tower of London, Buckingham Palace.
- West End theatre (book in advance).
- Day trip to Stonehenge and Bath.
- Walk along the South Bank (free, scenic, great food markets at Borough Market).

## US Open — New York

### Visa
- Chinese passport holders need a US B1/B2 visa.
- Apply 3–6 months in advance. Interview at the US Embassy/Consulate required.
- EVUS registration required after obtaining the visa.
- Use flyai: `flyai keyword-search --query "美国签证"`

### Weather (late August – mid September)
- Late summer: 25–35°C, humid. Occasional thunderstorms.
- **Must bring**: light breathable clothing, sunscreen, portable fan. The humidity is intense.
- Arthur Ashe Stadium has a retractable roof. Louis Armstrong Stadium also has a roof. Outer courts have no cover.

### Transport to venue
- USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center is in Flushing Meadows, Queens.
- Subway: 7 train to Mets-Willets Point (most convenient, direct from Times Square/Grand Central, ~40 min).
- LIRR from Penn Station to Mets-Willets Point (faster, ~20 min, but pricier).
- **Avoid driving** — parking is limited and traffic on the Van Wyck Expressway is notorious.

### Match-day tips
- The US Open is the loudest and most energetic Grand Slam. Crowds are rowdy, especially night sessions.
- Day session: 11:00 AM – ~5:00 PM. Night session: 7:00 PM onward.
- Night sessions on Ashe are the premium experience — floodlit matches under the NYC skyline.
- Arthur Ashe Kids' Day (before the tournament starts) is free and great for families.
- Ground pass ("Grounds Admission") includes access to all courts except Ashe and Armstrong. Best value early in the tournament.
- The Food Village has cuisine from NYC's top restaurants — quality is notably better than other Slams.
- Honey Deuce cocktail (Grey Goose vodka, lemonade, raspberry liqueur) is the signature US Open drink.

### Food & drink
- Venue food is excellent but expensive ($15–30 per item).
- Corona Park (surrounding the venue) has food carts with cheaper options.
- Flushing's Chinatown is a 10-min walk — some of the best Chinese food in the US.
- **Insider tip**: the Flushing food scene rivals Manhattan at a fraction of the price.

### Best add-on experiences
- Manhattan sightseeing: Times Square, Central Park, Brooklyn Bridge.
- Statue of Liberty & Ellis Island (book ferry tickets in advance).
- Broadway show (TKTS booth in Times Square for same-day discount tickets).
- MoMA, Met Museum, Guggenheim.
- Flushing Meadows Corona Park has the Unisphere and Queens Museum — walkable from the venue.
