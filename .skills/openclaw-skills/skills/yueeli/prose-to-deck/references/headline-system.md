# Headline System

Use this reference in Phase 2 and Phase 4.
Its job is simple: prevent full argument sentences from being pasted directly into oversized display type.

## Roles

- **Main claim** — the full judgment the slide must communicate.
- **Display headline** — the on-screen title language the eye lands on first.
- **Support line** — optional, but part of the title stack when the headline was compressed and needs to recover nuance.

`Main claim` and `Display headline` solve different problems. Do not copy the full claim into the headline by default.

## Defaults

- Prefer the shortest headline that still carries a real point.
- Short does **not** mean vague.
- If the accurate sentence is visually overlong, compress it into headline language and let the support line carry what was removed.
- Record only real exceptions — slides that intentionally use a longer declarative poster-style headline.

## Headline tiers

- **`hero-xl`** — cover, conclusion, or rare poster-style statement slides
- **`hero-lg`** — strong argument pages with short-to-medium headlines
- **`section-md`** — explanation, compare, framework, evidence, and most ordinary slides
- **`compact-md`** — denser slides where structure, cards, or diagrams do more of the work than the title

## Budget defaults

- Ordinary slides: headline usually stays within **1–2 lines**
- Cover / conclusion / explicit poster exceptions: may use **up to 3 lines**
- The combined title block (eyebrow + headline + support line) should usually stay within roughly **35–40%** of the usable content height

These are default budgets, not universal laws. Break them only when the composition clearly earns it.

## Overflow correction order

1. Rewrite the headline into sharper display language
2. Move recovered nuance into a support line
3. Downgrade the headline tier (`hero-xl` → `hero-lg` → `section-md` → `compact-md`)
4. Adjust the layout so structure carries more of the meaning
5. Only then fine-tune font size

## Avoid

- Treating every slide like a poster
- One-word abstract headlines that lose the point
- Inflating a long headline until it takes over the frame
- Using a very narrow `max-width` or similar hack just to force a dramatic multi-line title stack
