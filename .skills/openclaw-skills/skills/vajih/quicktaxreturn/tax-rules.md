## 2. STANDARD DEDUCTIONS — TY2025

Source: IRS Rev. Proc. 2024-40, Section 2.15 (IRC § 63(c)(2) and § 63(f))

| Filing Status                                              | Standard Deduction |
| ---------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------ |
| Single                                                     | $15,000            |
| Married Filing Separately (MFS)                            | $15,000            |
| Head of Household (HOH)                                    | $22,500            |
| Married Filing Jointly (MFJ) / Qualifying Surviving Spouse | $30,000            |

### Additional Standard Deduction (Age 65+ or Blind) — IRC § 63(f)

- **Married (MFJ or MFS)**: $1,600 per qualifying condition (age 65+, OR blind; $3,200 if both)
- **Single or HOH (not a surviving spouse)**: $2,000 per qualifying condition ($4,000 if both)

Each spouse's additional deduction is computed separately.

### Dependent Standard Deduction Cap — IRC § 63(c)(5)

For a dependent who can be claimed on another return: greater of (1) $1,350, or (2) $450 + individual's earned income. Cannot exceed the regular standard deduction.

## 3. CHILD TAX CREDIT (CTC) — TY2025

Source: IRS Schedule 8812 (Form 1040) Instructions (2025), dated Jan 23, 2026; Rev. Proc. 2024-40, Section 2.05 (IRC § 24)

### Maximum Credit

- **$2,200 per qualifying child** (increased from $2,000 for TY2025 per Schedule 8812 Instructions, 2025)
- Child must be under age 17 at the end of 2025
- Child must have a valid Social Security Number

### Phase-Out Thresholds (CTC Reduction)

- **MFJ**: Credit begins to phase out when modified AGI exceeds **$400,000**
- **All other filing statuses** (Single, HOH, MFS): Phase-out begins above **$200,000**
- Phase-out rate: $50 reduction for each $1,000 (or fraction thereof) of modified AGI above the threshold

### Additional Child Tax Credit (ACTC) — Refundable Portion

- **Maximum ACTC**: $1,700 per qualifying child — Source: Rev. Proc. 2024-40, Section 2.05; Schedule 8812 Instructions (2025)
- **Calculation**: 15% of earned income over **$2,500**
  - Example: Earned income $30,000 → ($30,000 − $2,500) × 15% = $4,125 (capped at $1,700 × number of children)
- Earned income threshold: $2,500 — Source: IRS Schedule 8812 Instructions (2025), Part II-A worksheet

## 5. EDUCATION CREDITS — TY2025

### 5a. American Opportunity Tax Credit (AOTC)

Source: IRS Form 8863 Instructions (2025); IRS.gov/credits-deductions/individuals/education-credits-aotc-and-llc

- **Maximum credit**: $2,500 per eligible student
  - 100% of first $2,000 of qualified education expenses + 25% of next $2,000
- **Refundable portion**: 40% (up to $1,000 per student)
- **Non-refundable portion**: 60% (up to $1,500 per student)
- **Available for**: First 4 years of post-secondary education only; student must be enrolled at least half-time
- **MAGI Phase-Out Range**:
  - Single / HOH / Qualifying Surviving Spouse: $80,000 – $90,000 (credit fully phased out at $90,000+)
  - MFJ: $160,000 – $180,000 (credit fully phased out at $180,000+)
- Source: Form 8863 Instructions (2025), Reminders section and line 3 instructions

### 5b. Lifetime Learning Credit (LLC)

Source: IRS Form 8863 Instructions (2025); IRS.gov/credits-deductions/individuals/education-credits-aotc-and-llc

- **Maximum credit**: $2,000 per return (not per student)
  - 20% of first $10,000 of qualified education expenses for all students on the return
- **Refundable**: No (non-refundable only)
- **Available for**: All years of post-secondary education; no limit on number of years
- **MAGI Phase-Out Range**:
  - Single / HOH / Qualifying Surviving Spouse: $80,000 – $90,000 (fully phased out at $90,000+)
  - MFJ: $160,000 – $180,000 (fully phased out at $180,000+)
- Source: Form 8863 Instructions (2025), Reminders section and line 14 instructions

NOTE: Only one credit (AOTC or LLC) may be claimed per student per year. Both may appear on the same return for different students.

## 7. SOCIAL SECURITY BENEFIT TAXABILITY — TY2025

Source: IRS Publication 915 (2025), "Social Security and Equivalent Railroad Retirement Benefits"

Provisional income = AGI + tax-exempt interest + 50% of Social Security benefits (SSA-1099 Box 5)

### Thresholds

| Filing Status                                | 0% Taxable (below)            | Up to 50% Taxable | Up to 85% Taxable (above) |
| -------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------- | ----------------- | ------------------------- |
| Single / HOH / Qualifying Surviving Spouse   | Below $25,000                 | $25,000 – $34,000 | Above $34,000             |
| MFJ                                          | Below $32,000                 | $32,000 – $44,000 | Above $44,000             |
| MFS (lived with spouse any time during year) | $0 (always taxable up to 85%) | —                 | Always up to 85%          |
| MFS (lived apart all year)                   | Below $25,000                 | $25,000 – $34,000 | Above $34,000             |

- **Maximum taxable percentage**: 85% of benefits
- Worksheet for taxable amount: IRS Form 1040 Instructions, or Publication 915, Worksheet 1
- Reported on: Form 1040 Line 6a (gross benefits), Line 6b (taxable amount)

### §7c. SS Taxability Worksheet — Calculation Formulas

Source: IRS Publication 915 (2025), Worksheet 1 (Pub 915 pp. 16–17)

Use these formulas to compute the taxable portion of Social Security benefits. Compute in this
order after all other income and adjustments are known. Do not round intermediate values.

**Define variables:**

- `B` = SSA-1099 Box 5 (net SS benefits received)
- `PI` = provisional income = (AGI excluding SS) + tax-exempt interest + (0.50 × B)
- `T1` = lower threshold (Single/HOH/QSS: $25,000 | MFJ: $32,000 | MFS-with-spouse: $0)
- `T2` = upper threshold (Single/HOH/QSS: $34,000 | MFJ: $44,000 | MFS-with-spouse: $0)

**Case 1 — Below lower threshold (PI < T1):**

```
Taxable SS = $0
```

**Case 2 — Middle tier (T1 ≤ PI ≤ T2):**

```
Taxable SS = min(0.50 × (PI − T1), 0.50 × B)
```

**Case 3 — Top tier (PI > T2):**

```
Step A = 0.85 × (PI − T2)
Step B = 0.50 × min((PI − T1), (T2 − T1))
Step C = Step A + Step B
Taxable SS = min(Step C, 0.85 × B)
```

**Case 4 — MFS who lived with spouse at any time during the year:**

```
T1 = T2 = $0 → always Case 3
Taxable SS = min(0.85 × PI, 0.85 × B)
```

(Note: if PI is very low, the $0 thresholds mean 85% of all income may apply — typically the
full 85% of benefits is taxable for this filing status unless SS is the only income.)

**Worked Example — MFJ, 85% tier (from §15):**

```
B = $18,000    T1 = $32,000    T2 = $44,000
AGI before SS = $40,000    Tax-exempt interest = $1,000
PI = $40,000 + $1,000 + (0.50 × $18,000) = $50,000    ← PI > T2 = Case 3

Step A = 0.85 × ($50,000 − $44,000) = 0.85 × $6,000   = $5,100.00
Step B = 0.50 × min($18,000, $12,000) = 0.50 × $12,000  = $6,000.00
Step C = $5,100 + $6,000 = $11,100.00
Cap    = 0.85 × $18,000 = $15,300.00
Taxable SS = min($11,100, $15,300) = $11,100.00  → 1040 Line 6b
```

**Worked Example — Single, 50% tier:**

```
B = $14,000    T1 = $25,000    T2 = $34,000
AGI before SS = $20,000    Tax-exempt interest = $0
PI = $20,000 + $0 + (0.50 × $14,000) = $27,000    ← T1 ≤ PI ≤ T2 = Case 2

Taxable SS = min(0.50 × ($27,000 − $25,000), 0.50 × $14,000)
           = min(0.50 × $2,000, $7,000)
           = min($1,000, $7,000) = $1,000.00  → 1040 Line 6b
```

## 8. FICA TAXES — TY2025

### Social Security Tax

Source: IRS Publication 15 (Circular E), Table 3 (2025); IRS.gov Topic No. 751

- **Social Security wage base (2025)**: $176,100 — Source: IRS Publication 15 (Employer's Tax Guide), Table 3
- **Employee rate**: 6.2% on wages up to $176,100
- **Employer rate**: 6.2% (matching)
- **Combined rate**: 12.4%
- No Social Security tax on wages above $176,100

### Medicare Tax

Source: IRS Publication 15, Table 3; IRS.gov Topic No. 751

- **Employee rate**: 1.45% on all wages (no wage base limit)
- **Employer rate**: 1.45% (matching)
- **Combined rate**: 2.9%

### Additional Medicare Tax (0.9%)

Source: IRC § 3101(b)(2); IRS.gov/individuals/net-investment-income-tax

Applies to wages, compensation, and self-employment income ABOVE these thresholds:

- **MFJ**: $250,000
- **MFS**: $125,000
- **Single / HOH / Qualifying Surviving Spouse**: $200,000

Employee withholding begins at $200,000 per employer regardless of filing status; any shortfall is settled on Form 8959 with the return.

### Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT) — 3.8%

Source: IRC § 1411; IRS.gov/individuals/net-investment-income-tax

Applies to net investment income (interest, dividends, capital gains, rents, royalties, passive income) ABOVE:

- **MFJ / Qualifying Surviving Spouse**: $250,000
- **MFS**: $125,000
- **Single / HOH**: $200,000

NIIT does not apply to wages or self-employment income. Reported on Form 8960.

## 15. BRACKET CALCULATION EXAMPLE (STEP-BY-STEP)

This is the required arithmetic format. Never use effective rate estimates.

### Example: Single filer, $75,000 taxable income (after standard deduction)

```
Taxable income: $75,000

Step 1 — 10% bracket: $0 to $11,925
  $11,925 × 10% = $1,192.50

Step 2 — 12% bracket: $11,926 to $48,475
  ($48,475 − $11,925) = $36,550 × 12% = $4,386.00

Step 3 — 22% bracket: $48,476 to $75,000
  ($75,000 − $48,475) = $26,525 × 22% = $5,835.50

Total income tax = $1,192.50 + $4,386.00 + $5,835.50 = $11,414.00

Effective rate = $11,414 / $75,000 = 15.2% (for reference only — never calculate this way)
Marginal rate = 22%
```

### Example: MFJ couple, $120,000 taxable income

```
Taxable income: $120,000

Step 1 — 10% bracket: $0 to $23,850
  $23,850 × 10% = $2,385.00

Step 2 — 12% bracket: $23,851 to $96,950
  ($96,950 − $23,850) = $73,100 × 12% = $8,772.00

Step 3 — 22% bracket: $96,951 to $120,000
  ($120,000 − $96,950) = $23,050 × 22% = $5,071.00

Total income tax = $2,385.00 + $8,772.00 + $5,071.00 = $16,228.00
```

### Example: ACTC calculation, 2 children, earned income $22,000

```
Earned income: $22,000
Threshold: $2,500
Excess: $22,000 − $2,500 = $19,500
ACTC rate: 15%
Calculated ACTC: $19,500 × 15% = $2,925
Maximum ACTC: $1,700 × 2 children = $3,400
Refundable credit = min($2,925, $3,400) = $2,925
```

### Example: Social Security taxability, MFJ couple

```
AGI (wages + interest, excluding SS): $40,000
Tax-exempt interest: $1,000
SS benefits (SSA-1099 Box 5): $18,000

Provisional income = $40,000 + $1,000 + (50% × $18,000)
                   = $40,000 + $1,000 + $9,000
                   = $50,000

$50,000 > $44,000 → top tier (up to 85% taxable)

Taxable SS = lesser of:
  (A) 85% × $18,000 = $15,300
  (B) 85% × ($50,000 − $44,000) + $6,000
      = 85% × $6,000 + $6,000
      = $5,100 + $6,000 = $11,100

Taxable SS = min($15,300, $11,100) = $11,100 → 1040 Line 6b
```
