# Negative Keywords

Negatives are the other half of relevance. Without them, broad and phrase match bleed budget to searchers who cannot buy what you sell.

## Three-List Structure

Create three shared negative keyword lists in your account:

1. **Global Exclusions** — applied to every campaign. Brand-safety terms, competitor names you never want to serve on, unambiguously wrong-intent terms.
2. **Non-Commercial** — informational, job seeker, student intent. Applied to all lead gen / solution campaigns.
3. **Campaign-Specific** — added at campaign level (not shared). For terms that are fine in one campaign but wrong in another (e.g., competitor brand terms fine in competitor campaign, negative everywhere else).

---

## Category 1: Educational & Informational Intent

These searchers want to learn, not buy. They click, consume, and never convert.

**Negative keywords:** course, courses, tutorial, tutorials, certification, certifications, training, class, classes, degree, university, college, school, academic, student, scholarship, syllabus, curriculum, textbook, exam, quiz, study, learn, learning, education, educational, define, definition, what is, meaning of, explain, example, examples, pdf, ppt, presentation, paper, thesis, dissertation, free course, online course

---

## Category 2: Job & Career Searches

Searchers looking for roles that include your product category.

**Negative keywords:** jobs, job, careers, career, hiring, vacancy, vacancies, salary, compensation, resume, CV, interview, recruiter, recruiting, recruitment, glassdoor, indeed, work from home, remote job, internship, intern, entry level, junior, associate, coordinator

---

## Category 3: Competitor Brand Names

Unless you have a dedicated competitive conquest campaign with comparison landing pages, competitor name searches waste budget.

**Rule:** Add all major competitor brand names as phrase match negatives at the account level. Apply campaign-specific exceptions for your competitor campaign.

**Also add:** login, sign in, dashboard, support, documentation (when preceded by a competitor name — these are existing-customer queries, not prospects).

---

## Category 4: Free & Open Source Intent

If you sell commercial software, these signals indicate the searcher isn't willing to pay.

**Negative keywords:** free, freeware, open source, open-source, free download, free tool, free software, free version, free alternative, cracked, pirated, torrent, github, community edition

**Exception:** only add "free" as a negative if you don't offer a free trial. If you do offer a trial, keep it — "free trial [category]" is transactional.

---

## Category 5: Product Name Collision

Your keyword overlaps with a non-software product, platform, or brand.

**Rule:** run a Search Term Report for the last 90 days and extract every non-relevant product or platform that appeared. This list is account-specific — there's no universal template.

---

## Category 6: Overly Generic & Ambiguous

Random browsers without purchase intent.

**Negative keywords:** what is, how to, meaning, definition, vs, versus (in solution campaigns — keep in competitor campaign), review, reviews, reddit, quora, forum, blog, article, news, comparison, compare, list

**Note:** "best [category]" can be high intent — evaluate per keyword before adding as a negative.

---

## Category 7: Wrong Geography or Language

If you only sell in specific markets, block searches from regions you can't serve.

**Negative keywords:** add country/language terms that trigger from your keywords but represent non-target markets. This is account-specific. Review autosuggest and STR for signals.

---

## Category 8: Existing Customer & Support Queries

Your own customers searching for help will trigger ads. You're paying to reach people who already bought.

**Negative keywords:** login, log in, sign in, support, help, documentation, docs, API, bug, error, issue, troubleshoot, download, update, upgrade, cancel, cancellation, refund

---

## B2B SaaS Quick-Reference Starter Buckets

Apply these on day 1 before launch, before you have any STR data:

**Consumer / DIY intent:** free, cheap, diy, how to, tutorial, course, certification, for beginners, template

**Job seekers:** jobs, careers, salary, hire, recruit, job description, internship

**Informational:** what is, definition, meaning, wikipedia, reddit, forum

**Student / research:** essay, thesis, research, assignment, university, course

---

## Review Cadence

- **First 60 days:** weekly — every new search term is a signal
- **After 60 days:** monthly — drift slows once the account matures

The three-way decision for every unexpected term in the Search Term Report: **promote** (new keyword), **demote** (new negative), or **ignore** (one-off noise). See `6-analyze.md` for the full STR audit workflow.
