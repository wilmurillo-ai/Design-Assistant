## Creative strategy

### Content, not ads

People scroll social media for entertainment. Anything that looks like an ad gets skipped - users associate ads with boring. The goal: make ads indistinguishable from native content on the platform. If the user's first reaction is "this is an ad," the creative has failed.

When generating creative concepts, optimize for this question: "Would someone share this in a group chat?" If no, the creative is too corporate.

### Creative IS the targeting

On Meta, broad targeting works because the algorithm finds buyers - but only if the creative self-selects the right audience. The hook and visual must make the target think "this is about me" within one second. Creative that speaks to everyone converts no one.

Elements that self-select: recognizable UI/icons/inage from the user's world, specific jargon or frustrations, scenarios only the target has experienced. When briefing creative, ask the user what visual or phrase would make their customer stop and think "how do they know?"

### Standing out is relative

Attention is contextual, not absolute. A bright red ad stands out in a feed of muted posts - but if every ad uses bright red, none stand out (supermarket aisle effect). Before designing, consider what the surrounding feed content looks like. Sometimes a minimal, text-only image outperforms a polished graphic because it breaks the visual pattern.

### Video over static (when possible)

Meta rewards content that keeps users on-platform. Video drives longer session time, so Meta gives it cheaper distribution. 10-second video usually outperforms a static image on reach and cost. When full video production is not feasible, use the hybrid trick (see Static section).
However, a good image > a bad video.

### Iteration model

No formula guarantees results. The process is: research the audience, generate creative hypotheses, test, read the data, iterate. Prioritize testing hooks over body content - the hook is the highest-leverage variable. A great body with a bad hook is never seen. A great hook with a mediocre body still gets clicks.

When results plateau, change the hook angle rather than tweaking colors or copy. When a hook works, exhaust its variations before moving to new concepts.

### Building creative instincts

Recommend the user maintain a swipe file: scroll platforms (Facebook, TikTok, Instagram) daily, save every ad or post that stops the scroll, then analyze why it worked - unexpected, relatable, sensory, emotional? Over time this builds pattern recognition that no framework can replace.

In the Meta Ad Library, ad longevity is a proxy for performance: ads running for months are likely profitable. Study their structure, not just their visual style.

Can use a tool like [Adkit Ad Library](https://adkit.so/features/ad-library?utm_source=skill) to immediately see the top-performing ads in the Ad Library and save ads.

## Hook techniques

The hook is the first thing seen: the opening frame of a video OR the dominant visual/headline of a static image. Users decide in less than 1 sec whether to engage or scroll. Videos autoplay, so the first frames ARE the hook.

Viewing order: image/video first, then headline, then body copy. The hook must work before any text is read.

| Type       | Mechanism                        | Example                                                         |
| ---------- | -------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Relatable  | Viewer thinks "this is about me" | Facebook "account restricted" icon for ad about preventing bans |
| Unexpected | Breaks scroll pattern            | Meat grinder + "my brain when I try to write" (QuillBot)        |
| Emotional  | Triggers feeling before message  | "This really helped my son" for kids math app                   |
| Sensory    | Visual satisfaction, ASMR        | BlendJet pouring videos with whisper voiceover                  |

The hook does NOT need to relate to the product. An unrelated but attention-grabbing visual (e.g. tomato dropped in blue liquid for a language learning app) works if it buys 1-2 seconds of attention. The connection to the product happens in the next frame or the copy.

Test multiple hooks against the same body/offer. The hook is the highest-leverage variable - always test it first.

## Static image production

### Checklist

- [ ] Single clear focal point (no busy collages)
- [ ] Key message in center 80% (edges cropped on some placements)
- [ ] Clear contrast with the rest of the platform, need to stand out
- [ ] Faces looking at camera if using people
- [ ] Works at mobile size (80%+ of impressions are mobile)
- [ ] Format matches placement (4:5 for feed, 9:16 for stories)
- [ ] No policy violations
- [ ] Looks like content, not an ad

### Sizes

| Format        | Ratio | Resolution | Best for                                |
| ------------- | ----- | ---------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Feed vertical | 4:5   | 1080x1350  | Feed - best performer, max screen space |
| Feed square   | 1:1   | 1080x1080  | Universal                               |
| Stories/Reels | 9:16  | 1080x1920  | Full-screen                             |

Max 30MB. JPG or PNG. Prioritize 4:5 (higher CTR than 1:1).

### The hybrid trick

Static + animated background = Meta counts as video. Add subtle motion (animated bg, text animation, gentle zoom) for video algorithm boost without full production.

### AI-generated images

Works for: abstract visuals, backgrounds, mockups, UI illustrations.
Fails for: human faces, lifestyle shots, health/food/beauty categories.
Gen Z / Millennial audiences especially skeptical. Use AI for ideation, real photography for hero creative.

### Tools

- **Non-designers:** Canva (canva.com) - free, drag-and-drop, lots of elements (stickers, backgrounds, etc)
- **Designers/teams:** Figma (figma.com) - precise control, components, no elements
- **Inspiration:** Meta Ad Library (facebook.com/ads/library), AdKit (adkit.so/features/ad-library?utm_source=skill)
- **AI generation:** AdKit Studio (adkit.so/generate-ads-ai/?utm_source=skill) - generate ads from scratch, clone winning ads, resize to formats. Web app, not CLI.
- **Other:** ifaketextmessage.com (text messages)

## Video production

### Checklist

- [ ] Hook in first 2 seconds (no fade-ins, no logos, no intros)
- [ ] Text overlay on hook frame (mandatory for sound-off viewers - 74% watch without sound)
- [ ] Captions throughout (bold black-on-white or white-on-black)
- [ ] Key content in center 65% safe zone (9:16)
- [ ] Works at both 9:16 and 4:5
- [ ] Under 30s for feed, under 15s for Stories/Reels
- [ ] CTA visible before end
- [ ] Sound-off test: message lands with no audio?
- [ ] Consider UGC: 4x higher CTR, 50% lower CPC vs. brand creative

### Safe zones (9:16)

- Top 15% (~250px): cropped in feed. No critical content.
- Bottom 20%: Reels UI overlay. Keep messaging above.
- Center 65%: safe zone for all key content.

Can use [Safe Zones Checker Tool](https://adkit.so/tools/safe-zones/meta?utm_source=skill) to verify safe zones.

### Sizes

| Format        | Ratio | Resolution | Length |
| ------------- | ----- | ---------- | ------ |
| Feed vertical | 4:5   | 1080x1350  | 15-30s |
| Stories/Reels | 9:16  | 1080x1920  | 6-15s  |
| Feed square   | 1:1   | 1080x1080  | 15-30s |

MP4/MOV, max 4GB.

### UGC platforms

| Budget                | Platform              | Notes                                                       |
| --------------------- | --------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Low ($100+/video)     | Billo (billo.app)     | Pre-made style, fast turnaround, but need very good script  |
| Mid ($100-500+/video) | Trend (trend.io)      | Per-video pricing, volume discounts available               |
| Higher / more control | Insense (insense.pro) | Self-service marketplace, brief-first, more creator options |

### AI UGC (use with caution)

- **Arcads** (arcads.ai): 1,000+ AI actors, good for script testing, multilingual
- **HeyGen** (heygen.com): video translation, lip-sync, localization
- Rule: "this worked for me" stories = real creators. "Here's how this works" = AI may be fine.
- Fails for: health, beauty, Gen Z, claim-heavy creative.

- **SaaS demos:** Screen Studio (Mac) - smooth zoom effects
- **Slideshows:** Canva - simple video, no skills needed
