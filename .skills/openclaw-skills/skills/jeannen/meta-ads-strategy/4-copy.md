## Principles

- **Sell the click**, not the product. The ad earns a landing page visit, not a sale.
- Each sentence must lead to the next. If it doesn't pull forward, cut it.
- Longer copy works IF the reader is hooked. Length isn't the problem; boring is.
- Remove everything unnecessary. If a word doesn't serve the hook, argument, or CTA, delete it.
- Emojis help readability. Break up text walls, add visual anchors.
- The ad competes with entertainment, not other ads.

## Deriving angles from the ad brief

Use the ad brief to generate copy angles. Each angle targets a different psychological lever:

| Ad brief section                         | Angle type               | Example                                 |
| ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------ | --------------------------------------- |
| Transformation: current situation        | Pain-point angle         | "Still doing X manually?"               |
| Transformation: desired situation        | Aspiration angle         | "Imagine if X just worked"              |
| Transformation: what caused them to look | Trigger angle            | "If you just realized X..."             |
| Psychographics: frustrations             | Frustration angle        | "Tired of X?"                           |
| Psychographics: beliefs                  | Challenge angle          | "You've been told X. Here's the truth." |
| Psychographics: fears                    | Fear angle               | "What happens if you don't fix X?"      |
| Success criteria                         | Result angle             | "X in Y days"                           |
| What else they tried                     | Comparison angle         | "Unlike X, Y does..."                   |
| Recurring objections                     | Objection-handling angle | "Yes, it works even if X"               |

## Checklist

- [ ] Hook in first line (pattern interrupt, question, bold claim, or specific number)
- [ ] No personal attribute assertions ("Are you struggling with weight loss?" gets rejected; "Many people struggle with weight loss" is better)
- [ ] CTA matches funnel stage (Shop Now for conversions, Learn More for awareness)
- [ ] Headline under 27 characters (hard truncation)
- [ ] No clickbait language ("You won't believe...", "Doctors HATE this...")
- [ ] No fake urgency or unsubstantiated statistics
- [ ] Primary text has benefit in first 125 characters (before fold)
- [ ] Copy angle matches ad brief section (use the derivation table above)
- [ ] Copy sells benefit, not product description
- [ ] One offer, one CTA per ad

## Copy structure

### Hook (first line)

7 tested formulas:

1. **Number proof**: "[Number] people already [achieved outcome] - here's how"
2. **Stop doing**: "Stop [doing X] if you want [desired result]"
3. **Easy way**: "The [adjective] way to [achieve outcome] without [pain point]"
4. **Belief break**: "Why [established belief] is wrong (and what actually works)"
5. **Personal story**: "I tried [X] for [time period] - here's what happened"
6. **Direct address**: "If you're [struggling with X], read this"
7. **Promise**: "[Outcome] in [short time frame] - no [common obstacle]"

The more specific to the target audience, the better. "Struggling to get more users for your travel agency?" > "Struggling to get more users?"

### Body

**Short copy**: impulse purchases, retargeting, simple offers, etc:

Use PAS (Problem-Agitation-Solution):

- Problem (or symptoms if audience is unaware of the problem)
- Agitation: consequences, what they're losing
- Solution: your product as the answer

**Long copy**: complex offers, top of funnel, etc:

Use the 5-step framework:

1. Identify the audience: call out who this is for
2. State the problem: pain they're experiencing right now
3. Offer the solution: your product as the answer
4. Remove objections: top 1-2 hesitations
5. Call to action: what to do next

**Match copy to awareness level** (applies to both frameworks):

- Unaware of problem: start with symptoms
- Aware of problem: start with the problem
- Knows your product: skip to benefits
- Retargeting: reminder, skip solution

### CTAs

- CTA should be adapted to the audience's stage of the funnel:
    - Cold: Doesn't know the brand/product => "Learn more" to lower friction
    - Warm: Already exploring solutions => "Sign up" / "Apply now" / etc
    - Hot: Ready to purchase => "Shop now" / "Subscribe" / "Get offer" / etc

- Urgency works: "Today Only", "Limited Offer", "Ends Sunday", but must be genuine or Meta flags it.
- Add a CTA in primary text (and at the end of video).

### Headlines

- 27 characters max before truncation (Meta spec)
- Headline = your CTA or value proposition in 4-5 words
- Good: "Shop Now - Free Shipping", "Try It Free Today", "Save 40% This Week"
- Bad: "Learn More", "Our Products" (wastes headline real estate)
