# Facing Layoff - Action Patterns

## Pattern: Leadership Communication

**When: Your manager tells you you're being laid off or hints at it**

**Immediate Actions:**
1. **Stay calm and professional** - Don't burn bridges, emotions lose leverage
2. **Ask clarifying questions**:
   - Why is this happening? (performance / restructuring / budget cuts?)
   - What's the timeline?
   - Are there internal transfer opportunities?
   - What does the severance package look like?

**Negotiation Points:**
- Severance: N+1? N+2? More? (N = years of service)
- Unused vacation days payout
- Reference letter wording (critical for next job)
- Extended benefits (health insurance continuation)
- Outplacement services (career counseling, resume help)

**Chinese Context:**
- **尝试和领导聊一下**: 主动沟通了解真实情况
  - 裁员原因：业绩？团队调整？战略变化？
  - 争取时间和资源：过渡期？内部转岗？
  - 谈判离职条件：补偿金（N+1？N+2？）、年假结算、离职证明措辞
  - 保持专业和尊严：冷静谈判，情绪化会失去筹码

## Pattern: Emergency Response Plan

**When: You know or suspect layoff is coming**

**Financial Assessment (Week 1):**
```
Current savings: $X
Monthly essential expenses: $Y
Runway: X/Y = Z months

If Z < 3 months → URGENT: Cut non-essentials NOW
If Z = 3-6 months → MODERATE: Start cutting, activate side income
If Z > 6 months → STABLE: Focus on quality job search
```

**Skill & Network Audit:**
- **Skills inventory**: What can I do? What's marketable right now?
- **Network activation**: Tell trusted contacts you're open to opportunities (don't wait!)
- **Evidence gathering**: Save project docs, performance reviews, work samples (before you lose access)

**Chinese Context:**
- **立即启动应急计划**:
  - 梳理资产：能撑几个月？需要削减哪些开支？
  - 盘点技能：我能做什么？市场需要什么？
  - 激活人脉：告诉靠谱的朋友和前同事你在找工作
  - 整理证据：工作成果、项目文档、绩效评价（以备劳动仲裁）

## Pattern: Legal Rights Protection

**When: You suspect illegal termination or unfair treatment**

**⚠️ IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER**: The information below is general guidance only. Labor laws vary significantly by country, state/province, and jurisdiction. Always consult a local employment lawyer or labor authority for advice specific to your situation. Do not rely solely on this information for legal decisions.

**Know Your Rights (General Overview):**

**US/International:**
- WARN Act (60-day notice for mass layoffs of 50+ employees)
- Severance negotiation (not legally required but often offered)
- Unemployment insurance eligibility
- COBRA health insurance continuation (18 months)
- Age discrimination (ADEA protects 40+)

**China:**
- **劳动合同法**: 裁员补偿标准（N = 工作年限）
- **违法解除赔偿**: 2N if termination is unlawful
- **失业保险金**: Check eligibility and application process
- **劳动仲裁**: Free arbitration for labor disputes

**When to Get Legal Help:**
- Fired without cause or proper documentation
- Discriminatory reasons (age, gender, race, disability)
- Not receiving legally required severance
- Company violated employment contract terms

**Resources:**
- Local labor board / 劳动仲裁委员会
- Free legal aid clinics
- Employment lawyers (many offer free consultations)

**Chinese Context:**
- **了解法律权益**: 
  - 裁员补偿标准（劳动合同法：N = 工作年限）
  - 违法解除劳动合同的赔偿（2N）
  - 失业保险金领取条件和流程
  - 必要时咨询劳动仲裁或律师（很多地方有免费法律援助）

## Pattern: Mental Reframing

**When: Feeling shame, anger, or failure after being laid off**

**Reframe the Narrative:**
- ❌ "I got fired because I'm not good enough"
- ✅ "The company made a business decision affected by factors beyond my control (economy, pivots, politics)"

**Reality Check:**
- Tech layoffs 2023-2024: Meta (11K), Google (12K), Amazon (27K) - these were top performers
- Economic cycles happen to everyone
- Being laid off ≠ being unemployable
- Many successful people have been laid off multiple times

**Chinese Context:**
- **心态调整**:
  - 这不是你的错：经济周期、公司决策，很多因素不可控
  - 被裁不等于无能：很多优秀的人也经历过裁员
  - 把它当成转折点：也许是逃离不适合岗位的机会

## Pattern: Day-of-Layoff Checklist

**Hour 0-2 (During the conversation):**
- [ ] Take notes on what's said
- [ ] Ask about severance package details
- [ ] Get timeline in writing (if possible)
- [ ] Ask when you'll lose system access

**Hour 2-24 (Before losing access):**
- [ ] Download personal files, contacts, calendar
- [ ] Save evidence of your work (projects, metrics, presentations)
- [ ] Update LinkedIn with current role details
- [ ] Forward important emails to personal account (check company policy)
- [ ] Export contact list of colleagues/clients

**Day 1-3 (Immediate aftermath):**
- [ ] File for unemployment benefits (don't wait!)
- [ ] Update resume with recent accomplishments
- [ ] Notify close contacts you're job hunting
- [ ] Set up job search tracking system
- [ ] Create daily routine/schedule

**Week 1 (Foundation building):**
- [ ] Complete LinkedIn profile optimization
- [ ] Apply to 3-5 well-matched positions
- [ ] Reach out to 5 network contacts
- [ ] Start financial budget tracking
- [ ] Schedule exercise and self-care

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to do)

❌ **Immediately accept the first offer out of panic** → You have more leverage than you think
❌ **Sign severance without reading/negotiating** → Everything is negotiable
❌ **Badmouth the company publicly** → Industry is smaller than you think
❌ **Isolate yourself** → Humans need support; unemployment is not shameful
❌ **Stop your daily routine** → Structure prevents depression
❌ **Spend savings on luxuries** → Runway = survival time
❌ **Give up after first rejections** → Average job search = 2-4 months
