## How to Use These Patterns

### 1. Identify Your Situation
Match your current challenge to a reference file:
- Just got laid off? → **facing-layoff.md**
- Been searching 1+ months with no results? → **job-search-plateau.md**
- Have an interview coming up? → **interview-answers.md**
- Feeling overwhelmed, anxious, or burnt out? → **mental-health-routines.md**

### 2. Find the Relevant Pattern
Each file contains multiple patterns for specific scenarios. Scan the headers to find your exact situation.

### 3. Follow the Pattern
Patterns include:
- **When to use** - Specific trigger conditions
- **Action steps** - What to do right now
- **Scripts/templates** - Copy-paste-ready examples
- **Anti-patterns** - What NOT to do
- **Bilingual support** - Chinese context where relevant

### 4. Adapt to Your Context
These are frameworks, not rigid rules. Adjust based on:
- Your industry (tech vs finance vs creative)
- Your location (US vs China vs Europe)
- Your situation (senior vs junior, layoff vs firing)
- Your personality (introvert vs extrovert)

## Pattern Philosophy

**What makes a good pattern:**
- ✅ Actionable - You can start RIGHT NOW
- ✅ Specific - Not "network more" but "send 3 LinkedIn messages this week to..."
- ✅ Tested - Based on real-world success stories
- ✅ Empathetic - Acknowledges emotions while driving action
- ✅ Realistic - No toxic positivity or false promises

**What patterns are NOT:**
- ❌ Magic bullets - There's no secret trick to instant job offers
- ❌ One-size-fits-all - Your mileage may vary, adapt as needed
- ❌ Replacements for professional help - Therapy, lawyers, financial advisors exist for a reason
- ❌ Guarantees - These improve your odds but can't control the economy or hiring managers

## Contributing Your Own Patterns

If you discover a pattern that worked for you:
1. Document what you tried and what happened
2. Identify the trigger (when to use this pattern)
3. List specific steps you took
4. Note what you'd do differently next time
5. Share it (add to these files or create new ones)

**Example contribution format:**
```markdown

2. [Second thing]
3. [etc.]

**Why it works:**
[Explanation]

**Anti-patterns:**
❌ [What NOT to do]

**Example:**
[Real or realistic scenario]
```
