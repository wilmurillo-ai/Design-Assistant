# IELTS Speaking Band Descriptors (Band 1-9)

Reference document for the IELTS Speaking Coach skill. These descriptors are based on the publicly available IELTS Speaking Band Descriptors and are used to anchor consistent scoring. Band 1-2 are rare (non-attendance or minimal output); Band 3-4 cover low-level candidates who can produce some language but with severe limitations.

## Fluency & Coherence (FC)

### Band 9
- Fluent with only very occasional repetition or self-correction.
- Any hesitation is content-related (thinking about what to say), NOT searching for words or grammar.
- Speech is coherent with fully appropriate cohesive features.
- Topic development is fully coherent and appropriate.

### Band 8
- Fluent with only very occasional repetition or self-correction.
- Hesitation may occasionally be used to find words or grammar, but MOST hesitation is content-related.
- Topic development is coherent and appropriate.
- Cohesive features are used appropriately.

### Band 7
- Able to keep going and readily produce long turns without noticeable effort.
- Some hesitation, repetition, and/or self-correction may occur, often mid-sentence.
- Uses a range of connectives and discourse markers with some flexibility.
- May occasionally lose coherence due to occasional repetition or self-correction.

### Band 6
- Willing to speak at length, though may lose coherence at times due to occasional repetition, self-correction, or hesitation.
- Uses a range of connectives and discourse markers but not always appropriately.
- Coherence may be lost at times.

### Band 5
- Usually maintains flow of speech but uses repetition, self-correction, and/or slow speech to keep going.
- May over-use certain connectives and discourse markers.
- Simple speech is produced fluently, but more complex communication causes disfluency.

### Band 4
- Cannot respond without noticeable pauses; may speak very slowly with frequent repetition.
- Limited coherence; often loses thread of what is being said.
- Limited use of connectives; simple listing only.

### Band 3
- Speaks with long pauses; has difficulty responding without significant support.
- Little coherence; responses are often disjointed or irrelevant.
- Minimal use of discourse markers; may rely on single words or phrases.

### Band 2
- Pauses are very long; communication rarely occurs.
- Speech limited to isolated words or memorised phrases.
- No coherent discourse; cannot sustain a turn.

### Band 1
- Does not attend or no communication possible.
- (Non-scorable)

## Lexical Resource (LR)

### Band 9
- Total flexibility and precise use in ALL contexts.
- Sustained use of accurate and IDIOMATIC language throughout.
- Natural and sophisticated control of lexical features.
- Rare minor "slips" are characteristic of native speaker speech.

### Band 8
- Wide vocabulary resource readily and flexibly used to convey precise meaning.
- Skillful use of uncommon and/or idiomatic items when appropriate.
- Occasional inaccuracies in word choice and collocation.
- Effective paraphrasing as required.

### Band 7
- Vocabulary resource flexibly used to discuss a variety of topics.
- Some ability to use less common and idiomatic items.
- Awareness of style and collocation, with some inappropriate choices.
- Effective paraphrasing used when needed.

### Band 6
- Vocabulary resource sufficient to discuss topics at length, making meaning clear in spite of inappropriacies.
- Generally paraphrases successfully.
- May use some vocabulary inaccurately, but meaning is usually clear.
- Some awareness of style and collocation.

### Band 5
- Vocabulary resource sufficient to discuss familiar topics but LIMITED in flexibility.
- Attempts paraphrase but with mixed success.
- May use simple vocabulary to convey complex ideas.
- Errors in word choice may cause difficulty for the listener.

### Band 4
- Very limited vocabulary; can convey only basic meaning on familiar topics.
- Frequent errors in word choice and collocation.
- May need to search for words or use circumlocution.

### Band 3
- Extremely limited vocabulary; only basic expressions.
- Frequent errors cause strain for the listener.
- May rely on memorised phrases or single words.

### Band 2
- Vocabulary limited to isolated words; no productive use.
- Cannot convey meaning beyond single items.
- Heavy reliance on memorised chunks.

### Band 1
- No productive vocabulary; does not attend.
- (Non-scorable)

## Grammatical Range & Accuracy (GRA)

### Band 9
- Structures are precise and accurate at ALL TIMES.
- Full range of structures used naturally and appropriately.
- Consistent production of error-free sentences.
- Only very occasional "slips" characteristic of native speaker speech.

### Band 8
- Wide range of structures flexibly used.
- MAJORITY of sentences are error-free.
- Occasional non-systematic errors and inappropriacies occur.
- Good control of complex structures.

### Band 7
- Range of complex structures used with some flexibility.
- Error-free sentences are FREQUENT.
- Some grammatical errors persist, but these rarely reduce communication.
- Good control of simple structures.

### Band 6
- Mix of short and complex sentence forms with LIMITED flexibility.
- Uses some complex structures but these tend to be faulty or limited in range.
- Errors occur frequently in complex structures, but rarely impede communication.
- Basic structures generally well controlled.

### Band 5
- Basic sentence forms fairly well controlled.
- Complex structures are attempted but tend to be faulty.
- Errors are frequent and may cause some difficulty for the listener.
- Limited range of structures with few complex forms.

### Band 4
- Only basic sentence forms; complex structures rarely attempted.
- Errors are frequent and may impede communication.
- May produce only short fragments or simple clauses.

### Band 3
- Produces only short fragments or memorised phrases.
- Errors are very frequent and impede basic communication.
- May have difficulty forming basic sentences.

### Band 2
- No grammatical structures produced; only isolated words.
- Cannot form sentences; communication severely limited.
- Memorised phrases may show no grammatical awareness.

### Band 1
- No language produced; does not attend.
- (Non-scorable)

## Pronunciation (PR)

### Band 9
- Full range of phonological features used to convey precise and/or subtle meaning.
- Effortlessly understood throughout.
- Accent has minimal effect on intelligibility.
- Flexible use of features such as intonation, stress, and rhythm.

### Band 8
- Wide range of phonological features used with precision.
- Sustains appropriate intonation and rhythm throughout.
- Can be EASILY understood throughout; accent has minimal effect.
- Occasional mispronunciations have minimal impact.

### Band 7
- Uses a range of pronunciation features with mixed control.
- Shows some effective use of features but control is VARIABLE.
- Generally understood without much effort.
- L1 features may occasionally affect clarity.

### Band 6
- Uses a range of pronunciation features but control is VARIABLE.
- Shows some effective use of features but lapses are common.
- Can generally be understood, but occasional lack of clarity.
- L1 influence may be noticeable.

### Band 5
- Shows some control of features but patterns are not sustained.
- More complex speech causes breakdowns in pronunciation.
- Simpler language may be produced more clearly.
- L1 features may be quite intrusive.

### Band 4
- Limited control of pronunciation features.
- May be difficult to understand at times.
- L1 influence is strong; may struggle with basic sounds.

### Band 3
- Very limited control; often difficult to understand.
- May rely on repetition or reformulation to be understood.
- L1 influence is very strong.

### Band 2
- Speech is largely unintelligible; isolated words may be recognisable.
- Cannot sustain intelligible production.
- L1 phonology dominates.

### Band 1
- No speech produced; does not attend.
- (Non-scorable)
