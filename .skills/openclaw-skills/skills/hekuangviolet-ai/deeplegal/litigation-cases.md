# Key Litigation Cases & Judicial Precedents / 重要诉讼案例与司法先例

This reference provides key cases and judicial interpretations organized by legal topic, for use when drafting contracts, reviewing clauses, or justifying revisions in track-change comments.

## PRC Contract Law Cases / 中国合同法案例

### Liquidated Damages Adjustment / 违约金调整

**Judicial Standard**: Courts may adjust liquidated damages per Civil Code Art. 585(2) if "obviously excessive" or "obviously lower than actual loss."

**Key Guidance**:
- 最高人民法院关于适用《中华人民共和国合同法》若干问题的解释(二) 第29条：当事人约定的违约金超过造成损失的百分之三十的，一般可以认定为"过分高于造成的损失"
- Practical threshold: 130% of actual loss is the general judicial benchmark for "reasonableness"
- Courts consider: actual loss, contract performance, degree of fault, and expected benefits

**Application**: When drafting penalty clauses, set amounts at or below 130% of foreseeable loss. In review comments, cite this standard to persuade counterparty to accept reasonable penalty caps.

### Standard Form Contracts (格式条款) / 格式合同效力

**Key Rules** (Civil Code Art. 496-498):
- Provider must take reasonable steps to draw attention to exemption/limitation clauses
- Failure to fulfill notification obligation: clause does not become part of the contract
- Art. 497: Standard form clauses that unreasonably exempt the provider's liability, increase the other party's liability, or exclude the other party's main rights are void

**Application**: When reviewing counterparty's standard terms, challenge unfair exemption clauses by citing Art. 497. In comments: "该格式条款不合理地免除了提供方的主要义务/加重了我方责任，依据民法典第497条可能被认定为无效。"

### Contract Formation & Performance / 合同成立与履行

**Key Principles**:
- 最高人民法院关于审理买卖合同纠纷案件适用法律问题的解释 (2020修正): Comprehensive guidance on sale contract disputes
- Obligation to mitigate: Non-breaching party must take reasonable measures to prevent loss expansion (Civil Code Art. 591)
- Performance in good faith: Supplementary obligations of notification, assistance, and confidentiality (Civil Code Art. 509)

### Force Majeure / 不可抗力

**COVID-era Guidance**:
- 最高人民法院关于依法妥善审理涉新冠肺炎疫情民事案件若干问题的指导意见: Courts should consider whether performance was actually hindered and whether mitigation measures were taken
- Epidemic alone does not automatically constitute force majeure; must prove causal link between the event and inability to perform
- Party claiming force majeure bears the burden of proof

**Application**: Draft force majeure clauses with specific triggering events and clear notification requirements. In review, ensure the clause includes a mitigation obligation.

## PRC Anti-Unfair Competition Cases / 反不正当竞争案例

### Trade Secret Protection / 商业秘密保护

**Key Judicial Interpretations**:
- 最高人民法院关于审理侵犯商业秘密民事案件适用法律若干问题的规定 (法释[2020]7号):
  - Clarified the "three elements" test for trade secrets: secrecy (秘密性), value (价值性), and protective measures (保密措施)
  - Defined "reasonable protective measures" (合理保密措施): includes confidentiality agreements, access restrictions, markings, physical/electronic security measures
  - Shifted burden of proof: once the right holder establishes prima facie case, alleged infringer bears burden of proving lawful source

**Application in Contract Drafting**:
- Ensure confidentiality clauses expressly satisfy the "reasonable protective measures" standard
- Include specific identification of protected information categories
- Require return/destruction of trade secrets upon termination
- In comments: "该保密条款的措辞不足以满足《反不正当竞争法》第九条及相关司法解释对'保密措施'的要求，建议补充具体保密措施描述。"

### Commercial Bribery / 商业贿赂

**Key Trends**:
- Anti-Unfair Competition Law Art. 7 expanded scope: covers benefits to counterparty's employees or agents that may influence transactions
- "Off-book" benefits (账外暗中给予) are presumed to constitute commercial bribery
- Both the briber and the entity whose employee accepts bribes may face liability

**Application**: Include robust anti-bribery representations and compliance clauses, especially in distribution, agency, and procurement agreements.

### Internet Unfair Competition / 互联网不正当竞争

**Landmark Cases**:
- Tencent v. Qihoo 360 (3Q大战): Supreme People's Court established principles for analyzing internet unfair competition, including the "consumer welfare" standard
- Data scraping cases: Courts have found unauthorized data collection from competitor platforms to constitute unfair competition under Art. 12
- Malicious incompatibility: Deliberately making products incompatible with competitors' products violates Art. 12

**Application**: In technology contracts, include data usage restrictions, API access terms, and interoperability obligations informed by these precedents.

## PRC Intellectual Property Cases / 知识产权案例

### Software Copyright / 软件著作权

**Key Principles**:
- Computer Software Protection Regulations (计算机软件保护条例): Work-for-hire software belongs to the employer unless otherwise agreed
- Source code comparison is primary evidence; functional similarity alone is insufficient
- Open-source license compliance is increasingly scrutinized by courts

### Non-Compete in Employment Context / 劳动竞业限制

**Key Rules** (Labor Contract Law Art. 23-24):
- Limited to senior management, senior technical personnel, and those with confidentiality obligations
- Maximum duration: 2 years after termination
- Monthly compensation required: ≥30% of average monthly salary in 12 months preceding departure
- If employer fails to pay compensation for 3 months, employee may petition to terminate the restriction
- 最高人民法院关于审理劳动争议案件适用法律问题的解释(一) (2020) Art. 36-40: Detailed guidance on non-compete enforcement

**Application**: Always verify that non-compete provisions meet these statutory requirements. In comments: "根据《劳动合同法》第23-24条及最高院劳动争议司法解释，竞业限制条款须满足主体适格、期限不超过两年、按月支付经济补偿等法定要件。"

## US Contract Law Cases / 美国合同法案例

### Limitation of Liability / 责任限制

**Key Principles**:
- Hadley v. Baxendale (1854): Established the foreseeability test for consequential damages - damages must be within the reasonable contemplation of the parties at the time of contracting
- Limitation of liability clauses are generally enforceable but cannot disclaim liability for fraud, willful misconduct, or bodily injury
- UCC § 2-719(3): Limitation of consequential damages for personal injury in consumer goods cases is prima facie unconscionable

**Application**: When drafting liability caps, cite Hadley v. Baxendale principle to justify consequential damages exclusion. In comments: "The exclusion of consequential damages is consistent with the established remoteness principle and industry standard practice."

### Implied Covenant of Good Faith / 诚实信用默示义务

**Key Principle**: Every contract contains an implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing (Restatement (Second) of Contracts § 205). A party cannot exercise contractual discretion in bad faith to deprive the other party of the benefit of the bargain.

**Application**: When reviewing contracts with broad discretionary powers for one party, flag the good faith limitation. In comments: "While this clause grants broad discretion, it remains subject to the implied covenant of good faith, which courts may invoke to limit abusive exercise."

### Non-Compete Enforceability / 竞业限制效力

**Key Variations**:
- California: Non-competes generally unenforceable (Bus. & Prof. Code § 16600), except in limited sale-of-business context
- New York: Enforceable if reasonable in scope, duration, and geography; must protect legitimate business interest
- Delaware: Generally enforceable; Chancery Court applies reasonableness standard
- FTC Non-Compete Rule developments (note: ongoing regulatory changes)

**Application**: Always specify governing law state and tailor non-compete provisions accordingly. For California-governed contracts, use confidentiality and non-solicitation instead of non-compete.

### Indemnification / 赔偿

**Key Principles**:
- Delaware: Indemnification obligations are strictly construed; anti-indemnity statutes exist in some contexts
- New York: Indemnification for one's own negligence requires explicit, unequivocal language (General Obligations Law § 5-322.1 in construction context)
- Duty to defend vs. duty to indemnify: Contractual duty to defend is broader and triggered by allegations, not proof

## Hong Kong Contract Law Cases / 香港合同法案例

### Contract Interpretation / 合同解释

**Key Principles**:
- Investors Compensation Scheme v. West Bromwich Building Society [1998] 1 WLR 896 (adopted in HK): Objective meaning in context; background facts admissible for interpretation but not prior negotiations
- HKSAR courts follow English law principles on contractual interpretation

### Unfair Contract Terms / 不公平合同条款

**Key Legislation**: Control of Exemption Clauses Ordinance (Cap. 71):
- Exemption clauses for negligence causing death/personal injury are void
- Other exemption clauses must satisfy the "reasonableness" test
- The burden of proving reasonableness is on the party seeking to rely on the clause

**Application**: When reviewing contracts governed by HK law, assess exclusion clauses against Cap. 71 reasonableness test. In comments: "This exemption clause must satisfy the reasonableness test under Cap. 71 of the Laws of Hong Kong. The current breadth of the exclusion may be challenged."

### Arbitration & Enforcement / 仲裁与执行

**Key Framework**:
- Arbitration Ordinance (Cap. 609): Based on UNCITRAL Model Law
- Mainland-HK Arrangement on Mutual Enforcement of Arbitral Awards: HKIAC awards can be enforced in Mainland China and vice versa
- Low threshold for enforcement; limited grounds for refusal (consistent with New York Convention)

**Application**: When choosing dispute resolution for cross-border (PRC-foreign) transactions, HKIAC is a strong choice due to the reciprocal enforcement framework. In comments: "HKIAC arbitration offers enforcement advantages in both Mainland China and internationally under the New York Convention."

## Cross-Jurisdictional Comparison / 跨法域比较

### Liquidated Damages / 违约金

| Jurisdiction | Enforceability Standard | Key Risk |
|---|---|---|
| PRC | Adjustable if >130% of actual loss (Civil Code Art. 585) | Courts may reduce on application |
| US | Must be reasonable pre-estimate; not a "penalty" (Restatement §356) | May be struck down if punitive |
| HK | Penalty vs. liquidated damages test (Dunlop v. New Garage) | Penalty clauses unenforceable |

### Non-Compete / 竞业限制

| Jurisdiction | Duration | Compensation Required | Key Limitation |
|---|---|---|---|
| PRC | Max 2 years | Yes (≥30% of avg salary) | Limited personnel scope |
| US (NY) | Reasonable (typically 1-2 years) | Not required | Must protect legitimate interest |
| US (CA) | Not enforceable | N/A | Prohibited except sale-of-business |
| HK | Reasonable | Not required | Must protect proprietary interest |

### Trade Secret Protection / 商业秘密保护

| Jurisdiction | Key Statute | Burden of Proof | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|---|
| PRC | Anti-Unfair Competition Law Art. 9 | Shifted to defendant (prima facie) | "Three elements" test |
| US | Defend Trade Secrets Act (DTSA) + state UTSA | Plaintiff bears initial burden | Reasonable secrecy measures |
| HK | Common law (breach of confidence) | Plaintiff | Information must have quality of confidence |
