# Legal Reference - Jurisdiction Guide / 法律参考 - 法域指南

## PRC Law (中国法)

### Primary Contract Legislation / 主要合同法律

- **Civil Code (民法典)** 2021: Book III Contracts (合同编) is the core contract law
  - Art. 464-988: General provisions, typical contracts, quasi-contracts
  - Art. 470: Essential contract clauses (合同一般条款)
  - Art. 496-498: Standard form contracts (格式条款) - provider must draw attention to exemption/limitation clauses; unfair standard terms void per Art. 497
  - Art. 500-501: Pre-contractual liability (缔约过失责任) and pre-contractual confidentiality
  - Art. 509: Good faith performance; supplementary obligations (通知、协助、保密)
  - Art. 563-566: Statutory termination rights and consequences
  - Art. 577-594: Breach of contract liability (违约责任)
  - Art. 584: Damages: foreseeable loss principle (可预见性规则)
  - Art. 585: Liquidated damages (约定违约金) - court may adjust if excessive (>130% of actual loss per judicial guidance) or insufficient
  - Art. 590: Force majeure (不可抗力)
  - Art. 591: Obligation to mitigate (减损义务)

### Anti-Unfair Competition Law (反不正当竞争法) 2019 Revised

**This is a critical statute for commercial contract practice.** Key provisions:

- **Art. 6 - Confusion/Misappropriation (混淆行为)**:
  - Unauthorized use of well-known trade names, packaging, trade dress
  - Unauthorized use of others' domain names, website names
  - Other acts sufficient to cause confusion with others' goods/services

- **Art. 7 - Commercial Bribery (商业贿赂)**:
  - Prohibition on providing property or other benefits to counterparty's employees, agents, or authorized representatives to secure competitive advantage
  - Off-book benefits (账外暗中) presumed to be bribery
  - Operator's liability for employees' bribery unless operator had no knowledge and did not condone

- **Art. 8 - False Advertising (虚假宣传)**:
  - False or misleading commercial promotions regarding quality, function, ingredients, origin, etc.
  - Platform operators organizing false transactions or fabricating reviews
  - Aiding others in false advertising

- **Art. 9 - Trade Secret Misappropriation (侵犯商业秘密)**:
  - Expanded definition of trade secrets: commercial, technical, or operational information meeting the "three elements" (秘密性、价值性、保密措施)
  - Prohibited acts: theft, bribery, fraud, electronic intrusion, inducing breach of confidentiality
  - Employee/former employee misappropriation: operators liable if they knew or should have known
  - Burden shifting: once right holder establishes prima facie case (possession + similarity + access), alleged infringer must prove lawful source

- **Art. 12 - Internet-Specific Unfair Competition (互联网不正当竞争)**:
  - Inserting links or forcing redirects in others' network products without consent
  - Misleading or forcing users to modify, close, or uninstall others' products
  - Malicious incompatibility with others' network products
  - Other acts disrupting normal operation of others' network products/services

- **Remedies**: Injunction, damages (including punitive damages up to 5x for willful IP infringement), cessation of infringement, public apology

### Other Key Legislation

- **Company Law (公司法)** 2024 revised: Corporate governance, shareholder rights, accelerated capital contribution obligations
- **Foreign Investment Law (外商投资法)** 2020: Foreign-invested enterprise rules, national treatment + negative list
- **Anti-Monopoly Law (反垄断法)** 2022 revised: M&A review thresholds (new turnover standards), restrictive agreements, abuse of dominance, strengthened platform economy regulation
- **Personal Information Protection Law (个人信息保护法, PIPL)** 2021: Data protection, cross-border transfer restrictions (security assessment / standard contract / certification)
- **Data Security Law (数据安全法)** 2021: Data classification, important data export review, data localization
- **Cybersecurity Law (网络安全法)** 2017: Network security, critical information infrastructure protection
- **Labor Contract Law (劳动合同法)** 2013 revised: Employment terms, non-compete (max 2 years, monthly compensation ≥30% of avg salary required)
- **Electronic Commerce Law (电子商务法)** 2019: E-commerce platform operator obligations, consumer protection

### PRC Dispute Resolution

- **Litigation**: People's Courts, two-instance system (两审终审)
  - IP cases: specialized IP courts in Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou; Internet Courts in Beijing, Hangzhou, Guangzhou
  - Jurisdiction: domicile of defendant or place of contract performance (Civil Procedure Law Art. 23-24)
- **Arbitration**: 
  - CIETAC (中国国际经济贸易仲裁委员会): Beijing/Shanghai/Shenzhen, premier for international commercial
  - BAC/BIAC (北京仲裁委员会): Beijing, strong for domestic commercial
  - SHIAC (上海国际仲裁中心): Shanghai
  - SCIA (深圳国际仲裁院): Shenzhen, Greater Bay Area focus
  - Choice of arbitration institution must be clear and unambiguous (otherwise clause may be void per Civil Code Art. 1175)
  - Foreign-related contracts: parties may choose foreign arbitration (e.g., HKIAC, ICC, SIAC)
- **Mediation**: People's mediation, commercial mediation (increasingly encouraged)

### PRC Key Practical Points

- Contract must be signed by legal representative (法定代表人) or authorized agent with power of attorney (授权委托书)
- Company seal (公章) is legally binding; contract seal (合同章) also acceptable
- Unified Social Credit Code (统一社会信用代码) should be included for party identification
- Penalty clauses: generally enforced but courts may adjust to ~130% of actual loss as upper guidance
- Non-compete: max 2 years after employment ends; employer must pay monthly compensation ≥30% of average salary; limited to senior management/technical/confidentiality personnel
- Governing law for purely domestic contracts cannot be foreign law
- Foreign-related contracts: parties can choose governing law; absent choice, closest-connection principle applies
- RMB or foreign currency payment: specify clearly; SAFE (外汇管理局) compliance for cross-border payments

## US Law

### Primary Sources
- **UCC (Uniform Commercial Code)**: Governs sale of goods (Article 2), secured transactions (Article 9), letters of credit (Article 5)
- **Common law**: Governs service contracts, real estate, employment, partnership
- **Restatement (Second) of Contracts**: Influential authority on contract principles
- **FTC Act Section 5**: Prohibits unfair or deceptive acts or practices (federal unfair competition)
- **Lanham Act (15 U.S.C. § 1125)**: Federal unfair competition, false advertising, trademark infringement
- **Defend Trade Secrets Act (DTSA)**: Federal trade secret protection
- **State UTSA (Uniform Trade Secrets Act)**: Adopted in most states; trade secret protection at state level

### State-Specific Considerations
- **Delaware**: Preferred for corporate governance, LLC agreements. Court of Chancery for equity disputes. Business-friendly contract law
- **New York**: Preferred for commercial/financial contracts. GOL §5-1401 (choice of law for contracts ≥$250k). Strong enforcement of contractual provisions
- **California**: Strong employee protections. Non-competes generally unenforceable (Bus. & Prof. Code §16600). CCPA/CPRA for data privacy. Strong consumer protection (UCL)
- **Texas**: Business-friendly. Texas Business & Commerce Code. Broad non-compete enforceability (with reasonableness limitations)

### US Key Practical Points
- **Consideration** required for contract formation (unlike PRC law)
- **Statute of Frauds**: Certain contracts must be in writing (land, goods >$500 under UCC, >1 year, suretyship)
- **Parol Evidence Rule**: Integrated contracts exclude prior oral/written agreements
- **Implied covenant of good faith**: Every contract contains this implied duty (Restatement §205)
- **Limitation of liability**: Generally enforceable but cannot disclaim liability for fraud, willful misconduct, or bodily injury
- **Indemnification**: Key clause in US contracts; clarify defense obligation, scope, and settlement consent
- **Non-compete**: Varies dramatically by state. California: unenforceable. Most states: must be reasonable in scope, duration, geography
- **Export controls**: EAR (BIS) and ITAR (DDTC) compliance required for technology transfers
- **CFIUS**: Foreign investment in US businesses may require CFIUS review
- **FCPA**: Anti-bribery compliance for international transactions
- **Sanctions**: OFAC (Office of Foreign Assets Control) compliance; SDN list screening

### US Dispute Resolution
- Federal vs. state court jurisdiction (diversity jurisdiction: >$75k, complete diversity)
- AAA (American Arbitration Association) for domestic commercial arbitration
- JAMS for complex commercial disputes and mediations
- ICC (International Chamber of Commerce) for international arbitration

## Hong Kong Law

### Primary Sources
- **Common law** system based on English law principles
- **Basic Law**: Constitutional document of HKSAR
- **Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Ordinance** (Cap. 623): Third-party rights in contracts
- **Sale of Goods Ordinance** (Cap. 26): Sale of goods terms (implied terms, remedies)
- **Supply of Services (Implied Terms) Ordinance** (Cap. 457): Service contract implied terms
- **Control of Exemption Clauses Ordinance** (Cap. 71): Unfair exemption clauses - reasonableness test
- **Arbitration Ordinance** (Cap. 609): Based on UNCITRAL Model Law
- **Competition Ordinance** (Cap. 619): Anti-competitive conduct (First Conduct Rule: anti-competitive agreements; Second Conduct Rule: abuse of market power)
- **Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance** (Cap. 486, PDPO): Six data protection principles
- **Prevention of Bribery Ordinance** (Cap. 201): Anti-corruption (covers private sector)
- **Companies Ordinance** (Cap. 622): Company formation, governance, and execution of documents
- **Trade Descriptions Ordinance** (Cap. 362): False trade descriptions, misleading omissions

### Hong Kong Key Practical Points
- **Consideration** required (same as English law) but deed (签署为契据) can bypass this requirement
- **Privity of contract**: Modified by Cap. 623 allowing third-party enforcement in specified cases
- **Contract execution**: No common seal required under Cap. 622; execution by two directors or one director + company secretary
- **Stamp duty**: Required for certain documents (property transfers, share transfers); contracts for services generally exempt
- **Choice of law**: Party autonomy respected; Hong Kong commonly chosen as neutral jurisdiction for China-foreign deals
- **Non-compete**: Enforced if reasonable (restraint of trade doctrine); assessed by reference to proprietary interest, scope, duration, geography
- **Exemption clauses**: Subject to Cap. 71 reasonableness test; cannot exclude negligence causing death/personal injury
- **Data privacy**: Six Data Protection Principles under PDPO; no mandatory data breach notification (but recommended); cross-border transfer restrictions

### Hong Kong Dispute Resolution
- **HKIAC** (Hong Kong International Arbitration Centre): Leading Asian arbitration institution; administered and ad hoc arbitration
- **Courts**: District Court (<HK$3M), Court of First Instance (>HK$3M), Court of Appeal, Court of Final Appeal
- **Mediation**: Encouraged; Hong Kong Mediation Ordinance (Cap. 620); mandatory mediation in some court proceedings
- Hong Kong commonly used as arbitration seat for PRC-related cross-border disputes due to:
  - Mainland-HK reciprocal enforcement of arbitral awards (Arrangement Concerning Mutual Enforcement)
  - Mainland-HK reciprocal enforcement of civil and commercial judgments (2019 Arrangement, effective 2024)
  - New York Convention member
  - Independent legal system with common law traditions

## Cross-Border Considerations / 跨境交易要点

### PRC-US Transactions
- CFIUS review for US-bound investment (mandatory filing for TID businesses)
- Export control compliance (EAR/ITAR for US side; PRC Export Control Law for PRC side)
- PIPL cross-border data transfer requirements (standard contract, certification, or security assessment)
- Anti-bribery compliance (FCPA + PRC anti-bribery laws)
- Sanctions screening (OFAC SDN List for US side)
- Currency controls: SAFE approval/registration for capital account transactions
- Tax: withholding tax, permanent establishment risk, US-PRC tax treaty (1984)
- Technology transfer: PRC technology import/export regulations; US Entity List considerations

### PRC-HK Transactions
- CEPA (Closer Economic Partnership Arrangement) benefits for trade in services
- Reciprocal enforcement of arbitral awards (Arrangement Concerning Mutual Enforcement, as supplemented)
- Reciprocal enforcement of civil judgments (2019 Arrangement)
- Greater Bay Area special policies (financial, professional services, IP)
- HK as common holding structure jurisdiction for PRC outbound investment
- Cross-border data: PIPL applies to PRC-to-HK personal information transfers

### General Cross-Border
- Choice of law: specify clearly; consider which jurisdiction's courts will enforce
- Dispute resolution: international arbitration preferred (enforceable under New York Convention in 170+ jurisdictions)
- Language: specify controlling language version in bilingual contracts
- Currency: specify clearly with exchange rate mechanism if needed
- Force majeure: define specifically; consider pandemic, sanctions, trade wars, regulatory changes
- Compliance: anti-corruption (FCPA, UK Bribery Act, PRC anti-bribery), sanctions, export controls, data privacy
- Service of process: specify addresses and acceptable methods (particularly important for PRC parties under Hague Convention)
- Sovereign immunity: consider if government or state-owned entities are involved
