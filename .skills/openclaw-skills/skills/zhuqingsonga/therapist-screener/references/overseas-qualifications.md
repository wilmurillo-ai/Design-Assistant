# Overseas Mental Health Professional Qualifications

## Overview

This document provides a systematic overview of authoritative mental health professional qualifications recognized in major Western countries (primarily the US, UK, Canada, and Australia).

---

## United States

### 1. Licensed Psychologist (LP / Psychologist License)
- **Issuing Authority**: State Licensing Boards
- **Requirements**:
  - Doctoral degree in psychology (PhD or PsyD)
  - 1,500-3,000 supervised practice hours (varies by state)
  - Passing the Examination for Professional Practice in Psychology (EPPP)
  - Passing state jurisprudence exam
- **Scope**: Can diagnose and treat mental health disorders, provide psychotherapy, psychological assessment
- **Verification**: State licensing board websites, Association of State and Provincial Psychology Boards (ASPPB)

### 2. Licensed Clinical Social Worker (LCSW)
- **Issuing Authority**: State Licensing Boards
- **Requirements**:
  - Master's degree in social work (MSW)
  - 2,000-4,000 supervised clinical hours (varies by state)
  - Passing the Association of Social Work Boards (ASWB) exam
- **Scope**: Psychotherapy, mental health assessment, case management
- **Verification**: State licensing board websites, ASWB

### 3. Licensed Professional Counselor (LPC) / Licensed Mental Health Counselor (LMHC)
- **Issuing Authority**: State Licensing Boards
- **Requirements**:
  - Master's degree in counseling or related field
  - 2,000-4,000 supervised clinical hours
  - Passing the National Counselor Examination (NCE)
- **Scope**: Psychotherapy, mental health counseling
- **Verification**: State licensing board websites, National Board for Certified Counselors (NBCC)

### 4. Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist (LMFT)
- **Issuing Authority**: State Licensing Boards
- **Requirements**:
  - Master's or doctoral degree in marriage and family therapy
  - 2,000-4,000 supervised clinical hours
  - Passing the Marital and Family Therapy National Exam
- **Scope**: Couples therapy, family therapy, individual therapy
- **Verification**: State licensing board websites, Association of Marital and Family Therapy Regulatory Boards (AMFTRB)

## United Kingdom

### 1. Clinical Psychologist (CPsychol)
- **Issuing Authority**: Health and Care Professions Council (HCPC)
- **Requirements**:
  - Doctoral degree in Clinical Psychology (DClinPsychol)
  - 3 years of postgraduate training including supervised practice
- **Scope**: Assessment, diagnosis, treatment of mental health disorders
- **Verification**: HCPC Register

### 2. Counselling Psychologist (CPsychol)
- **Issuing Authority**: Health and Care Professions Council (HCPC)
- **Requirements**:
  - Doctoral degree in Counselling Psychology
  - Structured postgraduate training
- **Scope**: Psychotherapy, psychological assessment
- **Verification**: HCPC Register

### 3. British Association for Counselling and Psychotherapy (BACP) Accreditation
- **Issuing Authority**: BACP
- **Requirements**:
  - BACP-accredited training program
  - Supervised practice hours
  - Continuing professional development
- **Scope**: Counselling and psychotherapy
- **Verification**: BACP Register

## Canada

### 1. Registered Psychologist (R.Psych)
- **Issuing Authority**: Provincial Regulatory Colleges
- **Requirements**:
  - Doctoral degree in psychology
  - Supervised practice hours
  - Passing the Examination for Professional Practice in Psychology (EPPP)
  - Provincial jurisprudence exam
- **Scope**: Psychological assessment, diagnosis, psychotherapy
- **Verification**: Provincial regulatory college websites

### 2. Registered Social Worker (RSW)
- **Issuing Authority**: Provincial Regulatory Colleges
- **Requirements**:
  - Bachelor's or Master's degree in social work
  - Supervised practice
  - Professional exam
- **Scope**: Clinical social work, psychotherapy (varies by province)
- **Verification**: Provincial regulatory college websites

## Australia

### 1. Clinical Psychologist
- **Issuing Authority**: Psychology Board of Australia
- **Requirements**:
  - 4-year psychology degree + 2-year supervised practice
  - or Doctoral degree in Clinical Psychology
  - Continuing professional development
- **Scope**: Assessment, diagnosis, treatment of mental health disorders
- **Verification**: Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA)

### 2. General Psychologist
- **Issuing Authority**: Psychology Board of Australia
- **Requirements**:
  - 4-year psychology degree + 2-year supervised practice
  - Continuing professional development
- **Scope**: Psychological services, counseling
- **Verification**: AHPRA

### 3. Accredited Mental Health Social Worker (AMHSW)
- **Issuing Authority**: Australian Association of Social Workers (AASW)
- **Requirements**:
  - Social work degree
  - Additional mental health training
  - Supervised practice
- **Scope**: Mental health services, counseling
- **Verification**: AASW

## Key Training and Experience Indicators

### 1. Long-term Systematic Training
- Look for 2+ years of structured, systematic training
- Degree programs from accredited institutions
- Recognized certification programs

### 2. Supervision
- Individual supervision (one-on-one with experienced clinician)
- Group supervision
- Ongoing supervision throughout practice

### 3. Continuing Education
- Regular participation in continuing professional development (CPD)
- Attendance at professional conferences and workshops
- Keeping up-to-date with current research and best practices

## How to Verify Credentials

### 1. License/Certification Lookup
- US: State licensing board websites, ASPPB
- UK: HCPC Register, BACP Register
- Canada: Provincial regulatory college websites
- Australia: AHPRA

### 2. Education Verification
- Institutional degree verification
- Transcript review (when necessary)

## Important Notes

### 1. Red Flags
- ❌ No verifiable license or certification
- ❌ Only short-term workshops or online courses (hours or weeks)
- ❌ Exaggerated claims ("100% cure," "guaranteed results")
- ❌ No clear privacy policy or informed consent
- ❌ Unclear or non-transparent fees
- ❌ Attempts to develop non-professional relationships

### 2. Good Indicators
- ✅ Clear license/certification with verifiable status
- ✅ Graduate degree in relevant field
- ✅ Long-term systematic training (2+ years)
- ✅ Ongoing supervision and continuing education
- ✅ Clear professional boundaries and ethical guidelines
- ✅ Transparent fee structure
- ✅ Informed consent process

### 3. Remember
- License requirements vary by country/jurisdiction
- Personal fit is important alongside professional qualifications
- Initial consultation can help assess compatibility
- Always verify credentials through official sources
