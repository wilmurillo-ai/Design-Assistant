# Voice Training Guide

## How to Train AI on Your Brand Voice

### Step 1: Collect Samples (15-20 minimum)
Choose your **best** content — not average work:
- Highest engagement posts
- Best client feedback pieces
- Content that "sounds most like you"

### Step 2: Analyze Patterns
Feed samples to AI with this prompt:
```
Analyze these writing samples and identify:
1. Average sentence length
2. Vocabulary level (simple/technical/mixed)
3. Tone markers (formal/casual/authoritative/friendly)
4. Structural patterns (paragraph length, use of lists, headers)
5. Signature phrases or expressions
6. What the writer NEVER does
```

### Step 3: Build Voice Profile
Convert analysis into instructions:
```
VOICE PROFILE: [Brand Name]
- Sentence style: [Short and punchy / Long and flowing / Mixed]
- Tone: [Direct / Conversational / Academic / Irreverent]
- Vocabulary: [Simple words / Industry jargon / Mix]
- Structure: [Short paragraphs / Long sections / Bullet-heavy]
- Signature moves: [List unique habits]
- Never use: [Words/phrases to avoid]
- Always do: [Consistent patterns]
```

### Step 4: Test and Refine
- Write same piece manually AND with AI voice profile
- Have someone who knows your work compare them
- Refine until indistinguishable
- Typically takes 2-3 iterations

### Common Voice Profiles

**The Expert**: Authoritative, data-heavy, measured
**The Friend**: Casual, story-driven, relatable
**The Provocateur**: Bold, contrarian, short sentences
**The Teacher**: Step-by-step, patient, thorough
**The Insider**: Industry jargon, exclusive knowledge, "behind the scenes"
