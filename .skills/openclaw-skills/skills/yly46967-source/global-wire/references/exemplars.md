# Exemplars

These short examples show the intended GlobalWire feel.

## Brief exemplar

```md
# GlobalWire Briefing

- Current style: wire
- Current length: brief

## 1) Oil rises as US-Iran tensions sharpen over new negotiation claims

**Lead**
Fresh claims of possible US-Iran diplomatic movement failed to ease market nerves after both sides signaled sharply different positions. The story mattered less as a negotiation headline than as a sign that military and diplomatic pressure were now running in parallel.

**Facts**
- Washington said a new channel for talks was under discussion.
- Tehran denied direct talks had materially advanced.
- Oil markets remained sensitive to any risk around shipping and regional escalation.

**Analysis**
- The key issue is whether diplomacy is genuinely opening or simply buying time inside a still-volatile confrontation.
- Market sensitivity suggests that even small shifts in the conflict path can matter quickly.

**Sources**
- Reuters
- AP
- Official statements

**Confidence**
Likely credible
```

## Chinese brief exemplar

```md
# GlobalWire 简报

- 当前风格: wire
- 当前长度: brief

## 1) 霍尔木兹风险抬高，油价与外交压力同步上升

**导语**
围绕美伊紧张局势的最新表态未能真正缓解市场担忧，能源与外交风险继续同步上升。当前最关键的问题不是口头上的缓和信号，而是局势是否正在从高压僵持转向真正降温。

**事实**
- 华盛顿释放了新的谈判信号。
- 德黑兰否认正式谈判已取得实质进展。
- 市场继续把霍尔木兹海峡相关风险计入油价。

**分析**
- 眼下最重要的不是措辞，而是未来几天是否出现可验证的降级动作。
- 如果航运与能源设施风险继续上升，全球市场仍会保持敏感。

**来源**
- Reuters
- AP
- 官方声明

**可信度**
较可信
```

For Chinese output, localize the structural labels as well as the body labels. Do not leave `Current style`, `Current length`, `Lead`, `Facts`, or `Confidence` in English unless the user explicitly requests bilingual formatting.

## Timeline exemplar

```md
# GlobalWire Timeline

- Current style: wire
- Current length: brief

## 2026 US-Iran conflict

**Current state**
The conflict remains unresolved, with military pressure, diplomatic signaling, and market stress moving at the same time. The most important question is whether current moves mark a path to de-escalation or simply a pause inside a wider confrontation.

**Timeline**
- 2026-02-28: US and Israeli strikes pushed the crisis into open military confrontation.
- 2026-03-10: Washington signaled the campaign would continue until Iranian capabilities were meaningfully degraded.
- 2026-03-18: attacks on energy-linked infrastructure widened the conflict's economic stakes.
- 2026-03-24: new ceasefire terms emerged alongside further military positioning, showing diplomacy and coercion were advancing together.

**Key turns**
- The conflict widened from deterrence to direct action.
- Energy and shipping risks became part of the strategic battlefield.

**Disputed or still developing**
- casualty counts
- some battlefield damage claims

**Sources**
- Reuters
- BBC
- Official statements

**Confidence**
Needs cross-verification
```
