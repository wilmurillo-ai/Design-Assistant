# Credibility Rules

GlobalWire grades answer reliability, not absolute truth.

## Confidence labels

- `Confirmed`
- `Likely credible`
- `Needs cross-verification`
- `Single-source lead`
- `Likely noise`

Translate them into the user's language when appropriate.

Recommended Chinese labels:

- `已证实`
- `较可信`
- `待交叉验证`
- `单源线索`
- `疑似噪声`

## How to use them

### Confirmed

Use when:

- the core event is strongly supported by reputable international reporting and/or official primary material
- the main points are stable

### Likely credible

Use when:

- the event is real and broadly supported
- but some operational details remain fluid

### Needs cross-verification

Use when:

- the answer may be useful
- but sourcing is still mixed, incomplete, or partially dependent on weaker reporting

### Single-source lead

Use when:

- the answer relies mostly on one source or one weak thread

### Likely noise

Use when:

- the answer is mostly rumor, circular citation, or hype

## Downgrade triggers

Downgrade the confidence when:

- domestic sources dominate a major geopolitics or conflict answer
- the timeline includes major nodes that lack strong support
- the answer mixes fact and speculation
- important details conflict across sources

Formatting rule:

- keep the confidence label itself short and stable
- if nuance is needed, add a separate note after the main sections
- do not write `Likely credible（但……）`-style hybrid labels
