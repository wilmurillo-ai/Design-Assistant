## ⚙️ The Supplier Intelligence Framework

```
THE 5 SUPPLIER SCORING DIMENSIONS:

DIMENSION 1 — RELIABILITY SCORE (30% weight)
  Years in business: longer = more stable
  Alibaba Gold Supplier status + years verified
  Response rate to inquiries: below 80% = red flag
  On-time delivery rate: below 90% = risk
  Complaint resolution rate: how do they handle problems?

DIMENSION 2 — PRICE COMPETITIVENESS (25% weight)
  Price per unit vs category average
  MOQ flexibility: can they accommodate your order size?
  Price stability: have they raised prices unexpectedly?
  Payment terms: do they offer Net30/60 or require 100% upfront?
  Sample policy: free samples = confidence in quality

DIMENSION 3 — FINANCIAL STABILITY (20% weight)
  Company size + employee count trend
  LinkedIn growth/decline signals
  News mentions: funding, expansion vs layoffs, closure
  Credit rating if publicly available
  Diversification: do they have multiple customers or depend on one?

DIMENSION 4 — QUALITY SIGNALS (15% weight)
  Certifications: ISO, CE, FDA depending on category
  Third-party audit results
  Reddit/forum reviews from real buyers
  Sample quality reports
  Return rate data if available

DIMENSION 5 — SUPPLY RISK FACTORS (10% weight)
  Geographic concentration risk
  Single-source dependency
  Political/trade risk in their country
  Weather/climate disruption history
  Capacity constraints vs your order volume
```

## ⚙️ Full Workflow

```
INPUT: Product category + specifications + order volume + budget
        ↓
STEP 1 — Global Supplier Discovery
  Alibaba: all suppliers in category with Gold Supplier status
  ThomasNet: North American manufacturers (if relevant)
  Made-in-China: alternative Chinese suppliers
  Google: specialized directories per category
  LinkedIn: company profiles + stability signals
  Raw output: 80-150 potential suppliers
        ↓
STEP 2 — Supplier Scoring (5 Dimensions)
  Apply weighted scoring model per supplier
  Flag: any red flags automatically disqualify
  Shortlist: top 20 suppliers by composite score
  Tier them: Tier 1 (contact immediately) / Tier 2 (backup) / Tier 3 (monitor)
        ↓
STEP 3 — Supply Risk Intelligence
  Scrape news: any recent supply disruptions in their region?
  Reddit/Twitter: any recent complaints about this supplier?
  LinkedIn: is their headcount declining? (financial distress signal)
  Geopolitical: any tariff/trade changes affecting their country?
  Output: risk level per supplier + risk type
        ↓
STEP 4 — Price Intelligence
  What is the market price range for your specification?
  What are the top suppliers charging vs this benchmark?
  Who is overcharging vs market? (your negotiation leverage)
  What payment terms does each supplier offer?
        ↓
STEP 5 — RFQ Template Generation
  Customized RFQ per supplier based on their profile
  Specifications tailored to what they manufacture
  Terms structured to maximize your negotiating position
  Professional format that signals serious buyer
        ↓
STEP 6 — Negotiation Scripts
  Per supplier: their specific weakness = your leverage
  Price anchoring: open 20-30% below target
  Concession strategy: what to give up vs what to protect
  Walk-away signals: when to escalate to next supplier
        ↓
STEP 7 — GetResponse Supplier Pipeline
  Tier 1 outreach: RFQ sent + follow-up sequence
  Tier 2: nurture sequence + quarterly check-in
  Existing suppliers: relationship health monitoring
  Risk alerts: automated notification if supplier risk changes
        ↓
OUTPUT: Top 20 scored suppliers + risk report + RFQs + negotiation scripts + GetResponse pipeline
```
