# Travel Tips

General travel advice, packing lists, and reminders.

## Packing Lists

### Warm Weather (20-30°C)
- Light clothing (t-shirts, shorts, dresses)
- Sunscreen SPF 30+
- Sunglasses
- Hat
- Sandals
- Swimwear (if beach/pool)

### Cool Weather (10-20°C)
- Light jacket or cardigan
- Long pants
- Closed-toe shoes
- Light scarf
- Layers (t-shirt + sweater)

### Cold Weather (0-10°C)
- Winter coat / down jacket
- Warm layers (thermal underwear)
- Gloves, hat, scarf
- Warm socks
- Boots
- Hand warmers

### Rainy Season
- Rain jacket or umbrella
- Waterproof shoes
- Plastic bags for electronics
- Quick-dry clothing

## Universal Packing List

- Passport + copies
- Travel insurance documents
- Credit cards + some cash
- Phone + charger
- Power adapter (check voltage)
- Medications + prescriptions
- Toiletries
- First aid kit (band-aids, pain reliever)
- Reusable water bottle
- Snacks for travel day

## Visa Requirements

### Japan
- Singapore citizens: Visa-free up to 90 days
- Check: https://www.mofa.go.jp/j_info/visit/visa/

### South Korea
- Singapore citizens: Visa-free up to 90 days (K-ETA required)
- Apply K-ETA: https://www.k-eta.go.kr/

### Europe (Schengen)
- Singapore citizens: Visa-free up to 90 days
- ETIAS required from 2025: https://travel-europe.europa.eu/etias_en

### USA
- Singapore citizens: ESTA required
- Apply: https://esta.cbp.dhs.gov/

## Travel Insurance

Recommended coverage:
- Medical expenses (minimum $50,000)
- Trip cancellation
- Lost luggage
- Flight delay/cancellation

Providers:
- Income Travel Insurance
- AXA Travel Insurance
- FWD Travel Insurance

## International Driving

### International Driving Permit (IDP)
- Required for: Japan, South Korea, USA, most of Europe
- Get from: AA Singapore or Singapore Automobile Association
- Validity: 1 year
- Cost: ~$20 SGD
- Must carry alongside Singapore license

### Countries accepting Singapore license directly
- Malaysia, Thailand (short term), Australia (with translation)

## Health & Safety

### Vaccinations
- Check requirements: https://www.healthhub.sg/programmes/travellers-health-advisory
- Common vaccines: Hepatitis A/B, Typhoid, Japanese Encephalitis (Asia)

### Travel Medications
- Anti-diarrhea (Imodium)
- Pain reliever (Paracetamol)
- Antihistamine (for allergies)
- Motion sickness pills
- Personal prescriptions (carry extra)

### Emergency Numbers
- Japan: 110 (Police), 119 (Ambulance)
- South Korea: 112 (Police), 119 (Ambulance)
- Europe: 112 (Universal emergency)
- USA: 911

## Money & Payments

### Best practices
- Notify bank of travel dates
- Carry 2-3 credit cards (in case one fails)
- Keep some local currency cash
- Use ATMs at banks (better rates than money changers)

### Credit card recommendations
- UOB One Card (fee-free overseas spending)
- DBS Live Fresh (fee-free overseas)
- AMEX (accepted widely, but not everywhere)

## Time Zones

- Tokyo (JST): UTC+9 (1h ahead of Singapore)
- Seoul (KST): UTC+9 (1h ahead of Singapore)
- London (GMT/BST): UTC+0/+1 (7-8h behind Singapore)
- New York (EST/EDT): UTC-5/-4 (12-13h behind Singapore)

## Cultural Tips

### Japan
- Remove shoes indoors
- Bow when greeting
- No tipping
- Speak quietly on trains
- Don't eat while walking

### South Korea
- Bow when greeting elders
- Use both hands when receiving items
- Take off shoes indoors
- Don't write names in red (bad luck)

### General
- Research local customs before arrival
- Learn basic phrases (hello, thank you, sorry)
- Dress modestly at religious sites
