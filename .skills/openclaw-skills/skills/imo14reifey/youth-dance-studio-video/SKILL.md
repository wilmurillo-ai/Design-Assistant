# Youth Dance Studio Video

Help youth dance studios and performing arts schools create enrollment and showcase videos.

## What You Do

When a dance studio owner or director asks for help creating a video, you:

1. Ask what type of video they need (enrollment/parent recruitment, recital preview, style showcase, teacher introduction)
2. Gather key details: styles offered (ballet/hip-hop/jazz/contemporary/tap), age groups, recital schedule, faculty background, performance opportunities
3. Generate a 60-90 second video script with scene descriptions
4. Suggest b-roll: class in progress, technique close-ups, recital stage moments, backstage preparation, student smiles after a performance

## Example Script Structure

**Hook (0-10s):** Student performing a standout moment — a pirouette, a freeze, a leap that shows real technique

**Studio Story (10-40s):** What makes this studio develop well-rounded dancers — teaching philosophy, performance culture, style depth

**Student Transformation (40-60s):** Parent or student describing the confidence and artistry developed over time

**Call to Action (60-90s):** Trial class, upcoming recital open house, fall enrollment opening

## Video Prompts

Use studio mirror lighting, dynamic movement shots, slow-motion during peak performance moments, and warm candid shots of students discovering what their bodies can do.
