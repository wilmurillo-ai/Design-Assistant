# Production Schedule — Structure Reference
*Compiled from StudioBinder, Evercast, and industry standards*

## Types of Production Schedules

### 1. One-Liner Schedule (Most Common on Set)
A condensed overview of the entire shoot. One line per scene. Used by all departments for planning. Created from the stripboard.

Format per line:
`[Scene #] | [Int/Ext] | [Location] | [Day/Night] | [Pages] | [Cast #s] | [Notes]`

Example:
```
DAY 1 — Monday, July 14 — LOCATION: Riverside Diner, 45 Main St
SC 1 | INT | DINER | DAY | 2 4/8 | Cast: 1,2,3 | Breakfast scene, 6 pages
SC 4 | INT | DINER KITCHEN | DAY | 1 2/8 | Cast: 2,4 | Aftermath
SC 7 | EXT | DINER PARKING LOT | DAY | 1 0/8 | Cast: 1 | Drive-off

DAY 2 — Tuesday, July 15 — LOCATION: Hayes Apartment, 88 Oak Ave
SC 2 | INT | BEDROOM | NIGHT | 3 4/8 | Cast: 1,5 | Crucial dialogue scene
SC 8 | INT | KITCHEN | NIGHT | 1 0/8 | Cast: 1 | Solo scene
```

### 2. Stripboard (Full Production Board)
Visual, color-coded board where each strip = one scene. Used to physically reorder scenes and plan shoot order.

Colors:
- **White** — Day interior scenes
- **Yellow** — Day exterior scenes
- **Blue** — Night interior scenes
- **Green** — Night exterior scenes
- **Red** — End of shoot day marker
- **Black** — Non-shooting days (travel, prep, hold)

### 3. Task/Department Schedule

## Key Scheduling Principles

### Order of Operations for Scene Scheduling
1. **Group by location** — minimize location moves (most expensive cost factor)
2. **Group by cast** — minimize days needed for expensive/busy actors
3. **Interior before exterior** — weather flexibility
4. **Nights last** — crew fatigue management
5. **Complex scenes early** — when crew is fresh
6. **Coverage/pickups last** — easier to add/remove

### Page Count & Timing Rules
- 1 script page ≈ 1 minute of screen time
- Industry standard: 3-5 pages per shoot day (narrative film)
- Faster pace: 5-8 pages/day (TV, commercial)
- Action/VFX heavy: 1-2 pages/day
- A "5/8 page scene" = 5 out of 8 equal sections of one page

### Turnaround Rules (Union Standards)
- **Minimum turnaround:** 12 hours between company wrap and next call (IATSE)
- **Forced call:** When turnaround is violated (requires premium pay)
- **Golden time:** 16+ continuous hours = double golden time (union)
- **French hours:** No formal meal break — rolling craft service only

### Day Out Of Days (DOOD) Chart
Tracks each actor's working/travel/hold days. Essential for SAG compliance and budgeting.

| Character | Actor | Mon Jul 14 | Tue Jul 15 | Wed Jul 16 | Total Work Days |
|-----------|-------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Detective Hayes | Jane Smith | W | W | H | 2 |
| Maria | Carlos Rivera | W | SW | | 1.5 |

W = Work Day | SW = Start Work | H = Hold | F = Finish | T = Travel | SWF = Start/Work/Finish
