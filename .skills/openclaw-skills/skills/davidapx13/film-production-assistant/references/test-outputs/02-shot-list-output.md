## COVERAGE STRATEGY
Start wide to establish geography and the power dynamic of the two characters at opposite ends of the kitchen. Build in on Hayes first — he's the aggressor, the scene's engine. Switch to tighter Maria coverage as her resistance starts to crack. The badge placement (Shot 6) is the pivot: shoot it beautiful and slow, it's the movie's visual thesis. After the mug smash, hold wide on Hayes's non-reaction before going to Maria's aftermath. End on her — alone, deciding what she's going to do next.

Do NOT shoot handheld. This scene is shot on sticks. The camera's stillness is the scene's tension. Let the actors move; the camera watches.

## LIGHTING CONSIDERATIONS
- Rain on window = natural motivation for a cool, slightly blue key light
- Practical kitchen light (overhead fluorescent or warm overhead) for Maria's side
- Hayes stays in slightly cooler/darker light — he's the unknown in the scene
- Rain shadow patterns on walls if exterior rain rig can achieve it
- No beauty light — this is a working kitchen at night, not cinematic unless it's earned
- Sunset/sunrise not a factor — all interior, schedule independent of natural light

## RISKS / FLAGS
1. MUG RESETS — each smash take requires cleanup and a new mug. Budget 10-15 min per reset. Have at least 5 breakaway mugs.
2. KITCHEN SPACE — will likely need to pull appliances or walls for lens positions. Scout carefully and confirm with DP.
3. RAIN CONTINUITY — if the rain sound drops between setups, audiences will notice. Keep rain playback consistent or use post.
4. REFRIGERATOR — turn off before rolling. It will kill your sound.
5. Shot 15 (slow dolly close) needs a smooth slider run in a potentially cramped space — pre-rig during setup.
