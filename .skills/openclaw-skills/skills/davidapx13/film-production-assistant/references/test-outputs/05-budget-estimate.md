BUDGET RISK FLAGS ⚠️
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. ⚠️ LOCATION FEE — WIDEST VARIABLE (±$1,500)
   A real coffee shop may charge $0 (negotiated access) or $500-1,500/day.
   Two full shoot days at The Daily Grind = $0-3,000 swing. Lock this first.
   HOW TO FIX: Approach the café owner directly. Offer: screen credit, still photos,
   a press mention, and promise to shoot in their slow window (Tuesday 6am-noon).
   Many small businesses say yes. This single negotiation is worth 4 hours of your time.

2. ⚠️ SOUND — NON-NEGOTIABLE QUALITY (±$600)
   This is a dialogue-heavy drama set in a noisy coffee shop. Bad sound will kill it
   at festivals before anyone watches the picture. $1,200-1,500 for a competent sound
   mixer with a proper kit is the floor. Do not go lower.
   DO NOT skip this to make budget. The editor will hate you.

3. ⚠️ CATERING — OFTEN UNDERESTIMATED (±$600)
   Crew morale = food. Productions that cut food get slower, unhappier crews.
   Build catering to $1,500 minimum for 3 days × 10 people. A fed crew shoots faster
   than a hungry crew. The math works out.

4. ⚠️ INSURANCE — COMMONLY SKIPPED, ALWAYS REGRETTED (±$800-1,200)
   One injury, one stolen camera, one location damage claim = lawsuit.
   The coffee shop location will likely REQUIRE proof of production insurance before
   they allow you in. Don't find out the hard way.
   GET A QUOTE: Athos Insurance, Front Row Insurance, or DeWitt Stern.

5. ⚠️ OVERTIME — NOT BUDGETED
   Current estimate assumes clean 12-hour days. Real shoots run 13-14 hours.
   If you're paying crew (even informally), 2 extra hours/day × 3 days × 8 crew = 48
   additional hours. At any rate, that adds up.
   BUFFER: Add $500-800 to your miscellaneous line for overtime gratuities/fees.

COST-SAVING RECOMMENDATIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. LOCATION TRADE: Negotiate free coffee shop access in exchange for:
   - Screen credit ("Filmed at The Daily Grind")
   - Professional still photos for their social media (use your BTS photographer)
   - Commitment to shoot in their slowest window
   Saves: $0 – $3,000

2. LENS RENTAL OVER CAMERA: You own the body — rent only the glass.
   A 3-lens set (35mm, 50mm, 85mm) for 3 days: $200-400 from LensRentals or BorrowLenses.
   Saves vs. full camera package: ~$600-900

3. STUDENT COMPOSER: Post an offer on Berklee Online, USC Thornton, or local film school boards.
   A student composer will often score a 15-minute short for deferred pay + festival credit.
   Saves: $200-400 vs. licensed music

4. DEFER EDITORIAL: You're already planning to edit. Execute the deferral agreement in writing —
   this is also a tax/accounting issue if the film is ever sold or screened commercially.

5. CREW FOOD STRATEGY: Instead of 3 catered meals/day, run craft service all day + 1 hot catered
   meal (lunch). Quality lunch, self-service breakfast and dinner snacks.
   Saves: ~$300-500 vs. 3 catered meals

Note: LA market rates are 30-50% above national average. Adjust significantly if shooting
in Atlanta, Albuquerque, Chicago, or other markets.
