## BUDGET NOTES

**What can be cut and where:**
- DP rate can drop to $350/day if you find someone building their reel (saves ~$1,200)
- Props budget could shrink to $1,500 with careful scouting of existing locations
- Catering: reduce crew size or bring on a craft service sponsor
- Post-production: edit in-house on DaVinci (free) to eliminate editing suite cost

**What should NOT be cut:**
- Stunt Coordinator (SC 20) — do not DIY this. It's a liability
- Sound package — bad sound kills indie films. Non-negotiable
- Contingency — NYC production on a deadline will need this
- Insurance — E&O required for any distribution deal

**Items needing real quotes:**
- NYC film permits (vary widely by location type and duration)
- Location fees (diner and precinct interior may require substantial negotiation)
- Bridge permit (NYC DOT — can be $0 to $5K+ depending on scope)
- Camera package (rates fluctuate; check with local NYC houses)

**Hidden costs to watch:**
- Overtime: 4 night shoots with 20-person crew. Even 1 hr forced OT = $3K
- Rain equipment (4 night exterior scenes in summer NYC = rain risk)
- Parking: NYC parking for a production truck can cost $200-300/day
- Generator rental for locations without adequate power

---

## COST-REDUCTION OPTIONS

1. **Reduce to 10 crew (skeleton crew):** Save ~$25K on crew wages. Trade: slower days, less coverage, more creative limitations. Works for a very contained story like Night Falls.

2. **Shoot non-union throughout:** Remove SAG fees entirely (~$4K savings). Trade: cannot do SAG festival screenings without a waiver.

3. **Defer post-production:** Pay editor on backend deal. Pay composer on backend. Focus cash budget on production. This is industry-standard for micro-budget features. Potential savings: $8-12K upfront.
