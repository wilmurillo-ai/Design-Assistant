# Call Sheet — Structure Reference
*Compiled from Celtx, CalArts 2 Pop, and industry standards*

## What Is a Call Sheet?
A call sheet is a document sent to all cast and crew the day before each shoot day. It contains all logistical information needed to execute that day's production. Usually prepared by the 1st AD, UPM, or Producer.

## Standard Call Sheet Sections

### Header Block
- Production Title
- Production Company
- Shoot Date
- Day X of Y (e.g., "Day 3 of 10")
- General Call Time (crew call)
- Location (address, GPS)
- Nearest Hospital + Police Station (emergency contacts)
- Weather Forecast (temp, precipitation)
- Sunrise / Sunset times

### Production Contact Info
- Director
- 1st AD
- UPM / Line Producer
- Producer
- Key phone numbers

### Scene List for the Day
For each scene:
- Scene # | Int/Ext | Location Name | Day/Night | Pages
- Estimated shoot time
- Cast required

### Advance Schedule
What is planned for TOMORROW — so departments can prep.

### Location Notes
- Basecamp address (where meals/meetings happen)
- Parking instructions
- Access codes or contact person on-site
- Directions and alternate routes

### Cast List (Day Players)
| Character | Actor Name | Character # | Makeup Call | Set Call | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Detective Hayes | Jane Smith | 1 | 5:30 AM | 7:00 AM | Hair/makeup trailer A |
| Maria | Carlos Rivera | 2 | 6:00 AM | 7:30 AM | Costume fitting first |

### Background / Extras
- How many, what type, call time

### Transport Notes
- Pick-up times and locations for cast
- Shuttle schedule
- Equipment truck arrival

### Crew Grid
| Name | Title | Department | Call Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sam Lee | Director of Photography | Camera | 6:00 AM |
| Alex Ford | Gaffer | Electric | 5:30 AM |

### Crew / Set Notes
- Parking permit info
- COVID/health protocols (if active)
- Required safety equipment
- Special instructions for the day
- Catering schedule (breakfast/lunch/dinner times)

### Advanced Scenes (Tomorrow)
Brief description of next day's scenes for prep purposes.

---

## Call Sheet Best Practices
- Distribute to ALL cast and crew minimum 12 hours before call time
- Always include emergency contacts (medical, fire, police)
- Weather info is critical for exterior shoots
- Mark any scenes involving stunts, minors, or animals clearly
- Include parking permits and access info in detail
- Note any equipment that must be pre-rigged or pre-lit
