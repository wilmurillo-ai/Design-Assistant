# How-to Format — Detailed Instructions

## What is a How-to Post?

A step-by-step guide that teaches the reader how to achieve a specific, actionable outcome. The most practical format — readers save and share these for later reference.

## Structure

```
HOOK (1-2 lines)
Promise a clear outcome.
Example: "How to build an AI content engine in 48 hours (without writing a single line of code)"
Alternative: "Most people get LinkedIn engagement wrong. Here's what actually works."

WHY (2-3 lines)
Why this matters, what most people get wrong, the cost of not doing it.
Example:
"90% of LinkedIn posts get under 100 impressions.
Not because the content is bad - because the strategy is.
Here's the system that took my posts from 200 to 20,000 average views."

STEPS (3-7 numbered steps)
Each step has:
- Clear action verb (Set up, Configure, Track, Build, etc.)
- Brief explanation of why it works (1 sentence)
- Specific tool, metric, or example when relevant

Example:
1. Set up your content calendar with 3 content pillars
→ Consistency beats creativity. Pick 3 topics you can write about weekly.

2. Use Brave Search to find trending data daily
→ Fresh data hooks get 3x more engagement than opinion-only posts.

3. Write your hook FIRST, then build the post around it
→ LinkedIn shows only 2 lines before "see more." Those 2 lines determine everything.

4. Post between 7-9 AM your audience's timezone
→ LinkedIn's algorithm gives maximum reach in the first 90 minutes.

5. Reply to every comment within 2 hours
→ Comment velocity signals quality to the algorithm. Early replies compound reach.

PRO TIP (1-2 lines)
One non-obvious shortcut or insight.
Example: "Pro tip - screenshot your best posts and keep a swipe file. Your past hits tell you exactly what your audience wants."

RESULT (1-2 lines)
What they will achieve if they follow these steps.
Example: "Do this for 30 days and you'll see 3-5x your current engagement. The data is consistent."

CTA (1-2 lines)
Example: "Try step 1 today and tell me how it goes."
```

## Constraints

- Every step must be specific and actionable, not vague
- Use real tools, numbers, and examples
- Tone: practical, mentor-to-peer, not lecturing
- ZERO asterisks (*) in output
- No em dashes (—), no URLs, plain text only
- For short posts: 3-4 steps, minimal explanation
- For medium posts: 4-6 steps, full structure
- For long posts: 5-7 steps, detailed explanations, multiple examples

## Example Output (Medium Length)

```
How to research and write a viral LinkedIn post in 30 minutes.

Most people spend 2 hours writing a post that gets 50 likes.
Not because they can't write - because they skip the research step.

Here's my exact process:

1. Pick a trending topic in your niche
→ Use search tools to find what's getting attention THIS week. Stale topics get stale engagement.

2. Find 2-3 specific data points
→ Numbers stop the scroll. "Revenue grew" is forgettable. "Revenue grew 340% in 8 months" is a hook.

3. Write the hook first
→ LinkedIn shows 2 lines before "see more." If those 2 lines don't create curiosity, nothing else matters.

4. Use the toplist or POV structure
→ Toplists work for curation. POVs work for opinions. Pick one and follow the template.

5. End with a genuine question
→ Comments drive reach. A specific question ("Which of these surprises you most?") beats generic ("Thoughts?")

Pro tip - write 3 hooks for every post and pick the most specific one. Specificity always wins.

Follow this for 2 weeks. You'll see the difference in your engagement metrics.

Which step do you usually skip?
```
