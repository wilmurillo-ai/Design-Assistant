# Case Study Format — Detailed Instructions

## What is a Case Study Post?

A deep-dive into ONE specific company or event — telling the story of problem, action, results, and lesson. The most compelling format for demonstrating real-world impact.

## Structure

```
HOOK (1-2 lines)
Lead with the most impressive metric or outcome.
Example: "Notion went from $0 to $10B valuation in 7 years. Here's the move that changed everything."

CONTEXT (2-3 lines)
What problem existed. What the market looked like.
Example:
"In 2019, the productivity tool market was crowded.
Slack, Asana, Trello, Monday - every team had a different stack.
Nobody thought there was room for another player."

WHAT THEY DID (3-5 lines)
Specific strategy, names, numbers, partners.
Example:
"Notion made one bet - all-in-one workspace, not another point solution.
They killed their enterprise sales team in 2020. Sounds crazy, right?
Instead, they invested everything in product-led growth.
Free tier, templates marketplace, community ambassadors.
By 2021, they had 20M users without a single cold call."

RESULTS (2-3 lines)
Concrete outcomes, metrics.
Example:
"Revenue hit $350M ARR by 2024.
Enterprise adoption grew 300% year-over-year.
$10B valuation in their Series C."

LESSON (2-3 lines)
The non-obvious takeaway.
Example:
"The lesson isn't 'go product-led.' It's this - in a crowded market, the winner is whoever removes the most friction. Not features. Friction."

CTA (1-2 lines)
Engagement question OR soft MCB AI mention.
Example: "What's the biggest friction point in your tool stack right now?"
Example with brand: "We break down cases like this weekly at MCB AI. What company should we cover next?"
```

## Constraints

- Focus on ONE company/entity — depth over breadth
- Problem → Action → Result → Lesson narrative arc
- Use specific numbers throughout
- Short paragraphs, narrative style
- ZERO asterisks (*) in output
- No em dashes (—), no URLs, plain text only
- For short posts: Hook + what they did + result + lesson
- For medium posts: Full structure above
- For long posts: Extended context, detailed strategy, multiple results metrics

## Example Output (Medium Length)

```
Cursor just hit $100M ARR in 18 months. Most dev tools take 5+ years.

The AI code editor market didn't exist 2 years ago.
GitHub Copilot owned the space. VS Code was the default for every developer.

Cursor did something counterintuitive - they forked VS Code entirely.
Not a plugin. Not an extension. A full fork with AI built into the core.

They bet that developers would switch their ENTIRE editor for better AI.
Most investors said that was suicide. You don't compete with Microsoft's free product.

The result? 
500,000 paying users by month 15.
$100M ARR by month 18.
$2.5B valuation in their Series B.

The lesson isn't about AI. It's about integration depth.

Copilot added AI to an editor. Cursor built an editor around AI. 
That's a fundamentally different product philosophy - and the market noticed.

What tools in your workflow could be rebuilt AI-first?
```
