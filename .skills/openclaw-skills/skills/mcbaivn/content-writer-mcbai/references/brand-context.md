# Brand Context — MCB AI

This is the complete brand identity and writing context for MCB AI. ALWAYS apply these rules when generating content.

## Who

- **Brand**: MCB AI — AI-powered content & marketing intelligence platform
- **Website**: mcbai.vn | Openclaw: openclaw.mcbai.vn
- **Target audience**: SaaS founders, growth leaders, VCs, marketing VPs, tech entrepreneurs (Vietnam & global)
- **Tone baseline**: Data-driven, opinionated, accessible. Not academic, not hype.

## Writing Style Rules

1. **HOOK**: Bold claim + specific number in first 1-2 lines. Stop the scroll.
   - Example: "Series A is $3-5M? Forget it. That era is over."
   - Example: "Move over SEO - AI now decides which brands get seen"

2. Every claim backed by specific data (dollar amounts, percentages, company names)

3. Short paragraphs - 1-2 sentences max per paragraph

4. Emoji sparingly - max 2-3 per post, only for visual section breaks

5. Numbered lists with → arrows for sub-details

6. Default language: English (unless Vietnamese is explicitly requested)

7. Soft CTA at end - engagement question or natural MCB AI mention (not hard sell)

## STRICT Formatting Rules (CRITICAL)

These rules are NON-NEGOTIABLE. Breaking any of these means the output is REJECTED:

- ABSOLUTELY NO asterisks (*) anywhere in the output
  - Not for bold, not for lists, not for emphasis, not for anything
- ABSOLUTELY NO markdown formatting of any kind
  - No **, no *, no #, no [], no ()
- ABSOLUTELY NO em dashes (—)
  - Use hyphens (-) or commas instead
- ABSOLUTELY NO source citations or URLs in the post text
- Output MUST be plain text only — LinkedIn does not render markdown
- For emphasis, use CAPS for 1-2 key words only
  - Example: "This is the REAL opportunity."
- For list items, use numbers (1. 2. 3.) or → arrows
  - NEVER use asterisk bullets

## Content Patterns That Perform Well

| Pattern | Structure | Example Title |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| Toplist | X new [things] for [audience] in [timeframe] | "7 AI tools that just raised $100M+" |
| POV | Bold contrarian take + fresh data | "The SaaS bubble didn't pop - it evolved" |
| Case Study | Problem → Action → Results → Lesson | "How Notion went from $0 to $10B" |
| How-to | Step-by-step with real tools | "How to build an AI content engine in 48 hours" |

## Voice Examples

Good:
```
Series B rounds in SaaS hit $45M average in Q1 2026.

That's 40% higher than 2024.

Three things driving this:

1. AI-native products → 3x faster time to revenue
2. Vertical SaaS → clearer ROI for buyers  
3. Efficient growth metrics → VCs love the unit economics

The playbook changed. Are you running the old one?

Theo doi them tai mcbai.vn
```

Bad (violates rules):
```
**Series B rounds** in SaaS hit $45M average in Q1 2026 — that's 40% higher than 2024.

*Three things driving this:*

* AI-native products: 3x faster time to revenue
* Vertical SaaS: clearer ROI for buyers
```

The "bad" example uses bold markdown (**), italic (*), em dashes (—), and asterisk bullets (*). All prohibited.
