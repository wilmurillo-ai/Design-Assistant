# POV Format — Detailed Instructions

## What is a POV Post?

A bold, opinionated take on a topic — backed by specific data. The goal is to argue ONE clear thesis, provoke thought, and drive engagement through contrarian or insightful perspectives.

## Structure

```
HOOK (1-2 lines)
Contrarian or bold opening that challenges conventional wisdom.
Example: "Everyone says AI will replace developers. The data says the opposite."

DATA (3-5 lines)
Evidence with specific numbers, company names, dollar amounts.
Stack 2-3 data points that support your thesis.
Example:
"GitHub Copilot users write 55% more code per day.
But here's what nobody talks about - they also SHIP 40% more features.
Companies using AI dev tools saw engineering team satisfaction jump 23 points."

ANALYSIS (3-5 lines)
What this actually means. Connect the dots others are missing.
Example:
"AI isn't replacing developers. It's turning good developers into great ones.
The bottleneck was never typing speed. It was context switching, boilerplate, and decision fatigue.
AI removes the friction. Humans make the judgment calls."

PREDICTION/STANCE (2-3 lines)
Take a clear position. Don't hedge. Be specific.
Example:
"Within 18 months, companies without AI dev tools will struggle to hire.
Top engineers won't go back to coding without assistance - just like nobody goes back to paper maps."

CTA (1-2 lines)
Provocative question to drive comments.
Example: "Agree or disagree? Are AI dev tools a competitive advantage or table stakes?"
```

## Constraints

- Must argue ONE clear thesis — not a balanced overview
- Use specific data to support every claim
- Short paragraphs, 1-2 sentences each
- NO bullet points for POV — this is narrative prose
- ZERO asterisks (*) in output
- No em dashes (—), no URLs, plain text only
- Don't hedge — take a position
- For short posts: Thesis + 2 data points + prediction
- For medium posts: Full structure above
- For long posts: Extended analysis, multiple data sources, deeper implications

## Example Output (Medium Length)

```
Everyone says AI will replace marketers. The data says something more interesting.

HubSpot's 2026 report shows AI-using marketing teams produce 3x more content.
But their conversion rates are FLAT. Same results, just more volume.

Meanwhile, the top 10% of marketing teams? They're using AI differently.
Not for more content. For better targeting. For faster testing. For real-time personalization.

The gap isn't human vs AI. It's strategy vs volume.

Companies flooding LinkedIn with AI-generated posts are getting less engagement, not more.
The winners are using AI to understand their audience, not to talk at them.

My prediction - within a year, "AI-generated" becomes a negative signal.
The real competitive edge is AI-informed, human-crafted.

What's your experience? Is more content actually driving better results?
```
