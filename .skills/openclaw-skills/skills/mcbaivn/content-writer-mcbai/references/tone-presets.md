# Tone Presets Reference

## Available Tones

### Default
**Style**: Data-driven, confident, accessible. Not academic, not hype.
**When to use**: Standard posts, most content. Professional but not stuffy.
**Voice**: "Here's what the data shows, and here's why it matters to you."

### Bold / Provocative
**Style**: Strong opinions, challenge conventional wisdom. Take positions that make people stop and react.
**When to use**: Hot takes, contrarian views, challenging industry norms.
**Voice**: "Everyone believes X. The data says they're wrong. Here's why."
**Tips**:
- Use rhetorical questions
- Challenge assumptions directly
- Be specific about what you disagree with
- Back every bold claim with data

### Educational
**Style**: Teacher mode. Break down complex ideas simply. Use analogies.
**When to use**: Explaining concepts, teaching frameworks, demystifying topics.
**Voice**: "Here's why this matters, and here's how to think about it."
**Tips**:
- Use "Here's why" and "Here's how" framing
- Include analogies to familiar concepts
- Define jargon when you use it
- Build understanding step by step

### Storytelling
**Style**: Narrative-driven. Start with a scene or moment. Build tension. Reveal the insight.
**When to use**: Company stories, founder journeys, pivotal moments.
**Voice**: "Picture this..." or "In 2019, three developers had a crazy idea..."
**Tips**:
- Start with a specific moment, not an abstract claim
- Create tension or curiosity before the reveal
- Use sensory details when relevant
- The insight should feel earned, not forced

### Analytical
**Style**: Deep data analysis, charts-in-words. Pattern recognition and comparison.
**When to use**: Market analysis, trend reports, competitive breakdowns.
**Voice**: "I analyzed 500 data points. Here's what I found."
**Tips**:
- Lead with methodology or data scope
- Compare numbers across time periods or companies
- Spot patterns others miss
- Quantify everything possible

### Viral / FOMO
**Style**: Kích cảm xúc mạnh, tạo urgency, FOMO-driven. Người đọc thấy "phải share ngay."
**When to use**: Facebook content, awareness posts, trend posts, khi muốn reach rộng.
**Voice**: "Mọi người đang làm điều này. Bạn thì sao?"
**Tips**:
- Dùng "Hầu hết mọi người không biết...", "Bí mật mà ít ai nói..."
- Tạo contrast: "Trong khi bạn đang X, người khác đang Y"
- Numbers cụ thể tạo credibility
- Kết thúc bằng CTA share/tag mạnh

### Empathetic
**Style**: Ấm áp, hiểu người đọc, tạo cảm giác "họ hiểu mình." Share cao vì tính relatable.
**When to use**: Facebook community posts, khi chủ đề liên quan đến struggle hoặc growth cá nhân.
**Voice**: "Bạn không đơn độc. Rất nhiều người cảm thấy như vậy."
**Tips**:
- Bắt đầu bằng sự công nhận: "Nếu bạn đang cảm thấy X..."
- Tránh phán xét, không có "đúng" hay "sai"
- Dùng "chúng ta" thay vì "bạn" để tạo community
- CTA: câu hỏi mở, mời chia sẻ cảm xúc

### Custom
**Style**: User-defined tone.
**When to use**: When the user provides their own tone description.
**How it works**: The user writes free-text tone instructions, which replace the preset.

## Tone + Format Combinations

Some tones pair especially well with certain formats:

| Format | Best Tones |
|--------|-----------|
| Toplist | Default, Analytical |
| POV | Bold, Storytelling |
| Case Study | Storytelling, Educational |
| How-to | Educational, Default |

These are suggestions, not rules. Any combination works.

## Language Interaction

### English (default)
- Write in standard American English
- Target US/global market
- All tones apply as described above

### Vietnamese
- Write the ENTIRE post in Vietnamese
- Use natural, conversational Vietnamese (not translation-style)
- Keep technical terms in English when commonly used: AI, SaaS, startup, funding, etc.
- Adapt cultural context for Vietnamese audience: founders, marketers, tech leaders in Vietnam
- All formatting rules still apply (no markdown, no em dashes, etc.)
- Tone presets work the same way, just expressed in Vietnamese
