# Language School Video — Enrollment Videos That Put Students Inside the Experience Before They Arrive

## Use Cases
1. Campus atmosphere videos capturing real classroom energy, conversation groups, and multicultural student interactions that text descriptions cannot convey.
2. Instructor introduction videos showing teaching style, nationality mix, and classroom personality in 60 seconds.
3. International student journey testimonials featuring students at different fluency levels describing their progress arc in their first or second language.
4. Program overview videos explaining course levels, certification tracks (IELTS prep, TOEFL, business English), and the specific outcomes graduates achieve.
5. Parent-targeted enrollment videos for immersion programs, showing facilities, supervision, safety, and the social environment their child will enter.
6. Social recruitment content for Instagram and TikTok targeted to prospective student demographics in key source countries.

## How It Works
Describe your school's programs, student demographics, and the enrollment decision your video needs to support. Provide any campus photos, student clips, or testimonial recordings you have. The tool sequences the narrative from aspiration to evidence to action — showing the experience, proving the outcome, and delivering a clear next step (book a call, request info, apply now).

## Output
- Campus culture and classroom atmosphere videos (60–120 seconds)
- Individual program overview videos per language level or course track
- Student testimonial videos (native-language and English-subtitled versions)
- Parent-targeted informational videos for immersion programs
- Short-form social content optimized for Instagram, TikTok, YouTube Shorts

## Example Prompt
"Create an enrollment video for our English immersion program in Dublin targeting adult students from Brazil, Mexico, and Colombia aged 22–35. Show the mix of nationalities in our classes, our conversation-focused teaching method, and feature two testimonials from Latin American graduates who found jobs after completing our advanced program."
