# Fence Company Video — AI Video Production for Fence Installation & Contractor Marketing

Create a continuous stream of marketing videos for fence companies. Generate before-and-after installation transformations, material comparison education content, storm damage repair urgency ads, commercial perimeter security portfolios, and neighborhood-targeted social media assets — built for the fence contractor who wins jobs by showing the finished product, not describing it.

## 1. Industry Context

Fencing is one of the most visually binary home improvement categories: the property either has a fence or it does not, and the difference between "no fence" and "new fence" is dramatic, immediate, and visible from the street. This makes fence installation inherently perfect for video marketing — a 15-second before-and-after clip communicates the transformation more powerfully than any written estimate or material brochure ever could.

The US residential fencing market generates over $10 billion annually, driven by new construction, replacement of aging fences (average fence lifespan is 15-20 years for wood, 25-30 for vinyl), privacy demand in increasingly dense suburban developments, and pool safety code compliance. The market is dominated by small operators — over 50,000 fencing companies in the US, most with fewer than 15 employees. Marketing sophistication is extremely low: the majority of fence companies rely on yard signs left at completed job sites, vehicle wraps, and word of mouth. Companies that produce video content have an outsized competitive advantage because so few competitors do.

The sales psychology of fence purchasing is unique among home services. Unlike a roof or an HVAC system where the homeowner cannot see the product after installation, a fence is the single most visible improvement to a property's exterior. Homeowners make fence purchasing decisions based heavily on how a specific fence style looks on properties similar to theirs. This is why the fence company that shows "here is a 6-foot cedar privacy fence we installed last week on a property just like yours in your neighborhood" closes at dramatically higher rates than the company that simply quotes a price per linear foot.

The production problem mirrors other trades: fence crews are measured on linear feet installed per day, not content created. A crew that installs 150-200 linear feet of fence daily across 2-3 job sites has zero interest in stopping to photograph each completion. And the most impactful content — the full-property reveal from the street view — requires walking back to the front of the property after the crew has already loaded tools and is driving to the next site. AI video production eliminates this friction entirely: describe the fence type, property style, and neighborhood, and get the marketing asset without any crew involvement.

Seasonality drives fence marketing in predictable waves. Spring is the primary selling season (March through June accounts for 40-50% of annual residential installs in most markets). Storm damage creates urgent demand spikes — a single windstorm can generate 50+ repair calls in one day. Fall brings a secondary push as homeowners prepare for winter or complete projects before HOA deadlines. Pool fence installations spike in late spring as families open pools and local code inspectors conduct seasonal sweeps. Smart fence companies pre-build video content for each seasonal wave rather than scrambling to create assets when demand spikes.

## 2. Video Categories and Specifications

### 2.1 Before-and-After Installation Showcases
- **Purpose**: Portfolio building, social media organic and paid, website gallery, sales consultations, Nextdoor community posts
- **Duration**: 15-45 seconds
- **Structure**: Open with the "before" state (old deteriorating fence, no fence at all, temporary construction barrier, chain link being replaced), reveal the completed installation from the most impactful angle (usually street view for front yards, patio view for backyards), close with company branding and CTA
- **Key rule**: The street-view reveal is the money shot for residential fences. Homeowners buy fences based on how they look from their own backyard and from the street. Always include both perspectives.
- **Variations**: Quick social cut (15s), detailed portfolio version (30-45s), neighborhood-specific version referencing the community name
- **Best content types**: Old rotting wood fence replaced with new cedar or vinyl, chain link upgraded to privacy fence, bare yard transformed with fence plus landscaping, commercial property secured with ornamental iron, pool area enclosed with code-compliant fencing

### 2.2 Material Comparison and Education Videos
- **Purpose**: Website material pages, YouTube education channel, social media organic, sales consultation support
- **Duration**: 60-120 seconds
- **Materials to cover**:
  - **Cedar/Wood**: Natural look, stainable, 15-20 year lifespan, requires maintenance (staining every 2-3 years), most popular for privacy fences, warps and weathers without maintenance
  - **Vinyl/PVC**: Zero maintenance, 25-30 year lifespan, limited color options (white, tan, gray most common), higher upfront cost but lower lifetime cost, does not rot or attract termites
  - **Aluminum/Ornamental**: Decorative appearance, pool code compliant, does not provide privacy, rust-resistant, best for front yards and pool enclosures
  - **Chain Link**: Lowest cost, functional security, commercial and agricultural primary use, can add privacy slats or windscreen, least aesthetically appealing
  - **Wrought Iron**: Premium decorative option, extremely durable, highest cost, custom fabrication available, best for high-end front yard and estate fencing
  - **Composite**: Wood appearance with vinyl durability, eco-friendly (recycled materials), limited availability, mid-to-high price point
- **Structure**: Show each material in a real installation context, highlight key differences (maintenance, lifespan, cost range, appearance), recommend based on use case (privacy vs decorative vs security vs pool), close with consultation CTA
- **Key rule**: Homeowners overwhelmingly search "wood fence vs vinyl fence" — this is the most common comparison. Always produce this specific comparison first.

### 2.3 Storm Damage and Emergency Repair Campaigns
- **Purpose**: Paid social media (rapid deployment after storms), Google Ads, direct mail QR code destination, Nextdoor urgent posts
- **Duration**: 15-30 seconds
- **Structure**: Acknowledge the storm event ("Recent storms have damaged fences across [City/Region]"), show common damage types (leaning posts, broken pickets, fallen panels, tree-on-fence), emphasize speed of response ("Same-week repair available"), close with phone number prominently displayed
- **Key rule**: Storm damage campaigns must be deployed within 24-48 hours of a significant weather event. Pre-build template videos for common storm types (wind, hail, ice, tornado, fallen trees) so only the location and phone number need updating.
- **Urgency elements**: "Limited crew availability — book now," "Insurance claim assistance available," "Free storm damage assessment"
- **Compliance**: Do not guarantee insurance coverage or claim outcomes. Use "we can help with insurance documentation" instead.

### 2.4 Privacy Fence Promotion Videos
- **Purpose**: Geo-targeted social media ads for residential neighborhoods, Google Local Services Ads, website landing pages
- **Duration**: 20-45 seconds
- **Structure**: Open with the privacy problem (overlooked backyard, no barrier between properties, visible from street), transition to the privacy fence solution showing the transformed space, emphasize lifestyle benefits (kids playing safely, backyard entertaining without neighbors watching, hot tub privacy, pet containment), close with linear-foot pricing range and free estimate CTA
- **Key insight**: Privacy is the #1 reason homeowners buy fences. Over 60% of residential fence purchases are primarily motivated by privacy. These videos should sell the feeling of a private outdoor space, not the fence itself.
- **Targeting**: New construction neighborhoods (homes 1-3 years old often still lack fences), neighborhoods with small lot sizes, areas near busy roads or commercial zones

### 2.5 Commercial and Security Fencing Portfolio Videos
- **Purpose**: Commercial bid proposals, property management presentations, government RFP support, industrial site security marketing
- **Duration**: 60-120 seconds
- **Structure**: Open with understanding of commercial fencing needs (security, liability, aesthetics, compliance), showcase relevant commercial portfolio by category (parking lots, construction sites, schools, industrial facilities, apartment communities, storage facilities), highlight specific capabilities (access control integration, barbed wire and razor wire, anti-climb features, crash-rated barriers), address reliability and scale (crew size, equipment, bonding and insurance), close with commercial consultation scheduling
- **Segments**: K-12 schools and universities, apartment complexes and multi-family, retail and office parks, construction site temporary fencing, industrial and manufacturing, government and military, self-storage facilities, sports facilities and parks
- **Key differentiator**: Commercial clients care about compliance (building codes, ADA requirements, security standards) and project timeline certainty more than aesthetics.

### 2.6 HOA-Compliant Fence Design Visualization
- **Purpose**: Sales consultations with homeowners in HOA communities, website HOA resource page, social media targeting HOA neighborhoods
- **Duration**: 30-60 seconds
- **Structure**: Acknowledge the HOA challenge ("Your HOA has specific fence requirements — we know them"), show fence options that comply with common HOA standards (height restrictions, material requirements, color limitations, setback rules), demonstrate the approval process experience ("We handle HOA architectural review submission"), show completed fences in HOA communities
- **Key insight**: HOA compliance is the #1 source of anxiety for homeowners buying fences in managed communities. A fence company that demonstrates HOA expertise closes at higher rates. Over 70 million Americans live in HOA-governed communities.

### 2.7 Pool Fence Safety and Compliance Videos
- **Purpose**: Pool fence service page, seasonal spring campaign, code compliance education, real estate transaction support
- **Duration**: 30-60 seconds
- **Structure**: Reference local pool barrier code requirements (most jurisdictions require 4-foot minimum height, self-closing/self-latching gates, no climbable features), show compliant pool fence options (aluminum, mesh removable, vinyl, ornamental), emphasize child safety messaging, close with free pool fence assessment CTA
- **Compliance**: Reference ICC/ANSI pool barrier standards generally — do not cite specific local codes unless verified for the target market. Always include "check your local building code requirements" disclaimer.

### 2.8 Automatic Gate and Access Control Videos
- **Purpose**: Gate service page, commercial access control marketing, residential estate gate showcase, upsell to existing fence clients
- **Duration**: 45-90 seconds
- **Structure**: Show gate styles (sliding, swing, cantilever, barrier arm), demonstrate access control options (keypad, remote, phone app, intercom, card reader), highlight security and convenience benefits, show residential and commercial applications, close with gate consultation CTA
- **Key insight**: Automatic gates are the highest-margin product for fence companies — a single residential gate installation runs $3,000-$15,000 vs $20-$40 per linear foot for standard fencing. Gate content should be prioritized for companies offering this service.

### 2.9 Social Media Organic Content
- **Purpose**: Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube Shorts — organic reach and brand awareness
- **Duration**: 15-60 seconds
- **Content types**:
  - Satisfying installations: Post driver pounding, level fence lines, perfect picket spacing
  - Dramatic transformations: Rotting fence to brand new, bare yard to fully enclosed
  - Material education: "This is what happens to an unstained cedar fence after 5 years"
  - Storm damage: Fallen fences, emergency repairs — timely content drives massive engagement
  - Crew and culture: Job site moments, tool showcases, new truck reveals
  - Fence fails: Common DIY mistakes, improperly installed fences, code violations found during replacements
  - Pet and kid content: Dogs enjoying new fenced yards, kids playing safely — emotional engagement drivers
- **Tone**: Blue-collar authentic. Fence content should feel like it comes from someone who builds fences, not someone who markets them.
- **Format**: 9:16 vertical, captions burned in, strong visual hook in first 2 seconds

### 2.10 Recruitment Videos
- **Purpose**: Indeed, Facebook job groups, trade school partnerships, company social media
- **Duration**: 60-120 seconds
- **Structure**: Show the outdoor work environment, highlight physical but skilled nature of fence installation (not just digging holes — layout, leveling, custom cuts, gate hanging), feature career progression (laborer to installer to crew lead to estimator/sales), showcase equipment and truck/trailer setups, emphasize year-round work (fence installation continues through most weather), close with application CTA
- **Context**: Fence installation faces the same labor shortage as all trades — 70% of contractors report difficulty finding skilled labor. Companies that market the trade as a career attract better candidates.

## 3. Production Workflow

### Step 1: Company Intake
Collect from the fence company:
- Company name, year established, owner name, number of crews and trucks
- Service area with specific cities, neighborhoods, and HOA communities served
- Complete service list: fence types installed (wood, vinyl, aluminum, chain link, iron, composite), gate types, repair and staining services, commercial capabilities
- Material brands carried (certifications as authorized installer for specific manufacturers)
- Project portfolio: descriptions of best completed projects with scope (linear feet, fence type, property type)
- Key differentiators: manufacturer warranties, crew experience, HOA expertise, same-day estimate promise, lifetime warranty on labor
- Brand assets: logo (vector), brand colors (hex codes), truck photos, job site photos, completed fence photos
- Google Business Profile URL, review count and rating
- Current marketing spend and channels
- Active promotions, financing options, seasonal offers
- Price ranges per linear foot by material type (for ad content)
- Average project size in linear feet and dollar value

### Step 2: Content Calendar Creation
Build a 30-day video calendar:
- Week 1: 2 before-and-after transformations + 1 material comparison video + 1 social organic clip
- Week 2: 1 privacy fence promotion + 1 seasonal/storm campaign + 2 social organic clips
- Week 3: 2 before-and-after transformations + 1 HOA-focused video + 1 commercial portfolio piece
- Week 4: 1 gate/access control video + 2 social organic clips + 1 recruitment or education video + 1 seasonal ad refresh
- Total: 14-18 videos per month, with transformation content weighted highest

### Step 3: Script and Visual Development
- Write scripts matching the company's voice — fence companies tend toward direct, no-nonsense communication
- Include specific local references: neighborhood names, HOA communities, local building codes for fence height and setback
- Material-specific content must accurately represent the material's real appearance, texture, and aging characteristics
- Seasonal content must match local conditions — storm prep content for hurricane markets, snow load considerations for northern markets
- Pricing references must align with the company's actual pricing structure

### Step 4: Production and Review
- Generate initial video draft
- Quality checklist: brand consistency, phone number accuracy, material accuracy (do not show vinyl when describing cedar), fence height proportions realistic, gate operation depicted correctly
- Client review with one round of revisions
- Final export: 16:9 (YouTube, Google), 9:16 (TikTok, Reels, Stories), 1:1 (Facebook feed), 4:5 (Instagram feed)

### Step 5: Distribution and Optimization
- File naming: `[company]-[fence-type]-[platform]-[date].[ext]`
- Include recommended post copy for each platform
- Targeting: radius from service area, homeowner status, home value range, home age (older homes = replacement market, newer homes = first-time installation)
- Storm damage content: pre-build templates, deploy within 24-48 hours of weather events
- Refresh creative every 14 days for paid placements

## 5. Compliance and Legal

- Contractor license number must be displayed in advertising where required by state or local law
- Building permit references: "Permits pulled as required" — do not suggest fences can be installed without permits in jurisdictions that require them
- Property line disclaimers: "Fence placement based on property survey — we recommend a current survey before installation"
- HOA compliance: "We assist with HOA architectural review but cannot guarantee approval"
- Utility locate: "We call 811 before every installation" — this is legally required and builds trust
- Height restrictions: Do not advertise fence heights that exceed local code maximums without referencing variance process
- Pool fence compliance: Reference general safety standards, include "verify local code requirements" disclaimer
- Warranty language: Clearly distinguish manufacturer material warranty from company labor warranty

## 6. Metrics and Optimization

Track these KPIs for fence company video marketing ROI:
- **Cost per estimate request**: Primary conversion metric — fence company average is $25-$75 per qualified lead from paid social
- **Before-and-after engagement rate**: Should be your highest-performing content type — if not, the transformations are not dramatic enough
- **Material comparison video completion rate**: 40%+ completion rate indicates strong education value
- **Storm damage ad response time**: Track hours from storm event to ad deployment — faster deployment = more captured demand
- **Neighborhood-specific ad performance**: Track which neighborhoods generate the highest estimate request rates
- **Close rate by content source**: Leads who saw video content before requesting estimates should close at 15-25% higher rates
- **Seasonal lead volume**: Compare spring peak vs off-season — video should help smooth the seasonal demand curve
- **Average project value from video leads**: Video-sourced leads tend to choose higher-value materials (they have already seen what premium looks like)

Refresh cycle: Rotate paid creative every 14 days. Maintain an evergreen portfolio library for sales consultations. Update storm damage templates before each storm season.
