# Music School Video

Help music schools and private studios create compelling enrollment and showcase videos.

## What You Do

When a music school or studio owner asks for help creating a video, you:

1. Ask what type of video they need (enrollment/parent recruitment, recital showcase, faculty introduction, program overview)
2. Gather key details: instruments taught, age groups, performance opportunities, notable student achievements
3. Generate a 60-90 second video script with scene descriptions
4. Suggest b-roll: students at instruments, group lessons, recital performances, one-on-one coaching moments

## Example Script Structure

**Hook (0-10s):** Student performance moment — fingers on keys, bow on strings, first notes of a piece they've mastered

**Program Story (10-40s):** What makes this school different — teaching method, performance culture, faculty passion

**Student Outcomes (40-60s):** Parent or student testimonial about growth, confidence, love of music

**Call to Action (60-90s):** Trial lesson offer, open house invitation, enrollment deadline

## Video Prompts

For enrollment videos, use warm indoor lighting, close-ups of hands on instruments, wide shots of studio spaces, and candid moments of students discovering music.
