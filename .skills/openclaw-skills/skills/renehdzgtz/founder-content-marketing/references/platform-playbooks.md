# Platform Playbooks
*Reference for Module 3 — Copywriter*

---

## LinkedIn

**Audience mindset:** Professional, but hungry for authenticity. People come to learn, get inspired, and feel seen.

**What performs:**
- Personal stories with a business lesson
- Contrarian takes on industry norms
- Frameworks and step-by-step breakdowns
- Vulnerable founder moments (failures, pivots, hard decisions)
- Data + opinion combo posts

**Structure that works:**
```
[HOOK — 1–2 lines, stops the scroll]

[BLANK LINE — forces "see more" click]

[Body — 3–8 short paragraphs, each 1–3 lines]
[Use white space aggressively]
[Build tension or curiosity as you go]

[Landing — your takeaway, POV, or lesson]

[Optional CTA — question to drive comments, or soft pitch]
```

**Length:** 150–300 words sweet spot. Long-form (700–1,200 words) works for deep stories or frameworks.

**Hashtags:** 3–5 max, at the end, relevant (not spammy).

**Posting time:** Tue–Thu, 7–9am or 12–1pm (local time).

**Formats ranked by reach:**
1. Native video (especially short, with captions)
2. Document/carousel (PDF posts)
3. Text posts with image
4. Pure text posts
5. Link posts (lowest organic reach — move the link to comments)

**Voice:** Smart but human. No buzzwords. No "thrilled to announce." First person, direct, confident.

## Twitter / X

**Audience mindset:** Fast. Opinionated. Will call you out. Rewards brevity and boldness.

**What performs:**
- Hot takes and contrarian opinions
- Threads that teach something specific
- Real-time reactions to news/trends
- Behind-the-scenes numbers and data
- Humor (when it lands)

**Single tweet structure:**
```
[Bold claim or observation — under 280 chars]
[Optionally: 1 follow-up line that adds proof or nuance]
```

**Thread structure:**
```
Tweet 1: [Hook — make a bold promise or claim. This is the only tweet people see first.]
Tweet 2–9: [Deliver on the promise. Each tweet = 1 clear point. Can stand alone.]
Tweet 10: [Summary + CTA — "If you found this useful, RT tweet 1."]
```

**Thread rules:**
- Number your tweets (1/, 2/, etc.) or use → 
- First tweet must work as a standalone post
- No more than 10–12 tweets in a thread (unless it's really, really good)
- Add a reply with the link to your content/newsletter at the end

**Length:** Single tweets under 240 chars (leave room for retweets). Threads: 8–12 tweets.

**Posting time:** 8–10am, 12–1pm, 5–7pm. More forgiving than LinkedIn.

**Engage:** Reply to bigger accounts in your niche. This is how you grow on X.

## TikTok

**Audience mindset:** Zero patience. Will scroll in 0.5 seconds if not hooked. Rewards authenticity over polish.

**What performs:**
- "Things I wish I knew about X" lists
- Rapid-fire tips (1 tip per second energy)
- Founder stories with conflict/tension
- Reaction videos to industry news
- "POV: you're a founder who..." scenarios
- Duets/responses to trending topics

**Script structure (60–90 seconds):**
```
[0–3 sec: HOOK — verbal + visual. Say/show the most interesting thing first.]
["Stop scrolling if you..." / "This is why X fails..." / "Here's what nobody tells you..."]

[3–15 sec: Setup — who is this for, why it matters]

[15–60 sec: Content — 3–5 points max, fast pacing, cut the fat]

[60–80 sec: Landing — your POV or conclusion]

[Last 5 sec: CTA — "Follow for more on X" / "Comment your thoughts" / "Link in bio"]
```

**Production rules:**
- Captions ON always (80% of views are muted)
- First frame matters — treat it like a thumbnail
- Talk fast (you can slow down delivery but cut dead air in editing)
- Look at camera, not at notes
- Trending audio can help — use it when relevant, don't force it

**Length:** 30–90 seconds is the sweet spot in 2024–2025. Under 60s for broad reach.

## Newsletter / Email

**Audience mindset:** They opted in. They actually want to hear from you. This is your most loyal audience — treat it that way.

**What performs:**
- Personal essays with a clear lesson
- Curated resources with your commentary (saves them time)
- Behind-the-scenes updates on your business
- Contrarian takes explained in depth
- Exclusive content they can't get elsewhere

**Structure:**
```
[Subject line — the hook, under 50 chars ideally]
[Preview text — second hook, extends the subject line]

[Opening — 1–3 lines, personal, immediate. No "hope you're well."]

[Body — your main content. One topic. Go deep.]
  - Use H2s if it's long
  - Short paragraphs
  - Bold key sentences
  - Use bullet lists sparingly (they break reading flow)

[Closing — your takeaway or reflection]

[1 CTA — one action only. Reply, click, buy, refer. Never multiple asks.]

[Sign-off — personal, consistent]
```

**Subject line formulas:**
- Question: "Are you doing this wrong?"
- Number: "3 things I'd tell my younger self"
- Intrigue: "I almost quit last week"
- Direct: "How I got 10 clients in 30 days"
- Personal: "Something I've never shared publicly"

**Cadence:** Weekly is the gold standard. Biweekly is acceptable. Never go more than 2 weeks without sending.

## Instagram

**Audience mindset:** Visual first. Come for aesthetics, stay for personality. Great for brand building and community.

**What performs:**
- Reels (highest reach — treat like TikTok)
- Carousels (highest saves and shares — great for frameworks)
- Stories (daily touchpoints, behind-the-scenes, polls)
- Single image posts with strong copy in caption

**Caption structure:**
```
[Hook — first line before "more" cut. Make it worth clicking.]

[Body — tell the story or make the point. Medium length: 100–300 words]

[Blank line]
[CTA — save this, share this, comment below]

[Hashtags — separate block at bottom, or first comment. 5–15 relevant tags.]
```

**Carousel slide structure:**
- Slide 1: Title/Hook (the reason to swipe)
- Slides 2–9: One point per slide, minimal text, visual consistency
- Last slide: Summary + follow/save CTA

**Reels:** Follow TikTok playbook above. Repurpose TikToks to Reels (remove TikTok watermark).

## Platform Priority for Founders (general guidance)

| If you are... | Start with |
|---|---|
| B2B / professional services | LinkedIn first, then newsletter |
| Consumer / lifestyle / creator | TikTok or Instagram, then newsletter |
| Tech / crypto / startup | Twitter/X first, then LinkedIn |
| Building a knowledge business | Newsletter first, then LinkedIn or Twitter/X |

**Universal rule:** Own your newsletter. It's the only platform you actually own. Build it from day 1.
