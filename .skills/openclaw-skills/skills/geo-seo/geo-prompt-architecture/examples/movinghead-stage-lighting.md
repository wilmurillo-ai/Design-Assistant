# Example: movinghead.net

## Business Type

`movinghead.net` is best treated as a `B2B stage-lighting manufacturer / supplier`.

This means it should not be modeled like:

- a DTC apparel brand
- a software platform
- a generic event-content site

## Why The Prompt Strategy Changes

The buyer often cares about:

- lighting category fit
- technical specifications
- venue and event use cases
- manufacturer trust
- certifications
- lead time
- OEM and procurement fit
- support after purchase

So the GEO prompt set should focus on:

- non-brand discovery around moving-head lighting categories and use cases
- competitor comparison around supplier, manufacturer, and product-line overlap
- brand-defense prompts around quality, specs, support, certifications, and fulfillment

## Suggested Prompt Bias

- discovery prompts for beam, wash, spot, and application-based lighting needs
- comparison prompts between manufacturers or stage-lighting brands
- brand-defense prompts around factory strength, reliability, shipping, and customization

## Example Prompt Slice

Below is a representative example set, not a complete prompt library.

### Non-brand discovery

These prompts test whether the brand can enter category and use-case answer spaces before the buyer knows the supplier.

| Prompt | Funnel | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| What is the best moving head light for indoor concert stages? | MOFU | Core category-entry prompt for venue-specific demand. |
| How do I choose between beam, wash, and spot moving head lights for live events? | TOFU | High-value educational prompt tied to category understanding. |
| What stage lighting manufacturer is best for clubs, theaters, and touring productions? | BOFU | Tests manufacturer visibility beyond product specs alone. |
| What features matter most when buying moving head lights for event production companies? | TOFU | Useful for procurement-oriented answer spaces. |
| Which moving head lights are best for rental companies that need reliable fixtures? | BOFU | Reveals visibility in high-commercial rental and production contexts. |
| How do I compare moving head light suppliers for OEM and bulk purchase needs? | BOFU | Strong B2B sourcing prompt. |

## Example Asset Implications

This kind of prompt set should often point toward:

- category pages
- specification pages
- use-case pages
- certification and factory pages
- support and shipping pages
- OEM and distributor information pages

## Why This Example Matters

It shows why an industrial or product-supplier client needs a more technical and procurement-aware prompt architecture than consumer brands or SaaS products.
