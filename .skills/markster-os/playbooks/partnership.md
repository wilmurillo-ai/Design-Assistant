# Partnership Playbook

Build referral relationships and alliances with complementary businesses. Partnerships are the fastest path to borrowed credibility and free distribution when you do not have an established network.

---

## Prerequisites

- [ ] ICP documented (so you can explain who your ideal client is to potential partners)
- [ ] Service or offering defined (so partners know exactly what to refer)
- [ ] At least one proof point ready (partners will ask what you have done before referring clients)

---

## What this produces

- A categorized list of target partner types and specific organizations to pursue
- A tailored pitch for each partner type
- A follow-up sequence for partnership conversations
- Partner records logged in CRM with relationship status

---

## Partner Types

| Type | Value Exchange |
|------|---------------|
| Peer groups and associations | Speaking slot generates introductions; members get free value |
| Complementary service providers | They do not do your service; you do not do theirs. Mutual referrals. |
| Technology vendors | Their customers need what you deliver; you send customers their way |
| Fractional advisors (CMO, CFO, etc.) | They advise; you execute. They become the trusted referral source. |
| Business coaches | Their clients need growth execution; you are the recommended vendor |
| Accounting and legal firms | They see which clients need growth help |

**How to prioritize:**
- Partners with 50+ clients who match your ICP are highest priority. Invest more time in customization.
- Partners who are direct competitors: do not pitch a partnership.
- Partners whose clients clearly do not match your ICP: deprioritize or skip.

---

## Steps

### Step 1: Build the Partner Target List

For each partner type, identify specific organizations and individuals:
- Peer groups and associations in your ICP's industry (search "[industry] peer group," "[industry] association," "[industry] mastermind")
- Complementary agencies and consultants (who do your clients hire for work adjacent to yours?)
- Business coaches and fractional advisors with visible client bases in your ICP segment
- Technology vendors whose customers have the same buyer profile as your ICP

Target 20-30 specific names and organizations across all types. Tier them:
- Tier 1: large networks, strong ICP overlap, credible in your target market
- Tier 2: smaller networks but good fit
- Tier 3: exploratory

---

### Step 2: Research Each Partner

Before outreach:
- What is their client base? Do their clients match your ICP?
- What do they lack that you provide? (This is your value exchange.)
- What are they currently recommending to clients who need what you do?
- Have they worked with your category before? (Reveals if they have an existing relationship to displace)

---

### Step 3: Design the Value Exchange

Make the value exchange asymmetric in their favor initially. You need the network more than they need you.

**What you can offer:**
- Referral commissions or revenue sharing
- Co-branded content that builds their authority with their audience
- Speaking at their events or contributing to their newsletters
- Priority service for their referred clients
- Warm introductions to clients or partners who need their service

**What they can offer:**
- Warm introductions to clients who match your ICP
- Speaking slots at events or peer group meetings
- Co-marketing and co-branding
- Credibility through association

For peer groups specifically: a no-pitch workshop offering is the most effective opening. Deliver genuine value to their members first. The introductions follow.

---

### Step 4: Generate the Pitch

**For peer groups and associations:**

Subject: Workshop for your members -- no sales pitch, just useful content

[Name],

I work with [ICP type] businesses on [specific problem you solve]. I know your members deal with this regularly.

I would love to run a 30-minute practical session for your group on [topic relevant to their members]:
- [Option 1: specific, useful, not a sales pitch]
- [Option 2: specific, useful, not a sales pitch]

No sales pitch to the group -- just practical content. If any members want to talk after, great.

Happy to send an outline or jump on a 10-minute call.

[Name]

---

**For complementary service providers:**

Subject: Referral partnership -- we keep running into your clients

[Name],

I noticed [their firm] does [their service]. We do [your service] for [ICP type] businesses.

We keep running into clients who need [their service] and we do not do it. I assume you get clients asking about [your area] too.

Simple idea: mutual referrals. No formal agreement needed -- just introducing clients to each other when it makes sense.

Worth a 15-minute call?

[Name]

---

**For fractional advisors:**

Subject: Your strategy, our execution?

[Name],

You advise on [their specialty]. We execute the [relevant part of it] -- [brief description of what you do].

When your clients need execution, you could refer to us. When our clients need strategic guidance, we refer to you. You stay the advisor; we handle delivery.

Our work is transparent by default -- clients see and approve everything. Easy for you to stay involved.

Happy to show you how we work so you are confident referring.

[Name]

---

### Step 5: Follow-Up and Relationship Building

Partnership conversations are slower than sales conversations. Expect a longer timeline.

**After the initial pitch:**
- Follow up once after 5 days if no reply
- If they respond with interest but no commitment, suggest a specific next step: "Worth a 20-minute call to see if there is a natural fit here?"
- If they agree to a call: treat it like a discovery call. Understand their situation first.

**After the first call:**
- Send a follow-up within 24 hours summarizing the conversation and the next step
- If they agreed to try referring clients: set up a simple tracking process (even an email thread works)
- Log the partner in CRM tagged as "Partner" with the agreed terms

**Maintaining active partnerships:**
- Touch base quarterly even when there is nothing to discuss
- When you refer a client to them, let them know explicitly
- When they refer a client to you, update them on the outcome (with permission)
- Co-create something once you have established trust: a joint piece of content, a co-hosted event, a shared case study

## Benchmarks

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Partner targets identified | 20-30 in first pass |
| Outreach response rate | 10-20% for peer groups; 20-30% for complementary providers |
| Introductions per active partner per quarter | 1-3 per active partner |
| Deals opened from partner referrals | Track per partner to know which are worth investing in |
| Time to first referral from new partner | 30-90 days after first agreement |

One good partnership with a peer group leader or fractional advisor can generate 3-5 client conversations per quarter. Invest accordingly.

---

## Common Mistakes

**Pitching too early.** Partners need to trust your work before referring clients. Offer value first (a workshop, a piece of content, a referral to them) before asking for referrals.

**No formal tracking.** If referrals are not tracked, partners do not see the reciprocity and the relationship fades. Track every referral you receive and every one you send.

**Treating all partner types the same.** A peer group pitch and a complementary agency pitch are completely different conversations. Customize based on what they care about.

**Asking for referrals on the first call.** The first conversation should focus on understanding their business and establishing whether there is genuine fit. Ask for referrals after you have delivered some value.

**Not following up consistently.** Partnership relationships do not build themselves. Quarterly check-ins, passing along relevant content or introductions, and keeping the relationship warm requires intentional effort.

**Pursuing partners whose clients do not match your ICP.** A large network of the wrong buyer type is not a valuable partnership. Prioritize partners whose clients match your ICP over partners with the largest audience.
