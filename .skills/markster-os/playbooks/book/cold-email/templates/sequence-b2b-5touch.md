# B2B Cold Email Sequence -- 5-Touch / 16 Days

Standard sequence for B2B service firm outreach. Use for SMB buyers, sub-$10K deals, and high-volume batch campaigns where research depth per contact is limited.

See `sequence-b2b.md` for shared rules, tone guide, and CTA matrix.

---

## Variables

- `[COMPANY]` -- prospect company name
- `[ICP_TYPE]` -- their business category
- `[CITY]` -- city or metro area
- `[PAIN_OBSERVATION]` -- one specific observation about their situation
- `[DATA_POINT]` -- a specific number (cost, ratio, benchmark)
- `[PROOF_DETAIL]` -- a real result from a similar client (company type + outcome + timeframe)
- `[YOUR_NAME]` -- your name

---

## E1 -- Day 0: Soft Opener

**Subject:** new in [city]

**Body:**

[Opening sentence specific to them: "Found [COMPANY] on Google while researching [ICP_TYPE]s in the area" or a real, verifiable observation.]

I just relocated to [CITY] after years helping service businesses build pipeline. Now I'm trying to understand how [ICP_TYPE]s here actually grow -- most places I've talked to say new clients still come primarily through referrals.

Happy to share what I've seen work across similar firms in exchange for 20 minutes of your perspective.

[YOUR_NAME]

**Notes:**
- No company name, no offer, no pitch. Goal is a reply.
- The "new to the area" framing positions you as a learner, not a vendor.
- Honest personalization only -- if you say "found you on Google," that must be true.

---

## E2 -- Day 4: Insight

**Subject:** re: new in [city]

**Body:**

One pattern I keep seeing with [ICP_TYPE]s -- [PAIN_OBSERVATION]. [DATA_POINT] of that is usually invisible until it compounds.

I spent years figuring out how to break that dependency. Now I need people like you to tell me where I'm wrong.

Would you be willing to share 20 minutes of your perspective? I'll bring something useful in exchange.

[YOUR_NAME]

**Notes:**
- The insight should come from real observation. Not invented.
- "Would you be willing to help me?" flips the dynamic -- you're asking for input, not selling.
- If you have a scorecard or benchmark, offer it here.

---

## E3 -- Day 8: First Mention of Offer

**Subject:** re: new in [city]

**Body:**

Did some math on what it costs to build a real marketing function from scratch:

A marketing manager runs $120-150K. An SDR is $80-100K. A content writer adds $70K. Tools add $25K. That's $300K/year minimum.

[PROOF_DETAIL: "A [ICP_TYPE] I worked with went from [situation] to [result] without building a full team."]

I figured out how to get the same output for a third of that. Worth 20 minutes if you're curious.

[YOUR_NAME]

**Notes:**
- First concrete mention of an alternative. Keep it brief.
- [PROOF_DETAIL] must be a real result. Do not invent numbers.
- Customize salary benchmarks to your target metro.

---

## E4 -- Day 12: Mirror

**Subject:** re: new in [city]

**Body:**

Different angle -- curious what you've already tried on the marketing side.

Most [ICP_TYPE] owners I talk to have done at least one of three things: hired an agency that didn't understand the business, tried to build content in-house and ran out of time, or bought leads that converted at single digits.

If any of that sounds familiar, there might be a conversation here. If not, totally fine.

[YOUR_NAME]

**Notes:**
- This email reflects their likely experience back at them.
- The "three things" framing uses what you know about common failed attempts. Customize to your ICP.
- No meeting ask. Goal is to get them talking.

---

## E5 -- Day 16: Breakup

**Subject:** re: new in [city]

**Body:**

Last one from me -- I don't want to be pushy.

If the timing isn't right, I completely understand. I'll stop reaching out.

If pipeline ever becomes a more pressing problem, you can always reach me at [email].

[YOUR_NAME]

**Notes:**
- Under 50 words. Brevity is the point.
- Do not apologize for reaching out. "I don't want to be pushy" is sufficient.
- "You can always reach me" signals organization and professionalism.
- Breakup emails often generate replies by removing all pressure.

---

## Timing Summary

| Email | Day | Goal |
|-------|-----|------|
| E1 | 0 | Get a reply |
| E2 | 4 | Add an insight, invite their perspective |
| E3 | 8 | First mention of alternative approach + proof |
| E4 | 12 | Mirror their experience, invite a conversation |
| E5 | 16 | Clean exit with door open |

---

## What to Test

- Subject line: "new in [city]" vs "quick question" vs "[icp_type] question"
- E1 opener: direct observation vs indirect
- E3 format: cost breakdown as bullets vs prose
- E5 length: current version vs one sentence only
- Timing: 0/4/8/12/16 vs 0/3/7/11/15
