# Follow-Up Templates

Templates for responding to replies in a cold email campaign.

---

## Positive Reply - Meeting Confirmation

Use when: prospect replies with interest and wants to talk.

---

[FIRST_NAME], great - let's do it.

Here are a few times that work: [TIME OPTION 1], [TIME OPTION 2], [TIME OPTION 3].

Or grab a time directly: [CALENDAR_LINK]

Before the call, would it help if I sent a quick overview of what we'd cover?

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## Positive Reply - Qualification Question First

Use when: prospect replies asking for more detail before agreeing to a call.

---

Happy to give you a quick overview.

[Answer their question in 2-3 sentences - be specific, not generic.]

The call would help me understand whether [ICP_SITUATION applies to them]. If it does, I can walk you through exactly how we'd approach it for [COMPANY].

[Two time options or calendar link]

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## Soft No - "Not right now"

Use when: prospect replies saying timing is off but doesn't close the door.

---

Totally understand - timing matters.

When would make sense to revisit? Q[X], or closer to [season/event]?

I'll make a note and reach back out then.

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## Soft No - "We already have someone for this"

Use when: prospect replies saying they have an existing vendor or solution.

---

Makes sense - if it's working, no reason to change.

One question out of curiosity: is there an area where [ICP_SITUATION] is still a challenge even with your current setup?

If not, happy to close the loop. If yes, might be worth a quick call.

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## Objection - "Too expensive"

Use when: prospect replies with price concern before seeing full value.

---

Fair reaction before seeing what's included.

The way we structure it: [brief description of your risk reversal - phase gate, guarantee, or outcome-based pricing logic].

Most clients compare it to [what the alternative costs - hiring, status quo, or the cost of the problem continuing].

Worth walking through the specifics on a call? It might not be a fit, but it's worth 15 minutes to find out.

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## Objection - "I've tried this before and it didn't work"

Use when: prospect has been burned by a previous vendor or attempt.

---

That's the most common reason people hesitate - and it's a fair one.

What typically breaks down is [root cause of the common failure in this category - be specific and accurate, not defensive].

The way we avoid that: [your specific mechanism that addresses the failure mode].

If you're open to it, I can show you exactly how we handle that on a 15-minute call. If it sounds like the same thing, I'll tell you honestly.

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## Quarterly Re-Engage

Use when: prospect said "not right now" and it has been 60-90 days.

---

[FIRST_NAME],

Following up from [month] when you mentioned timing wasn't right.

Has anything changed on the [ICP_SITUATION] front?

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## Notes on follow-up writing

- Respond within 4 hours during business hours. Speed signals seriousness.
- Never argue with an objection. Acknowledge it first, then respond.
- Keep follow-up replies short. The prospect already received a long message. They want a direct response.
- Every reply should move toward a meeting or a clear "no." Do not let threads drift.
