# B2B Cold Email Sequence Templates

Two sequences. Use the 5-touch for most outreach. Use the 7-touch for enterprise buyers, high-ACV deals, or when you need more runway to build credibility.

| Template | File | Use when |
|----------|------|---------|
| 5-touch / 16 days | `sequence-b2b-5touch.md` | Standard B2B service firm outreach, SMB buyers, sub-$10K deals |
| 7-touch / 25 days | `sequence-b2b-7touch.md` | Enterprise, high-ACV deals ($10K+), complex sales cycles, technical buyers |

---

## Rules That Apply to Both

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| Subject lines: 1-3 words, all lowercase | Mimics internal email. Bypasses the "marketing blast" schema. |
| Each email is independent | Never reference previous attempts. Prospect encounters each in a different mental state. |
| 50-75 words ideal, 100 maximum | Longer emails get lower reply rates. Under 50 for mobile-first industries. |
| Plain text only | HTML formatting signals mass email to spam filters and recipients. |
| Soft CTA, not a meeting ask | Permission to decline increases reply rate over pressure. |
| One idea per email | Multiple points dilute focus. |
| PS line where applicable | +35% reply rate when used correctly. Personal observation that didn't fit in the body. |
| Colleague test | Read aloud. If it sounds like a template, it still is. |

## Tone: Check the Title Before Writing

**Founder Mode** (Founder, Owner, CEO of smaller firm, Partner)
- Cares about: survival, vision, existential threats
- Tone: direct, blunt, no corporate speak
- Triggers: "stop bleeding cash," "fix the bottleneck," "before you scale this mistake"

**Manager Mode** (VP, Director, Head of, Manager)
- Cares about: KPIs, career safety, budget adherence, looking good to their boss
- Tone: professional, safety-oriented, metrics-focused
- Triggers: "streamline the workflow," "reduce compliance risk," "gives you the data to make the case"

Writing the wrong tone to the wrong title is one of the most common reasons cold email fails to convert even when the offer is right.

## Subject Line Frameworks

| Type | Example | Psychological trigger |
|------|---------|----------------------|
| Neighbor | "chicago question" | In-group bias -- local signals you're not blasting everyone |
| Peer | "idea for acme" | Mimics a colleague, not a marketer |
| Curiosity | "question about your role" | Zeigarnik effect -- must resolve the open loop |
| Internal | "pipeline" / "revenue" / "cac" | Internal camouflage -- reads like a colleague's message |
| Anti-subject | "intro" / "update" | Teflon against spam filters -- all weight on first line |

Lowercase performs 23% better than title case. Never use exclamation marks in subject lines.

## CTA Selection Matrix

Match to your research depth and sequence position.

**Strong research:**
- "Worth exploring?"
- "Make sense to discuss?"
- "Open to seeing how [peer company] did it?"

**Limited research:**
- "Is this even a priority?"
- "Am I off base?"
- "Relevant or not?"

**Engagement signal (multiple opens, link clicked):**
- "Tuesday or Thursday?"
- "15 minutes this week?"
- "Should we talk?"

**Breakup:**
- "Should I stop reaching out?"
- "Priority or not?"
- "Yes or no?"

**Value-first:**
- "Want the framework?"
- "Should I send the case study?"

The CTA is the last line. Nothing after it.

---

## What Does Not Belong in Cold Email

- "Hope this finds you well" / "My name is X and I'm from Y"
- "Run something by you" (signals pitch incoming)
- "Just following up" (creates resistance)
- "I came across your profile" (signals list scraping)
- HTML formatting, logos, or tracked images
- Calendly link before engagement
- Specific pricing before discovery
- Fake personalization ("I've been following your company closely")
- Over-personalization (5+ specific references feels invasive -- 2-3 is the sweet spot)
- Multiple asks in one email

## Benchmarks

| Metric | Target | Action if below |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Open rate | 30%+ | Deliverability or subject line problem |
| Reply rate | 2-5% | Message problem. Rewrite E1 hook. |
| Meeting rate | 0.25-0.5% | CTA or offer problem |

Fix one variable at a time. Evaluate after every 100 sends.
