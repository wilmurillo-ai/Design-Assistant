# LinkedIn Outreach Playbook

LinkedIn DMs outperform cold email 2:1 on average reply rates (10.3% vs. 5.1% in 2025). That average masks a 20x range: generic templates get 2-5%, genuinely specific personalized messages get 15-40%, voice notes get 40-60%, and comment-first sequences get 25-50%. The variance tracks to three variables: relationship warmth, signal-specificity of the message, and how long you wait to pitch.

This playbook operationalizes what actually moves the number. Not opinion -- practitioner-tested data from 2024-2026.

---

## Prerequisites

- [ ] F1 complete: ICP defined with specific job title, company type, and buying trigger
- [ ] Profile optimized: headline communicates who you help and how (if blank, people accept but don't respond)
- [ ] At least 10 minutes to research each contact before messaging high-value targets
- [ ] LinkedIn account in good standing: not in restriction from high-volume connection requests

**Volume ceiling:** Sending fewer than 25 connection requests per week achieves 40%+ acceptance rates -- nearly 2x higher than high-volume senders. LinkedIn is a personal outreach channel. Treat it like one.

---

## The Contact Relationship Map

Every LinkedIn outreach starts with identifying which type of relationship you have. The message is completely different by type.

| Relationship type | Definition | Strategy |
|------------------|-----------|---------|
| Cold connection | Connected but never messaged | Message within 24-48 hrs of connection |
| Dormant connection | Connected 1-3 years, never messaged | 4-week warm sequence before the ask |
| Lapsed conversation | Spoke once or twice, then went cold | Re-open door without a pitch, wait 30-90 days |
| Active conversation | Currently engaging | Skip to message mechanics |

---

## Before Connecting: Active User Filter

One filter that raises acceptance rate from 15% to 40%: only target people who have posted on LinkedIn in the last 30 days.

Inactive users may never see your message. Active users are already in the LinkedIn habit loop. Filter first.

In LinkedIn Sales Navigator: filter by "posted on LinkedIn in last 30 days."
In Apollo or similar tools: check for recent activity before importing.

---

## Connection Request: Blank vs. Note

**The data conflict:** Some practitioners report blank requests get higher acceptance; others report personalized notes improve acceptance 30-40%.

**Resolution:** If your headline clearly communicates who you help and how, blank works better -- it avoids triggering the sales pitch reflex. If your profile is weak or generic, a note bridges the gap.

**Profile test:** Read your own headline. Does a stranger immediately understand (a) who you help, (b) what outcome you produce, and (c) why you're credible? If not, fix the profile before optimizing the request.

**Connection note template (when needed):**
> "Saw you posted about [specific topic] -- thought it was worth connecting. I work with [ICP type] on [specific problem]."

Under 30 words. Not a pitch. Just enough context to justify the connection.

## Sequence by Relationship Type

### Cold Connection (Never Messaged)

**Window:** Message within 24-48 hours of connection acceptance. After that, they've forgotten they accepted.

**Message 1 -- Problem-oriented opener (not solution-oriented)**

Structure: felt gap + permissive ask, no product mention.

Template:
> "I'm talking with a few [their role]s who know they should be doing more on [X] to [outcome] but aren't sure where to start. At a high level, we help [ICP] with [specific outcome]. Would it be alright if I shared a few ideas?"

Why it works: targets a felt gap, doesn't mention a product, makes the ask permissive. Soft CTAs ("would it be alright") outperform hard CTAs ("let's schedule a call") by 180% in measured campaigns.

**What to personalize (2-3 references, not more):**
- Their specific role or niche (not just their job title)
- A recent post they made
- A company signal (new hire, funding, product launch)

2-3 references gets 340% higher replies. 5+ references feels invasive and hurts performance.

**Follow-up sequence (if no reply):**

| Day | Message | Content |
|-----|---------|---------|
| 1 | Message 1 | Problem-oriented opener |
| 4-6 | Follow-up 1 | New angle or new data point. Not a repeat of message 1. Under 50 words. |
| 10-14 | Follow-up 2 | Specific value delivery: relevant insight, case study, or framework tied to their situation. |
| 17-21 | Follow-up 3 | Low-pressure breakup: "If it's not relevant right now, totally understand." |

**Stop at 3-4 messages without a response.** Beyond 4 DMs damages your reputation with that contact. Switch to passive engagement: like and comment on their posts.

---

### Dormant Connection (Connected 1-3 Years, Never Messaged)

**The data:** People systematically overestimate the awkwardness of reaching out. Research on rosy retrospection suggests they remember past interactions more positively than they occurred. They are more likely to be flattered than annoyed.

**4-week warming sequence before the ask:**

| Week | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Comment on something they posted. Specific, adds a point of view -- not "great post." |
| 2 | Send a relevant article with a one-line note: "Thought of you when I saw this -- fits what you're doing with [X]." |
| 3 | Ask a specific question tied to their recent activity or expertise. |
| 4 | If there has been any engagement: suggest a brief call. |

**Message for week 4 (after engagement):**
> "Hey [Name] -- it's been way too long. I've been following your posts on [specific topic] and they've been insightful. I'm working on something I think you'd have perspective on -- would you be open to a 15-minute call in the next couple weeks? Totally fine if now's not a good time."

**If no engagement after weeks 1-3:** Stay on passive engagement (comments, likes). Do not send a direct ask to someone who hasn't responded to any of your touches.

---

### Lapsed Conversation (Spoke Before, Went Cold)

**Wait 30-90 days.** Long enough that the silence feels like life, not a snub. Their original problem almost certainly still exists.

**Goal of message 1:** Re-open the door. Not pitch. Not apologize. Just open the door.

Template (general):
> "Hey [Name] -- I know we lost touch after our conversation about [topic] back in [month]. Did you end up solving [challenge] or did it get sidelined for now?"

Template (if they chose a competitor):
> "Hey [Name] -- it's been a while since we talked about [project]. I know you ended up going with [competitor]. Curious how that's been working out for you."

Both are pure curiosity. No pitch, no pressure. If they don't reply, one follow-up 7 days later with something genuinely useful (relevant case study, new data, real insight -- not "just bumping this"). Do not send a third message if no response.

## Persona-Specific Rules

### Service Firm Founders (CPA, MSP, Agency, Consulting)

**These people have the highest documented discomfort with cold LinkedIn DMs.** AI-SDR flood in 2024-2025 accelerated irritation. Generic messages to this group are immediately deleted.

**What actually works:** Peer-to-peer framing. They respond to people who appear to understand their specific market, not people who appear to be running a campaign.

Rules:
- Reference their specific niche or client type (not their job title)
- Open with a question about their market, not a pitch about yours
- Flag the referral relationship angle -- they sell to the same clients you do

Sample opener:
> "I work mostly with [specific ICP type] -- same buyer as you. I've been referring people your way mentally when [situation] comes up. Would it make sense to make that formal?"

**Immediate deletions for this group:** Any opener with the word "solutions," "businesses like yours," or "help firms grow their pipeline."

---

### VC-Backed SaaS Founders

**Hardest audience on LinkedIn.** SaaS gets the highest volume of automated outreach. Baseline reply rate: 4.77% -- worst of any vertical. Trigger-based outreach changes this dramatically.

**Trigger-based is non-negotiable for this segment.** Messages sent within 24 hours of a trigger event (funding announcement, new hire, content posted) get 67% higher reply rates than baseline.

Rules:
- Reference the specific trigger explicitly: "Saw you just raised a Series B -- congrats. Companies at that stage usually run into [specific scaling problem]."
- Keep it under 50 words
- Frame as "here's what companies at your stage run into" -- not "here's what we sell"

**Bootstrapped SaaS:** Different framing. Lead with customer proximity and efficiency outcomes, not growth-at-all-costs language. Reference their specific product or niche.

---

### Investors and VCs

**The cardinal rule: one message. Never more.**

Multiple follow-up DMs permanently lock the door with VCs. If you send more than one message without a response, you've damaged the relationship.

**What works:** A specific question tied to something they've actually said or done -- not a pitch.

Sample openers that get replies:
> "You talked about using influencers to market SaaS two years ago. Do you think that's still a viable approach?"

> "I saw you invested in [Company]. What convinced you they could stand out in such a crowded space?"

These work because they're quick to answer and position the investor as an expert.

**If cold pitching (only after question gets a reply):** Personalized hook tied to their portfolio thesis + 2-3 traction signals with exact numbers + clear ask (20-minute call). Ask permission to share the deck -- do not drop a PDF upfront.

---

### Referral Partners (Same ICP, Complementary Service)

Most underused LinkedIn outreach use case in B2B services. Referral-sourced clients have 3x higher LTV, 25% lower churn, and close 50% faster than cold prospects.

**Opener:**
> "I noticed we both work with [specific ICP]. I've been sending clients your way mentally when [specific situation] comes up. Would it make sense to make that formal and see if there's fit?"

After the first conversation: build a follow-up system. Most referral partnerships die because neither party maintains them. Schedule quarterly check-ins. Treat referral partners like a pipeline.

## Voice Notes

The most underused tactical move. Reported reply rates: 40-60%. One practitioner's campaign: 71% reply rate vs. 24% for text-only.

**Rules:**
- Only available from LinkedIn mobile app
- Only to 1st-degree connections
- Maximum 60 seconds. Target 15-20 seconds.
- One pattern interrupt: "Quick voice note -- easier than typing"
- One specific reference to them (their role, a recent post, a company signal)
- One clear point
- One easy question at the end

**Why it works:** Tone of voice cannot be faked. A 20-second voice note conveys more sincerity than any carefully crafted paragraph. The inbox is still text-dominant enough that it stands out visually before it's even played.

## Message Mechanics

### What to say (and what not to)

**5 openers that kill reply rates (never use these):**
1. "Run something by you" -- signals a pitch is incoming
2. "Hope you're well" -- zero relevance, pattern-matches to bulk outreach
3. "Just following up" -- creates resistance
4. "I came across your profile" -- signals list scraping
5. "Do you have time" -- premature ask before any value

**Additional killers:**
- "Just checking in" on follow-ups
- Vague value props ("we help companies improve their sales process")
- Pitching in the first message
- Generic compliments ("great profile," "love what you're doing")
- Immediate Calendly link before any context
- Long paragraphs (not mobile-readable)

**The specificity test:** If this message could have been sent to 1,000 people without modification, it will feel like it was. Specificity is the only reliable proxy for authenticity.

### Length rules

| Message type | Target length |
|-------------|--------------|
| Connection note | Under 30 words |
| Opener (cold) | 50-100 words |
| Follow-ups | Under 50 words |
| Voice note | 15-20 seconds |

Under 30 words for the opener feels lazy. Over 150 words won't be read on mobile. Follow-ups should get shorter with each message, not longer.

## LinkedIn + Cold Email: Multi-Channel Stack

Campaigns combining LinkedIn with cold email report 287% higher response rates than single-channel.

**Sequencing:**
1. Connect on LinkedIn (blank request, no pitch)
2. Message on LinkedIn (problem-oriented opener, day 1-2 after connection)
3. If no reply by day 5-7: send cold email referencing the LinkedIn connection
4. Follow up on LinkedIn (day 10-14): new angle or insight
5. Second cold email (day 14-21): different angle

LinkedIn works as the warmup layer. Cold email hits the same person in a different context. The combination creates the "I keep seeing this person" effect without being annoying.

## Profile Checklist (Required Before Outreach)

Your profile is the first thing they check before replying. A weak profile kills reply rates even when the message is good.

- [ ] Headline: who you help + specific outcome (not your job title)
- [ ] About section: first 3 lines visible without clicking "see more" -- make them count
- [ ] Featured section: one proof point (case study, article, specific result)
- [ ] Recent activity: posted in the last 30 days (they check yours too)
- [ ] Photo: professional, clear, smiling -- not a logo
- [ ] Connection count: 500+ signals social proof
