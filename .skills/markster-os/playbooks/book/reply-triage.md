# Reply Triage

When outreach replies come in, speed and the right response type determine whether the reply converts to a meeting.

Respond to every reply within 4 hours during business hours. After 24 hours, conversion drops by 50%+.

---

## The 6 reply types

### Type 1: Positive -- "Yes, interested"

They said yes, they want to talk, they asked a question, or they asked for more info.

**Respond within 1 hour.**

Response goal: book the meeting immediately. Do not send more information. Do not explain the offer. Get the meeting.

Template:
"Great -- I'd love to connect. I have [time slot 1] or [time slot 2] this week. Which works better for you? Here's my calendar if easier: [link]"

If they asked a specific question first, answer it in one sentence then pivot to booking:
"[One sentence answer]. Happy to go deeper on a quick call -- [time slot 1] or [time slot 2] work?"

---

### Type 2: Soft interest -- "Maybe, tell me more"

They are curious but not yet committed. They asked for more details, a deck, or pricing before agreeing to a call.

**Do not send the deck. Do not send pricing.**

Response goal: qualify them with one question, then book.

Template:
"Happy to share more. Quick question first: [your one qualifying question from the ICP worksheet]? If yes, a 20-minute call is the fastest way to see if this is relevant -- [time slot 1] or [time slot 2]?"

If they resist the call and insist on information first, send a one-paragraph summary and one case study, then ask again:
"Here's a quick overview: [one paragraph]. [Client name] had a similar situation and [specific result]. Does that match what you're working on? Worth 20 minutes?"

---

### Type 3: Not now -- "Reach out in Q3" / "Busy right now"

They are not a no -- they are timing out.

**Add to a 90-day follow-up sequence. Do not argue.**

Response template:
"Completely understand. I'll circle back in [month]. Hope [their current priority] goes well."

Set a calendar reminder. Come back with a new hook (new result, new case study, relevant trigger event) -- not the same message.

---

### Type 4: Wrong person -- "Not my area" / "Try [name]"

They gave you a referral or told you who to contact.

**Respond fast and ask for the intro.**

Template:
"Thanks for the redirect -- really helpful. Would you be comfortable making a quick intro, or should I reach out to [name] directly and mention your name?"

If they say reach out directly: use their name in the subject line ("Reaching out on [their name]'s suggestion") and reference the context in the first line.

---

### Type 5: Hard no -- "Not interested" / "Remove me"

Acknowledge and remove. No argument. No follow-up.

Template:
"Understood -- I'll take you off my list. Thanks for the reply."

Then actually remove them. Continuing to message after a hard no damages reputation and is illegal in many jurisdictions.

---

### Type 6: Objection -- "We already have [X]" / "Too expensive" / "Not a priority"

An objection is not a no. It is a request for more information or a signal that you have not yet made the value clear.

**Do not defend. Ask a question.**

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We already have someone for this" | "Got it -- how is that working? I ask because most companies I work with had something in place before we started too." |
| "Too expensive" | "Fair. Can I ask -- what would the right outcome be worth to you over the next 12 months?" |
| "Not a priority right now" | "Understood. What would need to change for it to become one?" |
| "Send me more info" | (See Type 2 above -- do not send info, book a call) |
| "We tried something like this before and it did not work" | "What happened? I ask because the failure mode tells me a lot about whether this would be different." |

After the one question: if they engage, go for the meeting. If they disengage, treat as Type 3 (not now) and set a 90-day follow-up.

---

## CRM hygiene after every reply

After handling each reply, update your CRM or tracking sheet:
- Stage: interested / not now / wrong person / no / nurture
- Last contact date
- Next action and date
- What they specifically said (their words, not your summary)

The "what they said" field is your best research data. Read it before every follow-up.

---

## Processing volume

If you are getting more replies than you can process within 4 hours, you have a good problem. Fix it by:
1. Using canned responses for Types 3, 4, and 5 (personalize only the first line)
2. Booking in batches -- offer 2 time slots only, not a calendar link that requires decision-making
3. Adding capacity to reply handling before scaling send volume further
