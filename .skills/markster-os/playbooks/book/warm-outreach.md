# Warm Outreach

Reaching out to people you already have a relationship with is always the first step. It converts at 3-5x the rate of cold outreach because trust already exists.

Run this before cold email. Every time.

---

## Who goes on the warm list

**Tier 1 -- Direct fit (reach out first):**
- Former clients who match the ICP
- Current contacts who match the ICP exactly
- People who have mentioned the problem you solve

**Tier 2 -- Connectors (reach out second):**
- People who work with your ICP regularly (accountants, attorneys, agency owners, other vendors)
- Former colleagues now in companies that match your ICP
- Investors or advisors with relevant portfolio companies

**Tier 3 -- Weak ties (reach out if Tier 1 and 2 are exhausted):**
- Acquaintances from events or communities
- LinkedIn connections you have not spoken to in 2+ years

Build 20-30 Tier 1 and 2 contacts before touching Tier 3.

---

## The warm outreach rules

1. **Reference the relationship.** Start with something real: how you know them, what you remember about them, what you have in common now.

2. **One question. Not a pitch.** The goal of the first message is a reply, not a sale. Ask one question about their situation. Do not explain your whole offer.

3. **Short.** 4-6 sentences maximum. If you need more, you are pitching, not reaching out.

4. **No attachments, no links, no deck.** These kill reply rates. Get a reply first.

5. **Personalize every message.** No mail-merge tone. Each person gets a message that could only be for them.

## Templates

### Template 1: Former client or close contact

Subject: quick question

[Name],

Hope things are going well -- last I heard you were [something specific you remember].

I've been working with [company type] on [specific problem]. Wanted to check in: is [that problem] something you're dealing with right now, or has that been sorted?

Either way, would love to catch up.

[Your name]

---

### Template 2: Connector / referral path

Subject: quick intro request

[Name],

Good to connect -- hope [something specific] is going well.

I've been focused on helping [ICP description] with [specific problem]. I imagine you work with a few companies like that.

If anyone comes to mind who's dealing with [the problem], I'd appreciate an intro. Happy to return the favor -- I know you work with [their area].

[Your name]

---

### Template 3: Weak tie reactivation

Subject: [shared context]

[Name],

We connected at [event / through X] a while back. I've been following your work at [company] -- looks like [specific observation].

I've been working on [one-sentence description of what you do] and have been speaking with a lot of [their profile].

Curious -- is [the core problem] something that's on your radar right now?

[Your name]

---

### Follow-up (if no reply after 5-7 days)

[Name],

Bumping this up in case it got buried. Just the one question from before: is [the problem] something that's active for you right now?

[Your name]

---

## LinkedIn version

Same rules. Shorter.

Connection request note (300 character limit):
"[Name] -- [how you know them / shared context]. I've been working with [ICP] on [specific problem]. Would love to reconnect."

First message after connecting:
"Thanks for connecting. Quick question: is [the core problem] something that's on your radar this quarter?"

## Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Tier 1 reply rate | 40-60% |
| Tier 2 reply rate | 20-35% |
| Meetings booked from warm outreach | 3-5 per 20 contacts |
| Time from first message to meeting | 2-7 days |

If reply rates are below these targets: the message is either too long, sounds like a pitch, or the ICP match is weak.
