# Pre-Event Outreach Template

Goal: get on their radar before the event. Not to pitch. The pitch happens when you meet.

---

## When to Send

| Timing | What to do |
|--------|-----------|
| 7-10 days out | Research attendees, identify 5-10 priority targets |
| 5-7 days out | Send pre-event outreach (email or LinkedIn) |
| 2-3 days out | Follow up on any unanswered messages - one touch only |
| Day of | Check replies, set meeting locations/times |

Send too early (10+ days) and they have not shifted their attention to the event yet. Send too late (1-2 days out) and schedules are already locked.

---

## Variables

- `[FIRST_NAME]` - their first name
- `[EVENT_NAME]` - name of the event (be specific: "SaaStr Annual" not "the conference")
- `[SPECIFIC_REASON]` - why you are reaching out to them specifically. This is the most important field. Options:
  - They are on the agenda: "saw you're moderating the [PANEL_TOPIC] panel"
  - Something they published: "read your piece on [TOPIC] last month"
  - Their company's situation: "noticed [COMPANY] just expanded into [MARKET]"
  - Shared context: "both in [VERTICAL] and this crowd tends to surface good conversations"
- `[MUTUAL_NAME]` - name of the person you have in common (Version C only)
- `[YOUR_NAME]` - your name
- `[COMPANY_OR_TITLE]` - your company name or title (keep it short)

---

## Version A: Cold Email (if you have their address)

**Subject:** [EVENT_NAME] - worth connecting?

---

[FIRST_NAME],

I'll be at [EVENT_NAME] next week and wanted to reach out before the chaos starts.

[SPECIFIC_REASON - one sentence, specific to them. Not a compliment. A reason.]

Would you be open to 15 minutes - coffee before a session, or a quick chat between them?

[YOUR_NAME]
[COMPANY_OR_TITLE]

---

**Alternate subject lines:**
- [FIRST_NAME] - see you at [EVENT_NAME]?
- Quick note before [EVENT_NAME]
- [EVENT_NAME] - you on the attendee list?

---

## Version B: LinkedIn DM (no mutual connection)

Character limit: LinkedIn DMs cut off around 300 characters in preview. Front-load the reason.

---

[FIRST_NAME] - heading to [EVENT_NAME] and wanted to connect before everyone arrives. [SPECIFIC_REASON in one sentence]. Would be great to find 15 minutes if you're around.

[YOUR_NAME]

---

**Shorter version (for connection request message field - 300 char limit):**

[FIRST_NAME] - both at [EVENT_NAME] next week. [SPECIFIC_REASON compressed to 10 words]. Would love to connect in person.

---

## Version C: LinkedIn DM (with a mutual connection)

Mention the mutual in the first line. It doubles reply rate.

---

[FIRST_NAME] - [MUTUAL_NAME] mentioned you'd be at [EVENT_NAME]. Wanted to reach out before we all arrive. [SPECIFIC_REASON in one sentence]. Would love to find time to connect.

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## Version D: Short cold email (when you have less context on them)

Use when: you know they are relevant but do not have a strong specific hook.

**Subject:** [EVENT_NAME] - quick intro

---

[FIRST_NAME],

Reaching out because I'll be at [EVENT_NAME] and [SPECIFIC_REASON - broader: "the [type of attendee] crowd there tends to be worth knowing"].

I work with [ICP TYPE] on [OUTCOME in one phrase]. Worth connecting while we're both there?

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## Event Brief Format (run before the event)

Before attending any event, build a quick brief. This takes 20 minutes and dramatically increases what you get out of the day.

```
EVENT: [Name]
DATE / LOCATION: [Details]
TYPE: [Conference / Meetup / Dinner / Peer Group]
EXPECTED CROWD: [~N attendees, profile]

OBJECTIVES (rank 3):
1. [Primary: meetings, pipeline, intel?]
2. [Secondary]
3. [Tertiary]

PRIORITY TARGETS (from attendee list or agenda):
1. [Name] - [Company] - [Why relevant] - [Opening line]
2. [Name] - [Company] - [Why relevant] - [Opening line]
3. [Name] - [Company] - [Why relevant] - [Opening line]

YOUR PITCH FOR THIS CROWD:
[Tailor your one-liner to this specific audience. What do they care about?]

CONVERSATION STARTERS:
- [Relevant to event theme]
- [Question that works for this audience]
- [Data point or observation that opens a door]
```

---

## Principles

- **Specificity beats volume.** 5 well-researched outreach messages outperform 30 generic ones. "I saw you're on the [PANEL] panel" beats "I noticed we're both attending."
- **Your goal is a meeting slot, not a sale.** The ask is 15 minutes in person. Everything else comes after.
- **Save the pitch.** Do not put your full value prop in the pre-event message. That is what the meeting is for.
- **Send a confirmation.** If they say yes, send a confirmation note 24-48 hours before with a suggested time and place.
- **Two follow-ups maximum.** One pre-event message, one follow-up if no reply. If they do not respond, meet them in person and introduce yourself.

## After the Event

Within 48 hours of the event: send post-event follow-ups to every meaningful conversation. See `post-event.md` for templates and sequencing.
