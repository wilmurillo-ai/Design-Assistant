# Post-Event Follow-Up Template

The 48-hour rule: send within 48 hours of meeting them. Response rate drops sharply after that. The event is still fresh and your name is still recognizable.

---

## The Sequence

| Touch | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| Touch 1 (T1) | Within 48 hours | Reconnect, reference conversation, offer next step |
| Touch 2 (T2) | 3-5 days after T1 if no reply | Brief bump, specific reference |
| Touch 3 (T3) | 7-10 days after T2 if no reply | Final touch, leave the door open |

After three touches with no reply: stop. You've done your part. They know who you are.

---

## Variables

- `[FIRST_NAME]` - their first name
- `[EVENT_NAME]` - the specific event name
- `[CONVERSATION_DETAIL]` - what you actually discussed. Be specific. "Your point about the talent acquisition challenge in mid-market" not "our great conversation." This proves you were listening.
- `[RELEVANT_RESOURCE]` - something genuinely useful to share: an article, a case study, a template, an introduction to someone they should know. Only include this if the resource is actually relevant - do not invent reasons to attach things.
- `[NEXT_STEP]` - the specific thing you promised or offered to do next: "send the case study on X", "set up the intro with [NAME]", "walk you through how we handle Y"
- `[CALENDAR_LINK]` - your scheduling link, or offer two time options
- `[YOUR_NAME]` - your name

---

## T1: Strong Conversation (Clear Next Step Agreed)

Use when: you had a real conversation and agreed on something to do next.

**Subject:** Good to meet at [EVENT_NAME]

---

[FIRST_NAME],

Good to meet at [EVENT_NAME]. [CONVERSATION_DETAIL - one specific sentence about what you discussed. Not a compliment - a reference.]

As I mentioned, [NEXT_STEP].

[If sharing something now: here it is - [LINK or one-sentence description]]

Worth a 20-minute call to continue from where we left off?

[CALENDAR_LINK or: "I'm free Tuesday or Wednesday afternoon if either works."]

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## T1: Light Conversation (No Explicit Next Step)

Use when: you connected briefly but did not lock in a specific follow-on action.

**Subject:** [EVENT_NAME] - good to connect

---

[FIRST_NAME],

Good to connect briefly at [EVENT_NAME]. [CONVERSATION_DETAIL - even one sentence proves you remember them specifically.]

[RELEVANT_RESOURCE if you have one: "Thought this was relevant to what you mentioned about [TOPIC] - [LINK or 1-sentence description]."]

I work with [ICP TYPE] on [OUTCOME in one sentence]. Happy to share more if it's relevant to what you're working on right now.

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## T1: Very Brief Interaction (You Barely Talked)

Use when: you exchanged cards or connected on LinkedIn but barely spoke.

**Subject:** From [EVENT_NAME]

---

[FIRST_NAME],

We crossed paths briefly at [EVENT_NAME]. [One honest reference - even just: "You were heading out of the [SESSION NAME] session."]

[RELEVANT_RESOURCE or context that would make reaching out worth their time]

Worth staying connected - happy to share what we're working on if it's ever relevant.

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## T2: Follow-Up if No Reply (3-5 Days After T1)

Keep it short. Do not apologize for following up.

**Subject:** Re: [EVENT_NAME] - quick follow-up

---

[FIRST_NAME],

Following up in case my last note got buried.

[One sentence referencing the conversation again - more specific than the first email, not more vague.]

Worth a quick call?

[CALENDAR_LINK]

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## T2: Follow-Up With New Value (Better Version)

Use when you have a reason to add something rather than just bumping.

**Subject:** Re: [EVENT_NAME] - thought of you

---

[FIRST_NAME],

Wanted to follow up and share this - [RELEVANT_RESOURCE or news or article that connects to what they mentioned at the event].

[One sentence on the connection: "Given what you mentioned about [TOPIC], this seemed worth passing along."]

Still happy to connect if the timing is right. [CALENDAR_LINK or soft ask]

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## T3: Final Touch (7-10 Days After T2 if Still No Reply)

Leave the door open without being pushy.

**Subject:** Re: [EVENT_NAME]

---

[FIRST_NAME],

One last note - I know timing is everything.

[One sentence: either a new development on your end, or a simple acknowledgment that you are not going away frustrated.]

If it is ever useful to connect, you know where to find me. Happy to pick up where we left off.

[YOUR_NAME]

## Batch Follow-Up (When You Met Many People at One Event)

After a large conference or dinner, you may have 10-20 people to follow up with. Do not send one generic blast. Batch the work but personalize each message.

**Process:**
1. Right after the event (same night or next morning), write 3-5 words about each person: what you discussed, what was notable. Do this before the memories blur.
2. Tier them: Priority (follow up today), Secondary (follow up in 2-3 days), Keep-in-touch (LinkedIn connection only is fine).
3. Write Priority follow-ups first. Use Version A or B above.
4. Secondary: use a shorter version of T1.
5. Keep-in-touch: connect on LinkedIn with a brief note referencing the event. No email needed.

---

## What to Include in a Relevant Resource

A relevant resource is not a sales brochure. It is something that earns their attention. Options that work:

- An article or study directly related to a problem they named
- An introduction to someone they should know (with the other person's permission)
- A template or framework that solves something they mentioned
- A case study about a company in a similar situation (keep it relevant, not promotional)
- A data point or piece of research they would find useful

If you do not have a relevant resource, do not invent one. The follow-up email alone is sufficient.

---

## Principles

- **48-hour rule.** Send within 48 hours. Response rate drops by more than half after 72 hours.
- **Specific beats warm.** "Your point about the hiring challenge in Q4" beats "great to meet you." Specific references prove you were present.
- **One next step per email.** Do not give them five options. One clear ask: a call, a resource, a reply.
- **Vague follow-ups generate vague outcomes.** Every touch should move toward a defined next state: a meeting, a clear yes, or a clear not-now.
- **Three touches is the limit.** After that, stop. You have made the effort. They know you exist.
- **Do not pitch in T1.** The first follow-up is about reconnecting. Save the value proposition for when they show interest.
