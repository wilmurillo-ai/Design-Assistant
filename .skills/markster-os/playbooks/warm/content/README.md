# Content Playbook

A content machine built on your ICP. Produces consistent output that shortens sales cycles and generates inbound signals over time.

---

## Prerequisites

- [ ] ICP documented (determines what topics you write about and who you are writing for)
- [ ] Message documented (determines how you write and what problems you address)
- [ ] One primary publishing channel selected (LinkedIn is the default for B2B service businesses)
- [ ] Theme defined: the intersection of a problem your ICP cares about and a perspective only you can deliver credibly

If your theme is not clear, do not start publishing. Inconsistent or unfocused content builds no audience.

---

## What this produces

- A defined theme you will be known for (1-2 topics you own)
- A 30-day publishing calendar with specific titles
- Long-form content (articles, guides) at 2 pieces per month
- Short-form content (LinkedIn posts, punchy updates) at 3-5 per week
- A distribution system that extends reach beyond your existing followers
- Content assets that can be referenced in cold outreach and follow-up sequences

---

## How content fits into GTM

Content does two things in this system:

**1. Warms up cold outreach.** When someone receives a cold email from you and recognizes your name from your posts, reply rates increase. Content makes cold outreach feel warm.

**2. Generates inbound signals.** When prospects engage with your content (comment, share, DM), they signal buying intent without asking for a proposal. These signals are worth more than a cold list.

Content is not a standalone channel for companies under 20 people. It is a force multiplier for cold email, events, and referral. Run content alongside outbound, not instead of it.

## Steps

### Step 1: Define your core theme

Your theme is the intersection of:
- A problem your ICP cares about deeply
- A perspective only you can deliver credibly (from your experience or data)

The theme is not your category. "GTM strategy" is not a theme. "Why referral-dependent B2B firms hit a ceiling at $2M and what to do about it" is a theme.

A strong theme:
- Is specific enough that your ICP recognizes themselves in it
- Is broad enough that you can write about it for 12 months
- Has a contrarian or counterintuitive angle that makes it worth sharing
- Is grounded in evidence: your experience, client results, or data

**Exercise:** Write your theme as a single sentence starting with "I write about..." or "My point of view is..."

If you cannot write that sentence, your theme is not clear. Do not start publishing until it is.

**Content pillars to rotate within your theme:**

| Pillar | Frequency | What it builds |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Founder or practitioner story | 2x per week | Trust and relatability |
| Proof and results | 1x per week | Credibility and buying confidence |
| Industry insight or data | 1x per week | Authority and shareability |
| Contrarian take on a common belief | 1x per week | Engagement and differentiation |
| Practical how-to or framework | 2x per month | Usefulness and bookmarking |

---

### Step 2: Build a 30-day calendar

With your theme defined, map out 30 days of specific content ideas before you start writing.

**Content mix:**

| Format | Frequency | Purpose |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Long-form article (600-1500 words) | 2x per month | Depth, SEO, something worth bookmarking |
| LinkedIn post (150-300 words) | 3x per week | Presence and signal generation |
| Short-form hook post (under 100 words) | 2x per week | Reach and shareability |

**For each piece in the calendar, define before writing:**
- Topic (specific, not vague: "Why CPAs lose clients in year two" not "client retention")
- Format (article / LinkedIn post / hook)
- Core insight (the one thing the reader will take away)
- Connection to your ICP's situation (why does this matter to your buyer right now?)
- CTA (what should the reader do next: reply, read another post, book a call?)

**Title formulas that work:**
- "[Number] reasons [ICP type] [specific problem they have]"
- "Why [common industry belief] is wrong for [ICP type]"
- "The [specific thing] that [ICP type] keeps missing"
- "What I have learned from [number] [ICP type] clients about [topic]"

---

### Step 3: Write and publish

**Long-form writing process:**
1. Write the core insight in one paragraph first. What is the one thing you want the reader to take away?
2. Build the argument around that insight.
3. Use a real example from your own experience or a client situation (anonymize if needed).
4. End with a specific action the reader can take.

**Short-form writing process:**
- First line is everything: it must stop the scroll. Use a specific claim, a counterintuitive statement, or a question that names the reader's exact situation.
- Deliver the value in 3-5 short paragraphs or a numbered list.
- End with a CTA: a question, an invitation, or a soft next step.

**Voice rules:**
- Direct, no corporate language
- Short sentences
- Personal -- use "I," tell stories, name specific situations
- Data when available: specific numbers beat vague claims
- Never lead with AI or technology -- lead with outcomes and trust
- Okay to be polarizing: a clear point of view is more shareable than a hedge

**Templates:**
- [Long-form article template](long-form/templates/article.md)
- [LinkedIn post template](short-form/templates/linkedin-post.md)

**LinkedIn post structures that work:**

The Founder Story:
```
[Surprising personal fact or moment]

[2-3 lines of context]

[What changed or what I learned]

[Connection to what you are building]

[Question for the audience]
```

The Data Drop:
```
[Striking statistic]

[Why this matters to your reader]

[What most people get wrong about this]

[What you do differently, or what to do instead]

[Agree or disagree?]
```

The Contrarian Take:
```
[Bold statement most people disagree with]

Here is why:

[Reason 1 with proof]
[Reason 2 with proof]
[Reason 3 with proof]

[Restate the position]

[What do you think?]
```

The Client Win (anonymized if needed):
```
[Result in one line]

Here is what happened:

[Situation before]
[What was done]
[Result after]

[Lesson or takeaway]

[Who else has seen this?]
```

---

### Step 4: Distribute

Publishing is not distribution. Distribution is how your content reaches your ICP beyond the people who already follow you.

**Distribution system:**

1. **Post natively** on your primary channel (LinkedIn for most B2B service businesses)
2. **Send to your email list** even if it is small. 50 people who opted in are worth more than 5,000 passive followers.
3. **Share in communities** where your ICP is active: Slack groups, forums, newsletters that accept submissions
4. **Repurpose:** every long-form article generates 3-5 LinkedIn posts. Every LinkedIn post can become a cold email P.S. line or a DM opener.
5. **Reference in outreach:** when doing cold email or LinkedIn outreach, link to a relevant piece as proof of expertise. This is the highest-leverage distribution move for an early-stage GTM.

**Engagement after posting:**
- Reply to every comment within 24 hours. Early engagement signals feed the algorithm.
- Ask a follow-up question in your replies to extend the thread.
- DM people who comment substantively: they have self-selected as interested in your topic.

---

### Step 5: Track and adjust

Review performance every 30 days.

**What to change based on data:**
- Low impressions across all posts: topic is not resonating or cadence is too slow. Increase frequency or sharpen the theme.
- High impressions but no comments: content is readable but not provocative enough to respond to. Add a stronger point of view or a more specific question.
- Comments from your ICP type: this topic and format is working. Do more of it.
- DMs and inbound from content: the content is converting. Note which topics and formats generated these signals.

|--------|------------------|----------------------|
| Impressions per post | Is this topic resonating? | Trending upward week over week |
| Comments per post | Are you saying something worth responding to? | 3+ comments from ICP type |
| Profile visits from content | Is content driving discovery? | Visible uplift in profile views |
| Inbound DMs per month | Is content converting to conversations? | 2-5 per month by day 90 |
| Content mentioned in outreach replies | Is content warming cold contacts? | Track in reply log |
| Publishing consistency | Are you actually doing it? | No gap longer than 5 days |

## Common Mistakes

**No defined theme.** Posting about different topics each week builds no audience and no authority. Pick a theme and stick to it for at least 90 days.

**Writing for yourself, not for your buyer.** "We launched a new feature" or "I spoke at a conference" are posts about you. Posts that name the reader's specific struggle or situation outperform every time.

**Inconsistent cadence.** One great post per month and then silence destroys momentum. Consistent mediocre output beats occasional excellence for building an audience.

**No distribution plan.** Posting and waiting is not a strategy. Repurpose every long-form piece into short-form. Share in communities. Reference in outreach.

**Posting and not engaging.** The first 30-60 minutes after posting determines reach. Reply to every comment. Ask follow-up questions.

**Using jargon your buyer does not use.** "Pipeline," "GTM," "ICP," and "orchestration" are words your reader skips over. Write the way they think, not the way you think.

**Stopping before the compounding effect kicks in.** Most content audiences take 60-90 days of consistent posting to build. Quitting at week 4 because the numbers are small is the most common mistake.
