## What D1 Is in the GOD Engine

The GOD Engine has three distribution modes: paid, outbound, and community. D1 is the community-native layer - not paid ads, not cold sequences, not broadcasting to a follower base you own. It is distribution through presence: earning visibility inside spaces where your ICP already gathers, trusts each other, and makes vendor decisions based on peer proof before they ever raise a hand publicly.

D1 compounds. Ads stop when spend stops. Community reputation grows with every quality contribution. The tradeoff is timeline - D1 takes 90-120 days before generating consistent pipeline. Under ScaleOS, it is treated as a long-horizon channel with disproportionate returns for patient operators.

## ICP Presence Audit - Finding Where They Actually Are

Membership does not equal presence. Run this audit before investing time in any community:

**Step 1 - Post and reply velocity.** Look at the last 20 threads. Admin-driven posts dominating the feed signal a ghost town regardless of member count. You want member-generated threads.

**Step 2 - Engagement depth.** A Slack community where threads get 0-1 replies is not where your ICP spends time. Target: 5+ replies and back-and-forth dialogue per active thread.

**Step 3 - ICP keyword search.** Search for tools your ICP uses, pain keywords your service addresses, and competitor names. If these have not appeared in the last 90-120 days, your ICP is not there or not talking.

**Step 4 - Active user ratio.** Member count is vanity. Observe how quickly posts get responses. For Reddit, check posts-per-day and the online-now counter.

**Step 5 - Identity cross-reference.** Scan member profiles for job titles, company names, and bios. Ten minutes in a Slack directory tells you whether the community composition matches your buyer.

Decision rule:
- Join and invest: 3+ ICP keyword mentions in last 90 days + member-driven threads + 5+ replies per active thread
- Lurk and monitor: Active but low ICP density - watch 30 days before deciding
- Skip: Admin-driven posts, dead threads, or no keyword hits for your domain

## Contribution Before Extraction - The Exact Cadence

The working practitioner ratio is 10:1 - for every 1 post referencing your work or service, make 10+ contributions delivering value with zero pitch.

**Weeks 1-3:** Zero mentions of your business. Observation and lightweight engagement only - upvotes, short reactions, genuine questions. Map active voices, recurring problems, and moderator tolerance.

**Weeks 4-8:** Answer questions in your domain with complete, self-contained answers that solve the problem in the thread without requiring the person to visit your site or contact you. If your answer resolves the question fully, you are building the right equity.

**After 10+ genuine contributions:** A passing reference to your work is acceptable - as a data point, not a CTA. "We ran into this with a client last quarter; what worked was X" lands. "We solve this - here's our pricing page" does not.

**After community members start tagging you:** Your mentions carry no friction. This is the signal that D1 is working.

| Contribution Type | Frequency |
|---|---|
| Direct answer to a problem thread (no link) | 2-3x per week |
| Resource share (not yours) | Once per week |
| Original insight from client work | Once per week |
| Intro between two members | Whenever relevant |

## Content That Works for B2B Lead Gen in Communities

**1. Direct answers to active problem questions.** The person posting is in active evaluation mode. A complete, opinionated, specific answer - with a concrete example from your work - gets you researched and DMed without any CTA. Works equally on Reddit, Slack, LinkedIn Groups, and Discourse.

**2. Original frameworks shared in full.** No gating, no email capture. Post the actual questions, the actual decision criteria. People who find it useful come back and refer others.

**3. Original data from your own client work.** In a market saturated with recycled stats, your own numbers are unreplicable. Even small samples ("across 12 client campaigns this quarter, X outperformed Y by 40%") earn attention no competitor can replicate. This is what gets screenshotted and shared in private channels.

**4. Real-format case studies.** Structure: problem - what we tried - what did not work - what did work - measurable result. The "what did not work" section creates credibility. Polished marketing-grade case studies get ignored.

**5. Tools and calculators shared openly.** A small interactive tool gets bookmarked, reshared in private channels, and generates inbound months after posting.

What does not work: generic listicles, gated content dropped in threads, webinar invites as first interactions.

## Converting Community Engagement to Pipeline - The Transition

Community engagement is not a buying signal. Someone who upvoted your post is a person who found something interesting - not a lead. Three entry points govern when and how to move to 1:1.

**Entry Point 1 - Multiple engagements from the same person.**
Signal: 3+ interactions over 2-4 weeks.

DM: "Hey [Name] - noticed you've engaged with a couple of my posts on [topic]. Working on anything in that space right now, or mostly just tracking the landscape?"

**Entry Point 2 - They posted a problem you specifically solve.**
Step 1: Answer fully in the thread. No CTA, no link.
Step 2: 24-48 hours later, DM:
"Saw your question about [X] in [community] - gave an answer in the thread but it's hard to cover the nuances in a post. We've worked through this with a few [firm type] clients - happy to share what actually moved the needle if you want 15 minutes."

**Entry Point 3 - Multiple back-and-forth exchanges.**
DM: "We've been in a few of the same threads lately - seems like we're working on similar problems from different angles. What are you building or working on these days?"

Hard rules:
- Never DM within 24 hours of first contact
- Never reference more than one prior engagement point per DM
- One question per DM - no multiple agenda items
- No pitch in the first DM - the goal is a reply, not a booking
- If no reply: do not follow up via DM; continue in the community

## Member vs. Builder - When to Join vs. Build Your Own

**Participate in existing communities when:**
- Under $500K ARR or positioning not yet validated
- No existing audience who would join something you build
- Pipeline needed in under 6 months
- Still refining ICP or messaging

**Build your own community when:**
- Existing audience of 100+ engaged contacts with a reason to talk to each other
- Genuine gap in existing communities - not "we could do this better"
- Reason for the community is not "more leads" - communities built as lead farms die fastest
- 6-12 months timeline before expecting pipeline impact
- Capacity to moderate and seed content for the first 90 days

Decision table for sub-20-person B2B service firms:

| Condition | Action |
|---|---|
| Under $1M ARR, positioning not locked | Join 3-5 existing communities |
| $1M+ ARR, 200+ engaged existing contacts | Consider building on Slack or Skool |
| Defining a new category (not just serving one) | Building has strategic moat value |
| Need pipeline in under 6 months | Do not build - participate |
| Specific ICP niche with no existing community | Build may be warranted even at lower ARR |

## Platform Guide

| Platform | ICP Density | Promotion Tolerance | Best Content Type | Key Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Reddit (niche subreddits) | High - problem-focused | Low - personal accounts only | Direct answers, original data | High ban risk for brand accounts |
| LinkedIn Groups | Medium - varies | Medium | Frameworks, POV posts | Low |
| Slack (invite-only niche) | High when curated | Medium - relationship-dependent | Answers, peer intros, async Q&A | Admin removal |
| Skool | Medium, growing | Medium-high | Frameworks, tools, community building | Low |
| Discord | Varies by server | Low in technical communities | Real-time answers | Medium |
| Industry forums | Very high in specific verticals | Low - long-standing norms | Detailed answers, case data | Medium |

**Reddit:** Highest-leverage for genuine thought leadership but zero tolerance for brand accounts. Use founder or operator personal accounts only. Bluntness and specificity are rewarded; corporate polish gets downvoted.

**Slack communities** (Pavilion, RevGenius, Exit Five, Agency Hackers, vertical-specific groups) are where senior buyers and owner-operators actually spend time. Invite-only access signals real curation.

**Skool** is growing fast in the operator and consulting space. Higher promotion tolerance than Reddit - more forgiving for early contributors.

**Industry forums** carry the highest ICP density in specific verticals (accounting, IT/MSP, legal) and the lowest tolerance for marketing behavior. Earn presence slowly; the payoff is disproportionate.

## Tracking and Attributing Pipeline from Community Activity

30-50% of B2B traffic from private sharing arrives in analytics as direct visits. UTMs cannot capture dark social. Use three layers:

**Layer 1 - Self-reported attribution.** Add an open-text "How did you first hear about us?" field to every discovery call intake form. Make it required. Over 6-12 months this surfaces which communities generate warm inbound.

Discovery call script: "Before we dive in - what made you reach out to us specifically? And where did you first come across our work?"

**Layer 2 - UTM-tagged links.** When sharing links in communities:
- `utm_source` = platform (reddit, slack, linkedin-group, skool)
- `utm_medium` = community
- `utm_campaign` = specific community name (e.g., revops-slack, r-agency)

**Layer 3 - CRM tagging with two labels:**
- **Community-Sourced:** Opportunity originated from community activity. Tag at contact creation.
- **Community-Influenced:** Community interaction happened during an active deal cycle. Tag at deal stage.

Most teams only track sourced attribution - this understates community's actual pipeline impact significantly.

Minimum viable stack: GA4 with UTMs + open-text attribution field + two CRM deal tags. Do not buy a CLG attribution platform until you have 10+ community-sourced deals per quarter to analyze.

## Common Mistakes That Get You Banned or Lose Credibility

**Hard stops - these get accounts banned:**
1. Posting as a brand account on Reddit - content quality is irrelevant, moderators flag corporate accounts automatically
2. Sharing a link in the first 1-3 posts - triggers spam detection on virtually every platform
3. Dropping a gated lead magnet unprompted - ask first, let them opt in
4. Sending a sales DM immediately after someone engages with your post - destroys trust publicly

**Credibility destroyers - these close the channel permanently:**
5. Treating Reddit like LinkedIn - polished corporate language gets downvoted
6. Copy-pasting website or service description copy - people recognize broadcasting instantly
7. Pitching in replies to a problem post - answer the problem fully first, mention your work incidentally if at all
8. Commenting without reading the thread - generic advice that ignores context signals visibility farming
9. Connecting on LinkedIn and pitching the same day as a community interaction - community trust and outreach trust are separate accounts; drawing on one burns both
10. Joining and immediately asking "what tools do you use for X?" - recognized immediately as competitive intel gathering, not genuine participation
