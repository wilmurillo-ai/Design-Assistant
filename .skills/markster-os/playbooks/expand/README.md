# D3: Expand

**Pillar:** Delivery
**North Star:** Net Revenue Retention (NRR)

---

## What It Covers

Upsell and cross-sell, customer health scoring, churn prevention, referral programs, and win-back campaigns.

D3 grows revenue from existing clients. NRR above 100% means expansion revenue exceeds churn -- the business grows without needing to replace churned clients. The most capital-efficient growth in B2B service businesses comes from expansion, not acquisition.

---

## Key Questions It Answers

- How do we identify which clients are ready for expansion conversations?
- What does a systematic referral program look like vs. passive referral?
- How do we detect churn signals early enough to act before the client decides to leave?
- What expansion offers make sense given our current service model?
- How do we re-engage churned clients when conditions change?

## Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Net Revenue Retention (NRR) | 100-120%+ |
| Gross churn rate | Under 5% monthly |
| Expansion rate | 5-10%+ monthly |
| Referral rate (clients who refer) | 20-40% |
| Average client lifetime | 12-24+ months |

## Customer Health Score

Score each client monthly across five dimensions:

| Dimension | Weight | Scoring |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Engagement (are they using what you deliver?) | 25% | 1-10 |
| Results (are they seeing ROI?) | 25% | 1-10 |
| Payment (are they paying on time?) | 20% | 1-10 |
| Communication (are they responsive?) | 15% | 1-10 |
| Sentiment (how do they seem in calls?) | 15% | 1-10 |

Health thresholds:
- 80-100: Healthy -- initiate expansion conversation
- 60-79: Stable -- maintain current service, monitor
- 40-59: At risk -- initiate intervention call this week
- Below 40: Critical -- escalate to founder immediately

---

## Referral System Framework

Passive referral is not a channel. Active referral activation is a system.

**Trigger:** Health score 80+ for 60+ days (client is happy and stable)
**Timing:** After a specific win or result milestone, not randomly
**Ask:** "We're working with companies like yours in [specific situation]. Who in your network is dealing with [specific pain]?" -- specific ask, not "do you know anyone"
**Incentive options:** Formal (cash, credit), reciprocal (refer back), or simple acknowledgment (personal thank-you, LinkedIn recommendation)
**Follow-up:** Track referrals given, follow up with referrer on outcome, close the loop

---

## Churn Prevention Framework

| Signal | Detection | Response |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Health score drops below 60 | Monthly health review | Proactive check-in call within 1 week |
| Payment delayed 14+ days | Billing tracking | Outreach from Account Manager (not billing) |
| Engagement drops (not opening deliverables) | Delivery tracking | Usage check-in, offer to simplify or adjust |
| No response to communications | Communication log | Escalate to Delivery Owner, check-in via different channel |
| Contract renewal 60 days out | Calendar trigger | Renewal conversation started, not left to last minute |

## Common Failures

**Passive referral strategy:** "Tell your friends." This is not a strategy. Clients refer when they are happy, but they rarely do it unprompted. A structured ask with a specific target in mind produces 5-10x the referrals of passive waiting.

**Ignoring health scores:** Building a health scoring system and then not acting on the scores. A score of 40 requires action this week, not "we'll check in next quarter."

**Expansion conversations at the wrong time:** Proposing upsells when the client is not yet getting full value from the current scope. Health score must be 80+ before expansion conversations are productive.

**No win-back program:** Treating churned clients as permanently lost. Clients who churned due to timing, budget, or fit often become buyers again when their situation changes. A quarterly win-back cadence captures this.

---

## Expand Done Right

D3 is working when: NRR is above 100% consistently. Health scores are reviewed monthly by the Delivery Owner. Expansion conversations happen on a structured cadence with clients in the 80+ health range. Referrals come from a proactive system, not luck. The D3 metrics are reviewed weekly in the pillar review.
