# Warm Introduction Request Templates

> **Playbook:** G4 - Referral Activation
> **Use case:** Asking a connector (client or partner) to actively introduce you to a specific prospect
> **Distinction from ask.md:** These templates are for warm introductions - where the connector actively vouches and connects you - not for passive referrals where the prospect reaches out on their own

---

## Part 1 - Email to the Connector (Requesting the Introduction)

**When to use:** When you have a specific person in mind that your connector knows, and you want the connector to actively make the introduction rather than just pass your name.

**Subject:** Quick favor - intro to [Prospect First Name]?

---

Hey [Connector First Name],

Hope things are going well on [reference something specific - their project, a recent win, their business].

I am reaching out because I think [Prospect First Name] at [Prospect Company] might be a strong fit for what I do. Based on what you have mentioned and what I can see from their business, they are likely dealing with [specific problem: e.g., "referral-dependent revenue without a system behind it" / "a pipeline that works but only as long as the founder is personally selling"].

Given your relationship with them, an introduction from you would carry a lot of weight. Would you be open to making a quick intro - either by email or a three-way call?

I have written a short note below that you can edit and send as-is if it is easier. It takes about 2 minutes to adjust and forward.

Let me know either way - I do not want to put you in an awkward spot and I am happy to approach them differently if the timing is not right.

Thanks for even considering it.

[Your Name]

---

**Notes:**
- Address the connector's relationship with the prospect directly - acknowledge that their vouching is the value
- Name the specific problem the prospect is likely dealing with - this shows you have done your homework and makes it easy for the connector to confirm "yes, that sounds like them"
- Always include the forwardable draft (Part 2 below) in the same email - never ask the connector to compose the introduction message themselves
- Give a clean exit - "happy to approach them differently" removes guilt and preserves the relationship if they decline

---

## Part 2 - The Forwardable Message (Connector Sends This to the Prospect)

**When to use:** Include this in your email to the connector (Part 1 above) as a draft they can edit and send. The connector sends this from their own email address to the prospect.

**Instructions for the connector:** "Here is a short note you can edit and forward - feel free to adjust the wording however you like."

---

Hey [Prospect First Name],

I have been working with [Your Name] on [specific area: e.g., "building a referral activation system for our firm" / "systematizing our growth process"]. The results have been solid - [one specific outcome: e.g., "we sourced four new clients in the last quarter without doing any outbound" / "we went from sporadic referrals to a predictable system in about 90 days"].

I think you two should talk. Your situation sounds similar to where we were when we started.

Looping you in - [Your Name], meet [Prospect First Name].

[Connector Name]

---

**Notes:**
- This message is written in the connector's voice, not yours - use plain, conversational language
- One specific outcome is more credible than multiple vague claims - choose the result most relevant to the prospect's likely situation
- The final line ("Looping you in") is the active introduction mechanic - it puts both parties in the same email thread
- Keep the draft short - connectors are more likely to send a message they do not need to heavily edit

---

## Part 3 - Follow-up to Connector if Introduction Is Not Made Within 1 Week

**When to send:** 7 days after the connector agreed to make the intro but you have not been looped in or heard anything.

**Subject:** Re: Intro to [Prospect First Name]

---

Hey [Connector First Name],

Just checking in on the intro to [Prospect First Name] - wanted to make sure it did not fall off your radar given how much you have going on.

If it helps, here is the note again ready to copy and paste:

---

Hey [Prospect First Name] - I have been working with [Your Name] on [specific area]. Results have been solid. I think you two should connect. Looping them in - [Your Name], meet [Prospect First Name].

---

If the timing is not right or the fit is not as strong as we thought, no worries at all - just let me know and I will reach out to them through another path when the time makes more sense.

Thanks again for even considering it.

[Your Name]

---

**Notes:**
- Keep this shorter than the original message - the context is already established
- Paste the forwardable draft again - do not make the connector search for the previous email
- The exit line ("no worries at all") is load-bearing - it signals that you value the relationship more than the introduction, which paradoxically makes connectors more likely to follow through
- After one follow-up with no response, do not push further on this specific introduction. Move to a quarterly check-in and bring up a different give-action before any future ask.

---

## When to Use Each Format

| Situation | Template to Use |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Asking a connector to introduce you to a specific named prospect | Part 1 + Part 2 (combined in one email) |
| Connector agreed but has not made the intro after 7 days | Part 3 |
| Asking for a direct referral (no specific prospect named) | See `ask.md` Touch 1 |
| Following up on a promised but unmade direct referral | See `ask.md` - "Follow-up After a Referral Is Promised" |
