# Referral Ask Templates

> **Playbook:** G4 - Referral Activation
> **Use case:** Asking an existing client or warm network contact to refer or introduce you to a new prospect
> **Sequence:** 3 touches over 14 days - email, LinkedIn DM, follow-up email

---

## Touch 1 - Email (Day 0)

**When to send:** Immediately following a green light moment - a compliment, a milestone celebration, a positive NPS/CSAT response, or a renewal. Do not schedule this on a fixed date. Send it when the moment is live.

**Subject:** Quick question, [First Name]

---

Hey [First Name],

I wanted to say - the [specific outcome: e.g., "reduction in proposal turnaround time" / "pipeline we built in Q1" / "new system you now have running"] we got done for [Company Name] last [time period] was one of my favorite outcomes this quarter.

I'm actively looking to work with 2-3 more [ICP description: e.g., "ops-heavy B2B service firms under 50 people" / "software dev shops doing around $1M-$3M" / "advisory firms that are referral-dependent and want to change that"] over the next 60 days.

If anyone in your network comes to mind - even just a name - I'd love an intro.

Happy to draft the note for you if that makes it easier. Thanks.

[Your Name]

---

**Notes:**
- Replace [ICP description] with the precise language from your current targeting - industry, size, and the problem they are dealing with
- Keep it short - this is a single ask, not a sales email
- The offer to draft the note is important - it reduces the referrer's work to a 30-second edit and send
- Do not include your website, deck, or a long signature - it reads as promotional and softens the relational tone

---

## Touch 2 - LinkedIn DM (Day 4-5)

**When to send:** Only if no reply to the email. Do not send on weekends. If the client viewed your LinkedIn profile between Touch 1 and Touch 2, open with a brief acknowledgment.

---

Hey [First Name] - shot you an email earlier this week about a referral. No worries if timing is off.

If you do happen to think of someone in [ICP description: e.g., "B2B professional services under 50 people"] dealing with [specific problem: e.g., "growth that is stuck because referrals are inconsistent"], I would really value the connection.

Here is a one-liner you could forward if it is useful:

"[First Name] works with [ICP type] to [specific outcome - e.g., build a repeatable referral system that adds 3-5 qualified leads per month without a sales team]. Here is their calendar if you want to talk: [Calendly link]"

Either way - hope things are going well on [their project or initiative].

[Your Name]

---

**Notes:**
- Keep the forwardable one-liner in quotes so the referrer understands it is ready to copy-paste
- The Calendly link removes the scheduling step entirely - the prospect can book directly
- End with a personal note about something relevant to them - this is not a blast, it is a conversation
- If they have viewed your LinkedIn profile, open with: "Saw you came across my profile recently - good timing on my end actually..."

---

## Touch 3 - Follow-up Email (Day 9-10)

**When to send:** Only if no reply to either Touch 1 or Touch 2. This is the final touch in the ask sequence. Make it easy to ignore and easy to pass on. Always end with a relationship anchor.

**Subject:** Last one on this - and something you might find useful

---

Hey [First Name],

Last touch on this one - no action needed if it is not a fit or the timing is off.

Wanted to share this quickly in case it gives better context for who might benefit from what we do:

[3-4 sentence case study or recent result: e.g., "We worked with a 12-person advisory firm in California that was generating $800K from referrals but had no system behind it. In 90 days they added a structured referral activation process, a partner track, and CRM attribution for the first time. They sourced four new clients in the quarter without any outbound."]

If that sounds like someone in your world, I would genuinely appreciate the intro. If not - completely fine.

Either way, let's find time to catch up soon. I would like to hear what you are working on in [Q2 / the next quarter / current project]. I will send you a note next week just to connect.

[Your Name]

---

**Notes:**
- The case study creates a concrete image of who you help - it is more useful than restating the ICP abstractly
- "No action needed if it is not a fit" actively removes pressure and preserves the relationship
- The catch-up offer is genuine - after this sequence, drop the referral ask and shift to relationship maintenance
- After three touches with no response, move this contact to a quarterly value-first check-in cadence. Do not make another referral ask until you have given them something of value first.

---

## Follow-up After a Referral Is Promised but Not Made (Day 7 after promise)

**When to send:** When a client or contact said they would make an introduction or send a name, but you have not heard anything after 7 days.

**Subject:** Following up on the intro you mentioned

---

Hey [First Name],

Just following up on the intro you mentioned when we last spoke. No rush at all - wanted to make sure it did not fall through the cracks on your end given how busy things get.

If it would help, here is a short note you can paste and send in under 2 minutes:

---

"Hey [Prospect First Name] - I've been working with [Your Name] on [specific problem]. The results have been solid and I think you two should talk. Looping them in: [Your Name], meet [Prospect Name]."

---

If the timing is not right or it turns out not to be the right fit, no worries at all - just let me know and I will follow up with them separately another time.

Thanks again.

[Your Name]

---

**Notes:**
- This message is short and frictionless by design - the referrer already agreed to make the intro
- The pre-written note removes the last remaining barrier (composing the message)
- Give them a clean exit ("no worries at all") - this keeps the relationship intact regardless of outcome
