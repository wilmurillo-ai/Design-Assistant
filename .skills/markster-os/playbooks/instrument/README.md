# O3: Instrument

**Pillar:** Operations
**North Star:** Forecast accuracy and time-to-insight

---

## What It Covers

KPI definition, dashboard design, analytics setup, attribution tracking, and revenue forecasting.

O3 makes the right numbers visible to the right people on the right cadence. You cannot improve what you cannot see. You cannot forecast what you do not track. O3 is the operating system for decision-making.

---

## Key Questions It Answers

- What are the three North Star metrics that tell us if the business is healthy?
- What should each pillar owner review every week?
- How do we attribute revenue to specific channels and activities?
- What does a 30-day revenue forecast look like, and how accurate is it?
- When should a metric trigger a decision or an investigation?

---

## Metrics About Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Dashboard freshness | Real-time to daily |
| Forecast accuracy (30 days) | +/-15% |
| Time to insight (finding the answer to a business question) | Under 5 minutes |
| Metric coverage (% of key processes with tracking) | 90%+ |

---

## Dashboard Structure

**Executive Dashboard** (reviewed weekly by founder):
- MRR / ARR trend
- Pipeline value and coverage ratio
- Active client count and NRR
- Cash position and runway

**Growth Dashboard** (reviewed weekly by Growth Owner):
- Qualified prospects identified (G1)
- Engagement rate and nurture conversions (G2)
- Meetings booked, show rate, qualified rate (G3)

**Operations Dashboard** (reviewed weekly by Operations Owner):
- Process documentation coverage (O1)
- Hours saved per week (O2)
- Forecast accuracy, metric coverage (O3)

**Delivery Dashboard** (reviewed weekly by Delivery Owner):
- On-time delivery rate, NPS (D1)
- Case studies produced, proof assets available (D2)
- NRR, health score distribution, expansion rate (D3)

| Level | Examples | Review Cadence |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| North Star | MRR, pipeline value | Weekly |
| Pillar | Meetings booked/week, NPS, NRR | Weekly |
| Brick | Response rate, show rate, hours saved | Weekly |
| Diagnostic | Open rate, bounce rate | When investigating |

## Common Failures

**Tracking too many metrics:** Building a 40-metric dashboard that no one reviews because it takes 30 minutes to process. Three to five North Star metrics reviewed weekly is more useful than 40 metrics reviewed monthly.

**Metrics without owners:** A metric that has no named owner who is accountable for it will not improve. Every metric needs a brick owner who reviews it weekly and acts on it when it falls below threshold.

**Vanity metrics:** Tracking social media followers, website pageviews, or email subscribers when these are not tied to revenue or meeting bookings. Track what drives decisions, not what looks impressive.

**No forecast:** Running the business on trailing metrics only. A 30-day revenue forecast, even a rough one, fundamentally changes how decisions are made. You can see cash problems before they are crises.

---

## Instrument Done Right

O3 is working when: Every pillar owner can answer "how is my pillar performing this week?" in under five minutes by looking at their dashboard. Revenue is forecasted 30 days out with plus-or-minus 15% accuracy. Falling metrics trigger investigation and action within the week they are detected.
