# Prospect Brief Playbook

Generate a phone-readable cheat sheet before any meeting, call, or intentional conversation. Know who you are talking to, what they care about, and what to say -- in 60 seconds of reading.

---

## Prerequisites

- [ ] ICP criteria documented (to score fit)
- [ ] Objection handling documented (to pre-load responses)
- [ ] Core message documented (to select which pitch angle to lead with)

---

## What this produces

- A phone-readable brief covering: person, company, ICP fit, situation, conversation plan, expected objections
- Pre-meeting context logged to CRM
- A recommended opening line and pitch variation for this specific person

---

## Steps

### Step 1: Research the Person (5-10 minutes)

- Search by name, email, or LinkedIn URL in your contact database
- Check CRM for existing contact, deals, notes, and prior activity
- LinkedIn: title, tenure, recent posts, connections
- Web search: recent news, podcast appearances, blog posts, social activity

What you are looking for:
- What is their actual role and decision-making authority?
- What have they written or said publicly? (Reveals priorities and vocabulary)
- What is their context for this conversation? (Did they reply to outreach, come from an event, get a referral?)

---

### Step 2: Research the Company (5-10 minutes)

- Check your contact database: company record, employee count, domain
- CRM: company record, associated contacts, deals, notes
- Website: what do they sell, who do they sell to, what does their current presence look like?
- Recent news: funding, hires, product launches, any trigger event
- Industry: which segment do they belong to? What is typical for that category?

---

### Step 3: ICP Fit Assessment

Score the fit against your ICP criteria:
- Primary ICP: strong match on revenue range, employee count, industry, and pain indicators
- Secondary ICP: adjacent fit, lower priority
- Not a fit: disqualifying factors present

Note the specific fit signals or disqualification flags. If the company is too large or too small, flag it explicitly rather than carrying it as an unexamined deal.

---

### Step 4: Conversation Strategy

**Their likely pain:**
Map what you know about their company situation to the pain points your ICP typically has. Do not assume -- surface clues from research and prepare to validate on the call.

**Objections to expect:**
Review your objection handling documentation. Predict the top 2-3 objections this person is likely to raise based on their company size, industry, and situation. Pre-load responses.

**Which pitch angle to lead with:**
Select the variation of your core message that best fits their situation. Not a generic intro -- a variation framed around the specific problem they most likely have.

**Which proof point to use:**
Select the most relevant case study or result. Match industry if possible. If no direct match, select the closest analog and know the bridge: "Same structural problem -- [describe it]."

**Recommended tier:**
Based on their company size and situation, which engagement level makes the most sense? Flag if they may be too small to afford the minimum, or too large to fit your sweet spot.

---

### Step 5: Generate the Brief

Output format (designed for phone screen):

```
PROSPECT BRIEF
[Name] -- [Title]
[Company] -- [Industry] -- [Size] -- [Location]
ICP Fit: [Primary / Secondary / Not a fit] -- [one-line reason]

THEIR SITUATION
- [Key fact 1]
- [Key fact 2]
- [Pain signal or opportunity]

CONVERSATION PLAN
- Lead with: [specific opening based on their situation]
- Pitch angle: [which variation of your core message to use]
- Suggested engagement level: [appropriate tier based on company size]
- Proof point: [which result or case study to reference]

EXPECT THESE OBJECTIONS
1. "[Likely objection]" - [one-line response]
2. "[Likely objection]" - [one-line response]

ONE THING TO REMEMBER
[The single most important thing about this person or company]
```

---

### Step 6: Log to CRM

Before the meeting, add context to the contact record:
- ICP fit assessment
- Key research findings
- Conversation plan
- Expected objections

After the meeting, run the debrief playbook.

## Benchmarks

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Time to prepare a brief | Under 20 minutes per person |
| Brief quality check | Could you read it in 60 seconds on your phone and know what to say? |
| CRM update before meeting | 100% of meetings with ICP-fit prospects |

---

## Common Mistakes

**Going into meetings without preparation.** Researching while talking to someone signals that you did not think they were worth 15 minutes of prep. It also produces worse conversations.

**Researching but not preparing the conversation plan.** Research without a prepared opening line, pitch angle, and expected objections is information without a strategy.

**Not checking CRM for existing history.** If this person has been in your pipeline before, a prior conversation exists. Showing up without that context is a credibility loss.

**Over-researching low-priority prospects.** A 30-minute deep dive is appropriate for a high-value opportunity. A 10-minute scan is sufficient for an exploratory call with an unknown prospect.

**Selecting a generic proof point.** The most relevant proof point is one that mirrors their specific situation. If you have multiple case studies, pick the one that maps to their industry and company type. If none match directly, know the bridge.
