# Debrief Playbook

Turn every conversation (meeting, call, event encounter) into a qualified pipeline entry with follow-up in under 2 minutes of input. Nothing falls through the cracks.

---

## Prerequisites

- [ ] CRM set up with contact and deal tracking
- [ ] ICP criteria documented (to qualify fit)
- [ ] Follow-up email templates ready

---

## What this produces

- A CRM contact, company, and deal record for every qualifying conversation
- An ICP fit assessment with tier recommendation
- A follow-up email draft personalized to the specific conversation
- A LinkedIn connection message variant
- A conversation summary logged to the deal record

---

## Steps

### Step 1: Capture Input (within 2 hours of the conversation)

The minimum viable input is: "Met [name] at [place], they run [type of company]."

Preferred input:
- Who you met: name, company, title
- Where: event, referral, inbound reply, outbound reply, intro call
- What was discussed: their situation in 1-3 sentences
- What they said about their challenges
- What next step was agreed to, if any

The longer you wait, the more detail fades. Do this within 2 hours of the conversation.

---

### Step 2: Research and Enrich (5-10 minutes)

- Search your contact database by name and company
- Check CRM for existing contact, company, or deal records
- If not found: web search for company info and LinkedIn profile
- Compile: name, title, company, size, revenue estimate, industry, location

If there is an existing deal in CRM: update it, do not create a duplicate.

---

### Step 3: Qualify Against ICP

Score fit against your ICP criteria:
- Primary ICP: strong match
- Secondary ICP: adjacent, worth pursuing
- Not a fit: disqualifying factors present (flag but still log the contact)

Check for disqualification criteria (company too small, wrong industry, wrong decision-maker level).

For technology or MSP firms: flag as a priority segment if applicable.

Estimate which engagement level makes sense based on company size and what was discussed.

---

### Step 4: Assess the Conversation

Document these specifics while they are fresh:

- What pain did they express? Map it to your known pain point categories.
- What objections are likely? Note any resistance or hesitation you detected.
- What is their current setup? (Working with an agency, doing it themselves, nothing in place)
- Buying signals: did they mention a timeline, budget, urgency, or prior attempt to solve this?
- Red flags: price sensitivity, not the actual decision-maker, vague interest with no urgency

---

### Step 5: Create or Update CRM Records

**Contact record:** name, email, company, title, source (event / meeting / referral / outbound reply)

**Company record:** name, domain, industry, size estimate, revenue estimate

**Deal record:**
- Stage: Qualification (for new conversations) or the appropriate stage based on where things stand
- Deal value: estimated based on company size and likely engagement level
- Source: how they came into your pipeline
- Notes: full conversation summary including pain, situation, buying signals, and red flags

Link contact to company, company to deal.

---

### Step 6: Draft Follow-Up

Write the follow-up while the conversation is still fresh. Rules:

- Reference the specific thing discussed: a problem they mentioned, a comment about their situation, the context of where you met
- Tone: warm, not transactional. One business owner talking to another.
- Do not pitch in the first follow-up after a genuine conversation. Lead with the conversation.
- Include one clear next step: a call, a specific thing you will send, or a question that advances the relationship.
- Generate a LinkedIn connection request variant (shorter, more casual) for the same conversation.

**Follow-up structure:**

Opening: reference the specific conversation ("It was good talking through [topic] at [place/context]")
Middle: one relevant observation or value add based on what they shared
Close: one specific next step

---

### Step 7: Log

After sending the follow-up:
- Update the deal record with the follow-up sent date
- Set a next follow-up reminder based on the agreed next step or a default cadence:
  - If they agreed to a call: follow up in 3-5 days if no response
  - If they said "not now": follow up in 4-6 weeks with a relevant trigger
  - If unclear: follow up in 7 days with a short check-in

---

## Benchmarks

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Time from conversation to CRM record | Under 24 hours |
| Follow-up sent within 48 hours | 100% of qualifying conversations |
| Deals opened per event (10+ conversations) | 2-4 per event |
| Conversations that produce a next step | 30%+ of substantive conversations |

---

## Common Mistakes

**Waiting too long.** After 48 hours, context fades. After a week, you are sending a cold follow-up to someone who barely remembers the conversation. The window is short.

**Creating duplicate records.** Always check CRM before creating a new contact. Duplicate records split the history and create confusion.

**Generic follow-ups.** "Great to meet you" with no reference to the specific conversation is forgettable. One specific detail from what they said is worth three paragraphs of generic follow-up.

**Not logging red flags.** If you sensed price sensitivity, a budget issue, or a political obstacle, log it. This context is critical when the deal comes up in pipeline review 3 weeks later.

**No next step defined.** A conversation without a defined next step is not a deal -- it is a contact you met. Always define what happens next, even if it is just "I'll send them a relevant article in 2 weeks."

**Treating all post-event contacts as cold outbound.** Someone you met in person already knows you. The follow-up cadence, tone, and patience should be different from cold outbound. Do not put event contacts into your cold sequence.
