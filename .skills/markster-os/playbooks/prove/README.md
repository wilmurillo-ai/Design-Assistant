# D2: Prove

**Pillar:** Delivery
**North Star:** Case studies produced per quarter

---

## What It Covers

Results documentation, ROI calculation, case study creation, testimonial collection, and proof asset management.

D2 converts delivery results into reusable proof assets. Without D2, excellent work stays invisible. Proof fuels the Warm brick (G2) and shortens sales cycles in the Book brick (G3). The Proof Loop: Deliver well -> document the result -> use it in outreach -> book better clients -> deliver well again.

---

## Key Questions It Answers

- What results are we producing for clients that are specific and verifiable?
- At what point in the engagement do we collect proof?
- How do we document results in a format that makes cold outreach more effective?
- What types of proof assets do we need for each stage of the buyer journey?
- How do we get clients to give us specific, quotable testimonials?

## Proof Collection Timeline

**Day 30:** Quick win snapshot. Ask: "What is one result or change you've noticed in the first month?" Even a small win documented early prevents "I forgot what we achieved" syndrome.

**Day 60:** Quick testimonial. Two questions: "What problem were you trying to solve when we started?" and "What is different now?" Record the answers in their words.

**Day 90:** Case study interview. 20-minute call using the Case Study Template.

---

## Case Study Template

```
Overview: [company type, situation, outcome in one sentence]

Situation (Before):
- What problem were they trying to solve?
- What had they tried before?
- What was the cost of the problem continuing?

Solution:
- What did you do? (steps, not features)
- How long did it take?
- What was your role vs. their role?

Results (After):
- Specific metrics: [number, timeframe, comparison to before]
- Secondary results: [what else changed as a result]

Quote:
"[their words, not yours, describing the result]"
- [Name, Title, Company]

Key Takeaway:
[The one insight a similar prospect should take from this story]
```

## Common Failures

**Waiting for a perfect case study:** Waiting for a dramatic result before documenting anything. Most excellent outcomes are incremental. Document the incremental results. A "we saved 12 hours per week" result is more credible and more useful than a vague "we transformed their operations."

**Generic testimonials:** Asking "can I get a testimonial?" and receiving "Great team, highly recommend." This is useless in outreach. Ask specific questions that produce specific answers: "What result have you seen since we started working together?" and "What would you tell a colleague who is considering working with us?"

**Not using proof in outreach:** Collecting case studies and letting them sit in a folder. Proof assets must be actively referenced in G2 content and G3 outreach to work.

---

## Prove Done Right

D2 is working when: Two to four case studies are produced per quarter. New case studies are referenced in outreach within two weeks of creation. Proposals include at least one specific result from a similar client. The team can answer "what results have you produced?" with a specific, verifiable example in under 30 seconds.
