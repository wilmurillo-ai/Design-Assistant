# Case Study Template

A case study is not a testimonial. It is a structured story with a before state, a mechanism, a result, and a timeframe. All four must be present. A case study missing any of these cannot be used in outreach or proposals because it cannot be verified.

**The rule:** Every case study must pass this test: "A stranger reading this knows exactly who the client was (by type), what problem they had, what changed, and what the result was -- with a number and a timeframe."

---

## Case Study Template

**Client:** [Company type + size. Example: "12-person IT consultancy in Chicago." Do not use client name unless you have written permission.]

**Industry:** [Vertical]

**Situation:**
[2-3 sentences. What was their state before working with you? What was the specific problem? Use their words where possible.]

**What we did:**
[2-3 sentences. The mechanism -- not a list of deliverables, but the approach that created the outcome. One paragraph maximum. Enough to create credibility, not enough to DIY.]

**Result:**
[The outcome. Specific number + timeframe. Example: "Booked 23 qualified discovery calls in 60 days." "Reduced client acquisition cost from $4,200 to $1,800 in 90 days." "Grew MRR from $18K to $41K in 4 months."]

**Timeframe:** [From engagement start to result. Example: "90 days."]

**Client quote (if available):**
> "[Direct quote from client. First name or role + company type only if not using full name.]"

## Worked Example

**Client:** 8-person accounting firm in Atlanta

**Industry:** CPA / Professional Services

**Situation:**
The firm had been in business for 11 years and relied entirely on referrals for new clients. Revenue was flat for the third consecutive year. The owner knew he needed to build a more predictable pipeline but had no outbound infrastructure and no marketing resources.

**What we did:**
We defined a tight ICP (owner-operated businesses with 5-20 employees in the Atlanta metro), built a verified prospect list of 400 contacts, and ran a 3-touch cold email sequence focused on tax planning and cash flow. Replies were routed directly to the owner for follow-up.

**Result:**
11 qualified discovery calls booked in the first 45 days. 3 converted to new clients. $78K in new annual recurring revenue.

**Timeframe:** 45 days to first results, 90 days to close.

**Client quote:**
> "I was skeptical that cold email would work for an accounting firm. The first call I booked became our largest retainer client." -- Firm owner, Atlanta

## Quality Gates

Before using a case study in outreach, proposals, or the website:

- [ ] Client type named specifically (not "a B2B company")
- [ ] Problem stated in specific, concrete terms (not "they wanted to grow")
- [ ] Result includes a number (not "significantly improved")
- [ ] Timeframe included (not "within a short time")
- [ ] Mechanism described (not just "we helped them")
- [ ] Client quote obtained or documented as unavailable
- [ ] Client has approved the case study for use (written confirmation or email)

## Variations by Use Case

**Cold email proof point (one sentence):**
> "An 8-person accounting firm in Atlanta added $78K in new annual revenue in 90 days."

**Proposal proof section (two sentences):**
> "One of our clients, an 8-person accounting firm in Atlanta, was fully referral-dependent with flat revenue for three years. After 45 days running our outbound system, they had 11 qualified discovery calls and closed $78K in new annual retainers within 90 days."

**Website / full case study:**
Use the full template above. Add screenshots, metrics charts, or additional quotes if available.

## How to Collect Case Studies

The best time to collect a case study is immediately after the client hits a milestone -- while the result is fresh and the relationship is warm.

**Sequence:**
1. When a client hits a result: send a short congratulations message referencing the specific number
2. Ask: "Would you be willing to let me write up a short case study? I'll draft it and send it to you for approval -- you just confirm the numbers are right."
3. Write the case study yourself from your notes. Do not ask the client to write it.
4. Send for approval: "Here's the draft -- does this accurately capture what happened? Let me know if anything needs adjusting."
5. Get written confirmation (email reply is sufficient)
6. File in `playbooks/prove/` and add to your proof asset library

**If the client declines:** Ask if you can use an anonymized version (company type only, no name). Most clients who decline attribution will approve anonymized use.
