# Funnel Market Landscape

What has changed since 2015 and what it means for how you build funnels today.

---

## The Single Biggest Change: Proof Requirements

In 2015: screenshots and countdown timers were sufficient.
Now: 55% of B2B buyers report not knowing which information sources to trust.

The trust mechanism has shifted from urgency tactics to creator-validated, outcome-documented proof.

What this means in practice:
- Social proof must include company type + specific number + specific timeframe. Vague testimonials do not convert.
- Countdown timers that reset destroy credibility permanently.
- AI-generated copy is identifiable to buyers. Use it to structure and draft, not to fake authenticity.
- Real scarcity converts. Manufactured scarcity does not.

---

## The Linear Funnel is Dead (Deiss, 2024)

Ryan Deiss called the linear funnel dead in 2024. His replacement model: the "popcorn popper."

- Old model: lead magnet -> tripwire -> core offer -> profit maximizer -> return path (in sequence)
- New model: apply heat uniformly across all touchpoints. Capture buyers when they are ready, not when your sequence tells them to be.

What broke the linear model:
- Apple Mail Privacy Protection (MPP) destroyed open-rate-based segmentation
- ATT targeting degradation raised cold CPMs, making the slow value ladder uneconomical on paid traffic
- Buyer trust timelines lengthened - B2B buyers consume more content before making contact

Practical implication: email sequences alone are not enough. Build multi-channel touchpoints (email + retargeting + content + community) and let buyers convert when they are ready.

## VSL Position Shift

VSL as the first cold-traffic touch is declining in sophisticated B2B stacks.

- 2015 model: cold ad -> VSL -> order form
- Current model: cold content (YouTube, LinkedIn) -> behavioral signal -> VSL -> order form or application

The VSL has moved mid-funnel. Traffic that has already consumed trust content converts on VSLs at 3-4x the rate of pure cold traffic.

For affiliate funnels: the VSL-first model still works because the affiliate's list is warm to the affiliate. Borrowed authority from the affiliate substitutes for the trust-content layer.

---

## AI Offer Positioning

AI-powered tools and services in affiliate funnels convert comparably to traditional info products when:
- The mechanism is specific and demonstrable
- Proof is documented with real numbers
- The outcome is concrete ("reduces response time by 40%" not "uses AI to improve operations")

Generic "AI agent" or "AI tool" positioning converts poorly. The AI label alone is not a mechanism.

New pattern (KB research finding): AI diagnostic as lead magnet. A specific AI-powered audit or triage tool that surfaces a concrete problem qualifies buyers better than static PDF lead magnets. Brunson's confirmation-page affiliate activation mechanic is now being paired with AI diagnostics.

## What Has Changed by Dimension

| Dimension | 2015 | Now (2026) |
|-----------|------|------|
| Trust mechanism | Urgency, scarcity, countdown timers | Creator validation, outcome documentation |
| Front-end economics | Margin at $47 | 100% commission loss-leader, OTO stack captures margin |
| Proof standard | Screenshots, testimonials | Company type + number + timeframe |
| Platform (B2B) | JVZoo, ClickBank | impact.com, PartnerStack, direct JV deals |
| Close mechanic | Hard close, manufactured urgency | Diagnostic + silence + identity reinforcement |
| Content pre-launch | Email sequence | Video series + challenge + multi-channel |
| VSL position | First cold-traffic touch | Mid-funnel after behavioral qualification |
| Lead magnet format | PDF guide | Specific diagnostic or interactive tool |
| Email trigger | Open rate | Click behavior, page behavior, time-on-site |

---

## What Has NOT Changed

- The close still drives 40-60% of launch revenue. Cart close is still the highest-revenue 24-hour window.
- The PLF (Product Launch Formula) structure is still the dominant JV launch mechanic. AI and challenges are layers on top, not replacements.
- Recurring income outperforms one-time on affiliate retention. Always has.
- EPC is still the master metric affiliates use to evaluate whether to promote.
- High-ticket ($5K+) still requires a human call. Automation ceiling has not moved significantly.

---

## The Quarterly Challenge Wrapper (KB research finding)

Hormozi's pattern from the knowledge base: same core playbook, new brand framing, run four times per year. Each quarterly run is treated as a fresh launch with a challenge-format warm-up. This keeps the list engaged without requiring new product development.
