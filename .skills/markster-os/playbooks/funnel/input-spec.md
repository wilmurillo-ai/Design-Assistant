# Input Specification

What you must collect before writing any funnel asset.

Without these inputs, AI generates generic copy, invented statistics, and fabricated social proof. Every LLM fills input gaps with training data averages. Missing context produces hallucinated claims.

---

## Layer 1 - Offer Information

Must have before writing any asset.

| Input | What it is | Consequence of omitting |
|-------|-----------|------------------------|
| Mechanism name | The proprietary name for your process | AI writes "our approach" and "our system" |
| ICP definition | Company type, headcount, buying trigger, decision-maker title | AI targets "businesses" not a specific person |
| Offer stack | Front-end, OTO prices, continuity price | AI cannot write OTO copy or stack value |
| Outcome statement | What the client achieves, in their words, with a number | AI writes feature lists instead of outcomes |
| Proof point | One real result: company type + number + timeframe | AI invents social proof or omits it entirely |
| Guarantee | Exact terms (results-based, money-back, done-for-you) | AI writes vague "satisfaction guaranteed" |
| Brand voice | Tone, sentence style, phrases to avoid | AI defaults to generic marketing voice |

---

## Layer 2 - Buyer Psychology

Must have before writing persuasion copy.

| Input | What it is | Where to get it |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| Verbatim pain language | Exact words buyers use - not your paraphrase of their pain | Sales calls, reply emails, discovery conversations |
| Awareness level | Unaware / problem-aware / solution-aware / product-aware | Determines where the VSL or copy starts |
| Dominant emotion | What the buyer feels most: fear, frustration, hope, skepticism | Determines hook type and tone |
| Failed alternatives | What they tried before that did not work | Becomes the Vehicle Belief to demolish |
| Objection sequence | Top 3-4 objections in the order they arise on sales calls | Becomes the Three Secrets structure |

---

## Layer 3 - Structural Decisions

Must be locked before writing starts.

| Decision | Options | Impact |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Traffic source | Cold paid, warm retargeting, warm email, affiliate, organic | Determines VSL length and trust-building required |
| Funnel type | VSL, webinar, application, tripwire, challenge, lead magnet | Determines what assets to write |
| Conversion goal per page | Opt-in rate, purchase rate, application rate | Determines page length and CTA |
| Price tier | Under $1K / $1K-$5K / $5K+ | Determines whether to route to a call |
| Copywriting framework | Three Secrets / CVO / State-Story-Solution | Determines script structure |
| Compliance constraints | Industry regulations, claim restrictions | Determines what you can and cannot say |

---

## Layer 4 - Proof Requirements

Every claim needs a supporting evidence item. If the evidence does not exist, the claim does not get written.

| Claim type | Required evidence |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Outcome claim ("clients achieve X") | One documented case: company type + number + timeframe |
| Speed claim ("results in X days") | One case with a start date and end date |
| Volume claim ("worked with X clients") | Verifiable count |
| Mechanism claim ("our process does X") | Explainable in one sentence without jargon |
| Comparison claim ("better than X") | Named alternative with a named, documented weakness |

---

## Layer 5 - Output Quality Map

What AI produces without inputs vs with full spec.

| Output | Without inputs | With full spec |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| Hook variations | Low - generic patterns | High |
| Problem agitation | Medium - plausible but not specific | High |
| Mechanism explanation | Low - hallucinates details | High |
| Three Secrets / belief demolition | Low - invents objections | High |
| Proof section | Very low - fabricates results | High |
| Stack copy | Low - no prices or components | High |
| OTO copy | Low - no product context | High |
| Guarantee copy | Medium - vague | High |
| CTAs and reminder emails | High - structural, not content-dependent | High |
| FAQ structure | Medium | High |
