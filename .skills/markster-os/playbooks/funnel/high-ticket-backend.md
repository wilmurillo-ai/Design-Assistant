# High-Ticket Backend

How to design, sell, and fulfill offers above $3,000.

---

## The Core Principle

The front-end funnel qualifies buyers for the backend without explicitly selling it. Backend desire is manufactured in post-purchase email through story and case study - not by pitching the backend explicitly.

The tripwire or SLO has three jobs:
1. Imprint your methodology on the buyer
2. Surface the next problem (which the backend solves)
3. Create a segment of buyers who have demonstrated financial willingness

---

## Application Funnel vs. Direct Sales Page

For anything above $3,000, the application funnel wins.

It inverts the power dynamic: the prospect asks permission to work with you, not evaluating whether to buy from you.

| Mechanic | Show rate | Close rate |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Application funnel | 75-85% | 35-45% |
| Direct sales page | N/A | 3-8% total |

That is a 5-10x revenue difference from the same traffic volume.

The application funnel flow:
Traffic -> qualifying page or VSL -> application form -> confirmation -> call booking -> call -> close

The VSL or qualifying page does the pre-selling. The application form filters unqualified buyers. The call closes.

---

## Kennedy's Shock and Awe Package

A physical package sent after the application is submitted, before the call. Contents: FedEx envelope, pocket folder, printed case studies, book or guide, guarantee statement, handwritten note.

Economics at $3K-$25K offer sizes: $100 in fulfillment cost to arrive pre-sold is a trivial line item. The prospect has already been through your funnel, submitted an application, and received a physical package. By the time the call happens, the close is mostly done.

## The Three Close Scripts

| Script | Approach | Best when |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Hormozi - CLOSER | Systematic, diagnostic, gap-focused, silence after the close | Scaling to a team of closers |
| Kennedy | Authority-prescriptive, pre-call positioning does most of the selling | Strong brand positioning, warm audience |
| Kern | Fully conversational, transparency-based, diagnostic questions only | Founder-selling with relationship trust |

**CLOSER breakdown (Hormozi):**
- C - Clarify why they are on the call
- L - Label their problem (reflect it back in their words)
- O - Overview their past attempts (surfaces the failed vehicles)
- S - Sell the vacation not the plane (outcome, not features)
- E - Explain away concerns (handle objections)
- R - Reinforce the decision (post-close identity reinforcement)

**Silence after the close:** Waiting 8+ seconds after asking for the sale increases close rate approximately 30-32%. The average rep breaks at 2.7 seconds. This is trainable and produces a measurable output.

## Scalable Fulfillment

The only model that does not scale headcount linearly with revenue - three pillars:

1. **Pre-built curriculum** - async delivery, recorded, no live time required per client
2. **Group Q&A calls** - not 1:1. One call serves 10-50 clients simultaneously.
3. **Community container** - peer accountability removes the dependency on you for motivation

The named proprietary system is non-negotiable. It separates a productized backend from a consulting retainer. Without a named mechanism, you are selling time and expertise. With it, you are selling a system.

## Guarantee Stack (Hormozi Framework)

Guarantees increase conversion without increasing refund rate when structured correctly.

| Guarantee type | When to use | Refund risk |
|----------------|------------|-------------|
| Results guarantee | You control the inputs | Low if inputs are specific |
| Money-back | Unconditional | Higher - use only if product is strong |
| Done-for-you | You do the work regardless | Lowest - removes all risk from buyer |
| Win-back | Client earns back fee by completing actions | Very low - completers almost never take the refund |

**Win-back structure:** The client earns their money back by completing prescribed actions within the program. People who earn it back almost never take it - they apply it to the upsell instead. Only use if current refund rate is below 5%.

The guarantee belongs at the end of the close sequence, immediately before the CTA. Placing it earlier signals defensiveness.

## Retention and Ascension

First 30 days determine 12-month retention. Onboarding is the product, not the curriculum.

| Mechanic | When | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| Onboarding win | Days 1-14 | Client must experience one concrete result early |
| Progress review | Days 60-90 | Frame as celebration, not renewal pitch |
| Renewal conversation | Days 90-120 | Structured as "what's next" not "are you staying" |
| Alumni tier | After completion | Most underused retention mechanic |
| Referral program | After first win | Second most underused mechanic |
