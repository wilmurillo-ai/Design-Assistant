# Funnel Types

Pick the right funnel type before writing a single word of copy.

Traffic temperature must match the funnel type. This is the most commonly mismatched decision in funnel building. Cold traffic requires a trust-manufacturing mechanism before it will convert. Changing the page sequence without fixing the temperature mismatch will not move the number.

---

## The $5K Cliff

Below $5,000: automated funnels can close without a human call.
At $5,000+: the math almost always favors routing to a call.

| Close method | Offer price | Conversion rate |
|-------------|------------|----------------|
| Qualified human close | $10K offer | 35-45% |
| Pure automation | $10K offer | 15-25% |

The revenue difference from the same traffic volume is 2-3x. Use calls at $5K+. Use automation below it.

## The Ten Funnel Types

### 1. VSL Funnel

**Flow:** Opt-in page > VSL page > order form > OTO1 > OTO2 > thank you

**Traffic:** Cold and warm

**Price range:** $47-$2,997

**Primary mechanism:** Authority establishment + belief demolition

**Minimum trust level:** Built by the VSL itself. Requires 15-25 minute runtime to manufacture enough trust to close cold traffic without a call.

**Affiliate-ready:** Yes. Standalone flow with EPC measurable per link. Most commonly used format in affiliate and JV launches.

**When to use:**
- You have a named mechanism and can explain it on video
- Offer is below $3K
- You want affiliate distribution
- Traffic is cold and you cannot rely on pre-existing brand awareness

**When not to use:**
- Offer is above $3K (route to application instead)
- Your VSL has not been tested live yet (do not automate an untested VSL)

---

### 2. Webinar Funnel

**Flow:** Registration page > confirmation > reminder sequence > live or automated webinar > pitch > order form > OTOs

**Traffic:** Warm preferred. Cold traffic friction is high.

**Price range:** $297-$5,000

**Primary mechanism:** Education + authority + live close energy

**Minimum trust level:** Medium. Webinar format implies credibility. Registration act pre-qualifies interest.

**When to use:**
- Offer requires substantial education before the pitch
- You have an existing audience (email list, community)
- Product has complexity that benefits from demonstration

**When not to use:**
- Cold B2B outbound. Scheduling friction kills conversion.
- Offer is above $5K (route to application)
- You haven't validated the presentation live yet (automate only after live works)

**Benchmark:** Live webinar close rate 10-15%. Automate only after that is proven.

---

### 3. Application Funnel

**Flow:** Traffic > qualifying page or VSL > application form > confirmation > call booking > call > close

**Traffic:** Warm to hot

**Price range:** $3,000+

**Primary mechanism:** Qualification inversion. The prospect asks permission to work with you. This reverses the power dynamic before the call.

**Minimum trust level:** High. Requires either pre-existing credibility or a VSL that manufactures it before the application.

**Benchmarks:**
- Show rate: 75-85%
- Close rate: 35-45%
- Direct page close rate (same price): 3-8%
- Revenue difference from same traffic: 5-10x

**When to use:**
- Offer is $3K or above
- You want to sell without discounting
- You need to filter out unqualified buyers before they get to a call

**When not to use:**
- Offer is below $2K (friction exceeds the benefit)
- You do not have sales call capacity

---

### 4. Tripwire / SLO Funnel

**Flow:** Opt-in or ad > low-price offer page > order bump > OTO1 > OTO2 > thank you > email backend

**Traffic:** Cold

**Price range:** $7-$97 front end. Backend up to $2,997+.

**Primary mechanism:** Buyer creation. Converting a prospect into a buyer changes their psychological relationship with you. Even at $7.

**Minimum trust level:** Low. The price point removes the trust requirement for the front end.

**Critical rule:** The front end is not a revenue line. It is a customer acquisition mechanism. Evaluate it on cost per customer acquired, not on margin.

**When to use:**
- You have a high-value backend offer
- You want to run paid traffic without needing the funnel to break even on ad spend alone
- You want to build a buyer list faster than a free opt-in list

**Order bump benchmark:** 25-35% take rate when the bump is directly related to the front-end purchase.

---

### 5. Challenge Funnel

**Flow:** Opt-in > 5-7 day challenge content > pitch day > order form

**Traffic:** Cold to warm

**Price range:** $97-$2,000 pitch

**Primary mechanism:** Trust building over time + transformation proof during the challenge itself

**Minimum trust level:** Low at opt-in. Trust is manufactured during the challenge. Completers convert to paid at a significantly higher rate than non-completers.

**Benchmark:** 15-18%+ of challenge completers convert to the paid offer. Highest cold-traffic conversion rate of any funnel format.

**When to use:**
- Your offer involves a skill or transformation that can be demonstrated in 5-7 days
- You have content capacity to run daily sessions
- Offer is under $2K (above that, route completers to an application)

**When not to use:**
- You cannot commit to 5-7 days of live or pre-recorded daily content
- The transformation cannot be demonstrated in a short timeframe

---

### 6. Book Funnel

**Flow:** Ad > book landing page ($0 digital or $7.95 S&H) > order bump > OTO1 > OTO2

**Traffic:** Cold

**Price range:** $0-$7.95 front end. OTO stack $37-$997.

**Primary mechanism:** Authority establishment + buyer creation. A book signals credibility that no opt-in page can replicate.

**Minimum trust level:** Near zero. The book creates the trust.

**Benchmark:** Brunson average order value on book funnel: ~$238. Front end loss-leads into OTO stack.

**When to use:**
- You have or can produce a book or substantial written guide
- You want the highest-authority cold-traffic entry point
- You want to build a buyer list with physical book buyers (highest LTV segment in most markets)

**OTO stack structure:** OTO1 at $37-$97 (digital companion or implementation kit). OTO2 at $197-$997 (group program or done-for-you element).

---

### 7. Lead Magnet Funnel

**Flow:** Ad or organic > opt-in page > delivery > email nurture > offer

**Traffic:** Cold

**Price range:** Lead gen only. Converts into VSL, application, or tripwire as the next step.

**Primary mechanism:** Problem qualification. Attracts buyers who are actively aware of the problem.

**Minimum trust level:** Very low. Free exchange of value for contact info.

**Benchmark:** 40% opt-in rate on content-qualified traffic (DM benchmark). Below 25% usually indicates headline-offer mismatch.

**When to use:**
- You need to build a list before you can sell
- Your main offer requires warming before presenting
- You are using organic traffic (LinkedIn, YouTube) and need a capture mechanism

**When not to use:** As the only funnel. The lead magnet is a top-of-funnel entry point. It must connect to a conversion step within the email nurture sequence.

---

### 8. Product Demo Funnel

**Flow:** Traffic > demo opt-in > recorded or live demo > follow-up sequence > trial or purchase

**Traffic:** Warm

**Price range:** SaaS and productized services

**Primary mechanism:** Show the product solving the exact problem the prospect has described.

**Minimum trust level:** Medium. Demo requestors are already evaluating solutions.

**Benchmark:** Trial to paid conversion: 18.2%. Below 12% indicates either a product gap or a post-trial nurture gap.

**When to use:**
- SaaS or software product where seeing it live removes the primary objection
- Productized service where the deliverable can be shown, not described

---

### 9. Automated Webinar Funnel

**Flow:** Registration > confirmation > automated presentation > pitch > order form

**Traffic:** Cold to warm

**Price range:** $297-$5,000

**Primary mechanism:** Same as live webinar. Automated removes the scheduling constraint and allows unlimited sessions.

**Benchmarks:**
- Live close rate: 10-15%
- Automated close rate: 2-5%
- Automated show rate: 60-70%
- Live show rate: 25-30%

**Critical rule:** Only automate after the live version is dialed in. Running an untested presentation on autopilot locks in weak conversion permanently.

**When to use:**
- Live webinar is proven and converting above 10%
- You want to run the funnel 24/7 without presenting
- You need scale without adding calendar capacity

---

### 10. Affiliate / JV Launch Funnel

**Flow:** JV email > VSL or webinar registration > sequence > cart open > close

**Traffic:** Warm (affiliate list). The affiliate's credibility is transferred to you by the introduction.

**Price range:** $97-$1,997

**Primary mechanism:** Borrowed authority + urgency. The affiliate's endorsement bypasses the trust-building phase.

**Minimum trust level:** Low. Handled by the affiliate relationship.

**Benchmark:** Cart close still drives 40-60% of launch revenue. Do not neglect the close sequence.

**VSL as default format:** VSL dominates affiliate launches because it is standalone (no scheduling) and EPC is measurable per affiliate link. Webinars add complexity that most affiliates will not support.

**When to use:**
- You have affiliate or JV relationships with email lists
- Offer is $97-$1,997 (above that, affiliates need higher commissions and calls get complex)
- You want borrowed distribution without building your own list first

## Decision Matrix

Use this table to narrow down funnel type candidates. Cross the row (price) with the column (traffic temperature) and read the recommendation.

| Offer price | Cold traffic | Warm traffic | Hot / application |
|------------|-------------|-------------|------------------|
| Under $100 | Tripwire, Lead Magnet, Book | Tripwire, SLO | Direct offer page |
| $100-$500 | VSL Funnel, Challenge | Webinar, VSL | Webinar, Direct |
| $500-$2,997 | VSL Funnel | Webinar, Auto-webinar | Application, Webinar |
| $3,000-$5,000 | VSL > Application | Application, Webinar | Application |
| $5,000+ | Content > VSL > Application | Application | Application |

**Trust level override:** If your trust level is below the minimum for your preferred funnel type, add a trust-building step before the funnel entry. Do not skip this -- it is why cold-to-VSL outperforms cold-to-application in most markets.

## B2B Cold Outbound Architecture

For cold B2B outbound into an offer above $3K, the architecture is sequential. Each step qualifies and warms before passing to the next.

```
Lead magnet
  -> qualifies problem awareness (they have the problem and know it)

VSL
  -> manufactures authority, pre-sells the mechanism
  -> fires on behavioral trigger (clicked link, visited page)

Application form
  -> filters unqualified, inverts the power dynamic

Discovery call
  -> closes or routes to a follow-up sequence

Close
```

Do not run cold B2B outbound directly to an application form without a VSL or content warm-up layer. Show rates on cold-to-call are 40-60% lower than warm-to-call.

## VSL Position in 2025

VSL is no longer primarily a top-of-funnel mechanism in sophisticated B2B stacks.

**What has changed:**
- Cold-traffic VSL as the first touch is declining in B2B
- Buyers are more skeptical of video sales letters from unknown sources
- Top-of-funnel is now trust content: YouTube, LinkedIn, podcast appearances
- VSL fires after behavioral qualification signals, not as the cold entry point

**Current B2B stack:**

| Stage | Mechanism | Purpose |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| TOF | LinkedIn content, YouTube, podcast | Build trust before any offer is visible |
| MOF | VSL or webinar (triggered by behavior) | Convert warm interest to application intent |
| BOF | Application + call | Close with high show rate and close rate |

**What this means for you:** If you are running cold B2B outbound, your first touch is a value asset (case study, lead magnet, insight post). The VSL fires mid-funnel after a click or page visit signals intent. Direct-to-VSL from a cold email still works in B2C and in warm affiliate contexts.

## Funnel Type Checklist Before Building

Run this before committing to a funnel type.

- [ ] Traffic temperature identified: cold / warm / hot
- [ ] Offer price confirmed against the $5K cliff rule
- [ ] Trust level assessed: does the funnel type match current brand authority?
- [ ] Affiliate distribution: yes or no (affects format choice)
- [ ] Call capacity: confirmed if routing to application
- [ ] VSL tested live before automating: yes or not applicable

If any box is unchecked: resolve it before building. Funnel type mismatches cannot be fixed with better copy.
