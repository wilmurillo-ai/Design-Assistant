# Advisory: Financial, Legal, Coaching, Fractional

Covers: financial advisory (RIA, wealth management), legal services, executive coaching, business coaching, fractional C-suite (CFO, CMO, CRO, COO), M&A advisory.

What unifies these: you are selling access to your expertise and judgment on a relationship basis, the sale is almost entirely referral-driven, and professional licensing or credentials are table stakes for credibility.

---

## What is different about GTM

Advisory businesses are the most referral-dependent of any service category. The research benchmark: referrals account for 70-80%+ of new clients for most advisory firms.

This is not a weakness -- it is a structural advantage once built. A satisfied client who refers you to two peers, who each refer two more, compounds faster than any paid acquisition channel.

The problem is: referrals require trust, and trust is built through:
1. Exceptional delivery (D1, D2)
2. Visible expertise (G2 Warm -- LinkedIn, speaking, published writing)
3. Existing network depth (who do you already know in the right circles?)

Cold outreach and paid acquisition rarely work for advisory services at Stage 1-2. The exception: financial advisors and M&A advisory can use targeted LinkedIn and direct mail for specific trigger events (business milestone, liquidity event, funding announcement).

| COI (Center of Influence) relationships | The network that generates referrals | Quality over quantity; 10-20 strong COIs > 200 weak connections |

## The COI System (most important acquisition lever)

For advisory businesses, the Centers of Influence system is more valuable than any marketing playbook.

COIs are adjacent professionals who serve the same buyer without competing: CPAs refer to financial advisors; attorneys refer to M&A advisors; investors refer to fractional CFOs; HR leaders refer to executive coaches.

Build your COI list:
1. Who serves my ideal client 6-12 months before they need me?
2. Who serves my ideal client at the same time as me?
3. Who do my existing clients trust most?

Maintain COI relationships with quarterly value-exchanges: share relevant insights, make introductions, invite to events. This is not networking -- it is systematic referral infrastructure.

## Biggest failure modes

1. **Not systematizing referrals.** Most advisory firms get referrals passively and lose them passively. A deliberate referral program (ask every client after 90 days, track referral sources, reciprocate) dramatically increases referral volume.

2. **No thought leadership.** A financial advisor, attorney, or consultant who does not produce visible content is invisible to people who do not already know them. One LinkedIn post per week, one article per month, or one speaking slot per quarter compounds over 2-3 years into significant inbound.

3. **Generalism.** "Financial advisor" competes on price. "Financial advisor for tech founders with pre-liquidity equity" commands a premium and generates category-specific referrals.

4. **Ignoring trigger events.** Advisory services are purchased at specific moments: funding round, acquisition, divorce, retirement, regulatory change. Identify the triggers that precede your buyer's purchase and build a system to be present at those moments.
