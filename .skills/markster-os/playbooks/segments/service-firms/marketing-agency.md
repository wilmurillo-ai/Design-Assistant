# Marketing Agency

Covers: full-service marketing agencies, SEO agencies, paid media agencies, social media agencies, content marketing agencies, email marketing agencies, conversion rate optimization (CRO) shops.

What unifies these: you sell marketing outcomes on a monthly retainer, your product is invisible (you are a cost center until proven otherwise), and referrals are the engine that matters most before $1M.

---

## What is different about GTM

You are selling an outcome you cannot guarantee. That is the core tension in every agency sale.

The standard agency at $500K revenue gets 80%+ of clients from referrals. This is not strategy -- it is the default. The GOD Engine prescription for agencies is to make referrals systematic (D3 Expand) while building one additional inbound channel.

Cold outreach works for agencies but the bar is high. You are asking someone to fire their current vendor and hire you. The only way cold outreach converts is if: (1) your niche is specific enough that you look like the obvious expert, or (2) you can demonstrate results for a company exactly like theirs.

## Key metrics

| Metric | What it measures | Healthy benchmark |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Monthly retainer revenue | Core revenue metric | $15K-$25K average retainer is the target for sustainable margins |
| Client count | Scale indicator | 5-10 clients at Stage 2, 15-25 at Stage 3 |
| Average retainer length | Retention signal | 12+ months average |
| Gross margin | Revenue minus direct delivery cost (team time) | 50-60% for agency |
| Referral rate | % of new clients from referrals | 60%+ is healthy |
| Utilization rate | % of billable hours actually billed | 70-80% target |

## Biggest failure modes

1. **Generalism.** "Full-service marketing agency" competes with every other agency and wins only on price. "SEO for B2B SaaS companies" has a defensible position, commands a premium, and generates referrals within the niche.

2. **Underpricing to win clients.** A $2K/month retainer cannot be delivered profitably. Low prices attract demanding clients, destroy margins, and create resentment. The right first-year retainer is usually $5-10K/month minimum.

3. **No proof before outbound.** Cold outreach for an agency with no case studies fails. You are asking a prospect to trust you with their marketing budget. They will not do it without evidence.

4. **Founder-dependency.** When the founder does all the work, the agency cannot scale. The transition from founder-as-delivery to team-as-delivery must happen by Stage 2.

---

## Scaling constraint and exit

The agency scaling bottleneck is almost always talent: finding, hiring, and retaining people who can deliver client results. The businesses that scale past $2M solve this through:
- Standardized delivery processes that make new hires effective faster
- A proprietary methodology or framework that justifies premium pricing
- Productization: one service, one price, delivered the same way

PE roll-ups are active in the agency space. Exit multiples for agencies with $1M+ EBITDA and documented retention are typically 4-8x EBITDA. Building for exit means: documented SOPs, minimal founder dependency, sticky clients, recurring revenue.
