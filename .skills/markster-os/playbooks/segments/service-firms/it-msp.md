# IT Consulting, MSP, and Cybersecurity

Covers: IT consulting, managed service providers (MSP), managed security service providers (MSSP), cybersecurity consulting, ERP/CRM implementation, data analytics consulting, BPO (business process outsourcing).

What unifies these: you manage critical technical infrastructure for clients on a recurring basis, switching costs are high (a major advantage for retention), and new clients come primarily from vendor partner ecosystems or referrals.

---

## What is different about GTM

The MSP and IT consulting sale is a trust sale with a long evaluation cycle. Buyers are handing over access to their infrastructure. They need to trust you before they sign.

The two reliable acquisition channels at Stage 1-2:
1. Referrals from existing clients (most valuable)
2. Vendor partner ecosystems: Microsoft, Salesforce, AWS, SAP, Hubspot, etc. all have partner programs that generate qualified referrals in exchange for certifications and co-selling

Cold outreach works but converts slowly. The message must be highly specific: a business type, a pain they are experiencing right now, and proof you have solved it before.

The revenue model is monthly recurring ($X per user per month, or flat monthly retainer). This is the right model. Project-only billing creates lumpy cash and constant re-selling. Convert projects to managed service contracts wherever possible.

## Key metrics

| Metric | What it measures | Healthy benchmark |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Monthly recurring revenue (MRR) | Core revenue metric | $100-$200 per user/month for SMB |
| Average client MRR | Deal size | $2K-$4K/month for SMB clients |
| Client count | Scale indicator | 10-20 clients at Stage 2 |
| SLA compliance rate | Delivery quality | 95%+; below this = churn risk |
| Monthly churn | Client retention | Under 1% monthly (high switching costs help) |
| Technician-to-client ratio | Delivery capacity | 1 technician per 50-100 endpoints (MSP standard) |

## Biggest failure modes

1. **No vendor partnerships.** MSPs who grow purely on cold outreach plateau early. Partner ecosystems (Microsoft CSP, Cisco, SentinelOne, etc.) generate warm referrals and co-selling opportunities that cold channels cannot match.

2. **Project-only model.** Project work is not a business -- it is a series of one-off sales. The switch to managed services (recurring MRR) is the most important business model decision an IT firm makes.

3. **No specialization.** "IT support for businesses" competes on price. "IT and cybersecurity for healthcare practices in [city]" has a defensible position, commands higher rates, and generates vertical-specific referrals.

4. **Skipping vCISO.** Cybersecurity is the fastest-growing spend category in IT. MSPs who do not have a security offering are leaving money on the table and are vulnerable to a competitor who does.

---

## Scaling constraint and exit

The bottleneck is technician hiring and retention. The businesses that scale solve this through:
- Tiered service levels (Tier 1/2/3) that let junior techs handle most tickets
- RMM (Remote Monitoring and Management) automation that reduces manual work
- Specialization in one vertical or one platform (reduces variety, increases efficiency)

PE roll-up activity is high in MSP. Businesses with $500K+ EBITDA, documented processes, and low founder-dependency transact at 5-8x EBITDA.
