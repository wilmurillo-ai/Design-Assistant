# Consulting

Covers: strategy consulting, operations consulting, sales consulting, HR consulting, business coaching, fractional CFO/CMO/CRO/COO services, management consulting, DEI consulting, training and L&D.

What unifies these: you sell expertise and judgment (not execution), the relationship is the product, and almost everything under $1M ARR comes from one source -- someone you already know referring you to someone they know.

---

## What is different about GTM

The sale is a trust sale, not a logic sale. Buyers do not hire consultants because of a cold email or a well-designed website. They hire someone who was recommended by a peer they trust, or someone who has visibly demonstrated expertise on the exact problem they are facing.

This makes G2 Warm (thought leadership, speaking, writing, visible expertise) the most important long-term brick. But G2 takes 6-18 months to build. In the short term, the only reliable source of revenue is working your existing network hard (G3 Book via warm outreach).

The business model is hourly, project, or retainer. Retainer is the most valuable (predictable, recurring, scales better). Most consultants default to hourly or project and wonder why revenue is lumpy.

## Key metrics

| Metric | What it measures | Healthy benchmark |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Monthly retainer revenue | Predictable base | Target 50%+ of revenue on retainer by Stage 2 |
| Average project value | Deal size | $15K-$75K for most consulting engagements |
| Utilization rate | Billable hours / total hours | 60-70% for sustainable solo consultant |
| Pipeline conversion rate | Prospects to clients | 30-50% with warm referrals, 5-15% with cold |
| Referral rate | % of revenue from referrals | 70-80% is normal at Stage 1-2 |
| Engagement renewal rate | % of project clients who re-engage | 50%+ indicates strong delivery |

## Biggest failure modes

1. **Not converting projects to retainers.** A $30K project that ends is worth less than a $5K/month retainer that runs for 12 months. Build the retainer offer and pitch it at the end of every project.

2. **No visible expertise.** Referrals dry up and cold outreach fails when you have nothing to point to. LinkedIn posts, articles, case studies, or speaking slots all serve as proof that you know what you are doing.

3. **Hourly billing for senior work.** Hourly billing caps your income at hours x rate and creates a misaligned incentive (slow work = more money). Value-based pricing (charge for the outcome, not the time) unlocks higher margins and better client relationships.

4. **Too many niches.** "I help companies grow" is not a consulting practice. "I help B2B SaaS companies build their first repeatable sales process" is. The more specific, the more referrable.

## Scaling constraint

Consulting scales through one of three paths:
1. Team model: hire associate consultants, train them on your methodology, move yourself to business development and quality oversight
2. Productization: package the consulting into a course, group program, or fixed-scope engagement that does not require your personal time
3. Licensing: license your framework to other consultants or to client organizations

Most consultants avoid all three and wonder why they are stuck. Pick one path and pursue it deliberately.
