# B2B SaaS

Covers: B2B SaaS, vertical SaaS (legal, HR, finance, ops, healthcare, real estate), AI/LLM-native SaaS, cybersecurity products.

What unifies these: you sell subscriptions to businesses, retention determines whether you have a company, and the metric that matters above all others is Net Revenue Retention.

---

## What is different about GTM

Sales motion is either sales-led (ACV above $5K/yr, requires outbound + demo + close) or product-led (ACV below $2K/yr, self-serve trial + in-product upgrade). Most sub-$2M ARR companies try PLG before proving they can sell. That is backwards. Prove the sale first, automate it second.

The unit economics live or die on NRR. If you are churning 3% of customers per month, every new sale is partially filling a leaking bucket.

## Key metrics

| Metric | What it measures | Healthy benchmark |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| MRR | Monthly recurring revenue | 10-20% MoM growth at Stage 1 |
| NRR (Net Revenue Retention) | Expansion minus churn | 100% = flat, 110%+ = growing, 120%+ = excellent |
| Logo churn | % customers canceling per month | Under 2% monthly |
| LTV:CAC | Return on acquisition | 3x minimum, 5x healthy |
| CAC payback | Months to recover acquisition cost | Under 12 months |
| ACV | Annual contract value | Determines sales motion (PLG vs. sales-led) |

## Biggest failure modes

1. **Building before selling.** Most B2B SaaS founders spend 6+ months on product before getting a paying customer. The ICP, the problem, and the willingness to pay should be validated with warm conversations before significant build.

2. **PLG without volume.** Product-led growth requires large top-of-funnel. Under 10K monthly signups, PLG is not a strategy, it is an excuse to avoid sales.

3. **Ignoring churn.** A 5% monthly churn rate means you replace your entire customer base every 20 months. No amount of new business fixes this. Fix churn before scaling acquisition.

4. **Pricing too low.** Low price signals low value, attracts price-sensitive churners, and makes the LTV:CAC math impossible. Most B2B SaaS products under $2M ARR should be priced 2-3x higher than their initial instinct.

## Fundraising path

Pre-seed: $500K-$2M on a SAFE. Needs: credible team, market thesis, early signal (pilot customers or LOIs).
Seed: $2M-$5M. Needs: $200K-$1M ARR, clear ICP, repeatable close.
Series A: $5M-$20M. Needs: $1M-$4M ARR, 100%+ YoY growth, NRR above 100%.

Note: Series B bar has reset to $4-8M ARR with NRR above 110%.

Non-VC path: Revenue-based financing accessible at $100K ARR (Capchase, Lighter Capital, Founderpath). Does not require dilution.
