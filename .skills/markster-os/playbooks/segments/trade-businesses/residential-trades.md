# Residential Trade and Home Services

Covers: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, painting, landscaping and lawn care, residential cleaning, pest control, junk removal, window cleaning, pressure washing, appliance repair, flooring installation, tree service.

What unifies these: customers find you on their phone when something breaks or needs maintenance, Google Maps is the primary acquisition channel, and the businesses that build recurring revenue are worth dramatically more than the ones that do not.

---

## What is different about GTM

The funnel starts with a phone call. Not a landing page opt-in, not a nurture sequence. Someone searches "HVAC repair near me" at 7pm and calls the first result with good reviews.

Google Business Profile is your most important marketing asset. Not your website, not Instagram -- your Google listing. Local Services Ads (LSA) cost $168 per booked job on average. Angi and similar platforms cost $542 per booked job. The math is clear: own Google, use the platforms as overflow.

The other half of the acquisition model is referrals. Residential home service customers talk to their neighbors. A customer who had a great experience and was explicitly asked to refer becomes a referral source. Most trade businesses never ask.

## Key metrics

| Metric | What it measures | Healthy benchmark |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Google Maps ranking | Organic discovery | Top 3 in local pack for primary service |
| Review count and rating | Trust signal and ranking factor | 50+ reviews, 4.5+ stars before running LSA |
| Cost per booked job | Acquisition efficiency | LSA: $168/job. Angi/Thumbtack: $400-$600/job |
| Average ticket size | Revenue per job | Higher ticket = less volume needed |
| Maintenance agreement rate | % of clients on recurring plan | 30%+ significantly changes valuation |
| Gross margin | Revenue minus labor and materials | HVAC/Pest/Cleaning: 50-70%. Roofing: 40-55%. GC: 15-30%. |

## The recurring revenue multiplier

This is the single most important strategic decision for a residential trade business.

A business with 80%+ of revenue from recurring maintenance agreements (HVAC tune-up plans, pest control subscriptions, lawn care contracts, cleaning routes) is valued 50-75% higher than an identical business running on call-in work.

The math: 200 clients on $100/month maintenance agreements = $20K/month in guaranteed revenue before answering a single call. This changes your hiring decisions, your cash flow, your stress level, and your exit value.

Every trade business should have a maintenance program. Building it is a D3 Expand play.

---

## Biggest failure modes

1. **Ignoring Google Business Profile.** Letting a competitor accumulate 5x your reviews while you are doing the same quality work is a fixable problem. Ask for reviews after every job, automatically. Most field management software (Jobber, HousecallPro) does this.

2. **No maintenance program.** Call-in only businesses have volatile revenue, high re-acquisition cost, and low exit valuation. Building a maintenance program is the highest-ROI growth move for most trade businesses.

3. **Underpricing.** Most trade businesses price to match the market floor. The businesses that win charge a premium and justify it with speed, guarantee, and professionalism. The price-sensitive customer is not the customer you want.

4. **Founder bottleneck.** When the owner is in the truck, growth stops when the owner is full. The transition from doing the work to managing the crew is the critical scaling step. It requires written SOPs, a quality checklist, and someone who can hold the standard.
