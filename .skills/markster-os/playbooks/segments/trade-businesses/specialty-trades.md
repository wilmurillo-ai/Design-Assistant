# Specialty Trades

Covers: general contracting, remodeling, custom home building, flooring installation, tile and stone, windows and doors, pool installation and service, concrete and masonry, fencing, insulation, waterproofing, solar installation.

What unifies these: larger project sizes (vs. emergency call-in), longer sales cycles, heavy dependence on referrals, and the subcontractor network as the scaling model.

---

## What is different about GTM

Specialty trades are project businesses, not call-in businesses. The average ticket is larger ($5K-$250K+), the decision cycle is longer (days to weeks), and the customer does significantly more research before calling.

Referrals are even more dominant here than in residential services. For general contractors and remodelers, 70%+ of revenue comes from referrals. The customer has to trust you with their home and a large check. They want a recommendation from someone they know.

Google Maps and Houzz still matter for discovery, but they are the starting point. The customer then looks at your portfolio, reads reviews, and often asks for references before signing.

The subcontractor model is how specialty trades scale past the owner. Instead of hiring a full crew, you build a network of reliable subs for each trade. This reduces overhead and allows you to take on more projects without carrying fixed labor costs.

## Key metrics

| Metric | What it measures | Healthy benchmark |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Average project value | Revenue per job | Target projects above $25K for sustainable margins |
| Gross margin | Revenue minus labor and materials per project | GC/Remodeling: 15-30%. Flooring/Specialty: 30-45%. |
| Referral rate | % of projects from referrals | 60-70% at Stage 2+ |
| Project backlog | Pipeline health | 2-3 months of work in signed contracts |
| Change order rate | Scope management | Track separately; unmanaged change orders kill margins |
| Sub-network quality | Delivery capacity | 3+ reliable subs per trade type |

## The COI network for specialty trades

The equivalent of referrals in specialty trades is the COI (Center of Influence) network:
- Interior designers
- Architects
- Real estate agents (recommend contractors to buyers who need work done)
- Property managers
- Structural engineers

Building relationships with 10-15 COIs who serve your ideal client is worth more than any advertising spend. A good interior designer or architect can send 5-10 projects per year.

## Biggest failure modes

1. **No professional portfolio.** In remodeling and specialty trades, the portfolio IS the product. Professional before/after photography on every project is a non-negotiable business investment.

2. **Underestimating and overrunning.** Margin erosion from inaccurate estimates is the most common failure mode. Invest in estimating software and a consistent estimating process before scaling.

3. **No change order discipline.** Customers ask for changes. Without a signed change order process, those changes come out of your margin. Every change, no matter how small, requires documentation and approval before work begins.

4. **Taking every project.** Low-margin, high-difficulty projects create cost without profit. Define the minimum project size and type you will take and stick to it.
