# Commercial Services

Covers: commercial cleaning (janitorial), commercial HVAC, commercial plumbing and electrical, commercial landscaping and grounds maintenance, commercial pest control, staffing and workforce solutions, BPO (business process outsourcing), industrial maintenance.

What unifies these: the buyer is a business (not a homeowner), the sale is a contract (not a single job), and the relationship is maintained through consistent performance on a recurring basis.

---

## What is different about GTM

Commercial services are a B2B sale. This changes everything compared to residential.

The acquisition channel is not Google Maps. It is:
1. Direct outreach (walking in or calling property managers, facility managers, operations directors)
2. Referrals from other vendors serving the same building or business
3. Bids and RFPs for larger contracts

The decision cycle is longer. The buyer has a current vendor. They will not switch unless you give them a compelling reason and the timing is right. "Better price" works but creates a race to the bottom. "Better service with proof" is the right pitch.

The contract model is the goal. A commercial cleaning or HVAC maintenance contract that runs $3K-$10K/month for 12-24 months is the equivalent of a managed service retainer -- recurring, predictable, high-value.

## The outreach motion for commercial

This is the most direct B2B sales motion of any trade category. The process:

1. Build a target list: property management companies, facility managers for office parks, hospitals, schools, manufacturing plants (depends on your service)
2. Research trigger events: new property management company taking over a building, new construction coming online, facility complaint in local news
3. Outreach: direct call or walk-in + follow-up email. This is a relationship sale; persistence without being annoying wins.
4. Proposal: show up with a clear scope, clear price, and a reference from a similar property you already service

The reference is critical. "We service three similar buildings in this zip code -- here is a contact at each" closes more commercial deals than any other element of the proposal.

## Biggest failure modes

1. **Bidding on RFPs without relationships.** RFPs are often written around the incumbent vendor. Winning a cold RFP is possible but inefficient. Build relationships before the RFP goes out.

2. **Underpricing contracts to win.** Low-price commercial contracts destroy margin. The correct response to losing on price is to focus on clients who value service quality, not to cut further.

3. **No QA system.** Commercial clients expect consistent quality every visit. One bad week creates a complaint. Two complaints create a cancellation. A documented QA checklist and random inspection schedule is mandatory at Stage 2.

4. **Ignoring account expansion.** A commercial cleaning client who is happy is a candidate for window cleaning, day porter service, carpet cleaning, or pest control. Additional services per account is the highest-margin growth lever.
