# Proposal Template

A results-oriented proposal. Sells outcomes, not deliverables.
Replace all bracketed variables with specific content from your discovery call. Generic proposals lose -- every section should contain something only you could have written about this specific prospect.

---

# Proposal: [CLIENT COMPANY NAME]

Prepared for: [Contact Name], [Title]
Date: [Date]
Valid through: [Date + 14 days]

---

## Executive Summary

[3 sentences maximum. Do not pitch. State their problem, your solution, and the expected outcome.]

Example format:
"[Company name] has built a strong reputation through referrals and relationships, but the pipeline depends on [specific situation they described]. We build the client acquisition infrastructure that creates new conversations systematically, without requiring the owner to personally drive every deal. Most [firm type] in similar situations see [specific benchmark result] within [timeframe]."

---

## The Problem

In our conversation on [date], you described [2-3 sentences using their exact words -- reference what they actually said, not a generic version of their industry problem].

The core challenge: [their problem in one sentence, using their language].

What is at stake if this continues: [the specific consequence they named during discovery -- use the exact number or scenario they described, not a generic risk statement].

[If they named a specific event that prompted the conversation -- a lost client, a slow quarter, a key referrer going quiet -- reference it here. It proves you were listening.]

---

## Our Approach

Based on what you described, here is how we approach this:

**Phase 1 - Foundation ([timeframe, typically 2-4 weeks]):**

[What you build first. Be specific about what they will see and what gets set up. Examples: voice profile extracted from their existing content, prospect list built from their target customer definition, first outreach sequence written and ready for approval.]

Key output: [one concrete deliverable they hold at the end of Phase 1].

**Phase 2 - Launch ([timeframe, typically days 14-30]):**

[What gets activated. What they participate in. What they approve. How campaigns go live.]

Key output: [active campaign running, first responses coming in, etc.].

**Phase 3 - Operating rhythm ([timeframe, ongoing]):**

[What sustains. What gets better over time. What they own vs. what you manage. How results compound.]

First visible result: [what they will have or experience within the first 14 days].

---

## What You Get

Tied to your specific situation -- these are the outcomes that matter based on what you told us:

- [Outcome 1 -- specific to their situation, not a generic deliverable. "A prospect list of 500 verified contacts in [their target segment]" beats "prospect list."]
- [Outcome 2 -- another specific output from your approach]
- [Outcome 3 -- a longer-term result they care about based on the discovery call]
- [Outcome 4 -- optional, only if it was named in discovery as a priority]

[Optional -- if you have a relevant case study:]
"We recently worked with a [similar firm type] in a similar situation. [Specific result in one sentence -- company type, starting point, result, timeframe.]"

---

## What This Requires From You

Be honest. Managed services require minimal time but specific inputs. Describe exactly what you need.

Your time commitment: approximately [X hours in week 1] for setup, then [X hours per month] for approvals and feedback.

Specifically:
- [Week 1]: [What they provide -- examples: existing content samples, list of their target clients, access to their LinkedIn or website]
- [Ongoing]: [What they review and approve before anything goes live]
- [Optional]: [Anything else -- introductions, case study participation, feedback on results]

They do not need to understand the system. They need to approve what goes out and give honest feedback on results.
