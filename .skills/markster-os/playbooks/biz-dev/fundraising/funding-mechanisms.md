# Funding Mechanisms

VC is one path. Most companies -- including most of the ones that work -- use something else.

This file covers the full range of funding mechanisms by business type, when each applies, and what you need to qualify.

---

## For VC-backed startups (SaaS, marketplace, consumer, devtools)

| Mechanism | Amount | What you need | Timing |
|-----------|--------|---------------|--------|
| Friends and family | $25K-$250K | Relationship | Pre-product |
| Angels | $25K-$250K per check | Team + idea + early signal | Pre-seed |
| Accelerators (YC, Techstars, domain-specific) | $125K-$500K | Strong team, big market | Pre-seed |
| Micro-VC / pre-seed fund | $500K-$2M | Demo or pilot + team | Pre-seed |
| Seed VC | $2M-$8M | $200K-$1M ARR, PMF signals | Seed |
| Series A VC | $5M-$20M | $1M-$4M ARR, 100%+ YoY growth | Series A |
| Venture debt | $1M-$10M | VC-backed, or $3M+ ARR without VC | Post-seed |
| Revenue-based financing | Up to 50-70% of ARR | $100K+ ARR, B2B SaaS preferred | Post-revenue |

Revenue-based financing providers: Capchase, Lighter Capital, Founderpath, Arc. No equity given up. Repayment as a percentage of monthly revenue. Accessible at $100K ARR.

Note: SBIR/STTR grants are not listed. As of early 2026, SBIR authorization has lapsed. Do not build a funding roadmap that depends on these.

## For bootstrapped SaaS and productized services

| Mechanism | Amount | What you need | Timing |
|-----------|--------|---------------|--------|
| Revenue-based financing | $200K-$3M | $200K+ ARR, predictable MRR | Post-revenue |
| SBA 7(a) | Up to $5M | 2yr history, 650+ credit, $500K+ revenue | Growth |
| Business line of credit | $50K-$500K | 2yr history, profitability | Growth |
| Accounts receivable factoring | 70-95% of invoices | B2B clients with net terms | Ongoing |

The right default for a bootstrapped SaaS under $1M ARR is to fund growth from revenue. External capital is appropriate when there is a specific, high-ROI use case with a clear payback (e.g., hire one salesperson who will produce 3x their cost in ARR within 12 months).

## For professional services firms (agencies, consulting, advisory)

| Mechanism | Amount | What you need | Timing |
|-----------|--------|---------------|--------|
| SBA 7(a) | Up to $5M | 2yr history, 680+ credit | Growth / expansion |
| Business line of credit | $50K-$500K | 2yr history, strong cash flow | Growth |
| Invoice factoring | 70-95% of AR | B2B invoices, creditworthy clients | Ongoing |
| Equipment financing | $10K-$500K | Equipment as collateral | Specific asset purchases |
| Private equity (acquisition) | Company sale at 4-8x EBITDA | $1M+ EBITDA, documented processes | Exit |
| Family office | $1M-$10M | Industry alignment, growth story | Growth / acquisition |

PE activity in professional services is at multi-year highs (335+ M&A deals in professional services in H1 2025). Accounting, consulting, and agency roll-ups are the most active categories.

What PE buyers want in a professional services firm:
- $500K+ EBITDA minimum ($1M+ for better terms)
- Recurring or retainer revenue (not project-only)
- Documented SOPs and minimal founder dependency
- No single client above 25-30% of revenue
- Team that can run without the founder

## For trade and home service businesses

| Mechanism | Amount | What you need | Timing |
|-----------|--------|---------------|--------|
| SBA 7(a) | Up to $5M | 2yr history, 650+ credit | Growth / expansion |
| Equipment financing | $5K-$500K | Equipment as collateral, 24hr approval | Growth |
| Business line of credit | $25K-$500K | 2yr history, seasonal case | Seasonal / growth |
| Working capital loan | $5K-$500K | 12-month payback | Seasonal / payroll |
| SBA Microloan | Up to $50K | Via CDFI, startup-friendly | Early-stage |
| Invoice factoring | 70-95% of AR | Commercial clients with net terms | Commercial accounts |
| Merchant cash advance (MCA) | $5K-$250K | 6mo business, $10K-$15K/mo revenue | Growth (use carefully) |
| PE roll-up | Company acquisition | $500K+ EBITDA, documented processes | Exit |

MCA warning: merchant cash advances carry effective APRs of 40-150%. They are not a growth tool -- they are an emergency tool. If you need an MCA to make payroll, fix the cash flow problem structurally first.

PE roll-up activity is high in HVAC, pest control, roofing, and commercial cleaning. A trade business with recurring maintenance contracts, documented processes, and $500K+ EBITDA is an acquisition candidate.

## For e-commerce and DTC

| Mechanism | Amount | What you need | Timing |
|-----------|--------|---------------|--------|
| Shopify Capital | $200-$2M | 90+ days on Shopify, invite-only | Growth |
| Amazon Lending | $1K-$750K | Amazon seller, sales history | Growth |
| Stripe Capital | $500-$2M | Stripe merchant, payment volume | Growth |
| Inventory financing | $5K-$5M+ | Inventory as collateral, sales history | Growth |
| Revenue-based financing | Up to 50-70% of ARR | Predictable revenue | Growth |
| Equity crowdfunding (Reg CF) | Up to $5M/12 months | Consumer brand, audience | Growth |
| Rewards crowdfunding | $10K-$1M+ | Community, physical product | Launch |

The platform-native financing tools (Shopify Capital, Amazon Lending, Stripe Capital) are the most frictionless for e-commerce. They advance against your existing revenue with minimal paperwork.

## Equity crowdfunding (Reg CF and Reg A+)

**Reg CF:**
- Up to $5M per 12-month period
- Open to any US company
- Investors do not need to be accredited
- Requires SEC filing (Form C) -- cost: $5K-$20K in legal and accounting
- Best for: consumer brands with an engaged audience, mission-driven businesses

**Reg A+:**
- Up to $75M per 12-month period
- More expensive filing ($50K-$150K in legal/accounting)
- Essentially a mini-IPO
- Best for: companies with significant brand awareness and a retail investor audience

Crowdfunding is not free marketing. You are selling equity in exchange for capital, with all the complexity that entails. It requires an engaged audience, significant marketing effort, and months of preparation.

## Choosing the right mechanism

| Your situation | Recommended path |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Pre-revenue startup with big market | Angels + accelerator + SAFE |
| B2B SaaS at $200K+ ARR | Revenue-based financing for non-dilutive, or seed VC if you want to grow fast |
| Service business with 2+ years and profitability | SBA 7(a) for expansion, line of credit for working capital |
| Trade business buying equipment | Equipment financing (fast, cheap, collateralized) |
| Trade business with $500K+ EBITDA | PE roll-up conversation worth having |
| Consumer DTC with $100K+ monthly revenue | Platform-native financing (Shopify, Stripe) or inventory financing |
| Any business needing emergency cash | Do not use MCA. Fix the cash flow problem. Then line of credit. |
