# Traction by Archetype

What "traction" means is not universal. Investors in B2B SaaS want different signals than investors in marketplaces or consumer apps. This file maps what you need to show at each stage for each archetype.

---

## B2B SaaS (including vertical SaaS, AI SaaS, cybersecurity)

| Round | Traction required | Nice to have |
|-------|------------------|--------------|
| Pre-seed | 3-10 design partners with signed LOIs or paying pilots | Ex-operator or domain-expert founders |
| Seed | $200K-$1M ARR, clear ICP, at least 3 months of data showing retention | NRR above 100%, named customers willing to speak |
| Series A | $1M-$4M ARR, 100%+ YoY growth, NRR above 100% | Logo names from target verticals, documented repeatable sales motion |
| Series B | $4-8M ARR, NRR 110%+, 2-3x YoY growth | Multiple quarters of consistent growth, clear path to profitability |

Key signal investors watch above all others: **NRR**. NRR above 120% means the business grows from its existing customer base. Below 100% means it is slowly dying.

---

## Marketplace (2-sided)

| Round | Traction required | Nice to have |
|-------|------------------|--------------|
| Pre-seed | Proof of demand: waitlist, manual transactions, or pilot cohort | One side of the market seeded with density |
| Seed | $50K-$200K GMV/month, liquidity ratio above 20% in target geography | Supply-side retention above 50%, demand-side repeat rate |
| Series A | $500K+ GMV/month, clear take rate path, geographic expansion playbook | Both sides retained, network effects visible in retention data |

The hardest thing to show at seed: **liquidity**. A marketplace with supply and demand but zero matches is worth nothing. Investors want to see that the transaction happens reliably.

---

## DTC / Consumer

| Round | Traction required | Nice to have |
|-------|------------------|--------------|
| Pre-seed | Product-market fit signal: early cohort with D30 retention above 30%, or first 100 orders with repeat purchase | Low CAC from organic channel |
| Seed | LTV:CAC above 3x on at least one acquisition channel, D30 retention data from 2+ cohorts | Monthly organic growth coefficient above 0 |
| Series A | $1M+ ARR, LTV:CAC above 3x at scale, clear paid acquisition playbook | Subscription attach rate, brand organic following |

Consumer investors are most skeptical of: **paid-only growth**. If 100% of your customers come from Meta ads and your ROAS drops when you turn off spend, you have a media-buying business, not a consumer brand.

---

## Developer Tools / API / Open Source

| Round | Traction required | Nice to have |
|-------|------------------|--------------|
| Pre-seed | Working OSS project with early developer interest, or technical prototype with design partners | Strong technical team, prior OSS contributions |
| Seed | Meaningful OSS adoption (10K+ GitHub stars, or 1K+ active users), early enterprise pilots | First paying customers, developer community activity |
| Series A | $500K-$2M ARR, enterprise contracts signed, documented developer-to-enterprise conversion path | Expansion within accounts, developer community size |

The unusual thing about devtools: **usage is the traction**, even before revenue. Investors understand that monetization comes after adoption. Revenue before strong adoption is sometimes a negative signal (you closed too early, before real PMF).

---

## Hardware + Software

| Round | Traction required | Nice to have |
|-------|------------------|--------------|
| Pre-seed | Working prototype, manufacturing partner identified, strong founding team | Pre-orders or crowdfunding success |
| Seed | $500K-$2M from crowdfunding or pre-orders, manufacturing contract signed, first batch delivered | Subscription attach rate from first batch, customer testimonials |
| Series A | 1K-10K units shipped, subscription revenue growing, NRR on software above 100% | Retail channel LOIs, unit economics improving with volume |

Hardware-specific risk investors want addressed: **supply chain credibility**. Show you have a manufacturing partner, know your COGS, and have stress-tested the logistics.

---

## Service Firms (professional services)

VC is rarely the right path for service businesses. What applies here:

**PE acquisition (exit path):** $1M+ EBITDA, documented processes, recurring revenue, minimal founder dependency. Multiples: 4-8x EBITDA. PE roll-up activity in agencies and consulting is at multi-year highs.

**Revenue-based financing:** Once revenue is above $200K ARR and predictable, RBF provides growth capital without dilution.

**SBA 7(a):** Up to $5M for business acquisition, expansion, or working capital. Requires 2 years of history, 680+ credit, documented revenue.

Traction for an SBA loan: 2 years of tax returns showing consistent revenue, strong client concentration ratio (no single client above 30% of revenue).

---

## Trade and Local Businesses

VC is not the path. What applies:

**SBA 7(a):** Up to $5M. Equipment, working capital, business acquisition. Requires 2 years of history, 650+ credit.

**Equipment financing:** Fast approval (24-48 hours), equipment as collateral. Core tool for fleet and tool expansion.

**Business line of credit:** $25K-$500K once business has 2 years of history. Seasonal businesses with predictable revenue cycles qualify most easily.

**PE roll-up (exit):** HVAC, pest control, roofing, and commercial cleaning are active PE roll-up targets. A business with $500K+ EBITDA, documented processes, and recurring maintenance contracts is an acquisition candidate.

Traction for a trade business loan: 2 years of tax returns, documented revenue, strong credit, and evidence of recurring income (maintenance contracts help significantly).
