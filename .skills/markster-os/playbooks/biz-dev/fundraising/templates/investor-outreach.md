# Investor Outreach Templates

Cold and warm outreach for investor introductions. Keep it short. Lead with traction. No deck in the first email.

---

## How to Choose Your Approach

| Your situation | Use this template |
|---------------|-----------------|
| You have strong traction (revenue, customers, growth) | Cold - Traction-Led |
| Early stage, compelling thesis but light metrics | Cold - Thesis-Led |
| You have a mutual connection | Warm Intro Request + Forwardable Blurb |
| You met them briefly (event, conference) | Warm Follow-Up (see `follow-up.md`) |

---

## Variables

- `[INVESTOR_FIRST_NAME]` - their first name
- `[COMPANY]` - your company name
- `[WHAT_YOU_DO]` - one sentence: the problem you solve and for whom. Not your full pitch. One sentence.
- `[TOP_METRIC]` - your single strongest number: ARR, MRR, customer count, growth rate, retention. Pick one.
- `[SUPPORTING_METRIC]` - a second data point if it adds credibility (optional - only include if it strengthens, not dilutes)
- `[RAISE_AMOUNT]` - how much you are raising
- `[MILESTONE]` - the specific milestone this raise gets you to: "18 months runway", "Series A in 18 months", "launch in 3 new verticals"
- `[PERSONALIZATION_REASON]` - why this specific investor, not just any investor. Examples: portfolio company in adjacent space, tweet or article they wrote, sector thesis they have published, GP at fund you spoke with
- `[MARKET_INSIGHT]` - for thesis-led version: the one-sentence observation about why now, why this market, why the window is open
- `[MUTUAL_NAME]` - name of the mutual connection
- `[INVESTOR_NAME]` - investor's full name
- `[FUND]` - fund name
- `[YOUR_NAME]` - your name
- `[YOUR_EMAIL]` - your email (for the forwardable blurb)
- `[TITLE]` - your title

## Cold Email: Traction-Led

Use when: you have a metric worth leading with. If you have it, this is always the stronger approach.

**Subject:** [COMPANY] - [TOP_METRIC] in [timeframe]

Example subjects:
- "Acme - $180K ARR in 8 months"
- "Acme - 43 paying customers, zero churn"
- "Acme - 3.1x growth quarter over quarter"

---

[INVESTOR_FIRST_NAME],

[COMPANY] [WHAT_YOU_DO - one sentence, problem + solution].

Traction: [TOP_METRIC]. [SUPPORTING_METRIC if it adds something - otherwise skip this line].

Raising [RAISE_AMOUNT] to [MILESTONE].

[PERSONALIZATION_REASON - one sentence on why you reached out to them specifically. Make it real.]

Worth a 15-minute call?

[YOUR_NAME]

---

**Word count target: 60-80 words.** Cut anything that does not make the call more likely.

---

## Cold Email: Thesis-Led

Use when: you are early stage with honest but limited traction. Lead with the insight, not the numbers.

**Subject:** [COMPANY] - [MARKET_INSIGHT compressed to 5-8 words]

Example subjects:
- "Acme - why mid-market ops is breaking in 2026"
- "Acme - the infrastructure gap nobody is funding"

---

[INVESTOR_FIRST_NAME],

[MARKET_INSIGHT - one sentence. The observation about why now. Why this market. What has changed.].

[COMPANY] [WHAT_YOU_DO - one sentence].

We are [X months] in with [early traction or validation - be honest. "7 paying pilots" is fine. "Growing fast" is not.].

Raising [RAISE_AMOUNT] to [MILESTONE].

[PERSONALIZATION_REASON - saw your post on X, you backed Y which is adjacent, your fund thesis on Z].

Worth 15 minutes?

[YOUR_NAME]

---

**Word count target: 70-90 words.**

## Warm Intro Request (to send to a mutual connection)

Send this to someone who knows both you and the investor. Do not ask for a warm intro from someone who barely knows you - the intro is only as strong as the relationship.

---

[MUTUAL_FIRST_NAME],

Quick ask, no pressure.

I am building relationships with investors in [SECTOR] as we approach our raise. I noticed you know [INVESTOR_NAME] at [FUND].

If you think there is a fit, would you be willing to make an intro? Happy to send a short blurb you can forward - makes it easy on your end.

Only do it if you genuinely think it is a good match. No obligation.

[YOUR_NAME]

---

**Notes on warm intro requests:**
- Give the mutual an easy out. "Only if you think it is a good match" removes pressure and increases the quality of the intro if they do make it.
- Offer to write the blurb. Most people will take you up on it. It controls the framing.
- Do not ask for 10 intros at once. Pick the 2-3 most relevant.

---

## Forwardable Blurb (for your mutual to send)

Write this for them. Keep it to 4-5 sentences. They will use it verbatim or close to it.

---

[MUTUAL_NAME] suggested I pass along this intro.

[YOUR_NAME] is the [TITLE] of [COMPANY] - [WHAT_YOU_DO in one sentence].

Traction: [TOP_METRIC]. They are raising [RAISE_AMOUNT] to [MILESTONE]. [One sentence on why this investor specifically makes sense.]

[YOUR_NAME]'s email: [YOUR_EMAIL]

---

## LinkedIn InMail (Cold, No Mutual)

Use only when email is not available and you have checked that the investor is active on LinkedIn. InMails have lower reply rates than email - keep it shorter.

---

[INVESTOR_FIRST_NAME] - building [COMPANY], [WHAT_YOU_DO compressed]. [TOP_METRIC]. Raising [RAISE_AMOUNT].

[PERSONALIZATION_REASON].

Happy to send a short note if this is within your thesis. Worth connecting?

[YOUR_NAME]

---

**Character limit:** Keep LinkedIn cold outreach under 300 characters for the preview.

## Pre-Meeting Research Checklist

Before any investor meeting, know:

- [ ] Their fund's thesis and check size range
- [ ] Relevant portfolio companies (adjacent, complementary, or competitive)
- [ ] Any public writing (tweets, blog posts, podcasts) on your category
- [ ] Who else at the fund you might meet (Partner, Principal, Associate)
- [ ] The specific reason they might care about your space right now

Do not walk into a meeting without this. It shows.

## Principles

- **Keep cold emails under 100 words.** Investors receive hundreds per week. Long emails signal you cannot edit yourself - which is a signal about how you run a company.
- **Lead with the number, not the vision.** Vision is for the meeting. The email's only job is to get the meeting.
- **No deck in the first cold email.** Attaching a deck in a cold email is noise. If they are interested, they will ask.
- **Personalization is required.** "I invest in B2B SaaS" is not personalization. Find something specific to this person.
- **One ask.** The ask is always: worth a 15-minute call? Not a demo, not a deck review, not a deep-dive. A call.
- **Traction is your subject line.** If you have a number worth leading with, put it in the subject. If you do not, lead with the insight.
- **Track every touchpoint.** Memory fails over a 6-12 month fundraise. Log every email, call, and meeting in your CRM immediately.
