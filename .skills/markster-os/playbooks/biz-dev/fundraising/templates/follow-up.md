# Investor Follow-Up Templates

Post-meeting follow-ups and ongoing pipeline management.

---

## Post-Meeting Follow-Up (send within 24 hours)

**Subject:** Re: [meeting context] - next steps

---

[INVESTOR_FIRST_NAME],

Good to connect today. A few things from our conversation:

[1-2 sentences referencing something specific they said or responded to - not generic "great discussion"]

As discussed, [next step - sending deck, scheduling diligence call, introducing a reference customer, etc.].

[If sending deck: attached. If sending introductions: I'll set those up this week.]

[If they asked a question you did not fully answer: here is the fuller answer: [1-2 sentences]]

Following up [specific date] to check in.

[YOUR_NAME]

## Ask for Feedback After a Pass

---

[INVESTOR_FIRST_NAME],

Appreciate you taking the time to look at this.

Quick question if you are open to it: what would need to be different for this to be a clearer yes? Whether it is traction, timing, market size, or something else - I would find it genuinely useful to know.

No obligation to respond, but if you have two minutes it would help.

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## Notes on investor follow-up

- Persistence matters more than most founders realize. Most investments happen after 4-7 touchpoints.
- Only reach out with a reason - a milestone, a question they asked, a relevant article. Never just "following up."
- If they passed, they may still be useful: for introductions to other investors, for feedback on your positioning, or as a future investor when your metrics improve.
- Track every touchpoint in your CRM. Memory fails over a 6-month fundraise.
