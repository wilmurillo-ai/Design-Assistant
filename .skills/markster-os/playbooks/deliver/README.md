# D1: Deliver

**Pillar:** Delivery
**North Star:** On-time delivery rate and client satisfaction (NPS)

---

## What It Covers

Client onboarding, delivery workflows, quality assurance, communication frameworks, and scope management.

D1 is where revenue is earned or lost. Clients who experience excellent delivery renew, refer, and expand. Clients who experience inconsistent delivery churn, leave negative reviews, and poison the referral network. The Delivery pillar is not a support function -- it is the foundation of the Proof Loop that fuels all Growth activity.

---

## Key Questions It Answers

- What does an excellent client onboarding experience look like for our specific service?
- How do we deliver consistently without the founder personally overseeing every engagement?
- What QA process catches quality issues before the client does?
- How do we manage scope creep without damaging the client relationship?
- What communication cadence keeps clients informed without over-servicing?

---

## Onboarding Framework

Four phases for new client onboarding:

**Kickoff (Days 1-3):**
- Welcome call: goals, timeline, communication expectations, key contacts
- Access and credentials collected
- Kickoff summary document delivered

**Setup (Days 4-7):**
- All accounts, tools, and integrations configured
- First deliverable scope confirmed in writing
- Internal project brief created for delivery team

**Launch (Days 8-14):**
- First deliverable produced and reviewed internally
- Delivered to client with context: what you delivered, why, what comes next
- Feedback collected and addressed

**Stabilize (Days 15-30):**
- First weekly or biweekly check-in call
- Delivery rhythm established
- Success metrics agreed and documented

---

## Quality Assurance Framework

Four-level QA before any client delivery:

1. **Self-review:** Person who created the work reviews against the brief
2. **Peer review:** Second team member reviews for quality and accuracy
3. **Manager review:** Delivery Owner reviews against client standards and previous feedback
4. **Client delivery:** Delivered with a brief summary of what was done and why

Level 3 is optional for low-stakes routine deliverables once the client relationship is stable. Never skip Level 1 and 2.

---

## Common Failures

**No onboarding process:** Kicking off new clients with a "we'll figure it out as we go" approach. The first 30 days of a client engagement determine whether they renew. Inconsistent onboarding produces uncertain clients who escalate more and refer less.

**Scope creep without conversation:** Doing work outside the agreed scope without discussing additional cost. This erodes margin, trains clients that you will always do more for the same price, and creates resentment.

**Communication vacuum:** Delivering work without context. A client who receives a deliverable without knowing why decisions were made, what comes next, or how to evaluate it, experiences uncertainty -- even if the deliverable is excellent.

---

## Deliver Done Right

D1 is working when: New clients are onboarded via a consistent process without founder involvement. The QA framework catches quality issues before the client sees them. On-time delivery rate is above 90%. NPS is above 50. A delivery team member can onboard a new client independently using documentation.
