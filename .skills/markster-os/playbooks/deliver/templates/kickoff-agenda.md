# Kickoff Call Agenda

45-60 minutes. Runs without founder involvement once the account lead is trained on this format.

The kickoff call has one job: turn a signed contract into a working relationship with clear expectations, a confirmed first milestone, and a client who feels like they made the right decision.

---

## Before the Call

- [ ] Intake form reviewed
- [ ] Client's website, LinkedIn, and any public materials reviewed (10 minutes)
- [ ] First milestone and delivery date confirmed internally
- [ ] Any open access or technical dependencies identified
- [ ] Agenda shared with client 24 hours in advance

---

## Agenda

### Minutes 0-5: Open and Orient

"Thanks for being here. Here's how we're going to use the next 45 minutes..."

State the agenda:
1. Make sure we understand your situation fully before we start
2. Walk through exactly what we're doing and when
3. Agree on communication and success tracking
4. Confirm first steps so we can move immediately after this call

Ask: "Does that work for you, or is there something you'd want to prioritize?"

---

### Minutes 5-20: Discovery (Even Though They're Already a Client)

Most founders skip this. It is the most important part of the kickoff.

The sales process gave you their goals. The kickoff gives you the operational reality.

Questions to ask:
- "Walk me through what your current situation looks like. What's working, what's not?"
- "What specifically triggered you to move on this now versus six months ago?"
- "What have you tried before? What happened?"
- "Who else on your team is involved in this, and what do they care about?"
- "Is there anything that could slow this down on your end that we should plan for?"
- "What does success look like at 90 days? At 6 months?"

**Listen. Take notes using their exact words.** Their language will appear in your progress updates, case studies, and renewal conversations.

---

### Minutes 20-35: Walk Through the Engagement

Cover:
- What you are doing, in plain English
- The timeline: milestone by milestone, with dates
- What you need from them and when
- What they will see and when (deliverables, reports, check-ins)
- What success looks like at each stage

State the first milestone explicitly:
> "The first thing you will receive is [specific deliverable] by [specific date]. That's [X] days from today."

Ask: "Does that timeline work? Is there anything on your end that could affect it?"

---

### Minutes 35-45: Agreements and Next Steps

Cover:
- **Communication:** "We'll have a [weekly/biweekly] check-in on [day] at [time]. Between calls, the best way to reach us is [channel]. Response time is [timeframe]."
- **Decisions:** "If something comes up that changes the scope, we'll flag it immediately before doing anything. No surprises."
- **Access:** Confirm any credentials, tools, or introductions needed. Set a deadline for receipt.
- **First action:** "Before this call ends, the action on your end is [X] by [date]. On our end, [Y] by [date]."

End with:
> "Any questions or anything that feels unclear? I'd rather address it now than have it come up later."

---

### Minutes 45-60 (if needed): Buffer for Questions

Do not use this time for small talk. Use it for genuine questions they have about the work.

---

## Post-Call (Within 24 Hours)

Send a summary email:

Subject: Kickoff summary -- [Client Name]

[Client name],

Great call. Here's what we agreed to:

**What we're doing:**
[2-3 sentences on the engagement]

**First milestone:**
[Specific deliverable] by [specific date]

**What we need from you:**
[List of access, decisions, or inputs needed + deadlines]

**Communication:**
[Check-in cadence + best contact method]

Anything I missed or got wrong, reply here.

[Your name]

---

## Kickoff Quality Gates

Before ending the call, confirm:

- [ ] Client has stated their 90-day success criteria in their own words
- [ ] First milestone and date confirmed by both sides
- [ ] Communication cadence agreed and on the calendar
- [ ] At least one action item on the client's end, with a date
- [ ] Any access or dependencies have a deadline
- [ ] Client has direct contact information for their account lead

If any box is unchecked, do not end the call. Address it first.
