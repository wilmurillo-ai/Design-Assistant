# Client Onboarding Checklist

Onboarding without founder involvement. Every item on this checklist is owned by a named person, not by "the team" or "whoever has time."

Run this checklist for every new client, in order. A client who gets a poor onboarding experience churns before they get results. A client who gets an excellent one refers before the engagement is over.

---

## Before Day 1: Internal Setup

Owner: [Operations Lead]

- [ ] Client folder created in project management tool
- [ ] Contract signed and filed
- [ ] Payment method confirmed and first invoice sent
- [ ] Slack/Teams channel or communication channel created and named
- [ ] Team members assigned with clear roles documented
- [ ] Access credentials requested from client (list what you need)
- [ ] Kickoff call scheduled within 5 business days of contract signing
- [ ] Welcome email sent (see template below)

**Welcome email template:**

Subject: Welcome -- here's what happens next

[Client name],

Contract is signed and we're ready to go.

Here's what happens next:

1. I'll send a short intake form today -- takes about 10 minutes. Your answers feed directly into our kickoff call.
2. Kickoff call is scheduled for [date/time]. You'll get a calendar invite shortly.
3. After the kickoff, you'll have [first deliverable or first action] within [timeframe].

Any questions before then, reply here.

[Your name]

---

## Week 1: Kickoff and Intake

Owner: [Account Lead]

- [ ] Intake form sent within 24 hours of contract signing
- [ ] Intake form received and reviewed before kickoff call
- [ ] Kickoff call held (see kickoff agenda: `playbooks/deliver/templates/kickoff-agenda.md`)
- [ ] Kickoff notes documented and shared with client within 24 hours
- [ ] First milestone and date confirmed in writing
- [ ] Communication cadence agreed (weekly check-in / async / on-demand)
- [ ] Client has direct contact for questions (not a support inbox)

**Intake form fields to collect:**
- What does success look like for this engagement in 90 days?
- What have you already tried? What happened?
- Who else on your team is involved in this?
- What do you need from us to move fast?
- Is there anything we should know that wasn't in the sales process?

---

## Week 2: First Visible Result

Owner: [Delivery Lead]

- [ ] First deliverable produced and delivered
- [ ] First result or progress update sent to client
- [ ] Any blockers or dependencies surfaced and resolved
- [ ] Client confirms they have received and reviewed the deliverable
- [ ] Quick win documented for case study pipeline

**The quick win rule:** The client should see something real within 14 days of signing. Not a plan. Not a slide deck. A deliverable they can use or a metric that moved. This anchors confidence and reduces churn risk in the first 30 days.

---

## Week 3-4: Establish Rhythm

Owner: [Account Lead]

- [ ] Weekly or biweekly check-in cadence running
- [ ] Client feedback collected and logged
- [ ] Any scope questions or change requests documented and responded to
- [ ] Month 1 summary prepared for end-of-month review call
- [ ] Health score assessed (see health score rubric below)

---

## 30-Day Health Check

Score each dimension 1-3. Total below 10 = high churn risk. Take action immediately.

| Dimension | 1 (At Risk) | 2 (Stable) | 3 (Strong) |
|-----------|------------|-----------|-----------|
| Engagement | Unresponsive, skipping calls | Responsive, some delays | Proactive, asking questions |
| Progress | Behind on milestones | On track | Ahead of schedule |
| Results | No visible outcome yet | Early indicators positive | First result delivered |
| Relationship | Transactional, cold | Professional, functional | Warm, collaborative |
| Expansion signals | No mentions of other needs | Occasional references | Actively asking about other services |

**Score: __ / 15**

If score is below 10:
1. Schedule an unscheduled call this week. Do not wait for the next check-in.
2. Ask directly: "Are you getting what you expected from this engagement?"
3. Address the gap before they make a decision.

---

## Onboarding Complete Gate

Before marking onboarding complete, all of these must be true:

- [ ] Client has received at least one deliverable
- [ ] Client has seen at least one result (early metric, first output, or progress report)
- [ ] Communication cadence is established and running
- [ ] Client knows their primary contact and how to reach them
- [ ] 30-day health check completed and score is 10+
- [ ] Quick win documented for D2 (Prove) pipeline
