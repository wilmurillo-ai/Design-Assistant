# Research Prompt: Competitive Intelligence Deep-Dive

**To be executed by:** Perplexity, Gemini, or other research-capable LLM
**Estimated time:** 60-90 minutes of research
**Output format:** Markdown document with competitive analysis matrices

---

## Your Task

You are a competitive intelligence analyst. Your client needs a comprehensive map of the competitive landscape for AI-powered B2B growth services to identify positioning gaps, pricing strategies, go-to-market approaches, and weaknesses to exploit.

**Your job:** Analyze 20-30 competitors across AI tools, agencies, and hybrid services to build a strategic competitive map.

---

## Background Context

**What this type of service sells:**
- AI agents run outbound, content, pipeline building
- Managed service (not SaaS): $3-15K/mo
- Transparent approval mode = client approves everything before execution
- Target: B2B service companies, $500K-$5M revenue
- Key differentiator: Transparency (not a black box)

**Why this matters:**
We need to know:
- Where competitors are strong/weak
- What positioning is crowded vs open
- What pricing strategies work
- What proof/credibility tactics they use
- Where we can differentiate

---

## Research Questions

1. Who are the 20-30 direct and indirect competitors?
2. How do they position themselves? (category, messaging, differentiation)
3. What's their pricing model and range?
4. What proof do they show? (case studies, metrics, demos)
5. What are their weaknesses? (from reviews, buyer complaints)
6. Where is the white space? (underserved segments, positioning gaps)

---

## Research Method

### Part 1: Competitor Identification

**Find 20-30 competitors across these categories:**

**Category A: AI Sales Tools**
- Clay, Apollo.io, Instantly.ai, Lemlist, Smartlead, Woodpecker, Reply.io, Outreach, Salesloft, Seamless.ai

**Category B: AI SDR Services**
- 11x.ai, AiSDR, Artisan, Exceed.ai, Qualified, Conversica

**Category C: Traditional Growth Agencies**
- Search Google for "B2B growth agency", "demand gen agency", "outbound agency"
- Pick 5-7 that serve similar ICP ($500K-$5M companies)

**Category D: AI Marketing Agencies**
- Search LinkedIn/Google for "AI marketing agency", "AI growth agency"
- Pick 3-5

**For each competitor, compile:**

| Company | Category | Founded | Funding/Stage | Employee Count | Target Market |
|---------|----------|---------|---------------|----------------|---------------|
| Clay | AI Tool | 2021 | $60M Series B | 50-100 | Growth teams at startups |
| 11x.ai | AI SDR Service | 2022 | $24M Series A | 30-50 | Sales teams, SMB-MM |
| etc. | | | | | |

### Part 2: Positioning Analysis

**For each competitor, analyze their positioning:**

**Where to look:**
- Homepage (H1, subheader, first 2 screens)
- About page
- LinkedIn company page (description)

**What to extract:**

| Company | Category Claim | Core Message (1 sentence) | Differentiation Claim | Key Phrases Used | Tone |
|---------|----------------|---------------------------|----------------------|------------------|------|
| Clay | "Data enrichment platform" | "Enrich, clean, and automate your outbound data" | "50+ data providers in one" | "Waterfall enrichment", "Scale outbound" | Technical, tool-focused |
| 11x.ai | "Digital workers for revenue teams" | "AI SDRs that book meetings while you sleep" | "Autonomous, no management needed" | "Digital workers", "AI employees" | Bold, futuristic |
| etc. | | | | | |

**Patterns to identify:**
- How many claim "AI-powered"? (likely most)
- How many emphasize automation vs human oversight?
- How many show transparency vs black box?
- How many lead with outcome vs features?

### Part 3: Pricing Strategy Analysis

**For each competitor where pricing is public:**

| Company | Pricing Model | Price Range | Minimum Commitment | What's Included at Low End | What's Included at High End | Price Anchoring Strategy |
|---------|---------------|-------------|-------------------|---------------------------|----------------------------|------------------------|
| Clay | Usage-based + seat | $149-$800/mo per seat | Monthly | 10K credits | 200K credits + priority support | Free trial, then tiers |
| 11x.ai | Per-seat AI worker | $3K-$8K/mo per worker | 3-month | 1 AI SDR, email only | Multiple workers, phone calls | "Replace $80K SDR for $3K" |
| Agency X | Retainer | $5K-$15K/mo | 6-month | Basic campaigns | Full-service + strategy | Positioning as "investment" |
| etc. | | | | | | |

**Analysis questions:**
- What's the median price for tools vs services?
- How do they justify their pricing?
- What's the entry point (how cheap to start)?
- What's the commitment required?

### Part 4: Proof & Credibility Analysis

**For each competitor, what proof do they show?**

| Company | Case Studies? | Metrics Shown | Client Logos | Demo Available? | Reviews (G2/Capterra) | Social Proof |
|---------|---------------|---------------|--------------|----------------|---------------------|--------------|
| Clay | No | "50+ data providers" | Yes (big names) | Yes (interactive) | 4.9/5 (800+ reviews) | Heavy Twitter presence |
| 11x.ai | Yes (3-4) | "3x pipeline", "40% reply rate" | Yes | No (waitlist) | 4.7/5 (50+ reviews) | Founder on LinkedIn |
| etc. | | | | | | |

**Key insights:**
- Who has the BEST proof? (most credible case studies)
- Who has NO proof? (just claims)
- What metrics do they emphasize? (meetings booked, pipeline, ROI, time saved)
- What social proof do they use? (testimonials, reviews, logos, founder presence)

### Part 5: Weakness Analysis (from Reviews)

**Find G2, Capterra, TrustRadius reviews for 10+ competitors.**

**Focus on 3-star and below reviews. Extract:**

| Company | Most Common Complaint | Frequency | Severity | Example Quote | Our Opportunity |
|---------|----------------------|-----------|----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Clay | Data quality issues | High | Medium | "60% email bounce rate even after enrichment" | Show data verification in approval workflow |
| 11x.ai | Black box, no control | Medium | High | "Can't see what emails they're sending" | Lead with transparent approval mode |
| Apollo | Still requires manual work | High | Medium | "Tool doesn't run itself, I'm still doing the work" | Position as managed service |
| etc. | | | | | |

**Patterns to find:**
- What do buyers complain about across ALL competitors?
- What complaints are specific to tools vs agencies?
- What unmet needs emerge?

### Part 6: Go-to-Market Strategy Analysis

**How do competitors acquire customers?**

**Research their GTM by looking at:**
- Founder LinkedIn activity (posting frequency, content type)
- Company LinkedIn (followers, post engagement)
- Twitter/X presence
- Paid ads (search Google for "[company] alternatives" and see if they're advertising)
- Content marketing (blog, SEO - search "[category] guide" and see who ranks)
- Community presence (Reddit, Indie Hackers, Slack communities)

**Compile:**

| Company | Primary Channel | Founder Presence | Content Strategy | Paid Ads | Community | Free Tier/PLG |
|---------|----------------|------------------|------------------|----------|-----------|---------------|
| Clay | PLG + content | Low (team-focused) | Heavy SEO blog | Yes (high spend) | No | Yes (free tier) |
| 11x.ai | Founder-led | High (LinkedIn daily) | Case studies | Unknown | No | No (sales-led) |
| etc. | | | | | | |

---

## Expected Output

Deliver a markdown document with these sections:

### 1. Competitor Directory
Full table of 20-30 competitors with basic info (Part 1).

### 2. Positioning Map
Full positioning table (Part 2) + summary insights:
- What positioning is crowded? (everyone says the same thing)
- What positioning is open? (no one claims this)
- Where does a transparent AI service fit?

### 3. Pricing Landscape
Full pricing table (Part 3) + insights:
- What's the competitive price range?
- How to price relative to competitors?
- What's the optimal entry point?

### 4. Proof & Credibility Ranking
Full table (Part 4) + ranked list:
- **Best proof:** Top 3 competitors with strongest case studies/metrics
- **Weakest proof:** Top 3 competitors with just claims, no evidence
- **Proof gaps:** What proof does NO ONE show? (opportunity)

### 5. Weakness Matrix
Full table (Part 5) + strategic opportunities:
- **Universal pain:** Complaints across all competitors (must solve this)
- **Tool pain:** Complaints specific to tools (position against this)
- **Agency pain:** Complaints specific to agencies (position against this)
- **Exploitable weakness:** Competitor weaknesses where we have strength

### 6. GTM Strategy Comparison
Full table (Part 6) + recommendations:
- What GTM strategies are working? (evidence of traction)
- What GTM strategies are underused? (white space)
- What should we do? (founder-led, PLG, content, community)

### 7. Strategic Recommendations

Answer these questions:
1. **Positioning:** Where to sit relative to competitors?
2. **Pricing:** What price range makes sense?
3. **Differentiation:** What's the ONE thing that can be owned that competitors can't claim?
4. **Proof:** What evidence is needed to beat competitors?
5. **GTM:** What channel to double down on?

### 8. Competitive Battle Cards

For the top 5 closest competitors, create a battle card:

**Competitor: [Name]**
- **What they claim:** [Their positioning]
- **Their strength:** [What they're good at]
- **Their weakness:** [What buyers complain about]
- **How to position against them:** [What to say when prospects compare]
- **Proof to show:** [What evidence beats their story]

---

## Research Tips

- Use Perplexity citations to verify all claims
- For pricing, check if you can find leaked pricing docs or negotiation posts on Reddit
- Reviews are gold - read 20-30 negative reviews for top competitors
- Look for founder presence on LinkedIn/Twitter (indicates GTM motion)
- Identify who's growing fast (recent funding, hiring, press)

A single markdown file with all sections - this is your competitive intelligence reference for positioning, pricing, and GTM decisions.
