# Research Prompt: GTM Channel Effectiveness Analysis

**To be executed by:** Perplexity, Gemini, or other research-capable LLM
**Estimated time:** 45-60 minutes of research
**Output format:** Markdown document with channel strategy recommendations

---

## Your Task

You are a GTM strategist. Your client needs to identify which customer acquisition channels actually work for B2B services selling to $500K-$5M companies, and which channels are waste.

**Your job:** Research how B2B service companies and AI tools acquire customers, analyze channel effectiveness data, and recommend a prioritized channel strategy.

---

## Background Context

**What this type of service sells:**
- AI agents for outbound, content, pipeline (managed service)
- Price: $3-15K/mo
- Target: B2B service companies, $500K-$5M revenue
- ICP: Founder/CEO at capacity, agencies burned them, tools require too much work

**The question:**
Where should a founder spend their time to get customers?
- Cold outbound? (email, LinkedIn)
- Founder-led content? (LinkedIn, Twitter)
- Community? (Slack groups, Reddit, Indie Hackers)
- Paid ads? (Google, LinkedIn ads)
- SEO/content? (Blog, YouTube)
- Partnerships? (Referrals, resellers)
- Events? (Conferences, meetups)

---

## Research Questions

1. What channels do successful B2B service companies use to acquire customers?
2. What's the ROI/effectiveness of each channel at different stages (0-$1M ARR, $1-5M, $5M+)?
3. What channels work for founder-led, zero-budget growth?
4. What channels are saturated (high competition) vs blue ocean (underused)?
5. What channel to prioritize first?

---

## Research Method

### Part 1: Channel Performance Data

**Find data on B2B customer acquisition channel effectiveness:**

**Sources:**
- Marketing blogs: Gong, HubSpot, Salesforce, ProfitWell on channel ROI
- Founder Twitter threads: Search "how I got my first 10 customers"
- Indie Hackers: "what marketing channel works" posts
- Case studies: SaaS companies sharing CAC by channel
- Podcasts: SaaStr, My First Million, 20VC (search "customer acquisition")

**Extract data for each channel:**

| Channel | Avg CAC | Time to First Customer | Scalability | Best for Stage | Works for Services? | Source |
|---------|---------|------------------------|-------------|----------------|-------------------|--------|
| Cold Email | $500-2K | 1-2 months | High | All stages | Yes | Multiple sources |
| LinkedIn Outbound | $1-3K | 1-3 months | Medium | Seed-Series A | Yes | Founder threads |
| Founder Content (LinkedIn) | $0-500 | 3-6 months | Medium | Seed-Series A | Yes | Case studies |
| SEO/Blog | $100-500 | 6-12 months | High | Series A+ | Maybe | HubSpot data |
| Paid Ads (LinkedIn) | $3-10K | Immediate | High | Series A+ | No (too expensive for services) | Ad platforms |
| Community (Slack/Reddit) | $0-200 | 2-4 months | Low | Seed | Yes | Indie Hackers |
| Partnerships | $500-2K | 3-6 months | Medium | Series A+ | Yes | Case studies |
| Events/Conferences | $2-5K | Immediate | Low | All stages | Yes | Founder stories |

### Part 2: Founder-Led Growth Research

**How do founder-led B2B companies (especially services) get their first 10-50 customers?**

**Find 10-15 founder stories:**

**Sources:**
- Indie Hackers: "Milestone" posts ($10K MRR, first 10 customers)
- Twitter: "#buildinpublic" + "first customers"
- LinkedIn: Founders sharing "how we got started"
- Podcasts: My First Million, Indie Hackers, Founders Podcast

**Extract pattern:**

| Company | What They Sell | First Channel | Why It Worked | Time to $10K MRR | Scale Strategy |
|---------|----------------|---------------|---------------|-----------------|----------------|
| [B2B Service] | Sales consulting | Personal network | Trust + warm intros | 3 months | Referrals + content |
| [AI Tool] | Data enrichment | ProductHunt + Twitter | Launch buzz | 6 months | SEO + PLG |
| [Growth Agency] | Demand gen | Cold outreach | Volume + founder selling | 4 months | Case studies + LinkedIn |
| etc. | | | | | |

**Common patterns to identify:**
- What do most use FIRST? (usually: network, cold outreach, or community)
- What do they scale WITH? (content, SEO, paid, partnerships)
- What did NOT work? (often: paid ads, PR, cold calling)

### Part 3: Channel Saturation Analysis

**Which channels are crowded vs blue ocean?**

**For each major channel, assess competition:**

| Channel | Saturation Level | Why? | Opportunity? |
|---------|------------------|------|-------------|
| Cold Email | High | Everyone does it, inboxes flooded | Personalization + timing (trigger events) |
| LinkedIn DMs | High | Spam hell, low reply rates | Founder-authentic, not sales-y |
| LinkedIn Content | Medium-High | Lots of posts, but most are generic | Contrarian takes, real stories |
| Twitter/X | Medium | B2B founders active, but noisy | Niche, technical depth |
| SEO/Blog | High | Incumbents dominate | Long-tail keywords, niche topics |
| Reddit | Low | Most brands scared to be authentic | Real conversations, no pitch |
| Slack Communities | Low | Underused by agencies | Founder presence in niche groups |
| YouTube | Medium | Growing for B2B, but underused by services | Founder story, behind-the-scenes |
| Podcasts (guest) | Low-Medium | Long-form, high trust | Targeted shows with ICP listeners |

**Insight:**
- High saturation does not mean avoid (if it works, do it better)
- Low saturation does not always mean pursue (might not work at all)
- Best: High-ROI channel + differentiated execution

### Part 4: Channel-ICP Fit Analysis

**Where does the target ICP actually hang out?**

**ICP: B2B service company founders/CEOs, $500K-$5M revenue**

**Research where they spend time:**

**Sources:**
- LinkedIn: Search for target ICP profiles, see what they post/engage with
- Communities: Which Slacks, Reddits, Facebook groups do they mention?
- Podcasts: What do they listen to? (check LinkedIn profiles for "featured" podcasts)
- Events: What conferences do they attend? (LinkedIn event check-ins)

**Compile:**

| Where ICP Hangs Out | Activity Level | Content They Engage With | Opportunity |
|---------------------|----------------|-------------------------|-------------|
| LinkedIn | High | Founder stories, growth tips, case studies | Post contrarian takes, share real numbers |
| r/entrepreneur | Medium | "How to scale" questions | Answer questions, be helpful (not salesy) |
| Indie Hackers | Medium | "What's working" threads | Share wins/losses, build credibility |
| Private Slack groups | Low (but high-intent) | Tactical advice, recommendations | Join + contribute, no pitch |
| SaaStr events | Low (1-2x/year) | Networking, learning | In-person relationship building |
| Email newsletters | High | Tactical content (not fluff) | Sponsor niche newsletters? |

### Part 5: Channel Prioritization Framework

**Rank channels by:**

**Criteria:**
1. **Cost** (time + money)
2. **Speed to first customer**
3. **Scalability** (can you 10x it?)
4. **Founder fit** (does the founder enjoy it?)
5. **ICP presence** (where they hang out)

**Scoring example:**

| Channel | Cost | Speed | Scalability | Founder Fit | ICP Presence | Total Score |
|---------|------|-------|-------------|-------------|--------------|-------------|
| LinkedIn Content (Founder-led) | Low | Medium | Medium | High | High | 4/5 |
| Cold Outbound (Email + LinkedIn) | Medium | Fast | High | Medium | High | 4/5 |
| Community (Slack/Reddit) | Low | Medium | Low | High | Medium | 3.5/5 |
| SEO/Blog | High (time) | Slow | High | Medium | Medium | 3/5 |
| Paid Ads (LinkedIn) | High | Fast | High | Low | High | 3/5 |
| Partnerships | Medium | Slow | Medium | Medium | High | 3/5 |
| Events | High | Fast | Low | Medium | Low | 2.5/5 |

## Research Tips

- Prioritize PRIMARY DATA (founder interviews, case studies) over generic advice
- Look for RECENT data (2023-2026) - GTM changes fast
- Validate with multiple sources (don't trust one blog post)
- Focus on SERVICES, not just SaaS tools (different dynamics)
- Ask "what would work with $0 budget?" (most founders start here)
