# Research Prompt: First 60 Seconds Messaging Analysis

**To be executed by:** Perplexity, Gemini, Claude (fresh session), or other LLM
**Estimated time:** 30-45 minutes of research + drafting
**Output format:** Markdown document with message variations

---

## Your Task

You are a B2B messaging strategist. Your client struggles to explain what they do in a way that creates curiosity instead of dismissal. Prospects ask "how much?" and "what does it do?" then mentally categorize the service as "another AI thing" before the conversation gets to proof/demo.

**Your job:** Research how successful B2B companies explain complex/novel services in their first 60 seconds, then draft 5-7 alternative opening explanations that bypass skepticism.

---

## Background Context

**What this type of service actually is:**
- AI agents run outbound, content, and pipeline building for B2B companies
- Managed service (not SaaS) - they run it, client approves work before anything goes live
- Price: $3-15K/mo (replaces $225K/year in-house team)
- Target: B2B service companies, $500K-$5M revenue
- Key differentiator: Transparency (approval mode - nothing executes without client approval)

**Current explanation (typical):**
> "We're an AI-powered growth agency. We use AI to build outbound campaigns, content, and pipeline for B2B companies. Everything goes through approval mode where you approve or reject before it goes live."

**The problem:**
- By the time a founder says "AI-powered agency," prospects have already categorized it with "AI tools I've tried"
- They ask "how much?" (price objection) before understanding the value
- They never get to see the approval workflow or proof because they've already decided it's not for them

---

## Research Questions

1. How do successful B2B companies explain complex/novel offerings in 60 seconds?
2. What message structures work best? (problem-first, contrast-first, proof-first, story-first)
3. What language patterns create curiosity vs dismissal?
4. How do you answer "what do you do?" in a way that makes prospects ask "how does that work?"

### Part 2: Message Structure Patterns

Based on Part 1 analysis, identify **5-7 winning message structures**:

| Structure Name | Template | When It Works | Example (Generic) |
|----------------|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Problem-First | "Most [ICP] struggle with [pain]. We [solution in 1 sentence]." | When pain is obvious and universal | "Most B2B teams spend 40 hrs/mo on outbound. We do it for you - you approve, we execute." |
| Contrast-First | "[Old way] is [problem]. [New way] is [benefit]. We're [new way]." | When existing options suck and everyone knows it | "Agencies are black boxes. Tools require you to run them. We're different - AI does the work, you approve it." |
| Proof-First | "We [result for client]. Here's how: [1-sentence explanation]." | When you have undeniable results | "We took a client from $22M to $412M in 2 years. AI agents + transparent approval = accountable growth." |
| Identity-First | "We [radical action]. Now we do the same for [ICP]." | When your story IS the proof | "We replaced our entire team with AI agents. Revenue went up. We do this for other B2B companies." |
| Question-Flip | "You'd expect me to say [generic pitch]. Instead: [reframe]." | When you want to call out the eye-roll | "I could say 'AI agency' and you'd tune out. Real answer: have you paid for a black box or a tool you still run? We solved both." |
| Outcome-Only | "[Outcome achieved] without [pain]. That's it." | When the outcome is so good it stands alone | "Your growth team, but AI agents, approved by you. No hiring, no black box." |
| Two-Worlds | "[ICP A] do [X]. [ICP B] do [Y]. We combine them." | When you bridge two approaches | "Agencies give you Done-for-you. Tools give you control. We give you both - AI does the work, you approve it." |

### Part 3: Language Pattern Analysis

**Extract patterns from high-converting messages:**

**What TO do:**
- Lead with concrete, relatable nouns (team, work, growth, meetings)
- Use active verbs (build, run, approve, execute)
- Include one number that lands (specific agents, $ saved, 2x revenue)
- Speak in second person ("you") not first person ("we") after opening
- Make the alternative explicit (vs agencies, vs tools)

**What to AVOID:**
- AI jargon: "leverage AI", "AI-powered", "optimize", "scalable"
- Vague benefits: "better results", "faster growth", "more efficient"
- Feature lists: "We do X, Y, and Z"
- Question hooks: "Are you struggling with X?" (feels salesy)

**Compile into a checklist:**

| Do This | Don't Do This | Why |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| "Your growth team is AI agents" | "AI-powered growth solution" | Concrete vs jargon |
| "AI agents replaced our team" | "Leveraging advanced AI" | Specific vs vague |
| etc. | | |

## Research Tips

- Focus on B2B companies (not B2C)
- Look for recent examples (2023-2026) when possible
- Analyze the FIRST thing you see/hear, not the full pitch
- Test reading messages out loud (60 seconds spoken = ~120-150 words)
- Prioritize clarity over cleverness
