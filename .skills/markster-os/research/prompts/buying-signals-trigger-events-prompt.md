# Research Prompt: Buying Signals & Trigger Events Analysis

**To be executed by:** Perplexity, Gemini, or other research-capable LLM
**Estimated time:** 30-45 minutes of research
**Output format:** Markdown document with trigger event map and signal taxonomy

---

## Your Task

You are a sales intelligence analyst. Your client needs to identify the TRIGGER EVENTS that cause B2B companies to start looking for growth solutions, and the BUYING SIGNALS that indicate a prospect is ready to buy.

**Your job:** Map the events that trigger a search for growth help, and identify signals that indicate buying intent.

---

## Background Context

**What this type of service sells:**
- AI agents for outbound, content, pipeline building
- Managed service: $3-15K/mo
- Target: B2B service companies, $500K-$5M revenue

**Why this matters:**
Timing is everything. We need to know:
- What EVENTS trigger a founder/CEO to think "I need growth help"?
- What SIGNALS indicate they're ready to buy (not just browsing)?
- How do we detect these events/signals to prioritize outreach?

---

## Research Questions

1. What events trigger B2B companies to seek growth/marketing/sales solutions?
2. How soon after the trigger event do they start evaluating solutions?
3. What buying signals indicate high intent vs low intent?
4. Where can these signals be detected? (LinkedIn, company website, funding announcements, job posts, etc.)

---

## Research Method

### Part 1: Trigger Event Research

**Search for founder/CEO stories about WHEN they decided to get growth help:**

**Where to look:**
- Reddit: r/startups, r/entrepreneur, r/B2BMarketing
- Indie Hackers: "when did you hire your first..." posts
- LinkedIn: founders posting about "we're hiring" or "we need help with"
- Podcasts: SaaStr, My First Million, Indie Hackers (search "hiring sales" or "scaling marketing")
- Case studies: AI tool/agency websites often mention the trigger

**Search queries:**
- "when to hire first SDR"
- "when to hire growth agency"
- "revenue stalled need help"
- "can't scale outbound"
- "founder doing all the sales"

**Extract 15-20 trigger stories and categorize them:**

**Common trigger event categories:**
1. **Revenue plateau** - Growth stalled for 2+ quarters
2. **Founder capacity** - Founder at max capacity, can't do more
3. **Funding event** - Just raised money, need to deploy it
4. **Team change** - Sales/marketing person left, need to fill gap
5. **New product launch** - Launching new product, need pipeline
6. **Board pressure** - Investors asking "where's the growth?"
7. **Competitor threat** - Competitor growing faster, need to catch up
8. **Bad hire** - Hired someone (SDR, agency), it's not working
9. **Scaling threshold** - Hit $1M ARR, need to professionalize GTM
10. **Seasonal** - Q4 budget planning, Q1 goal-setting

**Compile into a table:**

| Trigger Event | Description | Time to Action | Urgency Level | How to Detect | Example Quote |
|---------------|-------------|----------------|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| Revenue plateau | No growth for 2+ quarters | 1-2 weeks | High | Lack of funding news, no hiring, LinkedIn posts about "tough quarter" | "We've been stuck at $1.5M for 6 months" |
| Founder at capacity | CEO/founder doing all sales | 2-4 weeks | Medium | Founder still listed as sales contact, no SDR/sales hires | "I'm spending 60 hours/week on outbound" |
| Just raised funding | Closed Series A/B | Immediate | High | Crunchbase, LinkedIn announcement | "We raised $5M and need to scale pipeline" |
| Sales person left | SDR/AE/VP Sales departed | 1 week | Urgent | LinkedIn profile change, job posting | "Our SDR quit, pipeline dried up" |
| etc. | | | | | |

### Part 2: Buying Signals Research

**What signals indicate a prospect is actively evaluating solutions?**

**High-intent signals (ready to buy in 1-4 weeks):**
- Posted job req for SDR/growth role (but hasn't hired yet)
- Founder/CEO actively posting about growth challenges on LinkedIn
- Recent funding announcement (within 30 days)
- Demo request or "looking for recommendations" post
- Asking specific questions about implementation/onboarding
- Engaging with competitor content (liking/commenting)
- Replying to cold outreach with interest (not just "not now")

**Medium-intent signals (evaluating in 1-3 months):**
- Posted about growth challenge but not urgently
- Job posting for sales/marketing role (hiring mode, but slow)
- Attended webinar/event about growth topics
- Downloaded content (e.g., "Guide to Scaling Outbound")
- Engaging with educational content (not vendor content)

**Low-intent signals (just browsing):**
- Following competitor on LinkedIn
- General LinkedIn engagement (likes, no comments)
- Visited website once
- No recent growth activity/posts

**Compile into a table:**

| Signal | Intent Level | Time to Buy | Where to Detect | How to Action |
|--------|--------------|-------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Job post: "SDR wanted" | High | 1-2 weeks | LinkedIn Jobs, company careers page | Immediate outreach: "hiring is hard, we can start tomorrow" |
| CEO posts: "pipeline is tough" | High | 1-4 weeks | LinkedIn | Comment + DM offering help |
| Recent funding ($2M+) | High | Immediate-2 weeks | Crunchbase, LinkedIn, press | Congratulations + "here's how to deploy capital into growth" |
| Job post: "VP Sales wanted" | Medium | 1-3 months | LinkedIn Jobs | Nurture: they're hiring senior, might need service later |
| etc. | | | | |

### Part 3: Signal Detection Playbook

**For each high-intent signal, create a detection + action plan:**

**Signal: Job posting for SDR/BDR**

**How to detect:**
- LinkedIn Jobs: Search "SDR" or "BDR" filtered by $500K-$5M company size
- Company careers pages: Monitor target accounts
- Job boards: Indeed, Wellfound (AngelList)

**Outreach timing:** Within 24 hours of posting

**Why this works:** Offers immediate relief while they're still hiring. Worst case: they hire and still use you. Best case: they skip hiring.

---

**Signal: Recent funding announcement**

**How to detect:**
- Crunchbase alerts
- LinkedIn funding posts
- TechCrunch, VentureBeat

**Outreach timing:** 1-7 days after announcement

**Why this works:** Timely, acknowledges their milestone, addresses their likely board ask.

---

**Signal: CEO/Founder posting about growth struggle on LinkedIn**

**How to detect:**
- LinkedIn search: "[target ICP terms] + growth OR pipeline OR outbound"
- Social listening tool (if available)

**Outreach timing:** Within 1-2 hours of post (while engagement is high)

**Outreach method:** Comment publicly first, then DM

**Why this works:** Public comment builds credibility, DM feels personal (not spray-and-pray).

### Part 4: Trigger Event Calendar

**Some events are predictable by season/time:**

| Time Period | Trigger Event | Why It Happens | How to Use This |
|-------------|---------------|----------------|-----------------|
| January | New year goal-setting | Q4 reviews showed pipeline gap, Q1 = fresh start | Outreach: "Q1 is pipeline season. Here's how to hit goals." |
| March-April | Post-Q1 panic | Q1 didn't hit, need to fix Q2 | Outreach: "Q1 miss? Q2 is recoverable if you start now." |
| June | Mid-year reviews | Board/investors asking about H1 performance | Outreach: "H2 starts in 30 days. Let's make it better than H1." |
| September | Q4 budget planning | Companies planning next-year budgets | Outreach: "Budget season. Here's how others are allocating growth spend." |
| November-December | Year-end urgency + "use it or lose it" budget | Need to spend remaining budget, show results before year-end | Outreach: "We can start in Dec and show results by Jan." |

## Research Tips

- Look for TIME-based patterns (when after X event do they buy?)
- High-intent signals are RARE but HIGH-CONVERTING (prioritize these)
- Volume signals (low-intent) are COMMON but LOW-CONVERTING (automate these)
- Founder posts on LinkedIn are gold (public buying signal + warm intro opportunity)
