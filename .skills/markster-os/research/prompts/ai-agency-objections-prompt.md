# Research Prompt: AI Agency/Marketing Objections Analysis

**To be executed by:** Perplexity, Gemini, or other research-capable LLM
**Estimated time:** 30-60 minutes of research
**Output format:** Markdown document with tables and lists

---

## Your Task

You are a B2B market research analyst. Your client sells AI-powered growth systems to B2B companies. When prospects hear "AI marketing agency" or "AI-powered growth," they roll their eyes and mentally dismiss it as "another AI thing."

**Your job:** Research and document WHY B2B buyers are skeptical of AI marketing/agency services, and identify specific patterns that trigger this skepticism.

---

## Background Context

**What this type of service sells:**
- AI agents that run outbound campaigns, content creation, and pipeline building
- Delivered as a managed service (not SaaS)
- Transparent approval mode: clients approve/reject all work before it goes live
- Price: $3-15K/mo (replaces $225K/year in-house team)
- Target: B2B service companies, $500K-$5M revenue

**The problem:**
When founders explain this type of service, prospects immediately ask "how much?" and "what does it do?" but never get to the proof/demo stage because they've already categorized it with "AI tools I've tried" or "agencies that overpromised."

---

## Research Questions

1. What specific experiences have burned B2B buyers on AI marketing tools or agencies?
2. What claims or phrases do AI agencies/tools use that trigger skepticism?
3. What proof or demonstrations actually cut through the skepticism?
4. How do the successful AI service companies position themselves differently?

---

## Research Method

### Part 1: Competitor Positioning Audit

**Find and analyze 15-20 AI marketing/sales tools and agencies:**
- Direct competitors: 11x.ai, Clay, Apollo.io, Instantly.ai, Seamless.ai
- AI agencies: Search Google/LinkedIn for "AI marketing agency", "AI growth agency", "AI SDR service"
- Traditional agencies claiming AI: Search "agency AI-powered" from 2024-2026

**For each competitor, document:**
1. Homepage headline (first sentence)
2. Key phrases used (look for: "leverage AI", "10x your output", "AI-powered workflows", etc.)
3. Proof shown (or not shown): case studies, metrics, client logos, demos
4. Transparency level: Do they show HOW it works, or is it a black box?
5. Pricing model: SaaS subscription, retainer, project-based

**Compile into a table:**

| Company | Category | Homepage Headline | Overused Phrases | Proof Shown | Transparency | Price Model |
|---------|----------|-------------------|------------------|-------------|--------------|-------------|
| 11x.ai | AI SDR | ... | ... | ... | Black box | ... |
| etc. | | | | | | |

### Part 2: Buyer Sentiment Analysis

**Search Reddit, Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Hacker News, Indie Hackers for:**
- "AI marketing tool didn't work"
- "AI agency scam" OR "AI agency waste"
- "tried Clay" OR "tried Apollo" OR "tried 11x"
- "hired AI agency regret"
- "AI SDR" + negative sentiment

**For each theme found (aim for 10-15 posts/comments), extract:**
1. The specific complaint (e.g., "spent $2K, got 3 bad meetings")
2. What they expected vs what they got
3. Root cause of frustration (data quality? spam copy? no results? black box?)
4. What would make them try again (if stated)

**Compile into a table:**

| Source | Quote (verbatim) | Pain Point | Root Cause | What They Wanted Instead |
|--------|------------------|------------|------------|--------------------------|
| Reddit r/sales | "Clay gave me 1000 leads, 80% bounced" | Data quality | No verification | Clean, verified contacts |
| etc. | | | | |

### Part 3: G2/Capterra Review Analysis

**Find reviews for:**
- Clay, Apollo, Instantly, 11x, Seamless (AI tools)
- 3-5 AI marketing agencies (search G2 for "AI marketing agency")

**Focus on 3-star and below reviews. Extract:**
1. Most common complaints (tally frequency)
2. Unmet expectations (what they thought they'd get)
3. Specific failure modes (what broke)

**Compile into a ranked list:**

| Complaint | Frequency | Example Quote |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| Data quality issues | 15x | "60% email bounce rate" |
| etc. | | |

---

## Expected Output

Deliver a markdown document with these sections:

### 1. Objection Pattern Map

A table showing:

| Objection Pattern | How Common | Root Cause | Triggered By (words/claims) | How Transparent AI Service Is Different |
|-------------------|------------|------------|----------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| "I tried AI tools, they didn't work" | Very common | Bad data quality, no human oversight | "AI-powered", "10x output" | Transparent approval mode = human approves everything |
| "Agencies are black boxes" | Common | No visibility into work being done | "We handle everything", "Done for you" | Transparent approval mode shows every decision |
| etc. | | | | |

### 2. Words to Avoid (Banned Phrases)

A list of 20-30 phrases that trigger eye-rolls, with evidence:

| Phrase | Why It Triggers Skepticism | Found Where |
|--------|---------------------------|-------------|
| "Leverage AI" | Overused, meaningless | 12/15 competitor homepages |
| "10x your output" | Implies quantity over quality | Reddit complaints about spam |
| etc. | | |

### 3. Positioning Gaps to Exploit

What are competitors NOT doing that creates an opening?

| Gap | Evidence | Opportunity |
|-----|----------|-------------|
| No transparency | All competitors are black boxes | Lead with approval mode visibility |
| No proof of quality | Show lead counts, not meeting quality | Show meeting -> close rates, not volume |
| etc. | | |

### 4. Proof That Cuts Through

What evidence do buyers actually trust?

| Proof Type | Effectiveness | Where Seen | How to Use This |
|------------|---------------|------------|-----------------|
| Before/after screenshots | High | Gong, Clay | Show approval UI + workflow |
| Client ROI (not vanity metrics) | High | Rare | Show specific revenue impact |
| etc. | | | |

### 5. Key Insights Summary

3-5 bullet points synthesizing the findings:
- What's the #1 reason buyers are skeptical?
- What claims are most dangerous to make?
- What proof matters most?
- What positioning angle is unexploited?

---

## Research Tips

- Use Perplexity's citation mode to include sources
- Prioritize recent content (2024-2026) for buyer sentiment
- Look for SPECIFIC complaints, not vague dissatisfaction
- When analyzing competitor copy, note the exact phrases (verbatim)
- For reviews, focus on patterns across multiple sources, not one-off complaints

---

## Deliverable

A single markdown file (or Google Doc) with all tables and insights formatted clearly.
