# Research Prompt: Buyer Jobs-to-be-Done Analysis

**To be executed by:** Perplexity, Gemini, or other research-capable LLM
**Estimated time:** 45-60 minutes of research
**Output format:** Markdown document with tables and buyer personas

---

## Your Task

You are a buyer research analyst. Your client needs to understand what "job" B2B buyers are actually trying to accomplish when they consider hiring an agency, buying an AI tool, or building an in-house growth team.

**Your job:** Use the Jobs-to-be-Done framework to map what buyers are hiring this category to DO (not what they say they want, but the underlying outcome they need).

---

## Background Context

**What this type of service sells:**
- AI agents that run outbound, content, and pipeline building
- Managed service: $3-15K/mo
- Transparent approval mode: client approves all work before execution
- Target: B2B service companies, $500K-$5M revenue

**The insight:**
When a buyer "hires" a growth solution (agency, tool, team), they're not buying features. They're trying to accomplish a job in their business. We need to understand:
- What job are they hiring a solution to DO?
- What are they trying to STOP doing?
- What outcome defines success?
- What constraints or "anxieties" affect the decision?

---

## Research Questions

1. What are the core "jobs" B2B buyers are trying to accomplish when they seek growth help?
2. What are the competing solutions for each job? (build in-house, hire agency, buy tool, do nothing)
3. What "forces" push buyers toward a new solution vs pull them back to status quo?
4. What does success look like for each job (outcome metrics, not feature lists)?

---

## Research Method

### Part 1: Job Story Extraction

**Search for buyer stories in these places:**
- Reddit: r/sales, r/marketing, r/entrepreneur, r/B2BMarketing
- Indie Hackers: posts about hiring for growth/marketing
- LinkedIn: founders posting about growth challenges
- G2/Capterra reviews: "I needed to..." statements in reviews
- Podcasts: Search "hiring growth" or "scaling marketing" (SaaStr, My First Million, Indie Hackers)

**Search queries:**
- "need to scale outbound"
- "can't afford full-time SDR"
- "tired of managing agency"
- "tried [tool] but still doing the work"
- "how to grow without hiring"

**What to extract (10-15 job stories):**

For each story, document:
1. **Who:** Company size, industry, role of decision-maker
2. **Current situation:** What are they doing now?
3. **Trigger:** What event made them look for a new solution?
4. **Job to be done:** What outcome do they need? (verb + object, e.g., "generate qualified pipeline")
5. **Success metric:** How do they measure if the job is done?
6. **Alternatives considered:** What else did they look at?
7. **Decision criteria:** What made them choose (or not choose) each option?

**Compile into a table:**

| Buyer Profile | Current Situation | Trigger | Job to Be Done | Success Metric | Alternatives | Decision Criteria |
|---------------|-------------------|---------|----------------|----------------|--------------|-------------------|
| $2M SaaS, CEO | Founder doing all outreach | Hit capacity, missing deals | Generate 10 qualified meetings/mo | Meeting -> demo rate >50% | Hire SDR ($80K), Agency ($5K/mo), Tool (Clay) | Cost, speed to results, trust |
| etc. | | | | | | |

### Part 2: Jobs Taxonomy

**Based on job stories, categorize into 3-5 core jobs:**

Common jobs in B2B growth (validate these with research):
1. **"Generate qualified pipeline"** - Need meetings with ICP, not just leads
2. **"Free up founder time"** - Founder is bottleneck, needs to delegate
3. **"Prove growth is working"** - Need visibility/metrics to show board/investors
4. **"Scale without headcount"** - Can't afford or don't want to hire
5. **"Fix broken growth function"** - Hired someone/something, it's not working

**For each job, document:**

| Job | Description | When It's Hired | Outcome Metric | Competing Solutions | Why Each Fails |
|-----|-------------|-----------------|----------------|---------------------|----------------|
| Generate qualified pipeline | Need X meetings/mo with ICP | Revenue stalled, founder at capacity | Meetings booked -> demos scheduled | SDR hire, Agency, Tool | SDR: expensive/slow. Agency: black box. Tool: you run it. |
| Free up founder time | Founder spending 40+ hrs/mo on GTM | Founder burning out, product suffering | Hours saved per week | Hire VP Sales, Agency, Freelancer | VP: too senior/expensive. Agency: no control. Freelancer: inconsistent. |
| etc. | | | | | |

### Part 3: Forces Diagram (Push/Pull Analysis)

**For each core job, map the forces:**

**Push (toward new solution):**
- What's broken/painful about current state?
- What event triggers the search?

**Pull (toward status quo):**
- What makes buyers hesitate or stick with current approach?
- What anxieties prevent change?

**Document for each job:**

| Job | Push Forces (toward new solution) | Pull Forces (toward status quo) | How Transparent AI Service Addresses Pull |
|-----|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Generate qualified pipeline | Stalled revenue, founder capacity | Fear of wasting $, been burned before | Transparent approval mode = no black box, see everything |
| etc. | | | |

### Part 4: Buyer Persona Mapping

**Create 3-4 buyer personas based on jobs:**

| Persona Name | Company Profile | Primary Job | Secondary Job | Current Approach | Pain Point | Buying Criteria | Decision Timeline |
|--------------|----------------|-------------|---------------|------------------|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| "Overwhelmed Founder" | $500K-$2M, 3-10 employees | Free up founder time | Generate pipeline | Doing it all themselves | Burning out, 60hr weeks | Must save time, not add complexity | 2-4 weeks |
| "Scaling CEO" | $2M-$5M, 10-30 employees | Scale without headcount | Prove growth is working | Has 1-2 marketing people, not enough | Can't hire fast enough | Cost per result, visibility | 4-8 weeks |
| "Agency-Burned VP" | $3M-$8M, 20-50 employees | Fix broken growth | Generate pipeline | Hired agency, getting no results | Paying $8K/mo for black box | Transparency, accountability | 1-3 weeks (urgent) |
| etc. | | | | | | | |

## Expected Output

Deliver a markdown document with these sections:

### 1. Job Stories (Raw Data)
Table of 10-15 job stories extracted from research (Part 1).

### 2. Jobs Taxonomy
Table of 3-5 core jobs with descriptions, outcomes, competing solutions (Part 2).

### 3. Forces Diagrams
For each core job, the push/pull analysis (Part 3).

### 4. Buyer Personas
3-4 persona profiles with jobs, pain points, buying criteria (Part 4).

### 5. Strategic Insights
Answer these questions:
- Which job is this service BEST positioned to solve? (where is the unfair advantage?)
- Which job has the most buyers? (market size)
- Which job has the least competition? (white space)
- Which job should we lead with in positioning?

### 6. Messaging Implications
Based on jobs analysis:
- **For "Generate qualified pipeline" job:** Lead with [X], prove with [Y]
- **For "Free up founder time" job:** Lead with [X], prove with [Y]
- etc.

---

## Research Tips

- Look for "I needed to..." statements in reviews and forum posts
- Focus on TRIGGERS (what event caused them to search)
- Ignore what buyers say they want, focus on what outcome they need
- Competing solutions include "do nothing" (status quo always competes)
- Pull forces are often more powerful than push forces (anxiety wins)

---

## Deliverable

A single markdown file with all sections - this will inform positioning, messaging, and sales strategy.
