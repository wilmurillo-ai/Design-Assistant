# Market Intelligence

This directory contains primary market research for the AI-native B2B services category. The files here are reference material -- not positioning copy, not sales scripts. They exist so that GTM decisions, pricing choices, and positioning claims are grounded in external evidence rather than internal assumptions.

**Read at least one file from this directory before starting any of the following:**
- GTM strategy work
- Pricing or packaging decisions
- Investor conversations or pitch prep
- Positioning revisions or messaging updates
- ICP refinement or vertical prioritization

---

## Files in This Directory

### `ai-native-services.md`

The most comprehensive file. A full structural analysis of why the AI-native managed services category exists, why now, and what risks every operator must hold honestly.

Sections:
- Investor side: VC consensus from Sequoia, a16z, YC, Emergence, and Bessemer -- what each firm published and where they agree and diverge
- Client side: What SMB professional service firms actually want, what they will pay for, and vertical-level evidence for accounting, HVAC, and IT/MSP
- Supply side: Inference cost collapse, outsourcing market size, SaaS structural decline
- Framework comparison table across five major VC firms
- Risk section: The "$0.03 problem," commoditization risk, SMB cognitive gap, quality at scale, race to the bottom, liability exposure
- Market sizing: TAM by segment, why the outsourcing behavior already exists in this buyer segment, proof comparables (Hatch/Yelp acquisition)

Read this first if you are doing investor prep, validating the thesis, or arguing for or against a vertical.

---

### `market-trends.md`

Ten macro trends in B2B GTM and AI-native services for 2026. Sourced from Forrester, YC, Sequoia, Skaled, and others.

Split into two sections:
- Trends already shaping the category (Service-as-Software, agentic multi-agent systems, unified GTM, buyer trust crisis, ABM/personalization depth, short pilots)
- Trends to monitor but not act on yet (AI buyer agents, experiential marketing, benchmark publishing opportunity)

Each trend includes a brief description and an implication note -- what the trend means for positioning and sales motion.

Read this before writing content strategy, updating a pitch deck, or preparing for an investor conversation where macro tailwinds will come up.

---

### `market-segments.md`

TAM/SAM/SOM breakdown and a 14-segment SF Bay Area agency census with AI adoption levels and relative opportunity ratings.

Includes:
- Total addressable market table with national and Bay Area figures
- Segment-by-segment opportunity analysis (which segments are crowded, which are white space)
- AI-native agency competitive landscape summary
- Beachhead math: IT/MSP entry metrics -- clients needed per ARR milestone, why penetration required is tiny, referral velocity logic
- Geographic expansion path from Bay Area to national

Read this before any ICP refinement, vertical prioritization, or go/no-go decision on a new segment.

## How to Use These Files

**Before GTM work:** Read `market-segments.md` for segment clarity, `market-trends.md` for macro context.

**Before investor conversations:** Read `ai-native-services.md` Sections 1 and 4. Know the VC thesis well enough to riff on it, not just recite it. Know the risks in Section 5 before anyone asks.

**Before positioning revisions:** Read `market-trends.md` for the buyer trust shift and short-pilot trend. Read `ai-native-services.md` Section 2 for what SMB buyers are actually paying for.

**Before vertical expansion decisions:** Read `market-segments.md` beachhead math and `ai-native-services.md` Section 6 for vertical selection logic (Sequoia's talent-shortage + outsourcing-already-normalized filter).

## Update Cadence

**Quarterly.** These files go stale fast in a category moving this quickly.

Suggested quarterly review checklist:
- Check for new VC publications on AI-native services (Sequoia, a16z, Emergence, Bessemer, YC RFS updates)
- Update AI inference pricing benchmarks in `ai-native-services.md` Section 3 -- costs are still falling
- Check SaaS growth rate data and revise the SaaS decline section if trends have shifted
- Update SMB outsourcing statistics (Emapta, US Chamber of Commerce, Intuit reports release annually)
- Check for new M&A comps in the category (Hatch/Yelp is currently the reference; look for new acquisitions)
- Update Bay Area agency census counts if there has been significant consolidation or new entrants
- Re-evaluate which "monitor" trends have moved to "act" status

**Who updates:** Whoever owns GTM research at the time. Changes should be committed with a clear note on what changed and what the new source is. Do not edit without a source -- these files are evidence, not opinion.

## Source Quality Notes

Most statistics in these files are sourced from primary research (Forrester, Gartner, Stanford AI Index, BLS, SBA, US Chamber of Commerce) or directly from published VC frameworks (Sequoia, a16z, Emergence, Bessemer). LinkedIn commentary and substack analysis are used only for framing, never for statistics. All reference URLs are included inline in `ai-native-services.md`.

If a statistic is surprising, check the footnote before repeating it in external materials.
