# F3: Organizational Structure

**The question:** Who owns what, who decides what, and how does the business run when the founder is not involved?

---

## Why this step matters

Most service businesses under 20 people have one person implicitly responsible for everything: the founder. This is not an organizational structure. It is a single point of failure.

When the founder is the default owner of every decision -- client delivery, pipeline, operations, finances -- the business cannot scale. It cannot hire effectively, cannot delegate reliably, and cannot operate without the founder's daily involvement.

F3 fixes this by establishing clear ownership before you add people. Ownership without clarity multiplies chaos. Ownership with clarity enables delegation, accountability, and eventual independence from the founder.

---

## The GOD Ownership Model

ScaleOS uses a simple ownership model: one owner per pillar, one owner per brick.

Each of the three GOD Engine pillars has a single owner who is accountable for that pillar's North Star metric:

| Pillar | North Star | Owner Accountable For |
|--------|------------|----------------------|
| Growth | Qualified meetings booked per week | Finding, warming, and booking qualified prospects |
| Operations | Hours saved per week through systems | Documenting, automating, and measuring the business |
| Delivery | Net Revenue Retention | Delivering, proving, and expanding client relationships |

The owner does not have to do all the work. They are accountable for the result. The distinction matters: a Growth Owner who runs all outreach themselves has not built a system. A Growth Owner whose pillar produces meetings consistently -- whether or not they are personally sending messages -- has.

**Ownership Rules:**

1. One owner per pillar. No co-ownership. If two people are responsible, no one is.
2. One owner per brick. Each of the nine bricks has a named person accountable for its metric.
3. Decisions within a pillar belong to the pillar owner. The founder does not override unless the decision is strategic.
4. Ownership without resources is not ownership. The owner needs budget, tools, and time to be held accountable.

---

## Team Structure by Stage

**Small team (1-5 people):** The founder owns all three pillars initially. The goal is to identify which pillar a first hire can take over. Document before hiring. The hire should inherit a working system, not build one from scratch.

**Growing team (5-15 people):** Dedicated owners for each pillar. Growth Owner (full-time on G1-G3), Operations Owner (responsible for O1-O3), Delivery Owner (owns D1-D3). Founder moves to strategic oversight.

**Scaling team (15+ people):** Each brick has a dedicated owner or sub-team. Pillar owners manage brick owners. Founder's role is Foundation-level decisions: positioning, pricing, organizational structure itself.

## Decision Rights Framework

Three levels of decisions. Each level has a defined owner:

| Level | Decision Type | Who Decides |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| Strategic | Positioning, pricing, hiring, partnerships | Founder or CEO |
| Pillar | Tools, processes, tactics within a pillar | Pillar Owner |
| Daily | Task prioritization, client communications, execution | Individual |

Decisions escalate when they cross a level. A Delivery Owner who wants to change the onboarding process does not need founder approval -- that is a pillar-level decision. A Delivery Owner who wants to change pricing requires founder approval -- that is a strategic decision.

**Decision Rights Template:**

| Decision | Decider | Consulted | Informed | Timeline | Reversibility |
|----------|---------|-----------|----------|----------|---------------|
| [decision] | [name/role] | [who] | [who] | [by when] | [easy/hard] |

Document 5-10 recurring decisions that currently create confusion. Assign a Decider for each. This alone eliminates a significant amount of founder bottleneck.

---

## The Founder Bottleneck

The $1M-$3M plateau is where most founder-led B2B service businesses stall. Revenue is real, clients are paying, but growth has stopped. The founder is at capacity. Every new client requires founder involvement. Every problem escalates to the founder. Every decision waits for the founder.

This is Founder Jail. Unable to scale but unwilling to let go.

The exit from Founder Jail requires three things:
1. Documented systems so others can execute without instruction
2. Clear ownership so others make decisions without asking
3. Accountability structures so the founder can verify results without being in the work

F3 builds the ownership layer. O1 (Standardize) builds the documentation layer. Both are prerequisites before the business can grow beyond the founder's personal capacity.

---

## Questions to answer in writing

1. Who currently owns each pillar in your business -- named, not implied?
2. Which decisions require the founder today that should not require the founder?
3. Can the business run for two weeks without founder involvement? What breaks first?
4. What is the first pillar you could hand to a dedicated owner?
5. What would you need to document before you could hire that person?
