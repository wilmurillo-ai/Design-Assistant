# Channel Guide

**The question:** Where do you reach your ICP first, given their profile and buying behavior?

---

## Why this step matters

Channel selection is not a preference. It is a function of your ICP's characteristics. A channel that works for one ICP type consistently fails for another.

Cold email works brilliantly for operational buyers at growth-stage B2B firms. It is nearly useless for institutional buyers at enterprise companies who route all vendor contacts through procurement.

Content works for ICPs who are actively researching a category and have time to read. It does not work for ICPs who are heads-down in execution mode and barely open LinkedIn.

Events work when your ICP is reliably in the same physical space and values peer connection. They fail when your ICP has stopped attending conferences due to budget cuts or schedule constraints.

This step gives you a framework to select the right channel for your specific ICP rather than copying what works for a different kind of company.

---

## Channel selection matrix

Score your ICP against these characteristics, then read the channel recommendations below.

For each row, mark which description fits your ICP better:

| Characteristic | Column A | Column B |
|----------------|----------|----------|
| Deal size | Under $5,000 | $5,000 and above |
| Decision-maker access | Direct (one person decides) | Committee or gatekeeper |
| Buying timeline | Days to weeks | Weeks to months |
| Trust requirement | Low (tries before committing) | High (needs proof before committing) |
| Research behavior | Searches actively, reads content | Relies on referrals and peer recommendations |
| Awareness of problem | Already knows they have the problem | Not yet aware or not yet naming it |
| Outbound receptivity | Responds to cold outreach | Ignores unsolicited contact |
| Community activity | Active in forums, groups, events | Stays inside their own network |

If most of your answers are Column A, start with cold outreach. If most are Column B, start with referral activation or events.

## Channel profiles

### Cold email
**Best for:** Column A ICPs. Direct decision-maker, $2,000-$15,000 deal size, aware of the problem, willing to engage with cold outreach, B2B service or SaaS.
**Realistic expectations:** 1-3% reply rate, 10-20% of replies convert to calls, 20-40% of calls lead to proposals. Volume matters. You need to reach 200-500 contacts before drawing conclusions.
**What it requires:** Verified email addresses, personalized first line, message that passes the "sounds like everyone else" test, inbox setup that lands in primary, a follow-up sequence.
**Time to results:** 3-6 weeks for first booked meetings. 8-12 weeks for a real data set.
**Playbook:** [cold-email](../../playbooks/book/cold-email/README.md)

### Warm outbound (LinkedIn + direct message)
**Best for:** ICPs where you have a warm signal (they visited your content, commented on a post, connected but never converted, or are second-degree via a mutual you can name). Medium deal size. Trust-based buying.
**Realistic expectations:** 5-15% reply rate for truly warm outreach. Better conversion than cold but lower volume.
**What it requires:** Content presence that generates the signals, a system for tracking who engaged, personalized messages that reference the specific signal.
**Time to results:** 4-8 weeks once your content system generates signals.

### Events
**Best for:** ICPs who attend predictable events. High-value deals. Relationship-based buying. Trust required before purchase.
**Realistic expectations:** 10-30 conversations per event, 3-10 qualified follow-up meetings, 1-3 deals per event cycle (4-6 month timeline from event to close).
**What it requires:** Event selection based on ICP attendance (not topic relevance), pre-event outreach, during-event conversation strategy, post-event sequence within 48 hours.
**Time to results:** First deals typically close 90-180 days after first event.
**Playbook:** [events](../../playbooks/warm/events/README.md)

### Content
**Best for:** ICPs who are actively researching, have time to read, and make trust-based decisions. Longer sales cycles. High-trust categories (finance, legal, professional services, tech).
**Realistic expectations:** Slow burn. First inbound inquiry from content typically takes 3-6 months to materialize. But content shortens all other channel conversion rates when it is running in parallel.
**What it requires:** A clear point of view, consistent publishing on the platform where your ICP reads, distribution strategy beyond just posting.
**Time to results:** 3-6 months for first inbound. 12 months to compound.
**Playbook:** [content](../../playbooks/warm/content/README.md)

### Referral activation
**Best for:** Any ICP type. Referrals have the highest close rate and shortest sales cycle of any channel because trust is transferred, not earned.
**Realistic expectations:** 50-80% close rate on referred leads. But referrals are not a system unless you build one.
**What it requires:** Proactively asking current clients for introductions. Giving referral partners a framing script. Creating a reason for them to send you business (reciprocity, incentive, or simply a clear ask).
**Time to results:** First referrals can materialize within days of asking the right people.
**What most companies miss:** They rely on referrals passively and call it a channel. Passive referral is not a channel - it is luck. Active referral activation is a system.

## When to use multiple channels

For companies under 20 people, running more than two channels simultaneously almost always degrades execution quality. Pick one primary channel and one support channel.

**Primary channel:** Your highest-probability motion given your ICP profile. Put 80% of your execution focus here.
**Support channel:** The channel that warms up your primary channel leads. Most commonly, content warms up cold outreach by making your name recognizable. Events warm up LinkedIn outreach by giving you a conversation reference.

Do not launch all channels at once. Validate one. Then add the second.

## Channel selection output

Answer these to confirm your channel selection:

1. What is my primary channel and why does it match my ICP profile?
2. What is my support channel?
3. What does success look like in the first 30 days for my primary channel?
4. What metrics will I track to know if the channel is working?
5. At what point (dates, metrics) will I evaluate whether to add or swap a channel?
