# Stage 4: Established

**Revenue range:** $2M+

---

## Situation

Mature operations. Optimization focus. The business runs without daily founder involvement. The founder operates at the strategy layer: positioning, pricing, organizational structure, major partnerships. Leadership team runs daily operations.

The central challenge at Stage 4 is converting operational scale into durable margin, and preventing the system from becoming brittle under growth pressure.

---

## Priorities

1. **D3: Expand optimization** -- NRR target 110% or above. Expansion revenue should exceed churn. The business grows without needing to replace churned clients.
2. **F4: Financial Architecture** -- Capital allocation is formal. Unit economics are reviewed quarterly. LTV:CAC, gross margin, and payback period are tracked at the leadership level.
3. **Leadership development** -- Pillar owners are developing into leaders who can run their function independently and make strategic decisions without founder input.

---

## Ongoing Cadences

**Daily:** Leadership team runs operations. Founder is available but not required.

**Weekly:** Pillar owners review North Star metrics. Escalations to founder are exceptions, not defaults.

**Monthly:** Leadership team reviews metric trends and quarterly goal progress. Founder participates but does not lead.

**Quarterly (half day):** Foundation review. GOD Engine assessment. Set priorities for next quarter. Update documentation. Annual planning.

---

## Success Signal

**Stage 4 ongoing health:** 30% or more year-over-year growth without proportional headcount increase. Margin expands as revenue grows.

Supporting signals:
- NRR consistently above 110%
- No single client represents more than 20% of revenue
- Founder is focused on strategy only -- the business runs for 4 weeks without founder operational involvement
- Leadership team makes director-level decisions without founder routing
- New hires onboard in under 30 days using documented systems

---

## Common Mistake

**Complacency.** Revenue is strong. Systems are working. The instinct is to protect what exists rather than continue improving. The businesses that stall at Stage 4 stop running quarterly Foundation reviews, let documentation go stale, and lose their North Star metric discipline. The GOD Engine requires maintenance to compound. Quarterly reviews and weekly cadences are not Stage 1 habits to outgrow -- they are the operating system of a scaled business.
