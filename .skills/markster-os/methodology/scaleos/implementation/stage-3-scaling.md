# Stage 3: Scaling

**Revenue range:** $500K - $2M

---

## Situation

Team building. Removing the founder from day-to-day operations. The business has a repeatable GTM system. Deals close consistently. The question is no longer whether the model works -- it is how to add volume without the founder being the operational ceiling.

The central challenge at Stage 3 is the transition from founder-dependent to system-dependent. Every function the founder still owns personally is a bottleneck.

---

## Priorities

1. **F3: Organizational Structure** -- Assign named owners to all nine bricks. Weekly accountability cadence. Decision rights documented and followed.
2. **Full Delivery pillar** -- D1 (Deliver), D2 (Prove), D3 (Expand) all running systematically with a named owner.
3. **All 9 bricks have owners** -- Not "the team is responsible." Named persons accountable for named metrics.

---

## Skip List

At Stage 3, the risk is not doing too little -- it is adding complexity before the existing system is solid:

- Do not launch a new product line until current delivery NRR is above 100%
- Do not hire a director for a function that is not yet systematized
- Do not expand to a new ICP until the current ICP pipeline is stable and predictable

---

## Quarterly Timeline

**Q1: Assign owners to all 9 bricks, establish weekly rhythm**
- Name a Growth Owner, Operations Owner, and Delivery Owner (can be the same person temporarily if team is small).
- Each brick has a named accountable person, even if they are the founder for now.
- Weekly review cadence: Growth / Operations / Delivery, 30 minutes per pillar, every Friday.

**Q2: Systematize the full Delivery pillar and optimize Expand**
- D1 (Deliver): Onboarding process documented and running without founder oversight.
- D2 (Prove): Proof collection cadence in place. 1-2 case studies per quarter minimum.
- D3 (Expand): Health scoring in place. Every client has a health score reviewed monthly. Expansion conversations happen systematically, not reactively.

---

## Success Signal

**Stage 3 is complete when:** The business runs for two weeks without founder involvement -- including sales, delivery, and operations.

Supporting signals:
- All 9 bricks have named owners other than the founder (or founder has a defined exit plan for their remaining brick ownership)
- NRR is above 100% (expansion revenue exceeds churn)
- The weekly review cadence runs without the founder initiating it
- A deal has closed without founder involvement in late-stage conversations

---

## Common Mistake

**The founder cannot let go.** The founder is still in everything -- reviewing every proposal, approving every delivery decision, being the first call for every escalation. This is Founder Jail at Stage 3. The business has enough revenue and team to scale, but the founder's continued involvement in execution prevents it.

The solution is not to work harder at delegation -- it is to build systems that do not require the founder's input. When a decision reaches the founder, the question to ask is not "what is the right answer" but "why is this reaching me, and what system would have handled it without me."
