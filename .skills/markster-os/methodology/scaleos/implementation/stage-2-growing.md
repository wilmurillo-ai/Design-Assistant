# Stage 2: Growing

**Revenue range:** $100K - $500K

---

## Situation

Revenue is happening. Seeking consistency. Team forming. The founder still does most things but systems are beginning. The bottleneck shifts from clarity to capacity -- you no longer wonder if your model works, you are running out of hours to do more of it.

---

## Priorities

1. **Full Growth pillar** -- G1, G2, G3 working as an integrated pipeline, not just founder-driven outreach.
2. **O2: Automate** -- Use automation to save 20+ hours per week. Repetitive tasks in G1, G3, and D1 are the first targets.
3. **O3: Instrument** -- Build the North Star dashboard. Three metrics minimum: meetings booked per week, hours saved per week, NRR.

---

## Skip List

Do not build these at Stage 2:

- Complex multi-tier expansion programs (D3 Expand is basic at this stage)
- Advanced financial modeling or capital allocation frameworks
- Full organizational structure changes (team is not large enough to require it)
- Multiple paid acquisition channels simultaneously

---

## Month-by-Month Timeline

**Month 1: Systematize Find and Book**
- G1: Automate list building. Apollo or similar pulling 50-100 new ICP-match prospects per week automatically.
- G3: Move outreach from manual LinkedIn to a structured email sequence with a warmed sending domain.
- Target: qualified outreach running without daily founder setup.

**Month 2: First automations saving 10+ hours per week**
- Identify the highest-volume repetitive tasks: meeting confirmation sequences, onboarding document generation, follow-up emails.
- Automate the top 3 using Make or Zapier or n8n.
- Track hours saved per week. Target: 10-20 hours saved by end of month 2.

**Month 3: North Star dashboard live**
- Build the three-panel dashboard: Growth (meetings booked), Operations (hours saved), Delivery (NRR).
- Review every Friday. Decisions made from data, not instinct.
- Target: 20+ hours per week recovered through systems.

---

## Success Signal

**Stage 2 is complete when:** 20 or more hours per week are recovered through systems -- without reducing revenue or delivery quality.

Supporting signals:
- Full Growth pillar (G1-G3) runs without daily founder involvement
- At least one team member other than the founder is executing delivery using documented processes
- North Star metrics are tracked weekly and reviewed
- First automation stack saving meaningful time

---

## Common Mistake

**Hiring before systematizing.** The instinct at Stage 2 is to hire to solve the capacity problem. This multiplies chaos. A new hire in an unsystematized business inherits undocumented processes, escalates every edge case to the founder, and reduces net capacity for the first 60-90 days. Systematize first. Then hire into a working system.
