# Stage 1: Early-Stage

**Revenue range:** $0 - $100K

---

## Situation

First customers. Proving the concept. The founder does everything. The goal is not to build infrastructure -- it is to gather enough signal to know what to systematize. The bottleneck is clarity, not capacity.

Stage 1 ends when you can describe who buys from you, why they buy, and how you win -- without hedging.

---

## Priorities

1. **G1-G3 systematized** -- Growth pillar working as a manual pipeline. ICP defined, outreach running, meetings booking consistently.
2. **O1: Standardize** -- Document your core delivery process. Write it down before you hire anyone to help deliver.
3. **D2: Prove** -- Start collecting proof. Even one specific result documented becomes the foundation of your first case study.

---

## Skip List

Do not build these at Stage 1:

- Advanced workflow automation
- Complex analytics dashboards
- Expansion or upsell programs
- Paid acquisition channels
- Detailed org charts or role definitions (team is still too small)

---

## Month-by-Month Timeline

**Month 1: Document core delivery**
- Write the delivery process step by step. Even if it is just you delivering, write how you would explain it to a new hire.
- Document the deal pattern: trigger > outreach > conversation > proposal > close. This becomes your first sales playbook.

**Month 2: First nurture sequence**
- Build a simple 3-5 touch follow-up sequence for warm contacts who have not yet bought.
- Content does not need to be elaborate. One useful insight per email. Problem-first, not feature-first.

**Month 3: First case study**
- Interview your first paying client (or best prospect) about the result.
- Use the Case Study Template: situation before, what was done, specific result, their words.
- This single asset multiplies the effectiveness of every channel.

---

## Success Signal

**Stage 1 is complete when:** You can hand off one delivery task to someone else using your documentation, and they execute it without asking you how.

Supporting signals:
- 20+ discovery conversations held with real ICP-match companies
- 2-3 paying clients or signed deals
- Deal pattern documented: trigger - outreach - conversation - proposal - close
- You know which 1-2 outreach angles consistently produce replies
- Positive reply rate on outreach: 5-10% consistently

---

## Common Mistake

**Skipping documentation.** The instinct is "I will remember." You will not. The moment you hire your first contractor, or try to delegate delivery, you will discover that every undocumented process lives in your head. Document while the process is fresh, even if it feels premature.

Do not move to Stage 2 before the documentation signal is present. Hiring before systematizing multiplies chaos.
