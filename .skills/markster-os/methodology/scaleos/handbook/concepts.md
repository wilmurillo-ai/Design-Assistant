# Key Concepts

The core mental models and named concepts of ScaleOS. These apply across Foundation, GOD Engine, and implementation.

---

## Founder Jail

The $1M-$3M revenue plateau where founder-led B2B service businesses stall.

The founder is at personal capacity. Every new client requires founder involvement. Every problem escalates to the founder. Every decision waits for the founder. Revenue is real, clients are paying, but growth has stopped because the founder is the bottleneck.

Unable to scale but unwilling to let go.

The exit from Founder Jail requires documented systems (O1), clear ownership (F3), and accountability structures (O3) that allow the business to run without founder involvement in execution. The goal is not to remove the founder -- it is to make the founder optional in execution so they can operate at the strategy layer.

---

## Revenue System

Integrated infrastructure of strategies, processes, and tools generating predictable sales without founder dependency.

A Revenue System is distinct from "doing marketing" or "having a CRM" or "running outreach." It is the complete, systematized pipeline from ICP identification through booking, delivery, and expansion -- running continuously with named owners and measurable outcomes.

A founder who personally generates all revenue does not have a Revenue System. They have a job. The distinction is whether the pipeline functions when the founder is absent.

---

## The Pricing Test

"If you're not losing some deals on price, you're probably underpriced."

Losing 10-20% of deals on price is the signal that pricing is correctly calibrated. Losing zero deals on price means there is margin being left on the table, buyers are not making price-based tradeoffs, or the client base is underqualified.

The test is not about maximizing deal loss. It is about calibrating price to the point where it is a real consideration in the buyer's decision -- which is the point at which you are capturing the actual value you create.

---

## LTV:CAC Rule

The foundational unit economics health metric.

- **Below 2:1:** The business is buying revenue unprofitably. Every new client costs more to acquire than they return.
- **2:1 to 3:1:** Marginal. Can survive but cannot scale.
- **3:1 minimum** before scaling. Below this threshold, scaling accelerates losses.
- **5:1+ target.** This is the economics that support sustainable growth, team investment, and marketing spend.

Calculate honestly: CAC must include founder time at its true opportunity cost, not just cash spend. Most founders dramatically undercount CAC.

---

## The Proof Loop

The compounding cycle that links Delivery and Growth.

```
Deliver well
    -> Document the result (D2: Prove)
        -> Reference in outreach and content (G2: Warm, G3: Book)
            -> Book better-fit clients
                -> Deliver well
```

Each turn of the loop produces better proof, which attracts better clients, which produces better results, which produces better proof. The loop compounds when D2 is systematized -- proof is collected and used without requiring founder effort.

Most businesses break the loop at D2: they deliver well but never document the results. The proof stays in the founder's memory, unreferenceable and un-scalable.

## Implementation Rhythm

The cadences that keep ScaleOS running.

**Daily:** Execute. Note friction points. Update metrics.

**Weekly (Friday, 30 minutes per pillar):**
- What worked this week?
- What did not work?
- One improvement to make next week.

**Monthly (2 hours):**
- Review metric trends across all three pillars.
- Assess quarterly goal progress.
- Pick one brick to improve this month.

**Quarterly (half day):**
- Full Foundation review (F1-F4).
- Full GOD Engine assessment (all 9 bricks).
- Set priorities for next quarter.
- Update and refresh documentation.

The rhythm is not optional at any stage. It is the operating system's heartbeat. Companies that stop weekly reviews stop improving.

---

## The Getting Started Sequence

Five steps. In order. Do not skip ahead.

1. **Assess current state** -- Score all 9 bricks 1-10 using the GOD Engine assessment. Be honest about what is actually running, not what you plan to do.

2. **Identify the Weakest Brick** -- The lowest-scoring brick is your first focus. Not the one you want to work on. The one the data says is most broken.

3. **Define success for that brick** -- Name the metric that proves it is working. Name the process. Name the owner. Write it down.

4. **Build v1** -- "70% complete and shipped beats 100% complete and theoretical." Build the minimum viable version of the system for that brick. Ship it. Run it for 30-90 days.

5. **Establish the Rhythm** -- Put the weekly review on the calendar before doing anything else. The system only compounds if it is reviewed and improved on a consistent cadence.

## Tagline

"Amateurs hustle. Pros systemize."

Hustle produces bursts. Systems produce compounding. The difference between a $500K business and a $3M business is not effort -- it is the presence or absence of systems that run without the founder.
