## F1: Positioning & Differentiation

### ICP Specificity

Your ICP isn't "B2B companies" or "small businesses." It's surgical.

**Weak ICP:** "Marketing agencies"

**Strong ICP:** "$2M-$8M marketing agencies in English-speaking markets who lost a major client in last 90 days and need to rebuild pipeline before they hemorrhage team."

The more specific, the easier to find, message, and convert.

## F2: Business Model Design

### Revenue Model Options

| Model | Characteristics | Best For |
|-------|-----------------|---------|
| Project-based | One-time, defined scope | Complex implementations |
| Retainer/Recurring | Monthly/annual commitment | Ongoing services |
| Productized service | Fixed scope, fixed price | Scalable delivery |
| Usage-based | Pay per consumption | Variable demand |
| Hybrid | Mix of above | Mature businesses |

### Pricing Architecture

**Cost-plus pricing:** Your costs + margin = price
- Problem: Ignores value delivered, races to bottom

**Competitor-based pricing:** Match or undercut market
- Problem: Assumes competitors priced correctly

**Value-based pricing:** Price based on value delivered to customer
- Correct approach: What is this outcome worth to them?

### Packaging Framework

```
TIER 1 (Entry): Low price, self-serve, proves concept
- Price: Low barrier
- Delivery: Automated/minimal touch
- Purpose: Volume, qualification, upsell path

TIER 2 (Core): Primary offer, balanced value/effort
- Price: Sustainable margin
- Delivery: Systematized, efficient
- Purpose: Revenue engine

TIER 3 (Premium): High price, high touch
- Price: Premium margin
- Delivery: Customized, intensive
- Purpose: Revenue maximization, case studies
```

### Unit Economics

| Metric | Definition | Healthy Range |
|--------|------------|---------------|
| CAC | Cost to Acquire Customer | Varies by model |
| LTV | Lifetime Value | 3x+ CAC |
| LTV:CAC | Ratio of lifetime value to acquisition cost | 3:1 minimum, 5:1 ideal |
| Gross Margin | (Revenue - Direct Costs) / Revenue | 50-80% for services |
| Payback Period | Months to recover CAC | <12 months |

## F3: Organizational Structure

### The GOD Ownership Model

Every business needs three functional owners:

| Function | Owns | Accountable For |
|----------|------|-----------------|
| Growth Owner | Find, Warm, Book | Pipeline generated |
| Operations Owner | Standardize, Automate, Instrument | Efficiency gains |
| Delivery Owner | Deliver, Prove, Expand | Customer success |

**Small team?** One person can own multiple functions. The principle remains: single ownership per brick.

### Ownership Rules

1. **One owner per outcome** -- Committees discuss, owners decide
2. **Authority matches responsibility** -- Can't own what you can't control
3. **Weekly accountability** -- Report outcomes, not activities
4. **Clear escalation path** -- When owner is stuck, where do they go?

## F4: Financial Architecture

### Unit Economics Benchmarks

| Metric | Poor | Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|--------|------|------------|------|-----------|
| LTV:CAC | <2:1 | 2-3:1 | 3-5:1 | >5:1 |
| Gross Margin | <40% | 40-60% | 60-75% | >75% |
| Payback (months) | >18 | 12-18 | 6-12 | <6 |
| Monthly Churn | >10% | 5-10% | 2-5% | <2% |
| Revenue/Employee | <$100K | $100-200K | $200-300K | >$300K |

### Cash Flow Rules

**Collection:**
- Collect upfront when possible (deposits, prepayment)
- Invoice immediately upon delivery
- Follow up on Day 1, Day 7, Day 14 of overdue
- Fire clients who don't pay

**Reserves:**
- Minimum 3 months operating expenses
- Target 6 months for stability
- 12 months for aggressive growth plays

# PART III: THE 5 LAWS

The 5 Laws are non-negotiable principles. Break them and ScaleOS fails.

---

## Law 1: Document Before You Scale

**The Principle:** You cannot automate what you haven't documented. You cannot delegate what isn't defined.

**Anti-Pattern:** "We'll document once things stabilize."

Things never stabilize. Document NOW, even if messy. A 70% complete SOP beats a 100% complete one that lives only in memory.

**The Test:** If key person disappeared tomorrow, could someone else do their job from documentation alone?

---

## Law 2: One Owner Per Outcome

**The Principle:** Committees discuss. Owners decide. Every metric, process, and project needs exactly one owner.

**Anti-Pattern:** "The team is responsible for that."

No team is responsible for anything. Individuals are. Teams support. One person owns.

**The Test:** Can you name one person accountable for every important outcome?

---

## Law 3: Weekly Cycles, Not Quarterly

**The Principle:** 52 opportunities per year to learn and adjust beats 4. Speed of iteration determines speed of improvement.

**Anti-Pattern:** "Let's revisit this next quarter."

Quarterly is too slow. By then you've wasted 13 weeks on the wrong thing.

**The Test:** Do you know this week's performance versus last week's?

---

## Law 4: Proof Over Promises

**The Principle:** Show, don't tell. Results beat rhetoric. Evidence beats assertions.

**Anti-Pattern:** "We deliver amazing results for our clients."

Prove it. What results? For whom? By how much? When?

**The Test:** Can you prove every claim you make with specific evidence?

---

## Law 5: System Over Heroics

**The Principle:** Heroes don't scale. The person who stayed up all night to save a project = evidence the system is broken.

**Anti-Pattern:** "Thank god Sarah was there to save the day!"

Wrong response. Right response: "Why did it need saving? How do we prevent this?"

**The Test:** Would your business survive if your best person took a month off?

# PART IV: IMPLEMENTATION BY STAGE

### Stage 0: Pre-Revenue ($0)

**Priority:** F1 Positioning, G1 Find, G3 Book
**Skip:** Complex automation, detailed SOPs, delivery systems
**Success Signal:** First qualified meeting booked

### Stage 1: Early-Stage ($0-$100K)

**Priority:** G1-G3 systematized, O1 delivery documentation, D2 proof collection
**Success Signal:** Can hand off one delivery task using documentation

### Stage 2: Growing ($100K-$500K)

**Priority:** Full Growth pillar, O2 Automate, O3 Instrument
**Success Signal:** 20+ hours/week recovered through systems

### Stage 3: Scaling ($500K-$2M)

**Priority:** F3 organizational structure, full Delivery pillar, owners for every brick
**Success Signal:** Business runs for 2 weeks without founder involvement

### Stage 4: Established ($2M+)

**Priority:** D3 Expand optimization (NRR 110%+), F4 Financial architecture, leadership development
**Success Signal:** 30%+ YoY growth without proportional headcount increase
